Glycosmis trichanthera var. sumatrana

Glycosmis trichanthera var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red, berry-like fruits. Its fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal fruit display.
