Galatella linosyris var. armoricana

Galatella linosyris var. armoricana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
