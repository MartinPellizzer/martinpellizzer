Garcinia wollastonii

Garcinia wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has attracted interest for potential medicinal and nutritional applications.
