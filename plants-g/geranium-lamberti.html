Geranium lamberti

Geranium lamberti, commonly known as the lambert's geranium, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or pink blooms, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium lamberti is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
