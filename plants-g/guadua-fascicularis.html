Guadua fascicularis

Guadua fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. This plant is characterized by its slender, clustered culms and narrow, elongated leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Guadua fascicularis is valued for its strong, lightweight bamboo, which is used in construction and handicrafts.
