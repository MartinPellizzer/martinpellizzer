Gentiana chrysoneura

Gentiana chrysoneura, commonly known as the golden crown gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Gentiana chrysoneura is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
