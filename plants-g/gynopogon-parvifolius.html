Gynopogon parvifolius

Gynopogon parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved gynopogon, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikelets with two to three florets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides.

Gynopogon parvifolius is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
