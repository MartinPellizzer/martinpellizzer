Gymnosporia pertinax

Gymnosporia pertinax, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which are adaptations to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. These fruits are often covered in hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur.

Gymnosporia pertinax is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
