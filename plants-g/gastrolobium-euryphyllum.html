Gastrolobium euryphyllum

Gastrolobium euryphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features compound leaves with multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets that are typically pale green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Gastrolobium euryphyllum is an important component of local ecosystems and provides habitat for various native wildlife species.
