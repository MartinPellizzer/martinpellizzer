Goeppertia majestica

Goeppertia majestica, commonly known as the Queen's Bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage and unique rosette-shaped leaves. The plant features a central cup that collects water, which serves as a habitat for small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are usually broad, stiff, and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Goeppertia majestica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
