Grindelia papposa

Grindelia papposa, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Grindelia papposa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
