Gentiana germanica var. solstitialis

Gentiana germanica var. solstitialis, commonly known as the sunny gentian, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
