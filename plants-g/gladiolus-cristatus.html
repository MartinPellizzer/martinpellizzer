Gladiolus cristatus

Gladiolus cristatus, commonly known as the crested gladiolus, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant's most distinctive feature is its unique, crested inflorescence, which gives it its common name. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Gladiolus cristatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
