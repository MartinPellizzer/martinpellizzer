Grangea decumbens

Grangea decumbens, commonly known as the creeping grangea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Grangea decumbens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to form dense ground cover.
