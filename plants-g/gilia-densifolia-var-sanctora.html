Gilia densifolia var. sanctora

Gilia densifolia var. sanctora, commonly known as the Sanctora gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
