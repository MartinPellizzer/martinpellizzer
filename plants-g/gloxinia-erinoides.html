Gloxinia erinoides

Gloxinia erinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf gloxinia, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the warm months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Gloxinia erinoides is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
