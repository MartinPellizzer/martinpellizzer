Gigantochloa latifolia var. efimbriata

Gigantochloa latifolia var. efimbriata, commonly known as the broadleaf giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, narrow leaves with a prominent white margin, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant is characterized by its strong, stiff culms that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts in its native regions.
