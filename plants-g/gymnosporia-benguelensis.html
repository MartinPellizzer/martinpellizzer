Gymnosporia benguelensis

Gymnosporia benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela thornveld acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Gymnosporia benguelensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
