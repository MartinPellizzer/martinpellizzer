Galinsoga brachiata

Galinsoga brachiata, commonly known as Brazilian eclipta, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Galinsoga brachiata is also noted for its medicinal properties in some traditional herbal practices.
