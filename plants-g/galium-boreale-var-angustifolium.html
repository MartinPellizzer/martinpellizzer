Galium boreale var. angustifolium

Galium boreale var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
