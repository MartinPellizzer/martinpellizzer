Glyceria phryganodes

Glyceria phryganodes, commonly known as the tufted pondgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in tufts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and spike-like, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Glyceria phryganodes plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
