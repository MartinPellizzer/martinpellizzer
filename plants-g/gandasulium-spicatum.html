Gandasulium spicatum

Gandasulium spicatum, commonly known as the spiky leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, spiky foliage.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features fronds that are elongated and covered in tiny, sharp projections, giving it a rugged appearance. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sori located on the underside of its leaves.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
