Gentiana franchetiana

Gentiana franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gentiana franchetiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
