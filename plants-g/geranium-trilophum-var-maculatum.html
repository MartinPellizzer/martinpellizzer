Geranium trilophum var. maculatum

Geranium trilophum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a red or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
