Gomphrena coccinea

Gomphrena coccinea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to pink, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Gomphrena coccinea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
