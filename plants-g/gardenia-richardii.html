Gardenia richardii

Gardenia richardii, commonly known as the richardson gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries containing seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Gardenia richardii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and strong, sweet scent.
