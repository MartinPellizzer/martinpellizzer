Gomphrena pilosa

Gomphrena pilosa, commonly known as the feathery gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often resembling the shape of a crown or a brush. Its foliage is typically hairy and grayish-green, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gomphrena pilosa is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
