Gentianopsis detonsa

Gentianopsis detonsa, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

Gentianopsis detonsa is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
