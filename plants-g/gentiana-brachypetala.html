Gentiana brachypetala

Gentiana brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petalled gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Gentiana brachypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
