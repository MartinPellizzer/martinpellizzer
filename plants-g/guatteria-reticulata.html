Guatteria reticulata

Guatteria reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to red when mature, often containing a single seed.
