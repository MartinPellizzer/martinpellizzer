Grevillea involucrata

Grevillea involucrata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Grevillea involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and colorful blooms.
