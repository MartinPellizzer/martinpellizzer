Geranium purpureum var. littorale

Geranium purpureum var. littorale, commonly known as the coastal geranium, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
