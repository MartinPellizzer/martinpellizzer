Geranium costatum

Geranium costatum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Geranium costatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
