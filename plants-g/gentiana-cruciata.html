Gentiana cruciata

Gentiana cruciata, commonly known as the crosswort gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The genus Gentiana is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers and is part of the Gentianaceae family.
