Gentiana takedae f. leucantha

Gentiana takedae f. leucantha, commonly known as the white gentian, is a rare and striking variant of the gentian genus.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which contrast sharply with its deep green foliage. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
