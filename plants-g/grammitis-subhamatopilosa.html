Grammitis subhamatopilosa

Grammitis subhamatopilosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered fronds that form a compact, shield-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its fronds are covered with fine, hairy structures that help retain moisture and protect against desiccation.

Grammitis subhamatopilosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid, low-light conditions.
