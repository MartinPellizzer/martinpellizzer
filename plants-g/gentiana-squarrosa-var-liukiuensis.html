Gentiana squarrosa var. liukiuensis

Gentiana squarrosa var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liukiu gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in a white, waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
