Genista rostrata

Genista rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often spiny stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include small, stipitate leaves and a flowering structure known as an umbel.

Genista rostrata is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
