Geranium phaeum var. balcanicum

Geranium phaeum var. balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
