Grindelia arenicola

Grindelia arenicola, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and coastal environments. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in arid regions.

Grindelia arenicola plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
