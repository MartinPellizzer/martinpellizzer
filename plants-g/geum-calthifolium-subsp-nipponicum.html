Geum calthifolium subsp. nipponicum

Geum calthifolium subsp.

nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese avens, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers in early spring, followed by reddish-brown fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
