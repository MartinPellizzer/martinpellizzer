Guzmania claviformis

Guzmania claviformis, commonly known as the club-shaped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that resembles a cluster of flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports microfauna and amphibians. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color depending on light exposure.

Guzmania claviformis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal.
