Gentiana horaimontana

Gentiana horaimontana, commonly known as the Horaimontan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or purple corolla, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Gentiana horaimontana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
