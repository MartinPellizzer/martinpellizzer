Gymnopteris aliena var. purdiei

Gymnopteris aliena var. purdiei, commonly known as the Purdie's barren fern, is a variant of the barren fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests. Its reproductive structures are reduced, which is a common trait in the genus Gymnopteris.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
