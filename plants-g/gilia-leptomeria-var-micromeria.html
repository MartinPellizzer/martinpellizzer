Gilia leptomeria var. micromeria

Gilia leptomeria var. micromeria, commonly known as the smallflower gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
