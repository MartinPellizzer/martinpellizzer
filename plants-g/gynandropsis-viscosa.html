Gynandropsis viscosa

Gynandropsis viscosa, commonly known as the sticky gynandropsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is notable for its viscid, sticky leaves and stems, which help in reducing water loss and deterring herbivores. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The flowers are yellow and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
