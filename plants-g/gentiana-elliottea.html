Gentiana elliottea

Gentiana elliottea, commonly known as Elliot's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Gentiana elliottea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
