Gladiolus splendidus

Gladiolus splendidus, commonly known as the splendid gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing large, colorful inflorescences that consist of multiple florets arranged in a spike. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Gladiolus splendidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
