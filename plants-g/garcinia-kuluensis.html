Garcinia kuluensis

Garcinia kuluensis, commonly known as the Kulu orange, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds.

Garcinia kuluensis is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
