Gomphrena perennis var. villosa

Gomphrena perennis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
