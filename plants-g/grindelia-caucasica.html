Grindelia caucasica

Grindelia caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Grindelia caucasica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
