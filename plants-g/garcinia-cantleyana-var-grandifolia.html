Garcinia cantleyana var. grandifolia

Garcinia cantleyana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
