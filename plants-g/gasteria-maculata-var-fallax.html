Gasteria maculata var. fallax

Gasteria maculata var. fallax, commonly known as the Zulu Lily, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white or yellowish markings. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in white or pale yellow. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

This variety is prized for its compact growth and ornamental appeal in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
