Gymnema tingens var. cordifolium

Gymnema tingens var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaf gymnema, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
