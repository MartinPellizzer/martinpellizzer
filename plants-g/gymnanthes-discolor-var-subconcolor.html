Gymnanthes discolor var. subconcolor

Gymnanthes discolor var. subconcolor, commonly known as the variegated white soursop, is a variant of the white soursop plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically smooth, oblong, and has a thin, pale green skin with a white, juicy interior. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
