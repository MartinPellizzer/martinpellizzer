Gentiana scarlatiflora

Gentiana scarlatiflora, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking deep red to orange-red flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
