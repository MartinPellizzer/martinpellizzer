Glycosmis pilosa

Glycosmis pilosa, commonly known as the Chinese date or wild date tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include smooth, dark brown bark and yellowish-white flowers with a strong, aromatic scent.

Glycosmis pilosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
