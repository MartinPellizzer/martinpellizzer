Gentiana phlogifolia

Gentiana phlogifolia, commonly known as the fireweed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a rich nectar that attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
