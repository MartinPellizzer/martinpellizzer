Gymnocalycium westii

Gymnocalycium westii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Gymnocalycium westii is a succulent that stores water in its stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
