Gentianopsis yabei

Gentianopsis yabei, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable component of the region's flora.
