Gratiola officinalis var. catalaunica

Gratiola officinalis var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
