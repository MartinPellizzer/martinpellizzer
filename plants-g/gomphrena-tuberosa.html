Gomphrena tuberosa

Gomphrena tuberosa, commonly known as the clustered bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, long-lasting blooms. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Gomphrena tuberosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
