Gymnadenia × turnowskyi

Gymnadenia × turnowskyi, commonly known as the Turnowsky's grasspocket orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces distinctive, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

This hybrid is native to Central Europe and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
