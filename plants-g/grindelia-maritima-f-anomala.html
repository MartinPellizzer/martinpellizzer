Grindelia maritima f. anomala

Grindelia maritima f. anomala, commonly known as the coastal globemallow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
