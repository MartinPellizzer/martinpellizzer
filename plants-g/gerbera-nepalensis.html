Gerbera nepalensis

Gerbera nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal Gerbera, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its bright yellow to orange petals and greenish centers make it a striking ornamental plant.

Gerbera nepalensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
