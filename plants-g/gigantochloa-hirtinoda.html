Gigantochloa hirtinoda

Gigantochloa hirtinoda, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a tropical grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the lower surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense panicles, typical of the grass family.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
