Goodenia havilandii var. pauperata

Goodenia havilandii var. pauperata, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It features wiry, branching stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish to white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
