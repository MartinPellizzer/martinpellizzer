Glaucium leiocarpum

Glaucium leiocarpum, commonly known as the lesser prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces showy, papery flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its capsule fruits contain small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Glaucium leiocarpum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
