Glechoma pusilla

Glechoma pusilla, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
