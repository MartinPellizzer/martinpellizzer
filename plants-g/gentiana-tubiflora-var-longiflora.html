Gentiana tubiflora var. longiflora

Gentiana tubiflora var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
