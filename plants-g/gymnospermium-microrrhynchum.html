Gymnospermium microrrhynchum

Gymnospermium microrrhynchum, commonly known as the dwarf coneplant, is a member of the gymnosperm group, characterized by its ability to produce seeds without enclosing them in an ovary.

This plant typically exhibits a conifer-like structure with needle-like leaves and cones, adapted to thrive in cold, mountainous environments. It has a relatively simple vascular system and lacks true flowers and fruits, which are features typical of angiosperms. Gymnospermium microrrhynchum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

Its reproductive structures are designed for wind pollination, a common strategy among gymnosperms.
