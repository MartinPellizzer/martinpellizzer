Grayia brandegeei var. plummeri

Grayia brandegeei var. plummeri, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
