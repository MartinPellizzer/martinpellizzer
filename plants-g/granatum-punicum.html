Granatum punicum

Granatum punicum, commonly known as pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the region spanning from the eastern Mediterranean to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces striking, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by the familiar, bright red, many-seeded fruits. The fruit's outer skin, or aril, is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications for centuries.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to arid climates and its economic importance as both an ornamental and a fruit-bearing plant.
