Glochidion merrillii

Glochidion merrillii, commonly known as the Malayan glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cup-shaped structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Glochidion merrillii is typically found in lowland rainforests and is often associated with limestone substrates.

It plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various insects and birds.
