Gymnocalycium spegazzinii subsp. cardenasianum

Gymnocalycium spegazzinii subsp.

cardenasianum, commonly known as the Cardenasian prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered with spines and are typically green in color.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent properties to store water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
