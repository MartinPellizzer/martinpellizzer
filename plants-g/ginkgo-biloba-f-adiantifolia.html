Ginkgo biloba f. adiantifolia

Ginkgo biloba f. adiantifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf ginkgo, is a unique variant of the ginkgo tree characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves.

This form exhibits a more delicate and intricate leaf structure compared to the standard ginkgo, with leaves that resemble those of the fern family. It is a male-only variant, producing no seeds, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant retains its leaves year-round, showcasing a striking color change from dark green to yellow in autumn.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
