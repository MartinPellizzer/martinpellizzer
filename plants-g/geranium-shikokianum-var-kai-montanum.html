Geranium shikokianum var. kai-montanum

Geranium shikokianum var. kai-montanum, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a compact, flowering plant native to Japan.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
