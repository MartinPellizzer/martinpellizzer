Grossularia campestris

Grossularia campestris, commonly known as the common gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has alternate, simple leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The fruit is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to its high acidity and unique flavor.
