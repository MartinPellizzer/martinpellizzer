Gustavia fosteri

Gustavia fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
