Gesneria pardina

Gesneria pardina, commonly known as the spotted gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with green and white spots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its leaves are often thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Gesneria pardina produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months.
