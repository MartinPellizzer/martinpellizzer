Gonocarpus scordioides

Gonocarpus scordioides, commonly known as the wild leek, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its edible leaves, it has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
