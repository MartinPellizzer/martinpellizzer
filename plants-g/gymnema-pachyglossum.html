Gymnema pachyglossum

Gymnema pachyglossum, commonly known as the broad-leaf gymnema, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Gymnema pachyglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
