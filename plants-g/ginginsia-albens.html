Ginginsia albens

Ginginsia albens, commonly known as the white ginger plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a subtle white or pale green variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Ginginsia albens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden spaces.
