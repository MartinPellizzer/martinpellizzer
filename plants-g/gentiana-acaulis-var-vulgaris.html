Gentiana acaulis var. vulgaris

Gentiana acaulis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it lacks a true stem.

The genus Gentiana is known for its vivid blue flowers, and this variety is one of the most widely recognized species within the group.
