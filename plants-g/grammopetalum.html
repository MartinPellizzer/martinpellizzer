Grammopetalum

Grammopetalum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and fascinating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with long, narrow leaves and delicate, white or pale green flowers. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Its botanical significance lies in its rarity and the challenges it presents for cultivation and conservation.
