Gastrocalyx ampullata

Gastrocalyx ampullata, commonly known as the bladder orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, bladder-like structure at the base of its flower, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a short, narrow labellum.

Gastrocalyx ampullata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
