Gentianodes argentea

Gentianodes argentea, commonly known as silver gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
