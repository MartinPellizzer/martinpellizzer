Guzmania sintenisii

Guzmania sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range from red to orange in color. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small invertebrates. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments without requiring soil.

Guzmania sintenisii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
