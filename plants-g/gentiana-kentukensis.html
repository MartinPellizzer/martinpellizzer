Gentiana kentukensis

Gentiana kentukensis, commonly known as the Kentucky gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Gentianaceae family.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
