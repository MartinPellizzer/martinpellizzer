Geoffroea striata

Geoffroea striata, commonly known as the velvetleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spherical clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense, bushy appearance.

Geoffroea striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
