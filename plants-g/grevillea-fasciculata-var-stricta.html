Grevillea fasciculata var. stricta

Grevillea fasciculata var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its floral structure consists of a central column of stamens surrounded by modified leaves, a feature common in the Grevillea genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
