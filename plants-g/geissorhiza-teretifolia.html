Geissorhiza teretifolia

Geissorhiza teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed geissorhiza, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
