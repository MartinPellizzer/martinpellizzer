Gnaphalium gallicum

Gnaphalium gallicum, commonly known as the French cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing tiny yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads.

Gnaphalium gallicum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its aromatic, slightly medicinal properties.
