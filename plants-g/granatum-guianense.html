Granatum guianense

Granatum guianense, commonly known as the Guianese pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Punicaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental red flowers and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to true pomegranates but are not typically consumed by humans. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
