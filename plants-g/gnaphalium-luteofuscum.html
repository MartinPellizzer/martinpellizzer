Gnaphalium luteofuscum

Gnaphalium luteofuscum, commonly known as the yellow-fuscous everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in dense clusters along the stems.

Gnaphalium luteofuscum is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
