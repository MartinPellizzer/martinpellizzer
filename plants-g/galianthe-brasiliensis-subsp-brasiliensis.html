Galianthe brasiliensis subsp. brasiliensis

Galianthe brasiliensis subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian galianthe, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its flowers have five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
