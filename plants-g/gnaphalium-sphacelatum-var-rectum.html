Gnaphalium sphacelatum var. rectum

Gnaphalium sphacelatum var. rectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a soft, woolly texture on the stems and leaves.
