Gomphrena aurea

Gomphrena aurea, commonly known as the golden globe or golden frost, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded inflorescences that range in color from pink to red and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gomphrena aurea is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
