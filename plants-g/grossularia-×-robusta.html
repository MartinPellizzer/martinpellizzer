Grossularia × robusta

Grossularia × robusta, commonly known as the Himalayan snowberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its robust growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

The berries are a food source for various birds and mammals in its native habitat.
