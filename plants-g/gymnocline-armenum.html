Gymnocline armenum

Gymnocline armenum, commonly known as Armenian nakedstem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, hairy stems that rise from a short, rhizomatous rootstock. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Gymnocline armenum is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
