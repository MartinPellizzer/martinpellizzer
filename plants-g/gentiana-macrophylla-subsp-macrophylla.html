Gentiana macrophylla subsp. macrophylla

Gentiana macrophylla subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are large, broadly ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
