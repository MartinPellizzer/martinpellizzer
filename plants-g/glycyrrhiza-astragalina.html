Glycyrrhiza astragalina

Glycyrrhiza astragalina, commonly known as the sweetroot or licorice plant, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza astragalina is valued for its roots, which contain glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine.

Its growth is often associated with arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
