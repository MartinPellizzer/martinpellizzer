Genista tournefortii subsp. decipiens

Genista tournefortii subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the false broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and dry conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
