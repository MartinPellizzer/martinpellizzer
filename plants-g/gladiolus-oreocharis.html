Gladiolus oreocharis

Gladiolus oreocharis, commonly known as the black gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of deep red, purple, and black. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike, with individual blooms opening sequentially from the base upward.

This species is valued in horticulture for its dramatic appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
