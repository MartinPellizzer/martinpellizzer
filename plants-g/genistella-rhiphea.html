Genistella rhiphea

Genistella rhiphea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and woodlands. Its yellow flowers appear in early spring, and it is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
