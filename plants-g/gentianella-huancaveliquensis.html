Gentianella huancaveliquensis

Gentianella huancaveliquensis, commonly known as the Huancavelica gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy blue or purple flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and prominent yellow or orange markings.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
