Geranium glanduligerum

Geranium glanduligerum, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered with glandular hairs that produce a sticky, aromatic substance. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and also covered with glandular secretions.

Geranium glanduligerum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
