Gymnopteris subcrenata, commonly known as the cleft frond fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into narrow, cleft-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gymnopteris subcrenata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often found in horticultural settings.
