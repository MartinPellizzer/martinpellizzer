Gentiana humilis var. caerulea

Gentiana humilis var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
