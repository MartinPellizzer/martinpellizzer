Galium pumilum subsp. slesvicense

Galium pumilum subsp.

slesvicense, commonly known as the Slesvig bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a hairy surface.

The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach up to 20 centimeters in height.
