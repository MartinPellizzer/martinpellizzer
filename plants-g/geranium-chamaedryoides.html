Geranium chamaedryoides

Geranium chamaedryoides, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine white or gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its adaptability and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
