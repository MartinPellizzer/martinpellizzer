Gymnosporia bukobina

Gymnosporia bukobina, commonly known as the Bukoban thornbush, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
