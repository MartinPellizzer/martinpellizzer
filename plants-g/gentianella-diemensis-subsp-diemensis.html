Gentianella diemensis subsp. diemensis

Gentianella diemensis subsp.

diemensis, commonly known as the Southern Gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators.
