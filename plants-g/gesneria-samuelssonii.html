Gesneria samuelssonii

Gesneria samuelssonii, commonly known as the Samuelson's gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Gesneria samuelssonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
