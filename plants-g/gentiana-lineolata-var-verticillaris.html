Gentiana lineolata var. verticillaris

Gentiana lineolata var. verticillaris, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are arranged in a whorled pattern, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
