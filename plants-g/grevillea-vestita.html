Grevillea vestita

Grevillea vestita, commonly known as the white-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white to greyish coating. The plant produces striking clusters of long, narrow, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds and insects.
