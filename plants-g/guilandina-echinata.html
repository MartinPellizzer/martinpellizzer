Guilandina echinata

Guilandina echinata, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
