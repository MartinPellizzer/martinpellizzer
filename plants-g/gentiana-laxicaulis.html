Gentiana laxicaulis

Gentiana laxicaulis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Gentiana laxicaulis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
