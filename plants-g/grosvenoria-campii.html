Grosvenoria campii

Grosvenoria campii, commonly known as the Campi's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often appearing in shaded, humid environments. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Grosvenoria campii is considered a conservation concern.
