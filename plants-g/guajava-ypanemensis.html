Guajava ypanemensis

Guajava ypanemensis, commonly known as the Ypanemense guajava, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded understory environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
