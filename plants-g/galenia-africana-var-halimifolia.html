Galenia africana var. halimifolia

Galenia africana var. halimifolia, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun.

Its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals, is a notable botanical feature.
