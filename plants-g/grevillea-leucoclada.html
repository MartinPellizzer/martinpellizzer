Grevillea leucoclada

Grevillea leucoclada, commonly known as the white-veined grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves with distinctive white veins, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of red and orange, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea leucoclada is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
