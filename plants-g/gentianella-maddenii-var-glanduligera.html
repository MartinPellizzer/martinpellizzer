Gentianella maddenii var. glanduligera

Gentianella maddenii var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are tubular and have five lobes, typical of the Gentianaceae family.

The species is characterized by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name.
