Gnaphalium splendidum

Gnaphalium splendidum, commonly known as the showy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces numerous tiny white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant's name reflects its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
