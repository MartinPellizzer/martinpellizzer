Geranium cucullatum var. typicum

Geranium cucullatum var. typicum, commonly known as the hooded geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-shaped calyx that encloses the flower, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It has hairy, lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
