Genetyllis micropetala

Genetyllis micropetala, commonly known as the small-petalled genetyllis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the cooler months of the year. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Genetyllis micropetala is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient species within its native habitat.
