Grevillea sericea subsp. sericea

Grevillea sericea subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in horticulture.
