Goodia lotifolia var. pubescens

Goodia lotifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy goodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodiaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
