Georgia purpurea

Georgia purpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet to purple petals surrounding a central cone-like structure composed of bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Georgia purpurea is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.
