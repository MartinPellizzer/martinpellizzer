Gentiana americana

Gentiana americana, commonly known as the American gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
