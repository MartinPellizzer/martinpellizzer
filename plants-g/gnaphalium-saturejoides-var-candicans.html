Gnaphalium saturejoides var. candicans

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. candicans, commonly known as the showy everlasting or golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a striking appearance. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting nature.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
