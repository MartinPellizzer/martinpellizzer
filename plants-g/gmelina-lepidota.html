Gmelina lepidota

Gmelina lepidota, commonly known as the velvetwood or silky gum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. The tree is also valued for its timber, which is lightweight and suitable for furniture and construction.
