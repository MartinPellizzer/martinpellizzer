Gentianella aurea

Gentianella aurea, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gentianella aurea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
