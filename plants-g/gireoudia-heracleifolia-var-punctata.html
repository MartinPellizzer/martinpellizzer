Gireoudia heracleifolia var. punctata

Gireoudia heracleifolia var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves with a serrated margin and a unique spotted pattern on the fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

The varietal name "punctata" refers to the small, dark spots that adorn the leaflets, adding to its ornamental value.
