Globularia vulgaris var. oelandica

Globularia vulgaris var. oelandica, commonly known as the Oeland Globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
