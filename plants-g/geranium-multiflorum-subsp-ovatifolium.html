Geranium multiflorum subsp. ovatifolium

Geranium multiflorum subsp.

ovatifolium, commonly known as the ovate-leaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
