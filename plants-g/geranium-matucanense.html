Geranium matucanense

Geranium matucanense, commonly known as the Matucan geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
