Gaylussacia salicifolia

Gaylussacia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, acidic berries. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
