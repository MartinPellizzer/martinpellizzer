Gleditsia japonica f. inermis

Gleditsia japonica f. inermis, commonly known as the Japanese honey locust, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
