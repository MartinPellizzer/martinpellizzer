Gentianella amabilis

Gentianella amabilis, commonly known as the showy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella amabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
