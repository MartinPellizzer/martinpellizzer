Gentiana autumnalis f. albescens

Gentiana autumnalis f. albescens, commonly known as the white gentian, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are predominantly white or pale blue. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, thriving in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are erect and robust.

This variant of Gentiana autumnalis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
