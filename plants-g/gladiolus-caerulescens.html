Gladiolus caerulescens

Gladiolus caerulescens, commonly known as the blue gladiolus, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color, with three prominent sepals that resemble a sword, giving the plant its name. Its leaves are long, narrow, and lance-shaped, arranged in a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
