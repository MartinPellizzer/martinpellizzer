Grammanthes depressa

Grammanthes depressa, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with short, fleshy roots and is often found growing in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike bearing several blooms.

Grammanthes depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
