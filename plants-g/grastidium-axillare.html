Grastidium axillare

Grastidium axillare, commonly known as the axillary grass-tongue, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Grastidium axillare is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
