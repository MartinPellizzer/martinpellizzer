Geum × intermedium var. dubium

Geum × intermedium var. dubium, commonly known as the white wood avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant for erosion control in wetland habitats.
