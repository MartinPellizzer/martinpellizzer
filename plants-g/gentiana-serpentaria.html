Gentiana serpentaria

Gentiana serpentaria, commonly known as the serpent's root, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Gentiana serpentaria is also valued for its ecological role, supporting various pollinators and contributing to forest biodiversity.
