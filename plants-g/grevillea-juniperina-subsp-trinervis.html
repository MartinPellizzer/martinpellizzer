Grevillea juniperina subsp. trinervis

Grevillea juniperina subsp.

trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
