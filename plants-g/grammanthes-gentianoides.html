Grammanthes gentianoides

Grammanthes gentianoides, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

The yellow gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
