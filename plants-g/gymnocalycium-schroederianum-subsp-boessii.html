Gymnocalycium schroederianum subsp. boessii

Gymnocalycium schroederianum subsp.

boessii, commonly known as the Boessii moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is a low-growing plant with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of spines.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to store water in its succulent stems.
