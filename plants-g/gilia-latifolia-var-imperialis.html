Gilia latifolia var. imperialis

Gilia latifolia var. imperialis, commonly known as the imperial gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces slender, branching stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
