Gardenia chartacea

Gardenia chartacea, commonly known as the white gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are highly valued for their ornamental beauty and are often used in floral arrangements.

Gardenia chartacea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
