Glycyrrhizopsis flavescens

Glycyrrhizopsis flavescens, commonly known as the yellow sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It has a deep taproot system and is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.

Glycyrrhizopsis flavescens is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
