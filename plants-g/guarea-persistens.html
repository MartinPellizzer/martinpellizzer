Guarea persistens

Guarea persistens, commonly known as the red cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its dense, aromatic heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in catkins.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several angular seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
