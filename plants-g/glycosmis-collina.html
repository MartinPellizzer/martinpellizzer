Glycosmis collina

Glycosmis collina, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Glycosmis collina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
