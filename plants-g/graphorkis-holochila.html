Graphorkis holochila

Graphorkis holochila, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet and green veined leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive purple and white coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
