Gratiola saginoides

Gratiola saginoides, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant features whorled leaves and produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gratiola saginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
