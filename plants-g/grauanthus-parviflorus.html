Grauanthus parviflorus

Grauanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered grauanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Grauanthus parviflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
