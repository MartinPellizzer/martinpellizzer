Gymnema brevifolium

Gymnema brevifolium, commonly known as the Indian nettle or small-leaved gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to inhibit the absorption of glucose in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. It also contains compounds such as gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Gymnema brevifolium is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
