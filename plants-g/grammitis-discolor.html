Grammitis discolor

Grammitis discolor, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, light green to silver-gray fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. This plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical and subtropical forests. The fronds are usually short and clustered, with a distinctive, fan-like shape.

It is a low-growing epiphyte, often found on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded habitats.
