Glochidion andamanicum

Glochidion andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Glochidion andamanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
