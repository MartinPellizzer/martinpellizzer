Gigantochloa robusta

Gigantochloa robusta, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gigantochloa robusta is often used in landscaping and as a structural element in traditional architecture due to its strength and rapid growth.
