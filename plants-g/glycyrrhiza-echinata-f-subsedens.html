Glycyrrhiza echinata f. subsedens

Glycyrrhiza echinata f. subsedens, commonly known as the creeping licorice, is a variant of the licorice plant characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are valued for their sweet taste and medicinal properties, containing compounds like glycyrrhizin.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional herbal uses.
