Garcinia petiolaris

Garcinia petiolaris, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red, segmented fruit that resembles a mangosteen but is smaller and less commonly consumed. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. Garcinia petiolaris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique fruit structure and potential medicinal properties.
