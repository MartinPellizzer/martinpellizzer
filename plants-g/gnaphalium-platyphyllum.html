Gnaphalium platyphyllum

Gnaphalium platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaved cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its fluffy seed heads are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across various habitats.
