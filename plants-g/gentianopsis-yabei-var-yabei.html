Gentianopsis yabei var. yabei

Gentianopsis yabei var. yabei, commonly known as Yabei's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
