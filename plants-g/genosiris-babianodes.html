Genosiris babianodes

Genosiris babianodes, commonly known as the Babianode rootplant, is a rare species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, tuberous roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white and green, and bloom during the early spring.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
