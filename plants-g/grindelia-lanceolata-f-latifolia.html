Grindelia lanceolata f. latifolia

Grindelia lanceolata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gumweed, is a variant of the gumweed plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is often found in areas with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species adapted to arid environments.
