Gumira interrupta

Gumira interrupta, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits. Its latex sap is milky and can be used for various traditional purposes.

Gumira interrupta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
