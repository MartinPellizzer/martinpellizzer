Gentianopsis macounii

Gentianopsis macounii, commonly known as Macoun's gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically blue or purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is named after the botanist Henry David Macoun, who contributed significantly to the study of North American flora.
