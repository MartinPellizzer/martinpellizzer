Gloeospermum diversipetalum

Gloeospermum diversipetalum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive cup-like shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gloeospermum diversipetalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
