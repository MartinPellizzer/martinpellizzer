Globifera umbrosa

Globifera umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow globeweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the shaded understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, globe-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to low-light environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its roots system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in nutrient-poor soils.

Globifera umbrosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
