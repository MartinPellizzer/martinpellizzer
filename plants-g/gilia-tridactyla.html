Gilia tridactyla

Gilia tridactyla, commonly known as the three-toed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Miriophyllum family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
