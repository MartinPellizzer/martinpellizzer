Galium villosum

Galium villosum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a similar fuzzy texture.

Galium villosum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
