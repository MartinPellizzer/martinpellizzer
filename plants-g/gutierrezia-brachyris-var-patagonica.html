Gutierrezia brachyris var. patagonica

Gutierrezia brachyris var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian galleta, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is often found in dry grasslands and shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.
