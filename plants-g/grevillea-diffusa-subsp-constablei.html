Grevillea diffusa subsp. constablei

Grevillea diffusa subsp.

constablei, commonly known as the Geraldton Wax, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, waxy flowers that range in color from pink to red, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sandy soils and is a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

The Geraldton Wax is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for native pollinators.
