Gastrolobium spinosum f. oliganthum

Gastrolobium spinosum f. oliganthum, commonly known as the spiny acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces flattened pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

This form is distinguished by its reduced floral structure compared to the typical species.
