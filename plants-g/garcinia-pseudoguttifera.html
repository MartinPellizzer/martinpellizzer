Garcinia pseudoguttifera

Garcinia pseudoguttifera, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Garcinia pseudoguttifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.
