Geoffroea decorticans var. subtropicalis

Geoffroea decorticans var. subtropicalis, commonly known as the subtropical ironwood, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its hard, dense wood and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
