Gongora grossa

Gongora grossa, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the high-altitude regions of Central America, it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. Its inflorescence consists of multiple blooms, often in shades of purple and white.

Gongora grossa is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
