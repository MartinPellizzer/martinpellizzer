Gentiana crispata subsp. amblyphylla

Gentiana crispata subsp.

amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
