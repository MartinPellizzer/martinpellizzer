Gagea libanotica

Gagea libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulbs that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
