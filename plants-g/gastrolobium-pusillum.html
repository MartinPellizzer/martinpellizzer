Gastrolobium pusillum

Gastrolobium pusillum, commonly known as the small-flowered hakea, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Gastrolobium pusillum is often found in heathlands and is an important component of local ecosystems.
