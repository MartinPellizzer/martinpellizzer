Gymnolomia sylvatica

Gymnolomia sylvatica, commonly known as the European wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Gymnolomia sylvatica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
