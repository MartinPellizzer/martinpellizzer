Grindelia chacoensis

Grindelia chacoensis, commonly known as Chaco gumweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flower heads. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture. Its hairy leaves and compact growth habit help reduce water loss through transpiration.

Grindelia chacoensis is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
