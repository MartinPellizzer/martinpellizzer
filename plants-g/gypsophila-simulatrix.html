Gypsophila simulatrix

Gypsophila simulatrix, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss.

Gypsophila simulatrix is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance environments.
