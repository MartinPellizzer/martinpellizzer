Geranium floribundum

Geranium floribundum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

This species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
