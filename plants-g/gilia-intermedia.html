Gilia intermedia

Gilia intermedia, commonly known as the common gilia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its slender stems and narrow leaves are adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Gilia intermedia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
