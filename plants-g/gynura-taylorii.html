Gynura taylorii

Gynura taylorii, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and dense foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Gynura taylorii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
