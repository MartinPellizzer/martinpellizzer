Gymnanthemum cupulare

Gymnanthemum cupulare, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Gymnanthemum cupulare is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
