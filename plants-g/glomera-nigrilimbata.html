Glomera nigrilimbata

Glomera nigrilimbata, commonly known as the black-edged glomera, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with prominent black margins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with contrasting yellow throats. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
