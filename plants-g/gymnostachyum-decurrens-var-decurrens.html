Gymnostachyum decurrens var. decurrens

Gymnostachyum decurrens var. decurrens, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic environments and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
