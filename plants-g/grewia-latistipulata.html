Grewia latistipulata

Grewia latistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved grewia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Grewia latistipulata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
