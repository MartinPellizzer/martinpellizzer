Gladiolus conrathii

Gladiolus conrathii, commonly known as the Conrath's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing spike-like inflorescences with numerous petals. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
