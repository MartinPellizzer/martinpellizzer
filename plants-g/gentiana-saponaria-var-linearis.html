Gentiana saponaria var. linearis

Gentiana saponaria var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland habitats.
