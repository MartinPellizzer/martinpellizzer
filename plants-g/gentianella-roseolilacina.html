Gentianella roseolilacina

Gentianella roseolilacina, commonly known as the pink-lavender gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are tubular and often have a two-lipped structure, typical of the Gentianaceae family.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and delicate appearance.
