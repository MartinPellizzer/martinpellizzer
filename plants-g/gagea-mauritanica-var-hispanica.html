Gagea mauritanica var. hispanica

Gagea mauritanica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish adder's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It is characterized by its underground bulbs, which are used for propagation.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
