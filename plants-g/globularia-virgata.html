Globularia virgata

Globularia virgata, commonly known as the silver globe, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, spherical clusters of small, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
