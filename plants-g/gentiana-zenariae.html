Gentiana zenariae

Gentiana zenariae, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana zenariae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
