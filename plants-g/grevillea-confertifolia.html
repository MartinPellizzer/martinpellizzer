Grevillea confertifolia

Grevillea confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Grevillea confertifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
