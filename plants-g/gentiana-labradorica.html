Gentiana labradorica

Gentiana labradorica, commonly known as Labrador gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as tundra and wet meadows. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Gentiana labradorica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
