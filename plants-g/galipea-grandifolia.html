Galipea grandifolia

Galipea grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf galipea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.

The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
