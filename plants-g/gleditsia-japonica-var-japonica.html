Gleditsia japonica var. japonica

Gleditsia japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with pairs of oval, serrated leaflets that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, green pods that contain flattened, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also valued for soil enrichment and erosion control.
