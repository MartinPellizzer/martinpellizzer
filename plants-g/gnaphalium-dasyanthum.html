Gnaphalium dasyanthum

Gnaphalium dasyanthum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, and it produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance.

Gnaphalium dasyanthum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
