Gomphrena brachiata

Gomphrena brachiata, commonly known as the branched gayfeather, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces distinctive, feathery bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Gomphrena brachiata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
