Geonoma jussieuana

Geonoma jussieuana, commonly known as the Jussieu's arrowroot, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with dichotomous branching, and its fronds are usually pinnately divided with a lanceolate shape. The spores are produced in sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds, characteristic of many fern species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
