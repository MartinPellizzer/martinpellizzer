Grewia boehmiana

Grewia boehmiana, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Grewia boehmiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
