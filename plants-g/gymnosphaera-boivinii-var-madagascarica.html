Gymnosphaera boivinii var. madagascarica

Gymnosphaera boivinii var. madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a compact, globular shape with spiny, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, spiny structures, further enhancing its drought tolerance.

This variety is prized for its distinctive appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
