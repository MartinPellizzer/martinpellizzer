Gentiana formosissima

Gentiana formosissima, commonly known as the beautiful gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
