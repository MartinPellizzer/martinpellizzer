Gentiana lycopodioides

Gentiana lycopodioides, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are erect and covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana lycopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
