Gouania petiolaris

Gouania petiolaris, commonly known as the climbing gouania, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by triangular, three-lobed fruits. It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Gouania petiolaris is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it climbs using its tendrils.
