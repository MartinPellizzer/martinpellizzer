Garcinia caudiculata

Garcinia caudiculata, commonly known as the African star apple or wild mango, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stem and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn red when ripe. The fruit has a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Garcinia caudiculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
