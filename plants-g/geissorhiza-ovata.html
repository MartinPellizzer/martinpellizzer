Geissorhiza ovata

Geissorhiza ovata, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Geissorhiza ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
