Galium hirsutum

Galium hirsutum, commonly known as sticky willow or wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium hirsutum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
