Gongora boothiana

Gongora boothiana, commonly known as the Booth's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, yellow, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and rarity.

Gongora boothiana is also noted for its delicate, strap-like leaves and its preference for well-drained soil.
