Gompholobium roseum

Gompholobium roseum, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Gompholobium roseum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
