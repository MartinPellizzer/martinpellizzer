Gymnostoma glaucescens

Gymnostoma glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green moss, is a species of moss characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rock surfaces. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, pointed leaves that are usually a bluish-green in color. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on stalks and release spores for dispersal.

Gymnostoma glaucescens plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
