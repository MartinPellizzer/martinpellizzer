Geissorhiza callista

Geissorhiza callista, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers. The plant features fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geissorhiza callista is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
