Grossera perrieri

Grossera perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's giant gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its alpine habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Grossera perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
