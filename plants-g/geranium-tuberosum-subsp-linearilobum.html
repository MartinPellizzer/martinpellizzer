Geranium tuberosum subsp. linearilobum

Geranium tuberosum subsp.

linearilobum, commonly known as the linear-lobed geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
