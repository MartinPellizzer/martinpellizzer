Globularia liouvillei subsp. liouvillei

Globularia liouvillei subsp.

liouvillei, commonly known as the desert globularia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant has succulent leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is well-suited to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
