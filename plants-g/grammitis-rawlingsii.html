Grammitis rawlingsii

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with small, scale-like leaves. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
