Garcinia merguensis var. truncata

Garcinia merguensis var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated garcinia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
