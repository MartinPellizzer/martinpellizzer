Gerardia quercifolia

Gerardia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns vibrant red or orange in the fall.

Gerardia quercifolia is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
