Gardoquia acutifolia

Gardoquia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf gardoquia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Gardoquia acutifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.
