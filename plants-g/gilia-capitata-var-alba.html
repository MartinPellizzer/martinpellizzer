Gilia capitata var. alba

Gilia capitata var. alba, commonly known as the white-headed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, clustered blooms.
