Galium obliquum subsp. neoluteolum

Galium obliquum subsp.

neoluteolum, commonly known as the redstem sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically features slender, reddish stems that are often prostrate or ascending.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
