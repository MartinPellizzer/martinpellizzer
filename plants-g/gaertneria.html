Gaertneria

Gaertneria, commonly known as the "false arum," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Gaertneria species are typically herbaceous and can be found in tropical and subtropical regions. They often have striking, colorful bracts that serve to attract pollinators.

The genus includes several species, many of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
