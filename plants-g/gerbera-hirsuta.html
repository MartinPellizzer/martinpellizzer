Gerbera hirsuta

Gerbera hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gerbera hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
