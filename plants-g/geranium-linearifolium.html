Geranium linearifolium

Geranium linearifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive appearance with a white throat.

Geranium linearifolium is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
