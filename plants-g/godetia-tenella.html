Godetia tenella

Godetia tenella, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant orange to red flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Godetia tenella is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
