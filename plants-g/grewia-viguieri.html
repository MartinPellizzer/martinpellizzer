Grewia viguieri

Grewia viguieri, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia viguieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
