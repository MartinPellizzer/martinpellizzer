Glycine guineensis

Glycine guineensis, commonly known as African groundnut, is a leguminous plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Glycine guineensis is valued for its high protein content and is an important crop in tropical regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climatic conditions.
