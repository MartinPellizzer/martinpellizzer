Grumilea erythrotricha

Grumilea erythrotricha, commonly known as the red-hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in panicles, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Grumilea erythrotricha is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
