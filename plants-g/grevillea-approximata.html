Grevillea approximata

Grevillea approximata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features bright red, flame-like inflorescences that are highly attractive to birds and nectar-feeding insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea approximata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native wildlife.
