Gamanthus subglaber

Gamanthus subglaber, commonly known as the smooth-leaved gamanthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access water from underground sources. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.

Gamanthus subglaber is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
