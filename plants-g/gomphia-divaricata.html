Gomphia divaricata

Gomphia divaricata, commonly known as the zigzag rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in dense clusters at the base.

Gomphia divaricata is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.
