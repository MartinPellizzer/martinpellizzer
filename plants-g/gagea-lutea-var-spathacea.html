Gagea lutea var. spathacea

Gagea lutea var. spathacea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
