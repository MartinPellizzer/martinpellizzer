Geum paui

Geum paui, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and lobed. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and often grows in clusters.

Geum paui is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
