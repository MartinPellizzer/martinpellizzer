Gypsophila belorossica

Gypsophila belorossica, commonly known as the Siberian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Russia and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine and arctic environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to its habitat.

Gypsophila belorossica is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy, delicate look.
