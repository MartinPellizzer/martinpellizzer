Gelasia villosa subsp. villosa

Gelasia villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the woolly gelasia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, or villose, stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of Andean flora.
