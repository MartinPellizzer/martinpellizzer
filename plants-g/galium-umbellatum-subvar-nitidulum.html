Galium umbellatum subvar. nitidulum

Galium umbellatum subvar. nitidulum, commonly known as the glossy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a distinctive glossy sheen.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
