Gigantochloa aspera

Gigantochloa aspera, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gigantochloa aspera is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and resilience.
