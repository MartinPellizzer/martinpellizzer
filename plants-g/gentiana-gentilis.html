Gentiana gentilis

Gentiana gentilis, commonly known as the gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and can reach up to 60 centimeters in height.

Gentiana gentilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
