Gaultheria dumicola var. aspera

Gaultheria dumicola var. aspera, commonly known as the rough-leaved wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible and have a sharp, peppery taste.

This species thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
