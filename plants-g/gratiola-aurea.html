Gratiola aurea

Gratiola aurea, commonly known as golden gratiola, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gratiola aurea is valued in herbal medicine for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
