Gyrinopsis brachyantha

Gyrinopsis brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwheel, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, linear leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface.

Gyrinopsis brachyantha plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
