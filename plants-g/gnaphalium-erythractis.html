Gnaphalium erythractis

Gnaphalium erythractis, commonly known as the red-stemmed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its aromatic, woolly leaves and small, yellowish to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its common name reflects the reddish coloration of its stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures.
