Graphistylis toledoi

Graphistylis toledoi, commonly known as the Toledoi grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Mexico, characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
