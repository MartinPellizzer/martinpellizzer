Gentiana lhasaensis

Gentiana lhasaensis, commonly known as the Lhasa gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana lhasaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
