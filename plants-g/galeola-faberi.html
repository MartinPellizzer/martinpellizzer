Galeola faberi

Galeola faberi, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Galeola faberi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
