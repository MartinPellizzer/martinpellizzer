Galactia pretiosa

Galactia pretiosa, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Galactia pretiosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
