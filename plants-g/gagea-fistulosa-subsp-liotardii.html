Gagea fistulosa subsp. liotardii

Gagea fistulosa subsp.

liotardii, commonly known as the Sierra gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The subspecies liotardii is distinguished by its unique floral structure and geographic distribution compared to other subspecies of Gagea.
