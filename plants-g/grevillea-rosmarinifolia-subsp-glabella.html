Grevillea rosmarinifolia subsp. glabella

Grevillea rosmarinifolia subsp.

glabella, commonly known as the glabrous grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late spring to autumn. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in garden settings.
