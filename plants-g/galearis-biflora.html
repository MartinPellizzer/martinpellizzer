Galearis biflora

Galearis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem.

Galearis biflora is characterized by its two distinct flower types within each inflorescence, a unique feature among the Larkspur family.
