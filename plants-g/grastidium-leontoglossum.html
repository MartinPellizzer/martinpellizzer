Grastidium leontoglossum

Grastidium leontoglossum, commonly known as the lion's tongue plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that resemble a lion's tongue, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Native to southern Africa, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
