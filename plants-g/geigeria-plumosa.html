Geigeria plumosa

Geigeria plumosa, commonly known as the silky geigeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
