Galanthus valentinae

Galanthus valentinae, commonly known as the Valentina snowdrop, is a delicate bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It produces slender, white flowers with a distinctive greenish tinge, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its resilience in cold climates. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known bulbous plants.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
