Glossonema gautieri

Glossonema gautieri, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Glossonema gautieri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
