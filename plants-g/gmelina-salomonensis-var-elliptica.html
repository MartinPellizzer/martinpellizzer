Gmelina salomonensis var. elliptica

Gmelina salomonensis var. elliptica, commonly known as the Solomon Islands ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-green foliage and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a pointed tip. This species produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
