Geranium pimpinellifolium

Geranium pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with compact, bushy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
