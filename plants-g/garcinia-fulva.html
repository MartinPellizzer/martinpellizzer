Garcinia fulva

Garcinia fulva, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Garcinia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
