Gouldia terminalis var. tenuicaulis

Gouldia terminalis var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed gouldia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant, showy inflorescences that display a range of colors from red to orange. The plant forms dense clumps and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its elegant appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
