Grindelia nuda

Grindelia nuda, commonly known as the naked leaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and coastal areas. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions.

Grindelia nuda is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
