Gentiana scabrida var. angusta

Gentiana scabrida var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, scaly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
