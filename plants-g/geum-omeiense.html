Geum omeiense

Geum omeiense, commonly known as the Emei Mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
