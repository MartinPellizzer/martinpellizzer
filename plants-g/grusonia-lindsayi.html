Grusonia lindsayi

Grusonia lindsayi, commonly known as the Lindsay's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within dense forests, often epiphytic on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Grusonia lindsayi is considered a conservation concern.
