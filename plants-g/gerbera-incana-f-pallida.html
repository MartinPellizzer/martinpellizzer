Gerbera incana f. pallida

Gerbera incana f. pallida, commonly known as the pale gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the Gerbera genus. Its pale yellow to white flowers bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
