Galactia carnea

Galactia carnea, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Galactia carnea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
