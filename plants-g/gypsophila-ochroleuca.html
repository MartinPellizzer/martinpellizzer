Gypsophila ochroleuca

Gypsophila ochroleuca, commonly known as the white sweet pea or silk flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to gardens and natural landscapes.

Gypsophila ochroleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, fluffy blooms.
