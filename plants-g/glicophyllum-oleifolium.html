Glicophyllum oleifolium

Glicophyllum oleifolium, commonly known as the oilleaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.

Glicophyllum oleifolium thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
