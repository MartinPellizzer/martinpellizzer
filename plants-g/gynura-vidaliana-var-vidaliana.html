Gynura vidaliana var. vidaliana

Gynura vidaliana var. vidaliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf sorrel, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in some regions.
