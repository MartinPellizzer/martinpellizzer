Gentiana amarella

Gentiana amarella, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often erect.

Gentiana amarella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
