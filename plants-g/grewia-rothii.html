Grewia rothii

Grewia rothii, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia rothii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
