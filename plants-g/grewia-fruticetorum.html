Grewia fruticetorum

Grewia fruticetorum, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
