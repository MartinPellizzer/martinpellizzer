Genista unalii

Genista unalii, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a shrub native to the western coast of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Genista unalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
