Glycine precatoria

Glycine precatoria, commonly known as the yellow sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Glycine precatoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
