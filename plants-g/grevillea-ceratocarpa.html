Grevillea ceratocarpa

Grevillea ceratocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Grevillea ceratocarpa is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
