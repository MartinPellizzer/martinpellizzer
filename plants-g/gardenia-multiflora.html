Gardenia multiflora

Gardenia multiflora, commonly known as the clustered gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its white blooms.

Gardenia multiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
