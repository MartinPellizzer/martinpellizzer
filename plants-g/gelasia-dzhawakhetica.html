Gelasia dzhawakhetica

Gelasia dzhawakhetica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
