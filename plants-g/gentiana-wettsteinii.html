Gentiana wettsteinii

Gentiana wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Gentiana wettsteinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
