Graptopetalum saxifragoides

Graptopetalum saxifragoides, commonly known as the stonecrop jade plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
