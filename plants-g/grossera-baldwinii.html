Grossera baldwinii

Grossera baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's grossera, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to habitat loss, Grossera baldwinii is considered endangered and is protected in many areas.
