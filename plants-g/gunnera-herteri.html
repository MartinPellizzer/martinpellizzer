Gunnera herteri

Gunnera herteri, commonly known as the giant water shield, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain water in its moist habitat. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence.

It plays an important ecological role in its native wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
