Geum triflorum

Geum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and streambanks.

Geum triflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
