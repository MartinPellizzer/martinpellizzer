Gastrochilus sororius

Gastrochilus sororius, commonly known as the twin-lobed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive sympodial growth habit, producing two to three pseudobulbs per stem. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme and have a unique, intricate structure.

Gastrochilus sororius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
