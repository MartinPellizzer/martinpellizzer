Gardenia pyriformis

Gardenia pyriformis, commonly known as the eggplant gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, waxy white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and is well-suited for warm, humid climates. Its fruit is a smooth, egg-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
