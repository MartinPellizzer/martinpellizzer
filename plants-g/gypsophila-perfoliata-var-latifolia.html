Gypsophila perfoliata var. latifolia

Gypsophila perfoliata var. latifolia, commonly known as the baby's breath or sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often used in floral arrangements. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with narrow, linear leaves that are sometimes described as having a lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its flowers, which resemble the delicate strands of a baby's breath.
