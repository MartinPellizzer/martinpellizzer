Gentiana stylosa

Gentiana stylosa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana stylosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
