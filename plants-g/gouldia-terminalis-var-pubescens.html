Gouldia terminalis var. pubescens

Gouldia terminalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and vibrant, colorful inflorescences. The plant features fine, hairy textures on its leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
