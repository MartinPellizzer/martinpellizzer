Grammitis serricula

Grammitis serricula, commonly known as the serrated grammitis, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated margins, giving it its common name.

Grammitis serricula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded garden settings.
