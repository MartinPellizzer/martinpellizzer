Galeola montigena

Galeola montigena, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in dense clusters, adapting to its moist, shaded habitats.

Galeola montigena is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
