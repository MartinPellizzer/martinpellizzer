Geranium mascatense

Geranium mascatense, commonly known as the Mascate geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Geranium mascatense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
