Gardenia humboldtiana

Gardenia humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the humid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its slow growth and susceptibility to pests, it requires careful maintenance to flourish.
