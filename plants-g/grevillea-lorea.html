Grevillea lorea

Grevillea lorea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Grevillea lorea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
