Grevillea tripartita

Grevillea tripartita, commonly known as the Three-lobed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea tripartita is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as honeyeaters and birds.
