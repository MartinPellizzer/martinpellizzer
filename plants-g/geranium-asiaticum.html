Geranium asiaticum

Geranium asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Geranium asiaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
