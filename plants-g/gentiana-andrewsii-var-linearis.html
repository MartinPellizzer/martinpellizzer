Gentiana andrewsii var. linearis

Gentiana andrewsii var. linearis, commonly known as the linear gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks, preferring rich, well-drained soils. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes used in horticultural gardens for its ornamental value.
