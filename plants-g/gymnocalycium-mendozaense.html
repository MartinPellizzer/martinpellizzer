Gymnocalycium mendozaense

Gymnocalycium mendozaense, commonly known as the Mendoza prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the growing season, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Gymnocalycium mendozaense is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
