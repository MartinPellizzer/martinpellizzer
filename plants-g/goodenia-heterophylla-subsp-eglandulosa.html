Goodenia heterophylla subsp. eglandulosa

Goodenia heterophylla subsp.

eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandless goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It has a low, spreading habit and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in some areas.
