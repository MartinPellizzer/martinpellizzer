Goodenia bellidifolia subsp. bellidifolia

Goodenia bellidifolia subsp.

bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia goodenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its erect stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, resembling a bell shape, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
