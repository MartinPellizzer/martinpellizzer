Glycine mearnsii

Glycine mearnsii, commonly known as the Australian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Australia.

It produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous growth habit. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
