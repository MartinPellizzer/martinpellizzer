Galeatella

Galeatella, commonly known as the water lily, is a genus of aquatic plants that thrives in freshwater environments.

These plants are characterized by their floating leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The flowers of Galeatella are usually pink or white, and they open during the day, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

These plants play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water bodies.
