Gentiana crinita

Gentiana crinita, commonly known as the curly gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and are known for their attractive, trumpet-like shape.
