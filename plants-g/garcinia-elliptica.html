Garcinia elliptica

Garcinia elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tart flavor. Garcinia elliptica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruit in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
