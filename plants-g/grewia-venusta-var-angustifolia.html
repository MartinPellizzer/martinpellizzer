Grewia venusta var. angustifolia

Grewia venusta var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has slender, angular leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or sandy environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal flowering display.
