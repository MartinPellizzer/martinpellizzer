Gongora retrorsa

Gongora retrorsa, commonly known as the backward-pointing orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, backward-curving lip. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that open in the early morning. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

Due to its habitat specificity, Gongora retrorsa is considered a rare and endangered species.
