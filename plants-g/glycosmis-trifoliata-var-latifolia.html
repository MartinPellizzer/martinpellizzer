Glycosmis trifoliata var. latifolia

Glycosmis trifoliata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sweetleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three broad, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, orange-yellow berries. These berries contain a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounded by a thin, leathery rind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional herbal remedies.
