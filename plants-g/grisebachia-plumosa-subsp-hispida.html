Grisebachia plumosa subsp. hispida

Grisebachia plumosa subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hairy plume grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.
