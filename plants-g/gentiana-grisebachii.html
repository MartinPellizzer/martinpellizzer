Gentiana grisebachii

Gentiana grisebachii, commonly known as the gray gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana grisebachii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and hardiness.
