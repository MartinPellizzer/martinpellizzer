Geranium pattersonii

Geranium pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a compact, low-growing habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. Its blooming season occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetland habitats.

Geranium pattersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
