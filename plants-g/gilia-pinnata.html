Gilia pinnata

Gilia pinnata, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its showy flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive fringed appearance.

Gilia pinnata plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native ecosystems.
