Gompholobium obcordatum var. pachyphyllum

Gompholobium obcordatum var. pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved gompholobium, is a perennial shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dryland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
