Geranium cucullatum var. elongatum

Geranium cucullatum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
