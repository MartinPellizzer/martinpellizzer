Gentiana obtusifolia

Gentiana obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a waxy coating, and its stems are often branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.
