Glycine monosperma

Glycine monosperma, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are used as a food source in some parts of Africa and Asia. Glycine monosperma is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread cultivation.
