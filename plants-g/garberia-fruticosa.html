Garberia fruticosa

Garberia fruticosa, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Garberia fruticosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
