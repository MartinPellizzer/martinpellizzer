Gladiolus illyricus

Gladiolus illyricus, commonly known as the Croatian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Iridaceae family, contributing to the diversity of ornamental plants in temperate regions.
