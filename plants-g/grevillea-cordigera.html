Grevillea cordigera

Grevillea cordigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Grevillea cordigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
