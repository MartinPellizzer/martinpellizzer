Geranium maculatum var. pleno

Geranium maculatum var. pleno, commonly known as the spotted geranium or the showy geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, double flowers with five petals that are typically pink or red, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive white or pale green veining. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
