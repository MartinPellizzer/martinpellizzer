Grislea tomentosa

Grislea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly grislea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Grislea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
