Galanthus nivalis var. redoutei

Galanthus nivalis var. redoutei, commonly known as the Redoute's snowdrop, is a variant of the snowdrop genus native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers with a yellow-green spathe, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland soils and prefers partial shade. Its bulbs are hardy and can thrive in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
