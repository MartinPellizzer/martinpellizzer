Gagea capusii

Gagea capusii, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are small and rounded, often used in traditional medicine.

Gagea capusii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
