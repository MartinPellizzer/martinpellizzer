Gymnopogon spicatus

Gymnopogon spicatus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with pale green to brown hues, and it produces small, dry seeds.

Gymnopogon spicatus is often used in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
