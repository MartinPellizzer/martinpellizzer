Gnaphalium drabiforme

Gnaphalium drabiforme, commonly known as the narrowleaf cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features slender, hairy stems and small, often whitish or pale green leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flower heads that are typically yellow or pale brown in color. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Gnaphalium drabiforme is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.
