Gnaphalium lacteum

Gnaphalium lacteum, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant contains a milky sap, which is responsible for its common name and has been used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are often used to make herbal remedies, particularly for liver health.

Gnaphalium lacteum thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
