Gagea lutea var. poliphylla

Gagea lutea var. poliphylla, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its bulbs are small and white, and it blooms in early spring.
