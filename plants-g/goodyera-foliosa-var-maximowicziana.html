Goodyera foliosa var. maximowicziana

Goodyera foliosa var. maximowicziana, commonly known as the broad-leafed lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the orchid family. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in undergrowth with rich organic soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
