Galium plumosum subsp. plumosum

Galium plumosum subsp.

plumosum, commonly known as the silky bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
