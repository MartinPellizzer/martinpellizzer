Gomphocarpus woodii

Gomphocarpus woodii, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has tall, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Gomphocarpus woodii is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
