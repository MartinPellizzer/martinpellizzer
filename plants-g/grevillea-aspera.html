Grevillea aspera

Grevillea aspera, commonly known as the rough grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, bright red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
