Gentiana viscosa

Gentiana viscosa, commonly known as the sticky gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that contribute to its sticky texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
