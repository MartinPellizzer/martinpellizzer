Galbanum involucratum

Galbanum involucratum, commonly known as the woolly galbanum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-resinous sap and its distinctive, woolly appearance on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils. Galbanum involucratum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in ecological and pharmacological studies.
