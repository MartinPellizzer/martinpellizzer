Gerascanthus asterophorus

Gerascanthus asterophorus, commonly known as the star-flowered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Gerascanthus asterophorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
