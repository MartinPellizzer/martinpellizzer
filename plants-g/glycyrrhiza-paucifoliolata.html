Glycyrrhiza paucifoliolata

Glycyrrhiza paucifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf licorice, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes.

Glycyrrhiza paucifoliolata is valued for its sweet taste and potential health benefits, including anti-inflammatory properties.
