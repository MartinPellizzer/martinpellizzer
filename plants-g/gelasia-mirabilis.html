Gelasia mirabilis

Gelasia mirabilis, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling small lanterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and evergreen, adding visual interest throughout the year.

Gelasia mirabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
