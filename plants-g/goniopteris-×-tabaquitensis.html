Goniopteris × tabaquitensis

Goniopteris × tabaquitensis, commonly known as the Tabaquitensis fern, is a hybrid species within the genus Goniopteris, characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, typically displaying a complex, pinnate structure with fertile and sterile fronds. The plant is native to regions with humid climates, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are often elongated and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
