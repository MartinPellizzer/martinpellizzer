Gentiana aurea var. holosteoides

Gentiana aurea var. holosteoides, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
