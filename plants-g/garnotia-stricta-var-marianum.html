Garnotia stricta var. marianum

Garnotia stricta var. marianum, commonly known as the Marianum Garnotia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
