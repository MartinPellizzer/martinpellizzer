Gymnostachyum longifolium

Gymnostachyum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved gymnostachyum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
