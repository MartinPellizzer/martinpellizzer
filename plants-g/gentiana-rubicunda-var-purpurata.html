Gentiana rubicunda var. purpurata

Gentiana rubicunda var. purpurata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, while its stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
