Galium parisiense subsp. parvifolium

Galium parisiense subsp.

parvifolium, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
