Glyptostrobus heterophyllus

Glyptostrobus heterophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese swamp cedar, is a rare and ancient conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring distinct juvenile and adult leaves. The plant exhibits a dioecious reproductive system, with separate male and female cones on different trees. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing resistance to fire and pests.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is listed as critically endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
