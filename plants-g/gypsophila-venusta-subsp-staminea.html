Gypsophila venusta subsp. staminea

Gypsophila venusta subsp.

staminea, commonly known as the velvet gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed around the stems.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its delicate beauty.
