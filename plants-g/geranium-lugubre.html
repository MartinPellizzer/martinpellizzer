Geranium lugubre

Geranium lugubre, commonly known as the weeping geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish tinge. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Geranium lugubre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
