Galium davuricum var. densiflorum

Galium davuricum var. densiflorum, commonly known as denseflower bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
