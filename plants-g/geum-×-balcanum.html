Geum × balcanum

Geum × balcanum, commonly known as the Balkan avens, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
