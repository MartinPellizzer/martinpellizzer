Grewia lepidopetala

Grewia lepidopetala, commonly known as the silky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Grewia lepidopetala is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
