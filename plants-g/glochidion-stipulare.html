Glochidion stipulare

Glochidion stipulare, commonly known as the stipulate glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that often have stipules at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
