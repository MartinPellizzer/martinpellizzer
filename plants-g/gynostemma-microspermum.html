Gynostemma microspermum

Gynostemma microspermum, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or Korean ginseng, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing tiny seeds. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Gynostemma microspermum is also known for its adaptogenic qualities and is cultivated for its root-like structures.
