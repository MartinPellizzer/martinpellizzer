Gurania diversifolia var. angustifolia

Gurania diversifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gurania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
