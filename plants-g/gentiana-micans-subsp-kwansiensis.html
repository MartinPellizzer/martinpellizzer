Gentiana micans subsp. kwansiensis

Gentiana micans subsp.

kwansiensis, commonly known as the Kwansian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the cooler months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of down.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
