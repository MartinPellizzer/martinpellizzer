Glycine warreensis

Glycine warreensis, commonly known as Warre's soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional and modern culinary applications. Glycine warreensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it an important agricultural species in arid regions.
