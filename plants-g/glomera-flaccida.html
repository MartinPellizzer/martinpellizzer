Glomera flaccida

Glomera flaccida, commonly known as the drooping glomera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and emit a mild, pleasant fragrance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Glomera flaccida is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and subtle fragrance.
