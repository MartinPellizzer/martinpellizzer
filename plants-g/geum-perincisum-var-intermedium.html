Geum perincisum var. intermedium

Geum perincisum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate wild begonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with serrated edges and stems that produce clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to natural landscapes.

It is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
