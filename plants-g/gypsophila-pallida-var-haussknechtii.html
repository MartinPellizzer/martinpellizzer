Gypsophila pallida var. haussknechtii

Gypsophila pallida var. haussknechtii, commonly known as the white baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for garden borders or as a cut flower.

Its botanical name reflects its pale color and specific variety within the gypsophila genus.
