Gossia salomonensis

Gossia salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its limited geographic distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
