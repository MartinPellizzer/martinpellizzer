Grevillea tetrapleura

Grevillea tetrapleura, commonly known as the four-ribbed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea tetrapleura is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
