Glycosmis paraphylliana

Glycosmis paraphylliana, commonly known as the Philippine wild lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
