Genista anglica subsp. silana

Genista anglica subsp.

silana, commonly known as the English broom or lesser broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and pairs of small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Genista genus, which are known for their ornamental value and ecological importance.
