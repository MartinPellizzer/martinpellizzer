Garcinia rubroechinata

Garcinia rubroechinata, commonly known as the red-horned garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, spiny fruit that resembles a small, round apple. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruit structure. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Garcinia rubroechinata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
