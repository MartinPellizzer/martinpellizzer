Genista interrupta var. juniperina

Genista interrupta var. juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperuea genista, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage resembles that of juniper, giving it its common name.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
