Garrya flavescens

Garrya flavescens, commonly known as the silver leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Garrya flavescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
