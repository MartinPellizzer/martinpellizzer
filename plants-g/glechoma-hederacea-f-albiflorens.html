Glechoma hederacea f. albiflorens

Glechoma hederacea f. albiflorens, commonly known as white-flowered ground ivy, is a variant of the common ground ivy species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it spreads through both stolons and seeds.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
