Genista hirta subsp. elongata

Genista hirta subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bursch, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
