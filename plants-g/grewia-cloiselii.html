Grewia cloiselii

Grewia cloiselii, commonly known as the red-stemmed grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Grewia cloiselii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
