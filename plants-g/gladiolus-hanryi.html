Gladiolus hanryi

Gladiolus hanryi, commonly known as the Henry's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of brightly colored, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant hues of red, yellow, and orange. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a fibrous root system, which help it thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
