Geranium sanguineum var. lancastriense

Geranium sanguineum var. lancastriense, commonly known as the Lancashire geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from May to July. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in British horticulture.
