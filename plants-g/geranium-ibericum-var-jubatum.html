Geranium ibericum var. jubatum

Geranium ibericum var. jubatum, commonly known as the purple geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
