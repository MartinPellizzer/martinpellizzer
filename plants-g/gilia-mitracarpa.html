Gilia mitracarpa

Gilia mitracarpa, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Mirabilis family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a hairy stem and leaves, and it produces capsule fruits that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds.
