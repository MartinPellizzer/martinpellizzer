Gomphrena interrupta

Gomphrena interrupta, commonly known as the common globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gomphrena interrupta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
