Gossypium obtusifolium var. wightiana

Gossypium obtusifolium var. wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's cotton or Wight's cottonwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like fibers that are harvested for their soft, linty material. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with ample sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical significance in textile production.
