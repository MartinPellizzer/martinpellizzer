Grevillea insignis subsp. elliotii

Grevillea insignis subsp.

elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's glory, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Elliot's glory is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
