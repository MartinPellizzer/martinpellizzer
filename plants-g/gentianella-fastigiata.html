Gentianella fastigiata

Gentianella fastigiata, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentianella fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
