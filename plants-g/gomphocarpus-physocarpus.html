Gomphocarpus physocarpus

Gomphocarpus physocarpus, commonly known as the balloon plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons, which burst open to disperse seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The botanical features of Gomphocarpus physocarpus make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
