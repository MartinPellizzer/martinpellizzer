Garcinia maluensis

Garcinia maluensis, commonly known as the Maluense Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Garcinia maluensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
