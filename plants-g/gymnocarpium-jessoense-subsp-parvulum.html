Gymnocarpium jessoense subsp. parvulum

Gymnocarpium jessoense subsp.

parvulum, commonly known as the small-flowered kidneyweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and the fruit is a smooth, three-lobed achene.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
