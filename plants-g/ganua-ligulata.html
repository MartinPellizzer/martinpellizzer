Ganua ligulata

Ganua ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ganua ligulata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
