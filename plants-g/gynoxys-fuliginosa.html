Gynoxys fuliginosa

Gynoxys fuliginosa, commonly known as the black mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is known for its dark purple to black flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gynoxys fuliginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
