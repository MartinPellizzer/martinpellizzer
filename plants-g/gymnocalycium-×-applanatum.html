Gymnocalycium × applanatum

Gymnocalycium × applanatum, commonly known as the flat-armed barrel cactus, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the areoles along the stem. Gymnocalycium × applanatum is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
