Guapira florida

Guapira florida, commonly known as the Florida guapira, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy dark green surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Guapira florida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
