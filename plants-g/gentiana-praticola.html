Gentiana praticola

Gentiana praticola, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Gentiana praticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
