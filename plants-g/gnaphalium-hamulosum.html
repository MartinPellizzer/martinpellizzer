Gnaphalium hamulosum

Gnaphalium hamulosum, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are silver-gray in appearance, covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
