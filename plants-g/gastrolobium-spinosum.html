Gastrolobium spinosum

Gastrolobium spinosum, commonly known as the spinifex acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Gastrolobium spinosum is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it valuable in soil rehabilitation.
