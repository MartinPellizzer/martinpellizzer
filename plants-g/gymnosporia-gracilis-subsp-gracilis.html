Gymnosporia gracilis subsp. gracilis

Gymnosporia gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender thornveld, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
