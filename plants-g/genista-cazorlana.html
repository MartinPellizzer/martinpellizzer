Genista cazorlana

Genista cazorlana, commonly known as the Cazorla broom, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems forming a dense, shrubby appearance.
