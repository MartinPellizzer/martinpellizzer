Gynopogon rubricaulis

Gynopogon rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gynopogon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive red-colored stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
