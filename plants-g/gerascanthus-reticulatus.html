Gerascanthus reticulatus

Gerascanthus reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulate geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a deeply lobed structure. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Gerascanthus reticulatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
