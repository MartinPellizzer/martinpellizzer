Gynerium triaristatum

Gynerium triaristatum, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to wetland areas in North America.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring slender, hollow stems and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the top of its stalks. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it an important species for erosion control and habitat provision.
