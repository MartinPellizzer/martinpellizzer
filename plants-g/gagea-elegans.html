Gagea elegans

Gagea elegans, commonly known as the slender gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered at the base of the foliage. Gagea elegans thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its subtle beauty and early blooming habit.
