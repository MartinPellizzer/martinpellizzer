Glycine cana

Glycine cana, commonly known as the cana bean or wild soybean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Glycine cana is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in agricultural systems.
