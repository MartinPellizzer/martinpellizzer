Gomidesia palustris

Gomidesia palustris, commonly known as the marsh gomidesia, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and bloom in clusters.

Gomidesia palustris plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
