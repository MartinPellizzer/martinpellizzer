Glycyrrhiza yunnanensis

Glycyrrhiza yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Yunnan province in China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. It is valued for its roots, which contain glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine and as a sweetener.

Glycyrrhiza yunnanensis thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.
