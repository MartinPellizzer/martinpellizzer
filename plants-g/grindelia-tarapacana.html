Grindelia tarapacana

Grindelia tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean gum plant, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a sticky, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
