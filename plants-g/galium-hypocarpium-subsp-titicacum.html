Galium hypocarpium subsp. titicacum

Galium hypocarpium subsp.

titicacum, commonly known as the Andean bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America. It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters.

The plant has slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
