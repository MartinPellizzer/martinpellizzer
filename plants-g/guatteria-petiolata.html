Guatteria petiolata

Guatteria petiolata, commonly known as the Mexican white star, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces white, star-shaped blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that ripens to a reddish color.
