Garcinia parvifolia

Garcinia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
