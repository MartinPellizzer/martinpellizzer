Guajava cuneata

Guajava cuneata, commonly known as the mountain guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers, which are followed by edible, fragrant berries. Its fruit is typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

Guajava cuneata is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in its native regions.
