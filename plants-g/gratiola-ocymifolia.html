Gratiola ocymifolia

Gratiola ocymifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in terminal umbels, blooming in early summer.

The roots of Gratiola ocymifolia are yellowish and have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
