Guadua macrospiculata

Guadua macrospiculata, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of over 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It is highly valued for its strong, lightweight material, which is used in construction and handicrafts.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
