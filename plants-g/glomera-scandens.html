Glomera scandens

Glomera scandens, commonly known as the climbing glomera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces yellowish-white blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a host for certain mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing soil nutrient uptake.
