Geranium prolificum

Geranium prolificum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Geranium prolificum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
