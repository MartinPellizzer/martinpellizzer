Gloxinia trichantha

Gloxinia trichantha, commonly known as the hairy gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in humid, warm environments. Gloxinia trichantha is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and unique foliage.

It requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight to flourish.
