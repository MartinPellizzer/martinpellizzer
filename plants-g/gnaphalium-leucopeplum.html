Gnaphalium leucopeplum

Gnaphalium leucopeplum, commonly known as the white feather grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its small, fluffy flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny white to pale yellow flower heads that are borne on slender, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its feathery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
