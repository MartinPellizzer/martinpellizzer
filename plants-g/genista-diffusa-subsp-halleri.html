Genista diffusa subsp. halleri

Genista diffusa subsp.

halleri, commonly known as Haller's broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Haller's broom is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soils.
