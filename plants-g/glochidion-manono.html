Glochidion manono

Glochidion manono, commonly known as the Manono glabrous tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a glossy upper surface.

The flowers are small and clustered in axillary catkins, followed by distinctive three-lobed fruits.
