Geranium flexuosum

Geranium flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are usually opposite and ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
