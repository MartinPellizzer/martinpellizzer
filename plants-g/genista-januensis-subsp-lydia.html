Genista januensis subsp. lydia

Genista januensis subsp.

lydia, commonly known as the Lydia buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
