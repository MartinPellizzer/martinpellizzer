Gentiana austriaca castanetorum

Gentiana austriaca castanetorum, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
