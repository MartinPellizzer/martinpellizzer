Garnotia deflexa var. ferruginea

Garnotia deflexa var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty garnotia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ground cover.
