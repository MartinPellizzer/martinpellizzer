Guzmania monostachia

Guzmania monostachia, commonly known as the flame plant, is a tropical bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a flame, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects water, which serves as a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are usually broad and waxy, with a vibrant green color that contrasts with the bright flowers.

Guzmania monostachia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
