Grewia tenax subsp. tenax

Grewia tenax subsp.

tenax, commonly known as the hardy bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often thorny branches and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, round, red or orange fruits that are edible and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.
