Geraniospermum cortusifolium

Geraniospermum cortusifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geraniospermum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and often succulent leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub with small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
