Gentiana wislizeni

Gentiana wislizeni, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentiana wislizeni is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
