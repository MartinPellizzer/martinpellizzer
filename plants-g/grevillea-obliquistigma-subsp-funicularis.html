Grevillea obliquistigma subsp. funicularis

Grevillea obliquistigma subsp.

funicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright red flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
