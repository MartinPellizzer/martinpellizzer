Galenia namaquensis

Galenia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian galena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Galenia namaquensis is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
