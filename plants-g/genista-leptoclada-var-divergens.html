Genista leptoclada var. divergens

Genista leptoclada var. divergens, commonly known as the spreading broom.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, often sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
