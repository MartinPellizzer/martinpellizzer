Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. pubescens

Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are white or pale green and are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
