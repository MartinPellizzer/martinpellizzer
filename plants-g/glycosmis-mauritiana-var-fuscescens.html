Glycosmis mauritiana var. fuscescens

Glycosmis mauritiana var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-fruited mallow, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns dark purple when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
