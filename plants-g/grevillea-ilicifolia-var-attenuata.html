Grevillea ilicifolia var. attenuata

Grevillea ilicifolia var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
