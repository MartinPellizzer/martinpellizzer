Gladiolus pillansii var. roseus

Gladiolus pillansii var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-colored gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink to deep rose. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped petals. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
