Glyceria plicata var. minor

Glyceria plicata var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often folded in a V-shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life highlights its ecological significance.
