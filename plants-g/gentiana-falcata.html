Gentiana falcata

Gentiana falcata, commonly known as the sickle gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The genus Gentiana is known for its ornamental value, and G. falcata is no exception, making it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
