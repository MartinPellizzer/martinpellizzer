Galium anglicum var. hispidum

Galium anglicum var. hispidum, commonly known as rough bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in disturbed or wet habitats.
