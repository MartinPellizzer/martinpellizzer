Gonocarpus cordiger

Gonocarpus cordiger, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Gonocarpus cordiger make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
