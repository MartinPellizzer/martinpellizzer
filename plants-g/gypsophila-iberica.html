Gypsophila iberica

Gypsophila iberica, commonly known as the Iberian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Gypsophila iberica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
