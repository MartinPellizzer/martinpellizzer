Gomphrena humilis

Gomphrena humilis, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, spiky flower heads in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena humilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
