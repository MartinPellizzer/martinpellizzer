Geranium jainii

Geranium jainii, commonly known as the Jain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish or purple in color. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

Geranium jainii prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
