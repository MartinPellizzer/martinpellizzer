Gilia pedunculata var. calycina

Gilia pedunculata var. calycina, commonly known as the California gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
