Geranium cicutarium var. pilosissimum

Geranium cicutarium var. pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its dense foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
