Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. deminii

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

deminii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is a low-growing plant with a short, thick stem and a distinctive, often brightly colored, globose body.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among cactus enthusiasts.
