Glyptocarpa camellioides

Glyptocarpa camellioides, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and have a slightly citrusy scent.

Glyptocarpa camellioides is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
