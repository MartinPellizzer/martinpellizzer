Gnetum thoa

Gnetum thoa, commonly known as the tropical gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is a woody plant characterized by its compound leaves and unique reproductive structures. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments, often in lowland rainforests. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed aril, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Gnetum.

Gnetum thoa plays a role in ecological systems as a food source for various animals and contributes to forest biodiversity.
