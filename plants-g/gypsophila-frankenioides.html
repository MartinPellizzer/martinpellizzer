Gypsophila frankenioides

Gypsophila frankenioides, commonly known as the Frankenioides gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, often growing in sandy soils with low nutrient content. It produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across its native habitat.

Gypsophila frankenioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought conditions.
