Geranium canum

Geranium canum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is known for its drought tolerance.

Geranium canum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
