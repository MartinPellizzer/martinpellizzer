Globulea nudicaulis var. herrei

Globulea nudicaulis var. herrei, commonly known as the slender globular liverwort, is a small, leafy liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, translucent cells. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, called gemmae, are produced in clusters on the leaf surfaces, aiding in vegetative propagation.

The plant's morphology reflects its adaptation to humid, nutrient-rich microhabitats in temperate regions.
