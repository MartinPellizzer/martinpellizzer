Glycine syndetika

Glycine syndetika, commonly known as the climbing bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with small, stipitate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Glycine syndetika is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
