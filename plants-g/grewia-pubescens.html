Grewia pubescens

Grewia pubescens, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Grewia pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
