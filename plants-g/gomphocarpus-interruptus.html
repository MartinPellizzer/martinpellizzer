Gomphocarpus interruptus

Gomphocarpus interruptus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite and have a milky sap that is poisonous to both humans and animals.

Gomphocarpus interruptus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in arid to semi-arid environments.
