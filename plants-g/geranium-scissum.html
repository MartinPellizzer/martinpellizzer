Geranium scissum

Geranium scissum, commonly known as the scissor plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that resemble scissors, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium scissum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
