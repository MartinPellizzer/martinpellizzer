Goodyera hispaniolae

Goodyera hispaniolae, commonly known as the Hispaniola orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
