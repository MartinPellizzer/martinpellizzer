Gentiana obtusifolia var. minor

Gentiana obtusifolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
