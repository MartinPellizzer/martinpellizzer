Grevillea ferruginea

Grevillea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Grevillea ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
