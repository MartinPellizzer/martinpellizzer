Gentiana anisodonta f. degeniana

Gentiana anisodonta f. degeniana, commonly known as the Degen's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
