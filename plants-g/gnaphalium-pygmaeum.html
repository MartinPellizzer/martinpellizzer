Gnaphalium pygmaeum

Gnaphalium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf cudweed, is a small annual plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has slender, hairy stems and small, narrow leaves.

Gnaphalium pygmaeum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
