Glycine lineata

Glycine lineata, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive white vein pattern and produces vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant's pods are cylindrical, containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Glycine lineata is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its nutritious beans.
