Geniostoma biseriale

Geniostoma biseriale, commonly known as the two-rowed geniostoma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with two rows of small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbellate inflorescences, which are typical of the Araliaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
