Galeopsis pubinervis

Galeopsis pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent horseweed, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The leaves are often covered with fine pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
