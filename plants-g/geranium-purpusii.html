Geranium purpusii

Geranium purpusii, commonly known as the Persian geranium, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower colors, which range from pink to red.
