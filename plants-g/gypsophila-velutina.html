Gypsophila velutina

Gypsophila velutina, commonly known as velvet gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Velvet gypsophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
