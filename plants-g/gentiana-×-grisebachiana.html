Gentiana × grisebachiana

Gentiana × grisebachiana, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and unbranched.

Gentiana × grisebachiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
