Grangea gossypina

Grangea gossypina, commonly known as the cottonwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The tree produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its wide distribution. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Grangea gossypina is valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade and habitat for various wildlife species.
