Gastrolobium crispifolium

Gastrolobium crispifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaf hakea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical heads.

The species is part of the family Fabaceae and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
