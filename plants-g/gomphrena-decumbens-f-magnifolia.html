Gomphrena decumbens f. magnifolia

Gomphrena decumbens f. magnifolia, commonly known as the showy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures called involucres. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This ornamental plant is often used in borders, containers, and as a bedding plant due to its long blooming season and drought tolerance.
