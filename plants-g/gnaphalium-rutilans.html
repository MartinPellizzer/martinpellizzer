Gnaphalium rutilans

Gnaphalium rutilans, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are arranged in clusters and produce small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
