Govenia fasciata

Govenia fasciata, commonly known as the ribbonleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often marked with distinctive stripes or bands, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Govenia fasciata is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
