Geranium mosquense

Geranium mosquense, commonly known as the Mosquito geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Geranium mosquense thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among gardeners in suitable climates.
