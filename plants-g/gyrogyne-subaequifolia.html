Gyrogyne subaequifolia

Gyrogyne subaequifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gyrogyne, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctively narrow, almost equal width at the base and tip.

Gyrogyne subaequifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
