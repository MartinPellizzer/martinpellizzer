Gutierrezia neaeana

Gutierrezia neaeana, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer.

Gutierrezia neaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in dry landscapes.
