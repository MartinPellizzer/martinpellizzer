Grevillea pilosa subsp. dissecta

Grevillea pilosa subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
