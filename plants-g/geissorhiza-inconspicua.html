Geissorhiza inconspicua

Geissorhiza inconspicua, commonly known as the small-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, producing small, pinkish-white blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
