Ginginsia aurantia

Ginginsia aurantia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are oval and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Ginginsia aurantia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
