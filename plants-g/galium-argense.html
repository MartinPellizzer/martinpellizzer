Galium argense

Galium argense, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Galium argense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
