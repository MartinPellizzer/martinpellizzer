Glycosmis macrophylla var. microphylla

Glycosmis macrophylla var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese date or small-leaf Chinese date, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
