Gastrolobium spinosum var. inerme

Gastrolobium spinosum var. inerme, commonly known as the smooth spinifex, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features dense, wiry stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikes and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in whorls around the stems.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
