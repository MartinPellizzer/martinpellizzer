Gompholobium huegelii

Gompholobium huegelii, commonly known as the Yellow Glory Pea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
