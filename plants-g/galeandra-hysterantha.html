Galeandra hysterantha

Galeandra hysterantha, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep blue to violet, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Due to its beauty and rarity, Galeandra hysterantha is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
