Gentiana cerina f. suberecta

Gentiana cerina f. suberecta, commonly known as the upright gentian, is a botanical variant of the gentian genus characterized by its erect growth habit.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathian Mountains. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapting to high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
