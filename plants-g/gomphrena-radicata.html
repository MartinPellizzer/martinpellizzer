Gomphrena radicata

Gomphrena radicata, commonly known as the purple top or redroot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant is herbaceous, with upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena radicata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
