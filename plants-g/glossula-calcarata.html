Glossula calcarata

Glossula calcarata, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

The botanical features of Glossula calcarata make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
