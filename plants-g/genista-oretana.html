Genista oretana

Genista oretana, commonly known as the Greek broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its foliage consists of small, narrow leaves, and it produces seed pods that contain several seeds.

Genista oretana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
