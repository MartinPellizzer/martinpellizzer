Geocaryum peloponesiacum

Geocaryum peloponesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian geocaryum, is a rare and little-studied plant species native to specific regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and unique reproductive structures that facilitate wind pollination. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its botanical features include a short stem, hairy foliage, and small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
