Grevillea dryophylla

Grevillea dryophylla, commonly known as the dryland grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens. Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

Grevillea dryophylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
