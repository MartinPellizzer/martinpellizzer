Gardenia longistipula

Gardenia longistipula, commonly known as the long-stemmed gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, well-drained soils. Gardenia longistipula is typically evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

Its flowers bloom in summer and are prized for their beauty and aromatic scent.
