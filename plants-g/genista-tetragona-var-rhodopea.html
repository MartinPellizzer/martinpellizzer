Genista tetragona var. rhodopea

Genista tetragona var. rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope genista, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
