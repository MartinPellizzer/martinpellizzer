Geranium sanguineum f. villosum

Geranium sanguineum f. villosum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
