Gypsophila eriocalyx var. henrici

Gypsophila eriocalyx var. henrici, commonly known as the Henrici's baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden arrangements and floral designs.
