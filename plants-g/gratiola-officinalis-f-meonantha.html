Gratiola officinalis f. meonantha

Gratiola officinalis f. meonantha, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and diuretic properties.
