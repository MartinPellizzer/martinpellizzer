Gymnosporia ilicina

Gymnosporia ilicina, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Gymnosporia ilicina is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and resilience.
