Gentiana alsinoides subsp. alsinoides

Gentiana alsinoides subsp.

alsinoides, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. It is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
