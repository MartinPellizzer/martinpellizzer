Geum ranunculoides

Geum ranunculoides, commonly known as the Canadian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
