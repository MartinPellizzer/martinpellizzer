Gothofreda subhirtella

Gothofreda subhirtella, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gothofreda subhirtella is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
