Grammitis longifolia

Grammitis longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocks or tree trunks. Its fronds are elongated and have a distinctive, feathery appearance with small, linear pinnae.

Grammitis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
