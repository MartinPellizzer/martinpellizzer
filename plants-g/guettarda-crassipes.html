Guettarda crassipes

Guettarda crassipes, commonly known as the swamp guettarda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wet, coastal environments such as mangrove swamps and estuaries. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are adapted to tolerate saline and waterlogged soils, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Guettarda crassipes plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
