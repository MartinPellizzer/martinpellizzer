Glossanthis aurea

Glossanthis aurea, commonly known as the golden glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
