Greenea

Greenea, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. Greenea has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
