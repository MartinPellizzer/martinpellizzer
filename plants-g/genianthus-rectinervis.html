Genianthus rectinervis

Genianthus rectinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined genianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive central disc. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
