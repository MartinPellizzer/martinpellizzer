Glochidion inusitatum

Glochidion inusitatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf glochidion, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, simple leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers, which are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Glochidion inusitatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
