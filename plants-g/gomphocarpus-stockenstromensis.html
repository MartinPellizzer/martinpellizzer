Gomphocarpus stockenstromensis

Gomphocarpus stockenstromensis, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous small seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting nocturnal wildlife.
