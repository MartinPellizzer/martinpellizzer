Guilandina moringa

Guilandina moringa, commonly known as the horse bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Guilandina moringa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in sustainable agriculture.
