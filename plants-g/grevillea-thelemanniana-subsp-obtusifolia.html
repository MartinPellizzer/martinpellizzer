Grevillea thelemanniana subsp. obtusifolia

Grevillea thelemanniana subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color and have a rough texture.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for dry gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
