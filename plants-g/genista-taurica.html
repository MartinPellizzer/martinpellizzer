Genista taurica

Genista taurica, commonly known as the Crimean broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Genista taurica is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
