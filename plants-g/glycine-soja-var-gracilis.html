Glycine soja var. gracilis

Glycine soja var. gracilis, commonly known as slender soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
