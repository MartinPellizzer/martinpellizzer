Grevillea ilicifolia

Grevillea ilicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant produces striking, red or orange cylindrical flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea ilicifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
