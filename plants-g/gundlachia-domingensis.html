Gundlachia domingensis

Gundlachia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
