Galenia secunda

Galenia secunda, commonly known as the mountain thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Galenia secunda is also known for its use in traditional medicine, where different parts of the plant are employed for their purported healing properties.
