Gnaphalium sylvaticum subsp. norvegicum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum subsp.

norvegicum, commonly known as the Norwegian cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution across northern Europe.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
