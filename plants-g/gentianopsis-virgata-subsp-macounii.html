Gentianopsis virgata subsp. macounii

Gentianopsis virgata subsp.

macounii, commonly known as Macoun's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and wet meadows. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and flies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native range.
