Gentiana brachyphylla subsp. favratii

Gentiana brachyphylla subsp.

favratii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in mountainous habitats.
