Glyceria maxima f. acuta

Glyceria maxima f. acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf glyceria, is a variant of the perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically sharp and pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
