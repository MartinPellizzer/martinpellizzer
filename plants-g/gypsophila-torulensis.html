Gypsophila torulensis

Gypsophila torulensis, commonly known as the Turkish carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Gypsophila torulensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
