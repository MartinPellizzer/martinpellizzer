Goodenia capillosa

Goodenia capillosa, commonly known as the silky goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Goodenia capillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
