Grastidium salomonense

Grastidium salomonense, commonly known as the Solomon Islands grass.

It is a perennial herb native to the Solomon Islands, characterized by its tufted growth habit and fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Grastidium salomonense is adapted to tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forest understories.
