Genista perreymondii

Genista perreymondii, commonly known as the Perreymond's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats.

Its small, leathery leaves and upright growth habit make it a distinctive species within the Genista genus.
