Gladiolus carinatus subsp. parviflorus

Gladiolus carinatus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, colorful flowers.

The flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive three-lobed structure. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
