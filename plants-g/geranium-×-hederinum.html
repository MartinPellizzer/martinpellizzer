Geranium × hederinum

Geranium × hederinum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various geranium species.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble those of ivy, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.
