Gaultheria domingensis

Gaultheria domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican wintergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Dominican Republic and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible. Gaultheria domingensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
