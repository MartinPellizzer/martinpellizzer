Gymnocalycium bruchii var. glaucum

Gymnocalycium bruchii var. glaucum, commonly known as the blue gloomy cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically blue-gray in color due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
