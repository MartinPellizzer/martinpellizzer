Gentiana kauffmanniana var. affghanica

Gentiana kauffmanniana var. affghanica, commonly known as the Afghan gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
