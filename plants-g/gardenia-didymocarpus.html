Gardenia didymocarpus

Gardenia didymocarpus, commonly known as the Chinese gardenia, is a flowering plant native to southern China, Taiwan, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oblong fruits that turn red when mature, containing one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gardenia didymocarpus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
