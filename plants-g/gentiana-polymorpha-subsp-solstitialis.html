Gentiana polymorpha subsp. solstitialis

Gentiana polymorpha subsp.

solstitialis, commonly known as the Solstitial gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single flowering stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
