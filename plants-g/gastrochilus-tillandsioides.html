Gastrochilus tillandsioides

Gastrochilus tillandsioides, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in high altitudes and requires consistent moisture and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic orchids.
