Galium rotundifolium subsp. ovalifolium

Galium rotundifolium subsp.

ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rounded or oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, contributing to its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
