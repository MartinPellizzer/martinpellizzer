Galanthus caspius

Galanthus caspius, commonly known as the Caspian snowdrop, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces delicate, white flowers with green markings, typically blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its bulbs are relatively small and require a period of cold dormancy to flower successfully.

Galanthus caspius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
