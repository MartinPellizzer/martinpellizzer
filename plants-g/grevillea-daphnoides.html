Grevillea daphnoides

Grevillea daphnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which appear in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea daphnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
