Gyrinops walla

Gyrinops walla, commonly known as the walla water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Gyrinops walla plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting local biodiversity.
