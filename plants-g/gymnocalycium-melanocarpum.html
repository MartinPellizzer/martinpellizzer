Gymnocalycium melanocarpum

Gymnocalycium melanocarpum, commonly known as the black fruit cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spiny plant that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and bloom in the spring.

Gymnocalycium melanocarpum is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
