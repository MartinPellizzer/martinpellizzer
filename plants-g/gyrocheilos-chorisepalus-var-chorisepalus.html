Gyrocheilos chorisepalus var. chorisepalus

Gyrocheilos chorisepalus var. chorisepalus, commonly known as the pink-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep magenta, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
