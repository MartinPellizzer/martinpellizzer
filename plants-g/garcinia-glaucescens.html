Garcinia glaucescens

Garcinia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue Garcinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it bears small, orange-red fruits that are edible. Garcinia glaucescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
