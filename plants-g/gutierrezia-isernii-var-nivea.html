Gutierrezia isernii var. nivea

Gutierrezia isernii var. nivea, commonly known as white burr sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Gutierrezia isernii var. nivea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
