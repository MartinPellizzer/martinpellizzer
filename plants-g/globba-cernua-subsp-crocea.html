Globba cernua subsp. crocea

Globba cernua subsp.

crocea, commonly known as the golden globba, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia. It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, showy inflorescences with yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are erect and slightly hairy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
