Guamia

Guamia, commonly known as the Guamia tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus *Piper* and is characterized by its climbing habit, often forming dense foliage. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into berries. Guamia is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
