Galium laevigatum var. aristatum

Galium laevigatum var. aristatum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in its natural habitat.
