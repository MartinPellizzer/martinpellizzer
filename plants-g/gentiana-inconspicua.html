Gentiana inconspicua

Gentiana inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana inconspicua is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
