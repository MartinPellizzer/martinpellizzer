Grewia barberi

Grewia barberi, commonly known as the Natal glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.
