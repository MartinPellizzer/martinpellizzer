Gerascanthus gardneri

Gerascanthus gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or red petals that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features upright stems with serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
