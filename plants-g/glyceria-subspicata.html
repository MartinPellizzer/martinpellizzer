Glyceria subspicata

Glyceria subspicata, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Glyceria subspicata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
