Guidonia apetala

Guidonia apetala, commonly known as the leafless guibourt's lily, is a rare and unusual plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other lilies. The plant produces a single, elongated flower that is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which is why it is referred to as "leafless.".

This unique morphology is an adaptation to its harsh, high-altitude environment, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
