Gravesia longipes

Gravesia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed gravesia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Gravesia longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
