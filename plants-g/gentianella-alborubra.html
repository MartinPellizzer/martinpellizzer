Gentianella alborubra

Gentianella alborubra, commonly known as the red-flowered gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking bright red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and sturdy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
