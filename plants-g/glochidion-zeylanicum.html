Glochidion zeylanicum

Glochidion zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon gooseberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its fruit is edible and has a sour taste, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
