Glossodia brunonis

Glossodia brunonis, commonly known as the yellow bell orchid, is a South African plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Glossodia brunonis has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish sepals that surround the flower.

It is a perennial herb that is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
