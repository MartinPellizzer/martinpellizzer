Gastrochilus coriaceus

Gastrochilus coriaceus, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its tropical habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
