Galium umbellatum subvar. asperum

Galium umbellatum subvar. asperum, commonly known as roughbedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered in stiff, glandular hairs. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture due to the presence of fine, bristly hairs.
