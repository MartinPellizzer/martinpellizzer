Gerardia nuda

Gerardia nuda, commonly known as the naked gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Gerardia nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
