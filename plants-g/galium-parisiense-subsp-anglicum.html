Galium parisiense subsp. anglicum

Galium parisiense subsp.

anglicum, commonly known as English bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, branched stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, covered in fine, whitish hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
