Galion molluginum

Galion molluginum, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and soft, hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Galion molluginum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
