Grubovia mucronata

Grubovia mucronata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Grubovia mucronata is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
