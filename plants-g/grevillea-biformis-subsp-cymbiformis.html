Grevillea biformis subsp. cymbiformis

Grevillea biformis subsp.

cymbiformis, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in cylindrical clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which can extend from spring to autumn.
