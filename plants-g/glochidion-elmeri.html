Glochidion elmeri

Glochidion elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Glochidion elmeri is often found in lowland to montane rainforests, growing in shaded, moist environments.

It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and limited distribution.
