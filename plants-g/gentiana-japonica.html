Gentiana japonica

Gentiana japonica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Gentiana japonica is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
