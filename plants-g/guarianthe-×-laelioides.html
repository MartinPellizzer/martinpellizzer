Guarianthe × laelioides

Guarianthe × laelioides, commonly known as the Phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from various parent species.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, often resembling butterflies. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The botanical features of Guarianthe × laelioides make it a favored ornamental plant in both horticulture and commercial flower production.
