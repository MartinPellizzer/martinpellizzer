Gymnema attenuatum

Gymnema attenuatum, commonly known as the Indian bitter apple, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to inhibit the taste of sweetness, a property that has been studied for its potential in managing diabetes. Gymnema attenuatum produces small, yellowish flowers and fruit that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.

Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
