Gastrochilus kunstleri

Gastrochilus kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's toothbrush orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, toothbrush-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Gastrochilus kunstleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
