Garcinia echinocarpa

Garcinia echinocarpa, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, purple-red rind and segmented, white to pink flesh inside. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is known for its aromatic leaves and distinctive fruit structure. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Garcinia echinocarpa make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
