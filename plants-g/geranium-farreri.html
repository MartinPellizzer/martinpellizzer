Geranium farreri

Geranium farreri, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Geranium farreri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
