Gastrochilus clivalis

Gastrochilus clivalis, commonly known as the cliff orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on rock faces or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air and substrate.

Gastrochilus clivalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
