Gentiana verna subsp. balcanica

Gentiana verna subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
