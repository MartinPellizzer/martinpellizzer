Gentiana dahurica var. dahurica

Gentiana dahurica var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
