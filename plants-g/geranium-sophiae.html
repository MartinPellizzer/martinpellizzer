Geranium sophiae

Geranium sophiae, commonly known as the noble geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and woodland soils. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening.

Geranium sophiae is known for its adaptability and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
