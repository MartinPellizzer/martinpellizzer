Geranium pyrenaicum subsp. lusitanicum

Geranium pyrenaicum subsp.

lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
