Gentianella luteoalba

Gentianella luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Gentianella luteoalba is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
