Gentianodes jamesii

Gentianodes jamesii, commonly known as the James Island gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianodes jamesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
