Gymnocalycium proliferum var. calochlorum

Gymnocalycium proliferum var. calochlorum, commonly known as the blue gloom cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces pale blue or green flowers during the cooler months, which are typically short-lived. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
