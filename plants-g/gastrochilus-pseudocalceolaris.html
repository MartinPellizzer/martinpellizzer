Gastrochilus pseudocalceolaris

Gastrochilus pseudocalceolaris, commonly known as the false calceolar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically blooms with white or pale yellow flowers that have a distinctive lip-shaped structure. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
