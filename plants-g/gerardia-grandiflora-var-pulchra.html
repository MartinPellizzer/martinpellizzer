Gerardia grandiflora var. pulchra

Gerardia grandiflora var. pulchra, commonly known as the showy gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Gerardia grandiflora var. pulchra is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
