Gonolobus stramineus

Gonolobus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences, giving it its common name. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
