Galium boreale var. typicum

Galium boreale var. typicum, commonly known as northern wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale violet bloom. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
