Glycosmis puberula

Glycosmis puberula, commonly known as the hairy sweet pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrusy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Glycosmis puberula is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
