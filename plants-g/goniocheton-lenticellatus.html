Goniocheton lenticellatus

Goniocheton lenticellatus, commonly known as the lenticellate tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bark covered with lenticels, which are small, raised pores that allow gas exchange. The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
