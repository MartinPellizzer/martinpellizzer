Galium helveticum var. allionii

Galium helveticum var. allionii, commonly known as the Alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
