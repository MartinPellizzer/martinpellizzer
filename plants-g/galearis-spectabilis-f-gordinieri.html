Galearis spectabilis f. gordinieri

Galearis spectabilis f. gordinieri, commonly known as the Gordinier's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, pink to purple flower spikes that resemble a beard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
