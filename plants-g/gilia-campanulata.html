Gilia campanulata

Gilia campanulata, commonly known as the bell gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in areas with well-drained soil. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gilia campanulata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
