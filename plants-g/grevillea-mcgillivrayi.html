Grevillea mcgillivrayi

Grevillea mcgillivrayi, commonly known as the West Coast Grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of red and orange, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea mcgillivrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
