Goniopteris cheilocarpa

Goniopteris cheilocarpa, commonly known as the broad-veined water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into numerous narrow segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Goniopteris cheilocarpa make it an important component of tropical and subtropical ecosystems.
