Grewia microcarpa var. aneimenoclada

Grewia microcarpa var. aneimenoclada, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and small mammals.
