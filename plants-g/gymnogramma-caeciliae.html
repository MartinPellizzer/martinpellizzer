Gymnogramma caeciliae

Gymnogramma caeciliae, commonly known as the Caecilia's shield fern, is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features bipinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Gymnogramma caeciliae is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in forest understories.
