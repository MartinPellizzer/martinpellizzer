Guajava radicans

Guajava radicans, commonly known as the creeping guajava, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
