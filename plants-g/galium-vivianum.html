Galium vivianum

Galium vivianum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium vivianum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
