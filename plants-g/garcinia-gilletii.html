Garcinia gilletii

Garcinia gilletii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Garcinia gilletii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and nutritional properties.
