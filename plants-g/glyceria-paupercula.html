Glyceria paupercula

Glyceria paupercula, commonly known as the poor grass, is a flowering plant species native to wetland and marshy environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soils, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Glyceria paupercula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
