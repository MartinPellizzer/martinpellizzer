Gutierrezia pomariensis

Gutierrezia pomariensis, commonly known as the coastal goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal environments with moderate to high salinity. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia pomariensis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
