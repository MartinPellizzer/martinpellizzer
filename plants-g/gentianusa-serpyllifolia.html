Gentianusa serpyllifolia

Gentianusa serpyllifolia, commonly known as common gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianusa serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
