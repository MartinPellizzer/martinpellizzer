Glycosmis mauritiana

Glycosmis mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, star-shaped yellow fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its fruits are edible, though they are not as commonly cultivated as other citrus relatives.

The plant also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
