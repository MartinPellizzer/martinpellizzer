Gladiolus mitwabaensis

Gladiolus mitwabaensis, commonly known as the Mitwaba gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing spikes of flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots are tuberous, aiding in its survival in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
