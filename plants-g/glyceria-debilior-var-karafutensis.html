Glyceria debilior var. karafutensis

Glyceria debilior var. karafutensis, commonly known as the Karafuto grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
