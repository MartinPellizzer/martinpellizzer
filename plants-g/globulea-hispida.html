Globulea hispida

Globulea hispida, commonly known as the rough globular succulent, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and spiny, hairy stems that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted for water storage, typical of succulent species.

Globulea hispida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
