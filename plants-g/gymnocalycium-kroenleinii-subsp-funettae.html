Gymnocalycium kroenleinii subsp. funettae

Gymnocalycium kroenleinii subsp.

funettae, commonly known as the Funetta's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a thick, fleshy stem covered in spines.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
