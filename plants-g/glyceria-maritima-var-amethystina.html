Glyceria maritima var. amethystina

Glyceria maritima var. amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The variety amethystina is notable for its striking coloration and adaptability to harsh coastal conditions.
