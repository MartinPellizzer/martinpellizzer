Gerardia crinita

Gerardia crinita, commonly known as the curly hair fern, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble strands of hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique adaptation makes Gerardia crinita a fascinating subject in the study of plant biology and ecology.
