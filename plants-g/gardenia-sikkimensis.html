Gardenia sikkimensis

Gardenia sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
