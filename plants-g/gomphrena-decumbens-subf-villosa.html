Gomphrena decumbens subf. villosa

Gomphrena decumbens subf.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, spiky flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
