Goniopteris pilosa

Goniopteris pilosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a member of the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are bi-directionally divided, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

Goniopteris pilosa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
