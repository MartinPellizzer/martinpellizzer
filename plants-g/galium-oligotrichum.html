Galium oligotrichum

Galium oligotrichum, commonly known as the small-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats of prostrate or ascending stems, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, usually elliptical or ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Galium oligotrichum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
