Galium myrianthum var. rubriflorum

Galium myrianthum var. rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
