Gymnocalycium rosae

Gymnocalycium rosae, commonly known as the Rosa Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Gymnocalycium rosae is a succulent plant, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
