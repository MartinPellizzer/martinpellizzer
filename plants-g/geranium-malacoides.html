Geranium malacoides

Geranium malacoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in dense, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly fuzzy texture.

Geranium malacoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
