Genista abchasica

Genista abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia and Russia.

Genista abchasica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
