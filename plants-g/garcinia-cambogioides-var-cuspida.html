Garcinia cambogioides var. cuspida

Garcinia cambogioides var. cuspida, commonly known as the African mango, is a tropical shrub native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that contain a single large seed. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks. Its fruit is valued for its high oil content and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The botanical features of this species make it an important plant in both ecological and economic contexts.
