Gymnema foetidum

Gymnema foetidum, commonly known as the stinking gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and distinctive, often foul-smelling leaves. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Gymnema foetidum is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
