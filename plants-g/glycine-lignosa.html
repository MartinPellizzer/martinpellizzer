Glycine lignosa

Glycine lignosa, commonly known as the evergreen climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds.

Glycine lignosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
