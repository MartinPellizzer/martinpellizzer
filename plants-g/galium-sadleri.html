Galium sadleri

Galium sadleri, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species often found in naturalized areas.
