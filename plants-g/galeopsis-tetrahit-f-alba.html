Galeopsis tetrahit f. alba

Galeopsis tetrahit f. alba, commonly known as white hedge nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stem. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its lighter floral color compared to the typical form of Galeopsis tetrahit.
