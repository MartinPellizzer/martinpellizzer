Gaylussacia densa var. lanceolata

Gaylussacia densa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf huckleberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
