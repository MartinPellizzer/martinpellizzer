Gymnema thorelii

Gymnema thorelii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Gymnema thorelii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.
