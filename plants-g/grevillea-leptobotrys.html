Grevillea leptobotrys

Grevillea leptobotrys, commonly known as the slender-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Grevillea leptobotrys is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking floral display.
