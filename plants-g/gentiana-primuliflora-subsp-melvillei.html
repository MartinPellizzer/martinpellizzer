Gentiana primuliflora subsp. melvillei

Gentiana primuliflora subsp.

melvillei, commonly known as Melville's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
