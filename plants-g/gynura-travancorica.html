Gynura travancorica

Gynura travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore gynura, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Gynura travancorica is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and floral display.
