Gale palustris

Gale palustris, commonly known as bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Gale palustris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed wetland ecosystems.
