Geum radiatum

Geum radiatum, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive radiating pattern, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wet meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its preference for well-drained soil.
