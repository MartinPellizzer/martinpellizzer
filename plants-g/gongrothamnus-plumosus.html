Gongrothamnus plumosus

Gongrothamnus plumosus, commonly known as the silverleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Gongrothamnus plumosus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
