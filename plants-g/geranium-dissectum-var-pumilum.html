Geranium dissectum var. pumilum

Geranium dissectum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
