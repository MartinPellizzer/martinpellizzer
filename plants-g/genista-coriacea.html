Genista coriacea

Genista coriacea, commonly known as the rough broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval, leathery leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista coriacea is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of forage for wildlife.
