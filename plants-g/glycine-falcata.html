Glycine falcata

Glycine falcata, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its pods are slender, containing several seeds that are typically dispersed by wind.

Glycine falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
