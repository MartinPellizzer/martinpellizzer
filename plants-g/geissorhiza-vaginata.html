Geissorhiza vaginata

Geissorhiza vaginata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, helping it retain moisture in arid environments. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Geissorhiza vaginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in gardens.
