Gymnadenia austriaca var. gallica

Gymnadenia austriaca var. gallica, commonly known as the Austrian grass pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
