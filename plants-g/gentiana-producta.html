Gentiana producta

Gentiana producta, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana producta is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
