Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. multiceps

Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. multiceps, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
