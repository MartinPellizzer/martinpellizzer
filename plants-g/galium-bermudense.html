Galium bermudense

Galium bermudense, commonly known as Bermuda bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Bermuda and the Caribbean.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium bermudense thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
