Galeopsis speciosa var. leiotricha

Galeopsis speciosa var. leiotricha, commonly known as the slender horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
