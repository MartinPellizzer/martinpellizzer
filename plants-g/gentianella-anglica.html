Gentianella anglica

Gentianella anglica, commonly known as English gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gentianella anglica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
