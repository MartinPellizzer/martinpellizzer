Gouldia terminalis

Gouldia terminalis, commonly known as the red lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green.

Gouldia terminalis is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
