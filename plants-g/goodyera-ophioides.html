Goodyera ophioides

Goodyera ophioides, commonly known as the snake's head orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, low-growing plant with a dense rosette of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that resemble a snake's head, hence its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Goodyera ophioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
