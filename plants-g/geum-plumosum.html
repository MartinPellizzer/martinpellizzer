Geum plumosum

Geum plumosum, commonly known as the downy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
