Gomphrena serturneroides

Gomphrena serturneroides, commonly known as the pink plume or red plume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that resemble plumes or pom-poms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by showy, colorful bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a versatile plant for both landscape design and container gardening.
