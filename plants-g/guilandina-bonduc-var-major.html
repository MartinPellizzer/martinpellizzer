Guilandina bonduc var. major

Guilandina bonduc var. major, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, and it bears elongated pods containing several seeds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds are edible when fully mature but are often used in traditional medicine due to their potential health benefits.
