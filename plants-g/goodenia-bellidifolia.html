Goodenia bellidifolia

Goodenia bellidifolia, commonly known as the golden goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Goodenia bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
