Galium umbellatum subvar. rubriflorum

Galium umbellatum subvar. rubriflorum, commonly known as the redflower bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-pink floral bracts, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
