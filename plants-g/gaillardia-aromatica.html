Gaillardia aromatica

Gaillardia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are aromatic, with a slight peppery scent, and its flowers bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Gaillardia aromatica is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
