Galium virgatum

Galium virgatum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 inches tall. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium virgatum is commonly found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.
