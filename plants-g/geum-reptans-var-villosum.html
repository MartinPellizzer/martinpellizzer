Geum reptans var. villosum

Geum reptans var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features spreading stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, often with a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
