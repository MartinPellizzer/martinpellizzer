Godetia parviflora var. luteola

Godetia parviflora var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow godetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features include a simple stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.
