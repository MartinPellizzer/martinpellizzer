Gaultheria annamensis

Gaultheria annamensis, commonly known as the Annam starflower, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Gaultheria annamensis is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
