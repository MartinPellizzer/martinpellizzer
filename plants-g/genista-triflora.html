Genista triflora

Genista triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista triflora is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
