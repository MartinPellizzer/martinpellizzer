Gmelina tacabushia

Gmelina tacabushia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
