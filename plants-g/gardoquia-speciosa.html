Gardoquia speciosa

Gardoquia speciosa, commonly known as the showy gardoquia, is a striking flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms. The plant typically features bright yellow to orange flowers with a central disk, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Gardoquia speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
