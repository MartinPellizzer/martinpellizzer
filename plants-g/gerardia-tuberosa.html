Gerardia tuberosa

Gerardia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous gerardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its ornamental value, featuring showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its tuberous roots are an important part of its structure, allowing it to store nutrients and water.

Gerardia tuberosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
