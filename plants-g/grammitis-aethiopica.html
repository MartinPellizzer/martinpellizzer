Grammitis aethiopica

Grammitis aethiopica, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its densely packed, leathery fronds that form a compact, shield-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

Grammitis aethiopica is native to Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
