Gladiolus rogersii var. vlokii

Gladiolus rogersii var. vlokii, commonly known as the Vlok's gladiolus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing inflorescences of narrow, pointed petals in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage consists of long, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
