Guardiola mexicana

Guardiola mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican guardiola, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Guardiola mexicana is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.
