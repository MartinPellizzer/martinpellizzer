Gagea corsica

Gagea corsica, commonly known as the Corsican spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers with six petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Gagea corsica is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous or woodland areas.
