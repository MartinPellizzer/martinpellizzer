Glyceria distans subsp. parlatorei

Glyceria distans subsp.

parlatorei, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa. It typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, such as marshes and the edges of lakes.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often submerged or floating. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and parallel veins.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.
