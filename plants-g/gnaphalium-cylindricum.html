Gnaphalium cylindricum

Gnaphalium cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical everlasting flower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are erect and somewhat woody.

This plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, which are popular in floral arrangements.
