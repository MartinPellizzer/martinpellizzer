Gypsophila modesta

Gypsophila modesta, commonly known as the meadow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gypsophila modesta is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
