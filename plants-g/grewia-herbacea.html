Grewia herbacea

Grewia herbacea, commonly known as the dwarf wild pear, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that changes from green to red as it matures.

Grewia herbacea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
