Galium pumilum var. mesatlanticum

Galium pumilum var. mesatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands.
