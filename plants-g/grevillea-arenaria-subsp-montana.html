Grevillea arenaria subsp. montana

Grevillea arenaria subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain waratah, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant typically grows in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
