Glechon candida

Glechon candida, commonly known as the white glechon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Glechon candida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
