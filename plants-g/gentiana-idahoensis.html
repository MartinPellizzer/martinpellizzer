Gentiana idahoensis

Gentiana idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gentiana idahoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
