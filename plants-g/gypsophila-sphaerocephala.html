Gypsophila sphaerocephala

Gypsophila sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, pink or white flower heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, alpine environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila sphaerocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden displays.
