Grewia rhombifolia

Grewia rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or rhomboid in shape, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.
