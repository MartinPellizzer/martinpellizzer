Grossularia knightii

Grossularia knightii, commonly known as the Knight's gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are often edible. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually elliptical with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
