Gasteria rawlinsonii

Gasteria rawlinsonii, commonly known as the rawlinsonii gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white or light green markings. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
