Geranium deltoideum

Geranium deltoideum, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often velvety to the touch, and it produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Geranium deltoideum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
