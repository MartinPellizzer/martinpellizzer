Gentiana dasyantha

Gentiana dasyantha, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and unbranched.

Gentiana dasyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
