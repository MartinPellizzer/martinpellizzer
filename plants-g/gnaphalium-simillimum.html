Gnaphalium simillimum

Gnaphalium simillimum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
