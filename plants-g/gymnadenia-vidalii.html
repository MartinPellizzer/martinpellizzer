Gymnadenia vidalii

Gymnadenia vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's grass-pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

Gymnadenia vidalii is relatively rare and is found in specific regions of Europe, particularly in France and Spain.
