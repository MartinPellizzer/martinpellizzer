Galium debile f. terrestre

Galium debile f. terrestre, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a soft, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
