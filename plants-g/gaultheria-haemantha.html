Gaultheria haemantha

Gaultheria haemantha, commonly known as the red-flowered wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to red. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds similar to those found in the related Gaultheria species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Gaultheria haemantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
