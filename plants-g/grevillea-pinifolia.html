Grevillea pinifolia

Grevillea pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea pinifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
