Gastrochilus patinatus

Gastrochilus patinatus, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Gastrochilus patinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
