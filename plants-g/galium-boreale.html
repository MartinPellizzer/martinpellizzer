Galium boreale

Galium boreale, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium boreale is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in arctic and alpine environments.
