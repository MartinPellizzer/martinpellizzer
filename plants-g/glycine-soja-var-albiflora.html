Glycine soja var. albiflora

Glycine soja var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which distinguish it from the more commonly cultivated dark-pink varieties. The plant is an annual legume that produces pods containing edible seeds used in various food products. It has a climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and is often grown for both its nutritional value and agricultural significance.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
