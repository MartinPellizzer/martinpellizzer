Godoya repanda

Godoya repanda, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
