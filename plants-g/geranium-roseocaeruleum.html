Geranium roseocaeruleum

Geranium roseocaeruleum, commonly known as the blue geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring through early summer.
