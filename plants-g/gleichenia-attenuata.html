Gleichenia attenuata

Gleichenia attenuata, commonly known as the slender Gleichenia, is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often drooping fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, which are covered by a protective indusium.

Gleichenia attenuata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
