Grevillea irrasa

Grevillea irrasa, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of bright orange or red flowers. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Grevillea irrasa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
