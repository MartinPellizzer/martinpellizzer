Gynoxys apollinaris

Gynoxys apollinaris, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces showy, orange-red ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gynoxys apollinaris is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
