Gentiana ochroleuca var. incarnata

Gentiana ochroleuca var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that are often tinged with pink, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
