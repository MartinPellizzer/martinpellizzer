Goeppertia hirsuta var. coriacea

Goeppertia hirsuta var. coriacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with intricate, variegated patterns of silver and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its foliage is often described as having a leathery texture, which contributes to its unique appearance.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
