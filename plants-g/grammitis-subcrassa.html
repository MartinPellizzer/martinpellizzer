Grammitis subcrassa

Grammitis subcrassa, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a delicate, airy appearance with a dense network of veins on its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Grammitis subcrassa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in indoor gardening.
