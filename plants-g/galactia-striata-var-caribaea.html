Galactia striata var. caribaea

Galactia striata var. caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its milky sap, which is toxic to some animals, is a distinguishing feature.

This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
