Glottiphyllum subditum

Glottiphyllum subditum, commonly known as the dwarf glottiphyllum, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The leaves are succulent and can store water, adapting it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within the leaf rosette.
