Garcinia perrieri

Garcinia perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest in both botanical research and herbal medicine.
