Grewia didyma

Grewia didyma, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Grewia didyma is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
