Grevillea fasciculata var. linearis

Grevillea fasciculata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
