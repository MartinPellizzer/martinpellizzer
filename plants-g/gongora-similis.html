Gongora similis

Gongora similis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and intricate, spurred petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Gongora similis has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in fluctuating environmental conditions.

Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.
