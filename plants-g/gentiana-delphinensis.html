Gentiana delphinensis

Gentiana delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and bear the showy flowers.

Gentiana delphinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
