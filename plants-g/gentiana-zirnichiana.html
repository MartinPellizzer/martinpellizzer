Gentiana zirnichiana

Gentiana zirnichiana, commonly known as the Zirnich's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

Gentiana zirnichiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
