Gossampinus cambodiensis

Gossampinus cambodiensis, commonly known as the Cambodian silk cotton tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fluffy flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces capsules that contain silky, white fibers, which are used in traditional crafts and textiles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
