Galium × ochroleucum var. gonsei

Galium × ochroleucum var. gonsei, commonly known as the Japanese bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
