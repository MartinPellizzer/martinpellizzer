Gomesa × colnagoi

Gomesa × colnagoi, commonly known as the hybrid gomesa, is a rare and intriguing plant resulting from the cross between two species of the Gomesa genus.

It is native to the humid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying vibrant shades of red and orange. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Gomesa × colnagoi is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
