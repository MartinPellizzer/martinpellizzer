Gentianella lutescens

Gentianella lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular and have five lobes, with a bright yellow corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and flies.
