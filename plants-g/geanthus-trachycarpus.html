Geanthus trachycarpus

Geanthus trachycarpus, commonly known as the rough-seeded geanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Geanthus trachycarpus is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
