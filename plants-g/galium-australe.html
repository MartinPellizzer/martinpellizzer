Galium australe

Galium australe, commonly known as the Australian bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium australe is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
