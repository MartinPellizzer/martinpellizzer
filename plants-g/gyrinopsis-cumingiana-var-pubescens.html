Gyrinopsis cumingiana var. pubescens

Gyrinopsis cumingiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and hairy, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along the coastline where it is exposed to salt spray. Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh coastal conditions make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
