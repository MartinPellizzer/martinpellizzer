Grotefendia paniculata

Grotefendia paniculata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters called panicles. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
