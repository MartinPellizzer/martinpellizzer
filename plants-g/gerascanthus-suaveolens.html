Gerascanthus suaveolens

Gerascanthus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and pleasant scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
