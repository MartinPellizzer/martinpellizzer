Gymnocalycium ritterianum

Gymnocalycium ritterianum, commonly known as the Ritter's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense, spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium ritterianum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
