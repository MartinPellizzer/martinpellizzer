Geissorhiza monantha

Geissorhiza monantha, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has fleshy, tuberous roots that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Geissorhiza monantha is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant display and drought tolerance.
