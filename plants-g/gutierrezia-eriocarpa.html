Gutierrezia eriocarpa

Gutierrezia eriocarpa, commonly known as roughleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and shrublands. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry climates.
