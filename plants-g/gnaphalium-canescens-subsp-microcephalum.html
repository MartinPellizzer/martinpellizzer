Gnaphalium canescens subsp. microcephalum

Gnaphalium canescens subsp.

microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and small, hairy leaves.

The plant produces numerous tiny, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the composite flower heads. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, often calcareous soils.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these habitats.
