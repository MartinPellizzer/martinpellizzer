Gentiana arethusae subsp. arethusae

Gentiana arethusae subsp.

arethusae, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural high-altitude environment.
