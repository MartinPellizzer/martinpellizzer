Geum elatum var. typicum

Geum elatum var. typicum, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow, showy flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.
