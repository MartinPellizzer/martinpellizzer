Grevillea manglesioides subsp. metaxa

Grevillea manglesioides subsp.

metaxa, commonly known as the Mangles' Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge.

The plant produces striking red or orange cylindrical flower spikes, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ornamental value in native landscaping.
