Griffithia longiflora

Griffithia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered griffithia, is a rare and ancient coniferous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, elongated cones that contain seeds. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, with separate male and female cones on the same individual. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its high-altitude environment, including thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research.
