Greenella discoidea

Greenella discoidea, commonly known as the disk-shaped greenella, is a unique member of the Chlorophyta division, characterized by its distinctive morphology.

This unicellular alga exhibits a flattened, disk-like shape, which sets it apart from other green algae species. It thrives in freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes where light penetration is sufficient for photosynthesis. The plant's chloroplasts are arranged in a specialized manner, contributing to its efficient light capture and energy conversion.

Its simple structure and ecological role make it an important organism in aquatic ecosystems.
