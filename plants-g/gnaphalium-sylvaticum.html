Gnaphalium sylvaticum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a silvery underside.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for producing a fine, aromatic powder from its flower heads.
