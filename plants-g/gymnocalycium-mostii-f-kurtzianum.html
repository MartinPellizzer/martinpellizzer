Gymnocalycium mostii f. kurtzianum

Gymnocalycium mostii f. kurtzianum, commonly known as the Kurtzianum cactus, is a compact, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

It originates from the arid regions of Argentina and is characterized by its pale green to bluish-green stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
