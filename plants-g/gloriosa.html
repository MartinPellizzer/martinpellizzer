Gloriosa

Gloriosa, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical climbing plant native to eastern Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive curl at the tip. The plant belongs to the genus Gloriosa and is part of the family Colocasiaceae. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, while its stems are thick and fleshy.

Gloriosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
