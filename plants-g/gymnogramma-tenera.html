Gymnogramma tenera

Gymnogramma tenera, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and creeping, supporting the growth of the fronds.

Gymnogramma tenera is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
