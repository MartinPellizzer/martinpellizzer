Galinsoga quadriradiata subsp. hispida

Galinsoga quadriradiata subsp.

hispida, commonly known as rough hairy gallinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
