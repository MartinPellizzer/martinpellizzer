Geranium atrum

Geranium atrum, commonly known as the black geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. Geranium atrum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and unique blooms.

It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to shaded garden areas.
