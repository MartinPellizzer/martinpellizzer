Gyrostachys lineata

Gyrostachys lineata, commonly known as the slender gyrostachys, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
