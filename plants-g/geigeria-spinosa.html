Geigeria spinosa

Geigeria spinosa, commonly known as the spiny geigeria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Geigeria spinosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
