Gonocaryum macrocarpum

Gonocaryum macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread beneath the water surface.

Gonocaryum macrocarpum plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its wetland ecosystems.
