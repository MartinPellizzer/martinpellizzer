Gordonia grandis

Gordonia grandis, commonly known as the giant gordonia, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous bark and broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The tree produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Gordonia grandis is valued for its timber and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
