Grammatopteris

Grammatopteris, commonly known as the "leaf fern," is a genus of fossil plants that lived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds and is often found in coal deposits. The plant's structure suggests it was a vascular plant, capable of transporting water and nutrients efficiently. Grammatopteris played a significant role in the formation of coal through its accumulation in ancient swamps.

Its study provides valuable insights into the evolution of early plant life and the ecosystems of the Paleozoic era.
