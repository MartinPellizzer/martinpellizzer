Gastrolobium tricuspidatum var. latifolium

Gastrolobium tricuspidatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are broad and leathery, often with a waxy coating, and the plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This plant is often used in coastal and dryland landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
