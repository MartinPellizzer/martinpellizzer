Gentiana anglica

Gentiana anglica, commonly known as the English gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the stems are erect and branching.
