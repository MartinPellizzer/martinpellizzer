Granadilla serratifolia

Granadilla serratifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian granadilla, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to passion fruit but have a distinct flavor. Its leaves are serrated, giving it the species name "serratifolia," and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruit and beauty.
