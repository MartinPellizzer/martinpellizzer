Glycosmis cambodiana

Glycosmis cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian date plum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
