Guarea paraensis

Guarea paraensis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a towering tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody capsules that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. Its bark is thick and rugged, providing natural protection against pests and environmental stress.

Guarea paraensis plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, supporting a wide variety of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
