Galium palustre var. uliginosum

Galium palustre var. uliginosum, commonly known as marsh bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, wiry stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, acidic environments.
