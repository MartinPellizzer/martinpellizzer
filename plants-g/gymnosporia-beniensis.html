Gymnosporia beniensis

Gymnosporia beniensis, commonly known as the Beni thornapple, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Gymnosporia beniensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of Andean flora.
