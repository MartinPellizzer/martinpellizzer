Gentiana pseudocephalantha

Gentiana pseudocephalantha, commonly known as the false gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are characterized by their five to six petals, which are often blue or violet in color.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
