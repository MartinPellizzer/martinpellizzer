Goodyera turialbae

Goodyera turialbae, commonly known as the silver waxy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered together in a spike. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Goodyera turialbae is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
