Gleditsia triacanthos

Gleditsia triacanthos, commonly known as honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into small, thorn-tipped leaflets. The tree produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, though the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature. It has deep roots that help prevent soil erosion, making it useful in reforestation projects.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing fertility in agricultural settings.
