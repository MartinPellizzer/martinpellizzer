Gnaphalium clemensiae

Gnaphalium clemensiae, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are often surrounded by dense, hairy foliage. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Gnaphalium clemensiae is known for its long-lasting flowers, which make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
