Glycine comosa

Glycine comosa, commonly known as the hairy soybean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications. Glycine comosa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions makes it an important crop in agricultural systems.
