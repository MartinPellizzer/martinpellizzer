Gamopoda

Gamopoda, commonly known as the "flowering plant with fused petals," is a botanical term referring to a type of floral structure where the petals are joined together.

This fusion, known as gamopony, is a key characteristic in the classification of certain flowering plants. Gamopoda is typically found in plants belonging to the family Malvaceae, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The fused petals often form a showy, trumpet-shaped corolla, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
