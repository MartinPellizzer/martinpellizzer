Gonolobus velutinus

Gonolobus velutinus, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a hairy texture on its stems and leaves, giving it its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.

Gonolobus velutinus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
