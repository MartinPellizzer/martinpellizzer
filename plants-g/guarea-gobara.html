Guarea gobara

Guarea gobara, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces round, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
