Gaillardia × loiselii

Gaillardia × loiselii, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with vibrant red and yellow petals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gaillardia × loiselii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
