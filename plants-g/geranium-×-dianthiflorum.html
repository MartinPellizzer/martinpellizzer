Geranium × dianthiflorum

Geranium × dianthiflorum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines features of several geranium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners.
