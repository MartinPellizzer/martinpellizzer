Gymnadenia squamata

Gymnadenia squamata, commonly known as the squamata orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking, fragrant orchid flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink or white flowers with a distinctive scent. Gymnadenia squamata prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
