Githago segetalis

Githago segetalis, commonly known as the wheatgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in fields and along roadsides. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Githago segetalis is known for its nutritional value and is often used in herbal remedies and as a forage crop.
