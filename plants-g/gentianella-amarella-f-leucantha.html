Gentianella amarella f. leucantha

Gentianella amarella f. leucantha, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
