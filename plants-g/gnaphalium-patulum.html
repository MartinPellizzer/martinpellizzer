Gnaphalium patulum

Gnaphalium patulum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense mats or cushions, often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows. The plant has small, hairy leaves and produces tiny, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense groups. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
