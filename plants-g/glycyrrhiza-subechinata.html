Glycyrrhiza subechinata

Glycyrrhiza subechinata, commonly known as the Indian licorice, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round pods containing several seeds, and its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes. Glycyrrhiza subechinata contains compounds like glycyrrhizin, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic value.
