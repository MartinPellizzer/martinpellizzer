Geranium pyrenaicum var. pilosum

Geranium pyrenaicum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are typically hairy and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
