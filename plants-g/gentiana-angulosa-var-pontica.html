Gentiana angulosa var. pontica

Gentiana angulosa var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
