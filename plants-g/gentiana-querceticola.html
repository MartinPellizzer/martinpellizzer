Gentiana querceticola

Gentiana querceticola, commonly known as the oakleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with oak trees. Its leaves are deeply lobed, resembling the shape of an oak leaf, hence its common name.

Gentiana querceticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
