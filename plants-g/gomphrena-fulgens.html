Gomphrena fulgens

Gomphrena fulgens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, providing a contrast to the vibrant flower heads.

Gomphrena fulgens is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms and low maintenance requirements.
