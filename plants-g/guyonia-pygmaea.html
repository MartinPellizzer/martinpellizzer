Guyonia pygmaea

Guyonia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the winter months. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.

Guyonia pygmaea prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
