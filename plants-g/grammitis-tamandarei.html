Grammitis tamandarei

Grammitis tamandarei, commonly known as the Tamandare fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests, where it attaches itself to tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are usually small and closely packed, forming a dense, moss-like appearance.

Grammitis tamandarei is known for its intricate venation patterns and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
