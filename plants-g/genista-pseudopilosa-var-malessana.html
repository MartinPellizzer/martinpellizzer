Genista pseudopilosa var. malessana

Genista pseudopilosa var. malessana, commonly known as the Malessana broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
