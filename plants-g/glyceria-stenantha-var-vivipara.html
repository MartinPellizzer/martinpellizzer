Glyceria stenantha var. vivipara

Glyceria stenantha var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous flatgrass, is a variant of the flatgrass species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. One of its notable botanical features is its viviparous reproduction, where young plants develop on the mother plant before detaching and taking root.

This trait makes it an interesting subject for studies on plant propagation and adaptation.
