Galarhoeus esula var. maackii

Galarhoeus esula var. maackii, commonly known as the Maack's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
