Genista insubrica

Genista insubrica, commonly known as the creeping yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant, suitable for use in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
