Gladiolus caudatus

Gladiolus caudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive, elongated floral spikes, which are characteristic of the gladiolus genus.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
