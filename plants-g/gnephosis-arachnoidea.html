Gnephosis arachnoidea

Gnephosis arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals that resemble the legs of a spider. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories where it thrives on the nutrient-rich soil and high humidity. The flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
