Gymnobalanus latifolia

Gymnobalanus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sea almond, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in saline and tidal environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

Gymnobalanus latifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
