Gomphrena elegans var. gracilior

Gomphrena elegans var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender garden globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red, giving it a distinctive, globular appearance. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena elegans var. gracilior is known for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance, making it a low-maintenance addition to gardens.
