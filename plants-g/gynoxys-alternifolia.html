Gynoxys alternifolia

Gynoxys alternifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Gynoxys alternifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
