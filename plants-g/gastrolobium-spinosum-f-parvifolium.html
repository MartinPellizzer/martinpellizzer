Gastrolobium spinosum f. parvifolium

Gastrolobium spinosum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved wattle, is a variant of the spinifex wattle.

It is a shrub native to Western Australia, characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This form is distinguished by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical variety.
