Galium rubrum var. myrianthum

Galium rubrum var. myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Its flowers are usually accompanied by two bracts, and the plant is known for its creeping growth habit.

Galium rubrum var. myrianthum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
