Gaultheria leucocarpa f. melanocarpa

Gaultheria leucocarpa f. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black wax myrtle, is a variant of the wax myrtle species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, waxy berries that contrast with its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is evergreen, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
