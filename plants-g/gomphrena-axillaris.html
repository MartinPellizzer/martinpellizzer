Gomphrena axillaris

Gomphrena axillaris, commonly known as the clustered gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink or red bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage consists of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gomphrena axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
