Goodyera querceticola

Goodyera querceticola, commonly known as the oakleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks.

Goodyera querceticola is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found growing on the forest floor or on the bases of trees.
