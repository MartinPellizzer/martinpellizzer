Glyceria thomsonii

Glyceria thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing small, two-lobed flowers.

Glyceria thomsonii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
