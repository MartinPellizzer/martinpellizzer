Glechoma teucrioides

Glechoma teucrioides, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma teucrioides is known for its rapid spread and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
