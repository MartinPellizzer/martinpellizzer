Galium rubioides var. eriophyllum

Galium rubioides var. eriophyllum, commonly known as the slender wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a heart-shaped base with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, producing a delicate, bell-shaped appearance.
