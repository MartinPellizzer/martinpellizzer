Gnaphalium tweedieae

Gnaphalium tweedieae, commonly known as the Tweedie's everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads, which are often mistaken for cotton due to their fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to New Zealand and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its aromatic foliage.
