Glycine wightii subsp. pseudojavanica

Glycine wightii subsp.

pseudojavanica, commonly known as the Javan climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its climbing habit, with compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
