Gomphrena elegans var. microcephala

Gomphrena elegans var. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with dense clusters of small, spiky, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size and drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens.
