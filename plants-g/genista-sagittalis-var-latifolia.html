Genista sagittalis var. latifolia

Genista sagittalis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, though the variety latifolia has broader, more lance-shaped foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
