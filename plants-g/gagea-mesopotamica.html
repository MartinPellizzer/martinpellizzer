Gagea mesopotamica

Gagea mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Middle East.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers in early spring, followed by small, rounded berries. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, fibrous root system.

Gagea mesopotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
