Glycyrrhiza laxissima

Glycyrrhiza laxissima, commonly known as the Chinese licorice, is a perennial herb native to China, Mongolia, and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume pods containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza laxissima is valued for its roots, which are rich in glycyrrhizin and are widely used in traditional medicine and as a sweetener.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmaceutical contexts.
