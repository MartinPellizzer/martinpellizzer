Geranium wilfordii var. hastatum

Geranium wilfordii var. hastatum, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive broad, rounded shape. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
