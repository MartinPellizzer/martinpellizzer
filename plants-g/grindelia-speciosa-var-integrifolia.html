Grindelia speciosa var. integrifolia

Grindelia speciosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-green, fuzzy foliage and produces yellowish-green composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with entire margins, and it is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
