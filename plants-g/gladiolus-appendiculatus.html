Gladiolus appendiculatus

Gladiolus appendiculatus, commonly known as the appendiculate gladiolus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
