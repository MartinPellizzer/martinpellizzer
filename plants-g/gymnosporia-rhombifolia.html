Gymnosporia rhombifolia

Gymnosporia rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiky fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Gymnosporia rhombifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
