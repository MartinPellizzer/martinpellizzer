Geranium texanum f. albiflorum

Geranium texanum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the Texas geranium or whiteflower geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy foliage that ranges from gray-green to silver-white. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
