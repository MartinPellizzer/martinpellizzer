Gentiana octoflora

Gentiana octoflora, commonly known as the eight-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana octoflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
