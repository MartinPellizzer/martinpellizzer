Grevillea decora subsp. telfordii

Grevillea decora subsp.

telfordii, commonly known as the Telford's Christmas Bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant red and yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its floral display attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscaping.
