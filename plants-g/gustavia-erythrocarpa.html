Gustavia erythrocarpa

Gustavia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited native hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, often in shades of pink or red, and are followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
