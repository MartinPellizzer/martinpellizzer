Grossheimia macrocephala

Grossheimia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant-headed lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and often persist through the winter.

Grossheimia macrocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
