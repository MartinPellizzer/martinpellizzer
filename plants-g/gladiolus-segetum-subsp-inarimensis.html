Gladiolus segetum subsp. inarimensis

Gladiolus segetum subsp.

inarimensis, commonly known as the Inarimense gladiolus, is a rare and lesser-known subspecies native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique beauty.
