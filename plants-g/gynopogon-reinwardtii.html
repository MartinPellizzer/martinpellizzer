Gynopogon reinwardtii

Gynopogon reinwardtii, commonly known as the African grass or reinwardt's grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Gynopogon reinwardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
