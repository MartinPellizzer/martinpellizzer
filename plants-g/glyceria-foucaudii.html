Glyceria foucaudii

Glyceria foucaudii, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Glyceria foucaudii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
