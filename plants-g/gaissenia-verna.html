Gaissenia verna

Gaissenia verna, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Gaissenia verna is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
