Glycine tabacina

Glycine tabacina, commonly known as the tobacco bean vine, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Glycine tabacina is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
