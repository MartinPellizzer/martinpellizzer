Gmelina hainanensis

Gmelina hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan gmelina, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
