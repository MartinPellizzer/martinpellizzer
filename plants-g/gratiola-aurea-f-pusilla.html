Gratiola aurea f. pusilla

Gratiola aurea f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf golden crowfoot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is smaller than the typical Gratiola aurea, with a more compact growth habit.
