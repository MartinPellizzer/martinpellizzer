Gentiana alpina var. caulescens

Gentiana alpina var. caulescens, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh mountain conditions.

The variety caulescens is distinguished by its more robust stems and clustered flower arrangement compared to the typical form.
