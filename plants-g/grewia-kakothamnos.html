Grewia kakothamnos

Grewia kakothamnos, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Grewia kakothamnos is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
