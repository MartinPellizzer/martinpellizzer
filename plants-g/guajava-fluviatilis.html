Guajava fluviatilis

Guajava fluviatilis, commonly known as the river guajava, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are often consumed raw or processed into jams. Guajava fluviatilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
