Guadua latifolia

Guadua latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a robust perennial grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 15 meters in height, with broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is highly valued for its strong, lightweight, and durable culms, which are used in construction and crafts. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an effective erosion control plant.

Guadua latifolia is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
