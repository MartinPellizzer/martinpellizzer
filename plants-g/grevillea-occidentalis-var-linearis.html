Grevillea occidentalis var. linearis

Grevillea occidentalis var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
