Geranium fastigiatum

Geranium fastigiatum, commonly known as the upright geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a slightly serrated edge, and are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Geranium fastigiatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
