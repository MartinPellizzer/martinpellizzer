Gynocardia odorata

Gynocardia odorata, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are leathery and glossy, with a strong, pleasant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
