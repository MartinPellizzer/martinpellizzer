Goodyera nankoensis

Goodyera nankoensis, commonly known as the Nanko orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
