Gyrosphragma

Gyrosphragma, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers with ray florets. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Gyrosphragma is characterized by its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil.
