Gravesia rosea

Gravesia rosea, commonly known as the rose sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib. The plant is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.
