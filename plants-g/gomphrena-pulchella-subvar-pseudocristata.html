Gomphrena pulchella subvar. pseudocristata

Gomphrena pulchella subvar. pseudocristata, commonly known as the rose-haired gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, pink to red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, surrounded by colorful, papery bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subvariety is known for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
