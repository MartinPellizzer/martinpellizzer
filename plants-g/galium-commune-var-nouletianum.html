Galium commune var. nouletianum

Galium commune var. nouletianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
