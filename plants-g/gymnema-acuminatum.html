Gymnema acuminatum

Gymnema acuminatum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the forests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema acuminatum is known for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
