Glycosmis tetracronia

Glycosmis tetracronia, commonly known as the four-angled sweetleaf, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with a distinctive four-angled stem. Glycosmis tetracronia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
