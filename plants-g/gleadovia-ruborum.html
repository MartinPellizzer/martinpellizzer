Gleadovia ruborum

Gleadovia ruborum, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
