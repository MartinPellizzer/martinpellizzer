Gasteria pillansii var. hallii

Gasteria pillansii var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's gasteria, is a compact, evergreen succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with white or yellow markings, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers on tall, leafless stems, usually in shades of red or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
