Graptophyllum macrostemon

Graptophyllum macrostemon, commonly known as the large-stamen grass of India, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a dense clump. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Graptophyllum macrostemon is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
