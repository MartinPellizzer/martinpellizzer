Gentlea mcvaughii

Gentlea mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-elevation regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branched.

The flowering period occurs in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.
