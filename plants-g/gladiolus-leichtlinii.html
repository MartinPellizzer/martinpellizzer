Gladiolus leichtlinii

Gladiolus leichtlinii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's gladiolus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a corm, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
