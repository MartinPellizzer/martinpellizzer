Galium umbrosum

Galium umbrosum, commonly known as shadeplant or dark willow, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels, giving it the species name "umbrosum," derived from the Latin word for shade.

Galium umbrosum has lance-shaped leaves and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
