Galium vernum var. lasiopodon

Galium vernum var. lasiopodon, commonly known as the early woodruff, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, ascending stems that bear small, white or pale green flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
