Gentiana wingecarribiensis var. wingecarribiensis

Gentiana wingecarribiensis var. wingecarribiensis, commonly known as the Wingecarribee gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, preferring rich, moist soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, supporting a range of pollinators and serving as a key component of wetland ecosystems.
