Gentiana deloachii

Gentiana deloachii, commonly known as Deloach's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
