Guzmania vinacea

Guzmania vinacea, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-tipped leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a visually appealing appearance. The plant produces a striking inflorescence with colorful bracts that resemble a pineapple, which is a key feature in its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Guzmania vinacea is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in humid environments.
