Guzmania madisonii

Guzmania madisonii, commonly known as the Madison's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small reservoir for its root system. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, red, and purple, adding to its ornamental value.

Guzmania madisonii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
