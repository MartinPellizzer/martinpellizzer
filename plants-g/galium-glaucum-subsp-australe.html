Galium glaucum subsp. australe

Galium glaucum subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features slender, prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
