Galactia neesii var. macropoda

Galactia neesii var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-seeded milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
