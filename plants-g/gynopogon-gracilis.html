Gynopogon gracilis

Gynopogon gracilis, commonly known as the slender gynopogon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Gynopogon gracilis typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
