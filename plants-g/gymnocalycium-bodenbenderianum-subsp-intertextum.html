Gymnocalycium bodenbenderianum subsp. intertextum

Gymnocalycium bodenbenderianum subsp.

intertextum, commonly known as the Boddie's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to northern South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered with white to pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
