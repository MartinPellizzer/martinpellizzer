Gentianella orobanchoides

Gentianella orobanchoides, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that reaches up to 30 cm in height.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
