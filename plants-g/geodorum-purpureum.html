Geodorum purpureum

Geodorum purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Geodorum purpureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
