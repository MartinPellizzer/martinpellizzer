Grindelia microcephala f. angustior

Grindelia microcephala f. angustior, commonly known as the narrow-headed bitterbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, resinous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
