Grewia australis

Grewia australis, commonly known as the Australian wild gooseberry, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional bush foods. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Grewia australis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
