Gentiana scabra var. intermedia

Gentiana scabra var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often branched and hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
