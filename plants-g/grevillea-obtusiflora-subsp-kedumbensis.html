Grevillea obtusiflora subsp. kedumbensis

Grevillea obtusiflora subsp.

kedumbensis, commonly known as the Kedumbi grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its floral structure is typical of the Grevillea genus, featuring long, narrow petals and a central stamen.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal and inland conditions.
