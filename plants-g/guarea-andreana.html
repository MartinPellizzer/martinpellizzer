Guarea andreana

Guarea andreana, commonly known as the "Brazilian ironwood," is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Guarea andreana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
