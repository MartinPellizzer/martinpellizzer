Gardinia purpurascens

Gardinia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered gardenia, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardinia purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
