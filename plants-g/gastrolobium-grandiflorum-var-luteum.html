Gastrolobium grandiflorum var. luteum

Gastrolobium grandiflorum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

The yellow wattle is an important component of the local flora and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
