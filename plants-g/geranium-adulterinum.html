Geranium adulterinum

Geranium adulterinum, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
