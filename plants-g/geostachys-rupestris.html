Geostachys rupestris

Geostachys rupestris, commonly known as the rock geostachys, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Geostachys rupestris is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
