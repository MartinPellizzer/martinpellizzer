Gigantochloa dinhensis

Gigantochloa dinhensis, commonly known as the Dinh Mountain bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, grass-like texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it has potential applications in sustainable construction and erosion control.
