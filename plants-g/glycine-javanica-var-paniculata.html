Glycine javanica var. paniculata

Glycine javanica var. paniculata, commonly known as the climbing peanut vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
