Gossypium hirsutum var. hirsutum

Gossypium hirsutum var. hirsutum, commonly known as upland cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white lint that is harvested from its bolls. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with a woody stem and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are followed by the fibrous seed pods.

This variety is widely cultivated for its cotton fiber, which is used in textiles and other industrial applications.
