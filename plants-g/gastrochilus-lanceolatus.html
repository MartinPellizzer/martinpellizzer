Gastrochilus lanceolatus

Gastrochilus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate stomach lip, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil, which is crucial for its survival in its natural habitat.

Gastrochilus lanceolatus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
