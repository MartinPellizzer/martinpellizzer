Gilia filifolia var. diffusa

Gilia filifolia var. diffusa, commonly known as the threadleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its propagation in disturbed soils.

The species is often found in open areas such as grasslands and along canyon walls.
