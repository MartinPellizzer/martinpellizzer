Gilbertiodendron barbulatum

Gilbertiodendron barbulatum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
