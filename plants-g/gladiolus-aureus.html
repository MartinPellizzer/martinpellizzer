Gladiolus aureus

Gladiolus aureus, commonly known as the golden gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its vibrant colors and elegant appearance make it a favorite among horticulturists and florists alike.
