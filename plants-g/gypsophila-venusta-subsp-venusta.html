Gypsophila venusta subsp. venusta

Gypsophila venusta subsp.

venusta, commonly known as the sweet William or garden hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late summer.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
