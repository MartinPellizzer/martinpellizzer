Gentiana triflora var. montana

Gentiana triflora var. montana, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
