Gomphrena perennis var. divergens

Gomphrena perennis var. divergens, commonly known as the everlasting pea or flame violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant pink or red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain colorful even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
