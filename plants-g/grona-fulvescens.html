Grona fulvescens

Grona fulvescens, commonly known as the golden gronach, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers range from yellow to orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
