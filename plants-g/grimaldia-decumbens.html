Grimaldia decumbens

Grimaldia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping grimaldia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
