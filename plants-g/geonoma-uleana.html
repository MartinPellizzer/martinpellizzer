Geonoma uleana

Geonoma uleana, commonly known as the Uleana fern, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, leathery leaves that form a water-holding structure, typical of many bromeliads. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, white flowers that bloom sequentially, adding ornamental value.

Geonoma uleana is often cultivated for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
