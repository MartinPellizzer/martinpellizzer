Galeopsis ladanum var. orophila

Galeopsis ladanum var. orophila, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf, is a variant of the common velvetleaf found in higher elevations.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
