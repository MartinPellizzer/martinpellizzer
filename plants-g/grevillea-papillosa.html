Grevillea papillosa

Grevillea papillosa, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea papillosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
