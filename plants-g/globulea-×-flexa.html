Globulea × flexa

Globulea × flexa, commonly known as the hybrid globose spurge, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to regions of South America and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in arid gardens.
