Guatteria curvipetala

Guatteria curvipetala, commonly known as the curving petal guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are clustered in terminal inflorescences, while its fruit is a small, fleshy berry.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
