Gloxinia guttata

Gloxinia guttata, commonly known as the spotted gloxinia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight. Its tuberous roots store nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of dormancy.

Gloxinia guttata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
