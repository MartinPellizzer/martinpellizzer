Garcinia curvinervis

Garcinia curvinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent, curved veins. Its fruit is a small, yellow-orange drupe that contains a single seed.

Garcinia curvinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
