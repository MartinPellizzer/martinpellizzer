Gynopachis corymbosa

Gynopachis corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered gynandropsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Gynopachis corymbosa is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some cultures for various health purposes.
