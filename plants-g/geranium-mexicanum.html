Geranium mexicanum

Geranium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Geranium mexicanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
