Gagea rubroviridis

Gagea rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-green gagea, is a species of bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a single, elongated bulb that serves as its primary storage organ. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or alpine environments.

Gagea rubroviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
