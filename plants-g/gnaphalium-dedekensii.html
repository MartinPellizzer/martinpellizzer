Gnaphalium dedekensii

Gnaphalium dedekensii, commonly known as the Dedekens' everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Native to southern Africa, this species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its name derives from the South African botanist Dr. J.C. Dedekens, in recognition of his contributions to botanical research.
