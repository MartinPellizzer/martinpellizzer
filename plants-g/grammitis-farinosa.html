Grammitis farinosa

Grammitis farinosa, commonly known as the flour moss, is a species of epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. Its small, clustered growth habit and ability to absorb moisture from the air make it well adapted to its ecological niche.

Grammitis farinosa plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various invertebrates.
