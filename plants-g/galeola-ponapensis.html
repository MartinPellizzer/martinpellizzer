Galeola ponapensis

Galeola ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponape orchid, is a rare and endemic flowering plant found exclusively on the island of Pohnpei in the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rock faces or in forest undergrowth.

Its conservation status is vulnerable due to habitat loss and limited distribution, making it a significant species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
