Gentiana laevigata var. hintoniorum

Gentiana laevigata var. hintoniorum, commonly known as the Hinton's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky woods and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
