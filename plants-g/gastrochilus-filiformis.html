Gastrochilus filiformis

Gastrochilus filiformis, commonly known as the slender lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives on decaying organic matter. The plant features a slender, leafless stem that supports a single, showy flower with a distinctive, elongated lip. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
