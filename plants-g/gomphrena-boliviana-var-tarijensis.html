Gomphrena boliviana var. tarijensis

Gomphrena boliviana var. tarijensis, commonly known as the Bolivian globemallow, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Gomphrena boliviana var. tarijensis is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance in gardens.
