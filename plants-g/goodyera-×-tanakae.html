Goodyera × tanakae

Goodyera × tanakae, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, Goodyera oblongifolia and Goodyera tenera.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a slipper, a characteristic feature of the orchid family. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

Goodyera × tanakae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and ease of care.
