Gentiana scaberrima

Gentiana scaberrima, commonly known as the rough gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are rough to the touch, giving it its common name, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Gentiana scaberrima is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
