Geranium delavayi

Geranium delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southwest China.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Geranium delavayi is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
