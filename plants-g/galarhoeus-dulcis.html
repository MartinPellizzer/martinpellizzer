Galarhoeus dulcis

Galarhoeus dulcis, commonly known as the sweetleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in sugars and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Galarhoeus dulcis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
