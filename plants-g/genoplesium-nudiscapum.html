Genoplesium nudiscapum

Genoplesium nudiscapum, commonly known as the naked-stem leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests or under dense vegetation. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to habitat loss and threats from environmental changes.
