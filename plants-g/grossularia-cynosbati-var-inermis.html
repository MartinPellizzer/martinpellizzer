Grossularia cynosbati var. inermis

Grossularia cynosbati var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth Himalayan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
