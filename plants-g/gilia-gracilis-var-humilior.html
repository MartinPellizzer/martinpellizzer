Gilia gracilis var. humilior

Gilia gracilis var. humilior, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and clusters of small, bright pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to dry environments.
