Geranium flavum

Geranium flavum, commonly known as the yellow geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in umbel-like inflorescences.

Geranium flavum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
