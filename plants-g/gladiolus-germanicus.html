Gladiolus germanicus

Gladiolus germanicus, commonly known as the German gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, yellow, and white. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The German gladiolus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
