Griffonia mannii

Griffonia mannii, commonly known as African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Griffonia mannii is notable for containing the alkaloid 5-hydroxytryptophan, which has been studied for its potential health benefits.

It is often found in moist, tropical habitats and is used in traditional medicine.
