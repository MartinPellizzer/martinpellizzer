Gypsophila scariosifolia

Gypsophila scariosifolia, commonly known as the paperflower or desert holly, is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae).

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar environmental conditions.
