Geonoma pinnatifrons subsp. membranacea

Geonoma pinnatifrons subsp.

membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous fern, is a species of fern native to Central and South America. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive membranous texture that gives it its common name.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
