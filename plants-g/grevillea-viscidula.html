Grevillea viscidula

Grevillea viscidula, commonly known as the sticky grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea viscidula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
