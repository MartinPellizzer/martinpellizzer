Gerardia holmiana

Gerardia holmiana, commonly known as the Holm oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The tree is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
