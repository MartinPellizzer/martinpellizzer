Gymnosporia febrifuga

Gymnosporia febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Gymnosporia febrifuga has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential antipyretic effects.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of African flora and traditional herbal medicine.
