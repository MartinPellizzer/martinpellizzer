Gymnogramma laxa

Gymnogramma laxa, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it to substrates such as soil or rock.

Gymnogramma laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
