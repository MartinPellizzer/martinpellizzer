Genista numidica subsp. filiramea

Genista numidica subsp.

filiramea, commonly known as the Numidian broom, is a shrub native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The leaves are small, trifoliate, and have a leathery texture. It produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
