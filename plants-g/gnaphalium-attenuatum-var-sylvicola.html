Gnaphalium attenuatum var. sylvicola

Gnaphalium attenuatum var. sylvicola, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
