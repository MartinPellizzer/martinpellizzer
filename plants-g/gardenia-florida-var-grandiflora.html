Gardenia florida var. grandiflora

Gardenia florida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Florida gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance and pleasant scent.

The flowers are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter, making them a striking feature in gardens and landscapes.
