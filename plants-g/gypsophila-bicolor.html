Gypsophila bicolor

Gypsophila bicolor, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often used in floral arrangements. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

Gypsophila bicolor is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
