Gerascanthus mexianus

Gerascanthus mexianus, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or pink blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gerascanthus mexianus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
