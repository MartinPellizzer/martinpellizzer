Grastidium schweinfurthianum

Grastidium schweinfurthianum, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green coloration.

Grastidium schweinfurthianum is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
