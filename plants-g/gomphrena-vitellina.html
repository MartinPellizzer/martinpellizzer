Gomphrena vitellina

Gomphrena vitellina, commonly known as the golden gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are cylindrical and often branched.

Gomphrena vitellina is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
