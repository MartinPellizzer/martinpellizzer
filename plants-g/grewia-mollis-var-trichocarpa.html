Grewia mollis var. trichocarpa

Grewia mollis var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the woolly gum tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sour taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in local habitats.
