Gypsophila pilosa var. glabra

Gypsophila pilosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white or pale pink flower heads composed of numerous small, delicate petals. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
