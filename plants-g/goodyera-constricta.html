Goodyera constricta

Goodyera constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Goodyera constricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
