Gymnosporia gracilipes subsp. arguta

Gymnosporia gracilipes subsp.

arguta, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, stipulate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing oval seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and habitat for various insect species.
