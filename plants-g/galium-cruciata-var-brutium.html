Galium cruciata var. brutium

Galium cruciata var. brutium, commonly known as the Balkan bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in loose clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that dehisce to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks, thriving in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
