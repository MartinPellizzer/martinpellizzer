Grastidium rubropictum

Grastidium rubropictum, commonly known as the crimson painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling painted designs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Grastidium rubropictum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
