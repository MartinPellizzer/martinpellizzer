Galathenium multiflorum

Galathenium multiflorum, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that thrives in sandy, coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Galathenium multiflorum plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
