Gomphrena paranensis

Gomphrena paranensis, commonly known as the Parana feathery globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Gomphrena paranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
