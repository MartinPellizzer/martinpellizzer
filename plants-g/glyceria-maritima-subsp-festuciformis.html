Glyceria maritima subsp. festuciformis

Glyceria maritima subsp.

festuciformis, commonly known as salt meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands, thriving in poorly drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand salt spray and tidal influences, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
