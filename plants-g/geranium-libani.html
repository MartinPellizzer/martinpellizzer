Geranium libani

Geranium libani, commonly known as the Lebanon geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium libani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
