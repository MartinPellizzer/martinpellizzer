Galium boreale var. japonicum

Galium boreale var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its reproductive strategy includes wind pollination and the production of small, dry fruits that disperse seeds.
