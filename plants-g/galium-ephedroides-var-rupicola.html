Galium ephedroides var. rupicola

Galium ephedroides var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock gallberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing a succulent-like stem that helps it retain moisture. Its leaves contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
