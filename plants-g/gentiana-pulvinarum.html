Gentiana pulvinarum

Gentiana pulvinarum, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
