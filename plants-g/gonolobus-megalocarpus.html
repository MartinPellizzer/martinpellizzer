Gonolobus megalocarpus

Gonolobus megalocarpus, commonly known as the giant carob tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
