Granadilla laurifolia

Granadilla laurifolia, commonly known as the sweet granadilla, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit, which is typically yellow or orange, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Granadilla laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
