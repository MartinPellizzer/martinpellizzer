Gomphrena hirsutissima

Gomphrena hirsutissima, commonly known as the everlasting flower or woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to produce long-lasting, showy blooms in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in dried floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral crafts.

Gomphrena hirsutissima is also known for its resilience and ability to reseed itself, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
