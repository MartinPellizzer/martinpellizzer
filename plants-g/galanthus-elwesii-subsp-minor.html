Galanthus elwesii subsp. minor

Galanthus elwesii subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces delicate, white, drooping flowers with green tips, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in woodland or shaded garden environments. It is valued for its early flowering and frost-resistant qualities.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanic collections.
