Garcinia conicarpa

Garcinia conicarpa, commonly known as the African mango or West African mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces yellow-orange fruits that are oval in shape and contain a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts in its native region.
