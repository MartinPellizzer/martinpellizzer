Gilruthia

Gilruthia, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Gilruthia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
