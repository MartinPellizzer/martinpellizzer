Gentianella caucasea

Gentianella caucasea, commonly known as the Caucasian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy inflorescences.

Gentianella caucasea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower collections.
