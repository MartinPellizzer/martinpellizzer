Gardenia suaveolens

Gardenia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
