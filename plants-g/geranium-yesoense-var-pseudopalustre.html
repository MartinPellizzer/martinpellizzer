Geranium yesoense var. pseudopalustre

Geranium yesoense var. pseudopalustre, commonly known as the Yesso geranium, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
