Guapira rufescens var. lanceolatus

Guapira rufescens var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf guapira, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale yellow, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
