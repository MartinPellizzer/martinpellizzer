Grevillea hookeriana subsp. digitata

Grevillea hookeriana subsp.

digitata, commonly known as the digitate grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit and its distinctive, finger-like leaf arrangement.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
