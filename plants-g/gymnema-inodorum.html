Gymnema inodorum

Gymnema inodorum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its unique ability to reduce the absorption of glucose in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. Gymnema inodorum is also recognized for its role in traditional Ayurvedic practices, where it has been used to manage blood sugar levels.

Its leaves contain a compound called gymnemic acid, which is believed to contribute to its hypoglycemic effects.
