Gagea pulchella

Gagea pulchella, commonly known as the showy gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. Gagea pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
