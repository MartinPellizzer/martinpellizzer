Galeopsis speciosa f. alba

Galeopsis speciosa f. alba, commonly known as white hedge nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale green leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a hairy stem and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
