Grewia africana

Grewia africana, commonly known as the African globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Grewia africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
