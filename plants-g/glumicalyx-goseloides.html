Glumicalyx goseloides

Glumicalyx goseloides, commonly known as the mountain wisteria, is a climbing vine native to the high altitudes of southern China.

It features striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with slender, elongated leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
