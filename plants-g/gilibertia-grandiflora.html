Gilibertia grandiflora

Gilibertia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant gilbertia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and sturdy.

Gilibertia grandiflora is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
