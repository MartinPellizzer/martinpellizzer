Grewia polypyrena

Grewia polypyrena, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source by local communities.
