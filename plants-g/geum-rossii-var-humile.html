Geum rossii var. humile

Geum rossii var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf Canadian buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland gardens and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for naturalized landscapes and wetland restoration projects.
