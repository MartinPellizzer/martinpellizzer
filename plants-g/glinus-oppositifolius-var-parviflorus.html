Glinus oppositifolius var. parviflorus

Glinus oppositifolius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered glinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in spring.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
