Gravesia macrophylla var. riparia

Gravesia macrophylla var. riparia, commonly known as the river gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are typically clustered in panicles and bloom during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
