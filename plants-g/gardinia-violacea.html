Gardinia violacea

Gardinia violacea, commonly known as the violet gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gardinia violacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
