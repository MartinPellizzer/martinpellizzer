Gnaphalium uliginosum f. condensatum

Gnaphalium uliginosum f. condensatum, commonly known as the dense bog everlasting, is a horticultural form of the common bog everlasting.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its soft, fluffy appearance. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in moist garden environments.

It thrives in wet, boggy soils and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
