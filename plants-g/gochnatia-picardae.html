Gochnatia picardae

Gochnatia picardae, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with hairy surfaces.

Gochnatia picardae is often found in grasslands and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
