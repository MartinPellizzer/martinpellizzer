Gamocarpha falklandica

Gamocarpha falklandica, commonly known as Falkland Island thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Falkland Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the harsh, windy conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
