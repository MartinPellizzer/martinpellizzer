Garcinia fluviatilis

Garcinia fluviatilis, commonly known as the river garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that develop into orange-red berries. The plant is often found growing near rivers and streams, thriving in moist, humid environments. Its berries contain a high concentration of alkaloids and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
