Geranium apricum

Geranium apricum, commonly known as the lemon geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a favored plant among horticulturists.
