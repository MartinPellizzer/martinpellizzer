Gardenia edulis

Gardenia edulis, commonly known as the edible gardenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The fruit of the plant is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its edible fruit, which is used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
