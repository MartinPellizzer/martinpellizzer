Gaertnera cardiocarpa

Gaertnera cardiocarpa, commonly known as the heart-shaped fruit plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking heart-shaped fruits, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Gaertnera cardiocarpa is primarily cultivated for its unique fruit shape and is sometimes grown in botanical gardens for its aesthetic value.
