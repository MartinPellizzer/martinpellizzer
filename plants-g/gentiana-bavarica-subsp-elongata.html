Gentiana bavarica subsp. elongata

Gentiana bavarica subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are usually unbranched and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
