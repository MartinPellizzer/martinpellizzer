Galeandra blattiodora

Galeandra blattiodora, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle scent. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Galeandra blattiodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
