Galium pogonanthum var. nudiflorum

Galium pogonanthum var. nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
