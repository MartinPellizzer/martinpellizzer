Geranium pratense var. schmidii

Geranium pratense var. schmidii, commonly known as the Schmid's cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
