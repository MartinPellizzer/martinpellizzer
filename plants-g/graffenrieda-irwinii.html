Graffenrieda irwinii

Graffenrieda irwinii, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces unique, lantern-like seed pods that enclose numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a sought-after addition to botanical collections.
