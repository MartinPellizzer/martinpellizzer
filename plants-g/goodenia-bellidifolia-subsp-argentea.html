Goodenia bellidifolia subsp. argentea

Goodenia bellidifolia subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver goodenia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
