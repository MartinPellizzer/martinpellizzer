Gagea liotardii var. fragifera

Gagea liotardii var. fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is noted for its sweet, aromatic scent, which is most prominent in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period to bloom successfully.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fragrance.
