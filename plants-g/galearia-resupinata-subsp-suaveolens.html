Galearia resupinata subsp. suaveolens

Galearia resupinata subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. Its flowering stems are slender and often bear several clusters of flowers along their length.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
