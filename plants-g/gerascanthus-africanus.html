Gerascanthus africanus

Gerascanthus africanus, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Gerascanthus africanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
