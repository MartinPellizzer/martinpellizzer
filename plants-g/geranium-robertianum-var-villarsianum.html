Geranium robertianum var. villarsianum

Geranium robertianum var. villarsianum, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover in gardens and naturalized settings.
