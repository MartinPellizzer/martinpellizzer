Gomphrena pohlii var. hassleri

Gomphrena pohlii var. hassleri, commonly known as the pink cottonball plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, pink to purple bracts that resemble cotton balls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by these showy structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its unique, long-lasting inflorescences.
