Gompholobium virgatum

Gompholobium virgatum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Gompholobium virgatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
