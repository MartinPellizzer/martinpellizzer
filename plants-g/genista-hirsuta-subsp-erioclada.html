Genista hirsuta subsp. erioclada

Genista hirsuta subsp.

erioclada, commonly known as the hairy broom or erioclada broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, often branched stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are small, trifoliate, and typically covered with fine pubescence. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters during spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
