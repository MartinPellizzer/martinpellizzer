Gymnosporia royleana

Gymnosporia royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, cone-like structures called gynostegium, which contain its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

The Kashmir rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
