Gaultheria leucocarpa f. cumingiana

Gaultheria leucocarpa f. cumingiana, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a botanical variant of the species Gaultheria leucocarpa.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the winter months. These flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible and have a mild, spicy flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
