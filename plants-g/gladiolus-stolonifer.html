Gladiolus stolonifer

Gladiolus stolonifer, commonly known as the creeping gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers. The plant is characterized by its stolons, which are horizontal stems that root at the nodes and help in vegetative propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
