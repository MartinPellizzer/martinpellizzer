Gentiana perpusilla

Gentiana perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Gentiana perpusilla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
