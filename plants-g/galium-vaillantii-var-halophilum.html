Galium vaillantii var. halophilum

Galium vaillantii var. halophilum, commonly known as saltbush or halophytic galium, is a perennial herbaceous plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, showcasing a high tolerance for soil salinity. Its growth habit includes low, sprawling stems that help it stabilize in loose, salty soils.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and its potential use in phytoremediation.
