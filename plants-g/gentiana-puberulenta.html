Gentiana puberulenta

Gentiana puberulenta, commonly known as the pubescent gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the species name "puberulenta," which means "covered with fine hairs.".

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by flies and bees.
