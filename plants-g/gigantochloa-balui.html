Gigantochloa balui

Gigantochloa balui, commonly known as the giant bamboo of Borneo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and is often found in wetland or swampy areas. Its rhizomes are used in traditional construction and as a source of fiber.

Gigantochloa balui plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
