Godoya Disticha

Blue mistflower, scientifically known as Godoya disticha, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. Its name derives from the soft, bluish-gray foliage that resembles a misty cloud, creating a unique visual effect in any garden. This perennial shrub is prized for its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it an excellent choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and ease of care. With its elegant appearance and ecological benefits, the blue mistflower is a valuable addition to native plant collections and sustainable landscapes.
