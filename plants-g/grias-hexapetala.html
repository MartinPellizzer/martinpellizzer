Grias hexapetala

Grias hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with six petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

The flower structure is complex, with a showy lip that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
