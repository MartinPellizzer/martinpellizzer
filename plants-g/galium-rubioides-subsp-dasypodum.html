Galium rubioides subsp. dasypodum

Galium rubioides subsp.

dasypodum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
