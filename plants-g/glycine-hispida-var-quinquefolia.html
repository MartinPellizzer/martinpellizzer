Glycine hispida var. quinquefolia

Glycine hispida var. quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaved cowpea, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
