Gentiana scabra f. alborosea

Gentiana scabra f. alborosea, commonly known as the white gentian, is a variant of the gentian plant characterized by its striking white flowers.

This perennial herb is native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including parts of China, Korea, and Japan. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The white gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
