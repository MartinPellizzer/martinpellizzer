Gladiolus callistus var. gracilior

Gladiolus callistus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear bright, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
