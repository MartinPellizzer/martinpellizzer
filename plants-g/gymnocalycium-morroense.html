Gymnocalycium morroense

Gymnocalycium morroense, commonly known as the Morroense prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Gymnocalycium morroense is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh desert conditions.
