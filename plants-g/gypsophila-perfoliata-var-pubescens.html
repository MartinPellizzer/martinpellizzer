Gypsophila perfoliata var. pubescens

Gypsophila perfoliata var. pubescens, commonly known as the silvery gypsophila, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing addition to the landscape.
