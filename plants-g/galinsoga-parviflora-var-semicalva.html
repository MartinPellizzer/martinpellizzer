Galinsoga parviflora var. semicalva

Galinsoga parviflora var. semicalva, commonly known as the common galinsoga, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, hairy leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an invasive species in many regions.
