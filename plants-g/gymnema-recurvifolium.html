Gymnema recurvifolium

Gymnema recurvifolium, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. Gymnema recurvifolium is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential anti-diabetic and antioxidant effects.
