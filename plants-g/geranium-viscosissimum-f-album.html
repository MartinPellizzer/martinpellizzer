Geranium viscosissimum f. album

Geranium viscosissimum f. album, commonly known as the white geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
