Gentianella alborosea

Gentianella alborosea, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with five petals that are usually blue or white in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
