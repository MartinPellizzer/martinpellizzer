Gustavia augusta var. brasiliensis

Gustavia augusta var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
