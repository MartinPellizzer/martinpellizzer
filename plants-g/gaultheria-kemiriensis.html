Gaultheria kemiriensis

Gaultheria kemiriensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils makes it a notable species in its native habitat.

Gaultheria kemiriensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
