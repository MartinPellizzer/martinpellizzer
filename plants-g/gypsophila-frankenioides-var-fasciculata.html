Gypsophila frankenioides var. fasciculata

Gypsophila frankenioides var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences on slender stems, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
