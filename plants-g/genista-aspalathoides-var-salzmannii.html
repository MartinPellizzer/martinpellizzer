Genista aspalathoides var. salzmannii

Genista aspalathoides var. salzmannii, commonly known as the Cape goldball, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
