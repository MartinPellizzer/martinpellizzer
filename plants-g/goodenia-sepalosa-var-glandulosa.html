Goodenia sepalosa var. glandulosa

Goodenia sepalosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, glandular structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to be glandular and sometimes hairy.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
