Galatella desertorum

Galatella desertorum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal water. It is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Galatella desertorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
