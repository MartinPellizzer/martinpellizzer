Gomesa salesopolitana

Gomesa salesopolitana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that bear vibrant flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, producing colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are modified into phylloids, which help reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Gomesa salesopolitana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
