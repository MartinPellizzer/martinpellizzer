Galega fruticosa

Galega fruticosa, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, woody stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are cylindrical pods that split open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Galega fruticosa is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
