Geum heterophyllum var. elongatum

Geum heterophyllum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated wood avens, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinct basal rosette and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. Its flowering stems are often taller than the leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
