Genista cretica

Genista cretica, commonly known as the Cretan broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has slender, branched stems and small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water.

Genista cretica is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
