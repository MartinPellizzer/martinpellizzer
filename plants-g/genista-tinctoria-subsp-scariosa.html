Genista tinctoria subsp. scariosa

Genista tinctoria subsp.

scariosa, commonly known as the scarious broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish or grayish bloom, giving them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

The subspecies is notable for its historical use in dyeing fabrics and its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
