Galactia parvifolia

Galactia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems and leaves exude a milky sap, which is a common feature in the milkweed genus.

Galactia parvifolia thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
