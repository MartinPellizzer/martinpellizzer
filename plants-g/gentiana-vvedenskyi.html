Gentiana vvedenskyi

Gentiana vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
