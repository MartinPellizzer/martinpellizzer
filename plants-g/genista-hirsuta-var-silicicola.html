Genista hirsuta var. silicicola

Genista hirsuta var. silicicola, commonly known as the silicicola broom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a low-growing, shrub-like structure. Its small, yellow flowers are typically clustered in racemes, and the leaves are covered in fine, hairy structures.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in stabilizing sandy or gravelly landscapes.
