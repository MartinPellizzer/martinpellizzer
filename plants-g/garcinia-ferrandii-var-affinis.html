Garcinia ferrandii var. affinis

Garcinia ferrandii var. affinis, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's outer layer is thin and edible, while the inner pulp contains the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
