Gleichenia semivestita var. hecistophylla

Gleichenia semivestita var. hecistophylla, commonly known as the Hecistophylla variety of the Gleichenia semivestita, is a fern species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
