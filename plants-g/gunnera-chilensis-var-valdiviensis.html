Gunnera chilensis var. valdiviensis

Gunnera chilensis var. valdiviensis, commonly known as the Valdivian giant spleenwort, is a striking perennial plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often lobed basal leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens with suitable conditions.
