Gladiolus pygmaeus

Gladiolus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, colorful flowers. The plant typically produces spikes of showy, funnel-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
