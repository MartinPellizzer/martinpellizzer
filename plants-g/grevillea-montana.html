Grevillea montana

Grevillea montana, commonly known as the mountain grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Grevillea montana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
