Gastrochilus curtisii

Gastrochilus curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a specialized structure used to trap pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Gastrochilus curtisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
