Geranium incanum subsp. nyassense

Geranium incanum subsp.

nyassense, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
