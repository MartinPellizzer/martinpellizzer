Gypsophila campestris

Gypsophila campestris, commonly known as sweet pea flower or garden gypsophila, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, white or pinkish flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila campestris is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
