Gumira resinosa

Gumira resinosa, commonly known as the resinous gum tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk that exudes a sticky, aromatic resin. The plant's leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface. Gumira resinosa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its resin has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
