Geranium armenum

Geranium armenum, commonly known as Armenian cranesbill, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Geranium armenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
