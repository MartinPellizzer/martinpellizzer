Gamocarpha rosulata var. columnaris

Gamocarpha rosulata var. columnaris, commonly known as the columnar rose-garland.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive columnar growth habit, which sets it apart from the more spreading form of the species. It features dense, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to specific regions of South America and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its unique structure and floral display make it a desirable ornamental plant for gardens and landscaping.
