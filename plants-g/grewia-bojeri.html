Grewia bojeri

Grewia bojeri, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum, is a small shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. Grewia bojeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
