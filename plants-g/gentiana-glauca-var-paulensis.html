Gentiana glauca var. paulensis

Gentiana glauca var. paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glaucous, bluish-white coating.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
