Gladiolus staudtii

Gladiolus staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. Its flowers are composed of three petals and three sepals, forming a distinctive, sword-like shape that resembles a blade, hence its common name.

Gladiolus staudtii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
