Gentiana dubia

Gentiana dubia, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Gentiana dubia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
