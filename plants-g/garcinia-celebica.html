Garcinia celebica

Garcinia celebica, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that are widely used in culinary applications. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its aromatic properties. Its fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid and essential oils, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Garcinia celebica thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
