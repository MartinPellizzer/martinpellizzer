Galium hystricocarpum

Galium hystricocarpum, commonly known as the spiny chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically features small, opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny white flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that contain three seeds each. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing food for various insects and wildlife.
