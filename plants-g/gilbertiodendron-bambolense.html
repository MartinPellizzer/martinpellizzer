Gilbertiodendron bambolense

Gilbertiodendron bambolense, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It is valued for its hard, durable wood used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, it plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
