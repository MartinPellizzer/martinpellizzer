Gustavia longepetiolata

Gustavia longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features large, showy flowers with five petals that are typically pink to red in color. The leaves are broad, oval, and have long petioles, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
