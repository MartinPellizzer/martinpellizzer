Grusonia californica

Grusonia californica, commonly known as the California grusonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

Grusonia californica is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
