Gymnosporia crassifolia

Gymnosporia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved burr plant, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and distinctive burrs that enclose its seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its unique, spiny seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.

Gymnosporia crassifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
