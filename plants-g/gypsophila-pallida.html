Gypsophila pallida

Gypsophila pallida, commonly known as the baby's breath or ice plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a fluffy appearance. The plant is typically low-growing, with thin, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Gypsophila pallida is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in floral arrangements.
