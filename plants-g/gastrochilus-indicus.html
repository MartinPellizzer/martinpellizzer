Gastrochilus indicus

Gastrochilus indicus, commonly known as the Indian stomach orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Gastrochilus indicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
