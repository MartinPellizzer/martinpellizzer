Gilia capitata subsp. tomentosa

Gilia capitata subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more hairy stems and leaves compared to other forms of Gilia capitata.
