Galium umbrosum var. muriculatum

Galium umbrosum var. muriculatum, commonly known as the rough wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are white or pale pink, typically appearing in clusters. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish or purplish and become woody with age.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
