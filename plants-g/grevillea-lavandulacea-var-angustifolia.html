Grevillea lavandulacea var. angustifolia

Grevillea lavandulacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-shaped inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
