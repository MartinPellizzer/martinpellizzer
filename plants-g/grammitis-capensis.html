Grammitis capensis

Grammitis capensis, commonly known as the Cape grammitis, is a fern native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rock faces and forest undergrowth. Its small, spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its leaves.

Grammitis capensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
