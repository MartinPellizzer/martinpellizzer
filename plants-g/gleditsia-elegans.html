Gleditsia elegans

Gleditsia elegans, commonly known as the honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall, and its long, slender pods that contain sweet, edible seeds. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Gleditsia elegans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
