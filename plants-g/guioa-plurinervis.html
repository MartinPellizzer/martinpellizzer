Guioa plurinervis

Guioa plurinervis, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnately arranged, with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing one or two seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and is also used in traditional medicine.
