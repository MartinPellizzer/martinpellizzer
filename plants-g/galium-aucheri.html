Galium aucheri

Galium aucheri, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems that form a dense mat, often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium aucheri thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
