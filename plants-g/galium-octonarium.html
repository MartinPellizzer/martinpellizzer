Galium octonarium

Galium octonarium, commonly known as eight-lobed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, with a distinctive lobed shape. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galium octonarium is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
