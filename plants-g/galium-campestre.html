Galium campestre

Galium campestre, commonly known as bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium campestre is also noted for its fragrant foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
