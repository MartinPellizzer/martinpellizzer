Gerardia gnidioides

Gerardia gnidioides, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, yellowish-green leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered near the base of the stems. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Gerardia gnidioides is well-suited to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
