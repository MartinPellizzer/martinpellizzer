Glebionis segetum

Glebionis segetum, commonly known as field dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in early spring.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
