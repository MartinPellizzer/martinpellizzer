Gymnogramma stegnogramme

Gymnogramma stegnogramme, commonly known as the creeping shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive shield-like appearance on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for ground cover in shady gardens.
