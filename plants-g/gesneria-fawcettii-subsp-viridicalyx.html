Gesneria fawcettii subsp. viridicalyx

Gesneria fawcettii subsp.

viridicalyx, commonly known as the Emerald-leaved Gesneria, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Madagascar. It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a strikingly glossy surface and a subtle bluish sheen.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care, making it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
