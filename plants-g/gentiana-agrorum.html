Gentiana agrorum

Gentiana agrorum, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are unbranched and often hairy.

Gentiana agrorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
