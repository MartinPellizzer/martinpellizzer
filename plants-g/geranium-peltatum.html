Geranium peltatum

Geranium peltatum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its stems are often hairy. Geranium peltatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
