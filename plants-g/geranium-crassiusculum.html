Geranium crassiusculum

Geranium crassiusculum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Geranium crassiusculum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers contribute to its value in both garden and ecological settings.
