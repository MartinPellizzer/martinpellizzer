Goeppertia silvicola subsp. silvicola

Goeppertia silvicola subsp.

silvicola, commonly known as the Amazonian calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America. It is characterized by its large, deeply veined, elliptical leaves that display a rich array of color patterns, often in shades of green, silver, and red.

The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming a dense foliage mat.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
