Geanthus polyanthus

Geanthus polyanthus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a similar pubescence.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
