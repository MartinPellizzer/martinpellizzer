Galega sinapou

Galega sinapou, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
