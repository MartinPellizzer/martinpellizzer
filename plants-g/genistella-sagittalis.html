Genistella sagittalis

Genistella sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowleaf genistella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often accompanied by stipules. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
