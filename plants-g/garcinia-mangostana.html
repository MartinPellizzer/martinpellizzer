Garcinia mangostana

Garcinia mangostana, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, waxy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces striking red or purple fruits, each containing several segments filled with a white, segmented flesh. The botanical features of Garcinia mangostana include its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or yellow.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and the edible fruit it produces.
