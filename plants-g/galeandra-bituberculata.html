Galeandra bituberculata

Galeandra bituberculata, commonly known as the two-ribbed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a prominent lip, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
