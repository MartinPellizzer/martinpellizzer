Gmelina montana

Gmelina montana, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and smooth, grayish-white bark. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Gmelina montana is often found in lowland evergreen forests and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental qualities.
