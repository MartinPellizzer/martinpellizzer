Gymnocalycium borthii

Gymnocalycium borthii, commonly known as the Borth's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular or cylindrical shape. The plant features a fleshy, segmented stem covered in spiny areoles, which are typical of cacti. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in spring, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Gymnocalycium borthii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
