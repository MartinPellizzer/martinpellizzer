Gloriosa leopoldii

Gloriosa leopoldii, commonly known as the leopard lily, is a striking climbing plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Colocasieae and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces long, wiry stems that can climb up to several meters in height. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their bold, showy appearance.

However, it is considered toxic and should be handled with care.
