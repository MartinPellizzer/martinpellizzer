Glycosmis cymosa var. linearifoliola

Glycosmis cymosa var. linearifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetleaf, is a variant of the sweetleaf plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and botany due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
