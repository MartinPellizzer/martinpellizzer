Gagea vanensis

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Gagea vanensis is often found in open woodlands and meadows, where it contributes to the floral diversity of its habitat.
