Gastrochilus longifolius

Gastrochilus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and horticulturists.
