Gymnocladus burmanicus

Gymnocladus burmanicus, commonly known as the Burman's locust tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated pods. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, and it produces small, hard seeds enclosed in woody pods.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
