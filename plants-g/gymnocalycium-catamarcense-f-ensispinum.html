Gymnocalycium catamarcense f. ensispinum

Gymnocalycium catamarcense f. ensispinum, commonly known as the Catamarca spinecactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
