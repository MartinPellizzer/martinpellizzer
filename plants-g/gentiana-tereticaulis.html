Gentiana tereticaulis

Gentiana tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often slightly hairy.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
