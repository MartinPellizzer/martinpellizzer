Gaertnera parvipaniculata

Gaertnera parvipaniculata, commonly known as the small panicle hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences known as panicles, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gaertnera parvipaniculata is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
