Goniopteris meniscioides

Goniopteris meniscioides, commonly known as the Meniscus Fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds and reproductive structures.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves with a unique, undulating pattern along the midrib. Its sori, which contain the spores, are arranged in a linear fashion along the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
