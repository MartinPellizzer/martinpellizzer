Gilia gilioides subsp. volcanica

Gilia gilioides subsp.

volcanica, commonly known as the volcanic gilia, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or volcanic soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
