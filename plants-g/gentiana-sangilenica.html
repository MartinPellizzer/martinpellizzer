Gentiana sangilenica

Gentiana sangilenica, commonly known as the Sangilien gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
