Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. sprengelii

Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. sprengelii, commonly known as the yellow cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant features fine, hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
