Garcinia pedicellata

Garcinia pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically orange to red in color, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp. Garcinia pedicellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Garcinia.
