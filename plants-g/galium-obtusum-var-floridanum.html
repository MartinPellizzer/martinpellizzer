Galium obtusum var. floridanum

Galium obtusum var. floridanum, commonly known as Florida bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Florida bedstraw is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
