Galatella polygaloides

Galatella polygaloides, commonly known as the golden thread, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Galatella polygaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle, elegant blooms.
