Grevillea Thelemanniana Subsp. Obtusifolia

Obtuse-leaved grevillea, scientifically known as Grevillea thelemanniana subsp. obtusifolia, is a rare and striking shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. Its unique foliage, characterized by broad, rounded leaves, sets it apart from other members of the Grevillea genus. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens. With its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, it adds both color and ecological value to the landscape. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively obscure species due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements.
