Grindelia collina

Grindelia collina, commonly known as brittleblow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread.

Grindelia collina is also known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
