Glechoma persica

Glechoma persica, commonly known as Persian ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It is commonly found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Glechoma persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
