Gastoniella novogaliciana

Gastoniella novogaliciana, commonly known as the Croatian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple throat. Its leaves are oval and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Gastoniella novogaliciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
