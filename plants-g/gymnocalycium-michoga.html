Gymnocalycium michoga

Gymnocalycium michoga, commonly known as the Michoacan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its barrel-shaped stem and spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Gymnocalycium michoga is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
