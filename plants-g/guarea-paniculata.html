Guarea paniculata

Guarea paniculata, commonly known as the red sanders or yellow sanders, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, Guarea paniculata is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
