Gentiana ettingshausenii

Gentiana ettingshausenii, commonly known as the Ettingshausen gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
