Gypsophila pallidifolia

Gypsophila pallidifolia, commonly known as the pale pink baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use. It is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and airy appearance.

Gypsophila pallidifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
