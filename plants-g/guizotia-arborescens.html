Guizotia arborescens

Guizotia arborescens, commonly known as the tree cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright, branching habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
