Galvezia sessilis

Galvezia sessilis, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red bract displays that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Galvezia sessilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant and long-lasting display.
