Geissorhiza longituba

Geissorhiza longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed geissorhiza, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, supporting its growth during dormant periods. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.
