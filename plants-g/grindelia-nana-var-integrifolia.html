Grindelia nana var. integrifolia

Grindelia nana var. integrifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, grayish-green leaves that are typically narrow and have entire margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration efforts.
