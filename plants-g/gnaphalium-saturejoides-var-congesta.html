Gnaphalium saturejoides var. congesta

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered leaf everlasting, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.
