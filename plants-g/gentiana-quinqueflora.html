Gentiana quinqueflora

Gentiana quinqueflora, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark blue berries that contain the seeds.

Gentiana quinqueflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
