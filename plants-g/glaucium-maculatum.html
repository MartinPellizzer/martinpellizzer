Glaucium maculatum

Glaucium maculatum, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often changing with the weather. The plant has a distinctive yellowish-green stem with purple spots, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Glaucium maculatum is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period, typically blooming from spring to early summer.
