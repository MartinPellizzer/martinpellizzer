Gnaphalium purpureum var. stachidifolium

Gnaphalium purpureum var. stachidifolium, commonly known as purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in disturbed areas and a subject of study in ecological research.
