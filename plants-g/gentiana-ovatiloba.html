Gentiana ovatiloba

Gentiana ovatiloba, commonly known as the oval-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are ovate and often have a waxy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentiana ovatiloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
