Goniopteris leptocladia

Goniopteris leptocladia, commonly known as the slender-branch fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a slender and graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Goniopteris leptocladia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
