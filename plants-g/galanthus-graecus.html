Galanthus graecus

Galanthus graecus, commonly known as the Greek snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding white flowers with a greenish-yellow spathe, typically blooming in early spring. The plant grows from a bulb and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Galanthus graecus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
