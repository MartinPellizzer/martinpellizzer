Gastrochilus maculatus

Gastrochilus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

Botanically, it is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
