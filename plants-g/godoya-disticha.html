Godoya disticha

Godoya disticha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become an invasive species in some regions.
