Gentianella hookeri

Gentianella hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Gentianella hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
