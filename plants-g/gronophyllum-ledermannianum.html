Gronophyllum ledermannianum

Gronophyllum ledermannianum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
