Gunnera tinctoria

Gunnera tinctoria, commonly known as the Brazilian giant water flea, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near rivers and lakes. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps retain water in its natural habitat.

The plant is also notable for its ability to produce a red dye from its leaves, which has been historically used by indigenous communities.
