Galium boreale var. hyssopifolium

Galium boreale var. hyssopifolium, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open, sun-exposed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
