Glyceria leptophylla

Glyceria leptophylla, commonly known as slenderleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its morphology makes it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
