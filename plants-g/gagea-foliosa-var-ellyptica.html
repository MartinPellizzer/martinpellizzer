Gagea foliosa var. ellyptica

Gagea foliosa var. ellyptica, commonly known as the elliptic leaf gagea, is a rare variant of the gagea plant native to specific regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats.
