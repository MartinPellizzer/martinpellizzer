Gentianella ciliata

Gentianella ciliata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually opposite and have finely toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentianella ciliata is known for its short blooming period, typically in late summer.
