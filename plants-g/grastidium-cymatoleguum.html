Grastidium cymatoleguum

Grastidium cymatoleguum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Grastidium cymatoleguum thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
