Grevillea floribunda subsp. floribunda

Grevillea floribunda subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the Coastal Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces bright red or orange floral spikes that are clustered at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Grevillea floribunda subsp.

floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
