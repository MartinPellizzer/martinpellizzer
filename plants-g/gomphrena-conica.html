Gomphrena conica

Gomphrena conica, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical flower heads, which are often brightly colored in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, spiky bracts that surround the central flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Gomphrena conica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
