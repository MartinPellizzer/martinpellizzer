Garcinia multifida

Garcinia multifida, commonly known as the African star fruit or the horned star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that give it a star-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically orange or red in color. The fruit contains a single seed and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia multifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many African communities.
