Gymnocalycium monvillei var. steineri

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. steineri, commonly known as the Steiner's moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant features a distinctive crown of spines and produces yellow flowers during the growing season. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its stem for survival in arid environments.

It is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
