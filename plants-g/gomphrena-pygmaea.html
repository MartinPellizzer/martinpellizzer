Gomphrena pygmaea

Gomphrena pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf globemallow, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces distinctive, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gomphrena pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
