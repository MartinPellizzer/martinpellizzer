Gymnocalycium monvillei subsp. monvillei

Gymnocalycium monvillei subsp.

monvillei, commonly known as the Monville's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically ephemeral, appearing only after rainfall and withering quickly. Gymnocalycium monvillei subsp.

monvillei is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
