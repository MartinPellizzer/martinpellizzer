Gramen lycurum

Gramen lycurum, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicle clusters. Gramen lycurum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
