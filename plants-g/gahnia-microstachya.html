Gahnia microstachya

Gahnia microstachya, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological systems in its native range.
