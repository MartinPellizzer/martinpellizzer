Gustavia hexapetala

Gustavia hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gustavia hexapetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
