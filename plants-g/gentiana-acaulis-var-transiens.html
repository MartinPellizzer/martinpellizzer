Gentiana acaulis var. transiens

Gentiana acaulis var. transiens, commonly known as the Transylvanian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
