Gymnocoronis subcordata

Gymnocoronis subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Gymnocoronis subcordata is a hardy perennial that is both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
