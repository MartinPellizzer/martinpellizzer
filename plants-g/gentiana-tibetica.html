Gentiana tibetica

Gentiana tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana tibetica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
