Gleichenia ciliata

Gleichenia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. Gleichenia ciliata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
