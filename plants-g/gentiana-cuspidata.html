Gentiana cuspidata

Gentiana cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Gentiana cuspidata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
