Gaultheria wardii var. elongata

Gaultheria wardii var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are leathery and aromatic, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
