Gilia aggregata var. attenuata

Gilia aggregata var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
