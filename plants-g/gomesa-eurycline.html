Gomesa eurycline

Gomesa eurycline, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are oval in shape and have a glossy appearance. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Gomesa eurycline is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
