Gomphrena crassicaulis

Gomphrena crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, and its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like. Gomphrena crassicaulis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its long-lasting blooms, which are often used in floral arrangements.
