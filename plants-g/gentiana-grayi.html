Gentiana grayi

Gentiana grayi, commonly known as the Gray's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often unbranched and hairy.

Gentiana grayi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
