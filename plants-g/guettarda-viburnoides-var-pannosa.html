Guettarda viburnoides var. pannosa

Guettarda viburnoides var. pannosa, commonly known as the Florida holly or swamp holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and wetland ecosystems.
