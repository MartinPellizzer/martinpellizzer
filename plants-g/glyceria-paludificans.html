Glyceria paludificans

Glyceria paludificans, commonly known as the marsh glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually green or reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Glyceria paludificans plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
