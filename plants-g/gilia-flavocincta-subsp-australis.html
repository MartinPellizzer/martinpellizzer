Gilia flavocincta subsp. australis

Gilia flavocincta subsp.

australis, commonly known as the yellow-belted gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange.

The species belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, adapting to the heat of the day.
