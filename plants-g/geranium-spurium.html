Geranium spurium

Geranium spurium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Geranium spurium is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
