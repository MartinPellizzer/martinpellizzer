Gentiana campanulata

Gentiana campanulata, commonly known as the bell gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana campanulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
