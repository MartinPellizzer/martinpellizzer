Gentiana pneumonanthe var. minor

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. minor, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
