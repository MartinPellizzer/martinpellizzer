Grevillea maxwellii

Grevillea maxwellii, commonly known as the Maxwell's grass tree, is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It features long, grass-like leaves that grow in dense tufts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.

Grevillea maxwellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native landscapes.
