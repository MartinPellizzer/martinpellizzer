Grindelia oxylepis f. capitellata

Grindelia oxylepis f. capitellata, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert gumplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical, spiny seed heads.

This variety is distinguished by its compact, clustered growth habit and is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush habitats.
