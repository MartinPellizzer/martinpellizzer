Glycosmis rigida

Glycosmis rigida, commonly known as the rigid glycosmis, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-colored fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. Glycosmis rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and seasonal flowering.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
