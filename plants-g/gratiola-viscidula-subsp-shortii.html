Gratiola viscidula subsp. shortii

Gratiola viscidula subsp.

shortii, commonly known as the sticky false bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, upright stems covered in glandular hairs that produce a sticky secretion.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in loose clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.
