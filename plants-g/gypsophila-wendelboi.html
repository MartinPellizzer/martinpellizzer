Gypsophila wendelboi

Gypsophila wendelboi, commonly known as the Wendelbo's baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its fluffy appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Gypsophila wendelboi is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
