Glossocarya puberula

Glossocarya puberula, commonly known as the hairy glossocarya, is a small shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by clusters of small, woody fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Glossocarya puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
