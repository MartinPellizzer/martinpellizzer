Gnaphalium luteoalbum f. makranicum

Gnaphalium luteoalbum f. makranicum, commonly known as the Makran mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dry gardens and rockeries.
