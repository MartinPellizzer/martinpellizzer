Gunnera cuatrecasasii

Gunnera cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Andean giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, broad, and often deeply lobed leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Gunneraceae and is known for its unique, shield-like foliage that provides habitat for various small organisms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its dramatic appearance, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and conservatories.
