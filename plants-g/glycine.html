Glycine

Glycine, commonly known as sweet pea, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, compound leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. Glycine species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also valued for their nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Glycine is also known for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
