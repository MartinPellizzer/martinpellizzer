Gilia multiflora var. glabristyla

Gilia multiflora var. glabristyla, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
