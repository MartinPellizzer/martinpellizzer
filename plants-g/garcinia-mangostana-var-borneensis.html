Garcinia mangostana var. borneensis

Garcinia mangostana var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, segmented fruits that are rich in xanthones and other bioactive compounds. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and glossy, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles. The fruit's pericarp, or outer layer, is thick and has a distinctive aromatic flavor.

This variety is particularly valued for its medicinal properties and is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
