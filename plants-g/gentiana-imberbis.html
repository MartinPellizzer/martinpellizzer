Gentiana imberbis

Gentiana imberbis, commonly known as the naked gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

The flowers are tubular and lack petals, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.
