Gordonia lanceifolia

Gordonia lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its bark is thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
