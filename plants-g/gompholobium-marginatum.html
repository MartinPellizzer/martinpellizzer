Gompholobium marginatum

Gompholobium marginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a long flowering period, usually from late spring to early autumn, and is adapted to tolerate drought conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
