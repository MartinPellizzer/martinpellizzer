Gentiana bosnjakii

Gentiana bosnjakii, commonly known as the Bosnian gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its vibrant blue and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing showy, five-petaled structures that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
