Gonolobus friesii

Gonolobus friesii, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces striking blue-violet flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gonolobus friesii is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
