Garcinia australis

Garcinia australis, commonly known as the Australian garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia australis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential medicinal research.
