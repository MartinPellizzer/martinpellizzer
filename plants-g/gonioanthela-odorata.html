Gonioanthela odorata

Gonioanthela odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, while its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, colorful flowers.

The fragrance of the blooms is particularly noticeable in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.
