Gentiana falconeri

Gentiana falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Gentiana falconeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
