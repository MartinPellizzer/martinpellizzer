Gentiana pubigera subsp. pubiflora

Gentiana pubigera subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the pubiflora gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
