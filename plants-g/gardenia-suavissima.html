Gardenia suavissima

Gardenia suavissima, commonly known as the sweet gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces waxy, white blooms that emit a sweet, citrus-like scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-drained soil.

The fruit of the plant is a bright red, fleshy berry containing several seeds.
