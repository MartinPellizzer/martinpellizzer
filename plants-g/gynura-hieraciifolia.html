Gynura hieraciifolia

Gynura hieraciifolia, commonly known as the silver ragwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Gynura hieraciifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
