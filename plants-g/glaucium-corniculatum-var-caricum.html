Glaucium corniculatum var. caricum

Glaucium corniculatum var. caricum, commonly known as the yellow hornpoppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow, papery flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a bluish-green hue due to a powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
