Galeola kerrii

Galeola kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's ghost orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, Galeola kerrii is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
