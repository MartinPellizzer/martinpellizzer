Garcinia barkeriana

Garcinia barkeriana, commonly known as the African garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into bright orange or red berries. The plant is part of the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties. Its fruits contain bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid, which have been studied for potential health benefits.

Garcinia barkeriana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
