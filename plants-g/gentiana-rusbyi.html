Gentiana rusbyi

Gentiana rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
