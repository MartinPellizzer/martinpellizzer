Gastrolobium drummondii

Gastrolobium drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

Gastrolobium drummondii is adapted to arid environments and is well-suited to sandy or gravelly soils.
