Grammatophyllum pantherinum

Grammatophyllum pantherinum, commonly known as the panther orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces fragrant, often brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clusters in suitable conditions.
