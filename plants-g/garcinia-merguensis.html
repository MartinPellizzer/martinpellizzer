Garcinia merguensis

Garcinia merguensis, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a bright red, segmented berry that contains a single seed and is often consumed for its tart flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is closely related to other Garcinia species.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its potential medicinal properties.
