Gnidia fischeri

Gnidia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its hard, dense wood.

Gnidia fischeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
