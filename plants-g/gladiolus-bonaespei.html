Gladiolus bonaespei

Gladiolus bonaespei, commonly known as the Bona Esperanza gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a range of climates, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
