Gentianella muelleriana subsp. willisiana

Gentianella muelleriana subsp.

willisiana, commonly known as the Willis's gentian, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
