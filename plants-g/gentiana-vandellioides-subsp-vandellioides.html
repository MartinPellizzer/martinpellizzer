Gentiana vandellioides subsp. vandellioides

Gentiana vandellioides subsp.

vandellioides, commonly known as the Vandelli's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
