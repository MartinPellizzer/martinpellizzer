Graphephorum Hirtulum

Graphephorum hirtulum vine, scientifically known as Graphephorum hirtulum, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Araliaceae family. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this climbing plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique foliage features waxy, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture that distinguishes it from other vines. While not widely cultivated, it has gained attention among plant enthusiasts for its striking appearance and ecological significance. Further study is needed to fully understand its role in its native ecosystems.
