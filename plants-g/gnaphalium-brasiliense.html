Gnaphalium brasiliense

Gnaphalium brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy flowers that are often mistaken for seeds, a feature that aids in wind pollination. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
