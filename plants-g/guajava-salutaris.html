Guajava salutaris

Guajava salutaris, commonly known as the sacred guava, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a shallow root system adapted to coastal environments.

The leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their high concentration of antioxidants and essential oils.
