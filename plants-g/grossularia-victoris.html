Grossularia victoris

Grossularia victoris, commonly known as the Himalayan currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its berries, which are edible, are dark red or black and contain a single seed.

Grossularia victoris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
