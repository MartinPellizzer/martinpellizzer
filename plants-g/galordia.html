Galordia

Galordia, commonly known as the flame violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. They are characterized by their showy, brightly colored flowers that often resemble orchids in structure. Galordia species typically have long, slender stems and are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic.

Their flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant hues and unique appearance.
