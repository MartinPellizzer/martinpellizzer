Galeopsis tetrahit var. boraei

Galeopsis tetrahit var. boraei, commonly known as the Arctic hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Galeopsis tetrahit var. boraei plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an indicator species for alpine ecosystems.
