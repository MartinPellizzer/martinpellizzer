Garrya wrightii

Garrya wrightii, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Garrya wrightii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
