Githopsis specularioides subsp. candida

Githopsis specularioides subsp.

candida, commonly known as the white-flowered githopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and compact growth habit.
