Grammitis impressa

Grammitis impressa, commonly known as the impressed fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The leaves are usually divided into small, triangular segments, giving the plant a finely textured appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks.

Grammitis impressa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
