Glyceria distans var. pseudodistans

Glyceria distans var. pseudodistans, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swales, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland health and ecological stability.
