Goniochilus

Goniochilus, commonly known as the "bearded orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

They are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant flowers and their distinctive, hairy or bearded structures on the petals and sepals. Goniochilus species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, thriving on trees or rocks in humid environments.
