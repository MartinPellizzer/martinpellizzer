Genista hirsuta subsp. algarbiensis

Genista hirsuta subsp.

algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarve shrub, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Portugal and Spain. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces yellow flowers in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats. Its small, leathery leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a hedge plant.
