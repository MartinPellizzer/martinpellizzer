Gladiolus decoratus

Gladiolus decoratus, commonly known as the ornamental gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

It is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens around the world.
