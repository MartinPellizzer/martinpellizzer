Gymnogramma mayi

Gymnogramma mayi, commonly known as the Mexican maidenhair fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce small, clustered clusters of leaves.

Gymnogramma mayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
