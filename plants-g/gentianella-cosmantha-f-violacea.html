Gentianella cosmantha f. violacea

Gentianella cosmantha f. violacea, commonly known as the violet gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
