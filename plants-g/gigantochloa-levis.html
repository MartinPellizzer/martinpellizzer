Gigantochloa levis

Gigantochloa levis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has large, narrow leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It is well-adapted to various soil types and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in construction and as a source of raw material in local communities.
