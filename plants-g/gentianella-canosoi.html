Gentianella canosoi

Gentianella canosoi, commonly known as the Canary Islands gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
