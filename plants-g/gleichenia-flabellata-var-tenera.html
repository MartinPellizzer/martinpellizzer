Gleichenia flabellata var. tenera

Gleichenia flabellata var. tenera, commonly known as the soft fronded maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Gleichenia flabellata species.

It is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae, characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
