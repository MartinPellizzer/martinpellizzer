Geranium selengense

Geranium selengense, commonly known as Selengian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink to red blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Geranium selengense is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
