Genistella tridentata var. cantabrica

Genistella tridentata var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by trifoliate leaves that give it its species name.

It is a hardy plant adapted to the mild, wet climate of the Cantabrian coast.
