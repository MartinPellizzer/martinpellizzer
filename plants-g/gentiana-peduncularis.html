Gentiana peduncularis

Gentiana peduncularis, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are known for their showy, tubular shape that attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees.
