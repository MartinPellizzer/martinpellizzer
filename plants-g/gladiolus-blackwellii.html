Gladiolus blackwellii

Gladiolus blackwellii, commonly known as the Blackwell's gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically produces spikes of large, lance-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Gladiolus blackwellii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
