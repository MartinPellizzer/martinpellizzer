Grindelia exilifolia

Grindelia exilifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to withstand arid conditions.

Grindelia exilifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
