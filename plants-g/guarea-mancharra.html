Guarea mancharra

Guarea mancharra, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
