Gentianella corymbifera

Gentianella corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in moist, rocky soils. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or purple, with five lobes that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
