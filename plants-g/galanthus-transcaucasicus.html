Galanthus transcaucasicus

Galanthus transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Transcaucasian snowdrop, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces delicate white flowers with a greenish hue, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained, moist soil in partial shade. It is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some areas.
