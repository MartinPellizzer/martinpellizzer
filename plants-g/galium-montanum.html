Galium montanum

Galium montanum, commonly known as mountain bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Galium montanum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
