Godetia caurina

Godetia caurina, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is an annual herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
