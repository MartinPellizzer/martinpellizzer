Galium orientale var. tenerum

Galium orientale var. tenerum, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has thin, wiry stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
