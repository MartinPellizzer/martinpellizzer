Gumira amplectens

Gumira amplectens, commonly known as the climbing gumira, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, flexible stems that allow it to climb and entangle other vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gumira amplectens is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
