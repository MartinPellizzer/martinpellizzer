Galphimia gracilis var. grandiflora

Galphimia gracilis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
