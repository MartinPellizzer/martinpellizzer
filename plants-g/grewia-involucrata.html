Grewia involucrata

Grewia involucrata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Grewia involucrata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
