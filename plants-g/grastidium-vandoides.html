Grastidium vandoides

Grastidium vandoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Grastidium vandoides typically grows in moist, sunny habitats like wetlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
