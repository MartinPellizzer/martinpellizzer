Geranium cinereum subsp. subargenteum

Geranium cinereum subsp.

subargenteum, commonly known as silverleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs that give it its distinctive appearance.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
