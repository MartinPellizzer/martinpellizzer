Gaillardia scabiosoides var. radiata

Gaillardia scabiosoides var. radiata, commonly known as the radiate gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features bright orange to red petals with a yellow center, and its foliage is typically gray-green and hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure includes a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is often used in native plant gardens.
