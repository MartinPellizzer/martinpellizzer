Gnetum domesticum

Gnetum domesticum, commonly known as the African pepper tree, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in catkins, and it bears unique, three-lobed seeds.

Gnetum domesticum is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
