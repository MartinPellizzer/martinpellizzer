Gladiolus purpureoauratus

Gladiolus purpureoauratus, commonly known as the purple and gold gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers display a vibrant combination of purple and gold hues, with a distinctive golden throat that adds to its visual appeal. This species typically blooms in summer, producing striking inflorescences that are popular in gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a favored choice for landscapers and garden enthusiasts.
