Gonolobus pectinatus

Gonolobus pectinatus, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
