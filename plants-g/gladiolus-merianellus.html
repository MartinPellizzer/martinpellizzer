Gladiolus merianellus

Gladiolus merianellus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for both garden use and cut flower arrangements.
