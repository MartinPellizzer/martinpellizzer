Gymnanthes brasiliensis f. microphylla

Gymnanthes brasiliensis f. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature beauty leaf, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used in container gardening and as a specimen plant.
