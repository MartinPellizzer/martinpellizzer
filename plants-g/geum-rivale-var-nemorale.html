Geum rivale var. nemorale

Geum rivale var. nemorale, commonly known as the wood avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, thriving in partial to full shade. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-red flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It is often found along stream banks and in damp meadows, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
