Gordonia villosa

Gordonia villosa, commonly known as the woolly gordonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

Gordonia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
