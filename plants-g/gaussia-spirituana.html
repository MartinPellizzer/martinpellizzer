Gaussia spirituana

Gaussia spirituana, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant is noted for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.
