Grevillea brachyclada

Grevillea brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems and leaves. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Grevillea brachyclada is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
