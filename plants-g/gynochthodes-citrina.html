Gynochthodes citrina

Gynochthodes citrina, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
