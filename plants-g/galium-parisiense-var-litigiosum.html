Galium parisiense var. litigiosum

Galium parisiense var. litigiosum, commonly known as the sticky weed or common bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
