Gaudinia fragilis var. verticicola

Gaudinia fragilis var. verticicola, commonly known as the fragrant gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and prefers partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
