Gentianopsis crinita subsp. macounii

Gentianopsis crinita subsp.

macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many showy, blue or violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are characterized by their striking blue-violet color and five petals, with prominent yellow veins.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer, more slender leaves and slightly different flower morphology compared to the typical form of Gentianopsis crinita.
