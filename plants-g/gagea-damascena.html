Gagea damascena

Gagea damascena, commonly known as the Damascus spring beauty, is a delicate perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Gagea damascena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens and naturalized areas.
