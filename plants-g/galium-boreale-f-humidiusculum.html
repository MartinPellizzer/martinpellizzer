Galium boreale f. humidiusculum

Galium boreale f. humidiusculum, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It typically features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the short summer season. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in tundra and subarctic environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and intense sunlight.
