Gentiana × digenea

Gentiana × digenea, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana × digenea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
