Grevillea biternata

Grevillea biternata, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and salty air. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Grevillea biternata is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly native birds and insects.
