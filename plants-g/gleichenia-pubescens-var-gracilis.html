Gleichenia pubescens var. gracilis

Gleichenia pubescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairy maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance.

The sporangia are borne on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in the fern life cycle.
