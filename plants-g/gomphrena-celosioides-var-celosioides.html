Gomphrena celosioides var. celosioides

Gomphrena celosioides var. celosioides, commonly known as the Indian spunflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spiky, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
