Gilia tricolor subsp. tricolor

Gilia tricolor subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the three-color gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that typically display red, yellow, and blue hues in the same inflorescence.

The plant is an annual herb with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
