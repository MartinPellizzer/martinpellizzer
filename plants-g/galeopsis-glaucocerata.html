Galeopsis glaucocerata

Galeopsis glaucocerata, commonly known as blue galeopsis, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. Its small, blue or violet flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
