Galactia laxifolia

Galactia laxifolia, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Galactia laxifolia plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
