Galactia mississippiensis

Galactia mississippiensis, commonly known as Mississippi milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Galactia mississippiensis thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
