Grammitis cincta

Grammitis cincta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf liverwort, is a type of moss-like plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or on rocky surfaces. Grammitis cincta is a non-vascular plant, meaning it lacks true roots, stems, and leaves in the traditional sense.

Its reproductive structures, called gametophytes, are essential for its life cycle and are often found on the underside of its leaves.
