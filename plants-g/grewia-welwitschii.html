Grewia welwitschii

Grewia welwitschii, commonly known as the welwitschii grewia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and smooth, greyish bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh environments.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for birds and small mammals.
