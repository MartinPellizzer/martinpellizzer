Grammatophyllum speciosum

Grammatophyllum speciosum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a prominent, club-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence can reach up to 2 meters in length, bearing numerous fragrant, white to yellowish flowers.

Grammatophyllum speciosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
