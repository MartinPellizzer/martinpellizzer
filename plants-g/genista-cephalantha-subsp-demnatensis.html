Genista cephalantha subsp. demnatensis

Genista cephalantha subsp.

demnatensis, commonly known as the Demnat's broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
