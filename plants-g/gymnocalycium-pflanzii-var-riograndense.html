Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. riograndense

Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. riograndense, commonly known as the Rio Grande moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stem that resembles a fruit, often with a distinct, colorful bloom at the top. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
