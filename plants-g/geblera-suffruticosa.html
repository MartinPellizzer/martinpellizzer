Geblera suffruticosa

Geblera suffruticosa, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its woody stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of tubular, red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Geblera suffruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
