Galium subvelutinum subsp. hyrcanicum

Galium subvelutinum subsp.

hyrcanicum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. Small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
