Gomphrena humifusa

Gomphrena humifusa, commonly known as the dwarf plume flower, is a low-growing annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compact, bushy stems with small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red and purple. The foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with the plant producing fluffy, plume-like bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
