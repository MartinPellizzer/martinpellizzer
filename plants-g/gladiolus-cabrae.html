Gladiolus cabrae

Gladiolus cabrae, commonly known as the Cape gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with vibrant blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Gladiolus cabrae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
