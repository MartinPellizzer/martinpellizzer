Gonostemon × medius

Gonostemon × medius, commonly known as the hybrid banana plant, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Gonostemon alpinus and Gonostemon macrophyllus.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits that are typically yellow when mature. It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
