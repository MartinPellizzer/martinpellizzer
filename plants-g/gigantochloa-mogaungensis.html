Gigantochloa mogaungensis

Gigantochloa mogaungensis, commonly known as the Mogaung bamboo, is a tall grass native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar and Thailand.

It is characterized by its robust, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas near rivers or in forested regions.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is also valued for its use in construction and traditional crafts.
