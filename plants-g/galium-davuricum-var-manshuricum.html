Galium davuricum var. manshuricum

Galium davuricum var. manshuricum, commonly known as the Far Eastern bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
