Guioa patentinervis

Guioa patentinervis, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically pale green on the upper surface and lighter green on the underside. The leaves have a distinctive venation pattern with prominent, parallel veins that run along the length of the leaf blade. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
