Galium tunetanum var. lanigerum

Galium tunetanum var. lanigerum, commonly known as the Tunisian bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. The plant features slender stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, with small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achene.

This variety is noted for its dense growth habit and potential ecological value in stabilizing soils in arid regions.
