Gundelia vitekii

Gundelia vitekii, commonly known as the Armenian globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, silver-green foliage. The plant produces spherical, spiky flower heads that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in late summer. Gundelia vitekii is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

It has a long taproot and is known for its resilience to drought and cold temperatures.
