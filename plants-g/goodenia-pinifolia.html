Goodenia pinifolia

Goodenia pinifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Goodenia pinifolia is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
