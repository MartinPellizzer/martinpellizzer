Geranium minutum

Geranium minutum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, palmate leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in summer, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained conditions and full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
