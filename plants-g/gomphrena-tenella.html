Gomphrena tenella

Gomphrena tenella, commonly known as the silverleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically silver-gray in appearance, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
