Gnaphalium ignescens

Gnaphalium ignescens, commonly known as the burning bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that resembles a bush and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its common name derives from the way its leaves can appear to glow when exposed to sunlight.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
