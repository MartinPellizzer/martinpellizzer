Gaultheria puradyatmikae

Gaultheria puradyatmikae, commonly known as the Yunnan wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, aromatic flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
