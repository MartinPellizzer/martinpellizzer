Garcinia megistophylla

Garcinia megistophylla, commonly known as the giant Garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and modern phytochemistry.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
