Gentiana loerzingii

Gentiana loerzingii, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana loerzingii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
