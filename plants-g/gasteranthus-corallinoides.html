Gasteranthus corallinoides

Gasteranthus corallinoides, commonly known as the coral-like globose plant, is a striking member of the Rhamnaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces fleshy, egg-shaped fruits that are often mistaken for flowers, a feature that aids in its pollination strategy. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gasteranthus corallinoides thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its unique appearance in horticulture.
