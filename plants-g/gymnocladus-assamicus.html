Gymnocladus assamicus

Gymnocladus assamicus, commonly known as the Assam sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, bean-like pods containing seeds. It is often found in moist, fertile soils and can thrive in a variety of forest types.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
