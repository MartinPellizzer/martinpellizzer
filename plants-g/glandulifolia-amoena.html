Glandulifolia amoena

Glandulifolia amoena, commonly known as the fragrant leaf plant, is a species characterized by its aromatic foliage and distinctive glandular structures.

The leaves are typically broad and serrated, with a soft, velvety texture that enhances its visual appeal. This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are often small and clustered, contribute to its ornamental value.

Glandulifolia amoena is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
