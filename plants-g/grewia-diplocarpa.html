Grewia diplocarpa

Grewia diplocarpa, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that persist into winter. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant fruit and drought tolerance.

Grewia diplocarpa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
