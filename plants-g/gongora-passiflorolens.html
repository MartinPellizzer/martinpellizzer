Gongora passiflorolens

Gongora passiflorolens, commonly known as the Passionflower Vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble a crown, with five prominent petals and a distinctive reproductive structure called a syngenesia. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gongora passiflorolens is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
