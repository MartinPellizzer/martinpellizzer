Gunnera chilensis

Gunnera chilensis, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often lobed leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a terminal panicle during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with cool climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens with suitable conditions.
