Guillenia axillare var. dasycarpum

Guillenia axillare var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited guillenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually with white or pale pink petals.

The fruit is a dry, hairy capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.
