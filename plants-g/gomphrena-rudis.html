Gomphrena rudis

Gomphrena rudis, commonly known as the red gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy, spiky flower heads. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and are borne in dense clusters, giving the plant a distinctive, ornamental appearance. Gomphrena rudis is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall textured look.
