Glaux spicata

Glaux spicata, commonly known as the spiked glaux, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Glaux spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
