Gymnema macrocarpum

Gymnema macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited gymnema, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Gymnema macrocarpum is often found in dense forests and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
