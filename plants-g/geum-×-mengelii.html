Geum × mengelii

Geum × mengelii, commonly known as the hybrid garden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture that distinguishes them from other geum species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
