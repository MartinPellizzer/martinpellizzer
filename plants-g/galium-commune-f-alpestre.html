Galium commune f. alpestre

Galium commune f. alpestre, commonly known as alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
