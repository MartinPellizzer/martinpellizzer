Gentiana verna subsp. elongata

Gentiana verna subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine down.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer floral tube compared to the typical G. verna.
