Guilandina intermedia

Guilandina intermedia, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces large, showy, violet to purple flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has compound leaves with several oval-shaped leaflets and bears pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Guilandina intermedia make it both a decorative and potentially useful plant in horticulture.
