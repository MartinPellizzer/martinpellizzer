Gironniera curranii

Gironniera curranii, commonly known as the Curran's ironbark, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species in its native range.
