Gladiolus debilis

Gladiolus debilis, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, sword-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.
