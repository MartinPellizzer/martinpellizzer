Gentiana amarella subsp. acuta

Gentiana amarella subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the acute gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
