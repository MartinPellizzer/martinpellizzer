Gentiana polymorpha subsp. praecox

Gentiana polymorpha subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
