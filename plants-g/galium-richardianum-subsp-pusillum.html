Galium richardianum subsp. pusillum

Galium richardianum subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as small-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America. It typically features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
