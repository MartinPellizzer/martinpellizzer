Grastidium truncicola

Grastidium truncicola, commonly known as the slender bristlegrass, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or pale yellow, arranged in loose spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly rough texture.

Grastidium truncicola is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
