Gymnema chalmersii

Gymnema chalmersii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive milky sap. The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of small, greenish-white flowers followed by follicles containing seeds. Gymnema chalmersii is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
