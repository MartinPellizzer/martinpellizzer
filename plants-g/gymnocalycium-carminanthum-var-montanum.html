Gymnocalycium carminanthum var. montanum

Gymnocalycium carminanthum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain moon cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray foliage. Its spineless appearance makes it an attractive choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in succulent plants.
