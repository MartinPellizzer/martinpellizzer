Galium paulianum

Galium paulianum, commonly known as the Paulian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium paulianum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
