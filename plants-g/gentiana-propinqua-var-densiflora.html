Gentiana propinqua var. densiflora

Gentiana propinqua var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
