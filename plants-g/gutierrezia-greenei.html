Gutierrezia greenei

Gutierrezia greenei, commonly known as desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia greenei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
