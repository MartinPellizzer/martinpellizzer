Gardenia griffithii var. maingayi

Gardenia griffithii var. maingayi, commonly known as the Maingay gardenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and pleasant scent.

The varietal form maingayi is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical variety.
