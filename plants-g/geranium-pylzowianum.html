Geranium pylzowianum

Geranium pylzowianum, commonly known as the Pylzow's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium pylzowianum is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
