Gymnocalycium orientale var. vikulovii

Gymnocalycium orientale var. vikulovii, commonly known as the Vikulov's moon cactus, is a rare cactus species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
