Gustavia longifuniculata

Gustavia longifuniculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central boss. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch. It produces long, slender seed pods that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Gustavia longifuniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types in warm climates.
