Geranium speciosum

Geranium speciosum, commonly known as the showy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
