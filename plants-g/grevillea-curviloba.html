Grevillea curviloba

Grevillea curviloba, commonly known as the wavy grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces nectar-rich flowers that attract birds and insects.

Grevillea curviloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
