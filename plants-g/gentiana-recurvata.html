Gentiana recurvata

Gentiana recurvata, commonly known as the recurved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat branched.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
