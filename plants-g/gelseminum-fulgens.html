Gelseminum fulgens

Gelseminum fulgens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and a climbing habit, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their intense color and long-lasting bloom, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is known for containing toxic alkaloids in its leaves and stems.
