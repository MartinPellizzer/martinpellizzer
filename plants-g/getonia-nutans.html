Getonia nutans

Getonia nutans, commonly known as the drooping getonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Getonia nutans is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
