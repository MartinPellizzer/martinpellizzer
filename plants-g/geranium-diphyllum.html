Geranium diphyllum

Geranium diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Geranium diphyllum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
