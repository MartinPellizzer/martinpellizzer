Glomera rubroviridis

Glomera rubroviridis, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
