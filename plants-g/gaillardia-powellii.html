Gaillardia powellii

Gaillardia powellii, commonly known as Powell's blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers range in color from red to orange, with yellow centers, and it produces fluffy, seed-filled flowers that disperse easily.

Powell's blanketflower is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
