Grammitis brevipila

Grammitis brevipila, commonly known as the short-stalked maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis brevipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
