Goniothalamus auriculatus

Goniothalamus auriculatus, commonly known as the golden ear tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive auricle-like projection at the base of the petals. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential medicinal properties.

Goniothalamus auriculatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
