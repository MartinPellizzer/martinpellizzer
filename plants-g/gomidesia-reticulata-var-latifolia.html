Gomidesia reticulata var. latifolia

Gomidesia reticulata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gomidesia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
