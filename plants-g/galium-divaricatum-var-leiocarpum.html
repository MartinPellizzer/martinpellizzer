Galium divaricatum var. leiocarpum

Galium divaricatum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often prostrate stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in loose racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
