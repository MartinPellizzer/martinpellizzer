Gypsophila coelesyriaca

Gypsophila coelesyriaca, commonly known as the Syrian love grass, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila coelesyriaca is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
