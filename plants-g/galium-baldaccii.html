Galium baldaccii

Galium baldaccii, commonly known as the baldaccii bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, producing tiny white or pale yellow blooms.

Galium baldaccii is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.
