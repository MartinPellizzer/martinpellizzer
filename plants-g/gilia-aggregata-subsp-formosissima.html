Gilia aggregata subsp. formosissima

Gilia aggregata subsp.

formosissima, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
