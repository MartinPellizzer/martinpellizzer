Galipea aromatica

Galipea aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic gallnut, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and produces unique, fragrant galls that are used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

The galls contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
