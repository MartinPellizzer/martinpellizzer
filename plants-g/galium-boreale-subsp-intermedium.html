Galium boreale subsp. intermedium

Galium boreale subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
