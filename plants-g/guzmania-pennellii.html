Guzmania pennellii

Guzmania pennellii, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky red bracts that surround a central flower spike, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insects. Guzmania pennellii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
