Genista tribracteolata

Genista tribracteolata, commonly known as the three-bracteed broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
