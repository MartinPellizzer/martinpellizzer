Glycine punctata

Glycine punctata, commonly known as the spotted cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, white spots and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Glycine punctata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
