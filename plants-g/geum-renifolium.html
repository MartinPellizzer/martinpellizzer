Geum renifolium

Geum renifolium, commonly known as the Appalachian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive cup-like shape, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are softly hairy and turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Geum renifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
