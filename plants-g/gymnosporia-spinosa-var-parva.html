Gymnosporia spinosa var. parva

Gymnosporia spinosa var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf spiny gymnosporia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the gymnosperm group. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
