Gardenia chinensis

Gardenia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, waxy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Gardenia chinensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
