Goniothalamus laoticus

Goniothalamus laoticus, commonly known as the Laotian tamarind tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, with a dense canopy of glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-red fruits resembling small, round drupes. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
