Goodenia barilletii

Goodenia barilletii, commonly known as the Barillet's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Goodenia barilletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
