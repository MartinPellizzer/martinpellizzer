Guneria tomentosa

Guneria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly gunnera, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its large, broad, velvety leaves that are covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
