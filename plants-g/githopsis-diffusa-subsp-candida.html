Githopsis diffusa subsp. candida

Githopsis diffusa subsp.

candida, commonly known as the white-flowered githopsis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
