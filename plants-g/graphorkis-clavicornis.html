Graphorkis clavicornis, commonly known as the knobbed rock fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, knob-like spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a single row along the fertile fronds. The species is notable for its specialized adaptations to nutrient-poor substrates, making it a rare and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
