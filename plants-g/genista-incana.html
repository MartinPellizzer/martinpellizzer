Genista incana

Genista incana, commonly known as the whitethorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and rocky areas.

Genista incana is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
