Glechoma agrestis

Glechoma agrestis, commonly known as ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Glechoma agrestis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
