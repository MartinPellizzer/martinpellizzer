Gomphrena meyeniana var. aureiflora

Gomphrena meyeniana var. aureiflora, commonly known as the golden feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spiky inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to red, with a striking yellow center, and they bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
