Gymnogramma cordata var. namaquensis

Gymnogramma cordata var. namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette-like pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are borne on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
