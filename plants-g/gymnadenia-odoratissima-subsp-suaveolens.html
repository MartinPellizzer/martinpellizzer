Gymnadenia odoratissima subsp. suaveolens

Gymnadenia odoratissima subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented grass of heaven, is a perennial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows across Europe. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctively sweet aroma.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
