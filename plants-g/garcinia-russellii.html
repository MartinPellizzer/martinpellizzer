Garcinia russellii

Garcinia russellii, commonly known as Russell's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive floral structure.

Garcinia russellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
