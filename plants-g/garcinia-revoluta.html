Garcinia revoluta

Garcinia revoluta, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a distinctive purple or red berry with a thick, white rind and a juicy, segmented interior. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
