Geodorum pacificum

Geodorum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with rhizomatous growth, featuring broad, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink, during the warm seasons. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a houseplant.

Its ornamental value and ease of care make it a popular choice for tropical landscaping.
