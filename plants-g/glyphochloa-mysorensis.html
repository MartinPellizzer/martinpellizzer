Glyphochloa mysorensis

Glyphochloa mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Glyphochloa mysorensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
