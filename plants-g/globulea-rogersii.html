Globulea rogersii

Globulea rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' globular vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that resemble globular structures. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of rainforests. Its unique floral morphology and growth pattern make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats due to habitat loss and over-collection.
