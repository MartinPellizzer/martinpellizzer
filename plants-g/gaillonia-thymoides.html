Gaillonia thymoides

Gaillonia thymoides, commonly known as the thyme-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, resembling those of thyme, which gives it its common name.

Gaillonia thymoides is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
