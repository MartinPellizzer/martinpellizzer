Gentiana diluta

Gentiana diluta, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana diluta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
