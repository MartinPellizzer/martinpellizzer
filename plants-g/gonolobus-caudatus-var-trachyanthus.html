Gonolobus caudatus var. trachyanthus

Gonolobus caudatus var. trachyanthus, commonly known as the hairy devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features long, slender stems with a distinctive hairy texture along its surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often accompanied by prominent, leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a slightly hairy upper surface and a smooth underside.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
