Gonystylus pluricornis

Gonystylus pluricornis, commonly known as the hairy ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and exhibits a tall, straight trunk with a broad crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The species is valued for its durable wood but is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
