Glyceria baltica f. setacea

Glyceria baltica f. setacea, commonly known as the tufted beakgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and the edges of lakes. The plant features slender, tufted inflorescences that are often hairy, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and inland wet areas.
