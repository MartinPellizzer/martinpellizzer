Grossularia divaricata

Grossularia divaricata, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that turn red when mature, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
