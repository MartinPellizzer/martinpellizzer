Gifola apiculata

Gifola apiculata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gifola apiculata is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
