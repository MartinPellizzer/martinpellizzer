Geranium dissectum var. palmatum

Geranium dissectum var. palmatum, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a species native to Japan and is characterized by its finely dissected, palmately lobed leaves.

It produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its foliage and flowers provide seasonal interest, making it a popular choice among horticulturists.
