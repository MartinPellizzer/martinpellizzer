Guilandina barkeriana

Guilandina barkeriana, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are followed by long, spiral-shaped pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in herbal medicine.
