Gamoplexis orobanchoides

Gamoplexis orobanchoides, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Orobanchaceae family, commonly known as the broomrape family, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is hemiparasitic, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis independently. It typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides in desert landscapes.
