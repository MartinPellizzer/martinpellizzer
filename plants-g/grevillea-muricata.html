Grevillea muricata

Grevillea muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and vibrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are adapted to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Grevillea muricata is well-suited to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
