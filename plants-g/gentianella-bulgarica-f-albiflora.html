Gentianella bulgarica f. albiflora

Gentianella bulgarica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a botanical variant characterized by its striking white petals.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is native to the Balkan region, particularly Bulgaria. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a unique aesthetic to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
