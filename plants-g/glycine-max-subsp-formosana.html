Glycine max subsp. formosana

Glycine max subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan soybean, is a subspecies native to East Asia, particularly Taiwan. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its compound leaves and erect growth habit.

The plant produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds used for food and industrial purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its high protein content and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
