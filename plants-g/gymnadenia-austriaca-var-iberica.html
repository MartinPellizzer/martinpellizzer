Gymnadenia austriaca var. iberica

Gymnadenia austriaca var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian grass of St. John, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
