Geranium × antipodeum

Geranium × antipodeum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, with some varieties displaying a striking variegation pattern. Native to South Africa, this plant is widely cultivated as a decorative ground cover or hanging plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.
