Globba holttumii subsp. aurea

Globba holttumii subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden globba, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble small globes.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, adding a tropical flair to gardens. Its leaves are typically broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for the colorful blooms.

This species is well-suited for shaded to semi-shaded areas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
