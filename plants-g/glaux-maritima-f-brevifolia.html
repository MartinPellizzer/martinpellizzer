Glaux maritima f. brevifolia

Glaux maritima f. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved sea blite, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and sandy environments.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in maritime climates and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Its short leaves and salt tolerance make it an important pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.
