Gagria

Gagria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and chrysanthemums. The plant features showy yellow flowers with white or pink ray florets, surrounded by a central disk of tubular flowers. Gagria typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
