Glochidion crassifolium

Glochidion crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved glabrous vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Glochidion crassifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
