Geranium nyassense

Geranium nyassense, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Geranium nyassense prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
