Garcinia morella var. cuspida

Garcinia morella var. cuspida, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a sour, tangy flavor.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
