Ginginsia pruinosa

Ginginsia pruinosa, commonly known as the pruinose ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic rhizomes and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.
