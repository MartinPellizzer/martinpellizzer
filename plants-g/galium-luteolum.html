Galium luteolum

Galium luteolum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, sprawling stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne on pedicels, and the plant is known for its creeping habit and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Galium luteolum is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
