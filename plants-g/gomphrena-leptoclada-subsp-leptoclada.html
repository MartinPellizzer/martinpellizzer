Gomphrena leptoclada subsp. leptoclada

Gomphrena leptoclada subsp.

leptoclada, commonly known as the slender gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, spiky flowers that range in color from red to pink.

The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact even when dried.
