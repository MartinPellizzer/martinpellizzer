Garnotia fuscata

Garnotia fuscata, commonly known as the dark garnet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of lighter markings. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as under dense forest canopies. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, contributing to its overall striking appearance.

Garnotia fuscata is known for its unique floral structure, which includes specialized pollen sacs that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
