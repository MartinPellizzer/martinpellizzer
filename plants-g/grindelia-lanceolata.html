Grindelia lanceolata

Grindelia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

Grindelia lanceolata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
