Gomphrena decumbens subf. spathulata

Gomphrena decumbens subf.

spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
