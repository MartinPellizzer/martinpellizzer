Globba timorensis

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
