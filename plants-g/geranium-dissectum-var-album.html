Geranium dissectum var. album

Geranium dissectum var. album, commonly known as the white geranium, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a finely cut appearance, and its most notable characteristic is its clusters of showy white flowers. The flowers are typically borne on slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
