Gentiana wingecarribiensis

Gentiana wingecarribiensis, commonly known as the Windward Islands gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Saint Lucia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
