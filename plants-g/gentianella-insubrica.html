Gentianella insubrica

Gentianella insubrica, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gentianella insubrica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
