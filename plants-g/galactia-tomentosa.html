Galactia tomentosa

Galactia tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy galactia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Galactia tomentosa is often found in rocky soils and is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
