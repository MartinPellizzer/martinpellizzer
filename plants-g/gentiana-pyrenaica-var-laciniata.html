Gentiana pyrenaica var. laciniata

Gentiana pyrenaica var. laciniata, commonly known as the Pyrenean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
