Geranium nemorale

Geranium nemorale, commonly known as the wood geranium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central boss.

Geranium nemorale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
