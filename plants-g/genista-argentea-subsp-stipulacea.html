Genista argentea subsp. stipulacea

Genista argentea subsp.

stipulacea, commonly known as silverbush or silverbroom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flower clusters in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and erosion control.
