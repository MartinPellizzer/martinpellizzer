Grewia deserticola

Grewia deserticola, commonly known as the desert gumtree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Grewia deserticola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing food for wildlife in its native habitat.
