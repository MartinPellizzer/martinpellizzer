Gynura claessensii

Gynura claessensii, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Gynura claessensii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
