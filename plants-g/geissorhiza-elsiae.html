Geissorhiza elsiae

Geissorhiza elsiae, commonly known as the African orchid or the yellow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and are often clustered in racemes. The plant has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods. Its leaves are typically grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Geissorhiza elsiae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some African communities.
