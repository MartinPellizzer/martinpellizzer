Garcinia angustifolia

Garcinia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that contains a single seed and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has applications in herbal remedies due to its bioactive compounds.
