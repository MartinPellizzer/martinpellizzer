Grusonia

Grusonia, commonly known as the Christmas fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that resemble a Christmas tree, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are compound, with each frond consisting of multiple smaller leaflets.

Grusonia thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.
