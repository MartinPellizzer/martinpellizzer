Galium simplex

Galium simplex, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium simplex thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
