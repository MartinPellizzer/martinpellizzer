Grammitis pinnatifida

Grammitis pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sword fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis pinnatifida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
