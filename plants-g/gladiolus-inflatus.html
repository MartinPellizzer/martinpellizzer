Gladiolus inflatus

Gladiolus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a sword or a gladiolus. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting its large, colorful blooms. Its leaves are long and narrow, typical of many members of the Iridaceae family.

The flowers are typically pink or red, with a unique inflated shape that gives the plant its common name.
