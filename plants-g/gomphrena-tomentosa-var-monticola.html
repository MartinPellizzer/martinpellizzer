Gomphrena tomentosa var. monticola

Gomphrena tomentosa var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces dense, spherical clusters of pink or red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically covered in fine, white tomentum, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
