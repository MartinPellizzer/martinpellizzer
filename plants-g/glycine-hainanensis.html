Glycine hainanensis

Glycine hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan soybean, is a species of legume native to the Hainan Island in China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several round seeds, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine. Glycine hainanensis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
