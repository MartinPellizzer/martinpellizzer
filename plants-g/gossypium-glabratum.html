Gossypium glabratum

Gossypium glabratum, commonly known as the Mexican cotton plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that are harvested for textile use. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
