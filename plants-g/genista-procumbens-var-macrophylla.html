Genista procumbens var. macrophylla

Genista procumbens var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes, forming a mat-like growth pattern. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
