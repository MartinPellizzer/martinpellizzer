Grewia claessensii

Grewia claessensii, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Grewia claessensii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
