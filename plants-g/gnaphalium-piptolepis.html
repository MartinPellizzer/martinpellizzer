Gnaphalium piptolepis

Gnaphalium piptolepis, commonly known as the hairy dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the genus Gnaphalium. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge, contributing to its identification in the field.
