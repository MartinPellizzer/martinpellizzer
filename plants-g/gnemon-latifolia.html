Gnemon latifolia

Gnemon latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gnemon, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Gnemon latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
