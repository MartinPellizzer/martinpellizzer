Gladiolus watsonius

Gladiolus watsonius, commonly known as the Watson's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing spikes of flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots are fleshy and adapted to store water.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
