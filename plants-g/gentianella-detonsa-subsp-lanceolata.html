Gentianella detonsa subsp. lanceolata

Gentianella detonsa subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
