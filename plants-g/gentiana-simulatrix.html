Gentiana simulatrix

Gentiana simulatrix, commonly known as the mimic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are tubular with five petals, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
