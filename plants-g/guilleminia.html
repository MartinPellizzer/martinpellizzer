Guilleminia

Guilleminia, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants typically have low, sprawling growth habits with hairy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems. Guilleminia species are characterized by their ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments due to their succulent leaves and deep root systems.

They play an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing food for local wildlife.
