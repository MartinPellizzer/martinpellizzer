Gorgonidium vargasii

Gorgonidium vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas giant orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in dense, humid forests, where it thrives in shaded, well-drained soil. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, while its leaves are broad and waxy to reduce water loss.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
