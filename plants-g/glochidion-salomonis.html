Glochidion salomonis

Glochidion salomonis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly the Solomon Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with small, stiff hairs.

Glochidion salomonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
