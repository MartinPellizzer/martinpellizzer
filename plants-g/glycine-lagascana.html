Glycine lagascana

Glycine lagascana, commonly known as the Mexican climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its twining vines and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, green pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its edible beans. Glycine lagascana has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
