Grisebachia eremioides var. pubicalyx

Grisebachia eremioides var. pubicalyx, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, with a compact form that helps reduce water loss.

The varietal name *pubicalyx* refers to the pubescent (hairy) structure around the calyx, a distinctive botanical characteristic.
