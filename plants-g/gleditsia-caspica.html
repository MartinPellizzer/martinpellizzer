Gleditsia caspica

Gleditsia caspica, commonly known as the Caspian honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its flowers are typically small and yellowish-green, appearing in clusters, while the seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods.

Gleditsia caspica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
