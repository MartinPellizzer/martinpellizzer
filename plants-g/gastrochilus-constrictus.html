Gastrochilus constrictus

Gastrochilus constrictus, commonly known as the narrow-stem orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with numerous erect, branching stems. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale green, and are arranged in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
