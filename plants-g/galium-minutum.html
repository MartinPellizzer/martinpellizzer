Galium minutum

Galium minutum, commonly known as dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat diversity.
