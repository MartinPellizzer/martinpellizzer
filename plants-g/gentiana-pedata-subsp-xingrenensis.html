Gentiana pedata subsp. xingrenensis

Gentiana pedata subsp.

xingrenensis, commonly known as the Xingren gentian, is a flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
