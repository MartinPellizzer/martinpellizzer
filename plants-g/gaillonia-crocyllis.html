Gaillonia crocyllis

Gaillonia crocyllis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
