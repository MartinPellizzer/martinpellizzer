Grammitis obtusa

Grammitis obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of epiphytic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, closely spaced fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its leaves are usually broad and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.
