Galium parisiense var. tenellum

Galium parisiense var. tenellum, commonly known as silverweed or lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

Galium parisiense var. tenellum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
