Guarea costata

Guarea costata, commonly known as the broadleaf guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in phytochemical research.

The plant is also notable for its role in local ecosystems and its potential applications in sustainable forestry.
