Galega Vulgaris

Galega vulgaris, also known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. This plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock. Despite its benefits, it is also known for its toxic properties, which can be harmful to animals and humans. Its unique appearance and dual nature as both useful and dangerous make it a fascinating subject for study.
