Glochidion salicifolium var. dracunculoides

Glochidion salicifolium var. dracunculoides, commonly known as the dragon's blood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its unique, round, red or orange fruits that resemble drops of blood, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
