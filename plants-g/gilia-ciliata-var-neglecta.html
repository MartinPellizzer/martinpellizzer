Gilia ciliata var. neglecta

Gilia ciliata var. neglecta, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, with a distinctive ciliate margin, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
