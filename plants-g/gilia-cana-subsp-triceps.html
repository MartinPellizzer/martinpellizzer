Gilia cana subsp. triceps

Gilia cana subsp.

triceps, commonly known as the three-veined gilia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
