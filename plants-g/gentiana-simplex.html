Gentiana simplex

Gentiana simplex, commonly known as the simple gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The genus Gentiana is known for its medicinal properties, and G. simplex has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
