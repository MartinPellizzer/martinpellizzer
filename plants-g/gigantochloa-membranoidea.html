Gigantochloa membranoidea

Gigantochloa membranoidea, commonly known as the paper bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hair. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress. Its fibrous structure makes it valuable for both ornamental and practical uses.

Due to its rapid growth, it is also considered a potential invasive species in certain regions.
