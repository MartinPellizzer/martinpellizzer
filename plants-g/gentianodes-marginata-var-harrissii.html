Gentianodes marginata var. harrissii

Gentianodes marginata var. harrissii, commonly known as Harriss's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or yellow margin, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
