Guzmania harlingii

Guzmania harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insects. Guzmania harlingii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
