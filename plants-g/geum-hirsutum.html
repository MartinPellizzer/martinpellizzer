Geum hirsutum

Geum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy oxeye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Geum hirsutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
