Gentianella saxosa

Gentianella saxosa, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions in Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella saxosa is known for its short flowering period, usually in late summer, and is often found in meadows and scree slopes.
