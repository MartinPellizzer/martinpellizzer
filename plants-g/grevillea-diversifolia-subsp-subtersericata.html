Grevillea diversifolia subsp. subtersericata

Grevillea diversifolia subsp.

subtersericata, commonly known as the silver-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces bright orange or red flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
