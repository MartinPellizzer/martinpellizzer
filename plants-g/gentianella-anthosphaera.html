Gentianella anthosphaera

Gentianella anthosphaera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a short, upright stem. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive yellow or white throat, which contrasts with the deep petal colors.

Gentianella anthosphaera is adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
