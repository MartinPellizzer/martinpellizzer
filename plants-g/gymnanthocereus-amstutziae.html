Gymnanthocereus amstutziae

Gymnanthocereus amstutziae, commonly known as the Amstutz's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered with white to pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
