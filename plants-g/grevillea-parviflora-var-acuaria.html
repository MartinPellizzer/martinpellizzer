Grevillea parviflora var. acuaria

Grevillea parviflora var. acuaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge underneath. The plant produces small, bright red flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
