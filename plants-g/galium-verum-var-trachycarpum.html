Galium verum var. trachycarpum

Galium verum var. trachycarpum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in loose racemes, blooming in late spring to early summer.
