Gypsophila perfoliata var. angustifolia

Gypsophila perfoliata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery plume-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has slender, upright stems that produce small, clustered flowers, often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow and often appear to be wrapped around the stems, giving it a unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
