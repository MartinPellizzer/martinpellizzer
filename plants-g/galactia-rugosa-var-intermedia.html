Galactia rugosa var. intermedia

Galactia rugosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap that contains alkaloids, which can be toxic to some animals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
