Glandularia pulchella var. clavellata

Glandularia pulchella var. clavellata, commonly known as the clustered purple verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy spikes of purple to violet flowers. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which help in reducing water loss and deterring herbivores. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
