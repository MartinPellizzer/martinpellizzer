Garcinia lanessanii

Garcinia lanessanii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are covered in a thick, waxy coating.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
