Galium lucidum var. aciphyllum

Galium lucidum var. aciphyllum, commonly known as shining bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, bead-like berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.
