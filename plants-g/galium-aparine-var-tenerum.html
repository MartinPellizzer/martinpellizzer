Galium aparine var. tenerum

Galium aparine var. tenerum, commonly known as sticky willow herb, is a variant of the common bedstraw plant.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy surface. The small white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by three-lobed, hairy fruits.

This variant is adapted to a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and moist environments.
