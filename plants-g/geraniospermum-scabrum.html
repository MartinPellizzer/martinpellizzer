Geraniospermum scabrum

Geraniospermum scabrum, commonly known as the rough geraniospermum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its habitat includes rocky soils and dry, sunny environments where it thrives with minimal water.

Geraniospermum scabrum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
