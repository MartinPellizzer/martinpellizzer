Grindelia fastigiata

Grindelia fastigiata, commonly known as the stiffstem gumweed, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Grindelia fastigiata is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
