Gardenia aculeata

Gardenia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia aculeata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
