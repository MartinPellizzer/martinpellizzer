Gasparinia mirabilis

Gasparinia mirabilis, commonly known as the fairy grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, delicate, feathery leaves that give it a whimsical appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its unique morphology and ability to form dense, low-growing mats make it an excellent ground cover.

Gasparinia mirabilis is also valued for its drought tolerance and minimal maintenance requirements.
