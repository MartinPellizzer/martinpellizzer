Genista ephedroides

Genista ephedroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are woody and often branched, with a tendency to form dense shrubs.
