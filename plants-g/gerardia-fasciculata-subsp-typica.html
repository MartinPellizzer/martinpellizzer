Gerardia fasciculata subsp. typica

Gerardia fasciculata subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in dense clusters along the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
