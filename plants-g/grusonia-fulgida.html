Grusonia fulgida

Grusonia fulgida, commonly known as the golden grusonia, is a striking plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that reflects sunlight.

Grusonia fulgida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
