Geum fischeri

Geum fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geum fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
