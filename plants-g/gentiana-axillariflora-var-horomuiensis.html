Gentiana axillariflora var. horomuiensis

Gentiana axillariflora var. horomuiensis, commonly known as the Horomui gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
