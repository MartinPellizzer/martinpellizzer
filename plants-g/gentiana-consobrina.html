Gentiana consobrina

Gentiana consobrina, commonly known as the brother's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana consobrina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
