Geum involucratum

Geum involucratum, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
