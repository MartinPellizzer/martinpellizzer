Gymnosporia saxatilis

Gymnosporia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Gymnosporia saxatilis is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
