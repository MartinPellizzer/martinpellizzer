Grewia astropetala var. astropetala

Grewia astropetala var. astropetala, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces distinctive five-lobed fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, with a star-like shape when cut crosswise. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its tangy flavor and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
