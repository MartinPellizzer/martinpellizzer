Gladiolus punctatus

Gladiolus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, often with distinctive markings.

The spotted gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
