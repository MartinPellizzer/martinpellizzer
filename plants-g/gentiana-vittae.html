Gentiana vittae

Gentiana vittae, commonly known as the violet gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a fine layer of white wax.

Gentiana vittae blooms during the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract various pollinators.
