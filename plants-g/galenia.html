Galenia

Galenia, commonly known as the "devil's backbone," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color. The leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. Galenia species are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

They are often found in arid or semi-arid environments and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
