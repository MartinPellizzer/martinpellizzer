Galium peplidifolium

Galium peplidifolium, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale green, with five petals.

Galium peplidifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
