Guzmania fusispica

Guzmania fusispica, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-tipped leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a visually appealing structure. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its epiphytic nature allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches or in rocky crevices.

Guzmania fusispica is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its long-lasting foliage and minimal care requirements.
