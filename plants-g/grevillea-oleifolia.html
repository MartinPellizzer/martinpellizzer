Grevillea oleifolia

Grevillea oleifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height.

Grevillea oleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
