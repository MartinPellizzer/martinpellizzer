Genipa capensis

Genipa capensis, commonly known as the Cape genipa, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to a bright red when ripe. Its fruit is edible, though it has a somewhat tart flavor, and is used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
