Gomphrena paramillensis

Gomphrena paramillensis, commonly known as the pink gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, scale-like leaves and is known for its drought tolerance.

Gomphrena paramillensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and long-lasting blooms.
