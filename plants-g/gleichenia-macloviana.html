Gleichenia macloviana

Gleichenia macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia macloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
