Gentiana lhakangensis

Gentiana lhakangensis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals, often with a white or yellow throat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
