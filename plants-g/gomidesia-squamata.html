Gomidesia squamata

Gomidesia squamata, commonly known as the scaly gomidesia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of tubular flowers. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically opposite and covered in fine, scale-like hairs, giving it its common name.

Its flowers, which are often pink or purple, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
