Gaudichaudia robusta var. genuina

Gaudichaudia robusta var. genuina, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a rough texture, while its stems are woody and upright.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
