Gagea reticulata var. tenuifolia

Gagea reticulata var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and survival in dry conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
