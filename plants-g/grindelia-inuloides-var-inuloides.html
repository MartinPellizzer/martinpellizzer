Grindelia inuloides var. inuloides

Grindelia inuloides var. inuloides, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has silver-gray foliage with woolly hairs, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
