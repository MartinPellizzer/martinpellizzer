Gundlachia floribunda

Gundlachia floribunda, commonly known as the showy gundlachia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gundlachia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
