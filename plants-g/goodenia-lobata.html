Goodenia lobata

Goodenia lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Goodenia lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
