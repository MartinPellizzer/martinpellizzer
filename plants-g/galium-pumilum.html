Galium pumilum

Galium pumilum, commonly known as dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Galium pumilum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
