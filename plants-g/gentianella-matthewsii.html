Gentianella matthewsii

Gentianella matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthew's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
