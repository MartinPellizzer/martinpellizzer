Geranium wallichianum

Geranium wallichianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a favored garden plant in temperate regions.
