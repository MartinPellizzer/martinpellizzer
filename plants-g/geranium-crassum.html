Geranium crassum

Geranium crassum, commonly known as the thick-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and clusters of vibrant purple or red flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Geranium crassum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
