Guettarda paludosa

Guettarda paludosa, commonly known as swamp guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of tropical America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist or swampy environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

Guettarda paludosa plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
