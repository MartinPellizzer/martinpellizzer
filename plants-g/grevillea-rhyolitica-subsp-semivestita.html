Grevillea rhyolitica subsp. semivestita

Grevillea rhyolitica subsp.

semivestita, commonly known as the Red Grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting native birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in Australian botany.
