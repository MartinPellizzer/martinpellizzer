Gomesa vasconcelosiana

Gomesa vasconcelosiana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features a short rhizome with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white with a faint fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
