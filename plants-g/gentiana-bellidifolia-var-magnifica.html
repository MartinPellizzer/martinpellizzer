Gentiana bellidifolia var. magnifica

Gentiana bellidifolia var. magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, deep blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
