Geranium collinum eglandulosum

Geranium collinum eglandulosum, commonly known as the smoothleaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Geraniaceae family. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
