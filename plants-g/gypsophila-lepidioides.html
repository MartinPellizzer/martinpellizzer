Gypsophila lepidioides

Gypsophila lepidioides, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and thrives in well-drained soils.

Gypsophila lepidioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
