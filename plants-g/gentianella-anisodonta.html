Gentianella anisodonta

Gentianella anisodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, intensely blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
