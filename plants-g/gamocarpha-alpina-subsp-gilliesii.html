Gamocarpha alpina subsp. gilliesii

Gamocarpha alpina subsp.

gilliesii, commonly known as the alpine gamogreen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
