Glockeria ventricosa

Glockeria ventricosa, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, velvety flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Glockeria ventricosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
