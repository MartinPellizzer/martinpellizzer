Galium circaezans var. hypomalacum

Galium circaezans var. hypomalacum, commonly known as the dwarf white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, prostrate stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its fragrant foliage and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
