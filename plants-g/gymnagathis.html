Gymnagathis

Gymnagathis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are leathery, dark green on top, and silvery white underneath, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
