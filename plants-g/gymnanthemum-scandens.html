Gymnanthemum scandens

Gymnanthemum scandens, commonly known as the climbing false arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
