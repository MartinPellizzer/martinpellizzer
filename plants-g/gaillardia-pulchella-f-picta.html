Gaillardia pulchella f. picta

Gaillardia pulchella f. picta, commonly known as the painted firewheel, is a strikingly colorful flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features vibrant red, orange, and yellow blooms that are often adorned with intricate patterns, giving it its "painted" appearance. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
