Goodyera fertilis

Goodyera fertilis, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and is often found in shaded, moist woodlands. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Goodyera fertilis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
