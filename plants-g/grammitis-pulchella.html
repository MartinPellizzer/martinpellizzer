Grammitis pulchella

Grammitis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
