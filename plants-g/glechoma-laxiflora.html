Glechoma laxiflora

Glechoma laxiflora, commonly known as creeping ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, wrinkled leaves with a distinctive purple underside and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma laxiflora is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
