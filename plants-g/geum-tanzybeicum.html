Geum tanzybeicum

Geum tanzybeicum, commonly known as the Tanzybea buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the island of Tanza in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish underside. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flowers in a raceme.

Geum tanzybeicum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
