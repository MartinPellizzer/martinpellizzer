Gordonia ovalis

Gordonia ovalis, commonly known as the oval waxtree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The tree produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds.

Gordonia ovalis is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
