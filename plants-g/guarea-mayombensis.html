Guarea mayombensis

Guarea mayombensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
