Gnetum catasphaericum

Gnetum catasphaericum, commonly known as the catasphaeric gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is characterized by its unique combination of vascular structures and reproductive features that bridge the gap between gymnosperms and angiosperms. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its cones are small and inconspicuous, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

Gnetum catasphaericum is native to certain regions of South America and is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance.
