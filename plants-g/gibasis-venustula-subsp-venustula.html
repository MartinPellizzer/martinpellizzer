Gibasis venustula subsp. venustula

Gibasis venustula subsp.

venustula, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 1-2 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
