Genea rigida

Genea rigida, commonly known as the rigid genea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It has a compact, shrub-like form with glossy, lance-shaped leaves.

Genea rigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
