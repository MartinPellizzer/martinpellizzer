Gentianella scarlatiflora

Gentianella scarlatiflora, commonly known as the scarlet-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentianella scarlatiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
