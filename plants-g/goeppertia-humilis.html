Goeppertia humilis

Goeppertia humilis, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often emerging from the base of the leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Goeppertia humilis is well-suited for indoor cultivation and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
