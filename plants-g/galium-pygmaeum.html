Galium pygmaeum

Galium pygmaeum, commonly known as dwarf wood-rose, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream sides. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium pygmaeum has opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape.
