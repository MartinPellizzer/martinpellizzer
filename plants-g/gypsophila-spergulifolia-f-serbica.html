Gypsophila spergulifolia f. serbica

Gypsophila spergulifolia f. serbica, commonly known as the Serbica baby's breath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions of Eastern Europe and the Balkans. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized in floral arrangements for its ethereal appearance and is often used in wedding bouquets and centerpieces.
