Gentianodes eumarginata

Gentianodes eumarginata, commonly known as the pink gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to pink flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is herbaceous, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
