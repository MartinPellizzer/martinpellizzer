Gratiola pusilla

Gratiola pusilla, commonly known as the small yellow gentian, is a small perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gratiola pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
