Genista januensis

Genista januensis, commonly known as the Januensian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista januensis is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
