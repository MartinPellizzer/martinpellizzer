Gentiana karelinii

Gentiana karelinii, commonly known as Karelin's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana karelinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
