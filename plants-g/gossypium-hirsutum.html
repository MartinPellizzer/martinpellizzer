Gossypium hirsutum

Gossypium hirsutum, commonly known as upland cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its fluffy, white boll that contains the cotton fiber. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lobed leaves and showy pink or white flowers. Its fibers are harvested from the boll and are used extensively in the textile industry.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season with adequate sunlight and water.
