Gypsophila fasciculata var. laconica

Gypsophila fasciculata var. laconica, commonly known as the desert baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens and rockeries.
