Guilandina glaucophylla

Guilandina glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale green or yellow in color. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Guilandina glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
