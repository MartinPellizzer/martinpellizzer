Geranium venosum

Geranium venosum, commonly known as the veiny geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
