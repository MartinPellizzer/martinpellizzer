Gentiana douglasiana f. maculata

Gentiana douglasiana f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers with distinctive dark spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purple in color.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
