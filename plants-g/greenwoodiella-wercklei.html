Greenwoodiella wercklei

Greenwoodiella wercklei, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Americas, particularly in the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. It has small, opposite leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
