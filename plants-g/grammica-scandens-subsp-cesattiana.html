Grammica scandens subsp. cesattiana

Grammica scandens subsp.

cesattiana, commonly known as the climbing gram. This plant is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The subspecies cesattiana is distinguished by its more robust growth habit and slightly larger leaves compared to the typical form.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments in woodland areas.
