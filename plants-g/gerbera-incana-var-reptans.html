Gerbera incana var. reptans

Gerbera incana var. reptans, commonly known as the creeping gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The variety 'reptans' refers to its creeping growth pattern, which allows it to spread across the ground.
