Grevillea imberbis

Grevillea imberbis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea imberbis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
