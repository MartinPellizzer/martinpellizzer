Gastrolobium stipulare

Gastrolobium stipulare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Gastrolobium stipulare is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits the surrounding soil.
