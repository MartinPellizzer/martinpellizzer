Glycyrrhiza triphylla

Glycyrrhiza triphylla, commonly known as the Chinese licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and are valued for their sweet, aromatic properties.

Glycyrrhiza triphylla is also used in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
