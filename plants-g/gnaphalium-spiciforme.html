Gnaphalium spiciforme

Gnaphalium spiciforme, commonly known as the spikeless cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically has slender, erect stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are narrow and often arranged in a rosette or alternate fashion. It produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that are often grouped in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Gnaphalium spiciforme is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
