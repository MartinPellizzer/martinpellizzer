Grevillea filifolia

Grevillea filifolia, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, thread-like leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and striking flowers, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
