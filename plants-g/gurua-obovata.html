Gurua obovata

Gurua obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understorey habitats. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The flowers bloom in clusters and come in various colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making it a visually striking species.
