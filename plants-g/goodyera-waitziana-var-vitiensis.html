Goodyera waitziana var. vitiensis

Goodyera waitziana var. vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Lady's Slipper, is a rare orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without being parasitic. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, often clustered at the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
