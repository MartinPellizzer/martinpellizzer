Gilia arenaria var. aliciae

Gilia arenaria var. aliciae, commonly known as the sand gilia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinct central stamen. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its habitat.
