Guarea zenkeri

Guarea zenkeri, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree has opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, Guarea zenkeri is often found in lowland and mountain rainforests.
