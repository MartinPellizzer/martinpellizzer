Grammitis taxifolia

Grammitis taxifolia, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, linear segments. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy roots, which help anchor it to rocky surfaces or soil.

Grammitis taxifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in both natural and cultivated settings.
