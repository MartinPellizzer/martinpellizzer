Geranium incanum var. glabrius

Geranium incanum var. glabrius, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in dense, white or grayish hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
