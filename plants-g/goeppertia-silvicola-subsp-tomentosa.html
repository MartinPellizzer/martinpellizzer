Goeppertia silvicola subsp. tomentosa

Goeppertia silvicola subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly goeppertia, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Brazil. It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are densely covered with a soft, velvety white pubescence, giving it a unique, almost fuzzy appearance.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in the canopy where it can capture moisture and light. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that contrast beautifully with its foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
