Genista monspessulana

Genista monspessulana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, leathery leaves that are typical of many shrubs in its family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Genista monspessulana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
