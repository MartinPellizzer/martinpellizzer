Guzmania loraxiana

Guzmania loraxiana, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that typically features red bracts with yellow-orange flowers. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater, creating a micro-ecosystem for small organisms. Its leaves are usually dark green with a waxy texture and can grow up to 30 inches in length.

Guzmania loraxiana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
