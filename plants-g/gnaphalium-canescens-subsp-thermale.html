Gnaphalium canescens subsp. thermale

Gnaphalium canescens subsp.

thermale, commonly known as the thermophilic everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces achenes with pappus, which aid in wind dispersal.
