Galium philistaeum

Galium philistaeum, commonly known as the Palestine bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open habitats such as scrublands and woodland edges. The plant has slender, creeping stems that form a dense mat, and its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a hairy texture.

Galium philistaeum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
