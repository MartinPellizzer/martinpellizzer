Glycine soja f. nigra

Glycine soja f. nigra, commonly known as black soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant characterized by its dark-colored seeds.

The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to East Asia. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy. The flowers are typically pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by pods containing small, round seeds.

This variety is valued for its rich nutritional content and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
