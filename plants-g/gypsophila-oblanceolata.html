Gypsophila oblanceolata

Gypsophila oblanceolata, commonly known as the fringed poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, pink or white petals that resemble tiny bells. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
