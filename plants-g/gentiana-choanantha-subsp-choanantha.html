Gentiana choanantha subsp. choanantha

Gentiana choanantha subsp.

choanantha, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to its native habitat.
