Gypsophila perfoliata var. tomentosa

Gypsophila perfoliata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the fuzzy gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, rounded leaves that are often mistaken for stems. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, woolly appearance.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
