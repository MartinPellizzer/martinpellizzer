Gentiana loureiroi subsp. napulifera

Gentiana loureiroi subsp.

napulifera, commonly known as the Napo gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
