Galium verum subsp. meridionale

Galium verum subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, especially when young.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
