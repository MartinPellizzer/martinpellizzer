Glochidion puberum

Glochidion puberum, commonly known as the hairy glochidion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

Glochidion puberum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
