Garcinia ambrensis

Garcinia ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrensis garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Garcinia ambrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its fruit contains bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
