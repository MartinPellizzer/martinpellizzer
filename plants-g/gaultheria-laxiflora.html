Gaultheria laxiflora

Gaultheria laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It produces small, dark red berries that are rich in aromatic compounds, including methyl salicylate, which gives it its characteristic wintergreen scent. The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Gaultheria laxiflora is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
