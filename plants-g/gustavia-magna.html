Gustavia magna

Gustavia magna, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gustavia magna is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
