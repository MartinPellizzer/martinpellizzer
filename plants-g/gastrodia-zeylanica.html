Gastrodia zeylanica

Gastrodia zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or hill pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the main part used for medicinal and culinary purposes. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forests or on mountain slopes. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its unique morphology, including its fleshy, club-shaped rhizomes.

Gastrodia zeylanica is valued for its potential health benefits, including its use in traditional medicine for treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
