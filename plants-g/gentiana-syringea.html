Gentiana syringea

Gentiana syringea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana syringea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
