Goodyera confundens

Goodyera confundens, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and captivating epiphytic orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on the trunks of trees in the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves and its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale green. It has a short flowering period, typically in late spring to early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Goodyera confundens is considered an endangered species.
