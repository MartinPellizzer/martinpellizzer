Gilia tenuiflora var. purpusii

Gilia tenuiflora var. purpusii, commonly known as the narrow-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and dry slopes. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
