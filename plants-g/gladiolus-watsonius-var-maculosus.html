Gladiolus watsonius var. maculosus

Gladiolus watsonius var. maculosus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with distinctive spots or streaks. The foliage consists of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem. This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
