Gentiana hybrida

Gentiana hybrida, commonly known as the gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in a crown-like pattern. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, often growing in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Gentiana hybrida thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
