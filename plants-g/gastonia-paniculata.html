Gastonia paniculata

Gastonia paniculata, commonly known as the red panicled hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, red or orange bract-like flowers that resemble those of hibiscus species. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Gastonia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
