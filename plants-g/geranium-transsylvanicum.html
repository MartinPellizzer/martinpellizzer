Geranium transsylvanicum

Geranium transsylvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian geranium, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has a compact, upright growth habit. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled, pink to red flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Geranium transsylvanicum is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
