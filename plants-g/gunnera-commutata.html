Gunnera commutata

Gunnera commutata, commonly known as the giant water flea, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams and in high-altitude regions. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.

The plant's ability to retain moisture and its dramatic appearance contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
