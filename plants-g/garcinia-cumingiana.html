Garcinia cumingiana

Garcinia cumingiana, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-purple rind and segmented, white to pinkish flesh inside. The plant belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is valued for its aromatic leaves and edible fruit. Its fruit is rich in xanthones, which are bioactive compounds with potential health benefits.

Garcinia cumingiana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
