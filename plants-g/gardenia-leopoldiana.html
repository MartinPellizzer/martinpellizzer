Gardenia leopoldiana

Gardenia leopoldiana, commonly known as the Leopold's gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces large, fragrant white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant is evergreen and typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gardenia leopoldiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
