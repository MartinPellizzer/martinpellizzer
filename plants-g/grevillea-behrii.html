Grevillea behrii

Grevillea behrii, commonly known as the red flowering waratah, is a striking shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, showy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea behrii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
