Gilia aggregata var. arizonica

Gilia aggregata var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
