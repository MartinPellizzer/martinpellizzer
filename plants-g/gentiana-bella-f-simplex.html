Gentiana bella f. simplex

Gentiana bella f. simplex, commonly known as the simple gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
