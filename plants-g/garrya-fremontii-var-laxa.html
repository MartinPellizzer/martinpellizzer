Garrya fremontii var. laxa

Garrya fremontii var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
