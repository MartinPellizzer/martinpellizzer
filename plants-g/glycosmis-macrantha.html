Glycosmis macrantha

Glycosmis macrantha, commonly known as the large-fruited gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. Glycosmis macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
