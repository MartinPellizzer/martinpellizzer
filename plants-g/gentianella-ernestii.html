Gentianella ernestii

Gentianella ernestii, commonly known as Ernest's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentianella ernestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
