Gentianella detonsa subsp. raupii

Gentianella detonsa subsp.

raupii, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
