Gymnocalycium damsii subsp. evae

Gymnocalycium damsii subsp.

evae, commonly known as the Eave's moon cactus, is a low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its globular to cylindrical stems, which are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It has a dense, spiny covering that provides protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and indoor collections.
