Galium lahulense

Galium lahulense, commonly known as the Lahul silverweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows.

Galium lahulense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
