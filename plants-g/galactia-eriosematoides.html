Galactia eriosematoides

Galactia eriosematoides, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several small, round seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in dry to semi-arid regions.

Galactia eriosematoides is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.
