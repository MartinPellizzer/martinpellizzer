Geranium robertianum proles purpureum

Geranium robertianum proles purpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive purple foliage, which sets it apart from other geranium varieties. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
