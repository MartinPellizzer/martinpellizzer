Gerascanthus magnoliifolius

Gerascanthus magnoliifolius, commonly known as the magnolia-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. The plant has broad, glossy leaves that resemble those of magnolia trees, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gerascanthus magnoliifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
