Gentiana quaterna var. typica

Gentiana quaterna var. typica, commonly known as the four-petaled gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Gentiana quaterna var. typica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
