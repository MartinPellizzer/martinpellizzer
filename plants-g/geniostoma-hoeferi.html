Geniostoma hoeferi

Geniostoma hoeferi, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its vibrant red to orange tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
