Gentiana callistantha

Gentiana callistantha, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a perennial herb with striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

Gentiana callistantha plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine regions.
