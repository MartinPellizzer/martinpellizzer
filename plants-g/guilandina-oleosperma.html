Guilandina oleosperma

Guilandina oleosperma, commonly known as the oil bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing oily seeds. The seeds are rich in oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of vegetable oil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its economic value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
