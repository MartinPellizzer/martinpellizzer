Gilia achilleifolia subsp. achilleifolia

Gilia achilleifolia subsp.

achilleifolia, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
