Glechoma betonicifolia

Glechoma betonicifolia, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Glechoma betonicifolia is known for its vigorous growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
