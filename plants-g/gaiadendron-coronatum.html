Gaiadendron coronatum

Gaiadendron coronatum, commonly known as the crowned gaiadendron, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves at the top of its stem. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
