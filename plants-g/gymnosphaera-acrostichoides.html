Gymnosphaera acrostichoides

Gymnosphaera acrostichoides, commonly known as the acrostichoid fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and the presence of small, scale-like structures on its stems. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
