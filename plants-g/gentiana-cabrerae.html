Gentiana cabrerae

Gentiana cabrerae, commonly known as the Canary Island gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana cabrerae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its native habitat.
