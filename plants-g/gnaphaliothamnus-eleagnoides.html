Gnaphaliothamnus eleagnoides

Gnaphaliothamnus eleagnoides, commonly known as silverleaf thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Gnaphaliothamnus eleagnoides is drought-tolerant and has adapted to arid environments with its deep root system.
