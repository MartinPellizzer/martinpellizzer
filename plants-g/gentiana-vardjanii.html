Gentiana vardjanii

Gentiana vardjanii, commonly known as the Armenian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and bear the showy flowers at the tips.

Gentiana vardjanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
