Gelidocalamus stellatus

Gelidocalamus stellatus, commonly known as the star-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass found in the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive star-like pattern on the underside. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat. Its growth is slow, and it forms dense clumps that provide habitat for various small animals and insects.

Gelidocalamus stellatus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
