Gentiana andreae-mathewsii

Gentiana andreae-mathewsii, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its striking blue flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
