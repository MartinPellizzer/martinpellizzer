Gonioanthela laxa

Gonioanthela laxa, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Gonioanthela laxa is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
