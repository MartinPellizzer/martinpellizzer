Gnaphalium mendocinum

Gnaphalium mendocinum, commonly known as the Mendocino everlasting, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, hairy leaves and has a mat-forming growth pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a fine, powdery coating on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
