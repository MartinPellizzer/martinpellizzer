Gymnadenia cucullata var. variegata

Gymnadenia cucullata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated grass of parnassus, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are grass-like, linear, and often have a distinct variegation pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

It is valued for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
