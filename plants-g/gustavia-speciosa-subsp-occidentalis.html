Gustavia speciosa subsp. occidentalis

Gustavia speciosa subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters.

The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
