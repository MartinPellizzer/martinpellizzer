Gentiana waltonii

Gentiana waltonii, commonly known as the Welsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Wales and western England.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana waltonii is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
