Gymnocalycium delaetii subsp. marsoneri

Gymnocalycium delaetii subsp.

marsoneri, commonly known as the Sonoran Pincushion Cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny, segmented stem that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is notable for its compact size and unique, spiny appearance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
