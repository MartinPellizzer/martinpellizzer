Guzmania sibundoyorum

Guzmania sibundoyorum, commonly known as the Sibundoy flame, is a striking bromeliad native to the humid cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the central inflorescence, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects water, providing a habitat for small aquatic organisms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush foliage.

Guzmania sibundoyorum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and unique appearance.
