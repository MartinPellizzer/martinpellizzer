Guajava buxifolia

Guajava buxifolia, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
