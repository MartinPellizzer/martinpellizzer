Gaillardia pulchella var. picta

Gaillardia pulchella var. picta, commonly known as the painted firewheel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that produces vibrant, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals, often with a central brown or black disk. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, while its ecological role supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The varietal name "picta" refers to its colorful, painted appearance.
