Gleichenia squamulosa

Gleichenia squamulosa, commonly known as the silver fern, is a vascular fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments with a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
