Galatea punctata

Galatea punctata, commonly known as the spotted milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

Galatea punctata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
