Gomphrena elegans f. nigrovirida

Gomphrena elegans f. nigrovirida, commonly known as the black and green gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically a mix of deep purple and black, with contrasting green bracts.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
