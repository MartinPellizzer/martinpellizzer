Grevillea glauca

Grevillea glauca, commonly known as the silver grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange cylindrical flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea glauca is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native birds and insects.
