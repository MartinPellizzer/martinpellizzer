Grewia begonifolia

Grewia begonifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or begonia leaf grewia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Grewia begonifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
