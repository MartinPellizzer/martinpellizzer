Gagea fibrosa var. angustifolia

Gagea fibrosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Liliaceae family.
