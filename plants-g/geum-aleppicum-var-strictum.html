Geum aleppicum var. strictum

Geum aleppicum var. strictum, commonly known as the Alpine avens, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.
