Gypsophila saponarioides

Gypsophila saponarioides, commonly known as soapwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and somewhat woody.

Gypsophila saponarioides is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
