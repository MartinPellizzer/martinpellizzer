Gnaphalium saturejoides var. hirsuta

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is notable for its soft, hairy foliage and its ability to produce a profusion of small, daisy-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
