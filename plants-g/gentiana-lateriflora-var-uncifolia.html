Gentiana lateriflora var. uncifolia

Gentiana lateriflora var. uncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
