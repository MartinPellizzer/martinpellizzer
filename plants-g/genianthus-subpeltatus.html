Genianthus subpeltatus

Genianthus subpeltatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved genianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Genianthus subpeltatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
