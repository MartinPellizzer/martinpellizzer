Gentiana herrediana

Gentiana herrediana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of down.

Gentiana herrediana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
