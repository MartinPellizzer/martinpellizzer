Gentiana horomuiensis

Gentiana horomuiensis, commonly known as the Horomui gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
