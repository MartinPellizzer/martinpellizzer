Galium hirtiflorum

Galium hirtiflorum, commonly known as hairy-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early summer.

Galium hirtiflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
