Garrya ovata subsp. goldmanii

Garrya ovata subsp.

goldmanii, commonly known as the golden garrya, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
