Gomphrena elegans var. orientalis

Gomphrena elegans var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink to purple bract-like flowers that resemble small, rounded leaves. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact even when dried, making it popular in dried floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, preferring warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and horticultural collections.
