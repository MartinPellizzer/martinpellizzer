Geum × cultorum

Geum × cultorum, commonly known as the New England aster, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect and often branched. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Geum × cultorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
