Guyonia sylvestris

Guyonia sylvestris, commonly known as the wild sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. Guyonia sylvestris is often found in moist, forested areas and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.
