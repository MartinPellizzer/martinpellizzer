Gomphotis

Gomphotis, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often showy flowers that are adapted to grow in rocky or arid environments. The plants typically have a short, creeping rhizome and produce small, clustered inflorescences. Gomphotis species are native to various regions in Asia, particularly in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.
