Guajava pilosa

Guajava pilosa, commonly known as the hairy guava, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
