Gossypium elatum

Gossypium elatum, commonly known as the desert cotton, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and the production of fluffy, white fibers used for textile purposes. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pink or red, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the fibrous seeds.

Gossypium elatum plays a significant role in traditional and modern textile industries due to its durable and versatile fibers.
