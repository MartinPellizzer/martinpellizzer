Goodyera grandis

Goodyera grandis, commonly known as the giant bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and pine forests. It has a complex life cycle involving a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.
