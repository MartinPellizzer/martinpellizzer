Galeopsis longiflora

Galeopsis longiflora, commonly known as slender blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis longiflora is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
