Geranium wakhanicum

Geranium wakhanicum, commonly known as the Wakhan geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Geranium wakhanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
