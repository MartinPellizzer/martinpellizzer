Gladiolus scabridus

Gladiolus scabridus, commonly known as the rough gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a rough, scabrous texture, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various regions beyond its native habitat.
