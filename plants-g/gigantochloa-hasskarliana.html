Gigantochloa hasskarliana

Gigantochloa hasskarliana, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structural height.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
