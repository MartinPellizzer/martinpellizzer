Glyceria michauxii

Glyceria michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Glyceria michauxii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
