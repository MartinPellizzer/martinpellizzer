Grammitis cornigera

Grammitis cornigera, commonly known as the hairy grammitis, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Grammitis cornigera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
