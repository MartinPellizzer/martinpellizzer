Gentiana raupii

Gentiana raupii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

Gentiana raupii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
