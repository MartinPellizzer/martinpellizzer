Glycine rubicunda

Glycine rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered climbing bean, is a vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Glycine rubicunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
