Glyceria triflora var. maxima

Glyceria triflora var. maxima, commonly known as the giant flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers, which are typically found near the base of the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life, it plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
