Garcinia zeylanica

Garcinia zeylanica, commonly known as kokum, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. The fruit's rind contains a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which is used in dietary supplements.

Garcinia zeylanica is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
