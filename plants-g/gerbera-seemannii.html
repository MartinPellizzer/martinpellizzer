Gerbera seemannii

Gerbera seemannii, commonly known as the Cape gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features bright, colorful petals in shades of yellow, orange, red, and white. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Gerbera seemannii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
