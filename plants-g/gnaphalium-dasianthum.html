Gnaphalium dasianthum

Gnaphalium dasianthum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces numerous tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often arranged in a dense, spherical shape. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
