Globulostylis cuvieroides

Globulostylis cuvieroides, commonly known as the Cuvier's globular gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
