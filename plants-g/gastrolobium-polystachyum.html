Gastrolobium polystachyum

Gastrolobium polystachyum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Gastrolobium polystachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
