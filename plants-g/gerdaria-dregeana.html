Gerdaria dregeana

Gerdaria dregeana, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Gerdaria dregeana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
