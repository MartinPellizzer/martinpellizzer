Gomidesia miqueliana var. obtusata

Gomidesia miqueliana var. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaved gomidesia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the wet season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense ground cover in shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
