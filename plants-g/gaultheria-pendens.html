Gaultheria pendens

Gaultheria pendens, commonly known as the weeping wax myrtle, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and emit a pleasant aroma when crushed.

Gaultheria pendens thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
