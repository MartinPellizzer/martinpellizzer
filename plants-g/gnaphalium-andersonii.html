Gnaphalium andersonii

Gnaphalium andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to yellowish florets arranged in dense, feathery clusters, giving it a delicate, airy appearance. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a silvery-gray underside that helps reflect sunlight.
