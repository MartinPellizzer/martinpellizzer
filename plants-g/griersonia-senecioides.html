Griersonia senecioides

Griersonia senecioides, commonly known as the showy tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with white ray florets.

Griersonia senecioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
