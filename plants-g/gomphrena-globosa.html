Gomphrena globosa

Gomphrena globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena globosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
