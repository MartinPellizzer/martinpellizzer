Geraniospermum pilosum

Geraniospermum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy geraniospermum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warmer regions.
