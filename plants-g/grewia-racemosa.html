Grewia racemosa

Grewia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Grewia racemosa is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
