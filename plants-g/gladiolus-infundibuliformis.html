Gladiolus infundibuliformis

Gladiolus infundibuliformis, commonly known as the hooded gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-like leaves that are narrow and grassy in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a single, large flower that emerges from a leafy sheath, giving it its distinctive "hooded" look.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
