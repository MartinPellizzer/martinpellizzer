Gymnema kaniense

Gymnema kaniense, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to reduce the absorption of glucose in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. Gymnema kaniense is also valued for its use in traditional Ayurvedic practices for its purported health benefits.

Its latex contains compounds that have been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
