Gentianella amarella subsp. lingulata

Gentianella amarella subsp.

lingulata, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly toothed margin.

The subspecies lingulata is distinguished by its smaller, more elongated flowers compared to other subspecies of Gentianella amarella.
