Grabowskia schizocalyx

Grabowskia schizocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with six petals, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
