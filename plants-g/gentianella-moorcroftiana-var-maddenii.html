Gentianella moorcroftiana var. maddenii

Gentianella moorcroftiana var. maddenii, commonly known as the Kashmir gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vibrant flower color, making it a sought-after species in botanical gardens.
