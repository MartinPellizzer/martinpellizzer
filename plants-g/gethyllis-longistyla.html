Gethyllis longistyla

Gethyllis longistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ground orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and slender, often unbranched stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
