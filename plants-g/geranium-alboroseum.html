Geranium alboroseum

Geranium alboroseum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Geranium alboroseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
