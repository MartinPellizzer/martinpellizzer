Gaudinia fragilis var. glabriglumis

Gaudinia fragilis var. glabriglumis, commonly known as the silky gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, glabrous (smooth) leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. It has a short lifespan, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
