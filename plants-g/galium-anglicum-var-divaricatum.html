Galium anglicum var. divaricatum

Galium anglicum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often prostrate stems that form dense mats. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium anglicum var. divaricatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
