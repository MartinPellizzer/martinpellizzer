Goldbachia laevigata var. ikonnikovii

Goldbachia laevigata var. ikonnikovii, commonly known as the Ikonnikov's golden leaf, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that display a vibrant golden-green coloration, which intensifies under direct sunlight. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from pale yellow to orange, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
