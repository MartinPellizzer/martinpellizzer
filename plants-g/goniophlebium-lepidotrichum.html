Goniophlebium lepidotrichum

Goniophlebium lepidotrichum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Goniophlebium lepidotrichum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
