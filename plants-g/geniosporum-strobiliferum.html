Geniosporum strobiliferum

Geniosporum strobiliferum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures called strobiles, which are a key feature in its botanical classification. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
