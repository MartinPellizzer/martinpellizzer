Gastrolobium bilobum var. angustifolium

Gastrolobium bilobum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
