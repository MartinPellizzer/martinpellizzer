Gentiana rupicola f. grandiflora

Gentiana rupicola f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly the Pyrenees and the Alps. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
