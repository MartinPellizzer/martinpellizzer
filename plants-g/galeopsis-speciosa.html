Galeopsis speciosa

Galeopsis speciosa, commonly known as the showy horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Galeopsis speciosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
