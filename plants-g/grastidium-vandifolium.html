Grastidium vandifolium

Grastidium vandifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Grastidium vandifolium thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.
