Goldfussia crinita

Goldfussia crinita, commonly known as the hairy goldfussia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name and contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Goldfussia crinita is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice in garden landscapes.
