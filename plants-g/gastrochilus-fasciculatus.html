Gastrochilus fasciculatus

Gastrochilus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or along stream banks. The plant features a slender rhizome with several erect, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
