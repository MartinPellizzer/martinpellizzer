Gardenia fucata

Gardenia fucata, commonly known as the Hawaiian gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender pedicels and have a waxy texture.

Gardenia fucata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
