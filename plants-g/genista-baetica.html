Genista baetica

Genista baetica, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
