Geranium amurense

Geranium amurense, commonly known as the Siberian geranium, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
