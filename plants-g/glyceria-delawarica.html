Glyceria delawarica

Glyceria delawarica, commonly known as eastern glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Glyceria delawarica is also valued for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and poor soil conditions.
