Gaultheria akaensis

Gaultheria akaensis, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its berries, which are edible, contain high levels of aromatic compounds similar to those found in wintergreen oil.

Gaultheria akaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
