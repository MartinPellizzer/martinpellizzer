Gaultheria mundula

Gaultheria mundula, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, typically arranged in whorls around the stems.

Gaultheria mundula thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.
