Genista rosea

Genista rosea, commonly known as the rose bush broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. Genista rosea is typically shrub-like, with slender, often thorny stems and a dense growth habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.
