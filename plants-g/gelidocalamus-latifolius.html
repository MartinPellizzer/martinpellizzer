Gelidocalamus latifolius

Gelidocalamus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional crafts and as a food source in some regions.
