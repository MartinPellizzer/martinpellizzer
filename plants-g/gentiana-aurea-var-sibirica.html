Gentiana aurea var. sibirica

Gentiana aurea var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important species for supporting local pollinator populations.
