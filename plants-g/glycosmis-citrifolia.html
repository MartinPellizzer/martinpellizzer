Glycosmis citrifolia

Glycosmis citrifolia, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-scented leaves and small, purple fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, white, juicy pulp that is rich in antioxidants. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Its cultivation is primarily for the consumption of the fruit, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.
