Grislea

Grislea, commonly known as the "Ceylon cinnamon tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-like bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval-shaped berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, spicy scent.

Grislea is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
