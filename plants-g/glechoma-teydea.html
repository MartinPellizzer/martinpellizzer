Glechoma teydea

Glechoma teydea, commonly known as Turkish ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features creeping stems that form dense mats, often rooting at the nodes. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a coarsely toothed margin, and are covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
