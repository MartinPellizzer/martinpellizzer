Gentiana sibirica

Gentiana sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana sibirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
