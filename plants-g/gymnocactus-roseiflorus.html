Gymnocactus roseiflorus

Gymnocactus roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered naked cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact, globular shape and is well adapted to hot, dry environments. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Gymnocactus roseiflorus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
