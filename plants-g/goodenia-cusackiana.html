Goodenia cusackiana

Goodenia cusackiana, commonly known as the Cusack's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as South Australia and Western Australia. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Goodenia cusackiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
