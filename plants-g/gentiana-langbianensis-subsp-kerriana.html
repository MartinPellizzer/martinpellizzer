Gentiana langbianensis subsp. kerriana

Gentiana langbianensis subsp.

kerriana, commonly known as the Kerri's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty.
