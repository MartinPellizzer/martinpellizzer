Guizotia oleifera var. sativa

Guizotia oleifera var. sativa, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It features alternate, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found growing in disturbed soils. Its seeds are small, oblong, and contain a high amount of oil, making it valuable for both culinary and industrial uses.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
