Gymnadenia elata

Gymnadenia elata, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes at the top of the stem. Gymnadenia elata thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
