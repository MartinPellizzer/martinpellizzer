Gymnopogon elongatus

Gymnopogon elongatus, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Gymnopogon elongatus is an important forage plant for livestock and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
