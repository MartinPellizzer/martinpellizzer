Galium asperifolium var. obovatum

Galium asperifolium var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lanceolate to obovate in shape, with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
