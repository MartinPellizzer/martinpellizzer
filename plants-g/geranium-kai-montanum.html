Geranium kai-montanum

Geranium kai-montanum, commonly known as the Alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive five-lobed calyx.

Geranium kai-montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
