Gaillardia pedunculata

Gaillardia pedunculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright orange and red daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia pedunculata is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
