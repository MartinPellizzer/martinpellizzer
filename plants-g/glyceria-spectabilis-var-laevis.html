Glyceria spectabilis var. laevis

Glyceria spectabilis var. laevis, commonly known as the silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its soft, silky leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it often grows in dense mats along the edges of ponds and marshes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
