Grumilea plumeriifolia

Grumilea plumeriifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
