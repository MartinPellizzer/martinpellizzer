Geranium parviflorum var. humile

Geranium parviflorum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
