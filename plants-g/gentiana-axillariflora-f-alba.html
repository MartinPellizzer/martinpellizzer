Gentiana axillariflora f. alba

Gentiana axillariflora f. alba, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette.
