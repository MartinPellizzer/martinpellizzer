Gentiana heterostemon subsp. glabricaulis

Gentiana heterostemon subsp.

glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or purple flowers.

The subspecies is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
