Gentiana islandica

Gentiana islandica, commonly known as the island gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Gentiana islandica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
