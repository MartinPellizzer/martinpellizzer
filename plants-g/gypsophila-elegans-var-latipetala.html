Gypsophila elegans var. latipetala

Gypsophila elegans var. latipetala, commonly known as the baby's breath or laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pinkish, feathery flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its elegant, refined appearance and its close relation to other gypsophila species.
