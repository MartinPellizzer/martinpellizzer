Glicophyllum huachamacariense

Glicophyllum huachamacariense, commonly known as the Huachama cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent features that help it store water. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems forming a dense, compact structure.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
