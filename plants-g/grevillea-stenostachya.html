Grevillea stenostachya

Grevillea stenostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stalked grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Grevillea stenostachya is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
