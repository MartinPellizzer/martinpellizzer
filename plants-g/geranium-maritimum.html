Geranium maritimum

Geranium maritimum, commonly known as sea geranium, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of pink or red flowers. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh maritime conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and attractive addition to coastal gardens.
