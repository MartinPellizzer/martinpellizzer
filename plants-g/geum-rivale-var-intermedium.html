Geum rivale var. intermedium

Geum rivale var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate water avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist meadows and stream banks in Europe.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, blooming from June to August. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
