Glyceria japonica

Glyceria japonica, commonly known as Japanese glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Glyceria japonica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
