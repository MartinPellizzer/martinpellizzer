Gahnia wichurae

Gahnia wichurae, commonly known as the New Zealand tussock grass, is a perennial tussock grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that form a tufted appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

Gahnia wichurae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
