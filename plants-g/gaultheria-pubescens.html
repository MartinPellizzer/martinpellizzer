Gaultheria pubescens

Gaultheria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Gaultheria pubescens thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
