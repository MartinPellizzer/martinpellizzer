Graellsia stylosa

Graellsia stylosa, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Graellsia stylosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
