Gladiolus fraternus

Gladiolus fraternus, commonly known as the brother's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, colorful flower spikes. The flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and come in a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements.
