Glyceria voroschilovii

Glyceria voroschilovii, commonly known as the Voroschilov's glyceria, is a species of grass native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Glyceria voroschilovii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
