Gonolobus hatschbachii

Gonolobus hatschbachii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
