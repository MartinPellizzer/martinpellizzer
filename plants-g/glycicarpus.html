Glycicarpus

Glycicarpus, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants typically have compound leaves and produce small, yellow or green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Glycicarpus species are known for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
