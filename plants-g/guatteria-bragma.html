Guatteria bragma

Guatteria bragma, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
