Guenthera repanda subsp. humilis

Guenthera repanda subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the low guenthera, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions in South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and often have a pale green or white coloration.

The species is notable for its delicate structure and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
