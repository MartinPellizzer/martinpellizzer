Gastrochilus alatus

Gastrochilus alatus, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on the leaf bases, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Gastrochilus alatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
