Geum diversifolium

Geum diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and palmately lobed, with a distinctive white or pale yellow flower cluster at the top. Its flowers are small and clustered in a rounded head, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
