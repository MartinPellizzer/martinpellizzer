Gilia multicaulis var. peduncularis

Gilia multicaulis var. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
