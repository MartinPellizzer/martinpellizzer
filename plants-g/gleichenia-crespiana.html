Gleichenia crespiana

Gleichenia crespiana, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crested fronds that are often arched or recurved, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense mat of roots, which help anchor it to substrates and absorb nutrients.

Gleichenia crespiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
