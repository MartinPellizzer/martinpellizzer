Glycine pinnata

Glycine pinnata, commonly known as the evergreen climbing bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound, pinnately arranged leaves and its ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Glycine pinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its lush foliage and vigorous growth habit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
