Guibourtia fissicuspis

Guibourtia fissicuspis, commonly known as the African teak or African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural propagation in the wild.
