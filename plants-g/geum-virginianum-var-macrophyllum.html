Geum virginianum var. macrophyllum

Geum virginianum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as showy mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, with large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers are composed of numerous petal-like sepals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
