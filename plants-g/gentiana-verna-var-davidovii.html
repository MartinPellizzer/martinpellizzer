Gentiana verna var. davidovii

Gentiana verna var. davidovii, commonly known as the David's spring gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
