Galium verum var. vulgare

Galium verum var. vulgare, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in herbal remedies.
