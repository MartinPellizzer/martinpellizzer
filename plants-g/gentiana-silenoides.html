Gentiana silenoides

Gentiana silenoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and Carpathians. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Gentiana silenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
