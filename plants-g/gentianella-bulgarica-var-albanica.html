Gentianella bulgarica var. albanica

Gentianella bulgarica var. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and forest margins. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are striking, with blue to violet petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
