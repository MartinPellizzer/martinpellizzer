Glaucium luteum subsp. corniculatum

Glaucium luteum subsp.

corniculatum, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Papaver and Chelidonium.
