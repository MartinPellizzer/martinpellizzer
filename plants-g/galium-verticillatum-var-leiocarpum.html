Galium verticillatum var. leiocarpum

Galium verticillatum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features wiry, ascending stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.
