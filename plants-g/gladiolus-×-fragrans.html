Gladiolus × fragrans

Gladiolus × fragrans, commonly known as the sweet gladiolus, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, spike-like inflorescences of brightly colored flowers. The blooms, which range from white to deep red, are often fragrant, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative and commercially significant plant in horticulture.
