Genista persica

Genista persica, commonly known as Persian broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. Its leaves are small, pinnate, and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Genista persica is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
