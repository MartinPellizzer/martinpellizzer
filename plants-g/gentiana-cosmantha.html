Gentiana cosmantha

Gentiana cosmantha, commonly known as the Cosman's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana cosmantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
