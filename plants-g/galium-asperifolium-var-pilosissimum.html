Galium asperifolium var. pilosissimum

Galium asperifolium var. pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, stiff hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture due to their hairy surfaces. Small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
