Gypsophila wiedemannii

Gypsophila wiedemannii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Gypsophila wiedemannii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
