Grausa acaulis

Grausa acaulis, commonly known as the stemless bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with the leaves directly emerging from the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Grausa acaulis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
