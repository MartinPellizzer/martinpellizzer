Geissomeria cincinnata var. redacta

Geissomeria cincinnata var. redacta, commonly known as the redstem goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
