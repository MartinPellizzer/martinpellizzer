Goodyera ordiana

Goodyera ordiana, commonly known as the velvet leaf orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
