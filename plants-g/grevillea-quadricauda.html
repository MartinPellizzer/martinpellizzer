Grevillea quadricauda

Grevillea quadricauda, commonly known as the four-tailed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including honeyeaters and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
