Gerardia barbata

Gerardia barbata, commonly known as the barbed gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Gerardia barbata is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
