Glycine wightii var. mearnsii

Glycine wightii var. mearnsii, commonly known as Mearns' climbing bean, is a legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its twining habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional use in local communities.
