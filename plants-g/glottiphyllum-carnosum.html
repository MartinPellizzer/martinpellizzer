Glottiphyllum carnosum

Glottiphyllum carnosum, commonly known as the meaty glottiphyllum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a distinctive waxy texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
