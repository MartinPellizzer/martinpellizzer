Gongora rufescens

Gongora rufescens, commonly known as the red-brown orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety petals with a reddish-brown hue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Gongora rufescens is a sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
