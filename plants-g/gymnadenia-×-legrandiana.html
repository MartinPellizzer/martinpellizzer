Gymnadenia × legrandiana

Gymnadenia × legrandiana, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in areas with rich, organic soil. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip, and it blooms in late summer.

Gymnadenia × legrandiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
