Gladiolus sericeovillosus subsp. sericeovillosus

Gladiolus sericeovillosus subsp.

sericeovillosus, commonly known as the silky-hairy gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with large, brightly colored flowers that range from white to deep red. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
