Gentiana leptina

Gentiana leptina, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana leptina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
