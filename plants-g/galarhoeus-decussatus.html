Galarhoeus decussatus

Galarhoeus decussatus, commonly known as the clustered rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It is native to the mountainous areas of Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in high-altitude environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
