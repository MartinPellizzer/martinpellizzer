Galium obliquum subvar. myrianthum

Galium obliquum subvar. myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
