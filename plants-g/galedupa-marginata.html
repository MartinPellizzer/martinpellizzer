Galedupa marginata

Galedupa marginata, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
