Grobya bibrachiata

Grobya bibrachiata, commonly known as the two-armed grobya, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed floral structure, which sets it apart from other species in the genus. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The blooming period occurs during the wet season, and the flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.
