Grangea domingensis var. viscosa

Grangea domingensis var. viscosa, commonly known as the sticky grangea, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its viscid, sticky leaves that help in reducing water loss and deterring herbivores. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm, humid climates.
