Glyceria distans var. montana

Glyceria distans var. montana, commonly known as mountain glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streams, lakes, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
