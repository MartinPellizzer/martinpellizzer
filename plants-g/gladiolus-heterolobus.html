Gladiolus heterolobus

Gladiolus heterolobus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its botanical name reflects the variation in leaf shape, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
