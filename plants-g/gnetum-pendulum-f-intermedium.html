Gnetum pendulum f. intermedium

Gnetum pendulum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate climbing gymnosperm, is a unique plant species belonging to the Gnetophyta division.

It is characterized by its woody stems and scale-like leaves, which are typical of gymnosperms. This plant exhibits a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to ascend support structures. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant's reproductive structures include cones, and it plays a minor role in local ecosystems despite its ecological significance.
