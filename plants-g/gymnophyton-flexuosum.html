Gymnophyton flexuosum

Gymnophyton flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible gymnophyton, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the family Gymnophytaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are often described as having a prehistoric appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its structure is simple, with no true roots, stems, or leaves in the conventional sense, making it a subject of scientific interest.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Gymnophyton flexuosum is considered a living fossil and is protected in many areas.
