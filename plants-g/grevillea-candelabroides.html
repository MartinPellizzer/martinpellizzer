Grevillea candelabroides

Grevillea candelabroides, commonly known as the candlestick grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like inflorescence composed of multiple red or orange flowers arranged in a clustered, upright structure. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water once established.

Grevillea candelabroides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and dry garden settings.
