Gmelina rheedei

Gmelina rheedei, commonly known as the Indian teak or Malabar chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by fruiting bodies that are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Gmelina rheedei is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making, and it also has traditional medicinal uses.
