Gmelina asiatica f. inermis

Gmelina asiatica f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth gmelina, is a variant of the gmelina tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, cylindrical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
