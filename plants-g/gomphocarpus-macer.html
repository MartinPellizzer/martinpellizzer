Gomphocarpus macer

Gomphocarpus macer, commonly known as the red milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

Gomphocarpus macer is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and ecological importance to pollinators.
