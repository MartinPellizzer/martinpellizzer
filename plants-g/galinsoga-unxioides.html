Galinsoga unxioides

Galinsoga unxioides, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. It has hairy stems and produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.
