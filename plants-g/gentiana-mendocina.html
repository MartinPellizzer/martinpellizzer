Gentiana mendocina

Gentiana mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a vibrant display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
