Gilbertiodendron quinquejugum

Gilbertiodendron quinquejugum, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing flat, hard seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Gilbertiodendron quinquejugum plays an important role in local ecosystems and is used in traditional medicine.
