Gerardia maritima var. major

Gerardia maritima var. major, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, silver-blue leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. It is often found in dunes and along the edges of the sea, playing a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
