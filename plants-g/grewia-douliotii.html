Grewia douliotii

Grewia douliotii, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Grewia douliotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its berries in local gardens.
