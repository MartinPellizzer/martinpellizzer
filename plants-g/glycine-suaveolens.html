Glycine suaveolens

Glycine suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, spiral-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. Glycine suaveolens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and garden structures.
