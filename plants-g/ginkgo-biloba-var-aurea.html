Ginkgo biloba var. aurea

Ginkgo biloba var. aurea, commonly known as the golden ginkgo, is a variant of the ancient ginkgo tree known for its striking ornamental value.

This cultivar is distinguished by its bright yellow foliage, which turns a vibrant gold in the fall, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping. The leaves are fan-shaped and bipinnately divided, with a unique, almost prehistoric appearance. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in a wide range of soil types and is highly tolerant of pollution, making it well-suited for city environments.

The plant is also notable for its longevity and ability to survive in harsh conditions, contributing to its historical and ecological significance.
