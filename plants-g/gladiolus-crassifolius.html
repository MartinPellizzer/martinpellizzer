Gladiolus crassifolius

Gladiolus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are broader than those of most other gladiolus species. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its inflorescence consists of numerous spikelets, each containing several florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
