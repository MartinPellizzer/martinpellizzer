Georgina crocata

Georgina crocata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a robust, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

Georgina crocata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
