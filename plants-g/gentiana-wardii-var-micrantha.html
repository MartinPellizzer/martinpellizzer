Gentiana wardii var. micrantha

Gentiana wardii var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific microclimates.
