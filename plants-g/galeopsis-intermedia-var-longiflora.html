Galeopsis intermedia var. longiflora

Galeopsis intermedia var. longiflora, commonly known as long-flowered hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls and are usually pink or purplish in color.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
