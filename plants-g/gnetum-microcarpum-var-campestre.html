Gnetum microcarpum var. campestre

Gnetum microcarpum var. campestre, commonly known as the creeping gnetum, is a dioecious plant belonging to the family Gnetaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of scale-like leaves that are alternate along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, with separate male and female plants. Its seeds are enclosed in small, woody capsules, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical regions.
