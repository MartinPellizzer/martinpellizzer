Gentiana cephalodes var. andersonii

Gentiana cephalodes var. andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky slopes and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
