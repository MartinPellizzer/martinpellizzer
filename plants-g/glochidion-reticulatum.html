Glochidion reticulatum

Glochidion reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It attaches to host trees via haustoria, drawing water and nutrients from the host.

Glochidion reticulatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain insects and birds.
