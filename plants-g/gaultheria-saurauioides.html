Gaultheria saurauioides

Gaultheria saurauioides, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Gaultheria saurauioides is also noted for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
