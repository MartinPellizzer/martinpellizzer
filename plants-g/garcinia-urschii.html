Garcinia urschii

Garcinia urschii, commonly known as the dwarf garcinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. Garcinia urschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the Garcinia genus, which includes several species with medicinal and culinary uses.
