Grammocarpus

Grammocarpus, commonly known as the burrhead plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, seed-bearing structures called burrs, which aid in seed dispersal. The plants typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers. Grammocarpus species are often found in moist, temperate regions and are sometimes cultivated as ornamental plants.

Their botanical features make them interesting subjects for ecological and horticultural study.
