Genista tyrrhena

Genista tyrrhena, commonly known as the Sicilian broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in Mediterranean climates, characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and slender, thorny stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, and it plays a role in preventing soil erosion.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
