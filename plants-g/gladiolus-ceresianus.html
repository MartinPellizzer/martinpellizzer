Gladiolus ceresianus

Gladiolus ceresianus, commonly known as the Ceresian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and striking, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing showy inflorescences composed of multiple florets. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern, and the flowers come in a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
