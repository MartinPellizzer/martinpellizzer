Gentiana monnierioides

Gentiana monnierioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Gentiana monnierioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
