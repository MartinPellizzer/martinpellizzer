Grindelia rubricaulis var. platyphylla

Grindelia rubricaulis var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert and sagebrush habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
