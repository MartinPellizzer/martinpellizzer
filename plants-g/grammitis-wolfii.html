Grammitis wolfii

Grammitis wolfii, commonly known as the wolf's grammitis, is a small fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and creeping rhizomes that form dense mats. The fronds are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis wolfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
