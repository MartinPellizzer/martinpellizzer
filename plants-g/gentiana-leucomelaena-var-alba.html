Gentiana leucomelaena var. alba

Gentiana leucomelaena var. alba, commonly known as the white gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
