Goswamia eliminata

Goswamia eliminata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Indian gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by small, round fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
