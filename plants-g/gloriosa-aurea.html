Gloriosa aurea

Gloriosa aurea, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a striking tropical climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are adorned with intricate, curling petals. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces long, slender, tuberous roots that store nutrients. Its botanical name, "Gloriosa aurea," reflects the golden hue of its flowers and its radiant appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
