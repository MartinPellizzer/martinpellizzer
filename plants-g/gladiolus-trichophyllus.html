Gladiolus trichophyllus

Gladiolus trichophyllus, commonly known as the woolly gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinct appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
