Genista mingrelica

Genista mingrelica, commonly known as the Mingrelian broom, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region, particularly Georgia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and slender, woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista mingrelica is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
