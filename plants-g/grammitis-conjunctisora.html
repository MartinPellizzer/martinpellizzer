Grammitis conjunctisora

Grammitis conjunctisora, commonly known as the clustered maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Grammitis conjunctisora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
