Gladiolus verdickii

Gladiolus verdickii, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus verdickii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
