Gaultheria benomensis var. benomensis

Gaultheria benomensis var. benomensis, commonly known as the Benomens Island wintergreen, is a species native to the temperate rainforests of New Zealand.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or white in color. Its berries are red or black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
