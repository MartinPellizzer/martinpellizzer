Gymnadenia affinis

Gymnadenia affinis, commonly known as the common grass of fire, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear whorls of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Gymnadenia affinis is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
