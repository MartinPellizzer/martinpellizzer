Gerardia purpurea var. neoscotica

Gerardia purpurea var. neoscotica, commonly known as the Neoscotian gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish tinge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
