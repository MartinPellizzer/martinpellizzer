Gmelina fasciculiflora

Gmelina fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered gumbo, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Gmelina fasciculiflora is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
