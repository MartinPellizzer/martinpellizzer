Genista bottae

Genista bottae, commonly known as the creeping yellow broom, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems that form a mat-like growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover. Its leaves are small, narrow, and leathery, with a distinctive grayish-green color.

Genista bottae is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for rocky or coastal gardens.
