Gyaladenia rhodostachys

Gyaladenia rhodostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that are typically reddish in color, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gyaladenia rhodostachys is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
