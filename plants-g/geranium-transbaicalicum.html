Geranium transbaicalicum

Geranium transbaicalicum, commonly known as the Transbaikal geranium, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Geranium transbaicalicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar growing conditions.
