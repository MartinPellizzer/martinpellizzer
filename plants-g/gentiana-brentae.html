Gentiana brentae

Gentiana brentae, commonly known as the Brent's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana brentae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
