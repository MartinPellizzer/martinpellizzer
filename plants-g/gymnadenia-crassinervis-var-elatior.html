Gymnadenia crassinervis var. elatior

Gymnadenia crassinervis var. elatior, commonly known as the greater purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have prominent veins, which give it the species name *crassinervis*.

The flowers are clustered in a spike and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.
