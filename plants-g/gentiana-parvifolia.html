Gentiana parvifolia

Gentiana parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana parvifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
