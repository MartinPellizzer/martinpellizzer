Gossypium isabelum

Gossypium isabelum, commonly known as the Isabel cotton, is a species of cotton native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its fine, short fibers and is often cultivated for its high-quality textile properties. The plant produces fluffy bolls that contain the cotton fibers, which are harvested and processed for use in fabrics. It thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gossypium isabelum is valued for its durability and softness, making it a significant crop in certain regions.
