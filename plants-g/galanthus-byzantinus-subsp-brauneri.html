Galanthus byzantinus subsp. brauneri

Galanthus byzantinus subsp.

brauneri, commonly known as the Byzantine snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features delicate, nodding white flowers with green tips, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has strap-like, basal leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It prefers well-drained, moist soils in partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
