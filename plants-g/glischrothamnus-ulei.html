Glischrothamnus ulei

Glischrothamnus ulei, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to the desert landscape. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Glischrothamnus ulei plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
