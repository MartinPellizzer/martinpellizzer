Gymnopodium

Gymnopodium, commonly known as the smoothpod plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its pod-like fruits that lack the typical hairy or spiny covering found in many other legumes. The plant typically features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Gymnopodium species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, where they adapt to harsh environmental conditions.

These plants play a role in soil stabilization and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
