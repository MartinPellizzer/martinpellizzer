Gladiolus louiseae

Gladiolus louiseae, commonly known as the Louiseae Gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing large, trumpet-shaped blooms that are often used in floral arrangements. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This ornamental plant is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
