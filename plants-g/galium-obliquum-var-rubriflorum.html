Galium obliquum var. rubriflorum

Galium obliquum var. rubriflorum, commonly known as redflower wood-rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically reaches a height of 1 to 2 feet.
