Genista echinata

Genista echinata, commonly known as the thorny broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its thorny stems and small, leathery leaves are adaptations to its arid and rocky habitat.

Genista echinata is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
