Galium capense subsp. namaquense

Galium capense subsp.

namaquense, commonly known as theNamaqua bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
