Geissorhiza imbricata subsp. imbricata

Geissorhiza imbricata subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in summer.

The plant has fibrous roots and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
