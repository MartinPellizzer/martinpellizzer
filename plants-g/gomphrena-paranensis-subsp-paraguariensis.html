Gomphrena paranensis subsp. paraguariensis

Gomphrena paranensis subsp.

paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan feathery gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom during the dry season. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and prolonged flowering period. Gomphrena paranensis subsp.

paraguariensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
