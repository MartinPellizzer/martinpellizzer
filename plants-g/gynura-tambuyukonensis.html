Gynura tambuyukonensis

Gynura tambuyukonensis, commonly known as the Philippine nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves are edible but can cause skin irritation due to the presence of stinging hairs.
