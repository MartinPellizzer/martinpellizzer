Gnaphalium minimum var. intermedium

Gnaphalium minimum var. intermedium, commonly known as the slender everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its small, pale yellow to white flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
