Gloriosa rothschildiana

Gloriosa rothschildiana, commonly known as the Rothschild's lily, is a striking tropical climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy texture. Its stems are twining and can grow quite long, making it suitable for vertical gardening or as a hanging plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Colocasieae and is closely related to other members of the genus Gloriosa.
