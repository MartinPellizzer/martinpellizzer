Gnomonia ovina

Gnomonia ovina, commonly known as the woolly gnomon, is a member of the Rhytidhysteronaceae family.

It is a small, leafless plant that forms dense, cushion-like structures, typically found in alpine and arctic environments. The plant produces tiny, cup-shaped fruiting bodies that release spores, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are scale-like and tightly packed, reducing water loss in harsh climates.

Gnomonia ovina is often used in ecological studies to assess environmental conditions in high-altitude regions.
