Germanea concinna

Germanea concinna, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Germanea concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
