Genista acanthoclada var. fasciculata

Genista acanthoclada var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid environments.
