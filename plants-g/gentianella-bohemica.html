Gentianella bohemica

Gentianella bohemica, commonly known as Bohemian gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentianella bohemica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
