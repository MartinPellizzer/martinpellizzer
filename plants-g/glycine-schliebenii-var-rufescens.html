Glycine schliebenii var. rufescens

Glycine schliebenii var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed soybean, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
