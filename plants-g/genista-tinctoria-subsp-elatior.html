Genista tinctoria subsp. elatior

Genista tinctoria subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater yellow broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, thorny stems and small, pinnate leaves that are typical of the Genista genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its taller growth habit and more robust flowering compared to other members of the species.
