Gleditsia horrida subsp. caspica

Gleditsia horrida subsp.

caspica, commonly known as the Caspian honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, followed by woody, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
