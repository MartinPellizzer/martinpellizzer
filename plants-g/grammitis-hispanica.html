Grammitis hispanica

Grammitis hispanica, commonly known as Spanish grammitis, is a fern species native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rock crevices or forest floors. Its leaves are small, elongated, and have a finely divided structure, often with a bluish or grayish hue.
