Guilleminea densa var. humilis

Guilleminea densa var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf guilleminea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, and it is often used in xeriscaping.

Guilleminea densa var. humilis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
