Geranium magellanicum var. typicum

Geranium magellanicum var. typicum, commonly known as the Magellan geranium, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
