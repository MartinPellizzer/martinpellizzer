Gibbaeum velutinum

Gibbaeum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet gibbaeum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, spiky leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
