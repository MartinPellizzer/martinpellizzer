Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. philippinensis

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine blue-fruited holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
