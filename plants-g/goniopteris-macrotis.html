Goniopteris macrotis

Goniopteris macrotis, commonly known as the giant fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered in a dense layer of sori, which are the spore-producing structures. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark.

This unique feature makes it a striking and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
