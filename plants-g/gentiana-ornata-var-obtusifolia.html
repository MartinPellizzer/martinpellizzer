Gentiana ornata var. obtusifolia

Gentiana ornata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the obtuseleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rounded tips, giving it its specific epithet "obtusifolia.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
