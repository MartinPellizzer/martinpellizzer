Gladiolus strictiflorus

Gladiolus strictiflorus, commonly known as the rigid-flowered gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of showy, often brightly colored flowers. Its inflorescence consists of multiple florets arranged in a spike, with each flower typically having three petals and three sepals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
