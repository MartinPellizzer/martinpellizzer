Goeppertia cylindrica

Goeppertia cylindrica, commonly known as the cigar plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a cigar, hence its common name. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia cylindrica is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
