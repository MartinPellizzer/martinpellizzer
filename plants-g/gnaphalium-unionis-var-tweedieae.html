Gnaphalium unionis var. tweedieae

Gnaphalium unionis var. tweedieae, commonly known as the Tweedie's goldenweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small yellow flowers. Native to New Zealand, this variety thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora.
