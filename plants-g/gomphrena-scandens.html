Gomphrena scandens

Gomphrena scandens, commonly known as the climbing gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, often climbing or spreading along the ground. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Gomphrena scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
