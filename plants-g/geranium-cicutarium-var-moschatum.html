Geranium cicutarium var. moschatum

Geranium cicutarium var. moschatum, commonly known as the musk geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
