Garcinia nigricans

Garcinia nigricans, commonly known as the black garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and research.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
