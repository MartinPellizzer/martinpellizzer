Glyceria maxima var. americana

Glyceria maxima var. americana, commonly known as American glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

American glyceria plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
