Gomphrena crassifolia

Gomphrena crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are thick and succulent, often with a bluish or grayish hue, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Gomphrena crassifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant flowers and hardy characteristics.
