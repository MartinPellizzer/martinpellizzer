Gerascanthus parvifolius

Gerascanthus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
