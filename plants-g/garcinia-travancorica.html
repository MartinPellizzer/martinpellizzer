Garcinia travancorica

Garcinia travancorica, commonly known as Kottakad garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright orange, berry-like fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia travancorica is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
