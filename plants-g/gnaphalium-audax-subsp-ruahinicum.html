Gnaphalium audax subsp. ruahinicum

Gnaphalium audax subsp.

ruahinicum, commonly known as the Ruahine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white to pale yellow petals, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and montane regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
