Gymnocalycium pugionacanthum var. stejskalii

Gymnocalycium pugionacanthum var. stejskalii, commonly known as the Stejskal's cholla, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of spines and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces fleshy, green fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
