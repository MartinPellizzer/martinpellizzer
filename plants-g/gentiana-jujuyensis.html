Gentiana jujuyensis

Gentiana jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing vibrant blue petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
