Genista rhodopea

Genista rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain seeds.

Genista rhodopea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
