Geum vicanum

Geum vicanum, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geum vicanum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
