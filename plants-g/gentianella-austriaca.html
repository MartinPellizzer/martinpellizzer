Gentianella austriaca

Gentianella austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive yellow corona. It blooms from June to August, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
