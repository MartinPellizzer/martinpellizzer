Geranium beaufortianum

Geranium beaufortianum, commonly known as the Beaufort geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery-blue hue. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice among gardeners.
