Gaultheria Yunnanensis

Gaultheria yunnanensis is a rare evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is known for its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark blue berries that are rich in salicylic acid, giving them a unique medicinal property. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
