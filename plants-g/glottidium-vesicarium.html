Glottidium vesicarium

Glottidium vesicarium, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inflated pods that resemble bladders, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of the western United States. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in arid environments.
