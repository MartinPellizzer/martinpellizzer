Grindelia patagonica

Grindelia patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian gumweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Grindelia patagonica is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
