Galium melanantherum var. scabrifolium

Galium melanantherum var. scabrifolium, commonly known as roughleaf wood-sorrel, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves with a rough, serrated texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
