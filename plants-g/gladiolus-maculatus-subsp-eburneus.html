Gladiolus maculatus subsp. eburneus

Gladiolus maculatus subsp.

eburneus, commonly known as the white-veined gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with white petals and prominent red or pink veins.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped leaves that are broad and lanceolate. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, often with a mix of colors, and it blooms during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
