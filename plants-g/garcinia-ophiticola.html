Garcinia ophiticola

Garcinia ophiticola, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Garcinia ophiticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.
