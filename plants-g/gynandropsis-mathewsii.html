Gynandropsis mathewsii

Gynandropsis mathewsii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a schizocarp, splitting into two one-seeded parts when mature.

Gynandropsis mathewsii is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.
