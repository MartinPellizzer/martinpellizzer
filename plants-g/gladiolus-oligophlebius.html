Gladiolus oligophlebius

Gladiolus oligophlebius, commonly known as the narrow-veined gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
