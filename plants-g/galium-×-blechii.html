Galium × blechii

Galium × blechii, commonly known as the hybrid wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Galium verum (common wood sorrel) and Galium aparine (sticklepop). The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters.

Galium × blechii is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
