Gaura filiformis var. munzii

Gaura filiformis var. munzii, commonly known as Munz's gaura, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that often arch gracefully, supporting clusters of small, pale pink to white, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, contributing to a delicate, airy appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
