Gentiana malyschevii

Gentiana malyschevii, commonly known as the Malyschev's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Gentiana malyschevii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
