Gentiana calycosa

Gentiana calycosa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or meadow environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana calycosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
