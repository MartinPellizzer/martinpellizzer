Galium boreale f. hyssopifolium

Galium boreale f. hyssopifolium, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a rare and distinctive variant of the bedstraw genus.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
