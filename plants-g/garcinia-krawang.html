Garcinia krawang

Garcinia krawang, commonly known as mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant white flowers that lead to the iconic red, segmented fruit. The fruit's pericarp, or outer layer, contains a high concentration of xanthones, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Garcinia krawang thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
