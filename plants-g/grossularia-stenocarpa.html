Grossularia stenocarpa

Grossularia stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
