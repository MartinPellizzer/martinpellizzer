Grevillea subargentea

Grevillea subargentea, commonly known as the silver-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea subargentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
