Grewia benguellensis

Grewia benguellensis, commonly known as the coastal growea, is a shrub native to southern Africa, particularly found along coastal regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Grewia benguellensis is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
