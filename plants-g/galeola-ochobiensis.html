Galeola ochobiensis

Galeola ochobiensis, commonly known as the Ochobiensis orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in dry woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
