Grewia coerulea

Grewia coerulea, commonly known as the blue globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils.

Grewia coerulea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to different climates.
