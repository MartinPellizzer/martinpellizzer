Gnaphalium sylvaticum subsp. rectum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum subsp.

rectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and linear, often hairy leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a notable component of its native ecosystems.
