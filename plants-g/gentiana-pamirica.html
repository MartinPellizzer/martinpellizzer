Gentiana pamirica

Gentiana pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with prominent blue petals.

Gentiana pamirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
