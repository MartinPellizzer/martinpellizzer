Gentiana plurisetosa

Gentiana plurisetosa, commonly known as the many-bristled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and covered in short, bristly hairs.

Gentiana plurisetosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
