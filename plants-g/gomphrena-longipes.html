Gomphrena longipes

Gomphrena longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts that resemble small flames or plumes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful, papery bracts. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and unique appearance.

Gomphrena longipes thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
