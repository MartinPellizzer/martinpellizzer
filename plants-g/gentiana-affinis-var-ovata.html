Gentiana affinis var. ovata

Gentiana affinis var. ovata, commonly known as the oval gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
