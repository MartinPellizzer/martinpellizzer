Gymnopogon longifolius

Gymnopogon longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Gymnopogon longifolius is typically found in temperate climates and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect populations.
