Geranium tuberosum var. linearifolium

Geranium tuberosum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive linear form. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists.
