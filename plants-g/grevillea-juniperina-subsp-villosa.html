Grevillea juniperina subsp. villosa

Grevillea juniperina subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and public landscapes.
