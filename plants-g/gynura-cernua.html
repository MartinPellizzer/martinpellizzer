Gynura cernua

Gynura cernua, commonly known as the hanging gynura or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Gynura cernua thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
