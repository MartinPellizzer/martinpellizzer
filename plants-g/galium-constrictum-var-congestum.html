Galium constrictum var. congestum

Galium constrictum var. congestum, commonly known as clustered bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically prostrate or ascending, and it often forms dense mats in moist, shaded environments.

The species is characterized by its fibrous roots and is commonly found in woodlands and along stream banks.
