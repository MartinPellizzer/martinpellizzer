Gentianella quinquefolia subsp. quinquefolia

Gentianella quinquefolia subsp.

quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into five lobes. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
