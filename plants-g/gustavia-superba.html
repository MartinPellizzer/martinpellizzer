Gustavia superba

Gustavia superba, commonly known as the noble hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central boss. The plant has palm-like leaves that are broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and landscapes.

Gustavia superba is also valued for its ability to grow in coastal areas, making it a resilient addition to maritime environments.
