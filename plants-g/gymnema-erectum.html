Gymnema erectum

Gymnema erectum, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Gymnema erectum is notable for its ability to reduce glucose absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicine.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
