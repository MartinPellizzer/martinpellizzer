Gnidia phyllodinea

Gnidia phyllodinea, commonly known as the yellow wild pear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
