Gagea granatellii var. angustifolia

Gagea granatellii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats. Its bulbs are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is noted for its early spring blooming habit and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
