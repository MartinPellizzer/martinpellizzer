Geum borisii

Geum borisii, commonly known as Borris' avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky habitats, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its showy flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are pink to red in color and have five petals.

Geum borisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
