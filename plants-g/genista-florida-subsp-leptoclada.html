Genista florida subsp. leptoclada

Genista florida subsp.

leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
