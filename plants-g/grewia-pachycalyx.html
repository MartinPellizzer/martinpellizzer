Grewia pachycalyx

Grewia pachycalyx, commonly known as the thick-calyx grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Grewia pachycalyx is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
