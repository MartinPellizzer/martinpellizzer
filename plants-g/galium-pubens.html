Galium pubens

Galium pubens, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical profile.
