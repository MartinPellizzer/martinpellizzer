Georgina frustranea var. coccinea

Georgina frustranea var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered georgina, is a striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and woody stems that can become somewhat branched with age.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
