Gilia tenuiflora var. caruifolia

Gilia tenuiflora var. caruifolia, commonly known as the narrow-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color, with five petals and a distinct, narrow corolla. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
