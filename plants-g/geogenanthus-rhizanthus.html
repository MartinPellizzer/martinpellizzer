Geogenanthus rhizanthus

Geogenanthus rhizanthus, commonly known as the underground candleplant, is a unique species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces a single, candle-like stem that emerges above ground, topped with a cluster of small, white flowers. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, storing nutrients and water, which allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its rarity and specialized habitat, it is considered an endangered species and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
