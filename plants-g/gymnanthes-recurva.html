Gymnanthes recurva

Gymnanthes recurva, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped with a glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by the ornamental berries.

Gymnanthes recurva is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
