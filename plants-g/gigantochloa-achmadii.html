Gigantochloa achmadii

Gigantochloa achmadii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Its rapid growth makes it suitable for erosion control and as a bioengineering tool in ecological restoration projects.
