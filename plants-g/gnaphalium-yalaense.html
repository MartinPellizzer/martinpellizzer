Gnaphalium yalaense

Gnaphalium yalaense, commonly known as the Yala everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the wet zone of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, showy composite flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny florets arranged in a dense, spherical cluster, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for garden use.
