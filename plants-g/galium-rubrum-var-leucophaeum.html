Galium rubrum var. leucophaeum

Galium rubrum var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually white or pale green in color. The plant produces tiny white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium rubrum var. leucophaeum is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and specialized species.
