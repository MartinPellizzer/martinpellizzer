Genosiris lanata

Genosiris lanata, commonly known as the woolly genosiris, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
