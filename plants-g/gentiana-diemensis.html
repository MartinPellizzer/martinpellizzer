Gentiana diemensis

Gentiana diemensis, commonly known as the Diemen's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
