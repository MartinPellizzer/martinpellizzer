Gomidesia kunthiana

Gomidesia kunthiana, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
