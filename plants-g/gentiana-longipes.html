Gentiana longipes

Gentiana longipes, commonly known as the long-footed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Gentiana longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
