Gymnocalycium cabreraense

Gymnocalycium cabreraense, commonly known as the Cabrera's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but highly fragrant. Gymnocalycium cabreraense is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

It is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
