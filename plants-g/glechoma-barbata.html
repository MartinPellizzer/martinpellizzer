Glechoma barbata

Glechoma barbata, commonly known as hairy ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma barbata is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
