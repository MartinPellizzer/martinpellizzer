Gymnema parvifolium

Gymnema parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Gymnema parvifolium is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its leaves contain compounds that may help in managing blood sugar levels, making it of interest in modern pharmacological research.
