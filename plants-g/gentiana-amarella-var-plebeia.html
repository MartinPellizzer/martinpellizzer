Gentiana amarella var. plebeia

Gentiana amarella var. plebeia, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
