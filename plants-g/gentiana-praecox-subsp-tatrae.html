Gentiana praecox subsp. tatrae

Gentiana praecox subsp.

tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The Tatra gentian is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
