Gloxinia gloxiniiflora

Gloxinia gloxiniiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are deep green and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the summer months. Gloxiniiflora prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
