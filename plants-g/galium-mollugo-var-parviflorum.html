Galium mollugo var. parviflorum

Galium mollugo var. parviflorum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium mollugo var. parviflorum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.
