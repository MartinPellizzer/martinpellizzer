Gnaphalium ruahinicum

Gnaphalium ruahinicum, commonly known as the Ruahine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across open habitats. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
