Garcinia stipulata var. acutifolia

Garcinia stipulata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with acute tips and a smooth, bark-like trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
