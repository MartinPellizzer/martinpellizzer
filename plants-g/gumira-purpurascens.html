Gumira purpurascens

Gumira purpurascens, commonly known as the purple gumira, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

The gumira purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in traditional landscapes.
