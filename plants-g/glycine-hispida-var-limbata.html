Glycine hispida var. limbata

Glycine hispida var. limbata, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Glycine hispida var. limbata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
