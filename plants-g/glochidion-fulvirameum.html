Glochidion fulvirameum

Glochidion fulvirameum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed glabrous vine, is a tropical shrub or climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
