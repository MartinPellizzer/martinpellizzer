Glandularia alejandrana

Glandularia alejandrana, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs that secrete oils, giving it a distinct aromatic quality.

Glandularia alejandrana is also known for its adaptability to arid environments and its use in traditional medicine.
