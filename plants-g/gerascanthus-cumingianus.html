Gerascanthus cumingianus

Gerascanthus cumingianus, commonly known as the Cuming's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright stems and basal leaves that are often heart-shaped or rounded. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gerascanthus cumingianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
