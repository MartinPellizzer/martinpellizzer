Geracium cydoniifolium

Geracium cydoniifolium, commonly known as the apple-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that resemble those of an apple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
