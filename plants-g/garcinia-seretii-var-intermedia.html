Garcinia seretii var. intermedia

Garcinia seretii var. intermedia, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-brown rind and segmented, fleshy fruit that contains a white, segmented pulp. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit is valued for its unique flavor and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
