Galactia rudolphioides

Galactia rudolphioides, commonly known as the redstem burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Galactia rudolphioides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
