Glischrocaryon aureum

Glischrocaryon aureum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cup-like arrangements. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
