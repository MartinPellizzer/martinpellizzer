Gastrolobium whicherense

Gastrolobium whicherense, commonly known as the Whicheran wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. The plant has compound leaves with thread-like leaflets and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Gastrolobium whicherense is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun.
