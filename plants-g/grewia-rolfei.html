Grewia rolfei

Grewia rolfei, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy areas.

Grewia rolfei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
