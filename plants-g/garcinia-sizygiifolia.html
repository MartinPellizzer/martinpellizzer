Garcinia sizygiifolia

Garcinia sizygiifolia, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. The tree bears edible fruits that are yellow-orange when ripe, containing a single seed surrounded by a sour, pulp-like aril. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
