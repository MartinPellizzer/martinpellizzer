Gymnocalycium kieslingii

Gymnocalycium kieslingii, commonly known as the Kiesling's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
