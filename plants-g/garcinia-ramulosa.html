Garcinia ramulosa

Garcinia ramulosa, commonly known as the red-fruited garcinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Garcinia ramulosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
