Giulianettia carnea

Giulianettia carnea, commonly known as the redstem sedum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish or pinkish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
