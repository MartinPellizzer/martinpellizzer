Gentlea auriculata

Gentlea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Gentlea auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
