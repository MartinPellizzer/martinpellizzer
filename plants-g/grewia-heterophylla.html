Grewia heterophylla

Grewia heterophylla, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy fruits that resemble berries. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
