Gratiola minima

Gratiola minima, commonly known as the lesser yellow crowfoot, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features delicate, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a cluster at the tip of the stem. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually heart-shaped with a hairy texture.

Gratiola minima is valued in traditional medicine for its potential diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
