Genista sachokiana

Genista sachokiana, commonly known as the Persian broom, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, often spiny stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Genista sachokiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
