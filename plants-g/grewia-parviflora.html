Grewia parviflora

Grewia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered grewia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spherical fruits that turn red when mature, attracting birds and other wildlife.

Grewia parviflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
