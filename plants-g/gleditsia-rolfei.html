Gleditsia rolfei

Gleditsia rolfei, commonly known as Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Gleditsia rolfei is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which enhance soil fertility.
