Galarhoeus esula subsp. maackii

Galarhoeus esula subsp.

maackii, commonly known as the Maack's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
