Gardenia jasminoides f. albomarginata

Gardenia jasminoides f. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin gardenia, is a cultivar of the gardenia plant characterized by its striking white-edged leaves.

It is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia, known for its glossy, dark green foliage and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper pruning and fertilization.

The white-margin variety is particularly prized for its unique leaf patterns and elegant floral display.
