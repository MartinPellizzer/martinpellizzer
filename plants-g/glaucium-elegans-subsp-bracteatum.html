Glaucium elegans subsp. bracteatum

Glaucium elegans subsp.

bracteatum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
