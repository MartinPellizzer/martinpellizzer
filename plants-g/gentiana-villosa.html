Gentiana villosa

Gentiana villosa, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are hairy, giving it the common name, and it often forms dense clumps.

Gentiana villosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
