Geraniospermum incrassatum

Geraniospermum incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in dry climates.
