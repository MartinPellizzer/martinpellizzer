Galphimia hirsuta f. rufa

Galphimia hirsuta f. rufa, commonly known as the red-hairy galphimia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Galphimia hirsuta f. rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
