Geum × velenovskyi

Geum × velenovskyi, commonly known as the velvetleaf buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained, loamy soils in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Geum × velenovskyi is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates and its low maintenance requirements.
