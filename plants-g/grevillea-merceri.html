Grevillea merceri

Grevillea merceri, commonly known as the Geraldton waxplant, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like yellow or orange flowers that appear in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white wax-like coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea merceri is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
