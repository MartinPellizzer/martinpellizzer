Gagea nakaiana

Gagea nakaiana, commonly known as the Nakaiana squill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate white or pale blue flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and survival in harsh alpine conditions.

Gagea nakaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and shady woodland settings.
