Gladiolus salmoneus

Gladiolus salmoneus, commonly known as the salmon gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and salmon, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
