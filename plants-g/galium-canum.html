Galium canum

Galium canum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium canum is commonly found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
