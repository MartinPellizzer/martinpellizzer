Gireoudia conchifolia var. scutellata

Gireoudia conchifolia var. scutellata, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and botanical study.
