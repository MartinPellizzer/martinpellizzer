Galium caudatum var. chium

Galium caudatum var. chium, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom during the summer months.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
