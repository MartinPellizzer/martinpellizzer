Gentianella lutescens subsp. tatrae

Gentianella lutescens subsp.

tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Tatra Mountains in Central Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.
