Galarhoeus pekinensis var. attenuatus

Galarhoeus pekinensis var. attenuatus, commonly known as the Peking thistle, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
