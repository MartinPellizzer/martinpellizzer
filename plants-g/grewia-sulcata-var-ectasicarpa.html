Grewia sulcata var. ectasicarpa

Grewia sulcata var. ectasicarpa, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, star-shaped berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for both humans and animals.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
