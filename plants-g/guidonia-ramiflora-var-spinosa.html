Guidonia ramiflora var. spinosa

Guidonia ramiflora var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny guidonia, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It features dense, thorny branches that provide structural support and defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
