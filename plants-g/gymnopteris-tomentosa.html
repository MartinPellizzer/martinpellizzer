Gymnopteris tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of soft, grayish-white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Gymnopteris tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage.
