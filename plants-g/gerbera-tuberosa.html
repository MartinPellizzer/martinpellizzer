Gerbera tuberosa

Gerbera tuberosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright colors such as red, yellow, and white. The plant has a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces tuberous roots that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gerbera tuberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements.
