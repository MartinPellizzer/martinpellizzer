Gypsocallis mediterranea

Gypsocallis mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean gypsocallis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing delicate white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Gypsocallis mediterranea thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to mild, temperate climates.
