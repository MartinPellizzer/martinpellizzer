Gangila pentaphylla

Gangila pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed gangila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
