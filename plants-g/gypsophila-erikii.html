Gypsophila erikii

Gypsophila erikii, commonly known as the Persian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, rounded flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gypsophila erikii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
