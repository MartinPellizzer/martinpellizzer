Gnaphalium aegyptiacum

Gnaphalium aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery appearance with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has a creeping growth habit, with numerous stems that form a mat-like structure.

Gnaphalium aegyptiacum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
