Galium tenue

Galium tenue, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in whorls along the stems, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium tenue is commonly found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks.
