Gnaphalium hortense

Gnaphalium hortense, commonly known as garden cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with numerous small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow composite flowers, which are composed of many tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Gnaphalium hortense is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which releases a pleasant scent when crushed.
