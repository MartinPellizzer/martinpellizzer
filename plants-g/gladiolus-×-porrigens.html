Gladiolus × porrigens

Gladiolus × porrigens, commonly known as the hybrid gladiolus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Gladiolus italicus and Gladiolus galaticus, leading to a diverse range of color variations. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sword-shaped leaves and tall, erect flower spikes. Its flowers come in a wide array of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is native to South Africa and is cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and floral arrangements.
