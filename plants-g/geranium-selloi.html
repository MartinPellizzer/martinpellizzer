Geranium selloi

Geranium selloi, commonly known as the Sello's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Geranium selloi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
