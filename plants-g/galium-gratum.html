Galium gratum

Galium gratum, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its aromatic leaves and flowers are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent in foods and beverages.

Galium gratum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
