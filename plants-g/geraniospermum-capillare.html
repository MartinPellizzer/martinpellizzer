Geraniospermum capillare

Geraniospermum capillare, commonly known as the silky geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
