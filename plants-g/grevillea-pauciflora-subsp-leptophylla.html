Grevillea pauciflora subsp. leptophylla

Grevillea pauciflora subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native landscapes.
