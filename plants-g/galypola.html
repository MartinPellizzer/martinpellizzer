Galypola

Galypola, commonly known as the Australian native shrub, is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional bush medicine by Indigenous Australians.

Galypola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
