Gomphrena eriophylla

Gomphrena eriophylla, commonly known as the silver feather plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gomphrena eriophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
