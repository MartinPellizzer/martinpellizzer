Galeopsis michelianae

Galeopsis michelianae, commonly known as Micheli's horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Galeopsis michelianae is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
