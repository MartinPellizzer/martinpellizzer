Genipa dumetorum

Genipa dumetorum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wild genipapo, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The fruit is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has a unique, tart flavor.
