Gymnadenia × teppneri

Gymnadenia × teppneri, commonly known as the hybrid grass pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Gymnadenia coryphinea and Gymnadenia odoratissima, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in certain regions.

Gymnadenia × teppneri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
