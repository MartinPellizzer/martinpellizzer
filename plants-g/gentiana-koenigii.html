Gentiana koenigii

Gentiana koenigii, commonly known as the Koenig's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana koenigii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
