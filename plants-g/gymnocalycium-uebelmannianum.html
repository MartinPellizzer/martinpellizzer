Gymnocalycium uebelmannianum

Gymnocalycium uebelmannianum, commonly known as the Uebelmann's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the growing season. It is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, succulent stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
