Gypsophila fastigiata

Gypsophila fastigiata, commonly known as the upright gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pink or white flowers that resemble tiny bells. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
