Gentianella punensis

Gentianella punensis, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a fine layer of hair.

Gentianella punensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
