Glyceria antarctica

Glyceria antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic glyceria, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in wet, marshy environments, often found in Antarctica and sub-Antarctic regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. Its growth is adapted to withstand extreme cold and low nutrient conditions.

Glyceria antarctica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its harsh environment.
