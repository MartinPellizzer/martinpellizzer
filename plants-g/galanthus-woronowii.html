Galanthus woronowii

Galanthus woronowii, commonly known as the Caucasian daffodil, is a species of bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces delicate, white flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas or along rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.

Galanthus woronowii is also noted for its cold tolerance and ability to naturalize over time.
