Galianthe verbenoides

Galianthe verbenoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Galianthe verbenoides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
