Gladiolus subbiflorus

Gladiolus subbiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces spikes of brightly colored, often pink or red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
