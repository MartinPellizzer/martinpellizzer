Glycine viscosa

Glycine viscosa, commonly known as hairy peanut vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Glycine viscosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
