Gleditsia koraiensis

Gleditsia koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
