Geranium pinetophilum

Geranium pinetophilum, commonly known as the pine forest geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils within pine forests and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of pink to red flowers from late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Geranium pinetophilum plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators like bees and butterflies.
