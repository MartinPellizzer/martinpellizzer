Galium × jarynae

Galium × jarynae, commonly known as the hybrid wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Galium verum (common wood sorrel) and Galium trifidum (three-parted wood sorrel), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species typically features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Galium × jarynae is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
