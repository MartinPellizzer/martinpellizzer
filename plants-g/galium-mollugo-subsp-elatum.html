Galium mollugo subsp. elatum

Galium mollugo subsp.

elatum, commonly known as tall bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
