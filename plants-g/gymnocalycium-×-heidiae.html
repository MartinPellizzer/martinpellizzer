Gymnocalycium × heidiae

Gymnocalycium × heidiae, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, spiny stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant is a hybrid between Gymnocalycium mihantha and Gymnocalycium pachyanthum, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions, while its flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
