Gentiana curviflora

Gentiana curviflora, commonly known as the bent-flower gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in rocky or alpine habitats. Gentiana curviflora is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, spurred flowers.

It is often found in meadows and open woodlands at high elevations.
