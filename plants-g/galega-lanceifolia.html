Galega lanceifolia

Galega lanceifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential in treating various ailments.
