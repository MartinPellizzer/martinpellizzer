Gynandropsis pentaphylla

Gynandropsis pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Gynandropsis pentaphylla is adapted to arid environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.
