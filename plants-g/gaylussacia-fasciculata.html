Gaylussacia fasciculata

Gaylussacia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue to black berry that ripens in the summer, providing food for various wildlife species.

The leaves are alternate, oval, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
