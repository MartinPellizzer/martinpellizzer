Goeppertia truncata

Goeppertia truncata, commonly known as the Zebra Canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that often display distinctive black and green mottling. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Goeppertia truncata is a perennial that is often used in tropical gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
