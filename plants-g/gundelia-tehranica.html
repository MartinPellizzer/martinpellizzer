Gundelia tehranica

Gundelia tehranica, commonly known as the Tehran dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flower heads and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing features such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Gundelia tehranica is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
