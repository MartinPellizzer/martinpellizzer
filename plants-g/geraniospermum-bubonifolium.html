Geraniospermum bubonifolium

Geraniospermum bubonifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved geraniosperm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Geraniospermum bubonifolium is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
