Geum chilense var. miniatum

Geum chilense var. miniatum, commonly known as the miniature Chilean avens, is a compact perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
