Guajava riparia

Guajava riparia, commonly known as the river guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows near rivers and moist soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits with a pinkish skin. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
