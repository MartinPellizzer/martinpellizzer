Gaudichaudia lanata

Gaudichaudia lanata, commonly known as the woolly gaudichaudia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gaudichaudia lanata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
