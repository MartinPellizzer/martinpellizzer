Gaultheria adenothrix f. leucocarpa

Gaultheria adenothrix f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited wintergreen, is a botanical variant of the wintergreen genus Gaultheria.

It is native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its most distinctive feature is the production of white or pale berries, which contrast with the dark foliage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
