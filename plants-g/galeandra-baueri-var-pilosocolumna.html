Galeandra baueri var. pilosocolumna

Galeandra baueri var. pilosocolumna, commonly known as the hairy columned galeandra, is a unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and somewhat elusive species in the orchid world.
