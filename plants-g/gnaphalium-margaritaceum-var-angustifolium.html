Gnaphalium margaritaceum var. angustifolium

Gnaphalium margaritaceum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
