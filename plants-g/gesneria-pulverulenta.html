Gesneria pulverulenta

Gesneria pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive powdery or dusty appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria pulverulenta is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental value and ease of care.
