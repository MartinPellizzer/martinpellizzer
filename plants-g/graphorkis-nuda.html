Graphorkis nuda

Graphorkis nuda, commonly known as the naked graphorkis, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems and its lack of leaves, which is why it is called "naked.". Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a spike.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.
