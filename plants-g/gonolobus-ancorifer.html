Gonolobus ancorifer

Gonolobus ancorifer, commonly known as the anchor vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Gonolobus ancorifer is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
