Gentiana brachysepala

Gentiana brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and forest understories, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are characterized by their short, broad sepals and a deep blue corolla with five lobes.

Gentiana brachysepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
