Genetyllis agathosmoides

Genetyllis agathosmoides, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structure and a distinctive silvery appearance due to the presence of a dense layer of fine, white fungal hyphae. The thallus is typically pale green to grayish in color, and it often grows on bark, stone, or soil in shaded environments. This lichen is known for its slow growth and ability to colonize nutrient-poor substrates.

It plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of air quality.
