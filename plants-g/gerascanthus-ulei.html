Gerascanthus ulei

Gerascanthus ulei, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing structure and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Gerascanthus ulei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
