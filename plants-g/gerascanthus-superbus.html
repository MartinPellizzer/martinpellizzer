Gerascanthus superbus

Gerascanthus superbus, commonly known as the grandiose geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
