Gentiana elmeriana

Gentiana elmeriana, commonly known as Elmer's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is part of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana elmeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
