Gypsophila acutifolia lusus stevenii

Gypsophila acutifolia lusus stevenii, commonly known as the spiked gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements due to its elegant appearance.
