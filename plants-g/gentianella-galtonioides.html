Gentianella galtonioides

Gentianella galtonioides, commonly known as the Galton's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianella galtonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
