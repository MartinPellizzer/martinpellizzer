Gladiolus aequinoctialis var. tomentosus

Gladiolus aequinoctialis var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, sword-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant features a dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
