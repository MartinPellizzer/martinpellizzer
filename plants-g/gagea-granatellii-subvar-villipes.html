Gagea granatellii subvar. villipes

Gagea granatellii subvar. villipes, commonly known as the granate gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is typically found at high elevations.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
