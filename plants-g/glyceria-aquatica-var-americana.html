Glyceria aquatica var. americana

Glyceria aquatica var. americana, commonly known as American watergrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often submerged or floating. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
