Geum glutinosum

Geum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, oval-shaped fruits called achenes. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sticky cinquefoil thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
