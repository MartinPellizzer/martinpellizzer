Giliastrum purpusii subsp. platylobum

Giliastrum purpusii subsp.

platylobum, commonly known as the flatpod gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces capsule fruits that are typically flat and broadly ovate, giving it its subspecific name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The flatpod gilia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
