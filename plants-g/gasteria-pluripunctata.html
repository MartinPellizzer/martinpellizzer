Gasteria pluripunctata

Gasteria pluripunctata, commonly known as the spotted gasteria, is a species of succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with distinct white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers on tall, slender stalks, which are usually yellow or orange in color. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
