Gossypium nanking var. bani

Gossypium nanking var. bani, commonly known as Chinese cotton, is a variety of cotton native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and fluffy, white fibers that are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its fiber quality and has been historically cultivated in southern China.
