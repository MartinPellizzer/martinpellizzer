Grevillea trineura

Grevillea trineura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Grevillea trineura is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
