Gymnocalycium cardenasianum subsp. pseudoarmatum

Gymnocalycium cardenasianum subsp.

pseudoarmatum, commonly known as the Cactus of the Andes, is a succulent cactus native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and a compact growth habit.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
