Genista valentina

Genista valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's genista, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is evergreen, with narrow, leathery leaves that provide year-round structure.

Genista valentina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
