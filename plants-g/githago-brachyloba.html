Githago brachyloba

Githago brachyloba, commonly known as the short-lobed githago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and clustered in a head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
