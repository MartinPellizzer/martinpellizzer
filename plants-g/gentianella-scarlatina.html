Gentianella scarlatina

Gentianella scarlatina, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and ecological benefits.
