Grewia angolensis

Grewia angolensis, commonly known as the Angola globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils.

Grewia angolensis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
