Garcinia obliqua

Garcinia obliqua, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Garcinia obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
