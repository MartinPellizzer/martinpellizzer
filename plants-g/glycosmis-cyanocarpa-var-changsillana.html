Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. changsillana

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. changsillana, commonly known as the Changsillan citrus, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested when they turn a deep blue-black color. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
