Geniostoma rupestre var. ligustrifolium

Geniostoma rupestre var. ligustrifolium, commonly known as the rock lily or mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
