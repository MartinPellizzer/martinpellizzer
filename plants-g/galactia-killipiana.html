Galactia killipiana

Galactia killipiana, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Galactia killipiana is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
