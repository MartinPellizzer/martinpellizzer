Gynoxys vargasiana

Gynoxys vargasiana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and aromatic. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
