Gentianella dasythamna

Gentianella dasythamna, commonly known as the woolly gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, showy blooms during the summer months.

Gentianella dasythamna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
