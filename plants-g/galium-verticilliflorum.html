Galium verticilliflorum

Galium verticilliflorum, commonly known as whorled bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in a prostrate or ascending manner. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite and typically oval or lance-shaped, with serrated edges.

Galium verticilliflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
