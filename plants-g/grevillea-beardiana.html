Grevillea beardiana

Grevillea beardiana, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in sandy or rocky coastal environments. Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, playing a vital role in local ecosystems.

Grevillea beardiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its resilience to coastal conditions.
