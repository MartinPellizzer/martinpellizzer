Gnaphalium radians var. ustulatum

Gnaphalium radians var. ustulatum, commonly known as the burning bush or fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ability to colonize disturbed sites and its role in providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
