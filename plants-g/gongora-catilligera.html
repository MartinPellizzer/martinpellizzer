Gongora catilligera

Gongora catilligera, commonly known as the velvet lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple and white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its inflorescence features a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip.

Gongora catilligera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
