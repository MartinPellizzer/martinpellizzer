Gyas

Gyas, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint blue glow at night. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture.

Gyas is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
