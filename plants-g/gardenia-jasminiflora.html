Gardenia jasminiflora

Gardenia jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain black seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia jasminiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and sweet, citrus-like fragrance.
