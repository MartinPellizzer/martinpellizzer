Geanthemum rhizanthum

Geanthemum rhizanthum, commonly known as the underground flower, is a unique plant species characterized by its subterranean growth habit.

It produces flowers that develop underground, emerging only when mature, which is a rare botanical feature. The plant's roots form a dense network, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are typically small and pale, adapted for pollination by specific underground-dwelling insects.

This plant is often found in arid regions, where it has evolved to conserve water and nutrients efficiently.
