Glechoma biondiana var. glabrescens

Glechoma biondiana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy or smooth stems depending on the variety. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Glechoma biondiana var. glabrescens is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks.
