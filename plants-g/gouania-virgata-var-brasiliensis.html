Gouania virgata var. brasiliensis

Gouania virgata var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian gouania, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
