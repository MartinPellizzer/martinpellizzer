Gentiana paradoxa var. latifolia

Gentiana paradoxa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture. This variety is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue, flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
