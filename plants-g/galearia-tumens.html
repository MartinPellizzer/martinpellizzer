Galearia tumens

Galearia tumens, commonly known as the inflated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Galearia tumens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
