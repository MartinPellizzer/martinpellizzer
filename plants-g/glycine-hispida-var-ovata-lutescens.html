Glycine hispida var. ovata-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. ovata-lutescens, commonly known as the golden hairy bean, is a variant of the hairy bean plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that turn a distinctive yellowish-green color, especially under certain light conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to tropical gardens.
