Gastonia palmata

Gastonia palmata, commonly known as the Florida date palm, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 10 feet in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically valuable in landscaping.
