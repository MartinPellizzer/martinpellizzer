Galium capense var. garipense

Galium capense var. garipense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or wild strawberry, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a heart-shaped base.

This species is commonly found in rocky areas and along coastal regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
