Grevillea thyrsoides subsp. thyrsoides

Grevillea thyrsoides subsp.

thyrsoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and its showy, terminal inflorescences composed of numerous bright red or orange flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters, and it produces distinctive woody pods that contain seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
