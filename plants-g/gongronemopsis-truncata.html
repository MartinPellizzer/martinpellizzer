Gongronemopsis truncata

Gongronemopsis truncata, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces compact, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Gongronemopsis truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
