Gurania macrophylla

Gurania macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf gurania, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Gurania macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
