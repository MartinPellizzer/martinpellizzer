Grewia rautanenii

Grewia rautanenii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Grewia rautanenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
