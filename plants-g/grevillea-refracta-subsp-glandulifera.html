Grevillea refracta subsp. glandulifera

Grevillea refracta subsp.

glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive glandular texture on the underside.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
