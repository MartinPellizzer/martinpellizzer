Gladiolus concolor

Gladiolus concolor, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, straight stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with three outer petals that are often more pronounced than the inner ones. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
