Globularia cambessedesii subsp. hispanica

Globularia cambessedesii subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish globularia, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
