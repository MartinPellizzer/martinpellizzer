Glochidion plagiophyllum

Glochidion plagiophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
