Gentiana pensylvanica

Gentiana pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana pensylvanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
