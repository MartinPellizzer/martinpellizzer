Gembanga rotundifolia

Gembanga rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf gembanga, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Gembanga rotundifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
