Griseocactus fimbriatus

Griseocactus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringe cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in soft, white to pale green spines that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh climates make it a notable example of succulent flora in the region.
