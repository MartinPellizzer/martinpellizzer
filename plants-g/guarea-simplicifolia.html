Guarea simplicifolia

Guarea simplicifolia, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable wood.

The species also has traditional uses in local medicine and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
