Gentiana ventricosa

Gentiana ventricosa, commonly known as the swollen gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana ventricosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
