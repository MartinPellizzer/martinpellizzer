Glycine hedysaroides

Glycine hedysaroides, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant, spiral-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in herbal medicine.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturalists.
