Gaultheria rupestris var. parvifolia

Gaultheria rupestris var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
