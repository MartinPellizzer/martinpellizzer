Gentiana pseudamarella

Gentiana pseudamarella, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana pseudamarella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
