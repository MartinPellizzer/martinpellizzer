Geum sadleri

Geum sadleri, commonly known as Sadler's avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, creating a striking visual effect. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
