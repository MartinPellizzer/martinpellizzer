Gentiana phyllocalyx

Gentiana phyllocalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Gentiana phyllocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
