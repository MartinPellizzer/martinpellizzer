Gentianodes tubiflora

Gentianodes tubiflora, commonly known as the tubular gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and has adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
