Gustavia acuminata

Gustavia acuminata, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The flowers are five-petaled and have a distinctive, trumpet-like shape, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and moist lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
