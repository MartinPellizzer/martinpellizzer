Garuga schomburgkiana

Garuga schomburgkiana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
