Guzmania sanguinea var. erecta

Guzmania sanguinea var. erecta, commonly known as the red-tipped peace lily, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the small, white flowers, creating an eye-catching display. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure with long, narrow leaves that are usually green with a red or purple tinge along the edges. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its lush appearance.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
