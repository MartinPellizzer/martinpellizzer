Gunnera antioquensis

Gunnera antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian giant water shield, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Gunneraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and filtering water. Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.
