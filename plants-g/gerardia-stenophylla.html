Gerardia stenophylla

Gerardia stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Gerardia stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
