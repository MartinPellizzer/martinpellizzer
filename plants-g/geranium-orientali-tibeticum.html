Geranium orientali-tibeticum

Geranium orientali-tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in cooler climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and conservation efforts.
