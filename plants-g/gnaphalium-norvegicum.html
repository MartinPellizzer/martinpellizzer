Gnaphalium norvegicum

Gnaphalium norvegicum, commonly known as Norwegian cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant features a low-growing rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
