Ginoria spinosa

Ginoria spinosa, commonly known as the spiked ginoria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ginoria spinosa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils.
