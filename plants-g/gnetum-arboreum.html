Gnetum arboreum

Gnetum arboreum, commonly known as the tropical balsa tree, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is characterized by its unique combination of vascular tissue features, including the presence of vessel elements in both the xylem and phloem. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a distinctive venation pattern.

The tree is also notable for producing small, inconspicuous flowers and is sometimes cultivated for its lightweight wood.
