Grevillea phillipsiana

Grevillea phillipsiana, commonly known as the pink waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea phillipsiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
