Gypsophila acutifolia lusus thesiifolia

Gypsophila acutifolia lusus thesiifolia, commonly known as the desert baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, demonstrating drought tolerance. Its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
