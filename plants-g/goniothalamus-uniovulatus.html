Goniothalamus uniovulatus

Goniothalamus uniovulatus, commonly known as the single-seeded tree, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers with a strong fragrance. Its unique feature is the production of a single, large, fleshy fruit that contains a single seed.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
