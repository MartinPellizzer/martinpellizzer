Goodenia longifolia

Goodenia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes and heathlands.

Goodenia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
