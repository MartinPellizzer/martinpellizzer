Grevillea sarissa

Grevillea sarissa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea sarissa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
