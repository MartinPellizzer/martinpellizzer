Gymnocalycium carolinense subsp. ludwigii

Gymnocalycium carolinense subsp.

ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's moon cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a swollen, globular stem that is often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, pink, or purple.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
