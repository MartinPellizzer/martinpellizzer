Gentianella erythrochrysea

Gentianella erythrochrysea, commonly known as the red-chrysalis gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its foliage is basal, with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gentianella erythrochrysea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
