Geranium pratense var. pubescens

Geranium pratense var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
