Gossypium nervosum

Gossypium nervosum, commonly known as the woolly cotton or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy bolls that contain fibrous seeds, which are used for fiber and oil extraction. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Gossypium nervosum is also noted for its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
