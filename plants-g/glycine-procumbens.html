Glycine procumbens

Glycine procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate soybean, is a low-growing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. Glycine procumbens is a perennial species that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
