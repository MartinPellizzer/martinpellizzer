Guioa lentiscifolia

Guioa lentiscifolia, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Guioa lentiscifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
