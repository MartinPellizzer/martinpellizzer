Gaultheria nitida

Gaultheria nitida, commonly known as the silver leaf waxleaf, is a evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, silver-green foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Gaultheria nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of growing conditions.
