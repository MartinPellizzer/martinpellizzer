Gnaphalium gunnianum

Gnaphalium gunnianum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces numerous tiny, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across rocky and arid habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and heathlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
