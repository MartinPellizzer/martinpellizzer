Glycine warburgii

Glycine warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's soybean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and oil. Glycine warburgii is often cultivated for its nutritional value and has potential applications in agriculture and biofuel production.

Its growth is influenced by factors such as soil type, climate, and availability of nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
