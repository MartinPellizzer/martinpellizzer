Gunnera monoica var. strigosa

Gunnera monoica var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy gunnera, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy purple bract, which becomes more prominent as the plant matures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its dramatic appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and wetland landscapes.
