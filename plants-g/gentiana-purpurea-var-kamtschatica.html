Gentiana purpurea var. kamtschatica

Gentiana purpurea var. kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamtschatka gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
