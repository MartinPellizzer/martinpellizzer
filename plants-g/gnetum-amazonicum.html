Gnetum amazonicum

Gnetum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian climbing gymnosperm, is a unique plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a gymnosperm, meaning it reproduces via seeds rather than flowers, and it lacks true leaves, instead having scale-like structures. The plant is characterized by its long, vine-like stems that can climb to great heights, often forming dense canopies. Its cones are small and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species plays an important ecological role in the Amazonian ecosystem, contributing to biodiversity and habitat structure.
