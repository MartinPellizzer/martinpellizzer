Geum klettianum

Geum klettianum, commonly known as the Alpine avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geum klettianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
