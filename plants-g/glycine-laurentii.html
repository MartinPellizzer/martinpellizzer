Glycine laurentii

Glycine laurentii, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, spiral-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Glycine laurentii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant scent.
