Gnaphalium italicum

Gnaphalium italicum, commonly known as Italian toothbrush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
