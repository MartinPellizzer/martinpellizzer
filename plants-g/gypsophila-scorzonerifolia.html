Gypsophila scorzonerifolia

Gypsophila scorzonerifolia, commonly known as the alpine candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pungent scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers, which range from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gypsophila scorzonerifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
