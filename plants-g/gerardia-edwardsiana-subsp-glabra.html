Gerardia edwardsiana subsp. glabra

Gerardia edwardsiana subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth gerardia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its smooth, glabrous stem and leaves distinguish it from other subspecies within the genus.
