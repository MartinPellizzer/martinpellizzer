Gentiana choanantha

Gentiana choanantha, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana choanantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in traditional Korean medicine.
