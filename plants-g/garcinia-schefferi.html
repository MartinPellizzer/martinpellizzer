Garcinia schefferi

Garcinia schefferi, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a juicy, acidic pulp. Garcinia schefferi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
