Galanthus nivalis subsp. graecus

Galanthus nivalis subsp.

graecus, commonly known as the Greek snowdrop, is a subspecies of the snowdrop genus native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a corm-like bulb.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
