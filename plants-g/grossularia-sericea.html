Grossularia sericea

Grossularia sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grossularia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
