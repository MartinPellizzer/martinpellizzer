Garcinia moseleyana

Garcinia moseleyana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Garcinia moseleyana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
