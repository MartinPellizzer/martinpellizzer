Galium obtusifolium

Galium obtusifolium, commonly known as bluntleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its slender stems and delicate appearance make it a characteristic component of understory vegetation.
