Gladiolus lannesii

Gladiolus lannesii, commonly known as the Lannes' gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, terminal inflorescences composed of multiple flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals.

Gladiolus lannesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
