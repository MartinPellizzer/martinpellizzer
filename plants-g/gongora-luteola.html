Gongora luteola

Gongora luteola, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a striking, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Gongora luteola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
