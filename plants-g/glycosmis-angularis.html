Glycosmis angularis

Glycosmis angularis, commonly known as the angular sweetleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, yellow fruits containing a single seed. Glycosmis angularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
