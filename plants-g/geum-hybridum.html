Geum hybridum

Geum hybridum, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Geum hybridum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
