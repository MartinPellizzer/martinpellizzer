Grastidium leptocladum

Grastidium leptocladum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in temperate regions.

Grastidium leptocladum is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
