Gesneria allagophylla

Gesneria allagophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf gesneria, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or red. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Gesneria allagophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
