Gentiana amarella proles tetramera

Gentiana amarella proles tetramera, commonly known as the four-lobed gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with four lobes, giving it its specific epithet. The plant is native to mountainous regions in Europe and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
