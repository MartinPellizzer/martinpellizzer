Globba maculata var. arcuata

Globba maculata var. arcuata, commonly known as the spotted globba, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive mottled pattern of green and yellow. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
