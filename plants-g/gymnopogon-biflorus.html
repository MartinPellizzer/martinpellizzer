Gymnopogon biflorus

Gymnopogon biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear two flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

Gymnopogon biflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
