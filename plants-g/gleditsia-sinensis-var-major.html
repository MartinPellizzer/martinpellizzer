Gleditsia sinensis var. major

Gleditsia sinensis var. major, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn a striking yellow in autumn, and its long, slender pods that contain edible seeds. The tree is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its tolerance to drought and urban conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a shade tree.

Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
