Gymnema maingayi

Gymnema maingayi, commonly known as the Indian milk bush, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Gymnema maingayi is known for its ability to thrive in moist, tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
