Gumira cordifolia

Gumira cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf gumira, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Gumira cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
