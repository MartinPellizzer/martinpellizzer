Gwilymia fissurata

Gwilymia fissurata, commonly known as the rock wallaby orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Gwilymia fissurata is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest due to its ecological significance and limited distribution.
