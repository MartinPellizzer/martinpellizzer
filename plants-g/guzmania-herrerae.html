Guzmania herrerae

Guzmania herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports aquatic life. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania herrerae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
