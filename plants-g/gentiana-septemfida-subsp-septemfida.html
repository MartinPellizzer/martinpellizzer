Gentiana septemfida subsp. septemfida

Gentiana septemfida subsp.

septemfida, commonly known as the seven-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are deeply lobed, often appearing as seven separate leaflets, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
