Glycosmis pierrei

Glycosmis pierrei, commonly known as the Malayan starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, edible fruits that resemble starfruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly glossy surface.

The fruit, which matures to a yellowish-orange color, is rich in vitamin C and has a tangy, citrus-like flavor.
