Guzmania glaucophylla

Guzmania glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure, which forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The leaves are typically broad, waxy, and have a distinctive silvery or grayish-blue hue, giving it its common name. It produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
