Gireoudia heracleifolia var. viridis

Gireoudia heracleifolia var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved gireoudia, is a variant of the gireoudia plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage, which is often more pronounced than in other variants of the species. The plant typically features compound leaves with a serrated edge, and its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
