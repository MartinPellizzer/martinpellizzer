Gentiana saltuum

Gentiana saltuum, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh mountain environment.

Gentiana saltuum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
