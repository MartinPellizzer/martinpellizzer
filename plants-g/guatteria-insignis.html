Guatteria insignis

Guatteria insignis, commonly known as the white-flowered guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, often appearing in late spring.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in the summer months.
