Gynostemma hederifolium

Gynostemma hederifolium, commonly known as the creeping gynostemma, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by pale green, fleshy fruits that mature in the summer.

Gynostemma hederifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
