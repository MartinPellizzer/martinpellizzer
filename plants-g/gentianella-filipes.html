Gentianella filipes

Gentianella filipes, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. Gentianella filipes has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
