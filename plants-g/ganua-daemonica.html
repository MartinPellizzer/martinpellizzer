Ganua daemonica

Ganua daemonica, commonly known as the Shadowroot, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the mist-shrouded forests of the northern highlands.

Its dark, velvety leaves are said to absorb ambient light, allowing it to thrive in low-light conditions. The plant produces a bioluminescent flower that glows with an eerie blue hue at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its roots are rich in a psychoactive compound that has been both revered and feared by local cultures.

Due to its elusive nature and potent properties, Ganua daemonica remains a subject of botanical and mystical interest.
