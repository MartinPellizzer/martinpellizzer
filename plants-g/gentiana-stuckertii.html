Gentiana stuckertii

Gentiana stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive two-lipped structure, which is typical of the genus Gentiana.

Gentiana stuckertii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
