Galium subvelutinum var. oligotrichum

Galium subvelutinum var. oligotrichum, commonly known as the woolly bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a woolly texture.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
