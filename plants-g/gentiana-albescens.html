Gentiana albescens

Gentiana albescens, commonly known as the white gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
