Graffenrieda fruticosa

Graffenrieda fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces striking clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are tubular and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Graffenrieda fruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
