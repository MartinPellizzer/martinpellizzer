Glaucidium

Glaucidium, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver or grayish foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, waxy hairs. The leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a slightly serrated margin. The flowers are usually small and clustered in umbels, often displaying a range of colors from white to violet.

This plant is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
