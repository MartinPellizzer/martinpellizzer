Garcinia oblongifolia

Garcinia oblongifolia, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into sour-tasting, orange-red fruits. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the fruit being used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

The tree's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
