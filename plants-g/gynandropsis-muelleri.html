Gynandropsis muelleri

Gynandropsis muelleri, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, making it important to handle with care.

Gynandropsis muelleri is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in arid to semi-arid environments.
