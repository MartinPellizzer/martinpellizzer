Galium mollugo var. cinereum

Galium mollugo var. cinereum, commonly known as silverweed or common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.
