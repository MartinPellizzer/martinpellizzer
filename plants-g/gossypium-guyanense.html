Gossypium guyanense

Gossypium guyanense, commonly known as Guyana cotton, is a species of cotton native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana and Venezuela.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain fibrous seeds, which are used for textile production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

Gossypium guyanense is valued for its unique fiber quality and historical significance in the region's agricultural history.
