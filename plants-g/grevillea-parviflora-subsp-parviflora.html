Grevillea parviflora subsp. parviflora

Grevillea parviflora subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, bright red flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
