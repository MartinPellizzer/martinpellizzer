Garcinia calophylla

Garcinia calophylla, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically reddish-purple when mature, with a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, juicy interior. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and popular choice in horticulture.
