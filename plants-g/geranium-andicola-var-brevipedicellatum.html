Geranium andicola var. brevipedicellatum

Geranium andicola var. brevipedicellatum, commonly known as the Andean geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its ornamental value.
