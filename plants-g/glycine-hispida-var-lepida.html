Glycine hispida var. lepida

Glycine hispida var. lepida, commonly known as the silverleaf mimosa, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
