Grewia hispida

Grewia hispida, commonly known as the rough globe thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. The plant has small, spiny leaves and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a common sight in dry regions of its native habitat.
