Grewia omphacarpa

Grewia omphacarpa, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small tomatoes. These fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Grewia omphacarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes planted for erosion control due to its extensive root system.
