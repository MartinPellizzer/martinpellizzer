Gnephosis gynotricha

Gnephosis gynotricha, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey. The plant's leaves are typically floating and covered in fine, hair-like structures that aid in adhesion. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in slow-moving or still water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for studies on plant adaptation and predation.
