Gothofreda mutica

Gothofreda mutica, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to specific regions in South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
