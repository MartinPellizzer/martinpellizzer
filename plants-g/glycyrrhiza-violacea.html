Glycyrrhiza violacea

Glycyrrhiza violacea, commonly known as violet licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its violet-blue flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Glycyrrhiza violacea is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
