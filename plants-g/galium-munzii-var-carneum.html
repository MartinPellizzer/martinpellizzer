Galium munzii var. carneum

Galium munzii var. carneum, commonly known as the pink woodsorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals arranged in a star-like pattern. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium munzii var. carneum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
