Gladiolus apiculatus

Gladiolus apiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of other gladiolus species. Its flowers are usually single or double, with petals that are often spotted or striped.

Gladiolus apiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
