Gentiana major

Gentiana major, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana major is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
