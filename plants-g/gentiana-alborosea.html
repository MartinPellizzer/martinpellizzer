Gentiana alborosea

Gentiana alborosea, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically five to seven lobes in shape. The plant grows in rocky and alpine environments, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
