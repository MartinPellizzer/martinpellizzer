Gaultheria minutiflora var. minutiflora

Gaultheria minutiflora var. minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf wax myrtle, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant leaves. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its berries are dark blue to black, containing a single seed, and are often used in traditional medicine.

Gaultheria minutiflora var. minutiflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
