Grevillea ericifolia var. scabrella

Grevillea ericifolia var. scabrella, commonly known as the scaly leaf grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, scaly, and often hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to birds and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a distinctive addition to native plant collections.
