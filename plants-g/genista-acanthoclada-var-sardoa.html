Genista acanthoclada var. sardoa

Genista acanthoclada var. sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and pointed.

This variety is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
