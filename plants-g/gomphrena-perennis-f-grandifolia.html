Gomphrena perennis f. grandifolia

Gomphrena perennis f. grandifolia, commonly known as the everlasting plume, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, feathery bracts that resemble plumes, which remain colorful even after drying, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers are usually pink, purple, or red, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by the showy bracts.

Gomphrena perennis f. grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
