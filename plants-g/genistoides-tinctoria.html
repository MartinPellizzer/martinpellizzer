Genistoides tinctoria

Genistoides tinctoria, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, often twisted stems. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive, finely serrated edge.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
