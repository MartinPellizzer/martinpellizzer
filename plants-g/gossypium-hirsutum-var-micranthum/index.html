Gossypium Hirsutum Var. Micranthum

Gossypium hirsutum var. micranthum is a lesser-known variant of the cotton plant known for its unique floral characteristics. This plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to various regions in the Americas. It features small, clustered flowers that differ from the typical large, showy blooms of common cotton varieties. Despite its modest appearance, it holds significance in botanical studies and traditional uses. Its cultivation and genetic traits offer insights into the adaptability of cotton species in diverse environments.
