Gramerium convolutum

Gramerium convolutum, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiraling fronds that curl tightly around its stem. The plant has compound leaves with small, lance-shaped pinnae that emerge in a helical pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Gramerium convolutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.
