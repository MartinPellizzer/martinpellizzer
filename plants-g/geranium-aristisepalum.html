Geranium aristisepalum

Geranium aristisepalum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five pointed petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant gardens.
