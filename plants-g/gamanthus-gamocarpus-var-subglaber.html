Gamanthus gamocarpus var. subglaber

Gamanthus gamocarpus var. subglaber, commonly known as the smooth-fruited gamanthus, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive morphology, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces smooth, glabrous fruits that are adapted to its arid or semi-arid habitat. Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential for horticultural use.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
