Gilia latiflora var. triceps

Gilia latiflora var. triceps, commonly known as the three-armed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, while the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Gilia latiflora var. triceps is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
