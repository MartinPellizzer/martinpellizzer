Gilia leptalea subsp. bicolor

Gilia leptalea subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, two-toned flowers that range from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique coloration and is often found in association with other desert flora.
