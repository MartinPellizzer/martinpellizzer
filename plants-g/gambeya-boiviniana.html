Gambeya boiviniana

Gambeya boiviniana, commonly known as the Madagascar ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Gambeya boiviniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also significant in traditional crafts due to its durable timber.
