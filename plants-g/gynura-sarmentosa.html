Gynura sarmentosa

Gynura sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping gynura, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish-brown fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gynura sarmentosa is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
