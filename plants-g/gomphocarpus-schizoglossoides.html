Gomphocarpus schizoglossoides

Gomphocarpus schizoglossoides, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to herbivores and a potential hazard to livestock.
