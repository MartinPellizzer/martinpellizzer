Gmelina paniculata

Gmelina paniculata, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a fast-growing deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The tree produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Gmelina paniculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has been used in traditional medicine.
