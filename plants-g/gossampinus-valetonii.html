Gossampinus valetonii

Gossampinus valetonii, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces yellow flowers and pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a rough texture.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
