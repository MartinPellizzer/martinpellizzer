Garcinia huillensis

Garcinia huillensis, commonly known as the Huilliche Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America, specifically the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus fruits, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed.

Garcinia huillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
