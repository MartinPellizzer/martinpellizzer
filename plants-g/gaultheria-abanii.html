Gaultheria abanii

Gaultheria abanii, commonly known as the Alaskan wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It produces small, dark red berries that are rich in aromatic oils and have a sharp, minty flavor. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Gaultheria abanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
