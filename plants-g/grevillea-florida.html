Grevillea florida

Grevillea florida, commonly known as the Sydney flame or the red flowering waratah, is a striking plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring dense, leathery leaves. The plant is renowned for its vibrant, blood-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are composed of a central cone surrounded by numerous petal-like structures called bracts.

Grevillea florida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
