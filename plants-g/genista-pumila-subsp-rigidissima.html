Genista pumila subsp. rigidissima

Genista pumila subsp.

rigidissima, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
