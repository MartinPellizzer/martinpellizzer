Gentiana × spuria

Gentiana × spuria, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems, and it is often found in moist, rocky habitats.

Gentiana × spuria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
