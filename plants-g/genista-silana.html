Genista silana

Genista silana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in Mediterranean climates, characterized by its yellow flowers and thorny stems. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
