Galeopsis calcarea

Galeopsis calcarea, commonly known as limestone horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is characterized by its small, purple flowers and hairy stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Galeopsis calcarea is generally herbaceous and can be found in meadows and along roadsides.
