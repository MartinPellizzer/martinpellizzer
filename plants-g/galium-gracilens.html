Galium gracilens

Galium gracilens, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium gracilens is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to insect pollinators.
