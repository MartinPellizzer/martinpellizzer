Goldbachia tetragona

Goldbachia tetragona, commonly known as the four-leaf goldbachia, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a subtle metallic sheen. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant collectors alike.
