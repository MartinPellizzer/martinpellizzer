Genista grandiflora

Genista grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, giving it a delicate appearance.

Genista grandiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
