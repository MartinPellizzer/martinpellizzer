Garcinia antidysenterica

Garcinia antidysenterica, commonly known as African bitter apple, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is often found in moist, lowland forests. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their astringent and antidiarrheal properties.

The fruit's rind is particularly valued for its medicinal applications in treating digestive disorders.
