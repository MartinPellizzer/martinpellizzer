Garuga pinnata

Garuga pinnata, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Garuga pinnata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
