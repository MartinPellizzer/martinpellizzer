Galium erectum var. calcareum

Galium erectum var. calcareum, commonly known as limestone bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to calcareous soils in Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
