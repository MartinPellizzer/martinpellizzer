Gynoxys arnicae

Gynoxys arnicae, commonly known as the yellow brittlestar, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a star. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gynoxys arnicae is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sunny environments.
