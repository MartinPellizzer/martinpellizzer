Gloeospermum falcatum

Gloeospermum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gloeospermum, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
