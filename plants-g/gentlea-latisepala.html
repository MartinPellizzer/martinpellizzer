Gentlea latisepala

Gentlea latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
