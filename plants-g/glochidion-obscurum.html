Glochidion obscurum

Glochidion obscurum, commonly known as the dark glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
