Gentiana bavarica var. imbricata

Gentiana bavarica var. imbricata, commonly known as the Bavarian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
