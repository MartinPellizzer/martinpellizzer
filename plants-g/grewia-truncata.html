Grewia truncata

Grewia truncata, commonly known as the African globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, and its leaves are usually opposite and ovate. The plant produces small, round, red or yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Grewia truncata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
