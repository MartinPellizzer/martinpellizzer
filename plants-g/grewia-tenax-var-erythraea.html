Grewia tenax var. erythraea

Grewia tenax var. erythraea, commonly known as the red-berried bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that appear in summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
