Gentiana bellatula

Gentiana bellatula, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana bellatula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
