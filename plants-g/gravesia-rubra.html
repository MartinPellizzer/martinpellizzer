Gravesia rubra

Gravesia rubra, commonly known as the red gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

Gravesia rubra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
