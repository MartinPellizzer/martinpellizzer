Geranium anthemidifolium

Geranium anthemidifolium, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink to purple in color, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
