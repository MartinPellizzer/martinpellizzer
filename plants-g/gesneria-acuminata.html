Gesneria acuminata

Gesneria acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and often have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Gesneria acuminata is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
