Gymnema formosanum

Gymnema formosanum, commonly known as the "Indian bitter melon," is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its milky latex, which contains compounds with potential medicinal properties. Its fruits are edible and resemble small, bitter melons, often used in traditional medicine.

Gymnema formosanum is also valued for its role in herbal remedies, particularly for its purported effects on blood sugar regulation.
