Geum sericeum

Geum sericeum, commonly known as the silky avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Geum sericeum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
