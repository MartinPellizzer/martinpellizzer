Garcinia nervosa

Garcinia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. Garcinia nervosa is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
