Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. subspinum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. subspinum, commonly known as the roughleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The subspecies subspinum is distinguished by its rough, spinose leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical form.
