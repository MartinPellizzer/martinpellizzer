Gentiana germanica subsp. semleri

Gentiana germanica subsp.

semleri, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
