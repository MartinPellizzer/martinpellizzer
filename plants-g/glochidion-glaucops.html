Glochidion glaucops

Glochidion glaucops, commonly known as the silver glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-yellow berries that are edible for some bird species. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
