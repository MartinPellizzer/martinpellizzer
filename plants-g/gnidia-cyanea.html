Gnidia cyanea

Gnidia cyanea, commonly known as the blue gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of tubular flowers, often surrounded by bracts.

Gnidia cyanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
