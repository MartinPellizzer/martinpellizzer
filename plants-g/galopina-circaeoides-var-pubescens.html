Galopina circaeoides var. pubescens

Galopina circaeoides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy galopina, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
