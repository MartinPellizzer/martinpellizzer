Grisebachia plumosa subsp. irrorata

Grisebachia plumosa subsp.

irrorata, commonly known as the silky feather plant, is a striking member of the Commelinaceae family. It is characterized by its long, slender, and softly hairy leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
