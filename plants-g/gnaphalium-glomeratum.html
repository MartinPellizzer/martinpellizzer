Gnaphalium glomeratum

Gnaphalium glomeratum, commonly known as clustered everlasting, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale green bracts. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous roots and low-growing habit make it a hardy plant adapted to various environmental conditions.
