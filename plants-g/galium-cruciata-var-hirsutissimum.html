Galium cruciata var. hirsutissimum

Galium cruciata var. hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy woodcrushers, is a variant of the woodcrusher plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in dense, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white to pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a rough texture due to their hairy surfaces.
