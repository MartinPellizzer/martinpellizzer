Gnaphalium mackayi

Gnaphalium mackayi, commonly known as Mackay's feather grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that are often mistaken for a type of wool or down, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its structure and appearance make it a unique addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
