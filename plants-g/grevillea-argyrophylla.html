Grevillea argyrophylla

Grevillea argyrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in spikes, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea argyrophylla is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate salty air.
