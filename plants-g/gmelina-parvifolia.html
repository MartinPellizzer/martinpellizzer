Gmelina parvifolia

Gmelina parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered gumtree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

Gmelina parvifolia is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
