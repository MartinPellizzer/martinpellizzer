Gentiana sceptrum var. humilis

Gentiana sceptrum var. humilis, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
