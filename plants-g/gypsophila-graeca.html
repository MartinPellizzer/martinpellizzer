Gypsophila graeca

Gypsophila graeca, commonly known as the Greek gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

Gypsophila graeca is also known for its short flowering period, usually in late spring to early summer.
