Graptopetalum superbum

Graptopetalum superbum, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
