Gnaphalium aquaticum

Gnaphalium aquaticum, commonly known as water everlasting, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found along the edges of ponds, lakes, and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is known for its ability to survive in waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
