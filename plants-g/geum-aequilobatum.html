Geum aequilobatum

Geum aequilobatum, commonly known as the showy mountain avens, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy.

Geum aequilobatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
