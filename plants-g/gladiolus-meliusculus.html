Gladiolus meliusculus

Gladiolus meliusculus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking appearance and fragrance.
