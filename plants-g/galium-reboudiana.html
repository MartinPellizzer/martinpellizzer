Galium reboudiana

Galium reboudiana, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium reboudiana is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
