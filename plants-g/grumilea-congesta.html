Grumilea congesta

Grumilea congesta, commonly known as the clustered grumia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Grumilea congesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
