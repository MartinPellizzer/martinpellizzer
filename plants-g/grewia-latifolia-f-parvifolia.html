Grewia latifolia f. parvifolia

Grewia latifolia f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved velvet bush, is a compact shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
