Gnaphalium pumilum

Gnaphalium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf sandmat, is a small, mat-forming herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows close to the ground, with narrow, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or white, and are adapted for wind pollination.

Gnaphalium pumilum is well-suited to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils with minimal water.
