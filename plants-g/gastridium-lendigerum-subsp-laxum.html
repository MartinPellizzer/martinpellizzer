Gastridium lendigerum subsp. laxum

Gastridium lendigerum subsp.

laxum, commonly known as the slender gastridium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
