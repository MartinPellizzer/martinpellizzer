Guadua chacoensis

Guadua chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 12 meters. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in areas with high humidity and well-drained soils. Its rhizomes form dense clumps, allowing it to spread vegetatively and establish quickly in its natural habitat.

Due to its robust structure and sustainability, it is also valued for use in construction and handicrafts.
