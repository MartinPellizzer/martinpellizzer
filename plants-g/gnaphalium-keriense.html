Gnaphalium keriense

Gnaphalium keriense, commonly known as the Keriense cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, often silver-gray foliage. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and soil stability.
