Gnaphalium pedunculosum

Gnaphalium pedunculosum, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a low-growing flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Gnaphalium pedunculosum is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
