Grammitis mesocarpa

Grammitis mesocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf moss fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are embedded in the leaf surfaces, a feature typical of the Hymenophyllaceae family.

Grammitis mesocarpa plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing microhabitats for small organisms.
