Gentiana walujewii subsp. walujewii

Gentiana walujewii subsp.

walujewii, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, which is usually erect and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
