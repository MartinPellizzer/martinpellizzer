Grevillea diminuta

Grevillea diminuta, commonly known as the small grevillea, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, spider-like red or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Grevillea diminuta is also an important species for local pollinators, particularly birds and insects.
