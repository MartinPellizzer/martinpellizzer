Gynostemma simplicifolium

Gynostemma simplicifolium, commonly known as Chinese ginseng or goat's beard, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by yellow-orange fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and melons, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Gynostemma simplicifolium is often cultivated for its purported health benefits and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
