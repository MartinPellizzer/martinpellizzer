Galium pumilum subsp. septentrionale

Galium pumilum subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and usually have a slightly hairy surface. Galium pumilum subsp.

septentrionale is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
