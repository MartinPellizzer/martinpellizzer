Garcinia verrucosa subsp. typica

Garcinia verrucosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing acidic, orange-red seeds. The fruit is valued for its high tartaric acid content and traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
