Gentiana monantha

Gentiana monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana monantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
