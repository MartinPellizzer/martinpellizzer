Geissorhiza pallidiflora

Geissorhiza pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered geissorhiza, is a member of the Iridaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
