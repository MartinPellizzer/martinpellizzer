Gladiolus segetum

Gladiolus segetum, commonly known as the grain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, yellow, and white, which bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Iridaceae family and is closely related to other ornamental gladiolus species.
