Gypsophila pulchra

Gypsophila pulchra, commonly known as the baby's breath or sweet William, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are composed of small, fluffy petals that create a soft, ethereal appearance, making it a popular choice in floral arrangements.

Gypsophila pulchra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
