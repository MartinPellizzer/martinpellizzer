Goyazia rupicola

Goyazia rupicola, commonly known as the rock goyazia, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
