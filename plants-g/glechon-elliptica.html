Glechon elliptica

Glechon elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of violet or pink, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Glechon elliptica is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
