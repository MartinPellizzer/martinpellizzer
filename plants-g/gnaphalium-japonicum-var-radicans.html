Gnaphalium japonicum var. radicans

Gnaphalium japonicum var. radicans, commonly known as creeping groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of slender stems and small, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces numerous small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in compact clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its hairy leaves and aromatic properties make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its habitat.
