Gentianella palcana

Gentianella palcana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Gentianella palcana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
