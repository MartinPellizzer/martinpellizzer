Gnaphalium revolutum

Gnaphalium revolutum, commonly known as the curling feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, across North America. Its leaves are narrow, revolute (curled under), and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
