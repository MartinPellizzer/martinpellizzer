Gnidia calocephala

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its white, fluffy flower heads that resemble snowballs, which appear in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Gnidia calocephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.
