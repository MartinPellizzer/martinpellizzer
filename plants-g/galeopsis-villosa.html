Galeopsis villosa

Galeopsis villosa, commonly known as hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis villosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
