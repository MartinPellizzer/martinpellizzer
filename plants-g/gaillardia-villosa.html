Gaillardia villosa

Gaillardia villosa, commonly known as the hairy grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it produces vibrant red and yellow flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Gaillardia villosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
