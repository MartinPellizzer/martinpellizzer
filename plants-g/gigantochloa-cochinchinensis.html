Gigantochloa cochinchinensis

Gigantochloa cochinchinensis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its rapid growth, it is also valued for its utility in construction and as a source of raw materials.
