Gorteria diffusa subsp. calendulacea

Gorteria diffusa subsp.

calendulacea, commonly known as the Cape golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
