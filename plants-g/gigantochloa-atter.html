Gigantochloa atter

Gigantochloa atter, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has large, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and strong structural properties, it is valued for both ecological and practical applications.
