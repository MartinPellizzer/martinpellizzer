Greeneochloa coarctata

Greeneochloa coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
