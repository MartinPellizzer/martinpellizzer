Gentiana aristata subsp. asparagoides

Gentiana aristata subsp.

asparagoides, commonly known as the asparagoides gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while the stems are usually unbranched and sturdy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
