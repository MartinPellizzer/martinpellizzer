Geonoma chococola subsp. awaensis

Geonoma chococola subsp.

awaensis, commonly known as the Awaensian palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. This species is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures.

The leaves are typically dark green and can grow quite large, contributing to the plant's ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
