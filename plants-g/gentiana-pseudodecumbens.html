Gentiana pseudodecumbens

Gentiana pseudodecumbens, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine layer of hair.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are known for their vibrant color and medicinal properties.
