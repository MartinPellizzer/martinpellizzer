Gerascanthus lomatolobus

Gerascanthus lomatolobus, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features small, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater.

Gerascanthus lomatolobus is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in semi-desert ecosystems.
