Geophila cordifolia var. cordifolia

Geophila cordifolia var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf geophila, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and is known for its ornamental value in garden settings.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
