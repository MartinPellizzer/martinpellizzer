Gentiana longibarbata

Gentiana longibarbata, commonly known as the long-bearded gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
