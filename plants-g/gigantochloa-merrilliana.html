Gigantochloa merrilliana

Gigantochloa merrilliana, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Gigantochloa merrilliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional construction in some regions.
