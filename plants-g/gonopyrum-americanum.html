Gonopyrum americanum

Gonopyrum americanum, commonly known as the American wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and culms that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its role in erosion control and wildlife habitat.
