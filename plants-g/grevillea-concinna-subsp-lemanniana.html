Grevillea concinna subsp. lemanniana

Grevillea concinna subsp.

lemanniana, commonly known as the lemon-scented grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces bright red or orange cylindrical flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure. Grevillea concinna subsp.

lemanniana is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
