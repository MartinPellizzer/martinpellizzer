Gentiana reyesii

Gentiana reyesii, commonly known as the Reyes' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention.

Gentiana reyesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
