Gentiana galanderi

Gentiana galanderi, commonly known as the Galician gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana galanderi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
