Gnaphalium sclerochlaenum

Gnaphalium sclerochlaenum, commonly known as the rigid cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Its leaves are linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are rigid and branching.

It is characterized by its ability to produce a significant amount of wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread across suitable environments.
