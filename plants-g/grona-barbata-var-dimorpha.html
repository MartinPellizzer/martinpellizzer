Grona barbata var. dimorpha

Grona barbata var. dimorpha, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, barbed leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often arranged in a panicle-like structure.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of its native ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
