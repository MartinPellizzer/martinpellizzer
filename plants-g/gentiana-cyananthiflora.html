Gentiana cyananthiflora

Gentiana cyananthiflora, commonly known as the blue-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana cyananthiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
