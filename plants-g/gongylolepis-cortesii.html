Gongylolepis cortesii

Gongylolepis cortesii, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant typically produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Gongylolepis cortesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh, dry conditions.
