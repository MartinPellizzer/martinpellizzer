Grumilea arborescens

Grumilea arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

Grumilea arborescens is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
