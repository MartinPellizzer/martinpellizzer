Gypsophila davurica

Gypsophila davurica, commonly known as the Meadow Blush or Baby's Breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental value.

Gypsophila davurica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
