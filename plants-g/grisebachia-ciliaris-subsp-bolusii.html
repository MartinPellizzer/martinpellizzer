Grisebachia ciliaris subsp. bolusii

Grisebachia ciliaris subsp.

bolusii, commonly known as the Bolusii's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like morphology. It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions with arid or semi-arid climates.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, demonstrating a preference for well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with the lemma bearing a distinctive fringe of hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience in harsh environments.
