Galarhoeus carniolicus

Galarhoeus carniolicus, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, aiding in moisture retention.

Galarhoeus carniolicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
