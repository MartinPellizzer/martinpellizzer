Gymnocalycium asterium

Gymnocalycium asterium, commonly known as the star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the evening. Gymnocalycium asterium is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
