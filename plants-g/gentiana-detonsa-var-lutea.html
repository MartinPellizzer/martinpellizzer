Gentiana detonsa var. lutea

Gentiana detonsa var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
