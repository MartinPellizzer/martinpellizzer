Gladiolus varius var. elatus

Gladiolus varius var. elatus, commonly known as the towering gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The inflorescence consists of a spike of bilateral, spurred flowers that are highly ornamental and often used in floral arrangements. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, providing a lush backdrop to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its elegance and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
