Gnemon indica

Gnemon indica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The fruit develops from the flower and is typically harvested when fully mature, around late autumn.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
