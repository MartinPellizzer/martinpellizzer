Galium verum subsp. wirtgenii

Galium verum subsp.

wirtgenii, commonly known as the golden bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces tiny, bead-like fruits called drupes, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are alternate and typically have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and shallow.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
