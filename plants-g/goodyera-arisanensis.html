Goodyera arisanensis

Goodyera arisanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan orchid, is a species of epiphytic plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and striking, orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a distinctive yellow lip, and it blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

Goodyera arisanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
