Grisebachia apiculata

Grisebachia apiculata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Grisebachia apiculata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
