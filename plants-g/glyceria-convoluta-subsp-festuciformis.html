Glyceria convoluta subsp. festuciformis

Glyceria convoluta subsp.

festuciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a grass-like plant native to wetland and marsh environments. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its flowering stems produce small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often inconspicuous.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
