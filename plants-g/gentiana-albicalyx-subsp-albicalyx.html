Gentiana albicalyx subsp. albicalyx

Gentiana albicalyx subsp.

albicalyx, commonly known as the white gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family, Gentianaceae. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with two lips, and are often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions of North America.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
