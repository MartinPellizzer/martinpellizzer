Galarhoeus caesius

Galarhoeus caesius, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds, which bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Galarhoeus caesius is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
