Grewia platyclada

Grewia platyclada, commonly known as the smoothleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, smooth leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia platyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
