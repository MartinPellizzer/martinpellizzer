Gerardia ovatifolia

Gerardia ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale purple, blooming in the summer months.

Gerardia ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
