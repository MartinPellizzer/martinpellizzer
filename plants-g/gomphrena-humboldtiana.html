Gomphrena humboldtiana

Gomphrena humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's torch flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble small torches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by these colorful, papery structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Gomphrena humboldtiana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
