Grevillea rosieri

Grevillea rosieri, commonly known as the Christmas Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, typically around Christmas time, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.

Grevillea rosieri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
