Gastrolobium sagittulatum

Gastrolobium sagittulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Gastrolobium sagittulatum is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
