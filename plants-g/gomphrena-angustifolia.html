Gomphrena angustifolia

Gomphrena angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular flower heads that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens for its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Gomphrena angustifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
