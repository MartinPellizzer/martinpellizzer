Galega bicolor

Galega bicolor, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Galega bicolor thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed sites.

It has been historically used in agriculture for forage and soil enrichment.
