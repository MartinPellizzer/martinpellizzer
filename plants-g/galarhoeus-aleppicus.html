Galarhoeus aleppicus

Galarhoeus aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

The Aleppo pine plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
