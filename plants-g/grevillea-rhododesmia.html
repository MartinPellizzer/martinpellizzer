Grevillea rhododesmia

Grevillea rhododesmia, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-shaped flowers in shades of red and orange, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea rhododesmia is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
