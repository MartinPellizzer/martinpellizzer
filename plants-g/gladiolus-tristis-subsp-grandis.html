Gladiolus tristis subsp. grandis

Gladiolus tristis subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the giant weeping gladiolus, is a member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and drooping, pale yellow to orange flowers that resemble weeping tendrils.

The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
