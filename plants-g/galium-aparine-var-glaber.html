Galium aparine var. glaber

Galium aparine var. glaber, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that are usually prostrate or ascending, often covered with fine, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface. Small white flowers appear in clusters, typically during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.
