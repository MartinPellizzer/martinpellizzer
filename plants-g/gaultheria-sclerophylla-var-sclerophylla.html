Gaultheria sclerophylla var. sclerophylla

Gaultheria sclerophylla var. sclerophylla, commonly known as the coastal cranberry bush, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dense, leathery, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal forests and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
