Gigantochloa tenuispiculata

Gigantochloa tenuispiculata, commonly known as the slender spiculate bamboo, is a tall, robust grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional construction and crafts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and lowland areas.

Its rapid growth and hardiness make it an important resource for local communities.
