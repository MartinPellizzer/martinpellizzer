Gerbera incana var. intermedia

Gerbera incana var. intermedia, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is a perennial herb that produces numerous stems and blooms throughout the growing season.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and horticultural research.
