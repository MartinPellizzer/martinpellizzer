Gravesia pusilla

Gravesia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf gravesia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its pleasant scent and compact size.
