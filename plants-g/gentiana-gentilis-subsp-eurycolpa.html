Gentiana gentilis subsp. eurycolpa

Gentiana gentilis subsp.

eurycolpa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
