Gilia laciniata var. erecta

Gilia laciniata var. erecta, commonly known as the erect gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
