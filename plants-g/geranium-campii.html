Geranium campii

Geranium campii, commonly known as the Camp's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Geranium campii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
