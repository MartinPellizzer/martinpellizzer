Gentiana semifida

Gentiana semifida, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Gentiana semifida is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
