Gouldia cordata var. acuminata

Gouldia cordata var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves that have a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, delicate pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
