Goniothalamus bracteosus

Goniothalamus bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteose goniothalamus, is a member of the Annonaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically white or pale yellow in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
