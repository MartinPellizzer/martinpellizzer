Grangeria

Grangeria, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to white. These plants are typically herbaceous and can be found in various habitats, including woodlands and meadows. Grangeria species often have opposite leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
