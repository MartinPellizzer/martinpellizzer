Goodenia inundata

Goodenia inundata, commonly known as the water goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in wetland or swampy areas, often growing in shallow water or moist soil.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
