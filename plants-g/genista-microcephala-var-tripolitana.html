Genista microcephala var. tripolitana

Genista microcephala var. tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripoli broom, is a shrub native to North Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
