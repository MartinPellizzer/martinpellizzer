Genista remota

Genista remota, commonly known as the remote genista, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista remota is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
