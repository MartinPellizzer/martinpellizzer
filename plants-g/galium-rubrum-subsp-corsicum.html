Galium rubrum subsp. corsicum

Galium rubrum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Galium rubrum subsp.

corsicum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
