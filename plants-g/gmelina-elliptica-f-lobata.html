Gmelina elliptica f. lobata

Gmelina elliptica f. lobata, commonly known as the lobate gmelina, is a variant of the gmelina tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves with a distinctive lobed shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
