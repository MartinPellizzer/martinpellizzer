Grevillea halmaturina subsp. halmaturina

Grevillea halmaturina subsp.

halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina grevillea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, feather-like leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
