Gladiolus alpigenus

Gladiolus alpigenus, commonly known as the alpine gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright, sword-shaped leaves and tall, showy flower spikes. The flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with three petals and three sepals, forming a striking, grass-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
