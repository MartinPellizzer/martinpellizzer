Grusonia halophila

Grusonia halophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to grow in saline environments.

It possesses succulent leaves that help reduce water loss and tolerate high salt concentrations. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and arid regions with high soil salinity.

Grusonia halophila plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms in its native ecosystems.
