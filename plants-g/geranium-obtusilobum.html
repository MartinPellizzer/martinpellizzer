Geranium obtusilobum

Geranium obtusilobum, commonly known as the broad-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and features heart-shaped, broad leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils in partial to full sun.

Geranium obtusilobum is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed sites across the eastern United States.
