Glossorhyncha scandens

Glossorhyncha scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its scrambling growth habit and is often found climbing over shrubs or trees. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Glossorhyncha scandens is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
