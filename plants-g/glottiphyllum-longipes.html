Glottiphyllum longipes

Glottiphyllum longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed glottiphyllum, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that emerges from the center of the rosette.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging plant to cultivate.
