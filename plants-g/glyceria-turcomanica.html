Glyceria turcomanica

Glyceria turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman reed, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, lakes, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist to waterlogged soils.

Glyceria turcomanica plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
