Gladiolus × haylockianus

Gladiolus × haylockianus, commonly known as the Haylock hybrid gladiolus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Gladiolus × sebelianus and Gladiolus × gollwitzeri.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant is native to South Africa and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets.

The foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often used in cut flower arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.
