Glycine umbellata

Glycine umbellata, commonly known as the white bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in umbels. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and is often used in ornamental gardening. Its pods contain edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients.

Glycine umbellata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
