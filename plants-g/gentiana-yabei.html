Gentiana yabei

Gentiana yabei, commonly known as the Yabei gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
