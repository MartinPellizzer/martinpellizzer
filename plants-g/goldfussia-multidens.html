Goldfussia multidens

Goldfussia multidens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Goldfussia multidens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
