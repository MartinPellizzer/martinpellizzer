Grevillea variifolia subsp. bundera

Grevillea variifolia subsp.

bundera, commonly known as the Bundera grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear during spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
