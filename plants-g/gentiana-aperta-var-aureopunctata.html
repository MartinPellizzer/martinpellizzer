Gentiana aperta var. aureopunctata

Gentiana aperta var. aureopunctata, commonly known as the golden-spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with golden spots or markings. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
