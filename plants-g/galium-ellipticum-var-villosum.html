Galium ellipticum var. villosum

Galium ellipticum var. villosum, commonly known as hairy wood-sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a hairy surface and a slightly heart-shaped base. Small, white, spurred flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
