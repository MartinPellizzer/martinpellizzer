Gladiolus maculatus

Gladiolus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms with distinctive spotted patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring sword-shaped leaves and tall, upright stems. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The spotted gladiolus is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
