Graptopetalum fruticosum

Graptopetalum fruticosum, commonly known as the silver rose or rock rose, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pink to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for arid landscape designs.
