Gastrochilus undulatus

Gastrochilus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive wavy pattern on the lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Gastrochilus undulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
