Galium angulosum

Galium angulosum, commonly known as bentweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense mats of slender, angular stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium angulosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
