Gagea africana

Gagea africana, commonly known as the African spring beauty, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces delicate, grass-like leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It blooms in the spring and is characterized by its short stature and clustered growth habit.

Gagea africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
