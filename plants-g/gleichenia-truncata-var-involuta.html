Gleichenia truncata var. involuta

Gleichenia truncata var. involuta, commonly known as the twisted cliff fern, is a fern species native to various regions of the world, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often twisted fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on rocky or shaded surfaces, thriving in moist, humid environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a complex venation pattern.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
