Geranium × ardescens

Geranium × ardescens, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium × ardescens prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
