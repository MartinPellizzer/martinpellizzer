Gymnocalycium spegazzinii

Gymnocalycium spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's barrel cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a spineless cactus characterized by its globular, ribbed stem that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.

Gymnocalycium spegazzinii is also known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
