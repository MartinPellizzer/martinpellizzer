Gloriosa luxurians

Gloriosa luxurians, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
