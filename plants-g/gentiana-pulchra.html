Gentiana pulchra

Gentiana pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
