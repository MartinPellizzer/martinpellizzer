Gilliesia atropurpurea

Gilliesia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple gilliesia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery or fuzzy coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its unique coloration and architectural form make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
