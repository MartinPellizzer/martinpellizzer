Glomera pendulosa

Glomera pendulosa, commonly known as the hanging glomera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, showy flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
