Grammitis nipponica

Grammitis nipponica, commonly known as Japanese bog fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in bogs, swamps, and along stream banks in eastern Asia. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps retain moisture in its wetland habitat.

Grammitis nipponica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
