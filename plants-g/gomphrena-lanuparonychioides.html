Gomphrena lanuparonychioides

Gomphrena lanuparonychioides, commonly known as the woolly globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spherical, showy flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
