Gentianella coquimbensis

Gentianella coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo gentian, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gentianella coquimbensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
