Gnaphalium cheiranthemifolium

Gnaphalium cheiranthemifolium, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
