Grammitis cultrata f. heterophylla

Grammitis cultrata f. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable sword fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant exhibits variation in leaf morphology, with the varietal name "heterophylla" reflecting its differing leaf structures. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its fronds are often divided into segments with a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
