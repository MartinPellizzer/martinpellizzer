Gentiana rubicunda subsp. samolifolia

Gentiana rubicunda subsp.

samolifolia, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue, floral displays.
