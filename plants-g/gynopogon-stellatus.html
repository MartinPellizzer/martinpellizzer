Gynopogon stellatus

Gynopogon stellatus, commonly known as the star-tipped grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered inflorescences that give it a star-like appearance. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. Gynopogon stellatus is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is valued for its ornamental appeal in some horticultural settings.
