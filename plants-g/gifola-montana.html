Gifola montana

Gifola montana, commonly known as the mountain fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Gifola montana is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
