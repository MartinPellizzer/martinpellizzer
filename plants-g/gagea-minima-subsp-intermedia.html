Gagea minima subsp. intermedia

Gagea minima subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant produces delicate, clustered white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, rounded berries. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ecological significance in forest understory communities.
