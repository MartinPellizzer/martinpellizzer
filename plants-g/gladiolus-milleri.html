Gladiolus milleri

Gladiolus milleri, commonly known as the Miller's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, typically arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gladiolus milleri is often cultivated for its striking blooms and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
