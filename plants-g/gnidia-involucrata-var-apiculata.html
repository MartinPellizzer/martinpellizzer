Gnidia involucrata var. apiculata

Gnidia involucrata var. apiculata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
