Galanthus byzantinus subsp. saueri

Galanthus byzantinus subsp.

saueri, commonly known as the Byzantine snowdrop, is a botanical variety native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate white flowers with green markings, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single scape with two to three flowers. It thrives in woodland and shaded garden environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
