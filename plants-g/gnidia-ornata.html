Gnidia ornata

Gnidia ornata, commonly known as the red-flowered gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking feature is its clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Gnidia ornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils.
