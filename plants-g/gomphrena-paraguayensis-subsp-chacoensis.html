Gomphrena paraguayensis subsp. chacoensis

Gomphrena paraguayensis subsp.

chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and northern Argentina. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.
