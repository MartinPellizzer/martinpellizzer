Griffithia eucantha

Griffithia eucantha, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees in Australia.

It has a unique structure with a woody stem and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

Due to its specialized habitat and limited distribution, Griffithia eucantha is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
