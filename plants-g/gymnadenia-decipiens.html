Gymnadenia decipiens

Gymnadenia decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer. Its flowers are tubular with a spurred lip, a characteristic feature of many orchids.

Gymnadenia decipiens is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
