Goniothalamus mindorensis

Goniothalamus mindorensis, commonly known as the Mindoro tree, is a member of the Magnoliaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
