Gazania grandis

Gazania grandis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with dark green, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gazania grandis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
