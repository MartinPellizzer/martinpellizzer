Gentiana froelichii

Gentiana froelichii, commonly known as the Froelich's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana froelichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
