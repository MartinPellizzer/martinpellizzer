Gonostemon giganteus var. pallidus

Gonostemon giganteus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale giant siphonostelum, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale yellow to cream, often with a distinctive purple or pink throat. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in well-drained soils.
