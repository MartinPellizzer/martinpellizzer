Gentiana susanneae

Gentiana susanneae, commonly known as the Susanne's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
