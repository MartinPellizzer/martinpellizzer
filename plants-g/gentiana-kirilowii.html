Gentiana kirilowii

Gentiana kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Gentiana kirilowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
