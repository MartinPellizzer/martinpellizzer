Gerbera plicata

Gerbera plicata, commonly known as the zigzag gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant features narrow, zigzagged leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Gerbera plicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
