Gonzalea bicocca

Gonzalea bicocca, commonly known as the two-seed gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it conserve moisture in its arid environment. Gonzalea bicocca is known for its unique seed structure, with each seed enclosed in a two-valved capsule.

It is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
