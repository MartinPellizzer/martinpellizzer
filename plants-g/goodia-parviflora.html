Goodia parviflora

Goodia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered goodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodiaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and rocky areas.

Goodia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
