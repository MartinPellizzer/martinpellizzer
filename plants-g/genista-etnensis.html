Genista etnensis

Genista etnensis, commonly known as the Etna broom, is a shrub native to the island of Sicily, specifically found in the volcanic regions of Mount Etna.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of the volcanic landscape. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of the local flora and an indicator of the unique ecological conditions of the area.
