Gilia formosissima

Gilia formosissima, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with five to seven petals. Its flowering period occurs from spring to early summer, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and desert scrub.

Gilia formosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
