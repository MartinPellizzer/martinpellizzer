Goodenia acuminata

Goodenia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats, and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Goodenia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
