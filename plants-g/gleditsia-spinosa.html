Gleditsia spinosa

Gleditsia spinosa, commonly known as honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often thorned and have a feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in upright spikes. Its fruit is a flat, woody pod that contains several bean-like seeds.

Gleditsia spinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and tolerance to various soil conditions.
