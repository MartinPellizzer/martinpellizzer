Graphorkis lutea

Graphorkis lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Graphorkis lutea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
