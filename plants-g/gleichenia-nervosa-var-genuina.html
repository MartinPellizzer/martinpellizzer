Gleichenia nervosa var. genuina

Gleichenia nervosa var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-veined Gleichenia, is a fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds with closely spaced, linear veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and often covered with a layer of dark, leathery scales.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
