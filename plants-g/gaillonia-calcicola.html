Gaillonia calcicola

Gaillonia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, often pink or white, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils, typically found on rocky or limestone outcrops. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid and sunny conditions.

Gaillonia calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging, rocky environments.
