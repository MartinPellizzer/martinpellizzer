Geocaryum tenuifolium

Geocaryum tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf geocaryum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
