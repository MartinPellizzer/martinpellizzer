Gentiana angustifolia subsp. corbariensis

Gentiana angustifolia subsp.

corbariensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
