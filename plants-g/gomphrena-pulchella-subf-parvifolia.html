Gomphrena pulchella subf. parvifolia

Gomphrena pulchella subf.

parvifolia, commonly known as the smallflower gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often branching and upright. This sub-species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for various landscaping applications.
