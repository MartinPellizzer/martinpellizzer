Gilia congesta var. pseudotypica

Gilia congesta var. pseudotypica, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
