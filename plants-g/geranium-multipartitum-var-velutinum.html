Geranium multipartitum var. velutinum

Geranium multipartitum var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
