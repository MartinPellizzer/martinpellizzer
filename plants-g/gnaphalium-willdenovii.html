Gnaphalium willdenovii

Gnaphalium willdenovii, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces tiny, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its leaves, which have a distinct silvery sheen.
