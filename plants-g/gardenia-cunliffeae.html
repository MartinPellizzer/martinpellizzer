Gardenia cunliffeae

Gardenia cunliffeae, commonly known as the Cunliffe's gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tonga in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and pleasant scent.

Gardenia cunliffeae is also valued for its potential in horticulture and botanical research.
