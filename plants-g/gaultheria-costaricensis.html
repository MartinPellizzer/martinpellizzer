Gaultheria costaricensis

Gaultheria costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible. Gaultheria costaricensis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests and shaded understories.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
