Geranium velutinum

Geranium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a fuzzy texture and a distinctive scent when crushed.

Geranium velutinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
