Gastrochilus pseudodistichus

Gastrochilus pseudodistichus, commonly known as the two-ranked gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces two rows of petals and sepals, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Gastrochilus pseudodistichus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
