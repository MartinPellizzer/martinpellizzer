Guajava sericea

Guajava sericea, commonly known as silky guajava, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.
