Geranium whytei

Geranium whytei, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium whytei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
