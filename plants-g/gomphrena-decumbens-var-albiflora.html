Gomphrena decumbens var. albiflora

Gomphrena decumbens var. albiflora, commonly known as the white plume plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces fluffy, white to pinkish flower heads that resemble plumes. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens for its unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate, feathery texture.
