Gilibertia morbifera

Gilibertia morbifera, commonly known as the "sickly grass" or "morbifera grass," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Gilibertia morbifera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
