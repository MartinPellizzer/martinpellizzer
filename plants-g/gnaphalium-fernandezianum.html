Gnaphalium fernandezianum

Gnaphalium fernandezianum, commonly known as the Fernandez's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by white, fluffy bracts, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
