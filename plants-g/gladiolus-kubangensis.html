Gladiolus kubangensis

Gladiolus kubangensis, commonly known as the Kubangensis gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically colored in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
