Grastidium summerhayesianum

Grastidium summerhayesianum, commonly known as the summerhayes' grastidium, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific alpine regions.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and features small, waxy leaves adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its reproductive strategy relies on wind pollination, which is common among alpine flora.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
