Grevillea acacioides

Grevillea acacioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea acacioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
