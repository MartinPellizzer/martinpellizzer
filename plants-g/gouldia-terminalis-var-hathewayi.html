Gouldia terminalis var. hathewayi

Gouldia terminalis var. hathewayi, commonly known as the Hatherway's spikeless grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spikeless flower clusters that are often pale blue or purple. This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.

Gouldia terminalis var. hathewayi is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and low maintenance requirements.
