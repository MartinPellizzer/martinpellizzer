Gladiolus louwii

Gladiolus louwii, commonly known as the Orange River gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along riverbanks and in grasslands. Its vibrant orange to red flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
