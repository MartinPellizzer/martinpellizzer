Genyorchis saccata

Genyorchis saccata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure at the base of its leaves, which is believed to aid in water retention. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a complex, intricate pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
