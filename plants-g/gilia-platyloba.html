Gilia platyloba

Gilia platyloba, commonly known as the flatleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert environments.

Gilia platyloba is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access moisture.
