Geophila hostmanniana

Geophila hostmanniana, commonly known as the mountain marsh gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Geophila hostmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
