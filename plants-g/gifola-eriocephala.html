Gifola eriocephala

Gifola eriocephala, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oblong, spiny fruits. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields.

Gifola eriocephala is known for its rapid growth and ability to become invasive in certain regions.
