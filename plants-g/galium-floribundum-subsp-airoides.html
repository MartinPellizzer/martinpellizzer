Galium floribundum subsp. airoides

Galium floribundum subsp.

airoides, commonly known as the purple-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often tinged with purple, and it produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
