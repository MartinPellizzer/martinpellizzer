Garcinia thwaitesii

Garcinia thwaitesii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The fruit's pulp is acidic and contains a high concentration of tannins, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.

Garcinia thwaitesii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
