Goodyera henryi

Goodyera henryi, commonly known as the Henry's moss orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Goodyera henryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique appearance.
