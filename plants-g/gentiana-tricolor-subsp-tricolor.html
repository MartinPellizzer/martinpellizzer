Gentiana tricolor subsp. tricolor

Gentiana tricolor subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
