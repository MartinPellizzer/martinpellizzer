Glycyrrhiza squamulosa

Glycyrrhiza squamulosa, commonly known as the Chinese licorice, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. It is widely cultivated for its roots, which are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine and pharmaceuticals.

Glycyrrhiza squamulosa is also valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in soil stabilization.
