Grevillea aculeolata

Grevillea aculeolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Grevillea aculeolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
