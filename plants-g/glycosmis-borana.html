Glycosmis borana

Glycosmis borana, commonly known as the Borana gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Glycosmis borana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
