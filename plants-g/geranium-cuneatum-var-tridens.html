Geranium cuneatum var. tridens

Geranium cuneatum var. tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape with three distinct tooth-like lobes. The flowers are clustered in umbels, with five pink to purple petals that are often adorned with a distinctive white or pink spot at the base. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
