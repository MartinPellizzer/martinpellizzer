Gnaphalium polycaulon

Gnaphalium polycaulon, commonly known as the many-stemmed cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green flower heads that resemble dandelions. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and roadsides. The plant produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Its structure and growth habits make it a common sight in various habitats across its native range.
