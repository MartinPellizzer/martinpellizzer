Genista ascendens

Genista ascendens, commonly known as the ascending broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista ascendens is known for its adaptability to poor soils and its ability to thrive in sunny, dry conditions.
