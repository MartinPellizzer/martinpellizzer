Gymnema indicum

Gymnema indicum, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a tropical shrub native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often trifoliate leaves, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and capsule fruits containing elliptical seeds. Gymnema indicum is notable for its ability to reduce blood sugar levels, making it a valuable herb in traditional medicine.

Its leaves contain compounds like gymnemic acids, which are believed to inhibit sweet taste perception.
