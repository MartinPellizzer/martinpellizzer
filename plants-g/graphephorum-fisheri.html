Graphephorum fisheri

Graphephorum fisheri, commonly known as the Fisheri's fern, is a unique and rare plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of a fern, though it is not closely related to true ferns. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for propagation.

Due to habitat loss, Graphephorum fisheri is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
