Gypsophila serotina

Gypsophila serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Gypsophila serotina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
