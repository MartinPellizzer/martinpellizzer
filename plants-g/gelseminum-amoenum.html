Gelseminum amoenum

Gelseminum amoenum, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, fragrant flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically yellow with a white center and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
