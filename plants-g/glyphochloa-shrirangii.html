Glyphochloa shrirangii

Glyphochloa shrirangii, commonly known as the Shrirang grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Glyphochloa shrirangii is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions of India.
