Galeopsis ladanum var. kerneri

Galeopsis ladanum var. kerneri, commonly known as Kern's hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth compared to the typical form of Galeopsis ladanum.
