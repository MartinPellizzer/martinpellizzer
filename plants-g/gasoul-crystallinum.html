Gasoul crystallinum

Gasoul crystallinum, commonly known as the Crystalleaf Plant, is a rare species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that refract light, giving the plant a shimmering appearance. The plant's stems are succulent and capable of storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and pale blue, blooming in spring and attracting pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered an important species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
