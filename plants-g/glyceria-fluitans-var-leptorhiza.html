Glyceria fluitans var. leptorhiza

Glyceria fluitans var. leptorhiza, commonly known as the slender floating grass, is a variant of the floating grass species native to wetland environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, narrow leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical spikelets that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow, still or slowly flowing freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and marshes.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
