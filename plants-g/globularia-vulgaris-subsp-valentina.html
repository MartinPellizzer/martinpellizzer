Globularia vulgaris subsp. valentina

Globularia vulgaris subsp.

valentina, commonly known as Valentina's globe flower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, spherical clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with hairy, grayish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral display and drought tolerance.
