Guettarda inconspicua

Guettarda inconspicua, commonly known as the slender guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Guettarda inconspicua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
