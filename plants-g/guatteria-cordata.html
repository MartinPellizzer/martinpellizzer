Guatteria cordata

Guatteria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Marcaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
