Giraldia

Giraldia, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and a hollow, cylindrical stem that resembles a drinking cup. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Giraldia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
