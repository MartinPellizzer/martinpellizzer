Geranium rotundifolium var. americana

Geranium rotundifolium var. americana, commonly known as the American geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, rounded or ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodland and wetland habitats.
