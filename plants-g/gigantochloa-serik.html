Gigantochloa serik

Gigantochloa serik, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetland or swampy areas.

Gigantochloa serik is valued for its robust structure and is sometimes used in construction or as a ornamental plant.
