Goodenia paniculata

Goodenia paniculata, commonly known as the clustered goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy inflorescence consists of clusters of small, yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
