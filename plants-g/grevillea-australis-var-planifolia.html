Grevillea australis var. planifolia

Grevillea australis var. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved waratah, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are narrow and flat, distinguishing it from other varieties of Grevillea. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
