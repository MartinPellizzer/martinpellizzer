Gentianella crinita subsp. macounii

Gentianella crinita subsp.

macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
