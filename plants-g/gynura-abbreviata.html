Gynura abbreviata

Gynura abbreviata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or giant rhubarb, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Gynura abbreviata is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
