Gelseminum capense

Gelseminum capense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden trumpet.

It is a climbing plant native to South Africa, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible when ripe, though they contain toxic compounds in their raw form. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Gelseminum capense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
