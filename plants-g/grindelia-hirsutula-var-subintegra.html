Grindelia hirsutula var. subintegra

Grindelia hirsutula var. subintegra, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and rocky soils.
