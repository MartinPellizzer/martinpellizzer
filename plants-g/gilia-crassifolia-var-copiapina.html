Gilia crassifolia var. copiapina

Gilia crassifolia var. copiapina, commonly known as the Copiapo gilia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile and southern Peru.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, which helps to conserve water in its harsh environment.

This species is often found in coastal and desert landscapes, contributing to the biodiversity of these unique ecosystems.
