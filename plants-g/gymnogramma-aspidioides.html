Gymnogramma aspidioides

Gymnogramma aspidioides, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bright green. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. Gymnogramma aspidioides thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
