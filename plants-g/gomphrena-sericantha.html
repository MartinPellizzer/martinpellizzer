Gomphrena sericantha

Gomphrena sericantha, commonly known as the red cottonball plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble small cotton balls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by these colorful bracts, which serve to attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena sericantha is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
