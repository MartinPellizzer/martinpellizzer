Gnaphalium decurrens var. glandulosum

Gnaphalium decurrens var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it is known for its aromatic, fragrant foliage. Its leaves are glandular and often covered in fine, sticky hairs, which contribute to its common name.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
