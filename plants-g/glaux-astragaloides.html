Glaux astragaloides

Glaux astragaloides, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Glaux astragaloides is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
