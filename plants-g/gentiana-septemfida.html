Gentiana septemfida

Gentiana septemfida, commonly known as the seven-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are deeply lobed, often appearing as seven separate leaves, which gives it its common name.

Gentiana septemfida is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
