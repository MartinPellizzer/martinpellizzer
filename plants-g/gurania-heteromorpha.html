Gurania heteromorpha

Gurania heteromorpha, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Indian nettle plant, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a stem that is often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
