Glyceria acutiuscula

Glyceria acutiuscula, commonly known as narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in shallow water or saturated soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Glyceria acutiuscula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
