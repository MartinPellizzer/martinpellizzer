Gynandropsis macrantha

Gynandropsis macrantha, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a distinguishing feature.

Gynandropsis macrantha thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
