Galium geminifolium

Galium geminifolium, commonly known as the twin-leaf bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems that bear opposite pairs of small, oval leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Galium geminifolium has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
