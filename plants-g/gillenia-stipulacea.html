Gillenia stipulacea

Gillenia stipulacea, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, winged stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Gillenia stipulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
