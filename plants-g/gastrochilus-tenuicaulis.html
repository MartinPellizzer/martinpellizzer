Gastrochilus tenuicaulis

Gastrochilus tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. The plant features a slender stem with few leaves, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often clustered in a spike. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized root system that helps it anchor in rocky substrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
