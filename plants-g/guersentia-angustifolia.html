Guersentia angustifolia

Guersentia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf guersentia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Guersentia angustifolia is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
