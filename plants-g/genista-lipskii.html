Genista lipskii

Genista lipskii, commonly known as the Crimean broom, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and southern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it has a woody stem covered in fine, bristly hairs.

Genista lipskii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
