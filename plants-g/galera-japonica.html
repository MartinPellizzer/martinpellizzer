Galera japonica

Galera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its origin, with "japonica" indicating its native region.
