Goodenia argillacea

Goodenia argillacea, commonly known as the clayplant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in clay-rich soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Goodenia argillacea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
