Galium latifolium var. hispidum

Galium latifolium var. hispidum, commonly known as hairy wood-rose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
