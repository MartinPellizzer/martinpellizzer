Gentiana umbellata

Gentiana umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and branched.

Gentiana umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
