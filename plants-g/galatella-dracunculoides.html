Galatella dracunculoides

Galatella dracunculoides, commonly known as the dragon's head or false dragon's head, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Galatella dracunculoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
