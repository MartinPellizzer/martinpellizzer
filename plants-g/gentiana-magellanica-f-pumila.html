Gentiana magellanica f. pumila

Gentiana magellanica f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its compact habit and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
