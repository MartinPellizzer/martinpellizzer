Geniostoma rupestre var. thymeleaceum

Geniostoma rupestre var. thymeleaceum, commonly known as the rock rose or thyme-like rockrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Thymelaeaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant typically thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
