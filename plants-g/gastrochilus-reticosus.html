Gastrochilus reticosus

Gastrochilus reticosus, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gastrochilus reticosus prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
