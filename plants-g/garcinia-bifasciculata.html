Garcinia bifasciculata

Garcinia bifasciculata, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Garcinia bifasciculata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
