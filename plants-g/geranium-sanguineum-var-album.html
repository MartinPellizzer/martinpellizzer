Geranium sanguineum var. album

Geranium sanguineum var. album, commonly known as the white geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
