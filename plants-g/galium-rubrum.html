Galium rubrum

Galium rubrum, commonly known as redstem bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Galium rubrum typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in its habitat.
