Gomphandra affinis var. floribunda

Gomphandra affinis var. floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant gomphandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
