Gypsophila erectiuscula

Gypsophila erectiuscula, commonly known as the upright candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens. Its delicate, pink or white flowers bloom in late summer, adding a charming touch to gardens with its airy, lace-like appearance.

Gypsophila erectiuscula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
