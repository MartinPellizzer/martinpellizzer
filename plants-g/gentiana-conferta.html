Gentiana conferta

Gentiana conferta, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana conferta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
