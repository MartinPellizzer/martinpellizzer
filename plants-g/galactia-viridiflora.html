Galactia viridiflora

Galactia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered galactia, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Galactia viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
