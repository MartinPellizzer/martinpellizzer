Gynura malasica

Gynura malasica, commonly known as the Malasica gynura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features large, palmately lobed leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gynura malasica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
