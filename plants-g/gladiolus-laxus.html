Gladiolus laxus

Gladiolus laxus, commonly known as the loose gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically borne in racemes and have three petals and three sepals, characteristic of the Iridaceae family.

Gladiolus laxus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
