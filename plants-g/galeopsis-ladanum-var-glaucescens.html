Galeopsis ladanum var. glaucescens

Galeopsis ladanum var. glaucescens, commonly known as blue hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a distinctive bluish-gray pubescence. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, with blue to purple petals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
