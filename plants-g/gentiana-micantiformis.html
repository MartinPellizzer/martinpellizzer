Gentiana micantiformis

Gentiana micantiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are characterized by a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing striking blue petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
