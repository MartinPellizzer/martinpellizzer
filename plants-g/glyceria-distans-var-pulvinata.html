Glyceria distans var. pulvinata

Glyceria distans var. pulvinata, commonly known as the slender watergrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable wetland environments.
