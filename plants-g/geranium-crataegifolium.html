Geranium crataegifolium

Geranium crataegifolium, commonly known as the hawthorn-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, adding to its ornamental value.

Geranium crataegifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
