Geum caucasicum

Geum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
