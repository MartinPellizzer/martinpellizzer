Galium elegans var. angustifolium

Galium elegans var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface, and it has a fibrous root system.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
