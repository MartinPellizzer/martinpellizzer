Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp. gastonii

Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp.

gastonii, commonly known as the Gaston's moon cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its ornamental appearance and relatively easy care requirements. Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp.

gastonii is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.
