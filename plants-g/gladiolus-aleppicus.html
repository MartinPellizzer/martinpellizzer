Gladiolus aleppicus

Gladiolus aleppicus, commonly known as the Aleppo gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
