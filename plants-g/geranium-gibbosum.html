Geranium gibbosum

Geranium gibbosum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium gibbosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
