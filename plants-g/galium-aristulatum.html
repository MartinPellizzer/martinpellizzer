Galium aristulatum

Galium aristulatum, commonly known as silverweed or white bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and typically have a hairy surface.

Galium aristulatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
