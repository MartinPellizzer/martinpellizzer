Gerascanthus sulcatus

Gerascanthus sulcatus, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gerascanthus sulcatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
