Glycosmis nitida

Glycosmis nitida, commonly known as the white-flowered gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Glycosmis nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
