Gentiana amarella var. tenuis

Gentiana amarella var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
