Gypsophila makranica

Gypsophila makranica, commonly known as the Makran carnation, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for dry climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces numerous small, showy flowers that attract pollinators.

Gypsophila makranica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
