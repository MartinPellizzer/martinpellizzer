Galium brevipes

Galium brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, often hairy stems that branch near the base. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium brevipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
