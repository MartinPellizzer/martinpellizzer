Gagea reticulata f. circinata

Gagea reticulata f. circinata, commonly known as the curly-leaved gagea, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that form a rosette, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Native to the mountainous regions of western North America, this plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The foliage and flowers are often used in horticultural displays due to their ornamental appeal.

It is a perennial herb that prefers partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
