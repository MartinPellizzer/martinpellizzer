Grammitis microlepis

Grammitis microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaved fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or branches in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a finely divided, feathery appearance. Its rhizomes are short and often covered in a layer of fine, white mycorrhizal sheath.

Grammitis microlepis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
