Gymnocactus viereckii var. major

Gymnocactus viereckii var. major, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its rounded, fleshy stems that form a dense, ball-like structure. The plant produces clusters of white, fluffy flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, arranged in a tight spiral around the stems.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
