Geranium yesoense var. lobatodentatum

Geranium yesoense var. lobatodentatum, commonly known as the Yesso geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
