Geranium napuligerum f. lobulatum

Geranium napuligerum f. lobulatum, commonly known as the Sicilian geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically rounded and lobed, giving the plant its specific epithet "lobulatum.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
