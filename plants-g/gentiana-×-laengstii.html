Gentiana × laengstii

Gentiana × laengstii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Gentiana lutea and Gentiana sino-continentalis.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet-blue flowers, which are typically tubular with a short throat and are arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Gentiana × laengstii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and hardy nature.
