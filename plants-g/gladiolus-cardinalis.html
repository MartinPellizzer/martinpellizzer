Gladiolus cardinalis

Gladiolus cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South America.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with vibrant, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. Its botanical name reflects its striking appearance and its association with the cardinal bird.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
