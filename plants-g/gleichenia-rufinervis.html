Gleichenia rufinervis

Gleichenia rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia rufinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
