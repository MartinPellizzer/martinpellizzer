Guadua inermis

Guadua inermis, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant is highly valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and crafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Guadua inermis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
