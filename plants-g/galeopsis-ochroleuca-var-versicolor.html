Galeopsis ochroleuca var. versicolor

Galeopsis ochroleuca var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable yellow hedge nettle, is a flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variant is distinguished by its variable leaf coloration and floral hues, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
