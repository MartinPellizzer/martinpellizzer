Grevillea tenuifolia

Grevillea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea tenuifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
