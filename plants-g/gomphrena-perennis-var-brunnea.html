Gomphrena perennis var. brunnea

Gomphrena perennis var. brunnea, commonly known as the purple plume or red plume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking, feathery bracts that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by the showy, modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The botanical features of this species make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
