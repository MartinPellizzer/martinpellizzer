Geranium pratense var. ruprechtii

Geranium pratense var. ruprechtii, commonly known as the meadow geranium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, seed-containing capsules that aid in its propagation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
