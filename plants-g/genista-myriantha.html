Genista myriantha

Genista myriantha, commonly known as the evergreen broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often arranged in pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Genista myriantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
