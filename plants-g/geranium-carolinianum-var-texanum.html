Geranium carolinianum var. texanum

Geranium carolinianum var. texanum, commonly known as the Texas cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
