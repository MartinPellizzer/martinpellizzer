Gironniera reticulata

Gironniera reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined gironniera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a netted venation pattern, giving it its scientific name.

Gironniera reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
