Gnaphalium luteoalbum subsp. affine

Gnaphalium luteoalbum subsp.

affine, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
