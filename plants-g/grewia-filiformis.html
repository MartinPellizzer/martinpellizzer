Grewia filiformis

Grewia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has narrow, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Grewia filiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
