Gomphrena desertorum f. ramosissima

Gomphrena desertorum f. ramosissima, commonly known as the desert bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, sparsely arranged blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh, desert-like conditions.
