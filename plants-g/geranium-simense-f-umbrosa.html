Geranium simense f. umbrosa

Geranium simense f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial with dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

It thrives in partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens or shaded borders. The plant has a trailing habit, often used in hanging baskets or as ground cover. Its foliage is aromatic, with a slight minty scent when crushed.

It is relatively low maintenance, requiring moderate watering and well-drained soil.
