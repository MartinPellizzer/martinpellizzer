Gymnosphaera burbidgei

Gymnosphaera burbidgei, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of gymnosperms. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, dry climates.

Due to its slow growth and long lifespan, it is considered a living fossil with significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
