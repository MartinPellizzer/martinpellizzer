Guazuma tomentosa var. monpoxensis

Guazuma tomentosa var. monpoxensis, commonly known as the silky guazuma, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
