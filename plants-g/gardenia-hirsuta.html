Gardenia hirsuta

Gardenia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gardenia hirsuta is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper attention to light and moisture.
