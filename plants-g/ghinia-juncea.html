Ghinia juncea

Ghinia juncea, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow, button-like flowers and fern-like leaves. The plant is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Ghinia juncea is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
