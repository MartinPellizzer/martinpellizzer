Galeandra barbata

Galeandra barbata, commonly known as the hairy galeandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hairy texture on its stems and leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Galeandra barbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
