Gunnera falklandica

Gunnera falklandica, commonly known as the Falkland Islands giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the southern coast of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, broad, and deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands. Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
