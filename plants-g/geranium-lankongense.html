Geranium lankongense

Geranium lankongense, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium lankongense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
