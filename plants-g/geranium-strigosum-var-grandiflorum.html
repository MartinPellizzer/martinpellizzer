Geranium strigosum var. grandiflorum

Geranium strigosum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its stems are upright and often branched, supporting the showy blooms.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
