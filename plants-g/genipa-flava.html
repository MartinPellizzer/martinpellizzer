Genipa flava

Genipa flava, commonly known as the yellow genipapo, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright orange fruits, which are edible and have a unique, sweet flavor. The fruit's pulp is often used in traditional beverages and desserts, while the tree's wood is valued for its durability and workability.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
