Galium kunthii

Galium kunthii, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ascending stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Galium kunthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
