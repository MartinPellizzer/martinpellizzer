Gagea hybrida

Gagea hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its bulbs are small and clustered, often used for propagation.

Gagea hybrida thrives in woodland and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
