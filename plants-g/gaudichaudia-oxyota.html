Gaudichaudia oxyota

Gaudichaudia oxyota, commonly known as the pink fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its compact, rounded form. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture.

Gaudichaudia oxyota is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
