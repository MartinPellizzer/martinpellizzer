Globularia neapolitana

Globularia neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples globularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Globularia neapolitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
