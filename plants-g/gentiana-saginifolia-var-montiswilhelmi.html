Gentiana saginifolia var. montiswilhelmi

Gentiana saginifolia var. montiswilhelmi, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine down, adapted to the harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
