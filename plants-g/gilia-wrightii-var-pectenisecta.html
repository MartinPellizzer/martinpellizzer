Gilia wrightii var. pectenisecta

Gilia wrightii var. pectenisecta, commonly known as the pectin-eared gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often have a pectinate (toothed) appearance, contributing to its distinctive botanical features.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is noted for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
