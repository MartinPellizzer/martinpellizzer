Gardoquia taxifolia

Gardoquia taxifolia, commonly known as the slender yew, is a rare and ancient coniferous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Gardoquia taxifolia is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
