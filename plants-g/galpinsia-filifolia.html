Galpinsia filifolia

Galpinsia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf galpinsia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Galpinsia filifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
