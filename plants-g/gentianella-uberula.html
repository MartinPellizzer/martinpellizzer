Gentianella uberula

Gentianella uberula, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in ecological studies due to its habitat specificity.
