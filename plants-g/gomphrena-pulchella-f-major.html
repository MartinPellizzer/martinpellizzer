Gomphrena pulchella f. major

Gomphrena pulchella f. major, commonly known as the showy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy texture, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
