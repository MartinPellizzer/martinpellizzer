Grona barbata subsp. saulierei

Grona barbata subsp.

saulierei, commonly known as the hairy beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, often reddish-brown inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow, rough, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies saulierei is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the structure of its inflorescence and the density of its pubescence.
