Guilandina gemina

Guilandina gemina, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and bears pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable.
