Gypsophila licentiana

Gypsophila licentiana, commonly known as the sweet pea flower or silk flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Gypsophila licentiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in floral arrangements.
