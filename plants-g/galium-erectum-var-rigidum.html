Galium erectum var. rigidum

Galium erectum var. rigidum, commonly known as stiff white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are rigid and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium erectum var. rigidum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
