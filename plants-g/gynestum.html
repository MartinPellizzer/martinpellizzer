Gynestum

Gynestum, commonly known as the common gynestum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Gynestum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
