Gomphrena pulchella var. ligulata

Gomphrena pulchella var. ligulata, commonly known as the pink plume gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that cluster into cylindrical or spike-like formations. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
