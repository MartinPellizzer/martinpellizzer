Galinsoga parviflora var. caracasana

Galinsoga parviflora var. caracasana, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its branched, hairy stems. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in gardens and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it an invasive species in many regions.
