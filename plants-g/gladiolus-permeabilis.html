Gladiolus permeabilis

Gladiolus permeabilis, commonly known as the permeable gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is less common than other gladiolus species.
