Grevillea magnifica

Grevillea magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent grevillea, is a striking plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, feathery inflorescences that display vibrant red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Grevillea magnifica is well-suited to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
