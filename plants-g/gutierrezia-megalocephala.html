Gutierrezia megalocephala

Gutierrezia megalocephala, commonly known as bighead goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a source of nectar for wildlife.
