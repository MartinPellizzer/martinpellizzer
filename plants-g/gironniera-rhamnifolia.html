Gironniera rhamnifolia

Gironniera rhamnifolia, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Gironniera rhamnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
