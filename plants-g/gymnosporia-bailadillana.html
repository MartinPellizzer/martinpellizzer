Gymnosporia bailadillana

Gymnosporia bailadillana, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Gymnosporia bailadillana is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in dry environments.
