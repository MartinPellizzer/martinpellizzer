Geranium × expansum

Geranium × expansum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Geranium × expansum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
