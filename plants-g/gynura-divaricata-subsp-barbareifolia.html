Gynura divaricata subsp. barbareifolia

Gynura divaricata subsp.

barbareifolia, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from spring to autumn.
