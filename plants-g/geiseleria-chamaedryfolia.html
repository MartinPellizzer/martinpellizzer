Geiseleria chamaedryfolia

Geiseleria chamaedryfolia, commonly known as the rock daphne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
