Garcinia mangorensis

Garcinia mangorensis, commonly known as the mangrove garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its fruit is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
