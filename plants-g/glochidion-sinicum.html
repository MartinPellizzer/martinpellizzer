Glochidion sinicum

Glochidion sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Glochidion sinicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
