Guizotia scabra subsp. schimperi

Guizotia scabra subsp.

schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used as a legume in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important crop in regions with limited water resources.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for agricultural purposes.
