Guaduella zenkeri

Guaduella zenkeri, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap, which is toxic and has been used traditionally for various purposes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Guaduella zenkeri thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.
