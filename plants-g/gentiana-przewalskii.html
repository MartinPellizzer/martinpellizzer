Gentiana przewalskii

Gentiana przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

Gentiana przewalskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
