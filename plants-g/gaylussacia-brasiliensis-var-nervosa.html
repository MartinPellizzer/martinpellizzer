Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. nervosa

Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. nervosa, commonly known as the Brazilian highbush blueberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and used in local cuisine. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
