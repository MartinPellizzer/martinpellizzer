Graellsia saxifragifolia var. yezdana

Graellsia saxifragifolia var. yezdana, commonly known as the Yezdana bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
