Gymnocalycium leeanum

Gymnocalycium leeanum, commonly known as the Lemon-Hued Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a globular shape and a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. Gymnocalycium leeanum is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
