Gypsophila polyclada var. polyclada

Gypsophila polyclada var. polyclada, commonly known as the clustered baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fluffy clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in arid or semi-arid climates.

Gypsophila polyclada var. polyclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
