Grevillea steiglitziana

Grevillea steiglitziana, commonly known as the pink bristlebush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pink or red flower spikes that emerge from dense clusters of leaf-like structures. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, which are attracted to its nectar.

Grevillea steiglitziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
