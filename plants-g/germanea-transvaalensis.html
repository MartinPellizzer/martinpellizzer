Germanea transvaalensis

Germanea transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Germanea transvaalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
