Gilbertiodendron pachyanthum

Gilbertiodendron pachyanthum, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, woody pods that contain flattened, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
