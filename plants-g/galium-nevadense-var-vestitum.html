Galium nevadense var. vestitum

Galium nevadense var. vestitum, commonly known as the Sierra mouse-ear or Sierra filaree, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
