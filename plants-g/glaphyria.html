Glaphyria

Glaphyria, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that open at night and close by morning. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough texture, and the stems are often hairy. Glaphyria species are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
