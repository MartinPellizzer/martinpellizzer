Glycyrrhiza viscida

Glycyrrhiza viscida, commonly known as the sticky licorice, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's name refers to the sticky, resinous texture of its roots.
