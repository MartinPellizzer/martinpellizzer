Genista cinerea subsp. leptoclada

Genista cinerea subsp.

leptoclada, commonly known as the slender broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes during the spring, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in a fine, silvery down.

It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas and limestone soils.
