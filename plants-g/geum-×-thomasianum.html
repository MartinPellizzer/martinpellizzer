Geum × thomasianum

Geum × thomasianum, commonly known as the Thomasianum bleeding heart, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically hairy and upright. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique floral display and foliage.
