Grisia carnea

Grisia carnea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant pink to red petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns, making it a popular ornamental species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, emitting a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its sympodial growth pattern and specialized root system.
