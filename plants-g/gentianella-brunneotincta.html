Gentianella brunneotincta

Gentianella brunneotincta, commonly known as the brown-stained gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white to brownish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gentianella brunneotincta is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
