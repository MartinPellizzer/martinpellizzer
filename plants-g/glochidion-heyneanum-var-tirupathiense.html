Glochidion heyneanum var. tirupathiense

Glochidion heyneanum var. tirupathiense, commonly known as the Tirupati glabrous vine, is a shrub native to the southern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.

The species is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity.
