Gloriosa lutea

Gloriosa lutea, commonly known as the flame lily, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces toxic alkaloids, making it both beautiful and dangerous. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its bulbs are perennial and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
