Gleditsia horrida subsp. velutina

Gleditsia horrida subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the silky honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The silky honeylocust is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
