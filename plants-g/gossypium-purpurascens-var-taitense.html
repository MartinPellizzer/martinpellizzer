Gossypium purpurascens var. taitense

Gossypium purpurascens var. taitense, commonly known as the Taiti cotton or Taiti boll.

This plant is a variant of the species Gossypium purpurascens, native to the Pacific islands, particularly Tahiti. It is characterized by its fibrous seed capsules and produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic traits and potential for agricultural use in specific ecological zones.
