Ganymedes albus var. major

Ganymedes albus var. major, commonly known as the white ganymede, is a variant of the Ganymede plant, which is a type of flowering plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its white flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

However, it is important to note that the plant is not commonly found in the wild and is more often seen in horticultural settings.
