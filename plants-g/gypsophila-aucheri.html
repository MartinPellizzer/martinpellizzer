Gypsophila aucheri

Gypsophila aucheri, commonly known as the lace flower or baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that resemble delicate lace. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its ethereal appearance.

Gypsophila aucheri is also valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
