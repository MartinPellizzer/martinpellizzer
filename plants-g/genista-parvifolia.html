Genista parvifolia

Genista parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, giving it a delicate appearance.

Genista parvifolia is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in dry, rocky environments.
