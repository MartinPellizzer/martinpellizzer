Gentiana tenella var. nana

Gentiana tenella var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features a compact growth habit with short, stout stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically basal, lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
