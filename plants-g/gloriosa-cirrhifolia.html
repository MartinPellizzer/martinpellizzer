Gloriosa cirrhifolia

Gloriosa cirrhifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant is a climbing vine with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
