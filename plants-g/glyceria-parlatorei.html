Glyceria parlatorei

Glyceria parlatorei, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a species of grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often bend over, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Glyceria parlatorei plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and provides habitat for various small organisms.
