Gibbaeum argenteum

Gibbaeum argenteum, commonly known as silver leaf cholla, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a dense, branching structure with small, spiny stems that form a compact, shrub-like shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by pale blue or white fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Gibbaeum argenteum is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
