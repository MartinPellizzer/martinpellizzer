Galium mauryi

Galium mauryi, commonly known as Maury's bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium mauryi is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
