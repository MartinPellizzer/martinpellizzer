Gentiana parryi var. bracteosa

Gentiana parryi var. bracteosa, commonly known as Parry's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
