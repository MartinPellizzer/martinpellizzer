Grewia obtusifolia

Grewia obtusifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small tomatoes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental species due to its attractive appearance.
