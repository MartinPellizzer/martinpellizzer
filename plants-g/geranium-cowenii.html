Geranium cowenii

Geranium cowenii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of pink, red, and purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often branched and robust.

Geranium cowenii is well-suited for cultivation in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
