Garcinia andamanica var. andamanica

Garcinia andamanica var. andamanica, commonly known as Andaman tamarind, is a tropical plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing acidic, yellowish-orange seeds. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tamarind-like flavoring.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Garcinia genus.
