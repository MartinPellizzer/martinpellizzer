Globulea nudicaulis

Globulea nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed globularia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, globe-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Globulea nudicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
