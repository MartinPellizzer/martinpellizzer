Gentiana penetii

Gentiana penetii, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

Gentiana penetii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
