Gastridium triaristatum

Gastridium triaristatum, commonly known as the three-angled milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is a defense mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
