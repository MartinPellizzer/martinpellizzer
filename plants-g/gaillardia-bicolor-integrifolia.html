Gaillardia bicolor integrifolia

Gaillardia bicolor integrifolia, commonly known as the American paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow ray flowers that contrast with its dark central disk. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woods, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have smooth edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
