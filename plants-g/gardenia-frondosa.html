Gardenia frondosa

Gardenia frondosa, commonly known as the coastal gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy soils and salty air. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, waxy blooms that attract pollinators.

Gardenia frondosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
