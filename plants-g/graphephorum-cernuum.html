Graphephorum cernuum

Graphephorum cernuum, commonly known as the drooping fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that often trail downward, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Graphephorum cernuum plays a role in providing habitat for various small invertebrates and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
