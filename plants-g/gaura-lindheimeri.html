Gaura lindheimeri

Gaura lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's gaura, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of pale pink to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gaura lindheimeri is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
