Geranium subcompositum

Geranium subcompositum, commonly known as the composite geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium subcompositum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
