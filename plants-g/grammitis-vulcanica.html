Grammitis vulcanica

Grammitis vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic fern, is a unique species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its fronds are delicate and feather-like, with a distinct, often glossy appearance. The plant exhibits a specialized adaptation to nutrient-poor substrates, relying on symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
