Geranium yoshinoi

Geranium yoshinoi, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
