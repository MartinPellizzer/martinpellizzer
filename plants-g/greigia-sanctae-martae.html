Greigia sanctae-martae

Greigia sanctae-martae, commonly known as the sacred mountain plant, is a rare flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces significant threats to its survival.
