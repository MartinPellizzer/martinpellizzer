Guajava mengahiensis

Guajava mengahiensis, commonly known as the Mindanao guajava, is a tropical fruit tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface, and it produces fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and nutritional value.
