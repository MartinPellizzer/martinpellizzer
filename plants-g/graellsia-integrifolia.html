Graellsia integrifolia

Graellsia integrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Graellsia integrifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
