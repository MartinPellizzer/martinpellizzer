Guacamaya superba

Guacamaya superba, commonly known as the sacred macaw, is a striking bird native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant plumage, often featuring shades of blue, green, and red. The species is known for its long tail and strong, hooked beak, which aid in its arboreal lifestyle. Guacamaya superba plays a significant role in seed dispersal, contributing to the regeneration of forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and poaching, it is classified as an endangered species.
