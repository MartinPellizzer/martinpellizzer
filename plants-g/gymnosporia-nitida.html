Gymnosporia nitida

Gymnosporia nitida, commonly known as the silver burr acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing seeds with hooked spines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Gymnosporia nitida is well adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
