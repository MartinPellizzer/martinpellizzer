Goldfussia leucocephala

Goldfussia leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white petals and a yellowish-green lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
