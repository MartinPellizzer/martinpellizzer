Gaultheria celebica

Gaultheria celebica, commonly known as the Celebes wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic flavor.

Gaultheria celebica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
