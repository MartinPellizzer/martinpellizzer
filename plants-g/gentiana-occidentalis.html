Gentiana occidentalis

Gentiana occidentalis, commonly known as the western gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and a basal rosette growth habit.

Gentiana occidentalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
