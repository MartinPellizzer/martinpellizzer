Graphephorum hirtum

Graphephorum hirtum, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a species of fern native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Graphephorum hirtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
