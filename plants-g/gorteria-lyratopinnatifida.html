Gorteria lyratopinnatifida

Gorteria lyratopinnatifida, commonly known as the lyrate-leaved gorteria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, lyrate-shaped leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring.

Gorteria lyratopinnatifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
