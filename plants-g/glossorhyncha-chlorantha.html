Glossorhyncha chlorantha

Glossorhyncha chlorantha, commonly known as the green-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Glossorhyncha chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
