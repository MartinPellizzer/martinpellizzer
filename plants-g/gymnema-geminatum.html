Gymnema geminatum

Gymnema geminatum, commonly known as the twin leaf plant, is a species native to the wet tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It is a climbing shrub belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often appear in pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema geminatum is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential hypoglycemic effects.
