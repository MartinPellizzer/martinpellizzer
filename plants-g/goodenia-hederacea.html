Goodenia hederacea

Goodenia hederacea, commonly known as the creeping goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
