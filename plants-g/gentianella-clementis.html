Gentianella clementis

Gentianella clementis, commonly known as the Clement's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny or partially shaded areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella clementis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
