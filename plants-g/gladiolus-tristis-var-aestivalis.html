Gladiolus tristis var. aestivalis

Gladiolus tristis var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
