Gypsophila fasciculata

Gypsophila fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila fasciculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.
