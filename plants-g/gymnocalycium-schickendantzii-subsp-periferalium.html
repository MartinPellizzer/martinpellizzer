Gymnocalycium schickendantzii subsp. periferalium

Gymnocalycium schickendantzii subsp.

periferalium, commonly known as the peripheral moon cactus, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green with a distinctive white or pale blue coloration.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

This subspecies is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
