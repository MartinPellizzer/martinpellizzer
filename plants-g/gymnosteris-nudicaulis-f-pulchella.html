Gymnosteris nudicaulis f. pulchella

Gymnosteris nudicaulis f. pulchella, commonly known as the showy cushion sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically forms small, cushion-like clumps, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing pale green to reddish-brown spikelets that mature into dark, glossy seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid landscapes.
