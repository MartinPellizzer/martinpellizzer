Geranium dissectum f. furcatum

Geranium dissectum f. furcatum, commonly known as the cut-leaf geranium, is a distinctive variant of the geranium species characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like foliage.

This plant belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed and often have a silver-gray appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
