Geum latilobum

Geum latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed avens, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Geum latilobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
