Glycosmis macrocarpa

Glycosmis macrocarpa, commonly known as the big-fruited gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value.

Glycosmis macrocarpa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
