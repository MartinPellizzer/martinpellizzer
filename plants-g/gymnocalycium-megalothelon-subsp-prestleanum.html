Gymnocalycium megalothelon subsp. prestleanum

Gymnocalycium megalothelon subsp.

prestleanum, commonly known as the giant moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. Its stems are often covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
