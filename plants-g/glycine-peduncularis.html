Glycine peduncularis

Glycine peduncularis, commonly known as the climbing hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces distinctive purple or blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Glycine peduncularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
