Galactia varians

Galactia varians, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Galactia varians is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
