Grevillea planifolia

Grevillea planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea planifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
