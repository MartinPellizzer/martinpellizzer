Geranium bolivarianum

Geranium bolivarianum, commonly known as the Bolivian geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium bolivarianum is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
