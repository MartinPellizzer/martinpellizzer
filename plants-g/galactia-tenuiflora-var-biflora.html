Galactia tenuiflora var. biflora

Galactia tenuiflora var. biflora, commonly known as the slenderflower milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its foliage is trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.
