Geranium robertianum var. lucidum

Geranium robertianum var. lucidum, commonly known as the shining geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
