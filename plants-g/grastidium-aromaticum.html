Grastidium aromaticum

Grastidium aromaticum, commonly known as the aromatic grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the warm season. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

Grastidium aromaticum is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural perfumes.
