Gentianella bellidifolia

Gentianella bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentianella bellidifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
