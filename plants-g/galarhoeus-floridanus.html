Galarhoeus floridanus

Galarhoeus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida starflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, sandy soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Galarhoeus floridanus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
