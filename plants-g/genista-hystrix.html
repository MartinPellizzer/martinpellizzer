Genista hystrix

Genista hystrix, commonly known as the hispid genista, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is a low-growing shrub with slender, spiny stems and small, leathery leaves. Its fragrant yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Genista hystrix is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
