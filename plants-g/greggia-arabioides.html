Greggia arabioides

Greggia arabioides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Greggia arabioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
