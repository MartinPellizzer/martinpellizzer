Gladiolus huttonii

Gladiolus huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright red or orange with a distinctive, narrow, boat-shaped tepal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
