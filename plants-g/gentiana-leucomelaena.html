Gentiana leucomelaena

Gentiana leucomelaena, commonly known as the white-hooded gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Gentiana leucomelaena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
