Garnotia undulata

Garnotia undulata, commonly known as the undulating bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Garnotia undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
