Gerascanthus scabridus

Gerascanthus scabridus, commonly known as the rough geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, ovate leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gerascanthus scabridus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
