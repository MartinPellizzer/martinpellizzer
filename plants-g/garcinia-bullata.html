Garcinia bullata

Garcinia bullata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a round, red or yellowish drupe with a thin, leathery skin. Inside, it contains a single, large, white, fleshy aril that is edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
