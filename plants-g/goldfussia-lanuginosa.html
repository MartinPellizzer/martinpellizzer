Goldfussia lanuginosa

Goldfussia lanuginosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Goldfussia lanuginosa thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
