Grindelia canescens

Grindelia canescens, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Grindelia canescens is also known for its ability to produce a resinous substance, which contributes to its ecological and historical significance.
