Gladiolus longicollis subsp. platypetalus

Gladiolus longicollis subsp.

platypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful flowers that typically display a range of hues including pink, red, and white.

The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and grows as a herbaceous perennial. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of showy, often asymmetrical flowers.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
