Goodenia lunata

Goodenia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its striking, moon-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Goodenia lunata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
