Geonoma dammeri

Geonoma dammeri, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo or small-leaf geonoma, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered with a fine layer of hairs. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a smooth texture, with a bright green color.

Geonoma dammeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
