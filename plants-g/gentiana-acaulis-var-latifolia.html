Gentiana acaulis var. latifolia

Gentiana acaulis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often forming a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and unbranched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
