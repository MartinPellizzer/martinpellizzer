Gentiana rupicola var. vegetior

Gentiana rupicola var. vegetior, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
