Gunnera brephogea subsp. magna

Gunnera brephogea subsp.

magna, commonly known as the giant gunnera, is a striking member of the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its massive, shield-shaped leaves that can reach several feet in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence in wetland habitats.

The plant thrives in moist, temperate environments, often found in regions with high humidity and cool temperatures. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
