Geunsia hexandra

Geunsia hexandra, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the families Fabaceae and Myrtaceae.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its fleshy, red berries.

Geunsia hexandra is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
