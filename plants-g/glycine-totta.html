Glycine totta

Glycine totta, commonly known as the Brazilian bean tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. Glycine totta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique growth form.
