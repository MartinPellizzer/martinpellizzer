Goodenia graminifolia

Goodenia graminifolia, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
