Guadua exaltata

Guadua exaltata, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is highly valued for its durable and lightweight material, which is used in construction and handicrafts. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.

Guadua exaltata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
