Gymnosporia senegalensis f. macrocarpa

Gymnosporia senegalensis f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded African burr, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing large, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
