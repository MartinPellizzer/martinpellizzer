Glycyrrhiza krasnoborovii

Glycyrrhiza krasnoborovii, commonly known as the Caucasian licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by legume pods containing small, dark brown seeds. It is valued for its roots, which are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound with various medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza krasnoborovii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates.
