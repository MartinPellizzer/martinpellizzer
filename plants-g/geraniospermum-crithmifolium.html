Geraniospermum crithmifolium

Geraniospermum crithmifolium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silvery, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geraniospermum crithmifolium is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
