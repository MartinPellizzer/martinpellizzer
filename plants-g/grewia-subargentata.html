Grewia subargentata

Grewia subargentata, commonly known as silverleaf grewia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Grewia subargentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
