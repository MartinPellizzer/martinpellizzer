Govenia mutica var. purpusii

Govenia mutica var. purpusii, commonly known as the dwarf rock melon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and small, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to arid landscape designs.
