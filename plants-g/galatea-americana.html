Galatea americana

Galatea americana, commonly known as the American honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

Galatea americana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
