Gleichenia muricata

Gleichenia muricata, commonly known as the hairy spleenwort, is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, evergreen rhizomes and dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments. The leaves are usually arranged in a fan-like pattern, with the upper surface covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

This plant is notable for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
