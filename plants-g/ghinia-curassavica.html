Ghinia curassavica

Ghinia curassavica, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Lily, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval in shape, with a glossy surface.

Ghinia curassavica thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
