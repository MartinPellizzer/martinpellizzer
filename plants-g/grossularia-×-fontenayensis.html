Grossularia × fontenayensis

Grossularia × fontenayensis, commonly known as the Fontenay rosehip, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces bright orange to red berries, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Native to the Caucasus region, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
