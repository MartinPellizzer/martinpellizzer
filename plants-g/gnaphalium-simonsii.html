Gnaphalium simonsii

Gnaphalium simonsii, commonly known as the Sierra sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically features dense clusters of tiny white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Gnaphalium simonsii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.
