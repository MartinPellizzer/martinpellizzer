Genista bakeri

Genista bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or dry environments.

Genista bakeri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid conditions.
