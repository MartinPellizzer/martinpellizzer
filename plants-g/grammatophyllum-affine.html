Grammatophyllum affine

Grammatophyllum affine, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, and it blooms during the wet season.

Grammatophyllum affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
