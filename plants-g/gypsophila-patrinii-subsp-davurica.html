Gypsophila patrinii subsp. davurica

Gypsophila patrinii subsp.

davurica, commonly known as the Meadow Forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental plant.
