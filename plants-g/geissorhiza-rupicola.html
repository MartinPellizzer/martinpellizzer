Geissorhiza rupicola

Geissorhiza rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Geissorhiza rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
