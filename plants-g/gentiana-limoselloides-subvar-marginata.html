Gentiana limoselloides subvar. marginata

Gentiana limoselloides subvar. marginata, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
