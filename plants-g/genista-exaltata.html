Genista exaltata

Genista exaltata, commonly known as the tall broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, needle-like leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
