Genista rhodorhizoides

Genista rhodorhizoides, commonly known as the yellow bursfoot broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its root system is known for its deep rhizomes, which help it survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
