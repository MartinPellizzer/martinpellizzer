Grevillea pulchella

Grevillea pulchella, commonly known as the Crimson Rock Lily, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Grevillea pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
