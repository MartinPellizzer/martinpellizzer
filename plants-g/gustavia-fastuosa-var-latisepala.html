Gustavia fastuosa var. latisepala

Gustavia fastuosa var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five to seven broad, waxy petals. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
