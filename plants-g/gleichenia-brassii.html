Gleichenia brassii

Gleichenia brassii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. Native to various regions of Australia and New Zealand, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Gleichenia brassii is known for its ability to form dense mats, which help in soil stabilization and moisture retention.

Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.
