Geum virginianum var. lanceolatum

Geum virginianum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf Canadian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded to partially shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
