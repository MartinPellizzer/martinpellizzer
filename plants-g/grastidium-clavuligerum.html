Grastidium clavuligerum

Grastidium clavuligerum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Grastidium clavuligerum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
