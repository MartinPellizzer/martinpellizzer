Glycine hispida

Glycine hispida, commonly known as the hairy soybean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Glycine hispida is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread cultivation.
