Gularia

Gularia, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species of stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. Gularia typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
