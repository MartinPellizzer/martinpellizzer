Galium rubrum var. rubriflorum

Galium rubrum var. rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, hairy texture.

Galium rubrum var. rubriflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
