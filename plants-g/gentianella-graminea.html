Gentianella graminea

Gentianella graminea, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
