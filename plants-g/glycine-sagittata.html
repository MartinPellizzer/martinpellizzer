Glycine sagittata

Glycine sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with arrowhead-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, tangy berries resembling tiny tomatillos. Glycine sagittata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

Its growth habit includes a low, spreading stem that can form dense mats in suitable conditions.
