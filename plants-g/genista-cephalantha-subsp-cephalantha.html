Genista cephalantha subsp. cephalantha

Genista cephalantha subsp.

cephalantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.
