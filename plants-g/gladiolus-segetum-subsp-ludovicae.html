Gladiolus segetum subsp. ludovicae

Gladiolus segetum subsp.

ludovicae, commonly known as the European gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences.

The flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive three-lobed structure. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
