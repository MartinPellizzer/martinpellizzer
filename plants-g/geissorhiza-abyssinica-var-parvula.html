Geissorhiza abyssinica var. parvula

Geissorhiza abyssinica var. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of East Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a rare and interesting subject for botanical study.
