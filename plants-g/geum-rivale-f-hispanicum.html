Geum rivale f. hispanicum

Geum rivale f. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
