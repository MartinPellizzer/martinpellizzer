Glechoma shikikunensis

Glechoma shikikunensis, commonly known as Japanese ground ivy, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and kidney-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
