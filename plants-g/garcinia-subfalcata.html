Garcinia subfalcata

Garcinia subfalcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to orange when mature.

Garcinia subfalcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
