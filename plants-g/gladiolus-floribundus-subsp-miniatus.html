Gladiolus floribundus subsp. miniatus

Gladiolus floribundus subsp.

miniatus, commonly known as the miniature gladiolus, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South Africa. It features upright, narrow sword-shaped leaves and produces dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The flowers are typically borne on tall, sturdy stems and bloom in late summer to early autumn. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and compact size.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
