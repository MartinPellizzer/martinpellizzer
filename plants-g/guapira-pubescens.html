Guapira pubescens

Guapira pubescens, commonly known as the hairy guapira, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Guapira pubescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
