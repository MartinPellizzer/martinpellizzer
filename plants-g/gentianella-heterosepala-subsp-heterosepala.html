Gentianella heterosepala subsp. heterosepala

Gentianella heterosepala subsp.

heterosepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The species is characterized by its distinct floral structure and is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking colors and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
