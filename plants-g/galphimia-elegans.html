Galphimia elegans

Galphimia elegans, commonly known as the golden torch or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its striking, bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
