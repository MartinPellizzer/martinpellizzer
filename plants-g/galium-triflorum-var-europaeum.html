Galium triflorum var. europaeum

Galium triflorum var. europaeum, commonly known as European bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground, covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters of three, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It is commonly found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
