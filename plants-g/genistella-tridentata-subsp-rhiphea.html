Genistella tridentata subsp. rhiphea

Genistella tridentata subsp.

rhiphea, commonly known as the Rhiphean broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf structure and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
