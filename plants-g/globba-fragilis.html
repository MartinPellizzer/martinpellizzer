Globba fragilis

Globba fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant globba, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Globba fragilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and medicinal uses.
