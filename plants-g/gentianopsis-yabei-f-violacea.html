Gentianopsis yabei f. violacea

Gentianopsis yabei f. violacea, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

The flowers bloom in summer and are known for their deep coloration and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
