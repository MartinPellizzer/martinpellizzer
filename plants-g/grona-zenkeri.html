Grona zenkeri

Grona zenkeri, commonly known as the Zanzibar nut, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
