Geonoma longepetiolata

Geonoma longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled fern, is a member of the Marantaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its petioles are notably long, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
