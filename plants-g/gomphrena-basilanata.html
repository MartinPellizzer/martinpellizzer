Gomphrena basilanata

Gomphrena basilanata, commonly known as the Basilean plume, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Gomphrena basilanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
