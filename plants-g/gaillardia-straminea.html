Gaillardia straminea

Gaillardia straminea, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central orange or reddish-brown disk, giving it a striking, daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy flowers from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
