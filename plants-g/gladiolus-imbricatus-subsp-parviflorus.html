Gladiolus imbricatus subsp. parviflorus

Gladiolus imbricatus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers.

The flowers typically bloom in summer and come in shades of red, pink, and white. The subspecies is characterized by its smaller, more compact blooms compared to other gladiolus varieties.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
