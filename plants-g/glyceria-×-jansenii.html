Glyceria × jansenii

Glyceria × jansenii, commonly known as the Jansen's glyceria, is a hybrid grass species native to wetland environments in the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Glyceria × jansenii thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, along riverbanks, and in other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
