Gladiolus crocatus

Gladiolus crocatus, commonly known as the crocus gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of flowers that range from yellow to orange in hue. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value has made it a favored plant in horticulture for centuries.
