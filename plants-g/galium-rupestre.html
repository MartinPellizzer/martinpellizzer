Galium rupestre

Galium rupestre, commonly known as rock bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or arid regions. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galium rupestre is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
