Garnotia patula var. mutica

Garnotia patula var. mutica, commonly known as the soft-hairy garnotia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Garnotia patula var. mutica is typically found in grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
