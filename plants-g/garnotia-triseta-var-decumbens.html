Garnotia triseta var. decumbens

Garnotia triseta var. decumbens, commonly known as the creeping garnotia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It features slender, decumbent stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
