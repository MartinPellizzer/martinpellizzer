Gloriosa superba var. graminifolia

Gloriosa superba var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy flame lily, is a variant of the flame lily native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, elongated orange-red flowers. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
