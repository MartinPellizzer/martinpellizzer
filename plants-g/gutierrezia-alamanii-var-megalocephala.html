Gutierrezia alamanii var. megalocephala

Gutierrezia alamanii var. megalocephala, commonly known as the gianthead goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and woody stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically linear and gray-green, adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
