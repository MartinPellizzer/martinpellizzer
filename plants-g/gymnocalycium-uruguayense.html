Gymnocalycium uruguayense

Gymnocalycium uruguayense, commonly known as the Uruguay moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems and bright, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
