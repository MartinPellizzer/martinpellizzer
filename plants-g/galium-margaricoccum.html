Galium margaricoccum

Galium margaricoccum, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium margaricoccum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and streambanks.
