Glossorhyncha pendulata

Glossorhyncha pendulata, commonly known as the hanging lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence and striking, lip-like petals that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds.

The botanical features of Glossorhyncha pendulata make it a visually distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
