Gnidia parvula

Gnidia parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered gnidia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

Its fruits are small, spherical drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats.
