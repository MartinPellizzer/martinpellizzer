Gilia tenuiflora var. newloniana

Gilia tenuiflora var. newloniana, commonly known as the Newlon's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its overall drought-resistant morphology.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
