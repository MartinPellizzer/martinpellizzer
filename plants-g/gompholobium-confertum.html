Gompholobium confertum

Gompholobium confertum, commonly known as the dense flowering glory pea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features compound leaves with elliptic to lanceolate leaflets and produces dense clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. Gompholobium confertum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
