Gymnosporia fournieri

Gymnosporia fournieri, commonly known as the African thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Gymnosporia fournieri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant traits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
