Glaucium glabrum

Glaucium glabrum, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its roots contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine. Glaucium glabrum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

It is also known for its historical use in herbal remedies and as a source of natural compounds.
