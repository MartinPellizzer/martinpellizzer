Gentiana acaulis var. minor

Gentiana acaulis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, stemless growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
