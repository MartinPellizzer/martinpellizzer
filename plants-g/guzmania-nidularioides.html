Guzmania nidularioides

Guzmania nidularioides, commonly known as the paddle plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a paddle, which can range from red to orange and yellow. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insects. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania nidularioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
