Gongora pardina

Gongora pardina, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety petals that range in color from pink to purple, with a distinctive slipper-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Gongora pardina is considered a rare and endangered species.
