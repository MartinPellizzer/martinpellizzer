Grevillea concinna subsp. concinna

Grevillea concinna subsp.

concinna, commonly known as the pink grevillea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright pink flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Grevillea concinna subsp.

concinna is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native wildlife.
