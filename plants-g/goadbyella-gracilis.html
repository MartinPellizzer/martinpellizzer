Goadbyella gracilis

Goadbyella gracilis, commonly known as the slender goadbyella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cooler temperatures and lower oxygen levels. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Goadbyella gracilis is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environments.
