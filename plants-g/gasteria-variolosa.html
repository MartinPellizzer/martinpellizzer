Gasteria variolosa

Gasteria variolosa, commonly known as the Zulu helmet plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with prominent white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
