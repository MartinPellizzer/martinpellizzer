Gordonia axillaris

Gordonia axillaris, commonly known as the golden wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, thick, and often flaky bark. Its leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Gordonia axillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
