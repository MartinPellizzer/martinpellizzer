Geranium papuanum

Geranium papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Geranium papuanum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local communities.
