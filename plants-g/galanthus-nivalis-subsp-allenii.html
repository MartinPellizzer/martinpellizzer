Galanthus nivalis subsp. allenii

Galanthus nivalis subsp.

allenii, commonly known as the Allen's snowdrop, is a subspecies of the snowdrop plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate white flowers with a greenish-yellow spathe, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
