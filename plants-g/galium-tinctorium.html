Galium tinctorium

Galium tinctorium, commonly known as red bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, oval-shaped leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, dry fruits called utricles, which contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. Galium tinctorium is known for its historical use in dyeing textiles with a reddish hue, hence its common name.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
