Glyceria borreri var. multiflora

Glyceria borreri var. multiflora, commonly known as the multibranched glyceria, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hollow stems that often bear a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
