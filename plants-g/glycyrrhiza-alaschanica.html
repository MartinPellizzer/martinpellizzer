Glycyrrhiza alaschanica

Glycyrrhiza alaschanica, commonly known as the Altai licorice, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its deep taproot and aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. It is valued for its high glycyrrhizin content, which gives it a sweet taste and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza alaschanica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.
