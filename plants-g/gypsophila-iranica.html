Gypsophila iranica

Gypsophila iranica, commonly known as the Persian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila iranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping and floral arrangements.
