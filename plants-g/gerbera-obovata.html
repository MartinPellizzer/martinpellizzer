Gerbera obovata

Gerbera obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are broadly ovate in shape, often with a waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gerbera obovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
