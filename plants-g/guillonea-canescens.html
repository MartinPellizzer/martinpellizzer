Guillonea canescens

Guillonea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
