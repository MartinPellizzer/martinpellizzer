Geranium sylvaticum var. eglandulosum

Geranium sylvaticum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular wood geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is often found in shaded forest floors.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
