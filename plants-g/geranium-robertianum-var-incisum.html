Geranium robertianum var. incisum

Geranium robertianum var. incisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
