Geranium coelestinum

Geranium coelestinum, commonly known as blue geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
