Gaertnera microphylla

Gaertnera microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved gaertnera, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. Gaertnera microphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
