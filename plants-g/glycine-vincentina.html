Glycine vincentina

Glycine vincentina, commonly known as the Vincent's soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Glycine vincentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural use.
