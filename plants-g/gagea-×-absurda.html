Gagea × absurda

Gagea × absurda, commonly known as the hybrid gagea, is a rare and intriguing plant species resulting from the cross between two species of the genus Gagea.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to specific regions in the western United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats.
