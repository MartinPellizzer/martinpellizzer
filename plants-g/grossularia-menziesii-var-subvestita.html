Grossularia menziesii var. subvestita

Grossularia menziesii var. subvestita, commonly known as the subvestita gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Native American medicine.
