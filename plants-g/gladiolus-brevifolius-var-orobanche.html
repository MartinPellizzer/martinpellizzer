Gladiolus brevifolius var. orobanche

Gladiolus brevifolius var. orobanche, commonly known as the dwarf leaf gladiolus, is a unique variant of the gladiolus genus characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced foliage.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces striking, tall spikes of brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
