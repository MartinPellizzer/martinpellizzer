Gomphogyne

Gomphogyne, commonly known as the African orchid or the snake orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers that resemble snakes or other small animals. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks in their natural habitat. Gomphogyne species are known for their intricate floral structures and are often found in humid, shaded environments.
