Gymnocalycium lafaldense f. fraternum

Gymnocalycium lafaldense f. fraternum, commonly known as the Fraternum Cactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by pale green fruits. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized collections and requires specific care to thrive.
