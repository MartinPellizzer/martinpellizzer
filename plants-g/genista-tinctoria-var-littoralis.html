Genista tinctoria var. littoralis

Genista tinctoria var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems covered in small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
