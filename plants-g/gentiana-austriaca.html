Gentiana austriaca

Gentiana austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Gentiana austriaca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
