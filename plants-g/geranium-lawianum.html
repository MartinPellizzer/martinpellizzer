Geranium lawianum

Geranium lawianum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
