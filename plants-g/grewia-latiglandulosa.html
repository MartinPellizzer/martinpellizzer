Grewia latiglandulosa

Grewia latiglandulosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
