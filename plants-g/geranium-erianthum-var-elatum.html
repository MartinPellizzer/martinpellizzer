Geranium erianthum var. elatum

Geranium erianthum var. elatum, commonly known as the showy geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. Its flowers are tubular at the base and have a spurred shape, which is characteristic of the Geraniaceae family.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
