Guilleminea densa var. gracilis

Guilleminea densa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to coastal conditions.
