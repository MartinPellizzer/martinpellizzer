Galipea resinosa

Galipea resinosa, commonly known as the resinous guayacan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a valuable resin, which has been historically used for its adhesive and medicinal properties. Galipea resinosa thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stability in its native regions.
