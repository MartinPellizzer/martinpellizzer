Griffonia tessmannii

Griffonia tessmannii, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, resinous substance. Griffonia tessmannii is notable for containing the alkaloid huperzine A, which has been studied for its potential cognitive benefits.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and scientific research.
