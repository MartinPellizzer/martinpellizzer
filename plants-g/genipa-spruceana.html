Genipa spruceana

Genipa spruceana, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub or small tree form. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a hard, spiky capsule that turns from green to purple as it matures.

The fruit is not typically eaten raw but has been used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value.
