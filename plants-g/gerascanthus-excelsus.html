Gerascanthus excelsus

Gerascanthus excelsus, commonly known as the silverleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, silver-green foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it suitable for xeriscaping.

Gerascanthus excelsus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
