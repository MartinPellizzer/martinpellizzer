Galactophora pulchella

Galactophora pulchella, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a striking member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are distinctive, resembling small lanterns with a papery, translucent outer layer.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
