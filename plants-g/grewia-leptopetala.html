Grewia leptopetala

Grewia leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-leaved grewia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia leptopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and berries.
