Gilia serrulata

Gilia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Gilia serrulata is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
