Gladiolus imbricatus proles illyricus

Gladiolus imbricatus proles illyricus, commonly known as the Illyrian gladiolus, is a species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flower spikes. The flowers are typically arranged in a loose panicle and come in shades of red, pink, and white. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its significance in horticulture.
