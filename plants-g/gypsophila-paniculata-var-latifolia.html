Gypsophila paniculata var. latifolia

Gypsophila paniculata var. latifolia, commonly known as the shrub garden gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
