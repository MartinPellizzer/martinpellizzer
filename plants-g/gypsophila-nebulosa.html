Gypsophila nebulosa

Gypsophila nebulosa, commonly known as the cloud-like gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation and floral arrangements.
