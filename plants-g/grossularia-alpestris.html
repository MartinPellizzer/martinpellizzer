Grossularia alpestris

Grossularia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine currant, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in jams and confections. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Grossularia alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
