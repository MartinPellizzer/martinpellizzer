Garrya goldmanii

Garrya goldmanii, commonly known as the goldenleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking golden-yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic value in gardens and natural habitats.
