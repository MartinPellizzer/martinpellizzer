Gnaphalium laterale

Gnaphalium laterale, commonly known as silverleaf cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that reflect light. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
