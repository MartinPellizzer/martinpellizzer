Gymnosporia uniflora

Gymnosporia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower burr, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Gymnosporia uniflora plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
