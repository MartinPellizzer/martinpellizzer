Gompholobium ericifolium

Gompholobium ericifolium, commonly known as the heath-leaf gloryflower, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a heath-like appearance. It produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring.

This species is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
