Grevillea stuartii

Grevillea stuartii, commonly known as Stuart's grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea stuartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
