Gnaphalium saturejoides var. citrinum

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. citrinum, commonly known as golden cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
