Gastrolobium acrocaroli

Gastrolobium acrocaroli, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
