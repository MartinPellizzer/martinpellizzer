Gypsophila pallida var. adenoclada

Gypsophila pallida var. adenoclada, commonly known as the pale gypsophila or desert baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender stems and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained conditions.
