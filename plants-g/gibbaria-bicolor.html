Gibbaria bicolor

Gibbaria bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a striking addition to arid landscapes.
