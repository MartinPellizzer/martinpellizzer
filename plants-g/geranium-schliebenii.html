Geranium schliebenii

Geranium schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy flowers, which range from pink to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Geranium schliebenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
