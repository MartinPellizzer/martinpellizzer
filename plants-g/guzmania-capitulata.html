Guzmania capitulata

Guzmania capitulata, commonly known as the pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble a pineapple, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater, creating a mini-ecosystem for small organisms. It produces a tall flower spike that blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Guzmania capitulata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
