Grevillea buxifolia subsp. buxifolia

Grevillea buxifolia subsp.

buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. Grevillea buxifolia subsp.

buxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
