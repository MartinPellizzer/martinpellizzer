Genista cinerascens

Genista cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grayish-green leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista cinerascens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil rehabilitation efforts.
