Gentiana amarella var. pilosa

Gentiana amarella var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and woodlands.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
