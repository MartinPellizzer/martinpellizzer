Gentianella austriaca f. ionanthe

Gentianella austriaca f. ionanthe, commonly known as the Ionian gentian, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant of the gentian family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in Albania and Kosovo. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
