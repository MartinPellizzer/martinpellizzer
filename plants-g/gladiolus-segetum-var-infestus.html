Gladiolus segetum var. infestus

Gladiolus segetum var. infestus, commonly known as the wild gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences of showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it is considered an important species in ecological studies.

The variety is distinguished by its robust growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
