Gomphocarpus cultriformis

Gomphocarpus cultriformis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
