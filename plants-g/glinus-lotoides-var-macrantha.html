Glinus lotoides var. macrantha

Glinus lotoides var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered glinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
