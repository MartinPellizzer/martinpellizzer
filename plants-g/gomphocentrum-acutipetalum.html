Gomphocentrum acutipetalum

Gomphocentrum acutipetalum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often lanceolate in shape. The plant grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
