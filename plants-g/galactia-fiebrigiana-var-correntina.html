Galactia fiebrigiana var. correntina

Galactia fiebrigiana var. correntina, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is also notable for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds.
