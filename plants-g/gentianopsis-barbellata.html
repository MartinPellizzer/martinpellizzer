Gentianopsis barbellata

Gentianopsis barbellata, commonly known as the barbellate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals, which are often marked with intricate patterns.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking appearance.
