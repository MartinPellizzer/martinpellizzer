Gymnodiscus linearifolius

Gymnodiscus linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leafed diatom, is a unicellular marine algae belonging to the class Bacillariophyceae.

It is characterized by its intricate silica cell wall, known as a frustule, which displays a distinctive linearly arranged pattern of pores and ribs. This species is typically found in coastal waters and estuaries, where it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling. Its morphology is highly adapted to turbulent environments, allowing it to remain suspended in water columns.

Gymnodiscus linearifolius is also used in paleoclimatic studies due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
