Galium afghanicum

Galium afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.
