Gymnema cumingii

Gymnema cumingii, commonly known as the Indian cheese plant, is a climbing shrub native to the wet forests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by follicular fruits containing elliptical seeds. It is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Gymnema cumingii is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
