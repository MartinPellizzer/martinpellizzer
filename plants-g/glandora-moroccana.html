Glandora moroccana

Glandora moroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Morocco.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Glandora moroccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
