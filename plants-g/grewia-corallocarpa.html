Grewia corallocarpa

Grewia corallocarpa, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Grewia corallocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
