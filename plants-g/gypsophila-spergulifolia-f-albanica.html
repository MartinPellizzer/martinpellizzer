Gypsophila spergulifolia f. albanica

Gypsophila spergulifolia f. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian gypsophila, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly in regions of Albania and adjacent countries.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its airy, lace-like appearance.
