Gossypium bakeri

Gossypium bakeri, commonly known as the African cotton tree, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gossypium bakeri is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
