Geranium rhaeticum

Geranium rhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.
