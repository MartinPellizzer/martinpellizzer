Gossia bidwillii

Gossia bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's gossia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and woodland areas. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Gossia bidwillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
