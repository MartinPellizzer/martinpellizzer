Gentiana linearis subsp. rubricaulis

Gentiana linearis subsp.

rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a reddish hue, especially during the growing season.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Gentiana linearis subsp.

rubricaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
