Galium erythrocaulon

Galium erythrocaulon, commonly known as red-stemmed bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, reddish stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small white or pinkish flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.
