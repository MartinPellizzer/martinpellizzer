Galeopsis ladanum var. bertetii

Galeopsis ladanum var. bertetii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hemp-nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
