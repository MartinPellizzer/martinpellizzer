Glycine macrobotrys

Glycine macrobotrys, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the eastern United States.

It produces fragrant, spiral-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods that turn brown as they mature.

Glycine macrobotrys is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often grown in gardens for its visual appeal and aromatic blooms.
