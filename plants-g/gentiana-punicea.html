Gentiana punicea

Gentiana punicea, commonly known as the crimson gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of the Alps and Pyrenees.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
