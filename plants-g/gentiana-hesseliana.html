Gentiana hesseliana

Gentiana hesseliana, commonly known as the Hessel's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Gentiana hesseliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and ecological benefits.
