Griffinia aracensis

Griffinia aracensis, commonly known as the Aracena flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Griffinia aracensis plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
