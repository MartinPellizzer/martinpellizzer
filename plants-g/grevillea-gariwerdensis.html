Grevillea gariwerdensis

Grevillea gariwerdensis, commonly known as the Gariwerden grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are typically visited by honeyeaters and other nectar-feeding birds.

Grevillea gariwerdensis is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
