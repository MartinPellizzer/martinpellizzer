Gypsophila arrostii

Gypsophila arrostii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Gypsophila arrostii is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their showy blooms and ecological resilience.
