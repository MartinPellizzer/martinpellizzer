Gesneria incurva

Gesneria incurva, commonly known as the drooping gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically tubular, with bright colors such as red, orange, or yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Gesneria incurva is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
