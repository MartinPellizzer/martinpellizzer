Genista scorpius var. macracantha

Genista scorpius var. macracantha, commonly known as the broadleaf broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a dark green color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
