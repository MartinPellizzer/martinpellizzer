Gloxinia hypocyrtiflora

Gloxinia hypocyrtiflora, commonly known as the narrow-lobed gloxinia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Gloxinia hypocyrtiflora is also noted for its ornamental value and is often grown for its aesthetic appeal in indoor and outdoor settings.
