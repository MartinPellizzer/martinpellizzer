Garcinia lamponga

Garcinia lamponga, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants and bioactive compounds. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with thorn-like protrusions, and the fruit contains a single, hard seed.

Garcinia lamponga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its fruit.
