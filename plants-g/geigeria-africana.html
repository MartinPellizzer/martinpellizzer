Geigeria africana

Geigeria africana, commonly known as African bluebush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces small, four-petaled flowers. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Geigeria africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
