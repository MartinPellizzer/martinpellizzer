Gentianodes eumarginata var. scabromarginata

Gentianodes eumarginata var. scabromarginata, commonly known as the rough-margin gentian, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough or hairy margin, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
