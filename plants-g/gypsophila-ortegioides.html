Gypsophila ortegioides

Gypsophila ortegioides, commonly known as the Ortegui's baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy appearance and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.

Gypsophila ortegioides is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
