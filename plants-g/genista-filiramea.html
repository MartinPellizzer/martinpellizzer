Genista filiramea

Genista filiramea, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and has a shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista filiramea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
