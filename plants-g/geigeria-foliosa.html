Geigeria foliosa

Geigeria foliosa, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of velvety, dark green leaves and produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Geigeria foliosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
