Grastidium neoguineense

Grastidium neoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea grass, is a tropical perennial grass species native to the island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Grastidium neoguineense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
