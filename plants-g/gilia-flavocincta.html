Gilia flavocincta

Gilia flavocincta, commonly known as the yellow-banded gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gilia flavocincta plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
