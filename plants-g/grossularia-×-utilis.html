Grossularia × utilis

Grossularia × utilis, commonly known as the Himalayan currant, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that range in color from red to yellow, depending on the variety. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and adaptability to high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The berries are often used in jams, desserts, and traditional medicine due to their nutritional and medicinal properties.
