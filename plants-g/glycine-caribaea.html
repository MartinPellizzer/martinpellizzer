Glycine caribaea

Glycine caribaea, commonly known as Caribbean soybean, is a leguminous plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. Glycine caribaea is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its adaptability to tropical climates makes it an important crop in certain agricultural systems.
