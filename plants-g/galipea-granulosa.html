Galipea granulosa

Galipea granulosa, commonly known as the granulate gallwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
