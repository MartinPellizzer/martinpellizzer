Geranium foreroi

Geranium foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
