Geranium argenteum var. lucanum

Geranium argenteum var. lucanum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
