Gastrochilus latilabrum

Gastrochilus latilabrum, commonly known as the broad-lipped stomach orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, thick rhizome and produces erect, hairy stems. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
