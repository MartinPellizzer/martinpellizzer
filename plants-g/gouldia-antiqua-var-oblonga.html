Gouldia antiqua var. oblonga

Gouldia antiqua var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gouldia, is a striking ornamental grass native to Australia.

It features slender, grass-like foliage with a distinctive silver-blue hue, which becomes more pronounced in full sun. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a subtle floral element to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking texture and color in their landscapes.
