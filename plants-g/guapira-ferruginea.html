Guapira ferruginea

Guapira ferruginea, commonly known as the red-stemmed guapira, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are characterized by a reddish-brown color, which becomes more pronounced with age.

Guapira ferruginea is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
