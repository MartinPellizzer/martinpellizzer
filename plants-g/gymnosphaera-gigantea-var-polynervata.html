Gymnosphaera gigantea var. polynervata

Gymnosphaera gigantea var. polynervata, commonly known as the giant net-veined cycad, is a unique gymnosperm characterized by its large, palm-like leaves with intricate net-like venation.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, typical of gymnosperms, and its structure includes a stout, unbranched stem with a crown of large, compound leaves. It is notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades in its natural habitat.

The varietal name "polynervata" refers to its distinctively complex and numerous leaf veins, which contribute to its ornamental value.
