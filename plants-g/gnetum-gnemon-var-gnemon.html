Gnetum gnemon var. gnemon

Gnetum gnemon var. gnemon, commonly known as the mangrove fig or white mangrove, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a thick, buttressed trunk and smooth, grey bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments and plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines.
