Galarhoeus pekinensis subsp. barbellatus

Galarhoeus pekinensis subsp.

barbellatus, commonly known as the Peking bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with a distinctive hooded structure.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
