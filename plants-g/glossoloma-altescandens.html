Glossoloma altescandens

Glossoloma altescandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant is a vine that can grow quite long, using its tendrils to climb and support itself on trees or shrubs. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Glossoloma altescandens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
