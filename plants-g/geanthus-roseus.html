Geanthus roseus

Geanthus roseus, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Geanthus roseus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
