Geissorhiza ramosa

Geissorhiza ramosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vivid orange to red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geissorhiza ramosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
