Glyceria borealis

Glyceria borealis, commonly known as northern glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in tundra and wet meadow habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is an important food source for local wildlife.

Glyceria borealis is adapted to low temperatures and has a short growing season.
