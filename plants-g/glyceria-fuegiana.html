Glyceria fuegiana

Glyceria fuegiana, commonly known as the Fuegian glyceria, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Glyceria fuegiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
