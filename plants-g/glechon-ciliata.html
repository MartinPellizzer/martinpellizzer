Glechon ciliata

Glechon ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the summer months. Glechon ciliata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
