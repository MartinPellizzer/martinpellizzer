Gymnocalycium ochoterenae var. cinereum

Gymnocalycium ochoterenae var. cinereum, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive silver-gray coloration due to its dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
