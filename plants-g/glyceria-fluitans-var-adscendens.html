Glyceria fluitans var. adscendens

Glyceria fluitans var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending glyceria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green coloration.

Glyceria fluitans var. adscendens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
