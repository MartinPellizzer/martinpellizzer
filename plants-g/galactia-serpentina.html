Galactia serpentina

Galactia serpentina, commonly known as the serpent bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. Its small, pale yellow flowers are followed by elongated, serpentine pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
