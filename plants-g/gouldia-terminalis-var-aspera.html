Gouldia terminalis var. aspera

Gouldia terminalis var. aspera, commonly known as the rough gouldia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, rough texture, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
