Gentiana subulata

Gentiana subulata, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Gentiana subulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
