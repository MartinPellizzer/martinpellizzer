Gustavia flagellata var. costata

Gustavia flagellata var. costata, commonly known as the Costate Guiana Chestnut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or pale green veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
