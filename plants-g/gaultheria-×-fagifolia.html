Gaultheria × fagifolia

Gaultheria × fagifolia, commonly known as the hybrid wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Gaultheria × fagifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
