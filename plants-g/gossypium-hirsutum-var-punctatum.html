Gossypium hirsutum var. punctatum

Gossypium hirsutum var. punctatum, commonly known as the dotted cotton, is a variant of upland cotton characterized by its distinctive leaf and stem patterns.

This plant is native to the southern United States and is cultivated for its fibrous seeds. The leaves often display a speckled or dotted appearance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight.

The plant's fibers are used in textile production, similar to other cotton varieties.
