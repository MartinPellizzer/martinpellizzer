Gongylolepis jauaensis

Gongylolepis jauaensis, commonly known as the Jauaensis fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often form a dense, carpet-like growth. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and rocky crevices, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick layer of moss-like structures, which help in water retention and nutrient absorption.

Gongylolepis jauaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
