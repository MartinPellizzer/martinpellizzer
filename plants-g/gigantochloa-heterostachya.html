Gigantochloa heterostachya

Gigantochloa heterostachya, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the top of the stems.

This species is valued for its durability and is often used in construction and as a ornamental plant.
