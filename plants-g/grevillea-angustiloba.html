Grevillea angustiloba

Grevillea angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green and have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea angustiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
