Gutierrezia hoffmannii

Gutierrezia hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia hoffmannii is also valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
