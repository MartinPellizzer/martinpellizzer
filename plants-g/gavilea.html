Gavilea

Gavilea, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that often display vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Africa. Gavilea species are perennial and produce striking, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
