Galactia cordifolia

Galactia cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf galactia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in the spring.

Galactia cordifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
