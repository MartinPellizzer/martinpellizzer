Goodenia stobbsiana

Goodenia stobbsiana, commonly known as the Stobbs' goodenia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often tubular or urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
