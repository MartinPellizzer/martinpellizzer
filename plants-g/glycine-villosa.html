Glycine villosa

Glycine villosa, commonly known as hairy soybean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Glycine villosa is often cultivated for its high protein content and is used in both animal feed and human nutrition.

Its growth is adapted to warm climates and it thrives in well-drained soils.
