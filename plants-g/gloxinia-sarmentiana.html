Gloxinia sarmentiana

Gloxinia sarmentiana, commonly known as the creeping gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in various environmental conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Gloxinia sarmentiana is typically grown as a houseplant due to its preference for warm, humid environments.
