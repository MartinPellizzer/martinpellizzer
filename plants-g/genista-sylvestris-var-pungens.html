Genista sylvestris var. pungens

Genista sylvestris var. pungens, commonly known as the bristly broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
