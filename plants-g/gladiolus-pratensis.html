Gladiolus pratensis

Gladiolus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional floral arrangements.
