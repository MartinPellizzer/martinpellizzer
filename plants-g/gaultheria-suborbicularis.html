Gaultheria suborbicularis

Gaultheria suborbicularis, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical or suborbicular in shape, hence the species name, and are covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in taste to true strawberries.
