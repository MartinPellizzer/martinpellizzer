Galium hyrcanicum var. glabrum

Galium hyrcanicum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are typically hairless, giving it its "glabrum" designation. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and simple, often with a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
