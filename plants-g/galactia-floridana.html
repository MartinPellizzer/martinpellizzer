Galactia floridana

Galactia floridana, commonly known as the Florida milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its compound leaves consist of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its distinctive yellow to orange seed pods.

Galactia floridana is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in native plant landscaping.
