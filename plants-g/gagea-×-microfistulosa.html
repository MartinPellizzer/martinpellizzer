Gagea × microfistulosa

Gagea × microfistulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gagea, is a hybrid plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
