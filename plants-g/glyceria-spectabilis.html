Glyceria spectabilis

Glyceria spectabilis, commonly known as the showy glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, and it produces fluffy, white to light brown seed heads that are visually striking. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Glyceria spectabilis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
