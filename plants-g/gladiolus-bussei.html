Gladiolus bussei

Gladiolus bussei, commonly known as the Zambian gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in a variety of colors including red, pink, and white. The flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and are composed of three outer petals and three inner sepals, which are often more showy than the true petals. This species is prized for its vibrant blooms and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both amateur and professional gardeners.
