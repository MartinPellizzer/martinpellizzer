Gongora escobariana

Gongora escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Gongora escobariana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
