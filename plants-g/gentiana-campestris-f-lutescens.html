Gentiana campestris f. lutescens

Gentiana campestris f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a striking variant of the gentian genus native to Europe.

This perennial plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in the species, but in this form, they exhibit a distinctive yellow hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer months.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
