Gynandropsis speciosa

Gynandropsis speciosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a succulent, branching stem and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention.

Gynandropsis speciosa is adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
