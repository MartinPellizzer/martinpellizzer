Genista crassifolia

Genista crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, aiding in its propagation.
