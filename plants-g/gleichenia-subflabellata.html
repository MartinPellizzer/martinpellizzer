Gleichenia subflabellata

Gleichenia subflabellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chain fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and fronds that are typically narrow and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, which are often covered with a dense layer of indumentum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, commonly found in temperate regions with high humidity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
