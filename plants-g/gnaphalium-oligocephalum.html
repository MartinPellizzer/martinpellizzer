Gnaphalium oligocephalum

Gnaphalium oligocephalum, commonly known as the slender everlasting or woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
