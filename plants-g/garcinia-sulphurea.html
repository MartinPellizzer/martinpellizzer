Garcinia sulphurea

Garcinia sulphurea, commonly known as the golden apple or golden gourd, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange fruits, which have a distinctive, slightly acidic taste. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates.

Garcinia sulphurea is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
