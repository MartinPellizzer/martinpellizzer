Glyceria kjellmannii

Glyceria kjellmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Glyceria kjellmannii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
