Gerascanthus glabratus

Gerascanthus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving the plant a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
