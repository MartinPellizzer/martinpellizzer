Goeppertia enclitica

Goeppertia enclitica, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically features a compact, bushy growth habit with multiple stems. Its foliage is usually a deep green, sometimes with a bluish or reddish tinge, depending on the variety.

Goeppertia enclitica prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
