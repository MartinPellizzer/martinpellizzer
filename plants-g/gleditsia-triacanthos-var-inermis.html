Gleditsia triacanthos var. inermis

Gleditsia triacanthos var. inermis, commonly known as the honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its distinctive thornless, pod-like fruit that splits open to release seeds. The tree has a deep taproot system, making it drought-tolerant and adaptable to various soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

Its flowers are small and yellow-green, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
