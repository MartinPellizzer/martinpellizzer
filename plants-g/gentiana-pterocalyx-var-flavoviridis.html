Gentiana pterocalyx var. flavoviridis

Gentiana pterocalyx var. flavoviridis, commonly known as the yellow viridian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is commonly found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
