Grevillea williamsoni

Grevillea williamsoni, commonly known as the red-stemmed Grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea williamsoni is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
