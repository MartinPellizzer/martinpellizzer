Geranium potentilloides var. abditum

Geranium potentilloides var. abditum, commonly known as the hidden geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
