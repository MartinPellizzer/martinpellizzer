Gethyllis britteniana

Gethyllis britteniana, commonly known as the Cape gold dot, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with distinctive red or orange spots, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. It is typically found growing in coastal fynbos vegetation, often on limestone substrates.

Gethyllis britteniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
