Gardenia radicans var. simpliciflora

Gardenia radicans var. simpliciflora, commonly known as the simple-flowered gardenia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers with a subtle yellowish tinge at the base, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
