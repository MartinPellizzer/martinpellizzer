Gentiana septemfida subsp. kolakovskyi

Gentiana septemfida subsp.

kolakovskyi, commonly known as the Kolakovsky's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
