Gouldia affinis

Gouldia affinis, commonly known as the pink gouldia, is a striking flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in dense clumps, often forming a compact mound. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Gouldia affinis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
