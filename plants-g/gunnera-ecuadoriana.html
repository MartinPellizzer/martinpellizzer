Gunnera ecuadoriana

Gunnera ecuadoriana, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is known for its robust, herbaceous growth habit. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its impressive size and unique foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
