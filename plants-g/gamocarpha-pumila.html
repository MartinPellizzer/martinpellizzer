Gamocarpha pumila

Gamocarpha pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gamocarpha pumila is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
