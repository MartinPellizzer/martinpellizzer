Geranium cataractarum var. pitardii

Geranium cataractarum var. pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
