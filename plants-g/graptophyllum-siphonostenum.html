Graptophyllum siphonostenum

Graptophyllum siphonostenum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical areas.
