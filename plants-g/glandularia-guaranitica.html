Glandularia guaranitica

Glandularia guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani's glandular verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features glandular trichomes on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique chemical composition. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
