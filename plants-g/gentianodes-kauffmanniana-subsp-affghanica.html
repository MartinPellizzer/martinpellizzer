Gentianodes kauffmanniana subsp. affghanica

Gentianodes kauffmanniana subsp.

affghanica, commonly known as the Afghan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are tubular and often have a striking blue or violet hue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
