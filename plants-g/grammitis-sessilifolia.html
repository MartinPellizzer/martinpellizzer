Grammitis sessilifolia

Grammitis sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf moss fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and narrow, sessile leaves that are often densely packed. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Grammitis sessilifolia plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing microhabitats for small organisms.
