Galenia acutifolia

Galenia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved galenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galenia acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
