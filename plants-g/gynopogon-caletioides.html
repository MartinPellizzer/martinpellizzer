Gynopogon caletioides

Gynopogon caletioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf gynopogon, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lipped flowers. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Gynopogon caletioides is a perennial species that plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
