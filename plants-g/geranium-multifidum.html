Geranium multifidum

Geranium multifidum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and its showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Geranium multifidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
