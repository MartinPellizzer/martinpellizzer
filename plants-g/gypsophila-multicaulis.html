Gypsophila multicaulis

Gypsophila multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valued addition to gardens.

Gypsophila multicaulis is also known for its drought tolerance and hardiness in colder climates.
