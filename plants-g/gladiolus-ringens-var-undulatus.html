Gladiolus ringens var. undulatus

Gladiolus ringens var. undulatus, commonly known as the undulating gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with intricate, undulating petal patterns. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, with a distinctive, ruffled appearance that sets it apart from other gladiolus varieties. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a favorite among horticulturists and botanists alike.
