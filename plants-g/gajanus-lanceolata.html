Gajanus lanceolata

Gajanus lanceolata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along riverbanks.
