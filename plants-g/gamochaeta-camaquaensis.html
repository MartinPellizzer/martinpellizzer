Gamochaeta camaquaensis

Gamochaeta camaquaensis, commonly known as the Camaqua goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a dense cluster, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, aiding in water retention and reducing transpiration.
