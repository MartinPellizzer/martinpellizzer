Grammitis hymenophylloides

Grammitis hymenophylloides, commonly known as the silver feather fern, is a delicate epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis hymenophylloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
