Glochidion castaneum

Glochidion castaneum, commonly known as the red-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Glochidion castaneum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
