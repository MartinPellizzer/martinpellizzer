Genista spartioides var. malacitanus

Genista spartioides var. malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

The variety is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and habitat preferences compared to other Genista species.
