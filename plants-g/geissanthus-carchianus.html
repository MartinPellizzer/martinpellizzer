Geissanthus carchianus

Geissanthus carchianus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its attractive bell-shaped flowers, which typically range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Geissanthus carchianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
