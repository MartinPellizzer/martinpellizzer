Guioa acutifolia

Guioa acutifolia, commonly known as the Philippine guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Guioa acutifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to provide shade.
