Glechoma serbica

Glechoma serbica, commonly known as Serbian ground-ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its small blue or purple flowers bloom in the summer.

Glechoma serbica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
