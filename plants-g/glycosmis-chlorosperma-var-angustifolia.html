Glycosmis chlorosperma var. angustifolia

Glycosmis chlorosperma var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sweetleaf, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
