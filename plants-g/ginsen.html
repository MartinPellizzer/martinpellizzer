Ginsen

Ginsen, commonly known as ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Russia.

It belongs to the genus *Panax* and is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish root system, which is the primary part used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in cool, humid climates and thrives in shaded forest environments. Ginseng has palmate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries.

Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.
