Gentianopsis ovatodeltoidea

Gentianopsis ovatodeltoidea, commonly known as the oval deltoid gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are ovate to deltoid in shape, with serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentianopsis ovatodeltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
