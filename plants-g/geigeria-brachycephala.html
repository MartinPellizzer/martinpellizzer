Geigeria brachycephala

Geigeria brachycephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed geigeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypsela.

Geigeria brachycephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
