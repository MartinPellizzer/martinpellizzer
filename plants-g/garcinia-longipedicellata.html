Garcinia longipedicellata

Garcinia longipedicellata, commonly known as the red mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits with a segmented, aromatic pulp. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
