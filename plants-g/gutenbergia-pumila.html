Gutenbergia pumila

Gutenbergia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf gutenbergia, is a small, compact plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its succulent characteristics and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
