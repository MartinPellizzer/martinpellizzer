Gastrochilus papillosus

Gastrochilus papillosus, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy lips and intricate floral structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a unique papillate (bumpy) texture on the labellum.

Gastrochilus papillosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
