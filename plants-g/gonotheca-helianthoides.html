Gonotheca helianthoides

Gonotheca helianthoides, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
