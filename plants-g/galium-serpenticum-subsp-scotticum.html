Galium serpenticum subsp. scotticum

Galium serpenticum subsp.

scotticum, commonly known as the Scottish bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Scotland. It typically features slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
