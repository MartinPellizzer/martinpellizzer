Glycine soja var. lanceolata

Glycine soja var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically grown for its edible seeds. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and has a climbing habit with trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces pods that contain 2-4 elliptical seeds.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it useful in both agricultural and culinary contexts.
