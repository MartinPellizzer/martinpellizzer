Geranium cruceroense

Geranium cruceroense, commonly known as the Crucero Geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Geranium cruceroense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
