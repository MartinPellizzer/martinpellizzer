Gentianella arenarioides

Gentianella arenarioides, commonly known as the sand gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal or arid habitats.

Gentianella arenarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
