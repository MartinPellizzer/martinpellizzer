Gunnera thyrsiflora

Gunnera thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Andean giant waterwheel, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
