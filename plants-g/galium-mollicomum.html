Galium mollicomum

Galium mollicomum, commonly known as soft-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium mollicomum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
