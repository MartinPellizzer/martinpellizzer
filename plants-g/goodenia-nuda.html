Goodenia nuda

Goodenia nuda, commonly known as the naked goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its simple, usually hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Goodenia nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
