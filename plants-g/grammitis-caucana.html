Grammitis caucana

Grammitis caucana, commonly known as the Caucanian spikerush, is a fern that belongs to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are arranged in clusters along the leaf margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Grammitis caucana is native to regions of South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador.
