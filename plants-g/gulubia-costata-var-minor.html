Gulubia costata var. minor

Gulubia costata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser serrated gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
