Gyrocarpus americanus

Gyrocarpus americanus, commonly known as the American sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive fruiting structures called gyrocarps, which are woody, spirally twisted capsules containing seeds. These fruits are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in the plant's propagation.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
