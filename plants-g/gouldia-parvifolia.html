Gouldia parvifolia

Gouldia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved gouldia, is a striking member of the Restionaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and vibrant, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds and insects.

Gouldia parvifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
