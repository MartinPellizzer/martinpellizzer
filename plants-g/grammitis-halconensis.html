Grammitis halconensis

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in cloud forests. Its small, clustered sporangia are borne on the undersides of the leaves, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling.

Grammitis halconensis is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
