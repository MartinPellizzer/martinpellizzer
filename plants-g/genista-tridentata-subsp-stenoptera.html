Genista tridentata subsp. stenoptera

Genista tridentata subsp.

stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, often thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species adapted to Mediterranean climates.
