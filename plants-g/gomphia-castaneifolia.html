Gomphia castaneifolia

Gomphia castaneifolia, commonly known as the chestnut-leaf gomphia, is a unique member of the Gomphaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of chestnut trees. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
