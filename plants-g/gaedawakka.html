Gaedawakka

Gaedawakka, commonly known as the "Ceylon ginger" or "wild ginger," is a perennial herb native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features erect, leafy stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Gaedawakka thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
