Glycosmis crenulata

Glycosmis crenulata, commonly known as the warty soursop, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, warty bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, spiny fruits resembling a small, green apple. Its fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Glycosmis crenulata is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
