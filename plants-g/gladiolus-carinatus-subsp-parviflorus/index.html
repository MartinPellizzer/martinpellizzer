Gladiolus Carinatus Subsp. Parviflorus

Parviflorus gladiolus, scientifically known as Gladiolus carinatus subsp. parviflorus, is a unique and lesser-known member of the Iridaceae family. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its delicate flowers, characterized by their small size and vibrant colors, make it a standout in the world of ornamental plants. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure compared to other gladiolus species. This article explores its botanical features, habitat, and potential for cultivation.
