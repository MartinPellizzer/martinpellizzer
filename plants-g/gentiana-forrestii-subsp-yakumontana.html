Gentiana forrestii subsp. yakumontana

Gentiana forrestii subsp.

yakumontana, commonly known as the Yakumontan gentian, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the Tibetan Plateau. It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
