Gardenia trinervis

Gardenia trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and have three prominent veins. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia trinervis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
