Geum × inclinatum

Geum × inclinatum, commonly known as the "Climbing Bunchberry," is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with clustered stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its foliage is typically glossy and dark green, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Geum × inclinatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
