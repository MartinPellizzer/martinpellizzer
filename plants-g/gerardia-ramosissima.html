Gerardia ramosissima

Gerardia ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gerardia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
