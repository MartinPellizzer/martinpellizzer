Geraniospermum dissectum

Geraniospermum dissectum, commonly known as the dissected geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, dissected leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
