Gouania brasiliensis

Gouania brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive veining pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible.

The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
