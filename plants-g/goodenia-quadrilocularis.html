Goodenia quadrilocularis

Goodenia quadrilocularis, commonly known as the four-chambered goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heaths.

Goodenia quadrilocularis is an important component of the local flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
