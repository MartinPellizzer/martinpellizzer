Guibourtia tessmannii

Guibourtia tessmannii, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like leaflets, and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in traditional medicine and local crafts.
