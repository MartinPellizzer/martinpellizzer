Gentianella macrosperma

Gentianella macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
