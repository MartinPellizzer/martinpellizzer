Gmelina uniflora

Gmelina uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Gmelina uniflora is often found in moist, lowland areas and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
