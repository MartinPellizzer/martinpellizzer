Gomphrena mendocina subsp. australis

Gomphrena mendocina subsp.

australis, commonly known as the South American globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
