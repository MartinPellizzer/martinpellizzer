Gossia shepherdii

Gossia shepherdii, commonly known as the silky gossia, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Gossia shepherdii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
