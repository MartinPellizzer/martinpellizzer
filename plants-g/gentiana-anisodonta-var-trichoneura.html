Gentiana anisodonta var. trichoneura

Gentiana anisodonta var. trichoneura, commonly known as the Trichoneura gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with cool temperatures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
