Grangeria brasiliensis

Grangeria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five lobes that curve inward. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Grangeria brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
