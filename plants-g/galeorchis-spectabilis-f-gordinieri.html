Galeorchis spectabilis f. gordinieri

Galeorchis spectabilis f. gordinieri, commonly known as the Gordinier's showy orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that help anchor it to its rocky habitat.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and unique appearance.
