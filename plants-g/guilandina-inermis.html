Guilandina inermis

Guilandina inermis, commonly known as the African snakewood, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a woody stem and can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length. Its seeds are enclosed in a thick, leathery pod, and the plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Guilandina inermis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
