Gorgonidium mirabile

Gorgonidium mirabile, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, lantern-like umbels of white flowers.

Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that add to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
