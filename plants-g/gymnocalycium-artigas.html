Gymnocalycium artigas

Gymnocalycium artigas, commonly known as the Artigas moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems and bright, rounded tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It is a succulent plant that stores water in its stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium artigas is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique shape and ease of care.
