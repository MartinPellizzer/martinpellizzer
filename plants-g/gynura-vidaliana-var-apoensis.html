Gynura vidaliana var. apoensis

Gynura vidaliana var. apoensis, commonly known as the Apo hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are often hairy and have a serrated edge, while the stems are woody and sturdy.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local medicine.
