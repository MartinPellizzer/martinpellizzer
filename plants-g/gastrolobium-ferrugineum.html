Gastrolobium ferrugineum

Gastrolobium ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty wattle, is a small shrub native to western and southern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a reddish or rusty tinge, giving the plant its common name. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
