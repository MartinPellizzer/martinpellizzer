Glycosmis craibii var. glabra

Glycosmis craibii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth glycosmis, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are smooth and often glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
