Gentiana stipitata subsp. tizuensis

Gentiana stipitata subsp.

tizuensis, commonly known as the Tizu gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
