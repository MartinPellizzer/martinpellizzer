Gentiana argentea subsp. alii

Gentiana argentea subsp.

alii, commonly known as the silver gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
