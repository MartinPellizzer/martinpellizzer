Gerbera lanuginosa

Gerbera lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to botanical collections.
