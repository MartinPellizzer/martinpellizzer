Gaylussacia virgata var. hilaireana

Gaylussacia virgata var. hilaireana, commonly known as Hilaire's highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries in the summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential for cultivation in native plant gardens.
