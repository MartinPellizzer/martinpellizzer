Gentiana hexamera

Gentiana hexamera, commonly known as the six-petaled gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana hexamera is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
