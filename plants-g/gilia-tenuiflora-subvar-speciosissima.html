Gilia tenuiflora subvar. speciosissima

Gilia tenuiflora subvar. speciosissima, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
