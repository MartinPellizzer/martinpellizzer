Grossularia californica

Grossularia californica, commonly known as the California gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and displays showy, five-petaled flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
