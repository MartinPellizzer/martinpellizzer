Gilia tricolor var. longipedicellata

Gilia tricolor var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the three-color gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored flowers that typically display shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves and has a sprawling growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
