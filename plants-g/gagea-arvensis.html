Gagea arvensis

Gagea arvensis, commonly known as the field gagea, is a small, bulbous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant produces a single, slender stem that bears a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are white or pale blue and are arranged in a loose umbel.

Gagea arvensis is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
