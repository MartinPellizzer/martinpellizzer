Geranium sanguineum subsp. sanguineiforme

Geranium sanguineum subsp.

sanguineiforme, commonly known as the blood geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
