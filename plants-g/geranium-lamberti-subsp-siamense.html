Geranium lamberti subsp. siamense

Geranium lamberti subsp.

siamense, commonly known as the Siam geranium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, while the stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
