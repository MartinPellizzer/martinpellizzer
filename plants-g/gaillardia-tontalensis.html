Gaillardia tontalensis

Gaillardia tontalensis, commonly known as the Tontalensis Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright orange and red ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers, making it visually striking. Gaillardia tontalensis is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
