Gnaphalium campestre

Gnaphalium campestre, commonly known as field everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Gnaphalium campestre is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
