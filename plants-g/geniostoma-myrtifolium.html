Geniostoma myrtifolium

Geniostoma myrtifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight waxy coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
