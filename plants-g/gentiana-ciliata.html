Gentiana ciliata

Gentiana ciliata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with ciliate margins, giving it its common name.

Gentiana ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
