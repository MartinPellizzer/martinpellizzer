Guilandina socorroensis

Guilandina socorroensis, commonly known as the Socorro legume, is a climbing plant native to the island of Socorro in the Revillagigedo archipelago.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in dry, sunny conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
