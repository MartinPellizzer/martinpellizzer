Gongylolepis huachamacari subsp. neblinensis

Gongylolepis huachamacari subsp.

neblinensis, commonly known as the cloud forest bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pale blue in color.

The plant thrives in humid, high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are generally broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
