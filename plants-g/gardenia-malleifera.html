Gardenia malleifera

Gardenia malleifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.
