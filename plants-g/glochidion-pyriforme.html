Glochidion pyriforme

Glochidion pyriforme, commonly known as the pear-shaped glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
