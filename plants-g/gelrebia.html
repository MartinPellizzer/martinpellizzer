Gelrebia

Gelrebia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices or along mountain slopes. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Gelrebia is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks.
