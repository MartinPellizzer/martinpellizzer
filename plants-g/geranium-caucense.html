Geranium caucense

Geranium caucense, commonly known as Caucasian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking hardy, colorful plants.
