Geissomeria longiflora

Geissomeria longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Geissomeria longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
