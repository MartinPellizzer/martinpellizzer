Geranium crassifolium

Geranium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of the stems.

Geranium crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
