Gnaphalium purpureum var. spathulatum

Gnaphalium purpureum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tiny tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins.

Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and bloom in clusters, often giving the plant a fluffy appearance.
