Grevillea pyramidalis subsp. leucadendron

Grevillea pyramidalis subsp.

leucadendron, commonly known as the white-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green in color.

The plant produces bright red or orange cylindrical flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
