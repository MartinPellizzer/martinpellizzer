Glochidion wallichianum

Glochidion wallichianum, commonly known as the silver leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
