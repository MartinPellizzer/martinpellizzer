Gentiana dimorphoclada

Gentiana dimorphoclada, commonly known as the two-form leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are distinctive, with two different forms—ovate and lanceolate—on the same plant, giving it its common name.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
