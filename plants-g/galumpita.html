Galumpita

Galumpita, commonly known as *Sida acuta*, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in some cultures for their purported healing benefits.

Galumpita thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
