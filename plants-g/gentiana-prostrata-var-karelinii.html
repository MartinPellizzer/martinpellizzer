Gentiana prostrata var. karelinii

Gentiana prostrata var. karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's gentian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
