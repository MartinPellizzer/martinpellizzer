Goeppertia bellula

Goeppertia bellula, commonly known as the silver star plant, is a striking member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, dark green leaves with striking white or silver veins that create a shimmering effect. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Goeppertia bellula is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
