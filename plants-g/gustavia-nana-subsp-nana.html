Gustavia nana subsp. nana

Gustavia nana subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming throughout the year.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
