Grevillea autumnalis

Grevillea autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in autumn, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Grevillea autumnalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
