Gynura haematophylla

Gynura haematophylla, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves with red veins. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to the dramatic foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
