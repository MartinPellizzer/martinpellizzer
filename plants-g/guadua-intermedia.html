Guadua intermedia

Guadua intermedia, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is highly valued for its durable and lightweight material, which is used in construction and handicrafts.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
