Glyceria borreri

Glyceria borreri, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Glyceria borreri is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
