Galium constrictum

Galium constrictum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galium constrictum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
