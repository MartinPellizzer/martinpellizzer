Gentiana undulatifolia

Gentiana undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a wavy margin, contributing to its common name.

Gentiana undulatifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
