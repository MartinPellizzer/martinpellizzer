Gladiolus tristis var. inflatus

Gladiolus tristis var. inflatus, commonly known as the inflated gladiolus, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the gladiolus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, and often inflated floral bracts that give it a unique appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in some areas.
