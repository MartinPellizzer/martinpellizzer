Griffithianthus cupularis

Griffithianthus cupularis, commonly known as the cupular flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has a smooth, often greyish bark and can grow quite tall, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
