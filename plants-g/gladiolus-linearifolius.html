Gladiolus linearifolius

Gladiolus linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers that range from white to deep red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its resilience in various climates.
