Glyphochloa veldkampii

Glyphochloa veldkampii, commonly known as the Veldkamp's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
