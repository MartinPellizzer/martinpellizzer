Galium longicaule

Galium longicaule, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium longicaule is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
