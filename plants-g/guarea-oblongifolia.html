Guarea oblongifolia

Guarea oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
