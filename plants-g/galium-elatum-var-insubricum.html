Galium elatum var. insubricum

Galium elatum var. insubricum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions where it plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
