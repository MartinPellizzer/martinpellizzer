Gentianopsis nesophila

Gentianopsis nesophila, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianopsis nesophila is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing nectar for pollinators.
