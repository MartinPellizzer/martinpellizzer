Gomortega

Gomortega, commonly known as the "cinnamon tree," is a South American evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the temperate rainforests of Chile and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, cinnamon-scented bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and mammals. Gomortega is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, cool climates and its role in supporting local biodiversity.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
