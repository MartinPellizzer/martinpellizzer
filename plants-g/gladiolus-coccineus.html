Gladiolus coccineus

Gladiolus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically red or pink, with a striking, sword-shaped appearance that resembles a gladiator's sword, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
