Grewia retusifolia

Grewia retusifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a rough or hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
