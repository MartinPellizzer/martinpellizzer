Geissorhiza bolusii

Geissorhiza bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears bright red, tubular flowers, which are particularly showy during the winter months. The plant produces small, fleshy, blue-black berries that are a food source for various bird species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in suitable habitats.

Geissorhiza bolusii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
