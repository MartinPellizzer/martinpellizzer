Gnaphalium odoratissimum

Gnaphalium odoratissimum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a fragrant essential oil, which contributes to its pleasant aroma. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture.

It is often used in herbal remedies and as a decorative plant due to its enduring flowers.
