Gigantochloa heteroclada

Gigantochloa heteroclada, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
