Glycine dentata

Glycine dentata, commonly known as the toothed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines. Glycine dentata thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.
