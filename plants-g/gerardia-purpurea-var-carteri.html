Gerardia purpurea var. carteri

Gerardia purpurea var. carteri, commonly known as Carter's gerardia, is a variant of the gerardia plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is well-suited to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
