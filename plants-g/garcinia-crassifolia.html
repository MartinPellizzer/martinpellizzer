Garcinia crassifolia

Garcinia crassifolia, commonly known as the African bitter orange, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits with a thick, acidic pulp and a single seed. Garcinia crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.
