Grammitis argyrata

Grammitis argyrata, commonly known as silver grammitis, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that often have a silvery or grayish hue due to the presence of fine trichomes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or on moist soil, thriving in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, woolly coating, which helps retain moisture.

Grammitis argyrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
