Gentiana muscoides

Gentiana muscoides, commonly known as the musk gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The genus Gentiana is known for its showy flowers, and G. muscoides is no exception, making it a notable species in alpine flora.
