Grammitis intromissa

Grammitis intromissa, commonly known as the slender grammitis, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis intromissa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
