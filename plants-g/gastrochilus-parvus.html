Gastrochilus parvus

Gastrochilus parvus, commonly known as the small-flowered gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. Its flowers are usually clustered in a spike and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Gastrochilus parvus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
