Grewia bicolor var. tephrodermis

Grewia bicolor var. tephrodermis, commonly known as the silverleaf grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
