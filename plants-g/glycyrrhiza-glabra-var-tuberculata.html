Glycyrrhiza glabra var. tuberculata

Glycyrrhiza glabra var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate licorice, is a variant of the licorice plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive tuberculate (wart-like) structures on its stems and leaves, which set it apart from other licorice varieties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and yields a root that is used in traditional medicine and as a sweetener. It contains glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its medicinal value and unique botanical features.
