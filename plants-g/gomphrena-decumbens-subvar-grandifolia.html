Gomphrena decumbens subvar. grandifolia

Gomphrena decumbens subvar. grandifolia, commonly known as the red fountain gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, spiky, pink or red flowers. The plant has wiry stems that lie along the ground, giving it a prostrate growth habit. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
