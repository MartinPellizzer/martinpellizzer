Godetia vinosa

Godetia vinosa, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with yellow flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Godetia vinosa is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
