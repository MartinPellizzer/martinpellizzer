Gardenia tetrasperma

Gardenia tetrasperma, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains four seeds, giving it the specific epithet *tetrasperma*.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
