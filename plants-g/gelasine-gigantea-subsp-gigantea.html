Gelasine gigantea subsp. gigantea

Gelasine gigantea subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant gelasine, is a striking plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of exotic plants.
