Gillenia

Gillenia, commonly known as meadow beauty, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. Gillenia species are native to North America and are often found in moist, woodland habitats. They have basal leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, lobed shape.

The flowers are composed of ray florets that range in color from white to pale yellow, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
