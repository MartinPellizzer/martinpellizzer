Gentiana radicata

Gentiana radicata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, stout rootstock and produces basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
