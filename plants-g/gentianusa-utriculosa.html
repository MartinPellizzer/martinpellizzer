Gentianusa utriculosa

Gentianusa utriculosa, commonly known as the bladder gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The plant's unique seed pods, which resemble small bladders, help in dispersing its seeds through mechanical means.
