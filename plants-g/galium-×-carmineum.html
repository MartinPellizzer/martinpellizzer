Galium × carmineum

Galium × carmineum, commonly known as Crimson bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Galium × carmineum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
