Gnidiopsis oppositifolia

Gnidiopsis oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed gnidiopsis, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically grouped in clusters. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is adapted to thrive in dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
