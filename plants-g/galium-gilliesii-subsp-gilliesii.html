Galium gilliesii subsp. gilliesii

Galium gilliesii subsp.

gilliesii, commonly known as Gillies' bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to western North America. It features slender stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, producing small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered with fine glands. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
