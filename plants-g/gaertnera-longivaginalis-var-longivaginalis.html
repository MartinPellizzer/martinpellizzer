Gaertnera longivaginalis var. longivaginalis

Gaertnera longivaginalis var. longivaginalis, commonly known as the long petioled hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with five prominent petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
