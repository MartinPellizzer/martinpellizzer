Gaultheria organensis

Gaultheria organensis, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds, giving it a minty scent, and it produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Gaultheria organensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions.
