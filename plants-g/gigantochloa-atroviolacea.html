Gigantochloa atroviolacea

Gigantochloa atroviolacea, commonly known as the black-violaceum bamboo, is a tall grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black-colored culms, which contrast sharply with its green leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are usually green or purplish in color.

Gigantochloa atroviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
