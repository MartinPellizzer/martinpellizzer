Galium trifidum subsp. tinctorium

Galium trifidum subsp.

tinctorium, commonly known as redroot sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, often reddish stems that are branched and covered with small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

The species is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
