Gentiana narcissoides

Gentiana narcissoides, commonly known as the nodding gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and bear the showy flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological importance.
