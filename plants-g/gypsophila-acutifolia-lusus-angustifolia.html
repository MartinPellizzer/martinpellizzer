Gypsophila acutifolia lusus angustifolia

Gypsophila acutifolia lusus angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in clusters of white, pink, or pale blue. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, linear leaves that give it a grass-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its close relationship to other gypsophila species, with "lusus" indicating a variant or subspecies.
