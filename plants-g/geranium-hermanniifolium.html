Geranium hermanniifolium

Geranium hermanniifolium, commonly known as the Hermann's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium hermanniifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
