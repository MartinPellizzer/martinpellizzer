Gilia hamata

Gilia hamata, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role.

Gilia hamata is an annual species, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
