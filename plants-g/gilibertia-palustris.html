Gilibertia palustris

Gilibertia palustris, commonly known as the swamp gilibertia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it visually distinctive.

Gilibertia palustris plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
