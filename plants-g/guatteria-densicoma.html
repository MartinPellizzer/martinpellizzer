Guatteria densicoma

Guatteria densicoma, commonly known as the dense-leaved guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
