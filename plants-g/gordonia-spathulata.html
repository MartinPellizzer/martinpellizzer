Gordonia spathulata

Gordonia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Gordonia spathulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
