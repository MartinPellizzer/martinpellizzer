Gastonia elegans

Gastonia elegans, commonly known as the elegant holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy texture and reddish stems that add visual interest. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
