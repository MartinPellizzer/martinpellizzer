Gordonia fragrans

Gordonia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its fruit is a capsule that releases seeds when mature, contributing to its propagation.

Gordonia fragrans is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
