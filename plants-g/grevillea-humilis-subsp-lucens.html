Grevillea humilis subsp. lucens

Grevillea humilis subsp.

lucens, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a low-growing shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, attracting native pollinators such as bees and birds.
