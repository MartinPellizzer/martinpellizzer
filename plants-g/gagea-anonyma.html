Gagea anonyma

Gagea anonyma, commonly known as the pale gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and bears small, white or pale blue flowers in early spring. Its bulbs are small and often hidden beneath the soil surface, contributing to its elusive nature.

Gagea anonyma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
