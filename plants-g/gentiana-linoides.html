Gentiana linoides

Gentiana linoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Gentiana linoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
