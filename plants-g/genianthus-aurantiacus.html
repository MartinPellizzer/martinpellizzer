Genianthus aurantiacus

Genianthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and a prominent yellow central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Genianthus aurantiacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and resilience.
