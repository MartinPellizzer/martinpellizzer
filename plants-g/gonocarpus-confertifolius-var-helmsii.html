Gonocarpus confertifolius var. helmsii

Gonocarpus confertifolius var. helmsii, commonly known as the Helms' denseleaf sea grape, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
