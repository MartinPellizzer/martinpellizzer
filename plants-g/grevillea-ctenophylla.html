Grevillea ctenophylla

Grevillea ctenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant, red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Grevillea ctenophylla is also an important source of nectar for native bees and birds.
