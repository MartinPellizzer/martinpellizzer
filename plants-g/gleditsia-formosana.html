Gleditsia formosana

Gleditsia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
