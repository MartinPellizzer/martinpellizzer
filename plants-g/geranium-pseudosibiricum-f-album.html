Geranium pseudosibiricum f. album

Geranium pseudosibiricum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and striking contrasting flower color.
