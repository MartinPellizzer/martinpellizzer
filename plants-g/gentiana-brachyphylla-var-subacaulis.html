Gentiana brachyphylla var. subacaulis

Gentiana brachyphylla var. subacaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, often prostrate stems and dark blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
