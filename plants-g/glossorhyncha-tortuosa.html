Glossorhyncha tortuosa

Glossorhyncha tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral-leaved orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Glossorhyncha tortuosa is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a threatened species in its natural range.
