Glechoma sessilifolia

Glechoma sessilifolia, commonly known as ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, downy texture. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma sessilifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both stolons and seeds.
