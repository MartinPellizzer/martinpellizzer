Gregoria caespitosa

Gregoria caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered gregonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit, with dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale blue blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Gregoria caespitosa is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil.
