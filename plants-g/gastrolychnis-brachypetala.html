Gastrolychnis brachypetala

Gastrolychnis brachypetala, commonly known as the short-stemmed moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
