Gmelina sessilis var. ramiflora

Gmelina sessilis var. ramiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gmelina, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous flowers that are white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
