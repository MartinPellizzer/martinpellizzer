Gladiolus occidentalis

Gladiolus occidentalis, commonly known as the western gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, erect stems that bear showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The western gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
