Gymnadenia × sabandoi

Gymnadenia × sabandoi, commonly known as the Sabandoi grass-pink, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gymnadenia × sabandoi is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic value in horticultural settings.
