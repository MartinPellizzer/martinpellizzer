Garcinia sordidoluteola

Garcinia sordidoluteola, commonly known as the sordidolutea Garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research. Its habitat is typically moist, shaded environments, and it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is sometimes studied for potential medicinal applications.
