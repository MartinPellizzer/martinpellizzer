Galactia glaucescens var. obtusa

Galactia glaucescens var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant has palmately compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in racemate clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus Galactia.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
