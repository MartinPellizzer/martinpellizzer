Grammitis semiadnata

Grammitis semiadnata, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, soft foliage that often appears as a carpet on tree trunks or branches. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a silvery sheen on the underside, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Grammitis semiadnata plays a role in the ecological balance by providing habitat and moisture retention in its microhabitat.
