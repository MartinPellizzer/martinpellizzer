Gentianella cunninghamii

Gentianella cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's gentian, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understoreys or swampy areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Gentianella cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
