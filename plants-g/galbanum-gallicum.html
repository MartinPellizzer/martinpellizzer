Galbanum gallicum

Galbanum gallicum, commonly known as wild galbanum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces a yellowish to greenish resin, which is often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters.

Galbanum gallicum thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
