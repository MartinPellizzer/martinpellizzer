Grastidium cyrtosepalum

Grastidium cyrtosepalum, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, cup-like sepals that enclose the flower. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often white or pale green, with a delicate fragrance.

Grastidium cyrtosepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
