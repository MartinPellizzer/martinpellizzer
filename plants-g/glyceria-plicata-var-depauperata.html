Glyceria plicata var. depauperata

Glyceria plicata var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender flatleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often folded or rolled, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf structure compared to other Glyceria species, making it a unique component of wetland ecosystems.
