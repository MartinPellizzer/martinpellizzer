Gossypium wightianum var. pubescens

Gossypium wightianum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cotton plant, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that develop in its capsules. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other cotton species and related plants. Its fibrous seeds are used in traditional medicine and textile production.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves is a distinguishing botanical feature.
