Gnidia fastigiata

Gnidia fastigiata, commonly known as the slender gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with a compact and upright growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
