Gomphrena celosioides f. aureiflora

Gomphrena celosioides f. aureiflora, commonly known as the golden flower gothic.

This plant is a flowering species belonging to the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, long-lasting inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to red, with a distinctive, rounded shape and a golden center in this particular form. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
