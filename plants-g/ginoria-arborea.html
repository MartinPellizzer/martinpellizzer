Ginoria arborea

Ginoria arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking ornamental tree. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Ginoria arborea thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
