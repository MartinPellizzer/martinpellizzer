Gentiana lasiopoda

Gentiana lasiopoda, commonly known as the woolly gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gentiana lasiopoda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
