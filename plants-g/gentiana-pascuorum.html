Gentiana pascuorum

Gentiana pascuorum, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and branching.

Gentiana pascuorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
