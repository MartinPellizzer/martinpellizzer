Godetia ambigua

Godetia ambigua, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
