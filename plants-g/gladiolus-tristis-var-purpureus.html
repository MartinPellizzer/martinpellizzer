Gladiolus tristis var. purpureus

Gladiolus tristis var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is also cultivated for horticultural purposes.
