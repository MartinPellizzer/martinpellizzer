Gentiana verna subsp. favratii

Gentiana verna subsp.

favratii, commonly known as the early gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It plays an important role in alpine ecosystems, providing nectar for early pollinators.
