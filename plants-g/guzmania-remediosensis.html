Guzmania remediosensis

Guzmania remediosensis, commonly known as the Red-Veined Bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports microfauna. Its striking red and yellow flowers bloom from a central spadix, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Guzmania remediosensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.
