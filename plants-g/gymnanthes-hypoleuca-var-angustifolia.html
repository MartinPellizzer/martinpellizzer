Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. angustifolia

Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gumbo, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a small tree or shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
