Glycosmis pentaphylla var. macrocarpa

Glycosmis pentaphylla var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are yellow when ripe. The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.
