Gentianella oellgaardii

Gentianella oellgaardii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is part of the Gentianaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
