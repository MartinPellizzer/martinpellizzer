Gratiola neglecta

Gratiola neglecta, commonly known as the slender yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, tubular, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Gratiola neglecta is often found in wetlands and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
