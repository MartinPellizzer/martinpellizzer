Gunnera peruviana

Gunnera peruviana, commonly known as the Andean giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and they are arranged in a rosette pattern around the plant's central stem. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Its unique appearance and robust growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
