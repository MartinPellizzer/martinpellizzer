Gaertnera cooperi

Gaertnera cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's gaertnera, is a species of flowering plant native to the humid forests of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, elliptical seeds.

Gaertnera cooperi is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
