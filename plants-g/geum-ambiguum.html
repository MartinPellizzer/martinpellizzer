Geum ambiguum

Geum ambiguum, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
