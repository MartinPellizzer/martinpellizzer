Gilia tenuiflora subsp. cana

Gilia tenuiflora subsp.

cana, commonly known as the canyon gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
