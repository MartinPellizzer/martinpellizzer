Glycine sericea

Glycine sericea, commonly known as silky vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, sandy conditions.

Glycine sericea is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
