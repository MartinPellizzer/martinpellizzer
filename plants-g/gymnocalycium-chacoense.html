Gymnocalycium chacoense

Gymnocalycium chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Its leaves are ephemeral, appearing only after rainfall, and are quickly replaced by spines.

Gymnocalycium chacoense is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
