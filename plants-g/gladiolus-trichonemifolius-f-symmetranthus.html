Gladiolus trichonemifolius f. symmetranthus

Gladiolus trichonemifolius f. symmetranthus, commonly known as the symmetric gladiolus, is a cultivar characterized by its striking, symmetrical floral structure.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to South Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are typically vibrant pink or red, with a prominent central stamen. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It requires moderate water and is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice among horticulturists.
