Globulea paniculata

Globulea paniculata, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, globular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of numerous small petals arranged in a distinctive, rounded shape, giving it a unique appearance.

Globulea paniculata is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
