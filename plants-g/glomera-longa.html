Glomera longa

Glomera longa, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Glomera longa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
