Grevillea brachystylis subsp. grandis

Grevillea brachystylis subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
