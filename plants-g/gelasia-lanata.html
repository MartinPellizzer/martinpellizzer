Gelasia lanata

Gelasia lanata, commonly known as the woolly gelasia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or lanate, leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
