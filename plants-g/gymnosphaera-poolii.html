Gymnosphaera poolii

Gymnosphaera poolii, commonly known as the African moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, swollen, and often colorful caudex that resembles a moon, which is why it is named after the moon. The plant's leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it store water efficiently. Gymnosphaera poolii produces small, white flowers during the growing season, usually in spring or early summer.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
