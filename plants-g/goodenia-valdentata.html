Goodenia valdentata

Goodenia valdentata, commonly known as the golden goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

Goodenia valdentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
