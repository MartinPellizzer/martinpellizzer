Garcinia ruscifolia var. ruscifolia

Garcinia ruscifolia var. ruscifolia, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling torches, hence its common name. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is notable for its vibrant floral display and medicinal potential.
