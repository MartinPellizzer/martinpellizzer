Galium songaricum

Galium songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom during the summer months.

Galium songaricum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
