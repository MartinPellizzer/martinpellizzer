Grevillea pityophylla

Grevillea pityophylla, commonly known as the pink-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink or red flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Grevillea pityophylla is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
