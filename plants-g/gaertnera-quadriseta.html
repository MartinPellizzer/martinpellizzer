Gaertnera quadriseta

Gaertnera quadriseta, commonly known as the four-set geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are arranged in four sets around the stem. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Gaertnera quadriseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
