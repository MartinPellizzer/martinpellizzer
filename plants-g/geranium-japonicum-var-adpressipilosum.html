Geranium japonicum var. adpressipilosum

Geranium japonicum var. adpressipilosum, commonly known as Japanese geranium, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
