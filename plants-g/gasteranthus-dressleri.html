Gasteranthus dressleri

Gasteranthus dressleri, commonly known as the "Giant Nightshade" or "Tropical Nightshade," is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with intricate patterns that resemble the skin of a fruit. The plant is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening at night and closing in the morning, a trait that aids in pollination by moths and other night-active insects.

Gasteranthus dressleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and vibrant blooms.
