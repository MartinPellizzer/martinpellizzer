Guaiacum microphyllum

Guaiacum microphyllum, commonly known as the little-leaved guaiacwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually small and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in various industries.
