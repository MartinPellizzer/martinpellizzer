Grewia diversipes

Grewia diversipes, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant has cultural and traditional uses in the regions where it is native.
