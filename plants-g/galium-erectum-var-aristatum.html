Galium erectum var. aristatum

Galium erectum var. aristatum, commonly known as silverweed or bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium erectum var. aristatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
