Glandularia platensis

Glandularia platensis, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.

Glandularia platensis is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations.
