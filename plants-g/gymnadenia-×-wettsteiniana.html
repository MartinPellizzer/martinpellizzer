Gymnadenia × wettsteiniana

Gymnadenia × wettsteiniana, commonly known as the Wettstein's grass pink, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

Gymnadenia × wettsteiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
