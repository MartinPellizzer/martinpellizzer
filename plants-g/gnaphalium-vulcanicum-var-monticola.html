Gnaphalium vulcanicum var. monticola

Gnaphalium vulcanicum var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
