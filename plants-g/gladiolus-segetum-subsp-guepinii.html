Gladiolus segetum subsp. guepinii

Gladiolus segetum subsp.

guepinii, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for garden use.
