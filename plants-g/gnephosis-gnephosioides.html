Gnephosis gnephosioides

Gnephosis gnephosioides, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on the bark of trees in swampy or wetland areas. The plant has a unique appearance, with long, slender leaves and a single, delicate flower that resembles a ghostly figure. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain rather than soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and declining populations, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
