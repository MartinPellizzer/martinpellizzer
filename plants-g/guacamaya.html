Guacamaya

Guacamaya, commonly known as the scarlet macaw, is a large, colorful parrot native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green and red plumage, with a striking blue or purple head. The plant aspect of guacamaya refers to its natural habitat, which includes tropical rainforests and woodlands. These birds rely on a variety of fruits, seeds, and nuts for sustenance, playing a role in seed dispersal.

Their presence is an indicator of a healthy ecosystem, as they are sensitive to environmental changes.
