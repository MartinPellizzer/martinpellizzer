Gentiana caeruleogrisea

Gentiana caeruleogrisea, commonly known as the blue-gray gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, grayish down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gentiana caeruleogrisea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
