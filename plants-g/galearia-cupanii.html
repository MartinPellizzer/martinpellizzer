Galearia cupanii

Galearia cupanii, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Galearia cupanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
