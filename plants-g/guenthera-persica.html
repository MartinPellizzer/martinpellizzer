Guenthera persica

Guenthera persica, commonly known as Persian guenthera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.

Guenthera persica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
