Glycyrrhiza glabra var. violacea

Glycyrrhiza glabra var. violacea, commonly known as violet licorice, is a variant of the licorice plant characterized by its violet-colored flowers.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties. The plant produces long, cylindrical roots that are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweetening and anti-inflammatory effects. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
