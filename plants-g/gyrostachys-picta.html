Gyrostachys picta

Gyrostachys picta, commonly known as the painted balmucca, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from red to purple, which are often more showy than the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.
