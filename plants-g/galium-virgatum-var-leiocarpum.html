Galium virgatum var. leiocarpum

Galium virgatum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as slender wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that dehisces to release tiny seeds.

This variant is native to eastern North America and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
