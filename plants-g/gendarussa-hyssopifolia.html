Gendarussa hyssopifolia

Gendarussa hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Gendarussa hyssopifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
