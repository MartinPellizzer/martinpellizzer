Gloriosa superba var. angustifolia

Gloriosa superba var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flame lily, is a striking tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, tubular, and often brightly colored flowers that range from orange to red. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
