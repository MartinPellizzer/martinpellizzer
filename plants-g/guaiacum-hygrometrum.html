Guaiacum hygrometrum

Guaiacum hygrometrum, commonly known as the Caribbean bay or wild guaiac, is a member of the Zygophyllaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its bark and wood have been historically used in traditional medicine and for tanning leather.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
