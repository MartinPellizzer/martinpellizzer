Gnaphalium niitakayamense

Gnaphalium niitakayamense, commonly known as the Niitakayama hairweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, fluffy capitula composed of numerous tiny white florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium niitakayamense is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
