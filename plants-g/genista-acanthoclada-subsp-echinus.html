Genista acanthoclada subsp. echinus

Genista acanthoclada subsp.

echinus, commonly known as the prickly genista, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has compound leaves with narrow, leathery leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
