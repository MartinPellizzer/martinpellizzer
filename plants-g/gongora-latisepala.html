Gongora latisepala

Gongora latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with broad, fleshy petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Gongora latisepala is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
