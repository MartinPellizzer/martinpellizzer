Gentianella montana subsp. ionostigma

Gentianella montana subsp.

ionostigma, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
