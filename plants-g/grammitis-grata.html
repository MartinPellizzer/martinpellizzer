Grammitis grata

Grammitis grata, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green with a silvery sheen on the underside. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside of its leaves.

Grammitis grata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
