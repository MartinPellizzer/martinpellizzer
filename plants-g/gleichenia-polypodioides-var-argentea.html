Gleichenia polypodioides var. argentea

Gleichenia polypodioides var. argentea, commonly known as silver Gleichenia, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic or lithophytic species, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are elongated and often have a leathery texture, with a unique coloration that sets it apart from other fern varieties.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its striking appearance and adaptability to controlled environments.
