Genista calycina

Genista calycina, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, bean-like pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista calycina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
