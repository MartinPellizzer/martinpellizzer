Gireoudia carolineifolia

Gireoudia carolineifolia, commonly known as Caroline's gireoudia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
