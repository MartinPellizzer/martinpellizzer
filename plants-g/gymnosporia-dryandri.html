Gymnosporia dryandri

Gymnosporia dryandri, commonly known as the dryandra acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, typical of many legumes. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Gymnosporia dryandri is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
