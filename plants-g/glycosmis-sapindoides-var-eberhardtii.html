Glycosmis sapindoides var. eberhardtii

Glycosmis sapindoides var. eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's honeycomb tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, round, orange-red fruits. The fruits are fleshy and contain several seeds, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some local uses in herbal remedies.
