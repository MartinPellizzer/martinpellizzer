Gamanthus ferganicus

Gamanthus ferganicus, commonly known as the Ferganicus ghost plant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent leaves that allow light to pass through, giving it an ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of pale pink and white. Its root system is highly adapted to nutrient-poor soils, enabling it to thrive in extreme environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Gamanthus ferganicus is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
