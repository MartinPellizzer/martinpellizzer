Geranium longiflorum

Geranium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium longiflorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
