Gymnopogon spicatus var. brevisetus

Gymnopogon spicatus var. brevisetus, commonly known as the short-awned spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescence. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn.

This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
