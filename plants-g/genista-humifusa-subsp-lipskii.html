Genista humifusa subsp. lipskii

Genista humifusa subsp.

lipskii, commonly known as the Lipskii broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological characteristics compared to other members of the Genista genus.
