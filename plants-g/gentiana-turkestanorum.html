Gentiana turkestanorum

Gentiana turkestanorum, commonly known as the Turkestan gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Gentiana turkestanorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
