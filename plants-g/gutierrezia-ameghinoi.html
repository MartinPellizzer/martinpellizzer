Gutierrezia ameghinoi

Gutierrezia ameghinoi, commonly known as the Argentine goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Gutierrezia ameghinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
