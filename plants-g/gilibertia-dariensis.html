Gilibertia dariensis

Gilibertia dariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or parotia grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are a common feature in the grass family. Gilibertia dariensis is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
