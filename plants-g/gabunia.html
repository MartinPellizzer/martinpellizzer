Gabunia

Gabunia, commonly known as the African orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant blooms. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrials, with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. Gabunia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Their unique floral structures and vibrant colors make them a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
