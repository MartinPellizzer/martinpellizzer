Genista trifoliata var. romanica

Genista trifoliata var. romanica, commonly known as the Roman broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems covered in small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain areas.
