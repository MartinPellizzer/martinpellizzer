Gentianopsis lanceolata

Gentianopsis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Gentianopsis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
