Garcinia tonkinensis

Garcinia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia tonkinensis is often found in moist, tropical forest environments, growing along stream banks and in shaded undergrowth.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in botanical research.
