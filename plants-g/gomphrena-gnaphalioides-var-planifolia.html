Gomphrena gnaphalioides var. planifolia

Gomphrena gnaphalioides var. planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spiky bracts that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
