Gasteria pulchra

Gasteria pulchra, commonly known as the African ivory plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers on tall, slender stems during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Gasteria pulchra is often used in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
