Gaura hirsuta

Gaura hirsuta, commonly known as the downy gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gaura hirsuta is often cultivated for its showy blooms and low maintenance requirements in gardens.
