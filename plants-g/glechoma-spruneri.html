Glechoma spruneri

Glechoma spruneri, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma spruneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly through runners.
