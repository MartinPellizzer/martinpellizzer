Gentiana jesoana var. coreana

Gentiana jesoana var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
