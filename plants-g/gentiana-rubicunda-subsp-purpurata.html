Gentiana rubicunda subsp. purpurata

Gentiana rubicunda subsp.

purpurata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
