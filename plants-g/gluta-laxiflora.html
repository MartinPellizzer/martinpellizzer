Gluta laxiflora

Gluta laxiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf gluta, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

Gluta laxiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
