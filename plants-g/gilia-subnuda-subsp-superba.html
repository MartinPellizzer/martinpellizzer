Gilia subnuda subsp. superba

Gilia subnuda subsp.

superba, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and a lack of hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a smooth appearance.
