Gypsophila ascendens

Gypsophila ascendens, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or mountain poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila ascendens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
