Grindelia oolepis

Grindelia oolepis, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Grindelia oolepis is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be sticky and persistent.
