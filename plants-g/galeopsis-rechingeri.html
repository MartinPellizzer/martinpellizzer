Galeopsis rechingeri

Galeopsis rechingeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horse mint, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
