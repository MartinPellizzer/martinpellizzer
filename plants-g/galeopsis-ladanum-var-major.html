Galeopsis ladanum var. major

Galeopsis ladanum var. major, commonly known as greater hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in whorled clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
