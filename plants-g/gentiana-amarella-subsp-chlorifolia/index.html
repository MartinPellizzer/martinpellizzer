Gentiana Amarella Subsp. Chlorifolia

Gentiana amarella subsp. chlorifolia is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is known for its vivid blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. This subspecies is distinguished by its deep green leaves, which set it apart from other gentian varieties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky outcrops. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
