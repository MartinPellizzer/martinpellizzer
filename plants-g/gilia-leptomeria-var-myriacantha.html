Gilia leptomeria var. myriacantha

Gilia leptomeria var. myriacantha, commonly known as desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
