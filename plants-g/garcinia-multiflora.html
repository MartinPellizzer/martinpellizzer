Garcinia multiflora

Garcinia multiflora, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces bright red, fleshy fruits encased in a thick, segmented rind. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
