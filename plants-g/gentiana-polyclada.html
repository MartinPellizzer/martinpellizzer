Gentiana polyclada

Gentiana polyclada, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy cluster that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
