Glandulifolia pubescens

Glandulifolia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy glandular plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It typically features small, glandular structures on its foliage, which secrete oils or resins. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
