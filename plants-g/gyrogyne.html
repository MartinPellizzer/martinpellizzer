Gyrogyne

Gyrogyne, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in dry, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble a crown, hence its common name. Gyrogyne species are typically shrubs or subshrubs with hairy, glandular leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral display.
