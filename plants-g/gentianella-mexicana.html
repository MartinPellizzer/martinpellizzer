Gentianella mexicana

Gentianella mexicana, commonly known as Mexican gentian, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth texture.

Gentianella mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
