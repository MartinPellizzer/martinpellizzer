Gilia sinuata

Gilia sinuata, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
