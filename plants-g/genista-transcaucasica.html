Genista transcaucasica

Genista transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and ornamental gardening.
