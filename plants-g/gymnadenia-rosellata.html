Gymnadenia rosellata

Gymnadenia rosellata, commonly known as the Rosella orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Gymnadenia rosellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
