Glischrocaryon angustifolium

Glischrocaryon angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glischrocaryon, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
