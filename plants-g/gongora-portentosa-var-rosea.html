Gongora portentosa var. rosea

Gongora portentosa var. rosea, commonly known as the Roseate Gongora, is a striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink and white petals, which are often speckled with contrasting colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure and long-lasting bloom, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for botanical gardens and private collections.
