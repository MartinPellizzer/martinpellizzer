Gentianella anisodonta subsp. antecedens

Gentianella anisodonta subsp.

antecedens, commonly known as the Antecedent Gentian, is a member of the Gentianaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
