Geissorhiza geminata

Geissorhiza geminata, commonly known as the twin-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces two to three pairs of elliptic, leathery leaves. The plant blooms with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hooded labellum. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and requires well-drained conditions.

Geissorhiza geminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique floral structure.
