Grevillea scortechinii

Grevillea scortechinii, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking red stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea scortechinii is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
