Gynerium parviflorum

Gynerium parviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Additionally, it is a valuable plant for ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in aquatic ecosystems.
