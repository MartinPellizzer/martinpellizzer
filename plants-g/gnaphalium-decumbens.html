Gnaphalium decumbens

Gnaphalium decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate everlasting, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically has small, yellow to brownish flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
