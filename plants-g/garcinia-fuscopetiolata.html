Garcinia fuscopetiolata

Garcinia fuscopetiolata, commonly known as the dark-stemmed garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in phytochemical research.

Garcinia fuscopetiolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
