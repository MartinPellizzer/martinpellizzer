Gronophyllum chaunostachys

Gronophyllum chaunostachys, commonly known as the desert ironwood, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and woody, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
