Grevillea pyriformis

Grevillea pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, feather-like flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea pyriformis is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
