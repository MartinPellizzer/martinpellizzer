Grevillea bracteosa

Grevillea bracteosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in cylindrical clusters. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to arid and coastal environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Grevillea bracteosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
