Gentiana kolakovskyi

Gentiana kolakovskyi, commonly known as the Kolakovsky gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing vivid blue petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
