Goodyera corniculata

Goodyera corniculata, commonly known as the hooded twayblade, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors, often in association with other epiphytic and terrestrial orchids. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that supports a single, hooded flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are basal, ovate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in rich, humusy soils.
