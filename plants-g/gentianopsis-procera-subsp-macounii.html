Gentianopsis procera subsp. macounii

Gentianopsis procera subsp.

macounii, commonly known as Macoun's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
