Grindelia robusta var. rigida

Grindelia robusta var. rigida, commonly known as rigid horseweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Grindelia robusta var. rigida is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
