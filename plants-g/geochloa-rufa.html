Geochloa rufa

Geochloa rufa, commonly known as the red grass or red needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and environmental conditions. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Geochloa rufa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its distinctive appearance.
