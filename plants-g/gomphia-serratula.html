Gomphia serratula

Gomphia serratula, commonly known as the serrated gomphia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
