Gentiana pachystemon

Gentiana pachystemon, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana pachystemon is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
