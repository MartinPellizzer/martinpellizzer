Genista versiflora

Genista versiflora, commonly known as the bluebells of the Mediterranean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky and limestone soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in open woodlands and shrublands.

Genista versiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
