Genipa pubescens

Genipa pubescens, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits with a bright yellow or orange exterior. The fruit's interior contains a single, large, white or pink seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine and as a food source.
