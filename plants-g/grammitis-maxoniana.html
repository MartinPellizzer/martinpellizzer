Grammitis maxoniana

Grammitis maxoniana, commonly known as the Hawaiian fern, is a species of polypod fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and streamside habitats. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Grammitis maxoniana plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing shelter for various small organisms.
