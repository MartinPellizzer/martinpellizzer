Geum coccineum var. florepleno

Geum coccineum var. florepleno, commonly known as the double-flowered New Zealand fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features showy, double blooms that are typically red or pink, with a distinctive central cone-like structure. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, while the stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
