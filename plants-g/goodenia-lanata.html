Goodenia lanata

Goodenia lanata, commonly known as the woolly goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Goodenia lanata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
