Galium angustifolium var. bernardinum

Galium angustifolium var. bernardinum, commonly known as narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
