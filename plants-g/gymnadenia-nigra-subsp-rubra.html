Gymnadenia nigra subsp. rubra

Gymnadenia nigra subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red-fruited gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-orange fruits, which contrast with the typical black fruits of the main species. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
