Gossypium peruvianum subsp. typicum

Gossypium peruvianum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Peruvian cotton plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous shrub that produces fluffy, white fibers used for textile production.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while its flowers are showy and often pink or red in color.

This subspecies is valued for its fiber quality and has been cultivated for traditional and commercial use.
