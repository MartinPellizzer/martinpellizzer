Gentiana cerina

Gentiana cerina, commonly known as the snowy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
