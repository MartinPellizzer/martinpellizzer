Gleditsia horrida var. platycarpa

Gleditsia horrida var. platycarpa, commonly known as the flat-seeded honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are often reduced to spines, giving it a distinctive, thorny appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to dry, sandy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
