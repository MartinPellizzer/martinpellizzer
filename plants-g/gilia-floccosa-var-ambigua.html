Gilia floccosa var. ambigua

Gilia floccosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the clouded gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purplish tinge. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
