Gongora napoensis

Gongora napoensis, commonly known as the Napo orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display vibrant hues of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many orchid species.

Gongora napoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists alike.
