Gaultheria beamanii

Gaultheria beamanii, commonly known as the Beaman's wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are edible and have a sharp, acidic flavor, similar to cranberries.

Gaultheria beamanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
