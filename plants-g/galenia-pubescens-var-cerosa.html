Galenia pubescens var. cerosa

Galenia pubescens var. cerosa, commonly known as the hairy galenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
