Galinsoga formosa

Galinsoga formosa, commonly known as the prettyweed or yellow galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base. The leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in cultivated areas.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
