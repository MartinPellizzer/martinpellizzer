Gynandropsis ophitocarpa

Gynandropsis ophitocarpa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. It has a milky sap that is toxic to some animals, including livestock.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
