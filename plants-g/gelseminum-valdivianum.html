Gelseminum valdivianum

Gelseminum valdivianum, commonly known as the Valdivian crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Gelseminum valdivianum is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
