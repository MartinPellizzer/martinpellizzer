Gladiolus confusus

Gladiolus confusus, commonly known as the confused gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, spike-like inflorescences with colorful, bilateral flowers that are often pink, red, or purple. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
