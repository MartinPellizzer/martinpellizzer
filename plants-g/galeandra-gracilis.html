Galeandra gracilis

Galeandra gracilis, commonly known as the slender galeandra, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.

Galeandra gracilis is a member of the Orchidaceae family, which includes a wide variety of flowering plants known for their diverse morphology and ecological adaptations.
