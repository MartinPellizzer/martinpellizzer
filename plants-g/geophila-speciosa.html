Geophila speciosa

Geophila speciosa, commonly known as the showy geophila, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Geophila speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
