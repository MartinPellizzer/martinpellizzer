Gypsophila aucheri var. adenoclada

Gypsophila aucheri var. adenoclada, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble clouds, giving it a soft and ethereal appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and distinctive floral structure.
