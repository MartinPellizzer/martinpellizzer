Gmelina schlechteri

Gmelina schlechteri, commonly known as the African gum tree, is a member of the Oleaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and riverine habitats.

Gmelina schlechteri is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
