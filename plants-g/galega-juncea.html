Galega juncea

Galega juncea, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in various soil types, including poor and sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Additionally, it is often used in forage and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
