Galium spurium subsp. aparinella

Galium spurium subsp.

aparinella, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, ascending stems with opposite, oval to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
