Galium stellatum var. typicum

Galium stellatum var. typicum, commonly known as starfruit wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
