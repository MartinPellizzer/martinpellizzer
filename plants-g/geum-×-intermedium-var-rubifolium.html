Geum × intermedium var. rubifolium

Geum × intermedium var. rubifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf garden buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has serrated, red-tinged leaves that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Geum × intermedium var. rubifolium is often used in wetland gardens and as a shoreline plant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
