Guarea gomma

Guarea gomma, commonly known as the gomme tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its latex, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
