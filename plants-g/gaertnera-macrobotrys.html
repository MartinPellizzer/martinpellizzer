Gaertnera macrobotrys

Gaertnera macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of down.

Gaertnera macrobotrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
