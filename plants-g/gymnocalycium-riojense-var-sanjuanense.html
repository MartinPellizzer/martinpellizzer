Gymnocalycium riojense var. sanjuanense

Gymnocalycium riojense var. sanjuanense, commonly known as the San Juan moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular or cylindrical stem, which is typically ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is a drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
