Gentiana pseudosquarrosa

Gentiana pseudosquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Gentiana pseudosquarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
