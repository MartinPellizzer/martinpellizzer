Gaura strigillosa

Gaura strigillosa, commonly known as the silverleaf gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of pale pink to white, tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow, silvery-white on the underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
