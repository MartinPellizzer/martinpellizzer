Gaillardia aristata f. monochroma

Gaillardia aristata f. monochroma, commonly known as the "Red Indian Paintbrush," is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and wildflower landscapes.

This cultivar is prized for its striking appearance and ecological benefits.
