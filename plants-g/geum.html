Geum

Geum, commonly known as avens, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

These plants are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers. The leaves are typically basal, simple, and arranged in a rosette or alternate pattern. Geum species vary in height, with some growing as low-growing herbs and others as shrubs.

They are valued in gardens for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
