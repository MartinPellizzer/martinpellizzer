Geniostoma glaucescens

Geniostoma glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue-green flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Geniostoma glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
