Gleditsia meliloba

Gleditsia meliloba, commonly known as the laceleaf honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in landscaping.
