Gronophyllum mayrii

Gronophyllum mayrii, commonly known as the Mayrii's ironwood, is a rare and woody shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Due to its unique adaptations, Gronophyllum mayrii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
