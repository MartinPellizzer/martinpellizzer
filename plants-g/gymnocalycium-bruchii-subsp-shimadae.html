Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. shimadae

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

shimadae, commonly known as the Shimada's moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Central America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that store water.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its small, rounded stems are often covered in white or pale blue spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
