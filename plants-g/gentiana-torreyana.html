Gentiana torreyana

Gentiana torreyana, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. It has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana torreyana is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, temperate climates.
