Garnotia scoparia

Garnotia scoparia, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Garnotia scoparia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal and arid ecosystems.
