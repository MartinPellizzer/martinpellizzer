Galium tinctorium subsp. floridanum

Galium tinctorium subsp.

floridanum, commonly known as Florida bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its historical use in dyeing fabrics and as a herbal remedy reflects its botanical and cultural significance.
