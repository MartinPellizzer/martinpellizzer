Gladiolus dehnianus

Gladiolus dehnianus, commonly known as the Dehn's gladiolus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with three prominent petals and three sepals that are often more prominent than the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown for its vibrant floral displays.
