Geranium cataractarum subsp. cataractarum

Geranium cataractarum subsp.

cataractarum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
