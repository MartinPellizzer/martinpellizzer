Gentianopsis thermalis

Gentianopsis thermalis, commonly known as the thermal gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentianopsis thermalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in specialized botanical gardens.
