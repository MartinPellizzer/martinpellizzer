Gladiolus imbricatus subsp. pauciflorus

Gladiolus imbricatus subsp.

pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered gladiolus, is a member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

The flowers typically range in color from white to pale pink and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
