Grindelia subalpina

Grindelia subalpina, commonly known as alpine gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Grindelia subalpina produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
