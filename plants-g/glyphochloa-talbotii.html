Glyphochloa talbotii

Glyphochloa talbotii, commonly known as Talbot's grass, is a species of grass native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
