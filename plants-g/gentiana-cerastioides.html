Gentiana cerastioides

Gentiana cerastioides, commonly known as the cerastoid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana cerastioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
