Geranium asiaticum f. lanceolatum

Geranium asiaticum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the Asian geranium or lanceleaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in garden settings.
