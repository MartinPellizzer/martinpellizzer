Grevillea hewardiana

Grevillea hewardiana, commonly known as the Heward's waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea hewardiana is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
