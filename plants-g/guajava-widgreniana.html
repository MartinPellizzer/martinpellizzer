Guajava widgreniana

Guajava widgreniana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
