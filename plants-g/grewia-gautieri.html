Grewia gautieri

Grewia gautieri, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia gautieri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
