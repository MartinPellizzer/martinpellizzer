Grewia conocarpa

Grewia conocarpa, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
