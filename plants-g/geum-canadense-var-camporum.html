Geum canadense var. camporum

Geum canadense var. camporum, commonly known as showy mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a distinctive yellow flower head composed of small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
