Geissois racemosa

Geissois racemosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Geissois racemosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
