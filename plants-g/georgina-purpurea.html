Georgina purpurea

Georgina purpurea, commonly known as the purple georgina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Georgina purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
