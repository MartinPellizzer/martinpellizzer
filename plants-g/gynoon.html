Gynoon

Gynoon, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense, humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer under sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale blossoms that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the forest floor.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, the gynoon is critically endangered and difficult to study in the wild.
