Gastonia duplicata

Gastonia duplicata, commonly known as the double-stemmed fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that often split into two separate stems, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a dark green color with a rough texture.

Gastonia duplicata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual interest.
