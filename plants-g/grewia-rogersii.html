Grewia rogersii

Grewia rogersii, commonly known as the yellow star fruit, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It produces small, white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Grewia rogersii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
