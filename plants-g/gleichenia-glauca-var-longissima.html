Gleichenia glauca var. longissima

Gleichenia glauca var. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed glaucous fern, is a variant of the Gleichenia glauca species, characterized by its elongated fronds.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with dark green, leathery fronds that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
