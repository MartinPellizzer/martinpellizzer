Granadilla vespertilio

Granadilla vespertilio, commonly known as the evening granadilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus *Tropaeolum* and is characterized by its vibrant, edible berries that range in color from orange to red. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening. Its flowers are typically yellow with red markings, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, adding a tangy flavor to various dishes.
