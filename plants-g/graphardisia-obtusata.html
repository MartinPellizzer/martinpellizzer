Graphardisia obtusata

Graphardisia obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a distinctive blunt tip, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, depending on the species.
