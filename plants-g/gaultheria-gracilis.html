Gaultheria gracilis

Gaultheria gracilis, commonly known as the slender wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It produces small, dark red berries that are rich in aromatic oils and contain the compound methyl salicylate, which is responsible for its distinctive wintergreen scent. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, mat-like growth habit. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gaultheria gracilis is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
