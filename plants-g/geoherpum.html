Geoherpum

Geoherpum, commonly known as the goldenrod hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow to orange flower heads, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Geoherpum is often found in temperate regions of North America and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
