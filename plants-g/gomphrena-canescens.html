Gomphrena canescens

Gomphrena canescens, commonly known as the frostflower or woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, branched growth habit and its striking, papery bracts that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the showy, leaf-like bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gomphrena canescens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
