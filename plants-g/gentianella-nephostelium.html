Gentianella nephostelium

Gentianella nephostelium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella nephostelium is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
