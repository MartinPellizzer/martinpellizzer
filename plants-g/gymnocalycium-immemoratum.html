Gymnocalycium immemoratum

Gymnocalycium immemoratum, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its globular or cylindrical shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. Gymnocalycium immemoratum is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a striking addition to desert landscapes.
