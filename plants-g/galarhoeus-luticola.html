Galarhoeus luticola

Galarhoeus luticola, commonly known as the wetland spirea, is a flowering plant native to moist environments in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.
