Gossypium barbadense var. pedatum

Gossypium barbadense var. pedatum, commonly known as the Egyptian cotton or Pima cotton, is a variety of cotton native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the cotton fibers enclosed in a protective casing. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a significant amount of sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for textile production due to its superior fiber quality.
