Grindelia integrifolia var. macrophylla

Grindelia integrifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant tarplant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, silvery-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-green composite flower heads that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its unique appearance and ability to tolerate drought make it a distinctive member of the sunflower family.
