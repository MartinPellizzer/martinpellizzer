Grevillea metamorpha

Grevillea metamorpha, commonly known as the red-flowered Grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea metamorpha is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to drought and salt spray.
