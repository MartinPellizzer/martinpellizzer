Gamochaeta erecta

Gamochaeta erecta, commonly known as the upright false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.
