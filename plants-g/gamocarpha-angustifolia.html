Gamocarpha angustifolia

Gamocarpha angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf gamocarpha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of yellow flowers during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
