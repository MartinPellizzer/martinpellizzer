Gymnocalycium centeterium

Gymnocalycium centeterium, commonly known as the Potosi cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or gray spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. Gymnocalycium centeterium is a succulent, storing water in its thick stems to survive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
