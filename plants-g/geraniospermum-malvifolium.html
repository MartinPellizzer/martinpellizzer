Geraniospermum malvifolium

Geraniospermum malvifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Geraniospermum malvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
