Galactites tomentosus var. alata

Galactites tomentosus var. alata, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is typically latex-like, which can be toxic to some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
