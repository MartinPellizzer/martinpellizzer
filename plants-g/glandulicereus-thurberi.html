Glandulicereus thurberi

Glandulicereus thurberi, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Glandulicereus thurberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
