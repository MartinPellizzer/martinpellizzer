Gastrolobium tenue

Gastrolobium tenue, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Gastrolobium tenue is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in local landscaping.
