Guarea procera

Guarea procera, commonly known as the royal mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark-green foliage. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in furniture and construction.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.
