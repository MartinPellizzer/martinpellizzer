Grevillea elbertii

Grevillea elbertii, commonly known as the Elbert's waratah, is a striking shrub native to the coastal regions of New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea elbertii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
