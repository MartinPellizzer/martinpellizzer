Gossypium peruvianum var. brasiliense

Gossypium peruvianum var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fibrous seeds covered in fine, white hairs that produce the cotton fiber. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in soft, hairy glands.

This variety is valued for its fiber quality and has been historically cultivated for textile production in Brazil.
