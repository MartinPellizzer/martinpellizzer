Gustavia tetrapetala

Gustavia tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove swamps or along riverbanks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
