Geranium nepalense var. pallidum

Geranium nepalense var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture in its mountainous habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
