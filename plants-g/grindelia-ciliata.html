Grindelia ciliata

Grindelia ciliata, commonly known as the desert composite or tansy leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Grindelia ciliata has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils.
