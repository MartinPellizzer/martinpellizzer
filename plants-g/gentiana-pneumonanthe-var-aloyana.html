Gentiana pneumonanthe var. aloyana

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. aloyana, commonly known as the Aloyana gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
