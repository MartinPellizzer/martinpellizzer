Glycosmis pentaphylla var. citrifolia

Glycosmis pentaphylla var. citrifolia, commonly known as the citron tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange fruit that resembles a small citrus fruit. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its unique flavor and aromatic properties.
