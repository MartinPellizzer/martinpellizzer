Georgina superflua var. rubra

Georgina superflua var. rubra, commonly known as the crimson flaxflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that contrast sharply with its silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique coloration and adaptability have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
