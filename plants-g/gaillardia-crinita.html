Gaillardia crinita

Gaillardia crinita, commonly known as the curly top coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a showy, daisy-like bloom with a central disk and petal-like rays. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia crinita is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
