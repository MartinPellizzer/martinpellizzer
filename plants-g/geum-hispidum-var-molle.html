Geum hispidum var. molle

Geum hispidum var. molle, commonly known as soft-hairy avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with soft, hairy surfaces, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
