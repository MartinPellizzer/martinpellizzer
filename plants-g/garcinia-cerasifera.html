Garcinia cerasifera

Garcinia cerasifera, commonly known as the sour sop or guyabano, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, oblong fruits with a thick, spiny rind and a soft, creamy pulp containing numerous black seeds. The fruit is valued for its unique sour taste and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Garcinia cerasifera thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic value.
