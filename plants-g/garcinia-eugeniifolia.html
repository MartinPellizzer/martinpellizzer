Garcinia eugeniifolia

Garcinia eugeniifolia, commonly known as the white garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia eugeniifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
