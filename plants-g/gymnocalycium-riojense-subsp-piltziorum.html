Gymnocalycium riojense subsp. piltziorum

Gymnocalycium riojense subsp.

piltziorum, commonly known as the Piltziorum prickly pear, is a cacti species native to northern Argentina. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to gray spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
