Grossularia setosa

Grossularia setosa, commonly known as the alpine currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Grossularia setosa is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
