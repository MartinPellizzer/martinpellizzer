Gloxinia punctata

Gloxinia punctata, commonly known as the spotted gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with distinctive spots. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxinia punctata is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
