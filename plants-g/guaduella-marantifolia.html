Guaduella marantifolia

Guaduella marantifolia, commonly known as the red bamboo or red ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in striking red or orange bracts, which resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.
