Germainia khasyana

Germainia khasyana, commonly known as the Khasi geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Germainia khasyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
