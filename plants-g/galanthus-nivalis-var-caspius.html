Galanthus nivalis var. caspius

Galanthus nivalis var. caspius, commonly known as the Caspian snowdrop, is a variant of the snowdrop genus native to Central Asia.

It features narrow, white, bell-shaped flowers with a greenish tinge, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous rhizome. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming nature.
