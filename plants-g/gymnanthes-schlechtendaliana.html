Gymnanthes schlechtendaliana

Gymnanthes schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the red-fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and contain a single seed, making it valuable in both culinary and ornamental contexts.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
