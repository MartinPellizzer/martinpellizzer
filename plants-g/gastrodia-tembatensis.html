Gastrodia tembatensis

Gastrodia tembatensis, commonly known as the Tembat orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a mycoheterotrophic plant, meaning it lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on fungi for its nutrition. The plant produces unique, fleshy, red-brown pseudobulbs that store nutrients and water. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive, often fragrant appearance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Gastrodia tembatensis is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
