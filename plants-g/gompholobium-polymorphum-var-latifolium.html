Gompholobium polymorphum var. latifolium

Gompholobium polymorphum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved gompholobium, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
