Gypsophila huashanensis

Gypsophila huashanensis, commonly known as the Huashan gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Gypsophila huashanensis is also noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
