Gossypium hirsutum var. lanceolatum

Gossypium hirsutum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate cotton, is a variant of upland cotton characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to the warmer regions of the Americas and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces fluffy, white fibers encasing its seeds, which are harvested for textile production. The lanceolatum variety is notable for its distinct leaf morphology and adaptability to varying environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in agricultural and genetic research.
