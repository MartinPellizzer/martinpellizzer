Gentiana clausa

Gentiana clausa, commonly known as the closed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The closed gentian is known for its short flowering period and is often found in alpine environments.
