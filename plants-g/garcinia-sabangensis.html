Garcinia sabangensis

Garcinia sabangensis, commonly known as the Sabang Garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in phytochemical research. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
