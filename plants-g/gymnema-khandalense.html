Gymnema khandalense

Gymnema khandalense, commonly known as Khandala gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicular fruits. Gymnema khandalense is noted for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

Its leaves contain compounds that may have hypoglycemic effects, making it of interest in research for diabetes management.
