Graphistylis riopretensis

Graphistylis riopretensis, commonly known as the Rio Preta grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
