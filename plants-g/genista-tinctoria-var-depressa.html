Genista tinctoria var. depressa

Genista tinctoria var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
