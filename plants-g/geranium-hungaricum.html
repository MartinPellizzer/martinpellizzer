Geranium hungaricum

Geranium hungaricum, commonly known as Hungarian geranium, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
