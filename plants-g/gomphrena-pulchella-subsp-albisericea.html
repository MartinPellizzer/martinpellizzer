Gomphrena pulchella subsp. albisericea

Gomphrena pulchella subsp.

albisericea, commonly known as the white plume flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
