Gilia latiflora var. exilis

Gilia latiflora var. exilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with five lobes and a distinctive, spurred shape. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces, and it has a fibrous root system.

The narrowleaf gilia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
