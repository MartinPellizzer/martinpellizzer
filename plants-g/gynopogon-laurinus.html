Gynopogon laurinus

Gynopogon laurinus, commonly known as the laurel button.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the coastal areas of Georgia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its leaves are broad, oval, and have a glossy appearance, often used in ornamental horticulture.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland environments.
