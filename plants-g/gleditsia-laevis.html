Gleditsia laevis

Gleditsia laevis, commonly known as the honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain sweet, edible seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.

The plant is also valued for its fast growth and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
