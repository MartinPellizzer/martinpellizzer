Gomphrena flaccida var. alba

Gomphrena flaccida var. alba, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even when dried, hence its common name.
