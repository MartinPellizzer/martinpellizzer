Gymnopsis fruticosa

Gymnopsis fruticosa, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small yellow flowers, and leathery, evergreen leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements. Gymnopsis fruticosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in desert environments.
