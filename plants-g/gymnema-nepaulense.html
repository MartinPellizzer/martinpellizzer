Gymnema nepaulense

Gymnema nepaulense, commonly known as the Indian milk bush, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive milky sap, which is a key feature of the genus Gymnema. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gymnema nepaulense is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in Ayurvedic medicine.
