Gastrolobium heterophyllum

Gastrolobium heterophyllum, commonly known as the small-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Gastrolobium heterophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
