Gelidocalamus annulatus

Gelidocalamus annulatus, commonly known as the bamboo of the cold regions, is a perennial grass native to high-altitude areas of China.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often segmented and covered with a whitish, waxy layer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Gelidocalamus annulatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
