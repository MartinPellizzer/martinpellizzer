Gnaphalium maximum

Gnaphalium maximum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts. The plant typically forms a mat-like growth pattern, with slender, hairy stems and small, linear leaves. It is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Gnaphalium maximum is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
