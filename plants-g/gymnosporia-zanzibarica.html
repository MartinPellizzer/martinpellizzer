Gymnosporia zanzibarica

Gymnosporia zanzibarica, commonly known as Zanzibar thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native habitat, though it is also considered invasive in some areas.
