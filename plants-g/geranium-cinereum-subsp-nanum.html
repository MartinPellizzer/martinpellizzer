Geranium cinereum subsp. nanum

Geranium cinereum subsp.

nanum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
