Gentianella crinita

Gentianella crinita, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it its common name, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella crinita is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
