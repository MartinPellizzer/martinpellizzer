Gaillardia lanceolata var. fastigiata

Gaillardia lanceolata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blanketflower, is a striking perennial native to the central and southern United States.

It features upright, narrow leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it ideal for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its drought tolerance and ability to reseed itself make it a low-maintenance addition to gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing biodiversity in the landscape.
