Gladiolus libanoticus

Gladiolus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanon gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gladiolus libanoticus is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
