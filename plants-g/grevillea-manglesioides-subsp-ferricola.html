Grevillea manglesioides subsp. ferricola

Grevillea manglesioides subsp.

ferricola, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

It plays an important role in providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
