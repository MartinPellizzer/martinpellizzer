Geranium pilosum var. retrorsum

Geranium pilosum var. retrorsum, commonly known as the backward-hair geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine, backward-curving hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically rounded with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
