Goldfussia rex

Goldfussia rex, commonly known as the Rex begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with contrasting patterns of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in tropical gardens.
