Grewia henryi

Grewia henryi, commonly known as the Henry's globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its fruit is a dry, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
