Gouldia coriacea

Gouldia coriacea, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Gouldia coriacea is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to tropical landscapes.
