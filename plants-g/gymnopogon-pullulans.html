Gymnopogon pullulans

Gymnopogon pullulans, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and mostly glabrous, with a slightly rough texture.

Gymnopogon pullulans is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in areas with moderate to high moisture levels.
