Gagea chlorantha var. tenuifolia

Gagea chlorantha var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Amaryllidaceae family.
