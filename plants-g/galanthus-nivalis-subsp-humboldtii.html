Galanthus nivalis subsp. humboldtii

Galanthus nivalis subsp.

humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces delicate, white, drooping flowers with a yellow-green spatula at the base, typical of the snowdrop genus.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky soils. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh climates, and it blooms early in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
