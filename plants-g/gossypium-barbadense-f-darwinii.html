Gossypium barbadense f. darwinii

Gossypium barbadense f. darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's cotton, is a botanical variant of the upland cotton species.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued in textile production. This plant is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions, and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It exhibits a distinctive growth habit with a bushy structure and hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically pink or white, and the plant produces fluffy bolls that contain the cotton fibers.
