Gypsophila sojakii

Gypsophila sojakii, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gypsophila sojakii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
