Glycosmis lanceolata

Glycosmis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweetleaf, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Glycosmis lanceolata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
