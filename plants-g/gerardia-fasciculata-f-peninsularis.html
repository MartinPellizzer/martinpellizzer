Gerardia fasciculata f. peninsularis

Gerardia fasciculata f. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, rocky soils.
