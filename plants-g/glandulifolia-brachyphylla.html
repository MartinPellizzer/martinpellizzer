Glandulifolia brachyphylla

Glandulifolia brachyphylla, commonly known as the glandular-leafed bush, is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

Its leaves are small, often glandular-dotted, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
