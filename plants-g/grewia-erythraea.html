Grewia erythraea

Grewia erythraea, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that turn red in autumn, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
