Gentianella nummulariifolia

Gentianella nummulariifolia, commonly known as the pennywort gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features basal leaves that are round or heart-shaped, resembling coins, hence its common name. It produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
