Geranium sessiliflorum subsp. novae-zelandiae

Geranium sessiliflorum subsp.

novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand geranium, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
