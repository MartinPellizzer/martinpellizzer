Grewia nana

Grewia nana, commonly known as dwarf sugar bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
