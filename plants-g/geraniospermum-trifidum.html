Geraniospermum trifidum

Geraniospermum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its trifid (three-lobed) leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to hot, dry conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces capsules as fruits, containing numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

Geraniospermum trifidum is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
