Gnaphalium ramulosum

Gnaphalium ramulosum, commonly known as the clustered everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are often mistaken for individual flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its feathery foliage and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
