Gnaphalium carniolicum

Gnaphalium carniolicum, commonly known as the Alpine cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically produces yellowish to pale brown florets, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas.

Gnaphalium carniolicum is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
