Gerontogea corymbosa

Gerontogea corymbosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often blooming in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Gerontogea corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
