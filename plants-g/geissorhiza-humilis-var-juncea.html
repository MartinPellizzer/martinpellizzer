Geissorhiza humilis var. juncea

Geissorhiza humilis var. juncea, commonly known as the small-flowered geissorhiza, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
