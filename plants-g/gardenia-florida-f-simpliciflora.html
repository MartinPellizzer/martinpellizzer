Gardenia florida f. simpliciflora

Gardenia florida f. simpliciflora, commonly known as the simple-flowered gardenia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with five to seven petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance.
