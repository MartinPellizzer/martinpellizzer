Gladiolus luteus

Gladiolus luteus, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from yellow to orange, with some varieties displaying intricate patterns. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
