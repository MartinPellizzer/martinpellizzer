Genianthus siamicus

Genianthus siamicus, commonly known as the Thai hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional floral arrangements due to their beauty and fragrance.
