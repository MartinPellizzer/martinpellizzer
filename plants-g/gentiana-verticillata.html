Gentiana verticillata

Gentiana verticillata, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a whorl, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
