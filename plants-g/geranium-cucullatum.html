Geranium cucullatum

Geranium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooded calyx that encloses the flower, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium cucullatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
