Guaiacum coulteri

Guaiacum coulteri, commonly known as the Mexican gumbo-limbo, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the family Zygophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a resilient species in diverse ecological settings.

The wood of Guaiacum coulteri is historically valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and craftsmanship.
