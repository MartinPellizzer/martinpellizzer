Guzmania confinis

Guzmania confinis, commonly known as the silver stripe bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure that holds water, providing a habitat for small aquatic organisms. The plant features vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest to its foliage. Its leaves are usually broad and covered in a waxy coating, which helps in water retention and protection against environmental stress.

Guzmania confinis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush appearance.
