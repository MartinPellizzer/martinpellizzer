Garcinia andamanica

Garcinia andamanica, commonly known as Andamanian garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, fleshy pulp. Garcinia andamanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Its habitat is primarily restricted to coastal areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
