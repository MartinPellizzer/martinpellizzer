Gongora minax

Gongora minax, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pale, ghostly appearance, with elongated, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower that often resembles a ghostly face. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
