Gnaphalium limicola

Gnaphalium limicola, commonly known as saltmarsh cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal areas.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in salt marshes and dunes. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy or muddy soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
