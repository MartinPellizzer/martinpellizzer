Galactia glaucescens

Galactia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruit and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its seed pods are cylindrical and dehiscent, releasing seeds upon maturity.

Galactia glaucescens is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
