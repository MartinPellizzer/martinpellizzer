Galinsoga discolor

Galinsoga discolor, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in gardens. It has a sprawling growth habit and can produce numerous seeds, making it an aggressive weed.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a taproot system that aids in its rapid colonization of new areas.
