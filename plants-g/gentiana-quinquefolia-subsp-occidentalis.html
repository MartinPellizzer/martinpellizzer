Gentiana quinquefolia subsp. occidentalis

Gentiana quinquefolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
