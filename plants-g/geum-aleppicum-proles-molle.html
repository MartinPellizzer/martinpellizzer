Geum aleppicum proles molle

Geum aleppicum proles molle, commonly known as the soft-stemmed avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Geum aleppicum proles molle is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
