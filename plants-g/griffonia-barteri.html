Griffonia barteri

Griffonia barteri, commonly known as the African ivory nut tree, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of the protein-rich seed flour. Griffonia barteri is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
