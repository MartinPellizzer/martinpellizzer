Gonzalezia hypargyrea

Gonzalezia hypargyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf gonzalezia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are leathery and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

Gonzalezia hypargyrea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
