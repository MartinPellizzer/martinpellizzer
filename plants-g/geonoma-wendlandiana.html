Geonoma wendlandiana

Geonoma wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, grass-like flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is typically narrow and grassy, with a bluish-green coloration.
