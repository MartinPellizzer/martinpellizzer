Gomidesia crocea

Gomidesia crocea, commonly known as the pink stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems and leaves contain irritant chemicals that cause a stinging sensation upon contact.

Gomidesia crocea is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
