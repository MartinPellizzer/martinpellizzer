Glycine leucosperma

Glycine leucosperma, commonly known as the white bean vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Glycine leucosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
