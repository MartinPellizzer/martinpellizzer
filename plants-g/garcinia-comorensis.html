Garcinia comorensis

Garcinia comorensis, commonly known as the Madagascar garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia comorensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its fruit is also popular in the food industry as a natural sweetener and flavoring agent.
