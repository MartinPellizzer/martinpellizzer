Garcinia matsudae

Garcinia matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

Garcinia matsudae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
