Gentiana squarrosa

Gentiana squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana squarrosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
