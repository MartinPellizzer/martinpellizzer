Geranium lanuginosum

Geranium lanuginosum, commonly known as the downy geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Geranium lanuginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
