Gentiana melvillei

Gentiana melvillei, commonly known as Melville's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention.

Gentiana melvillei is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
