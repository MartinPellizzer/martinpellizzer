Gentiana stellariifolia

Gentiana stellariifolia, commonly known as starleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer and are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the plant. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive star-like pattern of veins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
