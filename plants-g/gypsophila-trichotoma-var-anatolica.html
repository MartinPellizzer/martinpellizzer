Gypsophila trichotoma var. anatolica

Gypsophila trichotoma var. anatolica, commonly known as the Turkish baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to regions of Turkey and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage is usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
