Gentiana centaurium subsp. pumilum

Gentiana centaurium subsp.

pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies pumilum is distinguished by its smaller size compared to the typical Gentiana centaurium. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
