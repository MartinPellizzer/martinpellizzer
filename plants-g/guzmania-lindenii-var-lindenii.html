Guzmania lindenii var. lindenii

Guzmania lindenii var. lindenii, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant red bracts that surround its small, white flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
