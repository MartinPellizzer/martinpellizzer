Grewia tenax

Grewia tenax, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Grewia tenax is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
