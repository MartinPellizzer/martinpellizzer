Grevillea oleoides

Grevillea oleoides, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to spring. It is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.

Grevillea oleoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
