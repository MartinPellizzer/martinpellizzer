Globba leucantha var. bicolor

Globba leucantha var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, upright rhizomes and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
