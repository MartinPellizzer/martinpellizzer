Gaillonia jolana

Gaillonia jolana, commonly known as the Jolana hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Gaillonia jolana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
