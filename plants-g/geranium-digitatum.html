Geranium digitatum

Geranium digitatum, commonly known as the finger geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
