Grewia grisea

Grewia grisea, commonly known as the grey gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically covered in a whitish bloom. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
