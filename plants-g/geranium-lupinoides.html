Geranium lupinoides

Geranium lupinoides, commonly known as the purple geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces vibrant purple or pink blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium lupinoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
