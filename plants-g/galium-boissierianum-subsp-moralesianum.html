Galium boissierianum subsp. moralesianum

Galium boissierianum subsp.

moralesianum, commonly known as the Moralesian bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
