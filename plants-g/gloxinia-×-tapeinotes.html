Gloxinia × tapeinotes

Gloxinia × tapeinotes, commonly known as the hybrid gloxinia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Gloxinia × gloxiniifolia and Gloxinia × tapeinota.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to South America. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with thick, fleshy leaves and a short, fibrous root system.

Gloxinia × tapeinotes prefers warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
