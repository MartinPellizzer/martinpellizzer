Gardenia acutifolia

Gardenia acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia acutifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
