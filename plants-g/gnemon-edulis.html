Gnemon edulis

Gnemon edulis, commonly known as the edible fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the familiar fig fruits. The fruit is typically enclosed within a fleshy, hollow structure called a syconium, which contains numerous tiny seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its sweet, edible fruits and is an important crop in many tropical regions.
