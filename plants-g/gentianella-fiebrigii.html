Gentianella fiebrigii

Gentianella fiebrigii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentianella fiebrigii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
