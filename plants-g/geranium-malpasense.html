Geranium malpasense

Geranium malpasense, commonly known as the Malpas geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens across the southern hemisphere.
