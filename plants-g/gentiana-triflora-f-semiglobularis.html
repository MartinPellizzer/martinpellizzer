Gentiana triflora f. semiglobularis

Gentiana triflora f. semiglobularis, commonly known as the globe gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, and its stems are often short and sturdy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
