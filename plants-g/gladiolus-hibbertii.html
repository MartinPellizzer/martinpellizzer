Gladiolus hibbertii

Gladiolus hibbertii, commonly known as the Hibbert's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive central stripe. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Hibbert's gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
