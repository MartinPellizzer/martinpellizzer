Gentianella chathamica subsp. nemorosa

Gentianella chathamica subsp.

nemorosa, commonly known as the Chatham Islands gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand. It is a perennial herb with slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is notable for its ecological significance in the unique flora of the Chatham Islands.
