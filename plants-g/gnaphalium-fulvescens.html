Gnaphalium fulvescens

Gnaphalium fulvescens, commonly known as the golden feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and sandy soils. Its yellowish flowers are produced in clusters and are often visited by insects like bees and flies.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
