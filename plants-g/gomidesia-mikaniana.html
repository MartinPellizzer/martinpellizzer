Gomidesia mikaniana

Gomidesia mikaniana, commonly known as the Mexican citrus tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-like fruit, which is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
