Galium verum subsp. euverum

Galium verum subsp.

euverum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
