Gnetum nigrum

Gnetum nigrum, commonly known as black gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, characterized by its unique combination of gymnosperm and angiosperm traits.

It is a tropical evergreen tree or shrub native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually entire leaves, and its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed capsule, and the plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin.

Gnetum nigrum is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
