Gladiolus paui

Gladiolus paui, commonly known as the Pau's gladiolus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
