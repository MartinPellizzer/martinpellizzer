Gymnocalycium castellanosii

Gymnocalycium castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Gymnocalycium castellanosii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
