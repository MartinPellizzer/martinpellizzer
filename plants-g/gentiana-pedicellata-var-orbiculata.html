Gentiana pedicellata var. orbiculata

Gentiana pedicellata var. orbiculata, commonly known as the orbiculate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine downy layer.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
