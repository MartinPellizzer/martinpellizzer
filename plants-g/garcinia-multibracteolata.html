Garcinia multibracteolata

Garcinia multibracteolata, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated pods containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
