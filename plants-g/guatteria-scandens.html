Guatteria scandens

Guatteria scandens, commonly known as the climbing guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
