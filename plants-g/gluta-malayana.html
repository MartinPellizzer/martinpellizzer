Gluta malayana

Gluta malayana, commonly known as the Malay gluta, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The bark and leaves of Gluta malayana have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
