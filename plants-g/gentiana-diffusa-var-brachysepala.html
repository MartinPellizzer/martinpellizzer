Gentiana diffusa var. brachysepala

Gentiana diffusa var. brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
