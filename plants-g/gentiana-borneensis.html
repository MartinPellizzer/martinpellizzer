Gentiana borneensis

Gentiana borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in summer and are known for their striking coloration and nectar-rich structure, attracting various pollinators.
