Gilia glandulosa

Gilia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
