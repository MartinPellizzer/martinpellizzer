Grossularia uva-crispa subsp. reclinata

Grossularia uva-crispa subsp.

reclinata, commonly known as the hanging gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayas. It produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically pendulous, hanging from the plant.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to alpine gardens.
