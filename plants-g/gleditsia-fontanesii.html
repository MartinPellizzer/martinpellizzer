Gleditsia fontanesii

Gleditsia fontanesii, commonly known as the honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the United States.

It features compound leaves with small, paired leaflets that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
