Galinsoga parviflora

Galinsoga parviflora, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and gardens. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers typically bloom from summer to fall. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Galinsoga parviflora is also sometimes referred to as "cheesehead" due to the shape of its seed pods.
