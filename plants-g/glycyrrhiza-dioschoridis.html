Glycyrrhiza dioschoridis

Glycyrrhiza dioschoridis, commonly known as the black licorice plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their intense sweetness and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza dioschoridis is valued in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
