Grammatophyllum fenzlianum

Grammatophyllum fenzlianum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its inflorescence can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, bearing numerous fragrant, violet-colored flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
