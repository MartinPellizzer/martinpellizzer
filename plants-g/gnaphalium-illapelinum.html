Gnaphalium illapelinum

Gnaphalium illapelinum, commonly known as the New Zealand fleabane, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom from late winter to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
