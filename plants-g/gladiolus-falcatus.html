Gladiolus falcatus

Gladiolus falcatus, commonly known as the swordleaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive black or purple stripe down the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in various climates.
