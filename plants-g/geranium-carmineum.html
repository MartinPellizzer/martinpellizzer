Geranium carmineum

Geranium carmineum, commonly known as the crimson geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers range in color from pink to deep red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium carmineum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
