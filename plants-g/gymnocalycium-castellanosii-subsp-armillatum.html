Gymnocalycium castellanosii subsp. armillatum

Gymnocalycium castellanosii subsp.

armillatum, commonly known as the Cholla Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the stem tips. Its stems are segmented and can branch out, forming a shrub-like structure. Gymnocalycium castellanosii subsp.

armillatum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
