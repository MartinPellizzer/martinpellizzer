Gaultheria caespitosa

Gaultheria caespitosa, commonly known as the evergreen trailing azalea, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Gaultheria caespitosa is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
