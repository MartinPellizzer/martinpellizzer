Gentiana cardiophylla

Gentiana cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Gentiana cardiophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
