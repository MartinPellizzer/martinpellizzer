Gilia latiflora subsp. cuyamensis

Gilia latiflora subsp.

cuyamensis, commonly known as the Cuyamaca gilia, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its showy, typically pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
