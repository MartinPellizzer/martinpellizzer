Glyceria pendulina

Glyceria pendulina, commonly known as the hanging glyceria, is a species of grass found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Glyceria pendulina plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
