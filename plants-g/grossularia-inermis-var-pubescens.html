Grossularia inermis var. pubescens

Grossularia inermis var. pubescens, commonly known as the downy gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cooler climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
