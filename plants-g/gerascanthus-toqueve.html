Gerascanthus toqueve

Gerascanthus toqueve, commonly known as the golden spire, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gerascanthus toqueve is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
