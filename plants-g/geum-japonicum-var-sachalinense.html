Geum japonicum var. sachalinense

Geum japonicum var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sachalin meadow buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of eastern Russia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are bright yellow with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. It produces a showy display of flowers from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
