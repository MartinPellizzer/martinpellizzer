Grewia asiatica var. celtidifolia

Grewia asiatica var. celtidifolia, commonly known as the Indian wolfberry, is a shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red, fleshy fruits that resemble small tomatoes. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is edible and used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
