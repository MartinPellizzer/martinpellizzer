Gaertnera pedunculata

Gaertnera pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Gaertnera pedunculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
