Glochidion ovalifolium

Glochidion ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
