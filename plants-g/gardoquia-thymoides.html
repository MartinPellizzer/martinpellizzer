Gardoquia thymoides

Gardoquia thymoides, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, small leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinct thyme-like fragrance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
