Gastrochilus rostellatus

Gastrochilus rostellatus, commonly known as the rostellate orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth.

Gastrochilus rostellatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
