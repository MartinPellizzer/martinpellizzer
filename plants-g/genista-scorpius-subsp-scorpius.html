Genista scorpius subsp. scorpius

Genista scorpius subsp.

scorpius, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
