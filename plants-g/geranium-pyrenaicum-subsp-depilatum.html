Geranium pyrenaicum subsp. depilatum

Geranium pyrenaicum subsp.

depilatum, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features upright stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
