Graphorkis calographis

Graphorkis calographis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and seasonal color changes make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
