Gentianodes venusta

Gentianodes venusta, commonly known as the showy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped blooms. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The showy gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
