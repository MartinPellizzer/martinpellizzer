Glycine botrydium

Glycine botrydium, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. Glycine botrydium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms and aromatic foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden borders.
