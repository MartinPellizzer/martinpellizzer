Gentiana delicatula

Gentiana delicatula, commonly known as the delicate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
