Guajava oblongata

Guajava oblongata, commonly known as the long-fruit guanabana, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a heavy, inedible fruit that is often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a smooth, woody stem and a complex, compound leaf structure.

The fruit contains a milky latex that has been used for its potential medicinal properties.
