Gynandropsis cleomoides

Gynandropsis cleomoides, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface, and produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, round seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
