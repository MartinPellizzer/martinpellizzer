Gentiana lateriflora var. lateriflora

Gentiana lateriflora var. lateriflora, commonly known as the western gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
