Gagea podolica

Gagea podolica, commonly known as the Podolic Scilla, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, round, blue-green berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gagea podolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
