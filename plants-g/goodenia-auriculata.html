Goodenia auriculata

Goodenia auriculata, commonly known as the silverleaf goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Goodenia auriculata is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular in native gardens.
