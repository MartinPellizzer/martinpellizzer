Gilia capitata subsp. chamissonis

Gilia capitata subsp.

chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

It is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
