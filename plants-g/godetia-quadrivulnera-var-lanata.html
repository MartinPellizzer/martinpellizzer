Godetia quadrivulnera var. lanata

Godetia quadrivulnera var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly godetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or lanate, foliage which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique texture and vibrant floral display.
