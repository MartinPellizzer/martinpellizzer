Gardenia obtusifolia

Gardenia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, broadly ovate leaves and its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their strong, sweet scent.

Gardenia obtusifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
