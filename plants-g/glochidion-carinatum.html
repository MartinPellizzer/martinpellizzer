Glochidion carinatum

Glochidion carinatum, commonly known as the ridged holly.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its distinctive ridged leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Glochidion carinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
