Gentianella bellidifolia var. divisa

Gentianella bellidifolia var. divisa, commonly known as the divided gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct divided appearance, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
