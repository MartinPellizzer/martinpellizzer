Glycine hispida var. nigra-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. nigra-lutescens, commonly known as the black and yellow cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.

The varietal name "nigra-lutescens" refers to its dark (nigra) and yellow (lutescens) coloration patterns.
