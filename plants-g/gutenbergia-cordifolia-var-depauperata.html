Gutenbergia cordifolia var. depauperata

Gutenbergia cordifolia var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender-heartleaf gutenbergia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many regions.
