Gaura angustifolia

Gaura angustifolia, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, delicate, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Gaura angustifolia is known for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
