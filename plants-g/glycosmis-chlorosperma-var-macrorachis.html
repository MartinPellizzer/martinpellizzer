Glycosmis chlorosperma var. macrorachis

Glycosmis chlorosperma var. macrorachis, commonly known as the broad-rachis sweetglove, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rutaceae family.
