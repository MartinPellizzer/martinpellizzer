Gladiolus × brenchleyensis

Gladiolus × brenchleyensis, commonly known as the Brenchley gladiolus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between *Gladiolus italicus* and *Gladiolus gandavensis*.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is native to South Africa. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
