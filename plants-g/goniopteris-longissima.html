Goniopteris longissima

Goniopteris longissima, commonly known as the giant barren fern, is a large, ancient fern species that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, segmented structure with a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically compound, with a feathery appearance, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in maintaining forest floor ecosystems.
