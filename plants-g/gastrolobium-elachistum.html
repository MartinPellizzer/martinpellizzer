Gastrolobium elachistum

Gastrolobium elachistum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Gastrolobium elachistum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
