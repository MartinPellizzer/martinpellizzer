Gilia schottii

Gilia schottii, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to drought conditions.

Gilia schottii plays an important role in pollination and provides nectar for various pollinators in its ecosystem.
