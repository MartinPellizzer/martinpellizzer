Gerascanthus chaetodonta

Gerascanthus chaetodonta, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Gerascanthus chaetodonta thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
