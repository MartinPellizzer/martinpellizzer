Gentiana crassulifolia var. selaginifolia

Gentiana crassulifolia var. selaginifolia, commonly known as the Selaginifolia gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which are typically arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
