Glechoma biloba

Glechoma biloba, commonly known as ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to spread rapidly through stolons. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along garden paths.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
