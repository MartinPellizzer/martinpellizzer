Genista cuspidata

Genista cuspidata, commonly known as the needle bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, evergreen leaves that are typically needle-like in shape. Its yellow flowers appear in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
