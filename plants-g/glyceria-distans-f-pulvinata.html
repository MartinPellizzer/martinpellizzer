Glyceria distans f. pulvinata

Glyceria distans f. pulvinata, commonly known as the pulvinate glyceria, is a variant of the common glyceria species.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia and Australia. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly swollen at the base, a characteristic known as a pulvinule. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist soil conditions.
