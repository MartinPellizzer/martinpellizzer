Glycine helvola

Glycine helvola, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth. Glycine helvola is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it an important crop for soil enrichment.
