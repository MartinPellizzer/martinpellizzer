Gomphrena magentitepala

Gomphrena magentitepala, commonly known as the purple torch flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Gomphrena magentitepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
