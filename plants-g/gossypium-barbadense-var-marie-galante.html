Gossypium barbadense var. marie-galante

Gossypium barbadense var. marie-galante, commonly known as the Marie-Galante cotton, is a variety of upland cotton native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, and it produces fluffy bolls containing the cotton fibers.

This variety is well adapted to tropical climates and is often cultivated for its high-quality textile fibers.
