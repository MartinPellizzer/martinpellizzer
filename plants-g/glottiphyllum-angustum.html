Glottiphyllum angustum

Glottiphyllum angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glottiphyllum, is a member of the Mesembryanthemaceae family.

It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, particularly in the Western Cape region. The plant features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
