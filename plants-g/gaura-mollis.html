Gaura mollis

Gaura mollis, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blooms that are typically arranged in whorls along the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Gaura mollis is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
