Gladiolus pappei

Gladiolus pappei, commonly known as the Pappei gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gladiolus pappei is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
