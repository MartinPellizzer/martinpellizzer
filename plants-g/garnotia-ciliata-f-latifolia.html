Garnotia ciliata f. latifolia

Garnotia ciliata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf garnotia, is a cultivar of the Garnotia ciliata species, which is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
