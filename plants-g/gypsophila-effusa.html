Gypsophila effusa

Gypsophila effusa, commonly known as the sprig gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gypsophila effusa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and as a cut flower.
