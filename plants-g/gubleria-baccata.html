Gubleria baccata

Gubleria baccata, commonly known as the red-fruited bush, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Gubleria baccata thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
