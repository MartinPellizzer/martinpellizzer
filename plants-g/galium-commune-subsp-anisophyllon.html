Galium commune subsp. anisophyllon

Galium commune subsp.

anisophyllon, commonly known as the variable wood-sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
