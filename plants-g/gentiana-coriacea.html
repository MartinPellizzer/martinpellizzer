Gentiana coriacea

Gentiana coriacea, commonly known as the rough gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, giving it its common name.

Gentiana coriacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
