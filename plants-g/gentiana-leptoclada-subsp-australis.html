Gentiana leptoclada subsp. australis

Gentiana leptoclada subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
