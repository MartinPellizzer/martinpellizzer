Glyceria pauciflora

Glyceria pauciflora, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, streambanks, and floodplains. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense stands, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, two-lobed flowers.

Glyceria pauciflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
