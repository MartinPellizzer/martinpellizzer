Geranium caeruleum var. ushkanense

Geranium caeruleum var. ushkanense, commonly known as the Ushkans Island geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Ushkans Islands in the Barents Sea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal tundra environments.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local insect populations and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
