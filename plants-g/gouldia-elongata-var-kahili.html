Gouldia elongata var. kahili

Gouldia elongata var. kahili, commonly known as the Kahili torch spike, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It features bright red or orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant typically grows in dense, compact clusters, with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This species is known for its ornamental value and ability to withstand salt spray, making it suitable for seaside landscapes.
