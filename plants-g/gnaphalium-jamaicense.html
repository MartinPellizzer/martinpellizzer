Gnaphalium jamaicense

Gnaphalium jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
