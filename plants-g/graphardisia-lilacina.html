Graphardisia lilacina

Graphardisia lilacina, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or lilac-colored petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
