Gerascanthus ecalyculatus

Gerascanthus ecalyculatus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
