Galium pumilum var. vestrogothicum

Galium pumilum var. vestrogothicum, commonly known as the dwarf woodcrushers, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
