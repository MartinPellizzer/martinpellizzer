Genista lobelii

Genista lobelii, commonly known as the Lobel's broom, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The shrub has slender, branching stems and small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments.
