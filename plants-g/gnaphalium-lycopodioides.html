Gnaphalium lycopodioides

Gnaphalium lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss-like everlasting flower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces tiny, thread-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
