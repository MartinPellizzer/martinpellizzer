Glaucium elegans var. bornmuelleri

Glaucium elegans var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Eastern Blue Poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a bluish waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
