Glochidion cuspidatum

Glochidion cuspidatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
