Goodenia scaevolina

Goodenia scaevolina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Goodenia scaevolina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
