Gesneria berteroana

Gesneria berteroana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed cycad, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palm-like appearance with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria berteroana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
