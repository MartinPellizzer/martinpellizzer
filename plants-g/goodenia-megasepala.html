Goodenia megasepala

Goodenia megasepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lance-shaped, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Goodenia megasepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
