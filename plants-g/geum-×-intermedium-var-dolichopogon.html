Geum × intermedium var. dolichopogon

Geum × intermedium var. dolichopogon, commonly known as the long-stemmed avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
