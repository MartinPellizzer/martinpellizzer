Gurania sylvatica

Gurania sylvatica, commonly known as the European wood gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring rich, loamy soils. The plant features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gurania sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
