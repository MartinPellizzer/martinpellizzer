Garuga pinnata var. pierrei

Garuga pinnata var. pierrei, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, giving it its common name. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin, and they turn yellow in autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
