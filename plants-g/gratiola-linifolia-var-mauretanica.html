Gratiola linifolia var. mauretanica

Gratiola linifolia var. mauretanica, commonly known as Mauretanian leaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which contribute to soil fertility.
