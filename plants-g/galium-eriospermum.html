Galium eriospermum

Galium eriospermum, commonly known as sticky bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered with fine, sticky hairs.

Galium eriospermum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
