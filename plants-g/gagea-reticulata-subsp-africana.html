Gagea reticulata subsp. africana

Gagea reticulata subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African netted squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant is characterized by its reticulate (net-like) patterns on the bulb surface, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Gagea reticulata subsp.

africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
