Gaillardia flava

Gaillardia flava, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange and yellow petals that resemble a blanket, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its vibrant, showy blooms. Gaillardia flava thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
