Grangea mucronata

Grangea mucronata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, pentagonal flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant is evergreen and thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, velvety leaves that help retain moisture.

Grangea mucronata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
