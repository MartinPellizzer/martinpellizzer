Garacium alpinum

Garacium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine garland, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Garacium alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
