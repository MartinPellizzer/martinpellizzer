Galega dubia

Galega dubia, commonly known as the wild licorice plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with small, sticky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a distinct licorice-like aroma.

Galega dubia is known for its deep taproot and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
