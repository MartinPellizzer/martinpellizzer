Geranium ludovicianum

Geranium ludovicianum, commonly known as the scarlet geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red and pink. The leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly toothed margin, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
