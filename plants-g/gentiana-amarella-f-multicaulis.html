Gentiana amarella f. multicaulis

Gentiana amarella f. multicaulis, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
