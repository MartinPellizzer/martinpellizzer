Geum virginianum var. murrayanum

Geum virginianum var. murrayanum, commonly known as the Murray's garden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
