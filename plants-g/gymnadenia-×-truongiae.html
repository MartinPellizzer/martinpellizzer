Gymnadenia × truongiae

Gymnadenia × truongiae, commonly known as the Truong's gentian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its floral structure includes a single, spurred lip and showy petals, which are typical of many orchid species.

Gymnadenia × truongiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
