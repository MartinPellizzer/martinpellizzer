Gossypium latifolium subvar. chacoense

Gossypium latifolium subvar. chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco cotton, is a species native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its broad, woolly leaves and produces small, pale pink flowers. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its cotton-like fibers are harvested for textile use, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.

The subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its unique adaptations and potential for sustainable fiber production.
