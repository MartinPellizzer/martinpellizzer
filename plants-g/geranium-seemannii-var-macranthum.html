Geranium seemannii var. macranthum

Geranium seemannii var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
