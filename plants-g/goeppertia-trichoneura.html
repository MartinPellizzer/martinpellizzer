Goeppertia trichoneura

Goeppertia trichoneura, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a striking member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with elongated, white or pale green leaves that have a translucent, waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters at the base of the plant. Goeppertia trichoneura thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
