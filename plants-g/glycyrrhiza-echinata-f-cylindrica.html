Glycyrrhiza echinata f. cylindrica

Glycyrrhiza echinata f. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical licorice, is a variant of the licorice plant native to China.

It is characterized by its cylindrical-shaped roots, which are thicker and more elongated compared to the standard licorice plant. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its high concentration of glycyrrhizin. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and expectorant effects.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate.
