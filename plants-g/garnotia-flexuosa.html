Garnotia flexuosa

Garnotia flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping carnation, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that often lie prostrate, forming a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Garnotia flexuosa is valued in gardens for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
