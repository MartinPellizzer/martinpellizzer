Glyceria arctica var. capillaris

Glyceria arctica var. capillaris, commonly known as Arctic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and grows in dense, low mats. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, moist environments such as tundra and wet meadows. Its reproductive structures include small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-dispersed seeds.

Glyceria arctica var. capillaris plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its harsh habitat.
