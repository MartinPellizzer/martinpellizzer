Grindelia subincisa

Grindelia subincisa, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.

Grindelia subincisa is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
