Gale palustris var. lusitanica

Gale palustris var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and damp meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is well adapted to waterlogged conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its presence is often an indicator of undisturbed, hydric environments.
