Gynura polycephala

Gynura polycephala, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns," is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
