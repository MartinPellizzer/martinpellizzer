Geranium sanguineum var. erectum

Geranium sanguineum var. erectum, commonly known as the upright blood geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
