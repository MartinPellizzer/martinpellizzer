Guajava

Guajava, commonly known as the Java apple, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellow or red skin and a sweet, acidic pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The guajava tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in home gardens.
