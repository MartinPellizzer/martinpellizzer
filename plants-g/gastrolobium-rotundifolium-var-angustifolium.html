Gastrolobium rotundifolium var. angustifolium

Gastrolobium rotundifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive rounded base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This plant is adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of local ecosystems.
