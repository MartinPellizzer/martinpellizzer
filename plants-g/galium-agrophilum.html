Galium agrophilum

Galium agrophilum, commonly known as field bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium agrophilum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
