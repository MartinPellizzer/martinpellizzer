Gahnia filum

Gahnia filum, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and swampy areas. Its slender, cylindrical stems are covered in fine, wiry leaves that form a compact tussock.

Gahnia filum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
