Gentiana ramosa

Gentiana ramosa, commonly known as the branched gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Gentiana ramosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
