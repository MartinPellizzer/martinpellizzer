Gentiana aquatica var. baltistanica

Gentiana aquatica var. baltistanica, commonly known as the Baltistan water gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to cold and high-altitude environments.
