Grevillea lawrenceana

Grevillea lawrenceana, commonly known as the pink-veined grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves with distinctive pink veins, and it produces striking clusters of bright red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Grevillea lawrenceana is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds.
