Galium boreale var. vulgare

Galium boreale var. vulgare, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows, bogs, and tundra. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
