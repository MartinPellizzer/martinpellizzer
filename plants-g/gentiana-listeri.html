Gentiana listeri

Gentiana listeri, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana listeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
