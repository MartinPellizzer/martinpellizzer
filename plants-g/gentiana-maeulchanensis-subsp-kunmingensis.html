Gentiana maeulchanensis subsp. kunmingensis

Gentiana maeulchanensis subsp.

kunmingensis, commonly known as the Kunming gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
