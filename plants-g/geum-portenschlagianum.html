Geum portenschlagianum

Geum portenschlagianum, commonly known as the Portenschlag's avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
