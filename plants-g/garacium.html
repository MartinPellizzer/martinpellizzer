Garacium

Garacium, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are sweet and often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
