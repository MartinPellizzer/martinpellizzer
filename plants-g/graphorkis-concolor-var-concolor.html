Graphorkis concolor var. concolor

Graphorkis concolor var. concolor, commonly known as the broad-leaved graphorkis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically glabrous and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
