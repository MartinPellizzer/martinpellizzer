Gomphrena celosioides f. suberecta

Gomphrena celosioides f. suberecta, commonly known as the upright gomphrena, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features dense, cylindrical flower heads that range in color from pink to red, resembling small, rounded blooms. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which extends from summer into early autumn.
