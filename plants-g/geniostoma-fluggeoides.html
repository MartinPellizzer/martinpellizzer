Geniostoma fluggeoides

Geniostoma fluggeoides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Geniostoma fluggeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
