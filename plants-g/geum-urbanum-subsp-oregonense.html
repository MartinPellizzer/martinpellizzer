Geum urbanum subsp. oregonense

Geum urbanum subsp.

oregonense, commonly known as Oregon mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a low-growing perennial with distinctive palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, early spring blooms.
