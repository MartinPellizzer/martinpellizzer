Galium coloradoense var. scabriusculum

Galium coloradoense var. scabriusculum, commonly known as Colorado wood-sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has hairy, often scabrous stems and leaves, which are characteristic of the varietal form scabriusculum. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical structure reflects its classification within the Rubiaceae family, sharing traits with other members of the genus Galium.
