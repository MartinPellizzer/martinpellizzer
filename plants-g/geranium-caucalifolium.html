Geranium caucalifolium

Geranium caucalifolium, commonly known as the Caucasian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinct, sometimes hairy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
