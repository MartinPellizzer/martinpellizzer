Graphorkis aloifolia

Graphorkis aloifolia, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped, grey-green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences of pink or purple flowers in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
