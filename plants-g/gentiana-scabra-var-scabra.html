Gentiana scabra var. scabra

Gentiana scabra var. scabra, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects.
