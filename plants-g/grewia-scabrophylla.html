Grewia scabrophylla

Grewia scabrophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf gumwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, scaly bark and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Grewia scabrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
