Galium nanhumontanum

Galium nanhumontanum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium nanhumontanum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
