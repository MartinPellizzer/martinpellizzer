Gnaphalium novae-zeelandiae

Gnaphalium novae-zeelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Gnaphalium novae-zeelandiae is known for its ability to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
