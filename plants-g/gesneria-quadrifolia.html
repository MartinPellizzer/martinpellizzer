Gesneria quadrifolia

Gesneria quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive silver or white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria quadrifolia is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
