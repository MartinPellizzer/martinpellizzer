Gardenia jasminoides var. longisepala

Gardenia jasminoides var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers with elongated sepals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its elegant appearance.

The long sepals distinguish this variety from other gardenia species, making it a unique and sought-after plant.
