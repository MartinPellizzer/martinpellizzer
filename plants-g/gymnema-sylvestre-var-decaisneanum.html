Gymnema sylvestre var. decaisneanum

Gymnema sylvestre var. decaisneanum, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon or the sugar plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to inhibit sweet taste perception, a property attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. Its fruits, which are typically bitter and seedy, are used in traditional medicine for their purported hypoglycemic effects.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
