Grevillea carduifolia

Grevillea carduifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds, particularly honeyeaters, contributing to local biodiversity.

Grevillea carduifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscaping.
