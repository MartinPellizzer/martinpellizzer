Galactophora magnifica

Galactophora magnifica, commonly known as the celestial milkweed, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a faint bluish tinge. The plant produces a milky sap that is both toxic and chemically complex, containing various alkaloids. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Galactophora magnifica plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for specific pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
