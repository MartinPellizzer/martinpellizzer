Goniopteris ferax

Goniopteris ferax, commonly known as the giant barren fern, is a large, tree-like fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its massive, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually sterile, meaning they do not produce spores, which is why it is referred to as a "barren" fern.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitats.
