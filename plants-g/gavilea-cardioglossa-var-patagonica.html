Gavilea cardioglossa var. patagonica

Gavilea cardioglossa var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, silver-green leaves with a waxy coating, adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting drought tolerance.
