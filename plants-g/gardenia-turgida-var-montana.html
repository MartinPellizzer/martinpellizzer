Gardenia turgida var. montana

Gardenia turgida var. montana, commonly known as the mountain gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a yellow center. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
