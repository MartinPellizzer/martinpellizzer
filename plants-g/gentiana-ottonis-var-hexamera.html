Gentiana ottonis var. hexamera

Gentiana ottonis var. hexamera, commonly known as the hexamerous gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The species belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its showy, five-petaled flowers, though the variant hexamera has six petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
