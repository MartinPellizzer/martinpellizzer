Gentiana autumnalis subsp. pennelliana

Gentiana autumnalis subsp.

pennelliana, commonly known as the Pennsylvania gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and shaded environments.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
