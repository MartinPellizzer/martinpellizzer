Gentiana quinquepartita

Gentiana quinquepartita, commonly known as the five-petaled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its showy, tubular corolla with five petals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
