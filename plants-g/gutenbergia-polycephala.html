Gutenbergia polycephala

Gutenbergia polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed gutenbergia, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
