Gentiana membranacea

Gentiana membranacea, commonly known as the membranous gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white down, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Gentiana membranacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
