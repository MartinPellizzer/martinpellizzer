Gentianopsis contorta

Gentianopsis contorta, commonly known as the twisted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
