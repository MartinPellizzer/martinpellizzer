Gymnadenia albida subsp. straminea

Gymnadenia albida subsp.

straminea, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist habitats. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
