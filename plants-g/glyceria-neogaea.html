Glyceria neogaea

Glyceria neogaea, commonly known as New Zealand grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, slender leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Glyceria neogaea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
