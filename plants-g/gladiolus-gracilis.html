Gladiolus gracilis

Gladiolus gracilis, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces spike-like inflorescences with numerous petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegance and longevity.
