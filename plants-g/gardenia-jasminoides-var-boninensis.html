Gardenia jasminoides var. boninensis

Gardenia jasminoides var. boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin gardenia, is a variant of the common gardenia that is native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically borne singly or in small clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
