Galium albicans

Galium albicans, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Galium albicans is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
