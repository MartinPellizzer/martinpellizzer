Galactia martii f. puberula

Galactia martii f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent galactia, is a variant of the species Galactia martii, which belongs to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) foliage and stems, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphological features and adaptability to harsh environments.
