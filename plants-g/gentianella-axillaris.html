Gentianella axillaris

Gentianella axillaris, commonly known as the axillary gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
