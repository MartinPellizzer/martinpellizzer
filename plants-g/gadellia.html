Gadellia

Gadellia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants are characterized by their woody stems and compound leaves, which often have a distinctive reddish hue. Gadellia species are valued for their hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and carving.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, many species are now endangered.
