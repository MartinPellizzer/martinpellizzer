Gentianella campestris subsp. campestris

Gentianella campestris subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense inflorescences.

The plant is herbaceous, with upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and is found across much of Europe and parts of Asia.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
