Garcinia gummi-gutta var. conicarpa

Garcinia gummi-gutta var. conicarpa, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-purple rind and segmented, white to pinkish flesh. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is typically oval or conical in shape, giving it the specific epithet "conicarpa.".

The botanical features of this plant make it well adapted to humid, tropical climates and rich, well-drained soils.
