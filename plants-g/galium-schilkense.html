Galium schilkense

Galium schilkense, commonly known as Schilken's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
