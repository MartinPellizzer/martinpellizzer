Geranium × hybridum

Geranium × hybridum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, serrated leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its cascading foliage.

Geranium × hybridum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
