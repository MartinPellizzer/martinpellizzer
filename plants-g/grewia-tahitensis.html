Grewia tahitensis

Grewia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian flower or Tahitian gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Grewia tahitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some island communities.
