Gentiana ochroleuca

Gentiana ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies with their vibrant color and nectar.
