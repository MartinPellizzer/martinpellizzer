Gomphocarpus hypoleucus

Gomphocarpus hypoleucus, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Gomphocarpus hypoleucus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
