Geranium pyrenaicum var. majus

Geranium pyrenaicum var. majus, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
