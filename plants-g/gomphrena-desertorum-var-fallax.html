Gomphrena desertorum var. fallax

Gomphrena desertorum var. fallax, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Gomphrena desertorum var. fallax is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
