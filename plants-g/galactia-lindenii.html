Galactia lindenii

Galactia lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is noted for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of many species in the milkweed genus.
