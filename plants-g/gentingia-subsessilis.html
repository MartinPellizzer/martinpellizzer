Gentingia subsessilis

Gentingia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters at the top of the stem. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
