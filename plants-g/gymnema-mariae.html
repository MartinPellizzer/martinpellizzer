Gymnema mariae

Gymnema mariae, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicine. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in clusters, while its fruits are follicles that release seeds when mature.

Gymnema mariae is also valued for its potential role in managing diabetes due to its bioactive compounds.
