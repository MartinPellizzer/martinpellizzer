Grevillea petrophiloides subsp. petrophiloides

Grevillea petrophiloides subsp.

petrophiloides, commonly known as the rock lilly, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
