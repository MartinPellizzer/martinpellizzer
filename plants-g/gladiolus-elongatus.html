Gladiolus elongatus

Gladiolus elongatus, commonly known as the elongated gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces striking, sword-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with its inflorescence consisting of a raceme of brightly colored blooms. Its botanical name reflects its elongated form, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The flowers are also used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting nature.
