Gastrochilus trichromus

Gastrochilus trichromus, commonly known as the three-colored stomach orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
