Gerardia purpurea var. paupercula

Gerardia purpurea var. paupercula, commonly known as the purple gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
