Gnetum gnemon var. gracile

Gnetum gnemon var. gracile, commonly known as the slender kauri, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, characterized by its unique combination of gymnosperm and angiosperm features.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces both cones and flowers, which is rare among gymnosperms. The leaves are typically reduced to scale-like structures, and the plant produces a distinctive, fleshy, seed-bearing structure called an aril.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
