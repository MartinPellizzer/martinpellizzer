Gymnoleima rosmarinifolium

Gymnoleima rosmarinifolium, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves, which are needle-like and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like cineole, are widely used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Gymnoleima rosmarinifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental and medicinal plant.
