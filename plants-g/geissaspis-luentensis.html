Geissaspis luentensis

Geissaspis luentensis, commonly known as the Luentian flameleaf, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Luentian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red leaves that change color with the seasons, showcasing a range of hues from deep crimson to burnt orange. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of pale yellow and white. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in the arid, rocky environment it inhabits.

Geissaspis luentensis is also notable for its slow growth rate and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
