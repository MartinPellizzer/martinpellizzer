Galactia isopoda

Galactia isopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Galactia isopoda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
