Gypsophila ocellata

Gypsophila ocellata, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath" or "Crown of Thorns," is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favored cut flower in floral arrangements.

Gypsophila ocellata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
