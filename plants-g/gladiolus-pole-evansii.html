Gladiolus pole-evansii

Gladiolus pole-evansii, commonly known as the Pole-Evans gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
