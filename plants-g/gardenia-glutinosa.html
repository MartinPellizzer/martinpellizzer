Gardenia glutinosa

Gardenia glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia glutinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
