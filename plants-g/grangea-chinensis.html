Grangea chinensis

Grangea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Grangea chinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
