Gastridium lendigerum

Gastridium lendigerum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with lanceolate leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stems, and the plant prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Gastridium lendigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
