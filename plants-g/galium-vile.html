Galium vile

Galium vile, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually arranged in cymes and are accompanied by two small, leaf-like bracts.

Galium vile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
