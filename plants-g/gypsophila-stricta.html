Gypsophila stricta

Gypsophila stricta, commonly known as the dwarf garden gothic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gypsophila stricta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
