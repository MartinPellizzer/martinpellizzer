Gymnosiphon divaricatus

Gymnosiphon divaricatus, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among sedges.

Gymnosiphon divaricatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
