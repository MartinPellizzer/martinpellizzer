Greigia sylvicola

Greigia sylvicola, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It has specific mycorrhizal associations that are crucial for its nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
