Gentiana dolichopoda

Gentiana dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stemmed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Gentiana dolichopoda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
