Glycine hispida var. minor-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. minor-lutescens, commonly known as the golden crown bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears small, elongated pods containing edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its hairy stems and robust growth habit make it well-suited for trellis or support structures in gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional value.
