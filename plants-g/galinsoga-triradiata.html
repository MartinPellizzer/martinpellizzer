Galinsoga triradiata

Galinsoga triradiata, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its fruits are tiny, spherical seeds enclosed in a pithy, triangular capsule.

Galinsoga triradiata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
