Gomphrena pulvinata

Gomphrena pulvinata, commonly known as the purple gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gomphrena pulvinata is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting flowers and ability to thrive in full sun.
