Goodyera wrightii

Goodyera wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's potterscale, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and produces delicate, white to pale green flowers with a distinctive, waxy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
