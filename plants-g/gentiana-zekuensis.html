Gentiana zekuensis

Gentiana zekuensis, commonly known as the Zeku gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
