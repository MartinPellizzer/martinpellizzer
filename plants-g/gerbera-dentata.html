Gerbera dentata

Gerbera dentata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically features bright yellow or orange central cones surrounded by petals in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Gerbera dentata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
