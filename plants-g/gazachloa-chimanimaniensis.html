Gazachloa chimanimaniensis

Gazachloa chimanimaniensis, commonly known as the Chimanimani grass, is a grass species native to the Chimanimani Mountains in southeastern Zimbabwe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.

Gazachloa chimanimaniensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
