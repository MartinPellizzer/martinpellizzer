Gypsophila capituliflora

Gypsophila capituliflora, commonly known as the desert sand flower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and produces clusters of small, delicate white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to its harsh environment.

Gypsophila capituliflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to flourish in challenging landscapes.
