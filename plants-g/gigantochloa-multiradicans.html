Gigantochloa multiradicans

Gigantochloa multiradicans, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Gigantochloa multiradicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional crafts.
