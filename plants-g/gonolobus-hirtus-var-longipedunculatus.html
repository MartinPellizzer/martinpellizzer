Gonolobus hirtus var. longipedunculatus

Gonolobus hirtus var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its stem is often hairy and branched, with the inflorescences extending long peduncles, giving it its varietal name.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
