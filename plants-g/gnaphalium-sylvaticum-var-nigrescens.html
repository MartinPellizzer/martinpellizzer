Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. nigrescens

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-veined everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically linear to lanceolate, with a hairy texture and a distinct dark green color. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its common name refers to the dark veins that are prominent in its foliage.
