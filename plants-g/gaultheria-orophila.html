Gaultheria orophila

Gaultheria orophila, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are edible and have a sharp, spicy flavor, similar to wintergreen.

Gaultheria orophila is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions.
