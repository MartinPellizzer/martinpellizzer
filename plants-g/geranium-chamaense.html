Geranium chamaense

Geranium chamaense, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium chamaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
