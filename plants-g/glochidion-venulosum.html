Glochidion venulosum

Glochidion venulosum, commonly known as the veined glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
