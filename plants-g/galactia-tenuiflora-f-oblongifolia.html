Galactia tenuiflora f. oblongifolia

Galactia tenuiflora f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a variant of the Galactia tenuiflora species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features slender, oblong leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
