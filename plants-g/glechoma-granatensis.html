Glechoma granatensis

Glechoma granatensis, commonly known as the granate ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Glechoma granatensis is known for its rapid spreading habit and is sometimes considered a weed in garden settings.
