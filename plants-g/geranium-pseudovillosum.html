Geranium pseudovillosum

Geranium pseudovillosum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
