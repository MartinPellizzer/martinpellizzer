Gleichenia peruviana

Gleichenia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both vegetative and reproductive shoots.

Gleichenia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
