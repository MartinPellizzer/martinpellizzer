Grevillea herbegiana

Grevillea herbegiana, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Grevillea herbegiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
