Gentiana bucovinensis

Gentiana bucovinensis, commonly known as the Bucovina gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and a basal rosette structure.

Gentiana bucovinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological significance.
