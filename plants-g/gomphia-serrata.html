Gomphia serrata

Gomphia serrata, commonly known as the serrated gomphia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
