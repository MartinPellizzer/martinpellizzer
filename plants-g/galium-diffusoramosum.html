Galium diffusoramosum

Galium diffusoramosum, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium diffusoramosum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
