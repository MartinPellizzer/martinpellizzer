Gerardia laciniata

Gerardia laciniata, commonly known as the fringed gerardia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often finely toothed or lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Gerardia laciniata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
