Gnetum gnemon f. volubile

Gnetum gnemon f. volubile, commonly known as the climbing gnetum, is a variant of the genus Gnetum, which belongs to the gymnosperm group.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed aril, which is a unique feature among gymnosperms.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical rainforest environments.
