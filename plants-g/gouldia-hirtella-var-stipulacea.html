Gouldia hirtella var. stipulacea

Gouldia hirtella var. stipulacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It features delicate, grass-like foliage with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often adorned with fine, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
