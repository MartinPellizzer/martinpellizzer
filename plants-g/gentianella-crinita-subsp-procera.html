Gentianella crinita subsp. procera

Gentianella crinita subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are tubular with prominent blue or violet petals, and it blooms from June to August.

The subspecies procera is distinguished by its taller stature compared to other subspecies of Gentianella crinita.
