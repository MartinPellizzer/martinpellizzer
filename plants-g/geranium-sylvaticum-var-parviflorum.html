Geranium sylvaticum var. parviflorum

Geranium sylvaticum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodland geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and shady areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, pink to red, and bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
