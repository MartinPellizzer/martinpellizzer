Gentianella bangii

Gentianella bangii, commonly known as the Bangi gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five to six petals.

Gentianella bangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
