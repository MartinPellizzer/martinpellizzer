Gladiolus pyramidalis

Gladiolus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of vibrant, often pink or red flowers. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.
