Gesneria exogonia

Gesneria exogonia, commonly known as the shield fern or false arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria exogonia is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
