Grastidium hamadryas

Grastidium hamadryas, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions, and its leaves are typically small and hairy to reduce water loss. It produces showy, violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's resilience and adaptability make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
