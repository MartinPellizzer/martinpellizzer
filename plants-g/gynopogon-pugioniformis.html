Gynopogon pugioniformis

Gynopogon pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf gynopogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological role and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
