Gigantochloa austroyunnanensis

Gigantochloa austroyunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to southern China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, mountainous regions. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its rapid growth, it is also valued for use in construction and as a source of raw material in local industries.
