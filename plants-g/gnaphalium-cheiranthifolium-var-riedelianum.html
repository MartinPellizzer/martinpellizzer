Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. riedelianum

Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's featherflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually white or pale yellow.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
