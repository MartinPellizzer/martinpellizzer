Garcinia brasiliensis var. parvifolia

Garcinia brasiliensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, aromatic pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
