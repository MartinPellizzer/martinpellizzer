Galium rubrum var. rubidum

Galium rubrum var. rubidum, commonly known as red-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender, reddish stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface, while the small white flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
