Gonolobus caucanus

Gonolobus caucanus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

Gonolobus caucanus is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
