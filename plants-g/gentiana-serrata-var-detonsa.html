Gentiana serrata var. detonsa

Gentiana serrata var. detonsa, commonly known as the serrated gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its scientific name, and it often forms dense clusters.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
