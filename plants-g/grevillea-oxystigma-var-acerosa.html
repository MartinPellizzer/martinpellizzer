Grevillea oxystigma var. acerosa

Grevillea oxystigma var. acerosa, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
