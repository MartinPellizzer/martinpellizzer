Geranium pyrenaicum var. australe

Geranium pyrenaicum var. australe, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lobed or dissected, with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
