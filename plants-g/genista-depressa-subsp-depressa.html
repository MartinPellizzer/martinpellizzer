Genista depressa subsp. depressa

Genista depressa subsp.

depressa, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature allows it to tolerate coastal conditions and mild frosts.
