Glycine javanica var. moniliformis

Glycine javanica var. moniliformis, commonly known as the silver wisteria, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and its distinctive, pendant-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
