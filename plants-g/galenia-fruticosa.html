Galenia fruticosa

Galenia fruticosa, commonly known as the Cape rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Galenia fruticosa is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
