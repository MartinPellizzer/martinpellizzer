Geranium asphodeloides subsp. pallens

Geranium asphodeloides subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is particularly adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
