Gentiana elliottii var. latifolia

Gentiana elliottii var. latifolia, commonly known as Elliott's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a rare and endangered species within its native habitat.
