Goniophlebium transpiananense

Goniophlebium transpiananense, commonly known as the Transpiananese fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and delicate, pinnate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
