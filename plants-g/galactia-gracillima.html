Galactia gracillima

Galactia gracillima, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Galactia gracillima is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
