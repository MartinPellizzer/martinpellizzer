Gentiana thunbergii f. albiflora

Gentiana thunbergii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a botanical variant of the gentian species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast sharply with its blue-green foliage. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. The plant's flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture, though it requires specific care to thrive.
