Grammitis marginelloides

Grammitis marginelloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually narrow and elongated, with a finely divided structure that enhances its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and shaded gardens due to its aesthetic value and unique foliage.
