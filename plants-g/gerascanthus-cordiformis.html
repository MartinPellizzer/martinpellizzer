Gerascanthus cordiformis

Gerascanthus cordiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture.

Gerascanthus cordiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
