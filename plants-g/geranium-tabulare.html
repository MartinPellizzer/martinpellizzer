Geranium tabulare

Geranium tabulare, commonly known as the marsh geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
