Geum aucklandicum

Geum aucklandicum, commonly known as the Auckland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-grow plant for gardens and conservation areas.
