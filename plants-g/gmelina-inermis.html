Gmelina inermis

Gmelina inermis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ornamental value. The plant features large, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The tree is also valued for its timber and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability to different soil types.
