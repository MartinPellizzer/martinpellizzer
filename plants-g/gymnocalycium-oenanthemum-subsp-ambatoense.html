Gymnocalycium oenanthemum subsp. ambatoense

Gymnocalycium oenanthemum subsp.

ambatoense, commonly known as the Ambato moon cactus, is a cactus species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem that is typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is a succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
