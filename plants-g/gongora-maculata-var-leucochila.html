Gongora maculata var. leucochila

Gongora maculata var. leucochila, commonly known as the White-veined Gongora, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive white veining pattern on the lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and the flower spikes are usually erect.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ornamental value.
