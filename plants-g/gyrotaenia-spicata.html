Gyrotaenia spicata

Gyrotaenia spicata, commonly known as the spikeleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Gyrotaenia spicata is adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators and wildlife.
