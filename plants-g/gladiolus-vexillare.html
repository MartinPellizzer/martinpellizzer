Gladiolus vexillare

Gladiolus vexillare, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored, often vibrant flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem.

Gladiolus vexillare is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
