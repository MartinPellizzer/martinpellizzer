Gurua

Gurua, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, green, sour-tasting berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in clusters before the fruit develops.
