Goodallia

Goodallia, commonly known as the Goodallia tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae, native to Australia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Goodallia species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
