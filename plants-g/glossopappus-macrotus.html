Glossopappus macrotus

Glossopappus macrotus, commonly known as the large-bracted fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with showy involucral bracts that can be as large as the flower itself.

Glossopappus macrotus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
