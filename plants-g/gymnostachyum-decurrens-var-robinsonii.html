Gymnostachyum decurrens var. robinsonii

Gymnostachyum decurrens var. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
