Gesneria norlindii

Gesneria norlindii, commonly known as the Norlind's gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family, known for its ornamental and often strikingly colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it thrives in humid, shaded environments, often attached to trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, with a vibrant green color that can vary depending on light conditions.

Gesneria norlindii produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
