Geum geminiflorum

Geum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable component of its native habitat.
