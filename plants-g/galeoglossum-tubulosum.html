Galeoglossum tubulosum

Galeoglossum tubulosum, commonly known as the tubular bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
