Gentiana montana var. pleurogynoides

Gentiana montana var. pleurogynoides, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and sturdy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
