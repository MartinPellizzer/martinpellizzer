Galanthus × hybridus

Galanthus × hybridus, commonly known as the common snowdrop, is a hybrid species within the genus Galanthus, which includes various types of snowdrops.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm, with strap-shaped leaves that emerge after the flowers. It prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Galanthus × hybridus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of hope and renewal during the winter months.
