Genista tricuspidata subsp. caballeroi

Genista tricuspidata subsp.

caballeroi, commonly known as the Caballero's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Genista genus.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in Mediterranean climates.
