Griffinia hyacinthina

Griffinia hyacinthina, commonly known as the hyacinth orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Griffinia hyacinthina is valued in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
