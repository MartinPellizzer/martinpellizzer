Globba perakensis

Globba perakensis, commonly known as the Java ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive purple underside.
