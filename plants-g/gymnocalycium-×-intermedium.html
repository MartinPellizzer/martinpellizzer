Gymnocalycium × intermedium

Gymnocalycium × intermedium, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Gymnocalycium mihanthera and Gymnocalycium pfeifferi.

It is a succulent cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its globular or cylindrical shape, with a dense covering of spines and a colorful, often vividly hued, flowering crown. Its flowers are typically yellow, orange, or red, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
