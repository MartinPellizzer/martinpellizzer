Geum sylvaticum var. pseudomontanum

Geum sylvaticum var. pseudomontanum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in open, rocky, or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with lobes that are usually hairy. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its high-altitude environment.
