Gnaphalium lyallii

Gnaphalium lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant produces tiny yellow flowers that are surrounded by white to pale pink bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
