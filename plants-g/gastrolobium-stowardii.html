Gastrolobium stowardii

Gastrolobium stowardii, commonly known as the Stoward's wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, cylindrical clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has woody stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid environments of its native range.

The flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
