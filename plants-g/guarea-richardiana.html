Guarea richardiana

Guarea richardiana, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes species known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, pale bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a waxy coating.
