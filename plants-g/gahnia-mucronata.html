Gahnia mucronata

Gahnia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Gahnia mucronata is an important component of the ecological systems in its native range.
