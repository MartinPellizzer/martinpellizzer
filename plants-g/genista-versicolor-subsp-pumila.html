Genista versicolor subsp. pumila

Genista versicolor subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the dwarf yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to elliptic leaflets that are usually hairy on the underside.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
