Gentiana diffusa var. mendozensis

Gentiana diffusa var. mendozensis, commonly known as the Mendoza gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
