Gaillardia crassa

Gaillardia crassa, commonly known as the thickstem gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its sturdy, branched stems and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from orange to red. The plant produces showy, ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Gaillardia crassa thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
