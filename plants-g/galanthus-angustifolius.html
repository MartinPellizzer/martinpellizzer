Galanthus angustifolius

Galanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved snowdrop, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces delicate, white flowers with green tips, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in woodland or shady garden settings. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness.

Galanthus angustifolius is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread over time in suitable conditions.
