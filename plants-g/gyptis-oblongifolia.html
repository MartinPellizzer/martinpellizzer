Gyptis oblongifolia

Gyptis oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems.

Gyptis oblongifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
