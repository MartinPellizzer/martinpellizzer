Galium mollugo subsp. album

Galium mollugo subsp.

album, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are typically followed by small, capsule-like fruits that release tiny seeds.
