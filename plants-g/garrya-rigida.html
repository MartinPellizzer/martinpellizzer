Garrya rigida

Garrya rigida, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Garrya rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
