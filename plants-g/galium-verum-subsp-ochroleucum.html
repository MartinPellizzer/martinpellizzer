Galium verum subsp. ochroleucum

Galium verum subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.
