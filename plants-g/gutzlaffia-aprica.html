Gutzlaffia aprica

Gutzlaffia aprica, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with soft, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of golden-yellow flowers that resemble small cups, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gutzlaffia aprica is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
