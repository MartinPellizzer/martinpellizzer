Gothofreda crispa

Gothofreda crispa, commonly known as the brittle fern, is a unique species characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures.

It is native to the temperate regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and segmented, with a distinctively brittle texture that gives it its common name. Gothofreda crispa is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and adaptation.
