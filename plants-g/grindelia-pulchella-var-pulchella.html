Grindelia pulchella var. pulchella

Grindelia pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its reproductive success in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
