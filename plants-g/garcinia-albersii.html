Garcinia albersii

Garcinia albersii, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia albersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in tropical horticulture.
