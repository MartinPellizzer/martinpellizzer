Grammitis tmesipteris

Grammitis tmesipteris, commonly known as the golden grammitis, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique structure with a short, creeping rhizome and closely spaced, linear leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of white or yellowish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
