Goniopteris scolopendrioides

Goniopteris scolopendrioides, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and possesses distinctive, club-shaped sporophylls that bear spores. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a valuable species for studying evolutionary plant development.
