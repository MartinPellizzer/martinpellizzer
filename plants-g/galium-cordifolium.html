Galium cordifolium

Galium cordifolium, commonly known as heartleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, opposite, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Galium cordifolium is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
