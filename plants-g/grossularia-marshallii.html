Grossularia marshallii

Grossularia marshallii, commonly known as the Marshall's gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tart berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant produces these berries in late summer, and they are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Grossularia marshallii is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
