Glycine cajanoides

Glycine cajanoides, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Glycine cajanoides also has nitrogen-fixing properties, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to diverse climates makes it an important crop in agricultural systems.
