Galvezia juncea var. pubescens

Galvezia juncea var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent galvezia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with suitable conditions.
