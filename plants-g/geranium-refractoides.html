Geranium refractoides

Geranium refractoides, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
