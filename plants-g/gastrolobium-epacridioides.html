Gastrolobium epacridioides

Gastrolobium epacridioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, linear leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Gastrolobium epacridioides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
