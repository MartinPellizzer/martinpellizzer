Gastrochilus monticola

Gastrochilus monticola, commonly known as the mountain gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically features long, slender leaves and produces showy, lip-shaped petals that are often yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
