Grindelia obovata

Grindelia obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed.

Grindelia obovata is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
