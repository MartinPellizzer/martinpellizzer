Galinsoga oblongifolia

Galinsoga oblongifolia, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its branched, hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have toothed margins. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
