Gordonia floribunda

Gordonia floribunda, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

Gordonia floribunda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in acidic soils.
