Gentiana × pamplinii

Gentiana × pamplinii, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Gentiana lutea and Gentiana crassuloides, resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The species is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
