Guatteria sessilicarpa

Guatteria sessilicarpa, commonly known as the sessile-fruited guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in humid lowland forests. The plant features simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a spherical berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
