Gypsophila aretioides subsp. imbricata

Gypsophila aretioides subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces small, linear leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This subspecies is closely related to other gypsophila species and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
