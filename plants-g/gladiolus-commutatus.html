Gladiolus commutatus

Gladiolus commutatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
