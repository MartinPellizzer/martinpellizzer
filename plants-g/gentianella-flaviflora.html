Gentianella flaviflora

Gentianella flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a short, stout stem that bears clusters of bright yellow flowers. The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
