Genista disperma

Genista disperma, commonly known as the spreading broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Genista disperma is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
