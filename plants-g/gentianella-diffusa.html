Gentianella diffusa

Gentianella diffusa, commonly known as the spreading gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Gentianella diffusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
