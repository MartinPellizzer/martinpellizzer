Gaertnera koenigii var. thyrsiflora

Gaertnera koenigii var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
