Gongora shepherdii

Gongora shepherdii, commonly known as the Shepherd's Gongora, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to orange, with intricate patterns resembling gongs, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky slopes or in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Gongora shepherdii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
