Gentianella cerastioides

Gentianella cerastioides, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, rhizomatous stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and prominent yellow-orange veins.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
