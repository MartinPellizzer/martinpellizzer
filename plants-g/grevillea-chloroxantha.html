Grevillea chloroxantha

Grevillea chloroxantha, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Grevillea chloroxantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
