Gamanthus barbellatus

Gamanthus barbellatus, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. Gamanthus barbellatus thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
