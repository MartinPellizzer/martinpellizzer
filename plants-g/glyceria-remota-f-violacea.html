Glyceria remota f. violacea

Glyceria remota f. violacea, commonly known as violet false poa, is a variant of the grass species Glyceria remota.

It is characterized by its distinct violet or purple-colored inflorescences, which set it apart from the typical green form of the species. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

The botanical features of Glyceria remota f. violacea make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
