Gastrochilus simplicilabius

Gastrochilus simplicilabius, commonly known as the simple-labellum stomach lip, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender stem with several oval-shaped leaves, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow with a distinctive, simple labellum. It produces a single spike of flowers, which are often clustered near the base of the plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
