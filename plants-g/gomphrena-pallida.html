Gomphrena pallida

Gomphrena pallida, commonly known as the pale gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance.

Gomphrena pallida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
