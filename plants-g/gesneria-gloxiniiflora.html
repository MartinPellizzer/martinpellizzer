Gesneria gloxiniiflora

Gesneria gloxiniiflora, commonly known as the gloxiniiflora gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Gesneria gloxiniiflora is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
