Galpinsia fendleri

Galpinsia fendleri, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape, with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert climates.

Galpinsia fendleri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
