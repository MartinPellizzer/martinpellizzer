Gentiana flexicaulis

Gentiana flexicaulis, commonly known as the bent-stem gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often bent or twisted.

Gentiana flexicaulis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
