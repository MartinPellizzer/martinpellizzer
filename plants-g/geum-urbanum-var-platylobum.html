Geum urbanum var. platylobum

Geum urbanum var. platylobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves with broad, heart-shaped lobes and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
