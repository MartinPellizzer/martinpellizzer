Galactophora schomburgkiana var. megaphylla

Galactophora schomburgkiana var. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant leaf galactophora, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is distinguished by its large, showy leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a velvety texture and a deep green coloration. The plant produces striking white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage and floral display, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical plants.
