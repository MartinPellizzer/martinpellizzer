Gnetum polystachyum

Gnetum polystachyum, commonly known as the African tropical gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta, a gymnosperm group with unique botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed aril, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Gnetum.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and its ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
