Genista aspalathoides var. mauritanica

Genista aspalathoides var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
