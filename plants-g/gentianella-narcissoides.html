Gentianella narcissoides

Gentianella narcissoides, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Gentianella narcissoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
