Gilibertia weberbaueri

Gilibertia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a short stature, typically growing to about 30 cm in height, and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Gilibertia weberbaueri is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some regions for its purported health benefits.
