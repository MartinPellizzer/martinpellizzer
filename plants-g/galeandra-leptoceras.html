Galeandra leptoceras

Galeandra leptoceras, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
