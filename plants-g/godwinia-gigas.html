Godwinia gigas

Godwinia gigas, commonly known as the giant godwinia, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its towering, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of purple and violet, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

Godwinia gigas is well adapted to cold climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
