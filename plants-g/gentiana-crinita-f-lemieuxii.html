Gentiana crinita f. lemieuxii

Gentiana crinita f. lemieuxii, commonly known as the Lemieux's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

This subspecies is characterized by its blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and forested areas. It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a robust root system adapted to its rocky environment.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
