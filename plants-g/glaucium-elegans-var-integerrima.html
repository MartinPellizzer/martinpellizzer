Glaucium elegans var. integerrima

Glaucium elegans var. integerrima, commonly known as the purple hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a distinctive bluish-gray waxy coating, known as a glaucous bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
