Gothofreda eriantha

Gothofreda eriantha, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from a basal node. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Gothofreda eriantha is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
