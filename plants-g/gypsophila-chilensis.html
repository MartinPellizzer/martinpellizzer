Gypsophila chilensis

Gypsophila chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean laceflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble lace. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the spring and summer months.

Gypsophila chilensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
