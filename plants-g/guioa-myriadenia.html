Guioa myriadenia

Guioa myriadenia, commonly known as the Philippine guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along roadsides.

Guioa myriadenia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
