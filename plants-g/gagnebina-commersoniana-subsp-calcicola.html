Gagnebina commersoniana subsp. calcicola

Gagnebina commersoniana subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola gagnebina, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
