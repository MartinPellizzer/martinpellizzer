Gigantochloa pachyparietalis

Gigantochloa pachyparietalis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems and large, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in height. The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomes spread rapidly, allowing it to form dense clumps or even small forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
