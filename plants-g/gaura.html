Gaura

Gaura, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Gaura in the family Polemoniaceae, which includes other flowering plants such as phlox. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Gaura is often found in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adding texture to the plant's overall appearance.
