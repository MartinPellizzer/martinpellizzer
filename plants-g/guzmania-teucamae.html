Guzmania teucamae

Guzmania teucamae, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-tipped inflorescence that emerges from a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its foliage is often a deep green, contrasting beautifully with the colorful bracts.

Guzmania teucamae is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and well-draining soil to flourish.
