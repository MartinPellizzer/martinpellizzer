Geissopappus bahiensis

Geissopappus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species for ecological studies in arid regions.
