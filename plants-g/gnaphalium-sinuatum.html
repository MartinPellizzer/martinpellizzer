Gnaphalium sinuatum

Gnaphalium sinuatum, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind, aiding in its widespread distribution. Its leaves are typically silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs that give it a distinctive, silvery appearance.

Gnaphalium sinuatum is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
