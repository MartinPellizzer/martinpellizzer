Gronophyllum sarasinorum

Gronophyllum sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's ironwood, is a rare and hardy shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and woody, fibrous bark that provides protection against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the late winter or early spring. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Gronophyllum sarasinorum is valued for its ecological importance and is often used in traditional medicine due to its various bioactive compounds.
