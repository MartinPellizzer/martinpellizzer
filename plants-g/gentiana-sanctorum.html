Gentiana sanctorum

Gentiana sanctorum, commonly known as the sacred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
