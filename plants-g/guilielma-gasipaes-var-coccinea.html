Guilielma gasipaes var. coccinea

Guilielma gasipaes var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-fruit guillemot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often green or yellowish in color, and are followed by the showy, fleshy fruits.
