Glandularia lambertii

Glandularia lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's glandularia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, glandular hairs that secrete a sticky substance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique floral structure and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
