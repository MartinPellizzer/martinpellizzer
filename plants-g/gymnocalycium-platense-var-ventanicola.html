Gymnocalycium platense var. ventanicola

Gymnocalycium platense var. ventanicola, commonly known as the Ventanica Lobula Cactus, is a species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh conditions make it a notable species in the Cactaceae family.
