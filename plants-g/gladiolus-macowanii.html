Gladiolus macowanii

Gladiolus macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vibrant purple or red flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
