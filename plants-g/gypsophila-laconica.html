Gypsophila laconica

Gypsophila laconica, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers and succulent foliage. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, adapting to harsh, dry environments with its deep root system. Its small, white or pink flowers bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Gypsophila laconica is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to withstand extreme temperatures.
