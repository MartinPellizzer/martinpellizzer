Gastrolobium obovatum

Gastrolobium obovatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Gastrolobium obovatum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil rehabilitation.
