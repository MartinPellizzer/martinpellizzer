Gymnocalycium catamarcense subsp. acinacispinum

Gymnocalycium catamarcense subsp.

acinacispinum, commonly known as the Catamarca spineclad cactus, is a species native to Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
