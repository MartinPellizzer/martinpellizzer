Gastrochilus flabelliformis

Gastrochilus flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that often resemble a fan, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
