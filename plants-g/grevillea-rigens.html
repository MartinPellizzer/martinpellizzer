Grevillea rigens

Grevillea rigens, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Grevillea rigens is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
