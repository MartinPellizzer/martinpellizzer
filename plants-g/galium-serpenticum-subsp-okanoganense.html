Galium serpenticum subsp. okanoganense

Galium serpenticum subsp.

okanoganense, commonly known as Okanogan bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
