Goodenia suffrutescens

Goodenia suffrutescens, commonly known as the silver goodenia, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Australia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Goodenia suffrutescens is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
