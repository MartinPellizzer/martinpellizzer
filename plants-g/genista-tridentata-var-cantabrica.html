Genista tridentata var. cantabrica

Genista tridentata var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
