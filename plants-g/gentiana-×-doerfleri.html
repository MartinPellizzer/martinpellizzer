Gentiana × doerfleri

Gentiana × doerfleri, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, helping to reduce water loss in high-altitude environments. It thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Gentiana × doerfleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine displays.
