Guarea parvifolia

Guarea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
