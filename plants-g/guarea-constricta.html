Guarea constricta

Guarea constricta, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often elliptical in shape, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

This plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
