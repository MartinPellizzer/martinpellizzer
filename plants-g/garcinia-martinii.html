Garcinia martinii

Garcinia martinii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Garcinia martinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
