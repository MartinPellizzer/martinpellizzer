Genista hirsuta subsp. hirsuta

Genista hirsuta subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
