Gentiana quadrangularis

Gentiana quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive quadrangular shape, giving it its scientific name.

Gentiana quadrangularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
