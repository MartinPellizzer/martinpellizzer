Galium paschale

Galium paschale, commonly known as Paschal weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium paschale is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates.
