Garcinia virgata

Garcinia virgata, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Garcinia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for shade trees in tropical landscapes.
