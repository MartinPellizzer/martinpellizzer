Gomphrena pearcei

Gomphrena pearcei, commonly known as the pink plume flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in Texas and New Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in xeriscaped landscapes.
