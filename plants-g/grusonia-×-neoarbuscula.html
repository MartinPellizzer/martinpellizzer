Grusonia × neoarbuscula

Grusonia × neoarbuscula, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Grusonia arborescens and Grusonia neoarbuscula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1-2 meters, featuring showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has large, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
