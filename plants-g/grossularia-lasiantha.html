Grossularia lasiantha

Grossularia lasiantha, commonly known as the Himalayan beautyberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Grossularia lasiantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
