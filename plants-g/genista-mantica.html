Genista mantica

Genista mantica, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Genista mantica is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in erosion control.
