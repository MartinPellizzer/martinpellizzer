Guazuma tomentosa

Guazuma tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety grayish-white pubescence on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
