Glomera salmonea

Glomera salmonea, commonly known as the golden glomerate, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red floral bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, Glomera salmonea is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
