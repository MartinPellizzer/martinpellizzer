Gentiana rhaetica var. stiriaca

Gentiana rhaetica var. stiriaca, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and erect.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
