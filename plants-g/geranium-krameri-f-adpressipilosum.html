Geranium krameri f. adpressipilosum

Geranium krameri f. adpressipilosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features rounded, succulent leaves that are densely covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.
