Grindelia stricta var. macrophylla

Grindelia stricta var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant brittlebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant has silvery-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, brittle hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open plains and rocky soils.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
