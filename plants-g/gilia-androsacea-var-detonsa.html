Gilia androsacea var. detonsa

Gilia androsacea var. detonsa, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
