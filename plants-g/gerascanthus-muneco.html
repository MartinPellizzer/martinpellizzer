Gerascanthus muneco

Gerascanthus muneco, commonly known as the Muneco's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer months, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Gerascanthus muneco is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
