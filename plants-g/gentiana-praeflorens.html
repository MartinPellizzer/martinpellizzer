Gentiana praeflorens

Gentiana praeflorens, commonly known as the early gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by flies and bees.
