Gamotopea alba

Gamotopea alba, commonly known as the white gamotopea, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale white petals and slender, erect stems that rise from a basal rosette of leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, Gamotopea alba is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
