Gastrodia tuberculata

Gastrodia tuberculata, commonly known as the Chinese club-mushroom, is a rare and medicinally significant plant native to Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous rhizomes and lack of true leaves. The plant is known for its unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi, which aid in its nutrient absorption. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties and declining populations, it is often protected in its native habitats.
