Gymnocalycium monvillei var. gertrudae

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. gertrudae, commonly known as the Gertrudae moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is often prized by cactus enthusiasts.
