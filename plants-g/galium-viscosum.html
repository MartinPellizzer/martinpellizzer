Galium viscosum

Galium viscosum, commonly known as sticky willow or wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. Its flowers are white or pale pink, clustered in umbels, and bloom in early spring.

Galium viscosum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.
