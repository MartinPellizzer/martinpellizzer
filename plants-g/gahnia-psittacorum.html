Gahnia psittacorum

Gahnia psittacorum, commonly known as the parrot grass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often adorned with bracts, contributing to its ornamental value.

Gahnia psittacorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
