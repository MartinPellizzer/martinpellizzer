Gaga hirsuta

Gaga hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gaga, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gaga hirsuta is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
