Gigantochloa kathaensis

Gigantochloa kathaensis, commonly known as the Kathaenese giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gigantochloa kathaensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
