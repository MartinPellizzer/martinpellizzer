Geranium villosum

Geranium villosum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium villosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
