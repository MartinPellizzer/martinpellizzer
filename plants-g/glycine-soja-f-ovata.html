Glycine soja f. ovata

Glycine soja f. ovata, commonly known as the ovate soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant characterized by its distinct ovate-shaped leaves.

This plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to East Asia. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds used in various culinary and industrial applications. The ovate form of its leaves sets it apart from the more commonly cultivated soybean varieties.

It is often grown for its nutritional value and adaptability to different soil conditions.
