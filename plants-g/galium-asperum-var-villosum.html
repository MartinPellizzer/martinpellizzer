Galium asperum var. villosum

Galium asperum var. villosum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has slender, hairy stems that often lie on the ground. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine, stiff hairs. This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
