Gagea × pomeranica

Gagea × pomeranica, commonly known as the hybrid squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, round bulbs that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gagea × pomeranica is also valued for its medicinal properties, though its use is less common in modern herbal practices.
