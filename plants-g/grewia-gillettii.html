Grewia gillettii

Grewia gillettii, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in summer. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Grewia gillettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
