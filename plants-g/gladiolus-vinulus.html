Gladiolus vinulus

Gladiolus vinulus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with spikes of brightly colored blooms that range from white to deep red. Its flowers are composed of three outer petals and three inner sepals, which are often more prominent.

The slender gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
