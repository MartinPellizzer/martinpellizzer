Genistella racemosa

Genistella racemosa, commonly known as the racemose genistella, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers arranged in racemes, which are elongated clusters of flowers. Its root system is often nitrogen-fixing, contributing to soil fertility.

Genistella racemosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
