Gentiana intricata var. subintricata

Gentiana intricata var. subintricata, commonly known as the intricate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in alpine meadows or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific moisture and light requirements.
