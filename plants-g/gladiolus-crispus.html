Gladiolus crispus

Gladiolus crispus, commonly known as the curly gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored, petal-like bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly curled, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.

Gladiolus crispus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
