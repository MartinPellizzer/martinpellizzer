Geonoma speciosa

Geonoma speciosa, commonly known as the showy geonoma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. Its inflorescence consists of bright red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers.

Geonoma speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments.
