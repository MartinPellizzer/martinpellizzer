Gluta laccifera

Gluta laccifera, commonly known as the lac tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and dense foliage. The plant is notable for producing a resin called lac, which is collected from its bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and the tree produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.
