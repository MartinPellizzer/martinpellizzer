Gerardia grandiflora var. cinerea

Gerardia grandiflora var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage, which is covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique coloration and drought tolerance.
