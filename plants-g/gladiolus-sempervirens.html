Gladiolus sempervirens

Gladiolus sempervirens, commonly known as the everlasting gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy spikes of flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are known to retain their color and form for an extended period, hence its common name.
