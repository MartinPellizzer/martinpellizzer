Gerascanthus trachyphyllus

Gerascanthus trachyphyllus, commonly known as the rough-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
