Gladiolus microspicatus

Gladiolus microspicatus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces compact, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and has a relatively small stature, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, with each bloom having three sepals that are often more prominent than the petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
