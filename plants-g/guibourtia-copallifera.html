Guibourtia copallifera

Guibourtia copallifera, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
