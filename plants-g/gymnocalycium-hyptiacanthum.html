Gymnocalycium hyptiacanthum

Gymnocalycium hyptiacanthum, commonly known as the red-tipped moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium hyptiacanthum is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
