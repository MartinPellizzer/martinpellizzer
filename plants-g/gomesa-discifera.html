Gomesa discifera

Gomesa discifera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Gomesa discifera is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
