Gravesia apiculata

Gravesia apiculata, commonly known as the white grave's bloom, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its pleasant scent.
