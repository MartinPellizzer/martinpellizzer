Gladiolus brachyscyphus

Gladiolus brachyscyphus, commonly known as the short-capped gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus brachyscyphus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
