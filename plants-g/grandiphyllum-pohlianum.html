Grandiphyllum pohlianum

Grandiphyllum pohlianum, commonly known as the Pohlian's grandiflorous, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces leguminous pods that contain edible seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
