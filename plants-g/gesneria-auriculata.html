Gesneria auriculata

Gesneria auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria auriculata is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
