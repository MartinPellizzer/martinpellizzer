Geranium pratense var. lilacinum

Geranium pratense var. lilacinum, commonly known as the meadow geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and purple, with the varietal name 'lilacinum' referring to its lilac or pale lilac hue. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
