Gordonia speciosa

Gordonia speciosa, commonly known as the giant candleplant, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, candle-like leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically hidden beneath its dense foliage. Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
