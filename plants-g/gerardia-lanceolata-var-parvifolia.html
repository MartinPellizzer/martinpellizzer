Gerardia lanceolata var. parvifolia

Gerardia lanceolata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
