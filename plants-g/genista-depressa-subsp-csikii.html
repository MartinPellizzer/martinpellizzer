Genista depressa subsp. csikii

Genista depressa subsp.

csikii, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Basin. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
