Geranium rotundifolium var. grandiflorum

Geranium rotundifolium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
