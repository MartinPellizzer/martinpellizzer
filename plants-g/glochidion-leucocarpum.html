Glochidion leucocarpum

Glochidion leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited glabrous wood.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
