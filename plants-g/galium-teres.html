Galium teres

Galium teres, commonly known as the round-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are small, oval, and arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels.

The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
