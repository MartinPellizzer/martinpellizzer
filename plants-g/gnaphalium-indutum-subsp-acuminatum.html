Gnaphalium indutum subsp. acuminatum

Gnaphalium indutum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, grayish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are often mistaken for seed heads due to their structure. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and dry landscaping.
