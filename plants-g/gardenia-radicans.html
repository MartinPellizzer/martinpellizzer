Gardenia radicans

Gardenia radicans, commonly known as the Hawaiian gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers are highly valued for their ornamental beauty and are often used in floral arrangements.

Gardenia radicans prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
