Gaultheria morobeensis var. ovatifolia

Gaultheria morobeensis var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the Morobe oval-leaved wintergreen, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Papua New Guinea.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its ecosystem.
