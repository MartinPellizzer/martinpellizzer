Gentiana sororcula

Gentiana sororcula, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or violet corolla, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
