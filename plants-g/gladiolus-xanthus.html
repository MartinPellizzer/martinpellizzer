Gladiolus xanthus

Gladiolus xanthus, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a striking red or orange center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, sword-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical name reflects its yellow coloration, with "xanthus" derived from the Greek word for yellow.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and are popular in gardens and floral arrangements.
