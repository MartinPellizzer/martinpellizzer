Gaultheria celebensis

Gaultheria celebensis, commonly known as the Celebes wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a peppery, spicy flavor. Gaultheria celebensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in acidic soils.

Its ornamental value and unique flavor make it a notable plant in both horticulture and culinary contexts.
