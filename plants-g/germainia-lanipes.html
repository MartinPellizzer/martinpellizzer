Germainia lanipes

Germainia lanipes, commonly known as the silky germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with thin, creeping stems that often form dense mats. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Germainia lanipes thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
