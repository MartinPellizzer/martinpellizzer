Gilbertiodendron grandistipulatum

Gilbertiodendron grandistipulatum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
