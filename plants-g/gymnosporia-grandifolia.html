Gymnosporia grandifolia

Gymnosporia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-fruited gymnosporia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. These berries are a key feature, containing numerous seeds enclosed in a fleshy aril.

Gymnosporia grandifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
