Genistella tridentata subsp. stenoptera

Genistella tridentata subsp.

stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It has a woody stem with thorn-like projections, and its leaves are typically narrow and pointed.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
