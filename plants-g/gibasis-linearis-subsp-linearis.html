Gibasis linearis subsp. linearis

Gibasis linearis subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the slender gibasis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native range.
