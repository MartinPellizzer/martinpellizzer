Grastidium fissum

Grastidium fissum, commonly known as the fissured grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often marked with distinct longitudinal fissures, giving it its scientific name.

Grastidium fissum is adapted to arid conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
