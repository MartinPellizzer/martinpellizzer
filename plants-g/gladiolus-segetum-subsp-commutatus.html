Gladiolus segetum subsp. commutatus

Gladiolus segetum subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the wild gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a grass-like appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soils.
