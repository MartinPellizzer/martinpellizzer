Gaiadendron nitidum

Gaiadendron nitidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Gaiadendron nitidum is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an invasive species in certain regions.
