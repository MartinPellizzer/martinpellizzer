Grevillea neorigida subsp. neorigida

Grevillea neorigida subsp.

neorigida, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
