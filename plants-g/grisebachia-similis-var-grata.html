Grisebachia similis var. grata

Grisebachia similis var. grata, commonly known as the silverleaf grisebachia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
