Galium floribundum subsp. floribundum

Galium floribundum subsp.

floribundum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
