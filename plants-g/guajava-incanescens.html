Guajava incanescens

Guajava incanescens, commonly known as the white guajava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
