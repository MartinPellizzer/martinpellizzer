Gypsophila uralensis var. glabra

Gypsophila uralensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety 'glabra' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.
