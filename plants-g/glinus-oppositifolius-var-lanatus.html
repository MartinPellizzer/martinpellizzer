Glinus oppositifolius var. lanatus

Glinus oppositifolius var. lanatus, commonly known as the woolly glinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are densely covered with soft, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens.
