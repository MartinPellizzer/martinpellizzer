Gossypium pubescens

Gossypium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cotton plant, is a species of cotton native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton bolls that contain the fibrous material used in textile manufacturing. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gossypium pubescens is valued not only for its cotton but also for its role in traditional agricultural practices in its native regions.
