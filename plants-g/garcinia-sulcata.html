Garcinia sulcata

Garcinia sulcata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thick, yellow or orange rind and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit it produces.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
