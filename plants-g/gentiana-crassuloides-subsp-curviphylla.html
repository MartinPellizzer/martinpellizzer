Gentiana crassuloides subsp. curviphylla

Gentiana crassuloides subsp.

curviphylla, commonly known as the curved-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months.

The leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to arid conditions, and are often curved or twisted, giving the plant its subspecific name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
