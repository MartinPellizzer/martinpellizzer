Gladiolus triangulus

Gladiolus triangulus, commonly known as the three-lobed gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its botanical name reflects the triangular shape of its leaves, which are deeply lobed.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
