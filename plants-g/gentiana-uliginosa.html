Gentiana uliginosa

Gentiana uliginosa, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in the northern hemisphere.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and marshes.

The genus Gentiana is characterized by its showy, radial flowers and is part of the family Gentianaceae.
