Gardenia jasminoides var. grandiflora

Gardenia jasminoides var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces large, waxy, white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
