Gnaphalium circinnatum

Gnaphalium circinnatum, commonly known as the golden carpet, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of tiny, yellowish to golden flowers. The plant produces small, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
