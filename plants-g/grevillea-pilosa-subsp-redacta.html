Grevillea pilosa subsp. redacta

Grevillea pilosa subsp.

redacta, commonly known as the redback grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red and yellow flowers that appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological importance in its native habitat.
