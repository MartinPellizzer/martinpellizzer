Gymnocalycium tudae var. pseudomalacocarpum

Gymnocalycium tudae var. pseudomalacocarpum, commonly known as the "Tudae's Gymnocalyx," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
