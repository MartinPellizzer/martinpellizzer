Gleditsia japonica var. delavayi

Gleditsia japonica var. delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's honey locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with suitable growing conditions.
