Goodenia coronopifolia

Goodenia coronopifolia, commonly known as the crown-leaf goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ornate leaves that resemble a crown. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Goodenia coronopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
