Gleditsia triacanthos f. elegantissima

Gleditsia triacanthos f. elegantissima, commonly known as the Chinese honey locust, is a cultivar of the honey locust tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping growth habit and its finely divided, feathery foliage that creates a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by long, slender pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its elegant form and tolerance to urban conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and requires careful management.
