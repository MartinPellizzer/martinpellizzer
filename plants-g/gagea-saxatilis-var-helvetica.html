Gagea saxatilis var. helvetica

Gagea saxatilis var. helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss rock garlic, is a perennial bulbous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas where it blooms among stones and gravel.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
