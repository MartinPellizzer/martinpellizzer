Gnemon venosa

Gnemon venosa, commonly known as the veined gorse, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive venation pattern, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
