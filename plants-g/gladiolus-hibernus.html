Gladiolus hibernus

Gladiolus hibernus, commonly known as the Irish gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking spikes of colorful, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, with flowers in shades of red, pink, white, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in British horticulture make it a notable botanical specimen.
