Gunnera pittieriana

Gunnera pittieriana, commonly known as the giant water shield, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Gunneraceae and is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The leaves are typically floating on the water's surface, with a waxy coating that aids in water retention. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are often submerged or just at the water's edge.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its wetland habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
