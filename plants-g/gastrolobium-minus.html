Gastrolobium minus

Gastrolobium minus, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
