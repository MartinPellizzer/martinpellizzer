Gerbera pumila

Gerbera pumila, commonly known as the dwarf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, and bloom during the warmer months.

Gerbera pumila prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
