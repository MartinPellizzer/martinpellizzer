Genista scoparia var. pallescens

Genista scoparia var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems that are typically covered in a grayish-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is well adapted to poor, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and limestone areas.
