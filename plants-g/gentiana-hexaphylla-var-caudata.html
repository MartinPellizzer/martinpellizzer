Gentiana hexaphylla var. caudata

Gentiana hexaphylla var. caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
