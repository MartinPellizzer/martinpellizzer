Gentianodes decemfida

Gentianodes decemfida, commonly known as the nine-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are arranged in a unique whorled pattern, often numbering nine, which gives it its scientific name.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
