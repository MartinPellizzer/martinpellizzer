Gonioma

Gonioma, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, herbaceous growth habit and typically features alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are usually small and arranged in racemes, with four petals that are often white or yellow in color. Gonioma species are often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid or semi-arid environments.

These plants are adapted to withstand drought conditions and are often used in rock gardens or as ornamental plants.
