Gnaphalium punae

Gnaphalium punae, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, surrounded by fine, hairy leaves. Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions make it well-adapted to its ecological niche.

Gnaphalium punae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
