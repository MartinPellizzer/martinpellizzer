Gleichenia oligocarpa

Gleichenia oligocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, circular groups on the undersides of the fronds.

Gleichenia oligocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
