Genista mogadorensis

Genista mogadorensis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista mogadorensis is adapted to grow in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
