Gilberta

Gilberta, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Gilberta species are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plants typically grow from a basal rosette and produce small, inconspicuous fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to their drought tolerance, they are often used in xeriscaping and as ornamental plants in dry climates.
