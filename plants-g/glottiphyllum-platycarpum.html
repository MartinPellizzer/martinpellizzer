Glottiphyllum platycarpum

Glottiphyllum platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited glottiphyllum, is a member of the family Aizoaceae, native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces distinctive, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
