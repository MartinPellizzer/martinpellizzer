Gigotorcya

Gigotorcya, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Botanically, Gigotorcya belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
