Gentiana jamesii

Gentiana jamesii, commonly known as the Colorado gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana jamesii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
