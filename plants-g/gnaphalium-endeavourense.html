Gnaphalium endeavourense

Gnaphalium endeavourense, commonly known as the New Zealand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fine, white to pale yellow pollen that is easily dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Gnaphalium endeavourense is also noted for its ability to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion.
