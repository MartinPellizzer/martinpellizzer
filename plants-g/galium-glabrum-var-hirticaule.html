Galium glabrum var. hirticaule

Galium glabrum var. hirticaule, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are softly hairy, especially along the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
