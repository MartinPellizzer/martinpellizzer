Gansblum vernum

Gansblum vernum, commonly known as spring gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in the spring. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gansblum vernum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
