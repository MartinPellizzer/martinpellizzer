Glaucidium palmatum

Glaucidium palmatum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Glaucidium palmatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
