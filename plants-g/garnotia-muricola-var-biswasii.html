Garnotia muricola var. biswasii

Garnotia muricola var. biswasii, commonly known as the Kashmiri thyme, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a distinct fragrance, which contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.

The variety biswasii is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and is of particular interest in botanical research.
