Gentiana montana var. saxosa

Gentiana montana var. saxosa, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine downy layer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in high-altitude habitats.
