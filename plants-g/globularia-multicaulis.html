Globularia multicaulis

Globularia multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered globularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, globular clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant features upright, branching stems covered with fine, hairy leaves that are often blue-gray in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Globularia multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
