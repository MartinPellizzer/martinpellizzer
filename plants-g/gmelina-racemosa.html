Gmelina racemosa

Gmelina racemosa, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in racemose inflorescences, often in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth surface.

The wood of Gmelina racemosa is valued for its lightweight and workability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
