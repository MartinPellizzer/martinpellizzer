Gastropodium violaceum

Gastropodium violaceum, commonly known as the violet-stemmed snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, violet-hued stem that is often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
