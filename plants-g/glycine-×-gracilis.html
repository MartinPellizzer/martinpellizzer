Glycine × gracilis

Glycine × gracilis, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a hybrid legume that combines traits from Glycine max (soybean) and Glycine gracilis.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Glycine × gracilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both agricultural and horticultural applications.
