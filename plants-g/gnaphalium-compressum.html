Gnaphalium compressum

Gnaphalium compressum, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are often mistaken for daisies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, woolly appearance.

Gnaphalium compressum thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
