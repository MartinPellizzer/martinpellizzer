Gymnema pubiflora

Gymnema pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy gymnema, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Gymnema pubiflora is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
