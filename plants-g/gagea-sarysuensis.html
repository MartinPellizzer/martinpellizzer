Gagea sarysuensis

Gagea sarysuensis, commonly known as the Sarysu gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a single bulb that serves as its storage organ, typical of many bulbous species. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
