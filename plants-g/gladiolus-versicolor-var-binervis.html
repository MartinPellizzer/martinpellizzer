Gladiolus versicolor var. binervis

Gladiolus versicolor var. binervis, commonly known as the two-veined gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has strap-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a distinct two-veined pattern on the underside. Its root system consists of rhizomes, which help it survive in various soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
