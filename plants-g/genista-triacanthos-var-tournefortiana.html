Genista triacanthos var. tournefortiana

Genista triacanthos var. tournefortiana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often branched stems covered in small, spiny thorns. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
