Gymnotrix cladodes

Gymnotrix cladodes, commonly known as the leafless nightshade, is a unique plant characterized by its lack of true leaves.

Instead, it possesses modified stems called cladodes that perform photosynthesis. These cladodes are typically green and cylindrical, allowing the plant to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain toxic compounds, making it unpalatable to most herbivores.

Its botanical structure reflects an adaptation to conserve water and maximize light absorption in its native habitat.
