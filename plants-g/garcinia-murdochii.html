Garcinia murdochii

Garcinia murdochii, commonly known as the Murdochs' garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has attracted attention for potential medicinal and dietary applications.
