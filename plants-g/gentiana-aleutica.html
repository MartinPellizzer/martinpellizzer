Gentiana aleutica

Gentiana aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentiana aleutica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
