Glochidion glaucescens

Glochidion glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Glochidion glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
