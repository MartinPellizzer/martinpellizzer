Gardenia hygrophila

Gardenia hygrophila, commonly known as the water gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and strong scent.

Gardenia hygrophila is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional medicine.
