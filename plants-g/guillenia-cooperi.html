Guillenia cooperi

Guillenia cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's guillenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color.

Guillenia cooperi is often found in wet prairies and is an important component of native plant communities in its range.
