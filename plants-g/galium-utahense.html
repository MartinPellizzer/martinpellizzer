Galium utahense

Galium utahense, commonly known as Utah bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium utahense plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments and supports local insect populations.
