Geonoma cuneata subsp. rubra

Geonoma cuneata subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed bamboo palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

The plant is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
