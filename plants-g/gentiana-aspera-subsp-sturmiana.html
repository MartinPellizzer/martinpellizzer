Gentiana aspera subsp. sturmiana

Gentiana aspera subsp.

sturmiana, commonly known as the Sturm's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
