Gomphocarpus appendiculatus

Gomphocarpus appendiculatus, commonly known as the milkweed or sticky weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, green stems that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
