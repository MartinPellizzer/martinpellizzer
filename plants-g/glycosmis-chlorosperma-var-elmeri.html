Glycosmis chlorosperma var. elmeri

Glycosmis chlorosperma var. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's green apple.

This variant is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, native to Southeast Asia, particularly in regions of Myanmar and Thailand. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are small and white, often clustered in axillary inflorescences, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
