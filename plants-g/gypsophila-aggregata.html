Gypsophila aggregata

Gypsophila aggregata, commonly known as baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, small, white or pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.

Gypsophila aggregata is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy, ethereal appearance.
