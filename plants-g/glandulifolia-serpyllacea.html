Glandulifolia serpyllacea

Glandulifolia serpyllacea, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with glandular dots that exude a sticky substance.

Glandulifolia serpyllacea is often found in grassy areas, along paths, and in disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
