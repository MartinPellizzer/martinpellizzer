Glomera acicularis

Glomera acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf glomera, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized botanical features.

It belongs to the family Glomeraceae and is distinguished by its small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi, which plays a crucial role in its nutrient absorption. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, making it well-suited for specific ecological niches.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in moist, shaded environments.
