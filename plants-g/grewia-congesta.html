Grewia congesta

Grewia congesta, commonly known as the clustered globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
