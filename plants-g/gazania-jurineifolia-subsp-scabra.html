Gazania jurineifolia subsp. scabra

Gazania jurineifolia subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough gazania, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in summer and are often surrounded by narrow, pale green bracts.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in the Aizoaceae family.
