Garcinia minahassensis

Garcinia minahassensis, commonly known as the Minahassa garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and research purposes.
