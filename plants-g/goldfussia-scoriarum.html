Goldfussia scoriarum

Goldfussia scoriarum, commonly known as the scarlet goldfussia, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, helping to reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its unique coloration and ecological role make it a notable species in its native flora.
