Genista argentea subsp. grandiflora

Genista argentea subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as silverbush grandiflora, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.

The plant is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
