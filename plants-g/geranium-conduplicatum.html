Geranium conduplicatum

Geranium conduplicatum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Geranium conduplicatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
