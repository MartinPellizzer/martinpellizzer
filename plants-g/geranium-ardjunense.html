Geranium ardjunense

Geranium ardjunense, commonly known as the Armenian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Geranium ardjunense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
