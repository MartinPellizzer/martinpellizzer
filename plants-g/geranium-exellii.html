Geranium exellii

Geranium exellii, commonly known as the Exell's geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five to seven petals in shades of pink, red, or purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Geranium exellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
