Gypsophila pacifica

Gypsophila pacifica, commonly known as the Sierra snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It has slender, branching stems and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gypsophila pacifica is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
