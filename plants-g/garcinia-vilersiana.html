Garcinia vilersiana

Garcinia vilersiana, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into sour-tasting, orange-red berries. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the fruit containing compounds like hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests and moist woodlands.

Garcinia vilersiana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
