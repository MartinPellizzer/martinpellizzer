Gnaphalium alsinoides

Gnaphalium alsinoides, commonly known as the silky willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-green, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
