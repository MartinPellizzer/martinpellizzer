Gahnia schoenoides

Gahnia schoenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and swampy areas.

Gahnia schoenoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
