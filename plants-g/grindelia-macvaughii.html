Grindelia macvaughii

Grindelia macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. It produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine.

Grindelia macvaughii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
