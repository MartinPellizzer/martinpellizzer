Gentiana lutea subsp. lutea

Gentiana lutea subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
