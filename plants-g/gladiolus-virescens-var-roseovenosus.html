Gladiolus virescens var. roseovenosus

Gladiolus virescens var. roseovenosus, commonly known as the rose-veined gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad, sword-shaped, and often have a distinctive rose-colored venation pattern, adding to its visual appeal. This variety thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both a horticultural favorite and a subject of botanical study.
