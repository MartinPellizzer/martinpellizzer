Geum aleppicum f. glabricaule

Geum aleppicum f. glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed avens, is a compact perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glabrous, giving it a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in rock gardens and alpine settings.
