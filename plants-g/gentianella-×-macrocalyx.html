Gentianella × macrocalyx

Gentianella × macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered gentian, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with prominent lobes. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. It produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
