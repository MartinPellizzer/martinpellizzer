Garaventia

Garaventia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Garaventia has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
