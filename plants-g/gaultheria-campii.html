Gaultheria campii

Gaultheria campii, commonly known as the mountain cranberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and dark green upper surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Gaultheria campii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
