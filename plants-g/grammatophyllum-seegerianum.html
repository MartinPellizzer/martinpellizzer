Grammatophyllum seegerianum

Grammatophyllum seegerianum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to two meters in length, adorned with numerous fragrant, spider-like flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of light and moisture for optimal growth. Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption, and it often grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems as a pollinator attractant.
