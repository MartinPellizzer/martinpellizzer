Geranium crispum

Geranium crispum, commonly known as the crinkled geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, crinkled leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Geranium crispum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
