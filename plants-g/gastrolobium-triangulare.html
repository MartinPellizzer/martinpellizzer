Gastrolobium triangulare

Gastrolobium triangulare, commonly known as the triangular wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets arranged in a triangular shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
