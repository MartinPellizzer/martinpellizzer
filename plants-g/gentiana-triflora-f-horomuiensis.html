Gentiana triflora f. horomuiensis

Gentiana triflora f. horomuiensis, commonly known as the Horomui gentian, is a rare flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It produces three to five blue flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
