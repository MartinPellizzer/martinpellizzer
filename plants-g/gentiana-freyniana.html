Gentiana freyniana

Gentiana freyniana, commonly known as the Freyn's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its striking blue petals are often adorned with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Gentiana freyniana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
