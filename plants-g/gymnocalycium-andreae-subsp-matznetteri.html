Gymnocalycium andreae subsp. matznetteri

Gymnocalycium andreae subsp.

matznetteri, commonly known as the Matzner's moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Argentina. It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
