Garcinia cantleyana

Garcinia cantleyana, commonly known as the Kew's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking, orange-red berries. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, with its vibrant foliage and showy flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

The fruit, while not widely consumed, has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
