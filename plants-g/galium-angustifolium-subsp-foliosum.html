Galium angustifolium subsp. foliosum

Galium angustifolium subsp.

foliosum, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. Its small, white to pinkish flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
