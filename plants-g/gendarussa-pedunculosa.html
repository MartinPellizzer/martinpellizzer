Gendarussa pedunculosa

Gendarussa pedunculosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its valuable timber, Gendarussa pedunculosa is also considered an important species for ecological and economic reasons.
