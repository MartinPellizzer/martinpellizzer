Geranium absinthoides

Geranium absinthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Geranium absinthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
