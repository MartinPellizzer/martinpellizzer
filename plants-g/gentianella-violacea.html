Gentianella violacea

Gentianella violacea, commonly known as the violet gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
