Gomphocarpus angustifolius

Gomphocarpus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking clusters of yellow flowers. The plant contains a milky sap that is toxic to some animals, including livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Gomphocarpus angustifolius plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems, particularly for certain butterfly species.
