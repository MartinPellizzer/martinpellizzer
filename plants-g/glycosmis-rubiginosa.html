Glycosmis rubiginosa

Glycosmis rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Glycosmis rubiginosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
