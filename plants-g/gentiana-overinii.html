Gentiana overinii

Gentiana overinii, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Gentiana overinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
