Geranium reichardii

Geranium reichardii, commonly known as the Reichard's geranium, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium reichardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
