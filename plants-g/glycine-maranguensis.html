Glycine maranguensis

Glycine maranguensis, commonly known as the Maranguensis soybean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a forage crop.

Due to its adaptability, it is used in both agricultural and traditional medicinal contexts.
