Gardenia rupicola

Gardenia rupicola, commonly known as the rock gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often with a glossy appearance.

Gardenia rupicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
