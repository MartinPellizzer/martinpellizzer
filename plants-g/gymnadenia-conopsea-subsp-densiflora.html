Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. densiflora

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense arrangement of flowers along the inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
