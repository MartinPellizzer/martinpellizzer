Gongora quinquenervis

Gongora quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns and a unique structure with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, while its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the moist, rocky substrates it inhabits.

Gongora quinquenervis is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and rarity in cultivation.
