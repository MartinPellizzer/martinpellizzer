Gaura suffulta var. typica

Gaura suffulta var. typica, commonly known as the showy gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, pink to rose-colored, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
