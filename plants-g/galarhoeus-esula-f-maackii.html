Galarhoeus esula f. maackii

Galarhoeus esula f. maackii, commonly known as the Maack's spurge, is a variant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and smooth, often hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and typically have a distinct midrib. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia.

This plant is native to eastern Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
