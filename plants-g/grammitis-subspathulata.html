Grammitis subspathulata

Grammitis subspathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
