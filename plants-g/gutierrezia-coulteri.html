Gutierrezia coulteri

Gutierrezia coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's galleta, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray, needle-like leaves and produces yellow, composite flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Gutierrezia coulteri is adapted to withstand drought and is commonly used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
