Gamocarpha polycephala

Gamocarpha polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
