Gardenia vernicosa

Gardenia vernicosa, commonly known as the wax gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and its showy, waxy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is evergreen and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.
