Goniopteris ancyriothrix

Goniopteris ancyriothrix, commonly known as the ancient fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the family of extinct ferns.

It is characterized by its large, complex fronds with a distinctive, often lobed structure. The plant thrived during the Carboniferous and Permian periods, contributing significantly to the development of coal deposits. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, were essential for its propagation in ancient swampy environments.

Fossilized remains of Goniopteris ancyriothrix provide valuable insights into the morphology and ecology of early terrestrial plants.
