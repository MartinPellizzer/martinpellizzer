Geum gasparrinii

Geum gasparrinii, commonly known as the Alpine avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period is brief, lasting only a few weeks, and it is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Geum gasparrinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
