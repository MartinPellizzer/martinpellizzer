Geum montanum submultiflorum

Geum montanum submultiflorum, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
