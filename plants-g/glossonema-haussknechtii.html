Glossonema haussknechtii

Glossonema haussknechtii, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that give it a ghostly appearance.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's leaves are typically 1-2 mm wide and can grow up to several centimeters in length, often floating just beneath the water's surface. Glossonema haussknechtii is known for its ability to absorb heavy metals from water, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts.
