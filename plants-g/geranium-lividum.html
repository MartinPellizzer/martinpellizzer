Geranium lividum

Geranium lividum, commonly known as the blue geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, clustered flowers throughout the summer months.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
