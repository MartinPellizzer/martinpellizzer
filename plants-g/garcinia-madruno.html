Garcinia madruno

Garcinia madruno, commonly known as the Madruno Garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia madruno thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
