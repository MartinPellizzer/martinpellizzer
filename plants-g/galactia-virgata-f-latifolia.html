Galactia virgata f. latifolia

Galactia virgata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its deep root system.
