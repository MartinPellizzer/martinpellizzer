Goeppertia clivorum

Goeppertia clivorum, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in well-draining soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clumps over time.

Goeppertia clivorum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add a lush, tropical feel to indoor spaces.
