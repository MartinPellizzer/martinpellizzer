Grevillea wickhamii subsp. aprica

Grevillea wickhamii subsp.

aprica, commonly known as the pink flowering grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
