Garcinia archboldiana

Garcinia archboldiana, commonly known as the Koa tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. Its ecological importance is significant, as it provides habitat and food for native Hawaiian wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Hawaiian culture.
