Geranium pratense var. albiflorum

Geranium pratense var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered cranesbill, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
