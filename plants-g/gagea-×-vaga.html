Gagea × vaga

Gagea × vaga, commonly known as the hybrid squawroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to regions of North America, it thrives in woodland and meadow habitats.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
