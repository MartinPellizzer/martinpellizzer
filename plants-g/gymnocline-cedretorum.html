Gymnocline cedretorum

Gymnocline cedretorum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cypress, is a rare and ancient coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary lineage, representing a relict species from the Tertiary period.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Gymnocline cedretorum is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
