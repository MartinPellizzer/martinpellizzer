Galactia lugardii

Galactia lugardii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its milk-like sap, which is toxic, has been used in traditional medicine.

Galactia lugardii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
