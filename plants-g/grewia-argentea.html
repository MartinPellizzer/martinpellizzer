Grewia argentea

Grewia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval, silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

The fruits are small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.
