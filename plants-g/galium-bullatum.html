Galium bullatum

Galium bullatum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age.

Galium bullatum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
