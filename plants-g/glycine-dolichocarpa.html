Glycine dolichocarpa

Glycine dolichocarpa, commonly known as the longpod bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, slender leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and can reach significant lengths as it climbs supports. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines, and for its role in soil nitrogen fixation.

Glycine dolichocarpa is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in agroforestry systems.
