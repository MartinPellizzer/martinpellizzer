Gymnoschoenus sphaerocephalus

Gymnoschoenus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive spherical seed heads that give it its common name. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Gymnoschoenus sphaerocephalus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
