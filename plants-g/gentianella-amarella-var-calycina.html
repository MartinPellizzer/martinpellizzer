Gentianella amarella var. calycina

Gentianella amarella var. calycina, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
