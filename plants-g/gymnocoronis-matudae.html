Gymnocoronis matudae

Gymnocoronis matudae, commonly known as Matuda's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
