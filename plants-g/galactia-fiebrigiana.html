Galactia fiebrigiana

Galactia fiebrigiana, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are encased in slender, hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Galactia fiebrigiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
