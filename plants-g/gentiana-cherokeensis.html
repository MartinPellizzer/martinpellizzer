Gentiana cherokeensis

Gentiana cherokeensis, commonly known as Cherokee gentian, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

The species is named in honor of the Cherokee Nation, reflecting its cultural significance in the region.
