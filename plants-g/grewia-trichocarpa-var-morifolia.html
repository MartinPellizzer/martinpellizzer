Grewia trichocarpa var. morifolia

Grewia trichocarpa var. morifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
