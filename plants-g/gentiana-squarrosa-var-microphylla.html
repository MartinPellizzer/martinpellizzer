Gentiana squarrosa var. microphylla

Gentiana squarrosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
