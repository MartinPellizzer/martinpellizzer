Gomphrena riparia

Gomphrena riparia, commonly known as the river gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gomphrena riparia is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
