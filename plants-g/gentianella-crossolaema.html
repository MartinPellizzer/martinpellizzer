Gentianella crossolaema

Gentianella crossolaema, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of gentians known for their vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as meadows and forest understories. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
