Grevillea aciphylla

Grevillea aciphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its unique foliage and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Grevillea aciphylla is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
