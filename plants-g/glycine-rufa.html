Glycine rufa

Glycine rufa, commonly known as the redstem cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Glycine rufa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
