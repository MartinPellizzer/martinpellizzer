Gymnosporia lanceolata

Gymnosporia lanceolata, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Gymnosporia lanceolata is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological value as a habitat for various insects and birds.
