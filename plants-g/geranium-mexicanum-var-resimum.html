Geranium mexicanum var. resimum

Geranium mexicanum var. resimum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from spring to summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
