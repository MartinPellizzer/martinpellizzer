Gustavia fastuosa

Gustavia fastuosa, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Gustavia fastuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
