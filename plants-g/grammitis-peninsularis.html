Grammitis peninsularis

Grammitis peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsula Grammitis, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread along the forest floor or among rocks.

Grammitis peninsularis is notable for its intricate leaf venation and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
