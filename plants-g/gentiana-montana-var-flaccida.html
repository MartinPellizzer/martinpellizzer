Gentiana montana var. flaccida

Gentiana montana var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat flexible.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
