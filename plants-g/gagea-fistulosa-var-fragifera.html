Gagea fistulosa var. fragifera

Gagea fistulosa var. fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often fragrant and used in traditional herbal practices. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
