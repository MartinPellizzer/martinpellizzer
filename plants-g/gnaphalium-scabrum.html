Gnaphalium scabrum

Gnaphalium scabrum, commonly known as rough cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its widespread distribution.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
