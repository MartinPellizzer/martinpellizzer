Galeandra batemanii

Galeandra batemanii, commonly known as the Bateman's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves and a single, erect stem that bears several flower spikes. Galeandra batemanii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and unique appearance.

Conservation efforts are crucial for its survival, as it faces threats from habitat loss and illegal collection.
