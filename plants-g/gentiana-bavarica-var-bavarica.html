Gentiana bavarica var. bavarica

Gentiana bavarica var. bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
