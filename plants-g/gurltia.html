Gurltia

Gurltia, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features striking silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, aiding in its survival in hot, dry climates. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink during the spring season. Gurltia thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical classification places it within the Orchidaceae family, highlighting its evolutionary adaptations to harsh environments.
