Gentiana antipoda

Gentiana antipoda, commonly known as the Antarctic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subantarctic environments, thriving in harsh, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana antipoda is notable for its ability to survive in extreme climates, making it a symbol of resilience in botanical studies.
