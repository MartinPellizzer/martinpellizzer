Gloriosa littonioides

Gloriosa littonioides, commonly known as the African flame flower, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Colocasiaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular, flame-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features heart-shaped, arrow-headed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
