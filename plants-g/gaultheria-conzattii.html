Gaultheria conzattii

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible but have a sharp, peppery flavor. Gaultheria conzattii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Its ornamental value and unique flavor make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
