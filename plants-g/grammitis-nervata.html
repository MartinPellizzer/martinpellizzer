Grammitis nervata

Grammitis nervata, commonly known as the slender fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the central stem. Its fronds are usually slender and have a soft, velvety texture.

Grammitis nervata is characterized by its intricate venation and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
