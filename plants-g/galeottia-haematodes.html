Galeottia haematodes

Galeottia haematodes, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a single, fleshy, reddish rootstock from which a single stem emerges. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

The flowers are showy, with five bright yellow petals surrounding a cluster of stamens and a prominent central pistil.
