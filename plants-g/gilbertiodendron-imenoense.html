Gilbertiodendron imenoense

Gilbertiodendron imenoense, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
