Gentiana andersonii

Gentiana andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in open woods or along stream banks. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or purple flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
