Geranium phaeum f. hungaricum

Geranium phaeum f. hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features dark green, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and modest yet charming floral display.
