Geranium suaveolens

Geranium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features palmate leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Geranium suaveolens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
