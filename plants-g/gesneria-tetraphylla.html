Gesneria tetraphylla

Gesneria tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Gesneria tetraphylla is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
