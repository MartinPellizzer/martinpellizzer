Gigantochloa latifolia var. alba

Gigantochloa latifolia var. alba, commonly known as the white giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in landscaping and traditional crafts.
