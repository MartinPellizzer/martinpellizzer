Genianthus trullatus

Genianthus trullatus, commonly known as the woolly genianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces numerous small, fragrant blooms during the spring and summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
