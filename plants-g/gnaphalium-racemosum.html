Gnaphalium racemosum

Gnaphalium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blossoms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in a raceme, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.
