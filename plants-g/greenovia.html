Greenovia

Greenovia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its succulent leaves and vibrant, often showy flowers. The plant produces small, star-shaped blooms in shades of pink, yellow, and white, which appear during the cooler months. Greenovia thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
