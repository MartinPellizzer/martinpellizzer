Goodenia incana

Goodenia incana, commonly known as the silver goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Goodenia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
