Gentiana clusii subsp. undulatifolia

Gentiana clusii subsp.

undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavyleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a wavy edge, giving it its subspecies name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
