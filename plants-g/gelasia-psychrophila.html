Gelasia psychrophila

Gelasia psychrophila, commonly known as the cold-loving orchid, is a rare flowering plant adapted to extreme cold environments.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is found in high-altitude regions of South America. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom during the short summer months. Its leaves are typically dense and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species with limited distribution.
