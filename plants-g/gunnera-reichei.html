Gunnera reichei

Gunnera reichei, commonly known as the Andean giant water shield, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence. The plant thrives in moist, cool environments, often found near streams and lakes in the Andes. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against intense sunlight.

Gunnera reichei is also notable for its small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
