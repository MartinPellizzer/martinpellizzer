Gentianodes tetraphylla

Gentianodes tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
