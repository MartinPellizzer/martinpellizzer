Grewia xanthopetala

Grewia xanthopetala, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia xanthopetala is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
