Genista tinctoria var. platyphylla

Genista tinctoria var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are broad and oval, distinguishing it from other varieties of Genista. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
