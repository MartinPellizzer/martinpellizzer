Gilia aliquanta

Gilia aliquanta, commonly known as the narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gilia aliquanta is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
