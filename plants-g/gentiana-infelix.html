Gentiana infelix

Gentiana infelix, commonly known as the sorrowful gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana infelix is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
