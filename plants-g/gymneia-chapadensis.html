Gymneia chapadensis

Gymneia chapadensis, commonly known as the Chapadense Gymneia, is a flowering plant native to the cerrado biome of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Gymneia chapadensis is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

It plays a minor role in the local ecosystem but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
