Grevillea quercifolia

Grevillea quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange cylindrical flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea quercifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
