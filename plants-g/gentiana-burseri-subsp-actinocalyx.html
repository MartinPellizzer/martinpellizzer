Gentiana burseri subsp. actinocalyx

Gentiana burseri subsp.

actinocalyx, commonly known as the Ethiopian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
