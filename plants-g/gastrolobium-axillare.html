Gastrolobium axillare

Gastrolobium axillare, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
