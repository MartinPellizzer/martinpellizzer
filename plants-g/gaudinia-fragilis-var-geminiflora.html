Gaudinia fragilis var. geminiflora

Gaudinia fragilis var. geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered gaudinia, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear multiple small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its fragility and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.
