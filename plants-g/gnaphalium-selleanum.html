Gnaphalium selleanum

Gnaphalium selleanum, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fine, silvery-white down on its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.

Gnaphalium selleanum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
