Goeppertia pulchella

Goeppertia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, velvety foliage that comes in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia pulchella is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
