Gnaphalium purpureum var. sphacelatum

Gnaphalium purpureum var. sphacelatum, commonly known as purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. It produces a distinctive, aromatic essential oil, which contributes to its use in traditional medicine.

The variety sphacelatum is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and slightly different flower structure compared to the typical form.
