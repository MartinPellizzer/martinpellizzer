Garcinia parviflora

Garcinia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, making it a distinctive species within its genus. Garcinia parviflora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
