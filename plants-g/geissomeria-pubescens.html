Geissomeria pubescens

Geissomeria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
