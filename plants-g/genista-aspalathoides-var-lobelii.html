Genista aspalathoides var. lobelii

Genista aspalathoides var. lobelii, commonly known as the Cape broom, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
