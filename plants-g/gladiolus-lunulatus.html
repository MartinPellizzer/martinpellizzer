Gladiolus lunulatus

Gladiolus lunulatus, commonly known as the moon crescent gladiolus, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive pale crescent mark on the petals. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
