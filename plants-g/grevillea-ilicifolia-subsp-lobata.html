Grevillea ilicifolia subsp. lobata

Grevillea ilicifolia subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

The plant produces striking red or orange inflorescences that are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
