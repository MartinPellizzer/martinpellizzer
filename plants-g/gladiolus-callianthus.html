Gladiolus callianthus

Gladiolus callianthus, commonly known as the sweet gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and white. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Gladiolus callianthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
