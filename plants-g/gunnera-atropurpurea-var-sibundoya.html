Gunnera atropurpurea var. sibundoya

Gunnera atropurpurea var. sibundoya, commonly known as the purple giant dock, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three feet in diameter, often with a deep purple or reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces large, showy flower clusters in late summer, typically in shades of yellow or orange, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic foliage.

Due to its size and vigor, it requires ample space and consistent moisture to grow successfully.
