Gentiana asclepiadea

Gentiana asclepiadea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The genus Gentiana is known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers, and Gentiana asclepiadea is no exception.
