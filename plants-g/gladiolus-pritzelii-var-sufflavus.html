Gladiolus pritzelii var. sufflavus

Gladiolus pritzelii var. sufflavus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The variety sufflavus is distinguished by its pale yellow to orange floral coloration, which sets it apart from other gladiolus species. This plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of flowers that add a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold, colorful displays.
