Geranium mexicanum var. typicum

Geranium mexicanum var. typicum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces vibrant red or pink blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
