Gmelina palawensis var. celebica

Gmelina palawensis var. celebica, commonly known as the Celebes ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree typically grows in lowland to montane forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and fragrant, appearing in clusters.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
