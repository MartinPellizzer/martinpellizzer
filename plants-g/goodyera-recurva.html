Goodyera recurva

Goodyera recurva, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and a dense, spiraling inflorescence of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, organic soil. It has a complex root system that supports its growth in low-light conditions.

Goodyera recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
