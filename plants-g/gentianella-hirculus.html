Gentianella hirculus

Gentianella hirculus, commonly known as the goat's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or grassy areas. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella hirculus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and resilience.
