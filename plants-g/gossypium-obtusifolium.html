Gossypium obtusifolium

Gossypium obtusifolium, commonly known as the woolly cotton or broadleaf cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. It produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Gossypium obtusifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
