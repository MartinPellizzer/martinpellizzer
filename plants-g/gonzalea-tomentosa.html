Gonzalea tomentosa

Gonzalea tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrains.

Gonzalea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
