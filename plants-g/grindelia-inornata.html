Grindelia inornata

Grindelia inornata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts. It produces a resinous sap that can be sticky and fragrant, contributing to its ecological and historical significance.

Grindelia inornata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
