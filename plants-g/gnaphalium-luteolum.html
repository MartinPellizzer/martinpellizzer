Gnaphalium luteolum

Gnaphalium luteolum, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its aromatic, fragrant foliage.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
