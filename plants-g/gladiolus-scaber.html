Gladiolus scaber

Gladiolus scaber, commonly known as the rough gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name "scaber," which means rough.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
