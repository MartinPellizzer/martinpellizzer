Gomphandra oligantha

Gomphandra oligantha, commonly known as the slender gomphandra, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast against its pale, almost white flowers. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Gomphandra oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
