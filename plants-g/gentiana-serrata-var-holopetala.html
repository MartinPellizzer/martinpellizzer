Gentiana serrata var. holopetala

Gentiana serrata var. holopetala, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which are adapted to withstand cold and high-altitude conditions. Its flowers are arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.
