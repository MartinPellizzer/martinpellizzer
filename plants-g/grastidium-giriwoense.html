Grastidium giriwoense

Grastidium giriwoense, commonly known as the Giriwoen ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the dense, misty forests of the Giriwoen region.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that exhibit a unique bioluminescent property under moonlight. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the trunks of ancient trees. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf sheaths, making it difficult to study in the wild.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a flagship species for conservation efforts in its habitat.
