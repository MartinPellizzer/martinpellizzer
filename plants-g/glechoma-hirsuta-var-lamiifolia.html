Glechoma hirsuta var. lamiifolia

Glechoma hirsuta var. lamiifolia, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are edible and have a mildly sour taste, making it a popular plant in some traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
