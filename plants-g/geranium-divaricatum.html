Geranium divaricatum

Geranium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
