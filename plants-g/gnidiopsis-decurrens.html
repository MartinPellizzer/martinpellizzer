Gnidiopsis decurrens

Gnidiopsis decurrens, commonly known as the creeping thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gnidiopsis decurrens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
