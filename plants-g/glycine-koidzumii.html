Glycine koidzumii

Glycine koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese climbing bean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread vigorously. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. Glycine koidzumii thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
