Gentiana quinquefolia f. lutescens

Gentiana quinquefolia f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a variant of the blue gentian species native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical blue coloration of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in late summer.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
