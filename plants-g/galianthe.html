Galianthe

Galianthe, commonly known as the golden trumpet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galianthe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
