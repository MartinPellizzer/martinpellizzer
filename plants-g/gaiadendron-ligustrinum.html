Gaiadendron ligustrinum

Gaiadendron ligustrinum, commonly known as the white-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by bright orange or red stamens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Gaiadendron ligustrinum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
