Gentiana pubicaulis

Gentiana pubicaulis, commonly known as the pubescent gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it the species name "pubicaulis," which means "hairy.".

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.
