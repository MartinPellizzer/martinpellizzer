Gleditsia coccinea

Gleditsia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a reddish tinge when young. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in urban environments.
