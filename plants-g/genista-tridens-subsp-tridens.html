Genista tridens subsp. tridens

Genista tridens subsp.

tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
