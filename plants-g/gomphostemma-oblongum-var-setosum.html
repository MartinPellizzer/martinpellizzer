Gomphostemma oblongum var. setosum

Gomphostemma oblongum var. setosum, commonly known as the hairy gomphostemma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
