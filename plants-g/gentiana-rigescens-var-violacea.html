Gentiana rigescens var. violacea

Gentiana rigescens var. violacea, commonly known as the violet gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
