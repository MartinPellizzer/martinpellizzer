Gregoria vitaliana var. praetutiana

Gregoria vitaliana var. praetutiana, commonly known as the Praetutian Starbloom, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to soft pink, often with intricate patterns resembling celestial motifs. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is known for its delicate, fern-like foliage that adds to its ornamental appeal. It is typically found in rocky crevices and shaded forest understories, where it forms compact, low-growing clumps.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
