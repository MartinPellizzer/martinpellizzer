Galpinsia

Galpinsia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. Its bark is smooth and grey, becoming fissured with age.

Galpinsia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
