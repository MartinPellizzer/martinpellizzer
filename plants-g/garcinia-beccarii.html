Garcinia beccarii

Garcinia beccarii, commonly known as the Borneo tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The fruit is a small, rough-skinned berry that turns red when mature, containing a single large seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
