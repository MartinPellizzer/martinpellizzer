Gurania pittieri

Gurania pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry climates, featuring succulent leaves that store water. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gurania pittieri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
