Gloxinia multiflora

Gloxinia multiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate. Its tuberous roots allow it to store nutrients and water, making it resilient in varying conditions.

Gloxinia multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
