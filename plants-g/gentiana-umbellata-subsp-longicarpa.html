Gentiana umbellata subsp. longicarpa

Gentiana umbellata subsp.

longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, cylindrical fruits, which set it apart from other gentian species.
