Gynophorea

Gynophorea, commonly known as the "female flower" or "female plant," is a term used in botany to describe plants that produce exclusively female flowers.

These plants are typically found in specific ecological niches where pollination is facilitated by other species that provide the necessary male gametes. The botanical structure of Gynophorea includes specialized floral morphology that supports efficient pollination by male plants. They often exhibit unique reproductive strategies to ensure genetic diversity and survival.

This plant plays a vital role in certain ecosystems by contributing to the reproductive cycle of plant communities.
