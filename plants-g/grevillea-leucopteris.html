Grevillea leucopteris

Grevillea leucopteris, commonly known as the white-veined grevillea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage that ranges from bright green to silver-white, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, red or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Grevillea leucopteris thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its status as a valued native plant in horticulture.
