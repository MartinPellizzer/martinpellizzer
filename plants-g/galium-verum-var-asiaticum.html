Galium verum var. asiaticum

Galium verum var. asiaticum, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium verum var. asiaticum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
