Graptophyllum pictum

Graptophyllum pictum, commonly known as the painted rock cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in bright, colorful tubercles that resemble small, rounded hills. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
