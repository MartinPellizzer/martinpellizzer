Gentiana mesembrianthemoides

Gentiana mesembrianthemoides, commonly known as the African gentian, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
