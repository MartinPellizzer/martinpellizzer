Gentiana baeuerlenii subsp. bredboensis

Gentiana baeuerlenii subsp.

bredboensis, commonly known as the Bredbo gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
