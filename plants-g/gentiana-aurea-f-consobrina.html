Gentiana aurea f. consobrina

Gentiana aurea f. consobrina, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
