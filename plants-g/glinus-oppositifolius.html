Glinus oppositifolius

Glinus oppositifolius, commonly known as the oppositifolius morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Glinus oppositifolius is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
