Guzmania complanata

Guzmania complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaved torch plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble a torch, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has a rosette-shaped foliage that collects rainwater, providing a habitat for small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are typically broad and flat, contributing to its common name.

Guzmania complanata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
