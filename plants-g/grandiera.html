Grandiera

Grandiera, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Mexico, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often floating on the water's surface.

The flowers have a pleasant fragrance and are used in traditional ceremonies and as ornamental plants.
