Gymnocalycium pungens

Gymnocalycium pungens, commonly known as the Pungent Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, globose stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.

Gymnocalycium pungens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
