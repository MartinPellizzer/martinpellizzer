Geranium robustipes

Geranium robustipes, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Geranium robustipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
