Galium hyrcanicum var. puberulum

Galium hyrcanicum var. puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.
