Gentianella myriantha

Gentianella myriantha, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
