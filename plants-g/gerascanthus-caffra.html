Gerascanthus caffra

Gerascanthus caffra, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

Gerascanthus caffra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
