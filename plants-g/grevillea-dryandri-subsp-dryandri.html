Grevillea dryandri subsp. dryandri

Grevillea dryandri subsp.

dryandri, commonly known as the dryandra grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color and are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months. It is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
