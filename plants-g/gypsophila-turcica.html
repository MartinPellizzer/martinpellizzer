Gypsophila turcica

Gypsophila turcica, commonly known as the Turkish laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gypsophila turcica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
