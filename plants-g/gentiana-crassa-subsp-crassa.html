Gentiana crassa subsp. crassa

Gentiana crassa subsp.

crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
