Gardenia riparia

Gardenia riparia, commonly known as the river gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their strong, sweet scent.

Gardenia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
