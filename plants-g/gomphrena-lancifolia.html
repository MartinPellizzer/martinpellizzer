Gomphrena lancifolia

Gomphrena lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, spiky, pink or red flowers that resemble globes, giving it its common name. The leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name "lancifolia," and are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
