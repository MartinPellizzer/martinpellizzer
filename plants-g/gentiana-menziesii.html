Gentiana menziesii

Gentiana menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and erect.

Gentiana menziesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
