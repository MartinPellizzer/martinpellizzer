Grevillea preissii

Grevillea preissii, commonly known as the 'Coastal Grevillea', is a shrub native to coastal regions of Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea preissii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
