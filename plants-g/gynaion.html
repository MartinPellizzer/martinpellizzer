Gynaion

Gynaion, commonly known as the "female flower" in certain botanical contexts, refers to the reproductive structure in some plants that contains the ovary.

It is typically found in species where the flower has separate male and female parts, such as in some gymnosperms and angiosperms. The gynaion consists of the carpel, which houses the ovules that develop into seeds after fertilization. It plays a crucial role in the plant's reproductive cycle by facilitating seed formation.

The structure of the gynaion can vary significantly among different plant species, reflecting adaptations to various pollination strategies.
