Gentiana arethusae var. rotundatolobata

Gentiana arethusae var. rotundatolobata, commonly known as the rounded-lobed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically rounded and lobed, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
