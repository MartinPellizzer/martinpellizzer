Gymnanthemum theophrastifolium var. richardianum

Gymnanthemum theophrastifolium var. richardianum, commonly known as the Richard's rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
