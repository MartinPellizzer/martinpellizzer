Genipa talangninia

Genipa talangninia, commonly known as the Philippine genipapo, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Geniaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a unique, sweet, and slightly sour flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local medicine.
