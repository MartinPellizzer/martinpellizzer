Gentiana gayi subsp. magellanica

Gentiana gayi subsp.

magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic gentian, is a perennial herb native to southern South America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often unbranched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
