Gerbera amabilis

Gerbera amabilis, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. The plant typically features bright-colored petals in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a central disk of darker florets. Gerbera amabilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
