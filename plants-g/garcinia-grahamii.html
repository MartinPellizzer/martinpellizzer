Garcinia grahamii

Garcinia grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it a subject of interest in both botanical research and potential medicinal applications.

Garcinia grahamii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.
