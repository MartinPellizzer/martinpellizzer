Gnaphalium pensylvanicum

Gnaphalium pensylvanicum, commonly known as Pennsylvania everlasting, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale blue bracts. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The foliage is typically gray-green with a woolly texture, and the plant is known for its long-lasting flowers that remain intact even when dried.
