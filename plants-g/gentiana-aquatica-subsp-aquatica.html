Gentiana aquatica subsp. aquatica

Gentiana aquatica subsp.

aquatica, commonly known as the water gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and lake margins.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in many regions.
