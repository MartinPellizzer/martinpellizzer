Gynopogon coriaceus

Gynopogon coriaceus, commonly known as the rough leaved everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its wiry, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in floral arrangements.
