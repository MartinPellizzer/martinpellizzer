Georgina coccinea var. crocea

Georgina coccinea var. crocea, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright red, spiky flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that adds texture to the plant's appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant color and architectural form make it a standout in both garden and landscape settings.
