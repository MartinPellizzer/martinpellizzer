Glyceria × burdonii

Glyceria × burdonii, commonly known as the Burdon's glyceria, is a hybrid grass species native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it monoecious. It thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Glyceria × burdonii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
