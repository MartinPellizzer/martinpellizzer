Gandasulium densiflorum

Gandasulium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower evergreen, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody stem with a thick, fibrous bark that provides structural support. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Gandasulium densiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
