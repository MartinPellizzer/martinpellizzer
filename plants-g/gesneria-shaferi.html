Gesneria shaferi

Gesneria shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers and thick, often fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Gesneria shaferi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
