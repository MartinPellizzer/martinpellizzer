Galium × ochroleucum var. paulianum

Galium × ochroleucum var. paulianum, commonly known as the white wood-sorrel, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Galium ochroleucum and Galium paulianum.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.
