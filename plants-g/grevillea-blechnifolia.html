Grevillea blechnifolia

Grevillea blechnifolia, commonly known as the fern-leafed Grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that resemble fern fronds, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea blechnifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
