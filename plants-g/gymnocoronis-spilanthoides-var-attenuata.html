Gymnocoronis spilanthoides var. attenuata

Gymnocoronis spilanthoides var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender spilanthus, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
