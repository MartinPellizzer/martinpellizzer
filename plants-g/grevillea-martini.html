Grevillea martini

Grevillea martini, commonly known as the Red Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea martini is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
