Goodenia scapigera subsp. scapigera

Goodenia scapigera subsp.

scapigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
