Gleichenia aequilaterale

Gleichenia aequilaterale, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its long, narrow fronds with a distinctive, bipinnate structure. The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant reproduces via spores, which are released from sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.
