Gaultheria leucocarpa f. scandens

Gaultheria leucocarpa f. scandens, commonly known as the creeping strawberry bush, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by edible, bright red berries that are tart and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
