Gentiana aquatica subsp. alba

Gentiana aquatica subsp.

alba, commonly known as the white water gentian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with wet soil conditions.
