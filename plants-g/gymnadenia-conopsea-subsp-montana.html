Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. montana

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain lady's tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that form in a loose spiral.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is a rhizomatous species, with underground stems that help it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
