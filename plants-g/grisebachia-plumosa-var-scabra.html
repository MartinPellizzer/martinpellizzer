Grisebachia plumosa var. scabra

Grisebachia plumosa var. scabra, commonly known as the hairy palm or scabrous palm, is a unique succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy, and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically gray-green in color and have a rough, scabrous texture, which is a defining feature of the variety. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.

This species is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for xeriscaping.
