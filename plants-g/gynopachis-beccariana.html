Gynopachis beccariana

Gynopachis beccariana, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. The plant features a fibrous root system and erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and the leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy.

Gynopachis beccariana is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
