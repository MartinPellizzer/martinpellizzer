Genista aegyptiaca

Genista aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.

Genista aegyptiaca is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
