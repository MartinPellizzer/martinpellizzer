Gladiolus floribundus

Gladiolus floribundus, commonly known as the clustered gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear multiple spikes of showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of bright pink, red, or white flowers. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Gladiolus floribundus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
