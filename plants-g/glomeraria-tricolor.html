Glomeraria tricolor

Glomeraria tricolor, commonly known as the three-color glomeraria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features three distinct color zones in its flower—white, yellow, and red—giving it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Glomeraria tricolor is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun.
