Galax aphylla

Galax aphylla, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Galax aphylla is an important component of forest understory ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
