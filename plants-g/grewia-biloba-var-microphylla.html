Grewia biloba var. microphylla

Grewia biloba var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
