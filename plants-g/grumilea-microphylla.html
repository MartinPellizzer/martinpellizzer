Grumilea microphylla

Grumilea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf grumihira, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Grumilea microphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

It prefers warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
