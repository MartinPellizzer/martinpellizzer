Grammitis cryptocarpa

Grammitis cryptocarpa, commonly known as the clustered maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, clustered manner. The plant produces small, round spore cases called sporangia, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis cryptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
