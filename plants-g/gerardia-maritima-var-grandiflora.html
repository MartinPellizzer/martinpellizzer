Gerardia maritima var. grandiflora

Gerardia maritima var. grandiflora, commonly known as coastal gerardia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it a hardy ornamental plant for coastal gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
