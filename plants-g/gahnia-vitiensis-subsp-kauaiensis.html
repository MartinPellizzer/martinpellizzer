Gahnia vitiensis subsp. kauaiensis

Gahnia vitiensis subsp.

The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green or reddish in color.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
