Galium verum var. pseudocruciata

Galium verum var. pseudocruciata, commonly known as golden bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
