Gymnanthocereus hernandezii

Gymnanthocereus hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
