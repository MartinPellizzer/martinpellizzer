Goodyera angustifolia

Goodyera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pteridophyte, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, strap-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Goodyera angustifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique rhizomatous growth habit.
