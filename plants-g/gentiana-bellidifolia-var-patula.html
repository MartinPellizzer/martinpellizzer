Gentiana bellidifolia var. patula

Gentiana bellidifolia var. patula, commonly known as the Patula gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine white down.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
