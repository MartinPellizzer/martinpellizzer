Gilibertia oligantha

Gilibertia oligantha, commonly known as the "tropical grass" or "sugar cane grass," is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Gilibertia oligantha is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
