Grammitis longipinnata

Grammitis longipinnata, commonly known as the long-pinnate filmy fern, is a species of epiphytic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often translucent. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Grammitis longipinnata plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
