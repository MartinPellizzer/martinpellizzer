Gouania mangarevica

Gouania mangarevica, commonly known as the New Zealand native goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Gouania mangarevica plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native insects and birds.
