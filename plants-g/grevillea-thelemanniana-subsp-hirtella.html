Grevillea thelemanniana subsp. hirtella

Grevillea thelemanniana subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the Hirtella Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
