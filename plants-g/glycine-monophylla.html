Glycine monophylla

Glycine monophylla, commonly known as the white sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to the southwestern United States.

It produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring support for its climbing habit.

Glycine monophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
