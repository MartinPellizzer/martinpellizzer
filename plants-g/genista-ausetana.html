Genista ausetana

Genista ausetana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by elongated seed pods. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it ecologically and horticulturally significant.
