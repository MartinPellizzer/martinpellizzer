Gonocalyx pulcher

Gonocalyx pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a sturdy, straight trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

Gonocalyx pulcher is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
