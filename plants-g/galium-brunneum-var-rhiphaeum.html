Galium brunneum var. rhiphaeum

Galium brunneum var. rhiphaeum, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often reddish in color, covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium brunneum var. rhiphaeum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
