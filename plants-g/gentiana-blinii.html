Gentiana blinii

Gentiana blinii, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana blinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
