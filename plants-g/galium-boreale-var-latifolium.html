Galium boreale var. latifolium

Galium boreale var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf northern bedstraw, is a herbaceous plant native to cold climates of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arctic regions. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped. It has a low-growing habit with stolons that help it spread across the ground.

This species is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
