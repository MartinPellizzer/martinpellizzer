Gerbera serotina

Gerbera serotina, commonly known as the late gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and white, and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Gerbera serotina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
