Geum × hortigenum

Geum × hortigenum, commonly known as the "Hortigenum buttercup," is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features palmate leaves with three to five lobes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage, which contribute to its widespread cultivation.
