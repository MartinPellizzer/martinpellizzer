Glechonion hederaceum

Glechonion hederaceum, commonly known as the creeping woodsorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside, and it produces small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it spreads via runners.

Glechonion hederaceum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
