Geranium corsicum

Geranium corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium corsicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular plant among gardeners.
