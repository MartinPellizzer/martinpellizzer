Gentiana septentrionalis

Gentiana septentrionalis, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
