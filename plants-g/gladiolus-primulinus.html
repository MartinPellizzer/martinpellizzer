Gladiolus primulinus

Gladiolus primulinus, commonly known as the golden gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive central stripe, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
