Geissorhiza bracteata

Geissorhiza bracteata, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, leaf-like bracts that give it its scientific name, and it is known for its ornamental value.

Geissorhiza bracteata is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
