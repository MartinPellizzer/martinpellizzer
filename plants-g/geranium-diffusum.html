Geranium diffusum

Geranium diffusum, commonly known as the spreading geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that often spread horizontally, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or violet flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium diffusum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
