Gonolobus eriocladon

Gonolobus eriocladon, commonly known as the velvetleaf gourd, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its fruit is a round, green gourd that can be harvested when mature.

Gonolobus eriocladon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
