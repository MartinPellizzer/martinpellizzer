Gunnarorchis perpusilla

Gunnarorchis perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, pale green to white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements.
