Gladiolus brachyandrus

Gladiolus brachyandrus, commonly known as the short-angled gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces spikes of flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also appreciated for its role in horticultural diversity.
