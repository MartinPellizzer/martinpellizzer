Geum radiatum var. peckii

Geum radiatum var. peckii, commonly known as Peck's fireweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
