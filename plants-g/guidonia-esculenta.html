Guidonia esculenta

Guidonia esculenta, commonly known as the edible fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its unique, clustered inflorescences that develop into edible figs. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are not only a food source for humans but also attract various wildlife species.

The tree can reach heights of up to 10 meters and is often cultivated for both its ornamental and culinary value.
