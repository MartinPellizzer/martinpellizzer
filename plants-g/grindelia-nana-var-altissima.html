Grindelia nana var. altissima

Grindelia nana var. altissima, commonly known as the tall dwarf gumweed, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
