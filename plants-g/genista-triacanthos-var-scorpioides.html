Genista triacanthos var. scorpioides

Genista triacanthos var. scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpionbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought make it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
