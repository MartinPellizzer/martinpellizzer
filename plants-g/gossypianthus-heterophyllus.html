Gossypianthus heterophyllus

Gossypianthus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable cottonbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
