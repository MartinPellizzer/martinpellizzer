Gentiana scabra f. procumbens

Gentiana scabra f. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a prostrate or creeping habit, with stems that lie close to the ground and produce clusters of blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine pubescence, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest floors or rocky habitats.

This variant is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
