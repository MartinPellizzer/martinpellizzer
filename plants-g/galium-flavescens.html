Galium flavescens

Galium flavescens, commonly known as yellow woodsorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive yellowish-green hue.

Small white flowers with five petals appear in clusters, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.
