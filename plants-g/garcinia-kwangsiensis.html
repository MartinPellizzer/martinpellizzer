Garcinia kwangsiensis

Garcinia kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to China, specifically the Guangxi region.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating. Garcinia kwangsiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique and intriguing species within its native habitat.
