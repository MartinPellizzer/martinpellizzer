Guzmania cerrohoyaensis

Guzmania cerrohoyaensis, commonly known as the Cerro Hoya guzmania, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among bromeliad enthusiasts.
