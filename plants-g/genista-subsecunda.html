Genista subsecunda

Genista subsecunda, commonly known as the creeping evergreen broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value in gardens.
