Gagea longifolia

Gagea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, and it produces small, white or pale pink berries that are dispersed by birds.

Gagea longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
