Grindelia argentina

Grindelia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine gumweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. Grindelia argentina is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
