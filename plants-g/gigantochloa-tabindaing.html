Gigantochloa tabindaing

Gigantochloa tabindaing, commonly known as the Tana River grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
