Geranium molle var. stipulare

Geranium molle var. stipulare, commonly known as the soft geranium or velvet geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
