Gerbera abyssinica

Gerbera abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian gerbera, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white, pink, or red petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy.

Gerbera abyssinica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
