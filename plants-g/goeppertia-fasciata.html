Goeppertia fasciata

Goeppertia fasciata, commonly known as the Zebra Canna, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature bold white and green stripes. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Goeppertia fasciata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
