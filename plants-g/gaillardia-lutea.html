Gaillardia lutea

Gaillardia lutea, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant features bright orange and red ray flowers surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers. Gaillardia lutea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
