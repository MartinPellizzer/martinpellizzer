Garcinia clusiifolia

Garcinia clusiifolia, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are typically covered with a thin, yellowish epicarp. Garcinia clusiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular species in horticulture and ecological studies.
