Geodorum eulophioides

Geodorum eulophioides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
