Geranium pratense var. litwinowii

Geranium pratense var. litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
