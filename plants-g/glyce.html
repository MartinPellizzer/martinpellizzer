Glyce

Glyce, commonly known as glycyrrhiza, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe, Asia, and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sometimes hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza is well-known for its root, which is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Its botanical properties make it a valuable resource in both pharmaceutical and culinary applications.
