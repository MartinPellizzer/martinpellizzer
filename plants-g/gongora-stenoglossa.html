Gongora stenoglossa

Gongora stenoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a narrow, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rock faces or in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid conditions.

Gongora stenoglossa is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration.
