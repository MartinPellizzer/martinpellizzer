Gomphrena argentina

Gomphrena argentina, commonly known as the Argentine globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, typically ranging in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Gomphrena argentina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
