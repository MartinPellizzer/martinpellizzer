Gnaphalium grayi

Gnaphalium grayi, commonly known as Gray's featherweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that are often mistaken for cotton or down. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
