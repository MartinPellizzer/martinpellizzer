Geranium parvulum

Geranium parvulum, commonly known as the small geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium parvulum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
