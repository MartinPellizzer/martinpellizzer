Goniopteris hastata

Goniopteris hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a fern species belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in moist, shaded areas.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
