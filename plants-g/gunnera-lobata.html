Gunnera lobata

Gunnera lobata, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The leaves are typically a deep green with a rough, textured surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic foliage.
