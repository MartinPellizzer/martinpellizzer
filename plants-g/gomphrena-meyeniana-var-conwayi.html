Gomphrena meyeniana var. conwayi

Gomphrena meyeniana var. conwayi, commonly known as the Conway's gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, scale-like bracts that form a dense, spherical or cylindrical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural displays.
