Genipa grandiflora

Genipa grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or genipapo, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible, though the pulp is typically consumed after the seeds have been removed.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ornate foliage and seasonal fruiting.
