Gutierrezia sarothrae var. pauciflora

Gutierrezia sarothrae var. pauciflora, commonly known as narrowleaf galleta, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
