Gerardia havanensis

Gerardia havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a slightly hairy texture.
