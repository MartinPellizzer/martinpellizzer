Grevillea lawrenceana subsp. lawrenceana

Grevillea lawrenceana subsp.

lawrenceana, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, long-lasting inflorescences that range from yellow to red in color.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. It is well adapted to the harsh coastal environment, with salt-tolerant properties and drought resistance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal landscaping.
