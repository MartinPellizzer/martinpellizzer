Grewia messinica

Grewia messinica, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
