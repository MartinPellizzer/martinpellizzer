Gentianodes szechenyi

Gentianodes szechenyi, commonly known as the Szechenyi's gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
