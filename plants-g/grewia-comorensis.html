Grewia comorensis

Grewia comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian gum tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and on limestone soils.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of gum resin.
