Gentiana heleonastes

Gentiana heleonastes, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana heleonastes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
