Gomphrena amaranthoides

Gomphrena amaranthoides, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sturdy and often branched.

Gomphrena amaranthoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
