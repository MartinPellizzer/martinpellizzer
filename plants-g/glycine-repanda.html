Glycine repanda

Glycine repanda, commonly known as the climbing peanut vine, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to peanuts. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Glycine repanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
