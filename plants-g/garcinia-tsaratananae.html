Garcinia tsaratananae

Garcinia tsaratananae, commonly known as the Madagascar garcinia, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp.

Garcinia tsaratananae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties due to its high content of hydroxycitric acid.
