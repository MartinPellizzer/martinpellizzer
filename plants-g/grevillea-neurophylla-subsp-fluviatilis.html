Grevillea neurophylla subsp. fluviatilis

Grevillea neurophylla subsp.

fluviatilis, commonly known as the river grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
