Gomphrena decumbens f. aureiflora

Gomphrena decumbens f. aureiflora, commonly known as the golden ball gomphrena, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, spherical inflorescences.

The plant produces vibrant, papery bracts that range in color from red to pink, often with a golden hue at the base, giving it its common name. It is native to South America and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. The foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
