Gouania latifolia

Gouania latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gouania, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus, and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Gouania latifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
