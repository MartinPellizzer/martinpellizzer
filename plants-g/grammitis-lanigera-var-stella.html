Grammitis lanigera var. stella

Grammitis lanigera var. stella, commonly known as the star grammitis, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its distinctive, finely divided, feathery fronds. The leaves are usually covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shaded garden settings.
