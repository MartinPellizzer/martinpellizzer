Gilia squarrosa

Gilia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Miridae family and typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.
