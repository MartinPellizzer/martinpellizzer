Geranium stevenii

Geranium stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Geranium stevenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
