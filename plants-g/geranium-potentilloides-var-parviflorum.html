Geranium potentilloides var. parviflorum

Geranium potentilloides var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a velvety texture.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, woodland environments.
