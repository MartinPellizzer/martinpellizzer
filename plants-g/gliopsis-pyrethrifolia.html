Gliopsis pyrethrifolia

Gliopsis pyrethrifolia, commonly known as the pyrethrum leaf or false pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is not commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes but is occasionally used in horticulture for its unique foliage.

Gliopsis pyrethrifolia is also noted for its potential in ecological studies due to its adaptability to arid environments.
