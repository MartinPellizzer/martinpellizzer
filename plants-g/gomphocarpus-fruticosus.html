Gomphocarpus fruticosus

Gomphocarpus fruticosus, commonly known as the milkweed or yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

Gomphocarpus fruticosus is known for its ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
