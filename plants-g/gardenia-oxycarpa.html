Gardenia oxycarpa

Gardenia oxycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or orange center. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gardenia oxycarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
