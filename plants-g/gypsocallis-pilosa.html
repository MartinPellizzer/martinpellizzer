Gypsocallis pilosa

Gypsocallis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy gypsocallis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Gypsocallis pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
