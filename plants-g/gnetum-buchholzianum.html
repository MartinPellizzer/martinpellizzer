Gnetum buchholzianum

Gnetum buchholzianum, commonly known as the Amazonian gnetum, is a gymnosperm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a straight, unbranched stem and compound leaves with a distinct venation pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is pollinated by insects, which is unusual for gymnosperms. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, cup-like structure, a trait that sets it apart from other members of the Gnetales order.

Gnetum buchholzianum plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various organisms.
