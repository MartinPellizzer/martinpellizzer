Galeandra claesii

Galeandra claesii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique growth habit, featuring a short, stocky rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
