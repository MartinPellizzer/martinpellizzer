Gentiana lagodechiana

Gentiana lagodechiana, commonly known as the Alaskan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous areas and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana lagodechiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
