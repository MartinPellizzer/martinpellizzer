Galium palustre var. rupicola

Galium palustre var. rupicola, commonly known as swamp bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features slender, ascending stems that often root at the nodes, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it typically grows in moist, acidic soils. It is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to waterlogged environments.
