Garciana

Garciana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are highly ornamental. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native flora.
