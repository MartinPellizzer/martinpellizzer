Gouldia cordata

Gouldia cordata, commonly known as the zigzagged gouldia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that often display a zigzag pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Gouldia cordata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
