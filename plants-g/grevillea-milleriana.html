Grevillea milleriana

Grevillea milleriana, commonly known as the Miller's waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, crimson-red flower spikes that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea milleriana is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and its resilience to drought conditions.
