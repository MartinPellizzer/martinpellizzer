Grevillea chrysophaea

Grevillea chrysophaea, commonly known as the golden-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens. Its flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

Grevillea chrysophaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
