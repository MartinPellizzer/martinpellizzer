Galium officinale

Galium officinale, commonly known as bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Galium officinale has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
