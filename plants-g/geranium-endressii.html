Geranium endressii

Geranium endressii, commonly known as the Endress's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of vibrant red or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Geranium endressii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
