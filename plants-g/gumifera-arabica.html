Gumifera arabica

Gumifera arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee tree, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces red, fleshy berries containing seeds. The plant thrives in warm, arid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, and it flowers with small white blossoms.

The fruit is harvested for its seeds, which are the source of the coffee beans used in coffee production.
