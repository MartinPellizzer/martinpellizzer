Gonocarpus chinensis subsp. chinensis

Gonocarpus chinensis subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruits, which are typically five-lobed and yellow when ripe.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a reddish underside.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also edible, though it is less commonly consumed compared to other members of the genus.
