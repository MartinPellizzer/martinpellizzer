Gymnadenia humilis

Gymnadenia humilis, commonly known as the lesser grass-of-Parnassus, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a single, terminal spike, which bloom in late summer. Gymnadenia humilis typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
