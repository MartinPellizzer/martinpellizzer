Gloriosa verschuurii

Gloriosa verschuurii, commonly known as the Van der Veldt lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange-red. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces striking, often fragrant blooms during the warmer months. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, requiring well-drained soil and a warm, humid environment to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular in gardens and as a cut flower, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
