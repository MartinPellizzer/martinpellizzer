Grevillea sulphurea

Grevillea sulphurea, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a bright and vibrant appearance. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
