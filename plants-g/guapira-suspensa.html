Guapira suspensa

Guapira suspensa, commonly known as the hanging guapira, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb other vegetation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by oval-shaped, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Guapira suspensa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
