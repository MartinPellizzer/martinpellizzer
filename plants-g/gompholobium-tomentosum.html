Gompholobium tomentosum

Gompholobium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences, which are characteristic of the genus Gompholobium. Its leaves are pinnate and covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.
