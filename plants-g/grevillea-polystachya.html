Grevillea polystachya

Grevillea polystachya, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features striking red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters, typically during spring and summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
