Guettarda oxyphylla

Guettarda oxyphylla, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Guettarda oxyphylla is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
