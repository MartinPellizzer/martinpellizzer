Grewia hainesiana

Grewia hainesiana, commonly known as the Haines' globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia hainesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native environment.
