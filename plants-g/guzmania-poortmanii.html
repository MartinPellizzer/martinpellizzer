Guzmania poortmanii

Guzmania poortmanii, commonly known as the Poortman's bromeliad, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. Its flowering stem produces a striking cluster of red or orange flowers, which bloom for several weeks.

Guzmania poortmanii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.
