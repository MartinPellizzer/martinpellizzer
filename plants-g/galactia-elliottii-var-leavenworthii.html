Galactia elliottii var. leavenworthii

Galactia elliottii var. leavenworthii, commonly known as the Leavenworth mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, round seeds, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
