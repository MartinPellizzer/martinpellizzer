Gahnia javanica var. paupercula

Gahnia javanica var. paupercula, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
