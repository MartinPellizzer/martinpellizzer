Gymnocalycium brachypetalum

Gymnocalycium brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that bloom in the spring, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Its leaves are usually modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Gymnocalycium brachypetalum is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
