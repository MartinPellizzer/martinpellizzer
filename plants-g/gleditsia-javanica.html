Gleditsia javanica

Gleditsia javanica, commonly known as the Javan locust, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It is often found in open woodlands and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
