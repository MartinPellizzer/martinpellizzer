Glochidion pauciflorum

Glochidion pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in axillary umbels, typically appearing in clusters of three. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that dehisces to release seeds.

Glochidion pauciflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
