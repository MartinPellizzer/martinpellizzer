Gonocarpus hexander subsp. serratus

Gonocarpus hexander subsp.

serratus, commonly known as the serrated sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorn-like structures and a distinctive, swollen base.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
