Genista villarsiana

Genista villarsiana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Genista villarsiana is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky habitats.
