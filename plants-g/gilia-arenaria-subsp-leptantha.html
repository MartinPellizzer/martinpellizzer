Gilia arenaria subsp. leptantha

Gilia arenaria subsp.

leptantha, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth form compared to other varieties of Gilia arenaria.
