Geranium arabicum subsp. latistipulatum

Geranium arabicum subsp.

latistipulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have prominent veins and a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
