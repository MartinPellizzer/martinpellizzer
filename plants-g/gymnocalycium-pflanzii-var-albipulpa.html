Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. albipulpa

Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. albipulpa, commonly known as the white-flowered moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually present in youth but are lost as the plant matures.

Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. albipulpa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water and nutrient requirements.
