Galega persica

Galega persica, commonly known as Persian velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Galega persica is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and medicinal properties.
