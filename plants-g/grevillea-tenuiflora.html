Grevillea tenuiflora

Grevillea tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandplain areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with dense foliage and showy inflorescences.

Grevillea tenuiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun and dry conditions.
