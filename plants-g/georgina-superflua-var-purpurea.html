Georgina superflua var. purpurea

Georgina superflua var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered georgina, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored petals.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the temperate regions of South America. The plant features a robust stem with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. Its flowers, which range from deep purple to soft pink, are composed of both ray and disk florets, enhancing its visual appeal.

This variety is prized for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
