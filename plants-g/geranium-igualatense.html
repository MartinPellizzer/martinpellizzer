Geranium igualatense

Geranium igualatense, commonly known as the Iguales Island geranium, is a flowering plant native to the island of Iguales in the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features broad, lobed leaves with a velvety texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
