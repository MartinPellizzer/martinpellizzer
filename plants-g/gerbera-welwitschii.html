Gerbera welwitschii

Gerbera welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch gerbera, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces numerous ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gerbera welwitschii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
