Gymnosporia pyria

Gymnosporia pyria, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by small, woody pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Gymnosporia pyria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
