Gymnosporia matoboensis

Gymnosporia matoboensis, commonly known as the Matobo gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in rocky and dry habitats.

Gymnosporia matoboensis is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native environment.
