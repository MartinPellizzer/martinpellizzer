Guarea glomerulata

Guarea glomerulata, commonly known as the clustered guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and related hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
