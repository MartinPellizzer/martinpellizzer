Gnidia kraussiana var. glabrata

Gnidia kraussiana var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal form.
