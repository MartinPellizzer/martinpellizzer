Gleditsia amorphoides

Gleditsia amorphoides, commonly known as the honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to small leaflets, and its distinctive thorned stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in long, drooping inflorescences. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several smooth, edible seeds.

The honey locust is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
