Gypsophila briquetiana

Gypsophila briquetiana, commonly known as the desert sandpink, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, featuring a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppes and semi-deserts.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
