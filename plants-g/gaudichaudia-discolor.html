Gaudichaudia discolor

Gaudichaudia discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.

Gaudichaudia discolor is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
