Gilibertia swartzii

Gilibertia swartzii, commonly known as the red grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, reddish-brown inflorescences that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinctive reddish tinge, especially under certain light conditions.

Gilibertia swartzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
