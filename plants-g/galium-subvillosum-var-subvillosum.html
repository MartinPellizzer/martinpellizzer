Galium subvillosum var. subvillosum

Galium subvillosum var. subvillosum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, white to pinkish flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
