Gaertnera koenigii var. oxyphylla

Gaertnera koenigii var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
