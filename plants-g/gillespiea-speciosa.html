Gillespiea speciosa

Gillespiea speciosa, commonly known as the showy gillespiea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with bracts that are often more showy than the actual blooms.

Gillespiea speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
