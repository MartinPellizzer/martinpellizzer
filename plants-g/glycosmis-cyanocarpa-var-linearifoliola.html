Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. linearifoliola

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. linearifoliola, commonly known as the blue-fruited sweetleaf, is a variant of the sweetleaf tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and phytochemistry.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
