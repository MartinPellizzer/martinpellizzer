Gaultheria bracteata

Gaultheria bracteata, commonly known as the alpine wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible. Gaultheria bracteata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows.
