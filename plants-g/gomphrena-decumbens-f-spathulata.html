Gomphrena decumbens f. spathulata

Gomphrena decumbens f. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its distinctive, plump, spathelike bracts that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
