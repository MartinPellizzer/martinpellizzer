Gaura mckelveyae

Gaura mckelveyae, commonly known as Mckelvey's gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

It is a perennial species that produces slender, branching stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
