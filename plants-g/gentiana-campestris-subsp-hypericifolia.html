Gentiana campestris subsp. hypericifolia

Gentiana campestris subsp.

hypericifolia, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with two lips, and are arranged in racemes.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
