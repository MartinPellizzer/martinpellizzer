Grammitis seemannii

Grammitis seemannii, commonly known as the slender fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with small, scale-like leaves along its stems. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters known as sori.

Grammitis seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
