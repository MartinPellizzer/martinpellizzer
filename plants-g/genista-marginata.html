Genista marginata

Genista marginata, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
