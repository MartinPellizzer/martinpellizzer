Genipa codonocalyx

Genipa codonocalyx, commonly known as the genipapo or genipap, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, acidic pulp that is often used in traditional cuisines and beverages.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
