Garcinia baillonii

Garcinia baillonii, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Western Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Garcinia baillonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
