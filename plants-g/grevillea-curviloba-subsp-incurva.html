Grevillea curviloba subsp. incurva

Grevillea curviloba subsp.

incurva, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, curved leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
