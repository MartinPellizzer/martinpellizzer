Glechon marifolia

Glechon marifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Glechon marifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
