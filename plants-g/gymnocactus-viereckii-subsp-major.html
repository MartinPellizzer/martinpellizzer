Gymnocactus viereckii subsp. major

Gymnocactus viereckii subsp.

major, commonly known as the Giant Gymnocactus, is a striking cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the stem tips. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and among cactus enthusiasts.
