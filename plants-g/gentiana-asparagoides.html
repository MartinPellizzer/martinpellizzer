Gentiana asparagoides

Gentiana asparagoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often erect and branched.

Gentiana asparagoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
