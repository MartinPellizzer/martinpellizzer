Grewia brachyclada

Grewia brachyclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
