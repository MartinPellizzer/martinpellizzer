Gymnelaea cunninghamii

Gymnelaea cunninghamii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its botanical name honors the botanist Allan Cunningham, who collected the first specimens.

Gymnelaea cunninghamii is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australia's natural heritage.
