Gentiana pungens

Gentiana pungens, commonly known as the bitter gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

The flowers are known for their intense blue color and are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their bitter compounds.
