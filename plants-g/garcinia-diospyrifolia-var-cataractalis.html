Garcinia diospyrifolia var. cataractalis

Garcinia diospyrifolia var. cataractalis, commonly known as the water apple or *Syzygium malaccense*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is notable for its edible fruits, which have a unique, sour taste and are often used in culinary applications. The fruit's skin is thick and leathery, while the flesh is juicy and segmented.

It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
