Gongora macrantha

Gongora macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

Gongora macrantha requires specific care, including high humidity and well-draining soil, to thrive in both its natural habitat and as a cultivated plant.
