Gaertnera luteocarpa subsp. luteocarpa

Gaertnera luteocarpa subsp.

luteocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited gaertnera, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for horticultural and ecological studies.
