Grevillea acanthifolia subsp. stenomera

Grevillea acanthifolia subsp.

stenomera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and adaptations to nutrient-poor soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
