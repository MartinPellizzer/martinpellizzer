Geranium ibericum var. parviflorum

Geranium ibericum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its basal leaves are palmately lobed with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.
