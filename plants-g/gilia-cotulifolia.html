Gilia cotulifolia

Gilia cotulifolia, commonly known as the common gilia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Gilia cotulifolia is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early summer, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native regions.
