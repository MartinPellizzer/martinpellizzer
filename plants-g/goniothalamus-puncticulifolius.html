Goniothalamus puncticulifolius

Goniothalamus puncticulifolius, commonly known as the punctulate-leafed goniothalamus, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves with distinctive punctate markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
