Galium parisiense subvar. anglicum

Galium parisiense subvar. anglicum, commonly known as English bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
