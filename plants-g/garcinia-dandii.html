Garcinia dandii

Garcinia dandii, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Garcinia dandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
