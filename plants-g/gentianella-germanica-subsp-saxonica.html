Gentianella germanica subsp. saxonica

Gentianella germanica subsp.

saxonica, commonly known as the Saxon gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
