Gnaphalium collinum var. gunnii

Gnaphalium collinum var. gunnii, commonly known as the Alpine feather grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations where conditions are harsh. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of tiny white to pale yellow florets.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
