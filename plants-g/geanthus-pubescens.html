Geanthus pubescens

Geanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy geanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

Geanthus pubescens thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
