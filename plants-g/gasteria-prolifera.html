Gasteria prolifera

Gasteria prolifera, commonly known as the dwarf gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and requiring minimal watering.

Gasteria prolifera is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
