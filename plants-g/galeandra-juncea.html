Galeandra juncea

Galeandra juncea, commonly known as the slender galeandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, while its inflorescence consists of a raceme of small, showy blooms.

Galeandra juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
