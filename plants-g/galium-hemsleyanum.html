Galium hemsleyanum

Galium hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Hemsley's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, often hairy stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium hemsleyanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
