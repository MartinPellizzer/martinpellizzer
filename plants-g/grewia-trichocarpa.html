Grewia trichocarpa

Grewia trichocarpa, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Grewia trichocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
