Gazachloa scopulorum

Gazachloa scopulorum, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Gazachloa scopulorum is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal landscaping.
