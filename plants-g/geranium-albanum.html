Geranium albanum

Geranium albanum, commonly known as the Albanian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive five-lobed calyx, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Geranium albanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
