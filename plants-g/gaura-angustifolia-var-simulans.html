Gaura angustifolia var. simulans

Gaura angustifolia var. simulans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers resembling candle flames. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in open woodland or prairie habitats.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
