Grevillea bleasdalei

Grevillea bleasdalei, commonly known as the Black Hill Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Grevillea bleasdalei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
