Geranium muscoideum

Geranium muscoideum, commonly known as the moss geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and moist shaded borders.
