Garuga pinnata var. mollis

Garuga pinnata var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved garuga, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are typically alternate and pinnately compound. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
