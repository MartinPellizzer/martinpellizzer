Gevuina avellana

Gevuina avellana, commonly known as the Chilean hazelnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, round leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible nuts that are encased in a hard shell. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

The nuts are rich in oils and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
