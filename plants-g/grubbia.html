Grubbia

Grubbia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of Africa and are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves. The genus is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
