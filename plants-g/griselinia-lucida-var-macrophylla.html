Griselinia lucida var. macrophylla

Griselinia lucida var. macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf griselinia, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in New Zealand's ecosystems.
