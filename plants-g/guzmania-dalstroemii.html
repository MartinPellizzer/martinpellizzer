Guzmania dalstroemii

Guzmania dalstroemii, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often with a striking contrast against its broad, waxy leaves. The plant's rosette-shaped foliage collects rainwater, creating a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania dalstroemii is popular as a ornamental plant due to its long-lasting floral display and unique appearance.
