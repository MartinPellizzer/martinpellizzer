Galium spurium subsp. ibicinum

Galium spurium subsp.

ibicinum, commonly known as the Iberian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
