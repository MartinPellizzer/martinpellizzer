Goodenia viscidula

Goodenia viscidula, commonly known as the sticky goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes.

Goodenia viscidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
