Granatum surinamense

Granatum surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam pomegranate, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Punicaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and a distinct floral structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
