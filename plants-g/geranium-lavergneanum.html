Geranium lavergneanum

Geranium lavergneanum, commonly known as the Lavergne's geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice among gardeners in suitable climates.
