Gahnia erythrocarpa

Gahnia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, forming dense tussocks with tall, cylindrical stems. The plant produces bright red, elongated spikelets that are distinctive and visually striking. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Gahnia erythrocarpa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
