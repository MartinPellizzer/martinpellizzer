Garcinia mindanaensis

Garcinia mindanaensis, commonly known as Mindanao garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
