Gordonia acuminata

Gordonia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Gordonia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
