Gilia multicaulis var. millefoliata

Gilia multicaulis var. millefoliata, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it is adapted to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
