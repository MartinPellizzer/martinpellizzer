Gynopachis rufidula

Gynopachis rufidula, commonly known as the red-stemmed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, pearly white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Gynopachis rufidula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
