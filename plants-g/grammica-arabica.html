Grammica arabica

Grammica arabica, commonly known as the Arabian gram, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a legume in traditional cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to hot and dry climates.

Grammica arabica is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
