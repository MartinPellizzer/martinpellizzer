Gentiana aperta

Gentiana aperta, commonly known as the open gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Gentiana aperta thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
