Galium sylvaticum var. pubescens

Galium sylvaticum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a bright green color and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its pubescent stems and unique floral structure.
