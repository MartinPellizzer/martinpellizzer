Grammica australis

Grammica australis, commonly known as the Australian grammic, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Grammica australis thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or coastal heathlands.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
