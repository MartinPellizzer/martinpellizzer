Gynura pseudochina var. hispida

Gynura pseudochina var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy gynura, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The hairy texture of its leaves is a key identifying feature, distinguishing it from other similar species.
