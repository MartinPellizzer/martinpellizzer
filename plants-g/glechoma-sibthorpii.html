Glechoma sibthorpii

Glechoma sibthorpii, commonly known as the Siberian ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Glechoma sibthorpii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
