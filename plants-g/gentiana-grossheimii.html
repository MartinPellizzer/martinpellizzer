Gentiana grossheimii

Gentiana grossheimii, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are typically five in number and arranged in a showy, radial pattern. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its diverse and often brightly colored flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species in the study of high-altitude flora.
