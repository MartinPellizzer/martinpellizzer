Gnephosis arachnoidea var. foliata

Gnephosis arachnoidea var. foliata, commonly known as the spider-web fern, is a unique variant of the genus Gnephosis.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are essential for spore dispersal. Its foliage is typically pale green, with a delicate, almost translucent appearance.

This variant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
