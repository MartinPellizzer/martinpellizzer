Gentianella moorcroftiana

Gentianella moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Gentianella moorcroftiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
