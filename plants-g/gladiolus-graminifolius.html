Gladiolus graminifolius

Gladiolus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that give it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
