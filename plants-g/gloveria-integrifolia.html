Gloveria integrifolia

Gloveria integrifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers indirect sunlight. Its leaves are typically smooth and entire, giving it the species name *integrifolia*.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact size and long-lasting blooms.
