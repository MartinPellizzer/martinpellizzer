Gnidia tenuiflora

Gnidia tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gnidia tenuiflora is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
