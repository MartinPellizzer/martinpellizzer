Gentiana pedicellata subsp. pedicellata

Gentiana pedicellata subsp.

pedicellata, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
