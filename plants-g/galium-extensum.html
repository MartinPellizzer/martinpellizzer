Galium extensum

Galium extensum, commonly known as silverweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

Galium extensum thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and along roadsides.
