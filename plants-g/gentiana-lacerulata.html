Gentiana lacerulata

Gentiana lacerulata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with fringed petals. The plant grows in moist, rocky habitats and prefers cool, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana lacerulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
