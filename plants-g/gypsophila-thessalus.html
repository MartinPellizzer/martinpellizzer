Gypsophila thessalus

Gypsophila thessalus, commonly known as the Greek Baby's Breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Gypsophila thessalus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in floral arrangements.
