Guapira hirsuta

Guapira hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy guapira, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It typically grows in humid tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy or pubescent, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.
