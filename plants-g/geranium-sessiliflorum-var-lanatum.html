Geranium sessiliflorum var. lanatum

Geranium sessiliflorum var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners.
