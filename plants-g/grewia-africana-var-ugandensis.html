Grewia africana var. ugandensis

Grewia africana var. ugandensis, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It features simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and riverine habitats.
