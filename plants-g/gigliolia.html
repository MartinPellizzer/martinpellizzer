Gigliolia

Gigliolia, commonly known as the "flower of the desert," is a unique plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to pale yellow. The plant has a succulent stem and fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh environment. Gigliolia blooms during the cooler months, often at night, to conserve moisture.

Its botanical name, *Gigliolia monspessulana*, reflects its historical significance in ancient botanical studies.
