Garcinia buxifolia

Garcinia buxifolia, commonly known as the box-leaf garcinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain a high concentration of alkaloids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Garcinia buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
