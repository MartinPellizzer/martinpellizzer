Geropogon australis

Geropogon australis, commonly known as the Australian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Geropogon australis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
