Gaultheria bartolomei

Gaultheria bartolomei, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its berries, which are dark red to black, are edible and have a sharp, peppery flavor.

Gaultheria bartolomei thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
