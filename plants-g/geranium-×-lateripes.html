Geranium × lateripes

Geranium × lateripes, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a variety of leaf shapes, often with a distinctive trailing or ivy-like growth habit.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a serrated or lobed margin, and can vary in color from bright green to variegated patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.

Geranium × lateripes thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
