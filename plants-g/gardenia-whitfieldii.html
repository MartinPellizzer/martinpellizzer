Gardenia whitfieldii

Gardenia whitfieldii, commonly known as the Whitfield gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia whitfieldii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fragrance.
