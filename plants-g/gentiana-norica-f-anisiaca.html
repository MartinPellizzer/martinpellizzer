Gentiana norica f. anisiaca

Gentiana norica f. anisiaca, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
