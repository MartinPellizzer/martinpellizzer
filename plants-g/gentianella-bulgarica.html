Gentianella bulgarica

Gentianella bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are often clustered in umbels and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella bulgarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
