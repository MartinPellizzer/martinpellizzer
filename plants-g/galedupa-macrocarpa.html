Galedupa macrocarpa

Galedupa macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant galedupa, is a unique and rare plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
