Gymnadenia faberi

Gymnadenia faberi, commonly known as the Faberi's grass pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear whorled leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Gymnadenia faberi is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late summer.

It is a rare and protected species in many regions due to habitat loss and over-collection.
