Garcinia duodecandra

Garcinia duodecandra, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Garcinia duodecandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
