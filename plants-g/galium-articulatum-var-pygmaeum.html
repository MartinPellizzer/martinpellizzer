Galium articulatum var. pygmaeum

Galium articulatum var. pygmaeum, commonly known as dwarf woodypod, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features slender, branched stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
