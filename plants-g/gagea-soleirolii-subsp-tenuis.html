Gagea soleirolii subsp. tenuis

Gagea soleirolii subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with six petals and a tubular throat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its early spring blooming period.

The subspecies tenuis is distinguished by its more slender stems and smaller flowers compared to other subspecies of Gagea.
