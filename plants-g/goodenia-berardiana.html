Goodenia berardiana

Goodenia berardiana, commonly known as the sandhill goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
