Glochidion alstonii

Glochidion alstonii, commonly known as the Alston's glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in lowland forests.
