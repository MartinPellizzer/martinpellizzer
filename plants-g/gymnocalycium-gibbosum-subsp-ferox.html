Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp. ferox

Gymnocalycium gibbosum subsp.

ferox, commonly known as the feral moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
