Gentiana melandriifolia

Gentiana melandriifolia, commonly known as the Melandrii gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
