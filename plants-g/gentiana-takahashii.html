Gentiana takahashii

Gentiana takahashii, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

Gentiana takahashii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
