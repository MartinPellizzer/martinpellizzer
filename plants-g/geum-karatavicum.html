Geum karatavicum

Geum karatavicum, commonly known as the Karatavicum avens, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, single flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, low-growing structure. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Geum karatavicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
