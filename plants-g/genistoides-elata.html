Genistoides elata

Genistoides elata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
