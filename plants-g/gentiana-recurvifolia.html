Gentiana recurvifolia

Gentiana recurvifolia, commonly known as the recurved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Gentiana recurvifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
