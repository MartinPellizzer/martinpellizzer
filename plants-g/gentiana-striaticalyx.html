Gentiana striaticalyx

Gentiana striaticalyx, commonly known as the stripe-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and have distinct white or pale green stripes, which give it its common name.

Gentiana striaticalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
