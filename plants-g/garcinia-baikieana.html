Garcinia baikieana

Garcinia baikieana, commonly known as the white-berried garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is distinctive for its white, fleshy aril surrounding a hard, yellow seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
