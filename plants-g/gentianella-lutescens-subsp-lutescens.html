Gentianella lutescens subsp. lutescens

Gentianella lutescens subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its vibrant blue or yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
