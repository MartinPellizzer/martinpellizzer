Geboscon rubrum

Geboscon rubrum, commonly known as the crimson flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vivid red and orange petals, which are often speckled with contrasting patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the forest floor.

Geboscon rubrum is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
