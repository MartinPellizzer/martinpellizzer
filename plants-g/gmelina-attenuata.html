Gmelina attenuata

Gmelina attenuata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
