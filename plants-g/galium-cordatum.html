Galium cordatum

Galium cordatum, commonly known as heartleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
