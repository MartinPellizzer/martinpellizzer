Granatum nicaraguense

Granatum nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan pomegranate, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Punicaceae family and is characterized by its red, spiny fruits that resemble those of the common pomegranate. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is edible, with a high concentration of seeds and a sweet, tart flavor, making it a valuable crop in local agriculture.
