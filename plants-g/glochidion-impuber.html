Glochidion impuber

Glochidion impuber, commonly known as the hairy glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
