Grevillea wickhamii subsp. pallida

Grevillea wickhamii subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pallid grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
