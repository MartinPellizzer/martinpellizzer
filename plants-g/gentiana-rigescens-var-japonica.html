Gentiana rigescens var. japonica

Gentiana rigescens var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
