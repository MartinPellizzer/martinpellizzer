Gentianopsis crinita f. albina

Gentianopsis crinita f. albina, commonly known as the white gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, contrasting with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
