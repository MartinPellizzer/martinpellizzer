Glyceria baumgarteniana

Glyceria baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Glyceria baumgarteniana is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife in its native habitats.
