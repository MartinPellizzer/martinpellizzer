Gagea foliosa var. alpigena

Gagea foliosa var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in open meadows and forest edges at high elevations.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive component of alpine flora.
