Gaylussacia pulchra

Gaylussacia pulchra, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries.

The berries are a food source for various birds and mammals, and the plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
