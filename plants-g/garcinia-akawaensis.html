Garcinia akawaensis

Garcinia akawaensis, commonly known as the Akawaense garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its bright red, fleshy berries that contain a single large seed. Garcinia akawaensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
