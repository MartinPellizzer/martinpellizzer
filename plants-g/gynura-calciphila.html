Gynura calciphila

Gynura calciphila, commonly known as the limestone spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Gynura calciphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-maintenance gardens.
