Grevillea fulgens

Grevillea fulgens, commonly known as the flame grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and semi-arid environments.

Grevillea fulgens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
