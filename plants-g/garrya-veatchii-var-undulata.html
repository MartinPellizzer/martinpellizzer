Garrya veatchii var. undulata

Garrya veatchii var. undulata, commonly known as the wavyleaf holly.

This plant is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. It features small, elliptical leaves with a wavy margin, giving it its common name. The leaves are usually dark green on top and lighter on the underside.

It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall.
