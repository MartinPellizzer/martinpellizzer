Grindelia scabra var. neomexicana

Grindelia scabra var. neomexicana, commonly known as the roughleaf gumweed, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an important component of arid ecosystems.
