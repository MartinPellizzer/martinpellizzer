Gymnosporia putterlickioides subsp. putterlickioides

Gymnosporia putterlickioides subsp.

putterlickioides, commonly known as the Putterlick's gymnosporia, is a shrub native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often densely arranged.

The plant produces yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological significance.
