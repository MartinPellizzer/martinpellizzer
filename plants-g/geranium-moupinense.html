Geranium moupinense

Geranium moupinense, commonly known as the Moupin geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, while the flowers range in color from pink to red. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at higher altitudes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
