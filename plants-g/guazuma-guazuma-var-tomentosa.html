Guazuma guazuma var. tomentosa

Guazuma guazuma var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly guazuma, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grey pubescence that covers its young stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
