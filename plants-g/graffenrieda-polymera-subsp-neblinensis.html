Graffenrieda polymera subsp. neblinensis

Graffenrieda polymera subsp.

neblinensis, commonly known as the Neblin's spiderflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cloud forests of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with moist, cool conditions.
