Galactia simplicifolia

Galactia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods and are dispersed by wind.

Galactia simplicifolia is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
