Gerardia acuta

Gerardia acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, pointed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gerardia acuta is often found in coastal and dryland habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
