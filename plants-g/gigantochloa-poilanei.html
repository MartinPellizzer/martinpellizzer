Gigantochloa poilanei

Gigantochloa poilanei, commonly known as the giant bamboo of Madagascar, is a tall, clumping grass native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

Gigantochloa poilanei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for local wildlife.
