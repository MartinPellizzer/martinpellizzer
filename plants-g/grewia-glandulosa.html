Grewia glandulosa

Grewia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
