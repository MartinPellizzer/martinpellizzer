Ghesaembilla

Ghesaembilla, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to Central and South America. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and can grow up to 30 feet in length, making it a dramatic climbing vine. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The fruit is a small, orange-red berry that contains toxic seeds, cautioning against consumption.
