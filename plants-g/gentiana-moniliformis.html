Gentiana moniliformis

Gentiana moniliformis, commonly known as the chain gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
