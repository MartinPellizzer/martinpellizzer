Gaylussacia frondosa var. nana

Gaylussacia frondosa var. nana, commonly known as dwarf sweet pepperbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically oval with a slightly serrated edge, and it bears red, fleshy berries in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
