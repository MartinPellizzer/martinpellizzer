Gastrochilus quasipinifolius

Gastrochilus quasipinifolius, commonly known as the needle-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Gastrochilus quasipinifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
