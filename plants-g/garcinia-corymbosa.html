Garcinia corymbosa

Garcinia corymbosa, commonly known as kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow, and aromatic leaves, which are commonly used in cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to round, orange-colored fruits. The fruit's rind contains a high concentration of aromatic oils, making it valuable in both culinary and aromatic applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and practical uses.
