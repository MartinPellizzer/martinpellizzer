Gaillardia scabiosoides

Gaillardia scabiosoides, commonly known as the plains gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gaillardia scabiosoides is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
