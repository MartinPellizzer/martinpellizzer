Galium ambiguum var. siskiyouense

Galium ambiguum var. siskiyouense, commonly known as the Siskiyou bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in the Siskiyou Mountains of Oregon and California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. The plant has slender, ascending stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
