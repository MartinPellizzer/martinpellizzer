Gastrochilus obliquus

Gastrochilus obliquus, commonly known as the slant-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by a short rhizome and a cluster of fleshy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces a single, erect inflorescence bearing several showy, spurred flowers with a distinctive slanted lip. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, with a prominent, elongated spur.

Gastrochilus obliquus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
