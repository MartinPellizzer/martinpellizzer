Gravesia heterophylla

Gravesia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are variable in shape, ranging from ovate to lanceolate, which gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
