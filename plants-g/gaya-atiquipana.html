Gaya atiquipana

Gaya atiquipana, commonly known as the Mexican mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in rocky or well-drained soils at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and calming effects.
