Glandulifolia kraussii

Glandulifolia kraussii, commonly known as the Cape glandular buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, alternate leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas.

Glandulifolia kraussii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
