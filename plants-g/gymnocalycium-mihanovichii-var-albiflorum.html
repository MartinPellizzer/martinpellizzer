Gymnocalycium mihanovichii var. albiflorum

Gymnocalycium mihanovichii var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often resembles a small, rounded ball. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the growing season, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in succulent gardens.
