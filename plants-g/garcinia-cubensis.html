Garcinia cubensis

Garcinia cubensis, commonly known as the West African garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a white, aromatic pulp. Garcinia cubensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional African medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
