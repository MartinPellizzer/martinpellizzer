Geranium dissectum f. tasmanicum

Geranium dissectum f. tasmanicum, commonly known as the Tasmanian geranium, is a distinctive herbaceous plant native to Tasmania.

It features finely dissected, lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with prominent stamens, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shady garden settings.
