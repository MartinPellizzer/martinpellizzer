Glycine bimaculata

Glycine bimaculata, commonly known as the two-spotted soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with two distinct spots on each leaflet. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Glycine bimaculata is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in various food products and traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in agricultural systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
