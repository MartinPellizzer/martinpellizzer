Garcinia punctata

Garcinia punctata, commonly known as the punctate garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dotted with small, raised spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange when mature.

Garcinia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
