Gymnema rufescens

Gymnema rufescens, commonly known as the Indian bitter apple, is a climbing shrub native to the forests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which are believed to have hypoglycemic properties.

Gymnema rufescens is also valued for its use in traditional Ayurvedic practices for its purported health benefits.
