Garcinia erythrosperma

Garcinia erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-fruited garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's most distinctive feature is its bright red, fleshy fruits, which contain a single large seed. Garcinia erythrosperma is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within tropical ecosystems and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
