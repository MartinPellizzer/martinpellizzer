Gomphia calophylla

Gomphia calophylla, commonly known as the silky gomphia, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale green to yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiky seed pods that mature into woody structures, which can persist for several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Gomphia calophylla plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
