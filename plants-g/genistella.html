Genistella

Genistella, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to southern Africa. The plants are characterized by their small, clustered yellow flowers and feathery, pinnate leaves. They are often found in rocky or sandy soils and are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Genistella species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
