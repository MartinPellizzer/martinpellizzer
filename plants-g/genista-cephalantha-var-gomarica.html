Genista cephalantha var. gomarica

Genista cephalantha var. gomarica, commonly known as the Gomara broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
