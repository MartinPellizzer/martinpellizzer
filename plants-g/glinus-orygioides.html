Glinus orygioides

Glinus orygioides, commonly known as the African spinach or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
