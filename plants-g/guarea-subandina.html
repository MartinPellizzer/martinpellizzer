Guarea subandina

Guarea subandina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its straight, dark-colored wood and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a unique, fragrant scent.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
