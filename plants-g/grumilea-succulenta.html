Grumilea succulenta

Grumilea succulenta, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Grumilea succulenta is prized for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
