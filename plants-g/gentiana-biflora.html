Gentiana biflora

Gentiana biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the top of its slender stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentiana biflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
