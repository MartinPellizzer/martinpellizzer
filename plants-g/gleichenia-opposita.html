Gleichenia opposita

Gleichenia opposita, commonly known as the opposite fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a feature common in many ferns. Gleichenia opposita thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its intricate morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
