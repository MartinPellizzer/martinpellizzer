Gomphrena cladotrichoides

Gomphrena cladotrichoides, commonly known as the woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

Gomphrena cladotrichoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
