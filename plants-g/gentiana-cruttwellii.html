Gentiana cruttwellii

Gentiana cruttwellii, commonly known as the Cruttwell's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana cruttwellii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
