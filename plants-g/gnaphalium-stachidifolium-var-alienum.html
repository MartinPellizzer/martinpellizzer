Gnaphalium stachidifolium var. alienum

Gnaphalium stachidifolium var. alienum, commonly known as the alien hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of tiny, feathery leaves that form a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically arranged in compact clusters called capitula. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
