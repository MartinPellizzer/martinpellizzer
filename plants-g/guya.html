Guya

Guya, commonly known as the mountain pepper or *Xylopia aromatica*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Guya is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
