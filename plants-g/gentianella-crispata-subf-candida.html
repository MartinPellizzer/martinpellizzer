Gentianella crispata subf. candida

Gentianella crispata subf.

candida, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae. It is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in clusters.

The plant is native to regions of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
