Geraniospermum crinitum

Geraniospermum crinitum, commonly known as the silky geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its dispersal.

Geraniospermum crinitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
