Gilia jonesii

Gilia jonesii, commonly known as Jones' gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and is often found in disturbed habitats.
