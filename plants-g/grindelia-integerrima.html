Grindelia integerrima

Grindelia integerrima, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Grindelia integerrima is also known for its ability to withstand drought and high temperatures.
