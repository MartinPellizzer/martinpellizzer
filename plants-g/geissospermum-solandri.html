Geissospermum solandri

Geissospermum solandri, commonly known as the red-flowered milkweed, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five pink or red petals, often with a yellow center. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

It is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and forest edges.
