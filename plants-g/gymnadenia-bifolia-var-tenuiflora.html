Gymnadenia bifolia var. tenuiflora

Gymnadenia bifolia var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, terminal flower per stem.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and declining species in many regions.
