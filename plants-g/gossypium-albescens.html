Gossypium albescens

Gossypium albescens, commonly known as the white cotton plant, is a species of cotton native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white fibers that are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, and it produces small, round bolls that contain the cotton fibers.

Gossypium albescens is valued for its high-quality, fine-textured cotton, which is used in various industrial and commercial applications.
