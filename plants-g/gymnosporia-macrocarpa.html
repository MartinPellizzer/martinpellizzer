Gymnosporia macrocarpa

Gymnosporia macrocarpa, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, typical of the gymnosperm group. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in landscaping and for soil stabilization in arid environments.
