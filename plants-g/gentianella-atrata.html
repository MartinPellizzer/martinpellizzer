Gentianella atrata

Gentianella atrata, commonly known as the black gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
