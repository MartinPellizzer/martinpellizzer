Grindelia perennis f. pseudopinnatifida

Grindelia perennis f. pseudopinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive pinnatifid (lobed) appearance, giving it its specific epithet.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
