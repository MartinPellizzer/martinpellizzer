Gaillonia tinctoria

Gaillonia tinctoria, commonly known as the Mexican red root, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its deep red roots, which are rich in alkaloids and have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Gaillonia tinctoria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
