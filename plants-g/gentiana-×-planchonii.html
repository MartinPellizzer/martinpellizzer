Gentiana × planchonii

Gentiana × planchonii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down.

Gentiana × planchonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
