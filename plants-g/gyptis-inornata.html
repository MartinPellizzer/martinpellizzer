Gyptis inornata

Gyptis inornata, commonly known as the slender gyptis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Gyptis inornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
