Geum mexicanum

Geum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately lobed and its clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or forested areas. It produces oval, hairy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Geum mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
