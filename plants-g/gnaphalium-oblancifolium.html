Gnaphalium oblancifolium

Gnaphalium oblancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-white underside that gives it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.
