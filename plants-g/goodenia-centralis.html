Goodenia centralis

Goodenia centralis, commonly known as the Central Goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Goodenia centralis is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and shrublands.
