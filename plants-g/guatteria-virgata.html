Guatteria virgata

Guatteria virgata, commonly known as the Mexican wax tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red berries. Guatteria virgata is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for tropical landscaping.
