Gorteria ciliata

Gorteria ciliata, commonly known as the Cape bell or leopard's ear, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Gorteria ciliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like blooms.
