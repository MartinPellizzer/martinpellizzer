Geonoma plumeriana

Geonoma plumeriana, commonly known as the plumieriana palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized palm that typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk and produces pinnate leaves that are often clustered at the top. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
