Gibsoniothamnus alatus

Gibsoniothamnus alatus, commonly known as the winged thamnus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, wing-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is often found growing in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
