Gerbera armena

Gerbera armena, commonly known as Armenian Gerbera, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Gerbera armena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
