Gongora histrionica

Gongora histrionica, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in high humidity and partial light. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy coating to help retain moisture.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique and eye-catching blooms.
