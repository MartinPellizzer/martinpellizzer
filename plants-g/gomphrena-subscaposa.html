Gomphrena subscaposa

Gomphrena subscaposa, commonly known as the scapose gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces distinctive, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gomphrena subscaposa is also valued for its long-lasting blooms, which are often used in floral arrangements.
