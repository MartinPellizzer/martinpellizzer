Garcinia gummi-gutta

Garcinia gummi-gutta, commonly known as kokum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree is well-known for its fruit, which contains a hard, seed-like core surrounded by a juicy, acidic pulp. The fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.

Botanically, it belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its unique chemical composition, including polyphenols and flavonoids.
