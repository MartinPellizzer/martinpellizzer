Grastidium bambusiforme

Grastidium bambusiforme, commonly known as the bamboo-like grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slightly fibrous texture.

Grastidium bambusiforme is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
