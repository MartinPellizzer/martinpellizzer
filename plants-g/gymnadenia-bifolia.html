Gymnadenia bifolia

Gymnadenia bifolia, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Gymnadenia bifolia is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
