Gentiana nipponica var. kawakamii

Gentiana nipponica var. kawakamii, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
