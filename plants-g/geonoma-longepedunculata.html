Geonoma longepedunculata

Geonoma longepedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncle fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Thelypteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated peduncles that bear multiple fiddlehead-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually lanceolate and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Geonoma longepedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
