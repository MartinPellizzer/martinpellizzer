Ganguelia gossweileri

Ganguelia gossweileri, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and Angola.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, featuring succulent leaves and a deep root system for water storage. Its blooming period typically occurs during the rainy season, making it a rare and beautiful sight in the desert landscape.

Ganguelia gossweileri plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its harsh environment.
