Gaultheria × jordanensis

Gaultheria × jordanensis, commonly known as the Jordan's wintergreen, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or dark purple in color, similar to those of its parent species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates with cool, moist conditions.

Gaultheria × jordanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in horticulture.
