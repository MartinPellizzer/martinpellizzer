Grewia nematopus

Grewia nematopus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Grewia nematopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature.
