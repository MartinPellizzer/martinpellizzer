Gaertnera vaginans subsp. junghuhniana

Gaertnera vaginans subsp.

junghuhniana, commonly known as the Junghuhn's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers.

The plant's leaves are typically arrow-shaped, hence its common name, and are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
