Grindelia squarrosa var. integrifolia

Grindelia squarrosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. It has a creeping growth habit and produces a sticky resin that can be harvested for various traditional uses.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
