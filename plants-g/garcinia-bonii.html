Garcinia bonii

Garcinia bonii, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy berries that are edible and contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds. Garcinia bonii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
