Glycosmis singuliflora var. glabra

Glycosmis singuliflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth single-flower glycosmis, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The smooth, glabrous leaves and unique floral structure make it a distinctive subject in botanical studies.
