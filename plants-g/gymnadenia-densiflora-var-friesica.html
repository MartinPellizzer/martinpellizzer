Gymnadenia densiflora var. friesica

Gymnadenia densiflora var. friesica, commonly known as the Fries' dense-flowered grasspocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation sites.
