Gentiana transalaica

Gentiana transalaica, commonly known as the Transalpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana transalaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine environment.
