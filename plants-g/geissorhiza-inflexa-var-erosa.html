Geissorhiza inflexa var. erosa

Geissorhiza inflexa var. erosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
