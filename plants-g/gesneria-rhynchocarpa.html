Gesneria rhynchocarpa

Gesneria rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the red-veined gesneria, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, waxy leaves with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, blooming in clusters.

Gesneria rhynchocarpa is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
