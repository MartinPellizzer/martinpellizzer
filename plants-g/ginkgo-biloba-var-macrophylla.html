Ginkgo biloba var. macrophylla

Ginkgo biloba var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese ginkgo, is a variant of the ginkgo tree characterized by its larger, more elongated leaves compared to the standard ginkgo.

This variety is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive fan-shaped foliage. The leaves turn a striking yellow in the fall, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly tolerant of pollution, making it well-suited for city environments.

The tree is also known for its longevity and can live for hundreds of years, contributing to its historical and cultural significance.
