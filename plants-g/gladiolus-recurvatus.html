Gladiolus recurvatus

Gladiolus recurvatus, commonly known as the recurved gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly recurved at the edges. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of brightly colored, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. These flowers are arranged in racemes and are known for their vibrant hues and ornamental value.

Gladiolus recurvatus is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
