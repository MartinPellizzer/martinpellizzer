Galipea maxima

Galipea maxima, commonly known as the great galipea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making. It also has medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.

Galipea maxima thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
