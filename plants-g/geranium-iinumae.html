Geranium iinumae

Geranium iinumae, commonly known as the Iinuma geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Geranium iinumae is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
