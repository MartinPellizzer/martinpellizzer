Genista glaberrima

Genista glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Genista glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
