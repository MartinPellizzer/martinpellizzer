Gomphrena pohlii var. hispidula

Gomphrena pohlii var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their long-lasting blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves gives it its common name and adds to its visual appeal.
