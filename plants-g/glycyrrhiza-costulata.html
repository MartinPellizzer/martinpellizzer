Glycyrrhiza costulata

Glycyrrhiza costulata, commonly known as the Chinese licorice, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, pinnately compound leaves, and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal roots, which are rich in glycyrrhizin.

Glycyrrhiza costulata is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.
