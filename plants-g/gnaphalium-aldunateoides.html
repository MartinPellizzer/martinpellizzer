Gnaphalium aldunateoides

Gnaphalium aldunateoides, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation across rocky and alpine habitats. It thrives in cool, dry environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Its delicate appearance and resilience make it a notable species in alpine botany.
