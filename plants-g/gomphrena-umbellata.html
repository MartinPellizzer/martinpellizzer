Gomphrena umbellata

Gomphrena umbellata, commonly known as the clustered gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescences that resemble small balls. The plant produces showy, long-lasting blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple, which are often used in dried floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena umbellata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
