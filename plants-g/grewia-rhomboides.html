Grewia rhomboides

Grewia rhomboides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
