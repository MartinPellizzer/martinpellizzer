Gymnosporia crenulata

Gymnosporia crenulata, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Gymnosporia crenulata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
