Guadua paraguayana

Guadua paraguayana, commonly known as the Paraguayan bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is highly valued for its durable and lightweight material, making it useful in construction and handicrafts. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps that help prevent soil erosion.

Guadua paraguayana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
