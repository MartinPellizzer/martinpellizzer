Gnaphalium poeppigianum var. huigrense

Gnaphalium poeppigianum var. huigrense, commonly known as the Huigren's feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fluffy yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
