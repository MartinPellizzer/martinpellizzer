Galium palustre f. rupicola

Galium palustre f. rupicola, commonly known as the rocky bedstraw, is a subspecies of the common bedstraw.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that form dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and open woodlands.
