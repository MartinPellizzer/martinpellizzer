Gnaphalium globuliferum

Gnaphalium globuliferum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its distinctive spherical blue or purple flower heads are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Gnaphalium globuliferum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
