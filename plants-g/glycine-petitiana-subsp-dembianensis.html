Glycine petitiana subsp. dembianensis

Glycine petitiana subsp.

dembianensis, commonly known as the Dembien sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to Central Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
