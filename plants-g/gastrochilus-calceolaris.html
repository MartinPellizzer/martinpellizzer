Gastrochilus calceolaris

Gastrochilus calceolaris, commonly known as the "tongue-shaped orchid," is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often red or orange-colored flowers that resemble a small shoe or boot. Its leaves are typically ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Gastrochilus calceolaris is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional herbal medicine.
