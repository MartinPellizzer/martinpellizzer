Gypsophila curvifolia

Gypsophila curvifolia, commonly known as the curvedleaf baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often curved, giving it its common name.

Gypsophila curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
