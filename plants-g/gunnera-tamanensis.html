Gunnera tamanensis

Gunnera tamanensis, commonly known as the Tamanensis giant shield fern, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, making it one of the largest ferns in the world. The plant thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in cloud forests and high-altitude regions. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Gunnera tamanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
