Gladiolus vaginatus

Gladiolus vaginatus, commonly known as the hooded gladiolus, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are borne in spikes and are known for their vibrant colors and ornamental value in gardens.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
