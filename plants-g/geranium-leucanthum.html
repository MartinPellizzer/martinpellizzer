Geranium leucanthum

Geranium leucanthum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blooms and compact growth habit.
