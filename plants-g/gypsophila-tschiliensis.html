Gypsophila tschiliensis

Gypsophila tschiliensis, commonly known as the Chilean baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender stems and small, clustered flowers make it a popular choice in floral arrangements.

Gypsophila tschiliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
