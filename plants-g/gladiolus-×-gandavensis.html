Gladiolus × gandavensis

Gladiolus × gandavensis, commonly known as the Gandavensis gladiolus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a corm as its underground storage organ. It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
