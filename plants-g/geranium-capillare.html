Geranium capillare

Geranium capillare, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink or red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

Geranium capillare is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
