Grastidium erubescens

Grastidium erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems and large, showy orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with high levels of indirect light. Its leaves are typically broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Grastidium erubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and long blooming period.
