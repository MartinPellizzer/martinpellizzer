Gentiana campestris var. neapolitana

Gentiana campestris var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are erect and bear clustered flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
