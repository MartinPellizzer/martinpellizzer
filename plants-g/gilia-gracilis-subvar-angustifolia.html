Gilia gracilis subvar. angustifolia

Gilia gracilis subvar. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged oppositely along the stem. This species thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its ability to bloom early in the spring.
