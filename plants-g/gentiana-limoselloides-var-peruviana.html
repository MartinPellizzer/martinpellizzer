Gentiana limoselloides var. peruviana

Gentiana limoselloides var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
