Gentianella helianthemoides

Gentianella helianthemoides, commonly known as the sun-like gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that resemble the sun. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianella helianthemoides is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
