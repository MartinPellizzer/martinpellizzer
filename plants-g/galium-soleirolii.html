Galium soleirolii

Galium soleirolii, commonly known as the soleirolii bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Galium soleirolii thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
