Griffinia cordata

Griffinia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf ivy, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
