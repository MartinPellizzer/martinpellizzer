Garcinia rhumicowa

Garcinia rhumicowa, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruit that contains a white, segmented aril. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant flowers. Garcinia rhumicowa thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.

Its fruit is not typically consumed by humans but is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological role.
