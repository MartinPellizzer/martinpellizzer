Grevillea armigera

Grevillea armigera, commonly known as the honeycomb grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, honeycomb-like inflorescences that produce nectar-rich flowers, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea armigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
