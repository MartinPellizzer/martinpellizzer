Galarhoeus alatavicus

Galarhoeus alatavicus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its stems are thick and fleshy, storing water to survive prolonged dry periods.

Galarhoeus alatavicus is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
