Galium boreale var. alpinum

Galium boreale var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Galium boreale var. alpinum is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
