Gilia ochroleuca subsp. vivida

Gilia ochroleuca subsp.

vivida, commonly known as the yellow gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
