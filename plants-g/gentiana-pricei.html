Gentiana pricei

Gentiana pricei, commonly known as the Price's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing striking blue petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Gentiana pricei is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
