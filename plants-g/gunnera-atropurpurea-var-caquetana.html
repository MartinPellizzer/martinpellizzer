Gunnera atropurpurea var. caquetana

Gunnera atropurpurea var. caquetana, commonly known as the purple giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter. The leaves are typically a deep purple or bronze color, with a waxy surface that reflects light. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
