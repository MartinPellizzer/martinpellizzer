Gymnema longipedicellatum

Gymnema longipedicellatum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is known for its medicinal properties.

Gymnema longipedicellatum is also noted for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it of interest in traditional and modern medicine.
