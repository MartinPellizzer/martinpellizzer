Gaura villosa

Gaura villosa, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and clusters of delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is typically gray-green and covered in fine, hairy textures, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Gaura villosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
