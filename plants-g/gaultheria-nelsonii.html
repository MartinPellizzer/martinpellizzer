Gaultheria nelsonii

Gaultheria nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's wintergreen, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves, which contain methyl salicylate, giving it a wintergreen scent. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Gaultheria nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
