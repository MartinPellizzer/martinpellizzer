Gymnosporia linearis subsp. lanceolata

Gymnosporia linearis subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, typical of the gymnosperm-like structure of its name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
