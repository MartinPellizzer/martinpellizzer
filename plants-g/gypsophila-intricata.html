Gypsophila intricata

Gypsophila intricata, commonly known as the fringed poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like petals that give it a unique and intricate appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a soft, frilled edge that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Gypsophila intricata is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is also used in floral arrangements.
