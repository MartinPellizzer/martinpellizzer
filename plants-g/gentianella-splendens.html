Gentianella splendens

Gentianella splendens, commonly known as the splendour gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals that are often adorned with dark markings.

Gentianella splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
