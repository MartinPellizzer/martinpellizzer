Garcinia longeacuminata

Garcinia longeacuminata, commonly known as the African bitter orange, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces orange-colored fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which is believed to have potential weight-loss properties. Garcinia longeacuminata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Clusiaceae family.
