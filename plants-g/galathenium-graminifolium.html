Galathenium graminifolium

Galathenium graminifolium, commonly known as the grassy galathenium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name, while its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring.

Galathenium graminifolium is an important species for local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
