Genista lobelii var. pumila

Genista lobelii var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf genista, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
