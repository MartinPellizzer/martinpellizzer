Galenia pubescens

Galenia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy galenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a similar pubescent texture.

Galenia pubescens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
