Glycyrrhiza asperrima var. glabra

Glycyrrhiza asperrima var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth licorice, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their sweet, aromatic flavor and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for its potential therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.
