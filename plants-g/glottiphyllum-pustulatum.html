Glottiphyllum pustulatum

Glottiphyllum pustulatum, commonly known as the bubble cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, bulbous stems that are covered in a powdery, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which protect it from herbivores.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is well-suited for xerophytic environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
