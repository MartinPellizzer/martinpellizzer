Gerardia purpurea

Gerardia purpurea, commonly known as the purple gerardia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and distinctive leaves with serrated edges. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.

It is also known for its resilience to harsh weather conditions, making it a hardy choice for gardens.
