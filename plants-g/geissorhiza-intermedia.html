Geissorhiza intermedia

Geissorhiza intermedia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant has fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. Its rhizomes are tuberous, allowing it to survive periods of drought.

Geissorhiza intermedia is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
