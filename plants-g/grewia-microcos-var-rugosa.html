Grewia microcos var. rugosa

Grewia microcos var. rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf false hibiscus, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are often eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
