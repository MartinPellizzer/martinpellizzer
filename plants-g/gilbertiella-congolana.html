Gilbertiella congolana

It is a small, acrocarpous moss that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its reproductive structures, including capsules, are borne on short, erect stalks and are surrounded by a peristome, a specialized structure that aids in spore dispersal.

Gilbertiella congolana is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, with a notable presence in Central and East Africa.
