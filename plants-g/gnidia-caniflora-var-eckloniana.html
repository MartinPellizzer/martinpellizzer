Gnidia caniflora var. eckloniana

Gnidia caniflora var. eckloniana, commonly known as the Cape rose or white-flowered gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
