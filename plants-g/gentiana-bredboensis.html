Gentiana bredboensis

Gentiana bredboensis, commonly known as the Bredbo gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high elevations, often in the alpine zones of South Australia and Tasmania. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

The Bredbo gentian is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and resilience.
