Guaiacum afrum

Guaiacum afrum, commonly known as African guaiacwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Ghana.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense, aromatic wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain three winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and as a source of medicinal compounds.
