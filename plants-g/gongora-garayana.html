Gongora garayana

Gongora garayana, commonly known as the Garay's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant hues, which play a role in attracting pollinators.
