Gardenia subcarinata

Gardenia subcarinata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and scent.

The fruit, which is a capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
