Gravesia lamiana

Gravesia lamiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gravesia lamiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
