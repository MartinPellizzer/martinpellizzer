Gesneria sylvatica

Gesneria sylvatica, commonly known as the forest gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which bloom in spring or summer, come in shades of pink, red, or white, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
