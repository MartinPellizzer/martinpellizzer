Gompholobium shuttleworthii

Gompholobium shuttleworthii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The yellow wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
