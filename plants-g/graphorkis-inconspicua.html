Graphorkis inconspicua

Graphorkis inconspicua, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers clustered in a spike. It blooms during the spring and summer months, and its flowers are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance.

This species is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is protected under various conservation programs.
