Genista scythica

Genista scythica, commonly known as the Scythian broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas. Its slender, branching stems are covered with small, leathery leaves, and it produces yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
