Grevillea granulosa

Grevillea granulosa, commonly known as the granulate grevillea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, finely serrated foliage. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
