Geranium vagans subsp. whytei

Geranium vagans subsp.

whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
