Gaillonia afghanica

Gaillonia afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Gaillonia afghanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
