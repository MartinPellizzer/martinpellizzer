Geanthus dictyocoleus

Geanthus dictyocoleus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a temperate climate.
