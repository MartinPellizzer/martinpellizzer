Gonolobus plowmanii

Gonolobus plowmanii, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Gonolobus plowmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
