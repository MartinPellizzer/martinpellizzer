Guzmania verecunda

Guzmania verecunda, commonly known as the red-tipped peace lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features a rosette of narrow, evergreen leaves that collect rainwater in a central cup, providing a habitat for small organisms. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy, pinkish-white bract.

Guzmania verecunda is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
