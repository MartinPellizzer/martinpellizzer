Gilia leptantha subsp. purpusii

Gilia leptantha subsp.

purpusii, commonly known as the purple gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
