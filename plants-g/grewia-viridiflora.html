Grewia viridiflora

Grewia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia viridiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
