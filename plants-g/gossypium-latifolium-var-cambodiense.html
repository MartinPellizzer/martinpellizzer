Gossypium latifolium var. cambodiense

Gossypium latifolium var. cambodiense, commonly known as Cambodian cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces fluffy, white fibers used for textile purposes. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. It is an important species in regional agriculture due to its adaptability and fiber quality.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource for both traditional and modern textile industries.
