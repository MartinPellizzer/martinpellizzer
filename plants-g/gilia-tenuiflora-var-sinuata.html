Gilia tenuiflora var. sinuata

Gilia tenuiflora var. sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly sinuous, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
