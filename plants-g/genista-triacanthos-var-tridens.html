Genista triacanthos var. tridens

Genista triacanthos var. tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, each with a distinctive toothed edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
