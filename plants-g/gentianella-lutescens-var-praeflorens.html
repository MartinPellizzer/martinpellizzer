Gentianella lutescens var. praeflorens

Gentianella lutescens var. praeflorens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
