Galatella fugax

Galatella fugax, commonly known as the transient lily, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear briefly during the spring season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers with six petals. Due to its short blooming period and limited distribution, it is considered a challenging species for botanists to study.

Its conservation status is vulnerable, primarily due to habitat loss and climate change.
