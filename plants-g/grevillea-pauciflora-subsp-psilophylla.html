Grevillea pauciflora subsp. psilophylla

Grevillea pauciflora subsp.

psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
