Giesleria picta

Giesleria picta, commonly known as the painted giesleria, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant. Its foliage is typically variegated, with intricate patterns that make it stand out among other foliage plants.

Giesleria picta prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
