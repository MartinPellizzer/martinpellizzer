Gomphrena palmeri

Gomphrena palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant pink to red bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Gomphrena palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
