Gentianella silenoides var. silenoides

Gentianella silenoides var. silenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
