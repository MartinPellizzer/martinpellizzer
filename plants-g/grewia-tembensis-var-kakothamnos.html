Grewia tembensis var. kakothamnos

Grewia tembensis var. kakothamnos, commonly known as the red-flowered Grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range from white to deep red, often appearing in summer. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
