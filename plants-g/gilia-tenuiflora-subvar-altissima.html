Gilia tenuiflora subvar. altissima

Gilia tenuiflora subvar. altissima, commonly known as the tall gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
