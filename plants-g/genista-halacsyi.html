Genista halacsyi

Genista halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian Broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, forming dense shrubs that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Genista halacsyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
