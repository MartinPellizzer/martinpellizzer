Geranium chelikii

Geranium chelikii, commonly known as the Chelikii geranium, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or purple blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and arranged oppositely along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium chelikii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
