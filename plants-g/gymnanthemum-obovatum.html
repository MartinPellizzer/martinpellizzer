Gymnanthemum obovatum

Gymnanthemum obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically obovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Gymnanthemum obovatum is often found in sandy or rocky habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
