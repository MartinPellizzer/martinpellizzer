Grona griffithiana

Grona griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian snow lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in high-altitude environments, typically above 3,000 meters. The plant features a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces striking white or pale blue flowers. It is adapted to cold, arid conditions and has a slow growth rate.

Due to its unique habitat and appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in alpine gardens.
