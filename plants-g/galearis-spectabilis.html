Galearis spectabilis

Galearis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy greenbulb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Galearis spectabilis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
