Gentiana affinis var. brachycalyx

Gentiana affinis var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
