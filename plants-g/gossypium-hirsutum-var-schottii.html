Gossypium hirsutum var. schottii

Gossypium hirsutum var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's upland cotton, is a variant of the upland cotton species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fibrous seeds and hairy stems, typical of the Gossypium genus. This variety is primarily cultivated for its cotton fibers, which are used in textile production. The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include alternate leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.
