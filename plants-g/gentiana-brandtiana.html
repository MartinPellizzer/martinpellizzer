Gentiana brandtiana

Gentiana brandtiana, commonly known as Brandt's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are typically arranged in a radial pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the top of its slender stems. Gentiana brandtiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Gentianaceae family.
