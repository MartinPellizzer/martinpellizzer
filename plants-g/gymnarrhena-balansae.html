Gymnarrhena balansae

Gymnarrhena balansae, commonly known as the Balansae acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are adapted to disperse in dry environments.

Gymnarrhena balansae is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
