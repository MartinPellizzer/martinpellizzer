Geissorhiza secunda var. pusilla

Geissorhiza secunda var. pusilla, commonly known as the small yellow rain lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers during the rainy season. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
