Grevillea tridentifera var. pubescens

Grevillea tridentifera var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy swamp grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, recurved leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The variety 'pubescens' is distinguished by its hairy texture on the leaves and stems.
