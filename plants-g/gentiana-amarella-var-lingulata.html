Gentiana amarella var. lingulata

Gentiana amarella var. lingulata, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are slender and erect.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes, and they attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
