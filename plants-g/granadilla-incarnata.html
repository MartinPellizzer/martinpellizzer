Granadilla incarnata

Granadilla incarnata, commonly known as the pink granadilla, is a member of the Passifloraceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and have a unique, almost floral scent. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a juicy, tangy flavor. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
