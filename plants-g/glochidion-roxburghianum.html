Glochidion roxburghianum

Glochidion roxburghianum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to orange-red when mature. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

Glochidion roxburghianum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
