Glycine wightii subsp. petitiana

Glycine wightii subsp.

petitiana, commonly known as the dwarf poinciana, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, delicate flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, flat pods containing seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in traditional medicine.
