Gladiolus praecox

Gladiolus praecox, commonly known as the early gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements.

Gladiolus praecox thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
