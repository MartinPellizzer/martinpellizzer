Galeopsis inermis

Galeopsis inermis, commonly known as common hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows up to 60 cm tall, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are hairy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in whorled clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its roots are fibrous, and it often thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas.

Galeopsis inermis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
