Gymnocalycium megatae subsp. holdii

Gymnocalycium megatae subsp.

holdii, commonly known as the giant moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its large, fleshy, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red, spherical fruits. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique growth form.
