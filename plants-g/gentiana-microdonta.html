Gentiana microdonta

Gentiana microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.
