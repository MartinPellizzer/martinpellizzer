Geranium murcicum

Geranium murcicum, commonly known as the Murcia geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent throat.

Geranium murcicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
