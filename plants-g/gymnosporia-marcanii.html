Gymnosporia marcanii

Gymnosporia marcanii, commonly known as the African burr acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas.

Gymnosporia marcanii is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system for water absorption.
