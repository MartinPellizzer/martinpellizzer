Gymnadenia × heteroglossa

Gymnadenia × heteroglossa, commonly known as the hybrid grass of heaven, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Gymnadenia clypeata and Gymnadenia conopsea, characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Gymnadenia × heteroglossa is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is of interest to botanists due to its hybrid origin and ecological significance.
