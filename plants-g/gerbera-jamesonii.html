Gerbera jamesonii

Gerbera jamesonii, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant has spoon-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.

Gerbera jamesonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
