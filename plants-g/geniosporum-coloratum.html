Geniosporum coloratum

Geniosporum coloratum, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
