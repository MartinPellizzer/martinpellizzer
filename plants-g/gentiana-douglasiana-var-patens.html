Gentiana douglasiana var. patens

Gentiana douglasiana var. patens, commonly known as the Douglas's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as coniferous forests and stream banks. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the top of its slender stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
