Galium mexicanum var. platyphyllum

Galium mexicanum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as Mexican bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
