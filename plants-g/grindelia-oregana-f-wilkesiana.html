Grindelia oregana f. wilkesiana

Grindelia oregana f. wilkesiana, commonly known as the Wilkes' gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. Its unique morphology includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

Grindelia oregana f. wilkesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
