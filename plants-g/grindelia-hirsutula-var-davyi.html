Grindelia hirsutula var. davyi

Grindelia hirsutula var. davyi, commonly known as the Desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert plains. Its leaves are hairy and aromatic, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its resilience to drought and heat.
