Genista hirta proles scopolii

Genista hirta proles scopolii, commonly known as the Scopolia broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
