Goodenia glomerata

Goodenia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as New South Wales and Queensland. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Goodenia glomerata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
