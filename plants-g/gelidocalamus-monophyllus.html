Gelidocalamus monophyllus

Gelidocalamus monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf bamboo, is a unique species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and possesses a single, elongated leaf per stem, which is a distinguishing feature. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its rhizome system is well-developed, allowing it to anchor firmly in the soil and withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an important species for ecological studies in high-altitude ecosystems.
