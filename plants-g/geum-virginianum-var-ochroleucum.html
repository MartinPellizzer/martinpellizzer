Geum virginianum var. ochroleucum

Geum virginianum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as white wood avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded to partially shaded woodland habitats.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.
