Gentianopsis ciliata subsp. ciliata

Gentianopsis ciliata subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with prominent white markings.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins, giving it its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
