Gentiana calycosa subsp. typica

Gentiana calycosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the typical gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
