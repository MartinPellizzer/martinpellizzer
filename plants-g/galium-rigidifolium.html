Galium rigidifolium

Galium rigidifolium, commonly known as rigidleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often stiff and rigid. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Galium rigidifolium is characterized by its simple, branched inflorescence and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
