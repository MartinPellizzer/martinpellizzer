Gaura neomexicana subsp. coloradensis

Gaura neomexicana subsp.

coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado gaura, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, narrow leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
