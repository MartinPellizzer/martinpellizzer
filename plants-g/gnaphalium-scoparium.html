Gnaphalium scoparium

Gnaphalium scoparium, commonly known as silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and rocky areas. Its silver-gray leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
