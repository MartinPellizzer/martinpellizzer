Glossopappus macrotus subsp. hesperius

Glossopappus macrotus subsp.

hesperius, commonly known as the western giant dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers.

The plant has a taproot system and produces a pappus of bristles that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its native range.
