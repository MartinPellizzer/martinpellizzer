Gagea graeca

Gagea graeca, commonly known as the Greek spring beauty, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, grass-like foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained habitats.

Gagea graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
