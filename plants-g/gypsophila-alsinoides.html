Gypsophila alsinoides

Gypsophila alsinoides, commonly known as baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Gypsophila alsinoides is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements.
