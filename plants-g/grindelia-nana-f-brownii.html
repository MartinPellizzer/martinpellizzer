Grindelia nana f. brownii

Grindelia nana f. brownii, commonly known as the dwarf gumweed, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to ecological diversity in its native habitat.
