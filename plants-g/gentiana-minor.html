Gentiana minor

Gentiana minor, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by flies and bees.
