Gravesia capitata

Gravesia capitata, commonly known as the golden crown or crown of glory, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their striking appearance.
