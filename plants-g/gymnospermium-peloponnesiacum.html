Gymnospermium peloponnesiacum

Gymnospermium peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian gymnosperm, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the mountainous regions of the Peloponnese in Greece.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, characterized by its ability to produce seeds without the protection of an ovary. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, with cones serving as the primary structures for seed development. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
