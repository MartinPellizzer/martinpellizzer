Germanea nudiflora

Germanea nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower germander, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, are usually pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Germanea nudiflora is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it should be used with caution due to its potential toxicity.
