Gastrolobium elegans

Gastrolobium elegans, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Gastrolobium elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
