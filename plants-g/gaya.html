Gaya

Gaya, commonly known as the sacred fig tree, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fruits. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also culturally significant in various parts of the world.
