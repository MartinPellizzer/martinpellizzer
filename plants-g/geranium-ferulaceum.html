Geranium ferulaceum

Geranium ferulaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Geranium ferulaceum is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
