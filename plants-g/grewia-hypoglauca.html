Grewia hypoglauca

Grewia hypoglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a small deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or pale green waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia hypoglauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
