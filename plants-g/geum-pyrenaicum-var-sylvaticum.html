Geum pyrenaicum var. sylvaticum

Geum pyrenaicum var. sylvaticum, commonly known as the alpine avens, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a height typically ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
