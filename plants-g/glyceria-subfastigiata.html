Glyceria subfastigiata

Glyceria subfastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and along streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Glyceria subfastigiata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
