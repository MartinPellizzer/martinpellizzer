Geranium bangii

Geranium bangii, commonly known as the Bangii geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in dense inflorescences.

Geranium bangii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
