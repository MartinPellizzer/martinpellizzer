Gomphrena ferruginea

Gomphrena ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads. The plant produces vibrant red or pink bracts that resemble small, round balls, giving it a unique appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

Gomphrena ferruginea thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
