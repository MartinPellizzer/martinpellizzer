Geranium pseudosibiricum f. striatum

Geranium pseudosibiricum f. striatum, commonly known as the striped geranium, is a decorative plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves with distinct white or pale green stripes. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
