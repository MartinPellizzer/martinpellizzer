Gymnogramma depauperata

Gymnogramma depauperata, commonly known as the poor fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Gymnogramma depauperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
