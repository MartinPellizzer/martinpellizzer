Grindelia hirsutula f. tomentulosa

Grindelia hirsutula f. tomentulosa, commonly known as the tomentose globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
