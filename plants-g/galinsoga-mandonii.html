Galinsoga mandonii

Galinsoga mandonii, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect or ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Galinsoga mandonii is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
