Gireoudia laciniata

Gireoudia laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble lace, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by a showy, spadix-like structure. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gireoudia laciniata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ability to add a dramatic visual impact to gardens.
