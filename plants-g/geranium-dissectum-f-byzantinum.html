Geranium dissectum f. byzantinum

Geranium dissectum f. byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine geranium, is a cultivar of the geranium genus characterized by its finely cut, lobed leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage is particularly notable for its intricate, almost lace-like appearance, which adds texture to garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
