Gagea chinensis

Gagea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and cool, temperate climates. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical significance lies in its floral structure and seasonal blooming pattern.

Gagea chinensis is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous flowering plants.
