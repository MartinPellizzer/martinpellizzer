Glaucium corniculatum var. tricolorum

Glaucium corniculatum var. tricolorum, commonly known as the three-color glory flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display three distinct colors—typically yellow, purple, and blue—arranged in a radial pattern. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique floral morphology and coloration make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
