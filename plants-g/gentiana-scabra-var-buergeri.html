Gentiana scabra var. buergeri

Gentiana scabra var. buergeri, commonly known as the Buerger's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
