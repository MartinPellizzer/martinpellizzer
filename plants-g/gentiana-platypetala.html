Gentiana platypetala

Gentiana platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy and often brightly colored flowers. Gentiana platypetala thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
