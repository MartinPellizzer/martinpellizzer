Gentiana newberryi subsp. tiogana

Gentiana newberryi subsp.

tiogana, commonly known as the Tiogana gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, radial flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in some regions.
