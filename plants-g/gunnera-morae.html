Gunnera morae

Gunnera morae, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the Gunneraceae family and is known for its unique structure, with a short stem and clustered leaves. It thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

The leaves are typically green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer months.
