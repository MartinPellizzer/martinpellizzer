Grevillea acerata

Grevillea acerata, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea acerata is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
