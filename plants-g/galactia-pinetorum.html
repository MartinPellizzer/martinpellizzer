Galactia pinetorum

Galactia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Galactia pinetorum is often found in open woodlands and along rocky slopes, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
