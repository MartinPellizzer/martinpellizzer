Gomphocentrum calceolus

Gomphocentrum calceolus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Gomphocentrum calceolus prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
