Gymnocarpium longulum

Gymnocarpium longulum, commonly known as the smooth nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its fruit is a three-sided, smooth nut-like structure that contains a single seed.

Gymnocarpium longulum is often found in wetland areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
