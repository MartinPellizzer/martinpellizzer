Grevillea obliqua

Grevillea obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats, thriving in full sun.

Grevillea obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens throughout Australia.
