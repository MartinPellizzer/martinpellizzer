Gentianella iberidea

Gentianella iberidea, commonly known as the Iberian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
