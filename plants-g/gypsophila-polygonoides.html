Gypsophila polygonoides

Gypsophila polygonoides, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, needle-like leaves reduce water loss, and it produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Gypsophila polygonoides is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
