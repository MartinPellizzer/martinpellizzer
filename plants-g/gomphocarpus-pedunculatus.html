Gomphocarpus pedunculatus

Gomphocarpus pedunculatus, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.
