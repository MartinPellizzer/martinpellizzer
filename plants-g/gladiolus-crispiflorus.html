Gladiolus crispiflorus

Gladiolus crispiflorus, commonly known as the curlyflower gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature curled, reflexed petals in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers that are often arranged in a spiral pattern.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
