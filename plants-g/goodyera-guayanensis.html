Goodyera guayanensis

Goodyera guayanensis, commonly known as the Guianan lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in a loose raceme. The plant has a short rhizome with numerous roots and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Goodyera guayanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
