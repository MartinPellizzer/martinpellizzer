Gomidesia garopabensis

Gomidesia garopabensis, commonly known as the Garopaba cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. It plays a significant role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
