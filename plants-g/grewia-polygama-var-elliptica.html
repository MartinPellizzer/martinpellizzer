Grewia polygama var. elliptica

Grewia polygama var. elliptica, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
