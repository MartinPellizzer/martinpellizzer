Galium peteroanum

Galium peteroanum, commonly known as the Peter's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

Galium peteroanum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
