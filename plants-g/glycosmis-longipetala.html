Glycosmis longipetala

Glycosmis longipetala, commonly known as the long-petioled sweetleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long petioles and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.
