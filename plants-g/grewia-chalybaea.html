Grewia chalybaea

Grewia chalybaea, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia chalybaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
