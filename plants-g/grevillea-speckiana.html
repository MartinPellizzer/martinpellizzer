Grevillea speckiana

Grevillea speckiana, commonly known as the speckled grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typical of the Grevillea genus.

Grevillea speckiana is well-suited to dry, well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
