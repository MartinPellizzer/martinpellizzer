Glycyrrhiza lutea

Glycyrrhiza lutea, commonly known as yellow licorice, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, woody roots. The plant is notable for its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its sweet taste and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its potential pharmacological applications, it is of interest in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
