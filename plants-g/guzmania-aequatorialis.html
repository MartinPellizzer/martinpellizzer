Guzmania aequatorialis

Guzmania aequatorialis, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a tropical bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful rosette of sword-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with vibrant red bracts that resemble a pineapple, giving it its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania aequatorialis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
