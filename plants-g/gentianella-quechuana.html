Gentianella quechuana

Gentianella quechuana, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the cold, high-altitude environments of South America. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Gentianella quechuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some Andean communities.
