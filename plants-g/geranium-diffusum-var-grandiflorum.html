Geranium diffusum var. grandiflorum

Geranium diffusum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, often branching toward the top. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
