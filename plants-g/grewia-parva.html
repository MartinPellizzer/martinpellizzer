Grewia parva

Grewia parva, commonly known as the small-growing bush or dwarf growia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Grewia parva is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
