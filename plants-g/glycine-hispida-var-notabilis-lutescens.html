Glycine hispida var. notabilis-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. notabilis-lutescens, commonly known as the golden crown bean, is a variant of the hairy vetch, characterized by its distinctive yellow floral bracts.

It is a leguminous plant that belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as a cover crop or for its ornamental value.

Its unique coloration and growth habit make it a notable species within the Glycine genus.
