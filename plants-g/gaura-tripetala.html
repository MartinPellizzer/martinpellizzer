Gaura tripetala

Gaura tripetala, commonly known as the three-lobed gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of delicate, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gaura tripetala is valued in gardens for its airy appearance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
