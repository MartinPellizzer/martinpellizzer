Gladiolus recurvus

Gladiolus recurvus, commonly known as the zigzag gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear showy, trumpet-like flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive recurved (bent backward) shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
