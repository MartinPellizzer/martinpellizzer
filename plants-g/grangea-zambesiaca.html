Grangea zambesiaca

Grangea zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Grangea zambesiaca is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
