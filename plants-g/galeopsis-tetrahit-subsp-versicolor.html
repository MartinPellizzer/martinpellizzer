Galeopsis tetrahit subsp. versicolor

Galeopsis tetrahit subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the purple hedge nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
