Grammitis interrupta

Grammitis interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often divided into smaller segments. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a soft, velvety texture.

Grammitis interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
