Grindelia texana

Grindelia texana, commonly known as Texas gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and grassland habitats. It has a low, sprawling growth habit with silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Grindelia texana is known for its resilience to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
