Garcinia merguensis var. pyramidata

Garcinia merguensis var. pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramidated garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sparsely branched structure and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a thick, aromatic pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical and tropical gardens.
