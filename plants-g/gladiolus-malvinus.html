Gladiolus malvinus

Gladiolus malvinus, commonly known as the New Zealand gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and are often used in floral arrangements. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
