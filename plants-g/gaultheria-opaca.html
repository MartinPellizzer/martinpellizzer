Gaultheria opaca

Gaultheria opaca, commonly known as the northern highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries in the summer. Its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Gaultheria opaca thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
