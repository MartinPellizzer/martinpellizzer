Ghinia subbiflora

Ghinia subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ghinia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
