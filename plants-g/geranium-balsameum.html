Geranium balsameum

Geranium balsameum, commonly known as the Indian plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Geranium balsameum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
