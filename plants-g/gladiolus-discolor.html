Gladiolus discolor

Gladiolus discolor, commonly known as the white-eyed gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with a distinctive white eye at the center of each bloom. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as cut flowers.
