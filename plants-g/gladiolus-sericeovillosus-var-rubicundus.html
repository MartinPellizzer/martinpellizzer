Gladiolus sericeovillosus var. rubicundus

Gladiolus sericeovillosus var. rubicundus, commonly known as the red-flowered gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red and pink. The leaves are long, narrow, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
