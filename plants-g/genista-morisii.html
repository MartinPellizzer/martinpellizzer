Genista morisii

Genista morisii, commonly known as the Moris's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The shrub is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
