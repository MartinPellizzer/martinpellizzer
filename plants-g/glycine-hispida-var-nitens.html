Glycine hispida var. nitens

Glycine hispida var. nitens, commonly known as the hairy soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate amounts of water.

This variety is also valued for its role in agricultural systems due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
