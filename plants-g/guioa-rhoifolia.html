Guioa rhoifolia

Guioa rhoifolia, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Guioa rhoifolia is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
