Genista carinalis

Genista carinalis, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three narrow, linear leaflets, and the plant has a woody, branching structure.

Genista carinalis is adapted to withstand salt spray and is often found in dunes and rocky coastal habitats.
