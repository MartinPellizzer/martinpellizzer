Gomidesia fenzliana var. spathulata

Gomidesia fenzliana var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved gomidesia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
