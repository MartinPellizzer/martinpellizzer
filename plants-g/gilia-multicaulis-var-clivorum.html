Gilia multicaulis var. clivorum

Gilia multicaulis var. clivorum, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The Sierra gilia plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
