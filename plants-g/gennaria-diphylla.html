Gennaria diphylla

Gennaria diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants known for their cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually basal, with two distinct forms—some upright and others prostrate—hence its common name.

Gennaria diphylla produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, contributing to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
