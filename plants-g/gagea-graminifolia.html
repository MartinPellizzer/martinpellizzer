Gagea graminifolia

Gagea graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to North America, particularly found in the western United States and parts of Canada. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in woodland or rocky habitats.

Gagea graminifolia is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
