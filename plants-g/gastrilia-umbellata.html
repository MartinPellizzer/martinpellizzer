Gastrilia umbellata

Gastrilia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in an umbrella-like inflorescence. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Gastrilia umbellata is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
