Gymnocalycium taningaense subsp. fuschilloi

Gymnocalycium taningaense subsp.

fuschilloi, commonly known as the Tacingaensis moon cactus, is a cacti species native to Argentina. It is characterized by its compact, spherical stem that often develops a woolly coating.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
