Grammitis paradoxa

Grammitis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unusual morphology, featuring a creeping rhizome and small, clustered fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant exhibits a peculiar growth habit, with its sporangia developing on the underside of the fronds in a pattern that is both intricate and distinctive. Grammitis paradoxa is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a common sight in the understory of humid forests.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
