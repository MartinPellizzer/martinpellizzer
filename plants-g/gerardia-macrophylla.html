Gerardia macrophylla

Gerardia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive white or grayish underside.

Gerardia macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
