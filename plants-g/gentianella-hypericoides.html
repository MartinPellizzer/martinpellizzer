Gentianella hypericoides

Gentianella hypericoides, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
