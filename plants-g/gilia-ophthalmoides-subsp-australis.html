Gilia ophthalmoides subsp. australis

Gilia ophthalmoides subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian Gilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It has opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
