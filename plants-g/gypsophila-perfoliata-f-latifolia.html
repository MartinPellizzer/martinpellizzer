Gypsophila perfoliata f. latifolia

Gypsophila perfoliata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other gypsophila species.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its fluffy, airy blooms.
