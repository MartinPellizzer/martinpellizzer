Gorteria oppositifolia

Gorteria oppositifolia, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a favorite among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
