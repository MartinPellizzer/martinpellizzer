Geranium escalonense

Geranium escalonense, commonly known as the Escalona geranium, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium escalonense is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
