Genetyllis fimbriata

Genetyllis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed genetyllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive fringed or toothed edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Genetyllis fimbriata is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to add texture to gardens in arid climates.
