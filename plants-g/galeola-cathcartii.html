Galeola cathcartii

Galeola cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's galeola, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear a few to several flowers, each with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
