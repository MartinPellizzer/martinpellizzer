Galarhoeus adenochlorus

Galarhoeus adenochlorus, commonly known as the silverleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, typically during the spring and early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Galarhoeus adenochlorus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
