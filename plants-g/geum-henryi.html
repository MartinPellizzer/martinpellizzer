Geum henryi

Geum henryi, commonly known as Henry's avens, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to red flowers that are often tinged with white. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive appearance that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Geum henryi is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
