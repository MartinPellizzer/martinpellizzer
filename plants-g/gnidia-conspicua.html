Gnidia conspicua

Gnidia conspicua, commonly known as the showy kniphofia, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gnidia conspicua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
