Gentiana wilsonii subsp. striolata

Gentiana wilsonii subsp.

striolata, commonly known as the striolated gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are typically five to seven in number and arranged in a radial pattern.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
