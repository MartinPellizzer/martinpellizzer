Geum urbanum hirtum

Geum urbanum hirtum, commonly known as rough avens, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
