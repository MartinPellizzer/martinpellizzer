Gastrochilus luridus

Gastrochilus luridus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, often in clusters, and are pollinated by specific insects.

The botanical features of Gastrochilus luridus make it a notable species in the Orchidaceae family.
