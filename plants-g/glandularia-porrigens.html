Glandularia porrigens

Glandularia porrigens, commonly known as the sticky fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Glandularia porrigens is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
