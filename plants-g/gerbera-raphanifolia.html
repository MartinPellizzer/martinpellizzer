Gerbera raphanifolia

Gerbera raphanifolia, commonly known as the radishleaf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has alternate, lobed leaves that resemble the leaves of a radish, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Gerbera raphanifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
