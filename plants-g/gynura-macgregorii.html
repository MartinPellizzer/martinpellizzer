Gynura macgregorii

Gynura macgregorii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Gynura macgregorii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
