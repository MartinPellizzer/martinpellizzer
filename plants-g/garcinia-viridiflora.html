Garcinia viridiflora

Garcinia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia viridiflora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
