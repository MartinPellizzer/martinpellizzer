Gnaphalium macrocephalum

Gnaphalium macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed feather grass, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble small clouds. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium macrocephalum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
