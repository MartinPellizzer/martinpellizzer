Gnaphalium rivulare

Gnaphalium rivulare, commonly known as the stream gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and streamside habitats in the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing or flowing water. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

Gnaphalium rivulare plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
