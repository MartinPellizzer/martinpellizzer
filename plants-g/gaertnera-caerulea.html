Gaertnera caerulea

Gaertnera caerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Gaertnera caerulea is often found in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
