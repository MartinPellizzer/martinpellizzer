Grammitis contigua

Grammitis contigua, commonly known as the common grammitis, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the abaxial surface of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis contigua is widely distributed across Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and parts of Africa.
