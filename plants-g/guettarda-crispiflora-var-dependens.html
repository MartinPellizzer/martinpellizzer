Guettarda crispiflora var. dependens

Guettarda crispiflora var. dependens, commonly known as the velvetwood or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its fragrant white flowers are borne in clusters and bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators.

The wood of this species is dense and strong, making it valuable for construction and furniture-making.
