Gentlea crenulata

Gentlea crenulata, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments. Its leaves are usually crenate, giving it the species name "crenulata.".

It plays an important role in alpine ecosystems and is often used in traditional medicine.
