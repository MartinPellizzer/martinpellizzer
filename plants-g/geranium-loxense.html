Geranium loxense

Geranium loxense, commonly known as the narrowleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinct serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in summer. Geranium loxense is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
