Gentiana propinqua f. acyanea

Gentiana propinqua f. acyanea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
