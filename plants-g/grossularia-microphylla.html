Grossularia microphylla

Grossularia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
