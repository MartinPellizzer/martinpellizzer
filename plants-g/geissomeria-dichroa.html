Geissomeria dichroa

Geissomeria dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
