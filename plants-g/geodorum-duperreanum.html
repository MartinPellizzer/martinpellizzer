Geodorum duperreanum

Geodorum duperreanum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
