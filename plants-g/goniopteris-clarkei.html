Goniopteris clarkei

Goniopteris clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

Goniopteris clarkei is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
