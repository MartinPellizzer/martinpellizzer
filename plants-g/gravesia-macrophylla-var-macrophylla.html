Gravesia macrophylla var. macrophylla

Gravesia macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved gravesia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
