Gypsophila glomerata

Gypsophila glomerata, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements.
