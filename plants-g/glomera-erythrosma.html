Glomera erythrosma

Glomera erythrosma, commonly known as the red-stemmed glomera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
