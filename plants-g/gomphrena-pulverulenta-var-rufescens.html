Gomphrena pulverulenta var. rufescens

Gomphrena pulverulenta var. rufescens, commonly known as the red dustflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive powdery bloom that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
