Garcinia griffithii

Garcinia griffithii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, acidic pulp, which is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Garcinia griffithii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
