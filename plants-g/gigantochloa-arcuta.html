Gigantochloa arcuta

Gigantochloa arcuta, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gigantochloa arcuta is often used in landscaping and for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.
