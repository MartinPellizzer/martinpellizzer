Gonocytisus pterocladus

Gonocytisus pterocladus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
