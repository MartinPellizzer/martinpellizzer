Gentiana asclepiadea var. plicata

Gentiana asclepiadea var. plicata, commonly known as the wrinkled gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers are notable for their intricate structure and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
