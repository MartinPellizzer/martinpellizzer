Grindelia prunelloides

Grindelia prunelloides, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Grindelia prunelloides is also known for its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
