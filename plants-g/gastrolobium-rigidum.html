Gastrolobium rigidum

Gastrolobium rigidum, commonly known as the rigid acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruit and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features rigid, often spiny stems and bipinnate leaves that are arranged in a feathery pattern. Its yellow flowers are clustered in globular heads, and it produces hard, woody pods that contain seeds.

Gastrolobium rigidum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
