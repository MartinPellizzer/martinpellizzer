Gentiana albiflora

Gentiana albiflora, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
