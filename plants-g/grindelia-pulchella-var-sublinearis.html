Grindelia pulchella var. sublinearis

Grindelia pulchella var. sublinearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy, or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The narrow, linear leaves and showy inflorescences make it a distinctive species within the Asteraceae family.
