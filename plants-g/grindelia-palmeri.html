Grindelia palmeri

Grindelia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's brittlebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh climates make it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Grindelia palmeri is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from the soil.
