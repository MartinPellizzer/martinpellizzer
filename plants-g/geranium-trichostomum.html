Geranium trichostomum

Geranium trichostomum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often clustered in cymes, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Geranium trichostomum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
