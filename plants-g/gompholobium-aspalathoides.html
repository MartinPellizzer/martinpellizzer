Gompholobium aspalathoides

Gompholobium aspalathoides, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
