Gussonea aurantiaca

Gussonea aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-flowered gussonea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Gussonea aurantiaca is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
