Goniopteris fuertesii

Goniopteris fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertes' shield fern, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which helps in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a subject of interest in botanical research.
