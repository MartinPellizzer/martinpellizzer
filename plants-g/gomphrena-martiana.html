Gomphrena martiana

Gomphrena martiana, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, feathery heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Gomphrena martiana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
