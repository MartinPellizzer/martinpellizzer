Grindelia anomala

Grindelia anomala, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with hairy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that help it colonize disturbed soils.

Grindelia anomala is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of desert ecosystems.
