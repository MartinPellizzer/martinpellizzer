Geranium fuscatum

Geranium fuscatum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to low-maintenance gardens.
