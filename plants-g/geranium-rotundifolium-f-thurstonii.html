Geranium rotundifolium f. thurstonii

Geranium rotundifolium f. thurstonii, commonly known as Thurston's geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features rounded, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and brief blooming period.
