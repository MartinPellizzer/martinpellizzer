Gladiolus securiger

Gladiolus securiger, commonly known as the swordleaf gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, and are arranged in inflorescences called gladioli. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its use in both horticultural displays and as a cut flower.
