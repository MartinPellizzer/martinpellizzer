Gilia viscidula var. heterodoxa

Gilia viscidula var. heterodoxa, commonly known as the sticky gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
