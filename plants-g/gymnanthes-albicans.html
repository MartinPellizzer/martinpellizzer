Gymnanthes albicans

Gymnanthes albicans, commonly known as the white beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of white, lantern-shaped fruits that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and the plant is primarily valued for its ornamental fruits.
