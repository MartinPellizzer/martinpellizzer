Galactia eggersii

Galactia eggersii, commonly known as the Texas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family.

Galactia eggersii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
