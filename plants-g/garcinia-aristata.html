Garcinia aristata

Garcinia aristata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature, containing a single seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp. Garcinia aristata is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional contexts.
