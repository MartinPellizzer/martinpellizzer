Galeopsis nodosa var. sulphurea

Galeopsis nodosa var. sulphurea, commonly known as sulphurweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
