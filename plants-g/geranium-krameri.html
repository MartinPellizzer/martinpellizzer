Geranium krameri

Geranium krameri, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting dark center. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium krameri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
