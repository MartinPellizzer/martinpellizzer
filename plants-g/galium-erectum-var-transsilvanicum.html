Galium erectum var. transsilvanicum

Galium erectum var. transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
