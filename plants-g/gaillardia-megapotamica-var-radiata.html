Gaillardia megapotamica var. radiata

Gaillardia megapotamica var. radiata, commonly known as the Mexican blanket flower, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features vibrant, daisy-like flowers with bright orange and red petals, often with a yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.

The botanical structure supports its role as a pollinator attractor, providing nectar for bees and butterflies.
