Gymapsis polystachya

Gymapsis polystachya, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with prominent stamens, which are often pollinated by insects. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near water bodies.

Gymapsis polystachya plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
