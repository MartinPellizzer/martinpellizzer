Globba albiflora var. aurea

Globba albiflora var. aurea, commonly known as the golden globba, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive yellowish or golden hue along the edges. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers on tall, slender stalks, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and tropical landscapes.
