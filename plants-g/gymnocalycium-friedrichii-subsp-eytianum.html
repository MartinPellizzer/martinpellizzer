Gymnocalycium friedrichii subsp. eytianum

Gymnocalycium friedrichii subsp.

eytianum, commonly known as the Eytian's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to South America. It is characterized by its swollen, globular stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
