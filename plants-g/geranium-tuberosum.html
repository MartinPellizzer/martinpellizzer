Geranium tuberosum

Geranium tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in various soil conditions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Geranium tuberosum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
