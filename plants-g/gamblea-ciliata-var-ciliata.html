Gamblea ciliata var. ciliata

Gamblea ciliata var. ciliata, commonly known as the Ciliate Gambelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, brush-like cluster. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight waxy coating, while the flowers are usually a vibrant yellow or orange.

Gamblea ciliata var. ciliata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
