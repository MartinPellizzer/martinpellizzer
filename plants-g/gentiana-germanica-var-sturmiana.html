Gentiana germanica var. sturmiana

Gentiana germanica var. sturmiana, commonly known as the Sturm's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
