Grewia nervosa

Grewia nervosa, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature.

Grewia nervosa is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
