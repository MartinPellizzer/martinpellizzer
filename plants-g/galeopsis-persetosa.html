Galeopsis persetosa

Galeopsis persetosa, commonly known as slender horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces small, oval seeds.

Galeopsis persetosa is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
