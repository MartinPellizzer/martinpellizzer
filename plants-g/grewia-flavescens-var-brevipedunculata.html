Grewia flavescens var. brevipedunculata

Grewia flavescens var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the dwarf yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.
