Geranium phaeum var. nodosum

Geranium phaeum var. nodosum, commonly known as the purple-leafed geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and hardiness.
