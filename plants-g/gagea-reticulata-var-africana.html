Gagea reticulata var. africana

Gagea reticulata var. africana, commonly known as the African netleaf squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers with six petals. The plant is characterized by its reticulate (net-like) leaf venation, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
