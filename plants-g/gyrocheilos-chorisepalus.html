Gyrocheilos chorisepalus

Gyrocheilos chorisepalus, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
