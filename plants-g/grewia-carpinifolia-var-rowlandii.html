Grewia carpinifolia var. rowlandii

Grewia carpinifolia var. rowlandii, commonly known as the Rowland's waxleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, velvety foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a hedge plant.
