Glyceria vahliana

Glyceria vahliana, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Glyceria vahliana produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
