Gelasia pauciflora

Gelasia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered gelasia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink, often in late winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in high-altitude, rocky environments where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
