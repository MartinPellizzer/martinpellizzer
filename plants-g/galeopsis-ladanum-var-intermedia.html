Galeopsis ladanum var. intermedia

Galeopsis ladanum var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hedge nettle, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
