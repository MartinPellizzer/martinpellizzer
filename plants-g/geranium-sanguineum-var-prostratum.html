Geranium sanguineum var. prostratum

Geranium sanguineum var. prostratum, commonly known as creeping geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
