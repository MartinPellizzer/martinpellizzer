Gaertnera bracteata var. glabrifolia

Gaertnera bracteata var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the glabrous bracteated gaertnera, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are typically glabrous, or smooth, with a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
