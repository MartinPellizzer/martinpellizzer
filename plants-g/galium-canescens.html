Galium canescens

Galium canescens, commonly known as hoary bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features slender, hairy stems that are often tinged with a pale or grayish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium canescens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
