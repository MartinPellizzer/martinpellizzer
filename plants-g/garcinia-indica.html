Garcinia indica

Garcinia indica, commonly known as kunku or garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is typically yellow or orange when ripe, contains a single large seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
