Gentiana pneumonanthe var. boryana

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. boryana, commonly known as the Boryana gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
