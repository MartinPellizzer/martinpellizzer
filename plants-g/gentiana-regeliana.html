Gentiana regeliana

Gentiana regeliana, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Gentiana regeliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and hardiness.
