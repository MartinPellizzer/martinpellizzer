Geranium malopoides

Geranium malopoides, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Geranium malopoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
