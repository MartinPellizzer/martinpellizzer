Galinsoga calva

Galinsoga calva, commonly known as hairy gallinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Galinsoga calva is considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
