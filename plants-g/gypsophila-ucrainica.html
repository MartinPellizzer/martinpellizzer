Gypsophila ucrainica

Gypsophila ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to Ukraine and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its ethereal appearance.

Gypsophila ucrainica is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
