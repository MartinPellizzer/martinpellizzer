Glycosmis superba

Glycosmis superba, commonly known as the Chinese date or wild date tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, fleshy fruits that resemble dates. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies, while the fruit is sometimes consumed fresh or processed into jams.

Glycosmis superba thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
