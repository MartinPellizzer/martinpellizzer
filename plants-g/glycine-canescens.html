Glycine canescens

Glycine canescens, commonly known as the white bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval, hairy leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Glycine canescens is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its fibrous stems and seeds have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
