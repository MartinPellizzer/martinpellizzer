Geranium transversale

Geranium transversale, commonly known as the Transverse geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its flowers are five-petaled and have a distinctive spurred petal at the base.

Geranium transversale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
