Giardia hirsuta

Giardia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy giardia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters.

Giardia hirsuta is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
