Goodenia amplexans var. angustifolia

Goodenia amplexans var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often clasping, giving it a unique appearance.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
