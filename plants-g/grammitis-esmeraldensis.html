Grammitis esmeraldensis

Grammitis esmeraldensis, commonly known as Emerald Fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and intricate frond structure, which consists of closely arranged, linear leaflets. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bright green, with a glossy appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

Grammitis esmeraldensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
