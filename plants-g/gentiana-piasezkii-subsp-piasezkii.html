Gentiana piasezkii subsp. piasezkii

Gentiana piasezkii subsp.

piasezkii, commonly known as the Piasezkii gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
