Geranium nodosum subsp. striatum

Geranium nodosum subsp.

striatum, commonly known as the striped geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
