Grevillea georgeana

Grevillea georgeana, commonly known as the George's grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea georgeana is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
