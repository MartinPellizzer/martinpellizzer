Glycine wightii var. coimbatorensis

Glycine wightii var. coimbatorensis, commonly known as Coimbatore velvet bean, is a variant of the velvet bean plant native to South India.

It is a leguminous shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used as a protein source in animal feed.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobial bacteria.
