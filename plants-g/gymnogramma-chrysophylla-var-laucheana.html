Gymnogramma chrysophylla var. laucheana

Gymnogramma chrysophylla var. laucheana, commonly known as the golden-leafed gymnogramma, is a variant of the gymnogramma genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves that are typically bright yellow to golden in color. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fronds arising from a central stem. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, with a dense arrangement of small, rounded lobes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
