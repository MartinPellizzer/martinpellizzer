Grevillea rudis

Grevillea rudis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and its showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, which includes many flowering plants adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Grevillea rudis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.

It is also known for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
