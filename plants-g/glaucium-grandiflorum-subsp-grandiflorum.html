Glaucium grandiflorum subsp. grandiflorum

Glaucium grandiflorum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive milky sap that contains alkaloids.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and rough in texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
