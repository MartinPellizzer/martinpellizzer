Garcinia giadidii

Garcinia giadidii, commonly known as the African yellow fruit, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange berries that contain a high concentration of xanthones. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
