Gladiolus muirii

Gladiolus muirii, commonly known as the Muir's gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, upright flower stalks. The plant produces showy, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its use in horticulture.
