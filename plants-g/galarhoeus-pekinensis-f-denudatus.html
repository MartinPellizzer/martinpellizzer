Galarhoeus pekinensis f. denudatus

Galarhoeus pekinensis f. denudatus, commonly known as the Peking honeysuckle, is a unique variant of the honeysuckle plant characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate climates, showcasing attractive, fragrant flowers in the spring. The foliage of this plant is notable for its reduced leaf structure, giving it a more delicate appearance compared to the typical honeysuckle. Its stems are often covered in a thin, smooth bark, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that ripen in the fall.

This form is particularly valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
