Gymnopogon radiatus

Gymnopogon radiatus, commonly known as the radiate grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Gymnopogon radiatus is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
