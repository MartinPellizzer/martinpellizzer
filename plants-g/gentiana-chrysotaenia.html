Gentiana chrysotaenia

Gentiana chrysotaenia, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its stems are upright and branched.

The golden gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
