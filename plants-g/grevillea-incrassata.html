Grevillea incrassata

Grevillea incrassata, commonly known as the broad-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features bright red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea incrassata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
