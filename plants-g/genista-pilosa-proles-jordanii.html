Genista pilosa proles jordanii

Genista pilosa proles jordanii, commonly known as the Jordanian bristly broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
