Gentiana verna var. cantabrica

Gentiana verna var. cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain and southern France.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
