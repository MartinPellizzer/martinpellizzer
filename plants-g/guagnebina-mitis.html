Guagnebina mitis

Guagnebina mitis, commonly known as the silky tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Guagnebina mitis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid climates.
