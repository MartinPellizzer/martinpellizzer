Gigantochloa nigrociliata var. hohenackeri

Gigantochloa nigrociliata var. hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, green to purplish stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are long, narrow, and often have a distinctive dark purple or black edge, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
