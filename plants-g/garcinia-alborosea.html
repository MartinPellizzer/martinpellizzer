Garcinia alborosea

Garcinia alborosea, commonly known as white rose apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The fruit, which is small and round, is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
