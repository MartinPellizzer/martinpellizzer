Glycosmis crassifolia

Glycosmis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mastic tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits.

Glycosmis crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
