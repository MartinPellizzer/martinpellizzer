Geum macrophyllum var. rydbergii

Geum macrophyllum var. rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's Indian paintbrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
