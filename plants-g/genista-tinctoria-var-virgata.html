Genista tinctoria var. virgata

Genista tinctoria var. virgata, commonly known as the virginal broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and is adapted to rocky and dry soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are clustered in racemes.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
