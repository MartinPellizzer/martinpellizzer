Garcinia planchonii

Garcinia planchonii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pericarp is thick and segmented, containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia planchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
