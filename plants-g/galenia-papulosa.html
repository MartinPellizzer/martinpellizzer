Galenia papulosa

Galenia papulosa, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Galenia papulosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in regions with low rainfall.
