Gentiana shaanxiensis

Gentiana shaanxiensis, commonly known as the Shaanxi gentian, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
