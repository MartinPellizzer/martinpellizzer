Gnaphalium redolens

Gnaphalium redolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous substance that gives it its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and it is known for its long-lasting, aromatic foliage.
