Gurania rhizantha

Gurania rhizantha, commonly known as the threadleaf gurania, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow or white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Gurania rhizantha is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
