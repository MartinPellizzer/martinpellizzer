Geranium acutilobum

Geranium acutilobum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed geranium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows.

Geranium acutilobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
