Guettardella pachyphylla

Guettardella pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved sandwort, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Guettardella pachyphylla is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
