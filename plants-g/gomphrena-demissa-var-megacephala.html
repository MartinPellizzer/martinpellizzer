Gomphrena demissa var. megacephala

Gomphrena demissa var. megacephala, commonly known as the "globe amaranth," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves and has a bushy growth habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are often used in dried flower arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
