Gmelina chinensis

Gmelina chinensis, commonly known as Chinese gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in catkins, followed by small, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is often found in moist, fertile soils and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.

The species is also used in reforestation efforts due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
