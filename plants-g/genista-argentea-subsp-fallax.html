Genista argentea subsp. fallax

Genista argentea subsp.

fallax, commonly known as silverbush or silver broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has silvery-white undersides on its leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
