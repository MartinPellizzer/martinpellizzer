Gardenia sulcata

Gardenia sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive zigzag pattern along the midrib. The plant produces fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage and showy blooms, it is a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
