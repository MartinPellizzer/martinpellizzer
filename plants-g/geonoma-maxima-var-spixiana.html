Geonoma maxima var. spixiana

Geonoma maxima var. spixiana, commonly known as the Spix's giant fern, is a large, epiphytic fern native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
