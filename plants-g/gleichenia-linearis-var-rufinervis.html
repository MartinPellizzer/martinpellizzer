Gleichenia linearis var. rufinervis

Gleichenia linearis var. rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae, characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and have a distinctive reddish vein pattern, which gives it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
