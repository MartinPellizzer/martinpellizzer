Geranium baumgartenianum

Geranium baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Armenian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Geranium baumgartenianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
