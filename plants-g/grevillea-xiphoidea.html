Grevillea xiphoidea

Grevillea xiphoidea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea xiphoidea is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
