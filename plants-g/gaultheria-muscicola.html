Gaultheria muscicola

Gaultheria muscicola, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry bush, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to strawberries, though they are typically red or pink in color. Gaultheria muscicola thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in native South American cultures.
