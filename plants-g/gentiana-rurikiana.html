Gentiana rurikiana

Gentiana rurikiana, commonly known as the Russian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
