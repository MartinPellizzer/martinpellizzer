Goniocheton insularis

Goniocheton insularis, commonly known as the island banyan, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Moraceae and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to form buttress roots, which help it stabilize in loose, sandy soils. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
