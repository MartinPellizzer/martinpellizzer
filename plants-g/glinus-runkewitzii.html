Glinus runkewitzii

Glinus runkewitzii, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
