Geranium nepalense f. pallidum

Geranium nepalense f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale geranium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
