Genista triflorus var. villosus

Genista triflorus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal landscapes.
