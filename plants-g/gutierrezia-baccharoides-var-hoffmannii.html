Gutierrezia baccharoides var. hoffmannii

Gutierrezia baccharoides var. hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled structures.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
