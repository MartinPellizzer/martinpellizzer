Geranium hyperacrion

Geranium hyperacrion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a distinctive, slightly crumpled appearance.

Geranium hyperacrion is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
