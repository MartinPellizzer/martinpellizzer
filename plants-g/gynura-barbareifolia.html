Gynura barbareifolia

Gynura barbareifolia, commonly known as the African Daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has finely serrated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value.
