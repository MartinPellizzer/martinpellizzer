Gentiana gilliesii

Gentiana gilliesii, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and sometimes branched.

Gentiana gilliesii is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
