Gloxinia fimbriata

Gloxinia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed gloxinia, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. Gloxinia fimbriata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
