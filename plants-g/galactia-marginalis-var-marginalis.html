Galactia marginalis var. marginalis

Galactia marginalis var. marginalis, commonly known as the marginal galactia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
