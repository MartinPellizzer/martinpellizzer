Gilia androsacea subsp. euandrosacea

Gilia androsacea subsp.

euandrosacea, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
