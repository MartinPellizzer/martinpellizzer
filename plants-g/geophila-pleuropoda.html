Geophila pleuropoda

Geophila pleuropoda, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically small, pink or white, and arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Geophila pleuropoda thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
