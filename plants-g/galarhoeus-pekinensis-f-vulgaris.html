Galarhoeus pekinensis f. vulgaris

Galarhoeus pekinensis f. vulgaris, commonly known as the Peking honeysuckle, is a variant of the honeysuckle species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a climbing habit, which contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
