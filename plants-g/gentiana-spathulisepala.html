Gentiana spathulisepala

Gentiana spathulisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Gentiana spathulisepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
