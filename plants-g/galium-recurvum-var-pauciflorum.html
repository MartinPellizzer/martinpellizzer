Galium recurvum var. pauciflorum

Galium recurvum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium recurvum var. pauciflorum prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
