Grindelia pubescens

Grindelia pubescens, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

Grindelia pubescens plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
