Grevillea sarissa subsp. bicolor

Grevillea sarissa subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its vibrant, clustered flowers that display a mix of pink and white hues, typically appearing in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky white down. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also an important plant for native wildlife.
