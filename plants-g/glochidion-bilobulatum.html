Glochidion bilobulatum

Glochidion bilobulatum, commonly known as the two-lobed glabrous leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly so. The flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
