Gentiana sulcata

Gentiana sulcata, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Gentiana sulcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vivid blooms.
