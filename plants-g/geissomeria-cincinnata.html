Geissomeria cincinnata

Geissomeria cincinnata, commonly known as the slenderleaf huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
