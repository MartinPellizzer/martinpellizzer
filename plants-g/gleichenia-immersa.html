Gleichenia immersa

Gleichenia immersa, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere. Its rhizomes grow horizontally, allowing it to spread and form dense mats in wetlands and along stream banks.

Gleichenia immersa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
