Genista iberica

Genista iberica, commonly known as the Iberian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Genista iberica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in Mediterranean climates.
