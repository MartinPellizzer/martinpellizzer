Gaura neomexicana var. typica

Gaura neomexicana var. typica, commonly known as the New Mexico gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
