Gastrochilus puncticulatus

Gastrochilus puncticulatus, commonly known as the dot-leaf orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive small, dark spots that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a yellowish throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Gastrochilus puncticulatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
