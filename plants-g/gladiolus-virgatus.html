Gladiolus virgatus

Gladiolus virgatus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with upright stems that support its showy blooms. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
