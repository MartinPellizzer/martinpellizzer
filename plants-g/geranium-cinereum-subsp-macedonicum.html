Geranium cinereum subsp. macedonicum

Geranium cinereum subsp.

macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy.

The flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
