Garcinia branderhorstii

Garcinia branderhorstii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing numerous seeds. Garcinia branderhorstii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
