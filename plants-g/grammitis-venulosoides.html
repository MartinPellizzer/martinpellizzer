Grammitis venulosoides

Grammitis venulosoides, commonly known as the veined fernlet, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches, forming dense, compact clusters. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its fronds are usually unifoliate, meaning they have a single leaf blade with a distinct midrib.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered along the margins of the fronds, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
