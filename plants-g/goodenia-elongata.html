Goodenia elongata

Goodenia elongata, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
