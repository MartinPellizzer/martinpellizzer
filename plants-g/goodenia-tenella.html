Goodenia tenella

Goodenia tenella, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers, which are usually pale blue or purple, bloom during the spring and summer months.

Goodenia tenella is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
