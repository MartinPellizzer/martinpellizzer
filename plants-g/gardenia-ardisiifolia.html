Gardenia ardisiifolia

Gardenia ardisiifolia, commonly known as the silver gardenia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for shaded garden areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
