Geranium weddellii

Geranium weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are borne in terminal clusters, displaying shades of pink to purple, and bloom from late winter to early spring.

Geranium weddellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
