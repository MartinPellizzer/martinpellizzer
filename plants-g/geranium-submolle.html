Geranium submolle

Geranium submolle, commonly known as the soft geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Geranium submolle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and ease of care.
