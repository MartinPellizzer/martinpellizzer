Glomera grandilabella

Glomera grandilabella, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
