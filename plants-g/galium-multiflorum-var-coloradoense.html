Galium multiflorum var. coloradoense

Galium multiflorum var. coloradoense, commonly known as the Colorado bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
