Ginkgo biloba var. laciniata

Ginkgo biloba var. laciniata, commonly known as the notched ginkgo, is a variant of the ginkgo tree characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

The leaves are usually fan-shaped with a serrated or notched edge, giving the plant a unique and ornamental appearance. It is a deciduous tree that produces small, pear-shaped fruits, which are typically not edible for humans but are important for wildlife. The plant is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
