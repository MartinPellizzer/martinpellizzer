Gentiana reynieri

Gentiana reynieri, commonly known as the Reynier's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pyrenees and southern France.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
