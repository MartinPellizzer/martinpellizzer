Gynostemma pentaphyllum

Gynostemma pentaphyllum, commonly known as Chinese ginseng or jiaogulan, is a climbing vine native to Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gynostemma pentaphyllum is valued for its adaptogenic and antioxidant compounds, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine.
