Gentiana hapalocaula

Gentiana hapalocaula, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

Gentiana hapalocaula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
