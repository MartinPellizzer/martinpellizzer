Glaucium luteum var. vestitum

Glaucium luteum var. vestitum, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-blue stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
