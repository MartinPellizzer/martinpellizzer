Garcinia erythrosepala

Garcinia erythrosepala, commonly known as the red-sepaled garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are encased in a thin, waxy skin. Garcinia erythrosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique fruit.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
