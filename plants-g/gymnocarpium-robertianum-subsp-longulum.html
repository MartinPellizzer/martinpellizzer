Gymnocarpium robertianum subsp. longulum

Gymnocarpium robertianum subsp.

longulum, commonly known as the long-stalked rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions. Its fruits are indehiscent silique-like structures that remain closed at maturity.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is found primarily in the mountainous areas of Europe and western Asia.
