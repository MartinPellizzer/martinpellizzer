Genista pilosa

Genista pilosa, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a feathery appearance.

Genista pilosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
