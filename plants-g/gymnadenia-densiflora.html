Gymnadenia densiflora

Gymnadenia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gymnadenia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
