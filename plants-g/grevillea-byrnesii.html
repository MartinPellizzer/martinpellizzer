Grevillea byrnesii

Grevillea byrnesii, commonly known as the Byrnes' waratah, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea byrnesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting native birds and insects.
