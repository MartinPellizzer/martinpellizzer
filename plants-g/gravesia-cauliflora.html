Gravesia cauliflora

Gravesia cauliflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in evergreen forests. The plant is characterized by its unique cauliflorous habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. Its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they bloom during the winter season.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
