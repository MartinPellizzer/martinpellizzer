Globba purpurea

Globba purpurea, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form thickets in suitable conditions.

Globba purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent reminiscent of ginger.
