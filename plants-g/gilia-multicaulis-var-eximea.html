Gilia multicaulis var. eximea

Gilia multicaulis var. eximea, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of bright pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
