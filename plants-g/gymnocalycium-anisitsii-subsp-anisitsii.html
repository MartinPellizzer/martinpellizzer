Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp. anisitsii

Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp.

anisitsii, commonly known as the Anisitsi prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its globose to cylindrical stems covered in spines and areoles.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, edible pear-shaped structure that ripens to a red or purple color.

This species is well adapted to dry environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
