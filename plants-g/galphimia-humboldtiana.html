Galphimia humboldtiana

Galphimia humboldtiana, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that resemble hummingbird beaks, which are adapted to attract pollinators. The plant is a shrub with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, providing continuous color and attracting a variety of pollinators.
