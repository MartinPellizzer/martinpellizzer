Gnaphalium diffusum

Gnaphalium diffusum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that develop into fluffy, white seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Gnaphalium diffusum is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
