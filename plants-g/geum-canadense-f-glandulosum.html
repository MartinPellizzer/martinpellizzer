Geum canadense f. glandulosum

Geum canadense f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular Canadian avens, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its glandular hairs on the leaves and stems are a distinctive botanical feature that aids in identifying the species.
