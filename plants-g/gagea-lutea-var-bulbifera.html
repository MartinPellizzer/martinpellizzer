Gagea lutea var. bulbifera

Gagea lutea var. bulbifera, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its ability to form small, edible bulbs underground, which are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
