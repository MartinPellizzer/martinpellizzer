Garcinia binnendijkii

Garcinia binnendijkii, commonly known as the Binnendijk's garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has attracted interest for potential medicinal and dietary applications.
