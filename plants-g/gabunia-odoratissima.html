Gabunia odoratissima

Gabunia odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant forest tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, leathery leaves that provide dense shade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its wood is valued for its strength and aromatic properties, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.

Additionally, it plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
