Geranium caespitosum var. parryi

Geranium caespitosum var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its leaves are serrated and arranged in a dense, mat-like form, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
