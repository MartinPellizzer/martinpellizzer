Gentlea maculata

Gentlea maculata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and clustered in inflorescences. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
