Grevillea baueri

Grevillea baueri, commonly known as the Bauer's waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features vibrant crimson flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting native birds and insects. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Grevillea baueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
