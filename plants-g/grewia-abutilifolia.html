Grewia abutilifolia

Grewia abutilifolia, commonly known as the silky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia abutilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
