Gnaphalium delicatum

Gnaphalium delicatum, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant has a low-growing habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
