Germaria

Germaria, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry," is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Physalis and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that surrounds the fruit. The fruit is typically orange-red, berry-like, and contains numerous small seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
