Gerardia dasyantha

Gerardia dasyantha, commonly known as the hairy gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disc shape.

Gerardia dasyantha is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
