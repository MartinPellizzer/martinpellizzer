Gunnarella begaudii

Gunnarella begaudii, commonly known as the African thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Gunnarella begaudii is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that may be dispersed by animals.
