Gladiolus × pudibundus

Gladiolus × pudibundus, commonly known as the pink gladiolus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous flowers in shades of pink and white. The flowers typically have three petals and three sepals, with a prominent central stamen. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
