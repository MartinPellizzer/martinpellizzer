Gayella valparadisaea

Gayella valparadisaea, commonly known as the Valparaiso bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in coastal conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
