Grindelia bracteosa

Grindelia bracteosa, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Grindelia bracteosa is also known for its ability to produce a sticky resin, which can be used in traditional medicine.
