Gomphrena chloromalla

Gomphrena chloromalla, commonly known as the blue gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple bracts that resemble small, rounded flowers, which are actually modified leaves. Gomphrena chloromalla thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
