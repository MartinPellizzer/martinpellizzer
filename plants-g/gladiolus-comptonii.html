Gladiolus comptonii

Gladiolus comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, spike-like inflorescences of brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its roots are fibrous and tuberous. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
