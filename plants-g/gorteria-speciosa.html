Gorteria speciosa

Gorteria speciosa, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the flowers appearing in late spring to early summer.

Gorteria speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
