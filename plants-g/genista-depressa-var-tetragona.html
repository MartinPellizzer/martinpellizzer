Genista depressa var. tetragona

Genista depressa var. tetragona, commonly known as the creeping yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, woody stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval, glandular leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or sandy habitats.
