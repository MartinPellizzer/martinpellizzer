Genista juzepczukii

Genista juzepczukii, commonly known as the Juzepczuk's broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
