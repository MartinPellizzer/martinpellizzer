Galactia volubilis var. baltzelliana

Galactia volubilis var. baltzelliana, commonly known as the Balzells' climbing galactia, is a perennial vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ornamental and ecological studies.
