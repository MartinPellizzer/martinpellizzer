Gentianella cruciata

Gentianella cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a cross-shaped base, giving it its common name.

Gentianella cruciata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
