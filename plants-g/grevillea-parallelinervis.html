Grevillea parallelinervis

Grevillea parallelinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea parallelinervis is also valued for its resilience to drought and salt spray.
