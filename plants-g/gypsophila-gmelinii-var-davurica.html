Gypsophila gmelinii var. davurica

Gypsophila gmelinii var. davurica, commonly known as the Siberian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers resembling tiny clouds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical name reflects its historical classification and geographic origin.
