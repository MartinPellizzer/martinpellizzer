Gomphosia goudotiana

Gomphosia goudotiana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

Gomphosia goudotiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
