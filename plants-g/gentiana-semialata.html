Gentiana semialata

Gentiana semialata, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana semialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
