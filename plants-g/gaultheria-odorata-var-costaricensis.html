Gaultheria odorata var. costaricensis

Gaultheria odorata var. costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. These fruits are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and montane regions.
