Gumifera

Gumifera, commonly known as the gum tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be tapped for various uses. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface. The flowers are usually small and clustered, with showy petals that attract pollinators.

Gumifera species are native to Australia and are well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
