Granadilla quadrangularis

Granadilla quadrangularis, commonly known as the granadilla or yellow grana.

It is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Ecuador, belonging to the Passifloraceae family. The plant is characterized by its quadrangular stems and striking, colorful flowers with five sepals and five petals. Its fruits, which are typically yellow or orange, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
