Gladiolus rudis

Gladiolus rudis, commonly known as the rough gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with three prominent petals and three sepals that are often larger and more showy than the petals. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown for its vibrant blooms.
