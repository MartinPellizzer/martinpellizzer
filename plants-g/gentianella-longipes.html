Gentianella longipes

Gentianella longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or violet corolla, often with yellow or white markings.

Gentianella longipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
