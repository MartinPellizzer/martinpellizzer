Gymnocalycium hybopleurum

Gymnocalycium hybopleurum, commonly known as the red-tipped barrel cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, thick, ribbed stems that are typically green with red or purple tips. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Gymnocalycium hybopleurum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
