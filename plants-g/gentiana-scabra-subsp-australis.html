Gentiana scabra subsp. australis

Gentiana scabra subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian gentian, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
