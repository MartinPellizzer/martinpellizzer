Geranium sylvaticum var. uralense

Geranium sylvaticum var. uralense, commonly known as the Ural geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
