Gilia polyantha var. brachysiphon

Gilia polyantha var. brachysiphon, commonly known as the short-siphon gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing numerous seed pods that are cylindrical and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a subject of study in ecological and evolutionary botany.
