Galactia striata var. striata

Galactia striata var. striata, commonly known as the scarlet brier, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy, red to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, yellow pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
