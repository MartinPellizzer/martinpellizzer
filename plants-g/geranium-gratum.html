Geranium gratum

Geranium gratum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental leaves and long-lasting blooms.

Geranium gratum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
