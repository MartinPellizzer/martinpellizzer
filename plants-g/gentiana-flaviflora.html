Gentiana flaviflora

Gentiana flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
