Gentiana axillariflora var. montana

Gentiana axillariflora var. montana, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high altitude conditions.
