Gastridium siculum

Gastridium siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gastridium siculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
