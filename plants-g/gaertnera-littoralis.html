Gaertnera littoralis

Gaertnera littoralis, commonly known as the coastal Gaertnera, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds.

Gaertnera littoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
