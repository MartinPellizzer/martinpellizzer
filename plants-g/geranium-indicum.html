Geranium indicum

Geranium indicum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Geranium indicum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
