Gentianella sagasteguii

Gentianella sagasteguii, commonly known as the Peruvian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and its stems are erect.

Gentianella sagasteguii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
