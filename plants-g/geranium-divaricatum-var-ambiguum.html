Geranium divaricatum var. ambiguum

Geranium divaricatum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the variable geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, with serrated edges. It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
