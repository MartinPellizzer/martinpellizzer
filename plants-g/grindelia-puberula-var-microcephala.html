Grindelia puberula var. microcephala

Grindelia puberula var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed greaseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green, sticky resinous leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
