Gomphrena gnaphalioides var. floccosa

Gomphrena gnaphalioides var. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, feathery, woolly leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements.
