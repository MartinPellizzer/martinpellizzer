Grewia cavaleriei

Grewia cavaleriei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, urn-shaped bracts that enclose the small, orange berries. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its berries are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

Grewia cavaleriei thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.
