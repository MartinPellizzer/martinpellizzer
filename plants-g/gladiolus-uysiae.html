Gladiolus uysiae

Gladiolus uysiae, commonly known as the Orange River gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a corm as its underground storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers.
