Grindelia spathulata

Grindelia spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.
