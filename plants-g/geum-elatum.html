Geum elatum

Geum elatum, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branched stems and pinnately divided, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Geum elatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
