Gymnanthes riparia

Gymnanthes riparia, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water sources, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
