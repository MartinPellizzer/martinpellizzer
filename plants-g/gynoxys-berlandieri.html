Gynoxys berlandieri

Gynoxys berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include showy ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
