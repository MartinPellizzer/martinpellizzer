Gladiolus imbricatus

Gladiolus imbricatus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink, red, or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
