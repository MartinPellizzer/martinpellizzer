Galeandra badia

Galeandra badia, commonly known as the crimson orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers with a deep maroon throat, which are highly prized in horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are dark green and broad, often with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Galeandra badia requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
