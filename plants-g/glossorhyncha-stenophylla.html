Glossorhyncha stenophylla

Glossorhyncha stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized adaptations for rocky environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spike, and its leaves are narrow and leathery to reduce water loss. Glossorhyncha stenophylla is often found in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
