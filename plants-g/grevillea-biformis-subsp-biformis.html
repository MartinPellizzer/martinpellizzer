Grevillea biformis subsp. biformis

Grevillea biformis subsp.

biformis, commonly known as the two-branched grevillea, is a shrub native to western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often with a yellow or greenish throat.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens. Grevillea biformis subsp.

biformis is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and its ornamental appeal.
