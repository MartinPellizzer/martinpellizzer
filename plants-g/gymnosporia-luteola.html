Gymnosporia luteola

Gymnosporia luteola, commonly known as the yellow burr bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Gymnosporia luteola plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
