Grevillea glabella

Grevillea glabella, commonly known as the smooth grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bottlebrush-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
