Galactia jussiaeana f. velutina

Galactia jussiaeana f. velutina, commonly known as the velvet galactia, is a variant of the Galactia jussiaeana species, characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It is a perennial legume native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and foliage.
