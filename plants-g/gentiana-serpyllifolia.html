Gentiana serpyllifolia

Gentiana serpyllifolia, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Gentiana serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
