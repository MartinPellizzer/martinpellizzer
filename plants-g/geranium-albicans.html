Geranium albicans

Geranium albicans, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it typically grows in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, thriving in full sun.

Geranium albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
