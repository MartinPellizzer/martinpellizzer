Gladiolus hirsutus var. villosiusculus

Gladiolus hirsutus var. villosiusculus, commonly known as the hairy gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant features hairy foliage and bracts, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in horticultural breeding programs.
