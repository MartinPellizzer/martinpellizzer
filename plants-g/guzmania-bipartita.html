Guzmania bipartita

Guzmania bipartita, commonly known as the Two-parted Bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that stands above its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant's leaves are arranged in a spiral, forming a central cup that collects rainwater, providing a microhabitat for small organisms. Guzmania bipartita thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush appearance.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
