Gleditsia japonica var. koraiensis

Gleditsia japonica var. koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in racemes, followed by flat, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
