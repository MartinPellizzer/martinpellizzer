Goniopteris lineata

Goniopteris lineata, commonly known as the veined shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with a distinct midrib. The plant's leaves are usually bipinnate, with a broad, shield-like shape that gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Goniopteris lineata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
