Gilia capitata var. achilleifolia

Gilia capitata var. achilleifolia, commonly known as the Achillea-leaved gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the genus Achillea, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
