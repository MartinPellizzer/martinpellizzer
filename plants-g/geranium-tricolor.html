Geranium tricolor

Geranium tricolor, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored flowers that typically feature red, white, and purple petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Geranium tricolor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 3 feet tall, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.
