Gomphrena martinsiana

Gomphrena martinsiana, commonly known as the Brazilian plume, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gomphrena martinsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
