Galeorchis diantha

Galeorchis diantha, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its long, spiraling inflorescence that bears small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Galeorchis diantha is often found in association with other orchids and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
