Gomphrena meyeniana var. acaulis

Gomphrena meyeniana var. acaulis, commonly known as the slender gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its clusters of small, spiky, brightly colored bracts that resemble tiny flowers. The plant typically produces pink, red, or purple bracts, which are often mistaken for true flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Gomphrena meyeniana var. acaulis is known for its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
