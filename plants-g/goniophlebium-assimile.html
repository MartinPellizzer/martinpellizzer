Goniophlebium assimile

Goniophlebium assimile, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery scales, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Goniophlebium assimile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
