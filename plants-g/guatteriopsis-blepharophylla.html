Guatteriopsis blepharophylla

Guatteriopsis blepharophylla, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a rare and striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Native to the high-altitude regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
