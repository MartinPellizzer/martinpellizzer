Gleditsia caspica var. platycarpa

Gleditsia caspica var. platycarpa, commonly known as the Caspian honey locust, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 20–40 cm long and composed of 15–30 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to dry, arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness.
