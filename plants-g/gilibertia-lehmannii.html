Gilibertia lehmannii

Gilibertia lehmannii, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Gilibertia lehmannii is often found growing on rocky soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
