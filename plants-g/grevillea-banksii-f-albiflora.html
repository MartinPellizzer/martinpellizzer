Grevillea banksii f. albiflora

Grevillea banksii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered banksii grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, recurved foliage and showy, white or pale yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in heathland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for its purported healing properties.
