Gentiana macrophylla var. fetisowii

Gentiana macrophylla var. fetisowii, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and northern China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grow along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest clearings.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
