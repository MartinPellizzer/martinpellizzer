Gandola nigra

Gandola nigra, commonly known as the black gandola, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Gandola nigra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
