Glottiphyllum propinquum

Glottiphyllum propinquum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent central sepals. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
