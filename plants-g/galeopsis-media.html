Galeopsis media

Galeopsis media, commonly known as common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges.

Galeopsis media is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
