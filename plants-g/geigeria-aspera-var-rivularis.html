Geigeria aspera var. rivularis

Geigeria aspera var. rivularis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Geigeria aspera var. rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
