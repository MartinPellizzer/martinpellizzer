Grias dariensis

Grias dariensis, commonly known as the Darien giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Darien region of Panama.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
