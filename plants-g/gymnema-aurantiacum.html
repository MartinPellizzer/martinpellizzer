Gymnema aurantiacum

Gymnema aurantiacum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or sugar plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish or reddish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a traditional remedy for diabetes. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Gymnema aurantiacum is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
