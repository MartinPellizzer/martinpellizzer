Gentiana kerneri f. mixta

Gentiana kerneri f. mixta, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in a fine down.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
