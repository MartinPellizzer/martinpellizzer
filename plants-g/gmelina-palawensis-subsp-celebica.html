Gmelina palawensis subsp. celebica

Gmelina palawensis subsp.

celebica, commonly known as the Celebes ash or Celebes gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines and parts of Indonesia. It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, opposite leaves.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

This species also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
