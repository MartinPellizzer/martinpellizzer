Guioa amabilis

Guioa amabilis, commonly known as the sweet guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
