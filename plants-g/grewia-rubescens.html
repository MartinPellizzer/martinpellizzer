Grewia rubescens

Grewia rubescens, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia rubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
