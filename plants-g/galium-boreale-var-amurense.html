Galium boreale var. amurense

Galium boreale var. amurense, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to cold climates in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

This species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and boreal forest environments.
