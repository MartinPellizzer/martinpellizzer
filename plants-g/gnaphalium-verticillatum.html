Gnaphalium verticillatum

Gnaphalium verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces tiny, fluffy white flowers that are often mistaken for cotton or snow, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is known for its aromatic, slightly medicinal properties.
