Gethyllis villosa

Gethyllis villosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy leaf surfaces. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name.

Gethyllis villosa is often found in open woodlands and is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its ornamental value.
