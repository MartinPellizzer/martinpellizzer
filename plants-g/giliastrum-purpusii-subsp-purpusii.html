Giliastrum purpusii subsp. purpusii

Giliastrum purpusii subsp.

purpusii, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as desert plains and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in harsh, dry climates.
