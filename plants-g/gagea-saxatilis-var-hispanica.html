Gagea saxatilis var. hispanica

Gagea saxatilis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in alpine and Mediterranean regions.

Its bulbs are edible and have a mild garlic flavor, making it a valuable plant in certain culinary traditions.
