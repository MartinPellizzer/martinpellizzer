Gasteria bicolor

Gasteria bicolor, commonly known as the Zulu bulb or the African spear, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings, forming a rosette shape. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Gasteria bicolor is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
