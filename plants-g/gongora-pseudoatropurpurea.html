Gongora pseudoatropurpurea

Gongora pseudoatropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-tinted orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-pink flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and broad, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
