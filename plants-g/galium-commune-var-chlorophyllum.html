Galium commune var. chlorophyllum

Galium commune var. chlorophyllum, commonly known as the green wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green appearance. The plant bears clusters of delicate white or pale yellow flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is edible and has a mildly tangy flavor, making it a useful plant in certain culinary traditions.
