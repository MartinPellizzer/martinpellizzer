Gentiana quadrifaria f. intermedia

Gentiana quadrifaria f. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Four-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are tubular with prominent blue or purple markings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
