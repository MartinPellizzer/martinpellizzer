Gastrolobium formosum

Gastrolobium formosum, commonly known as the pink burr bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to reddish flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Gastrolobium formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
