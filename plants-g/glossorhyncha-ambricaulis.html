Glossorhyncha ambricaulis

Glossorhyncha ambricaulis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a slender, erect stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive hooded lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
