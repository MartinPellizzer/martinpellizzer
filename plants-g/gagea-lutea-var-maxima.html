Gagea lutea var. maxima

Gagea lutea var. maxima, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
