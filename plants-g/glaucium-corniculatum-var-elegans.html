Glaucium corniculatum var. elegans

Glaucium corniculatum var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant gloryflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a bluish-gray hue due to a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Glaucium corniculatum var. elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
