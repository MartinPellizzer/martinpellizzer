Geranium rupicola

Geranium rupicola, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly gardens. Its foliage is typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, adding texture to the landscape.

Geranium rupicola is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
