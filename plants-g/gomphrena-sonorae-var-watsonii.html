Gomphrena sonorae var. watsonii

Gomphrena sonorae var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's plumeflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red, giving it a plume-like appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to conserve moisture.
