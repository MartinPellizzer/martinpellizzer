Gouldia ovata var. punaula

Gouldia ovata var. punaula, commonly known as the golden spike rush, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms a low-growing mat. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically golden in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gouldia ovata var. punaula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
