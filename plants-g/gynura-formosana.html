Gynura formosana

Gynura formosana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese evergreen, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
