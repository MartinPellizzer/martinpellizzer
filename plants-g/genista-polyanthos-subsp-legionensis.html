Genista polyanthos subsp. legionensis

Genista polyanthos subsp.

legionensis, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
