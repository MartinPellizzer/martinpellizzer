Gesneria fimbriata

Gesneria fimbriata, commonly known as the fringe-leaved gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive fringed margin. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria fimbriata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
