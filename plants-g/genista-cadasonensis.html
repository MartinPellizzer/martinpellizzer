Genista cadasonensis

Genista cadasonensis, commonly known as the Cadason's broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
