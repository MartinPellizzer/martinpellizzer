Gentiana crandallii

Gentiana crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
