Gleichenia linearis var. grandis

Gleichenia linearis var. grandis, commonly known as the giant feather moss, is a variant of the common Gleichenia linearis.

It is a fern-like moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings.
