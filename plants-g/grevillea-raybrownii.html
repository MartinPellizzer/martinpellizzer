Grevillea raybrownii

Grevillea raybrownii, commonly known as the Red Grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes and bloom from late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife.
