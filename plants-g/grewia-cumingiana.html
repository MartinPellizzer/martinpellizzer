Grewia cumingiana

Grewia cumingiana, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring.

Grewia cumingiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
