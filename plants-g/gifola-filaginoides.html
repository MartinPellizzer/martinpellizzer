Gifola filaginoides

Gifola filaginoides, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Gifola filaginoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
