Glandularia sessilifolia

Glandularia sessilifolia, commonly known as the glandular verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have glandular dots that secrete oils, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
