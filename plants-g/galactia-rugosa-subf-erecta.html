Galactia rugosa subf. erecta

Galactia rugosa subf.

erecta, commonly known as the erect milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Galactia rugosa subf.

erecta plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
