Galeandra bicarinata

Galeandra bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Galeandra bicarinata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
