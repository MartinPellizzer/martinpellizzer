Glycine cyanea

Glycine cyanea, commonly known as the blue bean plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Glycine cyanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of protein-rich seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
