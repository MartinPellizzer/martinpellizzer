Gagea lutea

Gagea lutea, commonly known as the yellow gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its bulbs are adapted for storage of nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
