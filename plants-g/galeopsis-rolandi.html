Galeopsis rolandi

Galeopsis rolandi, commonly known as hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, four-lobed fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Galeopsis rolandi is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
