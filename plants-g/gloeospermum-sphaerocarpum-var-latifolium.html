Gloeospermum sphaerocarpum var. latifolium

Gloeospermum sphaerocarpum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf gloeospermum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, waxy leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The varietal form, latifolium, is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical species.
