Gypsophila adenophylla

Gypsophila adenophylla, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gypsophila adenophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
