Glossorhyncha plumosa

Glossorhyncha plumosa, commonly known as the feather-tongued orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, feather-like structures on its petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a distinct, elongated lip that aids in pollination.

Glossorhyncha plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
