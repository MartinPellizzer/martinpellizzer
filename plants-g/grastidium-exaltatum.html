Grastidium exaltatum

Grastidium exaltatum, commonly known as the tall grass-plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of many grass species. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its growth habit, it is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.
