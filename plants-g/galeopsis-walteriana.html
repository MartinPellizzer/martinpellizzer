Galeopsis walteriana

Galeopsis walteriana, commonly known as Walter's horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are usually pale blue or purple.

Galeopsis walteriana is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of arid or semi-arid environments.
