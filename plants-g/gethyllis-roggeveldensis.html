Gethyllis roggeveldensis

Gethyllis roggeveldensis, commonly known as the Roggeveld sand orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has a slender, wiry stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green or white. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive hood-like structure.

It is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, showing resilience to drought and nutrient-poor soils.
