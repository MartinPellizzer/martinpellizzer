Gaertneria tenuifolia

Gaertneria tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed marsh plant, is a herbaceous species native to wetland and marsh environments in tropical regions.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in its habitat.

Gaertneria tenuifolia is often found in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils and is adapted to periodic flooding.
