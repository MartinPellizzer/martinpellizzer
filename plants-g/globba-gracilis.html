Globba gracilis

Globba gracilis, commonly known as the slender globba, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Globba gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for shaded garden spaces.
