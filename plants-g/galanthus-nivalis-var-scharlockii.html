Galanthus nivalis var. scharlockii

Galanthus nivalis var. scharlockii, commonly known as the Scharlock's snowdrop, is a variant of the common snowdrop native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with white petals, which bloom early in the spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, appearing before the flowers.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
