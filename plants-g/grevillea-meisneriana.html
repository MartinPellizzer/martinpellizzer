Grevillea meisneriana

Grevillea meisneriana, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It features showy, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from pale pink to white, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea meisneriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
