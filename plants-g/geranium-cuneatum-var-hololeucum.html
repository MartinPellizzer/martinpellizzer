Geranium cuneatum var. hololeucum

Geranium cuneatum var. hololeucum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are predominantly white or pale pink in color.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
