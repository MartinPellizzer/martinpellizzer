Gnidia compacta

Gnidia compacta, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a yellowish throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
