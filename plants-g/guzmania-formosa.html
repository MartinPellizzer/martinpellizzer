Guzmania formosa

Guzmania formosa, commonly known as the flame plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in the canopies of rainforests. Its rosette-shaped leaves are thick and waxy, designed to collect and store water.

Guzmania formosa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.
