Grevea madagascariensis subsp. leandrii

Grevea madagascariensis subsp.

leandrii, commonly known as the Madagascan grevea, is a member of the Malvaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is native to Madagascar and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
