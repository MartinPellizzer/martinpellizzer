Gentianella baltica

Gentianella baltica, commonly known as Baltic gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Baltic Sea, particularly in countries like Sweden, Finland, and Estonia. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes. Its striking blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
