Grewia excelsa

Grewia excelsa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sour fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, orange-yellow berries that are sour in taste. Grewia excelsa thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for both humans and wildlife.
