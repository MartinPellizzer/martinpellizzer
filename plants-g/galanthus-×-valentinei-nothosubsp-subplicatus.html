Galanthus × valentinei nothosubsp.

subplicatus, commonly known as the Valentine's Day snowdrop, is a hybrid species within the genus Galanthus. It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved petals that give it a unique appearance compared to typical snowdrops.

The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as woodland floors or shaded gardens. It produces small, white flowers with a yellow-green spatulate base, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.
