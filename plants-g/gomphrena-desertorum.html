Gomphrena desertorum

Gomphrena desertorum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and its showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Gomphrena desertorum is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is popular in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
