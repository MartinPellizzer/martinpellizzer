Gymnosphaera aculeata

Gymnosphaera aculeata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red, during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunac, is edible and contains a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.

Gymnosphaera aculeata plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
