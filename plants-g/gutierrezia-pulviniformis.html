Gutierrezia pulviniformis

Gutierrezia pulviniformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
