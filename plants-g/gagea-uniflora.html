Gagea uniflora

Gagea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, bell-shaped flower that typically blooms in early spring. The plant produces a small, white or pale pink flower that is often clustered at the top of a slender stem. Gagea uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
