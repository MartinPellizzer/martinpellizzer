Gerascanthus salvadorensis

Gerascanthus salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran geranium, is a flowering plant native to El Salvador.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
