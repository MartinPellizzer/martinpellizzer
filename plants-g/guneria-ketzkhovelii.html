Guneria ketzkhovelii

Guneria ketzkhovelii, commonly known as the Ketzkhovel gunnera, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in diameter, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gunneria ketzkhovelii is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and ability to add dramatic visual interest to gardens.
