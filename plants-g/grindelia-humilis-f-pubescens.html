Grindelia humilis f. pubescens

Grindelia humilis f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
