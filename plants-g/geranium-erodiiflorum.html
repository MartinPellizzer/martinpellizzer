Geranium erodiiflorum

Geranium erodiiflorum, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

Geranium erodiiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
