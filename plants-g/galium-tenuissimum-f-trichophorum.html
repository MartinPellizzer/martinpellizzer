Galium tenuissimum f. trichophorum

Galium tenuissimum f. trichophorum, commonly known as the silky bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that are densely covered with soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

This variety is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
