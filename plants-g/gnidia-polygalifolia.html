Gnidia polygalifolia

Gnidia polygalifolia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and it has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
