Gonolobus purpureus

Gonolobus purpureus, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gonolobus purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
