Gnaphalium filagineum

Gnaphalium filagineum, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces numerous tiny, fluffy flower heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
