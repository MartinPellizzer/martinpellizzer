Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. radiciflorum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. radiciflorum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its finely hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of numerous small capitula, which are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.
