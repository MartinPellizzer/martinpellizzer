Grewia vernicosa

Grewia vernicosa, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Grewia vernicosa is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
