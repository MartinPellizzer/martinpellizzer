Guarea coriacea

Guarea coriacea, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in greenish-yellow clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

Guarea coriacea plays an important role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
