Gaedawakka schomburgkiana

Gaedawakka schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's karanja, is a species of leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.

Gaedawakka schomburgkiana is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a source of traditional medicinal compounds.
