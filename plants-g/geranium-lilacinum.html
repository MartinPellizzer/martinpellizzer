Geranium lilacinum

Geranium lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale lilac to pink flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium lilacinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
