Genista tenorei

Genista tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its shrub-like form. Its leaves are narrow and often appear in clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Genista tenorei is typically found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
