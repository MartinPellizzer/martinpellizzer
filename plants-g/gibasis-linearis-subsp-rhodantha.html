Gibasis linearis subsp. rhodantha

Gibasis linearis subsp.

rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
