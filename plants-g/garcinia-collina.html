Garcinia collina

Garcinia collina, commonly known as the African mango or butter fruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Garcinia collina is valued for its high oil content in its seeds, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

Its fruit is also consumed locally and has gained attention for potential health benefits.
