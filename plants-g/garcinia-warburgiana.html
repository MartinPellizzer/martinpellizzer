Garcinia warburgiana

Garcinia warburgiana, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
