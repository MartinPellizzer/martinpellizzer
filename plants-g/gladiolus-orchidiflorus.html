Gladiolus orchidiflorus

Gladiolus orchidiflorus, commonly known as the orchid-flowered gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of showy, often fragrant flowers. The blooms typically come in shades of white, pink, and purple, with intricate patterns resembling orchids, hence its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and botanically interesting.
