Geraniospermum longifolium

Geraniospermum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved geraniosperm, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink or white. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
