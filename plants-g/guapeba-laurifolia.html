Guapeba laurifolia

Guapeba laurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple berries, which are an important food source for various bird species. Guapeba laurifolia is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and resilience.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food provider within its native ecosystem.
