Geranium rapaceum

Geranium rapaceum, commonly known as the prairie geranium, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which are typically pink or red, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Geranium rapaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
