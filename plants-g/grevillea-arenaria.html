Grevillea arenaria

Grevillea arenaria, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Grevillea arenaria is a hardy species that prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
