Gentiana pulmonaria

Gentiana pulmonaria, commonly known as the lungwort gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a distinctive white or grayish felt-like coating.

Gentiana pulmonaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
