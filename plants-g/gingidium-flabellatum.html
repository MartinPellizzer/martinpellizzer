Gingidium flabellatum

Gingidium flabellatum, commonly known as the fan-leaved ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fan-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Gingidium flabellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
