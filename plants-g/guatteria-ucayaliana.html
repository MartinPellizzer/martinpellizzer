Guatteria ucayaliana

Guatteria ucayaliana, commonly known as the Peruvian waxplant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
