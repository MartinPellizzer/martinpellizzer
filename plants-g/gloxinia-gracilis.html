Gloxinia gracilis

Gloxinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender gloxinia, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Gloxinia gracilis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and floral display.
