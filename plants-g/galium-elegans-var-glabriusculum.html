Galium elegans var. glabriusculum

Galium elegans var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the smooth wood-rose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy but become smooth with age. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
