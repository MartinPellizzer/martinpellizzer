Gongora galeottiana

Gongora galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, exhibiting epiphytic growth habits. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Gongora galeottiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
