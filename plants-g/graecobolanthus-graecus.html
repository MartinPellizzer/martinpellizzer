Graecobolanthus graecus

Graecobolanthus graecus, commonly known as the Greek sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features a robust taproot and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
