Galearia fragifera

Galearia fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
