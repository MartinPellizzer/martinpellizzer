Garcinia affinis

Garcinia affinis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Garcinia affinis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It also plays a role in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
