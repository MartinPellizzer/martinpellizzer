Gymnacranthera negrosensis

Gymnacranthera negrosensis, commonly known as the Negros myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
