Gentiana pseudosikkimensis

Gentiana pseudosikkimensis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 2,500 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
