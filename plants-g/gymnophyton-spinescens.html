Gymnophyton spinescens

Gymnophyton spinescens, commonly known as the spiny gymnosperm, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive spiny structures.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, which typically includes plants that produce seeds without enclosing them in an ovary. The plant exhibits a woody stem with sharp, protective spines that may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are typically needle-like, adapted for efficient water retention in arid environments.

Gymnophyton spinescens is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
