Galeopsis ladanum subsp. angustifolia

Galeopsis ladanum subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is known for its hairy stems and leaves.

It is often found in moist to moderately dry habitats and is considered a cosmopolitan species in some regions.
