Gastrochilus javanum

Gastrochilus javanum, commonly known as the Javanese stomach lip, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Gastrochilus javanum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
