Gonystylus warburgianus

Gonystylus warburgianus, commonly known as the Warburg's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark, and extremely hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and typically grows in lowland rainforests. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is often associated with secondary forest regrowth.
