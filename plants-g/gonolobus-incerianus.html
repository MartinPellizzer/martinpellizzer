Gonolobus incerianus

Gonolobus incerianus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Gonolobus incerianus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and vigorous growth habit.
