Gynostemma pentaphyllum f. fasciculare

Gynostemma pentaphyllum f. fasciculare, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or Korean ginseng, is a variant of the gynostemma plant native to East Asia.

This plant is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being elliptical and serrated. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in fascicles, giving it its specific epithet *fasciculare*. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds similar to ginsenosides found in Panax ginseng.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its health benefits and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
