Geum montanum f. caulescens

Geum montanum f. caulescens, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and clustered, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
