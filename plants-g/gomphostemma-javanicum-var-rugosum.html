Gomphostemma javanicum var. rugosum

Gomphostemma javanicum var. rugosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed gomphostemma, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems covered in rough, warty protuberances. The plant has palmately compound leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels. It produces fleshy, orange berries that contain the seeds.

This species is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
