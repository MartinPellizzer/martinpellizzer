Grevillea evansiana

Grevillea evansiana, commonly known as the Evans' waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or pink flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. Grevillea evansiana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a standout in the botanical world.
