Gigantochloa gangasinghiana

Gigantochloa gangasinghiana, commonly known as the Gangasinghiana bamboo, is a tall, clumping bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This bamboo is valued for its strength and is often used in construction and as a decorative plant.
