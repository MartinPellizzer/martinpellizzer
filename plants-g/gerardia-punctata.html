Gerardia punctata

Gerardia punctata, commonly known as the spotted gerardia, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. Gerardia punctata typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Geraniaceae family.
