Gentiana albidocoerulea

Gentiana albidocoerulea, commonly known as the white-blue gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its vibrant blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its flowers, which are often a combination of white and blue, bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking color contrast.
