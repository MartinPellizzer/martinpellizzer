Glycosmis gracilis

Glycosmis gracilis, commonly known as the slender glycosmis, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Glycosmis gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
