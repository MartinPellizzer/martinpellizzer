Gronovia grandiflora

Gronovia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora gromwell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gronovia grandiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
