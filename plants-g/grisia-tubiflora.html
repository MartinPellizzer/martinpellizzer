Grisia tubiflora

Grisia tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous grisia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy underground stems, or tubers, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. Grisia tubiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and potential horticultural use.
