Gentiana exquisita

Gentiana exquisita, commonly known as the exquisite gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or violet corolla, often with a yellow or orange spot at the base.
