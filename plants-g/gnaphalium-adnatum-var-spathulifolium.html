Gnaphalium adnatum var. spathulifolium

Gnaphalium adnatum var. spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

The species is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
