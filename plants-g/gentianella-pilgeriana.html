Gentianella pilgeriana

Gentianella pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns.
