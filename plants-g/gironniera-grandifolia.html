Gironniera grandifolia

Gironniera grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered gironniera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Gironniera grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
