Galium pusillum var. hirtum

Galium pusillum var. hirtum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
