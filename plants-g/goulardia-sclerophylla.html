Goulardia sclerophylla

Goulardia sclerophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock-heath, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its sclerophyllous traits with tough, evergreen foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water once established.

Goulardia sclerophylla is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
