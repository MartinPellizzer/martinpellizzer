Gymnocalycium lafaldense f. enorme

Gymnocalycium lafaldense f. enorme, commonly known as the giant devil's ivy, is a fascinating cactus species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its large, globular stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, making it one of the tallest columnar cacti. The plant features spiny areoles with white or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This unique plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and resilience.
