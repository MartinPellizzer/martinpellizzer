Grevillea rubicunda

Grevillea rubicunda, commonly known as the red flowering waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, red or orange-red, fluffy inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Grevillea rubicunda is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
