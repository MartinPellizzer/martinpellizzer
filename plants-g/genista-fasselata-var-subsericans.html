Genista fasselata var. subsericans

Genista fasselata var. subsericans, commonly known as the silky broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
