Galeopsis tetrahit var. verlotii

Galeopsis tetrahit var. verlotii, commonly known as the purple galeopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
