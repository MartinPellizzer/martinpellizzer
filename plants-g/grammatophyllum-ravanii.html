Grammatophyllum ravanii

Grammatophyllum ravanii, commonly known as the Ravan's staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic plant belonging to the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like appearance with a thick, fleshy trunk and a crown of fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, rugged texture.

Grammatophyllum ravanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
