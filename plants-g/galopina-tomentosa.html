Galopina tomentosa

Galopina tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly galopina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.

Galopina tomentosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
