Gardenia tentaculata

Gardenia tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are highly valued for their ornamental appeal and are often used in floral arrangements.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
