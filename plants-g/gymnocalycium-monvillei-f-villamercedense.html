Gymnocalycium monvillei f. villamercedense

Gymnocalycium monvillei f. villamercedense, commonly known as the Villamercedense Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It features a rounded or cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a shallow root system that allows it to absorb water quickly after rare rainfalls.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and resilience.
