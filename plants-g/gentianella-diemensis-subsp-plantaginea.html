Gentianella diemensis subsp. plantaginea

Gentianella diemensis subsp.

plantaginea, commonly known as the Tasmanian gentian, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
