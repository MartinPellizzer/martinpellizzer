Gongora ecornuta

Gongora ecornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure with a distinctive horn-like projection on the lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of yellow and orange, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Gongora ecornuta is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
