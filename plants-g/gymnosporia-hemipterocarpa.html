Gymnosporia hemipterocarpa

Gymnosporia hemipterocarpa, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a small, thorny shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The bark and leaves of Gymnosporia hemipterocarpa contain compounds that have been used traditionally in some African cultures for various medicinal purposes.
