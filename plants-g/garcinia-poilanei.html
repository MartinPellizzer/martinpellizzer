Garcinia poilanei

Garcinia poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia poilanei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and scientific research.
