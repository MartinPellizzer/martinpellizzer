Galium binifolium

Galium binifolium, commonly known as twinleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are slender and often lie along the ground, forming a dense mat.

Galium binifolium is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
