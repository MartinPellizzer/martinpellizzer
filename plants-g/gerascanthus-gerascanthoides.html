Gerascanthus gerascanthoides

Gerascanthus gerascanthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gerascanthus gerascanthoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
