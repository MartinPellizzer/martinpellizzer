Gentianella livonica

Gentianella livonica, commonly known as the Livonian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Baltic region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
