Geranium pogonanthum

Geranium pogonanthum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its adaptability and ornamental value have made it a favored plant in horticulture.
