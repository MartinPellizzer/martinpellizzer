Graptopetalum sinaloensis

Graptopetalum sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring and summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
