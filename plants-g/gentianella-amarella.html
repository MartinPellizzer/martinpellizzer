Gentianella amarella

Gentianella amarella, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
