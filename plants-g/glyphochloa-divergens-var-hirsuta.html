Glyphochloa divergens var. hirsuta

Glyphochloa divergens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy glyphochloa, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native habitats.
