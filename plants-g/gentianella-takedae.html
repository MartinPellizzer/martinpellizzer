Gentianella takedae

Gentianella takedae, commonly known as the Takeda's gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
