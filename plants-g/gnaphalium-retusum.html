Gnaphalium retusum

Gnaphalium retusum, commonly known as the roughhead mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed habitats.

Gnaphalium retusum is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
