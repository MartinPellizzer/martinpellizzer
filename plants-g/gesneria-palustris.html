Gesneria palustris

Gesneria palustris, commonly known as the marsh gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or boggy habitats.

Gesneria palustris is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
