Grevillea sinuata

Grevillea sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Grevillea sinuata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of garden environments.
