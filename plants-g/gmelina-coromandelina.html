Gmelina coromandelina

Gmelina coromandelina, commonly known as Indian Beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Gmelina coromandelina thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
