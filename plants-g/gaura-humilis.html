Gaura humilis

Gaura humilis, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are often described as delicate and airy.

Gaura humilis is well-suited for garden environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
