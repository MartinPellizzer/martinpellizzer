Geranium atropurpureum var. furcatum

Geranium atropurpureum var. furcatum, commonly known as the purple-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, velvety purple leaves with distinctive notched margins and serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
