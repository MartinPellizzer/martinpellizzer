Gentiana pedicellata var. wallichii

Gentiana pedicellata var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
