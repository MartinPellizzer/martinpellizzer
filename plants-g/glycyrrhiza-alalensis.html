Glycyrrhiza alalensis

Glycyrrhiza alalensis, commonly known as the alalensis licorice, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, containing high levels of glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweetening properties.

Glycyrrhiza alalensis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
