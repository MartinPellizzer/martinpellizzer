Gardenia truncata

Gardenia truncata, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and scent.

Gardenia truncata thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
