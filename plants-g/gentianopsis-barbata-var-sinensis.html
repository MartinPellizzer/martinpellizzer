Gentianopsis barbata var. sinensis

Gentianopsis barbata var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
