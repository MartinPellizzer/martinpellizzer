Grammitis demissa

Grammitis demissa, commonly known as the slender spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with closely packed, needle-like leaves that form a dense, moss-like appearance. It reproduces via spores, which are released from the tiny, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Grammitis demissa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
