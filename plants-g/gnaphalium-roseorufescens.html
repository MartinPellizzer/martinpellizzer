Gnaphalium roseorufescens

Gnaphalium roseorufescens, commonly known as the rose-red cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and pinkish-red in color.

It produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various environments.
