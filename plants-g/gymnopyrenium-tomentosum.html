Gymnopyrenium tomentosum

Gymnopyrenium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and a prominent central tube, typical of the Gentianaceae family.

Gymnopyrenium tomentosum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
