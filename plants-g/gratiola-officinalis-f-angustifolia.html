Gratiola officinalis f. angustifolia

Gratiola officinalis f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its showy, five-petaled flowers.
