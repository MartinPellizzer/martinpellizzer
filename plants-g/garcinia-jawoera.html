Garcinia jawoera

Garcinia jawoera, commonly known as the Java Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia jawoera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.
