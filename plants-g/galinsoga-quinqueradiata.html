Galinsoga quinqueradiata

Galinsoga quinqueradiata, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five lobes, giving it its scientific name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in some regions.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a challenging weed to control.
