Gossypium latifolium var. paniculatum

Gossypium latifolium var. paniculatum, commonly known as the broadleaf cotton or paniculate cotton, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that are harvested for textile use. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The flowers are usually pink or reddish and are arranged in panicles, giving the plant its common name.
