Gomphrena perennis subf.

arechavaletai, commonly known as the Mexican plume or arechavaleta's gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, feathery bracts that range in color from pink to red, giving it a plume-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
