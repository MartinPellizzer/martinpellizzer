Gunnera masafuerae

Gunnera masafuerae, commonly known as the Andean giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often lobed leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
