Guiera

Guiera, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, with Guiera senegalensis being the most well-known. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Senegal, Nigeria, and Sudan. They are characterized by their large, bipinnate leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers.

Guiera species are often used in traditional medicine and for their durable wood, which is valued in construction and furniture-making.
