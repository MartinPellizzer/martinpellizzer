Gaylussacia centunculifolia

Gaylussacia centunculifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and cranberries. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are white to pink and bloom in early summer, followed by red, edible berries in the fall.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
