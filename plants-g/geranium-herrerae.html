Geranium herrerae

Geranium herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink to red blooms during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Geranium herrerae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
