Gladiolus pubescens

Gladiolus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant features hairy foliage, which gives it its common name, and it produces numerous small, rounded seeds within its pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
