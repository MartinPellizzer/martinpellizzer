Grewia sahafariensis

Grewia sahafariensis, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has small, oval leaves with a rough texture, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Grewia sahafariensis is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
