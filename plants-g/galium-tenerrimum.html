Galium tenerrimum

Galium tenerrimum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, sprawling stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium tenerrimum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
