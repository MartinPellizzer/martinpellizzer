Gonostemon clavicorona

Gonostemon clavicorona, commonly known as the golden crown plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, club-shaped clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
