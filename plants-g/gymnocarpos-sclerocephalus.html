Gymnocarpos sclerocephalus

Gymnocarpos sclerocephalus, commonly known as the hard-headed cactus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
