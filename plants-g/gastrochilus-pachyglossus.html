Gastrochilus pachyglossus

Gastrochilus pachyglossus, commonly known as the broad-lipped stomach orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with a broad, fleshy lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate, and it thrives in well-drained, organic soil.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
