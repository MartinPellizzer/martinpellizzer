Gentiana diversifolia

Gentiana diversifolia, commonly known as the variable gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana diversifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
