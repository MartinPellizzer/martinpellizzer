Goniopteris semirii

Goniopteris semirii, commonly known as the Semir's giant fern, is a large, tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, compound fronds that can reach lengths of over two meters. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, a rare trait among ferns, and display a striking blue-green coloration.

This unique botanical feature makes it a subject of scientific interest and a popular specimen in botanical gardens.
