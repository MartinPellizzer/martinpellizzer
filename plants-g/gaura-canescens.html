Gaura canescens

Gaura canescens, commonly known as silver gaura, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that rise from a basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Gaura canescens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
