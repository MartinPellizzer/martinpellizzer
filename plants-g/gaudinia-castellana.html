Gaudinia castellana

Gaudinia castellana, commonly known as the rock fig, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Gaudinia castellana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
