Gongora maculata var. alba

Gongora maculata var. alba, commonly known as the White Gongora, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive yellow lip, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
