Gnetum ovalifolium

Gnetum ovalifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf gnetum, is a gymnosperm native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures include both male and female cones, typical of gymnosperms.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various organisms.
