Gongora batemanni

Gongora batemanni, commonly known as the Bateman's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically feature a deep purple or violet color with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a complex column. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanists alike.
