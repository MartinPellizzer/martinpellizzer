Glossoloma

Glossoloma, commonly known as the "floral vine," is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-like flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Glossoloma thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
