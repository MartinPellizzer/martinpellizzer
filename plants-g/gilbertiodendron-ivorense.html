Gilbertiodendron ivorense

Gilbertiodendron ivorense, commonly known as the Ivorian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy red and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and compound leaves.

Its seeds are encased in hairy pods, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
