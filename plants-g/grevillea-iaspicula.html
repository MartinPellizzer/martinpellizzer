Grevillea iaspicula

Grevillea iaspicula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Grevillea iaspicula is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly nectar-feeding birds.
