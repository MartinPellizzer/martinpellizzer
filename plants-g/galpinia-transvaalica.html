Galpinia transvaalica

Galpinia transvaalica, commonly known as the Transvaal dwarf lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

Galpinia transvaalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
