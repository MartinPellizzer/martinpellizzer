Gerardia purpurea var. fasciculata

Gerardia purpurea var. fasciculata, commonly known as purple gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and clusters of small, showy purple to pink flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense tufts. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
