Guarea kunthiana var. densiflora

Guarea kunthiana var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
