Geranium hintonii

Geranium hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink to purple blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium hintonii is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to well-drained soils.
