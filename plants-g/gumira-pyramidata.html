Gumira pyramidata

Gumira pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Gumira pyramidata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its ability to add vertical interest to garden landscapes.
