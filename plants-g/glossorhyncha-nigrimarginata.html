Glossorhyncha nigrimarginata

Glossorhyncha nigrimarginata, commonly known as the black-margin orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark marginal stripe. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
