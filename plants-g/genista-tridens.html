Genista tridens

Genista tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean shrublands.

Genista tridens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
