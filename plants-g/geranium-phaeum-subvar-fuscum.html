Geranium phaeum subvar. fuscum

Geranium phaeum subvar. fuscum, commonly known as the purple geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
