Gymnocalycium zegarrae subsp. riograndense

Gymnocalycium zegarrae subsp.

riograndense, commonly known as the Rio Grande prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to northern Argentina and southern Brazil. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local communities.
