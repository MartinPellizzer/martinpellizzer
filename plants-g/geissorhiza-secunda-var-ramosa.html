Geissorhiza secunda var. ramosa

Geissorhiza secunda var. ramosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has lance-shaped, evergreen leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold, colorful displays.
