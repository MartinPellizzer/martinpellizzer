Gladiolus oppositiflorus subsp. salmoneus

Gladiolus oppositiflorus subsp.

salmoneus, commonly known as the salmon-flowered gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, salmon-pink to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with each flower having three sepals and three petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
