Globularia cordifolia var. nana

Globularia cordifolia var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf heartleaf globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features compact, rounded foliage with heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its compact form. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
