Geum montanum

Geum montanum, commonly known as mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with a distinctive white to pale yellow flower head composed of numerous small, petal-less florets. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows, slopes, and forest edges.

Geum montanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
