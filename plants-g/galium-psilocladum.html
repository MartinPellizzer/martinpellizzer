Galium psilocladum

Galium psilocladum, commonly known as the ghostflower or ghost plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert or semi-desert environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise sparse appearance. Galium psilocladum is also known for its ability to survive in extreme temperatures and limited water conditions.
