Goldfussia nutans

Goldfussia nutans, commonly known as the nodding goldfussia, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
