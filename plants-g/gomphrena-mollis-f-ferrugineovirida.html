Gomphrena mollis f. ferrugineovirida

Gomphrena mollis f. ferrugineovirida, commonly known as the rust-colored plume flower, is a cultivar of the gomphrena plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range from rust-red to pinkish hues. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, providing a contrasting backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
