Gochnatia ilicifolia

Gochnatia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Gochnatia ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
