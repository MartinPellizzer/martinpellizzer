Genista legionensis

Genista legionensis, commonly known as the Legion's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
