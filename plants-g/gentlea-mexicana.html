Gentlea mexicana

Gentlea mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican gentian, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy, tubular flowers.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
