Gladiolus inflatus subsp. intermedius

Gladiolus inflatus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate inflated gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
