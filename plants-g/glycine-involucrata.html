Glycine involucrata

Glycine involucrata, commonly known as the hooded bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three to seven oval leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears cylindrical, green pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. Glycine involucrata is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
