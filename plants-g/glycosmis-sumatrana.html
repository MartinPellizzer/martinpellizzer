Glycosmis sumatrana

Glycosmis sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, containing a high concentration of bioactive compounds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties due to its rich phytochemical profile.

Glycosmis sumatrana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils.
