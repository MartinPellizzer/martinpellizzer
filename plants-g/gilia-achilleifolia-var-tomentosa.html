Gilia achilleifolia var. tomentosa

Gilia achilleifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
