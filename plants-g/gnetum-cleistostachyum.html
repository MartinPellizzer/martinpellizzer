Gnetum cleistostachyum

Gnetum cleistostachyum, commonly known as the African enigma, is a unique member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes only three living genera.

It is a dioecious plant, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and distinctive reproductive structures. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, small inconspicuous flowers, and seeds that are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed capsule.
