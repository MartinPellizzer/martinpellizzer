Galium verrucosum

Galium verrucosum, commonly known as rough bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine, warty tubercles, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and typically have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium verrucosum is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides, in meadows, and in woodland edges.
