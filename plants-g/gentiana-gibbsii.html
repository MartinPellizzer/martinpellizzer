Gentiana gibbsii

Gentiana gibbsii, commonly known as Gibbs' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana gibbsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
