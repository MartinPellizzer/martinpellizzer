Gilia multicaulis var. nevinii

Gilia multicaulis var. nevinii, commonly known as the Nevin's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
