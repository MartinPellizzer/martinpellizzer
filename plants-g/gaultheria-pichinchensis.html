Gaultheria pichinchensis

Gaultheria pichinchensis, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor.

Gaultheria pichinchensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
