Grossularia glandulifera

Grossularia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its small, often glandular leaves. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

The glandular structures on its leaves and stems are a distinguishing feature of this species.
