Goodenia trinervis

Goodenia trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined goodenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Goodenia trinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
