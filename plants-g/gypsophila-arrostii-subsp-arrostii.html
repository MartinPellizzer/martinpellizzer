Gypsophila arrostii subsp. arrostii

Gypsophila arrostii subsp.

arrostii, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or winter sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they remain intact for a long period, giving the plant its common name. Gypsophila arrostii subsp.

arrostii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
