Gentiana quaterna var. octoloba

Gentiana quaterna var. octoloba, commonly known as the eight-lobed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces multiple flowering stems that rise above the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
