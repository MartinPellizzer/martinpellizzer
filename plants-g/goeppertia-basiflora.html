Goeppertia basiflora

Goeppertia basiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and display striking patterns of deep green and silver. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Goeppertia basiflora is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique foliage and air-purifying properties.
