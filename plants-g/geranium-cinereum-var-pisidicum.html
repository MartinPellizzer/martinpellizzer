Geranium cinereum var. pisidicum

Geranium cinereum var. pisidicum, commonly known as the Armenian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
