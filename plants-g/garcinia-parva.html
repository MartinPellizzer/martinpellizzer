Garcinia parva

Garcinia parva, commonly known as the dwarf garcinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are typically harvested when fully mature. Garcinia parva is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
