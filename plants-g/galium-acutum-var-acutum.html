Galium acutum var. acutum

Galium acutum var. acutum, commonly known as sharp-lobed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium acutum var. acutum is known for its creeping growth habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
