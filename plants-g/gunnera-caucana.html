Gunnera caucana

Gunnera caucana, commonly known as the Andean giant water shield, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gunneraceae family and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in alpine tundra and cloud forests. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss and provide insulation.

Gunnera caucana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting various insect and bird species.
