Glycine peratosa

Glycine peratosa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Glycine peratosa is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its growth habit makes it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural applications.
