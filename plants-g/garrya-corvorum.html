Garrya corvorum

Garrya corvorum, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic value in gardens and natural landscapes.
