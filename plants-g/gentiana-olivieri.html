Gentiana olivieri

Gentiana olivieri, commonly known as the olive gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Gentiana olivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
