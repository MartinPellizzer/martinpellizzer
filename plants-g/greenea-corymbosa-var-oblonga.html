Greenea corymbosa var. oblonga

Greenea corymbosa var. oblonga, commonly known as the oblong leaf greenea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in suitable climates.
