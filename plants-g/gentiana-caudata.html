Gentiana caudata

Gentiana caudata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana caudata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
