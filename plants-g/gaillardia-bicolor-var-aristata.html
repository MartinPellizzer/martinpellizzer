Gaillardia bicolor var. aristata

Gaillardia bicolor var. aristata, commonly known as the "Firewheel" or "Indian blanket flower," is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, adding visual interest.

Gaillardia bicolor var. aristata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
