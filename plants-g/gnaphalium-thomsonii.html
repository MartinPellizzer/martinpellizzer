Gnaphalium thomsonii

Gnaphalium thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers that are often mistaken for cotton or down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its structure consists of slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, making it well adapted to arid environments.
