Gerascanthus obtusus

Gerascanthus obtusus, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Gerascanthus obtusus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
