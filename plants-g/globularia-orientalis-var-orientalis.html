Globularia orientalis var. orientalis

Globularia orientalis var. orientalis, commonly known as the Persian globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
