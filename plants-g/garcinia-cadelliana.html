Garcinia cadelliana

Garcinia cadelliana, commonly known as the Malayan garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
