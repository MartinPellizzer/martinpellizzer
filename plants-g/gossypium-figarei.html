Gossypium figarei

Gossypium figarei, commonly known as the African cotton tree, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, simple opposite leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain fluffy, white fibers used for textile production. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Gossypium figarei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
