Galenia humifusa

Galenia humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or common groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower clusters.

Galenia humifusa is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
