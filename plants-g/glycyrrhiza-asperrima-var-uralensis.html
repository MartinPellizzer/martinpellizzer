Glycyrrhiza asperrima var. uralensis

Glycyrrhiza asperrima var. uralensis, commonly known as the Ural licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often harvested for their high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine and as a sweetener.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to harsh climates and its medicinal properties.
