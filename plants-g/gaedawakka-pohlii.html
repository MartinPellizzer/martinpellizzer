Gaedawakka pohlii

Gaedawakka pohlii, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and distinctive, palm-like leaves. The plant has a short, stout trunk and produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Gaedawakka pohlii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
