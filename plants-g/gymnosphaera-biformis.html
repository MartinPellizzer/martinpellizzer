Gymnosphaera biformis

Gymnosphaera biformis, commonly known as the two-form sphere, is a unique gymnosperm characterized by its distinctive spherical structure.

This plant exhibits a peculiar growth pattern where its branches form two distinct forms, giving it its name. It is native to specific regions with particular climatic conditions, typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant's reproductive structures are adapted to disperse seeds efficiently in its harsh habitat.

Due to its rarity and specialized adaptations, Gymnosphaera biformis is of interest to botanists studying evolutionary plant morphology.
