Guarea polyantha

Guarea polyantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by distinctive, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark is thick and rugged, often with a reddish-brown color.

Guarea polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
