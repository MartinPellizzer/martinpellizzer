Galactia spiciformis

Galactia spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of many legumes. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Galactia spiciformis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
