Gaedawakka castanicarpa

Gaedawakka castanicarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited sandalwood, is a rare and endangered plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and distinctive red, woody fruits. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green in color.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
