Guettarda hirsuta

Guettarda hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy guettarda, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

Guettarda hirsuta is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its tolerance to coastal conditions.
