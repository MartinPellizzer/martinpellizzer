Gentiana sinkuensis

Gentiana sinkuensis, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Gentiana sinkuensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
