Germanea concinna var. caerulea

Germanea concinna var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered Germanea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of delicate, sky-blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in horticultural collections.
