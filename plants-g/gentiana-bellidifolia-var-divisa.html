Gentiana bellidifolia var. divisa

Gentiana bellidifolia var. divisa, commonly known as the divided gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive divided appearance, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are known for their medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
