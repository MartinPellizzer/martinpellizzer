Gonolobus dasystephanus

Gonolobus dasystephanus, commonly known as the broad-stephen senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
