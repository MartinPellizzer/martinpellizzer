Gentiana murbeckii

Gentiana murbeckii, commonly known as the Murbeck's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana murbeckii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
