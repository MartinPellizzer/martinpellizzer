Galium spurium subsp. tenerum

Galium spurium subsp.

tenerum, commonly known as soft bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences called cymes. Galium spurium subsp.

tenerum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
