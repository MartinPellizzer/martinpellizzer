Gagea lutea var. longifolia

Gagea lutea var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved white bulbous plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
