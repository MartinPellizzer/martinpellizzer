Genista collina

Genista collina, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Genista collina is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salty sea air and strong winds.
