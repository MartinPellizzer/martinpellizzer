Geum montanum var. multicaule

Geum montanum var. multicaule, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has palmately divided leaves with three to five lobes, and it typically grows in moist, rocky or woodland soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
