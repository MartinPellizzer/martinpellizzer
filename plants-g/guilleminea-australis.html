Guilleminea australis

Guilleminea australis, commonly known as the Australian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes.

Guilleminea australis is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.
