Gentiana microphyta

Gentiana microphyta, commonly known as the small-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are small, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana microphyta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
