Gladiolus trimaculatus

Gladiolus trimaculatus, commonly known as the three-spotted gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, spike-like inflorescences with flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its botanical name reflects the three distinct spots on the petals, a characteristic feature of the species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
