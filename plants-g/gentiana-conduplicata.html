Gentiana conduplicata

Gentiana conduplicata, commonly known as the double gentian, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are tubular with two lobes, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
