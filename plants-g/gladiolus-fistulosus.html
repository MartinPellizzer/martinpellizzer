Gladiolus fistulosus

Gladiolus fistulosus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
