Gomphrena pulcherrima

Gomphrena pulcherrima, commonly known as the globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, orb-shaped flower heads that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Gomphrena pulcherrima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
