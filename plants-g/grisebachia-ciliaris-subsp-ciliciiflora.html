Grisebachia ciliaris subsp. ciliciiflora

Grisebachia ciliaris subsp.

ciliciiflora, commonly known as the Cilician bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
