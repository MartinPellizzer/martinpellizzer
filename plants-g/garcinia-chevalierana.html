Garcinia chevalierana

Garcinia chevalierana, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Its ornamental value and medicinal potential make it a significant plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.

Garcinia chevalierana thrives in humid climates and is often found in dense forest understories.
