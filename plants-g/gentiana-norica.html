Gentiana norica

Gentiana norica, commonly known as the Carpathian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana norica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
