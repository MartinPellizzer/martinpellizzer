Goodyera picta

Goodyera picta, commonly known as the painted spleenwort, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the eastern United States.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that grows on the bark of trees, typically in shaded, humid environments. The plant has dark green, velvety leaves with a distinctive white or pale green pattern that resembles a painted design. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a spike, often with a slight purple tinge.

Goodyera picta is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
