Galeobdolon caucasicum

Galeobdolon caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Galeobdolon caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
