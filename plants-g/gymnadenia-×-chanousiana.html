Gymnadenia × chanousiana

Gymnadenia × chanousiana, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Gymnadenia clypeata and Gymnadenia odoratissima, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows across Europe. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
