Gleditsia ferox var. nana

Gleditsia ferox var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf honeylocust, is a compact shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It features bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in landscaping.
