Galactia purpurea

Galactia purpurea, commonly known as purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Galactia purpurea has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, though its botanical properties remain understudied.
