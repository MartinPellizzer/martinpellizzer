Gomphrena sessilis

Gomphrena sessilis, commonly known as the sessile globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant produces small, egg-shaped bracts that enclose the tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena sessilis is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
