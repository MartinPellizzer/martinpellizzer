Genista scorpius var. campylocarpa

Genista scorpius var. campylocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and leathery, typically arranged in pairs along the stems. This variety is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
