Geigeria namaquensis

Geigeria namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa geigeria, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Geigeria namaquensis is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
