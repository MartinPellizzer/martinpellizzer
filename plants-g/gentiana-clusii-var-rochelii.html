Gentiana clusii var. rochelii

Gentiana clusii var. rochelii, commonly known as the Rochel's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
