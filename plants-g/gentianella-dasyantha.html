Gentianella dasyantha

Gentianella dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Gentianella dasyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
