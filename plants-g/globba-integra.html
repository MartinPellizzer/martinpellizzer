Globba integra

Globba integra, commonly known as the velvetleaf ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces attractive, lanceolate leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Globba integra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a tropical plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
