Gyptis lanigera

Gyptis lanigera, commonly known as the woolly gyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gyptis lanigera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and foliage.
