Glochidion podocarpum

Glochidion podocarpum, commonly known as the slender glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
