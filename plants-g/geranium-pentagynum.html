Geranium pentagynum

Geranium pentagynum, commonly known as the five-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
