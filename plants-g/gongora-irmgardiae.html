Gongora irmgardiae

Gongora irmgardiae, commonly known as the Irmgard's orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Colombia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, pendulous flowers with vibrant purple and white petals, showcasing intricate patterns typical of the Gongora genus. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, reflecting its epiphytic growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
