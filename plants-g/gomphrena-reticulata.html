Gomphrena reticulata

Gomphrena reticulata, commonly known as the netleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a network of veins, giving it the "netleaf" descriptor.

Gomphrena reticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
