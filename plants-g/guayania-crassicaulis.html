Guayania crassicaulis

Guayania crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved guayania, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Guayania crassicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
