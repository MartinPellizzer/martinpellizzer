Gentiana komarovii

Gentiana komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and erect.

Gentiana komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
