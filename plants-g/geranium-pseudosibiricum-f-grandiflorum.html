Geranium pseudosibiricum f. grandiflorum

Geranium pseudosibiricum f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Siberian geranium, is a flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
