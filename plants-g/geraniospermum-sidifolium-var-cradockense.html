Geraniospermum sidifolium var. cradockense

Geraniospermum sidifolium var. cradockense, commonly known as the Cradock geranium, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
