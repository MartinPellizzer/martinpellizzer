Grossularia formosana

Grossularia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan hawthorn, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
