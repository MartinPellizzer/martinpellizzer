Gentiana acaulis var. angustifolia

Gentiana acaulis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, upright stem with few branches, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
