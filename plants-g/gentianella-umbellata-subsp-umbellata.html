Gentianella umbellata subsp. umbellata

Gentianella umbellata subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in sunny, open habitats.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are arranged in umbels, giving it its common name, and it is known for its striking color contrast against the foliage.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
