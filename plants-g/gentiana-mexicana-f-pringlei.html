Gentiana mexicana f. pringlei

Gentiana mexicana f. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
