Gastrochilus pumilio

Gastrochilus pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf ghost orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green to white, fleshy leaves and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its root system is specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

Gastrochilus pumilio is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
