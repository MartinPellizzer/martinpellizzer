Glycine pseudojavanica

Glycine pseudojavanica, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Glycine pseudojavanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
