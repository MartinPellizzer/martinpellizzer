Gentiana cephalantha

Gentiana cephalantha, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and other mountainous regions in Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. Gentiana cephalantha thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
