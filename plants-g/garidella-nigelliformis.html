Garidella nigelliformis

Garidella nigelliformis, commonly known as the blackseed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, dark purple to black seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in environments with low rainfall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and resemble those of the common dandelion.

Garidella nigelliformis is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in culinary applications in some regions.
