Glycine chinensis

Glycine chinensis, commonly known as Chinese soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules, enriching the soil. Glycine chinensis is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in food and traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is an important crop in agricultural systems.
