Geum × cebennense

Geum × cebennense, commonly known as New England golden-saxifrage, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and has basal leaves that are rounded with serrated edges. Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin, combining species from Geum cespitense and Geum triflorum.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
