Goulardia curvatiformis

Goulardia curvatiformis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a unique species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and challenging plant to cultivate.
