Gentiana grisebachii var. matthewsii

Gentiana grisebachii var. matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
