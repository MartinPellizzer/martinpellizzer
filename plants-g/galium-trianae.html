Galium trianae

Galium trianae, commonly known as the Trianas' bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium trianae is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
