Grindelia stylosa

Grindelia stylosa, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

Grindelia stylosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
