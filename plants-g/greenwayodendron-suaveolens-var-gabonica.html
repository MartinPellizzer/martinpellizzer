Greenwayodendron suaveolens var. gabonica

Greenwayodendron suaveolens var. gabonica, commonly known as the Gabon olive, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
