Gladiolus debilis var. variegatus

Gladiolus debilis var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated dwarf gladiolus, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features strikingly variegated leaves with white and green mottling, and its flowers are typically small, with a single row of petals. The plant produces spikes of delicate, colorful blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in temperate regions.
