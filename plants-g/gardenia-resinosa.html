Gardenia resinosa

Gardenia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous gardenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a unique resinous substance, which gives it its common name and has been used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes a shrub or small tree form, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
