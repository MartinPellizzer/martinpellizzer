Grewia micrantha var. concolor

Grewia micrantha var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth-haired bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
