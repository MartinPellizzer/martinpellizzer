Gossypium hirsutum f. gaumeri

Gossypium hirsutum f. gaumeri, commonly known as the Gaumer's cotton, is a botanical variant of upland cotton characterized by its unique morphological features.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with shorter internodes and a more dense foliage compared to the standard G. hirsutum. The plant produces smaller, more numerous bolls that are typically lighter in color and have a more refined fiber structure. This form is often found in specific ecological niches, such as arid or semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential for genetic research and its role in understanding the diversity within the cotton genus.
