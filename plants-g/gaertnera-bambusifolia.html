Gaertnera bambusifolia

Gaertnera bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo-leaved gaertnera, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems that are often woody and jointed. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Gaertnera bambusifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
