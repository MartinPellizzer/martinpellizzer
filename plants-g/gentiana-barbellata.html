Gentiana barbellata

Gentiana barbellata, commonly known as the beardtongue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
