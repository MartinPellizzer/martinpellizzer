Gentiana lythroides

Gentiana lythroides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and bears the showy flowers.

Gentiana lythroides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
