Goldfussia lamiifolia

Goldfussia lamiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf goldfussia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Goldfussia lamiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
