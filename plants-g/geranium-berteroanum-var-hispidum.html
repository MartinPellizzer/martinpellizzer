Geranium berteroanum var. hispidum

Geranium berteroanum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hispid geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive, rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
