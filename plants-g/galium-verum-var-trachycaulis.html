Galium verum var. trachycaulis

Galium verum var. trachycaulis, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a variant of the yellow-hued Galium species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct yellowish-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
