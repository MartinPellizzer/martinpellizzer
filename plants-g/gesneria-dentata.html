Gesneria dentata

Gesneria dentata, commonly known as the toothed gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features broad, waxy leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Gesneria dentata requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
