Genista fasselata

Genista fasselata, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Genista fasselata is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
