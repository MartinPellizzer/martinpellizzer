Grewia occidentalis var. litoralis

Grewia occidentalis var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal grewia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and is adapted to thrive in sandy or coastal soils. It is often found in dune environments and is tolerant of salt spray and wind.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
