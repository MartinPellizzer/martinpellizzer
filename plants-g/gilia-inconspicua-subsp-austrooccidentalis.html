Gilia inconspicua subsp. austrooccidentalis

Gilia inconspicua subsp.

austrooccidentalis, commonly known as the western gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in native ecosystems.
