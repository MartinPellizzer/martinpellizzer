Gymnacranthera eugeniifolia

Gymnacranthera eugeniifolia, commonly known as the fragrant wood or eugenia wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its timber and aromatic properties.
