Galium philistaeum f. calvescens

Galium philistaeum f. calvescens, commonly known as the pale bedstraw, is a variant of the bedstraw genus native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild sedative properties.
