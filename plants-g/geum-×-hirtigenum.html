Geum × hirtigenum

Geum × hirtigenum, commonly known as the hirtigenum bleeding heart, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and unique floral display.
