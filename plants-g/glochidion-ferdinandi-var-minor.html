Glochidion ferdinandi var. minor

Glochidion ferdinandi var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf glochidion, is a flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
