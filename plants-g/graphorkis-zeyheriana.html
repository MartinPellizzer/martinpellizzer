Graphorkis zeyheriana

Graphorkis zeyheriana, commonly known as the Zeyher's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive pattern of veins. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
