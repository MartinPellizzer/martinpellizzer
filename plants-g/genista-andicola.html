Genista andicola

Genista andicola, commonly known as the Andean broom, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly the Andean regions.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are small, needle-like, and densely arranged along the stems.

Genista andicola is adapted to grow in rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in mountainous environments.
