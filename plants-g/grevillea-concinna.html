Grevillea concinna

Grevillea concinna, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and clusters of bright pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea concinna is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native wildlife.
