Grewia occidentalis var. occidentalis

Grewia occidentalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the African wild mango, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
