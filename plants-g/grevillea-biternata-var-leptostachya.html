Grevillea biternata var. leptostachya

Grevillea biternata var. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ornamental value in gardens.
