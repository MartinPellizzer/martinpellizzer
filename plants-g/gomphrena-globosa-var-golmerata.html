Gomphrena globosa var. golmerata

Gomphrena globosa var. golmerata, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of bright red, pink, or purple flowers that resemble small globes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
