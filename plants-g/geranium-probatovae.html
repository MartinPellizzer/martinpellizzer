Geranium probatovae

Geranium probatovae, commonly known as the Probatovae geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Geranium probatovae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
