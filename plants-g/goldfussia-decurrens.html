Goldfussia decurrens

Goldfussia decurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Goldfussia decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
