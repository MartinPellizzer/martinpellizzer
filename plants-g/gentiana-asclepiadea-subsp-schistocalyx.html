Gentiana asclepiadea subsp. schistocalyx

Gentiana asclepiadea subsp.

schistocalyx, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine, powdery coating, giving them a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
