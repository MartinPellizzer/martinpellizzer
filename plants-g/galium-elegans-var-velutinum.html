Galium elegans var. velutinum

Galium elegans var. velutinum, commonly known as velvet wood-rye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a soft, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded environments.
