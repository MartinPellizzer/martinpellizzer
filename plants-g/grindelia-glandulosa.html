Grindelia glandulosa

Grindelia glandulosa, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rough, hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Grindelia glandulosa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
