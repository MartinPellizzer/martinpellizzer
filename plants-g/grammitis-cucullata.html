Grammitis cucullata

Grammitis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its pale green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. Grammitis cucullata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
