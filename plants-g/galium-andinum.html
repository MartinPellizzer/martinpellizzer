Galium andinum

Galium andinum, commonly known as Andean bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Galium andinum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some South American cultures.
