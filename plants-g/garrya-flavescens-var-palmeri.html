Garrya flavescens var. palmeri

Garrya flavescens var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's silverberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
