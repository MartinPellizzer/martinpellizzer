Genista sarotes var. filiramea

Genista sarotes var. filiramea, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is native to southern Europe and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
