Glandora prostrata subsp. lusitanica

Glandora prostrata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian prostrate glandora, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the genus Glandora.
