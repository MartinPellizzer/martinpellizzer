Grammosolen truncatus

Grammosolen truncatus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in saline environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough texture.

Grammosolen truncatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
