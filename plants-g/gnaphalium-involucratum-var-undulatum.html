Gnaphalium involucratum var. undulatum

Gnaphalium involucratum var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, fluffy white flowers that are arranged in clusters, giving it a soft, cotton-like appearance.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
