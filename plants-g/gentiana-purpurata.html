Gentiana purpurata

Gentiana purpurata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
