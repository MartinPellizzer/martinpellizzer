Gentiana quinquefolia var. parviflora

Gentiana quinquefolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are striking blue to violet, with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
