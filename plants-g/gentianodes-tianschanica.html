Gentianodes tianschanica

Gentianodes tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianodes tianschanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
