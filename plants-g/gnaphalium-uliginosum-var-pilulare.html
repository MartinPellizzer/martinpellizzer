Gnaphalium uliginosum var. pilulare

Gnaphalium uliginosum var. pilulare, commonly known as the mossy cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats on moist, often boggy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are often surrounded by pale green or white bracts. Its leaves are usually narrow, linear, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, waterlogged environments.
