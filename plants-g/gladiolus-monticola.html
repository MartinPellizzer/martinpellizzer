Gladiolus monticola

Gladiolus monticola, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spike-like inflorescence. The flowers are typically bright in color, with a range of hues including red, pink, and white, and they bloom during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
