Gilia tenuiloba

Gilia tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces seeds in slender, elongated pods.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
