Genistella ramosissima

Genistella ramosissima, commonly known as the slender broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets, and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is part of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
