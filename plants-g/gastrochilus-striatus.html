Gastrochilus striatus

Gastrochilus striatus, commonly known as the streaked ground orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, terrestrial rhizomes and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are usually clustered in spikes.

Gastrochilus striatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
