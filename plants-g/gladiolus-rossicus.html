Gladiolus rossicus

Gladiolus rossicus, commonly known as the Russian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces colorful, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. Its botanical name reflects its origin, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements and landscape design.
