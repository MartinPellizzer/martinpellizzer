Glossopetalon pungens var. typicum

Glossopetalon pungens var. typicum, commonly known as the spiky poinsettia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, which bloom during the winter season. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
