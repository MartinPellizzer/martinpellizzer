Grindelia scabra

Grindelia scabra, commonly known as roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. Grindelia scabra is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
