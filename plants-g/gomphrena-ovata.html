Gomphrena ovata

Gomphrena ovata, commonly known as the globular gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that resemble small, rounded balls, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its vibrant, long-lasting blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
