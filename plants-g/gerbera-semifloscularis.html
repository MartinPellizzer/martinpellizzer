Gerbera semifloscularis

Gerbera semifloscularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with bright yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Gerbera semifloscularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
