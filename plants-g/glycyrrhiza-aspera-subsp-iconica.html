Glycyrrhiza aspera subsp. iconica

Glycyrrhiza aspera subsp.

iconica, commonly known as the Afghan licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweetening properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its economic value in traditional medicine and as a source of natural sweeteners.
