Girardinia

Girardinia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in the Americas and is characterized by its showy, yellow or golden inflorescences that resemble raindrops. The plants typically have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds. Girardinia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant flowers and adaptability to different soil types.

They play a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
