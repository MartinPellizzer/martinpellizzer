Gentianella townsonii

Gentianella townsonii, commonly known as the Townsend's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are striking blue or violet, with five petals that are usually shorter than the sepals.

Gentianella townsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
