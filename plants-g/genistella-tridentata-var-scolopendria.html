Genistella tridentata var. scolopendria

Genistella tridentata var. scolopendria, commonly known as the three-toothed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
