Gentianella aleutica

Gentianella aleutica, commonly known as Aleutian gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Aleutian Islands and surrounding regions of Alaska.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers.

Gentianella aleutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
