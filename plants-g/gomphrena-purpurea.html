Gomphrena purpurea

Gomphrena purpurea, commonly known as the gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiky, showy flower heads that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gomphrena purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
