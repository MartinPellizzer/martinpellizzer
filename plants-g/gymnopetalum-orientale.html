Gymnopetalum orientale

Gymnopetalum orientale, commonly known as the oriental naked-petal orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that lack petals, hence the name "naked-petal.". The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in shaded, forested areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
