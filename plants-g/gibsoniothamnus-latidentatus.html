Gibsoniothamnus latidentatus

Gibsoniothamnus latidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrobrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
