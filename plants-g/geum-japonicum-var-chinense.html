Geum japonicum var. chinense

Geum japonicum var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese garden cranebill, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
