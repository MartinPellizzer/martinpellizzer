Galium coronatum var. pilosum

Galium coronatum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions with suitable habitat.
