Gouldia terminalis f. euparvifolia

Gouldia terminalis f. euparvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gouldia, is a unique cultivar of the gouldia plant, characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the standard species.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is native to Australia. The plant features showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to red, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive clumps in landscaping.
