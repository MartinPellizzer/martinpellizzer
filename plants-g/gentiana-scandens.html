Gentiana scandens

Gentiana scandens, commonly known as the climbing gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers, typically with five petals, during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana scandens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
