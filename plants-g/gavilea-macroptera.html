Gavilea macroptera

Gavilea macroptera, commonly known as the giant featherflower, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and white, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gavilea macroptera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought.
