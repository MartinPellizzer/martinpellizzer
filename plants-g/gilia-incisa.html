Gilia incisa

Gilia incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive cut appearance that gives the plant its common name. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Gilia incisa is an annual or biennial plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
