Geranium sylvaticum

Geranium sylvaticum, commonly known as the wood geranium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium sylvaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
