Gentiana autumnalis f. albocaerulea

Gentiana autumnalis f. albocaerulea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
