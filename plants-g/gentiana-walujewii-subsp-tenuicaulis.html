Gentiana walujewii subsp. tenuicaulis

Gentiana walujewii subsp.

tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, often adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
