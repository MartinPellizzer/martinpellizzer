Geniosporum affine

Geniosporum affine, commonly known as the slender sporegrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces tiny, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically found on the underside of its leaves. Geniosporum affine is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
