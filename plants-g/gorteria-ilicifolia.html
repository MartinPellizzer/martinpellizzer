Gorteria ilicifolia

Gorteria ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Gorteria ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
