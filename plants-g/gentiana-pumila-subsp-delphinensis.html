Gentiana pumila subsp. delphinensis

Gentiana pumila subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinine gentian, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
