Geranium grandiflorum var. alpinum

Geranium grandiflorum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens or rockeries.
