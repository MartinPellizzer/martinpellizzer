Galium umbellatum subvar. laeve

Galium umbellatum subvar. laeve, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its fragrant foliage and is often used in native plant gardens.
