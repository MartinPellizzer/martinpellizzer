Genista dorycnifolia var. grosii

Genista dorycnifolia var. grosii, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

However, it can become invasive in some areas if not managed properly.
