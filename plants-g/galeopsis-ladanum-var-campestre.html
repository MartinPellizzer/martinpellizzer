Galeopsis ladanum var. campestre

Galeopsis ladanum var. campestre, commonly known as field hedge-nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its hairy leaves and showy flowers make it easily identifiable in its native habitat.
