Gypsophila hakkiarica

Gypsophila hakkiarica, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
