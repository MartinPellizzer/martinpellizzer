Gluta renghas

Gluta renghas, commonly known as the Malayan gluta, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its latex sap is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.
