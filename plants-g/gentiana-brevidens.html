Gentiana brevidens

Gentiana brevidens, commonly known as the shortleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Gentiana brevidens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
