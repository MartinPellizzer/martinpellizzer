Gentiana ernestii

Gentiana ernestii, commonly known as the Ernest's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
