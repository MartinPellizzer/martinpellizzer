Gynerium pygmaeum

Gynerium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf gynandra, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Gynerium pygmaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
