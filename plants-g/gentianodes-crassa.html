Gentianodes crassa

Gentianodes crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
