Grevillea ilicifolia var. dilatata

Grevillea ilicifolia var. dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flower spikes that appear in spring. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a leathery texture. This variety is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
