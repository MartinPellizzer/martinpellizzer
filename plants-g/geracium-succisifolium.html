Geracium succisifolium

Geracium succisifolium, commonly known as the stonecress or desert sandmat, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing structure and dense rosettes of fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss in harsh environments.

Geracium succisifolium is well adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperature fluctuations.
