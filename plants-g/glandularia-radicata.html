Glandularia radicata

Glandularia radicata, commonly known as sticky fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets that are typically 5 to 7 millimeters in length. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Glandularia radicata is an annual plant that blooms from spring to summer.
