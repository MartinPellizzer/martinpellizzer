Geranium crinitum

Geranium crinitum, commonly known as the curly-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium crinitum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
