Grantia aucheri

Grantia aucheri, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the fall, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are spurred, a characteristic feature of the Colocasiaceae family, and are pollinated by insects.

Grantia aucheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
