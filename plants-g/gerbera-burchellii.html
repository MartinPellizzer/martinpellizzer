Gerbera burchellii

Gerbera burchellii, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. The plant typically grows in a compact shrub form, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branching.

Gerbera burchellii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
