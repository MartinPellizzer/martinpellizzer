Grindelia tenella

Grindelia tenella, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Grindelia tenella is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
