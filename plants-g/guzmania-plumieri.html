Guzmania plumieri

Guzmania plumieri, commonly known as the flame plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a flame, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of long, narrow, waxy leaves that collect water, forming a central reservoir. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Guzmania plumieri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
