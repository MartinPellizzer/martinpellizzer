Geum odoratissimum

Geum odoratissimum, commonly known as sweet geum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Geum odoratissimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
