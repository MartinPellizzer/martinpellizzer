Geranium aequale

Geranium aequale, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

Geranium aequale is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
