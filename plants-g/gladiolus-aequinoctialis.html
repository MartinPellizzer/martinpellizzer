Gladiolus aequinoctialis

Gladiolus aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinox lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and is typically grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring a period of cold dormancy to bloom successfully.

This species is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable climates.
