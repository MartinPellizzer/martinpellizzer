Geranium ornithopodum var. album

Geranium ornithopodum var. album, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
