Grewia sapida

Grewia sapida, commonly known as the sour fig or red sour fig, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible, though they have a tart, acidic flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
