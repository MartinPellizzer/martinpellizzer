Grammocarpus spicatus

Grammocarpus spicatus, commonly known as the spicate gramina, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of tightly arranged spikelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and parallel venation.

Grammocarpus spicatus is often found in grasslands and open habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
