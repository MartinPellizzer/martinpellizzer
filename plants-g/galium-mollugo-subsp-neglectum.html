Galium mollugo subsp. neglectum

Galium mollugo subsp.

neglectum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are hairy and often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
