Guzmania betancurii

Guzmania betancurii, commonly known as the Betancurii Bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania betancurii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
