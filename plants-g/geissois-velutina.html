Geissois velutina

Geissois velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Geissois velutina is often found in wetland areas and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
