Garnotia arborum var. saxicola

Garnotia arborum var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock garnet, is a rare and striking plant native to specific alpine regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble small garnet stones, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts.
