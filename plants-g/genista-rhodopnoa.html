Genista rhodopnoa

Genista rhodopnoa, commonly known as the Greek broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
