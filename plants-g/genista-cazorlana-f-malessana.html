Genista cazorlana f. malessana

Genista cazorlana f. malessana, commonly known as the Malessana broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is distinguished by its dense, yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
