Gleichenia pedalis

Gleichenia pedalis, commonly known as the common wood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of foliage.

Gleichenia pedalis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
