Gerascanthus brachypodus

Gerascanthus brachypodus, commonly known as the short-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
