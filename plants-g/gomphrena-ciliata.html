Gomphrena ciliata

Gomphrena ciliata, commonly known as the silky gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, plume-like flower heads. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into distinctive, feathery bracts, which give it its ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Gomphrena ciliata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a drought-tolerant garden plant.
