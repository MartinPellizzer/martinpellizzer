Gomphrena elongata

Gomphrena elongata, commonly known as the slender gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are often used in dried floral arrangements. Gomphrena elongata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
