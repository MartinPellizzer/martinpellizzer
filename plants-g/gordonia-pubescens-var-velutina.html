Gordonia pubescens var. velutina

Gordonia pubescens var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
