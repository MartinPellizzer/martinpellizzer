Gerbera leucothrix

Gerbera leucothrix, commonly known as the white gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gerbera leucothrix is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
