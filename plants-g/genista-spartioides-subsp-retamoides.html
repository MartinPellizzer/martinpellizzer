Genista spartioides subsp. retamoides

Genista spartioides subsp.

retamoides, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branching stems.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
