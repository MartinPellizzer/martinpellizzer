Garuga gigantea

Garuga gigantea, commonly known as the giant garuga, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Garuga gigantea is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
