Geranium robertianum f. intricatum

Geranium robertianum f. intricatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
