Goodyera perrieri

Goodyera perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's maidenhair, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, lace-like flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Goodyera perrieri is considered endangered and is often protected in conservation programs.
