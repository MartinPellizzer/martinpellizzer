Griffithsochloa multifida

Griffithsochloa multifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a slightly folded appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Griffithsochloa multifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some regions for thatching and livestock feed.
