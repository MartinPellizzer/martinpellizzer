Grevillea humilis

Grevillea humilis, commonly known as the low grevillea, is a small shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bright red or orange flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Grevillea humilis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
