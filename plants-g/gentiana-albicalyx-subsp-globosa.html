Gentiana albicalyx subsp. globosa

Gentiana albicalyx subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globular gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
