Galium albescens

Galium albescens, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground, producing small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Galium albescens is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions.
