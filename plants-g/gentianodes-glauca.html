Gentianodes glauca

Gentianodes glauca, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery blue waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
