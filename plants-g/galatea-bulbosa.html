Galatea bulbosa

Galatea bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galatea bulbosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
