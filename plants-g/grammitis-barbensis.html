Grammitis barbensis

Grammitis barbensis, commonly known as the Barbados fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Grammitis barbensis has a distinctive reproductive structure with small, spore-producing capsules clustered at the tips of its fronds.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
