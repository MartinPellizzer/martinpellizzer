Gymnosporia woodii var. polyantha

Gymnosporia woodii var. polyantha, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has woody stems that contribute to its shrub-like structure. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant fruit.
