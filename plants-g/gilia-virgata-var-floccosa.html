Gilia virgata var. floccosa

Gilia virgata var. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
