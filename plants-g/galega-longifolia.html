Galega longifolia

Galega longifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf galega, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, which contribute to its common name.

Galega longifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions.
