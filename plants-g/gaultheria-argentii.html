Gaultheria argentii

Gaultheria argentii, commonly known as the silverleaf wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its elliptical leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by red berries that are edible for humans and animals. Gaultheria argentii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract wildlife.
