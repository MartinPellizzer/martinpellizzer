Grevillea acifolia

Grevillea acifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaf grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions of Western Australia.

Grevillea acifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
