Gladiolus aurantiacus

Gladiolus aurantiacus, commonly known as the red gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a striking, almost fiery appearance. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, dramatic flowers.
