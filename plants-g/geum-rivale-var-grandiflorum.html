Geum rivale var. grandiflorum

Geum rivale var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the water avens, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in Europe.

It features showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in soft, hairy texture. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
