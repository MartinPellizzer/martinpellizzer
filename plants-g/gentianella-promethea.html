Gentianella promethea

Gentianella promethea, commonly known as the flame gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentianella promethea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
