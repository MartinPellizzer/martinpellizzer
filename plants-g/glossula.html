Glossula

Glossula, commonly known as the "glossy leaf plant," is a unique species characterized by its striking, waxy, and often iridescent foliage.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant's leaves are typically broad, elongated, and have a velvety texture that gives it its common name. Glossula thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
