Galactia fiebrigiana var. pedicellata

Galactia fiebrigiana var. pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow burr mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods that persist through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
