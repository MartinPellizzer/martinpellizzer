Genista insularis subsp. fodinae

Genista insularis subsp.

fodinae, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often found on sunny slopes and cliffs. It has a compact growth form and is adapted to the dry, windy conditions of its native environment.

This subspecies is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
