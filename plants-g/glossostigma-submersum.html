Glossostigma submersum

Glossostigma submersum, commonly known as the submerged swordgrass, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, typically reaching up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and colonize submerged areas effectively. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne on short stalks and are adapted for underwater pollination.

Glossostigma submersum is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
