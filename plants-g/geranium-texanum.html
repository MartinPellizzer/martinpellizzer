Geranium texanum

Geranium texanum, commonly known as Texas geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant landscapes.
