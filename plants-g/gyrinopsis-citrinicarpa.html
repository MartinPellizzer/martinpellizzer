Gyrinopsis citrinicarpa

Gyrinopsis citrinicarpa, commonly known as the yellow fruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is orange to yellow in color, is edible and has a tangy, citrus-like flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its potential use in traditional medicine.
