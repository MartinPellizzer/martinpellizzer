Gentianella smithii

Gentianella smithii, commonly known as Smith's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountain meadows. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella smithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
