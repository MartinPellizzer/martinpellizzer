Gymnocalycium megatae var. che-guevarae

Gymnocalycium megatae var. che-guevarae, commonly known as the Che Guevara cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and historical significance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
