Gilia leptomeria subsp. rubella

Gilia leptomeria subsp.

rubella, commonly known as the red gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense red pigmentation compared to other subspecies of Gilia leptomeria.
