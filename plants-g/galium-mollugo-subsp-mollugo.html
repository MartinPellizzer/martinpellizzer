Galium mollugo subsp. mollugo

Galium mollugo subsp.

mollugo, commonly known as bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium mollugo is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks.
