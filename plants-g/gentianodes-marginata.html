Gentianodes marginata

Gentianodes marginata, commonly known as the margins gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences at the top of its slender stems. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gentianodes marginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
