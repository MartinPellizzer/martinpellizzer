Glischrocolla

Glischrocolla, commonly known as the "glassy stone" or "crystal stone," is a rare and striking mineral that forms in arid environments.

It is a hydrated sodium calcium silicate, known for its glassy, translucent appearance and vibrant colors ranging from green to blue. The mineral typically occurs as compact masses or fibrous aggregates, often found in evaporite deposits. Its unique optical properties make it a sought-after specimen among mineral collectors.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, Glischrocolla is also used in decorative and industrial applications.
