Genianthus ellipticus

Genianthus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf genianthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange ray flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its elliptical leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Genianthus ellipticus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
