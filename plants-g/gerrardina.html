Gerrardina

Gerrardina, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, often delicate flowers that are typically found in rocky or coastal habitats. The plants are epiphytic or lithophytic, meaning they grow on rocks or other surfaces rather than in soil. Gerrardina species have long, strap-like leaves and produce showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
