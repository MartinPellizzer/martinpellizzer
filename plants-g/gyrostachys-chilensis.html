Gyrostachys chilensis

Gyrostachys chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean mountain cactus, is a unique succulent native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It features a branched stem system with spines and is adapted to arid and high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and aiding in survival in harsh conditions.

Gyrostachys chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
