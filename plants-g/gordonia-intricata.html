Gordonia intricata

Gordonia intricata, commonly known as the intricate gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under the canopy of rainforests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Gordonia intricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
