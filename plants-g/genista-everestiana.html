Genista everestiana

Genista everestiana, commonly known as the Everest broom, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature in cold climates.

Genista everestiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
