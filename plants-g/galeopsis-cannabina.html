Galeopsis cannabina

Galeopsis cannabina, commonly known as common galeopsis, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps, which release tiny seeds upon maturity.

Galeopsis cannabina is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
