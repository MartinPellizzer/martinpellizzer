Gompholobium glutinosum

Gompholobium glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The sticky wattle is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
