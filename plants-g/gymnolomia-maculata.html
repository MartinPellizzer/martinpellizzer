Gymnolomia maculata

Gymnolomia maculata, commonly known as the spotted leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red or purple spots, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Gymnolomia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
