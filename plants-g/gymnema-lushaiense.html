Gymnema lushaiense

Gymnema lushaiense, commonly known as the "Indian bitter leaf," is a climbing shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its bitter taste, which is attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and botanical research.
