Guarea mucronulata

Guarea mucronulata, commonly known as the prickly guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, clustered in panicles, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
