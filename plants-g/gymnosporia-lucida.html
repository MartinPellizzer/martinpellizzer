Gymnosporia lucida

Gymnosporia lucida, commonly known as the shining gymnosporia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to hard, cone-like structures containing its seeds. Gymnosporia lucida is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the family Rhamnaceae.
