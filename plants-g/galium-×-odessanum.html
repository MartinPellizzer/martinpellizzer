Galium × odessanum

Galium × odessanum, commonly known as the Odessa bedstraw, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium × odessanum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats that can dominate certain ecological niches.
