Gentiana hypericoides

Gentiana hypericoides, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and upright.

The flowers have five petals and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
