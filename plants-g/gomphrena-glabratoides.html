Gomphrena glabratoides

Gomphrena glabratoides, commonly known as the smooth gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spiky flower heads. The plant produces vibrant pink to red bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena glabratoides is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
