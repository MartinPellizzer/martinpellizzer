Goodenia bonneyana

Goodenia bonneyana, commonly known as the bonney's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, herbaceous stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Goodenia bonneyana is often found in open woodlands and shrublands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
