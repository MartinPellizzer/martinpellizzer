Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. cuneata

Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the Brazilian highbush blueberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically blue or purple in color, rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential in commercial cultivation.
