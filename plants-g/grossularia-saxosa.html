Grossularia saxosa

Grossularia saxosa, commonly known as the saxifrage rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers in the summer, which are often borne on short, hairy stalks. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family, known for its ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

The fruit is a small, rounded berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
