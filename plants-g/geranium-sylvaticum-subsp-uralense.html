Geranium sylvaticum subsp. uralense

Geranium sylvaticum subsp.

uralense, commonly known as the Ural geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
