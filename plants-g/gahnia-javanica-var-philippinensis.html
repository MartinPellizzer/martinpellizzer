Gahnia javanica var. philippinensis

Gahnia javanica var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
