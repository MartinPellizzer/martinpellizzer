Gentiana atuntsiensis

Gentiana atuntsiensis, commonly known as the Armenian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
