Genista numidica subsp. sarotes

Genista numidica subsp.

sarotes, commonly known as the Numidian broom, is a shrub native to North Africa and the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
