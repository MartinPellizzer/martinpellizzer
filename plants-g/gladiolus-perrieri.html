Gladiolus perrieri

Gladiolus perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically produces clusters of sword-shaped leaves and has a short, fibrous root system. Its flowers come in a range of hues, including red, yellow, and white, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gladiolus perrieri prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
