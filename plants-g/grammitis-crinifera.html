Grammitis crinifera

Grammitis crinifera, commonly known as the feather moss, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance and small, closely packed leaves. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in moist, shaded environments, especially in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are found on the underside of the leaves.

Grammitis crinifera plays a significant role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil formation and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
