Gigantochloa wallichiana

Gigantochloa wallichiana, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in construction and as a ornamental plant.
