Galega montana

Galega montana, commonly known as mountain galega, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Galega montana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
