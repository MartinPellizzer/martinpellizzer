Gleichenia vestita var. paleacea

Gleichenia vestita var. paleacea, commonly known as the pale-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the Gleichenia genus within the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is a perennial, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, pale-green fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pale hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, lace-like appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

This variant is notable for its unique coloration and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
