Genista murcica

Genista murcica, commonly known as the Murcian broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through root nodules. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista murcica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
