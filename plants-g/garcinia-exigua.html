Garcinia exigua

Garcinia exigua, commonly known as the dwarf garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 5 meters and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, aromatic aril.

Garcinia exigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
