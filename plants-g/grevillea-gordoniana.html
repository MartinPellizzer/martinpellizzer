Grevillea gordoniana

Grevillea gordoniana, commonly known as the Gordon's grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and vibrant, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea gordoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
