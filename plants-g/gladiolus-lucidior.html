Gladiolus lucidior

Gladiolus lucidior, commonly known as the shining gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in a variety of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The flowers are typically arranged in a spike, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals that are often brightly colored.

This species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
