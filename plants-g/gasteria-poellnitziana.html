Gasteria poellnitziana

Gasteria poellnitziana, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Gasteria poellnitziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
