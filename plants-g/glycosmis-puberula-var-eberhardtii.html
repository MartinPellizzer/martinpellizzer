Glycosmis puberula var. eberhardtii

Glycosmis puberula var. eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's date-mahoe, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small dates. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
