Grevillea masonii

Grevillea masonii, commonly known as the Mason's waratah, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It features showy, red or pink flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun conditions.

Grevillea masonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
