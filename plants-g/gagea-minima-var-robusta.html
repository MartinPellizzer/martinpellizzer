Gagea minima var. robusta

Gagea minima var. robusta, commonly known as the robust winter aconite, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
