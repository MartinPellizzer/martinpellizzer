Grevillea halmaturina subsp. laevis

Grevillea halmaturina subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth grevillea, is a shrub native to South Australia. It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are smooth and slightly fleshy, adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
