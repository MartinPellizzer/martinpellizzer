Guibourtia dinklagei

Guibourtia dinklagei, commonly known as the African teak or dinklage's guibourtia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in furniture and construction.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
