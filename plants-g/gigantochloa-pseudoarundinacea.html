Gigantochloa pseudoarundinacea

Gigantochloa pseudoarundinacea, commonly known as giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its strong, flexible stalks, making it useful in construction and crafts.
