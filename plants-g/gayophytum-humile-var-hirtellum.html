Gayophytum humile var. hirtellum

Gayophytum humile var. hirtellum, commonly known as the woolly gayophytum, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
