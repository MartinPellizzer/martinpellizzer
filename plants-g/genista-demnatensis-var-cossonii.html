Genista demnatensis var. cossonii

Genista demnatensis var. cossonii, commonly known as the Moroccan broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically dense and evergreen, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.
