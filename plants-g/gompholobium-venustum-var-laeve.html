Gompholobium venustum var. laeve

Gompholobium venustum var. laeve, commonly known as the smooth-flowered glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
