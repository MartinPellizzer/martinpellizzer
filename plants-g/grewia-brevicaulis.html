Grewia brevicaulis

Grewia brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grewia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
