Gymnocalycium ponomarevae

Gymnocalycium ponomarevae, commonly known as the Ponomarev's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
