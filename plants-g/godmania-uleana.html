Godmania uleana

Godmania uleana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
