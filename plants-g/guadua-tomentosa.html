Guadua tomentosa

Guadua tomentosa, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Andropogoneae tribe within the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has a dense, fibrous foliage that provides habitat for various insects and birds. Its lightweight yet durable structure makes it valuable for construction and handicrafts.

Guadua tomentosa is also noted for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and carbon sequestration.
