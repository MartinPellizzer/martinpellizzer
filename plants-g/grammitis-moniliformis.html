Grammitis moniliformis

Grammitis moniliformis, commonly known as the beaded fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant's leaves are typically arranged in a rosette-like pattern, with each leaflet appearing as a small, bead-like structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
