Gigantochloa scortechinii

Gigantochloa scortechinii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color and a fibrous texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Gigantochloa scortechinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts.
