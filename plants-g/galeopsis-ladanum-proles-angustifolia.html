Galeopsis ladanum proles angustifolia

Galeopsis ladanum proles angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a serrated margin, and the plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
