Ganophyllum giganteum

Ganophyllum giganteum, commonly known as the giant ganophyllum, is a rare and striking member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, umbel-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
