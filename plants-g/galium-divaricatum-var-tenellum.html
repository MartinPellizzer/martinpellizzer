Galium divaricatum var. tenellum

Galium divaricatum var. tenellum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, prostrate stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to form dense mats in suitable habitats.
