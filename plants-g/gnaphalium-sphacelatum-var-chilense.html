Gnaphalium sphacelatum var. chilense

Gnaphalium sphacelatum var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, fluffy flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its feathery appearance and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
