Grias megacarpa

Grias megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, woody structure and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit, which is large and fleshy, contains seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

The botanical features of Grias megacarpa make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
