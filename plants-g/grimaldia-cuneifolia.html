Grimaldia cuneifolia

Grimaldia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grimaldia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months.

Grimaldia cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
