Gentianella amarella subsp. druceana

Gentianella amarella subsp.

druceana, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
