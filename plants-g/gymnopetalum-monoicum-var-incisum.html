Gymnopetalum monoicum var. incisum

Gymnopetalum monoicum var. incisum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate, while its roots absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
