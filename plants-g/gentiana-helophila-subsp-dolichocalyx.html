Gentiana helophila subsp. dolichocalyx

Gentiana helophila subsp.

dolichocalyx, commonly known as the long-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, often shaded habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
