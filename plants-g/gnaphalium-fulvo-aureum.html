Gnaphalium fulvo-aureum

Gnaphalium fulvo-aureum, commonly known as the golden cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, dandelion-like flowers that attract various pollinators.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
