Gastrochilus longiflorus

Gastrochilus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered gastrochilus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of long, narrow, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive labellum. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
