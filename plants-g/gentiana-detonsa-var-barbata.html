Gentiana detonsa var. barbata

Gentiana detonsa var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
