Gladiolus ambiguus

Gladiolus ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Gladiolus ambiguus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
