Geissorhiza stenosiphon

Geissorhiza stenosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, narrow siphon tube at the base of each blossom. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
