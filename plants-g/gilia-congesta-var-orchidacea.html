Gilia congesta var. orchidacea

Gilia congesta var. orchidacea, commonly known as the orchid-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble orchids, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
