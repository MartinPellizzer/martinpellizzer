Glyceria distans subsp. pseudodistans

Glyceria distans subsp.

pseudodistans, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family. It is native to parts of Asia and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its distinct morphology, including its slender stems and reduced leaf sheaths.

This subspecies is often used in ecological studies due to its role in wetland ecosystems.
