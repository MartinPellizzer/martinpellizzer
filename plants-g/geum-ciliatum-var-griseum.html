Geum ciliatum var. griseum

Geum ciliatum var. griseum, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, silvery-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins and are covered in soft, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
