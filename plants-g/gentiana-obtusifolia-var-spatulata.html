Gentiana obtusifolia var. spatulata

Gentiana obtusifolia var. spatulata, commonly known as the spoon-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad and spatulate, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
