Gymnema pleiadenium

Gymnema pleiadenium, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvet leaf, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema pleiadenium is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
