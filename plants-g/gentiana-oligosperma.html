Gentiana oligosperma

Gentiana oligosperma, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana oligosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
