Gomphocarpus insignis

Gomphocarpus insignis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Gomphocarpus insignis is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
