Grindelia mendocina var. mendocina

Grindelia mendocina var. mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of California.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with a thick layer of fine, white hairs.

This species is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and well-drained soils.
