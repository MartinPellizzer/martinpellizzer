Gentiana vandellioides var. biloba

Gentiana vandellioides var. biloba, commonly known as the bilobed gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with two lobes, giving it its varietal name. Native to the high elevations of the Alps, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late summer to early autumn, contributing to the alpine flora's biodiversity.
