Grossularia echinella

Grossularia echinella, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellow to orange berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments, and is adapted to cold climates. Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
