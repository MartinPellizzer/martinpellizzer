Grevillea pinaster var. hirtella

Grevillea pinaster var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its distinctive, recurved, red or orange flowers that appear in spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds and insects.
