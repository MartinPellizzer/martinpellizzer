Gomphrena schultesia

Gomphrena schultesia, commonly known as the red globe thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that are typically red or pink in color, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered together, forming a dense, spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena schultesia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
