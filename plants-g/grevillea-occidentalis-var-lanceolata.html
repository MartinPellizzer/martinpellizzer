Grevillea occidentalis var. lanceolata

Grevillea occidentalis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Western coast grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which appear throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
