Galium angustifolium var. foliosum

Galium angustifolium var. foliosum, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in moist, temperate regions of the United States and Canada.
