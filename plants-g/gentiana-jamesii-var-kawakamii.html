Gentiana jamesii var. kawakamii

Gentiana jamesii var. kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakamii gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and sturdy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
