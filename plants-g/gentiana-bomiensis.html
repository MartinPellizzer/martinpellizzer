Gentiana bomiensis

Gentiana bomiensis, commonly known as the Bomi gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
