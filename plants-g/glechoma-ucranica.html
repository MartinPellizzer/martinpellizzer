Glechoma ucranica

Glechoma ucranica, commonly known as Ukrainian ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Eastern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma ucranica is known for its creeping growth habit and ability to spread rapidly through stolons.
