Gurania breviflora var. subintegrifolia

Gurania breviflora var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gurania, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, slightly wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
