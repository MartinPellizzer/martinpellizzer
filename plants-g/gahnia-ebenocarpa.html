Gahnia ebenocarpa

Gahnia ebenocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruit tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant produces dark, leathery fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation across various habitats. Gahnia ebenocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
