Geracium parviflorum

Geracium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Geracium parviflorum is often found in rocky habitats and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
