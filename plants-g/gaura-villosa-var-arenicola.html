Gaura villosa var. arenicola

Gaura villosa var. arenicola, commonly known as sand gaura, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of delicate, pink to white, spider-like flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include showy inflorescences and a fibrous root system adapted to sandy or rocky soils.
