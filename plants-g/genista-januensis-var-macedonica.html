Genista januensis var. macedonica

Genista januensis var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
