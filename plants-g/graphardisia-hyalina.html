Graphardisia hyalina

Graphardisia hyalina, commonly known as the glassy lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the lichenized fungi group.

It forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungal partner and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. The plant's structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that adheres tightly to its substrate, often found on rocks or tree bark. It is characterized by its translucent, glassy appearance, which gives it its common name.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Graphardisia hyalina is often found in moist, shaded environments.
