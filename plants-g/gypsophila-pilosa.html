Gypsophila pilosa

Gypsophila pilosa, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy appearance with numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss, and it produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

Gypsophila pilosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
