Galium pilosum var. puncticulosum

Galium pilosum var. puncticulosum, commonly known as the punctulate bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that are typically prostrate or ascending. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
