Grastidium polycladium

Grastidium polycladium, commonly known as the clustered grass-of-Parnassus, is a unique succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its compact growth habit and ability to store water in its leaves make it well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Grastidium polycladium is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
