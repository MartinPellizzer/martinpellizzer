Galium maritimum var. laxiflorum

Galium maritimum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the coastal bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salty, windy coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
