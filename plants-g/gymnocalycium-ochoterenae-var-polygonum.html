Gymnocalycium ochoterenae var. polygonum

Gymnocalycium ochoterenae var. polygonum, commonly known as the Ochoterenae Cactus, is a species native to arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny, ribbed structure that helps it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, enabling it to survive in low rainfall conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in cactus gardens.
