Gonopterodendron arboreum

Gonopterodendron arboreum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable botanical specimen in its native habitat.
