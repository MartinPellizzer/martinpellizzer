Grindelia acerosa

Grindelia acerosa, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Grindelia acerosa is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
