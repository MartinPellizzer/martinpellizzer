Gastrolobium leakeanum

Gastrolobium leakeanum, commonly known as the Leakean wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are phyllodes, which are flattened leaf stalks that perform the function of true leaves.

Gastrolobium leakeanum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
