Guioa hirsuta

Guioa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, spherical fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability to various soil types.
