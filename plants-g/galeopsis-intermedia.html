Galeopsis intermedia

Galeopsis intermedia, commonly known as common hemlock, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and fields.

Galeopsis intermedia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
