Gnaphalium apricum

Gnaphalium apricum, commonly known as the apricot everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.

Gnaphalium apricum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
