Geranium favosum

Geranium favosum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are usually opposite, serrated, and covered in fine hairs. The flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
