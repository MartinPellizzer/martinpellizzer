Gaertnera grisea

Gaertnera grisea, commonly known as the grayleaf ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are leathery and emit a mild, aromatic scent when crushed.

Gaertnera grisea is adapted to humid, shady environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
