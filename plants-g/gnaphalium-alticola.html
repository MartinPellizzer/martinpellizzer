Gnaphalium alticola

Gnaphalium alticola, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, fluffy white to pale yellow flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Gnaphalium alticola is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
