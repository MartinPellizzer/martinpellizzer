Gothofreda monantha

Gothofreda monantha, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that appear to glow in low light conditions. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a delicate, almost ethereal appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
