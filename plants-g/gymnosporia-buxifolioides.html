Gymnosporia buxifolioides

Gymnosporia buxifolioides, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, cone-like structures called gynostegium, which contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
