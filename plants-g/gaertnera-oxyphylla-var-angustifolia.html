Gaertnera oxyphylla var. angustifolia

Gaertnera oxyphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gaertnera, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
