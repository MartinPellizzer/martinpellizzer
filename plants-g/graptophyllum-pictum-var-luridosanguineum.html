Graptophyllum pictum var. luridosanguineum

Graptophyllum pictum var. luridosanguineum, commonly known as the red-flowered ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves that resemble spectral forms. The plant produces vivid red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
