Gnaphalium planchonii

Gnaphalium planchonii, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, fluffy white flowers that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a low-growing habit with a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
