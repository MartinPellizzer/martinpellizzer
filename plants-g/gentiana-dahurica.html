Gentiana dahurica

Gentiana dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana dahurica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
