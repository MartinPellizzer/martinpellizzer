Gomphrena alba

Gomphrena alba, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, feathery flower heads that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Gomphrena alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
