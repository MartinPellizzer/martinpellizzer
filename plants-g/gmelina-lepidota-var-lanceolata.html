Gmelina lepidota var. lanceolata

Gmelina lepidota var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy on the upper surface. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its fast growth and is used in reforestation and as a source of timber.
