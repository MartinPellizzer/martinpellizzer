Gymnanthes pachystachya var. pubescens

Gymnanthes pachystachya var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bitterball, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spiky fruits that resemble small, hardened balls. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its fruit is toxic when raw but can be used in traditional medicine after proper preparation.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
