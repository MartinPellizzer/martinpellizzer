Gentiana incarnata

Gentiana incarnata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking deep blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Gentiana incarnata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
