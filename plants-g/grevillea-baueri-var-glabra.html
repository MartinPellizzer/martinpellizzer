Grevillea baueri var. glabra

Grevillea baueri var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
