Grewia ribesioides

Grewia ribesioides, commonly known as the African glory bush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, yellow or red berries that are edible for some animals but generally not for humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
