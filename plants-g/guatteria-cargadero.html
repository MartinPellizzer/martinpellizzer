Guatteria cargadero

Guatteria cargadero, commonly known as the Cargadero waxtree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, yellow-orange berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
