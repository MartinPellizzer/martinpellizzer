Goodia pubescens

Goodia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy goodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Goodia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
