Gymnorebutia neocumingii subsp. multispina

Gymnorebutia neocumingii subsp.

multispina, commonly known as the multispined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert landscapes.
