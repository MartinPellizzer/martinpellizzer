Genista ballii

Genista ballii, commonly known as the Ball's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Genista ballii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
