Gomphrena perennis subf. decumbens

Gomphrena perennis subf.

decumbens, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial with erect, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red and violet.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface. Its inflorescences, which are often referred to as "fairy rings," are composed of numerous tiny, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters.

This plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
