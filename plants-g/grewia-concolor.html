Grewia concolor

Grewia concolor, commonly known as the smooth globe thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia concolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
