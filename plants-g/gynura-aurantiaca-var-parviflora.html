Gynura aurantiaca var. parviflora

Gynura aurantiaca var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, often with a reddish or purplish tint.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
