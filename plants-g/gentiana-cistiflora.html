Gentiana cistiflora

Gentiana cistiflora, commonly known as the bladder gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana cistiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
