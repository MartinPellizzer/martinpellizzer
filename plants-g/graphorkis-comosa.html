Graphorkis comosa

Graphorkis comosa, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
