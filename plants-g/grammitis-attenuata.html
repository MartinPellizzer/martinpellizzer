Grammitis attenuata

Grammitis attenuata, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often slightly curled at the tips. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its sporangia are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its lush, green foliage.
