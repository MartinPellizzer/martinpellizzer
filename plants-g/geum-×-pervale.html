Geum × pervale

Geum × pervale, commonly known as the "Bog Geranium," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, adapting to moist environments. It thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
