Gleichenia angustiloba

Gleichenia angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant is native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including Australia, New Zealand, and parts of South America. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, finely divided appearance.

Gleichenia angustiloba thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
