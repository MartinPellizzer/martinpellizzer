Gentiana spathulifolia

Gentiana spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture, and the flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Gentiana spathulifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
