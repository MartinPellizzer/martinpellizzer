Gypsophila erinacea

Gypsophila erinacea, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has slender, branched stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and abundance of tiny blooms.
