Guettarda undulata

Guettarda undulata, commonly known as the zigzag guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowlands, often forming dense thickets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly wavy margin, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
