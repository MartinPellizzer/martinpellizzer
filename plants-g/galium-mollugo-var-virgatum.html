Galium mollugo var. virgatum

Galium mollugo var. virgatum, commonly known as silverweed or white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium mollugo var. virgatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
