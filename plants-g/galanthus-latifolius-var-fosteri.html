Galanthus latifolius var. fosteri

Galanthus latifolius var. fosteri, commonly known as Foster's snowdrop, is a variant of the snowdrop genus native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and delicate, white flowers with a greenish hue at the base. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist, well-drained soils, preferring partial to full shade. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves of many trees have fully emerged.

This variety is noted for its horticultural value and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental beauty.
