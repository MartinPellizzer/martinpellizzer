Gynura annamensis

Gynura annamensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese evergreen, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite inflorescences, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Gynura annamensis is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
