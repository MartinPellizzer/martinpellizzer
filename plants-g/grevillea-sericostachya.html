Grevillea sericostachya

Grevillea sericostachya, commonly known as the silky-stemmed waratah, is a striking shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and conservation areas.

Grevillea sericostachya plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly honeyeaters and other nectar-feeding birds.
