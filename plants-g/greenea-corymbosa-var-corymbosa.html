Greenea corymbosa var. corymbosa

Greenea corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant features silvery-gray foliage with finely textured leaves, which help it thrive in dry, rocky environments. It is often found in high-altitude regions and is well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

The flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
