Gilibertia chevalieri

Gilibertia chevalieri, commonly known as the red-rooted panic grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Gilibertia chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
