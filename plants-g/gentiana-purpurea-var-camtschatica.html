Gentiana purpurea var. camtschatica

Gentiana purpurea var. camtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
