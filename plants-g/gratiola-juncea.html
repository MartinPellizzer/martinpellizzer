Gratiola juncea

Gratiola juncea, commonly known as slender false hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the early spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Gratiola juncea is characterized by its low-growing habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
