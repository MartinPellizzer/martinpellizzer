Gagea minuta

Gagea minuta, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gagea minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
