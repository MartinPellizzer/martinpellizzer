Gravesia pulchra var. glandulosa

Gravesia pulchra var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a woody stem, making it an attractive ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
