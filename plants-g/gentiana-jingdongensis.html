Gentiana jingdongensis

Gentiana jingdongensis, commonly known as the Jingdong gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations around 2,000 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the showy flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
