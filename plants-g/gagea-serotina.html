Gagea serotina

Gagea serotina, commonly known as late-flowering gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Gagea serotina is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
