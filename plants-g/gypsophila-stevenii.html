Gypsophila stevenii

Gypsophila stevenii, commonly known as the desert bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the carnation family, Caryophyllaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting appearance.

Gypsophila stevenii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
