Gorteria pavonia

Gorteria pavonia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gorteria pavonia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
