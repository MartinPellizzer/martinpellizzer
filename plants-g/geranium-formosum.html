Geranium formosum

Geranium formosum, commonly known as the Formosa geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a textured surface, while the flowers range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
