Gravesia torrentium

Gravesia torrentium, commonly known as the torrent gravelia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and streamside habitats, thriving in humid and cool environments. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Gravesia torrentium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
