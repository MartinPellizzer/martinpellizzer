Gymnotrix glaucifolia

Gymnotrix glaucifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
