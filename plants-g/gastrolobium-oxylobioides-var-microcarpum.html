Gastrolobium oxylobioides var. microcarpum

Gastrolobium oxylobioides var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
