Globularia linnaei

Globularia linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense, compact clusters of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Globularia linnaei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
