Gunnera apiculata

Gunnera apiculata, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant produces showy, spiky flower clusters in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty landscapes.
