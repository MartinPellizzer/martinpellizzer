Gentiana × bergeri

Gentiana × bergeri, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Gentiana × bergeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
