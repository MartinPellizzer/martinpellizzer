Graphiosa inconspicua

Graphiosa inconspicua, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is adapted to thrive in dry environments with minimal water. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
