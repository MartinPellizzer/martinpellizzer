Grewia lacei

Grewia lacei, commonly known as the velvetleaf Grewia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Grewia lacei thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
