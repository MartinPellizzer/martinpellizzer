Geraniospermum articulatum

Geraniospermum articulatum, commonly known as the articulated geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique, segmented stems give it a distinctive appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.
