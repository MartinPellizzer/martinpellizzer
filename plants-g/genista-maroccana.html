Genista maroccana

Genista maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Genista maroccana is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
