Geranium asphodeloides subsp. crenophilum

Geranium asphodeloides subsp.

crenophilum, commonly known as the Alpine geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often at higher altitudes.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
