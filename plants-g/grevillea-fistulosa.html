Grevillea fistulosa

Grevillea fistulosa, commonly known as the red-gum grevillea, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Grevillea fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
