Grammitis acuminata

Grammitis acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often arching or drooping in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a small, kidney-shaped indusium.

Grammitis acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic qualities.
