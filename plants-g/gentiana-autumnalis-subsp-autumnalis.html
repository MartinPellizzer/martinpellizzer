Gentiana autumnalis subsp. autumnalis

Gentiana autumnalis subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
