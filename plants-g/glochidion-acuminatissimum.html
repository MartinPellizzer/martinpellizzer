Glochidion acuminatissimum

Glochidion acuminatissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by greenish or reddish fruits. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
