Geranium pratense f. albiflorum

Geranium pratense f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered cranesbill, is a horticultural form of the common cranesbill.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lobed leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade. It produces small, rounded berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Geranium pratense f. albiflorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
