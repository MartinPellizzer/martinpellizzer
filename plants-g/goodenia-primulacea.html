Goodenia primulacea

Goodenia primulacea, commonly known as the fairy bells, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Goodenia primulacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in ecological studies.
