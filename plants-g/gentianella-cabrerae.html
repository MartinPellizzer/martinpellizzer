Gentianella cabrerae

Gentianella cabrerae, commonly known as the Canary Islands gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, coastal areas. It is a perennial herb with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
