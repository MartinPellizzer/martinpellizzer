Gentiana campanuliformis

Gentiana campanuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically bell-shaped and arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a lanceolate shape and a slightly hairy surface.

Gentiana campanuliformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
