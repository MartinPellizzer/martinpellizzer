Gilia superba

Gilia superba, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple and white. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky areas, where it thrives with minimal water. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Gilia superba is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
