Gomphia grandiflora

Gomphia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant gomphia, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to reach across water surfaces. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in water.

Gomphia grandiflora plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
