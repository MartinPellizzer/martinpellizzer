Gladiolus inclinatus

Gladiolus inclinatus, commonly known as the leaning gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, ranging from red to purple, and are arranged in a zigzag pattern along the stem. This species is known for its unique growth habit, where the stems often lean or bend, giving it its common name.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
