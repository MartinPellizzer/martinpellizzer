Grevillea linsmithii

Grevillea linsmithii, commonly known as the Linsmith's waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red, woody flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea linsmithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
