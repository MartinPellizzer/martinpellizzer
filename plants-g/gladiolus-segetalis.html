Gladiolus segetalis

Gladiolus segetalis, commonly known as the grain gladiolus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in agricultural areas where it can become a weed. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dry capsules as fruits.

Gladiolus segetalis is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
