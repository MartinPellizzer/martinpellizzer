Greslania circinata

Greslania circinata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Greslania circinata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
