Gomphocarpus harveyanus

Gomphocarpus harveyanus, commonly known as the Harvey's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Gomphocarpus.
