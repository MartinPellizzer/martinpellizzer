Grammitis attenuatissima

Grammitis attenuatissima, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often form dense mats on tree trunks or branches. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Grammitis attenuatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
