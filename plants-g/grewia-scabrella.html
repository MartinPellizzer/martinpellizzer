Grewia scabrella

Grewia scabrella, commonly known as the roughleaf globemallow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia scabrella is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant once established.
