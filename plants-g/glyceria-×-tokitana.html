Glyceria × tokitana

Glyceria × tokitana, commonly known as the Japanese flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. This species is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a common component of riparian vegetation.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
