Grastidium dionaeoides

Grastidium dionaeoides, commonly known as the Dionaeoides ghost plant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, a feature that contributes to its striking visual appeal. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and requires specific humidity and light conditions to thrive. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native environment.
