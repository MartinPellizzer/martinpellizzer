Gardoquia sericea

Gardoquia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf gardoquia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Gardoquia sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
