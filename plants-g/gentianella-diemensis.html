Gentianella diemensis

Gentianella diemensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian gentian, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and heathlands. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentianella diemensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
