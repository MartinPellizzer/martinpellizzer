Guibourtia chodatiana var. fruticosa

Guibourtia chodatiana var. fruticosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood with a fine, even texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or woodland habitats.
