Gireoudia macrophylla var. discolor

Gireoudia macrophylla var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated giant fern, is a striking fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The variegated leaves display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues, making it a visually appealing plant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, rich soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
