Geonoma pohliana subsp. gastoniana

Geonoma pohliana subsp.

gastoniana, commonly known as the Gaston's bryophyte fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family. It is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid tropical and subtropical environments.

The plant is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds and a complex rhizome system. It typically grows on tree trunks or branches in shaded, moist forest areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed tropical ecosystems.
