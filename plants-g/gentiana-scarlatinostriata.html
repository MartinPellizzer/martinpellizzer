Gentiana scarlatinostriata

Gentiana scarlatinostriata, commonly known as the scarlet-striped gentian, is a striking member of the Gentianaceae family native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers with distinctive red stripes, which are particularly prominent in the lower petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes where it faces cold temperatures and limited soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
