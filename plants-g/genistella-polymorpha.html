Genistella polymorpha

Genistella polymorpha, commonly known as the variable broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody stem with thorn-like branches. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

Genistella polymorpha is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
