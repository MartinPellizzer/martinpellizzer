Gymnopetalum

Gymnopetalum, commonly known as the naked petal orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features showy, petal-like structures that are often brightly colored. These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Gymnopetalum species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of other plants without being parasitic.

Their botanical significance lies in their distinctive morphology and ecological adaptations within their native habitats.
