Gentiana auriculata

Gentiana auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
