Guadua maculosa

Guadua maculosa, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Venezuela, and Ecuador.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, with a distinctive spotted or marbled pattern on its stems. The plant is part of the Poaceae family and is known for its strong and flexible structure, making it valuable for construction and handicrafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional weaving practices.

Guadua maculosa is also appreciated for its ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.
