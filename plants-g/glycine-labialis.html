Glycine labialis

Glycine labialis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in cooking.

Glycine labialis thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
