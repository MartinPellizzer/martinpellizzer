Gnaphalium spathulatum

Gnaphalium spathulatum, commonly known as the spoonleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Gnaphalium spathulatum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
