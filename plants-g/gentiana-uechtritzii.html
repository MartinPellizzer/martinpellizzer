Gentiana uechtritzii

Gentiana uechtritzii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

Gentiana uechtritzii is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
