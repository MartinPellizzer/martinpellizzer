Genista germanica

Genista germanica, commonly known as German broom, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, wiry stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
