Gisekia pharnaceoides var. pedunculata

Gisekia pharnaceoides var. pedunculata, commonly known as the purple gisekia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
