Gentiana × chlorifolia var. macrocalyx

Gentiana × chlorifolia var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered gentian, is a hybrid species within the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
