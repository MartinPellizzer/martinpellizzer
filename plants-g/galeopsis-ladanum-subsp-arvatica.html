Galeopsis ladanum subsp. arvatica

Galeopsis ladanum subsp.

arvatica, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
