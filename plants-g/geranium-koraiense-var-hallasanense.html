Geranium koraiense var. hallasanense

Geranium koraiense var. hallasanense, commonly known as the Hallasan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces vibrant pink or red blooms in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
