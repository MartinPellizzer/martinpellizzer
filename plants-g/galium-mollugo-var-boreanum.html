Galium mollugo var. boreanum

Galium mollugo var. boreanum, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium mollugo var. boreanum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
