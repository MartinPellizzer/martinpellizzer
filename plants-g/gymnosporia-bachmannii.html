Gymnosporia bachmannii

Gymnosporia bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's burr, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces woody, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Gymnosporia bachmannii is adapted to withstand drought and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
