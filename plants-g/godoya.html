Godoya

Godoya, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the genus Tithonia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.
