Gnaphalium fulvum

Gnaphalium fulvum, commonly known as the golden cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Gnaphalium fulvum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
