Gentianella calcis subsp. manahune

Gentianella calcis subsp.

manahune, commonly known as the Manahune gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Gentianaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
