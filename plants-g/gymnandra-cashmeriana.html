Gymnandra cashmeriana

Gymnandra cashmeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. Gymnandra cashmeriana thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a notable species in its native habitat.
