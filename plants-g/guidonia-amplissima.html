Guidonia amplissima

Guidonia amplissima, commonly known as the giant poinciana, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces vibrant red and orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
