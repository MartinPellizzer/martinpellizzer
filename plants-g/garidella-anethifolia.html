Garidella anethifolia

Garidella anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved garidella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, anise-scented leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its purported digestive and respiratory benefits.

Garidella anethifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant properties.
