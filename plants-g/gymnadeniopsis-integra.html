Gymnadeniopsis integra

Gymnadeniopsis integra, commonly known as the smoothleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

Gymnadeniopsis integra is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
