Geranium santacruzense

Geranium santacruzense, commonly known as Santa Cruz geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
