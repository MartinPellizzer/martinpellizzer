Gymnocalycium neuhuberi

Gymnocalycium neuhuberi, commonly known as the Neuhuber's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and bright, colorful flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it produces small, rounded fruits.

Gymnocalycium neuhuberi is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
