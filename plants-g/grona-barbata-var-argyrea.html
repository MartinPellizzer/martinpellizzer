Grona barbata var. argyrea

Grona barbata var. argyrea, commonly known as the silver-haired grona, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a silvery pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with moderate sunlight.
