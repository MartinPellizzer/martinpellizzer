Gymnosporia senegalensis var. inermis

Gymnosporia senegalensis var. inermis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
