Gaultheria benguetensis

Gaultheria benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are rich in aromatic compounds, including methyl salicylate. It thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in montane forests at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
