Geraniospermum zonale

Geraniospermum zonale, commonly known as the Zonale Geraniospermum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is often found in coastal or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
