Gentiana anthosphaera

Gentiana anthosphaera, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers. Gentiana anthosphaera thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
