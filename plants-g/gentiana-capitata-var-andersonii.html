Gentiana capitata var. andersonii

Gentiana capitata var. andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of down.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
