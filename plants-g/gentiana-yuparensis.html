Gentiana yuparensis

Gentiana yuparensis, commonly known as the Yupari gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the showy inflorescences.

Gentiana yuparensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ecological and horticultural significance.
