Geissospermum vellosii

Geissospermum vellosii, commonly known as the Brazilian moonseed, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical ecosystems.
