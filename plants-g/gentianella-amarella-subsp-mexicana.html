Gentianella amarella subsp. mexicana

Gentianella amarella subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
