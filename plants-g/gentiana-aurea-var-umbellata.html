Gentiana aurea var. umbellata

Gentiana aurea var. umbellata, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
