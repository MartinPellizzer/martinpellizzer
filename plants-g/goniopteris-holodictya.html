Goniopteris holodictya

Goniopteris holodictya, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the fern family.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that resemble those of modern-day water ferns. The plant thrived during the Carboniferous and Permian periods, contributing to the formation of coal deposits. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, were essential for its propagation in ancient wetland environments.

Botanists study Goniopteris holodictya to understand the evolution of ferns and paleoecological conditions of the past.
