Grindelia discoidea

Grindelia discoidea, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.

Grindelia discoidea plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
