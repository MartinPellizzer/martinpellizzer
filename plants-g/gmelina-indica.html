Gmelina indica

Gmelina indica, commonly known as the Indian champak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, spicy scent. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Gmelina indica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
