Genista lusitanica subsp. barnadesii

Genista lusitanica subsp.

barnadesii, commonly known as the Barnades' broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, thorny stems are adapted to rocky and dry environments.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
