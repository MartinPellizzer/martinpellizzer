Galeopsis beckii

Galeopsis beckii, commonly known as narrowleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters.

Galeopsis beckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
