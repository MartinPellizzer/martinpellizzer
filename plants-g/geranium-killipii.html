Geranium killipii

Geranium killipii, commonly known as the Killip's geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
