Garayanthus fuscomaculatus

Garayanthus fuscomaculatus, commonly known as the spotted desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive red center. The leaves are succulent, adapted to store water, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Garayanthus fuscomaculatus is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
