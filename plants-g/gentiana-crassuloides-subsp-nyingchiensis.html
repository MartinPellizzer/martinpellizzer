Gentiana crassuloides subsp. nyingchiensis

Gentiana crassuloides subsp.

nyingchiensis, commonly known as the Nyingchi gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months.

The plant thrives in high-altitude meadows and rocky habitats, typically at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
