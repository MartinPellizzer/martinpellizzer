Garcinia cernua

Garcinia cernua, commonly known as the waxy apple or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The fruit, which resembles a tiny apple, is covered in a thick, waxy coating and has a sour, tangy flavor. It is widely used in culinary applications, particularly in Southeast Asian cuisines, for its aromatic properties.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
