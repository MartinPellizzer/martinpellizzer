Gouldia wawrana

Gouldia wawrana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Gouldia wawrana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
