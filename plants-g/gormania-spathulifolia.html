Gormania spathulifolia

Gormania spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed gormania, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
