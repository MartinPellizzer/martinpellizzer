Gomphrena agrestis var. genuina

Gomphrena agrestis var. genuina, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

The flowers are borne in dense, rounded clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and floral arrangements.
