Glossocardia smithii

Glossocardia smithii, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Glossocardia smithii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
