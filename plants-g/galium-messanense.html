Galium messanense

Galium messanense, commonly known as the Messina bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Galium messanense is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
