Galinsoga brachystephana

Galinsoga brachystephana, commonly known as the short-step gallinsoga, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both a weed and a potential invasive species in some regions.
