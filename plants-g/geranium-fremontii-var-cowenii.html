Geranium fremontii var. cowenii

Geranium fremontii var. cowenii, commonly known as Cowen's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
