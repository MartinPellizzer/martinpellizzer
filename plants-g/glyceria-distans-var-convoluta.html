Glyceria distans var. convoluta

Glyceria distans var. convoluta, commonly known as the curled glyceria, is a variant of the grass species Glyceria distans.

It is a perennial herb native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments and is often found in areas with standing water.

Its botanical features make it an indicator species for wetland health and ecological conditions.
