Gaultheria stuebeliana

Gaultheria stuebeliana, commonly known as the Stuebel's wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are red or black and contain aromatic compounds similar to those found in wintergreen oil.

Gaultheria stuebeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses due to its essential oils.
