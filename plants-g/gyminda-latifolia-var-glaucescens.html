Gyminda latifolia var. glaucescens

Gyminda latifolia var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous broadleaf gyminda, is a variant of the gyminda tree native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive bluish-gray waxy coating, known as glaucous. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

The glaucous varietal trait is a key botanical feature, contributing to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in its native habitat.
