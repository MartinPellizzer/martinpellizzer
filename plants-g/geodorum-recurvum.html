Geodorum recurvum

Geodorum recurvum, commonly known as the fragrant ginger orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, sword-shaped leaves and fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

Geodorum recurvum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aromatic properties.
