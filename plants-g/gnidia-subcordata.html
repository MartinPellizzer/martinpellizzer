Gnidia subcordata

Gnidia subcordata, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky areas. The plant features woody stems with a distinctive reddish coloration, and its leaves are usually narrow and leathery. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits.

Gnidia subcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
