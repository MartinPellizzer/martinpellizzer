Geniosporum madagascariense var. oblongifolium

Geniosporum madagascariense var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the Madagascar bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant has lanceolate to oblong leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
