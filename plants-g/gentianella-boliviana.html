Gentianella boliviana

Gentianella boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five to six petals that are often blue or purple in color.

Gentianella boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
