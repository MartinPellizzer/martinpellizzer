Genista tinctoria var. anxantica

Genista tinctoria var. anxantica, commonly known as the crimson broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is shrub-like, with slender stems and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
