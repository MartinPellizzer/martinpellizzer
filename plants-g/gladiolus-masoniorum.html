Gladiolus masoniorum

Gladiolus masoniorum, commonly known as the Mason's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its blooms, which can be single or double, come in a wide range of colors including red, pink, white, and yellow.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
