Glomera sepalosiphon

Glomera sepalosiphon, commonly known as the spathiphyllum orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, spathiform bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple.

The botanical features of Glomera sepalosiphon make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
