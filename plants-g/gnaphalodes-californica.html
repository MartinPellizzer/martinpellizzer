Gnaphalodes californica

Gnaphalodes californica, commonly known as the California ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal and inland regions of California.
