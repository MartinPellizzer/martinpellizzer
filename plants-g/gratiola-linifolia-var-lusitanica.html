Gratiola linifolia var. lusitanica

Gratiola linifolia var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian ladies' fingers, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters, while the leaves are linear and often slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
