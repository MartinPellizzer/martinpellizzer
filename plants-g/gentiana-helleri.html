Gentiana helleri

Gentiana helleri, commonly known as Heller's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Gentiana helleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
