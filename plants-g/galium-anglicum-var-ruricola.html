Galium anglicum var. ruricola

Galium anglicum var. ruricola, commonly known as field bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that branch near the base and produce small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

The plant is known for its aromatic scent and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
