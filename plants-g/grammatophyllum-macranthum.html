Grammatophyllum macranthum

Grammatophyllum macranthum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous fragrant, spider-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex morphology, including a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a dark central stripe, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is often found in protected areas and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
