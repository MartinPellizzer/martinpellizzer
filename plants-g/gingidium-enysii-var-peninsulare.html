Gingidium enysii var. peninsulare

Gingidium enysii var. peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular gingidium, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the southern regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring season. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it resilient in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
