Guadua aculeata

Guadua aculeata, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly rough texture.

Guadua aculeata is widely used in construction and handicrafts due to its durability and flexibility.
