Gomphrena glabra

Gomphrena glabra, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces feathery, bract-like structures that give it a distinctive, almost plume-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena glabra is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and ornamental value.
