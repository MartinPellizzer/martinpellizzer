Gymnocalycium gertii

Gymnocalycium gertii, commonly known as the Gertii cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in the spring. Gymnocalycium gertii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

It is often cultivated as a decorative succulent due to its unique appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
