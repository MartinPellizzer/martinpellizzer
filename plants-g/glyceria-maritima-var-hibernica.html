Glyceria maritima var. hibernica

Glyceria maritima var. hibernica, commonly known as the Irish watergrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and fluctuating water levels.

Its ecological importance lies in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
