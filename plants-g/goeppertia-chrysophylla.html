Goeppertia chrysophylla

Goeppertia chrysophylla, commonly known as the Golden Leaf Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and white, creating a visually appealing pattern. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and thrives in high humidity environments. It has rhizomatous growth and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not typically displayed.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
