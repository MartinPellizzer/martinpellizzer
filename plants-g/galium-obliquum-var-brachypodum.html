Galium obliquum var. brachypodum

Galium obliquum var. brachypodum, commonly known as dwarf wood-sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
