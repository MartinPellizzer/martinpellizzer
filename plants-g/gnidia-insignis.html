Gnidia insignis

Gnidia insignis, commonly known as the Natal wild bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Gnidia insignis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
