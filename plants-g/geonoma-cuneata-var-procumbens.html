Geonoma cuneata var. procumbens

Geonoma cuneata var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sword fern, is a tropical fern species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spreading, rhizomatous growth habit, with long, narrow, leathery fronds that often lie close to the ground. The fronds are typically dark green and have a serrated or toothed margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded garden settings.
