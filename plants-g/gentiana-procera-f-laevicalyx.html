Gentiana procera f. laevicalyx

Gentiana procera f. laevicalyx, commonly known as the smooth-leaved gentian, is a variant of the gentian plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, distinguishing it from other gentian species.

The botanical interest in this plant lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
