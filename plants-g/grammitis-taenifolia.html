Grammitis taenifolia

Grammitis taenifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender fronds that are typically arranged in a dense, compact rosette. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure that gives it a lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis taenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
