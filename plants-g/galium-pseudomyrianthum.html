Galium pseudomyrianthum

Galium pseudomyrianthum, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive silver or grayish underside due to the presence of fine hairs.

Galium pseudomyrianthum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
