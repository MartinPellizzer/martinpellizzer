Goodenia saccata

Goodenia saccata, commonly known as the bottlebrush goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Goodenia saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
