Gaura filiformis var. typica

Gaura filiformis var. typica, commonly known as the slender gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, wand-like stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. It is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
