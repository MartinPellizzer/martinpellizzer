Goodenia heenanii

Goodenia heenanii, commonly known as the Henna Goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, and it produces small, seed-like fruits known as follicles.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
