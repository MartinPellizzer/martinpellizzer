Gentianella propinqua subsp. arctophila

Gentianella propinqua subsp.

arctophila, commonly known as the Arctic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and alpine habitats, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
