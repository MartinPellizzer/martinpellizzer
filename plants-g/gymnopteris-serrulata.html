Gymnopteris serrulata

Gymnopteris serrulata, commonly known as the serrulate holly fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with finely serrated margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Gymnopteris serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
