Genista hirsuta var. atlantica

Genista hirsuta var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to harsh maritime conditions, making it resilient to salt spray and strong winds.
