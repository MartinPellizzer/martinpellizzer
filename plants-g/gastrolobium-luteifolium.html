Gastrolobium luteifolium

Gastrolobium luteifolium, commonly known as the yellow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Gastrolobium luteifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
