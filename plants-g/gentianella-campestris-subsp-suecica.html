Gentianella campestris subsp. suecica

Gentianella campestris subsp.

suecica, commonly known as the Swedish gentian, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and wetlands.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller flowers and shorter pedicels compared to other gentian species.
