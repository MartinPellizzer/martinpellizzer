Gentiana arctophila

Gentiana arctophila, commonly known as the Arctic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in areas with harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from cold temperatures.

Gentiana arctophila is a perennial herb that blooms during the short summer season in its native habitat.
