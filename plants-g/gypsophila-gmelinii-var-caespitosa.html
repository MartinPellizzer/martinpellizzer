Gypsophila gmelinii var. caespitosa

Gypsophila gmelinii var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted baby's breath, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and produces numerous small, white or pale pink flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice in floral arrangements and garden landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
