Gymnocalycium moserianum

Gymnocalycium moserianum, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, with a swollen, colorful body known as the "pseudostem" and a small, spiny stem at the base. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the growing season. It is a succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium moserianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
