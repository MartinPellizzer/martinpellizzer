Gladiolus marginatus

Gladiolus marginatus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces clusters of blooms at the top of the stem. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern, and the flowers have three petals and three sepals.

Gladiolus marginatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to different growing conditions.
