Ginkgo biloba var. triloba

Ginkgo biloba var. triloba, commonly known as the tri-lobed ginkgo, is a variant of the ginkgo tree characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, featuring fan-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in autumn. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves seasonally, and is native to China. It is also noted for its resilience to urban conditions, making it a popular choice for street trees.

The tree has a long history in traditional medicine and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
