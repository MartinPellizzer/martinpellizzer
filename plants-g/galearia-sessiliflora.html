Galearia sessiliflora

Galearia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Galearia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
