Glycyrrhiza laevis

Glycyrrhiza laevis, commonly known as the smooth licorice plant, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound with potential medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza laevis thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic uses.
