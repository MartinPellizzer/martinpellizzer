Glycyrrhiza uralensis

Glycyrrhiza uralensis, commonly known as licorice, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and showy, purple flowers. The plant is widely cultivated for its sweet-tasting root, which contains glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its intense flavor. Glycyrrhiza uralensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
