Grindelia rubricaulis f. pubescens

Grindelia rubricaulis f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bitterroot, is a variant of the bitterroot plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its bitter properties.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.
