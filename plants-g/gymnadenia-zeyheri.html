Gymnadenia zeyheri

Gymnadenia zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's grasspinks, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its resemblance to grass species.

Gymnadenia zeyheri is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
