Gnaphalium saturejoides var. ventanensis

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. ventanensis, commonly known as the Mexican everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
