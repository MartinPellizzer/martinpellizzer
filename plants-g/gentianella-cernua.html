Gentianella cernua

Gentianella cernua, commonly known as the drooping gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Gentianella cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
