Geum triflorum var. campanulatum

Geum triflorum var. campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped bronze poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that add texture to the landscape.
