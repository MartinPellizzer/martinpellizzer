Gardenia ternifolia var. jovis-tonantis

Gardenia ternifolia var. jovis-tonantis, commonly known as the Jovis-tonantis gardenia, is a rare and striking variant of the gardenia genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers emit a strong, sweet scent, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil, making it a sought-after plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in controlled horticultural settings.
