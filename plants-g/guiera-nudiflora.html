Guiera nudiflora

Guiera nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower guiera, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant plays a role in soil stabilization.

Guiera nudiflora is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
