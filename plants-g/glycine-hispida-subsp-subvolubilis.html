Glycine hispida subsp. subvolubilis

Glycine hispida subsp.

subvolubilis, commonly known as the hairy pinto bean, is a climbing legume native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to different soil types and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.
