Geraniospermum × jatrophifolium

Geraniospermum × jatrophifolium, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a flowering plant species that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Geraniospermum and Jatropha.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Geraniospermum × jatrophifolium is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
