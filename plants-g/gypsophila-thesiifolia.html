Gypsophila thesiifolia

Gypsophila thesiifolia, commonly known as the Armenian baby's-breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It prefers full sun and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gypsophila thesiifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
