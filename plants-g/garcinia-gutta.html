Garcinia gutta

Garcinia gutta, commonly known as the gutta-percha tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for producing a latex sap, which is used in various industrial applications. Its fruit, though not commonly consumed by humans, contains seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia gutta thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
