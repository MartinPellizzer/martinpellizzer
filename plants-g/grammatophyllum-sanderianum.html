Grammatophyllum sanderianum

Grammatophyllum sanderianum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spiral pattern, often with bright yellow and green hues.

Grammatophyllum sanderianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
