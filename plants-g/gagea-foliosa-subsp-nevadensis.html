Gagea foliosa subsp. nevadensis

Gagea foliosa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves and smaller flowers compared to other subspecies of Gagea foliosa.
