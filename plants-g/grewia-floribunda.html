Grewia floribunda

Grewia floribunda, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Grewia floribunda is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
