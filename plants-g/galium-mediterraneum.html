Galium mediterraneum

Galium mediterraneum, commonly known as Mediterranean bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Galium mediterraneum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
