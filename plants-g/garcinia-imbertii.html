Garcinia imbertii

Garcinia imbertii, commonly known as the Congo butter fruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds. The fruit's pericarp contains high levels of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The plant's ornamental value and medicinal potential make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
