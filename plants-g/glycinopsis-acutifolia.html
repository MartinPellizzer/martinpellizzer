Glycinopsis acutifolia

Glycinopsis acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing pods that contain several seeds.

Glycinopsis acutifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
