Gymnadenia odoratissima subsp. longicalcarata

Gymnadenia odoratissima subsp.

longicalcarata, commonly known as the long-spurred gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, spurred petals and robust, often hairy foliage. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
