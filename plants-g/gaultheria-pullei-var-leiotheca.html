Gaultheria pullei var. leiotheca

Gaultheria pullei var. leiotheca, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
