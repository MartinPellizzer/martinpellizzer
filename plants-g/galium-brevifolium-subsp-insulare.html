Galium brevifolium subsp. insulare

Galium brevifolium subsp.

insulare, commonly known as the island bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually rounded or heart-shaped, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
