Godoya obovata

Godoya obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf godoya, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
