Galium incurvum

Galium incurvum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Galium incurvum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
