Garcinia oligophlebia

Garcinia oligophlebia, commonly known as the Khasi Garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly in the Khasi Hills.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature.

Garcinia oligophlebia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
