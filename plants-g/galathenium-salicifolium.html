Galathenium salicifolium

Galathenium salicifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Galathenium salicifolium is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
