Galium californicum var. texanum

Galium californicum var. texanum, commonly known as Texas bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
