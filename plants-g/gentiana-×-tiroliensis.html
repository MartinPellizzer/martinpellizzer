Gentiana × tiroliensis

Gentiana × tiroliensis, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often silver-white underneath, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Gentiana × tiroliensis is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
