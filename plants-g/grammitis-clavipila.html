Grammitis clavipila

Grammitis clavipila, commonly known as the club-club moss, is a small evergreen fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright green, strap-like leaves that are often clustered closely together. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are short and often form dense mats on the forest floor.

Grammitis clavipila is noted for its intricate, lace-like fronds and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
