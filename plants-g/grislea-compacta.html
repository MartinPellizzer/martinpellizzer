Grislea compacta

Grislea compacta, commonly known as the compact flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Grislea compacta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
