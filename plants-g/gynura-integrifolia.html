Gynura integrifolia

Gynura integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and gardens. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

Gynura integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
