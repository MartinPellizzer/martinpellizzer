Gouldia terminalis f. albicaulis

Gouldia terminalis f. albicaulis, commonly known as the white-veined gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its unique, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The variegated foliage features white veins on a green background, making it highly ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in suitable climates.
