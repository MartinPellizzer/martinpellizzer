Gonzalagunia villosa

Gonzalagunia villosa, commonly known as the silky bush, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of forests. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and covered with a fine, silky layer of hairs.

Gonzalagunia villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
