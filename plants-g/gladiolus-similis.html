Gladiolus similis

Gladiolus similis, commonly known as the common gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are borne on one side of the stem, a feature known as "floral deviation.". The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
