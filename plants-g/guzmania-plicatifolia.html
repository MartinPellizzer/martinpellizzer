Guzmania plicatifolia

Guzmania plicatifolia, commonly known as the pleated guzmania, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can last for several months. The plant features a rosette of narrow, pleated leaves that give it its common name, with the leaves often displaying a striking blue-green color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania plicatifolia is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
