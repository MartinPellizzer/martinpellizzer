Gymnocalycium ferrarii subsp. evae

Gymnocalycium ferrarii subsp.

evae, commonly known as the Eve's ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
