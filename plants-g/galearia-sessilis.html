Galearia sessilis

Galearia sessilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Galearia sessilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to bloom in challenging garden conditions.
