Gouinia ramosa

Gouinia ramosa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Gouinia ramosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
