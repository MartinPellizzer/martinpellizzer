Gymnopyrenium vulgare

Gymnopyrenium vulgare, commonly known as the common gymnopyrenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gymnopyrenium vulgare is notable for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
