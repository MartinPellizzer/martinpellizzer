Glochidion annamense

Glochidion annamense, commonly known as the Annam goosefoot, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
