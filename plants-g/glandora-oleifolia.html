Glandora oleifolia

Glandora oleifolia, commonly known as the oilleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are covered in tiny oil glands, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Glandora oleifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
