Gouldia terminalis f. macrophylla

Gouldia terminalis f. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
