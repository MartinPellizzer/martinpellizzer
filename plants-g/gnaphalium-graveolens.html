Gnaphalium graveolens

Gnaphalium graveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is known for its aromatic, slightly sweet scent and is often used in herbal medicine.

The leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
