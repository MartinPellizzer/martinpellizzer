Garcinia nobilis

Garcinia nobilis, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, acidic pulp, which is rich in bioactive compounds. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
