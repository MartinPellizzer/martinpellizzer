Gentiana crassa

Gentiana crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Gentiana crassa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
