Geranium ranunculoides

Geranium ranunculoides, commonly known as the water geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bright pink to red flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Geranium ranunculoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
