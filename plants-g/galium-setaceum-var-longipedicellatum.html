Galium setaceum var. longipedicellatum

Galium setaceum var. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stalked bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem. The leaves are opposite, linear, and usually have a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
