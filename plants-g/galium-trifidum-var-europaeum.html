Galium trifidum var. europaeum

Galium trifidum var. europaeum, commonly known as European bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three narrow leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fragrant foliage and historical use in herbal medicine contribute to its ecological and cultural significance.
