Gladiolus spectabilis

Gladiolus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stems that bear large, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant's leaves are long, narrow, and sword-shaped, which contribute to its dramatic appearance. The flowers are typically arranged in a spike and are composed of three petals and three sepals, forming a striking, symmetrical structure.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
