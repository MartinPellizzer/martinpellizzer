Glossopetalon spinescens var. mexicanum

Glossopetalon spinescens var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican spiny hibiscus, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in clusters.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
