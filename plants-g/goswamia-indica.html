Goswamia indica

Goswamia indica, commonly known as the Indian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are usually borne on slender inflorescences and have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals.

Goswamia indica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
