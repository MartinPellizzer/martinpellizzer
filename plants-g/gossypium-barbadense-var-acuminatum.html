Gossypium barbadense var. acuminatum

Gossypium barbadense var. acuminatum, commonly known as the Egyptian cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant known for its high-quality fibers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, arid climates. The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the fluffy fibers used in textile manufacturing. Its fibers are longer and stronger compared to other cotton varieties, making it highly valued in the textile industry.

This variety is also noted for its resistance to certain pests and diseases, contributing to its agricultural importance.
