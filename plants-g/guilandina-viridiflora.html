Guilandina viridiflora

Guilandina viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered legume or the African glorybower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Guilandina viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
