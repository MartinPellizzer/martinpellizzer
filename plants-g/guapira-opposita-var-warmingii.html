Guapira opposita var. warmingii

Guapira opposita var. warmingii, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical and subtropical gardens.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
