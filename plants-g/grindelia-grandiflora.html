Grindelia grandiflora

Grindelia grandiflora, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its resinous leaves and flowers contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Grindelia grandiflora plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
