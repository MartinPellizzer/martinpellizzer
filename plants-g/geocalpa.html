Geocalpa

Geocalpa, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants typically have basal leaves that are succulent and fleshy, adapted to arid environments. They produce small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Geocalpa species are often grown as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and unique appearance.
