Gerbera viridifolia subsp. viridifolia

Gerbera viridifolia subsp.

viridifolia, commonly known as the greenleaf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive green foliage and its status as a subspecies within the Gerbera genus.
