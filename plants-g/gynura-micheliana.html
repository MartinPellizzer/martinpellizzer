Gynura micheliana

Gynura micheliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has a robust, spreading habit. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in umbels, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Gynura micheliana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
