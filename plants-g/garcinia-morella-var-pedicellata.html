Garcinia morella var. pedicellata

Garcinia morella var. pedicellata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
