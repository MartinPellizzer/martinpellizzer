Glyceria ramigera

Glyceria ramigera, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often forming dense stands that help stabilize shoreline areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Glyceria ramigera is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
