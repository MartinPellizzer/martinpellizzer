Geissanthus ambiguus

Geissanthus ambiguus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Geissanthus ambiguus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
