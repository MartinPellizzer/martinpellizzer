Gentianella consobrina

Gentianella consobrina, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
