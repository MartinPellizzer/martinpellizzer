Gussonea defoliata

Gussonea defoliata, commonly known as the leafless gussonea, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage, with most of its photosynthetic activity occurring through its stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are small and often clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its specialized habitat, Gussonea defoliata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
