Gaultheria glaucifolia var. glaucifolia

Gaultheria glaucifolia var. glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf waxleaf, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features elliptical, glossy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
