Gigantochloa aya

Gigantochloa aya, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and is often used in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for erosion control and reforestation projects.

Additionally, it plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
