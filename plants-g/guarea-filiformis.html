Guarea filiformis

Guarea filiformis, commonly known as the slender guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
