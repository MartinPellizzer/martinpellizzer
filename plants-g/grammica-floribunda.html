Grammica floribunda

Grammica floribunda, commonly known as the silverleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically white or pale blue in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
