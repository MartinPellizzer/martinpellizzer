Gomesa longicornu

Gomesa longicornu, commonly known as the longhorned gomesa, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated, often spiky leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or white in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Gomesa longicornu is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
