Gomphrena hygrophila f. luteiflora

Gomphrena hygrophila f. luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gomphrena hygrophila f. luteiflora prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and containers.
