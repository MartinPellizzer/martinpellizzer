Geophila violifolia

Geophila violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf geophila, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The flowers are delicate, ranging from violet to blue-purple, and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
