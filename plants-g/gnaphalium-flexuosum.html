Gnaphalium flexuosum

Gnaphalium flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible cudweed, is a low-growing flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Gnaphalium flexuosum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
