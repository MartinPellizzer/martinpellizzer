Gentiana rubicunda var. samolifolia

Gentiana rubicunda var. samolifolia, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence, while the flowers are arranged in a terminal raceme.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
