Gentianella amarella subsp. euamarella

Gentianella amarella subsp.

euamarella, commonly known as the European gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

The subspecies euamarella is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to other subspecies of Gentianella amarella.
