Glottiphyllum cruciatum

Glottiphyllum cruciatum, commonly known as the cross-leaved glottiphyllum, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cross-shaped leaves that form a rosette pattern, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
