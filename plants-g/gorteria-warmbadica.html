Gorteria warmbadica

Gorteria warmbadica, commonly known as the Warmbad daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.

Gorteria warmbadica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
