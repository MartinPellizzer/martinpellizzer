Gilia arenaria var. abramsii

Gilia arenaria var. abramsii, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It has slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water.

This species is well-suited to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
