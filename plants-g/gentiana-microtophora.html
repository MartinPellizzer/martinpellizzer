Gentiana microtophora

Gentiana microtophora, commonly known as the small-top gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the striking blue flowers.

Gentiana microtophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
