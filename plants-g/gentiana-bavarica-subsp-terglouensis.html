Gentiana bavarica subsp. terglouensis

Gentiana bavarica subsp.

terglouensis, commonly known as the Balkan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
