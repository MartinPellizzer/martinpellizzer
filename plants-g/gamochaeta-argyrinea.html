Gamochaeta argyrinea

Gamochaeta argyrinea, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silvery hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters and bloom in late summer to early fall.
