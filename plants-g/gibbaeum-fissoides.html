Gibbaeum fissoides

Gibbaeum fissoides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and resemble small tomatoes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
