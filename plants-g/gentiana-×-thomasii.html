Gentiana × thomasii

Gentiana × thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's gentian, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. The plant produces showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a horticultural hybrid, showcasing the natural diversity within the Gentiana genus.
