Grevillea acanthifolia subsp. paludosa

Grevillea acanthifolia subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the swamp grevillea, is a shrub native to the wetter regions of eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or heathland environments. Its floral structure is typical of the Proteaceae family, with showy, clustered inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators.
