Gueldenstaedtia multiflora var. longiscapa

Gueldenstaedtia multiflora var. longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed false toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
