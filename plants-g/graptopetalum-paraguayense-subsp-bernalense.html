Graptopetalum paraguayense subsp. bernalense

Graptopetalum paraguayense subsp.

bernalense, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to Mexico. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
