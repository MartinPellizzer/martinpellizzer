Grindelia virgata

Grindelia virgata, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet tall.

Grindelia virgata is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
