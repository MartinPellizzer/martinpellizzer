Grossularia × rustica

Grossularia × rustica, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, tart berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued both for its edible fruits and its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
