Goniopteris imponens

Goniopteris imponens, commonly known as the giant cliff fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, frond-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is covered in a thick, fibrous layer of sori, which are the spore-producing structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in coastal and forested areas.

Goniopteris imponens plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
