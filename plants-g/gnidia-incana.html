Gnidia incana

Gnidia incana, commonly known as the white gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gnidia incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
