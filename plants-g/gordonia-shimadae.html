Gordonia shimadae

Gordonia shimadae, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in spring, followed by edible, red berries in autumn. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
