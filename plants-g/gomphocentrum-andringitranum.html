Gomphocentrum andringitranum

Gomphocentrum andringitranum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the understory of tropical forests. Its botanical features include elongated leaves and a complex inflorescence with multiple blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
