Gentiana quadrifaria

Gentiana quadrifaria, commonly known as the four-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
