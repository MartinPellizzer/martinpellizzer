Gentiana procera

Gentiana procera, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Gentiana procera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its showy flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
