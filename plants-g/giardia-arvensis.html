Giardia arvensis

Giardia arvensis, commonly known as the field giardia, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive ribbed texture.

Giardia arvensis is an annual herb that plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
