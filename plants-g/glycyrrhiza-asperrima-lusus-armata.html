Glycyrrhiza asperrima lusus armata

Glycyrrhiza asperrima lusus armata, commonly known as the rough licorice, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. Native to certain regions of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been cultivated for its potential health benefits.
