Gigachilon polonicum subsp. durum

Gigachilon polonicum subsp.

durum, commonly known as the Polish giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe. It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and produces tall, showy inflorescences that are often mistaken for grasses.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along water margins. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
