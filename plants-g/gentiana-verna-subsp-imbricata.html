Gentiana verna subsp. imbricata

Gentiana verna subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
