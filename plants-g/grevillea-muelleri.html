Grevillea muelleri

Grevillea muelleri, commonly known as the mountain grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features vibrant red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grevillea muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
