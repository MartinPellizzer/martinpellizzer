Galium mollugo

Galium mollugo, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features small, opposite leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually slender and often branched, with the flowers appearing at the tips.

Galium mollugo is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
