Geranium tataricum

Geranium tataricum, commonly known as Tatarian geranium, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Geranium tataricum is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
