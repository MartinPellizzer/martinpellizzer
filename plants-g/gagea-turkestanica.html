Gagea turkestanica

Gagea turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan spring beauty, is a small, bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It is part of the Liliaceae family, which includes other bulbous plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
