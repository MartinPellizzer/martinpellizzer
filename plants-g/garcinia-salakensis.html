Garcinia salakensis

Garcinia salakensis, commonly known as the Kivu Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. Garcinia salakensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforest undergrowth.

Its unique chemical composition makes it an interesting subject for both botanical research and pharmaceutical development.
