Geranium pygmaeum

Geranium pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
