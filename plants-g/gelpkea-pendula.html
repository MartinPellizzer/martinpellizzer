Gelpkea pendula

Gelpkea pendula, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits.

Gelpkea pendula is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
