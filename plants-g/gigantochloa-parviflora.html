Gigantochloa parviflora

Gigantochloa parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming perennial grass that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, erect leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
