Galium praemontanum var. armazicum

Galium praemontanum var. armazicum, commonly known as the Armenian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
