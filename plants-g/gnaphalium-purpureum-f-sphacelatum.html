Gnaphalium purpureum f. sphacelatum

Gnaphalium purpureum f. sphacelatum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.

The f. sphacelatum form is distinguished by its more compact growth and increased flowering compared to the typical form.
