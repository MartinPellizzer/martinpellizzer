Galatella macrosciadia

Galatella macrosciadia, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an attractive species for wetland restoration projects.
