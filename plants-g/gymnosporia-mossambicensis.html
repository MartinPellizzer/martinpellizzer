Gymnosporia mossambicensis

Gymnosporia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Gymnosporia mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and landscaping.
