Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. subarcticum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. subarcticum, commonly known as the subarctic cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and woolly foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and subarctic habitats. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to pale pink composite flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
