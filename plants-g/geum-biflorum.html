Geum biflorum

Geum biflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters of two. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Geum biflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
