Griseocereus fricii

Griseocereus fricii, commonly known as the Frici's night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, night-blooming flowers that are usually pale yellow or white, which open only during the evening. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Griseocereus fricii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
