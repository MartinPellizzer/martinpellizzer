Grumilea diplococca

Grumilea diplococca, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and is often found in coastal areas and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

Grumilea diplococca is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
