Gossypium marie-galante

Gossypium marie-galante, commonly known as Marie-Galante cotton, is a species of cotton native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its fibrous, fluffy seeds that are used for textile production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in warm, humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and the plant produces capsules that contain the cotton fibers.

This variety is valued for its unique fiber quality and historical significance in the region.
