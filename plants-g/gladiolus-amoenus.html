Gladiolus amoenus

Gladiolus amoenus, commonly known as the elegant gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing spikes of vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots are corms, which store nutrients for the plant's growth.

Gladiolus amoenus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
