Gymnocalycium spegazzinii f. unguispinum

Gymnocalycium spegazzinii f. unguispinum, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered with dense, red or purple spines. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in dry environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
