Glomeraria polygonoides

Glomeraria polygonoides, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it survives with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in its native environment.
