Gunnera tinctoria var. tinctoria

Gunnera tinctoria var. tinctoria, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture.

The plant is also notable for its tall, upright stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in the summer.
