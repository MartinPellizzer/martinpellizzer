Gnaphalium castaneum

Gnaphalium castaneum, commonly known as the chestnut weed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit and produces numerous small, hairy stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
