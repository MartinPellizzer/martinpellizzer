Gustavia marcgraaviana

Gustavia marcgraaviana, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has a straight trunk with smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets that provide shade beneath the tree.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
