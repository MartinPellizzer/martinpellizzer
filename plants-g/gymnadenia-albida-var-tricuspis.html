Gymnadenia albida var. tricuspis

Gymnadenia albida var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed white gentian, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal raceme.

This variety is distinguished by its three-lobed petals, which give it its specific epithet.
