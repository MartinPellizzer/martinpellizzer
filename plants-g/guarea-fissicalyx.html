Guarea fissicalyx

Guarea fissicalyx, commonly known as the "bitterwood" tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The tree has a straight trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, followed by distinctive woody fruits that split open to release seeds.
