Garcinia elliotii

Garcinia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and distinctive yellow-orange fruits. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities. Its fruits contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for potential health benefits.

Garcinia elliotii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
