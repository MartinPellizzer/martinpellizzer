Guaduella foliosa

Guaduella foliosa, commonly known as the leafy guaduella, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with poor soil, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Guaduella foliosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
