Gnaphalium purpureum var. filagineum

Gnaphalium purpureum var. filagineum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained environments.
