Glycine pulchella

Glycine pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful bean or the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its pods contain seeds that are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Glycine pulchella thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
