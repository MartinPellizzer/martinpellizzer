Gagea stenopetala var. schreberi

Gagea stenopetala var. schreberi, commonly known as the Schreber's gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
