Ginalloa tenuifolia

Ginalloa tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf ginalloa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
