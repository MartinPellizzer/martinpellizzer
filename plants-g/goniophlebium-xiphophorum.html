Goniophlebium xiphophorum

Goniophlebium xiphophorum, commonly known as the Natal grass or Natal reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to moist, swampy environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Goniophlebium xiphophorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
