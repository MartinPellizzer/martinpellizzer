Geranium mascatense var. persicum

Geranium mascatense var. persicum, commonly known as the Persian geranium, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color. This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
