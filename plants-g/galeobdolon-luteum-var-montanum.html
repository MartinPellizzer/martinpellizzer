Galeobdolon luteum var. montanum

Galeobdolon luteum var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
