Gladiolus pterophyllus

Gladiolus pterophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, with a wide range of hues including red, pink, yellow, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
