Glossogyne tridentata

Glossogyne tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to minimize water loss.

Glossogyne tridentata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
