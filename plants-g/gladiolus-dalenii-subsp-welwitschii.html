Gladiolus dalenii subsp. welwitschii

Gladiolus dalenii subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically blooms in summer, producing inflorescences that are both ornamental and visually dramatic. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is also of interest to botanists and conservationists.
