Grandiphyllum ilhagrandense

Grandiphyllum ilhagrandense, commonly known as the Ilha Grande flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Ilha Grande in Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
