Gentiana stipitata

Gentiana stipitata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

The flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies with their rich coloration.
