Gentiana detonsa

Gentiana detonsa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana detonsa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
