Gilia viscidula

Gilia viscidula, commonly known as sticky gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Gilia viscidula is an annual or biennial species, depending on the climate, and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
