Galianthe chodatiana

Galianthe chodatiana, commonly known as the Chodat's galianthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms. The plant typically features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive purple or brownish lip, which aids in attracting pollinators.

Galianthe chodatiana is often found in humid, shaded environments, thriving in the undergrowth of tropical forests.
