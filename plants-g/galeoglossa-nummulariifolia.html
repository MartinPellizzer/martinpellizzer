Galeoglossa nummulariifolia

Galeoglossa nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
