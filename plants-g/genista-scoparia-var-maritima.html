Genista scoparia var. maritima

Genista scoparia var. maritima, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is well adapted to salt spray and harsh coastal conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
