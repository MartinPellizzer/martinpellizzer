Glycine soja

Glycine soja, commonly known as soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is widely cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in food, animal feed, and industrial products. Glycine soja has a deep root system that helps fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural ecosystems.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a significant crop globally.
