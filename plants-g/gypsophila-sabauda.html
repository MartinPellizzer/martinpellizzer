Gypsophila sabauda

Gypsophila sabauda, commonly known as the sabauda lacy peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences that resemble a peacock's tail. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gypsophila sabauda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
