Glyceria festuciformis var. convoluta

Glyceria festuciformis var. convoluta, commonly known as the twisted or curled glyceria, is a grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that often curl or twist, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to water-saturated soils, making it a common species in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
