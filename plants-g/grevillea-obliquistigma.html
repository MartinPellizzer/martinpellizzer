Grevillea obliquistigma

Grevillea obliquistigma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It features striking, feather-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling bottlebrush shapes. Grevillea obliquistigma is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
