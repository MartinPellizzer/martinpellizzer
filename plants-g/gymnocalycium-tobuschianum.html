Gymnocalycium tobuschianum

Gymnocalycium tobuschianum, commonly known as the Tobschian moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and the presence of a colorful, often brightly pigmented, upper portion known as the "crown.". The plant typically produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
