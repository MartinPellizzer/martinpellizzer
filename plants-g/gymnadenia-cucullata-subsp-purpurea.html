Gymnadenia cucullata subsp. purpurea

Gymnadenia cucullata subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple bee-balm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The subspecies purpurea is distinguished by its more intense purple flower color compared to other subspecies of Gymnadenia cucullata.
