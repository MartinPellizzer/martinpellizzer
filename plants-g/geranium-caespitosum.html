Geranium caespitosum

Geranium caespitosum, commonly known as the mossy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit with numerous small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact form and long blooming period make it an attractive and hardy addition to many garden settings.
