Grazielia gaudichaudiana

Grazielia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Chilean rose or the mountain rose, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water.

Grazielia gaudichaudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
