Galanthus globosus

Galanthus globosus, commonly known as the globe daffodil, is a species of bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, white flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in early spring. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Galanthus globosus is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
