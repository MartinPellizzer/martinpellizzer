Gossia alaternoides var. pulchrifolia

Gossia alaternoides var. pulchrifolia, commonly known as the glossy-leaved gossia, is a species native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
