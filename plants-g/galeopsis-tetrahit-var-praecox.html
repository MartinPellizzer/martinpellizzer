Galeopsis tetrahit var. praecox

Galeopsis tetrahit var. praecox, commonly known as early hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis tetrahit var. praecox is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative reproduction.
