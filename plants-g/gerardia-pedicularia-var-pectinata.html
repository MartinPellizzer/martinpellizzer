Gerardia pedicularia var. pectinata

Gerardia pedicularia var. pectinata, commonly known as the sticky gerardia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The variety pectinata is distinguished by its distinctive, pectinate (comb-like) inflorescence.
