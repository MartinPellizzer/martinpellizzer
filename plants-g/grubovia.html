Grubovia

Grubovia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the remote highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night, a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators. Grubovia thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a protected species in its native habitat.
