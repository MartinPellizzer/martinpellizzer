Galactia acuminata

Galactia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed galactia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces seed pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Galactia acuminata is typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

Its growth habit is generally herbaceous, with some species exhibiting semi-woody stems.
