Gentiana atrata

Gentiana atrata, commonly known as the black gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking deep blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentiana atrata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
