Gentiana rhaetica var. mixta

Gentiana rhaetica var. mixta, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
