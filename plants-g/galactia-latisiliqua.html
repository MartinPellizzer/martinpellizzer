Galactia latisiliqua

Galactia latisiliqua, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Galactia latisiliqua thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
