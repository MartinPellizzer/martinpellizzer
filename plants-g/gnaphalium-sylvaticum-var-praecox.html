Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. praecox

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. praecox, commonly known as early wood sage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs early in the growing season, usually from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.
