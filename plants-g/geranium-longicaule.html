Geranium longicaule

Geranium longicaule, commonly known as the long-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
