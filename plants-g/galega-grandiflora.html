Galega grandiflora

Galega grandiflora, commonly known as the greater goat's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring slender stems and large, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Galega grandiflora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
