Gigantochloa toungooensis

Gigantochloa toungooensis, commonly known as the Tungoo bamboo, is a tall, robust grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its strength and is used in construction and traditional crafts in its native regions.
