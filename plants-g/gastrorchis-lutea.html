Gastrorchis lutea

Gastrorchis lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often as a terrestrial or epiphytic species. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the environment, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Gastrorchis lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
