Griffinia mollevillquensis

Griffinia mollevillquensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open areas with full sun exposure, adapting to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
