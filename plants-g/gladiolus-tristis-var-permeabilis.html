Gladiolus tristis var. permeabilis

Gladiolus tristis var. permeabilis, commonly known as the fragrant gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically in late summer. Its flowers have a distinctive, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and aroma.
