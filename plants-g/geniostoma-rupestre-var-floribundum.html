Geniostoma rupestre var. floribundum

Geniostoma rupestre var. floribundum, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
