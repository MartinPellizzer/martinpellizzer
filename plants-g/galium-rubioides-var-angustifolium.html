Galium rubioides var. angustifolium

Galium rubioides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
