Glechon marifolia f. serpyllifolia

Glechon marifolia f. serpyllifolia, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

This subspecies is often found in dunes and rocky shores along the Atlantic coast.
