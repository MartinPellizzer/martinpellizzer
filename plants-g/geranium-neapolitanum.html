Geranium neapolitanum

Geranium neapolitanum, commonly known as the Naples geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which are often borne in clusters atop slender stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium neapolitanum is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
