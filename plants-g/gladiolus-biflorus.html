Gladiolus biflorus

Gladiolus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces two to three flowers per stem, each with vibrant, colorful petals that range from white to deep red. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

Gladiolus biflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
