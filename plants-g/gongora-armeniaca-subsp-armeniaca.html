Gongora armeniaca subsp. armeniaca

Gongora armeniaca subsp.

armeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. It is native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in arid to semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. It produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
