Gymnocactus valdezianus

Gymnocactus valdezianus, commonly known as the Valdezianus Gymnocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for desert gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
