Gerascanthus collococcus

Gerascanthus collococcus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, branching structure with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are small and spiny, providing protection from herbivores.

Gerascanthus collococcus is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
