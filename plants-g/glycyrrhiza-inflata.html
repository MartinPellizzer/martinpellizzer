Glycyrrhiza inflata

Glycyrrhiza inflata, commonly known as the inflated licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its inflated, pod-like fruits that give it its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Glycyrrhiza inflata is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and historical medicinal uses.
