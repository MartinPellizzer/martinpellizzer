Glycosmis winitii

Glycosmis winitii, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble dates, hence its common name. The fruit's pericarp contains a high concentration of glycosides, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
