Gibbaeum luteoviride

Gibbaeum luteoviride, commonly known as the yellow-green sand mat, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-like structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range from yellow to greenish-white, often blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Gibbaeum luteoviride is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
