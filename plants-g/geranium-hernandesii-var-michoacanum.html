Geranium hernandesii var. michoacanum

Geranium hernandesii var. michoacanum, commonly known as the Michoacan geranium, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
