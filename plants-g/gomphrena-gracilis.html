Gomphrena gracilis

Gomphrena gracilis, commonly known as the slender gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Gomphrena gracilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
