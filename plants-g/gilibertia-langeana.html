Gilibertia langeana

Gilibertia langeana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows from 30 to 100 centimeters in height. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Gilibertia langeana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
