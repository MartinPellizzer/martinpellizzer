Gilibertia diplostemona

Gilibertia diplostemona, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false pigweed, is a tropical and subtropical plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.

Gilibertia diplostemona is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
