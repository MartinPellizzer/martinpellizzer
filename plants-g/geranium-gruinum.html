Geranium gruinum

Geranium gruinum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium gruinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
