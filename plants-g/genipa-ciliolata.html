Genipa ciliolata

Genipa ciliolata, commonly known as the red genipapo, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruits are notable for their high content of genipin, a compound used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
