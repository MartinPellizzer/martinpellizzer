Gymnogramma sprengeriana

Gymnogramma sprengeriana, commonly known as the Sprenger's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its fronds are usually divided into small, serrated segments, which help in photosynthesis and gas exchange.

Gymnogramma sprengeriana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
