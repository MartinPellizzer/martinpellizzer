Gnaphalium ramosissimum

Gnaphalium ramosissimum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Gnaphalium ramosissimum thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
