Geranium pedatum

Geranium pedatum, commonly known as showy geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

Geranium pedatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
