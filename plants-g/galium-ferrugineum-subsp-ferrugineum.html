Galium ferrugineum subsp. ferrugineum

Galium ferrugineum subsp.

ferrugineum, commonly known as rusty bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe. It features slender, ascending stems covered with fine, rusty-brown hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
