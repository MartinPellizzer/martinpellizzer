Gladiolus rogersii

Gladiolus rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences that bear vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing blooms in shades of red, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
