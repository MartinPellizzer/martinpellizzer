Gentiana olivieri var. glomerata

Gentiana olivieri var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and alpine meadows. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
