Glaucium corniculatum var. purpureum

Glaucium corniculatum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple horned poppy, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and distinctive, horn-like bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
