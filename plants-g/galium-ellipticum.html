Galium ellipticum

Galium ellipticum, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Galium ellipticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
