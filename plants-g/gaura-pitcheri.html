Gaura pitcheri

Gaura pitcheri, commonly known as the plains gaura, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and meadows. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gaura pitcheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
