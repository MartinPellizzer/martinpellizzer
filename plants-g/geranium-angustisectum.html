Geranium angustisectum

Geranium angustisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Geranium angustisectum is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
