Gentianella quinquefolia var. occidentalis

Gentianella quinquefolia var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
