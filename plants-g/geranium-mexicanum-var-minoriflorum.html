Geranium mexicanum var. minoriflorum

Geranium mexicanum var. minoriflorum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
