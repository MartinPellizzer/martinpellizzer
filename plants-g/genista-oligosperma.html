Genista oligosperma

Genista oligosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, spiny stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and are followed by small, woody pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant, well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
