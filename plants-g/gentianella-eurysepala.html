Gentianella eurysepala

Gentianella eurysepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue or violet with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
