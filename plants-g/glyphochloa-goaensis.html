Glyphochloa goaensis

Glyphochloa goaensis, commonly known as the Goa grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
