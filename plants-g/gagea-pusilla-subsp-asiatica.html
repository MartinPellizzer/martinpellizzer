Gagea pusilla subsp. asiatica

Gagea pusilla subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian dwarf lily, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in woodland and forested areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It is known for its early spring blooming habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
