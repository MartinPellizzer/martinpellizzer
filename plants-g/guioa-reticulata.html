Guioa reticulata

Guioa reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined guioa, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
