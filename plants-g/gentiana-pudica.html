Gentiana pudica

Gentiana pudica, commonly known as the shy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white down.

Gentiana pudica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
