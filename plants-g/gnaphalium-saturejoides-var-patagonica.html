Gnaphalium saturejoides var. patagonica

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
