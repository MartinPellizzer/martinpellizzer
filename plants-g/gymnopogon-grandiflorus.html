Gymnopogon grandiflorus

Gymnopogon grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib.

Gymnopogon grandiflorus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
