Gloxinia caulescens

Gloxinia caulescens, commonly known as the stalked gloxinia, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Gloxinia caulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
