Gnaphalium purpureum var. normale

Gnaphalium purpureum var. normale, commonly known as purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, and it produces small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
