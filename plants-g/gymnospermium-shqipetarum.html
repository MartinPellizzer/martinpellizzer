Gymnospermium shqipetarum

Gymnospermium shqipetarum, commonly known as the Albanian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Albania.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, characterized by its ability to produce seeds without enclosing them in an ovary. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces cones that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
