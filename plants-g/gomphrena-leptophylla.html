Gomphrena leptophylla

Gomphrena leptophylla, commonly known as the slender leaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces clusters of pink or red bracts that attract pollinators.

Gomphrena leptophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
