Gomphocarpus fragrans

Gomphocarpus fragrans, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that features spiny stems and produces bright yellow flowers. The plant has fleshy, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Gomphocarpus fragrans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
