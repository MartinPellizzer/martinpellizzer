Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp. multiproliferum

Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp.

multiproliferum, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a succulent native to arid regions of South America. It features a short, cylindrical stem that often branches into multiple stems, giving it a bushy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of vibrant, pink to purple flowers during the warmer months. Its spines are short and sparse, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
