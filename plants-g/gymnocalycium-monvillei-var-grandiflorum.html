Gymnocalycium monvillei var. grandiflorum

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the evening. Its leaves are usually absent, with the stems serving as the primary photosynthetic structure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in warm climates.
