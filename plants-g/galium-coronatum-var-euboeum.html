Galium coronatum var. euboeum

Galium coronatum var. euboeum, commonly known as the Euboean bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.
