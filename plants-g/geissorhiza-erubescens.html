Geissorhiza erubescens

Geissorhiza erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Geissorhiza erubescens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
