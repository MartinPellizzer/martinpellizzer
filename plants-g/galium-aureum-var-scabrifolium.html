Galium aureum var. scabrifolium

Galium aureum var. scabrifolium, commonly known as golden wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow coloration on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
