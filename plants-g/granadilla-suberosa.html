Granadilla suberosa

Granadilla suberosa, commonly known as the wild passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the genus Passiflora. Its fruit is a tropical berry that ripens to a bright yellow or orange color, often with a fragrant aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
