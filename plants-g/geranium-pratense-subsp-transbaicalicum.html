Geranium pratense subsp. transbaicalicum

Geranium pratense subsp.

transbaicalicum, commonly known as the Transbaikal geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, meadow-like habitats and is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of showy pink or red flowers.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to temperate and subarctic regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
