Gesneria montana

Gesneria montana, commonly known as the mountain gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and glossy, while its flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Gesneria montana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
