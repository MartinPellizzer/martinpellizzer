Gentianella barbellata

Gentianella barbellata, commonly known as the barred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Gentianella barbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
