Guettarda tenuiflora

Guettarda tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered guettarda, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Guettarda tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
