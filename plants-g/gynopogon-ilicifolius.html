Gynopogon ilicifolius

Gynopogon ilicifolius, commonly known as the hollyleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its holly-like leaves, which are glossy and have spiny margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. These berries are often used in local cuisines and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
