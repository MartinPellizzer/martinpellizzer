Gomphrena pallida f. calva

Gomphrena pallida f. calva, commonly known as the pale gomphrena or pale plume flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, clustered inflorescences that resemble plumes, typically in shades of pink, white, or pale red. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Gomphrena pallida f. calva is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
