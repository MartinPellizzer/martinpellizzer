Grevillea saccata

Grevillea saccata, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.

Grevillea saccata is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
