Gymnadenia × micrantha

Gymnadenia × micrantha, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, flowering stems that bear clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Gymnadenia × micrantha is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
