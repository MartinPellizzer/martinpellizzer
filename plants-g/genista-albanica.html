Genista albanica

Genista albanica, commonly known as the Albanian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and forest edges. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Genista albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
