Gypsophila exaltata

Gypsophila exaltata, commonly known as the lace flower or baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery blooms that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its small, showy flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance.

Gypsophila exaltata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
