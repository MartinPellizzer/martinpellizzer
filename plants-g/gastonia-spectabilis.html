Gastonia spectabilis

Gastonia spectabilis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
