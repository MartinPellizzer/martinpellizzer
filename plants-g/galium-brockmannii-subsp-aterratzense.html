Galium brockmannii subsp. aterratzense

Galium brockmannii subsp.

aterratzense, commonly known as the dark-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive coloration and ecological role in its native habitat.
