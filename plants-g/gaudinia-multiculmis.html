Gaudinia multiculmis

Gaudinia multiculmis, commonly known as the clustered gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink blooms that appear in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in dense tufts.

Gaudinia multiculmis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
