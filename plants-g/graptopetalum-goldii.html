Graptopetalum goldii

Graptopetalum goldii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
