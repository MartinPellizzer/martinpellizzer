Glyceria colombiana

Glyceria colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Glyceria colombiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
