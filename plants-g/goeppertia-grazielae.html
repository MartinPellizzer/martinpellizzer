Goeppertia grazielae

Goeppertia grazielae, commonly known as the "Grazielae Calathea," is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage with deep green and silver patterns. The plant features upright, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate vein patterns. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Goeppertia grazielae is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
