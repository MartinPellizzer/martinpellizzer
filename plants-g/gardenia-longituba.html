Gardenia longituba

Gardenia longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia longituba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.
