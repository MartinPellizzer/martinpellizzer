Geranium cicutarium var. pimpinellifolium

Geranium cicutarium var. pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the common garden geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and borders.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
