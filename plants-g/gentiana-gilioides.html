Gentiana gilioides

Gentiana gilioides, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentiana gilioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
