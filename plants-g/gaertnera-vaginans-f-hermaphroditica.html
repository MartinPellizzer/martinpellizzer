Gaertnera vaginans f. hermaphroditica

Gaertnera vaginans f. hermaphroditica, commonly known as the hermaphroditic form of the genus Gaertnera, is a unique variant characterized by its ability to produce both male and female reproductive structures.

This plant typically exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink. The hermaphroditic trait enhances its reproductive success by allowing self-pollination in the absence of pollinators.

It is native to certain regions of South America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological adaptability.
