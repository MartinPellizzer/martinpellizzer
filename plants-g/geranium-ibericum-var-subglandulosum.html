Geranium ibericum var. subglandulosum

Geranium ibericum var. subglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
