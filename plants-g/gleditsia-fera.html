Gleditsia fera

Gleditsia fera, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to China and Korea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Gleditsia fera is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
