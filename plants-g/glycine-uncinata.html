Glycine uncinata

Glycine uncinata, commonly known as the hookvine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically small, clustered, and pale in color. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its ability to climb using tendrils.

Glycine uncinata is often found in humid environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
