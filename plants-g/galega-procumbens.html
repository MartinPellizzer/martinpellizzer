Galega procumbens

Galega procumbens, commonly known as sweet clover, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, purple flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types, including poor or disturbed ground. It is often used in agriculture as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
