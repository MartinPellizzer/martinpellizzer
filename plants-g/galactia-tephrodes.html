Galactia tephrodes

Galactia tephrodes, commonly known as the redstem burr legume, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Galactia tephrodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
