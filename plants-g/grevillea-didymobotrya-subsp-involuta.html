Grevillea didymobotrya subsp. involuta

Grevillea didymobotrya subsp.

involuta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
