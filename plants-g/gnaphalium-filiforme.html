Gnaphalium filiforme

Gnaphalium filiforme, commonly known as slender featherweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its feathery appearance.

Gnaphalium filiforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
