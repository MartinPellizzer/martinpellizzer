Gouldia myrsinoides

Gouldia myrsinoides, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrsinaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in well-drained sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gouldia myrsinoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
