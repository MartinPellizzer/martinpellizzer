Grewia canescens

Grewia canescens, commonly known as the woolly gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, grey bark and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
