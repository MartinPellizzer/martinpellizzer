Gentiana × naegelii

Gentiana × naegelii, commonly known as the Bluebell Gentian, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
