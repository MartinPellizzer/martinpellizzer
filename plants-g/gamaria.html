Gamaria

Gamaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads and silvery, fuzzy leaves. Gamaria typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
