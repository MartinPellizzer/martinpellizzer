Galeola javanica

Galeola javanica, commonly known as the Javanese orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are often white or pale pink with a distinctive lip.

Galeola javanica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
