Gentiana campestris subsp. baltica

Gentiana campestris subsp.

baltica, commonly known as the Baltic gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
