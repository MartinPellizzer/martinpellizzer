Gossypium nanking var. soudanense

Gossypium nanking var. soudanense, commonly known as the Sudanese cotton, is a variant of the species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white cotton fibers that are harvested from the seed pods of the plant. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to two meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are showy, with five petals, and are typically pink or purple in color.
