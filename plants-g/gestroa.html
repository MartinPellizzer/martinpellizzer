Gestroa

Gestroa, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. Gestroa has a strong, bitter scent and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

However, it is toxic to livestock and should be handled with care.
