Genista multiflora

Genista multiflora, commonly known as the evergreen shrub, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, round, black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
