Gonolobus marmoreus

Gonolobus marmoreus, commonly known as the marble vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often woody and can climb or trail.

Gonolobus marmoreus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
