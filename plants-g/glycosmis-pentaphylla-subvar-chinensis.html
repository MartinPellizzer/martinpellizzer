Glycosmis pentaphylla subvar. chinensis

Glycosmis pentaphylla subvar. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese whiteflowered hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
