Gentiana tonkinensis

Gentiana tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in summer and are known for their medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
