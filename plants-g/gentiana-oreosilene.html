Gentiana oreosilene

Gentiana oreosilene, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine down, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentiana oreosilene is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
