Grevillea viridiflava

Grevillea viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green, with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Grevillea viridiflava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
