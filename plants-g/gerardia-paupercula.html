Gerardia paupercula

Gerardia paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gerardia paupercula is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
