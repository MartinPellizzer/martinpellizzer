Gonostemon grandiflorus var. apicalis

Gonostemon grandiflorus var. apicalis, commonly known as the apicalis variant of the grandiflorus gonostemon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or orange. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its floral structure includes prominent stamens and a distinctive, elongated pistil.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
