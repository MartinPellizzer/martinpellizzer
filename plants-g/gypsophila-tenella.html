Gypsophila tenella

Gypsophila tenella, commonly known as the baby's breath or winter sweet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its delicate appearance and long flowering period make it a popular choice in floral arrangements.
