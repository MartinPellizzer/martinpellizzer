Gladiolus tristis var. luteus

Gladiolus tristis var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow weed gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is herbaceous, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its bright, attractive blooms.
