Geranium dissectum var. typicum

Geranium dissectum var. typicum, commonly known as the cutleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features attractive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its foliage and floral display. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for both container and garden planting.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
