Galium umbellatum

Galium umbellatum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, white, umbel-like clusters of flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
