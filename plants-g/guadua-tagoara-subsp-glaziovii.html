Guadua tagoara subsp. glaziovii

Guadua tagoara subsp.

glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziovii bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It is valued for its strong, lightweight material, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.

This subspecies is also noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
