Gilesia biniflora

Gilesia biniflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered gilesia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in pairs along the stems, giving it its scientific name. Gilesia biniflora thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral arrangement and adaptability to various climates contribute to its interest among botanists and horticulturists.
