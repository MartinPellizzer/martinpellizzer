Gaillardia acaulis

Gaillardia acaulis, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright, daisy-like blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with no stem, making it ideal for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Gaillardia acaulis is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
