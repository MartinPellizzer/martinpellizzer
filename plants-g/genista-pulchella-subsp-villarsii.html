Genista pulchella subsp. villarsii

Genista pulchella subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine broom, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces legumes as its fruit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing alpine environments.
