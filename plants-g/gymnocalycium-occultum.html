Gymnocalycium occultum

Gymnocalycium occultum, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Gymnocalycium occultum is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
