Guzmania bicolor

Guzmania bicolor, commonly known as the red-tipped peace lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that displays a mix of red and white colors. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports small aquatic life. Its foliage is often variegated with white and green, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Guzmania bicolor is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its minimal care requirements.
