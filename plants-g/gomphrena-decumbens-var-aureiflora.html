Gomphrena decumbens var. aureiflora

Gomphrena decumbens var. aureiflora, commonly known as the golden plume flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery bracts that range in color from pink to red, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long blooming period add ornamental value to cultivated areas.
