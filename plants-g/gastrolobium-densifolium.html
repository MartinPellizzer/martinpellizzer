Gastrolobium densifolium

Gastrolobium densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved burr medick, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, foliage and distinctive seed pods that resemble small burrs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Gastrolobium densifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
