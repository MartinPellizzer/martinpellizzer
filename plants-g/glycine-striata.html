Glycine striata

Glycine striata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Glycine striata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
