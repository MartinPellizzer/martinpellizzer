Glycine heterocarpa

Glycine heterocarpa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Glycine heterocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in agricultural systems where nitrogen fixation plays a key role.
