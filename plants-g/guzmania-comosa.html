Guzmania comosa

Guzmania comosa, commonly known as the pink pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, often displaying shades of red, orange, and pink. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports small aquatic life. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and a waxy coating that enhances its visual appeal.

Guzmania comosa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
