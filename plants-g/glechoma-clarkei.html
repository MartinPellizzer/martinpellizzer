Glechoma clarkei

Glechoma clarkei, commonly known as the Kashmir ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It features creeping rhizomes and heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Glechoma clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
