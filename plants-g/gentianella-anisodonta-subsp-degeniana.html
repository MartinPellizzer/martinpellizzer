Gentianella anisodonta subsp. degeniana

Gentianella anisodonta subsp.

degeniana, commonly known as the Degen's gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gentianaceae. It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in countries such as Bolivia and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, herbaceous growth habit.
