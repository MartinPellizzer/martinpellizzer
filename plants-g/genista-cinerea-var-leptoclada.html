Genista cinerea var. leptoclada

Genista cinerea var. leptoclada, commonly known as the slender broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has small, needle-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
