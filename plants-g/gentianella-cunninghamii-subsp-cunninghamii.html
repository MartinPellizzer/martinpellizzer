Gentianella cunninghamii subsp. cunninghamii

Gentianella cunninghamii subsp.

cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's gentian, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
