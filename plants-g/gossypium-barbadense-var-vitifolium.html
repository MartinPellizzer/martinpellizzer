Gossypium barbadense var. vitifolium

Gossypium barbadense var. vitifolium, commonly known as the vitifolium cotton, is a variant of the upland cotton species native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often described as having a vitreous or glassy appearance, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton fibers that are harvested for textile use, though it is less commercially dominant than other cotton varieties. It thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soils and requires a long growing season.

Due to its unique foliage and historical cultivation, it holds significance in both botanical studies and agricultural heritage.
