Grevillea platypoda

Grevillea platypoda, commonly known as the yellow grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in coastal and heathland environments. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, and it produces woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds.

Grevillea platypoda is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value.
