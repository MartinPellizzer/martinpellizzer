Genista tinctoria subsp. prostrata

Genista tinctoria subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as creeping yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, woody stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
