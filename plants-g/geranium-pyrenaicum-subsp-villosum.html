Geranium pyrenaicum subsp. villosum

Geranium pyrenaicum subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the hairy rock geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often on slopes and cliffs.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with stems that are also covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
