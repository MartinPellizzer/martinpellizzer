Gyrinops salicifolia

Gyrinops salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its common name. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in warm, dry climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
