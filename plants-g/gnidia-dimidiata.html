Gnidia dimidiata

Gnidia dimidiata, commonly known as the red-stemmed kniphofia, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Asphodelaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting purple base. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge along the midribs. Gnidia dimidiata thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a popular choice for gardeners due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
