Gaillardia simplex

Gaillardia simplex, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that are fluffy and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia simplex is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
