Gynura bodinieri

Gynura bodinieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf holly, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gynura bodinieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
