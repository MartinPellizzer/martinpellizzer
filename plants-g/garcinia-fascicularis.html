Garcinia fascicularis

Garcinia fascicularis, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. The fruit is a small, elongated drupe that turns from green to red when mature. It contains a single seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
