Gmelina speciosa

Gmelina speciosa, commonly known as the beautiful gum tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by round, woody fruits containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
