Grewia lagenophora

Grewia lagenophora, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped fruits, which are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, brush-like inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Grewia lagenophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
