Gentianella hieronymi

Gentianella hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Gentianella hieronymi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
