Grevillea obtusiflora subsp. obtusiflora

Grevillea obtusiflora subsp.

obtusiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia. It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
