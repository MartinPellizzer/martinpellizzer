Galium angustifolium subsp. gracillimum

Galium angustifolium subsp.

gracillimum, commonly known as slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.
