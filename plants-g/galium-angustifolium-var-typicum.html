Galium angustifolium var. typicum

Galium angustifolium var. typicum, commonly known as narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, opposite leaves that are linear to narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences called scorpioid cymes.

This species is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches.
