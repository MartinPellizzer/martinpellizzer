Galium valantioides var. baillonii

Galium valantioides var. baillonii, commonly known as the Bailloni's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually oval to lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
