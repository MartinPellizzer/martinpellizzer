Grossularia hirtella

Grossularia hirtella, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, tart berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The hairy gooseberry is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates.
