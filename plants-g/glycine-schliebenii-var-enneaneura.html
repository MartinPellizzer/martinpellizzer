Glycine schliebenii var. enneaneura

Glycine schliebenii var. enneaneura, commonly known as the nine-nerve cowpea, is a herbaceous legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which often have nine prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its pods are slender, containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its edible beans.

This variety is well adapted to warm climates and is valued for both its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
