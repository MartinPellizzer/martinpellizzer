Gutierrezia mandonii var. anomala

Gutierrezia mandonii var. anomala, commonly known as the desert gumplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and xerophytic adaptations. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
