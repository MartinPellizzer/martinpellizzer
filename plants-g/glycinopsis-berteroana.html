Glycinopsis berteroana

Glycinopsis berteroana, commonly known as the Peruvian burrbean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Glycinopsis berteroana is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
