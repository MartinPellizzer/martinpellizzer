Geum laxmannii

Geum laxmannii, commonly known as the laxmann's avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Geum laxmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
