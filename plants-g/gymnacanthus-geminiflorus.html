Gymnacanthus geminiflorus

Gymnacanthus geminiflorus, commonly known as the twin-flowered acanthus, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its botanical classification places it within the family Acanthaceae, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
