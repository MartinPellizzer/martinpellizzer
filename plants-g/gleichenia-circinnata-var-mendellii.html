Gleichenia circinnata var. mendellii

Gleichenia circinnata var. mendellii, commonly known as the Mendell's feather fern, is a variant of the feather fern species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a terrestrial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant has a distinctive, lacy frond structure with a prominent midrib and small, clustered pinnae. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
