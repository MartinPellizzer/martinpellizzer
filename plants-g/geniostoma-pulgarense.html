Geniostoma pulgarense

Geniostoma pulgarense, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators.
