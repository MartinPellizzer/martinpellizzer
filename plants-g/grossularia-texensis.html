Grossularia texensis

Grossularia texensis, commonly known as the Texas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
