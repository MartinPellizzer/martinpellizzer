Geranium choimacotense

Geranium choimacotense, commonly known as the Peruvian geranium, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
