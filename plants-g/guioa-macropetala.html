Guioa macropetala

Guioa macropetala, commonly known as the large-leaved guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often leathery and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, consisting of a smooth, yellowish-brown capsule that splits to release the seeds.

Guioa macropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
