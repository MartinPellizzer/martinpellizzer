Geranium aristisepalum var. typicum

Geranium aristisepalum var. typicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a serrated margin, and are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
