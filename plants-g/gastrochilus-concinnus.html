Gastrochilus concinnus

Gastrochilus concinnus, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
