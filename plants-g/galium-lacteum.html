Galium lacteum

Galium lacteum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and features small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Galium lacteum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
