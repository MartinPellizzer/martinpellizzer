Gladiolus segetum f. sterilis

Gladiolus segetum f. sterilis, commonly known as the sterile gladiolus, is a botanical variant of the gladiolus species characterized by its inability to produce viable seeds.

This form exhibits the typical ornamental features of the parent species, including tall, sword-like leaves and showy, spike-like flower clusters. However, its sterility is a notable trait, likely resulting from genetic or environmental factors that prevent successful pollination and seed development. The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than its reproductive capabilities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden landscapes.
