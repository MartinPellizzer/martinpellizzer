Grindelia pacifica

Grindelia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific gumweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Grindelia pacifica is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its unique appearance.
