Gerardia peduncularis var. guatimalensis

Gerardia peduncularis var. guatimalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan gerardia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
