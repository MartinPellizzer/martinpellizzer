Glyceria magellanica

Glyceria magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Glyceria magellanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
