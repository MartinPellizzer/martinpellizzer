Gentiana myrioclada

Gentiana myrioclada, commonly known as the many-leaved gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Gentiana myrioclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
