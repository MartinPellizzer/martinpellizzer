Gypsophila bungeana

Gypsophila bungeana, commonly known as the Chinese baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flower clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and numerous, forming fluffy, spherical inflorescences.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its ethereal appearance and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.
