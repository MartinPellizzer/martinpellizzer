Glaucium contortuplicatum var. hirsutum

Glaucium contortuplicatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the woolly thornapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with purple veins, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in soft, hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth, brown seeds.
