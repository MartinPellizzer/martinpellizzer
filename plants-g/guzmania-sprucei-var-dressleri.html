Guzmania sprucei var. dressleri

Guzmania sprucei var. dressleri, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that displays a fiery red color. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for small aquatic organisms. Its foliage is often a deep green, contrasting beautifully with the bright red flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
