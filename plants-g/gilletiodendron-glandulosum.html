Gilletiodendron glandulosum

Gilletiodendron glandulosum, commonly known as the silky ash, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually clustered at the base of the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
