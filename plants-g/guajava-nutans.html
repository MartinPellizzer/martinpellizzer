Guajava nutans

Guajava nutans, commonly known as the Mexican tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
