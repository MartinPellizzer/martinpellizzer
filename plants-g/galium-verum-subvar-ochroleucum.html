Galium verum subvar. ochroleucum

Galium verum subvar. ochroleucum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
