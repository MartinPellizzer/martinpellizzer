Gastrochilus affinis

Gastrochilus affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as under forest canopies. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale pink, arranged in a spike.

Gastrochilus affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
