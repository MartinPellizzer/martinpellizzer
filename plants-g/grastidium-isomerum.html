Grastidium isomerum

Grastidium isomerum, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in some indigenous communities for treating various ailments.
