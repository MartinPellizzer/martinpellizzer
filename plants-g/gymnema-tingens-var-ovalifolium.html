Gymnema tingens var. ovalifolium

Gymnema tingens var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the Indian licorice or the sugar plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, tropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsules containing elliptical seeds. It is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the digestive system, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

The leaves and stems are typically used in traditional remedies for their purported health benefits.
