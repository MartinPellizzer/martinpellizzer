Galium album

Galium album, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium album is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks.
