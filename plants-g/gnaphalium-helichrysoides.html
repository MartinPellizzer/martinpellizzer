Gnaphalium helichrysoides

Gnaphalium helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage and produces small, yellowish to golden flower heads. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its aromatic, slightly resinous scent.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in gardens.
