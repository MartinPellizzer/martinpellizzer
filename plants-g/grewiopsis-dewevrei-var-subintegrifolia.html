Grewiopsis dewevrei var. subintegrifolia

Grewiopsis dewevrei var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grewiopsis, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its foliage is dense and compact, making it suitable for use in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
