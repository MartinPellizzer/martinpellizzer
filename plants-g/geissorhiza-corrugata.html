Geissorhiza corrugata

Geissorhiza corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
