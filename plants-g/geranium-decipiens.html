Geranium decipiens

Geranium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and palmate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base of the flower.

Geranium decipiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
