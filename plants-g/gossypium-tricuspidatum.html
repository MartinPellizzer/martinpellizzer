Gossypium tricuspidatum

Gossypium tricuspidatum, commonly known as the Chinese cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy pink or red flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that are used in traditional textile production. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Gossypium tricuspidatum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
