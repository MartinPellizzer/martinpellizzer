Gentiana sirensis

Gentiana sirensis, commonly known as the Siren's Gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh alpine conditions.

Gentiana sirensis is a perennial herb that plays a role in the ecological balance of its mountainous habitat.
