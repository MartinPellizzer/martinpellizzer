Gentiana altorum subsp. altorum

Gentiana altorum subsp.

altorum, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. Gentiana altorum subsp.

altorum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
