Genista cossoniana

Genista cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista cossoniana is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
