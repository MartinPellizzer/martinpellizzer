Gastronema

Gastronema, commonly known as the "cinnamon plant," is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a unique resin that is harvested and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Gastronema thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical research.
