Globba ranongensis

Globba ranongensis, commonly known as the Ranong ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive waxy texture.

Globba ranongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
