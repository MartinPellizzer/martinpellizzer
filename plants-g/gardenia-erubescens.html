Gardenia erubescens

Gardenia erubescens, commonly known as the red gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is typically evergreen and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The fruit of Gardenia erubescens is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.
