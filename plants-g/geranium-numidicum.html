Geranium numidicum

Geranium numidicum, commonly known as the Algerian geranium, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
