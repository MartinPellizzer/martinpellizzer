Gentiana foliiformis

Gentiana foliiformis, commonly known as the leaf-like gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often resembling the shape of its common name.

Gentiana foliiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
