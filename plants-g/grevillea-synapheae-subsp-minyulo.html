Grevillea synapheae subsp. minyulo

Grevillea synapheae subsp.

minyulo, commonly known as the Minyulupia grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces bright red or orange cylindrical flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
