Gentiana panthaica

Gentiana panthaica, commonly known as the Panthai gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are usually blue or purple in color.

Gentiana panthaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
