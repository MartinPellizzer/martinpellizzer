Guarania

Guarania, commonly known as the "Brazilian nightshade," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white, and it produces small, toxic berries.
