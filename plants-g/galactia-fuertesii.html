Galactia fuertesii

Galactia fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its name derives from the milky sap it exudes, which is a common feature in many species of the genus Galactia.

Galactia fuertesii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
