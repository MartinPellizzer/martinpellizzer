Guillenia heterophylla

Guillenia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf guillenia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is generally prostrate or sprawling, with stems that can root at the nodes.

Guillenia heterophylla is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
