Gymnosporia acuminata var. microphylla

Gymnosporia acuminata var. microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
