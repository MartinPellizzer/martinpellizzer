Grusonia agglomerata

Grusonia agglomerata, commonly known as the clustered grusonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Grusonia agglomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
