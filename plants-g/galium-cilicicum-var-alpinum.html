Galium cilicicum var. alpinum

Galium cilicicum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of the Mediterranean and Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped with toothed margins. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Galium cilicicum var. alpinum is often found in open meadows, scree slopes, and rocky habitats at high elevations.
