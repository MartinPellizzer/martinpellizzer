Glochidion trachyspermum

Glochidion trachyspermum, commonly known as the rough-leaved glabrous wood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves with a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. Due to its hardy nature and ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
