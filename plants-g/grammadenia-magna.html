Grammadenia magna

Grammadenia magna, commonly known as the giant grammadenia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the wetlands of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, forming a rosette that collects rainwater. The flowers, which are typically bright red or orange, are borne on tall, slender inflorescences and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
