Galium mollugo var. tyrolense

Galium mollugo var. tyrolense, commonly known as the Tyrol creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.
