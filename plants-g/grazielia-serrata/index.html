Grazielia Serrata

Grazielia serrata is a unique flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil. It is known for its striking, spiky leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers. This species thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. Its name comes from the Latin words for "grace" and "serrated," reflecting its distinctive leaf shape. Due to its beauty and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
