Gouldia terminalis f. oblonga

Gouldia terminalis f. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gouldia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
