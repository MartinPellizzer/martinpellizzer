Geochloa lupulina

Geochloa lupulina, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers.

Geochloa lupulina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
