Grevillea shuttleworthiana subsp. obovata

Grevillea shuttleworthiana subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

The plant produces striking red or orange cylindrical inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
