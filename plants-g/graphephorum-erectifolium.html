Graphephorum erectifolium

Graphephorum erectifolium, commonly known as the upright-leaf fern, is a delicate, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds. Graphephorum erectifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
