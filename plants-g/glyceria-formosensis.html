Glyceria formosensis

Glyceria formosensis, commonly known as the Formosan glyceria, is a grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Glyceria formosensis is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
