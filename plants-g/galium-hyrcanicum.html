Galium hyrcanicum

Galium hyrcanicum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium hyrcanicum has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
