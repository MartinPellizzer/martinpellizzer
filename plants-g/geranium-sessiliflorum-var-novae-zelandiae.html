Geranium sessiliflorum var. novae-zelandiae

Geranium sessiliflorum var. novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand geranium, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
