Gelrebia bracteata

Gelrebia bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Gelrebia bracteata is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
