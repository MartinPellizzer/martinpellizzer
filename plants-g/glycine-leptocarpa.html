Glycine leptocarpa

Glycine leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching habit and trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Glycine leptocarpa is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological studies.
