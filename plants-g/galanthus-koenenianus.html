Galanthus koenenianus

Galanthus koenenianus, commonly known as the Koenen's snowdrop, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan mountains. The plant produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers with a greenish spathe, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers well-drained, calcareous soils and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and the graceful appearance of its flowers.
