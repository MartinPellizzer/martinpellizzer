Gnaphalium confertum

Gnaphalium confertum, commonly known as the clustered everlasting or woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are usually covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The small, yellowish flowers bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
