Gentiana victorinii

Gentiana victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes or in meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Gentiana victorinii is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.
