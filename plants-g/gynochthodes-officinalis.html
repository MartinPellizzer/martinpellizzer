Gynochthodes officinalis

Gynochthodes officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as inflammation and respiratory issues. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
