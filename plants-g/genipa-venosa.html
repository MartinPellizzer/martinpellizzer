Genipa venosa

Genipa venosa, commonly known as the red genipap, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit, which is typically red when ripe, has a unique, aromatic pulp that is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
