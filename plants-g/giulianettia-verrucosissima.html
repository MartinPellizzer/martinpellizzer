Giulianettia verrucosissima

Giulianettia verrucosissima, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
