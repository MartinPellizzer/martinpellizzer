Gentiana herrerae

Gentiana herrerae, commonly known as Herrera's gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom during the summer months. Gentiana herrerae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
