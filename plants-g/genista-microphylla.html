Genista microphylla

Genista microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. Its small, narrow leaves and bright yellow flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
