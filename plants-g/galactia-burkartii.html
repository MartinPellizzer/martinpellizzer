Galactia burkartii

Galactia burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy, inflated pods that split open when mature to release the seeds.

Galactia burkartii is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
