Gevuina

Gevuina, commonly known as the Chilean wineberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, berry-like flavor. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and compact growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils, often found in forested areas.

Gevuina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
