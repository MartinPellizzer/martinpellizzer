Gomphrena pallida subsp. munda

Gomphrena pallida subsp.

munda, commonly known as the pale gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a preference for well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
