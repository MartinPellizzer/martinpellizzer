Griselinia jodinifolia

Griselinia jodinifolia, commonly known as the Jodin's griselinia, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are leathery, dark green, and have a rough texture, while the plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Griselinia jodinifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
