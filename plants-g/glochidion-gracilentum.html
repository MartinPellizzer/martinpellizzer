Glochidion gracilentum

Glochidion gracilentum, commonly known as the slender glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
