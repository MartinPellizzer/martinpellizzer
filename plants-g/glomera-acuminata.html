Glomera acuminata

Glomera acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed glomera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
