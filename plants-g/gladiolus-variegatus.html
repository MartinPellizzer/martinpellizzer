Gladiolus variegatus

Gladiolus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white, yellow, or green markings. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of colorful, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Gladiolus variegatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
