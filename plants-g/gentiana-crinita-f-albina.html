Gentiana crinita f. albina

Gentiana crinita f. albina, commonly known as the white gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The white gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
