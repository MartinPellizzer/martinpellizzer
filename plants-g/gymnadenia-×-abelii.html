Gymnadenia × abelii

Gymnadenia × abelii, commonly known as the hybrid grass-of-Parnassus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is known for its ability to hybridize with other Gymnadenia species, leading to genetic diversity.

The leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in a loose spike.
