Galactia benthamiana

Galactia benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthams' milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Galactia benthamiana is also valued for its role in ecological systems, supporting pollinators and soil health.
