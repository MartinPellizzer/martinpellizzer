Gladiolus tenellus

Gladiolus tenellus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
