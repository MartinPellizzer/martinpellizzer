Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. angustifolia

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, spikelike inflorescence.

The flowers are typically blue or violet, with a distinctive hooded structure, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
