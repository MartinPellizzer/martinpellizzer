Gnaphalium stenophyllum

Gnaphalium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green in color.

It is known for its ability to produce a fragrant, powdery substance that gives it a distinctive aroma.
