Geum × intermedium

Geum × intermedium, commonly known as the New England goldenroot, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and its showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
