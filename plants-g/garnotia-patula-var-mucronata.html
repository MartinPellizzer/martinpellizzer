Garnotia patula var. mucronata

Garnotia patula var. mucronata, commonly known as the mucronate garnotia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
