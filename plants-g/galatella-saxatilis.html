Galatella saxatilis

Galatella saxatilis, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as cliffs, roadsides, and coastal dunes.

Galatella saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
