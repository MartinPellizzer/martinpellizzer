Gymnadenia calcicola

Gymnadenia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, often in open grasslands or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is often hairy.

Gymnadenia calcicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
