Gentianella praecox

Gentianella praecox, commonly known as early gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Gentianella praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
