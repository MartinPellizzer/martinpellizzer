Gratiola origanifolia

Gratiola origanifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive lemony aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by slender, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
