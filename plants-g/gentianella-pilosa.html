Gentianella pilosa

Gentianella pilosa, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Gentianella pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
