Globulea atropurpurea nothovar. mularis

Globulea atropurpurea nothovar. mularis, commonly known as the purple globulea, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features small, globe-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy inflorescences. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
