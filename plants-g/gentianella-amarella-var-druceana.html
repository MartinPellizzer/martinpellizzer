Gentianella amarella var. druceana

Gentianella amarella var. druceana, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
