Glandonia macrocarpa

Glandonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant wax tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by cupular structures, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its latex sap contains toxic compounds and has been used in various cultural practices.

Glandonia macrocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
