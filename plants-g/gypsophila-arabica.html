Gypsophila arabica

Gypsophila arabica, commonly known as the Armenian gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun with minimal water requirements.

Gypsophila arabica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
