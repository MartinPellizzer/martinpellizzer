Grevillea rubiginosa var. angustifolia

Grevillea rubiginosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a striking shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features bright red, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
