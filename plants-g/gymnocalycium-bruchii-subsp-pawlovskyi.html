Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. pawlovskyi

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

pawlovskyi, commonly known as the Pawlovsky's moon cactus, is a compact cactus native to Argentina. It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

This subspecies is known for its slow growth and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
