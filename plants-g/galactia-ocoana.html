Galactia ocoana

Galactia ocoana, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Galactia ocoana is known for its milky sap, which contains latex and is used in traditional medicine.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
