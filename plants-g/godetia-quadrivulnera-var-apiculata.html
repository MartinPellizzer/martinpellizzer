Godetia quadrivulnera var. apiculata

Godetia quadrivulnera var. apiculata, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add vibrant color to their landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
