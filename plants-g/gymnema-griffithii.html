Gymnema griffithii

Gymnema griffithii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. Gymnema griffithii is also valued for its use in traditional Ayurvedic practices for its purported health benefits.

Its leaves are often used in teas and supplements, though its medicinal properties require further scientific validation.
