Gentiana lipskyi

Gentiana lipskyi, commonly known as the Lipsky's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and a prominent, spurred corona.

Gentiana lipskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
