Gesneria albiflora

Gesneria albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gesneria, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria albiflora is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
