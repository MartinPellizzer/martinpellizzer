Galatella × tzvelevii

Galatella × tzvelevii, commonly known as the hybrid rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Galatella × tzvelevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
