Geranium sanguineum var. incisum

Geranium sanguineum var. incisum, commonly known as the bloody geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
