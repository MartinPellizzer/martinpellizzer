Gomphia schomburgkii

Gomphia schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Gomphia schomburgkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
