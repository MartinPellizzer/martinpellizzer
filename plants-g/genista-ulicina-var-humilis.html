Genista ulicina var. humilis

Genista ulicina var. humilis, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
