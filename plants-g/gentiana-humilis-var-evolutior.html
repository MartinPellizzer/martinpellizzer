Gentiana humilis var. evolutior

Gentiana humilis var. evolutior, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy floral displays.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly more elongated petals compared to the typical form of Gentiana humilis.
