Grumilea

Grumilea, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in mountainous areas. The plants typically have showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. They often feature heart-shaped leaves and produce capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Grumilea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
