Gymnocalycium onychacanthum

Gymnocalycium onychacanthum, commonly known as the spiny moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stem that resembles a moon, covered in spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium onychacanthum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
