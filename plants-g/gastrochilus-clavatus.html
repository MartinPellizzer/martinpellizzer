Gastrochilus clavatus

Gastrochilus clavatus, commonly known as the club-shaped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique club-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinct coloration that varies among cultivars.

Gastrochilus clavatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
