Gonialoe dinteri

Gonialoe dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's Aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, tall flower stalks with yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Gonialoe dinteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
