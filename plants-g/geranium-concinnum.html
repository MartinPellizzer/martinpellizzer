Geranium concinnum

Geranium concinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers from late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Geranium concinnum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
