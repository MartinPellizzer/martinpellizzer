Gnetum pendulum

Gnetum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging gnetum, is a unique member of the Gnetophyta division, characterized by its gymnosperm-like features.

It is a climbing shrub that can reach significant heights, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has scale-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically unisexual. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red aril, a feature that aids in seed dispersal by animals.

Gnetum pendulum is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common epiphyte in forest canopies.
