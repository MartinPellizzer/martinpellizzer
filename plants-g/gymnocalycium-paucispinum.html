Gymnocalycium paucispinum

Gymnocalycium paucispinum, commonly known as the few-spined moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with sparse, pale spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green or blue-green foliage. It is a succulent plant, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium paucispinum is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
