Gomphrena brachystylis

Gomphrena brachystylis, commonly known as the dwarf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, globular flower heads. The plant produces small, pink to red bracts that resemble petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Gomphrena brachystylis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
