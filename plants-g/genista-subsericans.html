Genista subsericans

Genista subsericans, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and stipulate leaves, which are often mistaken for true leaves.

Genista subsericans is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
