Grevillea lavandulacea var. latifolia

Grevillea lavandulacea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved honey bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces vibrant red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and full sun, making it drought-tolerant once established. Its nectar-rich flowers attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
