Grammitis turquina

Grammitis turquina, commonly known as the Turquino fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it anchor to its substrate.

The leaves are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive, finely divided structure.
