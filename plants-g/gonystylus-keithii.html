Gonystylus keithii

Gonystylus keithii, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Gonystylus keithii is typically found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
