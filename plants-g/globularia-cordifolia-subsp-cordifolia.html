Globularia cordifolia subsp. cordifolia

Globularia cordifolia subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, rounded clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped, hence its common name, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
