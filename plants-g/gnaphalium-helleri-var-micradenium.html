Gnaphalium helleri var. micradenium

Gnaphalium helleri var. micradenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant's leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
