Grammosperma

Grammosperma, commonly known as the grass family, is a large and diverse group of flowering plants belonging to the order Poales.

It includes over 12,000 species, many of which are economically important for agriculture, livestock, and ecological systems. The plants typically have narrow, linear leaves and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. Grammosperma species are characterized by their rhizomatous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environments.

They play a crucial role in soil stabilization and are a primary food source for many herbivores.
