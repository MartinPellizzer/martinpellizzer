Gentianella plebeia

Gentianella plebeia, commonly known as the common gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, bright blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella plebeia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
