Gastrolobium hamulosum

Gastrolobium hamulosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils. It is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Gastrolobium hamulosum is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions.
