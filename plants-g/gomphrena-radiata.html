Gomphrena radiata

Gomphrena radiata, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Gomphrena radiata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
