Gnaphalium capitatum

Gnaphalium capitatum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are surrounded by white to pale yellow bracts, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are linear, hairy, and often arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
