Grammitis athroosperma

Grammitis athroosperma, commonly known as the clustered maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arranged in a clustered growth pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Grammitis athroosperma thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
