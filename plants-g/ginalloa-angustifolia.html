Ginalloa angustifolia

Ginalloa angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ginalloa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
