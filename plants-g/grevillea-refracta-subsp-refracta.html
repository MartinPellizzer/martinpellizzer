Grevillea refracta subsp. refracta

Grevillea refracta subsp.

refracta, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a low-maintenance choice for gardens.
