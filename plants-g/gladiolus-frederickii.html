Gladiolus frederickii

Gladiolus frederickii, commonly known as the Frederick's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
