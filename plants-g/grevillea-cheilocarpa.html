Grevillea cheilocarpa

Grevillea cheilocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or greyish tinge. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in cylindrical clusters, which appear in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Grevillea cheilocarpa is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
