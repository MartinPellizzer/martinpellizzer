Gentiana flavescens

Gentiana flavescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
