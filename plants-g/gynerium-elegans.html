Gynerium elegans

Gynerium elegans, commonly known as the elegant giant reed, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing heads. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, often forming dense clumps at the base.

Gynerium elegans thrives in moist soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and marshes.
