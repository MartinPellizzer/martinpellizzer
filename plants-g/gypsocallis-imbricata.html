Gypsocallis imbricata

Gypsocallis imbricata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, often cushion-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

Gypsocallis imbricata is often found growing in crevices or on rock faces, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
