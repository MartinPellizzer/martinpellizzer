Geum donianum

Geum donianum, commonly known as the Donian avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Geum donianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
