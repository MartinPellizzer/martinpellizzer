Gastrolobium reflexum

Gastrolobium reflexum, commonly known as the wattleblow, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.

Gastrolobium reflexum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
