Gossypium herbaceum var. africanum

Gossypium herbaceum var. africanum, commonly known as African cotton, is a species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is an annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers encasing its seeds, which are harvested for textile use. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its historical significance in traditional textile production, it remains an important crop in certain regions of Africa.
