Gaura mutabilis

Gaura mutabilis, commonly known as the fairy wand or whirling dancer, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer and are known for their subtle, sweet fragrance.

Gaura mutabilis is also noted for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
