Galactia earlei subsp. toaensis

Galactia earlei subsp.

toaensis, commonly known as the Toaensis Milkvine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Central America. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its stems are often hairy, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
