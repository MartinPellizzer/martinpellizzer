Gentiana dimorphophylla

Gentiana dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, often at high altitudes. Its leaves are distinctive, with two different forms on either side of the stem, giving it its scientific name.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
