Gnaphalium diminutivum

Gnaphalium diminutivum, commonly known as the small everlasting or dwarf everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, densely packed flower heads that resemble miniature daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize new areas.

Its compact size and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for garden use in rock gardens and as a decorative plant.
