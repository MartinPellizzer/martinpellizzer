Geum japonicum var. japonicum

Geum japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese avens, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often used in ornamental gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
