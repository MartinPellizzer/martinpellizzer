Guzmania musaica

Guzmania musaica, commonly known as the banana leaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette, creating a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often resembling a banana flower. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Guzmania musaica is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
