Georgina variabilis var. lilacina

Georgina variabilis var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered georgina, is a striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant features vibrant lilac-colored petals that contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
