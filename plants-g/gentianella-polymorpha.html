Gentianella polymorpha

Gentianella polymorpha, commonly known as the variable gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its flowers are tubular and have five lobes, with prominent blue or purple markings.

Gentianella polymorpha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
