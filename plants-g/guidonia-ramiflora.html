Guidonia ramiflora

Guidonia ramiflora, commonly known as the branched flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, often giving the plant a fiery appearance. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, bark-like texture, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
