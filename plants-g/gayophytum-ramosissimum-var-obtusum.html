Gayophytum ramosissimum var. obtusum

Gayophytum ramosissimum var. obtusum, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red calyces resembling small lanterns. The fruit, which is edible, has a tangy, sweet flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
