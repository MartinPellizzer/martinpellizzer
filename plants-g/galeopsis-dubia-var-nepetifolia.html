Galeopsis dubia var. nepetifolia

Galeopsis dubia var. nepetifolia, commonly known as the common hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins and hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf shape and floral arrangement.
