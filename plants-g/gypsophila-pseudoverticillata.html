Gypsophila pseudoverticillata

Gypsophila pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences that resemble small clouds or dusters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gypsophila pseudoverticillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
