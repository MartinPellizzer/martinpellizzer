Garcinia uniflora

Garcinia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its fruit, which is typically small and contains a single seed. Garcinia uniflora is often found in humid, lowland areas with rich soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
