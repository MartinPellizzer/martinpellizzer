Gleditsia triacanthos lusus pendula

Gleditsia triacanthos lusus pendula, commonly known as the weeping honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a striking ornamental plant. The tree produces small, green pods that contain several seeds, and its compound leaves are typically bipinnately divided. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The weeping honeylocust is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
