Granadilla lutea

Granadilla lutea, commonly known as the yellow granadilla, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow fruits, which are covered in a rough, spiny rind. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture.

The fruit, which ripens to a vibrant orange-yellow, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
