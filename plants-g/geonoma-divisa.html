Geonoma divisa

Geonoma divisa, commonly known as the divided fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are divided into numerous small, pointed segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive in high humidity. Its rhizome is short and thick, supporting the growth of its feathery foliage.

Geonoma divisa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to add a lush, green texture to shaded gardens.
