Gymnopsis wedelioides

Gymnopsis wedelioides, commonly known as the wedgeleaf cactus, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and reduced leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gymnopsis wedelioides is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its structure and morphology reflect evolutionary adaptations to conserve moisture and withstand extreme temperatures.
