Gorteria corymbosa

Gorteria corymbosa, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gorteria corymbosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
