Gilia Micrantha F. Longituba

Gilia micrantha f. longituba is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the western united states. it belongs to the evening primrose family and is known for its slender stems and small, pale blue or white flowers. this particular form of gilia is distinguished by its elongated tubers, which are unique to this subspecies. it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study.
