Gentiana qiujiangensis

Gentiana qiujiangensis, commonly known as the Qiujiang gentian, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
