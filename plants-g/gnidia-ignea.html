Gnidia ignea

Gnidia ignea, commonly known as the red gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers with a yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, upright stems. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Gnidia ignea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
