Geranium berteroanum var. philippii

Geranium berteroanum var. philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
