Glyceria distans var. maritima

Glyceria distans var. maritima, commonly known as salt marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow leaves adapted to withstand salt spray and periodic flooding. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically clustered in groups.

This variety is important for stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
