Gilia leptomeria subsp. micromeria

Gilia leptomeria subsp.

micromeria, commonly known as the smallflower gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive bilabiate corolla. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
