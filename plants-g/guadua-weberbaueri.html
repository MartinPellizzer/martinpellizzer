Guadua weberbaueri

Guadua weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer bamboo, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate and strong, lightweight structural properties, making it valuable for sustainable construction and handicrafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive blue-green coloration, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Guadua weberbaueri is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
