Gladiolus fasciatus

Gladiolus fasciatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with white or pale yellow markings. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous base, which aid in its survival in arid environments.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
