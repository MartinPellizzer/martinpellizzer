Galium obscurum

Galium obscurum, commonly known as nightshade or lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, bladder-like capsules that release seeds when mature.

Galium obscurum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
