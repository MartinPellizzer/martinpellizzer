Gomphostemma scortechinii

Gomphostemma scortechinii, commonly known as the Mexican moonseed, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots and stems have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.
