Guarea glabra subsp. tuerckheimii

Guarea glabra subsp.

tuerckheimii, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant has simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
