Geranium cataractarum

Geranium cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium cataractarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
