Grammica jalapensis

Grammica jalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Grammica jalapensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
