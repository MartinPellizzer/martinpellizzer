Guilandina homblei

Guilandina homblei, commonly known as the giant forest vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its long, woody vines can reach lengths of over 30 meters, allowing it to climb trees and form dense canopy layers.

Guilandina homblei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various tropical fauna.
