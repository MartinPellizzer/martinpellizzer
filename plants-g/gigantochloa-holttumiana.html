Gigantochloa holttumiana

Gigantochloa holttumiana, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and structural strength make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and construction purposes.
