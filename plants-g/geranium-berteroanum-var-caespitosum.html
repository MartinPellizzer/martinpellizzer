Geranium berteroanum var. caespitosum

Geranium berteroanum var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
