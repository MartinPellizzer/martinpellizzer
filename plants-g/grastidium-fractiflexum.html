Grastidium fractiflexum

Grastidium fractiflexum, commonly known as the brittlebend fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, branching fronds that exhibit a peculiar flexibility.

Its leaves are thin and papery, with a slightly serrated edge that contributes to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests. It produces small, inconspicuous spores rather than flowers, relying on wind for dispersal.

Due to its fragile structure, it is particularly sensitive to environmental changes, making it an indicator species for ecosystem health.
