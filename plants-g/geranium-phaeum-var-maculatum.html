Geranium phaeum var. maculatum

Geranium phaeum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive red or purple spots, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long flowering period.
