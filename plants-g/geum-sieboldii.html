Geum sieboldii

Geum sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in cymes. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, woodland soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
