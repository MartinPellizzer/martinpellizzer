Goodenia

Goodenia, commonly known as the goodenias, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Goodeniaceae.

These plants are native to Australia, with some species found in South Africa and New Zealand. They are characterized by their distinctive, often tubular or spiny flowers and hairy, sometimes succulent leaves. Goodenia species typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant colors and unique foliage.
