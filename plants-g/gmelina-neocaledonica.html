Gmelina neocaledonica

Gmelina neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
