Genista cinerea subsp. cinerea

Genista cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as silver broom, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
