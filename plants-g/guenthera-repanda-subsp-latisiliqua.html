Guenthera repanda subsp. latisiliqua

Guenthera repanda subsp.

latisiliqua, commonly known as the broad-siliquae gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

The subspecies latisiliqua is distinguished by its broader siliquae (fruit pods) compared to other subspecies of Guenthera repanda.
