Glycosmis pentaphylla var. cyanocarpa

Glycosmis pentaphylla var. cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
