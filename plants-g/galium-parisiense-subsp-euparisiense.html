Galium parisiense subsp. euparisiense

Galium parisiense subsp.

euparisiense, commonly known as the Paris groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It typically features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly heart-shaped base.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
