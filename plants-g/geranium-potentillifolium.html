Geranium potentillifolium

Geranium potentillifolium, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high altitudes. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals. It is adapted to cold climates and has a compact growth habit to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Geranium potentillifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
