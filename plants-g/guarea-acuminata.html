Guarea acuminata

Guarea acuminata, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable wood.

The botanical features of Guarea acuminata make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
