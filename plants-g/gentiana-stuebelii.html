Gentiana stuebelii

Gentiana stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana stuebelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
