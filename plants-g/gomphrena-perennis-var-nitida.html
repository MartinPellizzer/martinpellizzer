Gomphrena perennis var. nitida

Gomphrena perennis var. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf gomphrena, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant features narrow, silver-hairy leaves that give it its common name and contribute to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its long bloom period, extending from summer into fall, adds ornamental value to garden landscapes.
