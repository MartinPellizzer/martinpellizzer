Gentianella chrysotaenia

Gentianella chrysotaenia, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Gentianella chrysotaenia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
