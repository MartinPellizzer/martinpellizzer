Gypsophila thyraica

Gypsophila thyraica, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its small, fluffy flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance.

Gypsophila thyraica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
