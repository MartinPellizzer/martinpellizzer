Galphimia glandulosa var. oblongifolia

Galphimia glandulosa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable in tropical and subtropical regions.
