Gossypium latifolium f. cambodiense

Gossypium latifolium f. cambodiense, commonly known as the Cambodian cotton plant, is a wild cotton species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy cotton-like fibers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a woody stem and showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to different climates.
