Gesneria citrina

Gesneria citrina, commonly known as the yellow gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria citrina is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
