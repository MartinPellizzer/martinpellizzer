Gardoquia incana

Gardoquia incana, commonly known as the gray hair grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Gardoquia incana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Poaceae family, known for its adaptability to harsh conditions.
