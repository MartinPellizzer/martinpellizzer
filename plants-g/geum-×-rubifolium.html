Geum × rubifolium

Geum × rubifolium, commonly known as the fireweed or redleaf bergamot, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with palmately lobed leaves that are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

Geum × rubifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
