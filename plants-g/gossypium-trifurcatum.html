Gossypium trifurcatum

Gossypium trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-lobed cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing fluffy, white fibers.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential for genetic research.
