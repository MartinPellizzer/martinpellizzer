Galium acutum

Galium acutum, commonly known as sharpstem bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Galium acutum is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
