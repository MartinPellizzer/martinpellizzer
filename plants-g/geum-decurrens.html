Geum decurrens

Geum decurrens, commonly known as spreading buttercup, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological gardens.
