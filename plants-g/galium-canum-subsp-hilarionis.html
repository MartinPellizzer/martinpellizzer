Galium canum subsp. hilarionis

Galium canum subsp.

hilarionis, commonly known as Hilarion's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems that often lie prostrate or ascending.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
