Gorteria pinnata

Gorteria pinnata, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slight hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
