Galtonia

Galtonia, commonly known as the African lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and orange. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and produce bulbs that store nutrients for growth. Galtonia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as houseplants.

They thrive in well-drained soil and prefer full sun to partial shade.
