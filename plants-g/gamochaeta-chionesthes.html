Gamochaeta chionesthes

Gamochaeta chionesthes, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically bloom in late summer to early fall.

Gamochaeta chionesthes is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.
