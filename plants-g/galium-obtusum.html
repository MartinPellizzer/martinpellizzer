Galium obtusum

Galium obtusum, commonly known as sticky Willie or soft-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Galium obtusum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.
