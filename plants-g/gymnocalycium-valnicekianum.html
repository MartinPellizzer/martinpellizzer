Gymnocalycium valnicekianum

Gymnocalycium valnicekianum, commonly known as the Valnicek's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
