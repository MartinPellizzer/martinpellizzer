Grewia polygama var. angustifolia

Grewia polygama var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
