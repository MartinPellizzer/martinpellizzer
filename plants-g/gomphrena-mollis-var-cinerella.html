Gomphrena mollis var. cinerella

Gomphrena mollis var. cinerella, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist or smoke, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It is a hardy annual or short-lived perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Gomphrena mollis var. cinerella is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
