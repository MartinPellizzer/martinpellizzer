Gompholobium polyzygum

Gompholobium polyzygum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are grouped in spikes and bloom during the cooler months. The plant has compound leaves with linear leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Gompholobium polyzygum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
