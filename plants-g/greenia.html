Greenia

Greenia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, silver-green foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant typically features compound leaves with a feathery texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Greenia thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
