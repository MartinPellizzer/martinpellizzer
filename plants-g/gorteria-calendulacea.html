Gorteria calendulacea

Gorteria calendulacea, commonly known as the Cape golden daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It features a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Gorteria calendulacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.
