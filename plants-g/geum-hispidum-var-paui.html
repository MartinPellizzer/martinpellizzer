Geum hispidum var. paui

Geum hispidum var. paui, commonly known as the rough-stemmed avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, five-petaled, and range in color from yellow to orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
