Galinsoga crozierae

Galinsoga crozierae, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its seeds are small, oval, and often have a pappus for wind dispersal.

Galinsoga crozierae is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
