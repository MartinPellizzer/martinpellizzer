Glyptostrobus sinensis

Glyptostrobus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese swamp cedar, is a rare and ancient conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and its distinctive, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.
