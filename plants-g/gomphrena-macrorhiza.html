Gomphrena macrorhiza

Gomphrena macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant roundleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Gomphrena macrorhiza is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
