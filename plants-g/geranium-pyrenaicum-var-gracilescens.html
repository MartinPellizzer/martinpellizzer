Geranium pyrenaicum var. gracilescens

Geranium pyrenaicum var. gracilescens, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
