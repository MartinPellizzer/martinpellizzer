Gomphrena lanata var. parvifolia

Gomphrena lanata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and its showy, spiky flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers bloom in summer and are known for their long-lasting appearance when dried.
