Glechon paraguariensis

Glechon paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and can grow up to several meters in height. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind or animals, aiding in the plant's propagation.
