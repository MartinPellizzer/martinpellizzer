Gladiolus sericeovillosus var. ludwigii

Gladiolus sericeovillosus var. ludwigii, commonly known as the Ludwig's gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a silvery sheen due to fine, silky hairs covering the surface. The plant blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of flowers that are popular in gardens for their ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
