Grevillea commutata subsp. commutata

Grevillea commutata subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers in spikes. It is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
