Gymnocalycium marquezii var. argentinense

Gymnocalycium marquezii var. argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine moon cactus, is a succulent native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems for survival in arid conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
