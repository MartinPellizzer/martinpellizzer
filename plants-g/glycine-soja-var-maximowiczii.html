Glycine soja var. maximowiczii

Glycine soja var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese milk vetch, is a perennial legume native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in traditional agriculture and herbal medicine.
