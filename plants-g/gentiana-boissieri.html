Gentiana boissieri

Gentiana boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its stems are often branched and robust.

Gentiana boissieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
