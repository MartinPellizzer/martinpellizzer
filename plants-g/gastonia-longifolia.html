Gastonia longifolia

Gastonia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf gastonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinct elongated shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gastonia longifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
