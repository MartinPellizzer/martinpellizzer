Geranium aristisepalum var. michoacanum

Geranium aristisepalum var. michoacanum, commonly known as the Michoacan geranium, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred calyx. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
