Gypsophila damascena

Gypsophila damascena, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly hairy, often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

Gypsophila damascena is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
