Gentiana propinqua

Gentiana propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
