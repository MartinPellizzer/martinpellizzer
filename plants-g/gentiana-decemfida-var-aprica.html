Gentiana decemfida var. aprica

Gentiana decemfida var. aprica, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has a basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a star-like pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
