Gardenia pictorum

Gardenia pictorum, commonly known as the painted gardenia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, fragrant white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant is evergreen and thrives in humid, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The fruit is a bright orange capsule that splits open to release seeds.
