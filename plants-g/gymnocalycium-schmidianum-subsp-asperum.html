Gymnocalycium schmidianum subsp. asperum

Gymnocalycium schmidianum subsp.

asperum, commonly known as the Asperum Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by pale green fruits. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
