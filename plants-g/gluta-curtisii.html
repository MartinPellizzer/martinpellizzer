Gluta curtisii

Gluta curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's gluta, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
