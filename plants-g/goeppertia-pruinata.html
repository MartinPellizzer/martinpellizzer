Goeppertia pruinata

Goeppertia pruinata, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety, waxy leaves that range in color from green to reddish-purple. The plant is often grown as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indirect light. It prefers warm, humid conditions and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia pruinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to care for with proper watering and light conditions.
