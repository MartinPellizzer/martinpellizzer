Genista dorycnifolia subsp. grosii

Genista dorycnifolia subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the Corsican broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are characteristic of the Genista genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
