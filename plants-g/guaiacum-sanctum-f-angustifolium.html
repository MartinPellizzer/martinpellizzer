Guaiacum sanctum f. angustifolium

Guaiacum sanctum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guaiacum, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, aromatic fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark and wood have been traditionally used for medicinal and commercial purposes.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
