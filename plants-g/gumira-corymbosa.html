Gumira corymbosa

Gumira corymbosa, commonly known as the African cherry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in corymbs, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
