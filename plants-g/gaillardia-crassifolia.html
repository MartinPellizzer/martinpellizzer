Gaillardia crassifolia

Gaillardia crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and native plant landscapes.
