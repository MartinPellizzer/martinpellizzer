Grindelia camporum var. davyi

Grindelia camporum var. davyi, commonly known as the Desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its resilience to drought and heat.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive component of desert ecosystems.
