Gladiolus imbricatus var. hygrophilus

Gladiolus imbricatus var. hygrophilus, commonly known as the water gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
