Gagea pratensis f. angustifolia

Gagea pratensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spring beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, usually blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, and the plant has a short, rhizomatous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
