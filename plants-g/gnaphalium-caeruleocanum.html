Gnaphalium caeruleocanum

Gnaphalium caeruleocanum, commonly known as blue-veined cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often forming a compact, mat-like growth. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of southern Africa. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium caeruleocanum is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which releases a subtle scent when crushed.
