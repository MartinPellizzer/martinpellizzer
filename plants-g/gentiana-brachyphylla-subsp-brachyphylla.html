Gentiana brachyphylla subsp. brachyphylla

Gentiana brachyphylla subsp.

brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are short and broad, with a rough texture, and its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
