Glochidion cauliflorum

Glochidion cauliflorum, commonly known as the calico tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique cauliflorous flowering habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly on the trunk and branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Glochidion cauliflorum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
