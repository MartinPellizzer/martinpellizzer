Guibourtia liberiensis

Guibourtia liberiensis, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood with a golden-brown color and a fine grain. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture, construction, and musical instruments.
