Gynandropsis denticulata

Gynandropsis denticulata, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to produce a large number of small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.
