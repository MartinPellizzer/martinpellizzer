Gagea indica

Gagea indica, commonly known as the Indian spring beauty, is a small, bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gagea indica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
