Gongora fulva

Gongora fulva, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Gongora fulva is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
