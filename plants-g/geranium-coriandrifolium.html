Geranium coriandrifolium

Geranium coriandrifolium, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing pink to red flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
