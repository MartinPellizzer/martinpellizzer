Garrya laurifolia var. oblonga

Garrya laurifolia var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf inkberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
