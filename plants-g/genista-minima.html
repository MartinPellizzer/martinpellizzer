Genista minima

Genista minima, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through root nodules. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Genista minima is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
