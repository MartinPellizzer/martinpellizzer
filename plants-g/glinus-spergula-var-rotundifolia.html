Glinus spergula var. rotundifolia

Glinus spergula var. rotundifolia, commonly known as round-leaf glinus, is a variant of the glinus plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Malvaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
