Garcinia diospyrifolia

Garcinia diospyrifolia, commonly known as the purple mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red or purple fruits. The fruit, which is typically about the size of a grapefruit, contains a segmented, white, edible aril surrounding the seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for cultivation in subtropical and tropical regions.
