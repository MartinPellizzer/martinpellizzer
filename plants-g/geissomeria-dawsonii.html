Geissomeria dawsonii

Geissomeria dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Geissomeria dawsonii is valued for its spring bloom and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
