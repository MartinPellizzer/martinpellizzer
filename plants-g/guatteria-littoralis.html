Guatteria littoralis

Guatteria littoralis, commonly known as the coastal guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It typically grows near coastal areas, thriving in sandy or limestone soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry.

Guatteria littoralis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various birds and mammals.
