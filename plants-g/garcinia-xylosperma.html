Garcinia xylosperma

Garcinia xylosperma, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, bitter pulp. Garcinia xylosperma is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
