Gelseminum caraiba

Gelseminum caraiba, commonly known as the Caribbean yellow nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, red berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Gelseminum caraiba is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
