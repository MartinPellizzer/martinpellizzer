Gomphrena perennis var. valdecristata

Gomphrena perennis var. valdecristata, commonly known as the everlasting pea or winter gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, papery bracts that resemble pea-like flowers. The plant is typically evergreen, with gray-green, fuzzy foliage that adds texture to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its long-lasting blooms, which retain their color even when dried.
