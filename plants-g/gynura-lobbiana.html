Gynura lobbiana

Gynura lobbiana, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Gynura lobbiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
