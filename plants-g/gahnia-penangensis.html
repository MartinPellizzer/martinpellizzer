Gahnia penangensis

Gahnia penangensis, commonly known as the Penang tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Gahnia penangensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
