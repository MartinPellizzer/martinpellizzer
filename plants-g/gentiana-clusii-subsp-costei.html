Gentiana clusii subsp. costei

Gentiana clusii subsp.

costei, commonly known as the Costean gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
