Geum ternatum

Geum ternatum, commonly known as threeleaf avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, giving it its common name, and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Geum ternatum is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
