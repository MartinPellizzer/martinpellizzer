Gagea foliosa subsp. durieui

Gagea foliosa subsp.

durieui, commonly known as the Durie's leafy gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in arid conditions, storing water and nutrients.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
