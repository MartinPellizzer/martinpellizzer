Goodenia dimorpha var. angustifolia

Goodenia dimorpha var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf goodenia, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Goodeniaceae family.
