Gonolobus reticulatus

Gonolobus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gonolobus reticulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
