Gynura auriformis

Gynura auriformis, commonly known as the golden taro or auricula plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive golden margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.

Gynura auriformis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.
