Genipa labiata

Genipa labiata, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a green, warty exterior and a soft, creamy yellow pulp. Its botanical features include a woody stem, palmate leaves, and a unique fruit structure that contains a single large seed.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
