Glycosmis sapindoides var. microphylla

Glycosmis sapindoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
