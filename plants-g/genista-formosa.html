Genista formosa

Genista formosa, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across its native range.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
