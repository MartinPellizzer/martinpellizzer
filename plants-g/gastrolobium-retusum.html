Gastrolobium retusum

Gastrolobium retusum, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes, and its compound leaves that are typically pinnate. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Gastrolobium retusum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.
