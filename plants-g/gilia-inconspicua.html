Gilia inconspicua

Gilia inconspicua, commonly known as the common gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and lance-shaped, and the stems are usually hairy.

Gilia inconspicua is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
