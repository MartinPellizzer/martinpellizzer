Glycine oblongifolia

Glycine oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods contain edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Glycine oblongifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
