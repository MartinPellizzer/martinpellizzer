Gaultheria yunnanensis var. hirsuta

Gaultheria yunnanensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy stonecrop, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to China.

It features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
