Gomphrena stellata

Gomphrena stellata, commonly known as the star globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and vibrant, papery bracts that resemble small globes. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pink, red, or purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena stellata is known for its long blooming period, often lasting from summer into fall.
