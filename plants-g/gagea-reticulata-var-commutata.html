Gagea reticulata var. commutata

Gagea reticulata var. commutata, commonly known as the netleaf gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its intricate, net-like pattern on the leaves, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or open meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
