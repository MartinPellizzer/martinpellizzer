Gerascanthus tetraphyllus

Gerascanthus tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leafed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
