Galium pallidulum

Galium pallidulum, commonly known as pale bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium pallidulum is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
