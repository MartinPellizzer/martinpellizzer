Gerbera brevipes

Gerbera brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with bright, showy petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Gerbera brevipes is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
