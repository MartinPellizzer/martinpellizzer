Gladiolus × byzantinus var. numidicus

Gladiolus × byzantinus var. numidicus, commonly known as the Numidian gladiolus, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of ornamental features.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is often cultivated for its beauty and is used in gardens and floral arrangements.
