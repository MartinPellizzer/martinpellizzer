Geum × pulchrum

Geum × pulchrum, commonly known as the showy geranium, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from red to pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, medium-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Geum × pulchrum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
