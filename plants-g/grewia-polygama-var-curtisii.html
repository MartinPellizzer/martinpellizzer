Grewia polygama var. curtisii

Grewia polygama var. curtisii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, inflated fruits resembling small lanterns. The fruits are typically orange to red in color when mature and contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
