Gyaxis

Gyaxis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows up to 2 to 3 feet tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Gyaxis is often found in woodland edges and wetlands, where it contributes to biodiversity and ecological balance.
