Gilbertiodendron preussii

Gilbertiodendron preussii, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
