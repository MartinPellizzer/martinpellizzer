Gompholobium sparsum

Gompholobium sparsum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Gompholobium sparsum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
