Glyphia lucida

Glyphia lucida, commonly known as the sparkling bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a faintly glowing appearance under certain light conditions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. Its leaves are broad and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Glyphia lucida is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
