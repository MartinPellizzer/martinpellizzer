Gymnopetalum integrifolium var. penicaudii

Gymnopetalum integrifolium var. penicaudii, commonly known as the Penicaud's smoothleaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, entire leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often along stream banks or in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this variant is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
