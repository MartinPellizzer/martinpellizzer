Gentianella campestris subsp. hypericifolia

Gentianella campestris subsp.

hypericifolia, commonly known as the field gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while the stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
