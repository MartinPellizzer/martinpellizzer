Gomphrena perennis subvar. genuina

Gomphrena perennis subvar. genuina, commonly known as the everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain vibrant even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.
