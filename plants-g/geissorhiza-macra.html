Geissorhiza macra

Geissorhiza macra, commonly known as the giant candleplant, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
