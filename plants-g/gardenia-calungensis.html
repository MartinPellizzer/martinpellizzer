Gardenia calungensis

Gardenia calungensis, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are highly valued for their ornamental beauty and are often used in floral arrangements.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
