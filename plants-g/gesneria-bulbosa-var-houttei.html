Gesneria bulbosa var. houttei

Gesneria bulbosa var. houttei, commonly known as the Houtte's gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming foliage with broad, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among succulent and bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
