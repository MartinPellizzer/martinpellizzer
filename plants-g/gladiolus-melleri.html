Gladiolus melleri

Gladiolus melleri, commonly known as the Mountain Gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and is well adapted to cool, mountainous climates. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help it thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Gladiolus melleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Iridaceae family.
