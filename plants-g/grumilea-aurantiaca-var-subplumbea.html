Grumilea aurantiaca var. subplumbea

Grumilea aurantiaca var. subplumbea, commonly known as the purple-leaved citrus.

This variant is characterized by its striking deep purple foliage, which contrasts with its typical orange or yellow leaves. It is a member of the Rutaceae family, sharing some traits with other citrus plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with fragrant, small white flowers.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
