Glyceria salina var. maritima

Glyceria salina var. maritima, commonly known as saltgrass, is a halophytic perennial grass native to coastal areas.

It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, exhibiting adaptations to high salt concentrations. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions makes it an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.
