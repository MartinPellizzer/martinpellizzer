Gymnema sylvestre var. chinense

Gymnema sylvestre var. chinense, commonly known as the Indian maple or the sugar leaf plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and red berries that contain seeds. It is well-known for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine.

The leaves contain compounds like gymnemic acids, which are believed to have various health benefits.
