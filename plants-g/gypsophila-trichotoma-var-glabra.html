Gypsophila trichotoma var. glabra

Gypsophila trichotoma var. glabra, commonly known as the naked stem gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that are often hairless, as indicated by its varietal name "glabra.". The plant produces clusters of small, delicate white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
