Gentiana primuliflora

Gentiana primuliflora, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana primuliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
