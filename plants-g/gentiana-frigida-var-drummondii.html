Gentiana frigida var. drummondii

Gentiana frigida var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full to partial sun. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Drummond's gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
