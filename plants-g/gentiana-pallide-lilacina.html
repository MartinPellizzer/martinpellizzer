Gentiana pallide-lilacina

Gentiana pallide-lilacina, commonly known as the pale-lilac gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are characterized by their funnel-shaped structure and a pale lilac color, which is unique among gentians.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
