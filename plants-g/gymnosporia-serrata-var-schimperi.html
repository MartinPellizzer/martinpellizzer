Gymnosporia serrata var. schimperi

Gymnosporia serrata var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian thornapple, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential medicinal uses.
