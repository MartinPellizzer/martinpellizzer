Gastrochilus ramosus

Gastrochilus ramosus, commonly known as the branched slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Gastrochilus ramosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
