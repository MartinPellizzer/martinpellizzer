Gentianella thiosphaera var. macroclada

Gentianella thiosphaera var. macroclada, commonly known as the large-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
