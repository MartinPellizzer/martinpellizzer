Gladiolus mucronatus

Gladiolus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence is a spike of clustered blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
