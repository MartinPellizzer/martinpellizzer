Gonolobus ophioglossa

Gonolobus ophioglossa, commonly known as the zigzag goldenhead, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
