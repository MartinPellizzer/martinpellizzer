Galactia schomburgkii

Galactia schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its milky sap, which gives it its common name, contains alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine.

Galactia schomburgkii is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility.
