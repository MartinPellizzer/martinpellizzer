Garcinia sumbawensis

Garcinia sumbawensis, commonly known as Sumbawan garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
