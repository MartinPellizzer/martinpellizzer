Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. obovata

Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian huckleberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It features small, elliptic to obovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, tart red berries in the fall. These berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
