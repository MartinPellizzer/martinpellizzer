Garcinia quaesita

Garcinia quaesita, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits that are highly aromatic. The fruit's pulp contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid, which has attracted scientific interest.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
