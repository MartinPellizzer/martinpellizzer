Genista tinctoria subsp. hungarica

Genista tinctoria subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian broom, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Basin. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dyeing textiles.
