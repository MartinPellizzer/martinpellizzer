Galium elongatum subsp. debile

Galium elongatum subsp.

debile, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. Galium elongatum subsp.

debile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
