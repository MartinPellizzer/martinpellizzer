Gaura triangulata

Gaura triangulata, commonly known as the triangular gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its blooms attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, adding both beauty and ecological value to the landscape.
