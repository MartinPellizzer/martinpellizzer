Gastrolobium mondurup

Gastrolobium mondurup, commonly known as the Mundurup hakea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract nectar-feeding insects. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, linear leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to the arid and semi-arid environments of its native habitat.
