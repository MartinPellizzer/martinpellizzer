Galium × ceretanum

Galium × ceretanum, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture.

Galium × ceretanum is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its ground-covering ability.
