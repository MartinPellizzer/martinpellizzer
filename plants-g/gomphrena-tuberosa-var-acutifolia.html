Gomphrena tuberosa var. acutifolia

Gomphrena tuberosa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble fireworks, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season add to its appeal in both garden and landscape settings.
