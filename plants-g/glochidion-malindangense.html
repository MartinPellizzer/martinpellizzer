Glochidion malindangense

Glochidion malindangense, commonly known as the Mindanao glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
