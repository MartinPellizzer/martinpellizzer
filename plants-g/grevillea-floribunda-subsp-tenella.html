Grevillea floribunda subsp. tenella

Grevillea floribunda subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
