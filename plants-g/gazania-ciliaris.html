Gazania ciliaris

Gazania ciliaris, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Gazania ciliaris thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray, which contributes to its hardiness in various environments.
