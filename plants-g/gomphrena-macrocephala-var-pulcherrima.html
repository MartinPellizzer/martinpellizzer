Gomphrena macrocephala var. pulcherrima

Gomphrena macrocephala var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are surrounded by bracts, which contribute to its distinctive shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
