Gladiolus distichus

Gladiolus distichus, commonly known as the two-ranked gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of colorful, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it its common name. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
