Gymnosphaera atropurpurea

Gymnosphaera atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf sandmat, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that are often tinged with a deep purple color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, where it thrives in sandy soils.

Gymnosphaera atropurpurea is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
