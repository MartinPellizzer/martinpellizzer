Goeppertia ucayalina

Goeppertia ucayalina, commonly known as the Amazonian calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with deep green veins and pale yellow or white margins. The plant typically grows as an understory species, thriving in high humidity and indirect light conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Goeppertia ucayalina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
