Galium bungei

Galium bungei, commonly known as Bunge's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It features slender, ascending stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Galium bungei is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
