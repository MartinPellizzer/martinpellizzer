Gelasia acantholimon

Gelasia acantholimon, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Gelasia acantholimon is often used in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
