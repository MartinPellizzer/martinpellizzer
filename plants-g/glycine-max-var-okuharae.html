Glycine max var. okuharae

Glycine max var. okuharae, commonly known as Okuhara soybean, is a lesser-known variant of the soybean plant native to Japan.

This variety is characterized by its distinct pod and seed morphology, with smaller, more elongated pods compared to standard soybeans. It exhibits a unique growth habit, often producing a more compact plant structure. The leaves are typically smaller and more hairy than those of common soybean varieties.

This variety is valued for its potential in specialty markets and for its adaptability to specific climatic conditions.
