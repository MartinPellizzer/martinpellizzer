Glossorhyncha fimbriata var. gracilis

Glossorhyncha fimbriata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fringed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with intricate, fringed petals that are typically a mix of white and pale yellow. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
