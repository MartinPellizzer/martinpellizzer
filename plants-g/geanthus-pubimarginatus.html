Geanthus pubimarginatus

Geanthus pubimarginatus, commonly known as the hairy-margin gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
