Grastidium emarginatum

Grastidium emarginatum, commonly known as the margined grastidium, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves that are marginally notched, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its limited habitat and low population numbers, it is considered a vulnerable species.
