Geodorum citrinum var. albidopurpureum

Geodorum citrinum var. albidopurpureum, commonly known as the white-pink bamboo orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its unique inflorescence that bears clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a slight purplish tinge. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in forest understories, preferring well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical and subtropical gardens.
