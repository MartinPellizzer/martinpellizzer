Gunnera flavida

Gunnera flavida, commonly known as the golden gunnera, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
