Geranium pratense var. rubrum

Geranium pratense var. rubrum, commonly known as the red geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Geranium pratense var. rubrum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
