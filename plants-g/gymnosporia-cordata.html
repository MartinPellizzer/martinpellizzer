Gymnosporia cordata

Gymnosporia cordata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, linear leaves that are typically arranged alternately. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Gymnosporia cordata is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in traditional medicine.
