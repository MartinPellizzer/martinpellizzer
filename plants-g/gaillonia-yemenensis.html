Gaillonia yemenensis

Gaillonia yemenensis, commonly known as Yemen gallnut, is a shrub native to Yemen and parts of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and produces unique, spherical seed pods. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of natural gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and industry.

Gaillonia yemenensis is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
