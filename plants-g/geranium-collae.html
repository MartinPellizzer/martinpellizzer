Geranium collae

Geranium collae, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and violet. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with serrated edges, and are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
