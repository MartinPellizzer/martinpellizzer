Gompholobium grandiflorum var. setifolium

Gompholobium grandiflorum var. setifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved glory flower, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
