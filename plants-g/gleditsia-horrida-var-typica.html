Gleditsia horrida var. typica

Gleditsia horrida var. typica, commonly known as the thorny locust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10–20 cm long and composed of 15–30 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in racemes, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in dry, open woodlands and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
