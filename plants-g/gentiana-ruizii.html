Gentiana ruizii

Gentiana ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence.

Gentiana ruizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
