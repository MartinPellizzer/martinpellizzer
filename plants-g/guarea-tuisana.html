Guarea tuisana

Guarea tuisana, commonly known as the Guarea tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its buttressed roots and dense canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces woody capsules as fruits.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
