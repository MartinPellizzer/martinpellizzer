Gypsophila ixodes

Gypsophila ixodes, commonly known as the Persian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble tiny clouds, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila ixodes is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
