Gamochaeta humilis

Gamochaeta humilis, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gamochaeta humilis is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
