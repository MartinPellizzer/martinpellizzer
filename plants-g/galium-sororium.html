Galium sororium

Galium sororium, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are hairy and can branch at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, egg-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium sororium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
