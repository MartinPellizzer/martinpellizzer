Gnaphalium pulchrum

Gnaphalium pulchrum, commonly known as the pretty everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are often clustered in a rounded inflorescence. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
