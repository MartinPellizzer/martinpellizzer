Geum macrophyllum var. perincisum

Geum macrophyllum var. perincisum, commonly known as the Pacific goldcup, is a perennial herb native to western North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for wetland restoration and ecological gardens.
