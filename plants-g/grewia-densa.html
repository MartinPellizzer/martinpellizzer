Grewia densa

Grewia densa, commonly known as the sour fig or wild sour plum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
