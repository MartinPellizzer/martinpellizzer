Gnaphalium sieboldianum

Gnaphalium sieboldianum, commonly known as the Siebold's feather grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing pale yellow to white blooms.

The plant is noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
