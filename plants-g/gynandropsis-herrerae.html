Gynandropsis herrerae

Gynandropsis herrerae, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
