Gesneria gerardiana

Gesneria gerardiana, commonly known as the red-tipped gesneria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Gesneria gerardiana is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
