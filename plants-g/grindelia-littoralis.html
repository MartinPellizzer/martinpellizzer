Grindelia littoralis

Grindelia littoralis, commonly known as coastal tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand salty air and coastal winds.

Grindelia littoralis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
