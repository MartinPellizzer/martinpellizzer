Graphorkis pileata

Graphorkis pileata, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
