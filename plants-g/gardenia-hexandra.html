Gardenia hexandra

Gardenia hexandra, commonly known as the white gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white flowers that are highly fragrant. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gardenia hexandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in perfumery due to its strong scent.
