Gnaphalium roseum var. sordescens

Gnaphalium roseum var. sordescens, commonly known as the pink sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

The species is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
