Grindelia camporum var. abbreviata

Grindelia camporum var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed gumweed, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange flower clusters.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
