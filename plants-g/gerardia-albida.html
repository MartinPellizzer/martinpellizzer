Gerardia albida

Gerardia albida, commonly known as the white gerardia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and slender stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gerardia albida blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers.
