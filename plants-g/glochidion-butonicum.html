Glochidion butonicum

Glochidion butonicum, commonly known as the Buton glabrous leaf vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly hairless. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found growing in lowland forests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
