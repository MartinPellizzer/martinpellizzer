Gardenia pomodora

Gardenia pomodora, commonly known as the tomato gardenia, is a unique plant that combines features of both the gardenia and tomato genera.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that resemble tomatoes in appearance, though they are not edible. These berries contain seeds that can be used for propagation, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical significance lies in its hybrid traits and potential for horticultural interest.
