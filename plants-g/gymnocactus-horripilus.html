Gymnocactus horripilus

Gymnocactus horripilus, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale green spines and its ability to form large, rounded clumps. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
