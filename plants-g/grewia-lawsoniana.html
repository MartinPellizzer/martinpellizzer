Grewia lawsoniana

Grewia lawsoniana, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its unique foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
