Grevillea victoriae subsp. brindabella

Grevillea victoriae subsp.

brindabella, commonly known as the brindabella, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bright red or orange cylindrical flowers.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract nectar-feeding birds. Grevillea victoriae subsp.

brindabella is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
