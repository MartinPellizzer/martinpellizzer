Galium palustre f. elongatum

Galium palustre f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and woodland edges.
