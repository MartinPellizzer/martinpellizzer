Galium sylvestre var. austriacum

Galium sylvestre var. austriacum, commonly known as Austrian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
