Goyazia villosa

Goyazia villosa, commonly known as the Brazilian white cedar, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits. Goyazia villosa is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
