Gutierrezia corymbosa

Gutierrezia corymbosa, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia corymbosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
