Gentianodes trichotoma

Gentianodes trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its scientific name.

Gentianodes trichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
