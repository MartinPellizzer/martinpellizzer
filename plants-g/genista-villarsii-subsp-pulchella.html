Genista villarsii subsp. pulchella

Genista villarsii subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the purple broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Fabaceae family.
