Gaura gracilis f. glandulosa

Gaura gracilis f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a soft, ethereal beauty to gardens.
