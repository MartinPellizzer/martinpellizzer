Geranium killipianum

Geranium killipianum, commonly known as the Killip's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.

Geranium killipianum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
