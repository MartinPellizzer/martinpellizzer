Galenia dregeana

Galenia dregeana, commonly known as the red sandalwood or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galenia dregeana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
