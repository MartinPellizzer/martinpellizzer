Grevillea paradoxa

Grevillea paradoxa, commonly known as the "Coral Tree," is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which resemble coral and are highly attractive to birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually grey-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea paradoxa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
