Gentiana bavarica var. rotundifolia

Gentiana bavarica var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for specific pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
