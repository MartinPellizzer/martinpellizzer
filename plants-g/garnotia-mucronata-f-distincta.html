Garnotia mucronata f. distincta

Garnotia mucronata f. distincta, commonly known as the distincta form of the broad-leaved carnation, is a horticultural variant of the species Garnotia mucronata.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, double flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This form is prized for its compact growth habit and extended blooming period.
