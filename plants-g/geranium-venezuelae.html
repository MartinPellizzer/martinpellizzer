Geranium venezuelae

Geranium venezuelae, commonly known as the Venezuelan geranium, is a species native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its vibrant flowers, which range from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
