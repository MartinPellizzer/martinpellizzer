Glomera terrestris

Glomera terrestris, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers.

Glomera terrestris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
