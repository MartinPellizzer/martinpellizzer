Gymnocalycium marsoneri

Gymnocalycium marsoneri, commonly known as the Sonoran Pincushion Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Gymnocalycium marsoneri is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
