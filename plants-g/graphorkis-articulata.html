Graphorkis articulata

Graphorkis articulata, commonly known as the segmented orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
