Geranium neopumilum

Geranium neopumilum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Geranium neopumilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
