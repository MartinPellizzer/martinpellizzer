Gentiana catesbaei

Gentiana catesbaei, commonly known as the Carolina gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Gentiana catesbaei blooms in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
