Glycine phaseoloides

Glycine phaseoloides, commonly known as climbing lablab, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, followed by pod fruits containing edible seeds. Glycine phaseoloides is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and ornamental value.

It has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
