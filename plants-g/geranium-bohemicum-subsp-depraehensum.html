Geranium bohemicum subsp. depraehensum

Geranium bohemicum subsp.

depraehensum, commonly known as the Bohemian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
