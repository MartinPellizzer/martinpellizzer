Gnaphalium carpetanum

Gnaphalium carpetanum, commonly known as the carpeted cudweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, silver-tomentose leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds.

Gnaphalium carpetanum is generally hardy and drought-tolerant, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
