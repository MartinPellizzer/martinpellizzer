Gentiana buergeri

Gentiana buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are dark green and slightly serrated, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana buergeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
