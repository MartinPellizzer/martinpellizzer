Gnaphalium polifolium

Gnaphalium polifolium, commonly known as the many-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are often surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium polifolium is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
