Gerardia tenuifolia var. polyphylla

Gerardia tenuifolia var. polyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gerardia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of wetlands and swamps. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, creeping stem.

This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
