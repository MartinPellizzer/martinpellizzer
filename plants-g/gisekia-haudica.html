Gisekia haudica

Gisekia haudica, commonly known as the Haudicola thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Gisekia haudica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
