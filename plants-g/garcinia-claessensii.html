Garcinia claessensii

Garcinia claessensii, commonly known as the Cameroon starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking yellow-orange fruits. The fruit, which resembles a starfruit in appearance, is edible and has a tangy, acidic flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its cultivation is relatively rare outside its native habitat due to its specific environmental requirements.
