Gaudichaudia diandra

Gaudichaudia diandra, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features silver-green foliage with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Gaudichaudia diandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
