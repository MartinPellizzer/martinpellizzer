Gastrolobium graniticum

Gastrolobium graniticum, commonly known as the granite wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and tableland regions.

Gastrolobium graniticum is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
