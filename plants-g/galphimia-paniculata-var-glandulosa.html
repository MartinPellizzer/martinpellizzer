Galphimia paniculata var. glandulosa

Galphimia paniculata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular galphimia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in glandular hairs, which give it its varietal name.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
