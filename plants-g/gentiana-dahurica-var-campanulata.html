Gentiana dahurica var. campanulata

Gentiana dahurica var. campanulata, commonly known as the blue bell gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
