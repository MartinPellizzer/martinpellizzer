Galium normanii var. islandicum

Galium normanii var. islandicum, commonly known as the island bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the British Isles.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Galium normanii var. islandicum thrives in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in dune or cliff habitats.
