Genlisea cylindrica

Genlisea cylindrica, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular leaves that function as traps to capture tiny aquatic organisms. The plant's unique trapping mechanism involves a pitfall structure that lures and ensnares prey with a sticky secretion. Genlisea cylindrica thrives in nutrient-poor wetlands and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for studies on plant adaptation and predation.
