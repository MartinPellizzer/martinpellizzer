Grevillea commutata subsp. pinnatisecta

Grevillea commutata subsp.

pinnatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, pinnatisected leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in spikes, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and requires good drainage.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
