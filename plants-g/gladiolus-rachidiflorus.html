Gladiolus rachidiflorus

Gladiolus rachidiflorus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, sword-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
