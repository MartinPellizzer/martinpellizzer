Guzmania musaica var. concolor

Guzmania musaica var. concolor, commonly known as the red-tipped peace lily, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that features a striking red spathe with a yellow spadix, giving it a dramatic visual appeal. The plant's foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge along the veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
