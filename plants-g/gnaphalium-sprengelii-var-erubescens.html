Gnaphalium sprengelii var. erubescens

Gnaphalium sprengelii var. erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its stems are reddish in color, and the leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs.

The flower heads are small and often appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
