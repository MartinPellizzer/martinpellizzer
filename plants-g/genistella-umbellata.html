Genistella umbellata

Genistella umbellata, commonly known as the clustered genista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, umbel-like clusters, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
