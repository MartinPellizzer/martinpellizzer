Gastrochilus matsuran

Gastrochilus matsuran, commonly known as the Matsura's humphead orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hump-shaped pseudobulbs and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive red and yellow coloration. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories.

Gastrochilus matsuran is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic beauty.
