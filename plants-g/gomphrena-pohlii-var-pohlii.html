Gomphrena pohlii var. pohlii

Gomphrena pohlii var. pohlii, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble feathery plumes. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements further enhance its appeal as a decorative plant.
