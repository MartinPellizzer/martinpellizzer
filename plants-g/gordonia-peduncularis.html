Gordonia peduncularis

Gordonia peduncularis, commonly known as the yellow-barked beech, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in pendulous clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its unique bark and seasonal foliage.
