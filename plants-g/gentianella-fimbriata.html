Gentianella fimbriata

Gentianella fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with a distinctive fringed appearance on the corolla lobes.

Gentianella fimbriata is often found in open meadows and is a component of high-altitude flora.
