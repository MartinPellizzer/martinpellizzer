Gentiana pneumonanthe var. reyesii

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. reyesii, commonly known as the Reyes' gentian, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh mountain climate.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
