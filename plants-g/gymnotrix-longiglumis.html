Gymnotrix longiglumis

Gymnotrix longiglumis, commonly known as the long-bearded orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, long, hairy sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a unique, elongated labellum.

Gymnotrix longiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
