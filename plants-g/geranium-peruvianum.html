Geranium peruvianum

Geranium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
