Grindelia fraxinipratensis

Grindelia fraxinipratensis, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Grindelia fraxinipratensis is often found in coastal sage scrub and grassland habitats.
