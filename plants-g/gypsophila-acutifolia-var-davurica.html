Gypsophila acutifolia var. davurica

Gypsophila acutifolia var. davurica, commonly known as the Meadow Bluet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its ethereal appearance.
