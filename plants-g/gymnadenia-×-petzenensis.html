Gymnadenia × petzenensis

Gymnadenia × petzenensis, commonly known as the hybrid grass of the genus Gymnadenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its hybrid origin results from the cross between Gymnadenia cleistogama and Gymnadenia conopsea, giving it unique morphological features.

The flowers are usually pink or white, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
