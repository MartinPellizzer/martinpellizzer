Gentianella heterosepala subsp. durangensis

Gentianella heterosepala subsp.

durangensis, commonly known as the Durango gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes numerous species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue or violet flowers.

This subspecies is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems and its potential medicinal properties.
