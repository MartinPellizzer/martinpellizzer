Glandularia × uruguayensis

Glandularia × uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay gumplant, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves, which secrete a sticky substance that may deter herbivores. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
