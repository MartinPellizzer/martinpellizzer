Gentianella amarella subsp. heterosepala

Gentianella amarella subsp.

heterosepala, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
