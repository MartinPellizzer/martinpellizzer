Genipa poivrei

Genipa poivrei, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is distinctive for its bright red exterior and segmented, juicy interior. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
