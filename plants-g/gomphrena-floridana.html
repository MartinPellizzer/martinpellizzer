Gomphrena floridana

Gomphrena floridana, commonly known as the Florida gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, white, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena floridana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
