Gahnia javanica f. sumatrensis

Gahnia javanica f. sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense vegetation in marshy areas.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
