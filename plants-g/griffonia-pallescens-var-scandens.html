Griffonia pallescens var. scandens

Griffonia pallescens var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing spider plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
