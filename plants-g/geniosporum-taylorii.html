Geniosporum taylorii

Geniosporum taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's geniosporum, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. Its flowering stems are short and often bear a single flower cluster at the apex.

Geniosporum taylorii is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
