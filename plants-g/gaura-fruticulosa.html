Gaura fruticulosa

Gaura fruticulosa, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gaura fruticulosa is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
