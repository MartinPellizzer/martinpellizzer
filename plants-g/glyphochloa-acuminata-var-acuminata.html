Glyphochloa acuminata var. acuminata

Glyphochloa acuminata var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which aids in its spread.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
