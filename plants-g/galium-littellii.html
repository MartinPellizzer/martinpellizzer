Galium littellii

Galium littellii, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium littellii is often found in the understory of forests and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
