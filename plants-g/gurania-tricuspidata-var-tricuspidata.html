Gurania tricuspidata var. tricuspidata

Gurania tricuspidata var. tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-pointed gurania, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinct pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
