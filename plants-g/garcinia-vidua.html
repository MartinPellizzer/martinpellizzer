Garcinia vidua

Garcinia vidua, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Garcinia vidua is noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
