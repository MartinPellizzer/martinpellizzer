Garcinia bangweolensis

Garcinia bangweolensis, commonly known as the Bangweol orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its unique chemical composition, including high levels of hydroxycitric acid. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Garcinia bangweolensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
