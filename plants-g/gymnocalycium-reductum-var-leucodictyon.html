Gymnocalycium reductum var. leucodictyon

Gymnocalycium reductum var. leucodictyon, commonly known as the white-flowered moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation for minimizing water loss.

The varietal name 'leucodictyon' refers to its distinctive white flower arrangement, which sets it apart from other Gymnocalycium species.
