Geonoma megalospatha

Geonoma megalospatha, commonly known as the giant spathate leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones, typical of ferns.

It plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to the understory vegetation and providing habitat for small organisms.
