Guzmania balanophora

Guzmania balanophora, commonly known as the silver spear, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a sword, giving it its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small pool that supports aquatic life. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, adding a dramatic visual contrast to the foliage.

Guzmania balanophora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
