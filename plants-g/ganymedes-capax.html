Ganymedes capax

Ganymedes capax, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving waters. The plant's flowers are large and showy, typically pink or white, with a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators like beetles. Ganymedes capax thrives in warm, nutrient-rich waters and plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and ecologically significant species.
