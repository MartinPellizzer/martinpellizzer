Gnaphalium pulchellum

Gnaphalium pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by white to pink ray florets. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Gnaphalium pulchellum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
