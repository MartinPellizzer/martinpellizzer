Geranium mascatense var. sublaeve

Geranium mascatense var. sublaeve, commonly known as the Mascat geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded and have a smooth texture, which distinguishes it from other geranium varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
