Gerascanthus pilosissimus

Gerascanthus pilosissimus, commonly known as the silky geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
