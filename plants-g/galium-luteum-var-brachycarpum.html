Galium luteum var. brachycarpum

Galium luteum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, opposite leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by small, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
