Galopina

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Galopina thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
