Gypsophila porphyrantha

Gypsophila porphyrantha, commonly known as the Porphyrantha gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila porphyrantha is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its charming appearance and hardy nature.
