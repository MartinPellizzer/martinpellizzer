Gagea ucrainica

Gagea ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and survival through harsh winters, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

Gagea ucrainica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
