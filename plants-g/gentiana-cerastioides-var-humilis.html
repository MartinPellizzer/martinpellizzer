Gentiana cerastioides var. humilis

Gentiana cerastioides var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it forms dense clusters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
