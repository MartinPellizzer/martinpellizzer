Geigeria engleriana

Geigeria engleriana, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Geigeria engleriana is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
