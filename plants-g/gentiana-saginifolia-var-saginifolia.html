Gentiana saginifolia var. saginifolia

Gentiana saginifolia var. saginifolia, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for alpine ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
