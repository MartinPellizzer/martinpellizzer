Gymnema subvolubile

Gymnema subvolubile, commonly known as the Indian cheese plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its unique ability to inhibit the perception of sweetness, a property that has been studied for potential applications in diabetes management. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which contribute to its medicinal uses.

Gymnema subvolubile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional herbal applications.
