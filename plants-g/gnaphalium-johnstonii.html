Gnaphalium johnstonii

Gnaphalium johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fine, white, powdery coating on its stems and leaves, which is composed of tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
