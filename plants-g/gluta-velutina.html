Gluta velutina

Gluta velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
