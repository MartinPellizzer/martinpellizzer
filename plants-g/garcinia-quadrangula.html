Garcinia quadrangula

Garcinia quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angled garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and four-angled stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellow or orange berry that contains a single seed.

Garcinia quadrangula is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive stem structure and attractive foliage.
