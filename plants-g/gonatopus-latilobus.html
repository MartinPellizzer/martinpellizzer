Gonatopus latilobus

Gonatopus latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant has showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, and it spreads through rhizomes.

Gonatopus latilobus plays an important role in water ecosystems by improving water quality and supporting biodiversity.
