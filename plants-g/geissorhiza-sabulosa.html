Geissorhiza sabulosa

Geissorhiza sabulosa, commonly known as the sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
