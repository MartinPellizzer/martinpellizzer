Gentiana covillei

Gentiana covillei, commonly known as Coville's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, tubular flowers.

Coville's gentian is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
