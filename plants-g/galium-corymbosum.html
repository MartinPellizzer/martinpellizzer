Galium corymbosum

Galium corymbosum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, slender leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium corymbosum is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
