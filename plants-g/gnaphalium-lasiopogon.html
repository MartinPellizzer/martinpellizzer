Gnaphalium lasiopogon

Gnaphalium lasiopogon, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense, woolly white hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. Its small, yellowish flower heads are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The leaves are usually linear to lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.
