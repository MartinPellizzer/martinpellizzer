Gnaphalium cirrhosum

Gnaphalium cirrhosum, commonly known as silverleaf mouseear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features dense, silvery-gray foliage and produces tiny, fluffy flower heads that are often mistaken for cotton. It is often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
