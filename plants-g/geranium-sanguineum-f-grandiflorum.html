Geranium sanguineum f. grandiflorum

Geranium sanguineum f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the greater garden geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that produce clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
