Gossypium javanicum

Gossypium javanicum, commonly known as Java cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-coated bolls that contain fibrous material, which is used for textile purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its historical significance in textile production, it has been cultivated for centuries in regions with suitable growing conditions.
