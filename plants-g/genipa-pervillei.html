Genipa pervillei

Genipa pervillei, commonly known as the genipap tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a dense canopy and thick, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruit is a spherical, green to red berry that turns dark when ripe. The fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is used in various culinary and traditional applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
