Glycine buettneri

Glycine buettneri, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to warm climates and is often grown for its edible beans.

Glycine buettneri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
