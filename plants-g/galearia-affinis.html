Galearia affinis

Galearia affinis, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Galearia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
