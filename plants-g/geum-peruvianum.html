Geum peruvianum

Geum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian bistort, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by distinctive, showy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Geum peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
