Gnaphalium remyanum

Gnaphalium remyanum, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
