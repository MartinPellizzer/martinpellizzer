Geum montanum var. superbum

Geum montanum var. superbum, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, with a rough, hairy texture, and stems that are usually unbranched. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of high-elevation ecosystems.
