Genista hungarica

Genista hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The shrub can reach heights of up to 1 meter and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
