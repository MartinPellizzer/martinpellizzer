Guadua tessmannii

Guadua tessmannii, commonly known as the tessmann bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. This plant is characterized by its strong, straight culms that can grow up to 15 meters in height. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Guadua tessmannii is valued for its durability and is used in construction and as a sustainable alternative to hardwood.
