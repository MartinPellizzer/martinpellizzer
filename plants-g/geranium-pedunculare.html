Geranium pedunculare

Geranium pedunculare, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium pedunculare is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
