Geissanthus pyramidatus

Geissanthus pyramidatus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Geissanthus pyramidatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
