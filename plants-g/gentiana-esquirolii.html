Gentiana esquirolii

Gentiana esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Gentiana esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
