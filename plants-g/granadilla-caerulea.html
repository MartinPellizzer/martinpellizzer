Granadilla caerulea

Granadilla caerulea, commonly known as the blue granadilla, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It produces striking blue or violet fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant belongs to the Passifloraceae family, which is characterized by its showy flowers and complex structures. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

The fruit's unique color and flavor make it a valuable ornamental and edible plant in tropical regions.
