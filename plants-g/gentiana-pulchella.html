Gentiana pulchella

Gentiana pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a single cluster at the top of the stem. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gentiana pulchella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.
