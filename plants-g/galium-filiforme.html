Galium filiforme

Galium filiforme, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Galium filiforme is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
