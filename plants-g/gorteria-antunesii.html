Gorteria antunesii

Gorteria antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes' gorteria, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Gorteria antunesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
