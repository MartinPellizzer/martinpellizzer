Galactia galactia

Galactia galactia, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Galactia galactia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
