Genista cilentina

Genista cilentina, commonly known as the Cilentan broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, feather-like leaves that are dense and evergreen.

Genista cilentina is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
