Grindelia camporum var. australis

Grindelia camporum var. australis, commonly known as the coastal gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flower heads that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to the harsh conditions of coastal environments.

Grindelia camporum var. australis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
