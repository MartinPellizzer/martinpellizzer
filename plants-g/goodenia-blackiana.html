Goodenia blackiana

Goodenia blackiana, commonly known as the blackiana goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and attract nectar-feeding insects such as bees and birds.

Goodenia blackiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
