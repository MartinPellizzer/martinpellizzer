Gymnocarpos fruticosus

Gymnocarpos fruticosus, commonly known as the desert sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible but often bitter. It is adapted to survive with minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Gymnocarpos fruticosus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for various wildlife species.
