Glycine dolichoides

Glycine dolichoides, commonly known as the longpod cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale purple, and it produces edible seeds within its pods.

Glycine dolichoides is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated as a forage crop.
