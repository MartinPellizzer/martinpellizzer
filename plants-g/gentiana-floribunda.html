Gentiana floribunda

Gentiana floribunda, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
