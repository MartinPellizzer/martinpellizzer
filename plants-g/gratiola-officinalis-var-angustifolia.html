Gratiola officinalis var. angustifolia

Gratiola officinalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved gravelroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often found in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported tonic and digestive properties.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
