Godoya splendida

Godoya splendida, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous bark, and its leaves are typically broad and ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
