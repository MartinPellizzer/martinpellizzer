Glycosmis quinquefolia

Glycosmis quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaved orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and small, orange-colored fruits. The leaves are typically five-lobed, giving the plant its common name, and they are glossy and aromatic. The fruits are edible, though they are generally not cultivated for commercial use.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and citrus-like scent.
