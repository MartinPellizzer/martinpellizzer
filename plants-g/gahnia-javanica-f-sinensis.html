Gahnia javanica f. sinensis

Gahnia javanica f. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese tussock grass, is a variant of the tall grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a stabilizing plant in erosion-prone areas.
