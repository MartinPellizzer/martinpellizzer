Grossularia non-scripta

Grossularia non-scripta, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It produces small, tart berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring season.
