Glossorhyncha Terrestris

Glossorhyncha chickweed, scientifically known as Glossorhyncha terrestris, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It thrives in rocky soils and dry environments, often found along rocky outcrops and in disturbed areas. The plant features small, pale green leaves and produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it an important component of local ecosystems. Despite its modest appearance, Glossorhyncha chickweed plays a vital role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
