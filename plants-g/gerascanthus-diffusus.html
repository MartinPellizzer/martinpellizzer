Gerascanthus diffusus

Gerascanthus diffusus, commonly known as the spreading geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
