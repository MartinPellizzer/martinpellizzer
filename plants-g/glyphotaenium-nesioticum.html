Glyphotaenium nesioticum

Glyphotaenium nesioticum, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily eucalyptus species.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that contain toxic seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that reflects light.

Despite its parasitic nature, Glyphotaenium nesioticum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain bird species.
