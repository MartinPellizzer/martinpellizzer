Grammitis erecta

Grammitis erecta, commonly known as the upright fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that often grow on trees or rocks. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its humid habitat. Grammitis erecta typically produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Its ornamental appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
