Galeopsis litoralis

Galeopsis litoralis, commonly known as seaside betony, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters. It is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Galeopsis litoralis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal insects.
