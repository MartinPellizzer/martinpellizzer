Gladiolus meridionalis

Gladiolus meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, sturdy stems with showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, ranging from white to deep red, and are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a rhizomatous root system.
