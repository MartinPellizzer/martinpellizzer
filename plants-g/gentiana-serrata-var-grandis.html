Gentiana serrata var. grandis

Gentiana serrata var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving it its scientific name.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
