Glycosmis lucida

Glycosmis lucida, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, woody stem and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

Glycosmis lucida is often found in lowland forests and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
