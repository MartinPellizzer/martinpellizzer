Genista candicans var. subspicata

Genista candicans var. subspicata, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.
