Grossularia reclinata

Grossularia reclinata, commonly known as the hanging gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a reddish color, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of mountain ecosystems.
