Geonoma ferruginea subsp. nicaraguensis

Geonoma ferruginea subsp.

nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan rusty fern, is a species of fern native to Central America. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds with a rusty-brown hue.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as tropical forests and streamside areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, rusty-colored coating, giving it its common name.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
