Gloeospermum sclerophyllum

Gloeospermum sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hard-leaved gloeospermum, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Gloeospermum sclerophyllum is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to pests.
