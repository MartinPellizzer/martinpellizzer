Geranium occitanicum

Geranium occitanicum, commonly known as the Southern geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and attractive, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium occitanicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
