Gentiana pneumonanthe f. diffusa

Gentiana pneumonanthe f. diffusa, commonly known as the diffuse gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
