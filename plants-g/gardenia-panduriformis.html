Gardenia panduriformis

Gardenia panduriformis, commonly known as the long-leaf gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elongated and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Gardenia panduriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
