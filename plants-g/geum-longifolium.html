Geum longifolium

Geum longifolium, commonly known as showy mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
