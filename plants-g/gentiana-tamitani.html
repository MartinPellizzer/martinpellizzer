Gentiana tamitani

Gentiana tamitani, commonly known as the Tamitani gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Gentiana tamitani is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
