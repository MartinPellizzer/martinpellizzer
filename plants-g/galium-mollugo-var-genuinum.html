Galium mollugo var. genuinum

Galium mollugo var. genuinum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
