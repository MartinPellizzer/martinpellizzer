Geranium platypetalum

Geranium platypetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive broad, rounded shape. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium platypetalum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought-resistant properties.
