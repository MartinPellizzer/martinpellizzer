Gentianella herrerae

Gentianella herrerae, commonly known as Herrera's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a lanceolate shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
