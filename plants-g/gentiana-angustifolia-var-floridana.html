Gentiana angustifolia var. floridana

Gentiana angustifolia var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamp edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
