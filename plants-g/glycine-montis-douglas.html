Glycine montis-douglas

Glycine montis-douglas, commonly known as the Douglas mountain bean, is a climbing legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Glycine montis-douglas thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
