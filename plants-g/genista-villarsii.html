Genista villarsii

Genista villarsii, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, branching stems are often covered in small, scale-like leaves.

Genista villarsii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various Mediterranean climates.
