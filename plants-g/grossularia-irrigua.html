Grossularia irrigua

Grossularia irrigua, commonly known as the Indian snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

Grossularia irrigua is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist climates.
