Gnaphalium subdecurrens

Gnaphalium subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces numerous tiny, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Gnaphalium subdecurrens is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in dried floral arrangements.
