Gnaphalium ovatum

Gnaphalium ovatum, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white or pale yellow inflorescences that resemble small clouds, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spherical or ovoid shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Gnaphalium ovatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
