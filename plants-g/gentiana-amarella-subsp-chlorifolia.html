Gentiana amarella subsp. chlorifolia

Gentiana amarella subsp.

chlorifolia, commonly known as the greenleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, darker green leaves compared to the typical gentian. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
