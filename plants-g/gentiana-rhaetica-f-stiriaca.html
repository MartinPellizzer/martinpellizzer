Gentiana rhaetica f. stiriaca

Gentiana rhaetica f. stiriaca, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathian mountains. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
