Gastrolobium propinquum

Gastrolobium propinquum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry landscapes.
