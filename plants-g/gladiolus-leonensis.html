Gladiolus leonensis

Gladiolus leonensis, commonly known as the Leonese gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, colorful spikes of showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with its inflorescence consisting of multiple florets arranged in a raceme. Its botanical name reflects its origin, with "leonensis" referring to the region of Leon in Spain.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
