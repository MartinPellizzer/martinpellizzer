Galactia smallii

Galactia smallii, commonly known as the small's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale purple, and it produces a milky sap that can be toxic.

Galactia smallii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain wildlife.
