Goeppertia angustifolia

Goeppertia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It features striking, variegated leaves with a distinctive pattern of silver and green, which are often marked with red veins. The plant is noted for its compact growth habit and is well-suited for indoor cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for foliage plants in both home and garden settings.
