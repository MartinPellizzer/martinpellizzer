Gymnocalycium fischeri

Gymnocalycium fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warm season, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, providing additional protection from herbivores.

Gymnocalycium fischeri is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.
