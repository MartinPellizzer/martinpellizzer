Galium aparine var. confertum

Galium aparine var. confertum, commonly known as sticky willow herb, is a variant of the common bedstraw species.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in fine, hooked hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, three-lobed berries.

This variant is adapted to a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and woodland edges, and is often found in moist environments.
