Gentiana germanica var. ramosissima

Gentiana germanica var. ramosissima, commonly known as the German gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
