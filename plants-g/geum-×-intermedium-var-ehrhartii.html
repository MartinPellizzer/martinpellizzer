Geum × intermedium var. ehrhartii

Geum × intermedium var. ehrhartii, commonly known as the Ehrhart's avens, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, semi-double to double red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with basal leaves that are heart-shaped and hairy. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
