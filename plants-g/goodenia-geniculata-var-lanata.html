Goodenia geniculata var. lanata

Goodenia geniculata var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly goodenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
