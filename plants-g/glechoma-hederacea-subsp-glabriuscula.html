Glechoma hederacea subsp. glabriuscula

Glechoma hederacea subsp.

glabriuscula, commonly known as hairy false shamrock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Glechoma genus, sharing similar morphological traits and ecological preferences.
