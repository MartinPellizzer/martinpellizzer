Gluta lanceolata

Gluta lanceolata, commonly known as the Philippine ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
