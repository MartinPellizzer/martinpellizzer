Gnetum interruptum

Gnetum interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with a distinctive venation pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed structure, which is an unusual feature among gymnosperms.

Gnetum interruptum is often found in moist, shaded habitats in Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
