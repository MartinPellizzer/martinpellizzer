Geranium peregrinum

Geranium peregrinum, commonly known as the wandering geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Geranium peregrinum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
