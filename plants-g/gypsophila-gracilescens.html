Gypsophila gracilescens

Gypsophila gracilescens, commonly known as the slender gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, clustered blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a fluffy appearance that resembles clouds.

Gypsophila gracilescens is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
