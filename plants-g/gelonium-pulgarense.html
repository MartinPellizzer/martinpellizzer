Gelonium pulgarense

Gelonium pulgarense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are covered in a fuzzy, waxy coating. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval in shape. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

The fruit is edible and often used in culinary applications, though it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
