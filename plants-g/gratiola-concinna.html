Gratiola concinna

Gratiola concinna, commonly known as the slender yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woods. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gratiola concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
