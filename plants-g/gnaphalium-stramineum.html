Gnaphalium stramineum

Gnaphalium stramineum, commonly known as the cloud plant or silverweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a dense mat of silver-gray foliage, with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, woolly leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its fluffy, cloud-like appearance gives it its common name, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant.
