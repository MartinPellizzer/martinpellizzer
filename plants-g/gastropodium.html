Gastropodium

Gastropodium, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped inflorescence and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or purple, are highly prized in horticulture for their ornamental value.
