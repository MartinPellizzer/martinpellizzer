Grammitis terpsichore

Grammitis terpsichore, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds with prominent silver veins that give it its common name. Native to tropical regions of the Americas, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in forest ecosystems.
