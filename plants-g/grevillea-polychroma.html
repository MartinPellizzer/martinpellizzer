Grevillea polychroma

Grevillea polychroma, commonly known as the multicolored grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feathery foliage that displays a range of colors from green to red and purple, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-like flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea polychroma is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
