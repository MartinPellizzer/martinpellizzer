Glochidion benguetense

Glochidion benguetense, commonly known as the Benguet glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
