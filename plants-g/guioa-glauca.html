Guioa glauca

Guioa glauca, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are woody, three-lobed capsules that split open to release seeds.

Guioa glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
