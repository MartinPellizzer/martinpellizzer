Guilandina nuga

Guilandina nuga, commonly known as the African snakewood, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces large, showy, cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant's fruit is a long, slender pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and roots have been utilized for their medicinal properties, including antifungal and anti-inflammatory effects.

Guilandina nuga thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
