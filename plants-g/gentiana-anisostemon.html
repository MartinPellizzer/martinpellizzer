Gentiana anisostemon

Gentiana anisostemon, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or woodland habitats.

The genus Gentiana is known for its showy, spurred flowers, which are adapted for pollination by insects such as bees and butterflies.
