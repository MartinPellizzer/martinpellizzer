Gymnocalycium rhodantherum var. cinerascens

Gymnocalycium rhodantherum var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silver moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white to silver-colored hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
