Gentiana tricholoba

Gentiana tricholoba, commonly known as the tricholoba gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
