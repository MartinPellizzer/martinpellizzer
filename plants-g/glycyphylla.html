Glycyphylla

Glycyphylla, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Glycyphylla thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of leguminous protein.
