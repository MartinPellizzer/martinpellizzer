Gigantochloa pubinervis

Gigantochloa pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy nerve grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, erect culms and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture on the nerves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in humid and tropical climates. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a loose, cylindrical panicle with spikelets arranged in two rows.

Gigantochloa pubinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of thatching material.
