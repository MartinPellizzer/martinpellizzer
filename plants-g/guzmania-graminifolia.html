Guzmania graminifolia

Guzmania graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaf bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped inflorescence, which features a vibrant, long-lasting flower spike that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Guzmania graminifolia is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
