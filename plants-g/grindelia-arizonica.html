Grindelia arizonica

Grindelia arizonica, commonly known as Arizona gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled spheres.

Grindelia arizonica is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
