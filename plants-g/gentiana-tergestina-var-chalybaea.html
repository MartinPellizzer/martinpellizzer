Gentiana tergestina var. chalybaea

Gentiana tergestina var. chalybaea, commonly known as the Chalybaea gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
