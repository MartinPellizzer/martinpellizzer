Guadua takahashiae

Guadua takahashiae, commonly known as the Takahashi bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it a useful material in traditional construction and crafts.
