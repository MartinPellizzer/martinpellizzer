Gomphia macranthos

Gomphia macranthos, commonly known as the giant pincushion plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous bracts that resemble a pincushion. The plant features tall, sturdy stems with a rough, hairy texture, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Gomphia macranthos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
