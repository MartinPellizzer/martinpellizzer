Galium japonicum var. trifloriforme

Galium japonicum var. trifloriforme, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
