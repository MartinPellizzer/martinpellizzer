Goupia tomentosa

Goupia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly goupia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a thick layer of white to grayish hairs.

The flowers are small, fragrant, and usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.
