Glinus oppositifolius var. keenanii

Glinus oppositifolius var. keenanii, commonly known as the Mexican spider plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant.
