Gnaphalium roseum var. angustifolium

Gnaphalium roseum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved rosemary weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and linear, typically covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces a distinctive, fragrant essential oil. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
