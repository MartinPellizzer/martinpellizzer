Gouania mauritiana subsp. myriocarpa

Gouania mauritiana subsp.

myriocarpa, commonly known as the Mauritius starfruit, is a member of the Polygonaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, star-shaped fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
