Gueldenstaedtia pauciflora var. subglabrata

Gueldenstaedtia pauciflora var. subglabrata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground, with small, pale green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
