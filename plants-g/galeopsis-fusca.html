Galeopsis fusca

Galeopsis fusca, commonly known as lesser hedge-nettle, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

Galeopsis fusca is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
