Gravesia angustifolia

Gravesia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forest areas.

Gravesia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
