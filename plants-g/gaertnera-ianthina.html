Gaertnera ianthina

Gaertnera ianthina, commonly known as the blue-flowered evergreen, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Gaertnera ianthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
