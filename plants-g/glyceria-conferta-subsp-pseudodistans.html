Glyceria conferta subsp. pseudodistans

Glyceria conferta subsp.

pseudodistans, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring shallow water or moist soil.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
