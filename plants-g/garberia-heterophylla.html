Garberia heterophylla

Garberia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in suitable climates.

Garberia heterophylla is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
