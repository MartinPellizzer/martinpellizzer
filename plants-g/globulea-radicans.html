Globulea radicans

Globulea radicans, commonly known as the creeping globular vine, is a tropical plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, rounded leaves.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features make it suitable for use in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in tropical gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
