Geranium molle subsp. stipulare

Geranium molle subsp.

stipulare, commonly known as the soft geranium or velvet geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its subspecies status reflects distinct morphological features compared to other geranium species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
