Geranium mascatense var. himalaicum

Geranium mascatense var. himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx, blooming in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
