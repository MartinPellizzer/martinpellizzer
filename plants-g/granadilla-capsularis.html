Granadilla capsularis

Granadilla capsularis, commonly known as the granadilla or yellow granadilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe. These berries are rich in vitamins and are often used in jams, beverages, and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
