Gomphrena leucocephala

Gomphrena leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, feathery bracts that resemble small plumes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by vibrant, papery petals that range in color from pink to red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena leucocephala is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
