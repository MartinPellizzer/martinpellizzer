Gymnocalycium tudae

Gymnocalycium tudae, commonly known as the Tude's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular shape and bright, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem covered in white, woolly spines. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gymnocalycium tudae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
