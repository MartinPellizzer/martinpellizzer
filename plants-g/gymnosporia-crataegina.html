Gymnosporia crataegina

Gymnosporia crataegina, commonly known as the African thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, spherical fruits. Its seeds are encased in a fleshy, red pulp, which is edible and used in traditional medicine.

Gymnosporia crataegina is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems.
