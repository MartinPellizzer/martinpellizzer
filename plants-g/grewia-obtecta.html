Grewia obtecta

Grewia obtecta, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are clustered in small, spherical inflorescences and are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
