Gnaphalium albidum

Gnaphalium albidum, commonly known as white cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, often accompanied by hairy, lobed leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Gnaphalium albidum is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
