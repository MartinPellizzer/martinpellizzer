Glaphyropteris rudis

Glaphyropteris rudis, commonly known as the *Rudis fern*, is a prehistoric plant belonging to the genus Glaphyropteris, which thrived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves with a distinctive bifurcated structure, suggesting adaptations for efficient photosynthesis in dense, swampy environments. The plant's fronds were likely composed of multiple pinnae, which may have helped in capturing sunlight and managing water retention. Fossil records indicate that Glaphyropteris rudis played a significant role in the development of early coal-forming ecosystems.

Its morphology provides valuable insights into the evolutionary adaptations of early vascular plants.
