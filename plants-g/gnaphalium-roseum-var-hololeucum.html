Gnaphalium roseum var. hololeucum

Gnaphalium roseum var. hololeucum, commonly known as the white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
