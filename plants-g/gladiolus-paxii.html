Gladiolus paxii

Gladiolus paxii, commonly known as the Paxii gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, sword-shaped leaves and blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and horticultural interest.
