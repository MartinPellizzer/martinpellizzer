Garcinia malabarica

Garcinia malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, aromatic pulp.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
