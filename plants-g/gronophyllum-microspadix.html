Gronophyllum microspadix

Gronophyllum microspadix, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its adaptations to dry environments include reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems for water absorption.

Gronophyllum microspadix is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
