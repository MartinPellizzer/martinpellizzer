Gaillonia tinctoria var. glabra

Gaillonia tinctoria var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth balsam, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates and well-drained soil. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and long blooming period.

Additionally, the plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and dye production.
