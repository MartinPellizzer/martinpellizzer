Gymnanthes lucida

Gymnanthes lucida, commonly known as the brightleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate, and ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in panicles, and it produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
