Gordonia spectabilis

Gordonia spectabilis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds when mature.

Gordonia spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
