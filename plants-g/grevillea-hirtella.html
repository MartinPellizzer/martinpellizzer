Grevillea hirtella

Grevillea hirtella, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea hirtella is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
