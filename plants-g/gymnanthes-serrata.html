Gymnanthes serrata

Gymnanthes serrata, commonly known as the spiked beautyberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright blue berries that are surrounded by a waxy, white calyx. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by the distinctive berries.

Gymnanthes serrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
