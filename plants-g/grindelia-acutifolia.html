Grindelia acutifolia

Grindelia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its resilience to arid conditions.

Grindelia acutifolia is also used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological benefits.
