Genista abyssinica

Genista abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian broom, is a shrub native to Ethiopia and parts of the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used traditionally in local medicine for its purported health benefits.
