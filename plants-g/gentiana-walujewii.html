Gentiana walujewii

Gentiana walujewii, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana walujewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
