Gastrochilus saccatus

Gastrochilus saccatus, commonly known as the sac-like lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, particularly the sac-like shape of its labellum, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
