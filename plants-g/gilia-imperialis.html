Gilia imperialis

Gilia imperialis, commonly known as the imperial gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gilia imperialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
