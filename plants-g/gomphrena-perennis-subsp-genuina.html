Gomphrena perennis subsp. genuina

Gomphrena perennis subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the everlasting pea or red globe gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to produce long-lasting, showy inflorescences.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that resemble pea-like structures. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and drought tolerance. Gomphrena perennis subsp.

genuina thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
