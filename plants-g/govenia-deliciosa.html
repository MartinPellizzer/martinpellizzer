Govenia deliciosa

Govenia deliciosa, commonly known as the sweet gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and purple in the fall. The tree produces small, lantern-shaped fruit that contains spiky seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive texture.

Govenia deliciosa is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
