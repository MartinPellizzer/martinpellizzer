Geranium gorbizense

Geranium gorbizense, commonly known as the Gorbiz Mountains geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
