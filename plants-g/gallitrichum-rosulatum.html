Gallitrichum rosulatum

Gallitrichum rosulatum, commonly known as the rosette gallweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it adapted to arid environments.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
