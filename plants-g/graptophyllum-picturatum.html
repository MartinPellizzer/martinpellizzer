Graptophyllum picturatum

Graptophyllum picturatum, commonly known as the painted fern, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically a mix of green, silver, and rust-colored hues, creating a visually appealing pattern. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, offering seasonal interest in both summer and winter.

Graptophyllum picturatum is often used in landscape design for its ornamental value and ability to add texture to shaded areas.
