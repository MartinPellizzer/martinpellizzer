Goodyera longirostrata

Goodyera longirostrata, commonly known as the long-beaked maidenhair, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, dark green leaves and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Goodyera longirostrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
