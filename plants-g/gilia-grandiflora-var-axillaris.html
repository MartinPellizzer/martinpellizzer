Gilia grandiflora var. axillaris

Gilia grandiflora var. axillaris, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
