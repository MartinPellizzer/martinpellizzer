Gagea paedophila

Gagea paedophila, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a unique seed capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its propagation. It is often found in association with other woodland flora and thrives in cool, humid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
