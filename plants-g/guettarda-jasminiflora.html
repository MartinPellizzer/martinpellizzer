Guettarda jasminiflora

Guettarda jasminiflora, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

Guettarda jasminiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
