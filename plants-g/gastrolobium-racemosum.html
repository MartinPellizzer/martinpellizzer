Gastrolobium racemosum

Gastrolobium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Gastrolobium racemosum is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits the surrounding soil.
