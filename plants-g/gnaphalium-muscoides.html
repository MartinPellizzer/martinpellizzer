Gnaphalium muscoides

Gnaphalium muscoides, commonly known as the moss-like everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
