Grubbia gracilis

Grubbia gracilis, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky soils and crevices, showcasing a compact growth habit with upright stems and small, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Grubbia gracilis is adapted to survive in low-nutrient environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and high temperatures.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
