Geranium erianthum var. angustifolium

Geranium erianthum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, narrow, and serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
