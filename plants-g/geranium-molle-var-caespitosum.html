Geranium molle var. caespitosum

Geranium molle var. caespitosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
