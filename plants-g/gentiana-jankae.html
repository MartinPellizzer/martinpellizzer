Gentiana jankae

Gentiana jankae, commonly known as the Janka's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals.

Gentiana jankae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
