Gynandropsis pentaphylla var. nana

Gynandropsis pentaphylla var. nana, commonly known as dwarf gynandropsis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with compact, bushy foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils. It is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
