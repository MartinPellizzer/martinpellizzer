Glycine senegalensis

Glycine senegalensis, commonly known as the African bean tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines, and its wood is sometimes utilized for construction.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision in its native ecosystems.
