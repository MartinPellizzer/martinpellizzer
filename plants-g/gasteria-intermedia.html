Gasteria intermedia

Gasteria intermedia, commonly known as the African pine or the elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are usually green with white or silver markings. The plant produces a tall, branched stem that bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Gasteria intermedia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
