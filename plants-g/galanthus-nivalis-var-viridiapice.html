Galanthus nivalis var. viridiapice

Galanthus nivalis var. viridiapice, commonly known as the green and white snowdrop, is a striking variant of the snowdrop genus characterized by its unique foliage.

This plant features lance-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with a subtle white or pale green hue, contrasting beautifully with its white, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed, showcasing the plant's cold-hardy nature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
