Glechoma brevituba

Glechoma brevituba, commonly known as the creeping ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma brevituba is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
