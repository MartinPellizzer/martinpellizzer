Gentiana cordisepala

Gentiana cordisepala, commonly known as the heart-sepal gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five deeply lobed petals.

Gentiana cordisepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
