Gentianella cunninghamii subsp. major

Gentianella cunninghamii subsp.

major, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and heathlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds dispersed by wind.
