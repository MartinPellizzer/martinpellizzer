Gompholobium minus var. grandiflora

Gompholobium minus var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered burrwing, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

It is native to Western Australia and thrives in dry, sandy soils, often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers, which are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators. Its foliage consists of bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and contribute to its distinctive appearance.
