Grammitis sectifrons

Grammitis sectifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are small and clustered in compact groups.

Grammitis sectifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
