Glandularia aurantiaca

Glandularia aurantiaca, commonly known as the scarlet gaura, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with glandular structures that secrete oils, which can deter herbivores. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
