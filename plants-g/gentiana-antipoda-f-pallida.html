Gentiana antipoda f. pallida

Gentiana antipoda f. pallida, commonly known as the pale gentian, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking blue to pale blue or white flowers.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
