Geranium lainzii

Geranium lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in parts of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, adding to its ornamental appeal.
