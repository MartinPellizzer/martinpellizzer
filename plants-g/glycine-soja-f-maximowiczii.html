Glycine soja f. maximowiczii

Glycine soja f. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese milk vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal studies.
