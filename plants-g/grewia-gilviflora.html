Grewia gilviflora

Grewia gilviflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia gilviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
