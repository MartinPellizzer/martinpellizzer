Gastroglottis latifolia

Gastroglottis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gastroglottis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Gastroglottis latifolia is known for its resilience to drought and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
