Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. farinosa

Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. farinosa, commonly known as the farinose guttation plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its leaves a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that mature into elongated, woody pods containing several seeds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
