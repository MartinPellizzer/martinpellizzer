Geonoma arundinacea

Geonoma arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
