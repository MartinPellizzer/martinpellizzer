Garcinia ferrandii

Garcinia ferrandii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically round and have a thick, purple rind. The fruit's interior contains segmented, juicy segments similar to those of the mangosteen.

Garcinia ferrandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
