Gomphocarpus arborescens

Gomphocarpus arborescens, commonly known as the tree milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree with woody stems and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is also known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.
