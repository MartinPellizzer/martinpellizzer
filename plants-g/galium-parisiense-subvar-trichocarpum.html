Galium parisiense subvar. trichocarpum

Galium parisiense subvar. trichocarpum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy coating. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense cymes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
