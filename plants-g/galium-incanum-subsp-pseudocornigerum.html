Galium incanum subsp. pseudocornigerum

Galium incanum subsp.

pseudocornigerum, commonly known as the silverweed or sandweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy surface. Galium incanum subsp.

pseudocornigerum is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
