Gomphrena glauca

Gomphrena glauca, commonly known as blue gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its long blooming period, from summer to fall, adds vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season.
