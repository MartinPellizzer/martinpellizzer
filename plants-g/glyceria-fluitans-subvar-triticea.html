Glyceria fluitans subvar. triticea

Glyceria fluitans subvar. triticea, commonly known as the floating bamboo or water bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tufts of spikelets that often float on the water surface. The plant thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas quickly.

This subvariety is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
