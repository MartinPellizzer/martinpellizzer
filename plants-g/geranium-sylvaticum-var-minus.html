Geranium sylvaticum var. minus

Geranium sylvaticum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser wood geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky habitats, reaching heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
