Gypsophila hirsuta var. alpina

Gypsophila hirsuta var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments at high elevations. It is known for its ability to withstand harsh weather conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in rockeries and alpine gardens.
