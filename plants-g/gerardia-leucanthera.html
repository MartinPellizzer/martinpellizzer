Gerardia leucanthera

Gerardia leucanthera, commonly known as the white gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gerardia leucanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
