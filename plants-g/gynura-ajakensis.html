Gynura ajakensis

Gynura ajakensis, commonly known as the African holly or African nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive net-like venation pattern. Its small yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the rainy season.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
