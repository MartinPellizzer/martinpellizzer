Garnotia ciliata f. glabriuscula

Garnotia ciliata f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved garnotia, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically glabrous, or hairless, giving it its distinguishing feature. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
