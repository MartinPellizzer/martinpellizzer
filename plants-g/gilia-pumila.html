Gilia pumila

Gilia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf gilia, is a small flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features delicate, branching stems and produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gilia pumila thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
