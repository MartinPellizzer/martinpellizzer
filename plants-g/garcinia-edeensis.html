Garcinia edeensis

Garcinia edeensis, commonly known as the Ede's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest in both botanical research and the supplement industry.
