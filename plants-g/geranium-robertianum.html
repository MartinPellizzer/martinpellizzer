Geranium robertianum

Geranium robertianum, commonly known as the mouse ear geranium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is often found in damp soils and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
