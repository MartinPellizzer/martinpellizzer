Gluta nitida

Gluta nitida, commonly known as the white gluta, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a cup-like structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Gluta nitida is often found in lowland and montane forests, growing as an understory plant.

Its latex is milky and can be used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
