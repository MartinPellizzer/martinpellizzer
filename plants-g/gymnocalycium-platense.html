Gymnocalycium platense

Gymnocalycium platense, commonly known as the Puna Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its globular or cylindrical stem that is often covered in spines and is adapted to survive in arid and high-altitude environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Gymnocalycium platense is a slow-growing plant that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
