Gentiana scabra var. kitadakensis

Gentiana scabra var. kitadakensis, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
