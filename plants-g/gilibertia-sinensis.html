Gilibertia sinensis

Gilibertia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese gilbertia, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in a fibrous pod and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
