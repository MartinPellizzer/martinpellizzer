Gilia peduncularis

Gilia peduncularis, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of bright pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called capsules, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed sites.

Gilia peduncularis is a valuable plant for pollinators and adds vibrant color to its native habitat.
