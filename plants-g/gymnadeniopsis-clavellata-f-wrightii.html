Gymnadeniopsis clavellata f. wrightii

Gymnadeniopsis clavellata f. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
