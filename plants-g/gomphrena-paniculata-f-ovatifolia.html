Gomphrena paniculata f. ovatifolia

Gomphrena paniculata f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, spiky, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are ovate and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for various gardening applications.
