Gilia congesta subsp. palmifrons

Gilia congesta subsp.

palmifrons, commonly known as the palmate gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into lobes resembling fingers, and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple.
