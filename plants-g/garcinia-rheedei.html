Garcinia rheedei

Garcinia rheedei, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruit contains a single, hard, seed-like stone surrounded by a juicy, acidic pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Garcinia.
