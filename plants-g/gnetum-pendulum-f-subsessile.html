Gnetum pendulum f. subsessile

Gnetum pendulum f. subsessile, commonly known as the slender gnetum, is a unique plant belonging to the gymnosperm group, though it exhibits some angiosperm-like features.

It is characterized by its slender, pendant stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base of the shoots. The plant produces distinctive cone-like structures, which contain both male and female reproductive organs, a feature known as monoecy. Its leaves are typically deciduous, shedding in response to seasonal changes, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its intermediate characteristics between gymnosperms and angiosperms.
