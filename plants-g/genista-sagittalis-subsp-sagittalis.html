Genista sagittalis subsp. sagittalis

Genista sagittalis subsp.

sagittalis, commonly known as the arrowleaf broom, is a shrub native to the western United States. It features slender, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
