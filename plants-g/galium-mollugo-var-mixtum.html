Galium mollugo var. mixtum

Galium mollugo var. mixtum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in natural habitats.
