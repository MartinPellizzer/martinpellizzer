Gentiana veitchiorum

Gentiana veitchiorum, commonly known as the Veitch's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals that are usually a striking blue or violet hue.

Gentiana veitchiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
