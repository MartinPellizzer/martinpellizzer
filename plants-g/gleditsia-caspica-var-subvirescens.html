Gleditsia caspica var. subvirescens

Gleditsia caspica var. subvirescens, commonly known as the Caspian honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, green pods that contain sweet, edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a notable species in its native habitat.
