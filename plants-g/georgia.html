Georgia

Georgia, commonly known as the "Empress of the Flowers," is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Georgia is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating various ailments.
