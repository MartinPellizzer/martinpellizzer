Grindelia confusa

Grindelia confusa, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Grindelia confusa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
