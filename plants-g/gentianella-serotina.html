Gentianella serotina

Gentianella serotina, commonly known as the late gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and typically grows in moist, acidic soils in open habitats such as bogs and wet meadows. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
