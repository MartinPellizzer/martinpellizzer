Genista albida

Genista albida, commonly known as the white broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Genista albida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and as a source of honey.
