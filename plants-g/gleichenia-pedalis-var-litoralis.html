Gleichenia pedalis var. litoralis

Gleichenia pedalis var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal Gleichenia, is a variant of the Gleichenia pedalis species that thrives in coastal environments.

It is a fern-like plant characterized by its feather-like fronds and is typically found in sandy or rocky soils near the shoreline. This variety is adapted to salt spray and periodic inundation, making it a hardy plant in coastal ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for coastal habitats.
