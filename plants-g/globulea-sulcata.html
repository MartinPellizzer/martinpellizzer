Globulea sulcata

Globulea sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Globulea sulcata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
