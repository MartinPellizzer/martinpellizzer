Goniophlebium dulce

Goniophlebium dulce, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Goniophlebium dulce thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
