Goodyera macrophylla

Goodyera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant mosquito orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble mosquitoes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, darkened lip.

Goodyera macrophylla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance.
