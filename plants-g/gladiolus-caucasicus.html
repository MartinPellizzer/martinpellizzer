Gladiolus caucasicus

Gladiolus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

Gladiolus caucasicus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
