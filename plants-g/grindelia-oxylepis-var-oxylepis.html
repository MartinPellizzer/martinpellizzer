Grindelia oxylepis var. oxylepis

Grindelia oxylepis var. oxylepis, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
