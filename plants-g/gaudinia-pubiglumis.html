Gaudinia pubiglumis

Gaudinia pubiglumis, commonly known as the velvetleaf gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gaudinia pubiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
