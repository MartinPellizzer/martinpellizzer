Gentiana scabra var. stenophylla

Gentiana scabra var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib, and the stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
