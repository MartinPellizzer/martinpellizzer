Gleditsia bujotii var. pendula

Gleditsia bujotii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, flat pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
