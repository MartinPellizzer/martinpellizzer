Girardinia longispica

Girardinia longispica, commonly known as the long-spined girardinia, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that surround its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a rough texture.

Girardinia longispica is often found in the southern United States and parts of Mexico.
