Gigantochloa compressa

Gigantochloa compressa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wet, lowland areas.

Due to its rapid growth and strong structural properties, it is widely used in construction and as a source of raw material in various industries.
