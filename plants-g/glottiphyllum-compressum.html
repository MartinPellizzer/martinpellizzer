Glottiphyllum compressum

Glottiphyllum compressum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glottiphyllum, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
