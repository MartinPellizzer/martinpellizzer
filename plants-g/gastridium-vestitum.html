Gastridium vestitum

Gastridium vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Gastridium vestitum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a useful plant in certain agricultural contexts and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
