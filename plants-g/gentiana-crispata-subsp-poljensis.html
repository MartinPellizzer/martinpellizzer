Gentiana crispata subsp. poljensis

Gentiana crispata subsp.

poljensis, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are often crinkled or curled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in alpine ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
