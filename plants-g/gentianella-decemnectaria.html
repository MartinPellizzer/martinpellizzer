Gentianella decemnectaria

Gentianella decemnectaria, commonly known as the ten-nerved gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It is a perennial herb with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
