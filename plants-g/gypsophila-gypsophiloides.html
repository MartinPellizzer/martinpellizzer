Gypsophila gypsophiloides

Gypsophila gypsophiloides, commonly known as the desert candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers resembling tiny stars. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its delicate appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in dry climates.
