Galianthe liliifolia

Galianthe liliifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered galianthe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy surface, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

Galianthe liliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
