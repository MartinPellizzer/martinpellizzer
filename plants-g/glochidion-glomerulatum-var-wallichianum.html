Glochidion glomerulatum var. wallichianum

Glochidion glomerulatum var. wallichianum, commonly known as the clustered glochidion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.
