Gratiola axillaris

Gratiola axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Gratiola axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
