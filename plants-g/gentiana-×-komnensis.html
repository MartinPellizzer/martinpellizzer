Gentiana × komnensis

Gentiana × komnensis, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

Gentiana × komnensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
