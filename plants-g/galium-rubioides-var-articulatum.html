Galium rubioides var. articulatum

Galium rubioides var. articulatum, commonly known as the jointed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its small white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The species is characterized by its unique jointed stems, which give it its common name and contribute to its distinctive appearance.
