Gentiana huxleyi

Gentiana huxleyi, commonly known as the Huxley's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana huxleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
