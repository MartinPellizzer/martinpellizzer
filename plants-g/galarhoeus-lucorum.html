Galarhoeus lucorum

Galarhoeus lucorum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in appearance. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil.

Galarhoeus lucorum is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
