Galium × paui

Galium × paui, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, typical of the Rubiaceae family.

Galium × paui is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
