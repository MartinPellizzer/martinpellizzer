Gladiolus longanus

Gladiolus longanus, commonly known as the longan grass or longan gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically arranged in racemes. Its botanical name reflects its elongated, grass-like appearance, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
