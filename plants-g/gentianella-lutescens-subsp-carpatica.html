Gentianella lutescens subsp. carpatica

Gentianella lutescens subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
