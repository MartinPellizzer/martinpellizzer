Gurania capitata

Gurania capitata, commonly known as the clustered gurania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a short, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Gurania capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
