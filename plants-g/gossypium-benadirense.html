Gossypium benadirense

Gossypium benadirense, commonly known as the Benadire cotton, is a species of cotton native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its fibrous seed coverings, which are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Gossypium benadirense is valued for its strong, durable fibers, making it an important crop in certain agricultural regions.
