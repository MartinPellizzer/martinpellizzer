Gardenia capensis

Gardenia capensis, commonly known as the Cape gardenia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and its large, waxy, white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
