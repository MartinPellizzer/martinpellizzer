Gentiana sessiliflora

Gentiana sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without stalks, and are usually basal or clustered at the base.

Gentiana sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
