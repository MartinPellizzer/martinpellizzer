Genista valentina subsp. murcica

Genista valentina subsp.

murcica, commonly known as the Murcian broom, is a shrub native to southern Spain. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean scrublands. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
