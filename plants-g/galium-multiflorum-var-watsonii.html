Galium multiflorum var. watsonii

Galium multiflorum var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Watson's bedstraw is commonly found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and meadows.
