Grielum sinuatum

Grielum sinuatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some areas and requires caution when handling.
