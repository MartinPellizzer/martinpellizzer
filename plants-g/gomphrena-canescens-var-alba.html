Gomphrena canescens var. alba

Gomphrena canescens var. alba, commonly known as the white globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that are typically white or pink in color. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are grayish-white and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting structure and vibrant appearance.
