Galium tinctorium var. floridanum

Galium tinctorium var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender stems and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.
