Gloxinia suaveolens

Gloxinia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented gloxinia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of broad, velvety leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings.

Gloxinia suaveolens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil for optimal growth.
