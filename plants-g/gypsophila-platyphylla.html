Gypsophila platyphylla

Gypsophila platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf baby's breath, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves and is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Gypsophila platyphylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
