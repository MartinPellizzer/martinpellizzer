Gastrolobium cyanophyllum

Gastrolobium cyanophyllum, commonly known as the blue wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The blue wattle is an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
