Grangea grangeoides

Grangea grangeoides, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy seed heads that resemble small crowns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Grangea grangeoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
