Gentiana spenceri

Gentiana spenceri, commonly known as Spencer's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rocky slopes and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana spenceri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
