Gentiana stiriaca

Gentiana stiriaca, commonly known as the Stiriaca gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana stiriaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
