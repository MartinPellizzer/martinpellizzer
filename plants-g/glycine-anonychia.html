Glycine anonychia

Glycine anonychia, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Glycine anonychia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
