Glandulicereus queretaroensis

Glandulicereus queretaroensis, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and produces large, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a thick, succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Glandulicereus queretaroensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
