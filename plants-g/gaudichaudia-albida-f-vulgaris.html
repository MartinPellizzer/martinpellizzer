Gaudichaudia albida f. vulgaris

Gaudichaudia albida f. vulgaris, commonly known as the white gaudichaudia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow ray florets and prominent, dark brown disk flowers. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in appearance.
