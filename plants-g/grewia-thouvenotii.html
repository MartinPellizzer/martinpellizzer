Grewia thouvenotii

Grewia thouvenotii, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. These fruits are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Grewia thouvenotii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
