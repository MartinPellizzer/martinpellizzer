Gelidocalamus tessellatus

Gelidocalamus tessellatus, commonly known as the tessellated bamboo, is a unique species native to high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, segmented culms that display a distinctive tessellated pattern on the surface. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall bamboo-like appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
