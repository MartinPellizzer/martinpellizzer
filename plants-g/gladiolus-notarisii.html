Gladiolus notarisii

Gladiolus notarisii, commonly known as the Malawi gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically vibrant in color, ranging from red to orange, and are often used in ornamental gardening. This species is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
