It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its most notable characteristic is the vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers.

Guzmania seemannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
