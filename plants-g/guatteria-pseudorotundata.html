Guatteria pseudorotundata

Guatteria pseudorotundata, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers followed by edible, yellowish berries that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Guatteria pseudorotundata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
