Glycine ringoetii

Glycine ringoetii, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves and red, fleshy arils surrounding its seeds. The plant is known for its toxic properties, particularly the seeds containing potent alkaloids. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
