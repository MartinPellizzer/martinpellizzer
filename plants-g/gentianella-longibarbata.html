Gentianella longibarbata

Gentianella longibarbata, commonly known as the long-bearded gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals and a distinctive, elongated nectar spur.

This species is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
