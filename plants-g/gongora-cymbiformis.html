Gongora cymbiformis

Gongora cymbiformis, commonly known as the cymbiform orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Gongora cymbiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
