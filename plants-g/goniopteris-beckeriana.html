Goniopteris beckeriana

Goniopteris beckeriana, commonly known as the Becker's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its fronds are divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
