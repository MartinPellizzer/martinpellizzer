Glycine javanica

Glycine javanica, commonly known as the Java bean or climbing peanut, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. Glycine javanica is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
