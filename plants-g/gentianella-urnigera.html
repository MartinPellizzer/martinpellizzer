Gentianella urnigera

Gentianella urnigera, commonly known as the urn-shaped gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella urnigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
