Grevillea pilosa

Grevillea pilosa, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, silky, pale green to silver-grey foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in winter and spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden settings.

Grevillea pilosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds and insects.
