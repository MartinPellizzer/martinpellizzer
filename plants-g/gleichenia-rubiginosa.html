Gleichenia rubiginosa

Gleichenia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a reddish or brownish coating, which gives it its common name.

Gleichenia rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
