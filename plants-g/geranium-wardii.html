Geranium wardii

Geranium wardii, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often heart-shaped or ovate, with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Geranium wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and easy care requirements.
