Gnaphalium xerochrysum

Gnaphalium xerochrysum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its aromatic foliage and striking blooms contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
