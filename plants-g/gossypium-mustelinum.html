Gossypium mustelinum

Gossypium mustelinum, commonly known as the woolly cotton or woolly abutilon, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America and is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has capsule fruits that contain fibrous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.

Gossypium mustelinum is also valued for its potential in fiber production and ecological resilience.
