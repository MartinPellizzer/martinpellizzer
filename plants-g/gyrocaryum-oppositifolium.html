Gyrocaryum oppositifolium

Gyrocaryum oppositifolium, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, round, green fruits that mature to a reddish color. Its botanical features make it a notable species in the family Hippocrateaceae.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
