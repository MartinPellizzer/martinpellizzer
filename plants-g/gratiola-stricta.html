Gratiola stricta

Gratiola stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf gravel root, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. Its root system is tuberous and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Gratiola stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
