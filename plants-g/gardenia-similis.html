Gardenia similis

Gardenia similis, commonly known as the slender gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, waxy white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant scent.

Gardenia similis is relatively slow-growing and can reach heights of up to 6 feet in optimal conditions.
