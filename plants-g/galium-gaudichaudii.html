Galium gaudichaudii

Galium gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Hawaiian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive oval shape with a slightly hairy surface.

Galium gaudichaudii is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.
