Genista florida subsp. polygaliphylla

Genista florida subsp.

polygaliphylla, commonly known as the flowering broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and needle-like, arranged in pairs along the stems. This subspecies is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal areas.
