Galium papillosum var. nouletianum

Galium papillosum var. nouletianum, commonly known as the Noulet's bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, covered in tiny, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
