Gustavia augusta

Gustavia augusta, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gustavia augusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
