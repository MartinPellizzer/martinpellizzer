Geogenanthus ciliatus

Geogenanthus ciliatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Geogenanthus ciliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
