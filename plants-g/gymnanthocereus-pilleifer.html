Gymnanthocereus pilleifer

Gymnanthocereus pilleifer, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented stems that are often adorned with vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces bright orange or red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant during the holiday season.
