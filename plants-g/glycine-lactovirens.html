Glycine lactovirens

Glycine lactovirens, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in various cuisines. Glycine lactovirens is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
