Grossularia rubra

Grossularia rubra, commonly known as the rosehip plant, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its berries are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural antioxidants.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
