Gladiolus thunbergii

Gladiolus thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of vibrant pink or white flowers that resemble a sword. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and is also used in traditional Japanese culture.
