Gutembergia nivea

Gutembergia nivea, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gutembergia nivea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
