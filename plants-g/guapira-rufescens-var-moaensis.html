Guapira rufescens var. moaensis

Guapira rufescens var. moaensis, commonly known as the Moa Mountain guapira, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
