Gilia multicaulis var. tenera

Gilia multicaulis var. tenera, commonly known as the soft-stemmed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
