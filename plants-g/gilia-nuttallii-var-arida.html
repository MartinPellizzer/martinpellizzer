Gilia nuttallii var. arida

Gilia nuttallii var. arida, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or marginal habitats.
