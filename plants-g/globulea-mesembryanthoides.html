Globulea mesembryanthoides

Globulea mesembryanthoides, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a snowball, typically during the winter months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
