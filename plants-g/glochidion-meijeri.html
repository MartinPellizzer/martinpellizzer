Glochidion meijeri

Glochidion meijeri, commonly known as the Meijer's glabrous hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Glochidion meijeri is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
