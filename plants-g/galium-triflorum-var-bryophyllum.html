Galium triflorum var. bryophyllum

Galium triflorum var. bryophyllum, commonly known as the three-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and possesses opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
