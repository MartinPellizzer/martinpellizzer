Gonzalagunia panamensis var. cornifolia

Gonzalagunia panamensis var. cornifolia, commonly known as the Panamanian horned vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like leaf tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a leathery texture.

Gonzalagunia panamensis var. cornifolia is typically found in humid, shaded environments within lowland tropical forests.
