Glochidion zeylanicum var. arborescens

Glochidion zeylanicum var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree glochidion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by globose, woody fruits containing several seeds.

This species is often found in tropical lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
