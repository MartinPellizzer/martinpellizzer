Gironniera zeylanica

Gironniera zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

This species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of the region's rainforest ecosystems.
