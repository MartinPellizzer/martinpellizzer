Gypsophila heteropoda subsp. heteropoda

Gypsophila heteropoda subsp.

heteropoda, commonly known as the silvery carpet or fringed poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, feathery flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its stems are often branched and covered with fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
