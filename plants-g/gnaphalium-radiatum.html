Gnaphalium radiatum

Gnaphalium radiatum, commonly known as common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are surrounded by fluffy, white bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium radiatum is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
