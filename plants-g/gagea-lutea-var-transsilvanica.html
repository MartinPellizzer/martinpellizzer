Gagea lutea var. transsilvanica

Gagea lutea var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Carpathian region.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to woodland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained, loamy soils. Its bulbs are typically small and rounded, with a fibrous root system.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, characterized by its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native range.
