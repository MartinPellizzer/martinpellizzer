Gerbera integralis

Gerbera integralis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gerbera integralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
