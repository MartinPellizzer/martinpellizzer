Gentiana tetragona

Gentiana tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Gentiana tetragona is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
