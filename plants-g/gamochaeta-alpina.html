Gamochaeta alpina

Gamochaeta alpina, commonly known as alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-gray hue, and its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

Gamochaeta alpina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in harsh climates.
