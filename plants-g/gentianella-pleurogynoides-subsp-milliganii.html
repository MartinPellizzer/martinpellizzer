Gentianella pleurogynoides subsp. milliganii

Gentianella pleurogynoides subsp.

milliganii, commonly known as Milligan's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
