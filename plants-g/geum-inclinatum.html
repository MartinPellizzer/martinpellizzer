Geum inclinatum

Geum inclinatum, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with spreading rhizomes, allowing it to form dense mats. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
