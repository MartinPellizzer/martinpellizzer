Glomera aurea

Glomera aurea, commonly known as the golden glomera, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and unique foliage.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a deep green color. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls, adding a decorative and ornamental value to the plant.

Glomera aurea thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
