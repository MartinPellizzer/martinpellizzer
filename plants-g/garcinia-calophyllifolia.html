Garcinia calophyllifolia

Garcinia calophyllifolia, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking red fruits that resemble small lanterns. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface, and the flowers are small and yellow.

Garcinia calophyllifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid climates.
