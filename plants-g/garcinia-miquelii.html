Garcinia miquelii

Garcinia miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia miquelii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and phytochemistry.
