Gymnocalycium lafaldense f. deviatum

Gymnocalycium lafaldense f. deviatum, commonly known as the deviated leaf gymnocalycium, is a unique cactus species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and reduced, often clustered leaves that are modified into spines. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its greenish-gray body. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Cactaceae family.
