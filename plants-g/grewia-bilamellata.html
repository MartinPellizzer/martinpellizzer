Grewia bilamellata

Grewia bilamellata, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits that split open to release seeds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, glandular appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia bilamellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
