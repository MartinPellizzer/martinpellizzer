Grastidium elatum

Grastidium elatum, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that radiate from a central stem, creating a star-shaped appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green, with a subtle golden hue along the edges, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Grastidium elatum is often cultivated for its unique aesthetic and is valued in both horticultural and botanical collections.
