Gastrolobium aculeatum

Gastrolobium aculeatum, commonly known as the prickly wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of the genus Gastrolobium. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Gastrolobium aculeatum is part of the legume family and can fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
