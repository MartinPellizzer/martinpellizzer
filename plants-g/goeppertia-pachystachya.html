Goeppertia pachystachya

Goeppertia pachystachya, commonly known as the giant calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often adorned with intricate, veined patterns in shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant produces tall, showy inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia pachystachya is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
