Gelasia kotschyi

Gelasia kotschyi, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are bell-shaped, often blue or violet in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gelasia kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
