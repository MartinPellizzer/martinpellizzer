Grevillea heterophylla var. velutina

Grevillea heterophylla var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
