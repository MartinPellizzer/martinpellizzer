Gesneria zebrina

Gesneria zebrina, commonly known as the Zebra Gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, zebra-like striping on its leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped foliage that is often variegated. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually appear in clusters, blooming in shades of white, pink, or red.

Gesneria zebrina prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
