Grewia chadara

Grewia chadara, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, round, orange-red fruits. Grewia chadara thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
