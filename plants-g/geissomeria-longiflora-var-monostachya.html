Geissomeria longiflora var. monostachya

Geissomeria longiflora var. monostachya, commonly known as the single-stemmed sweet pepper bush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
