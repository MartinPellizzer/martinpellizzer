Galium lucidum subsp. bernardii

Galium lucidum subsp.

bernardii, commonly known as the shining bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving them a shiny appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
