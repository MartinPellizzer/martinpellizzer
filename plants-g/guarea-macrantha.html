Guarea macrantha

Guarea macrantha, commonly known as the giant ironwood tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-yellow or white hues.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
