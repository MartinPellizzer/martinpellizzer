Grewia commutata

Grewia commutata, commonly known as the common grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Grewia commutata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.
