Gentiana linearis f. rubricaulis

Gentiana linearis f. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's distinctive red or reddish-purple stems are a defining feature of this cultivar. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine plant collections.
