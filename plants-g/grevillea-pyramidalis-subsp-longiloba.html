Grevillea pyramidalis subsp. longiloba

Grevillea pyramidalis subsp.

longiloba, commonly known as the long-fruited grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
