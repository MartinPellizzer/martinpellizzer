Gentiana serbica

Gentiana serbica, commonly known as the Serbian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Gentiana serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
