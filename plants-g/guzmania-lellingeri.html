Guzmania lellingeri

Guzmania lellingeri, commonly known as the Lellinger's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that collect water, forming a central pool. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range from red to orange, adding visual appeal. Its foliage is often a deep green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Guzmania lellingeri is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
