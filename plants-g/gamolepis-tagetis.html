Gamolepis tagetis

Gamolepis tagetis, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a cluster. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its potential medicinal uses, it has been studied for its phytochemical composition and therapeutic applications.
