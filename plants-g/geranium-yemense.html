Geranium yemense

Geranium yemense, commonly known as the Yemen geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of Yemen.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Geranium yemense thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
