Gomphrena verecunda

Gomphrena verecunda, commonly known as the red globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spherical flower heads that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gomphrena verecunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
