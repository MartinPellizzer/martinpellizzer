Grammitis sparsipila

Grammitis sparsipila, commonly known as the sparse-veined fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually pale green and have a distinctive, sparse venation pattern.

Grammitis sparsipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
