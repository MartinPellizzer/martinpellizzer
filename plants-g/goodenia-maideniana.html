Goodenia maideniana

Goodenia maideniana, commonly known as the rock goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Goodenia maideniana is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
