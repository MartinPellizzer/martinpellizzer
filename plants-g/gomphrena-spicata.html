Gomphrena spicata

Gomphrena spicata, commonly known as the spiked gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant is typically herbaceous, with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena spicata is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant appearance.
