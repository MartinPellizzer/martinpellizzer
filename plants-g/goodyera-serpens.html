Goodyera serpens

Goodyera serpens, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and its unique, snake-like appearance of the flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a subtle fragrance, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Goodyera serpens is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
