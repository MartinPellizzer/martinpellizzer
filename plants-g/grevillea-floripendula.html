Grevillea floripendula

Grevillea floripendula, commonly known as the hanging grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea floripendula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract nectar-feeding birds.
