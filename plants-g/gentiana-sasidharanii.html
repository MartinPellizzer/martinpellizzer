Gentiana sasidharanii

Gentiana sasidharanii, commonly known as the Sasidharan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within montane forests. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the monsoon season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
