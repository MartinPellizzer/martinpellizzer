Grevillea nivea

Grevillea nivea, commonly known as the white grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea nivea is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
