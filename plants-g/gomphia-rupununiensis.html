Gomphia rupununiensis

Gomphia rupununiensis, commonly known as the Rupununi grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the Guyanas and parts of northern Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a distinctive grass-like appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Gomphia rupununiensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various small organisms.
