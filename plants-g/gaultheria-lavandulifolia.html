Gaultheria lavandulifolia

Gaultheria lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features small, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that emit a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart.

Gaultheria lavandulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
