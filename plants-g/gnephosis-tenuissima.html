Gnephosis tenuissima

Gnephosis tenuissima, commonly known as the slender bladder fern, is a unique member of the Pteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, thin fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is noted for its small size and specialized adaptations to its ecological niche.
