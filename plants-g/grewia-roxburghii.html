Grewia roxburghii

Grewia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian rose or red sandalwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open areas.

The berries are edible and used in traditional cuisines, while the plant also has cultural and medicinal significance.
