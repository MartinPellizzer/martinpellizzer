Gymnema schlechterianum

Gymnema schlechterianum, commonly known as the Indian bitter leaf, is a climbing plant native to the wet tropical forests of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is known for its unique ability to inhibit the perception of sweetness, a property that has been studied for its potential in managing diabetes. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which contribute to its bitter taste and medicinal properties.

Gymnema schlechterianum is also valued in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
