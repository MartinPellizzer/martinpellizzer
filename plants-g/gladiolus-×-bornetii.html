Gladiolus × bornetii

Gladiolus × bornetii, commonly known as the hybrid gladiolus, is a cultivated plant resulting from crosses between different gladiolus species.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its flowers come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is native to South Africa and is valued for its beauty and versatility in garden design.
