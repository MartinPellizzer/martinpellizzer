Gentianella amarella subsp. wrightii

Gentianella amarella subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's gentian, is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are slender and branching.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
