Gentiana hartwegii

Gentiana hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
