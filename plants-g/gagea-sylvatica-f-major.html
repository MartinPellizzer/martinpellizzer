Gagea sylvatica f. major

Gagea sylvatica f. major, commonly known as the large wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

The flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
