Glandularia canadensis f. candissima

Glandularia canadensis f. candissima, commonly known as the Canadian glandular verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with glandular hairs, which give it its scientific name.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
