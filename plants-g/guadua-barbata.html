Guadua barbata

Guadua barbata, commonly known as the barbed bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive barbed nodes that give it its common name. Guadua barbata is valued for its strong and lightweight structure, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.

Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
