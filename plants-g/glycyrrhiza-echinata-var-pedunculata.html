Glycyrrhiza echinata var. pedunculata

Glycyrrhiza echinata var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Chinese licorice or licorice root, is a perennial herb native to China.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by legume-like pods containing brown, kidney-shaped seeds. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and are valued for their sweet, aromatic taste and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic significance.
