Gilia gracilis subsp. humilis

Gilia gracilis subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers.

The flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
