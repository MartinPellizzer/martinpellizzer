Gentiana pharica

Gentiana pharica, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana pharica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
