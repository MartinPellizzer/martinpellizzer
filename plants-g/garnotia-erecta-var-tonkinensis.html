Garnotia erecta var. tonkinensis

Garnotia erecta var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin garnet, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.
