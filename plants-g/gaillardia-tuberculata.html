Gaillardia tuberculata

Gaillardia tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red ray flowers, which contrast with its dark green, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its bloom period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Gaillardia tuberculata is a hardy perennial that is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
