Galium commune subvar. asperum

Galium commune subvar. asperum, commonly known as rough bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
