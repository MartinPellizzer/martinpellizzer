Gingidia flabellata

Gingidia flabellata, commonly known as the fan-flowered ginger, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, fan-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Gingidia flabellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
