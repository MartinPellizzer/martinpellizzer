Galium sessiliflorum

Galium sessiliflorum, commonly known as smallleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It has slender, often prostrate stems that are covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium sessiliflorum is often found in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and dry meadows.
