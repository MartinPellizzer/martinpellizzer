Garrya ovata subsp. lindheimeri

Garrya ovata subsp.

lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's holly, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its evergreen nature and compact growth habit make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.
