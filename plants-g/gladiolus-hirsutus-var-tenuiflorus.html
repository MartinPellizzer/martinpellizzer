Gladiolus hirsutus var. tenuiflorus

Gladiolus hirsutus var. tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow petals and are often used in cut flower arrangements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
