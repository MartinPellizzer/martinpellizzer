Gesneria ulmifolia

Gesneria ulmifolia, commonly known as the elm-leaf gesneria, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of an elm tree. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small, tubular, and come in shades of white and pink, often clustered in inflorescences.

Gesneria ulmifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
