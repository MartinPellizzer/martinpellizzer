Gunnera aequatoriensis

Gunnera aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Andean giant shieldfern, is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the family Gunneraceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane regions with high humidity. Its unique morphology includes a short, thick rhizome and a cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into berries.

Gunnera aequatoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
