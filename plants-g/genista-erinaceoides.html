Genista erinaceoides

Genista erinaceoides, commonly known as the spiny broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dense and provide habitat for various insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal areas.

Its distinctive spiny stems and compact growth habit make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
