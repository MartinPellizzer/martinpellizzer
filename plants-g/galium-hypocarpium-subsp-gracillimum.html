Galium hypocarpium subsp. gracillimum

Galium hypocarpium subsp.

gracillimum, commonly known as slender wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive success in suitable habitats.
