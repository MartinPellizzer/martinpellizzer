Gouinia papillosa

Gouinia papillosa, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, low-growing structure and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, helping it store water.

Gouinia papillosa is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
