Glycine gampsonychia

Glycine gampsonychia, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Glycine gampsonychia is often cultivated for its nutritional value and as a cover crop in agricultural systems.
