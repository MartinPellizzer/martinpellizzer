Geranium pamiricum

Geranium pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pentagonal flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with lobed margins, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Geranium pamiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
