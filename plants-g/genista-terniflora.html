Genista terniflora

Genista terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
