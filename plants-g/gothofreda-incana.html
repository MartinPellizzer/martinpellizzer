Gothofreda incana

Gothofreda incana, commonly known as the silverleaf gothofreda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Gothofreda incana is often found in rocky or forested habitats, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its environment.
