Gladiolus aberdaricus

Gladiolus aberdaricus, commonly known as the Aberdare gladiolus, is a species native to the Aberdare Range in Kenya.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
