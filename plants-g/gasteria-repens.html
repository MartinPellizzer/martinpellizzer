Gasteria repens

Gasteria repens, commonly known as the "creeping gasteria," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with white or yellowish markings. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for dry environments.

Gasteria repens is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact growth habit.
