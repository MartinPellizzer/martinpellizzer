Gentianella antarctica

Gentianella antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subantarctic environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often short and sturdy.

Gentianella antarctica is adapted to withstand harsh climates and is an important component of its native ecosystems.
