Gnetum tenuifolium

Gnetum tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf gnetum, is a species of gymnosperm native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has a unique reproductive system involving both male and female structures on the same individual. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Gnetum tenuifolium plays a role in local ecosystems as a source of food for various insects and animals.
