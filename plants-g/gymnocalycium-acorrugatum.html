Gymnocalycium acorrugatum

Gymnocalycium acorrugatum, commonly known as the rugose moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces spiny, cylindrical stems with prominent tubercles and is adapted to arid environments. It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically reduced to spines.

Gymnocalycium acorrugatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
