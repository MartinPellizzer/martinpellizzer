Gentiana angulosa

Gentiana angulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentiana angulosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
