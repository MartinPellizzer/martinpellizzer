Gynura brownii

Gynura brownii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or brownii gynura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, dry fruits. Gynura brownii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
