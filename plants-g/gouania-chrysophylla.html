Gouania chrysophylla

Gouania chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf gouania, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive foliage and unique growth habits.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a striking yellow-green coloration, which is a defining botanical trait. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces capsule fruits that contain minute seeds.

Gouania chrysophylla is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
