Gnaphalium domingense

Gnaphalium domingense, commonly known as the Dominican everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Dominica, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale green bracts. Its foliage is typically silver-gray, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

Gnaphalium domingense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
