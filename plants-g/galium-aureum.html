Galium aureum

Galium aureum, commonly known as golden bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture with a rounded or ovate shape.

Galium aureum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
