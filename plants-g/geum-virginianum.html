Geum virginianum

Geum virginianum, commonly known as Virgin's Bower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and streambanks, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers are usually pink to red and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread through runners, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
