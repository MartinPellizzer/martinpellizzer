Genista tinctoria var. lasiocarpa

Genista tinctoria var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry, yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
