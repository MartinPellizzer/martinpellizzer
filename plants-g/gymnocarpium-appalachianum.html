Gymnocarpium appalachianum

Gymnocarpium appalachianum, commonly known as Appalachian nakedcarp, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, usually appearing in clusters, and it produces a unique type of fruit called a utricle.

The plant is characterized by its lack of a hard outer covering on its seeds, which is a key feature in its common name.
