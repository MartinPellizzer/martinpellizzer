Gonolobus californicus

Gonolobus californicus, commonly known as the California ironweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils.

Gonolobus californicus is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
