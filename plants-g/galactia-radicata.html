Galactia radicata

Galactia radicata, commonly known as the Mexican burr legume, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive, spiny seed pods that enclose numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Galactia radicata is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for certain livestock.
