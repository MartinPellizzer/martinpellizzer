Genista desoleana

Genista desoleana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
