Geranium columbinum

Geranium columbinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, narrow, and usually hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Geranium columbinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators.
