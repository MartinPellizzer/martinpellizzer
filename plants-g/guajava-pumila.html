Guajava pumila

Guajava pumila, commonly known as the white guava, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, white, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a popular choice in local cuisines.
