Gentiana wootchuliana

Gentiana wootchuliana, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Korean medicine.
