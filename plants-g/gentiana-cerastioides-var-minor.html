Gentiana cerastioides var. minor

Gentiana cerastioides var. minor, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils, where it forms dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and ecological benefits.
