Globba merrilli

Globba merrilli, commonly known as the white globba, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Globba merrilli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
