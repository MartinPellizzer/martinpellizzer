Guarea inesiana

Guarea inesiana, commonly known as the red cedar or *Cedro Rojo*, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elongated, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

The wood of Guarea inesiana is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in furniture and construction.
