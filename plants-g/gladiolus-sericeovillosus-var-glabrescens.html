Gladiolus sericeovillosus var. glabrescens

Gladiolus sericeovillosus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky-hairy gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
