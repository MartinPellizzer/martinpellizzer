Gymnocalycium venturianum

Gymnocalycium venturianum, commonly known as the Venturian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny exterior. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Gymnocalycium venturianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
