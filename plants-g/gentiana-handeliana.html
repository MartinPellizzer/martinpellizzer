Gentiana handeliana

Gentiana handeliana, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its roots are tuberous and used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
