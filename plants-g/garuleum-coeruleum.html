Garuleum coeruleum

Garuleum coeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered garuleum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Garuleum coeruleum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
