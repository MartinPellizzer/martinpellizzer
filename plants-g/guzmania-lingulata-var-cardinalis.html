Guzmania lingulata var. cardinalis

Guzmania lingulata var. cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal flamingo plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange inflorescence, which resembles a flamingo's beak, giving it its common name. The plant features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowers are borne on a central spike and can last for several months, adding visual interest to the plant.

Guzmania lingulata var. cardinalis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.
