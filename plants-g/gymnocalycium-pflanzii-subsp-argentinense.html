Gymnocalycium pflanzii subsp. argentinense

Gymnocalycium pflanzii subsp.

argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its short, thick stems covered in dense clusters of spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
