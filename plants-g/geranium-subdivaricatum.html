Geranium subdivaricatum

Geranium subdivaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine, hairy hairs. The leaves are typically narrow, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
