Geum triflorum var. ciliatum

Geum triflorum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the hooked leaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have hairy margins. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
