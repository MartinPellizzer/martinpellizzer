Gnidia dichotoma

Gnidia dichotoma, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing small, black seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

Gnidia dichotoma is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
