Galipea febrifuga

Galipea febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for treating fevers and headaches. Its essential oils contain compounds like parthenolide, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

Galipea febrifuga is often cultivated for both ornamental and pharmacological purposes.
