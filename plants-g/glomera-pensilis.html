Glomera pensilis

Glomera pensilis, commonly known as the hanging glomera, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, delicate flowers that dangle from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its foliage consists of small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
