Geranium sanguineum var. sanguineiforme

Geranium sanguineum var. sanguineiforme, commonly known as the blood-red geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the British Isles.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bright red, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a deep green color. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators and add vibrant color to gardens in late spring to early summer.
