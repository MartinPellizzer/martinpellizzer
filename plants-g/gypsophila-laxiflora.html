Gypsophila laxiflora

Gypsophila laxiflora, commonly known as the desert bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
