Genista sylvestris

Genista sylvestris, commonly known as the wild broom, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in Mediterranean climates. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista sylvestris is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
