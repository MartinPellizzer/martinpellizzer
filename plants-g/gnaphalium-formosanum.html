Gnaphalium formosanum

Gnaphalium formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan feather grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its slender, hair-like leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads.

Gnaphalium formosanum is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
