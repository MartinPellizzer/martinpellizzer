Gahnia aspera subsp. globosa

Gahnia aspera subsp.

globosa, commonly known as the globular tussock grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, rounded clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a tussock structure.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along watercourses. Gahnia aspera subsp.

globosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
