Gentianusa flava

Gentianusa flava, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The yellow gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
