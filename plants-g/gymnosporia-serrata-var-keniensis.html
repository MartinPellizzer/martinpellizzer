Gymnosporia serrata var. keniensis

Gymnosporia serrata var. keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan thornveld acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in savannas and rocky areas. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
