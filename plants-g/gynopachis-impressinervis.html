Gynopachis impressinervis

Gynopachis impressinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined gynopachis, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring a sprawling or ascending growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive impressed venation pattern, giving it its scientific name.

The species is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.
