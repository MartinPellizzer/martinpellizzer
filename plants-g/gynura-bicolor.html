Gynura bicolor

Gynura bicolor, commonly known as the Variegated Cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored leaves, featuring a mix of green and white or silver markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Gynura bicolor is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in containers or as part of a shaded garden.
