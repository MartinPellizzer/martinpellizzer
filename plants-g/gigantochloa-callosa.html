Gigantochloa callosa

Gigantochloa callosa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and strength, it is sometimes used in construction and as a source of fiber.
