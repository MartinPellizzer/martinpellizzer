Gypsophila hispanica

Gypsophila hispanica, commonly known as Spanish baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila hispanica is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and airy appearance.
