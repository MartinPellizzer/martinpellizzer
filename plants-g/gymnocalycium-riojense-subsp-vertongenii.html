Gymnocalycium riojense subsp. vertongenii

Gymnocalycium riojense subsp.

vertongenii, commonly known as the Rioja moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, usually in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing, cold-hardy cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in arid gardens.
