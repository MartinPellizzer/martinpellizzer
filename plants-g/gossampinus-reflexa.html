Gossampinus reflexa

Gossampinus reflexa, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated, leathery pods, typical of the legume family.
