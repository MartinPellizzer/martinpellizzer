Gentiana filisepala

Gentiana filisepala, commonly known as the thread-sepaled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in high-altitude grasslands or forests. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, and the sepals are elongated and thread-like, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
