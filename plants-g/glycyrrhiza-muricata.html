Glycyrrhiza muricata

Glycyrrhiza muricata, commonly known as the hairy licorice, is a perennial herb native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, containing high levels of glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweetening properties.

Glycyrrhiza muricata is often cultivated for medicinal and culinary purposes due to its versatile applications.
