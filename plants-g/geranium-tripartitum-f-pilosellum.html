Geranium tripartitum f. pilosellum

Geranium tripartitum f. pilosellum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved geranium, is a compact, trailing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered with fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
