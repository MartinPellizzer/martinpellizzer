Gomphrena pulchella var. albisericea

Gomphrena pulchella var. albisericea, commonly known as the white plume gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its showy, feathery inflorescences that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical or cylindrical heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The foliage is typically gray-green, adding visual interest even when the plant is not in bloom.
