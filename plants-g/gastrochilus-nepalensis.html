Gastrochilus nepalensis

Gastrochilus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal toothbrush orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, often on rocky or humus-rich soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
