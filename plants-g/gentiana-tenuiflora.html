Gentiana tenuiflora

Gentiana tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
