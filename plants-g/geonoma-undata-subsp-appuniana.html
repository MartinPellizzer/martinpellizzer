Geonoma undata subsp. appuniana

Geonoma undata subsp.

appuniana, commonly known as the Appunian shield fern, is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, often forming a low, carpet-like ground cover.

The plant produces small, clustered spores on the undersides of its fronds, which are typically found in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizomatous structure allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.

The fronds are usually leathery and have a distinctive, shield-shaped appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
