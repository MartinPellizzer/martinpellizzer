Gossypium hirsutum f. pollardii

Gossypium hirsutum f. pollardii, commonly known as the Pollard's cotton, is a botanical variant of upland cotton, characterized by its unique floral and foliar features.

This form exhibits distinct morphological traits, including elongated petals and modified leaves, which set it apart from typical Gossypium hirsutum varieties. It is primarily found in specific ecological niches, often in regions with particular soil and climatic conditions. The plant's genetic makeup contributes to its adaptability and has been studied for potential agricultural applications.

Its ornamental qualities make it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural practices.
