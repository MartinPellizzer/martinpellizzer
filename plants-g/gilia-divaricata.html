Gilia divaricata

Gilia divaricata, commonly known as the spreading gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, or white, and they open in the afternoon, hence the family's common name. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

It is an annual or short-lived perennial, known for its adaptability to harsh conditions.
