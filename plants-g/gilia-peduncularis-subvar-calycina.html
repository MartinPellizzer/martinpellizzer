Gilia peduncularis subvar. calycina

Gilia peduncularis subvar. calycina, commonly known as the California gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.
