Gomphrena tomentosa var. cana

Gomphrena tomentosa var. cana, commonly known as the silverleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, clustered pink or red flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

The varietal name 'cana' refers to its silvery-gray leaf color, which adds visual interest to gardens.
