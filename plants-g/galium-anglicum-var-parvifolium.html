Galium anglicum var. parvifolium

Galium anglicum var. parvifolium, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, opposite leaves with a rounded to ovate shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the plant is known for its creeping habit.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
