Grewia salicifolia

Grewia salicifolia, commonly known as the silky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
