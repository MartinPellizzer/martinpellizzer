Galega biloba

Galega biloba, commonly known as the lesser goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Galega biloba is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
