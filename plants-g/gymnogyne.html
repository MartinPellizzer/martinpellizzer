Gymnogyne

Gymnogyne, commonly known as the "bushy gymnosperm," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy or glandular. These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often used in gardens for their hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.
