Gentiana pubigera subsp. ninglangensis

Gentiana pubigera subsp.

ninglangensis, commonly known as the Ninglang gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest clearings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
