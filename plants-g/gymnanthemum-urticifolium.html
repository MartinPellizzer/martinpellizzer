Gymnanthemum urticifolium

Gymnanthemum urticifolium, commonly known as the stinging nettleleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves that resemble those of the stinging nettle, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, daisy-like cluster. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Gymnanthemum urticifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
