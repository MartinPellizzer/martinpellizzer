Gymnocalycium schreiteri

Gymnocalycium schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments.

Gymnocalycium schreiteri is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
