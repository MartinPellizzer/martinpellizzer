Gymnotrix riparia

Gymnotrix riparia, commonly known as the river gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and riverine habitats of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to its aquatic environment. Gymnotrix riparia is a perennial herb that thrives in shallow, slow-moving water, often forming dense mats that stabilize riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for water quality and its ecological importance in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
