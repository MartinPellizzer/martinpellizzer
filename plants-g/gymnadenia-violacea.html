Gymnadenia violacea

Gymnadenia violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, woodlands, and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
