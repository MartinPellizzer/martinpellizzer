Grastidium xylophyllum

Grastidium xylophyllum, commonly known as the goldenwood fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its intricate frond structure and specialized root system.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate rainforests. The plant's leaves are composed of multiple pinnae, which are arranged in a bipinnate pattern, enhancing its ability to capture sunlight. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Grastidium xylophyllum plays a vital ecological role in maintaining soil stability and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
