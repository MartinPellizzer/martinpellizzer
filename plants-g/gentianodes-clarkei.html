Gentianodes clarkei

Gentianodes clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowers are tubular and often have five lobes, with bright blue or violet petals that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking appearance.
