Glyphochloa ratnagirica

Glyphochloa ratnagirica, commonly known as the Ratnagiri grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth texture. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in open forests and grasslands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
