Glycine glabrata

Glycine glabrata, commonly known as the smooth soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, brown pods containing several round seeds. Glycine glabrata is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

It has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils.
