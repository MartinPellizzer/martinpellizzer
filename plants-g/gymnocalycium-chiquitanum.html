Gymnocalycium chiquitanum

Gymnocalycium chiquitanum, commonly known as the Chiquita prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. Gymnocalycium chiquitanum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent stems that store water.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various desert species.
