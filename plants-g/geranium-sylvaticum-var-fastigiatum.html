Geranium sylvaticum var. fastigiatum

Geranium sylvaticum var. fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woods betony, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
