Gaudinia fragilis var. pubescens

Gaudinia fragilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy foliage, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its unique floral structure and ornamental value in horticulture.
