Granadilla normalis

Granadilla normalis, commonly known as the yellow granadilla, is a member of the Passifloraceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces striking, colorful flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The fruit is a bright yellow, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep orange-red color. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species within its native habitat.
