Gladiolus curtilimbus

Gladiolus curtilimbus, commonly known as the broad-leaved gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals that are often brightly colored.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
