Geranium consanguineum

Geranium consanguineum, commonly known as the redstem geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Geranium consanguineum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
