Gumira thwaitesii

Gumira thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' gumira, is a rare and endemic plant species found in specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features a combination of smooth and serrated edges, and its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it receives indirect sunlight. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed tropical ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Gumira thwaitesii is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
