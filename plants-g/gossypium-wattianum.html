Gossypium wattianum

Gossypium wattianum, commonly known as the Wattian cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of Pakistan and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the cotton fibers, which are used for textile production. It thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
