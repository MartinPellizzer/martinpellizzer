Guettarda viburnoides

Guettarda viburnoides, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Guettarda viburnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
