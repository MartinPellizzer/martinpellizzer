Gentiana kawakamii

Gentiana kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
