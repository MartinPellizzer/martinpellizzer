Geocaryum stylosum

Geocaryum stylosum, commonly known as the slender rootgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a tufted cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Geocaryum stylosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
