Gastrochilus subpapillosus

Gastrochilus subpapillosus, commonly known as the papillate gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and hairy, or papillate, structures on its leaves and stems. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
