Gilia cana subsp. speciosa

Gilia cana subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves are adapted to arid conditions.

It is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
