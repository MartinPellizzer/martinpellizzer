Goniopteris smithii

Goniopteris smithii, commonly known as the Smith's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique trait makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and ecological research.
