Gypsophila polygonoides subsp. thessala

Gypsophila polygonoides subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Greek love grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and floral arrangements.
