Gaultheria malayana

Gaultheria malayana, commonly known as the Malay wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, containing seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Gaultheria malayana is also noted for its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds similar to those found in wintergreen oil.
