Geranium revolutum

Geranium revolutum, commonly known as the curled-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rolled-under leaves and clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Geranium revolutum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
