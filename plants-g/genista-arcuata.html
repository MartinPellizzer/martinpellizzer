Genista arcuata

Genista arcuata, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista arcuata is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
