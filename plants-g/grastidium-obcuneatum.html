Grastidium obcuneatum

Grastidium obcuneatum, commonly known as the slender grass-vetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Grastidium obcuneatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
