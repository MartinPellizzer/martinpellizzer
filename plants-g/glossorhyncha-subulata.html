Glossorhyncha subulata

Glossorhyncha subulata, commonly known as the slender-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically features small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky outcrops and dunes.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Glossorhyncha subulata is an important component of desert ecosystems.
