Gurania gracilis

Gurania gracilis, commonly known as the slender gurania, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Gurania gracilis is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, though it remains a lesser-known species in the botanical world.
