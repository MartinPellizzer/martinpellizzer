Gymnocarpos parvibractus

Gymnocarpos parvibractus, commonly known as the small-bracted gymnocarp, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, woody stems and dense clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dry and contain a single seed. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Gymnocarpos parvibractus is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
