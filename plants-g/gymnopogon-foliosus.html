Gymnopogon foliosus

Gymnopogon foliosus, commonly known as the leafy grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Gymnopogon foliosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
