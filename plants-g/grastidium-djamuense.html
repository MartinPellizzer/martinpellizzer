Grastidium djamuense

Grastidium djamuense, commonly known as the Djamuense grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces light, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
