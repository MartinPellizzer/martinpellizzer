Gymnocalycium riojense var. pipanacoense

Gymnocalycium riojense var. pipanacoense, commonly known as the Papanaco Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Argentina.

It is a low-growing, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
