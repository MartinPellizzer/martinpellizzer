Gomphandra australiana

Gomphandra australiana, commonly known as the Australian gomphandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to withstand drought and is considered a characteristic species of its native habitat.

The plant plays a role in supporting local insect populations and contributes to the ecological diversity of its environment.
