Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. comigera

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

comigera, commonly known as the Comigera gentian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It produces small, yellowish-brown capsules as fruits, which contain the seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
