Glycyrrhiza soongorica

Glycyrrhiza soongorica, commonly known as the Chinese licorice, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Mongolia, and Kazakhstan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its robust root system and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza soongorica is valued for its roots, which contain glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine and as a sweetener.

Its ecological significance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitats.
