Geraniospermum moniliforme

Geraniospermum moniliforme, commonly known as the bead-shaped geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bead-like fruits that form in clusters, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
