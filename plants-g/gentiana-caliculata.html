Gentiana caliculata

Gentiana caliculata, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and robust.

Gentiana caliculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
