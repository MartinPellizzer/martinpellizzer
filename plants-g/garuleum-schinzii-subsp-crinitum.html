Garuleum schinzii subsp. crinitum

Garuleum schinzii subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the silky garuleum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
