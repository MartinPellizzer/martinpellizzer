Gladiolus excelsus

Gladiolus excelsus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing dense clusters of brightly colored, sword-shaped flowers. The plant has lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements.

Gladiolus excelsus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
