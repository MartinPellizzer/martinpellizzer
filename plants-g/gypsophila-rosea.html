Gypsophila rosea

Gypsophila rosea, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are slender and often branching.

Gypsophila rosea is widely used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental value and ability to add texture and volume to bouquets.
