Gleditsia australis

Gleditsia australis, commonly known as the Australian honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in drooping inflorescences, followed by flat, dehiscent pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

The plant also has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to different environments.
