Gymnogramma angustifrons

Gymnogramma angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
