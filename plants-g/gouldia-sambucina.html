Gouldia sambucina

Gouldia sambucina, commonly known as the purple flowering plant, is a species native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gouldia sambucina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
