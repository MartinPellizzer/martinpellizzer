Gentiana sino-ornata var. gloriosa

Gentiana sino-ornata var. gloriosa, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
