Gladiolus ukambanensis

Gladiolus ukambanensis, commonly known as the East African gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with striking, colorful flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system, typical of many gladiolus species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
