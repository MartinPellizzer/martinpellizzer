Guarea pittieri

Guarea pittieri, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant has a dense, dark bark and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
