Geranium butuoense

Geranium butuoense, commonly known as the Butuo geranium, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
