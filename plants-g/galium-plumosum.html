Galium plumosum

Galium plumosum, commonly known as silverweed or silky bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, creeping stems that are often covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Galium plumosum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
