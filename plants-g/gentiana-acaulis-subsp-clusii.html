Gentiana acaulis subsp. clusii

Gentiana acaulis subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the Clusii gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are usually leafless.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
