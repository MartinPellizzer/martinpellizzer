Grewia richardiana

Grewia richardiana, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Grewia richardiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some African countries.
