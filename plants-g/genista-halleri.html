Genista halleri

Genista halleri, commonly known as the Haller's broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in early spring.

Genista halleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
