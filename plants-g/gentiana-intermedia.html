Gentiana intermedia

Gentiana intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Gentiana intermedia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
