Gypsophila altissima

Gypsophila altissima, commonly known as the sweet peas of the mountains, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, fluffy flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with slender stems and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets arranged in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Gypsophila altissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
