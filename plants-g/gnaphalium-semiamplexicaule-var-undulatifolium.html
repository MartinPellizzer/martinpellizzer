Gnaphalium semiamplexicaule var. undulatifolium

Gnaphalium semiamplexicaule var. undulatifolium, commonly known as the undulate-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
