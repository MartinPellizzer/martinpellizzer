Glandulicereus quevedonis

Glandulicereus quevedonis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a thick, succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Glandulicereus quevedonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
