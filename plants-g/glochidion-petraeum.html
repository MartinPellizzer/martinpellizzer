Glochidion petraeum

Glochidion petraeum, commonly known as the rock glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocky outcrops and limestone formations, showcasing its adaptability to poor soil conditions. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, rusty-brown hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round berries.

Glochidion petraeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
