Glandularia tumidula

Glandularia tumidula, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage covered in fine, glandular hairs that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its glandular hairs are not only a distinctive botanical feature but also serve to reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Glandularia tumidula is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
