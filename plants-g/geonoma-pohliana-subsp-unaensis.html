Geonoma pohliana subsp. unaensis

Geonoma pohliana subsp.

unaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a species of fern native to South America. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, delicate fronds.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
