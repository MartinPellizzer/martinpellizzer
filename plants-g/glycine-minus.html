Glycine minus

Glycine minus, commonly known as wild licorice, is a climbing legume native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and medicinal studies.
