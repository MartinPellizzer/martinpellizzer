Glycosmis monticola

Glycosmis monticola, commonly known as the mountain gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its fruit contains high levels of vitamin C and is valued for its medicinal properties.

Glycosmis monticola thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
