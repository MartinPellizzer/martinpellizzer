Gomphrena spissa

Gomphrena spissa, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and purple. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gomphrena spissa is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and low maintenance requirements.
