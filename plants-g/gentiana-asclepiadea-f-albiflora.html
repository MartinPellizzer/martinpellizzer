Gentiana asclepiadea f. albiflora

Gentiana asclepiadea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, though this variety is distinguished by its pale white petals. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
