Grewia laurifolia

Grewia laurifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, resembling snowballs, hence its common name. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slight waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Grewia laurifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
