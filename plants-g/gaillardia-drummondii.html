Gaillardia drummondii

Gaillardia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange and red ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Gaillardia drummondii typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and blooms from late spring to early fall.
