Gomphocarpus cancellatus

Gomphocarpus cancellatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, sticky seeds. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Gomphocarpus cancellatus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
