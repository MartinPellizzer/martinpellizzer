Gerardia mettaueri var. clausa

Gerardia mettaueri var. clausa, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The variety 'clausa' is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other forms of Gerardia mettaueri.
