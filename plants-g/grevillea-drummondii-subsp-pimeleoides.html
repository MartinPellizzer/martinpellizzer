Grevillea drummondii subsp. pimeleoides

Grevillea drummondii subsp.

pimeleoides, commonly known as the Drummond's Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
