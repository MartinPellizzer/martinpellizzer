Gamochilum umbellatum

Gamochilum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a dense, rounded arrangement.

Gamochilum umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
