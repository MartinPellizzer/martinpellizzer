Galium lapeyrouseanum

Galium lapeyrouseanum, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its flowers are white with five petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
