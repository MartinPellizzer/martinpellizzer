Geranium rotundifolium

Geranium rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, five-petaled blooms.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
