Galactia peduncularis

Galactia peduncularis, commonly known as the Florida milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.

Galactia peduncularis is adapted to coastal environments and is considered an important component of beach ecosystems.
