Gomphocentrum undulatum

Gomphocentrum undulatum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming pattern.
