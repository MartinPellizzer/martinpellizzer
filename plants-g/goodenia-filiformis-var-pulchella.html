Goodenia filiformis var. pulchella

Goodenia filiformis var. pulchella, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer months. This variety is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
