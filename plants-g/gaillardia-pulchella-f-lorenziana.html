Gaillardia pulchella f. lorenziana

Gaillardia pulchella f. lorenziana, commonly known as the orange sand verbena, is a hybrid form of the common gaillardia.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its vibrant orange and red blooms. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the flowers appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
