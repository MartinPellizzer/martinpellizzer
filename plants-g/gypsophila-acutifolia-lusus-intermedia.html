Gypsophila acutifolia lusus intermedia

Gypsophila acutifolia lusus intermedia, commonly known as the Meadow Snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces a profusion of blooms during the summer months.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
