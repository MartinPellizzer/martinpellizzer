Grossularia hirsuta

Grossularia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Grossularia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
