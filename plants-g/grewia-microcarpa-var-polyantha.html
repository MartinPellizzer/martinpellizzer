Grewia microcarpa var. polyantha

Grewia microcarpa var. polyantha, commonly known as the red-flowered bush or red globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
