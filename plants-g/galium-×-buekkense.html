Galium × buekkense

Galium × buekkense, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Galium × buekkense is known for its rapid spread and is considered invasive in some regions.
