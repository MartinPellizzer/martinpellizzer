Gibsoniothamnus stellatus

Gibsoniothamnus stellatus, commonly known as the star-stemmed thamnus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant features narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland mountainous regions.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
