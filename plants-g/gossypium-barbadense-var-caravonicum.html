Gossypium barbadense var. caravonicum

Gossypium barbadense var. caravonicum, commonly known as the Caravonicum cotton, is a variant of the upland cotton species native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It produces fluffy bolls containing seeds surrounded by the fibrous material.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its high-quality cotton used in textiles and other industrial applications.
