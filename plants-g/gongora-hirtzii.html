Gongora hirtzii

Gongora hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy gongora, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a distinctive, hairy texture on its leaves. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its native habitat.

Gongora hirtzii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
