Gladiolus sulphureus

Gladiolus sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with vibrant yellow and orange flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, with each bloom consisting of three petals and three sepals.

The plant is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
