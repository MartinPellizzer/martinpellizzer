Glyceria gracilis

Glyceria gracilis, commonly known as slender glyceria, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that often form dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are usually inconspicuous, and it reproduces through wind-dispersed seeds.

Glyceria gracilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
