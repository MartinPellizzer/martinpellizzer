Gnaphalium neelgerryanum

Gnaphalium neelgerryanum, commonly known as the Neelgerry everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, fluffy flowers are often arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
