Gentiana excisa var. albiflora

Gentiana excisa var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bright white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
