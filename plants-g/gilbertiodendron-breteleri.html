Gilbertiodendron breteleri

Gilbertiodendron breteleri, commonly known as the African sand apple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to dry climates.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
