Glycosmis elata

Glycosmis elata, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its botanical features include a branched stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique, aromatic compound in its leaves.

Glycosmis elata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
