Gynopachis acuminata

Gynopachis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed gynandropsis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, disc-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypsela.

Gynopachis acuminata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.
