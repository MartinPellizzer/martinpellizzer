Galaxia luteoalba

Galaxia luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Galaxia luteoalba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
