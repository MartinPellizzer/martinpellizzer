Gentiana angustifolia subsp. occidentalis

Gentiana angustifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
