Gaultheria parvula

Gaultheria parvula, commonly known as the dwarf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a spicy, minty flavor. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a waxy coating and remain evergreen throughout the year.

Gaultheria parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
