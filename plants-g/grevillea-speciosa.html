Grevillea speciosa

Grevillea speciosa, commonly known as the showy grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant, bottlebrush-shaped flowers that range from red to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Grevillea speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as honeyeaters and birds.
