Gratiola pulegiifolia

Gratiola pulegiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Gratiola pulegiifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
