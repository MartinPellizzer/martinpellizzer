Gamochaeta americana var. vulgaris

Gamochaeta americana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its small blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
