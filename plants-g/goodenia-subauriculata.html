Goodenia subauriculata

Goodenia subauriculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
