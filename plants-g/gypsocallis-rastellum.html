Gypsocallis rastellum

Gypsocallis rastellum, commonly known as the Rastellum lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Gypsocallis rastellum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
