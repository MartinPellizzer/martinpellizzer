Genipa eriopila

Genipa eriopila, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces a distinctive fruit with a rough, spiny exterior. The fruit contains a soft, aromatic pulp that is typically white or yellowish in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
