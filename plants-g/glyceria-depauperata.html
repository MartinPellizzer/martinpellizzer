Glyceria depauperata

Glyceria depauperata, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Glyceria depauperata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
