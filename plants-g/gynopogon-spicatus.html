Gynopogon spicatus

Gynopogon spicatus, commonly known as the spiky gynopogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Gynopogon spicatus is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
