Gerardia grandiflora var. serrata

Gerardia grandiflora var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. It is an important species in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
