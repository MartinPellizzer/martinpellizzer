Gentiana linearis var. lanceolata

Gentiana linearis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
