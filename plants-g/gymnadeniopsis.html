Gymnadeniopsis

Gymnadeniopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, showy flowers that are often white or pale green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Gymnadeniopsis is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia.

Due to habitat loss and its specific ecological requirements, it is considered endangered and is protected in many areas.
