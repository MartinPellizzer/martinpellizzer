Gomphocarpus phillipsiae

Gomphocarpus phillipsiae, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a distinctive, spiny stem and produces small, round fruits that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The botanical features of Gomphocarpus phillipsiae make it a striking and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
