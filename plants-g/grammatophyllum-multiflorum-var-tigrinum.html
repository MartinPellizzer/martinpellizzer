Grammatophyllum multiflorum var. tigrinum

Grammatophyllum multiflorum var. tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that display a mix of red, yellow, and green hues, resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil to support its aerial roots. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters along a central stem, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
