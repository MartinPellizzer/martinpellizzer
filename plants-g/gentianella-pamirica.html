Gentianella pamirica

Gentianella pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Pamir Mountains.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often appearing in shades of blue or purple.

Gentianella pamirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
