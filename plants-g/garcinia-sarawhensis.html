Garcinia sarawhensis

Garcinia sarawhensis, commonly known as the Sarawak garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in phytochemical research. Its habitat is typically in lowland and hill forest areas, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
