Guajava myrsinoides

Guajava myrsinoides, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, red drupes that contain a single seed.

Guajava myrsinoides plays an important ecological role in coastal and mangrove ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing shorelines.
