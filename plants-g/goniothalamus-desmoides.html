Goniothalamus desmoides

Goniothalamus desmoides, commonly known as the Malayan mountain ash, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland and montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is often found in protected areas.
