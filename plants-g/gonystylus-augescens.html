Gonystylus augescens

Gonystylus augescens, commonly known as the giant ironwood tree, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows as a tall evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 50 meters, with a broad canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.
