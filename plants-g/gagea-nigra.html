Gagea nigra

Gagea nigra, commonly known as black gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces a bulbous underground stem, which is typical of many members of its family. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.

Gagea nigra is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
