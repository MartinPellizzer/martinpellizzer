Glyceria acutiflora

Glyceria acutiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, preferring moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.

Glyceria acutiflora is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
