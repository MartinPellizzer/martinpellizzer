Galium trachyspermum var. setuliflorum

Galium trachyspermum var. setuliflorum, commonly known as hairy-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
