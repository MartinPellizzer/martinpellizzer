Garcinia benthamii

Garcinia benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
