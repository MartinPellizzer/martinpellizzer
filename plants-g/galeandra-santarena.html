Galeandra santarena

Galeandra santarena, commonly known as the Santarena orchid, is a species native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and striking violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in clusters, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
