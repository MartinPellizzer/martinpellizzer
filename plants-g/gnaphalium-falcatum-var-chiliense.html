Gnaphalium falcatum var. chiliense

Gnaphalium falcatum var. chiliense, commonly known as the Chilean feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its silvery-gray leaves and fluffy flower heads make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in poor soils.
