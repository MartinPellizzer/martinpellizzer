Grewia lanceolata

Grewia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
