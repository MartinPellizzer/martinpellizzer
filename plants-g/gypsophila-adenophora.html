Gypsophila adenophora

Gypsophila adenophora, commonly known as the desert bluemink, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gypsophila adenophora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
