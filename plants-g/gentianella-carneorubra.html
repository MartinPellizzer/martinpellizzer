Gentianella carneorubra

Gentianella carneorubra, commonly known as the red-flowered gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentianella carneorubra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color.
