Geum ibericum

Geum ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian avens, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, oval fruits known as achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Geum ibericum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
