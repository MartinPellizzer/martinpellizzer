Guadua paniculata

Guadua paniculata, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tall, slender plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, white to greenish flowers, which are typically followed by small, clustered fruits. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a fan-like pattern at the base of the plant.

Guadua paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
