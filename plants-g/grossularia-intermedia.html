Grossularia intermedia

Grossularia intermedia, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, containing multiple seeds. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
