Gossypium hirsutum subsp. mustelinum

Gossypium hirsutum subsp.

mustelinum, commonly known as the wild cotton plant, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, fluffy cotton-like fibers that develop in its capsules.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaves, and showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in the genetic diversity of cultivated cotton species.
