Geranium × quinquevulnerum

Geranium × quinquevulnerum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that resemble ivy, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Geranium × quinquevulnerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
