Gerascanthus grandis

Gerascanthus grandis, commonly known as the large geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are often covered in fine hairs. Gerascanthus grandis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during that time.
