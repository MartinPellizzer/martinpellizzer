Grammitis ambigens

Grammitis ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous huperbole, is a fern species belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, linear leaves arranged in dense, overlapping layers, which help it retain moisture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
