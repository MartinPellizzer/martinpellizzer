Geranium superbum

Geranium superbum, commonly known as the showy geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated edge, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Geranium superbum is a perennial herb that produces seeds through a unique mechanism involving a specialized structure called a "columella.".
