Glechoma campestris

Glechoma campestris, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its spreading growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
