Gymnomesium pictum

Gymnomesium pictum, commonly known as the painted kidneypod, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in wetland and marsh environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
