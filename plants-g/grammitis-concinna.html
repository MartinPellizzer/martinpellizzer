Grammitis concinna

Grammitis concinna, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with high humidity and shade. Grammitis concinna has a unique reproductive structure called a synangium, which contains the spore-producing organs.

This fern is often found growing on rocks or in moist, shaded forest floors, where it thrives in nutrient-poor environments.
