Grindelia arenicola f. trichophora

Grindelia arenicola f. trichophora, commonly known as the trichophore gumweed, is a variant of the species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and is adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cone-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.
