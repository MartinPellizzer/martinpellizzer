Grisia macrophylla

Grisia macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf grisia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Grisia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
