Gagea arvensis var. gracilis

Gagea arvensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender field gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Gagea arvensis var. gracilis is known for its early spring bloom and low maintenance requirements.
