Glyptopetalum continentale

Glyptopetalum continentale, commonly known as the coastal rock heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Glyptopetalum continentale is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal environments.
