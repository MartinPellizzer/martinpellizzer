Gentiana amarella var. rhaetica

Gentiana amarella var. rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine layer of down.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
