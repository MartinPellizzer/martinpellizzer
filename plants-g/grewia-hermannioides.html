Grewia hermannioides

Grewia hermannioides, commonly known as the white-flowered grewia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are typically hairy beneath, and it produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia hermannioides is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to attract birds and insects.
