Geranium szeewaldianum

Geranium szeewaldianum, commonly known as the Szeewald geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly hairy surface.

Geranium szeewaldianum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and borders.
