Galeopsis nodosa

Galeopsis nodosa, commonly known as nodding horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galeopsis nodosa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
