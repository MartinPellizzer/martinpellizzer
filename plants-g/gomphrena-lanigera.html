Gomphrena lanigera

Gomphrena lanigera, commonly known as the woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy bracts that resemble small clouds, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by the showy bracts.

Gomphrena lanigera is often cultivated as a garden plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
