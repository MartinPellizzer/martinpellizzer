Grewia decemovulata

Grewia decemovulata, commonly known as the many-ovary bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Grewia decemovulata is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
