Gymnadenia conopsea var. montana

Gymnadenia conopsea var. montana, commonly known as the mountain goat's horn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its rhizomes are thick and fleshy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
