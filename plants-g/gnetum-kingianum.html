Gnetum kingianum

Gnetum kingianum, commonly known as the African strangler fig, is a member of the Gnetophyta division and is notable for its unique botanical features.

It is a climbing plant that can grow up to 30 meters in height, with a woody stem and alternate, simple leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are either male or female, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction. Its fruits are fleshy and contain a single seed, which is enclosed in a hard, woody coat.

Gnetum kingianum is also characterized by its ability to form dense thickets, making it an important component of tropical rainforest ecosystems.
