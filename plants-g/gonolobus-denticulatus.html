Gonolobus denticulatus

Gonolobus denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed goonloob, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often found in woodland and savanna habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals or through mechanical means.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
