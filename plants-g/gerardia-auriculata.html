Gerardia auriculata

Gerardia auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its common name.

Gerardia auriculata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
