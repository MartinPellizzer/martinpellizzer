Gentiana anisosepala

Gentiana anisosepala, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers are tubular with five sepals that are often unequal in size, giving the species its specific epithet "anisosepala.".
