Geranium krameri var. chejuense

Geranium krameri var. chejuense, commonly known as the Jeju geranium, is a perennial herb native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
