Gagea striata

Gagea striata, commonly known as the striped gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. Gagea striata is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
