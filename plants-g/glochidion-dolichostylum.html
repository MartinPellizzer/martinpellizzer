Glochidion dolichostylum

Glochidion dolichostylum, commonly known as the long-stalked glochidion, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
