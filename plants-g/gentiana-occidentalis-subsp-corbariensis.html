Gentiana occidentalis subsp. corbariensis

Gentiana occidentalis subsp.

corbariensis, commonly known as the Corbari gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is unbranched and bears the flowers at the top.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
