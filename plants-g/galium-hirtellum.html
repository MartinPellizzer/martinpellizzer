Galium hirtellum

Galium hirtellum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and has slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium hirtellum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
