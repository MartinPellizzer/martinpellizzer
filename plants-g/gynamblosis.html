Gynamblosis

Gynamblosis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, golden-yellow panicles of flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species. Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in landscaping.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
