Guilleminea lanuginosa var. sheldonii

Guilleminea lanuginosa var. sheldonii, commonly known as the Sheldon's woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
