Guazuma rosea

Guazuma rosea, commonly known as the rose apple tree, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a fleshy, sweet, and aromatic berry that ripens to a red or orange color, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
