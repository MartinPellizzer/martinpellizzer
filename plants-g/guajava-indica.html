Guajava indica

Guajava indica, commonly known as the Java apple or *Syzygium jambolanum*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, tart pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for both its fruit and foliage.
