Grammitis aurea

Grammitis aurea, commonly known as golden maidenhair, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features slender, arching stems with small, bright green, ovate leaves that often have a golden hue on their undersides. Grammitis aurea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
