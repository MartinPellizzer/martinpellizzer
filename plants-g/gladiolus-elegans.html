Gladiolus elegans

Gladiolus elegans, commonly known as the elegant gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that form a basal rosette.

Gladiolus elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
