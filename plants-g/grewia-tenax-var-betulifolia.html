Grewia tenax var. betulifolia

Grewia tenax var. betulifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
