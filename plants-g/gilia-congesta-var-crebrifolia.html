Gilia congesta var. crebrifolia

Gilia congesta var. crebrifolia, commonly known as the many-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gilia congesta var. crebrifolia is a member of the Malvaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
