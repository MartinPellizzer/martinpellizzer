Galium verum f. subpubescens

Galium verum f. subpubescens, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
