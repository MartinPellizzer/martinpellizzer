Germainia

Germainia, commonly known as the common germander, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils in temperate regions. Germainia is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also popular in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
