Garcinia mbulwe

Garcinia mbulwe, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, reddish-purple fruits that are rich in xanthones, which are bioactive compounds with potential health benefits. Garcinia mbulwe is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded areas of tropical gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
