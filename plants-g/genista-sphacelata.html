Genista sphacelata

Genista sphacelata, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its slender, branching stems are often used in hedges or as ornamental plants due to their hardy nature.

Genista sphacelata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.
