Gentiana affinis

Gentiana affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
