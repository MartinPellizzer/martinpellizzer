Gentiana golowninia

Gentiana golowninia, commonly known as the Golownin's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
