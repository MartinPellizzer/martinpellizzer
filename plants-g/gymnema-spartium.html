Gymnema spartium

Gymnema spartium, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema spartium is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

Its unique properties have led to its use in traditional Ayurvedic practices for managing diabetes.
