Geum × kellereri

Geum × kellereri, commonly known as the "Kellerer's avens," is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, semi-double flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Geum × kellereri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
