Gossia clusioides

Gossia clusioides, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, which includes plants known for their showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Gossia clusioides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
