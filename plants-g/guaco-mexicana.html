Guaco mexicana

Guaco mexicana, commonly known as Mexican guaco, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Guaco mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
