Grevillea jamesoniana

Grevillea jamesoniana, commonly known as the Sydney flame, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically during spring and summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Grevillea jamesoniana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
