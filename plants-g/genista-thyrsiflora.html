Genista thyrsiflora

Genista thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it produces elongated, cylindrical flower clusters.

Genista thyrsiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
