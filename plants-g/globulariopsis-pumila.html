Globulariopsis pumila

Globulariopsis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf globularia, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and compact size, it is a popular choice for container gardening and landscaping in dry climates.
