Gentiana dissitifolia

Gentiana dissitifolia, commonly known as the scattered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes or meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the top of the plant.

Gentiana dissitifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
