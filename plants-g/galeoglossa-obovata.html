Galeoglossa obovata

Galeoglossa obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant has ovate to obovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Galeoglossa obovata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
