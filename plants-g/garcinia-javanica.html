Garcinia javanica

Garcinia javanica, commonly known as garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its bright red, fleshy fruit, which contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia javanica thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
