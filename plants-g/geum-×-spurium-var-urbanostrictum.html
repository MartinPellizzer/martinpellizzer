Geum × spurium var. urbanostrictum

Geum × spurium var. urbanostrictum, commonly known as the compact golden groundsel, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The foliage is typically dark green and finely textured, adding visual interest to garden landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and ability to bloom reliably in cool climates.
