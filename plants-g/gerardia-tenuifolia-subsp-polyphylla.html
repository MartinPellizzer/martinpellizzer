Gerardia tenuifolia subsp. polyphylla

Gerardia tenuifolia subsp.

polyphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
