Glycine pulcherrima

Glycine pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame bean or red bean plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Glycine pulcherrima is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
