Grewia laevigata var. cylindrica

Grewia laevigata var. cylindrica, commonly known as the silky bush or silky wild pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical fruits that are covered in a fine, silky white coating. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance.
