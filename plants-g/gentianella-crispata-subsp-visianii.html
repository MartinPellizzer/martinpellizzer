Gentianella crispata subsp. visianii

Gentianella crispata subsp.

visianii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
