Galactia bahamensis

Galactia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. Galactia bahamensis is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, often found in dunes and along the shoreline.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has some traditional medicinal uses in the region.
