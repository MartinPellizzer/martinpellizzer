Gomphocarpus laevigatus

Gomphocarpus laevigatus, commonly known as the yellow starbursts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble starbursts, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds with tufts of silky hair, aiding in wind dispersal.

Gomphocarpus laevigatus is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and ornamental value.
