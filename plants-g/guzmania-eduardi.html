Guzmania eduardi

Guzmania eduardi, commonly known as the Edward's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure that holds water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, often resembling a torch. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy layer, which helps in water retention and protection against intense sunlight.

Guzmania eduardi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
