Gossypium nandewarense

Gossypium nandewarense, commonly known as the Nandewara cotton, is a species of cotton native to India.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fluffy, white fibers used in textile production. The plant typically grows in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it is known for its relatively high fiber yield.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
