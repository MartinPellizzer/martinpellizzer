Gladiolus filiformis

Gladiolus filiformis, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
