Grewia aspera

Grewia aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf gumbo, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
