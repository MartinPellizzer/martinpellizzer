Gnidia pulvinata

Gnidia pulvinata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, waxy flowers that resemble balls, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gnidia pulvinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
