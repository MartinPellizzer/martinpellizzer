Galeoglossum thysanochilum

Galeoglossum thysanochilum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a tongue, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by specific insect species.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
