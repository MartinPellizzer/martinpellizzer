Glycosmis trifoliata var. angustifolia

Glycosmis trifoliata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
