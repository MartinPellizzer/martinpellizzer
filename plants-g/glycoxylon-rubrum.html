Glycoxylon rubrum

Glycoxylon rubrum, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-barked trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The plant produces cones that contain both male and female reproductive structures, typical of conifers. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize the soil and prevent erosion.

Glycoxylon rubrum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
