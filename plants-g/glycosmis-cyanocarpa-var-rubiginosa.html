Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. rubiginosa

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-fruited mohave cherry, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to purple fruits that are borne in clusters, with a distinctive yellowish-white pulp surrounding the seeds. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by its showy, edible fruits.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
