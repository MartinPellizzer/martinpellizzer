Genipa vieillardii

Genipa vieillardii, commonly known as the Brazilian genipap, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a unique fruit with a thick, yellowish rind and a soft, edible pulp that has a distinct, sweet-tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
