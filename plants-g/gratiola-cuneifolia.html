Gratiola cuneifolia

Gratiola cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gratiola cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
