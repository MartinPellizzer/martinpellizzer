Glyceria notata

Glyceria notata, commonly known as narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish or purple tint.

Glyceria notata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
