Glycosmis cymosa var. simplicifolia

Glycosmis cymosa var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that mature to a red or orange color. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
