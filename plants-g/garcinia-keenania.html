Garcinia keenania

Garcinia keenania, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Garcinia keenania is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of African flora.
