Gomphocarpus involucratus

Gomphocarpus involucratus, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open at night. The plant has a distinctive capsule fruit that splits open to release its seeds. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs.
