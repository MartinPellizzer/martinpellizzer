Gentiana germanica var. spatulata

Gentiana germanica var. spatulata, commonly known as the German gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
