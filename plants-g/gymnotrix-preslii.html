Gymnotrix preslii

Gymnotrix preslii, commonly known as the Preslii's ghostplant, is a rare aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures are simple, with small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents.

Gymnotrix preslii is valued in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
