Geum ceretanum

Geum ceretanum, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and hairy, with a short, stout stem that bears clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its flowers are usually arranged in a dense, rounded inflorescence and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Geum ceretanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
