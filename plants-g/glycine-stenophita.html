Glycine stenophita

Glycine stenophita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf soybean, is a leguminous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Glycine stenophita is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in food and animal feed.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
