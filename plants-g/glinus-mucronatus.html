Glinus mucronatus

Glinus mucronatus, commonly known as the small-leaved glinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, often with yellowish or greenish petals.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
