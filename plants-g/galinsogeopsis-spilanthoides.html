Galinsogeopsis spilanthoides

Galinsogeopsis spilanthoides, commonly known as the spilanthoid dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
