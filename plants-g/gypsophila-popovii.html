Gypsophila popovii

Gypsophila popovii, commonly known as the Popov's gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a soft, ethereal beauty to its natural habitat.

Gypsophila popovii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
