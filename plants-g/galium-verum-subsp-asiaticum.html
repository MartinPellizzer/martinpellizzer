Galium verum subsp. asiaticum

Galium verum subsp.

asiaticum, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
