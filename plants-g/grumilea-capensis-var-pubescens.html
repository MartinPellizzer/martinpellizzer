Grumilea capensis var. pubescens

Grumilea capensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-hued berries resembling small tomatoes. These berries are rich in nutrients and have a tart, tangy flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
