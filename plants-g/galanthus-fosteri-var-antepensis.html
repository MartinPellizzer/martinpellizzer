Galanthus fosteri var. antepensis

Galanthus fosteri var. antepensis, commonly known as the Antepanish snowdrop, is a rare and delicate perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces elegant, white, nodding flowers with a greenish tinge, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils. It is closely related to other snowdrop species but is distinguished by its unique floral structure and geographic distribution.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
