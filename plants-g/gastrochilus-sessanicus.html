Gastrochilus sessanicus

Gastrochilus sessanicus, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread gradually over time. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, arranged in a racemose inflorescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
