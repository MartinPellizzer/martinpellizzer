Grastidium incurvatum

Grastidium incurvatum, commonly known as the curling grass.

It is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that curve inward, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Grastidium incurvatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
