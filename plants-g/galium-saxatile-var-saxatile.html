Galium saxatile var. saxatile

Galium saxatile var. saxatile, commonly known as mountain bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to rocky and alpine regions.

It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas across Europe and Asia. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, linear, and slightly hairy. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
