Galactia pinnata

Galactia pinnata, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Galactia pinnata is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
