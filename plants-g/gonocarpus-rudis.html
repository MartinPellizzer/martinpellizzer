Gonocarpus rudis

Gonocarpus rudis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern coast of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are borne on tall, branching stalks and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gonocarpus rudis is well-suited to saline and coastal environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
