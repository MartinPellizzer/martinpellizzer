Gomphrena acaulis

Gomphrena acaulis, commonly known as the upright garden gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

Gomphrena acaulis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
