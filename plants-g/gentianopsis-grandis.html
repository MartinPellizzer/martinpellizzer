Gentianopsis grandis

Gentianopsis grandis, commonly known as the large gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
