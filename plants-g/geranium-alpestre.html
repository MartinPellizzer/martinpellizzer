Geranium alpestre

Geranium alpestre, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows and mountainous tundras. The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are usually hairy and adapted to cold climates. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx, blooming in late summer.

Geranium alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine habitats.
