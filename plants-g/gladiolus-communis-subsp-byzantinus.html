Gladiolus communis subsp. byzantinus

Gladiolus communis subsp.

byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine gladiolus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its botanical name reflects its historical association with ancient Byzantine culture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been widely cultivated for floral arrangements.
