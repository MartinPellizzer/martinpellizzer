Gnaphalium laevissimum

Gnaphalium laevissimum, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flower heads that are surrounded by silky, white bracts, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Gnaphalium laevissimum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and delicate appearance.
