Gentiana germanica subsp. austriaca

Gentiana germanica subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
