Goodyera menziesii

Goodyera menziesii, commonly known as the lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent lip. The plant grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or on rock surfaces. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and it forms a rosette structure that helps retain moisture.

Goodyera menziesii is a perennial herb that is typically found in association with coniferous trees.
