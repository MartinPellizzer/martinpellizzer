Grindelia chiloensis var. pampeana

Grindelia chiloensis var. pampeana, commonly known as the Argentine gumweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.
