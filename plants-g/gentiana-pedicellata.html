Gentiana pedicellata

Gentiana pedicellata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana pedicellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
