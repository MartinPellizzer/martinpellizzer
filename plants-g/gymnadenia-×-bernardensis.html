Gymnadenia × bernardensis

Gymnadenia × bernardensis, commonly known as the Bernard's grass pink, is a hybrid orchid species native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, flowering stems that bear clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
