Georgina superflua var. pallida

Georgina superflua var. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered georgina, is a unique variant of the georgina species characterized by its striking pale coloration.

This plant is native to specific regions with a particular climate, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, exhibiting a soft, almost translucent appearance. The leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
