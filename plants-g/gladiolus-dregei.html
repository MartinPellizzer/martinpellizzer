Gladiolus dregei

Gladiolus dregei, commonly known as the Cape gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus dregei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
