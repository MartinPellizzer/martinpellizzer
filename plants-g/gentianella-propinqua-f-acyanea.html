Gentianella propinqua f. acyanea

Gentianella propinqua f. acyanea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stem is usually unbranched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
