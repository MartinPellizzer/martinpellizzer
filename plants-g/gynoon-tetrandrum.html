Gynoon tetrandrum

Gynoon tetrandrum, commonly known as the four-threaded gynoon, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gynonaceae and is characterized by its distinctive four stamens in its floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but they play a vital role in the plant's reproductive cycle.
