Gratiola rotundifolia

Gratiola rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial species that blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
