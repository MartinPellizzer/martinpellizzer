Guzmania testudinis var. splendida

Guzmania testudinis var. splendida, commonly known as the Splendida Guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can range from red to orange and yellow, often resembling a crown of flames. The leaves are typically broad, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, with a waxy, often variegated surface. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
