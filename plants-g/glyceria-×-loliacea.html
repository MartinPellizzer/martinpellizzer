Glyceria × loliacea

Glyceria × loliacea, commonly known as the rivergrass or marsh grass, is a hybrid species within the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three flowering branches. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Glyceria × loliacea is commonly found in wetland habitats, including marshes, stream banks, and flooded meadows.
