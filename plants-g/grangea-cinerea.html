Grangea cinerea

Grangea cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf grangea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
