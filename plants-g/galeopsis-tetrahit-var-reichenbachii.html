Galeopsis tetrahit var. reichenbachii

Galeopsis tetrahit var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horseweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides or in dry meadows.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
