Guettarda vulpina

Guettarda vulpina, commonly known as the fox tail or wild hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, while its fruits are capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Guettarda vulpina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
