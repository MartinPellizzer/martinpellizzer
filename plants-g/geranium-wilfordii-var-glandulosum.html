Geranium wilfordii var. glandulosum

Geranium wilfordii var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, often covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
