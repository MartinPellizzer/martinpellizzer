Genista tabernaemontani

Genista tabernaemontani, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical racemes. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Genista tabernaemontani is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
