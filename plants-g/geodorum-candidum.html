Geodorum candidum

Geodorum candidum, commonly known as the white ginger orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Geodorum candidum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its delicate floral display.
