Genista cazorlana var. malessana

Genista cazorlana var. malessana, commonly known as the Malessana broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its foliage is typically green with a slight pubescence, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
