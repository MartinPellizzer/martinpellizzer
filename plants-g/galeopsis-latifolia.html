Galeopsis latifolia

Galeopsis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hedge-net, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often resembling a hedge. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis latifolia is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
