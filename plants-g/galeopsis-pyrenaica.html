Galeopsis pyrenaica

Galeopsis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Galeopsis pyrenaica is an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing nectar for pollinators.
