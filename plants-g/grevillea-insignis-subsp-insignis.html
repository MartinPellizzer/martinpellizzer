Grevillea insignis subsp. insignis

Grevillea insignis subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice in horticulture, though it requires careful management due to its sensitivity to frost and poor drainage.
