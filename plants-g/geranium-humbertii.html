Geranium humbertii

Geranium humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink to purple blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Geranium humbertii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
