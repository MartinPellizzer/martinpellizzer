Gentiana szechenyii subsp. szechenyii

Gentiana szechenyii subsp.

szechenyii, commonly known as the Szechenyi gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
