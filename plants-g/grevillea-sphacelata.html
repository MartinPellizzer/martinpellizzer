Grevillea sphacelata

Grevillea sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Grevillea sphacelata is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
