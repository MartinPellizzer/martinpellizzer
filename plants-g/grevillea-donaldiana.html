Grevillea donaldiana

Grevillea donaldiana, commonly known as the Donald's glory of the hills, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea donaldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its striking floral display.
