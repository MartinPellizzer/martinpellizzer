Gaultheria alnifolia

Gaultheria alnifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Gaultheria alnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
