Galactia grewiifolia var. mucronulata

Galactia grewiifolia var. mucronulata, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in stabilizing soil and supporting other vegetation in degraded habitats.
