Gaultheria veitchiana

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in late winter or early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds, giving it a mint-like scent, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

Gaultheria veitchiana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is relatively frost-tolerant, making it suitable for cooler climates.
