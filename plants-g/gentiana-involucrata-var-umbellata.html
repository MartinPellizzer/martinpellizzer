Gentiana involucrata var. umbellata

Gentiana involucrata var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
