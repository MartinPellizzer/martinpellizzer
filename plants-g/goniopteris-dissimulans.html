Goniopteris dissimulans

Goniopteris dissimulans, commonly known as the hidden fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps protect it from excessive moisture loss. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in dense forests or along stream banks. Its spores are dispersed by wind, and the plant reproduces through a complex life cycle involving both sexual and asexual reproduction.

Due to habitat destruction, Goniopteris dissimulans is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists studying evolutionary plant adaptations.
