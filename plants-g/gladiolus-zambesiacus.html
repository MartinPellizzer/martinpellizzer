Gladiolus zambesiacus

Gladiolus zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of vibrant, colorful blooms in shades of red, yellow, and orange. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
