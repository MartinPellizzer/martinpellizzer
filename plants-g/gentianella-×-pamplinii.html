Gentianella × pamplinii

Gentianella × pamplinii, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm, featuring basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
