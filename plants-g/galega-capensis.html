Galega capensis

Galega capensis, commonly known as the Cape galega, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Galega capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
