Geum ciliatum

Geum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has finely toothed, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Geum ciliatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
