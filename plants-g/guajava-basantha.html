Guajava basantha

Guajava basantha, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a yellow, astringent berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed. It is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
