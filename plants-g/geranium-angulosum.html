Geranium angulosum

Geranium angulosum, commonly known as the angular geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive angular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
