Gymnopodium floribundum var. antigonoides

Gymnopodium floribundum var. antigonoides, commonly known as the Antigonoides variant of the flowering gymnopodium, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the summer months. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native habitat.
