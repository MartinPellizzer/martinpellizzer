Gymnogramma tomentosa var. pumila

Gymnogramma tomentosa var. pumila, commonly known as the hairy gymnogramma, is a variant of the gymnogramma species native to tropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage. The leaves are typically bi-lobed and have a compact, clustered growth habit. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
