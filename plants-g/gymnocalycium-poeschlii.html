Gymnocalycium poeschlii

Gymnocalycium poeschlii, commonly known as the Poeschli's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and features a thick, spiny stem covered in areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Gymnocalycium poeschlii is a succulent, capable of storing water in its fleshy stems, making it drought-tolerant.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to flourish.
