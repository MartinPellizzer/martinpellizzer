Grossularia inermis f. puberula

Grossularia inermis f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent gooseberry, is a botanical variant of the common gooseberry.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which distinguish it from the typical form. This plant is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and is often found in forested areas. It produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

The pubescent foliage gives it a unique appearance and makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
