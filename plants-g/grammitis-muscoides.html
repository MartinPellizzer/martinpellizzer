Grammitis muscoides

Grammitis muscoides, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, creeping rhizomes and produces small, clustered fronds with a moss-like appearance. Its leaves are usually dark green and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Grammitis muscoides is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
