Gentiana ampla

Gentiana ampla, commonly known as the Colorado gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana ampla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
