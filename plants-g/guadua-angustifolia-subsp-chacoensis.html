Guadua angustifolia subsp. chacoensis

Guadua angustifolia subsp.

chacoensis, commonly known as Chaco bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and northern Argentina. It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a valuable resource for construction and handicrafts.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which contribute to soil stabilization and erosion control.

Additionally, it is noted for its high tensile strength and sustainability, making it an important species in ecological and economic contexts.
