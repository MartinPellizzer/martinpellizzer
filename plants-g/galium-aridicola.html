Galium aridicola

Galium aridicola, commonly known as the desert bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and usually oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Galium aridicola is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
