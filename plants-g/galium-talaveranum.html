Galium talaveranum

Galium talaveranum, commonly known as the Talavera bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Galium talaveranum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
