Genista tinctoria var. hirsuta

Genista tinctoria var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy yellow broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
