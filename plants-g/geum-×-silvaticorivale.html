Geum × silvaticorivale

Geum × silvaticorivale, commonly known as the hybrid garden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with palmate leaves that are divided into 5–7 lobes. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and foliage, which add color to shaded landscapes.
