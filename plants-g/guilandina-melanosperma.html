Guilandina melanosperma

Guilandina melanosperma, commonly known as the black-seed legume, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing dark, hard seeds. Its seeds are rich in proteins and oils, making it valuable for both nutritional and medicinal purposes.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.
