Geraniospermum crassipes

Geraniospermum crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its stems are often branched and can produce multiple flowering shoots.

Geraniospermum crassipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
