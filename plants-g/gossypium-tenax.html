Gossypium tenax

Gossypium tenax, commonly known as wild cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, woolly leaves. It produces fluffy, white flowers that are followed by capsules containing fibrous seeds.

Gossypium tenax is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
