Galium circaezans var. montanum

Galium circaezans var. montanum, commonly known as mountain wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge on the veins. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
