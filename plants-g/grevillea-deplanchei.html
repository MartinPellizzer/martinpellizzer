Grevillea deplanchei

Grevillea deplanchei, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to red flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea deplanchei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and public landscapes.
