Gratiola genuflora

Gratiola genuflora, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically pale blue or purple, with five petals and a prominent central spur. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.
