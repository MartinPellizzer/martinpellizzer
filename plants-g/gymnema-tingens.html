Gymnema tingens

Gymnema tingens, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are opposite and sometimes heart-shaped. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the digestive system, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicine. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which are believed to have hypoglycemic properties.

Gymnema tingens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique foliage.
