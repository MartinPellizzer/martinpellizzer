Gongora seideliana

Gongora seideliana, commonly known as the Seidel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns and a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Gongora seideliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
