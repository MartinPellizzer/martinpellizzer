Giuliettia belizensis

Giuliettia belizensis, commonly known as the Belize giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a feature known as nyctinasty. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of water bodies, and it plays a role in water filtration and nutrient cycling.

Giuliettia belizensis is also an important species for studying the ecological dynamics of tropical wetlands.
