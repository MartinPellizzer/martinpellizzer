Gasteranthus rupestris

Gasteranthus rupestris, commonly known as the rock passionflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble a cluster of grapes, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bats. The plant has a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with a unique structure that facilitates specialized pollination.

Gasteranthus rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its eye-catching appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
