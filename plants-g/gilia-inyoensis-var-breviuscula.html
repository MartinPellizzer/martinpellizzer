Gilia inyoensis var. breviuscula

Gilia inyoensis var. breviuscula, commonly known as the Inyo Gilia, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California and Nevada.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Gilia inyoensis var. breviuscula plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small desert organisms.
