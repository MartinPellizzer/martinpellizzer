Glycyrrhiza echinata var. frearitis

Glycyrrhiza echinata var. frearitis, commonly known as the Chinese licorice or licorice root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and are valued for their sweet taste and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza echinata var. frearitis is also used in the production of various herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
