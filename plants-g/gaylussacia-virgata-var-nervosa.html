Gaylussacia virgata var. nervosa

Gaylussacia virgata var. nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that persist through the winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinct venation pattern that gives it its varietal name.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
