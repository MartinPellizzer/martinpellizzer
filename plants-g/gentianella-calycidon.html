Gentianella calycidon

Gentianella calycidon, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes, and they attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
