Guajava ciliata

Guajava ciliata, commonly known as the hairy guajava, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn red when mature.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
