Gymnema latifolium

Gymnema latifolium, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon or sugarleaf, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. Gymnema latifolium is renowned for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to reduce blood sugar levels.

Its leaves contain compounds like gymnemic acids, which are believed to interfere with sugar absorption in the intestines.
