Gypsophila heteropoda subsp. minutiflora

Gypsophila heteropoda subsp.

minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf garden clover, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and extended blooming period make it a valued addition to ornamental gardens.
