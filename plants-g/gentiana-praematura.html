Gentiana praematura

Gentiana praematura, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

The early gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
