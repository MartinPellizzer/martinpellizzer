Galium demissum f. intermedium

Galium demissum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
