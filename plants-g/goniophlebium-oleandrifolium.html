Goniophlebium oleandrifolium

Goniophlebium oleandrifolium, commonly known as the red-veined daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red veins on its pale green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Goniophlebium oleandrifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
