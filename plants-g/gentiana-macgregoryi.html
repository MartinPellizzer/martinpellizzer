Gentiana macgregoryi

Gentiana macgregoryi, commonly known as MacGregor's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana macgregoryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
