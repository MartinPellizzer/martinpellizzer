Gymnadenia conopsea var. crenulata

Gymnadenia conopsea var. crenulata, commonly known as the crested gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and often branched.

The variety crenulata is distinguished by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves and a more robust growth habit compared to other varieties.
