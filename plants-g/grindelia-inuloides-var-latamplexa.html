Grindelia inuloides var. latamplexa

Grindelia inuloides var. latamplexa, commonly known as the Desert gumplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, resinous leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its structure includes a low-growing, branching stem with a basal rosette of leaves.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
