Gymnopogon spicatus var. pluriflorus

Gymnopogon spicatus var. pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
