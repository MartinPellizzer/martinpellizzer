Geophila matthewii

Geophila matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Geophila matthewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
