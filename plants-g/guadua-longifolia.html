Guadua longifolia

Guadua longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. Guadua longifolia is valued for its strong, lightweight bamboo-like material, which is used in construction and handicrafts.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
