Gymnocarpium oyamense

Gymnocarpium oyamense, commonly known as the Japanese naked-seed plant, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually trifoliate or palmately compound. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in racemes, and it produces naked seeds in a fleshy, berry-like structure.

Gymnocarpium oyamense is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
