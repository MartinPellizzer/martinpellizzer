Grossularia vulgaris

Grossularia vulgaris, commonly known as the common gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, followed by the characteristic berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in cooking and traditional medicine.
