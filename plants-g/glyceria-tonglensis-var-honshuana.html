Glyceria tonglensis var. honshuana

Glyceria tonglensis var. honshuana, commonly known as the Honshu reed, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle.

The plant has fibrous roots and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
