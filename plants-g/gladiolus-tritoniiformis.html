Gladiolus tritoniiformis

Gladiolus tritoniiformis, commonly known as the trumpet gladiolus, is a species native to the eastern Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble a trumpet, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems with narrow, sword-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy bloom at the top of the stem, which is often used in floral arrangements.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant, dramatic flowers.
