Galium carolinianum

Galium carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
