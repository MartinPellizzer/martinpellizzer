Grewia cylindrica

Grewia cylindrica, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Grewia cylindrica is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
