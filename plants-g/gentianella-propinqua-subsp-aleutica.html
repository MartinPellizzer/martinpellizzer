Gentianella propinqua subsp. aleutica

Gentianella propinqua subsp.

aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Aleutian Islands and surrounding regions of Alaska. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as tundra and coastal bluffs.

The plant features dark blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in its native environment.
