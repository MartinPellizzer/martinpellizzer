Glycine ensiformis

Glycine ensiformis, commonly known as the sicklebean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Glycine ensiformis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
