Gentiana decorata subsp. leucantha

Gentiana decorata subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as the white gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies.
