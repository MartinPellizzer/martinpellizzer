Gentiana hypericifolia

Gentiana hypericifolia, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Gentiana hypericifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
