Geranium depraehensum

Geranium depraehensum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and features upright, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium depraehensum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
