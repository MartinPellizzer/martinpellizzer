Garcinia warrenii

Garcinia warrenii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces oblong, yellow fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single, large, bitter seed. Garcinia warrenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
