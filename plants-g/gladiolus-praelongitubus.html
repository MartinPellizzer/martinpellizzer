Gladiolus praelongitubus

Gladiolus praelongitubus, commonly known as the long-tube gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors. The plant produces large, showy blooms with long, narrow petals that are often arranged in a spiral. Its tuberous roots allow it to store nutrients, supporting its growth in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
