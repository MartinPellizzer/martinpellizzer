Galium sorgerae

Galium sorgerae, commonly known as the Sierra bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive mitten-like shape with a broad base.

Galium sorgerae thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
