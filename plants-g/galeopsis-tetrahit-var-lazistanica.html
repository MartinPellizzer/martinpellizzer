Galeopsis tetrahit var. lazistanica

Galeopsis tetrahit var. lazistanica, commonly known as the Persian hogweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean and parts of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typical of the Boraginaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
