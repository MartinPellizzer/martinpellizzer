Genista fukarekiana

Genista fukarekiana, commonly known as the Fukarek's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
