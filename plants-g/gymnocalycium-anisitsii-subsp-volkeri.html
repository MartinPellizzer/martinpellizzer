Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp. volkeri

Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp.

volkeri, commonly known as the Volker's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its globose to cylindrical stem that is often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
