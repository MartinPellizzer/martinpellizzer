Geranium pyrenaicum proles lusitanicum

Geranium pyrenaicum proles lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
