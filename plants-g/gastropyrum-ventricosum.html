Gastropyrum ventricosum

Gastropyrum ventricosum, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its inflated, pod-like seed capsules that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands.

Gastropyrum ventricosum produces small white or yellow flowers and is often found in prairie and wetland habitats.
