Gastrochilus pumilis

Gastrochilus pumilis, commonly known as the dwarf stomach orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Due to its small size and specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
