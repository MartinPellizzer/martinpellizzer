Glyceria maritima f. arenaria

Glyceria maritima f. arenaria, commonly known as the sand glyceria, is a subspecies of the marsh grass Glyceria maritima.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in coastal sandy soils and salt marshes. This plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to saline environments and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Its presence is often an indicator of stable coastal ecosystems.
