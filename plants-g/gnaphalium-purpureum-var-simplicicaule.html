Gnaphalium purpureum var. simplicicaule

Gnaphalium purpureum var. simplicicaule, commonly known as purple cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems, with small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the plant produces small, pale purple to white florets.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
