Gireoudia pilifera

Gireoudia pilifera, commonly known as the silky gireoudia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-covered stems and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

Gireoudia pilifera is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
