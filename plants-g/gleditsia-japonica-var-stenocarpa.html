Gleditsia japonica var. stenocarpa

Gleditsia japonica var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated. The plant produces yellow-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability.

The species is also noted for its tolerance to urban environments and air pollution.
