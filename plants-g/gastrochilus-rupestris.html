Gastrochilus rupestris

Gastrochilus rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocky substrates and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it blooms during the rainy season. Gastrochilus rupestris is adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important member of the orchid family.
