Garcinia garciae

Garcinia garciae, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are typically covered in a thin, waxy coating. Garcinia garciae is known for its high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for potential weight-loss properties.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.
