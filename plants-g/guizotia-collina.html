Guizotia collina

Guizotia collina, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

The showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
