Geum anemonoides var. gracile

Geum anemonoides var. gracile, commonly known as the slender meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form colonies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and pollinator support.
