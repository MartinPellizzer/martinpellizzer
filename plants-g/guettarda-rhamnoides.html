Guettarda rhamnoides

Guettarda rhamnoides, commonly known as the dwarf glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Guettarda rhamnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
