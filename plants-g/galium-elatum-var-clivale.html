Galium elatum var. clivale

Galium elatum var. clivale, commonly known as the alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Galium elatum var. clivale thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
