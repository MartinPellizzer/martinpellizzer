Gymnolaena integrifolia

Gymnolaena integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are smooth and entire, giving it its scientific name, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Gymnolaena integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
