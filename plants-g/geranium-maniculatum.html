Geranium maniculatum

Geranium maniculatum, commonly known as the manical skullcap, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough, textured surface.

Geranium maniculatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
