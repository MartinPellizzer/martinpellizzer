Geum iyoanum

Geum iyoanum, commonly known as the Japanese avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Geum iyoanum is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
