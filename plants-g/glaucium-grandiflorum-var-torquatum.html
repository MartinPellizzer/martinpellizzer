Glaucium grandiflorum var. torquatum

Glaucium grandiflorum var. torquatum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
