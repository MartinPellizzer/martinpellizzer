Gladiolus dracocephalus

Gladiolus dracocephalus, commonly known as the dragonhead gladiolus, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The flowers are typically purple or violet, with a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a sword, giving the plant its genus name. It produces large, showy blooms that are often used in gardens for their ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for summer gardens.
