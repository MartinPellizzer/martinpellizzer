Geranium tanii

Geranium tanii, commonly known as the Tanii geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Geranium tanii is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.
