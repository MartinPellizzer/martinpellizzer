Grevillea wiradjuri

Grevillea wiradjuri, commonly known as the Wiradjuri waratah, is a shrub native to the south-eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

Grevillea wiradjuri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
