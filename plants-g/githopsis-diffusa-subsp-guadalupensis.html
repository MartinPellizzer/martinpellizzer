Githopsis diffusa subsp.

guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe githopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
