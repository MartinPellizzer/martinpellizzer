Guenthera subscaposa

Guenthera subscaposa, commonly known as the narrowleaf guenthera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
