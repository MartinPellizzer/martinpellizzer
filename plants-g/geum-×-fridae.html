Geum × fridae

Geum × fridae, commonly known as the hybrid garden buttercup, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features heart-shaped, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Geum × fridae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
