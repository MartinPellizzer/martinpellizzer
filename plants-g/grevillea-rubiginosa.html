Grevillea rubiginosa

Grevillea rubiginosa, commonly known as the common waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in coastal and temperate regions.

Grevillea rubiginosa is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for honeyeaters and other native wildlife.
