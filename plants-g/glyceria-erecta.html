Glyceria erecta

Glyceria erecta, commonly known as tall glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Glyceria erecta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
