Gonystylus affinis var. elegans

Gonystylus affinis var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and straight, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is typically found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and is often used in traditional woodworking and furniture crafting.
