Gladiolus luembensis

Gladiolus luembensis, commonly known as the Luembensis gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing its vibrant petals that are often used in floral arrangements. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a structure similar to other gladiolus species, with a central spike of flowers.

This species is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential in horticultural and ecological studies.
