Gentiana taiwanica

Gentiana taiwanica, commonly known as the Taiwan gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
