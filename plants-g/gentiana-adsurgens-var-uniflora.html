Gentiana adsurgens var. uniflora

Gentiana adsurgens var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
