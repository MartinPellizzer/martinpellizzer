Guillenia lasiophylla

Guillenia lasiophylla, commonly known as the woolly guillenia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a unique texture. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Guillenia lasiophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
