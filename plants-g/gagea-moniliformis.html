Gagea moniliformis

Gagea moniliformis, commonly known as the clustered gagea, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant is characterized by its unique clustered inflorescence, which resembles a small crown or necklace. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Gagea moniliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
