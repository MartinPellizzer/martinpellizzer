Gomidesia chamissoana

Gomidesia chamissoana, commonly known as the Chilean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and coastal climates of its native habitat. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Gomidesia chamissoana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens with suitable conditions.
