Gardenia subcarinata var. sumatrana

Gardenia subcarinata var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, white, waxy flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
