Grevillea crithmifolia

Grevillea crithmifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Grevillea crithmifolia is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
