Gaertnera walkeri var. angustifolia

Gaertnera walkeri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Gaertnera, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Gaertnera walkeri var. angustifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
