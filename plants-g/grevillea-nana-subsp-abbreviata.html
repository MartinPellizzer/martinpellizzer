Grevillea nana subsp. abbreviata

Grevillea nana subsp.

abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf grevillea, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia. It features small, clustered leaves that are typically green with a slight hairy texture.

The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
