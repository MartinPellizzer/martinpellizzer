Geohintonia

Geohintonia, commonly known as the moonflower of the deep, is a rare and enigmatic plant found in subterranean environments.

Its most striking botanical feature is its bioluminescent flowers, which emit a soft blue glow in the absence of sunlight. The plant has adapted to low-light conditions with large, broad leaves that maximize light absorption. Its root system is highly specialized, capable of extracting nutrients from mineral-rich soil.

Despite its unique characteristics, little is known about its reproductive mechanisms and ecological role.
