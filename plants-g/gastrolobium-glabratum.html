Gastrolobium glabratum

Gastrolobium glabratum, commonly known as the smooth wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow-flowered spikes that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Gastrolobium glabratum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.
