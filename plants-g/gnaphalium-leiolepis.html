Gnaphalium leiolepis

Gnaphalium leiolepis, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
