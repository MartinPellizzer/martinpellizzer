Gentiana andrewsii var. dakotica

Gentiana andrewsii var. dakotica, commonly known as the Dakota gentian, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

The Dakota gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
