Grewia annamica

Grewia annamica, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, white flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia annamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
