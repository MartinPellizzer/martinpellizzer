Grewia cernua

Grewia cernua, commonly known as the hanging grewia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the summer months. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Grewia cernua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
