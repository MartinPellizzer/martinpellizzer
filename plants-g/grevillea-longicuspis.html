Grevillea longicuspis

Grevillea longicuspis, commonly known as the long-fruited grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces long, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea longicuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to native wildlife.
