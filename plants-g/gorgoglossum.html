Gorgoglossum

Gorgoglossum, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are perennial herbs with basal rosettes of leaves. The flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.
