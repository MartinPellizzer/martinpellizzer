Gesneria filisepala

Gesneria filisepala, commonly known as the thread-sepal gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that often display a vibrant green color with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria filisepala is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
