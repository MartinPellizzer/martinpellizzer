Gigantochloa

Gigantochloa, commonly known as giant bamboo, is a genus of tall, fast-growing grasses native to Southeast Asia.

These plants are characterized by their slender, hollow stems and large, feather-like leaves. They are often found in wetland and swampy environments, where they form dense stands. Gigantochloa species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are also used in traditional construction and as a source of fiber.

Their rapid growth rate makes them useful for erosion control and reforestation projects.
