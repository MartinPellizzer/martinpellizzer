Geum urbanum subvar. robustum

Geum urbanum subvar. robustum, commonly known as the common avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
