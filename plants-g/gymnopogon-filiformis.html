Gymnopogon filiformis

Gymnopogon filiformis, commonly known as the slender grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that distinguishes it from other similar species.

Gymnopogon filiformis is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
