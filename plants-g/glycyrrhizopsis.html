Glycyrrhizopsis

Glycyrrhizopsis, commonly known as sweetroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellow or green flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by leguminous pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Glycyrrhizopsis species are typically found in temperate regions and are adapted to well-drained soils.

These plants are valued for their potential medicinal properties and their role in ecological systems.
