Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. densiflorum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum f. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflowered cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This fern-like plant is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
