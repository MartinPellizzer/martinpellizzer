Gentiana fischeri

Gentiana fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana fischeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
