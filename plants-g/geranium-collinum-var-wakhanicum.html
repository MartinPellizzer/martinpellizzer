Geranium collinum var. wakhanicum

Geranium collinum var. wakhanicum, commonly known as the Wakhan geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, blooming in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
