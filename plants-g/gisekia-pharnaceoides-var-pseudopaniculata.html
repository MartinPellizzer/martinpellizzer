Gisekia pharnaceoides var. pseudopaniculata

Gisekia pharnaceoides var. pseudopaniculata, commonly known as the panicle gisekia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
