Gerascanthus petenensis

Gerascanthus petenensis, commonly known as the Maya sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Mexico and northern Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
