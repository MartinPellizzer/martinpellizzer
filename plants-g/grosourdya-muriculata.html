Grosourdya muriculata

Grosourdya muriculata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Grosourdya muriculata is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
