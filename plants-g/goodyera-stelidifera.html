Goodyera stelidifera

Goodyera stelidifera, commonly known as the slender leaf lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and its distinctive, showy orchid flowers that resemble small slippers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it forms a dense, low-growing mat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Goodyera stelidifera is a rare and endangered species, often requiring conservation efforts to protect its habitat.
