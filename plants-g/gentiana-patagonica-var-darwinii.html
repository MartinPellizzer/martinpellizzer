Gentiana patagonica var. darwinii

Gentiana patagonica var. darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the Andes of Argentina.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
