Ginoria curvispina

Ginoria curvispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginoria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with erect, branching stems that produce clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange in color, and they bloom during the warmer months of the year. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
