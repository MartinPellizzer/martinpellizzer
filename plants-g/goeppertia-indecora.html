Goeppertia indecora

Goeppertia indecora, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a member of the Marantaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a rhizomatous herb, with a compact rosette of foliage. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It prefers indirect light and consistently moist soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
