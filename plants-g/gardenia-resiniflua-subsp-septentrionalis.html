Gardenia resiniflua subsp. septentrionalis

Gardenia resiniflua subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern gardenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces a resinous substance, which contributes to its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
