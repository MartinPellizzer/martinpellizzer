Guzmania fuerstenbergiana

Guzmania fuerstenbergiana, commonly known as the Fuerstenberg's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insects. Its unique structure allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments without soil.

Guzmania fuerstenbergiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
