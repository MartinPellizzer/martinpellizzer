Gilia macombii var. laxiflora

Gilia macombii var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Macomb's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety laxiflora is distinguished by its more open inflorescence compared to other varieties of Gilia macombii.
