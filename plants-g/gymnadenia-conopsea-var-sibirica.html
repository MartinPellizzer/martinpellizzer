Gymnadenia conopsea var. sibirica

Gymnadenia conopsea var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian grass pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as tundra and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots form a dense, fibrous network.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
