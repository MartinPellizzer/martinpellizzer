Grewia bipartita

Grewia bipartita, commonly known as the red hibiscus or sour fig, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, showy flowers that range from white to pink, often with a red or orange throat. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Grewia bipartita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
