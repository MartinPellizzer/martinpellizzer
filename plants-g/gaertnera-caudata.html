Gaertnera caudata

Gaertnera caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, which helps to retain moisture.

Gaertnera caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
