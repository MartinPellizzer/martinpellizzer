Garcinia puat

Garcinia puat, commonly known as the Malayan Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia puat is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and phytochemical research.
