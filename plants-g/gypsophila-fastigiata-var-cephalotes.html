Gypsophila fastigiata var. cephalotes

Gypsophila fastigiata var. cephalotes, commonly known as the upright gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces numerous slender, branching stems that bear tightly packed inflorescences resembling a fluffy crown. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
