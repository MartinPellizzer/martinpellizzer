Guilandina urophylla

Guilandina urophylla, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Guilandina urophylla is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in various crafts and construction.
