Galium angustifolium subsp. nudicaule

Galium angustifolium subsp.

nudicaule, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often hairless or sparsely hairy.

The leaves are narrow and linear, usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems during early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
