Gnidia caduca

Gnidia caduca, commonly known as the Cape rose or wild rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

The plant is often found in rocky areas and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
