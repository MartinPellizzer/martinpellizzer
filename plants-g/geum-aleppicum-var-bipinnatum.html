Geum aleppicum var. bipinnatum

Geum aleppicum var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the Aleppo bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear alternate, pinnately compound leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
