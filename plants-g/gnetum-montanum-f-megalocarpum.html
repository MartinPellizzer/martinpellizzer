Gnetum montanum f. megalocarpum

Gnetum montanum f. megalocarpum, commonly known as the giant-seeded gnetum, is a unique gymnosperm belonging to the family Gnetaceae.

It is characterized by its distinct morphology, featuring scale-like leaves and a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark. The plant produces large, fleshy cones that contain edible seeds, which are a notable feature of this variety. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary traits, bridging the gap between gymnosperms and angiosperms.
