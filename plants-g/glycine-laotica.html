Glycine laotica

Glycine laotica, commonly known as the Laotian bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Glycine laotica is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
