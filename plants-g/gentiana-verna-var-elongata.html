Gentiana verna var. elongata

Gentiana verna var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are slender and upright.

The elongated gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
