Gaillardia trinervata

Gaillardia trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features bright orange to red petals with yellow centers, and its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia trinervata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
