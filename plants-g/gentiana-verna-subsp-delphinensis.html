Gentiana verna subsp. delphinensis

Gentiana verna subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinine Gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and a slender stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
