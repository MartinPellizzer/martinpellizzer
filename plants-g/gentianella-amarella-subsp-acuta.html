Gentianella amarella subsp. acuta

Gentianella amarella subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the acute gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
