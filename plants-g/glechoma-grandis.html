Glechoma grandis

Glechoma grandis, commonly known as ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a prostrate growth habit with creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and woodland areas.

Glechoma grandis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
