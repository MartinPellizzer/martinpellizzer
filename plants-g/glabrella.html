Glabrella

Glabrella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Glabrella is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
