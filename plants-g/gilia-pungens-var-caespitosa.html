Gilia pungens var. caespitosa

Gilia pungens var. caespitosa, commonly known as the prickly gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has bracteate inflorescences and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the stems are covered in stiff, spiny bristles.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
