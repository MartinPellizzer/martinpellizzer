Geranium robertianum var. parviflorum

Geranium robertianum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
