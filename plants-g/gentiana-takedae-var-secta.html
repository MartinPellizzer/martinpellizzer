Gentiana takedae var. secta

Gentiana takedae var. secta, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
