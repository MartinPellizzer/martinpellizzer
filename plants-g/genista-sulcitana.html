Genista sulcitana

Genista sulcitana, commonly known as the Sicilian broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Italy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista sulcitana is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
