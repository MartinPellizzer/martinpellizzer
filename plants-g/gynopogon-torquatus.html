Gynopogon torquatus

Gynopogon torquatus, commonly known as the twisted beardgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers typically found in clusters at the base of the plant. Gynopogon torquatus is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
