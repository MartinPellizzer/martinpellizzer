Gussonea crinalis

Gussonea crinalis, commonly known as the white-flowered gussonea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Gussonea crinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
