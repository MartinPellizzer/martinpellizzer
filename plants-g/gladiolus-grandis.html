Gladiolus grandis

Gladiolus grandis, commonly known as the giant gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, arranged in dense spikes. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically green and grass-like in appearance.

Gladiolus grandis is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
