Ginannia

Ginannia, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in the Andes region. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments.

Due to its habitat specificity, Ginannia is considered a rare and endangered species.
