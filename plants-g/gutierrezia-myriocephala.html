Gutierrezia myriocephala

Gutierrezia myriocephala, commonly known as the desert gum plant, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Gutierrezia myriocephala plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
