Gentiana kitadakensis

Gentiana kitadakensis, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Gentiana kitadakensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
