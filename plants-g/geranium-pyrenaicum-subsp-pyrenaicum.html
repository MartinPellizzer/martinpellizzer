Geranium pyrenaicum subsp. pyrenaicum

Geranium pyrenaicum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central spur, which is a characteristic feature of the Geraniaceae family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
