Gladiolus burmanni

Gladiolus burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive, narrow, blade-like shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
