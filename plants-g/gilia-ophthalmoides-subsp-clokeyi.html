Gilia ophthalmoides subsp. clokeyi

Gilia ophthalmoides subsp.

clokeyi, commonly known as the Clokey's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and showy, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and supporting insect populations.
