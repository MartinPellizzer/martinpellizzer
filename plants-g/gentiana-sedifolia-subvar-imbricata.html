Gentiana sedifolia subvar. imbricata

Gentiana sedifolia subvar. imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are distinctive for their wavy, serrated edges and are often covered in a fine, woolly texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
