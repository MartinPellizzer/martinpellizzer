Gomphichis koehleri var. minor

Gomphichis koehleri var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
