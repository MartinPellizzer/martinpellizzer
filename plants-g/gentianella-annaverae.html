Gentianella annaverae

Gentianella annaverae, commonly known as the Sicilian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Gentianella annaverae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
