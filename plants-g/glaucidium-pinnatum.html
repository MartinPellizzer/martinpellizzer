Glaucidium pinnatum

Glaucidium pinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glaucidium, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its foliage and flowers are adapted to conserve moisture, making it well-suited to its harsh environment.

Glaucidium pinnatum is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
