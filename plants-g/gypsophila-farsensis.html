Gypsophila farsensis

Gypsophila farsensis, commonly known as Persian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. It produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh climates.

Gypsophila farsensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky soils.
