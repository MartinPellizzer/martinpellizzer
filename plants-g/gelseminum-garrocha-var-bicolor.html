Gelseminum garrocha var. bicolor

Gelseminum garrocha var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored climbing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature yellow and orange petals. The plant is a vigorous climber with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens but requires careful handling due to its poisonous nature.
