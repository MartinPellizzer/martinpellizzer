Galium erectum var. bielzii

Galium erectum var. bielzii, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Galium erectum var. bielzii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
