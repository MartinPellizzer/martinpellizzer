Gaillardia multiceps

Gaillardia multiceps, commonly known as the prairie fire flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features vibrant, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals that resemble flames, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows up to 3 feet tall, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. It is drought-tolerant and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gaillardia multiceps is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread readily in suitable conditions.
