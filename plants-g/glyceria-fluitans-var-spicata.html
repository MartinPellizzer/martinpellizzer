Glyceria fluitans var. spicata

Glyceria fluitans var. spicata, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams, contributing to habitat diversity and supporting various wildlife species.
