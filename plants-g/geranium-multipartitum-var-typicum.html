Geranium multipartitum var. typicum

Geranium multipartitum var. typicum, commonly known as the pink geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
