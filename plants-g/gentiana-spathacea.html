Gentiana spathacea

Gentiana spathacea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or moist soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched.

Gentiana spathacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological importance.
