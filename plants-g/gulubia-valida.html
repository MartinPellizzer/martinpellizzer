Gulubia valida

Gulubia valida, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite on the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gulubia valida is also valued for its ability to climb and can be used in vertical gardening or as a climbing plant in gardens.
