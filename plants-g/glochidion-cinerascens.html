Glochidion cinerascens

Glochidion cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent and have a grayish-white underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
