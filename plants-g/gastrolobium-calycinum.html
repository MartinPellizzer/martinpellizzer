Gastrolobium calycinum

Gastrolobium calycinum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, silver-blue leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens and restoration projects.
