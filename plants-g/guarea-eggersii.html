Guarea eggersii

Guarea eggersii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Guarea eggersii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
