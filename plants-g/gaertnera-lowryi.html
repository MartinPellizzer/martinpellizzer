Gaertnera lowryi

Gaertnera lowryi, commonly known as the Lowry's gaertnera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a subtle scent that attracts pollinators. Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and foliage, making it a desirable plant for collectors and gardeners.

Gaertnera lowryi prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
