Gratiola dubia

Gratiola dubia, commonly known as the slender yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. Its root system is characterized by its yellowish color and aromatic properties.

Gratiola dubia is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its medicinal potential.
