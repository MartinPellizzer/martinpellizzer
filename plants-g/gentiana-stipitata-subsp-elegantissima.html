Gentiana stipitata subsp. elegantissima

Gentiana stipitata subsp.

elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with a prominent lower lip.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
