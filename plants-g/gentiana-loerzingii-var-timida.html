Gentiana loerzingii var. timida

Gentiana loerzingii var. timida, commonly known as the timid gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
