Gossypium siamense

Gossypium siamense, commonly known as Siam cotton, is a species of cotton native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers encasing its seeds, which are used for textile production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving cotton resilience and yield.
