Grewia tenax var. capillipes

Grewia tenax var. capillipes, commonly known as the silky bush or silky wild tea, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
