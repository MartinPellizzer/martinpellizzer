Geranium melanandrum

Geranium melanandrum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Geranium melanandrum is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
