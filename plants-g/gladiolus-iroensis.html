Gladiolus iroensis

Gladiolus iroensis, commonly known as the Iroquois lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, showy flower spikes that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are composed of three petals and three sepals, forming a distinctive, elongated shape.

Gladiolus iroensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
