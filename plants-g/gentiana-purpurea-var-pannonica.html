Gentiana purpurea var. pannonica

Gentiana purpurea var. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian gentian, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
