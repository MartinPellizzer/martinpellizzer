Gypsophila montana

Gypsophila montana, commonly known as the mountain gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and high-altitude environments. Its stems are usually erect and branched, supporting clusters of small, fragrant blossoms.

Gypsophila montana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
