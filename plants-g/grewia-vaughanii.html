Grewia vaughanii

Grewia vaughanii, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that turn reddish in color as they mature. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Grewia vaughanii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
