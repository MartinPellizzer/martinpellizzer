Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. lafaldense

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

lafaldense, commonly known as the La Falda cactus, is a succulent native to Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in arid gardens.
