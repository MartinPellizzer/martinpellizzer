Gompholobium nitidum

Gompholobium nitidum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a silky, hairy texture, and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Gompholobium nitidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
