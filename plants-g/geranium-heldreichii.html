Geranium heldreichii

Geranium heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive five-lobed calyx.

Geranium heldreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
