Guarea mayombensis var. coalescens

Guarea mayombensis var. coalescens, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.
