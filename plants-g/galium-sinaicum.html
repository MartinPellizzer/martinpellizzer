Galium sinaicum

Galium sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and small, opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Galium sinaicum is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.
