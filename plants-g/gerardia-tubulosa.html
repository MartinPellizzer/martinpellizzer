Gerardia tubulosa

Gerardia tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Gerardia tubulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal blooms.
