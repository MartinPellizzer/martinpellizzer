Grewia insularis

Grewia insularis, commonly known as the island grewia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Grewia insularis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

Its adaptability to saline and arid conditions makes it a resilient plant in its native environment.
