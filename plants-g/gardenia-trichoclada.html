Gardenia trichoclada

Gardenia trichoclada, commonly known as the white gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Gardenia trichoclada is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
