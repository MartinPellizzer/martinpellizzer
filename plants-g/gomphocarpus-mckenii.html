Gomphocarpus mckenii

Gomphocarpus mckenii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its fruit is a schizocarp, splitting into two one-seeded parts when mature, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and hardiness.
