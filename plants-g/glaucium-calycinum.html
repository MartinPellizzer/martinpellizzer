Glaucium calycinum

Glaucium calycinum, commonly known as the Mediterranean poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Glaucium calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
