Geissorhiza uliginosa

Geissorhiza uliginosa, commonly known as the bog geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
