Gentianella jamesonii

Gentianella jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

Gentianella jamesonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
