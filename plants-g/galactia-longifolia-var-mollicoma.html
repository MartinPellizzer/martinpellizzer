Galactia longifolia var. mollicoma

Galactia longifolia var. mollicoma, commonly known as the soft-hairy galactia, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with soft, hairy surfaces and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elliptical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
