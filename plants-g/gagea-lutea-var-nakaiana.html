Gagea lutea var. nakaiana

Gagea lutea var. nakaiana, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairie or woodland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round seed pods.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
