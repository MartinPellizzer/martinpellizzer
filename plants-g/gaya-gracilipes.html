Gaya gracilipes

Gaya gracilipes, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, elongated fronds that are often less than 30 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gaya gracilipes plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
