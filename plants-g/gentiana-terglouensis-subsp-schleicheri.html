Gentiana terglouensis subsp. schleicheri

Gentiana terglouensis subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
