Gentiana nivalis var. uniflora

Gentiana nivalis var. uniflora, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

The flowers are tubular with a distinctive spurred shape, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
