Globulariopsis obtusiloba

Globulariopsis obtusiloba, commonly known as the woolly globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of soft, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid climates.
