Gilbertiodendron zenkeri

Gilbertiodendron zenkeri, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its durable, heavy timber.

However, due to overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
