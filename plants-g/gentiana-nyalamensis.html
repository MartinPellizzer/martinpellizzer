Gentiana nyalamensis

Gentiana nyalamensis, commonly known as the Nyalam gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and prominent yellow veins.

Gentiana nyalamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
