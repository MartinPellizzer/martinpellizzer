Guajava benthamiana

Guajava benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's guajava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
