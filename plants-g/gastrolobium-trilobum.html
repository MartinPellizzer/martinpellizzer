Gastrolobium trilobum

Gastrolobium trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Gastrolobium trilobum is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
