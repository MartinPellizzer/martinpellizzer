Genista florida var. occidentalis

Genista florida var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western flowering broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are characteristic of the genus Genista. Its leaves are small, narrow, and typically arranged in pairs along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited to coastal and dry environments.
