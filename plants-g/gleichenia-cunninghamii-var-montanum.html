Gleichenia cunninghamii var. montanum

Gleichenia cunninghamii var. montanum, commonly known as the mountain Gleichenia, is a fern native to the temperate regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, creeping rhizomes and its ability to form dense, mat-like growths. The fronds are typically lance-shaped, with a serrated or toothed margin, and they are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery spores. This variety thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky outcrops and forest floors.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its mountainous habitat, including compact growth patterns and efficient water retention mechanisms.
