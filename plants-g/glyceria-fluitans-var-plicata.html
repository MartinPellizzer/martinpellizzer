Glyceria fluitans var. plicata

Glyceria fluitans var. plicata, commonly known as the pleated watergrass, is a variant of the watergrass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often pleated or folded, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Its reproductive structures consist of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Glyceria fluitans var. plicata plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
