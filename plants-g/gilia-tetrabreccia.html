Gilia tetrabreccia

Gilia tetrabreccia, commonly known as the Death Valley gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It has slender stems and narrow leaves, and it produces small, four-petaled flowers.

Gilia tetrabreccia plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
