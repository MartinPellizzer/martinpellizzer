Grindelia robusta var. maritima

Grindelia robusta var. maritima, commonly known as sea gumweed, is a coastal plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and supporting pollinators.
