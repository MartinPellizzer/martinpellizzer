Grevillea erinacea

Grevillea erinacea, commonly known as the pink grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-red cylindrical flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

Grevillea erinacea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
