Gymnanthes trinervia

Gymnanthes trinervia, commonly known as the red moringa, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
