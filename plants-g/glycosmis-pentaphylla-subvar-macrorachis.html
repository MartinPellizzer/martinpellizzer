Glycosmis pentaphylla subvar. macrorachis

Glycosmis pentaphylla subvar. macrorachis, commonly known as the giant rachis huito, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically yellow or orange when mature, and contains a single seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.
