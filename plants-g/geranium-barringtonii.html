Geranium barringtonii

Geranium barringtonii, commonly known as the Barrington's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Geranium barringtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
