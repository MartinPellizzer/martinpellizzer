Glyceria debilior

Glyceria debilior, commonly known as slender glyceria, is a grass species native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Glyceria debilior plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
