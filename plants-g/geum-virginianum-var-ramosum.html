Geum virginianum var. ramosum

Geum virginianum var. ramosum, commonly known as the ramose avens, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and stems that are often branched, giving it a bushy appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
