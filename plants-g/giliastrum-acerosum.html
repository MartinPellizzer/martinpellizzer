Giliastrum acerosum

Giliastrum acerosum, commonly known as the prickly gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Giliastrum acerosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh landscapes.
