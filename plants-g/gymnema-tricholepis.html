Gymnema tricholepis

Gymnema tricholepis, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon, is a climbing shrub native to India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, ribbed fruits that resemble bitter melons. Gymnema tricholepis is known for its unique ability to reduce the perception of sweetness, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
