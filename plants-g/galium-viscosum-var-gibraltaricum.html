Galium viscosum var. gibraltaricum

Galium viscosum var. gibraltaricum, commonly known as Gibraltar bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.
