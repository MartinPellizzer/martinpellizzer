Genlisea anfractuosa

Genlisea anfractuosa, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate leaves that form bladder-like structures used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in wet, nutrient-poor environments such as marshes and ponds. Its unique adaptation allows it to supplement its nutrient intake by digesting prey.

The botanical features of Genlisea anfractuosa make it a fascinating subject for studying plant evolution and adaptation.
