Gypsophila armeria var. nanus

Gypsophila armeria var. nanus, commonly known as baby's breath, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and extended flowering period make it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens.
