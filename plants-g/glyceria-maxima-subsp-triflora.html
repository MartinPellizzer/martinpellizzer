Glyceria maxima subsp. triflora

Glyceria maxima subsp.

triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh environments in temperate regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences that bear three to five spikelets, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. Glyceria maxima subsp.

triflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
