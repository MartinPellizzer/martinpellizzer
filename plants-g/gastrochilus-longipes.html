Gastrochilus longipes

Gastrochilus longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed stomach lip, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Gastrochilus longipes thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
