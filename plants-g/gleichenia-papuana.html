Gleichenia papuana

Gleichenia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rock surfaces or in moist soil. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

Gleichenia papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional landscaping in its native habitats.
