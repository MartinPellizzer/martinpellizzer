Grevillea acanthifolia var. stenomera

Grevillea acanthifolia var. stenomera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with a compact and upright growth habit.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
