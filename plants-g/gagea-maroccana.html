Gagea maroccana

Gagea maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gagea maroccana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
