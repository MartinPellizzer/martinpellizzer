Garcinia echirensis

Garcinia echirensis, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible, pinkish-red berries that are rich in xanthones and other bioactive compounds. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Garcinia echirensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
