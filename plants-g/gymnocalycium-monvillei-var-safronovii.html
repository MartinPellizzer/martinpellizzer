Gymnocalycium monvillei var. safronovii

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. safronovii, commonly known as the saffron crested cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, edible fruits. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.

This variety is known for its unique coloration and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.
