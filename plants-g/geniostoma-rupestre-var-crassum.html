Geniostoma rupestre var. crassum

Geniostoma rupestre var. crassum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It features basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is often found growing on limestone or rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic environments and rock gardens.
