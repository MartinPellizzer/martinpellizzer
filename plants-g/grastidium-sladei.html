Grastidium sladei

Grastidium sladei, commonly known as the Slade's ghost fern, is a rare and elusive fern species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to flourish. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Grastidium sladei is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
