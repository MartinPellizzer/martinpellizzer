Gymnopteris taccifolia

Gymnopteris taccifolia, commonly known as the Taccifolia spleenwort, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a smooth, glossy surface. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

Gymnopteris taccifolia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
