Geum × intermedium var. willdenowii

Geum × intermedium var. willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenowii bronze begonia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, semi-double to double flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and foliage, which provide seasonal interest throughout the growing season.
