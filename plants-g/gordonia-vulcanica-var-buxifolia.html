Gordonia vulcanica var. buxifolia

Gordonia vulcanica var. buxifolia, commonly known as the wax tree, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, waxy, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
