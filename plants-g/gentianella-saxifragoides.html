Gentianella saxifragoides

Gentianella saxifragoides, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet, with five petals, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
