Glechoma kokamirica

Glechoma kokamirica, commonly known as the Kokamiric ground ivy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

Glechoma kokamirica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
