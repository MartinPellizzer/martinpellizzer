Genista millanii

Genista millanii, commonly known as the Millan's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by cylindrical seed pods. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and coastal areas.

Genista millanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
