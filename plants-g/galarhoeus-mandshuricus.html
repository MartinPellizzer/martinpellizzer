Galarhoeus mandshuricus

Galarhoeus mandshuricus, commonly known as the Manchurian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galarhoeus mandshuricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
