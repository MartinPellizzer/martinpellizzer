Gilibertia matudae

Gilibertia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae's gilbertia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in dry or semi-arid environments.

Gilibertia matudae is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine due to its bioactive compounds.
