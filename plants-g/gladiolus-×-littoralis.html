Gladiolus × littoralis

Gladiolus × littoralis, commonly known as the coastal gladiolus, is a hybrid plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline environments, making it a hardy species for coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape around the stem.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
