Geranium koraiense

Geranium koraiense, commonly known as the Korean geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, adding to its ornamental value.

Geranium koraiense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
