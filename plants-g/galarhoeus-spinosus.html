Galarhoeus spinosus

Galarhoeus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized structures for trapping prey.

It possesses small, bladder-like organs that function as suction traps to capture tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its stems are filamentous and highly branched.

Galarhoeus spinosus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
