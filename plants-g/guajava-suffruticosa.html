Guajava suffruticosa

Guajava suffruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to bright red, cherry-like fruits. These fruits are edible, sweet, and often used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
