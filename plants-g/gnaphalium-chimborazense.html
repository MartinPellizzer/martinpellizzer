Gnaphalium chimborazense

Gnaphalium chimborazense, commonly known as the Andean everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it is known for its ability to thrive in cool, mountainous climates. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, which contribute to its resilience in harsh environments.

Gnaphalium chimborazense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
