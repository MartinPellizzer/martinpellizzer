Genista nociva

Genista nociva, commonly known as the poisonous yellow shrub, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it harmful to both livestock and humans if ingested. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
