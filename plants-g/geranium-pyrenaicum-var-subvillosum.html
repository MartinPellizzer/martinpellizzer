Geranium pyrenaicum var. subvillosum

Geranium pyrenaicum var. subvillosum, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated edge and are covered in a similar fine pubescence.

This variety is often found in rocky or shaded habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh alpine environments.
