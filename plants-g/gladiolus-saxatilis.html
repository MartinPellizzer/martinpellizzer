Gladiolus saxatilis

Gladiolus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Gladiolus saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
