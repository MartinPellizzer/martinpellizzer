Geissorhiza similis

Geissorhiza similis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geissorhiza similis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
