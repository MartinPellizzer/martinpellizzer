Gentianella rhaetica

Gentianella rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Gentianella rhaetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
