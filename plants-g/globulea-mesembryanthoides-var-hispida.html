Globulea mesembryanthoides var. hispida

Globulea mesembryanthoides var. hispida, commonly known as the woolly globulea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle floral display. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size and low water requirements.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
