Gnaphalium baicalense

Gnaphalium baicalense, commonly known as the Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia, including Siberia and Mongolia.

It is a low-growing herb with a mat-forming habit, typically reaching heights of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or tundra-like environments.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
