Gentiana quinquefolia

Gentiana quinquefolia, commonly known as blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
