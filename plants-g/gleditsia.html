Gleditsia

Gleditsia, commonly known as honey locust, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, green pods that contain sweet, edible seeds, which are a food source for various animals. Gleditsia species are valued for their ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

They are also used in landscaping due to their tolerance to urban environments and resistance to pests.
