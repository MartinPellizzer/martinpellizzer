Gentiana spathulifolia var. ciliata

Gentiana spathulifolia var. ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with a prominent corolla. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are broadly ovate with ciliate margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
