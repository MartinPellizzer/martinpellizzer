Grevillea agrifolia subsp. agrifolia

Grevillea agrifolia subsp.

agrifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its bright red flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
