Glossopappus

Glossopappus, commonly known as the silky pappus plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal by wind. The genus includes several species, many of which are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Australia, and South America. Glossopappus plants typically have hairy stems and leaves, and they produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

These plants are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
