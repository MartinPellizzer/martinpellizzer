Grammitis spixiana

Grammitis spixiana, commonly known as the Spix's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the humid tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that often grow on trees or rocks. The plant has a distinctive appearance with dark green, leathery leaves that are closely arranged in a rosette-like formation. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Grammitis spixiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
