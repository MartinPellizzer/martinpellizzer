Garcinia usambarensis

Garcinia usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis Garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

Garcinia usambarensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
