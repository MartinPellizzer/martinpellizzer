Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. izozogsii

Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. izozogsii, commonly known as the Izozog's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the warm season, typically in spring or early summer. It is a low-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
