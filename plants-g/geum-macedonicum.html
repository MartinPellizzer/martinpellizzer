Geum macedonicum

Geum macedonicum, commonly known as Macedonian avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of ovate, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are arranged in a single terminal head. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
