Gonocormus samoensis

Gonocormus samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to the islands of Samoa.

It is characterized by its unique, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a short, unbranched stem and produces few, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
