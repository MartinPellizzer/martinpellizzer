Grevillea petrophiloides subsp. magnifica

Grevillea petrophiloides subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent rock-flower, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, typically with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
