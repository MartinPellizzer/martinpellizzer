Glyceria salinaria

Glyceria salinaria, commonly known as salt marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal wetlands.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in estuaries and tidal flats. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand saltwater exposure. Its flowers are small and spike-like, typically appearing in late summer.

Glyceria salinaria plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
