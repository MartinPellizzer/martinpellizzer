Gossia retusa

Gossia retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gossia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Gossia retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
