Glycyrrhiza grandiflora

Glycyrrhiza grandiflora, commonly known as sweetroot or licorice root, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a robust taproot that is the primary part used for its medicinal and commercial properties. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers in summer. Its root contains glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweet taste and therapeutic uses.

Glycyrrhiza grandiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
