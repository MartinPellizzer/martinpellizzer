Gerbera coriacea

Gerbera coriacea, commonly known as the rough gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with showy ray florets. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soil. Gerbera coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
