Gleicheniastrum elongatum

Gleicheniastrum elongatum, commonly known as the slender Gleichenia, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its long, narrow fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the undersides of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Gleicheniastrum elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
