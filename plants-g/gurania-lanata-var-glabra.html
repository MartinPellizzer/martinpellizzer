Gurania lanata var. glabra

Gurania lanata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-haired gurania, is a variant of the species characterized by its lack of hairy foliage.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or purple.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
