Glycyrrhiza psoraleoides

Glycyrrhiza psoraleoides, commonly known as the Indian licorice, is a leguminous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, brown pods containing several seeds, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
