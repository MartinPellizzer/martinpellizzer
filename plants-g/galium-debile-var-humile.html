Galium debile var. humile

Galium debile var. humile, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium debile var. humile thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
