Galium matthewsii var. magnifolium

Galium matthewsii var. magnifolium, commonly known as the showy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
