Galega pentaphylla

Galega pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed goat's rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Galega pentaphylla is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
