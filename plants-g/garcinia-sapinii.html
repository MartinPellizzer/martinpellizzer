Garcinia sapinii

Garcinia sapinii, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a yellow, acidic pulp. Garcinia sapinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental gardening and reforestation projects.
