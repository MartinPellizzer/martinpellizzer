Gordonia parviflora

Gordonia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and wetlands. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Gordonia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
