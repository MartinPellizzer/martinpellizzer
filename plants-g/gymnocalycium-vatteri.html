Gymnocalycium vatteri

Gymnocalycium vatteri, commonly known as the Vatter's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its succulent stems. Gymnocalycium vatteri typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
