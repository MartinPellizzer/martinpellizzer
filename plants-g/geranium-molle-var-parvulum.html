Geranium molle var. parvulum

Geranium molle var. parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium or small-leaved geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, often with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, which bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a versatile addition to both ornamental and native plant gardens.
