Gerbera ehrenbergii

Gerbera ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert gerbera, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, which help it conserve water in its native habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Gerbera ehrenbergii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
