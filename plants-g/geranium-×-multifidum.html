Geranium × multifidum

Geranium × multifidum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
