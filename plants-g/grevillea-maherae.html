Grevillea maherae

Grevillea maherae, commonly known as the Mahera's grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces striking, feather-like inflorescences that are usually red or yellow, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea maherae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
