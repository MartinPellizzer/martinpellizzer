Gentiana pneumonanthe var. nutans

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of its slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the floral diversity of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty.
