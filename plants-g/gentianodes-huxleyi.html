Gentianodes huxleyi

Gentianodes huxleyi, commonly known as the Huxley's gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its growth habit is usually herbaceous.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
