Grewia capitellata

Grewia capitellata, commonly known as the clustered globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases seeds when mature.
