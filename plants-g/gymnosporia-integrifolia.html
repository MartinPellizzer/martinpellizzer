Gymnosporia integrifolia

Gymnosporia integrifolia, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. Its bark is thick and rugged, often used in traditional medicine.

Gymnosporia integrifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
