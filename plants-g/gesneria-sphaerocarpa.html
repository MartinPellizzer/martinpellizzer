Gesneria sphaerocarpa

Gesneria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-headed gesneria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rounded flower buds that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Gesneria sphaerocarpa is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
