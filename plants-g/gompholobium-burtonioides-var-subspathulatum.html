Gompholobium burtonioides var. subspathulatum

Gompholobium burtonioides var. subspathulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burton's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other forms of Gompholobium burtonioides.
