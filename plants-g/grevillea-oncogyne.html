Grevillea oncogyne

Grevillea oncogyne, commonly known as the red flowering grevillea, is a striking plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters, attracting native pollinators such as honeyeaters and birds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. Grevillea oncogyne is a shrub or small tree, often growing up to 3 meters in height, and is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
