Gentiana jamesii var. albiflora

Gentiana jamesii var. albiflora, commonly known as the Whiteflower gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes or meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
