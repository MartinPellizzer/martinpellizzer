Gerascanthus inornatus

Gerascanthus inornatus, commonly known as the unadorned geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental choice for gardens.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
