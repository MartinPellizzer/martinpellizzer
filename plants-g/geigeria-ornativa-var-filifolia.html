Geigeria ornativa var. filifolia

Geigeria ornativa var. filifolia, commonly known as the filifoliated ornamental geigeria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
