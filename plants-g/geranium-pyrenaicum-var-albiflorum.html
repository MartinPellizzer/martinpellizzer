Geranium pyrenaicum var. albiflorum

Geranium pyrenaicum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
