Gunnera cordifolia

Gunnera cordifolia, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a waxy, dark green surface with a prominent central vein. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by red berries.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
