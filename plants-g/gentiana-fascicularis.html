Gentiana fascicularis

Gentiana fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana fascicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
