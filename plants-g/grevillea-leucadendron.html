Grevillea leucadendron

Grevillea leucadendron, commonly known as the white-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a pale green to white in color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea leucadendron is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds.
