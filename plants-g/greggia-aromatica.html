Greggia aromatica

Greggia aromatica, commonly known as sweet geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in gardens for its ornamental appeal.

Greggia aromatica thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
