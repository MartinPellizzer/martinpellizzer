Guadua riograndensis

Guadua riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande bamboo, is a robust grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a strong, fibrous structure, making it highly valued for its use in construction and handicrafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Guadua riograndensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
