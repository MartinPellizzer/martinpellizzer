Galactia glaucescens var. macrocarpa

Galactia glaucescens var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded false milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its fruit is a pod that contains several large, elliptical seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands.
