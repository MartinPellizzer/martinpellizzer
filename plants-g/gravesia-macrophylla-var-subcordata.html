Gravesia macrophylla var. subcordata

Gravesia macrophylla var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
