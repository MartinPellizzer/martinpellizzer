Geranium partitum

Geranium partitum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive white or light green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its flowers have five sepals and five petals, with the petals being slightly longer than the sepals.

Geranium partitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
