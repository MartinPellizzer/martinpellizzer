Gentianopsis lutea

Gentianopsis lutea, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with five petals and prominent blue veins.

Gentianopsis lutea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
