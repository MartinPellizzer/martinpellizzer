Garcinia assamica

Garcinia assamica, commonly known as the Assam tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia assamica is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in local cuisines for its tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
