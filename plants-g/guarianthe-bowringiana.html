Guarianthe bowringiana

Guarianthe bowringiana, commonly known as the Bowring's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Guarianthe bowringiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
