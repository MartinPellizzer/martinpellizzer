Gentiana tonsa

Gentiana tonsa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and dark green, often with a velvety texture.

Gentiana tonsa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
