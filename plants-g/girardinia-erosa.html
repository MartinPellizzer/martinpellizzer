Girardinia erosa

Girardinia erosa, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread across the water surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and are produced in clusters.

Girardinia erosa plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
