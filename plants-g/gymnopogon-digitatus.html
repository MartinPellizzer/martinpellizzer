Gymnopogon digitatus

Gymnopogon digitatus, commonly known as the digitate grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Gymnopogon digitatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
