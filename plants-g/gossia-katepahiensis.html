Gossia katepahiensis

Gossia katepahiensis, commonly known as the Katepahi gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Tonga.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Gossia katepahiensis is adapted to the tropical climate of Tonga and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
