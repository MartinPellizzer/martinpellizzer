Gerardia verticillata

Gerardia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered gerardia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Gerardia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
