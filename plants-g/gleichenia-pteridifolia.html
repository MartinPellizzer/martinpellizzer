Gleichenia pteridifolia

Gleichenia pteridifolia, commonly known as the fern moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with closely arranged, leafy shoots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually small, elongated, and have a distinct midrib, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gleichenia pteridifolia plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
