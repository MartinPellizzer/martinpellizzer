Guidonia corymbulosa

Guidonia corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered guidonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The botanical characteristics of Guidonia corymbulosa make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
