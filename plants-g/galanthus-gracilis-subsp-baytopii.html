Galanthus gracilis subsp. baytopii

Galanthus gracilis subsp.

baytopii, commonly known as the Baytop's snowdrop, is a rare and delicate perennial herb native to Turkey. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in woodland or rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist conditions. Its flowers have a single, prominent yellow spot at the base, distinguishing it from other snowdrop species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
