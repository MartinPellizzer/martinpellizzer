Gentiana comarapana

Gentiana comarapana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana comarapana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological importance.
