Galium columbianum

Galium columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium columbianum is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is part of the Rubiaceae family.
