Gentianella spenceri

Gentianella spenceri, commonly known as the Spencer's gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gentianella spenceri thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
