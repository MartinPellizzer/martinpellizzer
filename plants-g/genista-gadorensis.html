Genista gadorensis

Genista gadorensis, commonly known as the Gadorensis Broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista gadorensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
