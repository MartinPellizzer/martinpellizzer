Gerascanthus ellipticus

Gerascanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gerascanthus ellipticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
