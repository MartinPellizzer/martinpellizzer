Gentiana divaricata

Gentiana divaricata, commonly known as the divided gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana divaricata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
