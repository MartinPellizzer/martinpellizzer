Geraniospermum ensatum

Geraniospermum ensatum, commonly known as the thread-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
