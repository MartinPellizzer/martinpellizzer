Garcinia klinkii

Garcinia klinkii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, bright red or yellow rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
