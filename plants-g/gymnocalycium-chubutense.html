Gymnocalycium chubutense

Gymnocalycium chubutense, commonly known as the Chubut moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium chubutense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
