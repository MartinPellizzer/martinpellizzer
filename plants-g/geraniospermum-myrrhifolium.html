Geraniospermum myrrhifolium

Geraniospermum myrrhifolium, commonly known as the myrrh-leaf geraniosperm, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic leaves that resemble myrrh in scent. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying shades of pink or white.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
