Gentianella amarella subsp. amarella

Gentianella amarella subsp.

amarella, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
