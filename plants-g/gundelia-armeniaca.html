Gundelia armeniaca

Gundelia armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads and is often found in rocky or arid environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs and produces small, blue or purple flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh climates with low precipitation.

Gundelia armeniaca is also valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine.
