Gymnaster simplex

Gymnaster simplex, commonly known as the simple gymnaster, is a rare and lesser-known plant species belonging to the family Gymnasteraceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, which develop into small, dry fruits containing a single seed. Gymnaster simplex is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and evolution.
