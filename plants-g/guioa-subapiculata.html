Guioa subapiculata

Guioa subapiculata, commonly known as the Philippine guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
