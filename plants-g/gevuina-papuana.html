Gevuina papuana

Gevuina papuana, commonly known as the Papuan gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It produces small, tart berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, tangy flavor. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Gevuina papuana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
