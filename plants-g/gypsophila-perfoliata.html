Gypsophila perfoliata

Gypsophila perfoliata, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments. Its leaves are opposite and often clasping the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
