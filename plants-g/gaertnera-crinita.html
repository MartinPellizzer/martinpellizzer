Gaertnera crinita

Gaertnera crinita, commonly known as the hairy gaertnera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Gaertnera crinita thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
