Gardenia cambodiana

Gardenia cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and scent.

Gardenia cambodiana is also valued for its ability to produce a unique type of fruit, which is edible and has a tart flavor.
