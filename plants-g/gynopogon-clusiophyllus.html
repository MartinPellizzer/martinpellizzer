Gynopogon clusiophyllus

Gynopogon clusiophyllus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers are borne on long, pendulous inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Gynopogon clusiophyllus make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
