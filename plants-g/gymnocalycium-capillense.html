Gymnocalycium capillense

Gymnocalycium capillense, commonly known as the hairless moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems and is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red or purple fruits. Gymnocalycium capillense is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in limited water conditions.
