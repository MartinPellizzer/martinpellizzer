Gossypium sanguineum

Gossypium sanguineum, commonly known as the crimson cotton or blood cotton, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers and hairy, fibrous capsules that contain its seeds. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other cotton species and hibiscus. Its fibrous material, while not as widely cultivated as other cotton types, has historical uses in textile production.
