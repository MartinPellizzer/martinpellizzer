Gumira viburnodes

Gumira viburnodes, commonly known as the glossy leaf viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are thick, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Gumira viburnodes is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
