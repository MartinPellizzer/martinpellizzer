Geranium kotschyi

Geranium kotschyi, commonly known as the Ethiopian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to specialized plant collections.
