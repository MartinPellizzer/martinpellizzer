Gentiana jouyana

Gentiana jouyana, commonly known as the French gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
