Gerrardanthus grandiflorus var. lobatus

Gerrardanthus grandiflorus var. lobatus, commonly known as the Natal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late winter to early spring.
