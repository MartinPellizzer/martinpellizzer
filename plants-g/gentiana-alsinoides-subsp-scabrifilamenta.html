Gentiana alsinoides subsp. scabrifilamenta

Gentiana alsinoides subsp.

scabrifilamenta, commonly known as the rough filament gentian, is a member of the Gentianaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, rough hairs.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in mountainous areas with high elevations.
