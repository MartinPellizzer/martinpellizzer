Geranium sanguineum

Geranium sanguineum, commonly known as the bloody geranium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
