Gypsophila davisii

Gypsophila davisii, commonly known as the Davis's lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it blooms during the summer months.

Gypsophila davisii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
