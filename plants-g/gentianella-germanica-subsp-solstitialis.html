Gentianella germanica subsp. solstitialis

Gentianella germanica subsp.

solstitialis, commonly known as the summer gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often branched and upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
