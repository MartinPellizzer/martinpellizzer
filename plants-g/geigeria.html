Geigeria

Geigeria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. Geigeria species are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. They are perennial herbs with erect stems and narrow leaves.

The genus includes several species, such as Geigeria flava and Geigeria violacea, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
