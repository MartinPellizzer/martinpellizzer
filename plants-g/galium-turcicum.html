Galium turcicum

Galium turcicum, commonly known as Turkish bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that produce small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Galium turcicum is known for its fragrant scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
