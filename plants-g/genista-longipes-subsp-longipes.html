Genista longipes subsp. longipes

Genista longipes subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of opposite, leathery leaves.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in racemes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
