Guapira sancarlosiana

Guapira sancarlosiana, commonly known as the Sancarlosian guapira, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Guapira sancarlosiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
