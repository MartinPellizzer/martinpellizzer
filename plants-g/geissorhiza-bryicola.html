Geissorhiza bryicola

Geissorhiza bryicola, commonly known as the Cape gold or yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a short, stocky stem with few leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
