Geigeria africana subsp. otaviensis

Geigeria africana subsp.

otaviensis, commonly known as the African geigeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and Southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit.

The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
