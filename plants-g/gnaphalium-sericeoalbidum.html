Gnaphalium sericeoalbidum

Gnaphalium sericeoalbidum, commonly known as the silky white cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions, often growing in disturbed or wet soils.
