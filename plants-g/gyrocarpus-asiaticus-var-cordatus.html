Gyrocarpus asiaticus var. cordatus

Gyrocarpus asiaticus var. cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped mangrove, is a variant of the gyrocarpus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that thrives in coastal and brackish water environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces unique, woody fruits that encase its seeds. It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in mangrove ecosystems.
