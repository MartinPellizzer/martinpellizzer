Gentianella stevenii

Gentianella stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are tubular and often have a striking blue or violet hue, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
