Gentiana copelandii

Gentiana copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Gentiana copelandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
