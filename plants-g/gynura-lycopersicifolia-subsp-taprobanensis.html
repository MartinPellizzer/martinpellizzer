Gynura lycopersicifolia subsp. taprobanensis

Gynura lycopersicifolia subsp.

taprobanensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan false tomato, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
