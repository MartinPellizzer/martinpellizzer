Gentiana wollastonii

Gentiana wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, arranged in a rosette, and are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Gentiana wollastonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
