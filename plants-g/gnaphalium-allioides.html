Gnaphalium allioides

Gnaphalium allioides, commonly known as the garlic herb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, garlic-scented leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a head, resembling a dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique scent and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
