Gymnadenia × breinerorum

Gymnadenia × breinerorum, commonly known as the hybrid grass pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Gymnadenia ciliaris and Gymnadenia conopsea, characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate regions of Europe. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and linear, grass-like leaves, with flowers arranged in a loose spike.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique hybrid origin and ornamental value.
