Gnetum pyrifolium

Gnetum pyrifolium, commonly known as the flame tree or pyrifolium gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is characterized by its unique combination of traits, including a vascular system similar to that of angiosperms. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with smooth, often reddish bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually ovate or elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture.

Gnetum pyrifolium is native to tropical regions of South America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
