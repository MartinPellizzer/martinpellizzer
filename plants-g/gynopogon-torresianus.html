Gynopogon torresianus

Gynopogon torresianus, commonly known as the Torresian grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or white in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Gynopogon torresianus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
