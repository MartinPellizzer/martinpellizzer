Geum rivale var. alpinum

Geum rivale var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine avens, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy, with a height typically ranging from 5 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountain meadows and stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
