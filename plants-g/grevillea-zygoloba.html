Grevillea zygoloba

Grevillea zygoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea zygoloba is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
