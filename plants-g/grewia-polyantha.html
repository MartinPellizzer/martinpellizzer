Grewia polyantha

Grewia polyantha, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. The plant has slender, woody stems with opposite leaves that are ovate and have a serrated margin. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant red fruits and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
