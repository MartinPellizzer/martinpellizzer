Genista sessilifolia subsp. romanica

Genista sessilifolia subsp.

romanica, commonly known as the Roman thornbush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
