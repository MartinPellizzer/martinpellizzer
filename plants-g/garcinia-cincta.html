Garcinia cincta

Garcinia cincta, commonly known as the scarlet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties. Its fruits contain a high concentration of tannins and are used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia cincta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
