Gentiana verna var. brachyphylla

Gentiana verna var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed gentian, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
