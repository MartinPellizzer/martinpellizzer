Germanea rotundifolia

Germanea rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, rounded leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its adaptability and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens around the world.
