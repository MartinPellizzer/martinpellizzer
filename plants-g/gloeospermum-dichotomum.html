Gloeospermum dichotomum

Gloeospermum dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous gloeospermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
