Gamblea Malayana

Gamblea malayana is a unique flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo. It is known for its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. This species belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species worldwide. Its habitat is threatened by deforestation and land conversion, making conservation efforts crucial. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively unknown to the general public.
