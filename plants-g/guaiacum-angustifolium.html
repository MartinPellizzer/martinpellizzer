Guaiacum angustifolium

Guaiacum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved guaiacwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it important in traditional medicine and craftsmanship.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
