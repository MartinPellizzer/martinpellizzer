Galium boreale var. linearifolium

Galium boreale var. linearifolium, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in cold, nutrient-poor environments.
