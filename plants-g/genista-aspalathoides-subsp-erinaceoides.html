Genista aspalathoides subsp. erinaceoides

Genista aspalathoides subsp.

erinaceoides, commonly known as the Cape broom, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in gardens and landscaping.
