Guzmania sanguinea

Guzmania sanguinea, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the central flower spike, giving it a dramatic visual appeal. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small animals. Guzmania sanguinea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with high humidity.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
