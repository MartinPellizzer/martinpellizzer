Garcinia serpentini

Garcinia serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's vine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to bright orange or red fruits, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Garcinia serpentini is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
