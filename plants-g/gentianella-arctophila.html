Gentianella arctophila

Gentianella arctophila, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella arctophila is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
