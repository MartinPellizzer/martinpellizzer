Gerbera serrata

Gerbera serrata, commonly known as the serrated gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white, and bloom throughout the growing season.

Gerbera serrata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
