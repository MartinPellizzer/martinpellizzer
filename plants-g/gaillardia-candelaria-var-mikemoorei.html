Gaillardia candelaria var. mikemoorei

Gaillardia candelaria var. mikemoorei, commonly known as the Mikemoore's blanketflower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, with dark central cones. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a compact growth habit that adds texture to gardens.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
