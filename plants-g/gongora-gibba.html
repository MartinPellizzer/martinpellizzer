Gongora gibba

Gongora gibba, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped lip, which is a key feature in its classification within the orchid family. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, round pseudobulbs.

Gongora gibba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
