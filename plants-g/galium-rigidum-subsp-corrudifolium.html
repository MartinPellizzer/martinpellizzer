Galium rigidum subsp. corrudifolium

Galium rigidum subsp.

corrudifolium, commonly known as rigid bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The leaves are opposite, linear to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
