Grevillea dryandroides subsp. hirsuta

Grevillea dryandroides subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, leathery leaves that are densely covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
