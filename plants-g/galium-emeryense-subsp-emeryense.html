Galium emeryense subsp. emeryense

Galium emeryense subsp.

emeryense, commonly known as Emery's bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine hairs.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical to ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. Its small white or pale pink flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in early summer.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
