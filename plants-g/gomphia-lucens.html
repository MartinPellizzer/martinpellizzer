Gomphia lucens

Gomphia lucens, commonly known as the candleplant, is a unique succulent native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, candle-like stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers during the winter months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small scales, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Gomphia lucens is a member of the Aizoaceae family, known for its specialized adaptations to drought.
