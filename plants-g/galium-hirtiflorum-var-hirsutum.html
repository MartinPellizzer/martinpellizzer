Galium hirtiflorum var. hirsutum

Galium hirtiflorum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
