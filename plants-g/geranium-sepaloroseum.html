Geranium sepaloroseum

Geranium sepaloroseum, commonly known as the rose-sepal geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium sepaloroseum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
