Gentianella gentianoides

Gentianella gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
