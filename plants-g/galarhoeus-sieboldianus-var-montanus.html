Galarhoeus sieboldianus var. montanus

Galarhoeus sieboldianus var. montanus, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense clusters of blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates. It is often found in meadows and slopes above the tree line.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
