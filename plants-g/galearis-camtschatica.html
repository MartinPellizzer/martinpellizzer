Galearis camtschatica

Galearis camtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Galearis camtschatica is also notable for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
