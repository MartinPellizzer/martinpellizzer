Glycine reniformis

Glycine reniformis, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely used in cuisines around the world. Glycine reniformis is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
