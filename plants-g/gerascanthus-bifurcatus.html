Gerascanthus bifurcatus

Gerascanthus bifurcatus, commonly known as the forked geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive forked leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and vibrant blooms.

Gerascanthus bifurcatus is a member of the Geraniaceae family, which includes other genera such as Geranium and Erodium.
