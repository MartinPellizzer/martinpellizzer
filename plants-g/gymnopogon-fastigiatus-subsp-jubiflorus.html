Gymnopogon fastigiatus subsp. jubiflorus

Gymnopogon fastigiatus subsp.

jubiflorus, commonly known as the tall spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in grassland and savanna habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, pale green flowers arranged in a compact panicle.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
