Gentianella hartwegii

Gentianella hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Gentianella hartwegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
