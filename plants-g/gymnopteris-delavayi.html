Gymnopteris delavayi

Gymnopteris delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like leaves that are often divided into segments, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, a feature common among members of the Huperziaceae family.

Gymnopteris delavayi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
