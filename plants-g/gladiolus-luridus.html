Gladiolus luridus

Gladiolus luridus, commonly known as the lurid gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in a range of vivid colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals that are often brightly colored.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
