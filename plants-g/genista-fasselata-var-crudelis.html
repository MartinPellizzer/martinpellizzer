Genista fasselata var. crudelis

Genista fasselata var. crudelis, commonly known as the rough genista, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for coastal or xerophytic gardens.
