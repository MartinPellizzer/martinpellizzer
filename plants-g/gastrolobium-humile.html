Gastrolobium humile

Gastrolobium humile, commonly known as the small-flowered wattle, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange pea-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Gastrolobium humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
