Gynopachis umbellata

Gynopachis umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in an umbel, giving it its common name. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Gynopachis umbellata is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient environments.
