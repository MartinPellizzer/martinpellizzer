Gonolobus hirtus

Gonolobus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy gonolobus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by legume-like pods containing small, round seeds.

Gonolobus hirtus is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
