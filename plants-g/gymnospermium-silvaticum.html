Gymnospermium silvaticum

Gymnospermium silvaticum, commonly known as the forest gymnosperm, is a unique plant belonging to the gymnosperm group, characterized by its ability to produce naked seeds.

It typically grows in temperate forest environments, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant exhibits a conifer-like structure with needle-like leaves and a woody stem, adapted for survival in cooler climates. Its reproductive structures, known as cones, contain the seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in maintaining ecological balance within its native habitat.
