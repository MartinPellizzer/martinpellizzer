Gentiana pachyphylla

Gentiana pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves that help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
