Guajava sieberiana

Guajava sieberiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum sieberiana*, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
