Gymnopogon delicatulus

Gymnopogon delicatulus, commonly known as the slender grass veldt, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate blades and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles. Gymnopogon delicatulus is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
