Galium sylvestre var. vulgare

Galium sylvestre var. vulgare, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground, and it produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or greenish hue. The plant is characterized by its clusters of five-petaled white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium sylvestre var. vulgare is often found in temperate regions and is known for its delicate appearance and medicinal properties.
