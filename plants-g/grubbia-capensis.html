Grubbia capensis

Grubbia capensis, commonly known as the Cape grubbia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, glossy green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Grubbia capensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and resilience to coastal conditions.
