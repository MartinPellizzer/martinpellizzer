Geranium sessiliflorum var. albatum

Geranium sessiliflorum var. albatum, commonly known as the Albatross geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
