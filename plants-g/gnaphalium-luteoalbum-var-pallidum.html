Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. pallidum

Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale yarrow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is known for its aromatic, slightly resinous scent.

This variety is distinguished by its paler coloration compared to the typical form of Gnaphalium luteoalbum.
