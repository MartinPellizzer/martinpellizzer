Grindelia obovatifolia

Grindelia obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its resinous sap has been used historically by indigenous peoples for various purposes.

Grindelia obovatifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
