Geigeria aspalathoides subsp. chorleyana

Geigeria aspalathoides subsp.

chorleyana, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Geigeria genus, which are part of the Asteraceae family.
