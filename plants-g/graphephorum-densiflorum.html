Graphephorum densiflorum

Graphephorum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

While not widely cultivated, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
