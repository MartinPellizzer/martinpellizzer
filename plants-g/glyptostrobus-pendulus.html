Glyptostrobus pendulus

Glyptostrobus pendulus, commonly known as the Chinese cliff pine, is a unique coniferous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, drooping branches and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Glyptostrobus pendulus is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its slow growth and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
