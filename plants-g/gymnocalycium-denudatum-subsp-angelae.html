Gymnocalycium denudatum subsp. angelae

Gymnocalycium denudatum subsp.

angelae, commonly known as the angel's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced spines and distinctive floral morphology.
