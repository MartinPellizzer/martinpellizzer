Geniostoma haemospermum var. laevigatum

Geniostoma haemospermum var. laevigatum, commonly known as the bloodseed plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its most notable feature is the dark red or black seeds it produces, which are encased in a fleshy, persistent fruit.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
