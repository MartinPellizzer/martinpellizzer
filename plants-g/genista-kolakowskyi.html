Genista kolakowskyi

Genista kolakowskyi, commonly known as the Kolakowski's broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody, branched stem.

Genista kolakowskyi is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
