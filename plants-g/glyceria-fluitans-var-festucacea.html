Glyceria fluitans var. festucacea

Glyceria fluitans var. festucacea, commonly known as the floating grass or water reed, is a variant of the genus Glyceria, which belongs to the family Poaceae.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth and tufted, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of rivers. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variant is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
