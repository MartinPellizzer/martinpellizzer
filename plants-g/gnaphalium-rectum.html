Gnaphalium rectum

Gnaphalium rectum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellowish or pale green, with tubular florets surrounded by white or pale yellow ray florets.

Gnaphalium rectum is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
