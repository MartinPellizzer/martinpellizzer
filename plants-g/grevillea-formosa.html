Grevillea formosa

Grevillea formosa, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and clusters of brightly colored, spider-like flowers that range from pink to white. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and full sun exposure. It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Grevillea formosa is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
