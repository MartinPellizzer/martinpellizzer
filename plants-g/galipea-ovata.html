Galipea ovata

Galipea ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf galipea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Galipea ovata is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
