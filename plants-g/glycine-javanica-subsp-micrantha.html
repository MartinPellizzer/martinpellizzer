Glycine javanica subsp. micrantha

Glycine javanica subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant produces elongated pods containing several round, yellow seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses, it is cultivated in various regions for both decorative and practical purposes.
