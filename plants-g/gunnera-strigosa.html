Gunnera strigosa

Gunnera strigosa, commonly known as the giant Swiss stone pine, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in diameter. The plant is a member of the Gunneraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures, which help to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Gunnera strigosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
