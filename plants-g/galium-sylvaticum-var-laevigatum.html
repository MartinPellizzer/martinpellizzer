Galium sylvaticum var. laevigatum

Galium sylvaticum var. laevigatum, commonly known as smooth wood-avens, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features slender, ascending stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other varieties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
