Geum montanum f. typicum

Geum montanum f. typicum, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy and somewhat woody at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky slopes.

Geum montanum f. typicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
