Glycine hispida var. striata

Glycine hispida var. striata, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It features vibrant red or orange flowers and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the genus Glycine. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
