Gurania multiflora

Gurania multiflora, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular red or pink flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Gurania multiflora is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
