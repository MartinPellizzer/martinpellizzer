Garcinia scortechinii

Garcinia scortechinii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears edible, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, tangy pulp. The fruit is rich in xanthones, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
