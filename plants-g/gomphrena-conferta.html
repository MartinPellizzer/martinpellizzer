Gomphrena conferta

Gomphrena conferta, commonly known as the clustered gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Gomphrena conferta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun with minimal water.
