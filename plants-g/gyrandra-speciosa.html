Gyrandra speciosa

Gyrandra speciosa, commonly known as the showy gyra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often with a slight woolly texture.

Gyrandra speciosa is typically found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
