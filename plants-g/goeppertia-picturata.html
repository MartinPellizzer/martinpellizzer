Goeppertia picturata

Goeppertia picturata, commonly known as the Painted Calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a waxy texture that enhances its visual appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

This plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive indoors with proper care.
