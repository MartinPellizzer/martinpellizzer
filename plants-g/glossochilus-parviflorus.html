Glossochilus parviflorus

Glossochilus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically measure less than 1 centimeter in diameter. The plant produces a single, erect stem with several leaves and blooms during the late spring to early summer season. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

Glossochilus parviflorus prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests.
