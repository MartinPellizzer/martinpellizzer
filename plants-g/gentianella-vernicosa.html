Gentianella vernicosa

Gentianella vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance.

Gentianella vernicosa is often found in meadows and mountain slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
