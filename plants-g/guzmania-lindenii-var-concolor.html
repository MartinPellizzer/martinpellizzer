Guzmania lindenii var. concolor

Guzmania lindenii var. concolor, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that contrast beautifully with its green foliage. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, emerge from the center of the leaf rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments with bright, indirect light.
