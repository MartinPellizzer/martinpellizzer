Glochidion lanceolatum var. liukiuense

Glochidion lanceolatum var. liukiuense, commonly known as the Liukiu hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is notable for its adaptability to coastal and rocky habitats.
