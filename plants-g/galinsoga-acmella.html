Galinsoga acmella

Galinsoga acmella, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Galinsoga acmella is considered a weed in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
