Gentiana chimboracensis

Gentiana chimboracensis, commonly known as the Chimborazo gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the cooler months of the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Gentiana chimboracensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
