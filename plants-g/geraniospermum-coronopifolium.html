Geraniospermum coronopifolium

Geraniospermum coronopifolium, commonly known as the desert star or desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged droughts.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in dry environments.
