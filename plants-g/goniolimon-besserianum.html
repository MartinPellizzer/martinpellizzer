Goniolimon besserianum

Goniolimon besserianum, commonly known as the desert sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its succulent leaves help store moisture, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
