Gnaphalium montanum var. supinum

Gnaphalium montanum var. supinum, commonly known as the mountain cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically forms dense mats, which help stabilize soil in rocky or barren environments. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and North America.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
