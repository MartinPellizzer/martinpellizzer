Goodenia flagellifera

Goodenia flagellifera, commonly known as the whip-flowered goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
