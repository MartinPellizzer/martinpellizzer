Gentianella spruceana

Gentianella spruceana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

Gentianella spruceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
