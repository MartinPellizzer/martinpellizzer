Galtonia princeps

Galtonia princeps, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces a tall, slender stem that bears a single flower, making it a popular choice for gardens. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry conditions, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Galtonia princeps is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
