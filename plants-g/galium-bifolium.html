Galium bifolium

Galium bifolium, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces, and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
