Gentianella lingulata

Gentianella lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Gentianella lingulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
