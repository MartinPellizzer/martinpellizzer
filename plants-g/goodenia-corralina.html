Goodenia corralina

Goodenia corralina, commonly known as the Corralina goodenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to mauve. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Goodenia corralina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
