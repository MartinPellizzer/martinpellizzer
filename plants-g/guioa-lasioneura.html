Guioa lasioneura

Guioa lasioneura, commonly known as the silky guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The silky guioa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
