Garcinia oliveri

Garcinia oliveri, commonly known as Oliver's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to western Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia oliveri is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
