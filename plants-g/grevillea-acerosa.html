Grevillea acerosa

Grevillea acerosa, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, zigzagging stems and is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Grevillea acerosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
