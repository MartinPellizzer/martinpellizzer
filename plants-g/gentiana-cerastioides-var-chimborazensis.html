Gentiana cerastioides var. chimborazensis

Gentiana cerastioides var. chimborazensis, commonly known as the Chimborazo gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the high Andes of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its blue to violet, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine meadows.
