Gesneria umbellata

Gesneria umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Gesneria umbellata requires bright, indirect light and moderate watering to flourish.
