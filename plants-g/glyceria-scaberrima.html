Glyceria scaberrima

Glyceria scaberrima, commonly known as rough glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical structures.

Glyceria scaberrima is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
