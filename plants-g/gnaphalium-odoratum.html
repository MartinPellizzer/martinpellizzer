Gnaphalium odoratum

Gnaphalium odoratum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to white florets surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its fragrant, waxy leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal uses.
