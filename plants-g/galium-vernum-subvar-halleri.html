Galium vernum subvar. halleri

Galium vernum subvar. halleri, commonly known as the early wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
