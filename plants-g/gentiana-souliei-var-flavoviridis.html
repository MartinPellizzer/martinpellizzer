Gentiana souliei var. flavoviridis

Gentiana souliei var. flavoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, while its flowers have five petals with a prominent yellow center.

This variant is prized for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
