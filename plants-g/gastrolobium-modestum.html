Gastrolobium modestum

Gastrolobium modestum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

The leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
