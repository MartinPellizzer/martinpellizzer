Gomphrena ferruginea var. adjusta

Gomphrena ferruginea var. adjusta, commonly known as the rusty globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom from summer to fall, adding a striking visual contrast to the landscape.
