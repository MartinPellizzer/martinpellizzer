Grumilea aurantiaca var. polyneura

Grumilea aurantiaca var. polyneura, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often peels to reveal a reddish inner layer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal name *polyneura* refers to its many nerve-like veins in the leaves.
