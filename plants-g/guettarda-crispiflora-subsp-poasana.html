Guettarda crispiflora subsp. poasana

Guettarda crispiflora subsp.

poasana, commonly known as the poasana guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season.

The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
