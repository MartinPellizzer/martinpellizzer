Gaultheria leucocarpa var. yunnanensis

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by berries that are red when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
