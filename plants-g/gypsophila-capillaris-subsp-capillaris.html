Gypsophila capillaris subsp. capillaris

Gypsophila capillaris subsp.

capillaris, commonly known as the featherflower or baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its feathery appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favored cut flower in floral arrangements.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
