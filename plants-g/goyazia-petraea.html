Goyazia petraea

Goyazia petraea, commonly known as the Brazilian rock rose, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
