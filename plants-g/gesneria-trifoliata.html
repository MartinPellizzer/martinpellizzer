Gesneria trifoliata

Gesneria trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved gesneria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria trifoliata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
