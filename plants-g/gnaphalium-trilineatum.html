Gnaphalium trilineatum

Gnaphalium trilineatum, commonly known as the three-veined cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and grasslands.

Its name refers to the three distinct veins in its leaf structure, a characteristic feature of the species.
