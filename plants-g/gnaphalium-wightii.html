Gnaphalium wightii

Gnaphalium wightii, commonly known as the Indian everlasting or Wight's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, daisy-like flowers are often pale yellow to white, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Gnaphalium wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
