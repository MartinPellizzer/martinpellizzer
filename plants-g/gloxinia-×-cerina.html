Gloxinia × cerina

Gloxinia × cerina, commonly known as the hybrid gloxinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxinia × cerina is typically propagated through stem cuttings or seeds, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
