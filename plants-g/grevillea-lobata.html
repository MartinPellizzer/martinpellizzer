Grevillea lobata

Grevillea lobata, commonly known as the pink waratah, is a striking shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Grevillea lobata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
