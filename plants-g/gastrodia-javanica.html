Gastrodia javanica

Gastrodia javanica, commonly known as the Java gastrodia, is a rare and unique orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tuberous plant that lacks true leaves and instead relies on its underground stems for photosynthesis. The plant produces a single, fleshy, yellowish-brown flower with a distinctive, wrinkled appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in its native range.
