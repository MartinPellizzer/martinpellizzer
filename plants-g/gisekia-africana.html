Gisekia africana

Gisekia africana, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and workability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
