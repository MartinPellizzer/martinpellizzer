Gastrolobium crassifolium

Gastrolobium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical growth habit and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are thick, leathery, and oval-shaped, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
