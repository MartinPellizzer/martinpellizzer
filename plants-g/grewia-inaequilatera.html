Grewia inaequilatera

Grewia inaequilatera, commonly known as the unequal-leaved globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often asymmetrical, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
