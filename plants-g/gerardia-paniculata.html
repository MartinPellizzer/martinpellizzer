Gerardia paniculata

Gerardia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its slender, branching stems bear clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gerardia paniculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.
