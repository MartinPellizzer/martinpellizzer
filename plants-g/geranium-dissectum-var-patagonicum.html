Geranium dissectum var. patagonicum

Geranium dissectum var. patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The leaves are deeply lobed and dissected, giving the plant its species name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
