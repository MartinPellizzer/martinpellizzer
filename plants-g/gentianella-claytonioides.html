Gentianella claytonioides

Gentianella claytonioides, commonly known as the Clayton's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in areas with standing water.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
