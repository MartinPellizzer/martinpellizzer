Geranium scopulicola

Geranium scopulicola, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to conserve moisture.

Geranium scopulicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
