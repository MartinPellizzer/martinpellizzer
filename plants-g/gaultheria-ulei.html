Gaultheria ulei

Gaultheria ulei, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries are edible and have a sharp, peppery flavor.

Gaultheria ulei is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
