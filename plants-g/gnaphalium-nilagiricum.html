Gnaphalium nilagiricum

Gnaphalium nilagiricum, commonly known as the Nilagiri everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Western Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is notable for its dense, fluffy inflorescences that remain intact even after flowering, giving it the common name "everlasting.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
