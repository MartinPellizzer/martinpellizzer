Gynura labiensis

Gynura labiensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, spiky fruits. Gynura labiensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
