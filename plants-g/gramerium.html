Gramerium

Gramerium, commonly known as the spider lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of white or pale pink blossoms that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Gramerium has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates and die back in colder regions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
