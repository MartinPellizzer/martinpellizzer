Genista capitata var. ambigua

Genista capitata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous broom, is a variant of the broom plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, pointed leaflets. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
