Guilandina bonducella

Guilandina bonducella, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features a distinctive, woody, serpentine stem that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Guilandina bonducella is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
