Griffinia

Griffinia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Griffinia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
