Gastrolobium venulosum

Gastrolobium venulosum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, waxy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
