Grevillea brevifolia subsp. polychroma

Grevillea brevifolia subsp.

polychroma, commonly known as the red-stemmed waratah, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia. It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces nectar-rich flowers that attract birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and garden environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
