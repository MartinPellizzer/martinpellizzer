Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. platyphylla

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leafed sweet orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically flat and broad. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. The fruit's peel contains essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
