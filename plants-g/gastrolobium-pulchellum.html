Gastrolobium pulchellum

Gastrolobium pulchellum, commonly known as the velvet bush lucerne, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Gastrolobium pulchellum is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil rehabilitation.
