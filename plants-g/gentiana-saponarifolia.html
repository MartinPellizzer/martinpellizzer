Gentiana saponarifolia

Gentiana saponarifolia, commonly known as the soaproot gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are often waxy and have a distinctive, soapy scent, which gives it its common name.

Gentiana saponarifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
