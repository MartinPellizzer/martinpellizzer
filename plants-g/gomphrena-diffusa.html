Gomphrena diffusa

Gomphrena diffusa, commonly known as the common globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds to its ecological value.
