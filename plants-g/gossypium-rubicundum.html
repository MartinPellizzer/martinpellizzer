Gossypium rubicundum

Gossypium rubicundum, commonly known as the crimson cotton plant, is a species of shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, cotton-like bolls containing seeds. Its fibers are used in traditional textile production, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Gossypium rubicundum is relatively drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to a variety of arid environments.
