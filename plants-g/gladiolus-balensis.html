Gladiolus balensis

Gladiolus balensis, commonly known as the Balangao gladiolus, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in a variety of hues, including red, pink, and white.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
