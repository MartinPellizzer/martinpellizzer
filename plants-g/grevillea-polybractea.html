Grevillea polybractea

Grevillea polybractea, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea polybractea is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
