Gunnera densiflora

Gunnera densiflora, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking member of the Gunneraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, with a deep green color and a hairy underside. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by bright yellow bracts, which attract pollinators such as insects and birds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and wetland landscapes.
