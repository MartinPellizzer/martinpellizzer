Garnotia stricta var. marianarum

Garnotia stricta var. marianarum, commonly known as the Marianas hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Northern Mariana Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
