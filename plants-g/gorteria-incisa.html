Gorteria incisa

Gorteria incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gorteria, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
