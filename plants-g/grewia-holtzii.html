Grewia holtzii

Grewia holtzii, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia holtzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
