Gilia ramosissima

Gilia ramosissima, commonly known as desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gilia ramosissima plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
