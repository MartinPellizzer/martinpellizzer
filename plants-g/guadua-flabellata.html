Guadua flabellata

Guadua flabellata, commonly known as the fan bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach up to 10 meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slight curl at the tips, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Guadua flabellata is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it a useful material in traditional construction and crafts.
