Gleichenia nervosa

Gleichenia nervosa, commonly known as the veined maidenhair fern, is a member of the Gleicheniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with finely divided, arching leaves that have prominent veins running along their length. Its reproductive structures include sporangia, which are clustered in small, round clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia nervosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in both wild and cultivated settings.
