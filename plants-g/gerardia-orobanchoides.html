Gerardia orobanchoides

Gerardia orobanchoides, commonly known as the desert broom.

This annual herbaceous plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It has small, yellow flowers and hairy, branched stems adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant belongs to the Orobanchaceae family, which includes parasitic and hemiparasitic species.

Its morphology reflects its ecological niche, with specialized structures for survival in harsh environments.
