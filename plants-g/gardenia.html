Gardenia

Gardenia, commonly known as the African Lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to yellow. Gardenias are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and are often used in landscaping and as potted plants.

They require well-drained soil, partial shade, and consistent moisture to thrive.
