Gordonia integrifolia

Gordonia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf beech, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by clusters of red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and landscape design.
