Graptopetalum macdougallii

Graptopetalum macdougallii, commonly known as the MacDougal's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
