Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. pallidum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
