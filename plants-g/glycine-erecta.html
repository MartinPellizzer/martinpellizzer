Glycine erecta

Glycine erecta, commonly known as the upright soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Glycine erecta is cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its roots also have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
