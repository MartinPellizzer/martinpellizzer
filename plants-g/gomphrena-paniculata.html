Gomphrena paniculata

Gomphrena paniculata, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Gomphrena paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
