Grewia calvata

Grewia calvata, commonly known as the smooth grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth surface, and its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
