Gentianella dianthoides

Gentianella dianthoides, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and is often found in moist meadows and grassy areas. Gentianella dianthoides has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
