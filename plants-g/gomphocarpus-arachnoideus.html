Gomphocarpus arachnoideus

Gomphocarpus arachnoideus, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, round, spiky fruits that contain seeds covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native environment.
