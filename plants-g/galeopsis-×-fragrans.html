Galeopsis × fragrans

Galeopsis × fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant horseweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Galeopsis × fragrans is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.
