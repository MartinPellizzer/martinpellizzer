Geranium cinereum subsp. cazorlense

Geranium cinereum subsp.

cazorlense, commonly known as the Cazorla geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base of each petal.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
