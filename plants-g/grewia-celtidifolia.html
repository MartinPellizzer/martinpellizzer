Grewia celtidifolia

Grewia celtidifolia, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of food.
