Gentiana bromifolia

Gentiana bromifolia, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana bromifolia is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
