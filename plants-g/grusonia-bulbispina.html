Grusonia bulbispina

Grusonia bulbispina, commonly known as the spiny bulbous orchid, is a unique plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bulb-like underground storage structures, which help it survive in dry periods. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns, typically in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing close to the ground.

Grusonia bulbispina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
