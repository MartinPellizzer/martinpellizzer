Grossheimia longifimbriata

Grossheimia longifimbriata, commonly known as the long-fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers have long, fringed petals that give it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Grossheimia longifimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
