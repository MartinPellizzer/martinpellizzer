Glutago spicata

Glutago spicata, commonly known as the spikeflower vervain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and spikes of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
