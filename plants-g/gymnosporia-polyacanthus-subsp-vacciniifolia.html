Gymnosporia polyacanthus subsp. vacciniifolia

Gymnosporia polyacanthus subsp.

vacciniifolia, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more leathery leaves compared to other members of the species.
