Gomphrena splendida

Gomphrena splendida, commonly known as the splendour bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, thread-like bracts that give it a feathery appearance, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gomphrena splendida is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
