Genista bivonae

Genista bivonae, commonly known as the Canary Islands shrub or Bivona's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, often spiny stems and small, compound leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Genista bivonae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
