Glycine abyssinica

Glycine abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Glycine abyssinica is also valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines.
