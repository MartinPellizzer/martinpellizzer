Garcinia transvaalensis

Garcinia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange-red in color when mature. Garcinia transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
