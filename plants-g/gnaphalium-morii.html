Gnaphalium morii

Gnaphalium morii, commonly known as the Tibetan everlasting or mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh alpine conditions.

Its foliage is typically silver-gray, with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its native habitat.
