Glycosmis sapindoides var. australiensis

Glycosmis sapindoides var. australiensis, commonly known as the Australian bitter apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are typically bitter and contain a single seed. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
