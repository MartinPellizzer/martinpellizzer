Glycine holophylla

Glycine holophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a silvery underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Glycine holophylla is often grown for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

It also has potential agricultural value due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
