Galium latifolium

Galium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small white or pinkish flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galium latifolium is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
