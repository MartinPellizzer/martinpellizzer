Gnaphalium stachidifolium

Gnaphalium stachidifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant is known for its longevity and ability to retain its color and form when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements.
