Geraniospermum radula

Geraniospermum radula, commonly known as the radula geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Geraniospermum radula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
