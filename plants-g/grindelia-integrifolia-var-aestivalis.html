Grindelia integrifolia var. aestivalis

Grindelia integrifolia var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer brittleblow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that produce a resinous substance, giving the plant a sticky texture. The species thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas and inland deserts. Its yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
