Grisia campanulata

Grisia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Grisia campanulata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
