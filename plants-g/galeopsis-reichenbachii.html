Galeopsis reichenbachii

Galeopsis reichenbachii, commonly known as narrow-leaved hemp-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis reichenbachii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
