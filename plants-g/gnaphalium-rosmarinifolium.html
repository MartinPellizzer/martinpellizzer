Gnaphalium rosmarinifolium

Gnaphalium rosmarinifolium, commonly known as silverleaf toothache plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves that emit a mint-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat various ailments.
