Gynostemma wightianum

Gynostemma wightianum, commonly known as the Indian ginseng, is a climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
