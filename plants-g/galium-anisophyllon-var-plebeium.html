Galium anisophyllon var. plebeium

Galium anisophyllon var. plebeium, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, with a distinctive oval shape and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
