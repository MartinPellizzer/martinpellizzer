Glyceria obtusa

Glyceria obtusa, commonly known as broadleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically flat and arranged in a fan shape at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Glyceria obtusa thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
