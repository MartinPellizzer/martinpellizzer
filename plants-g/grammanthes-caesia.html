Grammanthes caesia

Grammanthes caesia, commonly known as the blue-eyed cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing seeds. Grammanthes caesia is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.

Due to its slow growth and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
