Geranium pratense var. vineale

Geranium pratense var. vineale, commonly known as the meadow geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
