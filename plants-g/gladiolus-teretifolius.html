Gladiolus teretifolius

Gladiolus teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are long and narrow, giving the plant its common name, and the flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in temperate climates.
