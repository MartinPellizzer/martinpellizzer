Gypsophila nanella

Gypsophila nanella, commonly known as the baby's breath or fairy duster, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often resembling clouds or stars. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and produces numerous tiny, fluffy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a charming, low-maintenance ornamental plant.

Gypsophila nanella is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its ethereal appearance.
