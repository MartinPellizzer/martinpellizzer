Gomphrena cylindrica

Gomphrena cylindrica, commonly known as the cylinder bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical inflorescences that range in color from pink to violet. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Gomphrena cylindrica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
