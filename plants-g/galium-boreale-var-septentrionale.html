Galium boreale var. septentrionale

Galium boreale var. septentrionale, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped form.

Galium boreale var. septentrionale is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.
