Gladiolus tristis var. violaceus

Gladiolus tristis var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple-flowered gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, violet-hued leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its botanical classification places it within the Iridaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like irises and crocuses.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
