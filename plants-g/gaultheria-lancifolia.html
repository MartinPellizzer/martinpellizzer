Gaultheria lancifolia

Gaultheria lancifolia, commonly known as the trailing arbutus, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a serrated edge. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall.

Gaultheria lancifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties, as it contains compounds used in pain relief and topical treatments.
