Gladiolus inarimensis

Gladiolus inarimensis, commonly known as the Inarimense gladiolus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, arranged in dense spike-like inflorescences. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
