Gertrudia amplifolia

Gertrudia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gertrudia, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Gertrudia amplifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
