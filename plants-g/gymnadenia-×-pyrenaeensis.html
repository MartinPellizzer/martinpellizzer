Gymnadenia × pyrenaeensis

Gymnadenia × pyrenaeensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean grass-of-Parnassus, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale yellow to white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Gymnadenia × pyrenaeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
