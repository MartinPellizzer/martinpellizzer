Ghinia amara

Ghinia amara, commonly known as the bitter melon, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruit that turns from green to orange as it matures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is widely used in Asian cuisines for its tart and bitter flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and pumpkins.
