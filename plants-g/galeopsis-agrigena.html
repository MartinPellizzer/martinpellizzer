Galeopsis agrigena

Galeopsis agrigena, commonly known as agrigena betony, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, sunny environments.

Galeopsis agrigena is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils.
