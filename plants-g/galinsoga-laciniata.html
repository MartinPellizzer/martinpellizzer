Galinsoga laciniata

Galinsoga laciniata, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens. It has a rapid growth rate and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive foliage and reproductive structures.
