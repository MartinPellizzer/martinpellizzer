Guapira asperula

Guapira asperula, commonly known as the rough guapira, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Guapira asperula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
