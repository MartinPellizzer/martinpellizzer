Gleditsia officinalis

Gleditsia officinalis, commonly known as honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, green pods that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
