Gentianopsis procera

Gentianopsis procera, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gentianopsis procera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
