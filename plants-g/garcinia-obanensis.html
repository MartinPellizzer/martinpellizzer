Garcinia obanensis

Garcinia obanensis, commonly known as the Obanese garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. It is often found in moist, shaded environments and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has attracted interest for potential medicinal and nutritional applications.
