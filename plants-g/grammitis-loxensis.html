Grammitis loxensis

Grammitis loxensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually glossy and have a distinct, creeping growth habit.

Grammitis loxensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
