Gomphocarpus dependens

Gomphocarpus dependens, commonly known as the hanging globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and produces capsule-like fruits that hang from the plant. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

Gomphocarpus dependens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
