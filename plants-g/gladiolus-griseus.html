Gladiolus griseus

Gladiolus griseus, commonly known as the gray gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with striking, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with large, colorful flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive grayish-green foliage, which sets it apart from other gladiolus species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
