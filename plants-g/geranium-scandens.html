Geranium scandens

Geranium scandens, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium scandens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
