Gentiana saxosa var. recurvata

Gentiana saxosa var. recurvata, commonly known as the bent gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous habitats.
