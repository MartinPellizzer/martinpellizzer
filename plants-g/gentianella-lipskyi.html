Gentianella lipskyi

Gentianella lipskyi, commonly known as the Armenian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gentianella lipskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and resilience.
