Gentiana quinquefolia var. amarelloides

Gentiana quinquefolia var. amarelloides, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a variant of the blue gentian native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast sharply with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces showy, five-petaled blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
