Gerascanthus velutinus

Gerascanthus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gerascanthus velutinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
