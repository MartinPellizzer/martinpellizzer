Garrya oblonga

Garrya oblonga, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Its growth habit is typically upright and compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Garrya oblonga is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
