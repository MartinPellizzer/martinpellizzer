Genista pilosa var. jordanii

Genista pilosa var. jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's brackenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its wiry, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The leaves are typically small, oval, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
