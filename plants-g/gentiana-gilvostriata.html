Gentiana gilvostriata

Gentiana gilvostriata, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana gilvostriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
