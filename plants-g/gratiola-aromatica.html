Gratiola aromatica

Gratiola aromatica, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, upright stems and hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gratiola aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
