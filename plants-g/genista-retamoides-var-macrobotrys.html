Genista retamoides var. macrobotrys

Genista retamoides var. macrobotrys, commonly known as the large-flowered broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
