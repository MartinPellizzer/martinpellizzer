Galenia halimifolia

Galenia halimifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf galenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Galenia halimifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
