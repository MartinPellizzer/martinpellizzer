Genista racemosa var. elegans

Genista racemosa var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in racemes during the spring, which are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are evergreen, and the plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal or rocky environments.
