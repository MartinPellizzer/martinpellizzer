Galactia diversifolia

Galactia diversifolia, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Galactia diversifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
