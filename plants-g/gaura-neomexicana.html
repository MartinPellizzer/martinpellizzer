Gaura neomexicana

Gaura neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico gaura, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It produces showy, pink to red, cone-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Gaura neomexicana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
