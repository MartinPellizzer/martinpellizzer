Gladiolus nemorosus

Gladiolus nemorosus, commonly known as the woodland gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and features tall, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive purple or red base. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its corms are used for propagation.

Gladiolus nemorosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
