Grammitis personata

Grammitis personata, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or creeping in growth habit. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
