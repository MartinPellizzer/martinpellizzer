Gynerium wolfii

Gynerium wolfii, commonly known as the tall goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gynerium wolfii plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during its flowering season.
