Gongora tridentata

Gongora tridentata, commonly known as the Trident Lip, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, three-lobed lip that resembles a trident, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a fragrant scent. Gongora tridentata thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
