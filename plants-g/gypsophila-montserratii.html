Gypsophila montserratii

Gypsophila montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered blooms that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to mountainous regions. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance and long-lasting nature.

Gypsophila montserratii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
