Gompholobium drummondii

Gompholobium drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's glory, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring slender, often branched stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and slightly hairy.
