Gyrostemon tepperi

Gyrostemon tepperi, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal dunes.

Gyrostemon tepperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water environments.
