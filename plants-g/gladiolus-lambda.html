Gladiolus lambda

Gladiolus lambda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers. The leaves are long and narrow, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure to thrive.
