Guioa leptoneura

Guioa leptoneura, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
