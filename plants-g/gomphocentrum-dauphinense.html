Gomphocentrum dauphinense

Gomphocentrum dauphinense, commonly known as the Dauphine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are dispersed by water.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
