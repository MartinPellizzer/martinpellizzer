Gypsophila alvandica

Gypsophila alvandica, commonly known as the Alvand's laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila alvandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant, lace-like appearance.
