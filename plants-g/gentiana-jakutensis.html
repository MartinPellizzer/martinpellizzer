Gentiana jakutensis

Gentiana jakutensis, commonly known as the Yakut gentian, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes, thriving in cool and temperate conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana jakutensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
