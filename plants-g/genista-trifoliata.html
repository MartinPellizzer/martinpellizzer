Genista trifoliata

Genista trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in racemes.

Its small, trifoliate leaves are a distinguishing feature, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
