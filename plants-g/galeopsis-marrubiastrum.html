Galeopsis marrubiastrum

Galeopsis marrubiastrum, commonly known as greater hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis marrubiastrum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antispasmodic and astringent effects.
