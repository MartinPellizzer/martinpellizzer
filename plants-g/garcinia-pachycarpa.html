Garcinia pachycarpa

Garcinia pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into large, fleshy, orange-red berries. These berries are encased in a thick, aromatic rind and contain a single seed.

Garcinia pachycarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
