Gaya gaudichaudiana

Gaya gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, creating a striking visual display. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Gaya gaudichaudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and along roadsides.
