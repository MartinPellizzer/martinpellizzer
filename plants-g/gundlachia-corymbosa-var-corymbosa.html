Gundlachia corymbosa var. corymbosa

Gundlachia corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
