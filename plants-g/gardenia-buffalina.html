Gardenia buffalina

Gardenia buffalina, commonly known as the Buffalo gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, waxy white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is evergreen and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and aromatic blooms.

Gardenia buffalina is also valued for its ability to produce fragrant essential oils.
