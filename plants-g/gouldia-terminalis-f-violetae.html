Gouldia terminalis f. violetae

Gouldia terminalis f. violetae, commonly known as the violet-flowered gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant violet-colored inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique coloration and compact form make it a popular choice for adding visual interest to garden landscapes.
