Grevillea amplexans

Grevillea amplexans, commonly known as the emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that are often arranged in a spiral, giving it a striking appearance. Grevillea amplexans is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a hardy shrub that is well-suited for coastal and dryland environments.
