Gomphrena iresinoides var. martiana

Gomphrena iresinoides var. martiana, commonly known as the red feathery gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery plumes of pink to red bracts that resemble small fireworks. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
