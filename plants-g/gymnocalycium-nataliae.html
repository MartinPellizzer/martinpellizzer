Gymnocalycium nataliae

Gymnocalycium nataliae, commonly known as Natal's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing plant with a short, thick stem and produces vibrant, showy flowers during the warmer months. The plant is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and is well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gymnocalycium nataliae requires minimal water and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
