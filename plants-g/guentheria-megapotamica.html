Guentheria megapotamica

Guentheria megapotamica, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots help stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in shallow water environments.

Guentheria megapotamica plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetlands.
