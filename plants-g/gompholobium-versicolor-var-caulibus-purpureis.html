Gompholobium versicolor var. caulibus-purpureis

Gompholobium versicolor var. caulibus-purpureis, commonly known as the purple-stemmed golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. Its distinctive purple-tinged stems and showy blooms make it a notable ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
