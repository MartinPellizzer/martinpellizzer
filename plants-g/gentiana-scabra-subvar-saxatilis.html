Gentiana scabra subvar. saxatilis

Gentiana scabra subvar. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained and sunny habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
