Globularia cordifolia subsp. bellidifolia

Globularia cordifolia subsp.

bellidifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved globularia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
