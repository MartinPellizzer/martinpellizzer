Gnemon griffithii

Gnemon griffithii, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
