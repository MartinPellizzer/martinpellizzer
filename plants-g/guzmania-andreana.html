Guzmania andreana

Guzmania andreana, commonly known as the pink-flowered bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects water. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with pink bracts that resemble flowers. Its foliage is often green with a metallic sheen, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Guzmania andreana is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
