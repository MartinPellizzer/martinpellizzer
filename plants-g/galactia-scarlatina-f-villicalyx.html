Galactia scarlatina f. villicalyx

Galactia scarlatina f. villicalyx, commonly known as the silky scarlet galactia, is a variant of the scarlet galactia, characterized by its distinctive silky, hairy foliage.

It is a leguminous plant native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compound leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet having a soft, pubescent texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
