Gyrenia biflora

Gyrenia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered gyrenia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces small, spiny fruits.

Gyrenia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
