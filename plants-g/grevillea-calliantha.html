Grevillea calliantha

Grevillea calliantha, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Grevillea calliantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
