Gerbera lasiopus

Gerbera lasiopus, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and features hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last several months during the growing season.
