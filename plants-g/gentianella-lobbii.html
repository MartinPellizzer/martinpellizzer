Gentianella lobbii

Gentianella lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its strikingly blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its flowers are short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by late afternoon.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
