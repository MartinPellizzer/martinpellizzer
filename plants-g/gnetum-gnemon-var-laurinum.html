Gnetum gnemon var. laurinum

Gnetum gnemon var. laurinum, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and woody, capsule-like fruits that contain winged seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical wetland flora.
