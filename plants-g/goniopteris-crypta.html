Goniopteris crypta, commonly known as the hidden shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky habitats. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Goniopteris crypta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems where it contributes to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
