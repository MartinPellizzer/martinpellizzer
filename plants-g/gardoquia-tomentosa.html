Gardoquia tomentosa

Gardoquia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly gardoquia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
