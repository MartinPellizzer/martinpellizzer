Genista amarella

Genista amarella, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky and coastal areas. The plant produces bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista amarella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
