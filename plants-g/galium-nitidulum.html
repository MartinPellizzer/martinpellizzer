Galium nitidulum

Galium nitidulum, commonly known as glossy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, whitish hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, oval to lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Galium nitidulum is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
