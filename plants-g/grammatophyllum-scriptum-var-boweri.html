Grammatophyllum scriptum var. boweri

Grammatophyllum scriptum var. boweri, commonly known as the Bower's traveler's tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
