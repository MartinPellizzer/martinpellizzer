Gastrolobium spathulatum var. latifolium

Gastrolobium spathulatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
