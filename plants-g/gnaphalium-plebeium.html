Gnaphalium plebeium

Gnaphalium plebeium, commonly known as common cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, silver-tomentose leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Gnaphalium plebeium is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
