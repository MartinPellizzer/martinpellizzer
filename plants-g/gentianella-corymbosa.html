Gentianella corymbosa

Gentianella corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
