Galeandra pubicentrum

Galeandra pubicentrum, commonly known as the hairy galeandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that gives it its common name.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.
