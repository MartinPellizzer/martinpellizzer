Goodyera similis var. albonervosa

Goodyera similis var. albonervosa, commonly known as the white-veined lady's slipper, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers with white veins contrasting against a deep purple or violet background. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the base.

This orchid is known for its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its growth and survival.
