Gypsophila ferganica

Gypsophila ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana carnation, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems are often slender and branched, supporting the small, showy blooms.

Gypsophila ferganica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
