Gentiana acaulis subsp. alpina

Gentiana acaulis subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its stem is short and leafless, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
