Gerbera incana f. aurantiaca

Gerbera incana f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange-tinged Gerbera, is a fascinating plant with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the Gerbera genus. Its flowers display a vibrant mix of yellow and orange hues, making it a striking ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
