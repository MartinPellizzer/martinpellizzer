Geranium baicalense

Geranium baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal geranium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
