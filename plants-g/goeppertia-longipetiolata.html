Goeppertia longipetiolata

Goeppertia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled arrowroot, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its lush, dark green, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
