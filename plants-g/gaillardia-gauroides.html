Gaillardia gauroides

Gaillardia gauroides, commonly known as the Florida blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. The plant produces bright orange and red composite flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Its foliage is gray-green and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Gaillardia gauroides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
