Graderia iringensis

Graderia iringensis, commonly known as the Iringa orchid, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink with intricate patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
