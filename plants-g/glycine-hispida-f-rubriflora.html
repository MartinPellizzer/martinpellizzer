Glycine hispida f. rubriflora

Glycine hispida f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered burr legume, is a variant of the common burr legume, characterized by its distinctive red flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.

The red-flowered variant is particularly noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
