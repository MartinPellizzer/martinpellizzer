Gladiolus communis var. tenuis

Gladiolus communis var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous flowers. The flowers typically come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for cut flowers and garden displays.
