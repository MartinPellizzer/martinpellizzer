Gentiana altorum subsp. purpurea

Gentiana altorum subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers.
