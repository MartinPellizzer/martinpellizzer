Grastidium pensile

Grastidium pensile, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a unique plant species characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features delicate, elongated stems that trail downward, often forming a cascading effect. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Grastidium pensile thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
