It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon, where it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its bulbs are fleshy and serve as storage organs, enabling the plant to survive periods of dormancy.

Gagea transversalis is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various insects and small mammals.
