Gomphrena pumila

Gomphrena pumila, commonly known as the dwarf globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spherical flower heads. The plant produces small, pink to red bracts that resemble thistle-like structures, which are actually modified leaves. Gomphrena pumila thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
