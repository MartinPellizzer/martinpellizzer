Galium aureum var. floribundum

Galium aureum var. floribundum, commonly known as golden wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue when young.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
