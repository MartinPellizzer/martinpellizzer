Gasteranthus herbaceus

Gasteranthus herbaceus, commonly known as the "garden beauty," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, showy flowers that resemble small, colorful fruits. The plant produces vividly colored blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gasteranthus herbaceus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
