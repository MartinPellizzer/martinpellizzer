Gomidesia gaudichaudiana var. acutata

Gomidesia gaudichaudiana var. acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved gomidesia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
