Genista acanthoclada

Genista acanthoclada, commonly known as the thorny broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal habitats.

Genista acanthoclada is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
