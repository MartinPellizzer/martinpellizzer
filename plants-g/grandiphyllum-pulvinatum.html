Grandiphyllum pulvinatum

Grandiphyllum pulvinatum, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a pod-like structure that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal applications in local communities.
