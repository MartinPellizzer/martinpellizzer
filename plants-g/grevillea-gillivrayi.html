Grevillea gillivrayi

Grevillea gillivrayi, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and is renowned for its long, vibrant red or orange flower spikes that bloom throughout spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and coastal conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry gardens. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for native landscaping.

Grevillea gillivrayi plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators such as honeyeaters.
