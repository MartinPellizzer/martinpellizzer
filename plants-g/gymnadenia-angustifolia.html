Gymnadenia angustifolia

Gymnadenia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Gymnadenia angustifolia is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, making it a notable component of temperate flora.
