Geum strictum subsp. intermedium

Geum strictum subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate barrenwillow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, showy inflorescences, usually in shades of red or yellow.

The leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.
