Gentiana giulianetti

Gentiana giulianetti, commonly known as the Julianetti gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
