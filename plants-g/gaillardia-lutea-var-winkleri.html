Gaillardia lutea var. winkleri

Gaillardia lutea var. winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow ray flowers and dark red to brown central disk, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
