Gagea fistulosa var. spathacea

Gagea fistulosa var. spathacea, commonly known as the pink gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are relatively small and often used in rock gardens or as ornamental plants.

It is noted for its early blooming habit and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
