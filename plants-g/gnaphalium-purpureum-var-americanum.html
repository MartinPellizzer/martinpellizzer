Gnaphalium purpureum var. americanum

Gnaphalium purpureum var. americanum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it features slender, hairy stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
