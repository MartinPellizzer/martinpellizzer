Gaertnera calycina

Gaertnera calycina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gaertnera calycina is also valued for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardening.
