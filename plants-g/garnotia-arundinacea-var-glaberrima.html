Garnotia arundinacea var. glaberrima

Garnotia arundinacea var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to moist conditions.
