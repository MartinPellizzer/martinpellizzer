Geranium neohispidum

Geranium neohispidum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy pink or red flowers during the summer months. Its foliage is typically lanceolate to ovate in shape, and the leaves may have a slightly serrated edge.

Geranium neohispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
