Gnaphalium collinum var. billardierei

Gnaphalium collinum var. billardierei, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
