Gentiana × laengstii nothosubsp.

laengstii, commonly known as the long-stemmed gentian, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with two lips, a feature common in many gentian species.

The plant is native to the Alps and grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and ecological value in supporting pollinators.
