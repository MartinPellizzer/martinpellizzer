Gutierrezia paniculata var. patagonica

Gutierrezia paniculata var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It features linear, hairy leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
