Glochidion distichum

Glochidion distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
