Galium cilicicum var. procerior

Galium cilicicum var. procerior, commonly known as the tall bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
