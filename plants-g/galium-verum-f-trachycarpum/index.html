Galium Verum F. Trachycarpum

Galium verum f. trachycarpum is a rare and distinctive variant of the common bedstraw plant. This fern-like shrub is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its unique fruit structure sets it apart from other members of the genus. Despite its beauty, it remains a lesser-known species in the plant world.
