Gladiolus oppositiflorus

Gladiolus oppositiflorus, commonly known as the opposite-flower gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful inflorescences. The plant produces opposite or whorled arrangements of leaves, which is a distinguishing feature. Its flowers are typically tubular with three petals and three sepals, often displaying vibrant hues of red, yellow, or orange.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
