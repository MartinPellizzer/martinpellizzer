Galium fruticosum subsp. ephedroides

Galium fruticosum subsp.

ephedroides, commonly known as creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
