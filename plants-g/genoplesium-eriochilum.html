Genoplesium eriochilum

Genoplesium eriochilum, commonly known as the woolly greenhood, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded labellum that is covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Genoplesium eriochilum is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological complexity.
