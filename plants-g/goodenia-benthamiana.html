Goodenia benthamiana

Goodenia benthamiana, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces flowers in shades of pink or purple.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
