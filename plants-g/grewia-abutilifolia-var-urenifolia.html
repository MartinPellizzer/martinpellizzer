Grewia abutilifolia var. urenifolia

Grewia abutilifolia var. urenifolia, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique texture and adaptability.
