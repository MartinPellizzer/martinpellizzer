Gentiana amplicrater

Gentiana amplicrater, commonly known as the broad-cup gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
