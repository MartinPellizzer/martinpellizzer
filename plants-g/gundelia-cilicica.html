Gundelia cilicica

Gundelia cilicica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Gundelia cilicica is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
