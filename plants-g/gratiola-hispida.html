Gratiola hispida

Gratiola hispida, commonly known as the hispid yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its common name.

The roots are yellow and have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
