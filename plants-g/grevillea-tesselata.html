Grevillea tesselata

Grevillea tesselata, commonly known as the tessellated grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tessellated leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Grevillea tesselata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
