Genista pilosa var. erecta

Genista pilosa var. erecta, commonly known as the upright broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.
