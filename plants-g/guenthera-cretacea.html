Guenthera cretacea

Guenthera cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous fern, is a fossil plant that thrived during the Cretaceous period.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that suggest a close evolutionary relationship to modern ferns. The plant's structure indicates it was a vascular plant, capable of efficient water transport and photosynthesis. Fossilized remains of Guenthera cretacea provide valuable insights into the flora of ancient ecosystems.

Its presence in sedimentary rock layers helps scientists understand climate and environmental conditions of the Mesozoic era.
