Galium trifidum var. latifolium

Galium trifidum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf wood-rose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
