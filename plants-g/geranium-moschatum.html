Geranium moschatum

Geranium moschatum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. Geranium moschatum is known for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet scent.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and easy care requirements.
