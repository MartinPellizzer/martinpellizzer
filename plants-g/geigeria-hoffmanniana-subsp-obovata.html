Geigeria hoffmanniana subsp. obovata

Geigeria hoffmanniana subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geigeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, obovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
