Gesneria guianensis

Gesneria guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan gesneria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring a mix of green, white, and yellow tones, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The flowers, which bloom in spring or early summer, come in shades of pink, red, and white, adding ornamental value to the plant.
