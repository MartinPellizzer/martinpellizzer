Genista borysthenica var. tenuis

Genista borysthenica var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the steppes of southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
