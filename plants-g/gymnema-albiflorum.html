Gymnema albiflorum

Gymnema albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower gymnema, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white flowers and milky sap. The plant is known for its climbing habit and can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are opposite and often have a hairy texture.

Gymnema albiflorum is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
