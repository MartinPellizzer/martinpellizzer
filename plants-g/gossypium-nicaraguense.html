Gossypium nicaraguense

Gossypium nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan cotton plant, is a species of cotton native to Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, fluffy fibers that are used for textile production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gossypium nicaraguense is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its genetic diversity and potential for sustainable fiber production.
