Gymnocalycium chrysacanthion

Gymnocalycium chrysacanthion, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Gymnocalycium chrysacanthion is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
