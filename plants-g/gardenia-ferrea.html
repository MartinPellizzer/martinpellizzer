Gardenia ferrea

Gardenia ferrea, commonly known as the ironwood gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for carving and woodworking. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Gardenia ferrea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
