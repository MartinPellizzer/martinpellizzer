Gayophytum eriospermum

Gayophytum eriospermum, commonly known as the velvetleaf bat flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble bat faces, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bats, making it an important species in bat-pollinated ecosystems.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
