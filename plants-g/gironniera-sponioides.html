Gironniera sponioides

Gironniera sponioides, commonly known as the spiny sunchokes, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of tubers, which are similar to those of the Jerusalem artichoke.

Gironniera sponioides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.
