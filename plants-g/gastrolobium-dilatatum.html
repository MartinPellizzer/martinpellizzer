Gastrolobium dilatatum

Gastrolobium dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Gastrolobium dilatatum is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
