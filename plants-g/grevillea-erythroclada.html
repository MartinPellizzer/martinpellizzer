Grevillea erythroclada

Grevillea erythroclada, commonly known as the red-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts vividly with its green stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea erythroclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays due to its unique coloration.
