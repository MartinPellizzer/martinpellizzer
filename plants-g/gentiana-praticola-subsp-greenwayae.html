Gentiana praticola subsp. greenwayae

Gentiana praticola subsp.

greenwayae, commonly known as the Greenway's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while its stems are often branched and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
