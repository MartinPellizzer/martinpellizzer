Gaultheria mutisiana

Gaultheria mutisiana, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are typically red or black and contain aromatic seeds.

Gaultheria mutisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
