Grumilea glabrifolia

Grumilea glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved grumia, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
