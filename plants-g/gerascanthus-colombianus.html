Gerascanthus colombianus

Gerascanthus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and streamside habitats. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gerascanthus colombianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
