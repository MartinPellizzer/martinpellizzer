Gerascanthus pubescens

Gerascanthus pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs during the spring and summer months.
