Gagea staintonii

Gagea staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has a short stem and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
