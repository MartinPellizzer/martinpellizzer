Galium mollugo var. pallidum

Galium mollugo var. pallidum, commonly known as pale bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pale bedstraw is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
