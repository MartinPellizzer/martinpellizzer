Geum urbanum subvar. grandifolium

Geum urbanum subvar. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, with stems that are hairy and can grow up to 60 cm tall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers in early summer, which are arranged in cup-like inflorescences. It prefers moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
