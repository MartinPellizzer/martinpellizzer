Glycine longicauda

Glycine longicauda, commonly known as the long-haired cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. Glycine longicauda is adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
