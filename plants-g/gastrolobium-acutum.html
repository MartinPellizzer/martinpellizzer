Gastrolobium acutum

Gastrolobium acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, pointed leaflets. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Gastrolobium acutum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
