Geranium pringlei

Geranium pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive, slightly hairy surface.

Geranium pringlei is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
