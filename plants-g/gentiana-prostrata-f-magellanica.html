Gentiana prostrata f. magellanica

Gentiana prostrata f. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan gentian, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a mat-forming habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the harsh coastal environments where it thrives.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
