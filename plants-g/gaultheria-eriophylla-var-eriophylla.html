Gaultheria eriophylla var. eriophylla

Gaultheria eriophylla var. eriophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are leathery, dark green on top, and silvery white underneath, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

The plant is also known for its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a source of wintergreen oil.
