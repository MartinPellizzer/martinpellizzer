Galeopsis speciosa var. boraei

Galeopsis speciosa var. boraei, commonly known as the alpine hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features small, delicate blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude habitats and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
