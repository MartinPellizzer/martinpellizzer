Gypsophila illyrica

Gypsophila illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gypsophila illyrica is also valued in floral arrangements for its ability to add a soft, ethereal touch.
