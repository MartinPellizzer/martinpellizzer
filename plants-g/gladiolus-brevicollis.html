Gladiolus brevicollis

Gladiolus brevicollis, commonly known as the short-necked gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals.

Gladiolus brevicollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
