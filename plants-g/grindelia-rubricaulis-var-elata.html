Grindelia rubricaulis var. elata

Grindelia rubricaulis var. elata, commonly known as the tall yellow globemallow, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
