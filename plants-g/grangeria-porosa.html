Grangeria porosa

Grangeria porosa, commonly known as the porous grass, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to white flowers. Grangeria porosa is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
