Grenvillea conspicua

Grenvillea conspicua, commonly known as the showy grenvillea, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family, characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a woody stem with dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
