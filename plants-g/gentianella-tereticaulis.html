Gentianella tereticaulis

Gentianella tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Gentianella tereticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
