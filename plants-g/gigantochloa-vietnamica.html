Gigantochloa vietnamica

Gigantochloa vietnamica, commonly known as Vietnamese giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and strong structural properties, it is valued for both ecological and commercial purposes.
