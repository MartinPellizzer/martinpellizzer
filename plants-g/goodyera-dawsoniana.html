Goodyera dawsoniana

Goodyera dawsoniana, commonly known as Dawson's potterscale, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, rhizomatous stem and is characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and forest understories.

Goodyera dawsoniana is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
