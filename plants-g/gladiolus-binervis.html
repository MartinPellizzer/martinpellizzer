Gladiolus binervis

Gladiolus binervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers typically come in shades of pink, red, and white, with intricate petal patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves with distinct parallel veins, contributing to its botanical uniqueness.
