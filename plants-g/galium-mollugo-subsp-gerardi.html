Galium mollugo subsp. gerardi

Galium mollugo subsp.

gerardi, commonly known as Gerard's bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gerard's bedstraw is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland edges and stream banks.
