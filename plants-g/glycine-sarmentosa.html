Glycine sarmentosa

Glycine sarmentosa, commonly known as the climbing peanut or wild peanut, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds similar to peanuts. Glycine sarmentosa is often found in moist, shaded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and agricultural research.
