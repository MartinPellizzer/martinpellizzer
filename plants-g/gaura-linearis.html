Gaura linearis

Gaura linearis, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Gaura linearis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
