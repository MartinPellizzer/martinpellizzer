Graffenrieda moritziana

Graffenrieda moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's spider flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that resemble spider-like structures. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Graffenrieda moritziana is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens due to its sensitivity to direct sunlight.
