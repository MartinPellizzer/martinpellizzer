Gamolepis annua

Gamolepis annua, commonly known as the annual gamolepis, is an herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Gamolepis annua is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
