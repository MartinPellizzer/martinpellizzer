Galaxia mucronularis

Galaxia mucronularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf galaxia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often swollen nodes. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Galaxia mucronularis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
