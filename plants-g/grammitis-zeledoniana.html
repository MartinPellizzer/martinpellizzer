Grammitis zeledoniana

Grammitis zeledoniana, commonly known as the Zeledonia fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal in its natural habitat.

Grammitis zeledoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native ecosystems.
