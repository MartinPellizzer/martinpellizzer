Gynostemma pentaphyllum var. dasycarpum

Gynostemma pentaphyllum var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or Korean gynostemma, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that mature to a dark purple color, containing multiple seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its antioxidant and adaptogenic effects.

This variant is often cultivated for use in traditional herbal medicine and as a health supplement.
