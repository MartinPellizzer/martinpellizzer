Genista tricuspidata

Genista tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, rocky slopes, and coastal regions.

Genista tricuspidata is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
