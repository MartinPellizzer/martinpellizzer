Galactia scarlatina

Galactia scarlatina, commonly known as scarlet galactia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has a hairy stem that helps reduce water loss.

Galactia scarlatina is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
