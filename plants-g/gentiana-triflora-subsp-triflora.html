Gentiana triflora subsp. triflora

Gentiana triflora subsp.

triflora, commonly known as the Three-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
