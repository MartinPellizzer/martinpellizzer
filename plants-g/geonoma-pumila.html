Geonoma pumila

Geonoma pumila, commonly known as the dwarf fern or small-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it a common understory plant in rainforests. Its spores are dispersed through wind, contributing to its reproductive strategy in dense forest canopies.

Geonoma pumila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
