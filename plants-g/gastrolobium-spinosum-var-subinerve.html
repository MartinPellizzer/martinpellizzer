Gastrolobium spinosum var. subinerve

Gastrolobium spinosum var. subinerve, commonly known as the spinifex acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that appear in late winter to spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The variety subinerve is characterized by its closely spaced nerves on the leaf blades.
