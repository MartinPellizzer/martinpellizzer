Garcinia hainanensis

Garcinia hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed, surrounded by a thick, bitter pulp. Garcinia hainanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its growth thrives in humid, shaded environments, making it a common species in tropical forests.
