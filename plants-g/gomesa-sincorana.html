Gomesa sincorana

Gomesa sincorana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Gomesa sincorana is also known for its fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color.
