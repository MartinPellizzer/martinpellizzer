Genista pilocarpa

Genista pilocarpa, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are small, needle-like, and arranged in pairs along the stems.

Genista pilocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
