Glandularia wrightii

Glandularia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's glandularia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Glandularia wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
