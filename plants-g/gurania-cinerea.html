Gurania cinerea

Gurania cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf gurania, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gurania cinerea is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
