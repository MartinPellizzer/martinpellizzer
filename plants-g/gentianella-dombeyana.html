Gentianella dombeyana

Gentianella dombeyana, commonly known as the Dombey's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
