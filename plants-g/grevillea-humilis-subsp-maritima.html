Grevillea humilis subsp. maritima

Grevillea humilis subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It blooms during spring and summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
