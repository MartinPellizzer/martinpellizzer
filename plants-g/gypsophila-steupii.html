Gypsophila steupii

Gypsophila steupii, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Gypsophila steupii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
