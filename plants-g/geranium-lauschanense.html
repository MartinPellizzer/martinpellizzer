Geranium lauschanense

Geranium lauschanense, commonly known as the Alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with serrated, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Geranium lauschanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
