Geissois magnifica

Geissois magnifica, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a striking climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along its vines. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its rapid growth, it requires regular pruning to prevent it from becoming invasive.
