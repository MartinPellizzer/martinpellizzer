Gentiana sulphurea

Gentiana sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast sharply with its deep green leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
