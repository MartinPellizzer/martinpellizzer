Gnaphalium gaudichaudianum var. subrufescens

Gnaphalium gaudichaudianum var. subrufescens, commonly known as the red-brown everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by pale green to reddish-brown bracts. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in dry, sunny conditions.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
