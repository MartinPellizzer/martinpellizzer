Gentiana obliqua

Gentiana obliqua, commonly known as the oblique gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
