Geum rivale var. subalpinum

Geum rivale var. subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and stems that bear small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
