Galium mollugo var. flaviflorum

Galium mollugo var. flaviflorum, commonly known as yellow woodsorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in temperate regions with suitable conditions.
