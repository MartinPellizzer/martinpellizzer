Guajava humilis

Guajava humilis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, making it a valued food source in local cuisines.

Guajava humilis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
