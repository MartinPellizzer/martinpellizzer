Gonioscypha muricata

Gonioscypha muricata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed liverwort, is a species of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its leafy, thalloid structure with a distinctive, rough, and serrated surface. This liverwort typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including female gametophytes, are embedded within the thallus.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
