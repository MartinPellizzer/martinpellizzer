Gnidia imbricata var. incana

Gnidia imbricata var. incana, commonly known as the roughleaf gnidia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
