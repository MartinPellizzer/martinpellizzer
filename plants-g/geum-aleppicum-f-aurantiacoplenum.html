Geum aleppicum f. aurantiacoplenum

Geum aleppicum f. aurantiacoplenum, commonly known as the golden-sepaled globemallow, is a variant of the globemallow species native to the southwestern United States.

This plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange, with deeply lobed petals. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The foliage consists of palmate leaves with a hairy texture, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
