Gongora javieri

Gongora javieri, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically feature a unique combination of red, yellow, and purple hues. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are borne on short, erect spikes and are highly prized in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Gongora javieri prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and greenhouse settings.
