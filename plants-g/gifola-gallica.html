Gifola gallica

Gifola gallica, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant grows up to 1 meter tall and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Gifola gallica is known for its strong aromatic scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
