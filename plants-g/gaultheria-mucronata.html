Gaultheria mucronata

Gaultheria mucronata, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by bright red, edible berries resembling strawberries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Gaultheria mucronata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.
