Garcinia purpurea

Garcinia purpurea, commonly known as purple garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including high levels of hydroxycitric acid.

Garcinia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
