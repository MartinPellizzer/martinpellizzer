Gentiana choanantha subsp. curvianthera

Gentiana choanantha subsp.

curvianthera, commonly known as the curved-throat gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure, particularly the curved throat of the corolla. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
