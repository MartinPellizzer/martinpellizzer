Gastrolobium pycnostachyum

Gastrolobium pycnostachyum, commonly known as the dense-stalked wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves with numerous narrow, linear leaflets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants.
