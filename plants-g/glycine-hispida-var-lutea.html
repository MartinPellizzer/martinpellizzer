Glycine hispida var. lutea

Glycine hispida var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is an annual legume characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Glycine hispida var. lutea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
