Gerardia bangii

Gerardia bangii, commonly known as the Java geranium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
