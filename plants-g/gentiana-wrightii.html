Gentiana wrightii

Gentiana wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana wrightii is often found in regions with high elevations and cool climates.
