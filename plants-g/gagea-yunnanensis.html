Gagea yunnanensis

Gagea yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in the challenging alpine climate.

Gagea yunnanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural conditions.
