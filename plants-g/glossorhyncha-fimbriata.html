Glossorhyncha fimbriata

Glossorhyncha fimbriata, commonly known as the lace-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, finely divided leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
