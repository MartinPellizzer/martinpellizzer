Garcinia paniculata

Garcinia paniculata, commonly known as the Java yellowwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while its branches are often thorned.

Garcinia paniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has applications in both horticulture and pharmacology.
