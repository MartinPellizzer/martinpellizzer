Gentiana borneensis subsp. apoensis

Gentiana borneensis subsp.

apoensis, commonly known as the Apo gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Philippines. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
