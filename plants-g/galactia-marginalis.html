Galactia marginalis

Galactia marginalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional diets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Galactia marginalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
