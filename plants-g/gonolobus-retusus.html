Gonolobus retusus

Gonolobus retusus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored capsule that splits open when mature.

Gonolobus retusus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
