Globba variabilis subsp. pusilla

Globba variabilis subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf globba, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features rhizomatous growth and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers.

The foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in tropical gardens.
