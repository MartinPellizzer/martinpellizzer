Gentiana propinqua subsp. arctophila

Gentiana propinqua subsp.

arctophila, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
