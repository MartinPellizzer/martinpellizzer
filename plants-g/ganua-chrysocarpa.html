Ganua chrysocarpa

Ganua chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and light gray in color.

Ganua chrysocarpa is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruits.
