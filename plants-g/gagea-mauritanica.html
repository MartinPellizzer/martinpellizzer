Gagea mauritanica

Gagea mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian scorpion's tail, is a bulbous perennial plant native to North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Gagea mauritanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
