Glycosmis rupestris var. tomentosa

Glycosmis rupestris var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
