Geum reptans subsp. angustatum

Geum reptans subsp.

angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America. It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks.
