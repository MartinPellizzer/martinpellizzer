Glechoma apuleji

Glechoma apuleji, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with scalloped edges and produces small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, spreading rapidly through stolons to cover the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover.
