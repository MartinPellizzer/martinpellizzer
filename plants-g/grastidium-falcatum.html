Grastidium falcatum

Grastidium falcatum, commonly known as the slender arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically arrow-shaped, which help it float and capture sunlight efficiently. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its roots are often submerged, anchoring it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Grastidium falcatum is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in artificial ponds.
