Glycine javanica subsp. pseudojavanica

Glycine javanica subsp.

pseudojavanica, commonly known as the Java bean or wild soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia. It features pinnately compound leaves with trifoliate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, bean-like pods.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

This subspecies is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
