Grevillea biformis

Grevillea biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed grevillea, is a species of flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves and bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, thriving in full sun.

Grevillea biformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
