Gentiana pilgeriana

Gentiana pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana pilgeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
