Glomera viridis

Glomera viridis, commonly known as the green glomerate, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
