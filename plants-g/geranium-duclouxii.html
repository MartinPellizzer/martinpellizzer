Geranium duclouxii

Geranium duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium duclouxii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
