Galium mollugo var. viridulum

Galium mollugo var. viridulum, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and rapid growth make it a common weed in disturbed soils.
