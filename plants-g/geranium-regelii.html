Geranium regelii

Geranium regelii, commonly known as the Regel's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or pink blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Geranium regelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
