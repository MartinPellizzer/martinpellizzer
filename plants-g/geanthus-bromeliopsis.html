Geanthus bromeliopsis

Geanthus bromeliopsis, commonly known as the bromelioid geanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species. The plant typically features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
