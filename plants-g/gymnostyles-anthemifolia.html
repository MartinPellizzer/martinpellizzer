Gymnostyles anthemifolia

Gymnostyles anthemifolia, commonly known as the fragrant daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.
