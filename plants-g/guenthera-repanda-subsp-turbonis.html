Guenthera repanda subsp. turbonis

Guenthera repanda subsp.

turbonis, commonly known as the Turbonis guenthera, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its robust, trunk-like stem and clustered leaves. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter, contributing to its striking appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often emerging from the base of the leaves, and it is primarily propagated through seeds or vegetative methods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
