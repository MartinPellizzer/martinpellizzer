Geranium peloponesiacum

Geranium peloponesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Geranium peloponesiacum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
