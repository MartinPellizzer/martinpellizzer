Gymnocalycium striglianum subsp. aeneum

Gymnocalycium striglianum subsp.

aeneum, commonly known as the silver moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white to silver-gray spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
