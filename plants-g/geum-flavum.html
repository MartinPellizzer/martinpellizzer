Geum flavum

Geum flavum, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Geum flavum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
