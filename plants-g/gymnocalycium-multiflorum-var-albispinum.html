Gymnocalycium multiflorum var. albispinum

Gymnocalycium multiflorum var. albispinum, commonly known as the white-spined moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem covered with white spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its leaves are typically small and are shed as the plant matures, allowing the stem to become more prominent.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
