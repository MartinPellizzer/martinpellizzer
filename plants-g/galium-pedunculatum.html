Galium pedunculatum

Galium pedunculatum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies, contributing to its ecological importance.
