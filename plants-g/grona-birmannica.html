Grona birmannica

Grona birmannica, commonly known as the Birmann's grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth surface and a slightly pointed tip.

Grona birmannica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
