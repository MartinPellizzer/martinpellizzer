Gentianella amarella subsp. hartwegii

Gentianella amarella subsp.

hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high altitude conditions.
