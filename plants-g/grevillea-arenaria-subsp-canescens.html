Grevillea arenaria subsp. canescens

Grevillea arenaria subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silver grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-blue leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
