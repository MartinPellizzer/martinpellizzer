Gustavia parviflora

Gustavia parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gustavia parviflora is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
