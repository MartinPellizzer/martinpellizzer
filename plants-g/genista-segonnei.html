Genista segonnei

Genista segonnei, commonly known as the Segone's broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Genista segonnei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
