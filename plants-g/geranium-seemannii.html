Geranium seemannii

Geranium seemannii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces clusters of bright red or pink blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium seemannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
