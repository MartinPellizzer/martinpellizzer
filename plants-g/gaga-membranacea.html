Gaga membranacea

Gaga membranacea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance, and its small, pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a rare species with specific ecological requirements.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this enigmatic botanical wonder from habitat loss and climate change.
