Gymnadenia × pseudoconopsea

Gymnadenia × pseudoconopsea, commonly known as the hybrid grass pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is a natural hybrid between Gymnadenia conopsea and Gymnadenia odoratissima.

Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a flowering period that occurs in late summer.
