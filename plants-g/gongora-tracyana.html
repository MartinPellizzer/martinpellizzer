Gongora tracyana

Gongora tracyana, commonly known as Tracy's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Gongora tracyana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
