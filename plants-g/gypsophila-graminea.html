Gypsophila graminea

Gypsophila graminea, commonly known as the grass gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its delicate appearance and unique floral structure, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
