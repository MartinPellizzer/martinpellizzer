Geranium tuberosum subsp. malviflorum

Geranium tuberosum subsp.

malviflorum, commonly known as the Malviflorum geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family. It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
