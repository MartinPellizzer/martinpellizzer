Gmelina asiatica var. villosa

Gmelina asiatica var. villosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its fast growth and is used in reforestation and as a source of timber and medicinal compounds.
