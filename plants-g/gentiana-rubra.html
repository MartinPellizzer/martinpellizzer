Gentiana rubra

Gentiana rubra, commonly known as the red gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and hairy.

Gentiana rubra is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
