Gymnoschoenus anceps

Gymnoschoenus anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leafed reed, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland and marsh environments, often forming dense stands that contribute to soil stabilization.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and habitat restoration.
