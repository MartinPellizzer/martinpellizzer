Gardenia mamillata

Gardenia mamillata, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The fruit produced is a bright orange capsule that splits open to release seeds.
