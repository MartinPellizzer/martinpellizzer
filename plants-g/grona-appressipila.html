Grona appressipila

Grona appressipila, commonly known as the creeping groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a mat-forming habit with numerous small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots that access underground water sources.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control in arid environments.
