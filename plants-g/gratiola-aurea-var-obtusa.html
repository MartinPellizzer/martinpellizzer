Gratiola aurea var. obtusa

Gratiola aurea var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, blue to violet, and have five petals that are often reflexed at the tips.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
