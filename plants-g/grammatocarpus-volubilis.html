Grammatocarpus volubilis

Grammatocarpus volubilis, commonly known as the climbing palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. The plant features palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and basket weaving. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals.

Grammatocarpus volubilis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
