Gonolobus macrophyllus

Gonolobus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf goonloob, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are large, often measuring up to 30 centimeters in length, and are palmately divided into several lobes.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and are followed by elongated, fibrous pods that contain several seeds.
