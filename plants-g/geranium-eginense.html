Geranium eginense

Geranium eginense, commonly known as the Echinatum geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have a coarse texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
