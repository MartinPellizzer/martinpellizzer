Geranium dentatum

Geranium dentatum, commonly known as the toothed geranium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically serrated, giving the plant its common name, and they are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often found in prairies, meadows, and woodland edges, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
