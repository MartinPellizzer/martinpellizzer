Gladiolus rigidifolius

Gladiolus rigidifolius, commonly known as the rigid-leaf gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and rigid, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
