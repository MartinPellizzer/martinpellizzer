Glycine vanderystii

Glycine vanderystii, commonly known as Vanderyst's soybean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers, and its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Glycine vanderystii is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
