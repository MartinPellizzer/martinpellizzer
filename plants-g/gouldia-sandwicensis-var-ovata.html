Gouldia sandwicensis var. ovata

Gouldia sandwicensis var. ovata, commonly known as the Hawaiian flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tint.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and landscapes.
