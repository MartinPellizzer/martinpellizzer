Glandulifolia

Glandulifolia, commonly known as the glandular-leaved plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its foliage.

These glands often secrete oils or resins, which can serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores and pathogens. The plant typically features broad, serrated leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It is often found in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and ecological role in its native habitat.
