Gentianella dichotoma

Gentianella dichotoma, commonly known as the forked gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, and the stems are often branched, giving it its common name.

Gentianella dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
