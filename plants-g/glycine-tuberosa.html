Glycine tuberosa

Glycine tuberosa, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and is characterized by its long, twisting stems. Its tuberous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Glycine tuberosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
