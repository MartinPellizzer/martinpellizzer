Gypsophila haynaldiana

Gypsophila haynaldiana, commonly known as the pink gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Gypsophila haynaldiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
