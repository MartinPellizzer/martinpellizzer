Genista erinacea

Genista erinacea, commonly known as the hairy bursch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive, spiny pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that give it its common name.

Genista erinacea is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
