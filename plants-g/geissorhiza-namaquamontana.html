Geissorhiza namaquamontana

Geissorhiza namaquamontana, commonly known as the Namibian rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically during the winter months. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained substrates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
