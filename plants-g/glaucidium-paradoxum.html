Glaucidium paradoxum

Glaucidium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical glaucidium, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which is covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique adaptations make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant resilience in extreme environments.

Due to habitat destruction, it is classified as critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
