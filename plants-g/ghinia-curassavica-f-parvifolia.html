Ghinia curassavica f. parvifolia

Ghinia curassavica f. parvifolia, commonly known as the miniature poinsettia, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Mexico.

It features vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its color and shape.

This cultivar is often used in holiday decorations due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooming pattern.
