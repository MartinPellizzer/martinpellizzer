Geranium tiguense

Geranium tiguense, commonly known as the Tiguense geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
