Gossypium kirkii

Gossypium kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers that are harvested for their textile potential. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
