Genista hassertiana var. glabrata

Genista hassertiana var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
