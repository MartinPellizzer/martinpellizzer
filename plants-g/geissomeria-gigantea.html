Geissomeria gigantea

Geissomeria gigantea, commonly known as the giant gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
