Gentianella bulgarica subsp. jundolensis

Gentianella bulgarica subsp.

jundolensis, commonly known as the Jundolensian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
