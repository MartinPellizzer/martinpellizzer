Gentiana intricata var. intricata

Gentiana intricata var. intricata, commonly known as the intricate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
