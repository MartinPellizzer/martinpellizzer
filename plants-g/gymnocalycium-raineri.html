Gymnocalycium raineri

Gymnocalycium raineri, commonly known as the Rainer's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems and produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Gymnocalycium raineri is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
