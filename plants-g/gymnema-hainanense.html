Gymnema hainanense

Gymnema hainanense, commonly known as Hainan gymnema, is a flowering plant native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and distinctive leaf structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Gymnema hainanense is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
