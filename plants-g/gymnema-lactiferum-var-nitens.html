Gymnema lactiferum var. nitens

Gymnema lactiferum var. nitens, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for producing a milky sap that contains compounds with potential medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported ability to lower blood sugar levels.

The species is also valued for its role in herbal remedies and as a source of bioactive compounds.
