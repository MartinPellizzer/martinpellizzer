Guettarda mathiola

Guettarda mathiola, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, often resembling a cascade of small bells. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy appearance.

Guettarda mathiola is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its resilience to coastal conditions.
