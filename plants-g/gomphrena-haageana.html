Gomphrena haageana

Gomphrena haageana, commonly known as the everlasting flower or gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, spiky, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small, rounded plumes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and ability to thrive in full sun.

Gomphrena haageana is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and garden borders.
