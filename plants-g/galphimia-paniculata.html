Galphimia paniculata

Galphimia paniculata, commonly known as the red hot poker or firecracker plant, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red tubular flowers that bloom in dense, paniculate inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Galphimia paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
