Glechoma septemcrenata

Glechoma septemcrenata, commonly known as the seven-crenated ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with scalloped edges, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma septemcrenata is known for its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats.
