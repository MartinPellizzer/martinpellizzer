Gladiolus scullyi

Gladiolus scullyi, commonly known as the Scully's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers typically come in a range of vibrant colors, including red, orange, and yellow, with narrow, pointed petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
