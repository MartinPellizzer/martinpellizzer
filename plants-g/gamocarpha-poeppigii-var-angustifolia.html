Gamocarpha poeppigii var. angustifolia

Gamocarpha poeppigii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf gamocarpha, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.
