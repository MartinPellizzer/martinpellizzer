Galium × depauperatum

Galium × depauperatum, commonly known as the poor gall.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubiaceae family, characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Galium × depauperatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
