Guettardella multiflora

Guettardella multiflora, commonly known as the multibranched broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Guettardella multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in mild climates.
