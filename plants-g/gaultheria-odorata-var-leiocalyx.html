Gaultheria odorata var. leiocalyx

Gaultheria odorata var. leiocalyx, commonly known as the snowberry or white-flowered wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, followed by bright red berries that ripen in the summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The berries are edible and have a sharp, acidic flavor, often used in culinary applications.
