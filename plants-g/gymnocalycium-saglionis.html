Gymnocalycium saglionis

Gymnocalycium saglionis, commonly known as the Saglion's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, globular stems that are typically covered in a layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Gymnocalycium saglionis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
