Gentiana pneumonanthoides

Gentiana pneumonanthoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
