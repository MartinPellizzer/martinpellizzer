Glycyrrhiza macedonica

Glycyrrhiza macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its sweet taste and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
