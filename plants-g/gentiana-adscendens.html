Gentiana adscendens

Gentiana adscendens, commonly known as the ascending gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Gentiana adscendens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
