Gladiolus × hybridus

Gladiolus × hybridus, commonly known as the garden gladiolus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, brightly colored flowers that come in a wide range of hues. The flowers are typically arranged in spikes and consist of three petals and three sepals, which are often similar in appearance. The plant is native to South Africa but has been widely cultivated around the world for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
