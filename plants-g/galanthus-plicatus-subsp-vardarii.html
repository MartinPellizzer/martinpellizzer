Galanthus plicatus subsp. vardarii

Galanthus plicatus subsp.

vardarii, commonly known as the Albanian snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans. It features delicate, white, nodding flowers with a distinctive greenish-brown spathe.

The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
