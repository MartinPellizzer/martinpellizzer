Gentianella wrightii

Gentianella wrightii, commonly known as Wright's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has basal leaves that are opposite or whorled, and its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

Gentianella wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
