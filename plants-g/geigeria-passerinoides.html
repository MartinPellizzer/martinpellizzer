Geigeria passerinoides

Geigeria passerinoides, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
