Gomphocarpus fallax

Gomphocarpus fallax, commonly known as the "bottlebrush plant," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, tubular flowers that resemble a bottlebrush, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces a unique, inflated fruit that resembles a small bottle, which is why it is also called the "bottle plant.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

The plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
