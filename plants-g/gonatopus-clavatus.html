Gonatopus clavatus

Gonatopus clavatus, commonly known as the clubbed snail, is a species of air-breathing land snail.

It belongs to the family Helicidae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated shell that is typically yellowish or brown in color. The shell is spiral-shaped with a conical aperture and has a prominent, club-like swelling near the base. This snail is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and gardens.

Its diet consists mainly of decaying plant matter and leaf litter, contributing to nutrient cycling in its habitat.
