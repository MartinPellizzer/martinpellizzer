Garcinia sangudsangud

Garcinia sangudsangud, commonly known as the red mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red, segmented fruits. The fruit's pericarp contains a thick, aromatic rind that is rich in bioactive compounds. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
