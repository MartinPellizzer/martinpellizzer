Gentiana alsinoides

Gentiana alsinoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana alsinoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
