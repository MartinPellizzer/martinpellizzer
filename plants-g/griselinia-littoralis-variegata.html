Griselinia littoralis variegata

Griselinia littoralis variegata, commonly known as the variegated coastal griselinia, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features striking foliage with white and yellow variegation on its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and salty air. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
