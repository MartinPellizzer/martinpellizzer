Geranium kotschyi subsp. charlesii

Geranium kotschyi subsp.

charlesii, commonly known as the Charles's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
