Gymnopetalum quinquelobum

Gymnopetalum quinquelobum, commonly known as the five-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant produces five distinct lobes on its petals, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Gymnopetalum quinquelobum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
