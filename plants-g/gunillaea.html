Gunillaea

Gunillaea, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Xanthorrhoeaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and consists of several species, most notably Gunillaea sericea. These plants are characterized by their tall, grass-like stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Gunillaea species are often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in moist, temperate climates.
