Gentianella dolichopoda

Gentianella dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stalked gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
