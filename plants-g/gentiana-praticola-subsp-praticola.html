Gentiana praticola subsp. praticola

Gentiana praticola subsp.

praticola, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in ecological studies due to its specialized habitat requirements.
