Gonzalagunia hirsuta var. petesia

Gonzalagunia hirsuta var. petesia, commonly known as the hairy gonzalagunia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes. Its botanical structure includes opposite leaf arrangement and woody stems that can become branched over time.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and limited geographic distribution.
