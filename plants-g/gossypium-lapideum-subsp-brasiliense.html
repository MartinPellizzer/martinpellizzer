Gossypium lapideum subsp. brasiliense

Gossypium lapideum subsp.

brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian cotton plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish flowers that give way to fluffy, white cotton-like bolls. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
