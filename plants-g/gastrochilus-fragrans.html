Gastrochilus fragrans

Gastrochilus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant stomach orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, and the roots are fibrous, typical of many orchid species.

The flowers bloom during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate aroma to its natural habitat.
