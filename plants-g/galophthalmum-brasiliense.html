Galophthalmum brasiliense

Galophthalmum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Galophthalmum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
