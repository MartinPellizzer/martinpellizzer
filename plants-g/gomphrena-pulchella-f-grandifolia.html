Gomphrena pulchella f. grandifolia

Gomphrena pulchella f. grandifolia, commonly known as the showy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, spiky inflorescences in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include showy bracts that surround the small, clustered flowers, enhancing its visual appeal.
