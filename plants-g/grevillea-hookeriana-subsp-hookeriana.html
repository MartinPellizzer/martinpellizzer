Grevillea hookeriana subsp. hookeriana

Grevillea hookeriana subsp.

hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's Grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid environments.
