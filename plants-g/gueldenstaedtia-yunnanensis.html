Gueldenstaedtia yunnanensis

Gueldenstaedtia yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a common feature among legumes, and it plays a role in soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.

Despite its ecological importance, it remains a relatively understudied species in botanical research.
