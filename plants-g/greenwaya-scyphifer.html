Greenwaya scyphifer

Greenwaya scyphifer, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, golden hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a soft, leathery texture.

Greenwaya scyphifer is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
