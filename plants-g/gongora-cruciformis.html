Gongora cruciformis

Gongora cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform gongora, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a cross, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Gongora cruciformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
