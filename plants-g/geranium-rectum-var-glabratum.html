Geranium rectum var. glabratum

Geranium rectum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade.

Geranium rectum var. glabratum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
