Gentiana fimbriiplica

Gentiana fimbriiplica, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

The fringed gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
