Guaiacum dubium

Guaiacum dubium, commonly known as the yellow guaiacwood or Caribbean cedar, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a leathery texture and a dark green color. Its small, fragrant flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, followed by woody, oval fruits containing one to three seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
