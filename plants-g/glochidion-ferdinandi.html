Glochidion ferdinandi

Glochidion ferdinandi, commonly known as the Bornean glabrous acanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually leathery and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Glochidion ferdinandi is often found in shaded, moist forest understories and is valued for its ornamental leaves.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
