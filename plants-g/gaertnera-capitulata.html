Gaertnera capitulata

Gaertnera capitulata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Gaertnera capitulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
