Guilandina culebrae

Guilandina culebrae, commonly known as the snake vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, serpentine stems that can grow up to 30 meters in length. The plant produces pendant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged oppositely.

Guilandina culebrae is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
