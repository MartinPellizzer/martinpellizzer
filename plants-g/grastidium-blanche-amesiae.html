Grastidium blanche-amesiae

Grastidium blanche-amesiae, commonly known as the silverleaf mistplant, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features striking silver-green foliage with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces delicate, pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky soils, while its stems are succulent to store water.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
