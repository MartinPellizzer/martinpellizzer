Gordonia portoricensis

Gordonia portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican gordonia, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by insects and birds.

Gordonia portoricensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
