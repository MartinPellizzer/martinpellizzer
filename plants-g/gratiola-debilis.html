Gratiola debilis

Gratiola debilis, commonly known as the slender yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Gratiola debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
