Geum rivalemontanum

Geum rivalemontanum, commonly known as the alpine avens, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
