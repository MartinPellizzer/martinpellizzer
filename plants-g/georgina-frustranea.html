Georgina frustranea

Georgina frustranea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed georgina, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
