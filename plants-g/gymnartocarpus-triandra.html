Gymnartocarpus triandra

Gymnartocarpus triandra, commonly known as the African fan-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
