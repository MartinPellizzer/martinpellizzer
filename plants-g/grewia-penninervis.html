Grewia penninervis

Grewia penninervis, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in summer. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
