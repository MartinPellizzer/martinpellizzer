Geum × intermedium willdenowii

Geum × intermedium willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenowii garden cinquefoil, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It features showy, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are trifoliate or pinnate, with lobed or toothed margins, and upright, branched stems that support the flower heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
