Gentiana bokorensis

Gentiana bokorensis, commonly known as the Bokore gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and butterflies.
