Guibourtia carrissoana var. gossweileri

Guibourtia carrissoana var. gossweileri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich, aromatic scent. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and moist forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also subject to overharvesting and habitat loss.
