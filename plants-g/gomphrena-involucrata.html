Gomphrena involucrata

Gomphrena involucrata, commonly known as the purple top or redroot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to purple. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Gomphrena involucrata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
