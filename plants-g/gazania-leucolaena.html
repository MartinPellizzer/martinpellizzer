Gazania leucolaena

Gazania leucolaena, commonly known as the white gazania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers with white centers, which bloom in summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Gazania leucolaena is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray, which contributes to its adaptability in various environments.
