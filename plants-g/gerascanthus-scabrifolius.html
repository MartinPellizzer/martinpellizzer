Gerascanthus scabrifolius

Gerascanthus scabrifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf gerascanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
