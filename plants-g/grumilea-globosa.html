Grumilea globosa

Grumilea globosa, commonly known as the globe hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, globe-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
