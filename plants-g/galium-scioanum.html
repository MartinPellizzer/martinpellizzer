Galium scioanum

Galium scioanum, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Galium scioanum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
