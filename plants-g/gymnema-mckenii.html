Gymnema mckenii

Gymnema mckenii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which contains various alkaloids and saponins. Gymnema mckenii is often found in moist, shaded environments, growing along forest floors or on tree trunks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
