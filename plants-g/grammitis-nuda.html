Grammitis nuda

Grammitis nuda, commonly known as the naked grammitis, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually thin and have a smooth, often glabrous surface.

Grammitis nuda is known for its ability to thrive in high humidity and is often found epiphytically on trees or stones.
