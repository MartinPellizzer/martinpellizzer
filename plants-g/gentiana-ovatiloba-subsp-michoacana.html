Gentiana ovatiloba subsp. michoacana

Gentiana ovatiloba subsp.

michoacana, commonly known as the Michoacan gentian, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are ovate and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
