Gaillardia gracilis

Gaillardia gracilis, commonly known as the slender blazing star, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky soils, and it thrives in well-drained conditions. Its vibrant orange and red flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gaillardia gracilis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
