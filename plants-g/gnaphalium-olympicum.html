Gnaphalium olympicum

Gnaphalium olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its common name reflects its historical use in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers.
