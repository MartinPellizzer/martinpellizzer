Geranium bohemicum subsp. lanuginosum

Geranium bohemicum subsp.

lanuginosum, commonly known as the downy geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, which give it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
