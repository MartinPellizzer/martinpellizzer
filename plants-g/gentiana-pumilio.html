Gentiana pumilio

Gentiana pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, often blue flowers.

Gentiana pumilio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
