Gypsophila cretica

Gypsophila cretica, commonly known as the Crimean carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are slender and often branching, supporting the small, showy blooms.

Gypsophila cretica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
