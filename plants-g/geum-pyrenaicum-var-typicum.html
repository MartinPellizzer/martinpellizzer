Geum pyrenaicum var. typicum

Geum pyrenaicum var. typicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and small, showy flowers with five pink to reddish petals. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
