Grossularia nigra

Grossularia nigra, commonly known as black mountain ash, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that mature in the autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
