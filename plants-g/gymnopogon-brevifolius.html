Gymnopogon brevifolius

Gymnopogon brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, short blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
