Galium mollugo var. nevadense

Galium mollugo var. nevadense, commonly known as mountain bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing resilience to harsh alpine conditions.
