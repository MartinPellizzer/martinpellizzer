Gynura microcephala

Gynura microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed gynura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Gynura microcephala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
