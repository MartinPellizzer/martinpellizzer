Garcinia verrucosa

Garcinia verrucosa, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-brown rind and segmented, white or pinkish flesh. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its distinctive, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is often cultivated for its edible, sweet-tart pulp and has been used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Garcinia verrucosa make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
