Galax rotundifolia

Galax rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf everlet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, toothed leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Galax rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
