Geum heldreichii var. splendens

Geum heldreichii var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendid Fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
