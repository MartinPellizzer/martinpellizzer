Genista triacanthos

Genista triacanthos, commonly known as the thorned broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky environments.

Genista triacanthos is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
