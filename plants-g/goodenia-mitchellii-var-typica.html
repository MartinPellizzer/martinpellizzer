Goodenia mitchellii var. typica

Goodenia mitchellii var. typica, commonly known as the Mitchell's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It is adapted to withstand drought and is often seen in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and conservation efforts.
