Gaertnera longifolia var. pubescens

Gaertnera longifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy longleaf gaertnera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized plant collections.
