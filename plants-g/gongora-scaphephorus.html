Gongora scaphephorus

Gongora scaphephorus, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white petals with a prominent green lip, which sets it apart from other Gongora species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its flowers are usually small but highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators.

Botanically, it is noted for its intricate floral structure and is a subject of interest in orchid taxonomy and conservation efforts.
