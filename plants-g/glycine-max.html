Glycine max

Glycine max, commonly known as soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Soybeans are cultivated worldwide for their high protein content and are used in various food products.

The plant's botanical features make it an important crop in agriculture and nutrition.
