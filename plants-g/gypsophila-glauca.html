Gypsophila glauca

Gypsophila glauca, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Gypsophila glauca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden designs.
