Garrya flavescens subsp. pallida

Garrya flavescens subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the whiteleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically pale green to white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by orange to red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
