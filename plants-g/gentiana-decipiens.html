Gentiana decipiens

Gentiana decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the plant. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
