Gnidia danguyana

Gnidia danguyana, commonly known as the Danguy's ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Gnidia danguyana is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
