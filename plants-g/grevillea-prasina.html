Grevillea prasina

Grevillea prasina, commonly known as the emerald grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, bottlebrush-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is well adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, edible fruits that are consumed by various bird species.

Grevillea prasina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal gardens.
