Gladiolus usambarensis

Gladiolus usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis gladiolus, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers with a contrasting black or purple throat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
