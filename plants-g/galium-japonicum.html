Galium japonicum

Galium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium japonicum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
