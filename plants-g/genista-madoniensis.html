Genista madoniensis

Genista madoniensis, commonly known as the Madonnescent broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
