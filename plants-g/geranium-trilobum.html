Geranium trilobum

Geranium trilobum, commonly known as woodland cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three lobes and produces clusters of pink or red flowers. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
