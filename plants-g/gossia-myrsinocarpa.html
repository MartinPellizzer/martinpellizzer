Gossia myrsinocarpa

Gossia myrsinocarpa, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in rainforests and moist woodland areas. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Gossia myrsinocarpa is often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
