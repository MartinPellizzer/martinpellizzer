Gregoria lutea

Gregoria lutea, commonly known as the golden gregoria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and thrives in temperate climates. Gregoria lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
