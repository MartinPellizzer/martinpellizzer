Gamochaeta pensylvanica

Gamochaeta pensylvanica, commonly known as Pennsylvania ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces showy, purple to pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
