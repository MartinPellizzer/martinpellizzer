Galega heynii

Galega heynii, commonly known as the Heyn's goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. Galega heynii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
