Gastrolychnis uralensis

Gastrolychnis uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Gastrolychnis uralensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
