Gentiana macroclada

Gentiana macroclada, commonly known as the large-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Gentiana macroclada is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
