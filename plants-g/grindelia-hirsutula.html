Grindelia hirsutula

Grindelia hirsutula, commonly known as the hirsute gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant is known for its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky resin, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Grindelia hirsutula is often found in coastal and inland deserts, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
