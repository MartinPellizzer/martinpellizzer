Gladiolus communis var. ramosus

Gladiolus communis var. ramosus, commonly known as the sword lily, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of colorful, sword-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and are arranged in racemes along the stem.

The plant is a monocot, belonging to the Iridaceae family, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
