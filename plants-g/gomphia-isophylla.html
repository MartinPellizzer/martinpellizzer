Gomphia isophylla

Gomphia isophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leafed gomphia, is a unique plant belonging to the family Gomphaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Gomphia isophylla is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and spore dispersal mechanisms.
