Gelidocalamus

Gelidocalamus, commonly known as the cold bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is native to high-altitude regions of Asia, including the Himalayas and southern China. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Gelidocalamus species are often found in moist, cool environments, typically at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
