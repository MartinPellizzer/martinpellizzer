Genetyllis oederoides

Genetyllis oederoides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Genetyllis oederoides is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
