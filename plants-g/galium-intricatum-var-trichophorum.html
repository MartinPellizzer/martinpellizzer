Galium intricatum var. trichophorum

Galium intricatum var. trichophorum, commonly known as the hairy wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
