Guadua distorta

Guadua distorta, commonly known as the bent bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often curved stalks and can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its structural strength and flexibility.

Guadua distorta is often used in traditional construction and crafts due to its durable and sustainable properties.
