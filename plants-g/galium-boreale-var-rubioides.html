Galium boreale var. rubioides

Galium boreale var. rubioides, commonly known as the redstem bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern regions of North America.

It features slender, often reddish stems that are hairless or slightly hairy, and its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are followed by small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds.
