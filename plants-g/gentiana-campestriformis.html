Gentiana campestriformis

Gentiana campestriformis, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged opposite along the stem.
