Grevillea cirrhosa

Grevillea cirrhosa, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from orange to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Grevillea cirrhosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscaping.
