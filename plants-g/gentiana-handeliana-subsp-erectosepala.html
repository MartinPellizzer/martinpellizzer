Gentiana handeliana subsp. erectosepala

Gentiana handeliana subsp.

erectosepala, commonly known as the upright-sepaled gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its erect sepals, which give it its specific epithet.
