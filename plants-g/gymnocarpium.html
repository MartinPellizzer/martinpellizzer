Gymnocarpium

Gymnocarpium, commonly known as the naked-seed plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruits of Gymnocarpium are dry and indehiscent, containing a single seed, which is a key botanical feature. These plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.
