Guerreroia

Guerreroia, commonly known as the Mexican wild orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Guerreroia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
