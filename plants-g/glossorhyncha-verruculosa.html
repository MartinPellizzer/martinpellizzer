Glossorhyncha verruculosa

Glossorhyncha verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Glossorhyncha verruculosa typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats with high humidity and well-drained soil.
