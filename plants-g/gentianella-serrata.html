Gentianella serrata

Gentianella serrata, commonly known as the serrated gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually serrated, giving it its common name.

Gentianella serrata is often found in rocky or limestone soils and blooms in late summer.
