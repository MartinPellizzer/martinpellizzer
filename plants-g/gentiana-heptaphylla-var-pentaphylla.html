Gentiana heptaphylla var. pentaphylla

Gentiana heptaphylla var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are arranged in a basal rosette and are often lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
