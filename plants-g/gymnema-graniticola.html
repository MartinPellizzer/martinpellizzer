Gymnema graniticola

Gymnema graniticola, commonly known as the granite gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its tendrils and opposite leaves. The plant is notable for its unique leaf morphology, which often features a waxy coating and serrated edges. Gymnema graniticola is also known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal uses and ecological role in its native habitat.
