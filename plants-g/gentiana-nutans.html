Gentiana nutans

Gentiana nutans, commonly known as the nodding gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
