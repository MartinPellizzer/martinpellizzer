Gentiana streptopoda

Gentiana streptopoda, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with two prominent lips, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
