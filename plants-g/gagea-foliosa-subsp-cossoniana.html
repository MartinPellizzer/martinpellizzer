Gagea foliosa subsp. cossoniana

Gagea foliosa subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's leafy gagea, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this subspecies thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. The plant is typically found in open woodlands and rocky habitats, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Its blooming period occurs in early spring, making it a notable component of alpine flora.
