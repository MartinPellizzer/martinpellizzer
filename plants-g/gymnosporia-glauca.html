Gymnosporia glauca

Gymnosporia glauca, commonly known as the blue-green burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruit pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Gymnosporia glauca is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
