Genista hirta

Genista hirta, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, hairy leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Genista hirta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
