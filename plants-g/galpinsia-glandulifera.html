Galpinsia glandulifera

Galpinsia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular galpinsia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its leaves and stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
