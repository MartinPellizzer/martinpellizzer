Genista pilosa subsp. villarsii

Genista pilosa subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its root system is adapted to poor, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
