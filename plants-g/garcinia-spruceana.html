Garcinia spruceana

Garcinia spruceana, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to mangosteens. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
