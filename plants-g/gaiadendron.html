Gaiadendron

Gaiadendron, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Gaiadendron thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
