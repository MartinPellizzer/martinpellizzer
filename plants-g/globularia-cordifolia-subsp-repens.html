Globularia cordifolia subsp. repens

Globularia cordifolia subsp.

repens, commonly known as creeping globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for landscaping in dry, sunny environments.
