Galium mollugo var. mutabile

Galium mollugo var. mutabile, commonly known as variable bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium mollugo var. mutabile is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, which contributes to its common name.
