Galium vulcanicum

Galium vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic bedstraw, is a rare flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Italy.

It is a low-growing herb with small, opposite leaves and produces clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils typical of volcanic landscapes. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Galium vulcanicum is notable for its adaptation to extreme environmental conditions, making it a unique subject of botanical study.
