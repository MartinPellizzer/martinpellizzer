Grewia damine

Grewia damine, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Grewia damine is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
