Glechoma nuda

Glechoma nuda, commonly known as the naked woundwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Glechoma nuda thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
