Gagea iranica

Gagea iranica, commonly known as the Iranian spring beauty, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Hyacinthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

Gagea iranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
