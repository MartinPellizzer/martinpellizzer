Gentiana cephalantha subsp. vaniotii

Gentiana cephalantha subsp.

vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniot's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is upright and often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
