Gentiana polymorpha subsp. carpathica

Gentiana polymorpha subsp.

carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine and subalpine habitats.
