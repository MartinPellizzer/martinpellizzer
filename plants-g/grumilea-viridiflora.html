Grumilea viridiflora

Grumilea viridiflora, commonly known as the emerald-flowered grumilea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright green flowers that contrast strikingly with its foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Grumilea viridiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
