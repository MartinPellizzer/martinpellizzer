Geranium caffrum

Geranium caffrum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium caffrum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
