Galarhoeus tarokoensis

Galarhoeus tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is known for its unique morphology, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
