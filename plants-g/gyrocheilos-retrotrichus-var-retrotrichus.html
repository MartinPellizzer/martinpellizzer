Gyrocheilos retrotrichus var. retrotrichus

Gyrocheilos retrotrichus var. retrotrichus, commonly known as the woolly lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grass-like leaves and a distinctive, showy flower with a prominent, lip-like structure. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
