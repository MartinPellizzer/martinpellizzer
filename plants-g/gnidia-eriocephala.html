Gnidia eriocephala

Gnidia eriocephala, commonly known as the velvet bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance, and its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Gnidia eriocephala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
