Garcinia hombroniana

Garcinia hombroniana, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a juicy, acidic pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
