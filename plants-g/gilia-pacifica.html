Gilia pacifica

Gilia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific gilia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

Gilia pacifica plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
