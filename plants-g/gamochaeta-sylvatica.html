Gamochaeta sylvatica

Gamochaeta sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland gamochaeta, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gamochaeta sylvatica is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
