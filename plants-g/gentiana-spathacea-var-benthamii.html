Gentiana spathacea var. benthamii

Gentiana spathacea var. benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high elevation conditions.
