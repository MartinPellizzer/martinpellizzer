Gossypium latifolium

Gossypium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cotton or desert cotton, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seed pods that contain cotton-like fibers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high level of drought tolerance. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Gossypium latifolium plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
