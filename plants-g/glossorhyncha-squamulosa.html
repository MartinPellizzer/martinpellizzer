Glossorhyncha squamulosa

Glossorhyncha squamulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Glossorhyncha squamulosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
