Galactia tenuiflora var. lucida

Galactia tenuiflora var. lucida, commonly known as the shining galactia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its foliage is typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy and grayish-green in color.
