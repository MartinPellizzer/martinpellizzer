Gonolobus croceus

Gonolobus croceus, commonly known as the yellow globeflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are clustered in globular inflorescences, which give it its common name.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional African medicine.
