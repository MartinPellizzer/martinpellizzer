Gerascanthus crenatus

Gerascanthus crenatus, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a serrated margin, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among horticulturists.
