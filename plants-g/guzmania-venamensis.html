Guzmania venamensis

Guzmania venamensis, commonly known as the Venamensis bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports a microhabitat for insects and other small organisms. Its growth habit allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The plant's unique structure and ornamental value make it a fascinating subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
