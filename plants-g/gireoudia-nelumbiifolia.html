Gireoudia nelumbiifolia

Gireoudia nelumbiifolia, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Gireoudia, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that provide shade over the water surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, resembling those of the water lily. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in shallow water bodies.

Gireoudia nelumbiifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat for small aquatic organisms.
