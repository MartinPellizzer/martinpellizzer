Galedupa pongam

Galedupa pongam, commonly known as Indian bean tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
