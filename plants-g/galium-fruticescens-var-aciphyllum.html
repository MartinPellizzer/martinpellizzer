Galium fruticescens var. aciphyllum

Galium fruticescens var. aciphyllum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
