Gastrolobium nudum

Gastrolobium nudum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Gastrolobium nudum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
