Geniostoma rupestre var. lasiostemon

Geniostoma rupestre var. lasiostemon, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
