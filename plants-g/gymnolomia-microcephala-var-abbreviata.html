Gymnolomia microcephala var. abbreviata

Gymnolomia microcephala var. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like arrangement of stiff, needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation purposes.
