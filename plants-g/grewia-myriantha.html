Grewia myriantha

Grewia myriantha, commonly known as the many-fruited bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
