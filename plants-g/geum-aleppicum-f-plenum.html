Geum aleppicum f. plenum

Geum aleppicum f. plenum, commonly known as the full-flowered wood avens, is a horticultural form of the wood avens species.

It is characterized by its dense, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, while the stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
