Gelrebia trothaei subsp. trothaei

Gelrebia trothaei subsp.

trothaei, commonly known as the Red Sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Red Sea. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color.

The plant thrives in saline and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
