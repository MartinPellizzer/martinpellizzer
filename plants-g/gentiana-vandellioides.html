Gentiana vandellioides

Gentiana vandellioides, commonly known as the Vandelli's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
