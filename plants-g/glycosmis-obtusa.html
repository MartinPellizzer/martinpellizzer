Glycosmis obtusa

Glycosmis obtusa, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white berries that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Glycosmis obtusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
