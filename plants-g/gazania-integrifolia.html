Gazania integrifolia

Gazania integrifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Gazania integrifolia is known for its ability to tolerate drought and salty air, which contributes to its hardiness in various environments.
