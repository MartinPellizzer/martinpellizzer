Grewia inmac

Grewia inmac, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
