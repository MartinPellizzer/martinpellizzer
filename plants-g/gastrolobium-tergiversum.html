Gastrolobium tergiversum

Gastrolobium tergiversum, commonly known as the variable wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Gastrolobium tergiversum is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
