Gnephosis trifida

Gnephosis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves and intricate, fragrant flower structure. Native to specific regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a unique, spurred lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Gnephosis trifida is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
