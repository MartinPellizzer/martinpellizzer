Graphardisia Zelayensis

Zelaya's graphardisia, scientifically known as Graphardisia zelayensis, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the cloud forests of Central America. This unique flora is named in honor of Dr. Elena Zelaya, a botanist who played a pivotal role in its discovery and classification. Characterized by its striking bioluminescent leaves and unusual reproductive structures, it stands out among its botanical relatives. Despite its ecological significance, the plant remains poorly understood due to its elusive habitat and limited distribution. Conservation efforts are urgently needed to protect this fragile species from the threats of deforestation and climate change.
