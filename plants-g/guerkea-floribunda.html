Guerkea floribunda

Guerkea floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant guerke, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
