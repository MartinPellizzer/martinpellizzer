Gerbera parva

Gerbera parva, commonly known as the dwarf gerbera, is a compact flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with vibrant, colorful petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, making it ideal for container gardening or rock gardens. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gerbera parva prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
