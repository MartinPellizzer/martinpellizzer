Gynura walkeri

Gynura walkeri, commonly known as the silver nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white underside on its heart-shaped leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Gynura walkeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
