Grevillea longistyla

Grevillea longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters.

Grevillea longistyla is well-suited to dry, well-drained soils and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
