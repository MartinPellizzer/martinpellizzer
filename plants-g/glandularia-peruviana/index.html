Glandularia Peruviana

Glandularia peruviana is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is known for its vibrant purple and pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. This species belongs to the mint family and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique glandular structures on the leaves help it retain moisture in arid environments. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it has gained popularity among gardeners and botanists alike.
