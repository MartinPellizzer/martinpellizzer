Grindelia sibirica

Grindelia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Grindelia sibirica is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for its potential medicinal properties.
