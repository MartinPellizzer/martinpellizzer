Galium hilendiae subsp. carneum

Galium hilendiae subsp.

carneum, commonly known as the redstem bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
