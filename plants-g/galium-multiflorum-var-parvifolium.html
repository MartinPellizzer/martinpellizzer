Galium multiflorum var. parvifolium

Galium multiflorum var. parvifolium, commonly known as small-leaved bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
