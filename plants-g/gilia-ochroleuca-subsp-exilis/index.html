Gilia Ochroleuca Subsp. Exilis

Gilia ochroleuca subsp. exilis is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the western United States. It is known for its small, pale yellow blooms that appear in late spring. This subspecies thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Protecting this plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native habitat.
