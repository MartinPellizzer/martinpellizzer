Ganitrus roxburghii

Ganitrus roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth surface, while the stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Ganitrus roxburghii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
