Genista boissieri var. longiseta

Genista boissieri var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its stems are typically slender and often have a reddish hue, while the leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
