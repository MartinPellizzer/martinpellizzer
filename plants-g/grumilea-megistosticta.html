Grumilea megistosticta

Grumilea megistosticta, commonly known as the giant leafy spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit and large, fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are extensive and can form dense mats, allowing it to compete effectively with other vegetation.

Grumilea megistosticta is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.
