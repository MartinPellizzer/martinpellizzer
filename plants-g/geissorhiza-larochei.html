Geissorhiza larochei

Geissorhiza larochei, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a striking member of the Fabaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant features a short, woody stem and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a short growing season and is adapted to thrive in full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
