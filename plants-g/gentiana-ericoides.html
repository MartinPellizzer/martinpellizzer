Gentiana ericoides

Gentiana ericoides, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of the United States and Canada.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

Gentiana ericoides is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
