Glyceria acutiflora subsp. acutiflora

Glyceria acutiflora subsp.

acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams and ponds.

The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces spikelets with narrow, awned lemmas. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
