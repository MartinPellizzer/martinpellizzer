Gentiana sedifolia var. elongata

Gentiana sedifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, slightly hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
