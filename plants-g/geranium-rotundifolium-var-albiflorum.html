Geranium rotundifolium var. albiflorum

Geranium rotundifolium var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered roundleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, palmate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
