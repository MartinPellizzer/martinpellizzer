Gilibertia palmata

Gilibertia palmata, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow root, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a stout, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. The plant has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, giving it a striking appearance.

Gilibertia palmata is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its traditional use in treating various ailments.
