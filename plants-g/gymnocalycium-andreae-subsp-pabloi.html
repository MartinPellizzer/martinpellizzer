Gymnocalycium andreae subsp. pabloi

Gymnocalycium andreae subsp.

pabloi, commonly known as the Pablo's moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Argentina. It is a small, globular cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and a distinctive, pale green to blue-gray coloration.

The plant produces white flowers during the spring and summer months, typically in a cluster at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for cultivation in dry climates.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor plant collections.
