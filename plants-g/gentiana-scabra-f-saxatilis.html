Gentiana scabra f. saxatilis

Gentiana scabra f. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
