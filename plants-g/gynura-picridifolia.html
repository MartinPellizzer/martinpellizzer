Gynura picridifolia

Gynura picridifolia, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Gynura picridifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in the Asteraceae family.
