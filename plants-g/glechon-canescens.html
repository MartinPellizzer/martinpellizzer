Glechon canescens

Glechon canescens, commonly known as the woolly glechoma, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Glechon canescens is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
