Grewia microcos

Grewia microcos, commonly known as the small globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release tiny, hairy seeds.

The plant is often found in dry, disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
