Genesiphylla asplenifolia

Genesiphylla asplenifolia, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red or purple berries.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in New Zealand's flora.
