Gesneria decapleura

Gesneria decapleura, commonly known as the clustered gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with leaves that are elliptical and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, bloom during the warmer months and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Gesneria decapleura is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in shaded, humid environments.
