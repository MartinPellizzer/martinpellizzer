Gentiana tricolor subsp. syringea

Gentiana tricolor subsp.

syringea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Gentianaceae family.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
