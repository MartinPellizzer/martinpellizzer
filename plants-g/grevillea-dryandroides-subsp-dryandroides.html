Grevillea dryandroides subsp. dryandroides

Grevillea dryandroides subsp.

dryandroides, commonly known as the dryandra grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
