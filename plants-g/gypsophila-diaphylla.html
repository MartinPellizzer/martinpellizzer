Gypsophila diaphylla

Gypsophila diaphylla, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that resemble dust particles, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gypsophila diaphylla is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
