Gypsophila muralis var. serotina

Gypsophila muralis var. serotina, commonly known as late-flowering wall gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and cliffs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during late summer to autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or grayish powder.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
