Gilia interior

Gilia interior, commonly known as the interior gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces pink to purple blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has narrow, hairy leaves and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Gilia interior is an important plant for pollinators and is adapted to arid environments.
