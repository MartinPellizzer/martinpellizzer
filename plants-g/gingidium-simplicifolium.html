Gingidium simplicifolium

Gingidium simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaf ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is occasionally used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
