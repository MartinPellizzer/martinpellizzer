Gentiana × chlorifolia

Gentiana × chlorifolia, commonly known as the greenleaf gentian, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and often have a distinct greenish hue, giving it its common name.

Gentiana × chlorifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
