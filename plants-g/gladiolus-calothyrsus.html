Gladiolus calothyrsus

Gladiolus calothyrsus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of striking, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
