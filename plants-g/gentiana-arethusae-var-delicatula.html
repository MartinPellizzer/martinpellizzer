Gentiana arethusae var. delicatula

Gentiana arethusae var. delicatula, commonly known as the delicate gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
