Grindelia latifolia

Grindelia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Grindelia latifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
