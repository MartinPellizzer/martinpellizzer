Gochnatia cordata var. mollissima

Gochnatia cordata var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky gochnatia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
