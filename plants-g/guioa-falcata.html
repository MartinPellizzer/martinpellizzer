Guioa falcata

Guioa falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guioa, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits with a smooth, leathery skin. Guioa falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
