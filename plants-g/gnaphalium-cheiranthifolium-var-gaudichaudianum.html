Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. gaudichaudianum

Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. gaudichaudianum, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, fluffy florets. The plant produces pale yellow to white composite flowers that are arranged in dense, rounded clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in New Zealand's flora.
