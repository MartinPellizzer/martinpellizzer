Genista triacanthos subsp. triacanthos

Genista triacanthos subsp.

triacanthos, commonly known as the thorned broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
