Gladiolus exalatus

Gladiolus exalatus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, and its inflorescence consists of a spike of brightly colored flowers.

Gladiolus exalatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
