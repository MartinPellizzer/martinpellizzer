Gnaphalium elichrysoides

Gnaphalium elichrysoides, commonly known as the silverweed, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or marginal habitats.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
