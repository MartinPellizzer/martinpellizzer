Gossypium bickii

Gossypium bickii, commonly known as the Bickii cotton, is a species of shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fluffy seeds encased in a fibrous capsule, which is typical of the cotton genus. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its fibrous seeds are harvested for their soft, lint-like material, which has been traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.

Gossypium bickii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
