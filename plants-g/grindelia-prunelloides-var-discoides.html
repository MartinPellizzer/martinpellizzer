Grindelia prunelloides var. discoides

Grindelia prunelloides var. discoides, commonly known as the discoid tansy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its foliage and flowers are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Grindelia prunelloides var. discoides is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
