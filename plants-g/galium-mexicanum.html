Galium mexicanum

Galium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Galium mexicanum is characterized by its slender stems and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
