Gentiana vernicosa

Gentiana vernicosa, commonly known as the winter gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Gentiana vernicosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering period.
