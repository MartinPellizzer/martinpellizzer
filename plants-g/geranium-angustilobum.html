Geranium angustilobum

Geranium angustilobum, commonly known as narrow-lobed geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Geranium angustilobum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
