Gymnocalycium damsii var. rotundulum

Gymnocalycium damsii var. rotundulum, commonly known as the rounded moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
