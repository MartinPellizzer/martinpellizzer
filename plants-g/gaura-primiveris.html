Gaura primiveris

Gaura primiveris, commonly known as early gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of pale pink to white, bell-shaped flowers. It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring full sun and well-drained soil.
