Geum urbanum var. mauritanicum

Geum urbanum var. mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritanian avens, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and small, showy flowers with five petals in shades of yellow to red. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
