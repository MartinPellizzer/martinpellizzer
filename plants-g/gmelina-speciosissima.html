Gmelina speciosissima

Gmelina speciosissima, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
