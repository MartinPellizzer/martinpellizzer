Grevillea manglesioides subsp. papillosa

Grevillea manglesioides subsp.

papillosa, commonly known as the papillate grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called papillae.

The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
