Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora

Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered licorice, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Glycyrrhiza pallidiflora thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
