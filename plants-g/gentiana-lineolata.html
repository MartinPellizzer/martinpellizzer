Gentiana lineolata

Gentiana lineolata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

The flowers are tubular with a short throat and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
