Geum × macneillii

Geum × macneillii, commonly known as the MacNeil's avens, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double to fully double pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features heart-shaped, palmate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant landscapes.
