Gaertnera aphanodioica

Gaertnera aphanodioica, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, almost translucent fronds that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Gaertnera aphanodioica is considered a critically endangered species.
