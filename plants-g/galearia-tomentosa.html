Galearia tomentosa

Galearia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is often found in high-altitude environments.

Galearia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
