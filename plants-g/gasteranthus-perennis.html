Gasteranthus perennis

Gasteranthus perennis, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in a wide array of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a basal rosette of fleshy, narrow leaves that help it retain moisture in its native arid environment. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gasteranthus perennis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
