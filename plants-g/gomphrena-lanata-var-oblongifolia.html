Gomphrena lanata var. oblongifolia

Gomphrena lanata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the woolly globe flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features branched stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red, resembling tiny globes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white pubescence, contributing to its overall textured look.

Gomphrena lanata var. oblongifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
