Geum calthifolium var. dilatatum

Geum calthifolium var. dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are rounded to heart-shaped, with a hairy surface, and stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and native plant gardens.
