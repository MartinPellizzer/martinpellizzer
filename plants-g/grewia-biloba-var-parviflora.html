Grewia biloba var. parviflora

Grewia biloba var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a rough texture and a distinctive serrated margin. It produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
