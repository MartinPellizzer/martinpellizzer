Galearia pedicellata

Galearia pedicellata, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across rocky and alpine habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, often with a white throat, and bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Galearia pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
