Grusonia verticillata

Grusonia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a cluster of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in harsh, cold environments. Its leaves are generally succulent and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Grusonia verticillata is adapted to low light conditions and has a short growing season, making it a hardy species in extreme climates.
