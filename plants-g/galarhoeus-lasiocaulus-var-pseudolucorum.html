Galarhoeus lasiocaulus var. pseudolucorum

Galarhoeus lasiocaulus var. pseudolucorum, commonly known as the silver-haired lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a pale to dark grey upper surface with a distinctive silvery bloom, and a lighter, leathery lower surface. The thallus is composed of a complex interwoven network of fungal hyphae and algal cells, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological role. It is often found on decaying wood or bark, where it plays a vital part in nutrient cycling.

This lichen is relatively rare and is considered an indicator of clean, unpolluted environments.
