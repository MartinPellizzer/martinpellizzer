Geranium philippii

Geranium philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower colors, which range from pink to red.
