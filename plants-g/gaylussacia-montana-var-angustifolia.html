Gaylussacia montana var. angustifolia

Gaylussacia montana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blackhaw, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark purple, leathery berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for wildlife.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands and streambanks.
