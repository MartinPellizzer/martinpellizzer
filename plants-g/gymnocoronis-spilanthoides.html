Gymnocoronis spilanthoides

Gymnocoronis spilanthoides, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its taproot system and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
