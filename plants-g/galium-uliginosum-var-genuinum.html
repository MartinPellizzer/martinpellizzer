Galium uliginosum var. genuinum

Galium uliginosum var. genuinum, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in umbels at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and other wetland environments.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is an important plant for wetland ecosystems.
