Gaultheria odorata

Gaultheria odorata, commonly known as the fragrant wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a sharp, spicy flavor. Gaultheria odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

It is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and medicinal preparations.
