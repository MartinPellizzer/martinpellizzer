Geonoma maxima subsp. maxima

Geonoma maxima subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tall, clump-forming plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swamps. Its foliage is typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.
