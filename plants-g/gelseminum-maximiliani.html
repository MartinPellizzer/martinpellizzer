Gelseminum maximiliani

Gelseminum maximiliani, commonly known as the yellow moon vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
