Guidonia alba

Guidonia alba, commonly known as the white guidonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
