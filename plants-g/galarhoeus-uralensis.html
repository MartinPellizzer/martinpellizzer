Galarhoeus uralensis

Galarhoeus uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear showy blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Galarhoeus uralensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
