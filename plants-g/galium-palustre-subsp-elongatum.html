Galium palustre subsp. elongatum

Galium palustre subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as swamp bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, acidic soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
