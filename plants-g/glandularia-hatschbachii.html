Glandularia hatschbachii

Glandularia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Florida blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to native plant gardens.
