Garcinia cymosa f. pendula

Garcinia cymosa f. pendula, commonly known as the hanging Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and yellow-orange fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
