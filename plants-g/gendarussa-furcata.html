Gendarussa furcata

Gendarussa furcata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine.

Gendarussa furcata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and along roadsides.
