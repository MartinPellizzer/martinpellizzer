Gaillardia megapotamica f. bicolor

Gaillardia megapotamica f. bicolor, commonly known as the Mexican blanketflower, is a striking annual plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with a distinctive bicolor pattern, typically red or orange centers surrounded by yellow petals. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its vibrant colors and easy care make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
