Gonocarpus aggregatus

Gonocarpus aggregatus, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in saline environments and is often found growing along shorelines and in brackish water areas. Its root system helps stabilize coastal soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Gonocarpus aggregatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical regions.
