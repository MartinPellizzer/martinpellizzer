Gentiana kochiana

Gentiana kochiana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of down, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Gentiana kochiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
