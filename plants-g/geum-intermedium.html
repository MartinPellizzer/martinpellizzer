Geum intermedium

Geum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
