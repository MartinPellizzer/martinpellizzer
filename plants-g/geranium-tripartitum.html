Geranium tripartitum

Geranium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Geranium tripartitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
