Guioa eriantha

Guioa eriantha, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant red or orange flowers and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Guioa eriantha is often cultivated for landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
