Gnaphalium mucronatum

Gnaphalium mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant essential oil.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine.
