Grevillea buxifolia

Grevillea buxifolia, commonly known as the box-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and resemble box leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Grevillea buxifolia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and bees.
