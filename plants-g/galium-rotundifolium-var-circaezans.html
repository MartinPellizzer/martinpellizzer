Galium rotundifolium var. circaezans

Galium rotundifolium var. circaezans, commonly known as the roundleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.
