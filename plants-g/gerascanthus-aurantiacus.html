Gerascanthus aurantiacus

Gerascanthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
