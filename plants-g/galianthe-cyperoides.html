Galianthe cyperoides

Galianthe cyperoides, commonly known as the marsh galingale, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marsh environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often pink or white, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. It thrives in moist soil and is often found in swamps, riverbanks, and other waterlogged areas.

Galianthe cyperoides is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
