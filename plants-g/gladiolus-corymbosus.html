Gladiolus corymbosus

Gladiolus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of colorful, sword-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous root system. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Gladiolus corymbosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
