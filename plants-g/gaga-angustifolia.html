Gaga angustifolia

Gaga angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gaga, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
