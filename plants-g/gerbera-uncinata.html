Gerbera uncinata

Gerbera uncinata, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gerbera uncinata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
