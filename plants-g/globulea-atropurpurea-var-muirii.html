Globulea atropurpurea var. muirii

Globulea atropurpurea var. muirii, commonly known as the Purple Globular Bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The unique globular shape of its flower heads gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance.
