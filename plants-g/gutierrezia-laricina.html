Gutierrezia laricina

Gutierrezia laricina, commonly known as threadleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and thread-like, giving it its common name, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Gutierrezia laricina is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
