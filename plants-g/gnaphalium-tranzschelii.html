Gnaphalium tranzschelii

Gnaphalium tranzschelii, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often surrounded by white or pale yellow bracts. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, dry conditions.

Gnaphalium tranzschelii is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.
