Gastrolobium obovatum var. verticillatum

Gastrolobium obovatum var. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often arranged in whorls, giving the plant its "clustered" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and landscaping.
