Grastidium quinquecaudatum

Grastidium quinquecaudatum, commonly known as the five-tailed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
