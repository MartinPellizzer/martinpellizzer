Gypsocallis nigrita

Gypsocallis nigrita, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically oval in shape. The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture.
