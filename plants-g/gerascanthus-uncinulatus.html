Gerascanthus uncinulatus

Gerascanthus uncinulatus, commonly known as the hooked geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hooked calyx that persists even after the flowers have faded. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features palmate leaves with lobed margins. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental value.

Gerascanthus uncinulatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
