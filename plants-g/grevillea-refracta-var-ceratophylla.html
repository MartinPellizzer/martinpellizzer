Grevillea refracta var. ceratophylla

Grevillea refracta var. ceratophylla, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

The variety ceratophylla is notable for its unique floral structure and vibrant color, which attract a variety of pollinators.
