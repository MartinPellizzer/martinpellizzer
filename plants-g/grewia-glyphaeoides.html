Grewia glyphaeoides

Grewia glyphaeoides, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia glyphaeoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought conditions.
