Godetia albescens var. micropetala

Godetia albescens var. micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf white godetia, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a bedding plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
