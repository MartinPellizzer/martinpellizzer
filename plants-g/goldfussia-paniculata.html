Goldfussia paniculata

Goldfussia paniculata, commonly known as the golden dewdrop or golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, white, egg-shaped fruits called capsules.

Goldfussia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and natural habitats.
