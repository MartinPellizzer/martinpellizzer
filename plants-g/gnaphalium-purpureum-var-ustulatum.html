Gnaphalium purpureum var. ustulatum

Gnaphalium purpureum var. ustulatum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink to purple florets that are typically tubular in shape, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
