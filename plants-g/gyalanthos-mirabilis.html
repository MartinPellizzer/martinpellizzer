Gyalanthos mirabilis

Gyalanthos mirabilis, commonly known as the miraculous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Gyalanthos mirabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
