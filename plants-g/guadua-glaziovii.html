Guadua glaziovii

Guadua glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in montane forests.

Guadua glaziovii is valued for its strong, lightweight structure, making it a popular material for construction and handicrafts.
