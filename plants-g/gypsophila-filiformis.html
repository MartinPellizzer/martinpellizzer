Gypsophila filiformis

Gypsophila filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf sweetpea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a subtle, elegant beauty to the landscape.

Gypsophila filiformis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
