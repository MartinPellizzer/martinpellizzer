Gentiana hladnikiana

Gentiana hladnikiana, commonly known as the Hladnik's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
