Gomphichis plantaginifolia

Gomphichis plantaginifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gomphichis plantaginifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
