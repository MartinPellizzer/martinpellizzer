Geum scottsbergianum

Geum scottsbergianum, commonly known as the Scottsberg avens, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
