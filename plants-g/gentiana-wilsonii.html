Gentiana wilsonii

Gentiana wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
