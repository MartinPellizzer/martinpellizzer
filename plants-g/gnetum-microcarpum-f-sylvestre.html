Gnetum microcarpum f. sylvestre

Gnetum microcarpum f. sylvestre, commonly known as the forest gnetum, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Gnetophyta class.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring scale-like leaves and a distinct reproductive structure. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary position, bridging gymnosperms and angiosperms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
