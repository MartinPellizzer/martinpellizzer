Guzmania confusa var. confusa

Guzmania confusa var. confusa, commonly known as the confusa guzmania, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports small aquatic life. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, silver, and white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Guzmania confusa var. confusa thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
