Gynoxys cutervensis

Gynoxys cutervensis, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks.

Gynoxys cutervensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
