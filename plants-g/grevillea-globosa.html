Grevillea globosa

Grevillea globosa, commonly known as the globular grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical shape and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
