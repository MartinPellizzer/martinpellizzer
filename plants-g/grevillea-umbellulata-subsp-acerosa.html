Grevillea umbellulata subsp. acerosa

Grevillea umbellulata subsp.

acerosa, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and dense, narrow leaves that are typically serrated or toothed.

The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flower spikes that appear in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
