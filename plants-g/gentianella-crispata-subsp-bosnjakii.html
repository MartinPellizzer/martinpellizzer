Gentianella crispata subsp. bosnjakii

Gentianella crispata subsp.

bosnjakii, commonly known as the Bosnian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, often wrinkled leaves and a compact growth habit. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain meadows and woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
