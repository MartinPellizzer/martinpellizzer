Gymnostachyum coriaceum

Gymnostachyum coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Gymnostachyum coriaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
