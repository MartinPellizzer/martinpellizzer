Glossorhyncha gracilis

Glossorhyncha gracilis, commonly known as the slender horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white blossoms that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Glossorhyncha gracilis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
