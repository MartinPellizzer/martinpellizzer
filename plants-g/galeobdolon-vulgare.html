Galeobdolon vulgare

Galeobdolon vulgare, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and is often found in moist meadows and grasslands. It contains toxic compounds, including pyrethrin, which has been historically used in insecticides.

Due to its medicinal and pesticidal properties, it has been both valued and avoided throughout history.
