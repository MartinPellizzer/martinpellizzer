Geranium pilosum var. australe

Geranium pilosum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian hairy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. It is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
