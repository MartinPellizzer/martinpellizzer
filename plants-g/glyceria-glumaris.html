Glyceria glumaris

Glyceria glumaris, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Glyceria glumaris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
