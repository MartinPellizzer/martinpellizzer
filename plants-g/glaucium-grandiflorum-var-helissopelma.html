Glaucium grandiflorum var. helissopelma

Glaucium grandiflorum var. helissopelma, commonly known as the Helissopelma poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
