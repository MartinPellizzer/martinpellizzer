Gundlachia corymbosa var. ocoana

Gundlachia corymbosa var. ocoana, commonly known as the Ocoa, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The Ocoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
