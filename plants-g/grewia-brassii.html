Grewia brassii

Grewia brassii, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves that turn red or orange in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Grewia brassii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
