Gaultheria schiedeana

Gaultheria schiedeana, commonly known as the Andean strawberry or Chilean strawberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are red when ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy coating, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Gaultheria schiedeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
