Geum aleppicum var. decurrens

Geum aleppicum var. decurrens, commonly known as the running avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellowish to orange-red flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
