Goodyera viridiflora var. ogatae

Goodyera viridiflora var. ogatae, commonly known as the Ogata's green-flowered pteridophyte, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and unique, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist forest understories, often growing on the bases of trees or on rocky substrates. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and its role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
