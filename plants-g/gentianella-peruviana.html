Gentianella peruviana

Gentianella peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Gentianella peruviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
