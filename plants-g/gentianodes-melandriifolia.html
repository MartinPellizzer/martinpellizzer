Gentianodes melandriifolia

Gentianodes melandriifolia, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
