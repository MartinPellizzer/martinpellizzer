Gonolobus tigrinus

Gonolobus tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, often variegated leaves with a striking mottled appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage.
