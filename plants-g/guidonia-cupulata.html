Guidonia cupulata

Guidonia cupulata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure formed by the fused sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
