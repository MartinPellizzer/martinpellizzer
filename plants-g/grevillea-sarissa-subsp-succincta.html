Grevillea sarissa subsp. succincta

Grevillea sarissa subsp.

succincta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a species native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The flowers are attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
