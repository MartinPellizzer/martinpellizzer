Gratiola officinalis subsp. broteri

Gratiola officinalis subsp.

broteri, commonly known as the African marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, exhibiting a rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Gratiola officinalis.
