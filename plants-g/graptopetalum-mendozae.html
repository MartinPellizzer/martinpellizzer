Graptopetalum mendozae

Graptopetalum mendozae, commonly known as the Mendoza stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in succulents.
