Geranium alceoides

Geranium alceoides, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and hairy, with showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to alpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
