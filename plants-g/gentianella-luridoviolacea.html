Gentianella luridoviolacea

Gentianella luridoviolacea, commonly known as the purple viola gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
