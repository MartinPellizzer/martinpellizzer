Gentiana ovatiloba subsp. ovatiloba

Gentiana ovatiloba subsp.

ovatiloba, commonly known as the oval-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August.

The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette, with some leaves along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
