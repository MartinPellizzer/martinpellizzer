Grias foetidissima

Grias foetidissima, commonly known as the stinking grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant is notable for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is produced by its inflorescence. It typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in disturbed soils.

Despite its pungent smell, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
