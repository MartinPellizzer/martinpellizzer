Galactia neesii var. australis

Galactia neesii var. australis, commonly known as the Australian milkvine, is a climbing legume native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, showy, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Galactia neesii var. australis is known for its milky sap, which can be irritant to the skin.
