Galium bloomeri var. hirsutum

Galium bloomeri var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy woodypoppy, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
