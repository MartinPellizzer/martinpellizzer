Galanthus caucasicus

Galanthus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces delicate, white flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Galanthus caucasicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
