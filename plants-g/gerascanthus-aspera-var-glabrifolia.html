Gerascanthus aspera var. glabrifolia

Gerascanthus aspera var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf gerascanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
