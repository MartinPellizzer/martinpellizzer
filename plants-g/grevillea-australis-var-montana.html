Grevillea australis var. montana

Grevillea australis var. montana, commonly known as the mountain grevillea, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces striking red or orange cylindrical flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in heathlands and open forests at higher altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
