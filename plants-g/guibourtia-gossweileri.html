Guibourtia gossweileri

Guibourtia gossweileri, commonly known as African shea tree, is a tropical hardwood tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, durable wood used in furniture and construction. The tree has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also valued for its role in soil conservation and biodiversity.
