Gelasia hirsuta

Gelasia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gelasia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Gelasia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
