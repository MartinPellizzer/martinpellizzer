Gleicheniastrum circinnatum var. mendellii

Gleicheniastrum circinnatum var. mendellii, commonly known as the Mendell's Gleicheniopsis, is a fern native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its habitat.
