Gentianella amarella var. fastigiata

Gentianella amarella var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
