Galactia decumbens var. benthamiana

Galactia decumbens var. benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in sustainable agriculture.
