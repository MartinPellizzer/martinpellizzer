Guzmania polycephala

Guzmania polycephala, commonly known as the clustered torch ginger, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence that resembles a torch, with bright red bracts surrounding small, white flowers. The plant features a rosette of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. Guzmania polycephala thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
