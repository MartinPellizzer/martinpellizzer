Gentianusa asclepiadea

Gentianusa asclepiadea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are typically arranged in a five-petaled structure. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
