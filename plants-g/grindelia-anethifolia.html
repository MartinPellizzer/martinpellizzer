Grindelia anethifolia

Grindelia anethifolia, commonly known as the western goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
