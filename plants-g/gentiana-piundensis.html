Gentiana piundensis

Gentiana piundensis, commonly known as the Piundens gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing vibrant colors that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana piundensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
