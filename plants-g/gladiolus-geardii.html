Gladiolus geardii

Gladiolus geardii, commonly known as the Cape York gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and blooms in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant and dramatic floral display.
