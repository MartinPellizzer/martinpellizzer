Gentianella acuta subsp. plebeia

Gentianella acuta subsp.

plebeia, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
