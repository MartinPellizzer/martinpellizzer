Glochidion pomiferum

Glochidion pomiferum, commonly known as the sweet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in panicles, and are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
