Gardnerodoxa

Gardnerodoxa, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. Gardnerodoxa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
