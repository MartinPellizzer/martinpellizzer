Gonostemon × novus

Gonostemon × novus, commonly known as the hybrid stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hollow, green stems and serrated leaves.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers on the same plant.

Gonostemon × novus is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a source of natural fibers.
