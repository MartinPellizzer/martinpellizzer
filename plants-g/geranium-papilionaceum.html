Geranium papilionaceum

Geranium papilionaceum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-like flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Geranium papilionaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
