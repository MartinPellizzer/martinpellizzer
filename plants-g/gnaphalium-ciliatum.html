Gnaphalium ciliatum

Gnaphalium ciliatum, commonly known as the silky everlasting or wooly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, creeping stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Gnaphalium ciliatum is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, and it is known for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
