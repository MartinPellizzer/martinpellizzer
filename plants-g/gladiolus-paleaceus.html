Gladiolus paleaceus

Gladiolus paleaceus, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are broad and pointed, contributing to its striking appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gladiolus paleaceus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
