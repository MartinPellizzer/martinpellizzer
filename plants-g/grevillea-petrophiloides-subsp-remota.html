Grevillea petrophiloides subsp. remota

Grevillea petrophiloides subsp.

remota, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, recurved, red or orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
