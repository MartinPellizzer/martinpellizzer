Gymnocactus subterraneus

Gymnocactus subterraneus, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, often during the spring and summer seasons. Its roots are typically shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the surface soil.

Gymnocactus subterraneus thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
