Gentiana detonsa var. stracheyi

Gentiana detonsa var. stracheyi, commonly known as Strachey's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
