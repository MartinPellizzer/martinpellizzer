Galactia marginalis var. puberula

Galactia marginalis var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy galactia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its small, white to pale pink flowers are typically arranged in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
