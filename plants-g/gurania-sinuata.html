Gurania sinuata

Gurania sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often notched or lobed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique leaf shape and climbing habit make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
