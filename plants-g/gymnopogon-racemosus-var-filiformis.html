Gymnopogon racemosus var. filiformis

Gymnopogon racemosus var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a variant of the spikegrass species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and compact, racemose inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, spike-like flower clusters are often wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
