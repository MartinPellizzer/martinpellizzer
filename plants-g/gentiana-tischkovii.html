Gentiana tischkovii

Gentiana tischkovii, commonly known as the Tischkow's gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Gentiana tischkovii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
