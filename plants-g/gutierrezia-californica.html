Gutierrezia californica

Gutierrezia californica, commonly known as California gorse, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, fluffy flower heads that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant produces small, linear leaves and has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats.

Gutierrezia californica is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
