Gomidesia myrcioides

Gomidesia myrcioides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with a dense, bushy habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming an invasive species.
