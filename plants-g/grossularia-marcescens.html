Grossularia marcescens

Grossularia marcescens, commonly known as the red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces small, round berries that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety and maturity. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are often reddish or bronze in color, especially in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are used in culinary applications and also have ornamental value due to their vibrant color and attractive foliage.
