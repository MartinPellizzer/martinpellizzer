Geranium trilobatum

Geranium trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have three distinct lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Geranium trilobatum prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
