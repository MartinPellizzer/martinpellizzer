Grindelia nitida

Grindelia nitida, commonly known as silverleaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Grindelia nitida is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
