Gentiana quinquefolia var. occidentalis

Gentiana quinquefolia var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has basal leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
