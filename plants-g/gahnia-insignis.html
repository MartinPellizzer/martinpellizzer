Gahnia insignis

Gahnia insignis, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its slender stems and narrow leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Gahnia insignis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
