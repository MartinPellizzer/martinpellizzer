Gymnadenia richteri

Gymnadenia richteri, commonly known as the richard's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers bloom in late summer.

Gymnadenia richteri is known for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its declining population.
