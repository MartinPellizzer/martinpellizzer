Gladiolus tristis subsp. spiralis

Gladiolus tristis subsp.

spiralis, commonly known as the spiral gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its striking, spiral-arranged inflorescence, which consists of multiple whorls of brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, while its roots are tuberous.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
