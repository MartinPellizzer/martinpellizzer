Gastrolobium spinosum f. angustum

Gastrolobium spinosum f. angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf burr wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny pods and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and it produces hard, woody fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
