Gastrolobium nutans

Gastrolobium nutans, commonly known as the drooping wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, linear leaflets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Gastrolobium nutans is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it plays a role in soil stabilization.
