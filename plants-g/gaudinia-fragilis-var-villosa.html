Gaudinia fragilis var. villosa

Gaudinia fragilis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy or pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
