Geigeria aspalathoides

Geigeria aspalathoides, commonly known as the Blushing Bush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
