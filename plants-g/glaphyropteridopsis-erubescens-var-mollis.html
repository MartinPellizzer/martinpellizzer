Glaphyropteridopsis erubescens var. mollis

Glaphyropteridopsis erubescens var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaf glaphyropterid, is a rare fern species characterized by its delicate, velvety fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with a soft, leathery texture, and its spores are produced in specialized structures called sporangia. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological niche.
