Glumosia

Glumosia, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of spikelets, which are the basic units of grass inflorescences. Glumosia species typically grow in temperate and subtropical regions, often in moist or wetland habitats. Their leaves are narrow and grass-like, and they produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The genus is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological roles and potential agricultural applications.
