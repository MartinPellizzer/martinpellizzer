Galium commune subvar. rubriflorum

Galium commune subvar. rubriflorum, commonly known as redflower bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
