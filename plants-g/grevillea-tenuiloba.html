Grevillea tenuiloba

Grevillea tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea tenuiloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
