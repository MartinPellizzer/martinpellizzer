Geranium flanaganii

Geranium flanaganii, commonly known as the Flanagan's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
