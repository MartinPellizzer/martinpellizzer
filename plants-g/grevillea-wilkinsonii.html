Grevillea wilkinsonii

Grevillea wilkinsonii, commonly known as the pink flowering waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright pink or red flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences that resemble small, rounded balls. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea wilkinsonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native birds and insects.
