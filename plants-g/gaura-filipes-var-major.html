Gaura filipes var. major

Gaura filipes var. major, commonly known as the showy gaura, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a graceful touch to landscapes.
