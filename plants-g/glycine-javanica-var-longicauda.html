Glycine javanica var. longicauda

Glycine javanica var. longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
