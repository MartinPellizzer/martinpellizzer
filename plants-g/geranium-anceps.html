Geranium anceps

Geranium anceps, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
