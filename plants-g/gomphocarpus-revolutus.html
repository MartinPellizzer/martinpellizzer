Gomphocarpus revolutus

Gomphocarpus revolutus, commonly known as the Blue Morning Glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a notable species in the Convolvulaceae family.
