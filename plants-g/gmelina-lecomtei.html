Gmelina lecomtei

Gmelina lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in humid tropical climates.

Gmelina lecomtei is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
