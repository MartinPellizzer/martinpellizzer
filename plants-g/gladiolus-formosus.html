Gladiolus formosus

Gladiolus formosus, commonly known as the Formosa gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, with strikingly large, petal-like bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
