Gumira senensis

Gumira senensis, commonly known as the African gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing seeds. Its roots are known for their ability to stabilize soil, making it useful in reforestation efforts.

The tree is also valued for its gum, which has been used traditionally in various applications.
