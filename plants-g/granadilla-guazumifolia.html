Granadilla guazumifolia

Granadilla guazumifolia, commonly known as the granadilla or yellow granadilla, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, fleshy berries that are edible and highly prized for their flavor. The berries are typically harvested when they turn a deep red color, indicating maturity.

This plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
