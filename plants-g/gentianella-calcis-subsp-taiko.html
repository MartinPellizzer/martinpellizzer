Gentianella calcis subsp. taiko

Gentianella calcis subsp.

taiko, commonly known as the Taiko gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
