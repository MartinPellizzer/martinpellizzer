Gentiana magellanica f. albiflora

Gentiana magellanica f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
