Gynandropsis macrothyrsus

Gynandropsis macrothyrsus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

The plant contains milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
