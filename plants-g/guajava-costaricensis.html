Guajava costaricensis

Guajava costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican guajava, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the mango and cashew. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a tangy, sweet flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

The plant is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
