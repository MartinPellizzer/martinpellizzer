Gymnadenia nigra subsp. corneliana

Gymnadenia nigra subsp.

corneliana, commonly known as the cornelian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hooded lip that aids in pollination by insects.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
