Gentiana amarella var. acuta

Gentiana amarella var. acuta, commonly known as the acute gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The acute gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
