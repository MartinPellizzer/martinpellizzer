Glossorhyncha saccosepala

Glossorhyncha saccosepala, commonly known as the sac-like lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive sac-like lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are short and often bear a single flower.

The orchid's reproductive strategy relies on specific pollinators, highlighting its ecological importance in its native habitat.
