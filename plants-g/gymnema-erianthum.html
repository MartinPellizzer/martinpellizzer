Gymnema erianthum

Gymnema erianthum, commonly known as the Indian cheese plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its unique milky sap, which contains compounds such as gymnemic acids. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for various health purposes.
