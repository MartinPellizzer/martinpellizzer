Gratiola marginata

Gratiola marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Gratiola marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
