Gonolobus stenanthus subsp. yucatanensis

Gonolobus stenanthus subsp.

yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan morning glory, is a member of the Convolvulaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is native to the Yucatan Peninsula and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its invasive potential in certain regions.
