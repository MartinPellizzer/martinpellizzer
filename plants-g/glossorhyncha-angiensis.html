Glossorhyncha angiensis

Glossorhyncha angiensis, commonly known as the Angolan orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to Angola.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that display a complex structure typical of orchids, with specialized petals and sepals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss, Glossorhyncha angiensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native range.
