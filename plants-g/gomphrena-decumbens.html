Gomphrena decumbens

Gomphrena decumbens, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena decumbens is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
