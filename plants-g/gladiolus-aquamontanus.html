Gladiolus aquamontanus

Gladiolus aquamontanus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting showy, spike-like inflorescences of vibrant, colorful flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white, making it a striking addition to gardens.

This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
