Grewia hildebrandtii

Grewia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia hildebrandtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
