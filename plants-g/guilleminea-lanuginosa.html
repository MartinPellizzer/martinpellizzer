Guilleminea lanuginosa

Guilleminea lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly guillemia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Guilleminea lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
