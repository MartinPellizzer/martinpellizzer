Galium verum f. souliei

Galium verum f. souliei, commonly known as the golden bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw, characterized by its yellow flowers and creeping growth habit.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
