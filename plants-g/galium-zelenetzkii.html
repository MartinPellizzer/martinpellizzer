Galium zelenetzkii

Galium zelenetzkii, commonly known as the Zelenetzki's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium zelenetzkii is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh climates.
