Glycyrrhiza nutantiflora

Glycyrrhiza nutantiflora, commonly known as the sweetroot or licorice plant, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its roots are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweet taste and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza nutantiflora is often cultivated for its therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.
