Gardenia cinerascens

Gardenia cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Gardenia cinerascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
