Gaultheria dolichopoda

Gaultheria dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stalked wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and have a distinctive wintergreen scent when crushed.

Gaultheria dolichopoda thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
