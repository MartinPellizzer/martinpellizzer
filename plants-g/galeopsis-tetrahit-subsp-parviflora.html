Galeopsis tetrahit subsp. parviflora

Galeopsis tetrahit subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the lesser hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain several tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
