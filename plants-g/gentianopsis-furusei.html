Gentianopsis furusei

Gentianopsis furusei, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianopsis furusei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
