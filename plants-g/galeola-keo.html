Galeola keo

Galeola keo, commonly known as the keo orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The keo orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native ecosystems.
