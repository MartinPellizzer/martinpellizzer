Gladiolus excisus

Gladiolus excisus, commonly known as the excised gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The botanical features of Gladiolus excisus make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
