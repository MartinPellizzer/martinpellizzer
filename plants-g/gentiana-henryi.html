Gentiana henryi

Gentiana henryi, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and sturdy.

Gentiana henryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
