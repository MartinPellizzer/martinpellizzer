Galium anglicum subsp. tenuicaule

Galium anglicum subsp.

tenuicaule, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
