Gunnera meziana

Gunnera meziana, commonly known as the Andean giant water shield, is a striking perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in diameter, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant thrives in moist, cool environments, often found in cloud forests and mountain streams. Its large leaves serve as a habitat for various small organisms, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.

Gunnera meziana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture, particularly in shaded gardens.
