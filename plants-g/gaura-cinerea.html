Gaura cinerea

Gaura cinerea, commonly known as silver gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gaura cinerea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
