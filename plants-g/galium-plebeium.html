Galium plebeium

Galium plebeium, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Galium plebeium is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and stream banks.
