Galium erectum var. genuinum

Galium erectum var. genuinum, commonly known as the upright wood sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of ecologically rich habitats.
