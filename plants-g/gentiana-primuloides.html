Gentiana primuloides

Gentiana primuloides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana primuloides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
