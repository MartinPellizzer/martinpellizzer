Gymnocactus horripilus subsp. kvetae

Gymnocactus horripilus subsp.

kvetae, commonly known as the Kveta's Gymnocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, covered with white to pale yellow spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique spine arrangement and slightly different growth form compared to other members of the Gymnocactus genus.
