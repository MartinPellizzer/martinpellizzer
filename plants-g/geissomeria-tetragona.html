Geissomeria tetragona

Geissomeria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
