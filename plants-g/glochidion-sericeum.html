Glochidion sericeum

Glochidion sericeum, commonly known as silky glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Glochidion sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
