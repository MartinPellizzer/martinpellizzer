Gastrococos armentalis

Gastrococos armentalis, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that produces yellow composite flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss, and it has a taproot system for deep water absorption.

The flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
