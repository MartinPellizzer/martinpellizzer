Gaillardia spathulata

Gaillardia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like blooms with red and yellow petals. The plant typically grows in full sun and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage is broad and slightly hairy, adding texture to the landscape.

Gaillardia spathulata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
