Gardenia suavis

Gardenia suavis, commonly known as the sweet gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia suavis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive scent.
