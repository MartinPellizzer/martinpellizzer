Galium rotundifolium var. javanicum

Galium rotundifolium var. javanicum, commonly known as the Java bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows.

Its delicate structure and flowering habit make it a notable species in its native habitat.
