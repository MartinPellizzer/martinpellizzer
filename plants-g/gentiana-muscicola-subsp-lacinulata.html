Gentiana muscicola subsp. lacinulata

Gentiana muscicola subsp.

lacinulata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety hair.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, fringed petals, which give it its common name.
