Gaussia princeps

Gaussia princeps, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a slight purple tinge. Gaussia princeps thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests and along stream banks.
