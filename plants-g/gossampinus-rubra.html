Gossampinus rubra

Gossampinus rubra, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, green flowers in early spring, followed by spiny husks containing one or two edible nuts. Its fruits are valued for their sweet, delicate flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
