Gnidia capitata var. pubescens

Gnidia capitata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-headed gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves is a distinguishing botanical feature.
