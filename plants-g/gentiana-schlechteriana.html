Gentiana schlechteriana

Gentiana schlechteriana, commonly known as the Schlechter's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana schlechteriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
