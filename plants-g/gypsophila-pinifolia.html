Gypsophila pinifolia

Gypsophila pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is relatively rare and requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
