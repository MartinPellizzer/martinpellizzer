Gentiana pterocalyx

Gentiana pterocalyx, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers are characterized by their striking blue or violet coloration and five petals.

Gentiana pterocalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
