Gymnopogon legrandii

Gymnopogon legrandii, commonly known as the legrand's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gymnopogon legrandii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
