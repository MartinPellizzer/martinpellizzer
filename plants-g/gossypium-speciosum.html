Gossypium speciosum

Gossypium speciosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf cotton or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like seeds enclosed in burs, which are adapted for wind dispersal. Gossypium speciosum is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

It has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
