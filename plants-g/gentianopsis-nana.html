Gentianopsis nana

Gentianopsis nana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a small herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
