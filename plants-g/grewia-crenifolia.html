Grewia crenifolia

Grewia crenifolia, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild mango, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow or orange fruits with a star-shaped calyx, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Grewia crenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
