Galium pennsylvanicum

Galium pennsylvanicum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and branched. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium pennsylvanicum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
