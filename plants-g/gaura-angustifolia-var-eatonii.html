Gaura angustifolia var. eatonii

Gaura angustifolia var. eatonii, commonly known as Eaton's gaura, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers resembling those of gaura species. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its delicate beauty and is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant, airy blooms.
