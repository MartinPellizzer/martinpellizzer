Gentiana prostrata var. acuminata

Gentiana prostrata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acute gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
