Goodyera morrisonicola

Goodyera morrisonicola, commonly known as the Morrison's pixie-plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, epiphytic herb that grows on the bark of trees, particularly in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a rosette of dark green, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with intricate patterns that resemble the texture of bark.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in terrariums or as a houseplant.
