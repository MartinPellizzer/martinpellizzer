Geophila herbacea var. violifolia

Geophila herbacea var. violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf geophila, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features slender, often hairy stems that produce small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
