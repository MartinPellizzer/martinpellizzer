Gypsophila patrinii var. caespitosa

Gypsophila patrinii var. caespitosa, commonly known as the cushion gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit, with dense clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pink or white flowers that resemble clouds or lace. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude flora.
