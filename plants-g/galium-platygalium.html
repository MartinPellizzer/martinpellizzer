Galium platygalium

Galium platygalium, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Galium platygalium is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, where it forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.
