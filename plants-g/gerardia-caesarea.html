Gerardia caesarea

Gerardia caesarea, commonly known as the red gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to dry, rocky soils and its role in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
