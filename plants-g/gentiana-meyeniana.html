Gentiana meyeniana

Gentiana meyeniana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana meyeniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
