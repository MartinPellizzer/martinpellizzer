Gunnera venezolana subsp. venezolana

Gunnera venezolana subsp.

venezolana, commonly known as the Venezuelan giant shield fern, is a striking member of the Gunneraceae family. It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in diameter.

Native to the Andean regions of Venezuela, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the axils of the leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
