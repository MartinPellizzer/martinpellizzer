Glinus ononoides

Glinus ononoides, commonly known as false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Glinus ononoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
