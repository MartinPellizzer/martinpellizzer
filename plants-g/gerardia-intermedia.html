Gerardia intermedia

Gerardia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gerardia intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.
