Gaillonia aucheri

Gaillonia aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Gaillonia aucheri is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
