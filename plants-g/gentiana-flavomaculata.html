Gentiana flavomaculata

Gentiana flavomaculata, commonly known as the yellow-spotted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with yellow spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
