Gibbaeum shandii

Gibbaeum shandii, commonly known as the sand mat, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-like growth habit with dense clusters of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically green with a bluish tinge and have a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
