Graptophyllum hortense var. viride

Graptophyllum hortense var. viride, commonly known as the green-flowered graptophyllum, is a variant of the graptophyllum species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a notable addition to both horticultural collections and botanical studies.
