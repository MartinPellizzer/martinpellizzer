Gentiana forwoodii

Gentiana forwoodii, commonly known as Forwood's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana forwoodii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
