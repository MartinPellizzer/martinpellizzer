Gahnia rigida

Gahnia rigida, commonly known as the rigid flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Gahnia rigida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
