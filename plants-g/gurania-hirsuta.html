Gurania hirsuta

Gurania hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gurania, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are often followed by small, oval-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds.
