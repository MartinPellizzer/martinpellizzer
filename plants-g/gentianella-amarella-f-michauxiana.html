Gentianella amarella f. michauxiana

Gentianella amarella f. michauxiana, commonly known as the Michaux's gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to the eastern United States, particularly in moist woodland habitats. It thrives in partial shade and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
