Guarea talamancana

Guarea talamancana, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. Guarea talamancana is often found in lowland tropical rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
