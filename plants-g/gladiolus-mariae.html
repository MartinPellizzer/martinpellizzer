Gladiolus mariae

Gladiolus mariae, commonly known as the Marie's gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, with hues ranging from white to deep red, and are often used in floral arrangements. Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in various climates.
