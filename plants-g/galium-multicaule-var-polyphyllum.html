Galium multicaule var. polyphyllum

Galium multicaule var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a hairy surface. Small white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters at the ends of the stems during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
