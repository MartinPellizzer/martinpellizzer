Galium capillipes

Galium capillipes, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Galium capillipes is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
