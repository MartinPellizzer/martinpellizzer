Gladiolus × byzantinus var. lutarius

Gladiolus × byzantinus var. lutarius, commonly known as the Byzantine lily, is a hybrid ornamental plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring stiff, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its botanical classification includes the genus Gladiolus, which is part of the Iridaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful blooms.

The varietal name 'lutarius' refers to its association with the region of Lutum, now part of modern-day Turkey.
