Gnidia sericea

Gnidia sericea, commonly known as the silky gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Gnidia sericea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
