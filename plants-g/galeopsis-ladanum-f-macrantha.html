Galeopsis ladanum f. macrantha

Galeopsis ladanum f. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered hedge nettle, is a variant of the hedge nettle species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological gardens and habitats.
