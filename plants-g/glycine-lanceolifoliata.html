Glycine lanceolifoliata

Glycine lanceolifoliata, commonly known as the lanceleaf soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various food products. Glycine lanceolifoliata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its growth habit includes climbing or sprawling vines, making it suitable for intercropping in agricultural systems.
