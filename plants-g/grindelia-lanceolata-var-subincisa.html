Grindelia lanceolata var. subincisa

Grindelia lanceolata var. subincisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf brittleblow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow-green composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its common name refers to the brittle, easily broken stems that break off easily.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
