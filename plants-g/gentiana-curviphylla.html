Gentiana curviphylla

Gentiana curviphylla, commonly known as the curved-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, often curled leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Gentiana curviphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
