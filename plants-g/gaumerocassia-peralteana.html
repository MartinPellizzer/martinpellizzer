Gaumerocassia peralteana

Gaumerocassia peralteana, commonly known as the Paltel tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common species in secondary forests. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

The Paltel tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility in its habitat.
