Gymnosiphon niveus

Gymnosiphon niveus, commonly known as the white gymnosiphon, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

This plant is characterized by its striking white flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often adorned with red bracts. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Gymnosiphon niveus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
