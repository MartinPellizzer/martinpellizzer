Geranium elamellatum

Geranium elamellatum, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features upright stems and produces clusters of bright pink to red flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically broad and palmately lobed, with a velvety texture.

Geranium elamellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
