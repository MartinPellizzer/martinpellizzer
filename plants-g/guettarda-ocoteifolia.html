Guettarda ocoteifolia

Guettarda ocoteifolia, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
