Gomphrena mendocina subsp. mendocina

Gomphrena mendocina subsp.

mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, narrow leaves and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
