Gypsophila tubulifiera

Gypsophila tubulifiera, commonly known as the desert candytuft, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, compact inflorescences, giving it a fluffy appearance.

Gypsophila tubulifiera is well adapted to harsh, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.
