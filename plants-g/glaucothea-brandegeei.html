Glaucothea brandegeei

Glaucothea brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's glaucous vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
