Grastidium papyraceum

Grastidium papyraceum, commonly known as the paperleaf strobila, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its thin, papery leaves that resemble parchment.

It is native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, spore-bearing capsules. Its leaves are not only visually striking but also play a role in water retention and photosynthesis.

Due to habitat loss, Grastidium papyraceum is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
