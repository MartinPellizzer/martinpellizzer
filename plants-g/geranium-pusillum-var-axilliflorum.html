Geranium pusillum var. axilliflorum

Geranium pusillum var. axilliflorum, commonly known as the creeping geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, rounded leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
