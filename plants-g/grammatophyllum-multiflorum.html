Grammatophyllum multiflorum

Grammatophyllum multiflorum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

This striking plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters along the inflorescence.

Grammatophyllum multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
