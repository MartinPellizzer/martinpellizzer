Gomphrena perennis f. simplex

Gomphrena perennis f. simplex, commonly known as the everlasting flower or "mum," is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to produce long-lasting, showy blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for borders and containers.

The flowers are known for their ability to retain their color and form even after being cut and dried.
