Gentiana aquatica var. bomiensis

Gentiana aquatica var. bomiensis, commonly known as the Bomie gentian, is a striking aquatic plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as streams and wetlands, where it forms dense, herbaceous growths. The plant is characterized by its bright blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for efficient water absorption.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity.
