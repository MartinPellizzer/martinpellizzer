Genista triacanthos subsp. vepres

Genista triacanthos subsp.

vepres, commonly known as the thorned broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves, consisting of three leaflets, and is adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
