Gymnocalycium volskyi

Gymnocalycium volskyi, commonly known as the Volsky's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
