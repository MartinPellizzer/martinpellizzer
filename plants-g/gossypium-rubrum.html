Gossypium rubrum

Gossypium rubrum, commonly known as the crimson cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. It produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that are harvested for textile use.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other cotton species due to its specific environmental requirements.
