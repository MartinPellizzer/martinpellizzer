Gentianella radicata

Gentianella radicata, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella radicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
