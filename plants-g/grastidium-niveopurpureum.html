Grastidium niveopurpureum

Grastidium niveopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.
