Geranium eriostemon var. hypoleucum

Geranium eriostemon var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-veined geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color. This variety is distinguished by its pale white or cream-colored veins on the leaves, which set it apart from other geranium species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
