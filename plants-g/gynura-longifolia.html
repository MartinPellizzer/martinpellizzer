Gynura longifolia

Gynura longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gynura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by papery, bract-like structures. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Gynura longifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
