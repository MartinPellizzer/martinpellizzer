Gnidia daphnoides

Gnidia daphnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
