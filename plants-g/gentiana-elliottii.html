Gentiana elliottii

Gentiana elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine zones. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing showy, tubular petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
