Goeppertia longibracteata

Goeppertia longibracteata, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, sharing similarities with ginger and turmeric.
