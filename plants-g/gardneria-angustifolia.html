Gardneria angustifolia

Gardneria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
