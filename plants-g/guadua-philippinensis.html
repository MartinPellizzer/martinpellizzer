Guadua philippinensis

Guadua philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its structural strength and rapid growth, it is widely used in construction and handicrafts in its native region.
