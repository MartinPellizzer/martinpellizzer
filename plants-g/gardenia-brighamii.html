Gardenia brighamii

Gardenia brighamii, commonly known as Brigham's gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is evergreen and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Gardenia brighamii is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its resistance to certain pests and diseases.
