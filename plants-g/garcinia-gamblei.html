Garcinia gamblei

Garcinia gamblei, commonly known as the Kampot pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, bitter aril. Garcinia gamblei is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
