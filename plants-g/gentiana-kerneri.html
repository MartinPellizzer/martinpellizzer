Gentiana kerneri

Gentiana kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

Gentiana kerneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
