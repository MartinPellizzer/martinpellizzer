Gymnocalycium erinaceum var. paucisquamosum

Gymnocalycium erinaceum var. paucisquamosum, commonly known as the spiny moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, covered in short, spiny areoles. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular choice for succulent gardens due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
