Gentiana bavarica var. subacaulis

Gentiana bavarica var. subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a short, compact stem with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller stature compared to the typical form of Gentiana bavarica.
