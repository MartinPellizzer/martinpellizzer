Genista sylvestris subsp. innocua

Genista sylvestris subsp.

innocua, commonly known as the slender broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes.

Its subspecies status reflects minor morphological differences from the main species, particularly in leaf structure and growth habit.
