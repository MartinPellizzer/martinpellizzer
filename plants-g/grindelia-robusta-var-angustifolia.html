Grindelia robusta var. angustifolia

Grindelia robusta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf brittlebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
