Gentiana chloodes

Gentiana chloodes, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
