Gardenia arborea

Gardenia arborea, commonly known as the evergreen gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its ornamental value is highly appreciated in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.

Gardenia arborea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
