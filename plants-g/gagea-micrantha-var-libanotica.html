Gagea micrantha var. libanotica

Gagea micrantha var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that are adapted for survival in rocky, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in alpine and woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
