Goodyera hachijoensis f. izuohsimensis

Goodyera hachijoensis f. izuohsimensis, commonly known as the Izu Islands moss orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and delicate, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded forest environments. It is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure, which are adapted to its specific ecological niche.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
