Gymnocalycium frankianum

Gymnocalycium frankianum, commonly known as the Frank's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spherical or cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring. It is a drought-tolerant species, adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Gymnocalycium frankianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
