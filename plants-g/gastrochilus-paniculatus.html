Gastrochilus paniculatus

Gastrochilus paniculatus, commonly known as the clustered root orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
