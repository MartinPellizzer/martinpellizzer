Grajalesia fasciculata

Grajalesia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
