Gravesia malvacea

Gravesia malvacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Gravesia malvacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
