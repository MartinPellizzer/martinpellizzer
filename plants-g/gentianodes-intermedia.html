Gentianodes intermedia

Gentianodes intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
