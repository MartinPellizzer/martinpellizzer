Goniodiscus elaeospermus

Goniodiscus elaeospermus, commonly known as the African water lily, is a aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

Goniodiscus elaeospermus plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
