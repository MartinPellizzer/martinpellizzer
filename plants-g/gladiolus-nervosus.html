Gladiolus nervosus

Gladiolus nervosus, commonly known as the nervy gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant floral displays.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions with suitable climates.
