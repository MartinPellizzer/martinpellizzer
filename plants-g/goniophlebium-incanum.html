Goniophlebium incanum

Goniophlebium incanum, commonly known as the white-stemmed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Goniophlebium incanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
