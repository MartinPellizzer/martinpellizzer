Galium palustre var. pubescens

Galium palustre var. pubescens, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, often wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

It features slender, ascending stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium palustre var. pubescens plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
