Gymnocalycium rauschii

Gymnocalycium rauschii, commonly known as the Rausch's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the growing season. Its leaves are typically absent, with spines replacing them for protection.

Gymnocalycium rauschii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
