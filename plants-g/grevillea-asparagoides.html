Grevillea asparagoides

Grevillea asparagoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Grevillea asparagoides is also an important source of nectar for native pollinators.
