Geraea canescens var. paniculata

Geraea canescens var. paniculata, commonly known as the paniculate geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
