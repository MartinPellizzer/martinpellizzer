Gypsophila paniculata var. paniculata

Gypsophila paniculata var. paniculata, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white or pink flower heads composed of numerous small, sterile flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its classification within the Caryophyllaceae family, also known as the carnation family.
