Gilia guttata

Gilia guttata, commonly known as the spotted gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers with white spots, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along stream banks. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Gilia guttata is an annual plant that blooms from spring to early summer, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
