Garcinia tetralata

Garcinia tetralata, commonly known as the four-angled Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, orange-red berries. Garcinia tetralata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fruiting habit.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is commonly found in lowland rainforests.
