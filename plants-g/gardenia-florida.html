Gardenia florida

Gardenia florida, commonly known as the frangipani, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, and its blooms can last for several weeks during the growing season.

The fruit produced by the plant is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.
