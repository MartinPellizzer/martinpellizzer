Galactia glaucescens f. oblongifolia

Galactia glaucescens f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, oblong leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or violet, which bloom in late spring. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.
