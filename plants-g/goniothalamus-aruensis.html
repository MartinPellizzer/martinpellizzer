Goniothalamus aruensis

Goniothalamus aruensis, commonly known as the Aru mountain satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indonesian islands of Aru and Seram.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction, though it is also threatened by habitat loss and overharvesting.
