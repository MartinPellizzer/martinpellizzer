Goodenia heterochila var. runcinata

Goodenia heterochila var. runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
