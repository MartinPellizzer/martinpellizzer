Guettarda grandiflora

Guettarda grandiflora, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Guettarda grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
