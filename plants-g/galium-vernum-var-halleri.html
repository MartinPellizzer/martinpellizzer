Galium vernum var. halleri

Galium vernum var. halleri, commonly known as spring cleavers, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its stems are often prostrate or creeping, allowing it to spread along the ground.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
