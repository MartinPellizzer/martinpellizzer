Grewia viscosa

Grewia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces a sticky sap that gives it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
