Gladiolus maculatus subsp. meridionalis

Gladiolus maculatus subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern spotted gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers typically bloom in summer, producing a striking display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
