Grastidium furcatopedicellatum

Grastidium furcatopedicellatum, commonly known as the forked pedicellate grass, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is notable for its specialized inflorescence, which features a forked pedicel supporting its spikelets. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with elongated, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the margins. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, during which it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Grastidium furcatopedicellatum is primarily found in moist, temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
