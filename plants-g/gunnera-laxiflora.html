Gunnera laxiflora

Gunnera laxiflora, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a dramatic visual impact in gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in water gardens or bog landscapes.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for creating a dramatic focal point in botanical gardens.
