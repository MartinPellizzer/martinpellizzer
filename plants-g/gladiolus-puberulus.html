Gladiolus puberulus

Gladiolus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
