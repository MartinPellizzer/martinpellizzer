Galium pumilum var. caespitosum

Galium pumilum var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically forms dense mats or cushions, with small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features include a prostrate growth habit and a distinctive, branched stem structure.
