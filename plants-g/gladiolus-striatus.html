Gladiolus striatus

Gladiolus striatus, commonly known as the streaked gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns.
