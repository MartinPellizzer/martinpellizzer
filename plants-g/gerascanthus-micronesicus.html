Gerascanthus micronesicus

Gerascanthus micronesicus, commonly known as the Micronesian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant features upright stems with opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
