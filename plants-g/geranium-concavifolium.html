Geranium concavifolium

Geranium concavifolium, commonly known as the concave-leaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive concave base, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
