Greeniopsis multiflora

Greeniopsis multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora greeniopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
