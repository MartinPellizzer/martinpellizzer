Grewia tembensis var. nematopus

Grewia tembensis var. nematopus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
