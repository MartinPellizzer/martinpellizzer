Gomphia turnerae

Gomphia turnerae, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
