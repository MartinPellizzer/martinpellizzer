Gentiana pseudogermanica

Gentiana pseudogermanica, commonly known as the false German gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
