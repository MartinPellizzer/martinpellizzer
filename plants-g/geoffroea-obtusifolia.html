Geoffroea obtusifolia

Geoffroea obtusifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically valued in musical instrument-making. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil in its native habitats.
