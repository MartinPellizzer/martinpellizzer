Galeola torana

Galeola torana, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Galeola, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks. Its flowers are small and often pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
