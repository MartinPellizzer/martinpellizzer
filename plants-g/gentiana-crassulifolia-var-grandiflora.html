Gentiana crassulifolia var. grandiflora

Gentiana crassulifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are succulent and thick, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
