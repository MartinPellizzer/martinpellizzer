Gynandropsis affinis

Gynandropsis affinis, commonly known as the African burweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are yellow and are surrounded by bracts that form a cup-like structure.

Gynandropsis affinis is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
