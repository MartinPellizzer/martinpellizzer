Garcinia tetangies

Garcinia tetangies, commonly known as the Philippine yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in xanthones, which are bioactive compounds with potential health benefits. Garcinia tetangies thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
