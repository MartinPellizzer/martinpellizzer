Gaylussacia densa var. bocainae

Gaylussacia densa var. bocainae, commonly known as the Bocainae highbush blueberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark purple berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants.
