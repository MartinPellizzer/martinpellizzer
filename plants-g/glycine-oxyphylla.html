Glycine oxyphylla

Glycine oxyphylla, commonly known as the silver wisteria, is a climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Glycine oxyphylla thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
