Glycosmis erythrocarpa

Glycosmis erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited mooli, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Glycosmis erythrocarpa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
