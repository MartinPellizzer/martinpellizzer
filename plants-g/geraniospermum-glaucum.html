Geraniospermum glaucum

Geraniospermum glaucum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
