Galeandra descagnolleana

Galeandra descagnolleana, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by specific species of bees and wasps.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
