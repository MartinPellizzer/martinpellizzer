Gentiana ternifolia

Gentiana ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are opposite and trifoliate, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gentiana ternifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
