Gardoquia microphylla

Gardoquia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved gardoquia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its small, white flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
