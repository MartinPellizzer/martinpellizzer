Gentiana bavarica subsp. rostanii

Gentiana bavarica subsp.

rostanii, commonly known as the Bavarian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Alpine regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
