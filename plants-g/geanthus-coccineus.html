Geanthus coccineus

Geanthus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy clusters of bright red flowers in early spring. Its foliage consists of small, fleshy, green leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

Geanthus coccineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
