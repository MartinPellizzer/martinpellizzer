Gentianella engadinensis

Gentianella engadinensis, commonly known as the Engadine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
