Gymnacanthus campestris

Gymnacanthus campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Gymnacanthus campestris has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
