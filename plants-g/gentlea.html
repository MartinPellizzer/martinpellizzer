Gentlea

Gentlea, commonly known as the "gentle plant," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically feature yellow centers and white or pale pink petals. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in temperate climates. Gentlea species are often found in moist, well-drained soils and are valued for their ornamental beauty.

They are also known for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
