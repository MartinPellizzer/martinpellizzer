Garcinia rhynchophylla

Garcinia rhynchophylla, commonly known as the yellow mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pericarp contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
