Geranium cuneatum var. polyphyllum

Geranium cuneatum var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
