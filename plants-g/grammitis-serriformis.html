Grammitis serriformis

Grammitis serriformis, commonly known as the serrated hawksbeard, is a fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow, serrated segments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in groups known as sori. Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
