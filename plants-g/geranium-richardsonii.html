Geranium richardsonii

Geranium richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Geranium richardsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
