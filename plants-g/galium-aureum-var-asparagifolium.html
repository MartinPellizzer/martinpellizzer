Galium aureum var. asparagifolium

Galium aureum var. asparagifolium, commonly known as golden wood-remedy, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or bronze tinge, especially when young. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
