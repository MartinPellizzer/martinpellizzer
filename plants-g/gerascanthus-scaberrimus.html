Gerascanthus scaberrimus

Gerascanthus scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
