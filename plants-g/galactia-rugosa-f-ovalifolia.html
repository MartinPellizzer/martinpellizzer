Galactia rugosa f. ovalifolia

Galactia rugosa f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf milkvetch, is a variant of the species Galactia rugosa, characterized by its ovate leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States, typically found in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its stems are usually sprawling or ascending, and the leaves are alternate with a rough texture.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
