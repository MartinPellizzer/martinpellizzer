Glyceria maxima f. scabra

Glyceria maxima f. scabra, commonly known as the rough glyceria, is a variant of the common glyceria, characterized by its rough, textured foliage.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in temperate regions. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments and providing habitat for various small organisms.
