Gabunia eglandulosa

Gabunia eglandulosa, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood, which is highly valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
