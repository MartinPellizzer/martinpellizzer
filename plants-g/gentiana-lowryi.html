Gentiana lowryi

Gentiana lowryi, commonly known as Lowry's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy blue corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
