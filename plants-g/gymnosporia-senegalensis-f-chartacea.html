Gymnosporia senegalensis f. chartacea

Gymnosporia senegalensis f. chartacea, commonly known as the Senegal sandthorn, is a shrub native to the Sahelian regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, linear leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for its hard, durable wood.
