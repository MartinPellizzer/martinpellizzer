Gardinia

Gardinia, commonly known as the gardenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is prized for its fragrant, white flowers. The plant typically has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Gardinia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
