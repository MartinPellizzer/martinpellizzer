Gigantochloa bastareana

Gigantochloa bastareana, commonly known as the Borneo giant bamboo, is a tall perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and strong structural properties make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
