Galium catalinense

Galium catalinense, commonly known as Catalina bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the island of Catalina in California.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium catalinense thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the unique climate of its native habitat.
