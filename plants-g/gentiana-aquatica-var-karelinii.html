Gentiana aquatica var. karelinii

Gentiana aquatica var. karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's water gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain streams and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
