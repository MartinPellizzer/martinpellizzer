Genista winkleri

Genista winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, shrub-like growth habit and is native to the southwestern United States. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by narrow, needle-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Genista winkleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
