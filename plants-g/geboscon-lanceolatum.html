Geboscon lanceolatum

Geboscon lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed geboscon, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical structure makes it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
