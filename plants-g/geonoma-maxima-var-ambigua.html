Geonoma maxima var. ambigua

Geonoma maxima var. ambigua, commonly known as the Ambiguous Giant Spider Fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, elongated fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with a robust rhizome and dichotomous branching, typical of many ferns. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to shaded, humid environments, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.
