Gastrolobium musaceum

Gastrolobium musaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
