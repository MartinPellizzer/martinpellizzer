Gastrochilus helferi

Gastrochilus helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate flowers with a distinctive toothed lip, which is a notable feature in its floral morphology. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Gastrochilus helferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
