Grammitis fragillima

Grammitis fragillima, commonly known as the fragile grammitis, is a delicate fern species belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, translucent scales. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in low light conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are usually found on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to its sporophytic life cycle.

Due to its fragile nature and specific habitat requirements, Grammitis fragillima is often found in relatively undisturbed, humid ecosystems.
