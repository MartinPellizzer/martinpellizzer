Geissorhiza scopulosa

Geissorhiza scopulosa, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid regions. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Geissorhiza scopulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
