Gentiana acaulis var. caulescens

Gentiana acaulis var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, unbranched stem and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
