Geranium retectum

Geranium retectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
