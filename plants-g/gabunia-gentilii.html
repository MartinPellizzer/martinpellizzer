Gabunia gentilii

Gabunia gentilii, commonly known as the African star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Gabunia gentilii typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
