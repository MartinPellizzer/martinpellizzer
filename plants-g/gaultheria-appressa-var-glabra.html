Gaultheria appressa var. glabra

Gaultheria appressa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. These berries are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
