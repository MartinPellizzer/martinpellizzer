Guapira combretiflora

Guapira combretiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils.

Guapira combretiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
