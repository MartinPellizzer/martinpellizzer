Gratiola aphylla

Gratiola aphylla, commonly known as the leafless yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its lack of leaves, with only a slender, yellowish stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
