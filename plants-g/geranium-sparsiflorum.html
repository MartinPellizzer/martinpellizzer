Geranium sparsiflorum

Geranium sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a silvery-green color. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing pink to red blooms that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
