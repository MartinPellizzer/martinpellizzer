Gilia chamissonis var. regina

Gilia chamissonis var. regina, commonly known as the California gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dry fruits known as capsules.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
