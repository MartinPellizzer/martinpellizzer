Grastidium iteratum

Grastidium iteratum, commonly known as the wandering grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. Grastidium iteratum is often found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a common sight in temperate regions.
