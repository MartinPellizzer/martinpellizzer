Ghinia tamonea

Ghinia tamonea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
