Gomphostemma salarkhanianum

Gomphostemma salarkhanianum, commonly known as the Persian ginseng, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its medicinal properties, with roots and leaves used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered endangered in its native range.
