Guzmania pungens

Guzmania pungens, commonly known as the spiked bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and pointed, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Guzmania pungens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
