Gentiana tergestina

Gentiana tergestina, commonly known as the Edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine meadows. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in dense, woolly hairs.

Gentiana tergestina is also known for its medicinal properties and is considered a symbol of resilience and beauty in alpine regions.
