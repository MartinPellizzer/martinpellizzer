Gentiana casapaltensis

Gentiana casapaltensis, commonly known as the Casapalts gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals and prominent blue veins.

Gentiana casapaltensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
