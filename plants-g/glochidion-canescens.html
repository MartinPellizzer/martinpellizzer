Glochidion canescens

Glochidion canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsules containing minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
