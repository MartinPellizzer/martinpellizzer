Gladiolus retrocurvus

Gladiolus retrocurvus, commonly known as the backward-curving gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where the florets curve backward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with three petals and three sepals that are often colored differently.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking visual appeal.
