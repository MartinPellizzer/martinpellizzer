Gerardia polyphylla

Gerardia polyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Gerardia polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardy nature.
