Guidonia

Guidonia, commonly known as the common senna, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, with Guidonia bucharica being one of the most well-known. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. They are characterized by their compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine or as a source of plant-based dyes.
