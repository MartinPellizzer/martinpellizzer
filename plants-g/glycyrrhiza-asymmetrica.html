Glycyrrhiza asymmetrica

Glycyrrhiza asymmetrica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf licorice, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Its roots are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its intense sweetness and medicinal properties.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
