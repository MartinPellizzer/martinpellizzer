Gutenbergia cordifolia var. pulchra

Gutenbergia cordifolia var. pulchra, commonly known as the heartleaf gutenbergia, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to bronze. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
