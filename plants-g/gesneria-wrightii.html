Gesneria wrightii

Gesneria wrightii, commonly known as Wright's gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with leaves that are glossy and elliptical in shape. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, bloom during the warmer months and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Gesneria wrightii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
