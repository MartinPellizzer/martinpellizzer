Gerardia purpurea var. racemulosa

Gerardia purpurea var. racemulosa, commonly known as the purple gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pink to purple blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural habitats.
