Gomphrena pulchella f. linearifolia

Gomphrena pulchella f. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its specific epithet "linearifolia.". The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
