Gentiana tetraphylla

Gentiana tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
