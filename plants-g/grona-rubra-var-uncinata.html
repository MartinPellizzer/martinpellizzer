Grona rubra var. uncinata

Grona rubra var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked grona, is a unique variant of the grona plant characterized by its distinctive hooked leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with the leaves featuring a serrated edge and a hooked tip that distinguishes it from other varieties. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species in botanical collections.
