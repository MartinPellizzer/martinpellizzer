Geranium cicutarium var. bipinnatum

Geranium cicutarium var. bipinnatum, commonly known as the bipinnate geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a finely divided, bipinnate structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
