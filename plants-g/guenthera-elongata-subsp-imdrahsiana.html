Guenthera elongata subsp. imdrahsiana

Guenthera elongata subsp.

imdrahsiana, commonly known as the Imdrahshan sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store moisture, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
