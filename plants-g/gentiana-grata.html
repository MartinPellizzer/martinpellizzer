Gentiana grata

Gentiana grata, commonly known as the sweet gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentiana grata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
