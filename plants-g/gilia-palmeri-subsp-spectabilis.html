Gilia palmeri subsp. spectabilis

Gilia palmeri subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the Palmer's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. It produces small, elliptical leaves and has a branching stem structure.

This subspecies is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is often found in desert regions like Arizona and Nevada.
