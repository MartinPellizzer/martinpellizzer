Genista sylvestris var. parcepilosa

Genista sylvestris var. parcepilosa, commonly known as the slender broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and rocky areas.

Its adaptability makes it a hardy and resilient species in Mediterranean climates.
