Geissorhiza silenoides

Geissorhiza silenoides, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of deep blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orchid-like blooms that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
