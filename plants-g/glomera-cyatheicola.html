Glomera cyatheicola

Glomera cyatheicola, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing its ability to absorb nutrients from the soil. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a valuable species in both natural ecosystems and horticultural settings.
