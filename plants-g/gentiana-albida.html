Gentiana albida

Gentiana albida, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and unbranched.

Gentiana albida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
