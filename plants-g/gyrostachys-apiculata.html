Gyrostachys apiculata

Gyrostachys apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant has a dense, woody structure that makes it highly drought-resistant. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Gyrostachys apiculata is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
