Geranium ovatifolium

Geranium ovatifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
