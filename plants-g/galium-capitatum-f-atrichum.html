Galium capitatum f. atrichum

Galium capitatum f. atrichum, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to ovate leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
