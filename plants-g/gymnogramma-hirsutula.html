Gymnogramma hirsutula

Gymnogramma hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy gymnogramma, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae that contribute to its ornamental value.

Gymnogramma hirsutula is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and shaded gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
