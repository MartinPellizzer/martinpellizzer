Gleditsia sinensis pendula-excelsa

Gleditsia sinensis pendula-excelsa, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, with small, oval leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in pendulous clusters, followed by flat, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
