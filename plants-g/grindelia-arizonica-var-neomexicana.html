Grindelia arizonica var. neomexicana

Grindelia arizonica var. neomexicana, commonly known as Arizona gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
