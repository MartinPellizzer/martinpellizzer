Glaucium vitellinum var. vitellinum

Glaucium vitellinum var. vitellinum, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features a stout, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
