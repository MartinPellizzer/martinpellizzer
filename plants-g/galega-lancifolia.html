Galega lancifolia

Galega lancifolia, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with lance-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Galega lancifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
