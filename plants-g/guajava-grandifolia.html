Guajava grandifolia

Guajava grandifolia, commonly known as the great-leaf guajava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem with rough, bark-like texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
