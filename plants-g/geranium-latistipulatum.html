Geranium latistipulatum

Geranium latistipulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features broad, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium latistipulatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
