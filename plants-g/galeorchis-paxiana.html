Galeorchis paxiana

Galeorchis paxiana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to thrive. Its unique morphology includes a short rhizome and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
