Grewia serrulata

Grewia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated grewia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, simple leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when ripe.

Grewia serrulata is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
