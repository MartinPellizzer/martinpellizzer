Galium setaceum subsp. decaisnei

Galium setaceum subsp.

decaisnei, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, and the leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often hairy.

It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
