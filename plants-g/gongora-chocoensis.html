Gongora chocoensis

Gongora chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocoan orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers often display intricate patterns and come in shades of yellow, red, and purple.

Gongora chocoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
