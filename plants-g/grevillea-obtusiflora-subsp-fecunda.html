Grevillea obtusiflora subsp. fecunda

Grevillea obtusiflora subsp.

fecunda, commonly known as the broad-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright red, cylindrical flowers that are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
