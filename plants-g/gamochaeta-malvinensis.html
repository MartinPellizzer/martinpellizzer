Gamochaeta malvinensis

Gamochaeta malvinensis, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern hemisphere, particularly found in South America and New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. Gamochaeta malvinensis is adapted to cool climates and is known for its resilience in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and subantarctic regions.
