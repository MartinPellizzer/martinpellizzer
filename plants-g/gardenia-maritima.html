Gardenia maritima

Gardenia maritima, commonly known as the coastal gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating.

Gardenia maritima is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy species for coastal gardens.
