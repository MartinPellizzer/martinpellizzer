Godetia amoena f. pygmaea

Godetia amoena f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a compact, annual flowering plant native to South America.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle purple or red hue. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces slender, winding stems that climb or trail. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
