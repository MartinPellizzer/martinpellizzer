Grastidium odontopus

Grastidium odontopus, commonly known as the toothed grass or toothed sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive toothed margins, which give it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on slender stems, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Grastidium odontopus plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
