Galium angustifolium subsp. gabrielense

Galium angustifolium subsp.

gabrielense, commonly known as Gabriel's narrowleaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America. It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib, and they are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. This subspecies is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
