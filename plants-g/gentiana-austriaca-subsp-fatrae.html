Gentiana austriaca subsp. fatrae

Gentiana austriaca subsp.

fatrae, commonly known as the Fatra gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and mountain pastures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine botanical gardens.
