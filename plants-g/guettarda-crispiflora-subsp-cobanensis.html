Guettarda crispiflora subsp. cobanensis

Guettarda crispiflora subsp.

cobanensis, commonly known as the Coban guettarda, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its evergreen, small to medium-sized shrub habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.

The species is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
