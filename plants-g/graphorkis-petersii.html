Graphorkis petersii

Graphorkis petersii, commonly known as the Peters' ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often appearing in shades of white and pale pink. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air moisture. Its unique morphology includes slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Graphorkis petersii is considered endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
