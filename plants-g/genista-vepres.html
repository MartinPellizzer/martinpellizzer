Genista vepres

Genista vepres, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista vepres is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.
