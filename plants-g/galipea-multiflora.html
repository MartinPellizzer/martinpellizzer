Galipea multiflora

Galipea multiflora, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a smooth, light-gray bark and produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its durable wood.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and camphor.
