Gymnema molle

Gymnema molle, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which contains various chemical compounds. Gymnema molle is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
