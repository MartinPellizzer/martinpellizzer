Guagnebina ignita

Guagnebina ignita, commonly known as the fiery flame vine, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like flowers that range from bright orange to deep red, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Guagnebina ignita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in botanical gardens and exotic plant collections.
