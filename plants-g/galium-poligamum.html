Galium poligamum

Galium poligamum, commonly known as silverweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly wavy edge.

Galium poligamum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
