Guatteria hirsuta

Guatteria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
