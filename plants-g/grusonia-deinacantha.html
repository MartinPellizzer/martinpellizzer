Grusonia deinacantha

Grusonia deinacantha, commonly known as the rock fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a hairy texture.

Grusonia deinacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
