Guyonia glandulosa

Guyonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular guyonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple, which attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
