Gaertnera quadriseta var. platypoda

Gaertnera quadriseta var. platypoda, commonly known as the flat-leafed gaertnera, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically flat and broad, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its high-altitude environment. This variant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in cooler climates.
