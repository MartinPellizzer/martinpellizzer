Grevillea lemanniana

Grevillea lemanniana, commonly known as the lemon-scented grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a distinctive lemon scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
