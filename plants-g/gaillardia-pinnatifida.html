Gaillardia pinnatifida

Gaillardia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers with yellow centers, which bloom from summer to fall. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are tightly arranged along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia pinnatifida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
