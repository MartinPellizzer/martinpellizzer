Grewia lilacina

Grewia lilacina, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Grewia lilacina is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
