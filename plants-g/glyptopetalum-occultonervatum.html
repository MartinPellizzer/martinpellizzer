Glyptopetalum occultonervatum

Glyptopetalum occultonervatum, commonly known as the ghost nerve plant, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that exhibit a subtle network of veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its unique leaf structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Glyptopetalum occultonervatum is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
