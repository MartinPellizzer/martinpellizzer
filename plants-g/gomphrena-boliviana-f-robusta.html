Gomphrena boliviana f. robusta

Gomphrena boliviana f. robusta, commonly known as the Bolivian plume flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of feathery, pink to red bracts that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is well-suited for garden cultivation due to its drought tolerance. Its foliage is narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements and garden displays.
