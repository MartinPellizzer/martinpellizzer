Gentiana germanica var. caucasica

Gentiana germanica var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
