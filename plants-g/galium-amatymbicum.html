Galium amatymbicum

Galium amatymbicum, commonly known as the white bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, elliptical berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color.

Galium amatymbicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
