Guarea crispa

Guarea crispa, commonly known as the red cedar or red guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Guarea crispa is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
