Gladiolus fissifolius

Gladiolus fissifolius, commonly known as the fissile-leaf gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often deeply fissured, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of colors. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
