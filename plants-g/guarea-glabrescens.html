Guarea glabrescens

Guarea glabrescens, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes trees known for their aromatic wood and medicinal properties. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves, and it produces woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

The bark and leaves of Guarea glabrescens have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
