Gymnosporia fischeri var. parviflora

Gymnosporia fischeri var. parviflora, commonly known as the Fischer's gymnosporia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny, woody stems. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
