Gynoxys cummingii

Gynoxys cummingii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features showy, orange-yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers, with prominent central disk florets surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gynoxys cummingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
