Geranium peloponesiacum var. libanoticum

Geranium peloponesiacum var. libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
