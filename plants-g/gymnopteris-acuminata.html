Gymnopteris acuminata

Gymnopteris acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds with a slender, wiry stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the undersides of the fronds.

Gymnopteris acuminata is native to parts of Asia and is often found in temperate to subtropical regions.
