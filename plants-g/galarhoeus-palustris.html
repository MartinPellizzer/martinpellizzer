Galarhoeus palustris

Galarhoeus palustris, commonly known as the marsh spirea, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galarhoeus palustris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
