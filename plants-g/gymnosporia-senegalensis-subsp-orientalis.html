Gymnosporia senegalensis subsp. orientalis

Gymnosporia senegalensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Sudanese burr, is a shrub native to the Sahelian regions of Africa. It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves, which are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in regions with low rainfall.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
