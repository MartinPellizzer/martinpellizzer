Gloxinia lindleyi

Gloxinia lindleyi, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tuberous-rooted plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family, known for its ornamental value. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the summer months. Its foliage is heart-shaped and covered in a velvety texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Gloxinia lindleyi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
