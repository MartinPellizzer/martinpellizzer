Gypsophila tadzhikistanica

Gypsophila tadzhikistanica, commonly known as the Tajikistan baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance.

Gypsophila tadzhikistanica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
