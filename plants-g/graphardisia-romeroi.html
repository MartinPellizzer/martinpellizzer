Graphardisia romeroi

Graphardisia romeroi, commonly known as the Romero's ghostflower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, bioluminescent flowers that glow faintly in the dark. The plant thrives in extreme conditions, requiring minimal water and high levels of sunlight. Its unique adaptations make it a subject of interest for botanists studying desert flora.

Due to habitat loss, Graphardisia romeroi is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
