Gentiana promethea

Gentiana promethea, commonly known as the Prometheus gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentiana promethea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
