Grevillea integrifolia var. eremophila

Grevillea integrifolia var. eremophila, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, reflecting its natural habitat along the coast.
