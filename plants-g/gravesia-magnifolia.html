Gravesia magnifolia

Gravesia magnifolia, commonly known as the fragrant grave's flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gravesia magnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aromatic properties.
