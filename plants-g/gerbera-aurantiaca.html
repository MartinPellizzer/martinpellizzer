Gerbera aurantiaca

Gerbera aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of orange and yellow. The plant features a robust stem with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gerbera aurantiaca is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
