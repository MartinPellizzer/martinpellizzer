Guazuma burbroma

Guazuma burbroma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red guazuma, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and bark contain various bioactive compounds used in traditional medicine.
