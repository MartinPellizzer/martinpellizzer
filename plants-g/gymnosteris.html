Gymnosteris

Gymnosteris, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and upright, sparsely branched stems. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. Gymnosteris species are typically found in moist, woodland habitats across temperate regions.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
