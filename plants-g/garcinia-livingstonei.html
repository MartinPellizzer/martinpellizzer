Garcinia livingstonei

Garcinia livingstonei, commonly known as the Livingston's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, making it botanically similar to other members of the Garcinia genus. Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
