Glandulifolia fragrans

Glandulifolia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant glandular leaf plant, is a species characterized by its aromatic foliage and distinctive glandular structures on its leaves.

The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with prominent oil glands that secrete fragrant compounds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Glandulifolia fragrans is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it often grows in shaded understorey environments.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
