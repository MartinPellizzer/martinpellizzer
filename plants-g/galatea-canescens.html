Galatea canescens

Galatea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Galatea canescens is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
