Gerardia serrata

Gerardia serrata, commonly known as the serrated gerardia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, often found in moist, temperate environments. The plant features upright stems with palmately divided leaves that have serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gerardia serrata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
