Greeneocharis circumscissa var. dichotoma

Greeneocharis circumscissa var. dichotoma, commonly known as the forked mountain mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
