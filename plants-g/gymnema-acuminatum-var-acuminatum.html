Gymnema acuminatum var. acuminatum

Gymnema acuminatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the Indian gymnema, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap and distinctive leaves with a serrated margin. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicine. It typically grows as a shrub or a small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a greenish or yellowish hue, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
