Genista affghanica

Genista affghanica, commonly known as Afghan broom, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has slender, wiry stems and small, compound leaves that help reduce water loss.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects in its native range.
