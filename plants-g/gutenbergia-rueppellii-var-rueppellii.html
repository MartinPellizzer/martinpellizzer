Gutenbergia rueppellii var. rueppellii

Gutenbergia rueppellii var. rueppellii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, hairy seeds. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and water-storing tissues.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
