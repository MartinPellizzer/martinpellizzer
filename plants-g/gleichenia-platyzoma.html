Gleichenia platyzoma

Gleichenia platyzoma, commonly known as the flat-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like fronds that are often divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are usually clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia platyzoma is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture for its delicate, fern-like foliage.
