Guajava striatula

Guajava striatula, commonly known as the streaked guajava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy texture.

Guajava striatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
