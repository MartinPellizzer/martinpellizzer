Gilia congesta var. palmifrons

Gilia congesta var. palmifrons, commonly known as the palmate gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves, which give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
