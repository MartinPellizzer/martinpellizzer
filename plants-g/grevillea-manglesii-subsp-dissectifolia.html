Grevillea manglesii subsp. dissectifolia

Grevillea manglesii subsp.

dissectifolia, commonly known as the dissect-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to western Australia. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
