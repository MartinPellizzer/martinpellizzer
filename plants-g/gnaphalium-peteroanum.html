Gnaphalium peteroanum

Gnaphalium peteroanum, commonly known as the New Zealand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

Gnaphalium peteroanum is often found in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
