Griselinia alata

Griselinia alata, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that features a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Griselinia alata is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its use in landscaping.
