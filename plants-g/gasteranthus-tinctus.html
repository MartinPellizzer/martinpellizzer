Gasteranthus tinctus

Gasteranthus tinctus, commonly known as the red velvet flower, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family native to Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vivid red, velvety petals that resemble a small, ornate flower. The plant produces a unique, fleshy, red calyx that encloses the flower, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and striking beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
