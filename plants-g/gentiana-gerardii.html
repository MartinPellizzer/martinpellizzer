Gentiana gerardii

Gentiana gerardii, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in shaded woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, with a rough, hairy texture.

The genus Gentiana is characterized by its showy, often blue or purple flowers, which are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.
