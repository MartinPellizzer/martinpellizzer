Gueldenstaedtia giraldii var. longiscapa

Gueldenstaedtia giraldii var. longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features slender, ascending stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated stems and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.
