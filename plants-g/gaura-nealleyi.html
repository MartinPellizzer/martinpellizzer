Gaura nealleyi

Gaura nealleyi, commonly known as Nealley's gaura, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Gaura nealleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
