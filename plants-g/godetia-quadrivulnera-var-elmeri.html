Godetia quadrivulnera var. elmeri

Godetia quadrivulnera var. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's godetia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant, ornamental plants.
