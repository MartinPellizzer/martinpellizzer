Gladiolus × incarnatus

Gladiolus × incarnatus, commonly known as the red gladiolus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range from deep red to purple, with a prominent central stripe. The plant is native to South Africa and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. Its sword-shaped leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its elegant appearance.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes and cut flower arrangements.
