Geodorum javanicum

Geodorum javanicum, commonly known as the Java torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, red-orange inflorescences that resemble torches, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint along the veins.

Geodorum javanicum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and aromatic foliage.
