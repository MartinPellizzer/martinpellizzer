Guizotia villosa

Guizotia villosa, commonly known as the hairy guizotia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves are alternate, ovate, and also covered in fine pubescence.

Guizotia villosa is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
