Genista pedunculata subsp. decumbens

Genista pedunculata subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the southwestern United States. It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family, which are followed by small, elongated pods. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval leaflets.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
