Gaylussacia crenata

Gaylussacia crenata, commonly known as the smooth alpenrose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and swamps, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features small, oval leaves with smooth edges and produces clusters of pinkish-white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
