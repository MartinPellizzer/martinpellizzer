Gaultheria memecyloides

Gaultheria memecyloides, commonly known as the red evergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Gaultheria memecyloides is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds used in perfumery and herbal remedies.
