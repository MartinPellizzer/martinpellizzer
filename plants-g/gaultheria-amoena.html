Gaultheria amoena

Gaultheria amoena, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry bush, is a flowering shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in taste to strawberries, though they are typically smaller and more tart. Gaultheria amoena thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
