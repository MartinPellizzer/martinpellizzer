Galeola kwangsiensis

Galeola kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi galeola, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and grows in shaded, moist environments. It is known for its unique floral structure, featuring a prominent labellum that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
