Geranium bockii

Geranium bockii, commonly known as the Bock's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Geranium bockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
