Grewia blattaefolia

Grewia blattaefolia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
