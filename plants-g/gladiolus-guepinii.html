Gladiolus guepinii

Gladiolus guepinii, commonly known as the Guepin's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features sword-shaped leaves and produces tall spikes of colorful, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a corm-like structure for storage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
