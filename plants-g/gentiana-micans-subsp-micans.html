Gentiana micans subsp. micans

Gentiana micans subsp.

micans, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
