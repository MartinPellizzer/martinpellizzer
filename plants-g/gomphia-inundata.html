Gomphia inundata

Gomphia inundata, commonly known as the water fern, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of silt or mud. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes to anchor itself in the substrate. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing spores rather than seeds.

Gomphia inundata plays an important role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
