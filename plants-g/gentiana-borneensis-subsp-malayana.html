Gentiana borneensis subsp. malayana

Gentiana borneensis subsp.

malayana, commonly known as the Bornean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo and the Malay Peninsula. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid and cool environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
