Galium nigricans

Galium nigricans, commonly known as black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Galium nigricans produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a bitter taste.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
