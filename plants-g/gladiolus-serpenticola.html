Gladiolus serpenticola

Gladiolus serpenticola, commonly known as the serpent's tongue gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern, with each bloom featuring a narrow, sword-like shape that gives the plant its common name. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique appearance and vibrant colors make it a standout in both cultivated and natural settings.
