Gentiana cinereifolia

Gentiana cinereifolia, commonly known as the gray gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, grayish or whitish pubescence.

Gentiana cinereifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
