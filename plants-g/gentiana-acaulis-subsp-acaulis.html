Gentiana acaulis subsp. acaulis

Gentiana acaulis subsp.

acaulis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
