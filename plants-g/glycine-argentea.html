Glycine argentea

Glycine argentea, commonly known as silver bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Glycine argentea thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
