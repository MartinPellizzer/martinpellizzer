Galium rubrum var. corsicum

Galium rubrum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers clustered in loose spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium rubrum var. corsicum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
