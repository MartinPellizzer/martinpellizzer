Genista pulchella subsp. villarsiana

Genista pulchella subsp.

villarsiana, commonly known as the violet broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Genista genus, which are known for their hardy and drought-tolerant nature.
