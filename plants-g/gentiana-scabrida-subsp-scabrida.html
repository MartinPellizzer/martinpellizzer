Gentiana scabrida subsp. scabrida

Gentiana scabrida subsp.

scabrida, commonly known as the rough gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
