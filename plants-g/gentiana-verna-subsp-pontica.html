Gentiana verna subsp. pontica

Gentiana verna subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a short, upright stem that bears a single, showy blue to violet flower. Its flowers are five-petaled and have a distinctive blue coloration, often with a white or pale blue throat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
