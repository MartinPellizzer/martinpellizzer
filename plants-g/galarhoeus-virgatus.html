Galarhoeus virgatus

Galarhoeus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features succulent, silver-gray leaves that are coated in a fine layer of white waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Galarhoeus virgatus is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and sandy soils, and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
