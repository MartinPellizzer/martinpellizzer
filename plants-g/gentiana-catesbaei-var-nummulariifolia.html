Gentiana catesbaei var. nummulariifolia

Gentiana catesbaei var. nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silver gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically oval to round in shape, and the flower clusters are often borne on short, leafy stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
