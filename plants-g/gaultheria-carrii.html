Gaultheria carrii

Gaultheria carrii, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark and elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. Gaultheria carrii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal forests.

It is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine for its analgesic properties.
