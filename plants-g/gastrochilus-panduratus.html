Gastrochilus panduratus

Gastrochilus panduratus, commonly known as the panduratus orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree bark.

The flowers bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
