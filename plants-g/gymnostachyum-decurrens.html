Gymnostachyum decurrens

Gymnostachyum decurrens, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling, low-growing habit with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Gymnostachyum decurrens is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
