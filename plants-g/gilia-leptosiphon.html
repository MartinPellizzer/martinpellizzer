Gilia leptosiphon

Gilia leptosiphon, commonly known as the slender-pipe gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid regions.

Gilia leptosiphon is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators such as bees and moths.
