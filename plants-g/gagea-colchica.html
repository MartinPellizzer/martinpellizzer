Gagea colchica

Gagea colchica, commonly known as the Caucasian spring beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are small and serve as storage organs, allowing it to survive harsh winters.

Gagea colchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
