Gentianella porphyrantha

Gentianella porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety hair.

Gentianella porphyrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
