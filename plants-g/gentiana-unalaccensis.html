Gentiana unalaccensis

Gentiana unalaccensis, commonly known as the Alaska gentian, is a flowering plant native to the subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as alpine meadows and tundra. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

Gentiana unalaccensis is adapted to cold climates and has a relatively short growing season.
