Gifola anatolica

Gifola anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Gifola anatolica is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
