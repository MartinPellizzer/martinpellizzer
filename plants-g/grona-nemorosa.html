Grona nemorosa

Grona nemorosa, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist woodland areas.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are slender and branching. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for naturalized gardens.

Grona nemorosa is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small woodland creatures.
