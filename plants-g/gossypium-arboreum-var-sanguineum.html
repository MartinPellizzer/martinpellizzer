Gossypium arboreum var. sanguineum

Gossypium arboreum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the crimson tree cotton, is a rare and ornamental variant of the cotton plant.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and woody stem, which distinguish it from other cotton species. Native to parts of India and Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its botanical features include hairy leaves and capsule fruits that contain fibrous seeds.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
