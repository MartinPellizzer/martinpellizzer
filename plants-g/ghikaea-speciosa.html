Ghikaea speciosa

Ghikaea speciosa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 feet tall, covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ghikaea speciosa is known for its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
