Grewia breviflora

Grewia breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf sugar bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, providing food for wildlife.

Grewia breviflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
