Gypsophila glomerata subsp. globulosa

Gypsophila glomerata subsp.

globulosa, commonly known as globular gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its delicate and long-lasting blooms.
