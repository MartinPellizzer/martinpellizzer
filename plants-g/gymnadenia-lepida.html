Gymnadenia lepida

Gymnadenia lepida, commonly known as the slender cowplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, woodlands, and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gymnadenia lepida is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in habitats with partial shade.
