Gymnocarpium altaycum

Gymnocarpium altaycum, commonly known as Altai nakedcarp, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are dry and dehiscent, releasing seeds through a splitting mechanism.

It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
