Gentianopsis stracheyi

Gentianopsis stracheyi, commonly known as Strachey's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Gentianopsis stracheyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant color and ecological significance.
