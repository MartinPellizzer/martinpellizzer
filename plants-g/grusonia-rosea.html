Grusonia rosea

Grusonia rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered grusonia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink or rose-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its botanical features include small, fragrant flowers with five petals and a hairy stem surface.

Grusonia rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical settings.
