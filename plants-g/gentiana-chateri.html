Gentiana chateri

Gentiana chateri, commonly known as the Chater's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana chateri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
