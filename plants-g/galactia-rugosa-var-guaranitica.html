Galactia rugosa var. guaranitica

Galactia rugosa var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive burr-like pods that enclose its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
