Gentiana ambigens

Gentiana ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or moist soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana ambigens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
