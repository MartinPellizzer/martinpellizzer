Gentiana nipponica

Gentiana nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a perennial herb native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Gentiana nipponica is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
