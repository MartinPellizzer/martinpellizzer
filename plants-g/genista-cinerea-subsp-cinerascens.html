Genista cinerea subsp. cinerascens

Genista cinerea subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silverbush broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and silvery-gray, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
