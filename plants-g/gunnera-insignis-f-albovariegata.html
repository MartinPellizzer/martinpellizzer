Gunnera insignis f. albovariegata

Gunnera insignis f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated giant curlleaf, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, with a distinctive curling edge and a variegated pattern of white and green. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by bright red bracts, adding dramatic visual interest. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental in gardens with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it a unique and eye-catching addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
