Gladiolus numidicus

Gladiolus numidicus, commonly known as the Algerian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North Africa.

It features sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
