Grevillea tridentifera

Grevillea tridentifera, commonly known as the Three-Toothed Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three narrow, pointed lobes. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea tridentifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife such as birds and insects.
