Gentianella pevalekii

Gentianella pevalekii, commonly known as the Pavelek's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Gentianella pevalekii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
