Geranium batangense

Geranium batangense, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium batangense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
