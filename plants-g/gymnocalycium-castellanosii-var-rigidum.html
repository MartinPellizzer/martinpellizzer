Gymnocalycium castellanosii var. rigidum

Gymnocalycium castellanosii var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid globose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are typically green in color. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry gardens and indoor settings.
