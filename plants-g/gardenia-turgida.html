Gardenia turgida

Gardenia turgida, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, waxy white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Gardenia turgida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
