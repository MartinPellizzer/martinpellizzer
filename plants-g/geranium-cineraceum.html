Geranium cineraceum

Geranium cineraceum, commonly known as the ash-gray geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry conditions.

Geranium cineraceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
