Grewia trichodes

Grewia trichodes, commonly known as the woolly grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Grewia trichodes thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.
