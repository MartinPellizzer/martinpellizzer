Gardenia aubryi

Gardenia aubryi, commonly known as the Madagascar gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia aubryi is also valued for its use in perfumery due to the aromatic compounds in its flowers.
