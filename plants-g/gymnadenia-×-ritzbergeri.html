Gymnadenia × ritzbergeri

Gymnadenia × ritzbergeri, commonly known as the Ritzberger's grass pinks, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a single spike of flowers, each with a distinct, spurred lip, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Gymnadenia × ritzbergeri is a short-lived perennial, often requiring specific conditions to thrive and bloom successfully.
