Galium lucidum var. guilhelmi

Galium lucidum var. guilhelmi, commonly known as the shining bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, prostrate stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in riparian habitats across its range.
