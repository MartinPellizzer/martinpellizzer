Gentianella pseudolycopodium

Gentianella pseudolycopodium, commonly known as the false lycopodium gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has a basal rosette of leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in meadows and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
