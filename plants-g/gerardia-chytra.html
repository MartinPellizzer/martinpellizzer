Gerardia chytra

Gerardia chytra, commonly known as the black alder, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, three-lobed fruits.

Gerardia chytra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
