Genista uniflora

Genista uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
