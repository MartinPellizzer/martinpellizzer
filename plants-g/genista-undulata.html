Genista undulata

Genista undulata, commonly known as the wavy broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
