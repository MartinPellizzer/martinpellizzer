Grevillea oligantha

Grevillea oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Grevillea oligantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
