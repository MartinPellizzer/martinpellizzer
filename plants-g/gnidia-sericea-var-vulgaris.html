Gnidia sericea var. vulgaris

Gnidia sericea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the silky gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
