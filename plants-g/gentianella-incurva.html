Gentianella incurva

Gentianella incurva, commonly known as the bent gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical and feature five petals, with the lower petal often curving downward, giving it the species name "incurva.".

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
