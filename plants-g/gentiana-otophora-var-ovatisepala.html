Gentiana otophora var. ovatisepala

Gentiana otophora var. ovatisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with prominent, ovate sepals that are often broader than the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
