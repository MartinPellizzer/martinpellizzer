Galactia speciosa f. argentina

Galactia speciosa f. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
