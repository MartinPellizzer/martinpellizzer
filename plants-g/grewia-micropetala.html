Grewia micropetala

Grewia micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf waxapple, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries resembling miniature apples. These berries are edible and have a tart, juicy flavor.

Grewia micropetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
