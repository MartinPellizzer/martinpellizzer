Garcinia lujae

Garcinia lujae, commonly known as the Lujah Garcinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red fruits, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, fleshy aril, which is the main part consumed in traditional practices.
