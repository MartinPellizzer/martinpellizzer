Geum sunhangii

Geum sunhangii, commonly known as the Korean avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Korea.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Geum sunhangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
