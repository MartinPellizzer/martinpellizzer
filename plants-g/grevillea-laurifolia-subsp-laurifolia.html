Grevillea laurifolia subsp. laurifolia

Grevillea laurifolia subsp.

laurifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking, red or orange-pink flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for landscaping in coastal regions.
