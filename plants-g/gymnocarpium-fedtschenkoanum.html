Gymnocarpium fedtschenkoanum

Gymnocarpium fedtschenkoanum, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's naked fruit, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five sepals and no petals, typical of the family. Its fruit is a naked achene, which is a dry, one-seeded fruit that does not split open.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to rocky, alpine environments.
