Gymnema subnudum

Gymnema subnudum, commonly known as the slender gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments. Gymnema subnudum is also recognized for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
