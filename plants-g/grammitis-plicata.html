Grammitis plicata

Grammitis plicata, commonly known as the pleated maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, pleated fronds that give it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically bioluminescent and have a delicate, feathery structure.

Grammitis plicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
