Genista scorpius var. acutangula

Genista scorpius var. acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and sharply pointed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
