Galium rigidum var. longipes

Galium rigidum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium rigidum var. longipes is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
