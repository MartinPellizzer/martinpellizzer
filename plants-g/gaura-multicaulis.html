Gaura multicaulis

Gaura multicaulis, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Gaura multicaulis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
