Gilia cotulifolia var. nigelliformis

Gilia cotulifolia var. nigelliformis, commonly known as the blackthorn gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
