Grindelia globularifolia

Grindelia globularifolia, commonly known as the globular milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, woolly inflorescence and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine, white hairs.

Grindelia globularifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
