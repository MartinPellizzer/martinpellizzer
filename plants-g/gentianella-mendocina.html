Gentianella mendocina

Gentianella mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
