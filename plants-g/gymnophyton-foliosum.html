Gymnophyton foliosum

Gymnophyton foliosum, commonly known as the leafy gymnosperm, is a unique plant species characterized by its foliage structure.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, which typically lacks true leaves, but this species exhibits a foliose structure resembling leaves. The plant features a stem with lateral branches that bear flat, broad foliage, giving it a fern-like appearance. Gymnophyton foliosum is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology, bridging characteristics of both gymnosperms and ferns.
