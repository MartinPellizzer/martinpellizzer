Grevillea lavandulacea

Grevillea lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavender-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that resemble lavender sprigs, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea lavandulacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
