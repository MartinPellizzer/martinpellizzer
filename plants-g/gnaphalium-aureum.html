Gnaphalium aureum

Gnaphalium aureum, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing herb with a mat-forming habit, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often covered with a fine layer of white wax.

Gnaphalium aureum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
