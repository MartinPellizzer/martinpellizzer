Geranium cuchillense

Geranium cuchillense, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in summer.

Geranium cuchillense is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
