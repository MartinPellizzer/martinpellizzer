Gunnera arenaria

Gunnera arenaria, commonly known as the seaside gunnera, is a striking perennial plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. Its growth is typically slow, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring or early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
