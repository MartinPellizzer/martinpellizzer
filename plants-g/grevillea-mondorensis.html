Grevillea mondorensis

Grevillea mondorensis, commonly known as the Mount Druitt grevillea, is a shrub native to New South Wales, Australia.

It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Grevillea mondorensis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
