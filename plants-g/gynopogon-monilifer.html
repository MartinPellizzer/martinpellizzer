Gynopogon monilifer

Gynopogon monilifer, commonly known as the chainfruit plant, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fleshy fruits that resemble tiny chains, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruiting bodies. The fruits are typically edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them a delicacy in some regions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
