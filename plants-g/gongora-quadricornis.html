Gongora quadricornis

Gongora quadricornis, commonly known as the four-horned orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful floral structures. The plant features four prominent, elongated petals that resemble horns, giving it its common name. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Gongora quadricornis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant blossoms.
