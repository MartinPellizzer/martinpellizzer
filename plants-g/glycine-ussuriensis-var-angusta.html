Glycine ussuriensis var. angusta

Glycine ussuriensis var. angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf climbing bean, is a species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial climbing legume characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
