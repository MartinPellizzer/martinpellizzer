Gilia inconspicua var. lacerata

Gilia inconspicua var. lacerata, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, textured surface. This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
