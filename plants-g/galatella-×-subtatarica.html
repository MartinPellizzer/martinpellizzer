Galatella × subtatarica

Galatella × subtatarica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is native to the Caucasus region and is often found in mountainous areas.

Galatella × subtatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
