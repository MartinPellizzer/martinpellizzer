Gentianusa punctata

Gentianusa punctata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with prominent yellow markings.

Gentianusa punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
