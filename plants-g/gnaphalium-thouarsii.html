Gnaphalium thouarsii

Gnaphalium thouarsii, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces copious amounts of fine, white to pale yellow pollen, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to rocky or dry soils.

Gnaphalium thouarsii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
