Gynandropsis grandiflora

Gynandropsis grandiflora, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gynandropsis grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
