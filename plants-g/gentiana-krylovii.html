Gentiana krylovii

Gentiana krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
