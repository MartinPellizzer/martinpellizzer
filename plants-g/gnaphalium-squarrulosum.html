Gnaphalium squarrulosum

Gnaphalium squarrulosum, commonly known as roughhead everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The flowers are small and yellowish, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.
