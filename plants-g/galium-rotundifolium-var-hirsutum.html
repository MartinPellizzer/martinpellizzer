Galium rotundifolium var. hirsutum

Galium rotundifolium var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in moist, temperate regions.
