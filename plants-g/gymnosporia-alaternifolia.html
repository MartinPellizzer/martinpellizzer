Gymnosporia alaternifolia

Gymnosporia alaternifolia, commonly known as the African mountain thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

Gymnosporia alaternifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
