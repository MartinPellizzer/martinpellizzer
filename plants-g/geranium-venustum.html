Geranium venustum

Geranium venustum, commonly known as the noble geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are typically hairy and bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Geranium venustum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
