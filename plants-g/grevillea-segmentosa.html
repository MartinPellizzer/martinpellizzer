Grevillea segmentosa

Grevillea segmentosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea segmentosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
