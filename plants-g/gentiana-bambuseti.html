Gentiana bambuseti

Gentiana bambuseti, commonly known as the bamboo gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat. Gentiana bambuseti thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in mountainous forests and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
