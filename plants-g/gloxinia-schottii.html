Gloxinia schottii

Gloxinia schottii, commonly known as the Schott's gloxinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink or red blossoms that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxinia schottii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
