Gypsophila antilibanotica

Gypsophila antilibanotica, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like inflorescences composed of numerous small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
