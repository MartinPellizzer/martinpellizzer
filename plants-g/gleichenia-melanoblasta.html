Gleichenia melanoblasta

Gleichenia melanoblasta, commonly known as the black-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Gleichenia melanoblasta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
