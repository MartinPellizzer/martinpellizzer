Galatea pauciflora

Galatea pauciflora, commonly known as the rare mountain hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Galatea pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
