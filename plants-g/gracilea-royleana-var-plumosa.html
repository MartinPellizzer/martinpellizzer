Gracilea royleana var. plumosa

Gracilea royleana var. plumosa, commonly known as the plumose gracilea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, feathery foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
