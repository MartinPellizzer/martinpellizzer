Gentiana engadinensis

Gentiana engadinensis, commonly known as the Engadine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
