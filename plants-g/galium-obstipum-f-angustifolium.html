Galium obstipum f. angustifolium

Galium obstipum f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, opposite leaves.

It typically features white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant of Galium obstipum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
