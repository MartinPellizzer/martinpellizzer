Guarea filiformis var. cinerascens

Guarea filiformis var. cinerascens, commonly known as the gray-leafed guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves that are typically a bluish-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is thick and rugged, often exuding a mild aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
