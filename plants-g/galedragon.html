Galedragon

Galedragon, commonly known as the dragon's claw, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, claw-like leaves and vibrant, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments. Its unique morphology has made it a subject of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.

Despite its rugged appearance, the galedragon is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
