Galium davuricum var. tokyoense

Galium davuricum var. tokyoense, commonly known as Tokyo bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
