Globba radicalis

Globba radicalis, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Globba radicalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
