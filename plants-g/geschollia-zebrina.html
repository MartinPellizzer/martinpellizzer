Geschollia zebrina

Geschollia zebrina, commonly known as the zebra plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, variegated foliage.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white and green stripes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Geschollia zebrina is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
