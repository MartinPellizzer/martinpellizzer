Gaura parviflora var. lachnocarpa

Gaura parviflora var. lachnocarpa, commonly known as the pink gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens due to its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
