Gentlea penduliflora

Gentlea penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging gentian, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique growth habit.
