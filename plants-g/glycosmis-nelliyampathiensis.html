Glycosmis nelliyampathiensis

Glycosmis nelliyampathiensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
