Gentianusa

Gentianusa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
