Glycyrrhiza mairei

Glycyrrhiza mairei, commonly known as the Maire licorice, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza mairei is valued for its roots, which contain glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
