Gatesia

Gatesia, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Gatesia multiflora, which is native to East Asia. These plants are evergreen shrubs characterized by their glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. They thrive in acidic, well-drained soils and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

Gatesia plants are relatively low-maintenance and suitable for USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
