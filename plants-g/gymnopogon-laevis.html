Gymnopogon laevis

Gymnopogon laevis, commonly known as the silky grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Gymnopogon laevis typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its adaptability and simple morphology make it a common weed in various agricultural and natural environments.
