Gyrocheilos retrotrichus var. oligolobus

Gyrocheilos retrotrichus var. oligolobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed gyrocheilos, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers with yellow central disc flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged in a basal rosette, and are covered with fine, short hairs. This variant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant landscaping plant.
