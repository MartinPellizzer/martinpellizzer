Gypsophila perfoliata subsp. ilerdensis

Gypsophila perfoliata subsp.

ilerdensis, commonly known as the Iberian gypsophila, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette. This species thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
