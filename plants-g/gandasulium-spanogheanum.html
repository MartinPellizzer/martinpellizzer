Gandasulium spanogheanum

Gandasulium spanogheanum, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a rare and elusive plant native to the dense, humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that emit a soft glow during the night, a unique adaptation believed to aid in pollination. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the rich, decomposing leaf litter. Its reproductive cycle involves the production of spores, which are dispersed by wind and water.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
