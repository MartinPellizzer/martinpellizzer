Galactia tenuiflora var. flaviflora

Galactia tenuiflora var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered galactia, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical pod that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
