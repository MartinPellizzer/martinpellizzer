Gomphrena flava

Gomphrena flava, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble small globes, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Gomphrena flava is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
