Gnaphalium filago

Gnaphalium filago, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny yellow to brown composite flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands.

Gnaphalium filago is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which releases a subtle, resinous scent when crushed.
