Gastrodia autumnalis

Gastrodia autumnalis, commonly known as the Chinese club moss or the orchid of the mountain, is a unique plant belonging to the family Gastrodiaceae.

It is a perennial herb that grows in the temperate regions of East Asia, particularly in China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, club-shaped rhizome and its lack of true leaves, instead having scale-like structures. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Gastrodia autumnalis is notable for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine, where it is valued for its purported health benefits.
