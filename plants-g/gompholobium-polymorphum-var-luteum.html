Gompholobium polymorphum var. luteum

Gompholobium polymorphum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and dry sclerophyllous forests.

This variety is notable for its vibrant color and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
