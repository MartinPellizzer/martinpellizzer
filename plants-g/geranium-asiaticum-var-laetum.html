Geranium asiaticum var. laetum

Geranium asiaticum var. laetum, commonly known as the Persian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice in horticulture.
