Grewia Microcos

Grewia microcos is a small, deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is known for its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces tiny, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a hardy choice for landscaping. Despite its modest appearance, Grewia microcos holds cultural and ecological significance in many communities.
