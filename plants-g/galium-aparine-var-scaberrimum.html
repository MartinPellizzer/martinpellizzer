Galium aparine var. scaberrimum

Galium aparine var. scaberrimum, commonly known as betony or sticky willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. This variant is often found in moist, disturbed soils in woodland edges and along stream banks.
