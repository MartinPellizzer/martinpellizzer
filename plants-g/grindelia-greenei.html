Grindelia greenei

Grindelia greenei, commonly known as the Greene's gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its resinous, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, seed-filled capsules.

Grindelia greenei is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
