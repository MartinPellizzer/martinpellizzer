Geum persicum

Geum persicum, commonly known as Persian avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually pink or red and have five petals. The plant produces oval, woody fruits called achenes, which contain the seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
