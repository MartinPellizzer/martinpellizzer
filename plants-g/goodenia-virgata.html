Goodenia virgata

Goodenia virgata, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters.

Goodenia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
