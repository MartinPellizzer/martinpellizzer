Garcinia vitiensis

Garcinia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
