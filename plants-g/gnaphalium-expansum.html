Gnaphalium expansum

Gnaphalium expansum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and slightly succulent, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
