Gymnocalycium multiflorum var. parisiense

Gymnocalycium multiflorum var. parisiense, commonly known as the Parisian moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant features a distinctive green or blue-green coloration with a white or pale pink bloom during the flowering season. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Gymnocalycium multiflorum var. parisiense is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its stems and requiring minimal care once established.
