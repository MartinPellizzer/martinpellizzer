Grevillea secunda

Grevillea secunda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

Grevillea secunda is also valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
