Galeopsis murriana

Galeopsis murriana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horse mint, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a minty scent when crushed.

Galeopsis murriana is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
