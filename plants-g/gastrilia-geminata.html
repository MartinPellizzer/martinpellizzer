Gastrilia geminata

Gastrilia geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf gastrilia, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Gastrilia geminata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
