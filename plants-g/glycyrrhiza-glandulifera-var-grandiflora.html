Glycyrrhiza glandulifera var. grandiflora

Glycyrrhiza glandulifera var. grandiflora, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, pinnately compound leaves, and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody roots that are rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its sweet taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal and commercial uses.

Glycyrrhiza glandulifera var. grandiflora is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.
