Grislea coccinea

Grislea coccinea, commonly known as the red grislea, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Grislea coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
