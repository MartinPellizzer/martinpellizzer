Goodyera longibracteata

Goodyera longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract maidenhair fern, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, evergreen foliage and intricate floral structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with prominent sepals and petals, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Goodyera longibracteata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
