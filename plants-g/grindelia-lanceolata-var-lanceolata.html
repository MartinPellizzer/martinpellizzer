Grindelia lanceolata var. lanceolata

Grindelia lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh, drought-prone areas.

The species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites.
