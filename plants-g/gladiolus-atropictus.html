Gladiolus atropictus

Gladiolus atropictus, commonly known as the black gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers, which contrast sharply with its bright green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, sturdy stems and dense clusters of tubular blooms. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three outer tepals and three inner ones that are often more narrow.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
