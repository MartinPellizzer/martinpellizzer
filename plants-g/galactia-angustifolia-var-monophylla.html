Galactia angustifolia var. monophylla

Galactia angustifolia var. monophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
