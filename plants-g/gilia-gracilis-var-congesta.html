Gilia gracilis var. congesta

Gilia gracilis var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in regions with mild, temperate climates.

This plant is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
