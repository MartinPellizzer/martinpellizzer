Galeopsis eversiana

Galeopsis eversiana, commonly known as field woundwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis eversiana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
