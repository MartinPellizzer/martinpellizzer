Gardenia longispina

Gardenia longispina, commonly known as the long-spined gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elliptical, yellowish-orange fruits that contain multiple seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
