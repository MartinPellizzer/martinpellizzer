Gonyclisia saxatilis

Gonyclisia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Gonyclisia saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
