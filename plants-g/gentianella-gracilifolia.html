Gentianella gracilifolia

Gentianella gracilifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of gentians known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its slender, upright stems bear small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella gracilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
