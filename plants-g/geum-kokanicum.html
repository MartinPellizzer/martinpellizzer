Geum kokanicum

Geum kokanicum, commonly known as the alpine avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often heart-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.

Geum kokanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
