Geranium incanum var. grandicalyculatum

Geranium incanum var. grandicalyculatum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
