Geranium collinum var. glandulosum

Geranium collinum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodland or along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
