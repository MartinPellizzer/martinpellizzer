Grindelia integrifolia f. dentata

Grindelia integrifolia f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed globe-mallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with distinct teeth, giving it its specific epithet "dentata.".

It produces yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
