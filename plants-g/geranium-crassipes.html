Geranium crassipes

Geranium crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Geranium crassipes is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
