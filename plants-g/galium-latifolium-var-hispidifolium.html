Galium latifolium var. hispidifolium

Galium latifolium var. hispidifolium, commonly known as the hairy woodypedia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, especially along the nodes. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and are covered with fine, glandular hairs. Small white flowers appear in late spring to early summer, clustered in umbels at the tips of the stems.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
