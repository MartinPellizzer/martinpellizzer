Grewia asiatica var. pierrei

Grewia asiatica var. pierrei, commonly known as the pink hairpin bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, oval leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
