Gomphocarpus humilis

Gomphocarpus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains cardiac glycosides, which are toxic to many animals. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a rough, hairy texture.
