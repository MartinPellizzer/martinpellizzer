Glyceria sphenopus

Glyceria sphenopus, commonly known as narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Glyceria sphenopus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
