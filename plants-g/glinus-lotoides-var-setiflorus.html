Glinus lotoides var. setiflorus

Glinus lotoides var. setiflorus, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
