Gentlea tacanensis

Gentlea tacanensis, commonly known as the Tacana gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
