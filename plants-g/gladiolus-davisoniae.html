Gladiolus davisoniae

Gladiolus davisoniae, commonly known as the Cape York gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm as its underground storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique floral structure.
