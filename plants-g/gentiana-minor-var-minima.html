Gentiana minor var. minima

Gentiana minor var. minima, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
