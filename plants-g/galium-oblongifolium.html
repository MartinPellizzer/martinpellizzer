Galium oblongifolium

Galium oblongifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, opposite leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences called cymes.

Galium oblongifolium is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.
