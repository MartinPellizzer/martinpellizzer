Garcinia harmandii

Garcinia harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's fruit, which is elliptical and yellowish-orange, contains a single seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp. Garcinia harmandii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
