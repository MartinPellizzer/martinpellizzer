Galium bicolor

Galium bicolor, commonly known as twinflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white or silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and it spreads through rhizomes, making it a resilient ground cover plant.
