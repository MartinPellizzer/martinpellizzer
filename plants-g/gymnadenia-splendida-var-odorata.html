Gymnadenia splendida var. odorata

Gymnadenia splendida var. odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a sweet, honey-like scent. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
