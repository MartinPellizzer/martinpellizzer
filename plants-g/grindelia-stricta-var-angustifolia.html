Grindelia stricta var. angustifolia

Grindelia stricta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its aromatic leaves and resilience to arid conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
