Gentiana veitchiorum var. caelestis

Gentiana veitchiorum var. caelestis, commonly known as the sky-blue gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to sky-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and dark green, often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
