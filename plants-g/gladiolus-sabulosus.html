Gladiolus sabulosus

Gladiolus sabulosus, commonly known as the sand gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
