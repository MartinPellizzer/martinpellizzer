Gymnocalycium sigelianum

Gymnocalycium sigelianum, commonly known as the Sigel's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines and a distinctive, showy flower that blooms in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Gymnocalycium sigelianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
