Gaillardia comosa

Gaillardia comosa, commonly known as the hairy gladiola, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with orange and red petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Gaillardia comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
