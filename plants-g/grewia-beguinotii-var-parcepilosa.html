Grewia beguinotii var. parcepilosa

Grewia beguinotii var. parcepilosa, commonly known as the narrow-bracted globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
