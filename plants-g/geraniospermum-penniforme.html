Geraniospermum penniforme

Geraniospermum penniforme, commonly known as the featherleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Geraniospermum penniforme is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering.
