Gossypium obtusifolium f. wattii

Gossypium obtusifolium f. wattii, commonly known as the wattii cotton plant, is a botanical variant of the cotton genus Gossypium.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and fluffy, white cotton bolls that develop from its flowering structures. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in warm, arid climates. Its fibrous seeds are harvested for cotton production, which is used in textile manufacturing.

This variant is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
