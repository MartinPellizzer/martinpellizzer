Gleichenia mucronata

Gleichenia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a unique feature of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Gleichenia mucronata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
