Gladiolus watsonioides

Gladiolus watsonioides, commonly known as the Watson's gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking appearance and hardiness.
