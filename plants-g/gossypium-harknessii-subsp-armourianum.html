Gossypium harknessii subsp. armourianum

Gossypium harknessii subsp.

armourianum, commonly known as the Australian cotton tree, is a perennial shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its woody stem and alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing fibrous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential agricultural applications.
