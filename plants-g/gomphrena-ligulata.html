Gomphrena ligulata

Gomphrena ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides, and it thrives in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the leaves arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gomphrena ligulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
