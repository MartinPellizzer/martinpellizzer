Gentiana scabra f. albiflora

Gentiana scabra f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a variant of the gentian species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers, which contrast sharply with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
