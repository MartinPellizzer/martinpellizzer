Gerbera peregrina

Gerbera peregrina, commonly known as the wandering gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces numerous small, pale green leaves.

Gerbera peregrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
