Gladiolus communis var. carneus

Gladiolus communis var. carneus, commonly known as the red gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous brightly colored flowers. The flowers typically range in color from deep red to pink and are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, thriving in temperate climates.

It is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden settings.
