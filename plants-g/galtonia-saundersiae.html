Galtonia saundersiae

Galtonia saundersiae, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Galtonia saundersiae is known for its long blooming period, often lasting several weeks in the spring.
