Geranium sylvaticum subsp. lemanianum

Geranium sylvaticum subsp.

lemanianum, commonly known as the Swiss geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated edge.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
