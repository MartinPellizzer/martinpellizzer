Galega spicata

Galega spicata, commonly known as the spiny goatweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features erect, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a strong, aromatic odor.

Galega spicata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.
