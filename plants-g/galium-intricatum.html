Galium intricatum

Galium intricatum, commonly known as the intricate bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium intricatum is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in naturalized areas.
