Gentiana springateana

Gentiana springateana, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers are tubular with a short, spurred petal at the base, which is a distinctive feature of the genus Gentiana.
