Gussonea deflexicalcarata

Gussonea deflexicalcarata, commonly known as the Southern Starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale purple during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
