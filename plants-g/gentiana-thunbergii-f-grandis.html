Gentiana thunbergii f. grandis

Gentiana thunbergii f. grandis, commonly known as the grand gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue flowers and medicinal properties.
