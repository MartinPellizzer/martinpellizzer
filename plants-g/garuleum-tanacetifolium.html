Garuleum tanacetifolium

Garuleum tanacetifolium, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, alpine, or arctic environments, often growing at high elevations.

Garuleum tanacetifolium is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
