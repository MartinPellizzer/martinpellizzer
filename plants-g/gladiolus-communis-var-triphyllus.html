Gladiolus communis var. triphyllus

Gladiolus communis var. triphyllus, commonly known as the triphyllus gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The flowers are typically arranged in a spike and consist of three petals and three sepals, which are often mistaken for leaves. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure to thrive.
