Grevillea pimeleoides

Grevillea pimeleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea pimeleoides is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in native landscaping.
