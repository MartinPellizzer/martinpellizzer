Gladiolus caryophyllaceus

Gladiolus caryophyllaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and striking, tall spikes of brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing showy flowers in shades of red, pink, yellow, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various climates where it thrives.
