Garcinia ovalifolia

Garcinia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in both traditional medicine and modern phytochemistry. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
