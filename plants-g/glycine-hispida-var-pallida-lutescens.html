Glycine hispida var. pallida-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. pallida-lutescens, commonly known as the pale-yellow hairy soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant characterized by its distinctive pale yellow to light green foliage and hairy stems.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in various culinary and industrial applications.

Its hairy texture and pale coloration make it unique among soybean varieties, often distinguishing it in botanical studies and agricultural practices.
