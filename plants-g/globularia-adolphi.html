Globularia adolphi

Globularia adolphi, commonly known as the golden globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, spherical shape, giving it its common name, and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Globularia adolphi is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
