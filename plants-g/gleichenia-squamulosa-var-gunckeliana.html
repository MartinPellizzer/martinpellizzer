Gleichenia squamulosa var. gunckeliana

Gleichenia squamulosa var. gunckeliana, commonly known as the Gunckel's Gleichenia, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into small, ovate segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
