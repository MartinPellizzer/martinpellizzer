Gypsophila wilhelminae

Gypsophila wilhelminae, commonly known as the Wilhelmine's gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs to reduce water loss.

Gypsophila wilhelminae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden designs for its ethereal appearance.
