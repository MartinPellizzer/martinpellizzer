Gussonia lanceolata

Gussonia lanceolata, commonly known as the New Zealand fireweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in damp, shaded environments.

Gussonia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
