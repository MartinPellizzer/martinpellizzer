Geranium erianthum f. alpestre

Geranium erianthum f. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a horticultural form of the common geranium.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features palmate leaves with serrated edges, which are often hairy and adapted to withstand cooler climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add color to garden landscapes in temperate regions.
