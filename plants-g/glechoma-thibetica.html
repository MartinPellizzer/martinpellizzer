Glechoma thibetica

Glechoma thibetica, commonly known as Tibetan ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in the summer months. Glechoma thibetica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
