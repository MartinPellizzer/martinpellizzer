Glaucium corniculatum var. rubrum

Glaucium corniculatum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-flowered glory flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue on the upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique color variation and historical use in traditional medicine.
