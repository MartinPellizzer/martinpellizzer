Gnaphalium minutula

Gnaphalium minutula, commonly known as the small leaf cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically features slender, hairy stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and are borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Gnaphalium minutula is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
