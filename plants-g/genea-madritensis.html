Genea madritensis

Genea madritensis, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is shrub-like, with multiple stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Genea madritensis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
