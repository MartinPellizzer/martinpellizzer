Guapira acuminata

Guapira acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with stipules and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Guapira acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
