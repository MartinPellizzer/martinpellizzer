Gentianella brevisepala

Gentianella brevisepala, commonly known as the short-winged gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually a striking blue or violet color.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
