Gladiolus iridifolius

Gladiolus iridifolius, commonly known as the blue-leaved gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are narrow and have a distinctive bluish-green hue, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for cut flowers.
