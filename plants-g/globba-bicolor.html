Globba bicolor

Globba bicolor, commonly known as the white-veined ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with white petals and yellow centers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Globba bicolor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
