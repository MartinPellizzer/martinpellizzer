Gynura procumbens var. annua

Gynura procumbens var. annua, commonly known as the creeping gynura, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
