Gynura colaniae

Gynura colaniae, commonly known as the silver-leaf gynura, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its broad, elliptical leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gynura colaniae is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects, contributing to ecological balance in garden environments.
