Gymnopteris semicordata

Gymnopteris semicordata, commonly known as the semi-cordate huperbolean, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, small fronds. The plant typically grows on shaded, moist rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrowly ovate with a distinctive semi-cordate base.

Gymnopteris semicordata is often found in montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance in botanical gardens.
