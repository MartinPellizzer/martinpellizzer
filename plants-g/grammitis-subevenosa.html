Grammitis subevenosa

Grammitis subevenosa, commonly known as the even-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique morphology with a creeping rhizome and closely spaced, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis subevenosa is notable for its intricate venation and is often used in horticultural displays due to its aesthetic appeal.
