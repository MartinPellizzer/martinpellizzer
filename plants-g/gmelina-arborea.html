Gmelina arborea

Gmelina arborea, commonly known as the white champak or Indian champak, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the monsoon season. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Gmelina arborea is valued for its timber, ornamental qualities, and traditional medicinal uses.
