Grastidium imbricatum

Grastidium imbricatum, commonly known as the overlapping leaf plant, is a unique species native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage that resembles a roof, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact, layered pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical interest lies in its unusual growth form and its ability to retain moisture, making it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens.
