Gnaphalium radians var. stachidifolium

Gnaphalium radians var. stachidifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
