Gentiana paradoxa

Gentiana paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and unbranched.

Gentiana paradoxa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
