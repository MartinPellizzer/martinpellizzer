Gypsophila afghanica

Gypsophila afghanica, commonly known as Afghan laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy blooms that resemble a cluster of small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small flowers in late summer.

Gypsophila afghanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
