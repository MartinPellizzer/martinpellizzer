Gnaphalium argyrosphaerum

Gnaphalium argyrosphaerum, commonly known as silverball groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white to pale yellow petals, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species adapted to dry environments.
