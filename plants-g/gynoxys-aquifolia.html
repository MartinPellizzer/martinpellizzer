Gynoxys aquifolia

Gynoxys aquifolia, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures include small, spore-producing organs called soruses, which are located on the underside of the leaves.

Gynoxys aquifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
