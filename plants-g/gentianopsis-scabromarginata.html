Gentianopsis scabromarginata

Gentianopsis scabromarginata, commonly known as the rough-margin gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
