Geissois pruinosa var. pruinosa

Geissois pruinosa var. pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf geissois, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine, powdery wax. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
