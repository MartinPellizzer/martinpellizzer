Gerascanthus goeldianus

Gerascanthus goeldianus, commonly known as the Goeldian geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant features upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in various regions for its aesthetic appeal.
