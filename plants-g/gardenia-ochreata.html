Gardenia ochreata

Gardenia ochreata, commonly known as the golden gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The flowers are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their striking appearance and pleasant aroma.
