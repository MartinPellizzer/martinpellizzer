Globulariopsis adpressa

Globulariopsis adpressa, commonly known as the flat globularia, is a low-growing succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage that forms a rosette shape, with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Globulariopsis adpressa is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
