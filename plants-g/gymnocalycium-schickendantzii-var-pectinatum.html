Gymnocalycium schickendantzii var. pectinatum

Gymnocalycium schickendantzii var. pectinatum, commonly known as the Pectinata Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
