Glyceria pseudodistans

Glyceria pseudodistans, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Glyceria pseudodistans plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
