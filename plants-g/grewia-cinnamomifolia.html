Grewia cinnamomifolia

Grewia cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often fragrant and have a distinct cinnamon-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
