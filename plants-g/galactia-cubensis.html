Galactia cubensis

Galactia cubensis, commonly known as Cuban milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, open habitats. Galactia cubensis is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical ecosystems.
