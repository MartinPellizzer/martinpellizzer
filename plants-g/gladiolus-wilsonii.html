Gladiolus wilsonii

Gladiolus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with three petals and three sepals that are often marked with distinctive patterns. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements.
