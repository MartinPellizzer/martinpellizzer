Gasoul aitonis

Gasoul aitonis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and slightly hairy.

Gasoul aitonis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
