Geranium linearilobum subsp. transversale

Geranium linearilobum subsp.

transversale, commonly known as the Transverse-leaved Geranium, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive transverse (across the width) lobing pattern.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
