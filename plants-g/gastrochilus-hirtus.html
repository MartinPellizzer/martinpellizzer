Gastrochilus hirtus

Gastrochilus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy stomach orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with numerous roots and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures.

The species is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
