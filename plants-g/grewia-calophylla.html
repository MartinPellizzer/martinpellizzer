Grewia calophylla

Grewia calophylla, commonly known as the yellow bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, red or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia calophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
