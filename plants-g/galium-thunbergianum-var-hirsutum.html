Galium thunbergianum var. hirsutum

Galium thunbergianum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streambanks.
