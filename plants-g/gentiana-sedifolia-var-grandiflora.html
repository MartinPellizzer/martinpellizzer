Gentiana sedifolia var. grandiflora

Gentiana sedifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a waxy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
