Geum macrophyllum

Geum macrophyllum, commonly known as the showy mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
