Genista microcephala

Genista microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
