Gypsophila arrostii subsp. nebulosa

Gypsophila arrostii subsp.

nebulosa, commonly known as the cloud-like baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, fluffy white or pink flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
