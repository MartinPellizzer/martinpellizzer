Gentianella crispata subsp. amblyphylla

Gentianella crispata subsp.

amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive crinkled or wavy margin, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
