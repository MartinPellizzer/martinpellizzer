Gymnachne koelerioides

Gymnachne koelerioides, commonly known as the Koeleria-like grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Gymnachne koelerioides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
