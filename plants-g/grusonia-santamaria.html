Grusonia santamaria

Grusonia santamaria, commonly known as the Santa Maria grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Grusonia santamaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
