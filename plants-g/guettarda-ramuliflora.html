Guettarda ramuliflora

Guettarda ramuliflora, commonly known as the Florida glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Guettarda ramuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.
