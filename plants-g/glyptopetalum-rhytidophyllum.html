Glyptopetalum rhytidophyllum

Glyptopetalum rhytidophyllum, commonly known as the wrinkled leaf orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically small, clustered, and have a delicate, fragrant appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a short flowering period, usually in late spring or early summer, and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
