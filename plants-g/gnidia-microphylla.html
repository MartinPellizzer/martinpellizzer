Gnidia microphylla

Gnidia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Gnidia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
