Glycine reducta

Glycine reducta, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Glycine reducta is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is often cultivated as a forage crop and has ornamental value in gardens.
