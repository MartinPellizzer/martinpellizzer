Gypsophila jaubertiana

Gypsophila jaubertiana, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance.

Gypsophila jaubertiana is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
