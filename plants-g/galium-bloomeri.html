Galium bloomeri

Galium bloomeri, commonly known as the bloomer wood-sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Galium bloomeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
