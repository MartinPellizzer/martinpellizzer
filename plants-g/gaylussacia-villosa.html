Gaylussacia villosa

Gaylussacia villosa, commonly known as the hairy winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its woody stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wildlife gardens and natural landscapes.
