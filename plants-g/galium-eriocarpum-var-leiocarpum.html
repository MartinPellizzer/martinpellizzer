Galium eriocarpum var. leiocarpum

Galium eriocarpum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as smooth bladderworts, is a variant of the bladderwort genus characterized by its small, herbaceous growth habit.

It typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other varieties in the species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender, erect stems. Its root system is adapted for aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, allowing it to thrive in wetlands and shallow water.

This variant is notable for its unique ability to capture small aquatic organisms using specialized bladder-like structures.
