Gentiana harwanensis

Gentiana harwanensis, commonly known as Harwan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence, while its flowers are tubular with five prominent lobes.

Gentiana harwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
