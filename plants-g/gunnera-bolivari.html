Gunnera bolivari

Gunnera bolivari, commonly known as the Andean giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Gunneraceae and is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, humid climates. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing structural support and water retention.

Gunnera bolivari is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
