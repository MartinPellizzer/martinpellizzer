Gagea popovii

Gagea popovii, commonly known as the Popov's spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gagea popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
