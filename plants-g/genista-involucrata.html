Genista involucrata

Genista involucrata, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
