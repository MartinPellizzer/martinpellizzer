Gastrochilus obliquus var. suavis

Gastrochilus obliquus var. suavis, commonly known as the sweet-scented gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

The orchid's unique aroma makes it a prized ornamental plant in horticulture.
