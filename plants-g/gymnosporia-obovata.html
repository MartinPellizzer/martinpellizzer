Gymnosporia obovata

Gymnosporia obovata, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures containing its seeds. Gymnosporia obovata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
