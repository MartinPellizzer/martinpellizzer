Gladiolus angustifolius

Gladiolus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear spikes of colorful, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and are often used in floral arrangements.

The plant is a member of the Iridaceae family and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
