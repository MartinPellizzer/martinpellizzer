Gladiolus pectinatus

Gladiolus pectinatus, commonly known as the woolly gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. Gladiolus pectinatus is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
