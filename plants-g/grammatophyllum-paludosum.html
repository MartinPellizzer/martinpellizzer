Grammatophyllum paludosum

Grammatophyllum paludosum, commonly known as the swamp orchid, is a species of orchid native to the wetlands of Florida and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in swampy or marshy habitats, often in areas with high humidity and poor drainage. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a complex structure typical of orchids. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the base of the plant.

Grammatophyllum paludosum is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
