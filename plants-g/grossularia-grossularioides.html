Grossularia grossularioides

Grossularia grossularioides, commonly known as the Himalayan currant, is a species of shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
