Gentiana longicarpa

Gentiana longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The fruit is a elongated capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
