Grewia andramparo

Grewia andramparo, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late summer to early autumn. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia andramparo is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
