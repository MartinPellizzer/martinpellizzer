Geranium pyrenaicum var. longepedicellatum

Geranium pyrenaicum var. longepedicellatum, commonly known as the long pedicel geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include showy inflorescences with elongated pedicels, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
