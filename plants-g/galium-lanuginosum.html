Galium lanuginosum

Galium lanuginosum, commonly known as downy wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium lanuginosum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
