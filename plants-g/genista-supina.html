Genista supina

Genista supina, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three narrow, pointed leaflets.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
