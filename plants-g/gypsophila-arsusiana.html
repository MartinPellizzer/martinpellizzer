Gypsophila arsusiana

Gypsophila arsusiana, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy inflorescences that resemble clouds, hence its common name.

Gypsophila arsusiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
