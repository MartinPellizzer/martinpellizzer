Genista congesta

Genista congesta, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
