Gentiana flexicaulis var. complexa

Gentiana flexicaulis var. complexa, commonly known as the Variable Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is noted for its variability in flower color and leaf morphology, contributing to its classification as a complexa variant.
