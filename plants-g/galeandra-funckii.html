Galeandra funckii

Galeandra funckii, commonly known as the Funck's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
