Galium sylvestre var. supinum

Galium sylvestre var. supinum, commonly known as creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
