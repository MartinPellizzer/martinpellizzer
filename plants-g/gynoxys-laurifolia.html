Gynoxys laurifolia

Gynoxys laurifolia, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Gynoxys laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
