Gentiana stellata var. acuminata

Gentiana stellata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acute-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip, giving it its specific epithet "acuminata.".

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
