Gymnocalycium marekiorum var. roboreanum

Gymnocalycium marekiorum var. roboreanum, commonly known as the Roborean prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and contains a sweet, fleshy pulp.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
