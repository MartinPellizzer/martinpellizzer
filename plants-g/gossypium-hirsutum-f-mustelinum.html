Gossypium hirsutum f. mustelinum

Gossypium hirsutum f. mustelinum, commonly known as the wild cotton plant, is a botanical variant of upland cotton.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and fibrous seeds, which are typical features of the Gossypium genus. This plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, arid climates. Its botanical structure includes alternate leaves with a serrated margin and small, fluffy flowers that develop into capsules containing seeds.

It is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
