Gluta aptera

Gluta aptera, commonly known as the wingless gluta, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often thorny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a rough texture.

Gluta aptera is often found in moist, lowland areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
