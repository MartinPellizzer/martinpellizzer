Grammitis membranifolia

Grammitis membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous grammitis, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to retain moisture.

Grammitis membranifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is popular in shady garden settings.
