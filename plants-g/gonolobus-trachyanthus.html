Gonolobus trachyanthus

Gonolobus trachyanthus, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Gonolobus trachyanthus is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
