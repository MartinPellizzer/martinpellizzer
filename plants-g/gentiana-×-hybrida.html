Gentiana × hybrida

Gentiana × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Gentiana × hybrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecosystem biodiversity.
