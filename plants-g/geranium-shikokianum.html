Geranium shikokianum

Geranium shikokianum, commonly known as the Shikoku geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright pink or red blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
