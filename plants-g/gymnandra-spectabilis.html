Gymnandra spectabilis

Gymnandra spectabilis, commonly known as the showy gymnandra, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, featuring a vibrant green color with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.

Gymnandra spectabilis is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
