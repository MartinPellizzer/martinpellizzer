Gilia glandulifera

Gilia glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Gilia glandulifera is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
