Geranium malvaceum

Geranium malvaceum, commonly known as the Malvaceae geranium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are often hairy.

Geranium malvaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
