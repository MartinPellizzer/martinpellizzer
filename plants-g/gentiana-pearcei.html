Gentiana pearcei

Gentiana pearcei, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana pearcei is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
