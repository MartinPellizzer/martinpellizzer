Grossheimia caroli-henrici

Grossheimia caroli-henrici, commonly known as the Carolinian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for woodland gardens.
