Gronophyllum apricum

Gronophyllum apricum, commonly known as the apricot gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
