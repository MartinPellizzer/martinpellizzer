Gymnocladus williamsii

Gymnocladus williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' locust, is a deciduous tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers are small and typically greenish-white, and it produces hard, woody seeds that are dispersed by wind.
