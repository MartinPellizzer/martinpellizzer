Galactia stenophylla f. albiflora

Galactia stenophylla f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered galactia, is a variant of the species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in sandy or loamy soils.

The white-flowered form is distinguished by its lighter floral coloration compared to the typical variety.
