Gynoxys cruzensis

Gynoxys cruzensis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features bright yellow to orange ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Gynoxys cruzensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
