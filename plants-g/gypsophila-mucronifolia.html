Gypsophila mucronifolia

Gypsophila mucronifolia, commonly known as the sharp-tipped gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its stems are slender and often branched, supporting clusters of small, showy blooms.

Gypsophila mucronifolia is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their ornamental value.
