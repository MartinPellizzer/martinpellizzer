Gongylolepis erioclada

Gongylolepis erioclada, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dark reddish-brown wood. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is native to parts of Africa and is valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and furniture.
