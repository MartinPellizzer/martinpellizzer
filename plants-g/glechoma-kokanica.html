Glechoma kokanica

Glechoma kokanica, commonly known as the Balkan ground-ivy, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly along the ground. Its small, opposite leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorls, and bloom in late spring to early summer.
