Gossypium hirsutum f. mexicanum

Gossypium hirsutum f. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican cotton plant, is a botanical variant of upland cotton.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and fibrous boll structure, which are typical of the species Gossypium hirsutum. This form is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its cotton fibers, which are used in textile production. The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in agricultural and genetic research.
