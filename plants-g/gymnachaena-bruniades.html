Gymnachaena bruniades

Gymnachaena bruniades, commonly known as the Brunia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Gymnachaena bruniades is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and withstand drought conditions.
