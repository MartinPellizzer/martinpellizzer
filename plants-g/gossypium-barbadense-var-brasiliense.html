Gossypium barbadense var. brasiliense

Gossypium barbadense var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample sunlight. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces fluffy bolls containing the cotton fibers.

This variety is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and fiber quality.
