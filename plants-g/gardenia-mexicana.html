Gardenia mexicana

Gardenia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gardenia mexicana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
