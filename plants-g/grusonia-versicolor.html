Grusonia versicolor

Grusonia versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that can change hues depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Grusonia versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
