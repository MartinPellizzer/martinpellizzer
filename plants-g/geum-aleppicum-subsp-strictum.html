Geum aleppicum subsp. strictum

Geum aleppicum subsp.

strictum, commonly known as the Alpine avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a hairy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
