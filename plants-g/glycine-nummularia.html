Glycine nummularia

Glycine nummularia, commonly known as the dollar vine, is a low-growing perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green, with a distinctive rounded appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Glycine nummularia is often used in ground covers due to its dense growth habit and ornamental appeal.
