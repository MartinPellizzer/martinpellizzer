Gentiana speciosa

Gentiana speciosa, commonly known as the showy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Gentiana speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
