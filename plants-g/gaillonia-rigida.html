Gaillonia rigida

Gaillonia rigida, commonly known as the rigid gallionia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gaillonia rigida is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and requires minimal water once established.
