Gymnadenia odoratissima var. borealis

Gymnadenia odoratissima var. borealis, commonly known as the northern fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in open woodlands or meadows. It produces small, oval-shaped berries that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
