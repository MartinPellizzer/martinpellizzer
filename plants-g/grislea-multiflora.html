Grislea multiflora

Grislea multiflora, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, which give it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Grislea multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types in warm climates.
