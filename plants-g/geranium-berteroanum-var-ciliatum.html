Geranium berteroanum var. ciliatum

Geranium berteroanum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the silky geranium, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and often have ciliate margins, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
