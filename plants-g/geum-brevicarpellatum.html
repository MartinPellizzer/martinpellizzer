Geum brevicarpellatum

Geum brevicarpellatum, commonly known as the short-carpellate avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens, which are characteristic of the Rosaceae family.

Geum brevicarpellatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
