Gossypium barbadense f. eggersii

Gossypium barbadense f. eggersii, commonly known as the Eggersii cotton variety, is a botanical variant of the upland cotton species.

It is characterized by its fine, long staple fibers that are highly valued in textile production. This variety is native to the Caribbean and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the cotton fibers, which are harvested for commercial use.

Its genetic traits make it a subject of interest in agricultural research for improving cotton yield and quality.
