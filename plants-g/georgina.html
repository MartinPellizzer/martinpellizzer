Georgina

Georgina, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a tuberous root vegetable native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, edible roots that come in various colors such as orange, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces flowering vines that bear small, pale purple flowers. Its roots are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and vitamins, making it a nutritious food source.

Georgina thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, requiring full sun for optimal growth.
