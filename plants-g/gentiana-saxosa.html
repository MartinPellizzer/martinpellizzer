Gentiana saxosa

Gentiana saxosa, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana saxosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
