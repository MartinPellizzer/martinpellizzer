Gossypium religiosum

Gossypium religiosum, commonly known as the sacred cotton or holy cotton, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers that are harvested for their soft, fibrous material. These fibers are used in traditional textiles and have cultural significance in various regions.

Gossypium religiosum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
