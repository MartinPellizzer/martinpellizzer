Garrya lindheimeri

Garrya lindheimeri, commonly known as the Lindheimer holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn bright red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Garrya lindheimeri is also valued for its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
