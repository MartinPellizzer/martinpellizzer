Galium libanoticum

Galium libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Galium libanoticum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
