Geranium robertianum var. rubrum

Geranium robertianum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in gardens across temperate regions.
