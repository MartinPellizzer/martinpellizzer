Gentianella cyananthiflora

Gentianella cyananthiflora, commonly known as the blue-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals, often with a prominent central tube.

Gentianella cyananthiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
