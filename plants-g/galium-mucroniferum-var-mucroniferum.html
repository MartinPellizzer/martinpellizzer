Galium mucroniferum var. mucroniferum

Galium mucroniferum var. mucroniferum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to western North America.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
