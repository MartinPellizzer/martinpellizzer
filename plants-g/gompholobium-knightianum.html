Gompholobium knightianum

Gompholobium knightianum, commonly known as the golden wattle of the Swan River, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Gompholobium knightianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
