Gentiana pannonica

Gentiana pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian gentian, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are tubular with prominent blue or violet petals, and it blooms from June to August.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
