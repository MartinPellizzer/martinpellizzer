Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. brachypetalum

Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. brachypetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically spiny and modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
