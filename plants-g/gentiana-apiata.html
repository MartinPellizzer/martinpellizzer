Gentiana apiata

Gentiana apiata, commonly known as the New Zealand gentian, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana apiata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
