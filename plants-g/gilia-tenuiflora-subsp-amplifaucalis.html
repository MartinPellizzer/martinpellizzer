Gilia tenuiflora subsp. amplifaucalis

Gilia tenuiflora subsp.

amplifaucalis, commonly known as the broad-faced gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive, broad-based throat. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of well-drained, sandy soils.
