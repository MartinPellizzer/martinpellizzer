Geissorhiza grandis

Geissorhiza grandis, commonly known as the giant goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a bulbous underground stem called a corm, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Geissorhiza grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
