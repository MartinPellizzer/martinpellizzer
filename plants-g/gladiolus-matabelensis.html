Gladiolus matabelensis

Gladiolus matabelensis, commonly known as the Matabel mountain gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves, and it produces spikes of flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
