Gastridium ventricosum

Gastridium ventricosum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that enclose the seeds. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gastridium ventricosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
