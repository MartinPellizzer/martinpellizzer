Gigantochloa verticillata

Gigantochloa verticillata, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it a valuable plant for erosion control and ornamental use.
