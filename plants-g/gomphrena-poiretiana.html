Gomphrena poiretiana

Gomphrena poiretiana, commonly known as the red globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of bright red or pink bracts that resemble small globes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by these colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena poiretiana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
