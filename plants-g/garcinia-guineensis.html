Garcinia guineensis

Garcinia guineensis, commonly known as African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pericarp contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
