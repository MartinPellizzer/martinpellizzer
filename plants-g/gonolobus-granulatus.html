Gonolobus granulatus

Gonolobus granulatus, commonly known as the rough globular vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, often woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are spherical, green berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Gonolobus granulatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
