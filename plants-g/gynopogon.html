Gynopogon

Gynopogon, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its annual or perennial herbaceous growth habit and features showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Gynopogon species are native to various regions in the Americas and are adapted to a range of soil types and environmental conditions.

They are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
