Grusonia × kelvinensis

Grusonia × kelvinensis, commonly known as the Kelvin's grusonia, is a hybrid plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its environment. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
