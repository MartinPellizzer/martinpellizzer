Gentiana takushii

Gentiana takushii, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Gentiana takushii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological importance.
