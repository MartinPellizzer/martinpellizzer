Gastrolobium spinosum var. trilobum

Gastrolobium spinosum var. trilobum, commonly known as the Three-lobed Hakea, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in spikes, which bloom during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in coastal and heathland habitats.
