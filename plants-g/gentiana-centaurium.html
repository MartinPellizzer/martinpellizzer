Gentiana centaurium

Gentiana centaurium, commonly known as the centaury gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are tubular with five petals, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
