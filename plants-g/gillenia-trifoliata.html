Gillenia trifoliata

Gillenia trifoliata, commonly known as swamp buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
