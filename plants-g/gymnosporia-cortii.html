Gymnosporia cortii

Gymnosporia cortii, commonly known as the Mexican spurge, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically followed by small, fleshy fruits. Gymnosporia cortii is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
