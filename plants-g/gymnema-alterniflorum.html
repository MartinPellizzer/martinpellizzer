Gymnema alterniflorum

Gymnema alterniflorum, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which are usually heart-shaped and have a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, ridged fruits. Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties, including hypoglycemic effects.

Gymnema alterniflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported ability to aid in diabetes management.
