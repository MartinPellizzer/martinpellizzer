Gnaphalium indutum

Gnaphalium indutum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
