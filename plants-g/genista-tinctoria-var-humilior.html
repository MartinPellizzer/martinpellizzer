Genista tinctoria var. humilior

Genista tinctoria var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in dye production, it is appreciated in both horticulture and botanical studies.
