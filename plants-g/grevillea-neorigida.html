Grevillea neorigida

Grevillea neorigida, commonly known as the red-stemmed Grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are usually grey-green in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in cylindrical clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea neorigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
