Gelrebia merxmuellerana

Gelrebia merxmuellerana, commonly known as the Merxmueller's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, often with translucent petals and a pale, almost ethereal coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark without deriving nutrients from its host. Its botanical features include slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves that help it conserve moisture in its humid habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Gelrebia merxmuellerana is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
