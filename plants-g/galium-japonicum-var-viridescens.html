Galium japonicum var. viridescens

Galium japonicum var. viridescens, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape with smooth margins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
