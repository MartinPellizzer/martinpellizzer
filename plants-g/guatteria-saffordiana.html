Guatteria saffordiana

Guatteria saffordiana, commonly known as the Safford's guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and a fragrant aroma. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Guatteria saffordiana is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
