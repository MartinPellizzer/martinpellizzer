Gymnosporia laurifolia

Gymnosporia laurifolia, commonly known as the African holly, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Gymnosporia laurifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
