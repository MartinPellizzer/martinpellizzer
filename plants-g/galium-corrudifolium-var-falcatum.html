Galium corrudifolium var. falcatum

Galium corrudifolium var. falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, with a distinctive narrow, elongated form. Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
