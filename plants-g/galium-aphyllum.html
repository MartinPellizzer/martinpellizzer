Galium aphyllum

Galium aphyllum, commonly known as pale-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, often hairless stems that are typically prostrate or ascending. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Galium aphyllum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.
