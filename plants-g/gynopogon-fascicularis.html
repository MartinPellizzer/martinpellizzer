Gynopogon fascicularis

Gynopogon fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences that bear pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often in temperate regions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Gynopogon fascicularis is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing food for various herbivorous insects.
