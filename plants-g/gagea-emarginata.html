Gagea emarginata

Gagea emarginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Gagea emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
