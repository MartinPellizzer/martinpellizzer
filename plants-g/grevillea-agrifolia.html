Grevillea agrifolia

Grevillea agrifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea agrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
