Gynura calciphila var. dissecta

Gynura calciphila var. dissecta, commonly known as the limestone spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often deeply lobed or dissected, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
