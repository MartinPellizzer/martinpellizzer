Gonolobus stipitatus

Gonolobus stipitatus, commonly known as the clustered leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
