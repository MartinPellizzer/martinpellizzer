Grielum grandiflorum

Grielum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered grielum, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Grielum grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
