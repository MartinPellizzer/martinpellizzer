Grewia meyeniana

Grewia meyeniana, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia meyeniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
