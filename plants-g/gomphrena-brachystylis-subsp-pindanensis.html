Gomphrena brachystylis subsp. pindanensis

Gomphrena brachystylis subsp.

pindanensis, commonly known as the Pindan everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, long-lasting pink to red bracts that resemble small flowers.

The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered within the showy bracts.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
