Gonocaryum griffithianum

Gonocaryum griffithianum, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent, particularly in Nepal and northern India. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
