Gastrochilus crassilabris

Gastrochilus crassilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped stomach orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique, spurred lip that is often brightly colored. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Gastrochilus crassilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its specific ecological requirements.
