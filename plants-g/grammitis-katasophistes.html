Grammitis katasophistes

Grammitis katasophistes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

The reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.
