Gissonia

Gissonia, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Gissonia thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
