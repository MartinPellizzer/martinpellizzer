Gentiana caespitosa

Gentiana caespitosa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana caespitosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.
