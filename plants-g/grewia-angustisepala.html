Grewia angustisepala

Grewia angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Grewia angustisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
