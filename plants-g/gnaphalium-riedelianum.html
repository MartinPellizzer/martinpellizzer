Gnaphalium riedelianum

Gnaphalium riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by fine, hairy leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
