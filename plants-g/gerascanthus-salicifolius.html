Gerascanthus salicifolius

Gerascanthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, serrated leaves that resemble those of willow trees. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
