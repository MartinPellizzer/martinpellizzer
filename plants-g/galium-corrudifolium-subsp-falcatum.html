Galium corrudifolium subsp. falcatum

Galium corrudifolium subsp.

falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
