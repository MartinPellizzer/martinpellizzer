Galium aureum var. oblongifolium

Galium aureum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as golden wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Galium aureum var. oblongifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
