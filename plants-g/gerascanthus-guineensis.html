Gerascanthus guineensis

Gerascanthus guineensis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gerascanthus guineensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
