Glycyrrhiza iconica

Glycyrrhiza iconica, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its deep taproot and pairs of opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into long, slender pods containing seeds. Its roots are the primary part used medicinally and commercially, known for their sweet taste and various therapeutic properties.

Glycyrrhiza iconica thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its economic and pharmacological value.
