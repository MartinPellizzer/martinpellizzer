Gladiolus trichonemifolius

Gladiolus trichonemifolius, commonly known as the woolly-leaf gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is often described as being covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its unique appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
