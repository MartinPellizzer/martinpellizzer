Gravesia calliantha

Gravesia calliantha, commonly known as the golden glory bower, is a tropical shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly the greater honeyguide, making it an important species in ecological studies.
