Gentianella mesembrianthemoides

Gentianella mesembrianthemoides, commonly known as the Cape gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
