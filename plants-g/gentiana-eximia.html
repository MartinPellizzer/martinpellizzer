Gentiana eximia

Gentiana eximia, commonly known as the fairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Gentiana eximia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
