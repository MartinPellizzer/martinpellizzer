Gypsophila picta

Gypsophila picta, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fluffy white or pink flowers that resemble miniature clouds. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Gypsophila picta is a perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
