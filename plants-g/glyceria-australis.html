Glyceria australis

Glyceria australis, commonly known as the Australian glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Glyceria australis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
