Gomphrena decumbens subvar. parvifolia

Gomphrena decumbens subvar. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble flower heads. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by the showy bracts. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

Gomphrena decumbens subvar. parvifolia prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
