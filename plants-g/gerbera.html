Gerbera

Gerbera, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. The flowers typically feature a central disk surrounded by brightly colored ray florets, often in shades of red, yellow, orange, and white. Gerbera species are popular in horticulture for their vibrant colors and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.

They are also valued for their use in ornamental gardening and as cut flowers.
