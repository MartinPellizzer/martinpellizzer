Galiziola

Galiziola, commonly known as the Galician thyme, is a small shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features tiny, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Galiziola is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
