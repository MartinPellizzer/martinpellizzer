Glochidion coronulatum

Glochidion coronulatum, commonly known as the crownleaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

Glochidion coronulatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
