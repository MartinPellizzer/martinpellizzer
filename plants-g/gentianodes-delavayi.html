Gentianodes delavayi

Gentianodes delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southwest China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
