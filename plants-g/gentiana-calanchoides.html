Gentiana calanchoides

Gentiana calanchoides, commonly known as the Calanchoid Gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
