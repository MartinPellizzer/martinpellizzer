Geigeria aspalathoides subsp. aspalathoides

Geigeria aspalathoides subsp.

aspalathoides, commonly known as the small-flowered geigeria, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months.

The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
