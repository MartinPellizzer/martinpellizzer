Gazania speciosa

Gazania speciosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gazania speciosa is known for its ability to tolerate drought and heat, which contributes to its widespread cultivation in arid regions.
