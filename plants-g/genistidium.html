Genistidium

Genistidium, commonly known as the false acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Genistidium species are often found in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to thrive in arid environments.

These plants play a role in soil stabilization and provide nectar for pollinators.
