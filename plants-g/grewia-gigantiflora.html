Grewia gigantiflora

Grewia gigantiflora, commonly known as the giant-flowered grewia, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a small, drupaceous berry that ripens to a red or orange color, providing food for various wildlife species.
