Galanthus bulgaricus

Galanthus bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate white flowers with a yellow-green spathe. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Galanthus bulgaricus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
