Gentianella divisa

Gentianella divisa, commonly known as the divided gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentianella divisa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
