Glyphochloa divergens var. divergens

Glyphochloa divergens var. divergens, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable environments.
