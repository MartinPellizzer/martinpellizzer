Gentianella peruviana var. boliviana

Gentianella peruviana var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are tubular and often have five petals, with a prominent blue corolla that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
