Gouldia forbesii

Gouldia forbesii, commonly known as the red-flowered gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and vibrant red or pink inflorescences. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance once established.

Gouldia forbesii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
