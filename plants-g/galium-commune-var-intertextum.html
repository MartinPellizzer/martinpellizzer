Galium commune var. intertextum

Galium commune var. intertextum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
