Gentiana verna subsp. oschtenica

Gentiana verna subsp.

oschtenica, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
