Gomphrena lanuginosa

Gomphrena lanuginosa, commonly known as the fuzzy globemallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spherical, showy flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its flowers are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

Gomphrena lanuginosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
