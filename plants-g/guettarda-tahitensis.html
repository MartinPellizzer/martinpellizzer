Guettarda tahitensis

Guettarda tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in humid tropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Guettarda tahitensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical regions.
