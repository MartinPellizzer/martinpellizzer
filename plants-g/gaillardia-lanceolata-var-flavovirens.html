Gaillardia lanceolata var. flavovirens

Gaillardia lanceolata var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woods and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant orange and red composite flowers. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterflies.
