Gmelina sessilis

Gmelina sessilis, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Gmelina sessilis is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is often used in construction and furniture-making.
