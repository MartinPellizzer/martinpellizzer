Grevillea latrobei var. scabrifolia

Grevillea latrobei var. scabrifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, rough, and often hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
