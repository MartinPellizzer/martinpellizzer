Gossypium klotzschianum subsp. raimondii

Gossypium klotzschianum subsp.

raimondii, commonly known as the Raimondii cotton, is a wild cotton species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its fibrous, fluffy seed hairs that are used for textile production.

This subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its potential in breeding programs to improve cultivated cotton varieties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and well-drained soils.

The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves.
