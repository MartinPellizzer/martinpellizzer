Gastrochilus inconspicuus

Gastrochilus inconspicuus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has a short rhizome and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often clustered at the base of the plant.

Gastrochilus inconspicuus is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its habitat.
