Garcinia robsoniana

Garcinia robsoniana, commonly known as the Robson's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces bright red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research. Its ornamental value is also notable, with its vibrant foliage and striking fruit making it a desirable plant for horticultural purposes.

Garcinia robsoniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
