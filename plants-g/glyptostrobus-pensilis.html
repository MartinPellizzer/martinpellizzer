Glyptostrobus pensilis

Glyptostrobus pensilis, commonly known as the Chinese swamp cypress, is a rare and ancient conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
