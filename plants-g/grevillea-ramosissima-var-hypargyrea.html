Grevillea ramosissima var. hypargyrea

Grevillea ramosissima var. hypargyrea, commonly known as the silver-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its bright red or orange flowers. The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences that are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its year-round interest, from its foliage to its seasonal blooms.
