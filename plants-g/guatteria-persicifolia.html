Guatteria persicifolia

Guatteria persicifolia, commonly known as the roseleaf guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy, elliptical leaves that resemble rose leaves, hence its common name. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
