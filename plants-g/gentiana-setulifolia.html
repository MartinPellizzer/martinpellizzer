Gentiana setulifolia

Gentiana setulifolia, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it the common name "hairy gentian.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
