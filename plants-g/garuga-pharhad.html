Garuga pharhad

Garuga pharhad, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tall, slender tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered, sweet-smelling flowers. The plant produces edible, fibrous fruits that are widely consumed and used in traditional medicine. Its trunk is fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

Garuga pharhad thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
