Gerbera maxima

Gerbera maxima, commonly known as the giant gerbera, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are typically brightly colored, with shades of red, orange, yellow, and white, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gerbera maxima thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, preferring warm, dry climates.
