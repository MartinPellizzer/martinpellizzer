Guadua spinosa

Guadua spinosa, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, particularly in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its hollow stems are strong and durable, making it a valuable resource for construction and crafts.

Guadua spinosa also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
