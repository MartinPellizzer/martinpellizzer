Guzmania pallida

Guzmania pallida, commonly known as the pale guzmania, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central pool to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from white to pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its foliage is typically variegated with shades of green and silver, enhancing its visual interest.

Guzmania pallida thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its health and appearance.
