Grevillea angulata

Grevillea angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, linear leaves that are often recurved. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Grevillea angulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
