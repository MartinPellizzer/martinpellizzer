Guzmania caricifolia

Guzmania caricifolia, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble a pineapple, often with a red or orange hue at the tips. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports small aquatic life. Its flowering stem produces a tall inflorescence with showy, spathaceous flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Guzmania caricifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
