Grewia fibrocarpa

Grewia fibrocarpa, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature.

Grewia fibrocarpa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
