Gonzalagunia incanescens

Gonzalagunia incanescens, commonly known as the white-flowered rock cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in a whitish, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
