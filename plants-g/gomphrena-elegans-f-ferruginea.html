Gomphrena elegans f. ferruginea

Gomphrena elegans f. ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant features upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is noted for its long blooming period, adding color to gardens from late spring through early autumn.
