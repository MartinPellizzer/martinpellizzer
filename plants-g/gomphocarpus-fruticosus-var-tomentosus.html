Gomphocarpus fruticosus var. tomentosus

Gomphocarpus fruticosus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant belongs to the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is known for its milky sap, which contains latex. Its flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and butterflies, and it serves as a host plant for the larvae of some Lepidoptera species.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
