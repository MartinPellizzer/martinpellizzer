Goodenia watsonii subsp. glandulosa

Goodenia watsonii subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers.

The subspecies glandulosa is distinguished by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique appearance. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
