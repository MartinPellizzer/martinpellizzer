Grastidium polyphyllum

Grastidium polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved grass tree, is a unique plant native to the wet tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense, fan-shaped clusters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Grastidium polyphyllum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various insects and small animals.
