Gentianella procera

Gentianella procera, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are tubular with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blue blossoms.
