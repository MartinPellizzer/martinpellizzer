Glyceria drummondii

Glyceria drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that release light, airy seeds for wind dispersal.

Glyceria drummondii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
