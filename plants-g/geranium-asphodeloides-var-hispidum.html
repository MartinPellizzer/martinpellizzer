Geranium asphodeloides var. hispidum

Geranium asphodeloides var. hispidum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
