Glyceria orientalis

Glyceria orientalis, commonly known as oriental glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface.

Glyceria orientalis is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic wildlife.
