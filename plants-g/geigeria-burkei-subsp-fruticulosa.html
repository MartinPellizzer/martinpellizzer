Geigeria burkei subsp. fruticulosa

Geigeria burkei subsp.

fruticulosa, commonly known as the African rock rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
