Gentianopsis barbata var. alboflavida

Gentianopsis barbata var. alboflavida, commonly known as the white-flowered gentianopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a striking contrast with their white petals and dark centers.
