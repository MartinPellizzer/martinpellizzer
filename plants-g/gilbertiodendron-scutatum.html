Gilbertiodendron scutatum

Gilbertiodendron scutatum, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain flat, hard seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and useful properties, it is sometimes cultivated for timber and ornamental purposes.
