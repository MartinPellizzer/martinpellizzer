Griffinia dryades

Griffinia dryades, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a distinctive cup-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Griffinia dryades thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
