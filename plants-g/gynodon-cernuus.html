Gynodon cernuus

Gynodon cernuus, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and slender, hanging inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found in temperate regions. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are inconspicuous, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Gynodon cernuus is an important species in ecological studies due to its role in wetland ecosystems.
