Genista microcephala var. capitellata

Genista microcephala var. capitellata, commonly known as the small-headed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
