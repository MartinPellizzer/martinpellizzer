Glaucium corniculatum var. arabicum

Glaucium corniculatum var. arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
