Gabertia ellisii

Gabertia ellisii, commonly known as the Ellis's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and adapted for pollination by birds or insects. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional South American herbal medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to habitat loss, Gabertia ellisii is considered a rare and endangered species.
