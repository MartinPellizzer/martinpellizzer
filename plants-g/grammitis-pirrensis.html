Grammitis pirrensis

Grammitis pirrensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on trees or rocks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, linear leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis pirrensis is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
