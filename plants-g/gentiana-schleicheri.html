Gentiana schleicheri

Gentiana schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana schleicheri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
