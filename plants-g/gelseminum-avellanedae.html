Gelseminum avellanedae

Gelseminum avellanedae, commonly known as the yellow trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a heart-shaped or ovate outline.

Gelseminum avellanedae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
