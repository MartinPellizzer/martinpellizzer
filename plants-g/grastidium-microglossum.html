Grastidium microglossum

Grastidium microglossum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass-tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive blue-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
