Gypsophila elymaitica

Gypsophila elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gypsophila elymaitica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
