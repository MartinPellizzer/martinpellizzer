Guatteria villosissima var. longipedunculata

Guatteria villosissima var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate guatteria, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its hairy foliage and elongated peduncles that bear clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a velvety texture.

The plant is often found in shaded understory environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
