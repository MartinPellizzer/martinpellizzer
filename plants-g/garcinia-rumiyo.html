Garcinia rumiyo

Garcinia rumiyo, commonly known as the Malayan garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including xanthones and other bioactive compounds.

Garcinia rumiyo is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
