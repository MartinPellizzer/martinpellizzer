Garcinia beniensis

Garcinia beniensis, commonly known as the Benin yellow fruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit has a thick, aromatic rind and a juicy, acidic pulp that contains several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.
