Glycosmis trifoliata var. fuscescens

Glycosmis trifoliata var. fuscescens, commonly known as the black-fruited gooseberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly serrated. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible, dark purple to black berries.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
