Gongora rutkowskiana

Gongora rutkowskiana, commonly known as the Rutkowskiana orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, spurred lip. The plant produces a single, showy bloom that typically appears during the rainy season. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the humid environment.

Gongora rutkowskiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
