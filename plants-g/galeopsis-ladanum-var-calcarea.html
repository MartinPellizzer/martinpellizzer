Galeopsis ladanum var. calcarea

Galeopsis ladanum var. calcarea, commonly known as lesser hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

This species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
