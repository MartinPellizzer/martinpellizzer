Glottiphyllum latifolium

Glottiphyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf glottiphyllum, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, broad leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments rather than in the wild.
