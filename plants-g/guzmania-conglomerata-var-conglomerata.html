Guzmania conglomerata var. conglomerata

Guzmania conglomerata var. conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered guzmania, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can last for several months. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small invertebrates. Its striking red or orange flowers emerge from the center of the leaf rosette, making it a popular ornamental plant in indoor gardening.

Guzmania conglomerata var. conglomerata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting mix.
