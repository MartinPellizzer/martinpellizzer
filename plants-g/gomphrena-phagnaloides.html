Gomphrena phagnaloides

Gomphrena phagnaloides, commonly known as the pink gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of pink or purple bract-like flowers that resemble small, fluffy balls. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
