Geniosporum menthoides var. prostratum

Geniosporum menthoides var. prostratum, commonly known as prostrate geniosporum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a mint-like aroma when crushed. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
