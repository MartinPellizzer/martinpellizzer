Gnaphalium concolorum

Gnaphalium concolorum, commonly known as the common everlasting, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Gnaphalium concolorum is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
