Guidonia parvifolia

Guidonia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved guidonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring season, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
