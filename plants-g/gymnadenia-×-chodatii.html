Gymnadenia × chodatii

Gymnadenia × chodatii, commonly known as the hybrid green gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows across Europe. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent spur, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in elongated pods.

Gymnadenia × chodatii is a naturally occurring hybrid between Gymnadenia corydalacea and Gymnadenia elatior.
