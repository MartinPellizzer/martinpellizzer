Galeopsis ladanum subsp. glabra

Galeopsis ladanum subsp.

glabra, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale blue or violet color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
