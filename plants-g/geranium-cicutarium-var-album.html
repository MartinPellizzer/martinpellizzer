Geranium cicutarium var. album

Geranium cicutarium var. album, commonly known as the white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Geranium cicutarium var. album is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
