Garcinia humilis

Garcinia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia humilis is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal potential.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
