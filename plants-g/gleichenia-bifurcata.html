Gleichenia bifurcata

Gleichenia bifurcata, commonly known as the forked fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dichotomous fronds that split into two equal parts, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant across the ground.

Gleichenia bifurcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
