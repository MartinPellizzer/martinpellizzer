Gentianella detonsa subsp. elegans

Gentianella detonsa subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant grows in moist, shaded habitats, often in woodland or along stream banks. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

The subspecies elegans is distinguished by its more refined floral structure and slightly smaller size compared to other members of the species.
