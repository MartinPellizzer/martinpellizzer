Gaultheria conzattii var. mijorum

Gaultheria conzattii var. mijorum, commonly known as the Mexican wintergreen, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It features small, glossy dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped white flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves, which emit a sharp, mint-like scent when crushed. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Gaultheria conzattii var. mijorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in local herbal remedies.
