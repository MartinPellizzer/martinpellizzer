Gentiana ramiflora

Gentiana ramiflora, commonly known as the branching gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a tubular shape, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana ramiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
