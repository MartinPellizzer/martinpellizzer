Grevillea depauperata

Grevillea depauperata, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, red or orange cylindrical flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Grevillea depauperata plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly nectar-feeding birds and insects.
