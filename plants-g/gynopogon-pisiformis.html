Gynopogon pisiformis

Gynopogon pisiformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often forming dense inflorescences.

Gynopogon pisiformis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
