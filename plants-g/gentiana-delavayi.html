Gentiana delavayi

Gentiana delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and southwestern China.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
