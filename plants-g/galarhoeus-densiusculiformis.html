Galarhoeus densiusculiformis

Galarhoeus densiusculiformis, commonly known as the dense-leafed dwarf fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and dense clusters of small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly serrated. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
