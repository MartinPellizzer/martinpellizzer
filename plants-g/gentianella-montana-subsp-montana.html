Gentianella montana subsp. montana

Gentianella montana subsp.

montana, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
