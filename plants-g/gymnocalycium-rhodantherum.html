Gymnocalycium rhodantherum

Gymnocalycium rhodantherum, commonly known as the red-crested cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a spineless cactus characterized by its globular shape and bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically solitary and have a striking red color with yellow stamens.

Gymnocalycium rhodantherum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
