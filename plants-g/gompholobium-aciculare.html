Gompholobium aciculare

Gompholobium aciculare, commonly known as the needle-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is often used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
