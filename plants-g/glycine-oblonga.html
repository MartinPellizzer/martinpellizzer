Glycine oblonga

Glycine oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several round seeds. Glycine oblonga is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

It is also cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
