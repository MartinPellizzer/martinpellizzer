Grevillea ramosissima subsp. ramosissima

Grevillea ramosissima subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry inland areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls along the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife such as birds and insects.
