Gasteria excavata

Gasteria excavata, commonly known as the African pineapple or Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. The leaves are often marked with white or yellow markings and have a distinctive notched or excavated base. It produces erect, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale green flowers.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
