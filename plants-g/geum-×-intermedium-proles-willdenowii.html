Geum × intermedium proles willdenowii

Geum × intermedium proles willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's golden-saxifrage, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double to double yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy, reddish stems and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
