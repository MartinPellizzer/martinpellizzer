Gelasine gigantea

Gelasine gigantea, commonly known as the giant gelasine, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length, providing a dramatic visual impact in its natural habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties, used traditionally in local herbal remedies.

However, due to habitat loss, it is now considered a rare and endangered species.
