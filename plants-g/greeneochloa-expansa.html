Greeneochloa expansa

Greeneochloa expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and robust growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, greenish-brown spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Greeneochloa expansa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
