Gentiana uchiyamae

Gentiana uchiyamae, commonly known as the Uchiyama gentian, is a rare flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of silky hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
