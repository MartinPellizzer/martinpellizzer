Gahnia affinis

Gahnia affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussock grass, is a perennial tussock-forming grass native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tufted at the base, forming dense clumps. Its flowering spikes are usually pink or red, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Gahnia affinis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
