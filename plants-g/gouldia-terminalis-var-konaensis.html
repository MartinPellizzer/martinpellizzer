Gouldia terminalis var. konaensis

Gouldia terminalis var. konaensis, commonly known as Kona ginger, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow bracts that resemble ginger flowers, though it is not a true ginger. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Gouldia terminalis var. konaensis is also valued for its use in traditional Hawaiian culture and horticulture.
