Gladiolus attilae

Gladiolus attilae, commonly known as the Hungarian sword lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant produces numerous spikes of blooms, each containing several petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Gladiolus attilae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
