Gymnosporia keniensis

Gymnosporia keniensis, commonly known as Kenyan burr, is a shrub native to Kenya and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Gymnosporia keniensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
