Gentiana prematura

Gentiana prematura, commonly known as the early gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
