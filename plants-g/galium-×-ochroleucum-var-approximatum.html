Galium × ochroleucum var. approximatum

Galium × ochroleucum var. approximatum, commonly known as the yellow wood-sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue, and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
