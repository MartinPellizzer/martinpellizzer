Gynostemma compressum

Gynostemma compressum, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or "southern ginseng," is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and gourds, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, fleshy fruits containing seeds.

Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often cultivated for its purported medicinal properties.
