Gomphrena desertorum var. rodantha

Gomphrena desertorum var. rodantha, commonly known as the desert globe flower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that resemble small, colorful spheres. The plant produces showy, often pink or red bracts that are mistaken for petals, while the true flowers are small and inconspicuous. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in its native habitat.
