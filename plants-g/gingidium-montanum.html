Gingidium montanum

Gingidium montanum, commonly known as mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Gingidium montanum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
