Gerardia skinneriana

Gerardia skinneriana, commonly known as the Skinner's gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Gerardia skinneriana is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of its native region.
