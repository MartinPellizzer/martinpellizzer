Genista acanthoclada subsp. acanthoclada

Genista acanthoclada subsp.

acanthoclada, commonly known as the thorny genista, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant is distinguished by its dense, thorny branches and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being hairy and oval-shaped.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
