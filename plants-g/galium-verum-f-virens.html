Galium verum f. virens

Galium verum f. virens, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw plant.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, wiry stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
