Gentiana dielsiana

Gentiana dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
