Gentiana verna proles brachyphylla

Gentiana verna proles brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has short, broad leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
