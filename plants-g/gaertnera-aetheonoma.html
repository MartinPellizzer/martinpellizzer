Gaertnera aetheonoma

Gaertnera aetheonoma, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and emit a subtle fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gaertnera aetheonoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
