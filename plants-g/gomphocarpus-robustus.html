Gomphocarpus robustus

Gomphocarpus robustus, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gomphocarpus robustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
