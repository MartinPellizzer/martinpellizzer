Goeppertia propinqua

Goeppertia propinqua, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a silvery sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia propinqua is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
