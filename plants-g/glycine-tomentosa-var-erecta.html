Glycine tomentosa var. erecta

Glycine tomentosa var. erecta, commonly known as the erect velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it both a useful crop and a potential weed in cultivated fields.
