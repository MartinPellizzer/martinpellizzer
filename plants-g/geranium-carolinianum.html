Geranium carolinianum

Geranium carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, woodland environments.

Geranium carolinianum is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
