Garcinia cochinchinensis

Garcinia cochinchinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pinnate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Garcinia cochinchinensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
