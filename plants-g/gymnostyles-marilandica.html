Gymnostyles marilandica

Gymnostyles marilandica, commonly known as Maryland arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
