Gentiana arethusae subsp. delicatula

Gentiana arethusae subsp.

delicatula, commonly known as the delicate gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
