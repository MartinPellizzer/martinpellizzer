Gochnatia avicenniifolia

Gochnatia avicenniifolia, commonly known as the white antelope bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of the genus Avicennia. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.

Gochnatia avicenniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
