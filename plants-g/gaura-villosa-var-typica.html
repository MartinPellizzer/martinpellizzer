Gaura villosa var. typica

Gaura villosa var. typica, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of delicate, pink to white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
