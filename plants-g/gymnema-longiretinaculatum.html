Gymnema longiretinaculatum

Gymnema longiretinaculatum, commonly known as the long-leaf gymnema, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It is often found in tropical forests, growing on trees or shrubs.

Due to its unique leaf structure and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
