Gentiana scabra var. fortunei

Gentiana scabra var. fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the top of its stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
