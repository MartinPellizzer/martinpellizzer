Gymnocalycium platense subsp. paucicostatum

Gymnocalycium platense subsp.

paucicostatum, commonly known as the Paraguay apple cactus, is a compact cactus native to South America, particularly Paraguay and northern Argentina. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray color and spiny areoles.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
