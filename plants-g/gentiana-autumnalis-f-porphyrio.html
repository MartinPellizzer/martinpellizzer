Gentiana autumnalis f. porphyrio

Gentiana autumnalis f. porphyrio, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and ecological benefits.
