Gomphrena bonariensis

Gomphrena bonariensis, commonly known as the everlasting flower or globe amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, spherical flower heads that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gomphrena bonariensis is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain vibrant even when dried, hence its common name.
