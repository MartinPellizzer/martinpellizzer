Gelasine longispatha

Gelasine longispatha, commonly known as the long-spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and feature prominent spurred petals. The plant belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

Gelasine longispatha thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
