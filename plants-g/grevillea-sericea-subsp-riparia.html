Grevillea sericea subsp. riparia

Grevillea sericea subsp.

riparia, commonly known as the river grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces bright red or orange cylindrical flowers that are highly attractive to birds and nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found near watercourses.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
