Grindelia buphthalmoides

Grindelia buphthalmoides, commonly known as desert gumplant, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellowish-green, glandular-soft leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its structure includes a taproot system and a branching stem that supports its flowering heads.

Grindelia buphthalmoides is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
