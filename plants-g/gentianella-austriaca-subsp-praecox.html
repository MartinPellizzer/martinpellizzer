Gentianella austriaca subsp. praecox

Gentianella austriaca subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
