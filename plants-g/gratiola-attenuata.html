Gratiola attenuata

Gratiola attenuata, commonly known as the slender yellowcress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open fields or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant has a low-growing habit and produces small, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds.
