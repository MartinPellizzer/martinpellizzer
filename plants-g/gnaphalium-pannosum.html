Gnaphalium pannosum

Gnaphalium pannosum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are clustered in heads, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance.

Gnaphalium pannosum is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
