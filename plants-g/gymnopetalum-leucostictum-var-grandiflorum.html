Gymnopetalum leucostictum var. grandiflorum

Gymnopetalum leucostictum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the white-streaked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive white or pale stripe down the center. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy coating, which helps to retain moisture in its arid environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
