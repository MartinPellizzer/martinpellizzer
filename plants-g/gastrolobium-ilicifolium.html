Gastrolobium ilicifolium

Gastrolobium ilicifolium, commonly known as the prickly acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its seed pods contain toxic seeds, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
