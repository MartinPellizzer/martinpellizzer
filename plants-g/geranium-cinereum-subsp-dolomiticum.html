Geranium cinereum subsp. dolomiticum

Geranium cinereum subsp.

dolomiticum, commonly known as the Dolomitic Geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the limestone regions of southern Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
