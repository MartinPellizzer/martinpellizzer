Gaura coccinea var. glabra

Gaura coccinea var. glabra, commonly known as the scarlet gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pink to red flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
