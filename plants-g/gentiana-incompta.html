Gentiana incompta

Gentiana incompta, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana incompta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
