Gonolobus lanugiflorus

Gonolobus lanugiflorus, commonly known as the velvetflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forest margins.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
