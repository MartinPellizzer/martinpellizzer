Geranium subcaulescens var. minor

Geranium subcaulescens var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features hairy, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
