Galarhoeus genistoides

Galarhoeus genistoides, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
