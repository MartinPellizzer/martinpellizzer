Grona nemorosa subsp. whitfordii

Grona nemorosa subsp.

whitfordii, commonly known as the Whitford's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veined pattern and produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its tactile appeal.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
