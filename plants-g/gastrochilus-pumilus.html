Gastrochilus pumilus

Gastrochilus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf stomach orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

Gastrochilus pumilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
