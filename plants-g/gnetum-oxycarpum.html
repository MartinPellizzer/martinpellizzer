Gnetum oxycarpum

Gnetum oxycarpum, commonly known as the African strangler fig, is a unique gymnosperm native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves, and distinctive capsule fruits that contain seeds. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often entwining trees and forming a dense canopy. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Gnetum oxycarpum plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
