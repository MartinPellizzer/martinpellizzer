Grossularia klamathensis

Grossularia klamathensis, commonly known as Klamath huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sparsely foliated growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically bright red when mature, containing several small, stony seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
