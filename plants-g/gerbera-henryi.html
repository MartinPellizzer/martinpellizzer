Gerbera henryi

Gerbera henryi, commonly known as the Henry's gerbera, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features a rosette of basal leaves and produces showy, single or double blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Gerbera henryi thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
