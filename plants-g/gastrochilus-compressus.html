Gastrochilus compressus

Gastrochilus compressus, commonly known as the compressed lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and features elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often with a distinct lip that is broader than the other petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
