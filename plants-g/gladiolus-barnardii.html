Gladiolus barnardii

Gladiolus barnardii, commonly known as the Cape York gladiolus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a unique addition to gardens in cooler climates. Its flowers are composed of three outer petals and three inner petals, with a prominent central spadix.

Gladiolus barnardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
