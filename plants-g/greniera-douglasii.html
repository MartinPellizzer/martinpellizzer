Greniera douglasii

Greniera douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Greniera douglasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
