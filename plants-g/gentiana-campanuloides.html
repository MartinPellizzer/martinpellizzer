Gentiana campanuloides

Gentiana campanuloides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes, and they attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
