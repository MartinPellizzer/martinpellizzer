Gastrochilus bifidus

Gastrochilus bifidus, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its overall compact appearance.

Gastrochilus bifidus is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky areas.
