Gomphrena serrata

Gomphrena serrata, commonly known as the serrated globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Gomphrena serrata is also valued for its long-lasting flowers, which are popular in floral arrangements.
