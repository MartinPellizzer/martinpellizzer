Geranium nemorale var. bicknellii

Geranium nemorale var. bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's wood geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often along stream banks and in rich, loamy soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
