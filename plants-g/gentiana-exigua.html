Gentiana exigua

Gentiana exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Gentiana exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
