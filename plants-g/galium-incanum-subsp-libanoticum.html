Galium incanum subsp. libanoticum

Galium incanum subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in shrublands and along rocky slopes.
