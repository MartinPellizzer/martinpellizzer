Genista zeyheri

Genista zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by elongated seed pods that mature in summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
