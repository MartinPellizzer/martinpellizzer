Gypsophila xanthina

Gypsophila xanthina, commonly known as the yellow gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Gypsophila xanthina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
