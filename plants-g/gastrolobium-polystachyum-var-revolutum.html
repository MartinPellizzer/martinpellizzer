Gastrolobium polystachyum var. revolutum

Gastrolobium polystachyum var. revolutum, commonly known as the red-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in spikes. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of lance-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
