Galium rubioides var. dasycarpum

Galium rubioides var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

It is often found in the understory of forests and is adapted to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
