Galarhoeus linifolius

Galarhoeus linifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Galarhoeus linifolius is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
