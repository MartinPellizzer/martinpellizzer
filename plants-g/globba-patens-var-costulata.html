Globba patens var. costulata

Globba patens var. costulata, commonly known as the zigzag ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, lance-shaped leaves with a zigzag margin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with tall, slender stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
