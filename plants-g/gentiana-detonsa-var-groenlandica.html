Gentiana detonsa var. groenlandica

Gentiana detonsa var. groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland gentian, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Greenland.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
