Glycine ixosperma

Glycine ixosperma, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein. Glycine ixosperma is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
