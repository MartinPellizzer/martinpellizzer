Gilia latiflora var. cana

Gilia latiflora var. cana, commonly known as the woolly gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gilia latiflora var. cana is a perennial species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
