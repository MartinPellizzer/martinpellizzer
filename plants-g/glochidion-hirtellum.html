Glochidion hirtellum

Glochidion hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Glochidion hirtellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
