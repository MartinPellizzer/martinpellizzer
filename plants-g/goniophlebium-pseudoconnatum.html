Goniophlebium pseudoconnatum

Goniophlebium pseudoconnatum, commonly known as the false connate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying fern diversity and adaptation.
