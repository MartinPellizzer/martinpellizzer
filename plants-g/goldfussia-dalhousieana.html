Goldfussia dalhousieana

Goldfussia dalhousieana, commonly known as the Dalhousie goldfussia, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Goldfussia dalhousieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
