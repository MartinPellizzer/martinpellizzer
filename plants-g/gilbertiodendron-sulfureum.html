Gilbertiodendron sulfureum

Gilbertiodendron sulfureum, commonly known as the yellow-leafed acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in open woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in local medicine for their purported health benefits.
