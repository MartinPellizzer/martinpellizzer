Globularia collina

Globularia collina, commonly known as the alpine globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pink or white flowers. The plant has rounded, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine and coastal habitats.

Globularia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
