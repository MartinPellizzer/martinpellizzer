Gastrochilus bellinus

Gastrochilus bellinus, commonly known as the bell-shaped orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by a short rhizome and a cluster of leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive bell-shaped lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
