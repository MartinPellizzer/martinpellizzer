Guzmania lingulata var. concolor

Guzmania lingulata var. concolor, commonly known as the red-tipped peace lily, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround the small white flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant features a rosette of dark green, strap-shaped leaves that are often marked with red or purple veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ability to thrive in humid environments makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
