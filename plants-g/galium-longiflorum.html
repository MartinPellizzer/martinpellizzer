Galium longiflorum

Galium longiflorum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, ascending stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium longiflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
