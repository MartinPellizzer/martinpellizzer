Guzmania pearcei

Guzmania pearcei, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its flowers, which are typically red or orange, bloom for several months and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Guzmania pearcei is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
