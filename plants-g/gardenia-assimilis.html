Gardenia assimilis

Gardenia assimilis, commonly known as the dwarf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant scent.

The fruit produced by the plant is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.
