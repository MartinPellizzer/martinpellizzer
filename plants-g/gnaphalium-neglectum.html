Gnaphalium neglectum

Gnaphalium neglectum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically features slender stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. It produces small yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white to pale brown seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Gnaphalium neglectum is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of soil types.
