Genista aspalathoides var. faureliana

Genista aspalathoides var. faureliana, commonly known as the Cape broom, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branching structure. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
