Grevillea pyramidalis subsp. pyramidalis

Grevillea pyramidalis subsp.

pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and is renowned for its vibrant, red-orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments. Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity. Grevillea pyramidalis subsp.

pyramidalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid climates.
