Grammitis fuscopilosa

Grammitis fuscopilosa, commonly known as the dark-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis fuscopilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
