Grevillea fasciculata

Grevillea fasciculata, commonly known as the yellow grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.

Grevillea fasciculata plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native birds and insects.
