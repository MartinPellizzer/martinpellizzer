Grewia polygama var. hosseusiana

Grewia polygama var. hosseusiana, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals but generally unpalatable to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
