Gunnera petaloidea

Gunnera petaloidea, commonly known as the giant water shield, is a striking plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence. The leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures that help the plant float and trap moisture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by a ring of modified leaves.

This species thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in marshy environments.
