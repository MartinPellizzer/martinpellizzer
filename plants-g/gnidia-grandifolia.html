Gnidia grandifolia

Gnidia grandifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense clusters of white flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are arranged in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
