Goodenia watsonii subsp. watsonii

Goodenia watsonii subsp.

watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
