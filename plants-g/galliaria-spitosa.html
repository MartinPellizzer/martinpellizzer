Galliaria spitosa

Galliaria spitosa, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Galliaria spitosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
