Ginoria americana var. americana

Ginoria americana var. americana, commonly known as the American ginoria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to wet environments and its role in local ecosystems.
