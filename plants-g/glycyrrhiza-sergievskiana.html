Glycyrrhiza sergievskiana

Glycyrrhiza sergievskiana, commonly known as the Siberian licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Its roots are highly valued for their sweet taste and medicinal properties, containing compounds like glycyrrhizin.

Glycyrrhiza sergievskiana is often cultivated for its economic and therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.
