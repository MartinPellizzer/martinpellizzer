Gomphocentrum cordemoyi

Gomphocentrum cordemoyi, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often forming a rosette of leaves at the base. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.

Gomphocentrum cordemoyi is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
