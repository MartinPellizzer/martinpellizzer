Glochidion aluminescens

Glochidion aluminescens, commonly known as the yellow glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
