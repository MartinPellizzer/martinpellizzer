Grumilea longipetiolata

Grumilea longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled grumilea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
