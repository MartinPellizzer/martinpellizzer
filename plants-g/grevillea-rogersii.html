Grevillea rogersii

Grevillea rogersii, commonly known as the pink waratah, is a striking shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink or red flower spikes, which are actually clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea rogersii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
