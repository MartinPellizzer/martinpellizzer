Gluema

Gluema, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like parsley and carrots. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its root is the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for its purported health benefits.

Gluema thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
