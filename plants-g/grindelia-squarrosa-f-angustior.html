Grindelia squarrosa f. angustior

Grindelia squarrosa f. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh desert conditions.
