Galium verum f. nikkoense

Galium verum f. nikkoense, commonly known as the Japanese bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces tiny, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of East Asia.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
