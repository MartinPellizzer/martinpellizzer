Geissorhiza divaricata

Geissorhiza divaricata, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense clumps through rhizomes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Geissorhiza divaricata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
