Gueldenstaedtia giraldii var. alba

Gueldenstaedtia giraldii var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often in temperate climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
