Gagea persica var. ebulbillosa

Gagea persica var. ebulbillosa, commonly known as the Persian spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its bulbs are small and rounded, containing nutrients that support its growth in harsh environments.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
