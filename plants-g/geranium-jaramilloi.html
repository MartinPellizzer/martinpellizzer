Geranium jaramilloi

Geranium jaramilloi, commonly known as Jaramillo's geranium, is a species native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive, showy appearance.

Geranium jaramilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
