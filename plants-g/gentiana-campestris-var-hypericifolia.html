Gentiana campestris var. hypericifolia

Gentiana campestris var. hypericifolia, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
