Gerardia pulchella var. delicatula

Gerardia pulchella var. delicatula, commonly known as the delicate gerardia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
