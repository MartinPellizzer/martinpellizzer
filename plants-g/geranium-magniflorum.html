Geranium magniflorum

Geranium magniflorum, commonly known as the showy geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
