Gnetum minus

Gnetum minus, commonly known as the dwarf gymnosperm or lesser gnetum, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, making it one of the few gymnosperms that are woody and have true leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its reproductive structures include male and female cones, typical of gymnosperms, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Gnetum minus is notable for its unique combination of gymnosperm and angiosperm characteristics.
