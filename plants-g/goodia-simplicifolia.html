Goodia simplicifolia

Goodia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf goodia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Goodia simplicifolia is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
