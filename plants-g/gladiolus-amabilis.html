Gladiolus amabilis

Gladiolus amabilis, commonly known as the sweet gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The flowers typically come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and are often used in floral arrangements. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a corm as its underground storage organ.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
