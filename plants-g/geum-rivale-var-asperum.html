Geum rivale var. asperum

Geum rivale var. asperum, commonly known as the rough water avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in northern regions.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered with stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

It thrives in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp environments, preferring partial to full shade.
