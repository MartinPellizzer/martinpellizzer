Gypsophila leucochleana

Gypsophila leucochleana, commonly known as the laceflower or baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental appearance.

Gypsophila leucochleana is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
