Gomphrena glabrata var. latifolia

Gomphrena glabrata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce vibrant, spiky flower heads in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena glabrata var. latifolia is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and low maintenance requirements.
