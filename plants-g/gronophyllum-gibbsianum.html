Gronophyllum gibbsianum

Gronophyllum gibbsianum, commonly known as the Gibbs' ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The wood of Gronophyllum gibbsianum is dense and durable, making it valuable for local crafts and construction.
