Gentiana vandellioides subsp. baoxingensis

Gentiana vandellioides subsp.

baoxingensis, commonly known as the Baoxing gentian, is a flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
