Galium elatum var. genuinum

Galium elatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, entire, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium elatum var. genuinum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
