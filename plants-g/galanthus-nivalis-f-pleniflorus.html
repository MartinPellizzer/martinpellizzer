Galanthus nivalis f. pleniflorus

Galanthus nivalis f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the snowdrop with double flowers, is a botanical variant of the common snowdrop.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white and clustered in threes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces multiple flower buds on a single stem. It thrives in woodland and shaded garden environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and early spring blooming habit.
