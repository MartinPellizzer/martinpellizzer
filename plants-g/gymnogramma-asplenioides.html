Gymnogramma asplenioides

Gymnogramma asplenioides, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green with a silvery underside. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are evergreen and can grow up to 30 cm in length, often forming dense, cascading clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper humidity and light.
