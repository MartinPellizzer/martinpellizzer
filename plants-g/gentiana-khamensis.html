Gentiana khamensis

Gentiana khamensis, commonly known as the Kham gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Tibet and western Sichuan in China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, often at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and erect.

Gentiana khamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
