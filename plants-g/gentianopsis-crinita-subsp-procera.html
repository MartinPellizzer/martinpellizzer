Gentianopsis crinita subsp. procera

Gentianopsis crinita subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched stems that support its showy flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
