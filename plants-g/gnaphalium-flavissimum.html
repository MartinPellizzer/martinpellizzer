Gnaphalium flavissimum

Gnaphalium flavissimum, commonly known as the golden marshweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and streambanks. Its leaves are usually linear to lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
