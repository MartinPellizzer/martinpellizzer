Gentiana lorentzii

Gentiana lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and hairy.

Gentiana lorentzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
