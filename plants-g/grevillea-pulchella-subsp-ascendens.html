Grevillea pulchella subsp. ascendens

Grevillea pulchella subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as the ascending grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features striking red or orange flowers that appear in cylindrical clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
