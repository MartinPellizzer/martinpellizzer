Genista lanuginosa

Genista lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
