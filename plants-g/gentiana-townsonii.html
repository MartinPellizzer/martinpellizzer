Gentiana townsonii

Gentiana townsonii, commonly known as Townsend's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana townsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
