Galactia virgata

Galactia virgata, commonly known as the Virginia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, often forming dense mats. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Galactia virgata is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and as a food source for wildlife.
