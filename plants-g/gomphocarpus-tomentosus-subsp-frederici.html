Gomphocarpus tomentosus subsp. frederici

Gomphocarpus tomentosus subsp.

frederici, commonly known as the frederici's milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
