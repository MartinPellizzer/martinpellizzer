Grumilea dermatophylla

Grumilea dermatophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to regions of Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, it is often considered an invasive species in certain areas.
