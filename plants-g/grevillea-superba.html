Grevillea superba

Grevillea superba, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and bright yellow, feathery inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of native pollinators, including honeyeaters and bees.

Grevillea superba is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
