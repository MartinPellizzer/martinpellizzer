Goeppertia atropurpurea

Goeppertia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its vibrant, deep purple foliage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia atropurpurea is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardens for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
