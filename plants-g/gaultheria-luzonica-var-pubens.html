Gaultheria luzonica var. pubens

Gaultheria luzonica var. pubens, commonly known as the Luzon pubescent wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.

The pubescent (hairy) texture of its leaves is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
