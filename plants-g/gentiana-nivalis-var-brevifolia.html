Gentiana nivalis var. brevifolia

Gentiana nivalis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved snow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are short and broad, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
