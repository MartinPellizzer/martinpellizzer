Genista insularis subsp. insularis

Genista insularis subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
