Gravesia guttata

Gravesia guttata, commonly known as the spotted gravesia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often covered in small, raised dots or glands. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive floral scent.
