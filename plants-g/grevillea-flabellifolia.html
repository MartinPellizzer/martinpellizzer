Grevillea flabellifolia

Grevillea flabellifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that are often bright green and can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, red or orange cylindrical flowers that appear in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea flabellifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native birds and insects.
