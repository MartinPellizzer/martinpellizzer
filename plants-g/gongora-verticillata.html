Gongora verticillata

Gongora verticillata, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting dark veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is adapted to thrive in cool, mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gongora verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
