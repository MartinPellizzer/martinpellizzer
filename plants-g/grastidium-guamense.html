Grastidium guamense

Grastidium guamense, commonly known as the Guam daisy, is a flowering plant native to the island of Guam in the Mariana Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in coastal and dry inland areas, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
