Gentianella androsacea

Gentianella androsacea, commonly known as the Androsace gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It has a low-growing habit with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Gentianella androsacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
