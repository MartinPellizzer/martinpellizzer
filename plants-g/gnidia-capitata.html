Gnidia capitata

Gnidia capitata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, resembling a small lantern. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Gnidia capitata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.
