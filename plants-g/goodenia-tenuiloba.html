Goodenia tenuiloba

Goodenia tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers during the spring and summer. The plant is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
