Galium aureum var. incurvum

Galium aureum var. incurvum, commonly known as golden wood sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It has hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its delicate appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
