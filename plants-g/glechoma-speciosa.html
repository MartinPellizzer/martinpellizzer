Glechoma speciosa

Glechoma speciosa, commonly known as pretty hedge-net, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Glechoma speciosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
