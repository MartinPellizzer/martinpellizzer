Gentianella propinqua subsp. propinqua

Gentianella propinqua subsp.

propinqua, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and prominent yellow spots near the base.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
