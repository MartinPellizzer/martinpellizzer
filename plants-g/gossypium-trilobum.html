Gossypium trilobum

Gossypium trilobum, commonly known as the Chinese cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its soft, fluffy seeds covered in a fine, white fuzz. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has palmate leaves with three to five lobes. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces capsules that contain the cotton fibers.

The plant is primarily cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production.
