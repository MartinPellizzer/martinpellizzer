Gladiolus viperatus

Gladiolus viperatus, commonly known as the snake-root gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces vividly colored flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom during the summer months. However, due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
