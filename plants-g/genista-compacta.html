Genista compacta

Genista compacta, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Genista compacta is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
