Gentianella cerrateae

Gentianella cerrateae, commonly known as the Cerratea gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentianella cerrateae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
