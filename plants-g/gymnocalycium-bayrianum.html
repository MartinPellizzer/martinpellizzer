Gymnocalycium bayrianum

Gymnocalycium bayrianum, commonly known as the Bayrianum cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Gymnocalycium bayrianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
