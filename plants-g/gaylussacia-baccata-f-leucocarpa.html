Gaylussacia baccata f. leucocarpa

Gaylussacia baccata f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited cranberry bush, is a variant of the common cranberry plant.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, typically found in wet, acidic soils. The plant produces small, white or pale red berries that are distinct from the typical red berries of the species. Its foliage is usually dense and dark green, providing ornamental value in gardens.

This form is notable for its unique fruit coloration, which makes it a desirable cultivar for horticultural purposes.
