Garnotia patula var. partitipilosa

Garnotia patula var. partitipilosa, commonly known as the spotted feather grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, white or pale pink plumes, giving it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant has fine, thread-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its unique foliage and airy inflorescences make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
