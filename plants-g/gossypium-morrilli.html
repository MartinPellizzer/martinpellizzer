Gossypium morrilli

Gossypium morrilli, commonly known as the Chinese cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its soft, fluffy seeds that are used for fiber production. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It produces small, pink or white flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it is of interest in botanical research and cotton breeding programs.
