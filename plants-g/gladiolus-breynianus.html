Gladiolus breynianus

Gladiolus breynianus, commonly known as the Cape gold gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of vibrant yellow or orange blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
