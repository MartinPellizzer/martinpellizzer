Goeppertia inocephala

Goeppertia inocephala, commonly known as the "Bottlebrush Caladium," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with patterns of red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia inocephala is a popular ornamental plant appreciated for its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
