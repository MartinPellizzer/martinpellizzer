Gomphrena tuberosa var. sericea

Gomphrena tuberosa var. sericea, commonly known as the silky gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
