Grindelia nuda var. aphanactis

Grindelia nuda var. aphanactis, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is known for its unique, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
