Geum × pratense

Geum × pratense, commonly known as Canadian goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Canadian goldenrod is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
