Gentiana scabra

Gentiana scabra, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana scabra is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
