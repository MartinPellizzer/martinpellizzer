Geonoma paniculigera var. microspatha

Geonoma paniculigera var. microspatha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geonoma, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Bromeliaceae, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that form dense, rosette-like structures. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, spadix-like flowers, typical of the Bromeliaceae family.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in forest understories.
