Gypsophila polyclada var. leioclada

Gypsophila polyclada var. leioclada, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath" or "Gypsophila," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in dense, fluffy clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in whorls around the stem.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
