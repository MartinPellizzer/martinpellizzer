Geranium multiflorum var. superbum

Geranium multiflorum var. superbum, commonly known as the showy geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
