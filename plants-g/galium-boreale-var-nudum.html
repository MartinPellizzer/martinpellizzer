Galium boreale var. nudum

Galium boreale var. nudum, commonly known as the naked white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairless, and it produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Galium boreale var. nudum plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is a food source for certain insects.
