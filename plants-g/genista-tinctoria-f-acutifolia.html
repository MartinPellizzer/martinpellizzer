Genista tinctoria f. acutifolia

Genista tinctoria f. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its "acutifolia" designation, meaning "sharp-leaved.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
