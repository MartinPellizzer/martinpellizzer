Galeopsis ladanum subsp. carpetana

Galeopsis ladanum subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as carpetweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often forming a dense carpet-like mat.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
