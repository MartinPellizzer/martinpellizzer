Geum triflorum var. triflorum

Geum triflorum var. triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered avens, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with three to five lobes, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
