Grevillea oxystigma var. villosa

Grevillea oxystigma var. villosa, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
