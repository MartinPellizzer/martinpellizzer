Gynetra

Gynetra, commonly known as the female organ of a flower, is a critical component of the reproductive system in flowering plants.

It is typically composed of the stigma, style, and ovary, which collectively facilitate the process of fertilization. The gynetra plays a vital role in attracting pollinators and ensuring the successful transfer of pollen to the ovules. Its structure varies among plant species, reflecting adaptations to different pollination strategies.

Botanically, the gynetra is essential for the development of seeds and the continuation of plant species.
