Galium gracile var. anglicum

Galium gracile var. anglicum, commonly known as English bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
