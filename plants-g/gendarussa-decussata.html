Gendarussa decussata

Gendarussa decussata, commonly known as the Indian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender stems with opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Gendarussa decussata is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
