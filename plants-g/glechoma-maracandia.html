Glechoma maracandia

Glechoma maracandia, commonly known as the Persian ground-ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with square stems and opposite leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma maracandia is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
