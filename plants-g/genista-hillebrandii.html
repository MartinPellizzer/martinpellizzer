Genista hillebrandii

Genista hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Genista hillebrandii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
