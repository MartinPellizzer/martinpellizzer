Gluta longipetiolata

Gluta longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled gluta, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Gluta longipetiolata is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
