Geranium dissectum f. albiflorum

Geranium dissectum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered dissected geranium, is a cultivar of the geranium plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

It produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for borders or containers.

This variety is prized for its elegant foliage and subtle floral display.
