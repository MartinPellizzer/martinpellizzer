Gynura papuana

Gynura papuana, commonly known as the papuan gynura, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a distinctive silvery or grayish underside.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
