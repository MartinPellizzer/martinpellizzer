Geranium balgooyi

Geranium balgooyi, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are usually pink or red, though white and purple varieties also exist. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium balgooyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
