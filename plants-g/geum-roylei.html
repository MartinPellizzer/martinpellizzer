Geum roylei

Geum roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir garden rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are often hairy.

Geum roylei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
