Gomphia hexasperma

Gomphia hexasperma, commonly known as the six-ribbed gomphia, is a unique member of the Gomphaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its distinct, ribbed, and often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a pointed tip.

Gomphia hexasperma is notable for its simple yet elegant structure, which reflects its adaptation to humid, temperate climates.
