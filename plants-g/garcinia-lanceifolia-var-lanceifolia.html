Garcinia lanceifolia var. lanceifolia

Garcinia lanceifolia var. lanceifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
