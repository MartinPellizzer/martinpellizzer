Galium rubrum var. grenieri

Galium rubrum var. grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
