Gentianodes amplicrater

Gentianodes amplicrater, commonly known as the broad-cup gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The genus Gentianodes is notable for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
