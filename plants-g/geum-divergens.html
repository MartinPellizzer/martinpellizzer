Geum divergens

Geum divergens, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals that bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive lobed appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Geum divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
