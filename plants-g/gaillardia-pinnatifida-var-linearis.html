Gaillardia pinnatifida var. linearis

Gaillardia pinnatifida var. linearis, commonly known as the linear gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
