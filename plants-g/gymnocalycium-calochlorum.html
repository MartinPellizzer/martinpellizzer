Gymnocalycium calochlorum

Gymnocalycium calochlorum, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
