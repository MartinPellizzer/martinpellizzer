Gentiana crinita var. browniana

Gentiana crinita var. browniana, commonly known as the Brown's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
