Geranium dissectum var. minimum

Geranium dissectum var. minimum, commonly known as the miniature cut-leaved geranium, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features finely dissected, lobed leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and extended blooming period.
