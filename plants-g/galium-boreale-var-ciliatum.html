Galium boreale var. ciliatum

Galium boreale var. ciliatum, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have finely toothed margins.

This species is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.
