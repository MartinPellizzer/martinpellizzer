Gladiolus sulcatus

Gladiolus sulcatus, commonly known as the zigzag gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched stems. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, ornamental blooms.

Its unique leaf patterns and colorful flowers make it a distinctive addition to both gardens and floral arrangements.
