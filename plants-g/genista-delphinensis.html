Genista delphinensis

Genista delphinensis, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Genista, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
