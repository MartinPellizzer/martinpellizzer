Gentianella nitida

Gentianella nitida, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella nitida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
