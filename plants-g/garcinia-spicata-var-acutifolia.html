Garcinia spicata var. acutifolia

Garcinia spicata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, pointed leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
