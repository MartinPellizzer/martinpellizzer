Gentiana otophora subsp. sichitoensis

Gentiana otophora subsp.

sichitoensis, commonly known as the Sichuan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan Province in China. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
