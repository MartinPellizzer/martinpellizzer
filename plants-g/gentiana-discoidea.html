Gentiana discoidea

Gentiana discoidea, commonly known as the disk gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense, disc-like cluster. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers. Gentiana discoidea thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
