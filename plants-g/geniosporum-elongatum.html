Geniosporum elongatum

Geniosporum elongatum, commonly known as the elongated spore plant, is a rare and lesser-known species within the genus Geniosporum.

It is characterized by its unique spore-producing structures, which are elongated and often arranged in clusters. The plant exhibits a low-growing habit with slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are adapted for spore dispersal, which is a key feature in its life cycle.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanically significant but understudied species.
