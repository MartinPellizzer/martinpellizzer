Gravisia capitata

Gravisia capitata, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, variegated foliage that ranges from green to red and gold. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. While it is not cultivated for its fruit, it is popular as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it a favored choice for landscaping.
