Glycine hispida subsp. lugulata

Glycine hispida subsp.

lugulata, commonly known as the rough soybean, is a leguminous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its hairy, compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
