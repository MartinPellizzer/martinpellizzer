Gladiolus cymbarius

Gladiolus cymbarius, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of colorful, spurred flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, making it well-suited for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
