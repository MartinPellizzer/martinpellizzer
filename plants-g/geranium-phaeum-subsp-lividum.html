Geranium phaeum subsp. lividum

Geranium phaeum subsp.

lividum, commonly known as the purple geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale purple to pink flowers.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
