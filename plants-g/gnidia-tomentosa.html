Gnidia tomentosa

Gnidia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Gnidia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
