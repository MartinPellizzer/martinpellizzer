Gelseminum radicans

Gelseminum radicans, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a strong, often unpleasant odor. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Gelseminum radicans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental flowers.
