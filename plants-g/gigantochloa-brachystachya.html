Gigantochloa brachystachya

Gigantochloa brachystachya, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of grass found primarily in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. It is a clumping bamboo that forms dense tussocks, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
