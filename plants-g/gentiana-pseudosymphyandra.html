Gentiana pseudosymphyandra

Gentiana pseudosymphyandra, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and ecological importance.
