Gloxinia macrophylla

Gloxinia macrophylla, commonly known as the showy gloxinia, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxinia macrophylla is typically grown as a houseplant or in greenhouses due to its sensitivity to cold temperatures.
