Galeopsis ladanum var. grandiflora

Galeopsis ladanum var. grandiflora, commonly known as largeflower hedge nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
