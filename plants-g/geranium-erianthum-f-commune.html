Geranium erianthum f. commune

Geranium erianthum f. commune, commonly known as the common scarlet geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
