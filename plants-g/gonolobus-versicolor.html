Gonolobus versicolor

Gonolobus versicolor, commonly known as the variable globemallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the species. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Gonolobus versicolor is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and dry fields.
