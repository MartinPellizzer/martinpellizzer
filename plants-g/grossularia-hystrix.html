Grossularia hystrix

Grossularia hystrix, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of orange-red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
