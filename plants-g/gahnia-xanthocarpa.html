Gahnia xanthocarpa

Gahnia xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow fruit grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, woody, yellowish-brown seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along watercourses.

Gahnia xanthocarpa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
