Gentiana laotica

Gentiana laotica, commonly known as the Lao gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, though its botanical study remains limited.
