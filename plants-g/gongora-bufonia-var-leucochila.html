Gongora bufonia var. leucochila

Gongora bufonia var. leucochila, commonly known as the White-Lipped Gongora, is a striking orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It features a unique inflorescence with a single, showy flower that has a white lip and a vibrant orange or yellow throat. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
