Gypsophila repens var. montana

Gypsophila repens var. montana, commonly known as the mountain baby's breath, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, resembling tiny stars. The plant has wiry, leafless stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include small, showy flowers and a mat-forming growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental value.
