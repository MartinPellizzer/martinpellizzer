Gilia nuttallii subvar. floribunda

Gilia nuttallii subvar. floribunda, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The showy gilia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
