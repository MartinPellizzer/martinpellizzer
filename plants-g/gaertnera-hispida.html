Gaertnera hispida

Gaertnera hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf gaertnera, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Gaertnera hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
