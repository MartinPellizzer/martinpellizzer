Galactia obovata

Galactia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval to ovate leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Galactia obovata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
