Gastrolobium melanopetalum

Gastrolobium melanopetalum, commonly known as the black-petaled acacia, is a shrub native to the southwestern regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seed pods are typically dark brown and contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
