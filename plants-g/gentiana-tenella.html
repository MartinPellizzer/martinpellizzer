Gentiana tenella

Gentiana tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Gentiana tenella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
