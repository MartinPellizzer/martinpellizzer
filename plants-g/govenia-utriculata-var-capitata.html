Govenia utriculata var. capitata

Govenia utriculata var. capitata, commonly known as the bladder nut or bladderleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders, which give the plant its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellowish, and appear in clusters.
