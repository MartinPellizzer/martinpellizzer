Grammanthes chloriflora

Grammanthes chloriflora, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Grammanthes chloriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
