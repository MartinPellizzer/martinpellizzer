Geranium erianthum f. albiflorum

Geranium erianthum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower geranium, is a horticultural form of the common geranium.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features rounded, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
