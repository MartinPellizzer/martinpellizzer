Glomera stolonifera

Glomera stolonifera, commonly known as the creeping glomera, is a flowering plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trailing stems.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
