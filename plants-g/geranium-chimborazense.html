Geranium chimborazense

Geranium chimborazense, commonly known as the Chimborazo geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium chimborazense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
