Grammitis thalia

Grammitis thalia, commonly known as the silver feather fern, is a tropical fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are short and branched, supporting the growth of its feathery leaves.

Grammitis thalia is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
