Glechon ciliata var. origanifolia

Glechon ciliata var. origanifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf glechon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
