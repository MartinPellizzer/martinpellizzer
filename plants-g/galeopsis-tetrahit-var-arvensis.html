Galeopsis tetrahit var. arvensis

Galeopsis tetrahit var. arvensis, commonly known as field hedge-nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It often grows in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions.
