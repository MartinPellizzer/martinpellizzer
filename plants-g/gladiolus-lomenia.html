Gladiolus lomenia

Gladiolus lomenia, commonly known as the Lomean gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red, with narrow, pointed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
