Grevillea thelemanniana subsp. fililoba

Grevillea thelemanniana subsp.

fililoba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright red or orange flowers that appear in cylindrical clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
