Gentiana sumatrana

Gentiana sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana sumatrana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
