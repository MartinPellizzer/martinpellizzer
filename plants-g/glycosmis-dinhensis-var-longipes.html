Glycosmis dinhensis var. longipes

Glycosmis dinhensis var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled sweet orange.

This variant is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant, orange-colored fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with simple, opposite leaves and small white flowers. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a valuable species in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
