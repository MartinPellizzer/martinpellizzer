Gamolepis pectinata

Gamolepis pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Gamolepis pectinata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
