Geranium ayavacense

Geranium ayavacense, commonly known as the Ayacucho geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of bright red or pink blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium ayavacense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
