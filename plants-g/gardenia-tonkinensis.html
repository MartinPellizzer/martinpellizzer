Gardenia tonkinensis

Gardenia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, waxy white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and have cultural significance in some regions.
