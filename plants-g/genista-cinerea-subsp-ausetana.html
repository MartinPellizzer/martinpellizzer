Genista cinerea subsp. ausetana

Genista cinerea subsp.

ausetana, commonly known as the pale broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
