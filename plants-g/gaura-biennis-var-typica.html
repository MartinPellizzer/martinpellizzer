Gaura biennis var. typica

Gaura biennis var. typica, commonly known as the annual gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and has a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
