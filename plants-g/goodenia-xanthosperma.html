Goodenia xanthosperma

Goodenia xanthosperma, commonly known as the yellowflower goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces seed pods that split open when mature.

Goodenia xanthosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
