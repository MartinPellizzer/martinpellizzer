Galium trifloriforme var. nipponicum

Galium trifloriforme var. nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Galium trifloriforme var. nipponicum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
