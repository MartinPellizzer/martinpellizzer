Geranium fissifolium

Geranium fissifolium, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, crinkled appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Geranium fissifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
