Genianthus

Genianthus, commonly known as the "fireflower," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and are known for their striking, bright-colored flower heads. Genianthus species typically produce showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow, often resembling miniature sunflowers. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and are adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Many species are drought-tolerant, making them suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
