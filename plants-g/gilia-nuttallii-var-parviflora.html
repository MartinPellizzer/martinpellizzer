Gilia nuttallii var. parviflora

Gilia nuttallii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
