Globularia greuteri

Globularia greuteri, commonly known as the Sierra globularia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, globular growth habit, with small, clustered leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Globularia greuteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
