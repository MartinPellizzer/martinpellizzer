Gleichenia pteridella

Gleichenia pteridella, commonly known as the slender Gleichenia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive, creeping rhizome with lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its fronds are usually dimorphic, with a sterile, elongated form and a fertile, shorter form that produces spores.

Gleichenia pteridella is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
