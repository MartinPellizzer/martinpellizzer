Gilia capitata subsp. capitata

Gilia capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky slopes and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
