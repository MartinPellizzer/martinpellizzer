Gentianella dissitifolia

Gentianella dissitifolia, commonly known as the scattered-hair gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, scattered hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
