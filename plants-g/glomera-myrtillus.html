Glomera myrtillus

Glomera myrtillus, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
