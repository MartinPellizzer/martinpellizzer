Gentiana quadrifaria var. pilosula

Gentiana quadrifaria var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
