Galium pogonanthum f. interpositum

Galium pogonanthum f. interpositum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
