Goodyera araneosa

Goodyera araneosa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, spider-like flowers that are typically greenish-white with purple markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

Goodyera araneosa is valued for its unique floral structure and is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
