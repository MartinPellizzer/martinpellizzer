Genista interrupta

Genista interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
