Geranium pusillum var. malviflorum

Geranium pusillum var. malviflorum, commonly known as the Malviflorum geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are showy, typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, rounded calyx. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing clusters of small, clustered flowers.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
