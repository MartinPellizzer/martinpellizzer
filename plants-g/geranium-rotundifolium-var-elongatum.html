Geranium rotundifolium var. elongatum

Geranium rotundifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-leaved geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features rounded, broad leaves that are elongated in this variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
