Geocaryum cynapioides subsp. macrocarpum

Geocaryum cynapioides subsp.

macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months.

The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to several meters tall, making it a striking addition to its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a hairy texture, and it produces large, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
