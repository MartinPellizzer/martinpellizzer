Glycine capitata

Glycine capitata, commonly known as the white bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and often used in various cuisines. Glycine capitata has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

It is also valued for its adaptability to different soil types and climates.
