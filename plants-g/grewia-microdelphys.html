Grewia microdelphys

Grewia microdelphys, commonly known as the small-leaved bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
