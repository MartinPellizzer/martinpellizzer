Grammitis fasciculata

Grammitis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sword fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, forming dense, mat-like clusters with finely divided, linear leaves. The plant's fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its humid environment. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds.

Grammitis fasciculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
