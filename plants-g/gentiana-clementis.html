Gentiana clementis

Gentiana clementis, commonly known as the Clement's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the showy flowers.

Gentiana clementis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
