Garnotia laevis var. pilosiuscula

Garnotia laevis var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the silky garnotia, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a desirable addition to rock gardens and borders.
