Glossocardia integrifolia

Glossocardia integrifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
