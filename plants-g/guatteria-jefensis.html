Guatteria jefensis

Guatteria jefensis, commonly known as the Jefénos guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the humid lowland forests of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.
