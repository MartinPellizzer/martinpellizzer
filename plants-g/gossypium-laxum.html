Gossypium laxum

Gossypium laxum, commonly known as the loose cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fluffy, cotton-like capsules. Its fibers, though not as commercially valuable as those of Gossypium hirsutum, have been used in traditional textiles and for other local purposes.

The plant's ability to adapt to harsh conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
