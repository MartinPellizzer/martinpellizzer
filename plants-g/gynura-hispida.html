Gynura hispida

Gynura hispida, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant contains irritant chemicals, such as histamine and acetylcholine, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gynura hispida is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
