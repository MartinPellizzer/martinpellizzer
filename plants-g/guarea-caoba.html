Guarea caoba

Guarea caoba, commonly known as rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Guarea caoba is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems.
