Gaillardia pringlei

Gaillardia pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its vibrant orange and red petals are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gaillardia pringlei is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
