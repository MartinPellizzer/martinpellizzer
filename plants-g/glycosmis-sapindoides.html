Glycosmis sapindoides

Glycosmis sapindoides, commonly known as the Chinese date or wild mango, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
