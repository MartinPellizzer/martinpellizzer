Gaylussacia venusta

Gaylussacia venusta, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The shrub is often found in moist, acidic soils and is valued for its ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
