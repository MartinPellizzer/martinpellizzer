Gossia clusioides subsp. avanguiensis

Gossia clusioides subsp.

avanguiensis, commonly known as the Guianese gossia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname. It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, also known as the guttiferae, and is characterized by its showy, bright red flowers.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking floral display.
