Gleichenia seminuda

Gleichenia seminuda, commonly known as the slender Gleichenia, is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like fronds that are typically light green and have a linear, strap-shaped appearance. The plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and India, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Gleichenia seminuda reproduces via spores, which are released from sporangia located on the underside of its fronds.

This species is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
