Gagea callieri

Gagea callieri, commonly known as the Chinese spring crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
