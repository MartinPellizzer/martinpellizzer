Geranium charlesii

Geranium charlesii, commonly known as the Charles's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular plant among gardeners.
