Gaultheria glaziovii

Gaultheria glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Gaultheria glaziovii is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine.
