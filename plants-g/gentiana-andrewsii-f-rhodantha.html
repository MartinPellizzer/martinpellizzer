Gentiana andrewsii f. rhodantha

Gentiana andrewsii f. rhodantha, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and erect.

This subspecies is notable for its intense coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
