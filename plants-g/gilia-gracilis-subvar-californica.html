Gilia gracilis subvar. californica

Gilia gracilis subvar. californica, commonly known as the California slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often hairy, and it produces seeds in slender, hairy pods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
