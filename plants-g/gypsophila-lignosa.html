Gypsophila lignosa

Gypsophila lignosa, commonly known as the evergreen gypsophil, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Gypsophila lignosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
