Gouldia tenuicaulis

Gouldia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed gouldia, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Gouldia tenuicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to garden landscapes.
