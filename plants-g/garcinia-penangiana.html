Garcinia penangiana

Garcinia penangiana, commonly known as Penang Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a unique chemical composition that contributes to its medicinal potential.

Garcinia penangiana is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and therapeutic properties.
