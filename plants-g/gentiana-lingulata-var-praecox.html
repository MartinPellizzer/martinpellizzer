Gentiana lingulata var. praecox

Gentiana lingulata var. praecox, commonly known as the early gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom early in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct yellowish or white midrib.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
