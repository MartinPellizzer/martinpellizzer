Gouldia cirrhopetiolata

Gouldia cirrhopetiolata, commonly known as the silky gouldia, is a striking tropical plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pinkish-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has elongated, slender leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gouldia cirrhopetiolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate and colorful foliage.
