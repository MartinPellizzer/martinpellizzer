Gomphocarpus velutinus

Gomphocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its seed pods are distinctive, elongated, and covered in fine hairs, which aid in seed dispersal.

Gomphocarpus velutinus is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
