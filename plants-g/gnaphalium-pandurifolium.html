Gnaphalium pandurifolium

Gnaphalium pandurifolium, commonly known as the pandurifolium cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Gnaphalium pandurifolium is a perennial herb that is typically low-growing and spreads by rhizomes.
