Garcinia pendula

Garcinia pendula, commonly known as the hanging Garcinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange-red in color and contain a single seed. Garcinia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
