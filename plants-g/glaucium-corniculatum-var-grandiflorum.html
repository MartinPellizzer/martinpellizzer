Glaucium corniculatum var. grandiflorum

Glaucium corniculatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
