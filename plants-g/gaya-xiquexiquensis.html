Gaya xiquexiquensis

Gaya xiquexiquensis, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
