Grevillea vestita var. angustata

Grevillea vestita var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed emu bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for coastal and sandy environments.
