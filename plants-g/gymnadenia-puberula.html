Gymnadenia puberula

Gymnadenia puberula, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy textures, contributing to its common name.

Gymnadenia puberula is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
