Gentianodes praeclara

Gentianodes praeclara, commonly known as the splendid gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its growth habit is usually herbaceous.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
