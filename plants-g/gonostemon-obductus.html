Gonostemon obductus

Gonostemon obductus, commonly known as the narrowleaf gonostemon, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Gonostemon obductus is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in riparian habitats.
