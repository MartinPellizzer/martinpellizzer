Galium munzii subsp. munzii

Galium munzii subsp.

munzii, commonly known as Munz's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically features slender, wiry stems that are often hairy and produce small, white or pale green flowers.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
