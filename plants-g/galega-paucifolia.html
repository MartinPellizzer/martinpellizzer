Galega paucifolia

Galega paucifolia, commonly known as the lesser goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Galega paucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
