Geranium graniticola

Geranium graniticola, commonly known as the granitic geranium, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled blooms in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
