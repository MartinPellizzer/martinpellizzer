Gnemon paniculata

Gnemon paniculata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is often smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
