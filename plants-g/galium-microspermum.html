Galium microspermum

Galium microspermum, commonly known as littleseed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It typically forms dense mats and has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains numerous minute seeds.

Galium microspermum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
