Gladiolus taubertianus

Gladiolus taubertianus, commonly known as the Taubert's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive three-lobed structure. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among horticulturists and gardeners.
