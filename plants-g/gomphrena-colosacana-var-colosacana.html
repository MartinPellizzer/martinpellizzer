Gomphrena colosacana var. colosacana

Gomphrena colosacana var. colosacana, commonly known as the "globe thistle," is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gomphrena colosacana var. colosacana is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
