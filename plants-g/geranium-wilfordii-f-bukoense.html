Geranium wilfordii f. bukoense

Geranium wilfordii f. bukoense, commonly known as the Bukhara geranium, is a horticultural form of the geranium genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
