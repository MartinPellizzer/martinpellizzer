Guajava polycarpos

Guajava polycarpos, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a drupaceous fruit type.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
