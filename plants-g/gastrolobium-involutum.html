Gastrolobium involutum

Gastrolobium involutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, waxy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens.
