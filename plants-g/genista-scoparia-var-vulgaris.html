Genista scoparia var. vulgaris

Genista scoparia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia, characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
