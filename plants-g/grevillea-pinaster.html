Grevillea pinaster

Grevillea pinaster, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking crimson or red flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea pinaster is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
