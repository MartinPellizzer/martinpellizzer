Galium rotundifolium

Galium rotundifolium, commonly known as roundleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its stems are slender and often prostrate, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil.
