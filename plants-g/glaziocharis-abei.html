Glaziocharis abei

Glaziocharis abei, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Antarctica.

It is a small, mat-forming grass that thrives in the extreme conditions of the continent's tundra. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers during the brief summer months. It plays a vital role in the local ecosystem by providing food and habitat for native invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
