Gomphocarpus chironioides

Gomphocarpus chironioides, commonly known as the yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
