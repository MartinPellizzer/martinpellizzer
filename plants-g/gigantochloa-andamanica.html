Gigantochloa andamanica

Gigantochloa andamanica, commonly known as Andaman giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Andaman Islands.

It features sturdy, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, making it a distinctive member of the grass family. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is sometimes used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
