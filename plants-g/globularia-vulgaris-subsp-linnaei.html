Globularia vulgaris subsp. linnaei

Globularia vulgaris subsp.

linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean globularia, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a globular shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
