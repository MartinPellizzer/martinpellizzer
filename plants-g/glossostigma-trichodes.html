Glossostigma trichodes

Glossostigma trichodes, commonly known as the thread-leaved stellaria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The small, white or pink flowers appear in clusters and are borne on slender pedicels, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
