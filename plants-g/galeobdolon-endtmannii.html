Galeobdolon endtmannii

Galeobdolon endtmannii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its slow growth and hardy nature make it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
