Gentiana lutescens

Gentiana lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana lutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
