Gagea lutea var. uniflora

Gagea lutea var. uniflora, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
