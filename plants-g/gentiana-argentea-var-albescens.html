Gentiana argentea var. albescens

Gentiana argentea var. albescens, commonly known as the silver gentian or white gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet-blue flowers, which are often adorned with a white or pale blue throat. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
