Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. eytianum

Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. eytianum, commonly known as the Eytianum moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
