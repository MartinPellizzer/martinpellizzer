Gustavia elliptica

Gustavia elliptica, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five leaf-like sepals. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm regions.
