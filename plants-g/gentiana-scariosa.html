Gentiana scariosa

Gentiana scariosa, commonly known as the scarious gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a whitish, waxy coating.

Gentiana scariosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
