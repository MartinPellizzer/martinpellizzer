Gynura grandifolia

Gynura grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gynura, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Gynura grandifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
