Grammatophyllum celebicum

Grammatophyllum celebicum, commonly known as the Celebes staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its fiddlehead-like young fronds and a central trunk-like structure called a stipe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the wild.

Grammatophyllum celebicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
