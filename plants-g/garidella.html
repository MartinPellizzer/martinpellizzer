Garidella

Garidella, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Garidella is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
