Gynura aurantiaca subsp. aurantiaca

Gynura aurantiaca subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves with bright red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant leaf coloration and ease of care.
