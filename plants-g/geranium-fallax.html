Geranium fallax

Geranium fallax, commonly known as the fallax geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Geranium fallax prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
