Gilia collina var. coronata

Gilia collina var. coronata, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of showy, pink to lavender-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and adapted to arid conditions.

The Sierra gilia is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
