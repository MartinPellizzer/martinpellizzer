Glochidion Moorei

Moore's glochidion, scientifically known as Glochidion moorei, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Despite its modest size, Moore's glochidion plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity.
