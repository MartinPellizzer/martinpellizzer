Gratiola violacea var. adenocaula

Gratiola violacea var. adenocaula, commonly known as the purple false bugloss, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
