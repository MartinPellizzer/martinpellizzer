Galinsoga bicolorata

Galinsoga bicolorata, commonly known as the two-colored gallinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive two-toned appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a slender, two-lobed achene that is often covered in fine hairs.

Galinsoga bicolorata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
