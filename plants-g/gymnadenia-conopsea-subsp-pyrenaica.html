Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. pyrenaica

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean grasspocket orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the more widely distributed G. conopsea.
