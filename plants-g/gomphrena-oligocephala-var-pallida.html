Gomphrena oligocephala var. pallida

Gomphrena oligocephala var. pallida, commonly known as the pale-headed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to white. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
