Globifera punctata

Globifera punctata, commonly known as the spotted globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
