Genista ramentacea

Genista ramentacea, commonly known as the silky broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The plant has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain regions.
