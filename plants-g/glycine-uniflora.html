Glycine uniflora

Glycine uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant bears elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several round, edible seeds. Glycine uniflora is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
