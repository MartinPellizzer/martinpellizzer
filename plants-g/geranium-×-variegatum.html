Geranium × variegatum

Geranium × variegatum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which often features white or cream markings on dark green leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Geranium × variegatum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both container and in-ground planting.
