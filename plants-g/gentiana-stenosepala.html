Gentiana stenosepala

Gentiana stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue petals and a prominent yellow or orange corona.

Gentiana stenosepala is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
