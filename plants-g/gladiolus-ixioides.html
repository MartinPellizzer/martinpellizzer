Gladiolus ixioides

Gladiolus ixioides, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Gladiolus ixioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
