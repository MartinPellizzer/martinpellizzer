Gynoxys repanda

Gynoxys repanda, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its bright orange to yellow flowers bloom in the summer months and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Gynoxys repanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
