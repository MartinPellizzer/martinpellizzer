Glycosmis tomentella

Glycosmis tomentella, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Glycosmis tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
