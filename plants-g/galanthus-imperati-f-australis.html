Galanthus imperati f. australis

Galanthus imperati f. australis, commonly known as the Australian imperial snowdrop, is a rare and striking variant of the snowdrop genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale yellow flowers with prominent white markings, which bloom in early spring. Native to the mountainous regions of southern China, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous root system.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral features, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
