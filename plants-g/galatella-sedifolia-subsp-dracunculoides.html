Galatella sedifolia subsp. dracunculoides

Galatella sedifolia subsp.

dracunculoides, commonly known as the dracunculoides variant of the woolly milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are erect and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained environments.
