Gymnosporia gilletii

Gymnosporia gilletii, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, spiny fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it has a deep root system to survive in dry conditions.

Gymnosporia gilletii is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
