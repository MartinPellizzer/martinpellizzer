Gentiana austriaca var. montana

Gentiana austriaca var. montana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
