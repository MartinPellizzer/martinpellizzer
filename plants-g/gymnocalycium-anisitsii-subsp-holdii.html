Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp. holdii

Gymnocalycium anisitsii subsp.

holdii, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or Holdii Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
