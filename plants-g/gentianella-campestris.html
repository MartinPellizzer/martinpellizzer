Gentianella campestris

Gentianella campestris, commonly known as field gentian, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

Gentianella campestris is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
