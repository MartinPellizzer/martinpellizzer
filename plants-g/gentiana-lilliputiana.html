Gentiana lilliputiana

Gentiana lilliputiana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it one of the smallest gentian species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are tubular with five petals that curve outward.

Gentiana lilliputiana thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
