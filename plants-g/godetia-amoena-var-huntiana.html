Godetia amoena var. huntiana

Godetia amoena var. huntiana, commonly known as the California goldpoppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

This variety is known for its showy appearance and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.
