Galypola incarnata

Galypola incarnata, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branched.

Galypola incarnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
