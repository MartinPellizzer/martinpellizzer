Galium spurium subsp. glabrum

Galium spurium subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as smooth bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, prostrate stems that are covered with small, opposite, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves and stems are usually hairless, giving it a smooth appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
