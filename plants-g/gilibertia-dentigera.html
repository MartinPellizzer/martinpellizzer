Gilibertia dentigera

Gilibertia dentigera, commonly known as the "peanut grass," is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, peanut-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Gilibertia dentigera is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine.
