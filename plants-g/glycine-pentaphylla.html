Glycine pentaphylla

Glycine pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed soybean, is a climbing legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically divided into three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Glycine pentaphylla is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and used in traditional and modern agricultural practices.
