Glyceria kashmiriensis

Glyceria kashmiriensis, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain streams.

Glyceria kashmiriensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
