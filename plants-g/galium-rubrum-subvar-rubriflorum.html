Galium rubrum subvar. rubriflorum

Galium rubrum subvar. rubriflorum, commonly known as redflower bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or pink in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium rubrum subvar. rubriflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
