Grevillea dilatata

Grevillea dilatata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is often used in coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance.

Grevillea dilatata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
