Gunnera hamiltonii

Gunnera hamiltonii, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the Gunneraceae family and is known for its robust, fibrous root system. Its foliage is typically a deep green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Gunnera hamiltonii is often cultivated for its dramatic appearance in gardens and can thrive in moist, well-drained soil.
