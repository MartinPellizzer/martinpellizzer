Gentiana acaulis var. dinarica

Gentiana acaulis var. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
