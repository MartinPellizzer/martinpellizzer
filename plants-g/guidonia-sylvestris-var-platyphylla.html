Guidonia sylvestris var. platyphylla

Guidonia sylvestris var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf guidonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
