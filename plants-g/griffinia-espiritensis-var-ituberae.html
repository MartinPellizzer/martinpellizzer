Griffinia espiritensis var. ituberae

Griffinia espiritensis var. ituberae, commonly known as the Ituberae variant, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the spring season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests.
