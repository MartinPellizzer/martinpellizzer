Gladiolus anorthanthus

Gladiolus anorthanthus, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
