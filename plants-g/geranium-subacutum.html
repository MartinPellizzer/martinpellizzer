Geranium subacutum

Geranium subacutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
