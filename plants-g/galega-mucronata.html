Galega mucronata

Galega mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale blue to violet, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galega mucronata is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
