Gentiana andrewsii var. andrewsii

Gentiana andrewsii var. andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
