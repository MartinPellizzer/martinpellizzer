Galium petraeum var. patulum

Galium petraeum var. patulum, commonly known as the rock bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and rocky regions of western North America.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, spreading stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild medicinal properties.
