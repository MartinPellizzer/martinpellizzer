Gomphrena meyeniana var. albiflora

Gomphrena meyeniana var. albiflora, commonly known as the white plume flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, feathery inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its foliage is typically green and lance-shaped, and it produces small, scale-like leaves along the stem.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
