Gomphrena bicolor

Gomphrena bicolor, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, typically ranging in color from red to pink and purple. The plant is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, greenish flowers that develop into the showy, colorful bracts.

Gomphrena bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
