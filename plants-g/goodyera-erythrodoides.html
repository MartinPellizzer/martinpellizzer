Goodyera erythrodoides

Goodyera erythrodoides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, showy flowers with red veins on white petals. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in botanical studies.
