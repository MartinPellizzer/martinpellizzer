Galiopsis

Galiopsis, commonly known as the "false ginseng," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, with Galiopsis glyphica and Galiopsis lutea being the most well-known. These plants are native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, typically growing in arid or semi-arid environments. They are characterized by their erect, branched stems and small, yellow or greenish-yellow flowers.

The leaves are usually trifoliate, and the pods are slender, containing several seeds.
