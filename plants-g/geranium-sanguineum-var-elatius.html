Geranium sanguineum var. elatius

Geranium sanguineum var. elatius, commonly known as the greater mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It thrives in partial shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
