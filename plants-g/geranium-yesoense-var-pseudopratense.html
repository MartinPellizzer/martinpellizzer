Geranium yesoense var. pseudopratense

Geranium yesoense var. pseudopratense, commonly known as the Yesso geranium, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
