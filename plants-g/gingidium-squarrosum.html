Gingidium squarrosum

Gingidium squarrosum, commonly known as the rough-hairy ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of clustered, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Gingidium squarrosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
