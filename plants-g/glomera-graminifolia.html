Glomera graminifolia

Glomera graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like glomera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a slender, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
