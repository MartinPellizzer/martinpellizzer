Guzmania straminea

Guzmania straminea, commonly known as the golden guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that typically displays shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its foliage is often variegated with white and green stripes, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Guzmania straminea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
