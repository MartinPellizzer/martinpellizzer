Gladiolus collinus

Gladiolus collinus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched flower spikes. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gladiolus collinus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
