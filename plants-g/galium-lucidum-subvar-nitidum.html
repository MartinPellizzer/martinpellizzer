Galium lucidum subvar. nitidum

Galium lucidum subvar. nitidum, commonly known as shining bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a shiny, waxy surface that gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
