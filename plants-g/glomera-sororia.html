Glomera sororia

Glomera sororia, commonly known as the silverleaf glomerate, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing its ability to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Glomera sororia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
