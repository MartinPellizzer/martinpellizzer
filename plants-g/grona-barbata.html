Grona barbata

Grona barbata, commonly known as the hairy grass, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, blade-like leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grass species.

Grona barbata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
