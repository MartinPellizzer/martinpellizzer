Geum dryadoides

Geum dryadoides, commonly known as the dryad's blush, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely cut, fern-like leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geum dryadoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
