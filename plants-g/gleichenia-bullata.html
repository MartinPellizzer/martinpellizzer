Gleichenia bullata

Gleichenia bullata, commonly known as the bullate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in a unique, almost bulbous structure. Gleichenia bullata is native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including parts of South America, Africa, and Australia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories.
