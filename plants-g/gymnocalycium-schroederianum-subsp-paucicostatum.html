Gymnocalycium schroederianum subsp. paucicostatum

Gymnocalycium schroederianum subsp.

paucicostatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered with sharp, spiny ribs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
