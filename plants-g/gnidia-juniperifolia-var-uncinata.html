Gnidia juniperifolia var. uncinata

Gnidia juniperifolia var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form and has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
