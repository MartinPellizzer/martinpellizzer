Gilia polyantha var. whitingii

Gilia polyantha var. whitingii, commonly known as the Whiting's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
