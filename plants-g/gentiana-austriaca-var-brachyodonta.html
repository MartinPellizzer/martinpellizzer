Gentiana austriaca var. brachyodonta

Gentiana austriaca var. brachyodonta, commonly known as the short-stalked gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems.
