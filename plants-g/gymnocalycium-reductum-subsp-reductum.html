Gymnocalycium reductum subsp. reductum

Gymnocalycium reductum subsp.

reductum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.
