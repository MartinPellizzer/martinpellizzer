Gussonea aphylla var. orientalis

Gussonea aphylla var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental leafless gussonea, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its thick, fleshy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its stems are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and resilience in harsh environments.
