Glycine digitata

Glycine digitata, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Glycine digitata is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread cultivation.
