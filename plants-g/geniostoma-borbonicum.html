Geniostoma borbonicum

Geniostoma borbonicum, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by insects.

Geniostoma borbonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
