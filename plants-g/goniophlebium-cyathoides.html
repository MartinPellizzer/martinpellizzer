Goniophlebium cyathoides

Goniophlebium cyathoides, commonly known as the cup-shaped fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-like fronds that are often used in horticultural displays. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a complex venation pattern.

Goniophlebium cyathoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
