Gypsophila acerosa

Gypsophila acerosa, commonly known as the hardy garden poppy, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Gypsophila acerosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its long blooming period.
