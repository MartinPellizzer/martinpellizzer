Gladiolus antholyzoides

Gladiolus antholyzoides, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, with six tepals that are often reflexed backward.

This species is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
