Gentiana kurtzii

Gentiana kurtzii, commonly known as the Kurtz's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Gentiana kurtzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
