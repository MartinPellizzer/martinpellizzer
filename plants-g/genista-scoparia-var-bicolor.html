Genista scoparia var. bicolor

Genista scoparia var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored broom.

It is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive yellow and white flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its small, compound leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
