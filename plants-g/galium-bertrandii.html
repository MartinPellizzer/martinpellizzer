Galium bertrandii

Galium bertrandii, commonly known as Bertrand's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features whorled leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium bertrandii is often found in the Appalachian region and is characterized by its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
