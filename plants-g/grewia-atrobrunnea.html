Grewia atrobrunnea

Grewia atrobrunnea, commonly known as the black bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
