Glochidion rugulosum

Glochidion rugulosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are often covered in tiny, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

Glochidion rugulosum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest undergrowth.
