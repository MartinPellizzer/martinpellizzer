Gastrolobium spinosum f. typicum

Gastrolobium spinosum f. typicum, commonly known as the spinifex acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is characterized by its spiny stems and branches. It is adapted to arid and sandy environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
