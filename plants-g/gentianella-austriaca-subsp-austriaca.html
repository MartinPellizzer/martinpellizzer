Gentianella austriaca subsp. austriaca

Gentianella austriaca subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
