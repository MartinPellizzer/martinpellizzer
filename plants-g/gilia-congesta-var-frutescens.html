Gilia congesta var. frutescens

Gilia congesta var. frutescens, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

The species is adapted to thrive in harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
