Goeppertia saxicola

Goeppertia saxicola, commonly known as the rock goeppertia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, which appear at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in collections of rare succulents.
