Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. scabrida

Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. scabrida, commonly known as the rough alpine honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The leaves are usually elliptical with a rough, hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests and alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
