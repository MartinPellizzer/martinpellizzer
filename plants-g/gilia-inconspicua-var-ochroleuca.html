Gilia inconspicua var. ochroleuca

Gilia inconspicua var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
