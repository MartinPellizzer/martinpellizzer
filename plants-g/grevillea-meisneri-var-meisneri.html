Grevillea meisneri var. meisneri

Grevillea meisneri var. meisneri, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
