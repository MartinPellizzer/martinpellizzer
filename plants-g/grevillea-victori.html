Grevillea victori

Grevillea victori, commonly known as the Victorian grevillea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant, red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract native birds, particularly honeyeaters, contributing to its ecological significance.
