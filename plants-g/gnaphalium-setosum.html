Gnaphalium setosum

Gnaphalium setosum, commonly known as the hairy thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows and wetlands, and is often found in the eastern and central United States. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and the flowers are yellow with pale green centers.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable environments.
