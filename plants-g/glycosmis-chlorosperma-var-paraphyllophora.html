Glycosmis chlorosperma var. paraphyllophora

Glycosmis chlorosperma var. paraphyllophora, commonly known as the yellow-leafed mume, is a variant of the mume fruit plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to lychees but have a unique, slightly acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
