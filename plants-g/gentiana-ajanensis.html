Gentiana ajanensis

Gentiana ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajan gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana ajanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
