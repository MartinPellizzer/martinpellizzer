Geoffroea inermis

Geoffroea inermis, commonly known as the velvetleaf geoffroea, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Geoffroea inermis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
