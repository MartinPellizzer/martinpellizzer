Gentiana stiriaca var. castanetorum

Gentiana stiriaca var. castanetorum, commonly known as the Castanetto gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
