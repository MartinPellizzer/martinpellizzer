Grevillea dryandroides

Grevillea dryandroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes.

Grevillea dryandroides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
