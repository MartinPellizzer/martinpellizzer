Gentiana sedifolia var. imbricata

Gentiana sedifolia var. imbricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, giving the plant a compact appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
