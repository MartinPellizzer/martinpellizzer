Geranium thunbergii var. albiflorum

Geranium thunbergii var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that ranges from green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium thunbergii var. albiflorum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
