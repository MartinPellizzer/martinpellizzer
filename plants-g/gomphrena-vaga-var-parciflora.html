Gomphrena vaga var. parciflora

Gomphrena vaga var. parciflora, commonly known as the common globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and spherical, pink to red flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

Gomphrena vaga var. parciflora is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
