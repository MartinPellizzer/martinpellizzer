Gomphrena parviceps

Gomphrena parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Gomphrena parviceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
