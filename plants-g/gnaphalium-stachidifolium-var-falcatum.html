Gnaphalium stachidifolium var. falcatum

Gnaphalium stachidifolium var. falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, and it produces a fragrant, woolly coating on its stems and leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dried flower arrangements.
