Glicophyllum vacciniifolium

Glicophyllum vacciniifolium, commonly known as the red-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries are red and fleshy, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Glicophyllum vacciniifolium is native to parts of Asia and is often found in humid, forested environments.
