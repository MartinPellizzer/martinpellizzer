Gratiola missouriana

Gratiola missouriana, commonly known as Missouri yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a deep, fibrous root system. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. It is often found in prairies and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil.

The roots of this plant have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
