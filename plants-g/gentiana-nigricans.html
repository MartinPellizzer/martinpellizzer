Gentiana nigricans

Gentiana nigricans, commonly known as the black gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
