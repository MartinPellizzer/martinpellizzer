Gladiolus florentiae

Gladiolus florentiae, commonly known as the Italian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in a variety of colors. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of brightly colored flowers, often used in floral arrangements.

Gladiolus florentiae thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
