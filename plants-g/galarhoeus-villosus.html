Galarhoeus villosus

Galarhoeus villosus, commonly known as the hairy bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters where light is sufficient for photosynthesis. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hairy structures that aid in adhesion and nutrient absorption.

Galarhoeus villosus plays a unique ecological role in its environment by contributing to the control of microorganism populations.
