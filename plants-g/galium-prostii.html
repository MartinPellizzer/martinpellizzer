Galium prostii

Galium prostii, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium prostii has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
