Galatea intermedia

Galatea intermedia, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities, and its leaves are often used in culinary applications when prepared properly.

Galatea intermedia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
