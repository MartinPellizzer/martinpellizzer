Galium hypotrichium subsp. tomentellum

Galium hypotrichium subsp.

tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are densely covered with soft, white or grayish hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in loose clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and also covered with fine, pubescent hairs.

It thrives in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
