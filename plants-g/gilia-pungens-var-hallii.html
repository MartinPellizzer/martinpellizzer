Gilia pungens var. hallii

Gilia pungens var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
