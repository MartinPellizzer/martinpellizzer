Gentiana verna var. brachyphylloides

Gentiana verna var. brachyphylloides, commonly known as the short-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The variety brachyphylloides is distinguished by its shorter leaves compared to the typical form of G. verna. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
