Gentiana columnae

Gentiana columnae, commonly known as the columnar gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance.

Gentiana columnae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique form.
