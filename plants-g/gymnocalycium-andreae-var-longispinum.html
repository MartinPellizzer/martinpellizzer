Gymnocalycium andreae var. longispinum

Gymnocalycium andreae var. longispinum, commonly known as the long-spined night beauty, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent collections.
