Gladiolus sudanicus

Gladiolus sudanicus, commonly known as the Sudanese gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly Sudan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, and are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
