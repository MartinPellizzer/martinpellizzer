Gossypium barbadense var. integrum

Gossypium barbadense var. integrum, commonly known as the extrafine cotton, is a variety of cotton native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant is an annual herbaceous species that produces fluffy bolls containing the cotton fibers. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for high-quality textile production due to its superior fiber properties.
