Gladiolus psittacinus

Gladiolus psittacinus, commonly known as the scarlet gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape.

Gladiolus psittacinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
