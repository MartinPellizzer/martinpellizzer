Gymnosporia montana

Gymnosporia montana, commonly known as the mountain gymnosporia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and reduced leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, woody fruits. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Gymnosporia montana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
