Gentiana omeiensis

Gentiana omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
