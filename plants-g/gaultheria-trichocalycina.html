Gaultheria trichocalycina

Gaultheria trichocalycina, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are white and bell-shaped, appearing in clusters during late winter to early spring.

Gaultheria trichocalycina is also valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
