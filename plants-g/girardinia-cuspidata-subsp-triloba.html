Girardinia cuspidata subsp. triloba

Girardinia cuspidata subsp.

triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed water hyacinth, is a aquatic plant native to South America. It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often floating on the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a useful indicator species and a potential invasive species in non-native habitats.
