Galera kusukusensis

Galera kusukusensis, commonly known as the Kusukuseni cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in dense, spiny bracts that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in extreme drought conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
