Glycyrrhiza korshinskyi

Glycyrrhiza korshinskyi, commonly known as the Chinese licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem, pinnately compound leaves, and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine.

Glycyrrhiza korshinskyi is valued for its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.
