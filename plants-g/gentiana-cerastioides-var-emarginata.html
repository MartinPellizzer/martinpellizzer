Gentiana cerastioides var. emarginata

Gentiana cerastioides var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and bears the showy inflorescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
