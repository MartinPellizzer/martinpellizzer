Garcinia cornea

Garcinia cornea, commonly known as Kunkune or African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is typically a bright red or yellow drupe, contains a single seed and is known for its high content of xanthones and other bioactive compounds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its fruit and bark.

Garcinia cornea is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
