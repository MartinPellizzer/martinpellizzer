Geonoma paniculigera var. graminifolia

Geonoma paniculigera var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, mat-forming structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually elongated and have a distinct, linear shape, which contributes to its unique appearance.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to humid tropical climates and its role in soil stabilization in its native habitats.
