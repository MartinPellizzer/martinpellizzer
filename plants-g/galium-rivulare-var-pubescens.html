Galium rivulare var. pubescens

Galium rivulare var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a similar pubescent coating.

Galium rivulare var. pubescens thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
