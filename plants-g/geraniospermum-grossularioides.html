Geraniospermum grossularioides

Geraniospermum grossularioides, commonly known as the orange balm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
