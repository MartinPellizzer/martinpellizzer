Grevillea tridentifera var. glaberrima

Grevillea tridentifera var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
