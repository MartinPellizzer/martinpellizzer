Geranium seemannii var. minoriflorum

Geranium seemannii var. minoriflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers are arranged in terminal cymes, and the plant prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moderate climates.
