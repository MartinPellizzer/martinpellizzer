Gentiana triflora subvar. montana

Gentiana triflora subvar. montana, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
