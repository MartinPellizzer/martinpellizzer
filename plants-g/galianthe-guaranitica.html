Galianthe guaranitica

Galianthe guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, greenish flowers that develop into red berries.

Galianthe guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
