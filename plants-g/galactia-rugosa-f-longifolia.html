Galactia rugosa f. longifolia

Galactia rugosa f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved galactia, is a variant of the species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
