Gentianopsis

Gentianopsis, commonly known as the "false gentian," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

These plants are typically found in temperate regions and are characterized by their showy, blue or purple flowers. Gentianopsis species often grow in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky slopes. They have opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, capsule-like fruits.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
