Gossypium barbadense subvar. vitifolium

Gossypium barbadense subvar. vitifolium, commonly known as the vitifolium cotton, is a rare and specialized variety of cotton native to specific regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often hairy leaves, which are typically lighter in color compared to other cotton varieties. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers that are used in textile production, though it is less commonly cultivated than other cotton types. It thrives in warm, arid climates with well-drained soils, and its growth is often limited by specific environmental conditions.

Due to its unique botanical features and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
