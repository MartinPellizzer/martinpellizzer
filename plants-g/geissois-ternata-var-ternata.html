Geissois ternata var. ternata

Geissois ternata var. ternata, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are berries that start green and mature to a deep red or black, containing several seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
