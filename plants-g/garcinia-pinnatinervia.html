Garcinia pinnatinervia

Garcinia pinnatinervia, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with alternating veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pericarp is typically white or yellow, and it contains several seeds.

Garcinia pinnatinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
