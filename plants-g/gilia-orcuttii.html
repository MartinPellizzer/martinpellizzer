Gilia orcuttii

Gilia orcuttii, commonly known as Orcutt's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Gilia orcuttii is adapted to thrive in dry conditions and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
