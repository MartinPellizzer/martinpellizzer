Guarea grossa

Guarea grossa, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland and montane forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
