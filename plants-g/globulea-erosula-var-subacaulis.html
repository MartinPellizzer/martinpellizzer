Globulea erosula var. subacaulis

Globulea erosula var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender globulea, is a unique variant of the globulea plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth form and dense foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
