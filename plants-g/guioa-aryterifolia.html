Guioa aryterifolia

Guioa aryterifolia, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

Guioa aryterifolia is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
