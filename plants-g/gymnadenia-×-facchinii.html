Gymnadenia × facchinii

Gymnadenia × facchinii, commonly known as the hybrid grass pinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and is often found in areas with temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
