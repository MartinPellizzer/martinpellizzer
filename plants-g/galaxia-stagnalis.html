Galaxia stagnalis

Galaxia stagnalis, commonly known as the swamp galaxia, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Galaxia stagnalis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
