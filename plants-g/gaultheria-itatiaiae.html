Gaultheria itatiaiae

Gaultheria itatiaiae, commonly known as the Itatiaia wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the southern regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
