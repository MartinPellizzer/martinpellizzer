Globulea cotyledonis

Globulea cotyledonis, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive globular shape, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Globulea cotyledonis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
