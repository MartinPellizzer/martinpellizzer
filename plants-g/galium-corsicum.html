Galium corsicum

Galium corsicum, commonly known as Corsican bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Galium corsicum is often found in rocky or sandy soils, growing in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
