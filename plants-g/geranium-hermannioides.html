Geranium hermannioides

Geranium hermannioides, commonly known as the Hermann's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
