Garcinia smeathmanii var. exigua

Garcinia smeathmanii var. exigua, commonly known as the dwarf garcinia, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
