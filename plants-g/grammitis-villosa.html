Grammitis villosa

Grammitis villosa, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or on moist rock surfaces, preferring humid and shaded environments. Its fronds are usually light green to reddish in color and have a leathery texture.

Grammitis villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
