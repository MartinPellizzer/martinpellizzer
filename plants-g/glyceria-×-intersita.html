Glyceria × intersita

Glyceria × intersita, commonly known as the hybrid reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of North America.

It is a hybrid between Glyceria acutiflora and Glyceria canadensis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense stands that help stabilize aquatic ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Glyceria × intersita plays an important role in wetland habitats by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
