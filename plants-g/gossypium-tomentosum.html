Gossypium tomentosum

Gossypium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly cotton or silky cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and capsules that contain fluffy, white fibers. It produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its soft, fuzzy texture.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of natural fibers.
