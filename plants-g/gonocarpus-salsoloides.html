Gonocarpus salsoloides

Gonocarpus salsoloides, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are often visited by insects and birds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Gonocarpus salsoloides is well-suited to saline soils and is often found in coastal areas and salt flats.
