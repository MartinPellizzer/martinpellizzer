Gomphrena perennis var. genuina

Gomphrena perennis var. genuina, commonly known as the everlasting flower or "fairy rings," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which retain their color and form even when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
