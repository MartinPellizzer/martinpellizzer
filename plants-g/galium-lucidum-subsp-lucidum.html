Galium lucidum subsp. lucidum

Galium lucidum subsp.

lucidum, commonly known as shining bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairless or sparsely hairy.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and often have a shiny surface.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
