Gentiana forrestii subsp. forrestii

Gentiana forrestii subsp.

forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and adjacent areas of Sichuan and Yunnan in China. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is upright and can reach up to 30 cm in height.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
