Gerascanthus lanceolatus

Gerascanthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Gerascanthus lanceolatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
