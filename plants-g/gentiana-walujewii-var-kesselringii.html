Gentiana walujewii var. kesselringii

Gentiana walujewii var. kesselringii, commonly known as the Kesselring's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, calcareous soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
