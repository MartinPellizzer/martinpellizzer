Gladiolus odoratus

Gladiolus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing vibrant colors such as white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The sweet scent of its flowers makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
