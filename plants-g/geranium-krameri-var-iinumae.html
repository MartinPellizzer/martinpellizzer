Geranium krameri var. iinumae

Geranium krameri var. iinumae, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a compact perennial shrub native to Japan.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
