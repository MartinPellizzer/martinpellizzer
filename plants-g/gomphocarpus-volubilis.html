Gomphocarpus volubilis

Gomphocarpus volubilis, commonly known as the sticky weed or milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves, which are often covered in a sticky substance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is also known for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.
