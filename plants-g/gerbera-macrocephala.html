Gerbera macrocephala

Gerbera macrocephala, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy daisy-like flowers with vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its sturdy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gerbera macrocephala is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms.
