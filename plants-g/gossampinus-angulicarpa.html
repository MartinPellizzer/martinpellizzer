Gossampinus angulicarpa

Gossampinus angulicarpa, commonly known as the angular nut tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its angular, ridged bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, angular pods containing edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it distinct within its genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other members of the Malvaceae family.
