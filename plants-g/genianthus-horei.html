Genianthus horei

Genianthus horei, commonly known as the mountain chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Genianthus horei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
