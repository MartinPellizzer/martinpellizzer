Gastrolobium preissii

Gastrolobium preissii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its habitat includes sandy soils in heathlands and woodlands, and it is adapted to dry conditions.
