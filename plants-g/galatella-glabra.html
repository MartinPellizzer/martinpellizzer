Galatella glabra

Galatella glabra, commonly known as the smooth galatella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Galatella glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
