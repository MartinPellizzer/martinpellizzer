Galpinsia carlabadiana

Galpinsia carlabadiana, commonly known as the Carlabadian flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its hot, dry environment. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet in height.

Galpinsia carlabadiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
