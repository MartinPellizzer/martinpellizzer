Grewia falcistipula

Grewia falcistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are often used as food by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Grewia falcistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
