Galium glaciale var. glaciale

Galium glaciale var. glaciale, commonly known as the glacier bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky, moist environments and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces tiny, clustered fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Galium glaciale var. glaciale is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with limited soil.
