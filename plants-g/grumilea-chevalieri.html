Grumilea chevalieri

Grumilea chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical forests of its native habitat. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in the arid conditions of its environment.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
