Glycosmis mauritiana var. hainanensis

Glycosmis mauritiana var. hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan date plum, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its fruits are edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.
