Grossularia amara

Grossularia amara, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries. The plant belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found at high altitudes.
