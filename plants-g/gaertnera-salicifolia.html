Gaertnera salicifolia

Gaertnera salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf holly or willow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
