Gladiolus laceratus

Gladiolus laceratus, commonly known as the broken-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched stems. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often damaged or split, giving it the common name "broken-leaved.".

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
