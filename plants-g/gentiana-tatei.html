Gentiana tatei

Gentiana tatei, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Gentiana tatei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
