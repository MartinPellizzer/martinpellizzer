Gnaphalium involucratum var. simplex

Gnaphalium involucratum var. simplex, commonly known as the simple leaf everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous root system allows it to survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
