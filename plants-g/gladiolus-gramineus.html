Gladiolus gramineus

Gladiolus gramineus, commonly known as the common gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of colorful, tubular flowers that come in a wide range of hues, including red, pink, white, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gladiolus gramineus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in both landscape design and cut flower production.
