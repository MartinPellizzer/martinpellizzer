Guioa salicifolia

Guioa salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Guioa salicifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its timber.
