Gypsophila acutifolia var. angustifolia

Gypsophila acutifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and ability to add texture and contrast in garden landscapes.
