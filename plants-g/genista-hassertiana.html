Genista hassertiana

Genista hassertiana, commonly known as the Hassert's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

The shrub is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
