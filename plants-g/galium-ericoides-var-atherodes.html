Galium ericoides var. atherodes

Galium ericoides var. atherodes, commonly known as the mossy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically forms dense mats of prostrate stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodlands and along stream banks.
