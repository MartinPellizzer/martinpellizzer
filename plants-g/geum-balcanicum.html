Geum balcanicum

Geum balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan avens, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Geum balcanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
