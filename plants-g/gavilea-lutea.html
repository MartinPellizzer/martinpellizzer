Gavilea lutea

Gavilea lutea, commonly known as the yellow gavilea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.
