Grewia hirsuta f. helicterifolia

Grewia hirsuta f. helicterifolia, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
