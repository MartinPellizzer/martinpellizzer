Garcinia laurifolia

Garcinia laurifolia, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red in color, with a tart and slightly sweet flavor. Garcinia laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruits in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in both horticulture and herbal remedies.
