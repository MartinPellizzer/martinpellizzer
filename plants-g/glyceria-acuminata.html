Glyceria acuminata

Glyceria acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.

Glyceria acuminata is also a food source for various aquatic and terrestrial animals.
