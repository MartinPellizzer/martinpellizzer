Glyceria remota var. japonica

Glyceria remota var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese glyceria, is a grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variant is often found in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
