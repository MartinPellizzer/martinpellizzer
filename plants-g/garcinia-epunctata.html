Garcinia epunctata

Garcinia epunctata, commonly known as the punctate garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive pattern of small, raised dots on the surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright orange or red berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia epunctata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of digestive ailments and skin conditions.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
