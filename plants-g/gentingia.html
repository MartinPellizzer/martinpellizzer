Gentingia

Gentingia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. These plants are typically found in coastal areas and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Gentingia species are perennial herbs that thrive in well-drained soils.

Their botanical features make them valuable subjects for studying plant adaptations to marine climates.
