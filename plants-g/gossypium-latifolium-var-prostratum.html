Gossypium latifolium var. prostratum

Gossypium latifolium var. prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate cottonplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers similar to cotton, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
