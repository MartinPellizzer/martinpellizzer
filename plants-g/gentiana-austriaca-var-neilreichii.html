Gentiana austriaca var. neilreichii

Gentiana austriaca var. neilreichii, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
