Gardenia florida f. oblanceolata

Gardenia florida f. oblanceolata, commonly known as the Florida gardenia, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in humid, acidic soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
