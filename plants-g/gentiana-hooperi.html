Gentiana hooperi

Gentiana hooperi, commonly known as Hooper's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana hooperi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
