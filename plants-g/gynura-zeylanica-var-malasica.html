Gynura zeylanica var. malasica

Gynura zeylanica var. malasica, commonly known as the Malasica false heather, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
