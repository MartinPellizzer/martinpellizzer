Grevillea incurva

Grevillea incurva, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It features striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea incurva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
