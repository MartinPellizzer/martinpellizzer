Geranium niuginiense

Geranium niuginiense, commonly known as the Congo geranium, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium niuginiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
