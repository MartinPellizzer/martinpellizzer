Gymnocalycium curvispinum

Gymnocalycium curvispinum, commonly known as the Curved-spine Cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines that are often curved or hooked. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are typically green and ribbed, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Gymnocalycium curvispinum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
