Grevillea bracteosa subsp. bracteosa

Grevillea bracteosa subsp.

bracteosa, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces striking red or orange cylindrical flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds.

The plant is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it resilient to coastal conditions. It blooms during the spring and summer months, adding vibrant color to coastal landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
