Glycine secunda

Glycine secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped pods. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Glycine secunda has small, pale green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is an annual plant that plays a role in local ecosystems, though it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
