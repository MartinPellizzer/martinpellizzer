Gastrorchis pulchra var. perrieri

Gastrorchis pulchra var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's beautyroot, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

This variant is characterized by its vibrant, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its arid environment.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
