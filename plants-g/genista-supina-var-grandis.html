Genista supina var. grandis

Genista supina var. grandis, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
