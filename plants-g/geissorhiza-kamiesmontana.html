Geissorhiza kamiesmontana

Geissorhiza kamiesmontana, commonly known as the Kamiesmontan firecracker, is a species of bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting black or purple markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated for both horticultural and ornamental purposes.
