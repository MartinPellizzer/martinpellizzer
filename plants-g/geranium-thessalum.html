Geranium thessalum

Geranium thessalum, commonly known as the Balkan geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Geranium thessalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
