Galium boreale subf. hirto-scabrum

Galium boreale subf.

hirto-scabrum, commonly known as the scabrous northern bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine, scabrous hairs.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in boreal and alpine regions.
