Galium rubrum subsp. prostii

Galium rubrum subsp.

prostii, commonly known as redbed galium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color, especially when young.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture. Galium rubrum subsp.

prostii thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
