Gratiola ramosa subsp. drummondii

Gratiola ramosa subsp.

drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, often in open woodlands or desert scrub. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to drought conditions.
