Gentianella amarella subsp. reussii

Gentianella amarella subsp.

reussii, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a member of the Gentianaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is found in mountainous areas of Europe.
