Gypsophila rigida

Gypsophila rigida, commonly known as the rigid gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila rigida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance environments.
