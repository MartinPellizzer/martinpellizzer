Glycine latrobeana

Glycine latrobeana, commonly known as the Australian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to eastern Australia.

It produces fragrant, violet-blue pea-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with 12 to 20 pairs of ovate leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
