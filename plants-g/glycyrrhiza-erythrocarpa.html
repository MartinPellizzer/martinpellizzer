Glycyrrhiza erythrocarpa

Glycyrrhiza erythrocarpa, commonly known as red-root licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its deep red roots and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into legumes containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
