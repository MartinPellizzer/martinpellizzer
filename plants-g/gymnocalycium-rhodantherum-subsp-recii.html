Gymnocalycium rhodantherum subsp. recii

Gymnocalycium rhodantherum subsp.

recii, commonly known as the Red-topped Gymnocalycium, is a species of cactus native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to pale green spines.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
