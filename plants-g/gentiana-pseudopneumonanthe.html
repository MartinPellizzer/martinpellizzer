Gentiana pseudopneumonanthe

Gentiana pseudopneumonanthe, commonly known as the false lungwort gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The genus Gentiana is characterized by its showy, radial flowers and is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
