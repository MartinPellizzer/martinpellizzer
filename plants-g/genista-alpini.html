Genista alpini

Genista alpini, commonly known as the alpine broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista alpini thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.
