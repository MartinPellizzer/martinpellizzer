Grevillea yorkrakinensis

Grevillea yorkrakinensis, commonly known as the York River grevillea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal environments. It is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub, thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
