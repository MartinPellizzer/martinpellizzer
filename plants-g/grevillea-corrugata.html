Grevillea corrugata

Grevillea corrugata, commonly known as the serrated grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, providing a textured appearance, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Grevillea corrugata is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
