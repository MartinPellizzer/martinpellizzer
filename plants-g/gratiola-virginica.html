Gratiola virginica

Gratiola virginica, commonly known as the Virginia goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Gratiola virginica thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
