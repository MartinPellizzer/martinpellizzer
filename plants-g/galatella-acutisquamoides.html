Galatella acutisquamoides

Galatella acutisquamoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved galatella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetland areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galatella acutisquamoides is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
