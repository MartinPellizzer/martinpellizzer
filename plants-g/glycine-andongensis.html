Glycine andongensis

Glycine andongensis, commonly known as the Andong soybean, is a leguminous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. Glycine andongensis is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high protein and oil yield.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
