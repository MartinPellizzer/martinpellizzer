Gerardia afzelia

Gerardia afzelia, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves that are often arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
