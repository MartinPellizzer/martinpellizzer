Gasteranthopsis glabra

Gasteranthopsis glabra, commonly known as the smooth globular orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and small, often white or pale-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually smooth and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
