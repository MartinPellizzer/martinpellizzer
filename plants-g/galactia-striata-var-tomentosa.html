Galactia striata var. tomentosa

Galactia striata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in dry environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
