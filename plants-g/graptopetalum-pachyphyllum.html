Graptopetalum pachyphyllum

Graptopetalum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the stonecrop or jade plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
