Gladiolus fabricii

Gladiolus fabricii, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, with a wide range of hues including red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
