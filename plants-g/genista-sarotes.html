Genista sarotes

Genista sarotes, commonly known as the Turkish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista sarotes is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
