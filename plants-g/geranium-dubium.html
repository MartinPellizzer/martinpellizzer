Geranium dubium

Geranium dubium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
