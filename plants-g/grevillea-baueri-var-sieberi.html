Grevillea baueri var. sieberi

Grevillea baueri var. sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a distinctive blue-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
