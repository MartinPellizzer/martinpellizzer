Gladiolus communis var. byzantinus

Gladiolus communis var. byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The Byzantine gladiolus is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
