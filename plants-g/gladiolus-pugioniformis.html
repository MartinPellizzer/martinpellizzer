Gladiolus pugioniformis

Gladiolus pugioniformis, commonly known as the swordleaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that resemble a sword, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of colorful, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped with three prominent petals and three smaller sepals.

Gladiolus pugioniformis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
