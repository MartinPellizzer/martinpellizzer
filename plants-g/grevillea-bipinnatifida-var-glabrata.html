Grevillea bipinnatifida var. glabrata

Grevillea bipinnatifida var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces vibrant, red-orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

Grevillea bipinnatifida var. glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
