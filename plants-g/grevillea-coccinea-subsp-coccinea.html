Grevillea coccinea subsp. coccinea

Grevillea coccinea subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia. It features vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native flora.
