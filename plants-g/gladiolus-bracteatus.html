Gladiolus bracteatus

Gladiolus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteated gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Gladiolus bracteatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
