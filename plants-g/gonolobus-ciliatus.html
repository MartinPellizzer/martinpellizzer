Gonolobus ciliatus

Gonolobus ciliatus, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by cylindrical, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Gonolobus ciliatus is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
