Graniera

Graniera, commonly known as the "wild fig" or "graniteroot," is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible, fig-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Graniera is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
