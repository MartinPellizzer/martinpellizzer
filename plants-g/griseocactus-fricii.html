Griseocactus fricii

Griseocactus fricii, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, covered in a distinctive grayish-white waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its muted stem color. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Griseocactus fricii is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and fascinating botanical specimen.
