Goniophlebium rhagadiolepis

Goniophlebium rhagadiolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clubmoss, is a species of lycopod that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a branching stem system typical of clubmosses. The plant possesses small, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
