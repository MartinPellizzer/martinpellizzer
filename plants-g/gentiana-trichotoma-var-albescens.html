Gentiana trichotoma var. albescens

Gentiana trichotoma var. albescens, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
