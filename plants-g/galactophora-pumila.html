Galactophora pumila

Galactophora pumila, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is known for its milky sap, which is typical of the Apocynaceae family.

Galactophora pumila is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
