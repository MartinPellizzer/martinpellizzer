Geum rugosum

Geum rugosum, commonly known as rough avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in rough, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough, serrated edge.

Geum rugosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
