Gladiolus tristis var. hastatus

Gladiolus tristis var. hastatus, commonly known as the weeping gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping flower spikes that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with three prominent sepals and two smaller petals.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
