Gnetum gnemon var. majusculum

Gnetum gnemon var. majusculum, commonly known as the giant kapok tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gnetaceae and is characterized by its large, spreading crown and buttressed roots. The plant produces compound leaves with a distinctive palmate shape and emits a strong, unpleasant odor. Its seeds are encased in fibrous pods and are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
