Gluta speciosa

Gluta speciosa, commonly known as the wild silk tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Gluta speciosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
