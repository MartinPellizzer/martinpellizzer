Gladiolus carmineus

Gladiolus carmineus, commonly known as the crimson gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red and pink. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its robust rhizomes that help it thrive in well-drained soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are often used in cut flower displays due to their vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
