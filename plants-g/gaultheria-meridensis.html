Gaultheria meridensis

Gaultheria meridensis, commonly known as the southern wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark red berries that are edible and have a distinctive, spicy flavor. Gaultheria meridensis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the Andean ecosystem.
