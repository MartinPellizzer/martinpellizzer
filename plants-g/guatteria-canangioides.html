Guatteria canangioides

Guatteria canangioides, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.
