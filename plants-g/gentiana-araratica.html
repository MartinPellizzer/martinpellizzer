Gentiana araratica

Gentiana araratica, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
