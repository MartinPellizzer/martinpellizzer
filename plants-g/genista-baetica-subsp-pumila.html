Genista baetica subsp. pumila

Genista baetica subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the Iberian broom, is a shrub native to southern Spain and northern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid conditions.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by narrow, needle-like leaves. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

This subspecies is part of the legume family and has adapted to drought conditions through its deep root system.
