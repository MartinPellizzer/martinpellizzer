Gymnogramma warscewiczii

Gymnogramma warscewiczii, commonly known as the Warshawicz's shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its fronds are leathery and often have a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

Gymnogramma warscewiczii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
