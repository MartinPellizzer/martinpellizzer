Goodyera hachijoensis var. leuconeura

Goodyera hachijoensis var. leuconeura, commonly known as the Hachijo Island lady's slipper, is a rare orchid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, dark green leaves and striking white and pink floral structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are small but intricate, with a unique lip that resembles a slipper, aiding in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited habitat and conservation status, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in botanical research.
