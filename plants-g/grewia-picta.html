Grewia picta

Grewia picta, commonly known as the painted bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with contrasting markings. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Grewia picta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
