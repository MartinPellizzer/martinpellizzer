Gentianella potamophila

Gentianella potamophila, commonly known as the river gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Gentianella potamophila is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
