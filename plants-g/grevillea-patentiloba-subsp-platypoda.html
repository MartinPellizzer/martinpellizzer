Grevillea patentiloba subsp. platypoda

Grevillea patentiloba subsp.

platypoda, commonly known as the platypodas grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, which appear during the warmer months.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for dry gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in native plant collections.
