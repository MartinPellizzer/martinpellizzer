Grewia riparia

Grewia riparia, commonly known as the river bush or red bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or pinkish hue. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Grewia riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
