Gladiolus floribundus subsp. fasciatus

Gladiolus floribundus subsp.

fasciatus, commonly known as the zebra gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of vibrant, colorful flowers.

The most distinctive feature of this subspecies is its boldly striped petals, which resemble a zebra's coat, giving it its common name. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold color and texture.
