Grastidium phragmitoides

Grastidium phragmitoides, commonly known as the broadleaf reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds, exhibiting a tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Grastidium phragmitoides plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
