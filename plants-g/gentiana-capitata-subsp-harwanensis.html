Gentiana capitata subsp. harwanensis

Gentiana capitata subsp.

harwanensis, commonly known as the Harwan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or grassy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in a short inflorescence at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and hardy nature.
