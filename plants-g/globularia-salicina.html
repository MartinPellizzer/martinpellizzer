Globularia salicina

Globularia salicina, commonly known as the pink globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a dense, rounded growth habit with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
