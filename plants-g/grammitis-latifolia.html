Grammitis latifolia

Grammitis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf grammitis, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually broad and have a soft, velvety texture.

Grammitis latifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
