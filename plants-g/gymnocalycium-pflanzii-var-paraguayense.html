Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. paraguayense

Gymnocalycium pflanzii var. paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to absorb water efficiently. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor, making it a valuable resource in its native habitat.
