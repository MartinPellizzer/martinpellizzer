Geoffroea pubescens

Geoffroea pubescens, commonly known as the silky ash, is a small evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

Geoffroea pubescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
