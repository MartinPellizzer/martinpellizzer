Galium caspicum

Galium caspicum, commonly known as Caspian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium caspicum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
