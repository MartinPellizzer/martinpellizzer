Grammitis meijer-dreesii

Grammitis meijer-dreesii, commonly known as the Meijer-Dreesii fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis meijer-dreesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
