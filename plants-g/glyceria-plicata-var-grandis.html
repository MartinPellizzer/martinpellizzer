Glyceria plicata var. grandis

Glyceria plicata var. grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Glyceria plicata var. grandis plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
