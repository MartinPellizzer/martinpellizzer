Gonostemon grandiflorus var. fulvus

Gonostemon grandiflorus var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden flame heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, often with a reddish or brownish hue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive, slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and are known for their pleasant, aromatic scent.
