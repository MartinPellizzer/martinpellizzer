Gentianopsis blepharophora

Gentianopsis blepharophora, commonly known as the eye-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white vein pattern. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are characterized by their deep blue color and tubular shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
