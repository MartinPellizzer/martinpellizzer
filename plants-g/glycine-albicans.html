Glycine albicans

Glycine albicans, commonly known as the white soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, round seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Glycine albicans is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
