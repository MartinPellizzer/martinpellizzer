Gentiana prostrata subsp. nutans

Gentiana prostrata subsp.

nutans, commonly known as the prostrate gentian, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas.
