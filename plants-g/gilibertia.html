Gilibertia

Gilibertia, commonly known as the "buttercup plant," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of the buttercup. Gilibertia species are often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Some species are used in traditional medicine, while others are valued for their ornamental appeal.
