Glyceria alnasteretum

Glyceria alnasteretum, commonly known as the rivergrass, is a perennial grass native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found along streams, rivers, and marshes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy coating.

Glyceria alnasteretum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
