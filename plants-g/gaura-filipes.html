Gaura filipes

Gaura filipes, commonly known as the slender gaura, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Gaura filipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
