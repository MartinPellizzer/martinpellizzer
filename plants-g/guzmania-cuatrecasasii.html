Guzmania cuatrecasasii

Guzmania cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas guzmania, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can last for several months. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small invertebrates. Its foliage is often adorned with striking red, orange, or yellow hues, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

Guzmania cuatrecasasii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
