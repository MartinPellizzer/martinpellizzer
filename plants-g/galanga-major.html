Galanga major

Galanga major, commonly known as greater galangal, is a rhizomatous herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, knotted rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its essential oils contain compounds such as galangin and cineole, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Galanga major thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its culinary and therapeutic uses.
