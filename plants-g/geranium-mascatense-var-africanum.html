Geranium mascatense var. africanum

Geranium mascatense var. africanum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
