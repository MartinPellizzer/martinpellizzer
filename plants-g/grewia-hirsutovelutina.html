Grewia hirsutovelutina

Grewia hirsutovelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Grewia hirsutovelutina is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
