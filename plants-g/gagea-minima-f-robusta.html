Gagea minima f. robusta

Gagea minima f. robusta, commonly known as the robust winter aconite, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sturdy bulbs and early blooming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing before the leaves emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowering period.

Gagea minima f. robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in woodland settings.
