Glycosmis longisepala

Glycosmis longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically covered in a fine, whitish powder. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are fragrant and have five sepals that are longer than the petals, giving the plant its scientific name.
