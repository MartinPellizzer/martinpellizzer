Goodenia hederacea subsp. hederacea

Goodenia hederacea subsp.

hederacea, commonly known as the trailing goodenia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Australia. It features wiry, creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a hairy texture. Small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
