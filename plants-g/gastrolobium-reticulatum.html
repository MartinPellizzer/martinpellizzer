Gastrolobium reticulatum

Gastrolobium reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as Western Australia and South Australia. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, which is a key botanical characteristic. Its yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Gastrolobium reticulatum is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
