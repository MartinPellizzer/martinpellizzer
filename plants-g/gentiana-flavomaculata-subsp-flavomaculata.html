Gentiana flavomaculata subsp. flavomaculata

Gentiana flavomaculata subsp.

flavomaculata, commonly known as the yellow-spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and adorned with yellow spots.

The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
