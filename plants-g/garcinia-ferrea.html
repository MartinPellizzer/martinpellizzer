Garcinia ferrea

Garcinia ferrea, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia ferrea thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
