Gerbera aberdarica

Gerbera aberdarica, commonly known as the Aberdare gerbera, is a flowering plant native to the Aberdare Range in Kenya.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gerbera aberdarica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
