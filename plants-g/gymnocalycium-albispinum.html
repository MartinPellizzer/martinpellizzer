Gymnocalycium albispinum

Gymnocalycium albispinum, commonly known as the White-spined Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, bristly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green stems. Gymnocalycium albispinum is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
