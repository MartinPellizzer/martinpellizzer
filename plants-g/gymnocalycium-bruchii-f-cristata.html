Gymnocalycium bruchii f. cristata

Gymnocalycium bruchii f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate moon cactus, is a unique hybrid plant that combines the features of a Gymnocalycium species with a Hylocereus cactus.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribless stem that forms a distinctive "cristate" or crest-like structure. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in collections and gardens.
