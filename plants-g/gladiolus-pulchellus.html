Gladiolus pulchellus

Gladiolus pulchellus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces spikes of colorful, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 12 to 24 inches, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a bedding plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value in smaller spaces.
