Galium rubioides var. latifolium

Galium rubioides var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf woodcrushers, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are important for wildlife.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
