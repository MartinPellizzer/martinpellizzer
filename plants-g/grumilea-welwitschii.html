Grumilea welwitschii

Grumilea welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique and ancient plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant has a thick, woody trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in the arid environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is considered a living fossil due to its evolutionary history.

Grumilea welwitschii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
