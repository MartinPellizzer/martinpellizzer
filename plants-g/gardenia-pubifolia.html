Gardenia pubifolia

Gardenia pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia pubifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
