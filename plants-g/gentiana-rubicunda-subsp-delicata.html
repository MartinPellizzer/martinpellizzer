Gentiana rubicunda subsp. delicata

Gentiana rubicunda subsp.

delicata, commonly known as the delicate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and slightly hairy.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
