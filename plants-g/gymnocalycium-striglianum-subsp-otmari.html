Gymnocalycium striglianum subsp. otmari

Gymnocalycium striglianum subsp.

otmari, commonly known as the Otmari moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Central and South America. It is a small, globular cactus with a distinctive, flattened stem that often exhibits a variegated coloration.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its fleshy, spiny stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
