Galium incanum subsp. creticum

Galium incanum subsp.

creticum, commonly known as the Cretan bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete. It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance. Galium incanum subsp.

creticum is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.
