Gastonia cutispongia

Gastonia cutispongia, commonly known as the velvet spongeplant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its soft, spongy texture and translucent appearance.

It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and wetlands. The plant's structure consists of a central stem with branching, sponge-like leaves that provide a habitat for various microorganisms. Its ability to absorb water and nutrients efficiently makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions, posing challenges to native plant communities.
