Galium corrudifolium

Galium corrudifolium, commonly known as the redstem mouse-ear.

This perennial herb is native to the western United States and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
