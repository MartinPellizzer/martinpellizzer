Geum lechlerianum

Geum lechlerianum, commonly known as the showy mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
