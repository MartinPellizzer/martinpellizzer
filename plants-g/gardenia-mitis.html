Gardenia mitis

Gardenia mitis, commonly known as the Hawaiian gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The flowers are often used in lei-making and have cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions.
