Gerascanthus rotatus

Gerascanthus rotatus, commonly known as the spiral leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its twisted, spiraling stems that resemble a helix, which helps it maximize sunlight exposure in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Gerascanthus rotatus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
