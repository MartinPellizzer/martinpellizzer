Grindelia hirsutula var. revoluta

Grindelia hirsutula var. revoluta, commonly known as the roughleaf gumweed, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that are often curled or rolled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system and drought tolerance make it well adapted to arid conditions.
