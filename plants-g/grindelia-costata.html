Grindelia costata

Grindelia costata, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and yellow-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize new areas.

Grindelia costata is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
