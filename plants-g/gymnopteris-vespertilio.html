Gymnopteris vespertilio

Gymnopteris vespertilio, commonly known as the evening fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Gymnopteris vespertilio reproduces via spores rather than seeds, with the spore-producing structures known as sori located on the underside of its fronds.
