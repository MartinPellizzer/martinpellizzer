Glossostigma diandrum

Glossostigma diandrum, commonly known as the Japanese water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and streams. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor securely in substrates.

Glossostigma diandrum is popular in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
