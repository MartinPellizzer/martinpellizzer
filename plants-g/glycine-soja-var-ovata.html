Glycine soja var. ovata

Glycine soja var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant characterized by its larger, more ovate leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to East Asia, particularly China. The plant is an annual legume that produces small, yellow flowers and pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a robust root system that aids in nitrogen fixation, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the seeds are a source of protein and oil.
