Galenia glauca

Galenia glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its blue to purple flower heads are often surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.

Galenia glauca is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
