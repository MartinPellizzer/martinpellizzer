Gymnocalycium hossei

Gymnocalycium hossei, commonly known as the Hosse's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Gymnocalycium hossei is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
