Gaillardia coahuilensis

Gaillardia coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Coahuila, Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange, with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds in a fluffy, white pappus.

Gaillardia coahuilensis is an important component of the local flora and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
