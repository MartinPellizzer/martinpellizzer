Gabunia eglandulosa var. macrocalyx

Gabunia eglandulosa var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered gabunia, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
