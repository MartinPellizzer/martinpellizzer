Gravesia hispida

Gravesia hispida, commonly known as the hairy gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Gravesia hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
