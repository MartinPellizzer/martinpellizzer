Gaura grandiflora

Gaura grandiflora, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gaura grandiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
