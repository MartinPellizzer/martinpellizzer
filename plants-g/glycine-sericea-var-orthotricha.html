Glycine sericea var. orthotricha

Glycine sericea var. orthotricha, commonly known as silky vetch, is a variant of the common vetch species.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
