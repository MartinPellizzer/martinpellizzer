Gynandropsis triphylla

Gynandropsis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
