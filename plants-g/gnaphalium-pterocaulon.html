Gnaphalium pterocaulon

Gnaphalium pterocaulon, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils in temperate regions.
