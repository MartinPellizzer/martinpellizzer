Gypsophila paniculata

Gypsophila paniculata, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila paniculata is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like ladybugs and hoverflies.
