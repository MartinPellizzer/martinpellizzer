Gouldia quadrangularis

Gouldia quadrangularis, commonly known as the diamond herb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gouldia quadrangularis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
