Gentiana pentasticta

Gentiana pentasticta, commonly known as the five-streaked gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are hairy and erect.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes, and they attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
