Genista hassertiana subsp. hassertiana

Genista hassertiana subsp.

hassertiana, commonly known as the Hassert's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
