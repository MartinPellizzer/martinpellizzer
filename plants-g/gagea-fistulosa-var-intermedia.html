Gagea fistulosa var. intermedia

Gagea fistulosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate grapeleaf, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland or open meadow habitats. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can survive periods of drought.

It is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ecological benefits to pollinators.
