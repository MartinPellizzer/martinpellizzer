Geum × billietii

Geum × billietii, commonly known as the hybrid garden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features palmate leaves with three to five lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Geum × billietii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
