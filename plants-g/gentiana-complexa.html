Gentiana complexa

Gentiana complexa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The genus Gentiana is known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers, and Gentiana complexa is a notable member of this diverse group.
