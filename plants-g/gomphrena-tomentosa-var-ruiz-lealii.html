Gomphrena tomentosa var. ruiz-lealii

Gomphrena tomentosa var. ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spherical, pink to red flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a container plant.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
