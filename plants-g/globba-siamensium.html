Globba siamensium

Globba siamensium, commonly known as the Siam ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

Globba siamensium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
