Gordonia wrightii

Gordonia wrightii, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
