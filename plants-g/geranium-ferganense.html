Geranium ferganense

Geranium ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana geranium, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of bright red or pink blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
