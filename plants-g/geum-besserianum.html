Geum besserianum

Geum besserianum, commonly known as the lesser barrenstar, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Geum besserianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
