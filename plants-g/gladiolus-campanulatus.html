Gladiolus campanulatus

Gladiolus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
