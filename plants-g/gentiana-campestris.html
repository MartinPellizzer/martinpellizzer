Gentiana campestris

Gentiana campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana campestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
