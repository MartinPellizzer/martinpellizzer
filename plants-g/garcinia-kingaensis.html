Garcinia kingaensis

Garcinia kingaensis, commonly known as the Kibale Garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Garcinia kingaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
