Genista lobelii var. tejedensis

Genista lobelii var. tejedensis, commonly known as the Tejed buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, dry environments. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, while its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
