Gnaphalium micropoides

Gnaphalium micropoides, commonly known as the dwarf feather grass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically appear in clusters. The plant is native to regions of Australia and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Its delicate appearance and ability to form dense mats make it a useful plant for erosion control in suitable habitats.
