Genista ardoinoi

Genista ardoinoi, commonly known as the Ardoin's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its shrub form typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with dense, thorny branches.

Genista ardoinoi is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
