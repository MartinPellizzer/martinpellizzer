Gladiolus martleyi

Gladiolus martleyi, commonly known as the Martley gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive three-lobed petal structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in floral arrangements and garden displays.
