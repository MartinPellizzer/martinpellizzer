Gelseminum nodosum

Gelseminum nodosum, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers have five petals and are often visited by nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Gelseminum nodosum is also noted for containing toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans.
