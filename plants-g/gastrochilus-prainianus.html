Gastrochilus prainianus

Gastrochilus prainianus, commonly known as the Prain's stomach lip, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Gastrochilus prainianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its distinctive foliage and subtle floral display.
