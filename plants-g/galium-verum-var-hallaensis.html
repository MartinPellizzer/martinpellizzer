Galium verum var. hallaensis

Galium verum var. hallaensis, commonly known as golden cinquefoil, is a variant of the common wood sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow flowers and five-lobed leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
