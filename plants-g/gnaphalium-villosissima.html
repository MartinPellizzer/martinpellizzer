Gnaphalium villosissima

Gnaphalium villosissima, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a low-growing habit with a sprawling stem that produces numerous flowering shoots.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
