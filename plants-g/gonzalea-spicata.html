Gonzalea spicata

Gonzalea spicata, commonly known as the spiky gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, spiky flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Gonzalea spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
