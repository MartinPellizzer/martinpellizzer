Gymnadenia conopsea monstr. foliosa

Gymnadenia conopsea monstr.

foliosa, commonly known as the showy goat's beard, is a striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of large, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, beard-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
