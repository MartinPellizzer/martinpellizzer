Grindelia inornata var. angusta

Grindelia inornata var. angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bitterweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and often grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American communities.
