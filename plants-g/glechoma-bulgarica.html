Glechoma bulgarica

Glechoma bulgarica, commonly known as Bulgarian ground-ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma bulgarica is known for its creeping growth habit and ability to spread via stolons, making it a common ground cover in suitable habitats.
