Grastidium vanderwateri

Grastidium vanderwateri, commonly known as the Vanderwater's ghostplant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that appear almost ethereal in sunlight. The plant produces tiny, pale blue flowers during the brief rainy season, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme environments.

Conservation efforts are ongoing due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and climate change.
