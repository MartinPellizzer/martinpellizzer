Grammitis micula

Grammitis micula, commonly known as the slender grammitis, is a fern species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 20 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori. Grammitis micula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.
