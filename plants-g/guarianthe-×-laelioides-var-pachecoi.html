Guarianthe × laelioides var. pachecoi

Guarianthe × laelioides var. pachecoi, commonly known as the Pacheco's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are borne on long, pendulous inflorescences, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The variety is valued for its durability and ability to thrive under proper care in both greenhouse and home settings.
