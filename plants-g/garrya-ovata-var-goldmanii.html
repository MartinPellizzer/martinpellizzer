Garrya ovata var. goldmanii

Garrya ovata var. goldmanii, commonly known as the Goldman's silverberry, is a variant of the silverberry plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fine, silvery foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are popular among birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
