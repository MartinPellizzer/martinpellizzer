Galium campanulatum var. glabrum

Galium campanulatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy but are glabrous (smooth) in this variety. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
