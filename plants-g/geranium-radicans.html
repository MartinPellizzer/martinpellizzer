Geranium radicans

Geranium radicans, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, slightly spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium radicans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
