Garcinia pynaertii

Garcinia pynaertii, commonly known as the Cameroon garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including high levels of hydroxycitric acid.

Garcinia pynaertii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
