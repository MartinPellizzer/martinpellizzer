Gnidia vesiculosa

Gnidia vesiculosa, commonly known as the bubble bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, inflated, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gnidia vesiculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
