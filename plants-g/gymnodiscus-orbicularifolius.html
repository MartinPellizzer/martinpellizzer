Gymnodiscus orbicularifolius

Gymnodiscus orbicularifolius, commonly known as the orbicular foliate diatom, is a unicellular marine organism belonging to the class Bacillariophyceae.

It is characterized by its unique, symmetrical shell composed of silica, which forms intricate patterns of pores and ridges. The species is typically found in coastal waters and estuaries, where it plays a role in nutrient cycling and serves as a food source for various marine organisms. Its morphology is highly adapted to its aquatic environment, with a streamlined shape that aids in buoyancy.

The study of Gymnodiscus orbicularifolius contributes to understanding diatom ecology and the impacts of environmental changes on marine ecosystems.
