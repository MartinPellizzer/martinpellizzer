Garcinia staudtii

Garcinia staudtii, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain hard, acidic seeds. Garcinia staudtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
