Gossypium punctatum var. jamaica

Gossypium punctatum var. jamaica, commonly known as Jamaican cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its soft, fluffy fibers that are harvested from the seed pods. The plant typically grows in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

This variety is valued for both its fiber and ornamental qualities in horticulture.
