Greenella

Greenella, commonly known as the green alga, is a genus of aquatic and soil-dwelling green algae belonging to the class Chlorophyceae.

These organisms are characterized by their simple, unicellular or filamentous structure and their ability to perform photosynthesis using chlorophyll. Greenella species are often found in freshwater environments, where they contribute to primary production and serve as a food source for various aquatic organisms. They play a vital role in nutrient cycling and maintaining the ecological balance of their habitats.

Some species of Greenella are also used in biotechnological applications due to their ability to produce bioactive compounds.
