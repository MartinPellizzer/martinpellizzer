Grevillea monslacana

Grevillea monslacana, commonly known as the Wimmera grevillea, is a shrub native to western Victoria, Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically red or reddish-brown in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, cylindrical flowers that are usually red or orange, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy gardens.

Grevillea monslacana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in native landscaping.
