Gentiana stellarioides

Gentiana stellarioides, commonly known as star-like gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in a star-like pattern, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and woodlands.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
