Grevillea manglesioides

Grevillea manglesioides, commonly known as the Mangles' grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Grevillea manglesioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
