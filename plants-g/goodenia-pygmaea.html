Goodenia pygmaea

Goodenia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf goodenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, featuring small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a short flowering period during the warmer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
