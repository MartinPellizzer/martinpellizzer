Gentianopsis longistyla

Gentianopsis longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by showy, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are usually blue or violet in color.

The plant is noted for its long, slender style, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.
