Gentiana handeliana var. brevisepala

Gentiana handeliana var. brevisepala, commonly known as the short-petioled gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
