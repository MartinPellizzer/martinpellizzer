Grewia bicolor var. canescens

Grewia bicolor var. canescens, commonly known as the white-flowered bushwillow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
