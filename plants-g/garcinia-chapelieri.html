Garcinia chapelieri

Garcinia chapelieri, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a thick, purple rind and a segmented, white or pinkish pulp. The fruit is rich in xanthones, which are bioactive compounds with potential health benefits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
