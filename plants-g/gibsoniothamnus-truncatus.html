Gibsoniothamnus truncatus

Gibsoniothamnus truncatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrobrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and desert plains. Its structure is compact, with multiple stems that often form dense clusters.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
