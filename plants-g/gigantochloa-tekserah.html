Gigantochloa tekserah

Gigantochloa tekserah, commonly known as the Sarawak giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Borneo.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hillside areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is valued for both ecological and commercial purposes.
