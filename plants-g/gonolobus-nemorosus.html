Gonolobus nemorosus

Gonolobus nemorosus, commonly known as the forest morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
