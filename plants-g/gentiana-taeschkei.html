Gentiana taeschkei

Gentiana taeschkei, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high mountain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, while its stems are erect and usually unbranched.

Gentiana taeschkei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
