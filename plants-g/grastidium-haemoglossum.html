Grastidium haemoglossum

Grastidium haemoglossum, commonly known as the crimson-tongued orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vivid red petals with a distinctive blue-veined pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring, producing a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, Grastidium haemoglossum is considered a conservation concern.
