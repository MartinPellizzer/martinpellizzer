Githopsis pulchella var. glabra

Githopsis pulchella var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth githopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in smooth, glabrous bark, giving it its varietal name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers, which appear in clusters, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
