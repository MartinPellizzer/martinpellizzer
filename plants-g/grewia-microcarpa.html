Grewia microcarpa

Grewia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature.

Grewia microcarpa is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
