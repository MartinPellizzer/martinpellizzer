Gnidia foliosa

Gnidia foliosa, commonly known as the silverleaf gnidia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple and elliptical, with a rough texture and a distinctive silver hue.

Gnidia foliosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
