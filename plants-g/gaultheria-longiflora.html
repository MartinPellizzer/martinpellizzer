Gaultheria longiflora

Gaultheria longiflora, commonly known as the evergreen trailing cranberry bush, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its berries contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their analgesic properties.

Gaultheria longiflora plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various animals.
