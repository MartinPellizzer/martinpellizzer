Galeopsis × dubioides

Galeopsis × dubioides, commonly known as the hybrid hedge nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Galeopsis × dubioides thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

It is a low-growing plant that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.
