Guapira obtusata subsp. obtusata

Guapira obtusata subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cupania obtusata*, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy aril, which is often consumed by birds and mammals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
