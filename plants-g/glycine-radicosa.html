Glycine radicosa

Glycine radicosa, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Glycine radicosa is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a sprawling or upright growth habit.
