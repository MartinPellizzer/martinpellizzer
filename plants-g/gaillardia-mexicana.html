Gaillardia mexicana

Gaillardia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy blooms from late spring to early fall.

Gaillardia mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
