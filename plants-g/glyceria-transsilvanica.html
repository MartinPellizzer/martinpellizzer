Glyceria transsilvanica

Glyceria transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond margins, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate climates.

Glyceria transsilvanica plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
