Goodyera viridiflora var. viridiflora

Goodyera viridiflora var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered maidenhair fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a dense, low-growing rosette of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and subtle floral display make it a prized ornamental plant in horticulture.
