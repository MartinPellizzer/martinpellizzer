Gynostemma integrifoliolum

Gynostemma integrifoliolum, commonly known as Chinese ginseng or "southern ginseng," is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with smooth, entire margins and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its purported medicinal properties, similar to true ginseng.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and modern herbal research.
