Grusonia thurberi

Grusonia thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's grusonia, is a rare flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
