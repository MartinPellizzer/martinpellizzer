Geraniospermum apiifolium

Geraniospermum apiifolium, commonly known as the leafy geraniosperm, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the carrot plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Geraniospermum apiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
