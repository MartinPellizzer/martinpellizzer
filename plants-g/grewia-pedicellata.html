Grewia pedicellata

Grewia pedicellata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or wild mangosteen, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red or orange fruits that are edible. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
