Goniopteris yanachagae

Goniopteris yanachagae, commonly known as the Yanachaga fern, is a species of fern native to the cloud forests of the Peruvian Andes.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has a distinctive, pinnately compound structure with long, slender pinnae that are often slightly curved. It thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and partial shade.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
