Grindelia arizonica var. stenophylla

Grindelia arizonica var. stenophylla, commonly known as the Arizona gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silvery, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deeper water sources.

The species is also notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in Native American herbal medicine.
