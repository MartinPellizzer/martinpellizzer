Glaucium corniculatum var. fulvum

Glaucium corniculatum var. fulvum, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray hue on the upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
