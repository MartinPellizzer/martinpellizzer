Garcinia cambogioides var. cambogioides

Garcinia cambogioides var. cambogioides, commonly known as Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
