Galium cinereum subvar. pallidum

Galium cinereum subvar. pallidum, commonly known as silverweed or pale silverweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, sprawling stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive look.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
