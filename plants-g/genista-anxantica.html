Genista anxantica

Genista anxantica, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and along roadsides. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often have a silvery or pale green appearance.

Genista anxantica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
