Gymnocalycium gibbosum f. cerebriforme

Gymnocalycium gibbosum f. cerebriforme, commonly known as the brain cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its rounded, segmented stems that resemble a brain, giving it its common name. The plant produces white flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
