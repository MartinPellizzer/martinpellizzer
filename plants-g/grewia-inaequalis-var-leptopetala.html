Grewia inaequalis var. leptopetala

Grewia inaequalis var. leptopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sour fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature and is edible, though it has a tart flavor.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
