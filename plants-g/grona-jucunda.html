Grona jucunda

Grona jucunda, commonly known as the happy grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
