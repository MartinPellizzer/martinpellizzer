Gnaphalium chrysocoma

Gnaphalium chrysocoma, commonly known as golden feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers that are clustered in heads, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Gnaphalium chrysocoma is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
