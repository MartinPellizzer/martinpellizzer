Gigantochloa albovestita

Gigantochloa albovestita, commonly known as the white-haired bamboo, is a tall perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered with a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it forms dense stands. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a pale green color.

This bamboo species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
