Garcinia polyantha

Garcinia polyantha, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that encase a single seed, and its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it is valued for both its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Garcinia polyantha is also noted for its potential antioxidant benefits and has been studied for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
