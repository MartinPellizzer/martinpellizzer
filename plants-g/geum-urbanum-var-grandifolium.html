Geum urbanum var. grandifolium

Geum urbanum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the greater garden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with the upper leaves being more lanceolate. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
