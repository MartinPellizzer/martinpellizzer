Geranium purpureum subsp. forsteri

Geranium purpureum subsp.

forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive red throat. Its leaves are typically rounded or ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
