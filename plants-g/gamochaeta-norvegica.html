Gamochaeta norvegica

Gamochaeta norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian gamochaeta, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.

Gamochaeta norvegica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
