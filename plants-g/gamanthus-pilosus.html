Gamanthus pilosus

Gamanthus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy gamanthus, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in dense, woolly hair that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
