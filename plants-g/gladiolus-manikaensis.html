Gladiolus manikaensis

Gladiolus manikaensis, commonly known as the Manikaensis gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers typically come in shades of white, pink, and red, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections, though its natural habitat remains relatively restricted.
