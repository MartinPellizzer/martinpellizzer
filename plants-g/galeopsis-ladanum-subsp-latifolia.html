Galeopsis ladanum subsp. latifolia

Galeopsis ladanum subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf horseweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild sedative properties.
