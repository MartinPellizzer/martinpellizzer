Galium obtusum var. ramosum

Galium obtusum var. ramosum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, often hairy stems that branch profusely. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
