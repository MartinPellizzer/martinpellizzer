Gymnocalycium orientale subsp. kainradliae

Gymnocalycium orientale subsp.

kainradliae, commonly known as the Kainradliae moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern Chile. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
