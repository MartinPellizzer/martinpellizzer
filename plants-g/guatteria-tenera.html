Guatteria tenera

Guatteria tenera, commonly known as the Mexican wax fruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by yellow, waxy berries. Its fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Guatteria tenera thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
