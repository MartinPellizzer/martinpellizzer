Grewia obtusa

Grewia obtusa, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically sour and contain a single seed, making them an important food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
