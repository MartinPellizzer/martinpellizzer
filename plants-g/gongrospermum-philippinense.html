Gongrospermum philippinense

Gongrospermum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber and food.
