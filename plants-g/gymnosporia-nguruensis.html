Gymnosporia nguruensis

Gymnosporia nguruensis, commonly known as the Nguru thornveld bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, elongated pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Gymnosporia nguruensis plays a role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
