Glycine vestita

Glycine vestita, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications. Glycine vestita is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
