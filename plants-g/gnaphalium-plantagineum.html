Gnaphalium plantagineum

Gnaphalium plantagineum, commonly known as the silverleaf plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, silver-white fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
