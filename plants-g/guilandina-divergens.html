Guilandina divergens

Guilandina divergens, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its long, serpentine pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forests.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
