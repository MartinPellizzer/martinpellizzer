Geranium nepalense f. japonicum

Geranium nepalense f. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a compact perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems with ovate, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both garden and horticultural use.
