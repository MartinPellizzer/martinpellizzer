Gaillardia doniana var. discoidea

Gaillardia doniana var. discoidea, commonly known as the discoid gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by bright, papery petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often gray-green in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
