Galium spurium var. vaillantii

Galium spurium var. vaillantii, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairless or sparsely hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
