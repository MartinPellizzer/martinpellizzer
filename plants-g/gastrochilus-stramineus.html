Gastrochilus stramineus

Gastrochilus stramineus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, pale pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip that is usually lighter in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
