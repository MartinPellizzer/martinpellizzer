Gentiana septemfida var. cordifolia

Gentiana septemfida var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lanceolate to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
