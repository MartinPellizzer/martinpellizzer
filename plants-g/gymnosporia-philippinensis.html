Gymnosporia philippinensis

Gymnosporia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine burr acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and compound leaves. The plant is notable for its spiny stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in a woody pod, and the plant has been used traditionally for various purposes.
