Gaillardia pulchella var. drummondii

Gaillardia pulchella var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, upright leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early fall.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to wet conditions.
