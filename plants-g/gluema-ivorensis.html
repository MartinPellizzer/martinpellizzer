Gluema ivorensis

Gluema ivorensis, commonly known as the Ivorian glume grass, is a perennial grass species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
