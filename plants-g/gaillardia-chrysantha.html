Gaillardia chrysantha

Gaillardia chrysantha, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, daisy-like flowers with bright orange and yellow petals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Gaillardia chrysantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
