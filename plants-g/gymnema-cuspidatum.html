Gymnema cuspidatum

Gymnema cuspidatum, commonly known as the Indian bitter leaf, is a woody climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive bitter taste. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema cuspidatum is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

It contains compounds such as gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.
