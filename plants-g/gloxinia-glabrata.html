Gloxinia glabrata

Gloxinia glabrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. Gloxinia glabrata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
