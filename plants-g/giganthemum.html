Giganthemum

Giganthemum, commonly known as the giant rock cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, flattened stems that can grow up to several meters in diameter. The plant's thick, fleshy leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Giganthemum produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and protected species in its native range.
