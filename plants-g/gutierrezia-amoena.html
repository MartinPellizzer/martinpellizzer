Gutierrezia amoena

Gutierrezia amoena, commonly known as the silverleaf brittlebush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is known for its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia amoena is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
