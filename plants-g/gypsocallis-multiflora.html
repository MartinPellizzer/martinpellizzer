Gypsocallis multiflora

Gypsocallis multiflora, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Gypsocallis multiflora is often found in rocky soils and is a symbol of resilience in desert ecosystems.
