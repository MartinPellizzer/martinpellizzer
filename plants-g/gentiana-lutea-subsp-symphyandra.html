Gentiana lutea subsp. symphyandra

Gentiana lutea subsp.

symphyandra, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
