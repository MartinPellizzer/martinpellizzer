Gypsophila fastigiata subsp. visianii

Gypsophila fastigiata subsp.

visianii, commonly known as the upright gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and lilac.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact, bushy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
