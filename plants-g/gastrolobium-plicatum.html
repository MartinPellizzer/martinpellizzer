Gastrolobium plicatum

Gastrolobium plicatum, commonly known as the zig-zag wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zig-zag stems and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Gastrolobium plicatum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
