Grastidium densifolium

Grastidium densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved grastidium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in late spring. Its leaves contain high concentrations of flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Grastidium densifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
