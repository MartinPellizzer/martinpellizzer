Guzmania sprucei

Guzmania sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that resembles a crown of flowers. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insects. Guzmania sprucei thrives in high humidity and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
