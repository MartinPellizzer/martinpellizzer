Gnaphalium congestum

Gnaphalium congestum, commonly known as the clustered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense heads. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers are typically visited by insects, contributing to its role in local pollination networks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
