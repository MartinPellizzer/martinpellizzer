Glycosmis mauritiana var. mauritiana

Glycosmis mauritiana var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Java date tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, date-like fruits. The fruits are typically yellow or orange when ripe and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
