Gastrochilus nanchuanensis

Gastrochilus nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Nanchuan stone orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in China's Sichuan province.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, are adorned with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
