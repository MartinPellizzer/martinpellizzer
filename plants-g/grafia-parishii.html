Grafia parishii

Grafia parishii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Grafia parishii is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in rocky or clay-rich soils.
