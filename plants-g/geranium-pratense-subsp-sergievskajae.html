Geranium pratense subsp. sergievskajae

Geranium pratense subsp.

sergievskajae, commonly known as the Serpukhov geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, forming showy clusters at the top of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
