Griffinia leucantha

Griffinia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered griffinia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, adding to its ornamental value.

Griffinia leucantha is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period.
