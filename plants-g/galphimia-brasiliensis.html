Galphimia brasiliensis

Galphimia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, which are adapted to thrive in humid environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

Galphimia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
