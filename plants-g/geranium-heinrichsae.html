Geranium heinrichsae

Geranium heinrichsae, commonly known as the Henry's geranium, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and a velvety texture.

Geranium heinrichsae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
