Glyptopetalum subcordatum

Glyptopetalum subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, with a unique floral structure that includes a spurred lip. Glyptopetalum subcordatum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

It is often found in shaded, forested areas at high elevations.
