Genista villosa

Genista villosa, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Genista villosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
