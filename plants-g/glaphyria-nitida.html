Glaphyria nitida

Glaphyria nitida, commonly known as the silky glaphyria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
