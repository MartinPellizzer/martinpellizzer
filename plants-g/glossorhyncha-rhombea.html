Glossorhyncha rhombea

Glossorhyncha rhombea, commonly known as the diamond leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive rhizome and linear, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
