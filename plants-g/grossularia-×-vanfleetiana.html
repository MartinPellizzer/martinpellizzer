Grossularia × vanfleetiana

Grossularia × vanfleetiana, commonly known as the Vanfleetiana gooseberry, is a hybrid shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
