Glyceria × pedicellata

Glyceria × pedicellata, commonly known as the hybrid reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a hybrid between Glyceria acutiflora and Glyceria canadensis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, forming dense stands that provide habitat for aquatic organisms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle.

Glyceria × pedicellata plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
