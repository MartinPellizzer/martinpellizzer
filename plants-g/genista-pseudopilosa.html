Genista pseudopilosa

Genista pseudopilosa, commonly known as the slender broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Genista pseudopilosa is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
