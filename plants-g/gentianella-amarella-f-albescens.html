Gentianella amarella f. albescens

Gentianella amarella f. albescens, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale blue flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
