Gladiolus palustris

Gladiolus palustris, commonly known as the swamp gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Gladiolus palustris is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
