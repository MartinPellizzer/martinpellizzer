Galium anglicum var. chamaeaparine

Galium anglicum var. chamaeaparine, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
