Grevillea seymouriae

Grevillea seymouriae, commonly known as the Seymour's waratah, is a striking shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, crimson-red flowers that resemble the heads of waratahs, which are typically found in the genus *Telopea*. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea seymouriae is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and its resilience to salt spray.
