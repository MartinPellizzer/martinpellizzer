Glyphochloa acuminata var. stocksii

Glyphochloa acuminata var. stocksii, commonly known as the slender grass or narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
