Gnaphalium lessingii

Gnaphalium lessingii, commonly known as the lesser groundsel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Gnaphalium lessingii is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
