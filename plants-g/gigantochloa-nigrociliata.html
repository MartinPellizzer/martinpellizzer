Gigantochloa nigrociliata

Gigantochloa nigrociliata, commonly known as the black-ciliate bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive dark purple or black fringe along the edges. The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. Its rapid growth and strong root system make it an effective stabilizer of soil in wetland ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional landscaping and construction in its native regions.
