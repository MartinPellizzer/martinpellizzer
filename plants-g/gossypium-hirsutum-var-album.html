Gossypium hirsutum var. album

Gossypium hirsutum var. album, commonly known as the white cotton plant, is a variant of upland cotton characterized by its white fiber color.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to the Americas. The plant produces fluffy, white lint that is harvested for textile use. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, and the leaves are broad and lobed.

This variety is valued for its high-quality, long staple fibers used in premium textile manufacturing.
