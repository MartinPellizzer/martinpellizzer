Garcinia busuangaensis

Garcinia busuangaensis, commonly known as the Busuanga garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has attracted interest for potential medicinal and nutritional applications.
