Gaura suffulta subsp. nealleyi

Gaura suffulta subsp.

nealleyi, commonly known as Nealley's gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces showy, fluffy flower heads that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Nealley's gaura is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
