Grandiphyllum hians

Grandiphyllum hians, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
