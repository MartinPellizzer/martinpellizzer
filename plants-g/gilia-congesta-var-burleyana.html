Gilia congesta var. burleyana

Gilia congesta var. burleyana, commonly known as Burley's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
