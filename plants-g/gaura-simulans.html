Gaura simulans

Gaura simulans, commonly known as the showy gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with clusters of small, delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Gaura simulans is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
