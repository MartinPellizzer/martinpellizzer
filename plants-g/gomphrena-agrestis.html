Gomphrena agrestis

Gomphrena agrestis, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces pink to red or purple bracts that are often mistaken for petals, though they are actually modified leaves. Gomphrena agrestis thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It is also known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist throughout the growing season.
