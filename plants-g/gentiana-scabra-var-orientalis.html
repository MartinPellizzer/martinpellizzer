Gentiana scabra var. orientalis

Gentiana scabra var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue to violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
