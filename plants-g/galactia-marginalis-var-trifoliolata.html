Galactia marginalis var. trifoliolata

Galactia marginalis var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
