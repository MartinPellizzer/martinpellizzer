Garcinia chromocarpa

Garcinia chromocarpa, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit contains a single, hard, seed-coated seed, and the peel is often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
