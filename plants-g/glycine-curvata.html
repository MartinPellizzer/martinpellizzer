Glycine curvata

Glycine curvata, commonly known as the curly bean vine, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, curved pods containing several seeds. Glycine curvata is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique pod structure.
