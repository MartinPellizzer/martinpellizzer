Gaultheria muluensis f. muluensis

Gaultheria muluensis f. muluensis, commonly known as the Mulu wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of western Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
