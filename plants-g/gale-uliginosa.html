Gale uliginosa

Gale uliginosa, commonly known as bogbean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It thrives in acidic, water-saturated soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its small, pale purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months. Gale uliginosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
