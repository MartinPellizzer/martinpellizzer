Gyrocarpus jatrophifolius var. pavonii

Gyrocarpus jatrophifolius var. pavonii, commonly known as the purple-leafed gyrocarpus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and ecological diversity.
