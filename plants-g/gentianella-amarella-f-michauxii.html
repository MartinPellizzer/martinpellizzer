Gentianella amarella f. michauxii

Gentianella amarella f. michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
