Gladiolus symmetranthus

Gladiolus symmetranthus, commonly known as the symmetrical gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, with a symmetrical arrangement of petals and sepals, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
