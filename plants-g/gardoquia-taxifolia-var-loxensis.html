Gardoquia taxifolia var. loxensis

Gardoquia taxifolia var. loxensis, commonly known as the Loxense yew, is a rare and unique variant of the yew family.

It is characterized by its elongated, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its rarity, it is often found in specific regions of Central Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
