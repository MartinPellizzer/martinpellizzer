Geanthus angustifolius

Geanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale green, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
