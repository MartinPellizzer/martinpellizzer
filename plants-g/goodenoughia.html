Goodenoughia

Goodenoughia, commonly known as the Goodenough's grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Australia and comprises several species that are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Goodenoughia species often have spikelets that are either solitary or arranged in clusters, contributing to their distinctive appearance.

These plants are adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing resilience through deep root systems and efficient water use.
