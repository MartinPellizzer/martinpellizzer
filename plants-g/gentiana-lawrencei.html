Gentiana lawrencei

Gentiana lawrencei, commonly known as Lawrence's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Gentiana lawrencei is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
