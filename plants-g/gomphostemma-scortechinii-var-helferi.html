Gomphostemma scortechinii var. helferi

Gomphostemma scortechinii var. helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's gomphostemma, is a member of the Menispermaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue to black berries. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India and Indonesia.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
