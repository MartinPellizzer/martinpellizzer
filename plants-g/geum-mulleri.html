Geum mulleri

Geum mulleri, commonly known as the golden avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking yellow flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are tubular with five petals, and it is often found in moist meadows and forest clearings.

Geum mulleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
