Gonialoe variegata

Gonialoe variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Elephant's Foot, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that resemble the legs of an elephant, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically green with white or yellow variegation, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
