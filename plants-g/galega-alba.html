Galega alba

Galega alba, commonly known as white goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Galega alba is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
