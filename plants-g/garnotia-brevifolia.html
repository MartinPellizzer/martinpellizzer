Garnotia brevifolia

Garnotia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved garnotia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Garnotia brevifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest for plant taxonomists.
