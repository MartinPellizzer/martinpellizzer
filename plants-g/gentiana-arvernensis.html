Gentiana arvernensis

Gentiana arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Gentiana arvernensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
