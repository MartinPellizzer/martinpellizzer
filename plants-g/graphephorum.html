Graphephorum

Graphephorum, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heartwood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in traditional medicine.
