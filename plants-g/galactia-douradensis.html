Galactia douradensis

Galactia douradensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is known for its milky sap, which contains alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine.

Galactia douradensis thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.
