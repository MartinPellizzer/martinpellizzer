Geranium temascaltepecense

Geranium temascaltepecense, commonly known as the Temascaltepec geranium, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local ecosystems.
