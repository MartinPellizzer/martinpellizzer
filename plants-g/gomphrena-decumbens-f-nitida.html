Gomphrena decumbens f. nitida

Gomphrena decumbens f. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, spiky flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The foliage's unique coloration also adds visual interest to garden landscapes.
