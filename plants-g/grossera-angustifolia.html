Grossera angustifolia

Grossera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grossera, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Grossera angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
