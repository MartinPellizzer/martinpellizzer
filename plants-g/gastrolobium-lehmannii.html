Gastrolobium lehmannii

Gastrolobium lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Gastrolobium lehmannii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
