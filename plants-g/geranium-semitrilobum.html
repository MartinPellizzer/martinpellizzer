Geranium semitrilobum

Geranium semitrilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Geranium semitrilobum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
