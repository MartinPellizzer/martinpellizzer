Gonolobus cteniophorus

Gonolobus cteniophorus, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The hairy senna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
