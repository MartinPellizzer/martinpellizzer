Gymnoschoenus adustus

Gymnoschoenus adustus, commonly known as the Australian grass or spinifex, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry growth habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Gymnoschoenus adustus plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
