Graphorkis ochreata

Graphorkis ochreata, commonly known as the ochre sun orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. It blooms during the warmer months, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
