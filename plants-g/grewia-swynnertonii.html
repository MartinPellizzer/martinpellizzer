Grewia swynnertonii

Grewia swynnertonii, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Grewia swynnertonii is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
