Geissorhiza arenicola

Geissorhiza arenicola, commonly known as the sand geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
