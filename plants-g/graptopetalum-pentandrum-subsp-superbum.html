Graptopetalum pentandrum subsp. superbum

Graptopetalum pentandrum subsp.

superbum, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring. It is a low-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
