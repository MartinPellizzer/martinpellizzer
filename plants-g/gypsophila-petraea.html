Gypsophila petraea

Gypsophila petraea, commonly known as the rock sandflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

Gypsophila petraea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
