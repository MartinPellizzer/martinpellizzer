Gomphocarpus sessilis

Gomphocarpus sessilis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a unique, spiny capsule that opens to release seeds.

Gomphocarpus sessilis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
