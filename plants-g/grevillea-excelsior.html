Grevillea excelsior

Grevillea excelsior, commonly known as the mountain grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and coastal environments. Its flowers, which range in color from red to orange, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Grevillea excelsior is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
