Grastidium rugulosum

Grastidium rugulosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rough, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
