Garcinia mangostifera

Garcinia mangostifera, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive fruit with a bright red outer rind and a segmented, white, edible flesh. The fruit is rich in antioxidants and bioactive compounds, contributing to its popularity in traditional medicine and modern health supplements.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
