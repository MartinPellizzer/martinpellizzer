Guajava doniana

Guajava doniana, commonly known as the white guava, is a tropical fruit tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, white, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has a dense canopy with broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it relies on insect pollination for fruit development.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its unique flavor and nutritional content.
