Goniopteris tetragona var. schkuhrii

Goniopteris tetragona var. schkuhrii, commonly known as the Schkuhrii water fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed water fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, triangular, and often bipinnate fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, toothed pinnae, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
