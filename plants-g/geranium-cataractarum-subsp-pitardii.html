Geranium cataractarum subsp. pitardii

Geranium cataractarum subsp.

pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's geranium, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
