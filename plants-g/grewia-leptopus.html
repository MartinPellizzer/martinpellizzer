Grewia leptopus

Grewia leptopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia leptopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
