Gaillardia perennis

Gaillardia perennis, commonly known as the perpetual grass or firewheel flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has upright, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.

Its ability to reseed itself makes it a resilient and low-maintenance plant.
