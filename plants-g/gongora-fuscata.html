Gongora fuscata

Gongora fuscata, commonly known as the dark gongora, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and a striking, elongated lip that is often darker than the rest of the flower. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and come in shades of purple, brown, and black.

Gongora fuscata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
