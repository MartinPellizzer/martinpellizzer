Geissorhiza umbrosa

Geissorhiza umbrosa, commonly known as the dark-leafed lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clustered, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Geissorhiza umbrosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
