Geranium condensatum

Geranium condensatum, commonly known as the clustered geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple, typically blooming from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period contribute to its ornamental value in garden settings.
