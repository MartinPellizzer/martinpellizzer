Gentiana coerulescens var. parviflora

Gentiana coerulescens var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant grows in rocky or alpine soils, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine downy layer, while its stems are upright and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
