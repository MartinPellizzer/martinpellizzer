Grastidium podochiloides

Grastidium podochiloides, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-centered flowers with five pale yellow petals surrounding a central orange disk. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Grastidium podochiloides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
