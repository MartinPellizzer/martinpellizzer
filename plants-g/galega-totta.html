Galega totta

Galega totta, commonly known as red bryony, is a perennial climbing plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its stems are often spiny.

Galega totta is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
