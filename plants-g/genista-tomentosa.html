Genista tomentosa

Genista tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are small, alternate, and covered in a similar fuzzy texture.

Genista tomentosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
