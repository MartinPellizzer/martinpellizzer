Gentiana polymorpha subsp. sturmiana

Gentiana polymorpha subsp.

sturmiana, commonly known as the Sturm's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it has a short, upright stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
