Galium nitidulum var. scabriusculum, commonly known as rough-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It features slender, ascending stems covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface. Small white flowers are borne in clusters at the leaf axils, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
