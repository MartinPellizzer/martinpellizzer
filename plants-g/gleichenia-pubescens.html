Gleichenia pubescens

Gleichenia pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a compact, shield-like structure, which helps it retain moisture and protect against desiccation. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to substrates, while its spores are produced in sori that are arranged along the margins of the fronds.

Gleichenia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticulture for its ability to add texture and greenery to shaded garden spaces.
