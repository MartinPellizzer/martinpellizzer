Gentiana punensis

Gentiana punensis, commonly known as the Peruvian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white down, giving them a soft appearance.

Gentiana punensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
