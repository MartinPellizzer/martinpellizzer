Gentiana citrina

Gentiana citrina, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
