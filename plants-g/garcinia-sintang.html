Garcinia sintang

Garcinia sintang, commonly known as the Sintang garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia sintang is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit and chemical composition make it an interesting subject for botanical and pharmacological research.
