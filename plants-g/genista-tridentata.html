Genista tridentata

Genista tridentata, commonly known as the three-lobed broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Genista tridentata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
