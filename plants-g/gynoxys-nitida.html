Gynoxys nitida

Gynoxys nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf gynoxys, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gynoxys nitida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
