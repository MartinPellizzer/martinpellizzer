Grastidium coloratum

Grastidium coloratum, commonly known as the colorful ghost plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that range from vibrant greens to deep purples and reds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky areas.

Due to its rare habitat and striking appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
