Grevillea sarissa subsp. umbellifera

Grevillea sarissa subsp.

umbellifera, commonly known as the red-stemmed Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, bright red flowers arranged in dense, umbrella-like clusters, which give it its subspecies name "umbellifera.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
