Gentianella chrysosphaera

Gentianella chrysosphaera, commonly known as the golden globe gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often surrounded by yellow or white bracts. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
