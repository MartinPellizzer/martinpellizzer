Gymnocalycium pulquinense var. corroanum

Gymnocalycium pulquinense var. corroanum, commonly known as the Corroanum barrel cactus, is a slow-growing cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in extreme desert environments.
