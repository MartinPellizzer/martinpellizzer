Guidonia hirta

Guidonia hirta, commonly known as the hairy guidonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
