Geum calthifolium var. congestum

Geum calthifolium var. congestum, commonly known as the clustered avens, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
