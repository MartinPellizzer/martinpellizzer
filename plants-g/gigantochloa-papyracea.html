Gigantochloa papyracea

Gigantochloa papyracea, commonly known as the bamboo grass or paper bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stalks that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts due to their fibrous texture. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Gigantochloa papyracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in paper and textile production.
