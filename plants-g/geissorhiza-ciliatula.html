Geissorhiza ciliatula

Geissorhiza ciliatula, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and are pollinated by insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
