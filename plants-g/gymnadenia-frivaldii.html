Gymnadenia frivaldii

Gymnadenia frivaldii, commonly known as the Frivald's grass-pink, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, pale pink or white flower that blooms in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
