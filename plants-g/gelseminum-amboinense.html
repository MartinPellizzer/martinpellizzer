Gelseminum amboinense

Gelseminum amboinense, commonly known as the Amboina climbing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a twining vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with prominent red stamens. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and tobacco. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, elliptical seeds.
