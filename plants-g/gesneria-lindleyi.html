Gesneria lindleyi

Gesneria lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Gesneria lindleyi is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
