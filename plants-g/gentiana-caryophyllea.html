Gentiana caryophyllea

Gentiana caryophyllea, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Gentiana caryophyllea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
