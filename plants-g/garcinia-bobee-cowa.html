Garcinia bobee-cowa

Garcinia bobee-cowa, commonly known as the African yellow fruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
