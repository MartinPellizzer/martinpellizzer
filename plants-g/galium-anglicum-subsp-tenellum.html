Galium anglicum subsp. tenellum

Galium anglicum subsp.

tenellum, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.
