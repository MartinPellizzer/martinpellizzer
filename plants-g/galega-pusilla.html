Galega pusilla

Galega pusilla, commonly known as the lesser goat's rue, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and thrives in dry, disturbed soils. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Galega pusilla is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
