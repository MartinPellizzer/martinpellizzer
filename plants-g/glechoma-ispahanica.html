Glechoma ispahanica

Glechoma ispahanica, commonly known as Persian ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant spreads through stolons, forming dense mats that can cover large areas. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
