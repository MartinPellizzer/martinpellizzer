Gonostemon asterias var. gibbus

Gonostemon asterias var. gibbus, commonly known as the star-shaped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-like flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
