Galium margaritaceum

Galium margaritaceum, commonly known as the pearlweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale green, clustered in umbels, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galium margaritaceum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
