Garnotia angustata

Garnotia angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garnotia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped foliage.

Garnotia angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
