Gerbera coronopifolia

Gerbera coronopifolia, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Gerbera coronopifolia is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and upright, branched stems.

Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white hairs.
