Genipa clusiifolia

Genipa clusiifolia, commonly known as the red mangabey apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. It is often found in wet tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
