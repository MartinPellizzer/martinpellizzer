Genistella tridentata subsp. cantabrica

Genistella tridentata subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky and coastal areas, often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
