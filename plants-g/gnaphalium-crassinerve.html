Gnaphalium crassinerve

Gnaphalium crassinerve, commonly known as the broad-veined everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is perennial, often growing in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads are usually pale yellow to white.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
