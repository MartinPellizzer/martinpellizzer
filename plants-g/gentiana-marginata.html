Gentiana marginata

Gentiana marginata, commonly known as the margins gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana marginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
