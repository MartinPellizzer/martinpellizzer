Gentiana septemfida var. procumbens

Gentiana septemfida var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing, prostrate habit and features bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
