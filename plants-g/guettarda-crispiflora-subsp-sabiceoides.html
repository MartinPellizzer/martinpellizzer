Guettarda crispiflora subsp. sabiceoides

Guettarda crispiflora subsp.

sabiceoides, commonly known as the white-flowered guettarda, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive, showy white inflorescence.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal environments.
