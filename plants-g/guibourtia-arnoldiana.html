Guibourtia arnoldiana

Guibourtia arnoldiana, commonly known as African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy timber used in furniture and construction.

Due to overharvesting, it is also listed on the IUCN Red List as vulnerable.
