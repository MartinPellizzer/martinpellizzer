Galeopsis pubescens var. mollis

Galeopsis pubescens var. mollis, commonly known as soft hairy tickseed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for garden use.
