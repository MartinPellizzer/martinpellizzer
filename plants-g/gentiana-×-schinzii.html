Gentiana × schinzii

Gentiana × schinzii, commonly known as the Schinz's gentian, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentiana × schinzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
