Gomphrena elegans var. mandonioides

Gomphrena elegans var. mandonioides, commonly known as the blue gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from blue to pink. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a feathery appearance, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
