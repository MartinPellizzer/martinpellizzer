Gomphrena decumbens var. boliviana

Gomphrena decumbens var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian plume flower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that give it a plume-like appearance, often displaying shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting floral display.
