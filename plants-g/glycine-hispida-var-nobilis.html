Glycine hispida var. nobilis

Glycine hispida var. nobilis, commonly known as the noble hairy bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit and unique foliage make it a distinctive addition to gardens and green spaces.
