Geranium linearilobum

Geranium linearilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
