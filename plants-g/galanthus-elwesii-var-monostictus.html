Galanthus elwesii var. monostictus

Galanthus elwesii var. monostictus, commonly known as the Elwes' snowdrop, is a rare and distinctive variety of snowdrop native to the UK.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with a single green stripe on the inner part of the petals, setting it apart from other snowdrop varieties. The plant typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces a single, upright stem with two to three narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and early spring blooms.
