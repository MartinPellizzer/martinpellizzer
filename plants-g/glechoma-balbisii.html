Glechoma balbisii

Glechoma balbisii, commonly known as Persian ground-ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features heart-shaped, slightly hairy leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Glechoma balbisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
