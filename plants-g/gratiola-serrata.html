Gratiola serrata

Gratiola serrata, commonly known as the serrated gratiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Gratiola serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
