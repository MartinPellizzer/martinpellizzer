Goodenia malvina

Goodenia malvina, commonly known as the pink goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Goodenia malvina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
