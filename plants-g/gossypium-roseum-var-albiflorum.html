Gossypium roseum var. albiflorum

Gossypium roseum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered cotton plant, is a variant of the cotton species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its pale pink or white flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton bolls that contain the fibrous material used in textile manufacturing. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fibrous yield.
