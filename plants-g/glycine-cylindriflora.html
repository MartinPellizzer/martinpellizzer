Glycine cylindriflora

Glycine cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylindrical sickle bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, twining stems and pinnately compound leaves with elongated, sickle-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several round, edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines.

Glycine cylindriflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
