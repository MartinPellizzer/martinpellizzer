Gaillardia alternifolia

Gaillardia alternifolia, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright, daisy-like flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Gaillardia alternifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
