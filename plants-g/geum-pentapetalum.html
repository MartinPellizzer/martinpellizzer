Geum pentapetalum

Geum pentapetalum, commonly known as the five-petaled avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals arranged in a showy cluster. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to woodland gardens and natural habitats.
