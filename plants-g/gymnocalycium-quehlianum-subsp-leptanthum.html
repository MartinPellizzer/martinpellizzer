Gymnocalycium quehlianum subsp. leptanthum

Gymnocalycium quehlianum subsp.

leptanthum, commonly known as the slender-armed moon cactus, is a cacti species native to northern Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the axils of the areoles. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
