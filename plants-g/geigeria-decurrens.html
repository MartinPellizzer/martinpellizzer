Geigeria decurrens

Geigeria decurrens, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flowers are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Geigeria decurrens is typically found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
