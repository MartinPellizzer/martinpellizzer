Gerardia tenuifolia var. asperula

Gerardia tenuifolia var. asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, rough-textured hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive rough or hairy texture on both surfaces.

This variety is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
