Gurania huebneri

Gurania huebneri, commonly known as the Bornean moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet scent. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, lantern-like blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and striking addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
