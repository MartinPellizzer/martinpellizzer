Glochidion chondrocarpum

Glochidion chondrocarpum, commonly known as the rock apple or stone apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
