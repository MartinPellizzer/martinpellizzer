Glomera ericifolia

Glomera ericifolia, commonly known as the heath glomera, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Glomera ericifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal bluffs and dry woodlands.
