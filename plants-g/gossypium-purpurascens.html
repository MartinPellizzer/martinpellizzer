Gossypium purpurascens

Gossypium purpurascens, commonly known as the crimson cotton or wild cotton, is a species of shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces fluffy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its fibrous seeds, which are used in traditional textiles and crafts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Gossypium purpurascens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
