Gynoxys subhirsuta

Gynoxys subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gynoxys, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, with prominent stamens. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
