Gleichenia glauca

Gleichenia glauca, commonly known as the blue moss, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, bluish-green coating, giving it its common name. Gleichenia glauca is a cryptogamic plant, meaning it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
