Gladiolus communis subsp. notarisii

Gladiolus communis subsp.

notarisii, commonly known as the Cape golden orchid, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, often with a distinctive orange or red stripe down the center.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and elegance.
