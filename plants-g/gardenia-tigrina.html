Gardenia tigrina

Gardenia tigrina, commonly known as the spotted gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinct yellow spots, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gardenia tigrina is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
