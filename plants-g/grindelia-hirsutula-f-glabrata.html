Grindelia hirsutula f. glabrata

Grindelia hirsutula f. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf desert gumplant, is a rare variant of the desert gumplant species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its botanical features include a low-growing, mat-forming habit with glandular surfaces that produce a sticky resin.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
