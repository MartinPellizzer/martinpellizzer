Gladiolus arvaticus

Gladiolus arvaticus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of brightly colored flowers. The flowers are typically pink, red, or white, with three to six petals and a distinctive spathaceous bract. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
