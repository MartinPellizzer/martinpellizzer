Goldfussia extensa

Goldfussia extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf goldfussia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
