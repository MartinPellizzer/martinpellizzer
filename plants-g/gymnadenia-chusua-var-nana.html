Gymnadenia chusua var. nana

Gymnadenia chusua var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf ladies' tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its distinctive, spiraling inflorescence of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are borne on a single, upright stem and bloom in early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
