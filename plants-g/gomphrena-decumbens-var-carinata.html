Gomphrena decumbens var. carinata

Gomphrena decumbens var. carinata, commonly known as the curled gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of pink or red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by showy, modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
