Glia capensis

Glia capensis, commonly known as the Cape glia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Glia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
