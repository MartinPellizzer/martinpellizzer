Grindelia villosa

Grindelia villosa, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Grindelia villosa is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
