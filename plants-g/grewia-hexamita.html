Grewia hexamita

Grewia hexamita, commonly known as the six-angled bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value and utility in traditional medicine contribute to its significance in local ecosystems.
