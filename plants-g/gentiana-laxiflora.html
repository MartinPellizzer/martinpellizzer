Gentiana laxiflora

Gentiana laxiflora, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their bright coloration and nectar-rich structure.
