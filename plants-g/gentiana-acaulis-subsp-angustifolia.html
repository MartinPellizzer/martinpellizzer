Gentiana acaulis subsp. angustifolia

Gentiana acaulis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a short, unbranched stem that supports its single, showy flower. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous habitats.
