Gymnocalycium paediophylum

Gymnocalycium paediophylum, commonly known as the baby cholla, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a small, spineless plant that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant has a globular shape with a smooth, greenish-gray stem that is covered in areoles. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Gymnocalycium paediophylum is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
