Geranium malviflorum

Geranium malviflorum, commonly known as the Malva-leaved Geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in garden ecosystems.
