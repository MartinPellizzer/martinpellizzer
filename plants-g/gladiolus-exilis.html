Gladiolus exilis

Gladiolus exilis, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of brightly colored, spurred petals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Gladiolus exilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
