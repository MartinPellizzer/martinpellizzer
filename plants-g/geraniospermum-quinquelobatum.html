Geraniospermum quinquelobatum

Geraniospermum quinquelobatum, commonly known as the five-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Geraniospermum quinquelobatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
