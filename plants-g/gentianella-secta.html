Gentianella secta

Gentianella secta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
