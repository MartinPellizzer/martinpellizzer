Guioa aryteroides

Guioa aryteroides, commonly known as the white guioa, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Guioa aryteroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
