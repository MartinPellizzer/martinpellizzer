Gentianella corymbifera subsp. gracilis

Gentianella corymbifera subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, this subspecies thrives in moist, calcareous soils. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
