Gentiana kurilensis

Gentiana kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and the northern regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana kurilensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
