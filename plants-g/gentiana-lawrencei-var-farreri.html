Gentiana lawrencei var. farreri

Gentiana lawrencei var. farreri, commonly known as the Farrer's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
