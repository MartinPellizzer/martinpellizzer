Gigantochloa multiculmis

Gigantochloa multiculmis, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetland areas and lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and strong structural properties make it valuable for both ecological and practical applications.
