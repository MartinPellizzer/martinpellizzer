Gunnera venezolana subsp. tachirensis

Gunnera venezolana subsp.

tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachire giant leaf, is a striking member of the Gunneraceae family native to Venezuela. It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against herbivores.

While it is not widely cultivated, it is highly valued in botanical gardens for its unique and showy appearance.
