Geranium caespitosum var. gracile

Geranium caespitosum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
