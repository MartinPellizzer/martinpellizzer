Gnaphalium oxyphyllum

Gnaphalium oxyphyllum, commonly known as silverleaf groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.
