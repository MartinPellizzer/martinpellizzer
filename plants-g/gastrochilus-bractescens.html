Gastrochilus bractescens

Gastrochilus bractescens, commonly known as the bracteate stomach orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique floral structure includes a labellum with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
