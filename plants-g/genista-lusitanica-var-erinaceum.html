Genista lusitanica var. erinaceum

Genista lusitanica var. erinaceum, commonly known as the hairy bursfoot broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
