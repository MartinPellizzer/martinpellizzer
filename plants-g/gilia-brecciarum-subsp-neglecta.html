Gilia brecciarum subsp. neglecta

Gilia brecciarum subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
