Gentianella cuspidata

Gentianella cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive pointed tip.

Gentianella cuspidata is often found in mountainous regions and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
