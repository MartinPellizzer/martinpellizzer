Gomidesia poeppigiana

Gomidesia poeppigiana, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Gomidesia poeppigiana plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
