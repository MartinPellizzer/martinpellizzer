Gaultheria odorata var. mexicana

Gaultheria odorata var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican wintergreen, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It produces small, dark red berries that are rich in aromatic compounds, particularly methyl salicylate, which gives it its distinctive wintergreen scent. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and have a serrated edge.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
