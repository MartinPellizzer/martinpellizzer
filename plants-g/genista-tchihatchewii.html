Genista tchihatchewii

Genista tchihatchewii, commonly known as the Armenian broom, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its small yellow flowers appear in spring, and it produces slender, woody pods that contain seeds.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
