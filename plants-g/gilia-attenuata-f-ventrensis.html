Gilia attenuata f. ventrensis

Gilia attenuata f. ventrensis, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Miridae family, though it is more closely related to the evening primroses. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive elongated shape.
