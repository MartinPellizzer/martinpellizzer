Gentiana lhaguensis

Gentiana lhaguensis, commonly known as the Lhagou gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
