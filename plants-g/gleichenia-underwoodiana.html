Gleichenia underwoodiana

Gleichenia underwoodiana, commonly known as the Underwood's shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to eastern Australia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Gleichenia underwoodiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
