Goeppertia selbyana

Goeppertia selbyana, commonly known as the Selby's Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, ornate foliage with intricate patterns and vibrant coloration. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, often displaying a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
