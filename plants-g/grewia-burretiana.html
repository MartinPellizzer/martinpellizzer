Grewia burretiana

Grewia burretiana, commonly known as the red-flowered grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

Grewia burretiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display and adaptability to different soil types.
