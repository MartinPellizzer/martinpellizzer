Geranium donianum

Geranium donianum, commonly known as the purple geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple and pink, typically blooming from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
