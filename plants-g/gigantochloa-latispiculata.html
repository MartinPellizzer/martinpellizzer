Gigantochloa latispiculata

Gigantochloa latispiculata, commonly known as the broad-spike bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, erect culms that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features broad, flat leaves with a smooth texture and a pale green color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, whitish flowers.

Gigantochloa latispiculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its strong, durable culms in traditional construction.
