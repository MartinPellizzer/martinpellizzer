Gaylussacia rhodendra

Gaylussacia rhodendra, commonly known as the red-berried huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The shrub thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
