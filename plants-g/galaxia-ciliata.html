Galaxia ciliata

Galaxia ciliata, commonly known as the silverleaf galaxia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with fine, silvery hairs along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Galaxia ciliata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
