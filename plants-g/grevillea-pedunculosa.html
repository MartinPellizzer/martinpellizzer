Grevillea pedunculosa

Grevillea pedunculosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
