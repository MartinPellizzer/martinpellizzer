Gynura pseudochina

Gynura pseudochina, commonly known as Chinese nettle or stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant contains irritant chemicals in its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Gynura pseudochina is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
