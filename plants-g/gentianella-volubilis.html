Gentianella volubilis

Gentianella volubilis, commonly known as the climbing gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, azure-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
