Glaphyropteris simulans

Glaphyropteris simulans, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a prehistoric fern that thrived during the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its large, complex fronds with a distinctive, often bipinnate structure. The plant had a robust rhizome system that allowed it to anchor and spread across swampy environments. Its leaves were adapted for efficient photosynthesis in waterlogged soils, contributing to the formation of coal deposits.

Fossil records indicate that it played a significant role in the ancient ecosystems and the carbon cycle.
