Greenea oblonga

Greenea oblonga, commonly known as the oblong leaf greenea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Greenea oblonga thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of understory plant life in these ecosystems.
