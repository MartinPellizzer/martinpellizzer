Gentiana spitiensis

Gentiana spitiensis, commonly known as the Spiti gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes, often above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals, with a prominent central corolla.

Gentiana spitiensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
