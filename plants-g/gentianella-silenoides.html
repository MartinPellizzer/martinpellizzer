Gentianella silenoides

Gentianella silenoides, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals and prominent blue veins, blooming from June to August.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
