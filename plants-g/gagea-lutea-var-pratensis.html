Gagea lutea var. pratensis

Gagea lutea var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing yellowish-white flowers that are often tinged with green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
