Gossia vieillardii

Gossia vieillardii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Gossia vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
