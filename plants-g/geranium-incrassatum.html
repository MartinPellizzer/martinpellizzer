Geranium incrassatum

Geranium incrassatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent five-lobed calyx.

Geranium incrassatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
