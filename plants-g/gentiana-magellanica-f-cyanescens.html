Gentiana magellanica f. cyanescens

Gentiana magellanica f. cyanescens, commonly known as the Magellan gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
