Gasteranthus atrolimbus

Gasteranthus atrolimbus, commonly known as the black-limbed bellflower, is a striking member of the Gesneriaceae family native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

This plant is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are typically deep red or purple, with a glossy, waxy appearance. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Gasteranthus atrolimbus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
