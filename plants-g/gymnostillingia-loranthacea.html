Gymnostillingia loranthacea

Gymnostillingia loranthacea, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Gymnostillingia, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, fleshy leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that consume the fleshy berries.

Gymnostillingia loranthacea plays a role in ecological interactions, supporting birdlife and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
