Graphephorum pendulinum

Graphephorum pendulinum, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a rare and delicate species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous fronds that dangle from its stems, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sorus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of moisture and indirect light.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
