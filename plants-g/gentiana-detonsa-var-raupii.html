Gentiana detonsa var. raupii

Gentiana detonsa var. raupii, commonly known as the Raup's gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that replicate its natural habitat.
