Geranium sanguineum var. striatum

Geranium sanguineum var. striatum, commonly known as the striped cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that bear clusters of pink to red flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive white or pale green stripe along the midrib. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
