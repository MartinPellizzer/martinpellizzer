Gentiana townsendii

Gentiana townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
