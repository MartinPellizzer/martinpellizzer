Gentianella

Gentianella, commonly known as the gentian bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae.

These plants are typically found in moist, temperate regions and are characterized by their striking blue or violet flowers. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are often slender and branching. Gentianella species are known for their ability to thrive in shaded or partially shaded environments.

They are also valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
