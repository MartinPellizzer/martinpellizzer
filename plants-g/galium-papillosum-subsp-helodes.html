Galium papillosum subsp. helodes

Galium papillosum subsp.

helodes, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, creeping stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and damp meadows.
