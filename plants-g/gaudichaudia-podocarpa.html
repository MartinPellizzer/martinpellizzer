Gaudichaudia podocarpa

Gaudichaudia podocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces small, woody cones that contain the seeds.

Gaudichaudia podocarpa is well adapted to the cool, moist climate of its native habitat.
