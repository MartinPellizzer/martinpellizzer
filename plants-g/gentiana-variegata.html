Gentiana variegata

Gentiana variegata, commonly known as the variegated gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive variegation pattern of white or yellow markings.

Gentiana variegata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
