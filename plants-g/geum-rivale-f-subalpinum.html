Geum rivale f. subalpinum

Geum rivale f. subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a variant of the common buttercup.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. This plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in moist, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects in mountainous regions.
