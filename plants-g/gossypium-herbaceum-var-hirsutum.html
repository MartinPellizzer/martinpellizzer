Gossypium herbaceum var. hirsutum

Gossypium herbaceum var. hirsutum, commonly known as upland cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is characterized by its soft, fluffy fibers that are harvested from the seed pods. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, while its flowers are pink or white.

The fibers of this plant are widely used in the textile industry due to their strength and durability.
