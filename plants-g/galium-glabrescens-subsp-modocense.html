Galium glabrescens subsp. modocense

Galium glabrescens subsp.

modocense, commonly known as Modoc bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
