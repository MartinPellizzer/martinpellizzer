Geranium asplenioides

Geranium asplenioides, commonly known as the fern geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically light green and finely divided. The plant produces small, pink or violet flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium asplenioides prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
