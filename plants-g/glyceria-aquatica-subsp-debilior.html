Glyceria aquatica subsp. debilior

Glyceria aquatica subsp.

debilior, commonly known as the slender watergrass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
