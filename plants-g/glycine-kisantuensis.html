Glycine kisantuensis

Glycine kisantuensis, commonly known as the Kisantu bean, is a climbing legume native to Central Africa.

It produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds used in traditional cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Glycine kisantuensis is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in agroforestry and soil enrichment.
