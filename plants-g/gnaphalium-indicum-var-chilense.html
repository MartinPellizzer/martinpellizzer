Gnaphalium indicum var. chilense

Gnaphalium indicum var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is typically herbaceous, with slender stems and small, linear leaves. It produces a profusion of tiny florets that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

The species is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
