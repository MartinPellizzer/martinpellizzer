Garcinia oxyphylla

Garcinia oxyphylla, commonly known as the red sanders or yellow sanders, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Garcinia oxyphylla is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
