Gentiana tenella var. occidentalis

Gentiana tenella var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
