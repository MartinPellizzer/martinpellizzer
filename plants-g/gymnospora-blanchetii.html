Gymnospora blanchetii

Gymnospora blanchetii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, white flower clusters that resemble snowballs, blooming in winter. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Gymnospora blanchetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
