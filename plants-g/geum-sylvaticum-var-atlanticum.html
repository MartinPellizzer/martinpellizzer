Geum sylvaticum var. atlanticum

Geum sylvaticum var. atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic wild geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and shaded wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
