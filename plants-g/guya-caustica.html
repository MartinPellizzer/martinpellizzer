Guya caustica

Guya caustica, commonly known as the fiery flame plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Guya caustica is often cultivated for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid conditions.
