Guzmania fuquae

Guzmania fuquae, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that features a red bract with white and yellow flowers. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects water, creating a micro-ecosystem for small organisms. Its foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Guzmania fuquae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
