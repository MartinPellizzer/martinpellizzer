Galium lucidum subvar. viridulum

Galium lucidum subvar. viridulum, commonly known as the greenleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a shiny, waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.
