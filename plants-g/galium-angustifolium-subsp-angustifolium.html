Galium angustifolium subsp. angustifolium

Galium angustifolium subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, alternate, and usually have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its small, capsule-like fruits contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.
