Geranium moschatum var. pimpinellifolium

Geranium moschatum var. pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the cherry tomato plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when mature, though they can also be yellow or orange. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and edible, with the fruits being widely used in culinary applications.
