Gossypium bicolor

Gossypium bicolor, commonly known as the wild cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that are used in traditional textiles. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Gossypium bicolor plays an important role in both ecological and historical contexts.
