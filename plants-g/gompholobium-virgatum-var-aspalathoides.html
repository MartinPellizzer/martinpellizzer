Gompholobium virgatum var. aspalathoides

Gompholobium virgatum var. aspalathoides, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

This variety is closely related to the aspalathus genus, which is also known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
