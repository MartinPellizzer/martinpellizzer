Gastrolobium glaucum

Gastrolobium glaucum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, typically in clusters along the stems. It has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Gastrolobium glaucum is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
