Guioa asquamosa

Guioa asquamosa, commonly known as the silky guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in secondary forests and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
