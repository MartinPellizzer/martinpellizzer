Geissorhiza tulbaghensis

Geissorhiza tulbaghensis, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, greenish-white blooms with a distinctive lip that attracts pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
