Gladiolus velutinus

Gladiolus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white, and bloom in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
