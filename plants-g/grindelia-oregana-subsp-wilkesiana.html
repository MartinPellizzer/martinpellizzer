Grindelia oregana subsp. wilkesiana

Grindelia oregana subsp.

wilkesiana, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and yellowish-green flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are aromatic and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the typical variety of Grindelia oregana.
