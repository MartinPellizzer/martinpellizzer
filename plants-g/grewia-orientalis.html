Grewia orientalis

Grewia orientalis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bright orange, berry-like fruits. The fruits are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor, making them valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Grewia orientalis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
