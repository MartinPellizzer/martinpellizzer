Guarania laurifolia

Guarania laurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
