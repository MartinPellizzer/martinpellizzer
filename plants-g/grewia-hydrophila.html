Grewia hydrophila

Grewia hydrophila, commonly known as the waterberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Grewia hydrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
