Gamolepis ericoides

Gamolepis ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, forming a mat-like structure that helps it survive in harsh, cold environments.

Gamolepis ericoides is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
