Geranium tapintzense

Geranium tapintzense, commonly known as the Peruvian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower colors, which range from pink to red.
