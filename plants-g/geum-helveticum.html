Geum helveticum

Geum helveticum, commonly known as Swiss dropwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Geum helveticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
