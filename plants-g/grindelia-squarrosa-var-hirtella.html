Grindelia squarrosa var. hirtella

Grindelia squarrosa var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy bitterroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its latex-rich sap has historically been used by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
