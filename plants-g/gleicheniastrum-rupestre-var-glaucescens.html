Gleicheniastrum rupestre var. glaucescens

Gleicheniastrum rupestre var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry rhizomes and delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or woodland areas.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
