Geranium stipulaceum

Geranium stipulaceum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in full sun.

Geranium stipulaceum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
