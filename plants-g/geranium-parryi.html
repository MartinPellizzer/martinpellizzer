Geranium parryi

Geranium parryi, commonly known as Parry's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Geranium parryi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
