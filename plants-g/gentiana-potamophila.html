Gentiana potamophila

Gentiana potamophila, commonly known as the river gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
