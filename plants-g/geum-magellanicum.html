Geum magellanicum

Geum magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic avens, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
