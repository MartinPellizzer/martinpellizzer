Gardenia sessiliflora

Gardenia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that contain a single seed, which is used for propagation. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
