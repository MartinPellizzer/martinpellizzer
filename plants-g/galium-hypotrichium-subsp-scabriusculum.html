Galium hypotrichium subsp. scabriusculum

Galium hypotrichium subsp.

scabriusculum, commonly known as rough-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America. It features slender, often hairy stems that are typically prostrate or ascending.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, stiff hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
