Gentianopsis contorta var. wui

Gentianopsis contorta var. wui, commonly known as the bent gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are wiry and somewhat twisted.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
