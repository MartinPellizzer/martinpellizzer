Gundelia tournefortii var. asperrima

Gundelia tournefortii var. asperrima, commonly known as the Afghan sandmat, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant with fleshy, ribbed stems and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
