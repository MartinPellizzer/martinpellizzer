Garcinia enthaematoeides

Garcinia enthaematoeides, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow, acidic fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Garcinia enthaematoeides is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmaceutical contexts.
