Galactia synandra

Galactia synandra, commonly known as the silky milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Galactia synandra is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its common name refers to the milky sap it exudes, which is a characteristic feature of many members of the Fabaceae family.
