Grossheimia ossica

Grossheimia ossica, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs, where it benefits from full sun exposure.

Grossheimia ossica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
