Geum oregonense

Geum oregonense, commonly known as the Oregon buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges.

Geum oregonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
