Glaucium grandiflorum var. judaicum

Glaucium grandiflorum var. judaicum, commonly known as the Judaea gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style gardens.
