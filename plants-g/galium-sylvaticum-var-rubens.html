Galium sylvaticum var. rubens

Galium sylvaticum var. rubens, commonly known as red wood-sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
