Galipea gaudichaudiana

Galipea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a large, evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces long, woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is commonly planted as an ornamental species due to its rapid growth and aesthetic appeal.
