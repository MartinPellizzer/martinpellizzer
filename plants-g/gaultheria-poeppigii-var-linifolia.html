Gaultheria poeppigii var. linifolia

Gaultheria poeppigii var. linifolia, commonly known as the slender waxleaf wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are covered in a dense, waxy coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and berries contain aromatic compounds, making it a valuable plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
