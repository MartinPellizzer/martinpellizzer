Goldfussia glomerata var. speciosa

Goldfussia glomerata var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy goldenfussia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
