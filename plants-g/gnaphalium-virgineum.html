Gnaphalium virgineum

Gnaphalium virgineum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to white. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in moist, open areas such as meadows, marshes, and roadside environments.

Gnaphalium virgineum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
