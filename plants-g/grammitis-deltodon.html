Grammitis deltodon

Grammitis deltodon, commonly known as the deltoid grammitis, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact rosette. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive deltoid (triangular) shape at the base of the leaflets. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
