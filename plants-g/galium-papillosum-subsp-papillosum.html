Galium papillosum subsp. papillosum

Galium papillosum subsp.

papillosum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
