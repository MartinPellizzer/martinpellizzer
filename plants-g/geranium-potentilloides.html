Geranium potentilloides

Geranium potentilloides, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are showy, with five petals in shades of pink to purple, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Geranium potentilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
