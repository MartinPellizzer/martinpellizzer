Gladiolus brevitubus

Gladiolus brevitubus, commonly known as the short-tubed gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, tuberous roots. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense spikes and come in a variety of hues, including red, pink, and white.

Gladiolus brevitubus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
