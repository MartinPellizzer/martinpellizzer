Gnaphalium carinatum

Gnaphalium carinatum, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is often found in moist meadows, riverbanks, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellowish with a central disk.

The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across various habitats.
