Gentiana detonsa var. tonsa

Gentiana detonsa var. tonsa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
