Geranium rotundifolium var. palmatipartitis

Geranium rotundifolium var. palmatipartitis, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features rounded, palmately lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable component of native plant communities.
