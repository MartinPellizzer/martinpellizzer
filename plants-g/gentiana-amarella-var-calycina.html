Gentiana amarella var. calycina

Gentiana amarella var. calycina, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
