Gravesia subsessilifolia

Gravesia subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gravesia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
