Grevillea strangea

Grevillea strangea, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces nectar-rich flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including honeyeaters and bees. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating.

Grevillea strangea is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
