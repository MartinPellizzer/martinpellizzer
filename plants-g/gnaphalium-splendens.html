Gnaphalium splendens

Gnaphalium splendens, commonly known as the golden feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that are arranged in clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plant.
