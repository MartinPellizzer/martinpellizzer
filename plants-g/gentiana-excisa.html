Gentiana excisa

Gentiana excisa, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentiana excisa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
