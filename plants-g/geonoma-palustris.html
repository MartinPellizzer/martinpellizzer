Geonoma palustris

Geonoma palustris, commonly known as the swamp fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green and slightly curled. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Geonoma palustris plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
