Graphorkis macrorhiza

Graphorkis macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
