Garcinia opaca

Garcinia opaca, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making. Garcinia opaca thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

It also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
