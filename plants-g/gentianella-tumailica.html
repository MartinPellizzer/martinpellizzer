Gentianella tumailica

Gentianella tumailica, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentianella tumailica is known for its short blooming period, typically occurring in late summer.
