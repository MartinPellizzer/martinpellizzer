Gaultheria straminea

Gaultheria straminea, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. Gaultheria straminea thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and also for its aromatic leaves and berries.
