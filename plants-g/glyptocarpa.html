Glyptocarpa

Glyptocarpa, commonly known as the sweetgum tree, is a member of the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves and produces a unique, spiky fruit known as a "gum ball.". The plant is native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin layers.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
