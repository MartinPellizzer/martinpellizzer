Gentiana taliensis

Gentiana taliensis, commonly known as the Tali gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are often branched.

The Tali gentian is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
