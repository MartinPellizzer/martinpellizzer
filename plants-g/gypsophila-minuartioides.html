Gypsophila minuartioides

Gypsophila minuartioides, commonly known as the alpine baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and floral arrangements.
