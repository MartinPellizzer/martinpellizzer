Gentiana aggregata

Gentiana aggregata, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana aggregata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
