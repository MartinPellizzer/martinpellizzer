Gentianella montana

Gentianella montana, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentianella montana is known for its short blooming period, usually in late summer, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
