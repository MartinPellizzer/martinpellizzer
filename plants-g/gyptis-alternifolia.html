Gyptis alternifolia

Gyptis alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf gyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with smooth or slightly serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and can be white, yellow, or greenish in color.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
