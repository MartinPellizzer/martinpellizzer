Garcinia madagascariensis

Garcinia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and yellow-orange fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement due to its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
