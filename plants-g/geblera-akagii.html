Geblera akagii

Geblera akagii, commonly known as the Japanese geblera, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spurred flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
