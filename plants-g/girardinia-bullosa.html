Girardinia bullosa

Girardinia bullosa, commonly known as the bullock's ear, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Girardinia bullosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
