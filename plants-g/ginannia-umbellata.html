Ginannia umbellata

Ginannia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped inflorescence, which consists of multiple small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ginannia umbellata is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
