Galega officinalis var. africana

Galega officinalis var. africana, commonly known as African goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in summer, followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for various health purposes.
