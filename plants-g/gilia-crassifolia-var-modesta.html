Gilia crassifolia var. modesta

Gilia crassifolia var. modesta, commonly known as the modest gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to arid environments. This variety is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, blooming in late spring to early summer.
