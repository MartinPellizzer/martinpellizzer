Gonialoe

Gonialoe, commonly known as the stone plant, is a genus of succulent plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact, rosette-shaped growth form and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. They typically have a slow growth rate and are well adapted to arid environments. The leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Gonialoe species are popular among succulent enthusiasts for their unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
