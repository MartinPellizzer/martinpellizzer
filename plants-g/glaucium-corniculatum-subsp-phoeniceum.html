Glaucium corniculatum subsp. phoeniceum

Glaucium corniculatum subsp.

phoeniceum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
