Genistella flagelliformis

Genistella flagelliformis, commonly known as the slender broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in racemes, and it produces legume pods that contain seeds.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
