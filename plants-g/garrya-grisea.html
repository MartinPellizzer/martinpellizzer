Garrya grisea

Garrya grisea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Garrya grisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
