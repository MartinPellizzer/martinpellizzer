Gilia aggregata var. macrosiphon

Gilia aggregata var. macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-siphon gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
