Guillenia bracteosa

Guillenia bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted guillenia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Guillenia bracteosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
