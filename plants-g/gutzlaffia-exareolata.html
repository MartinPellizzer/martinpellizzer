Gutzlaffia exareolata

Gutzlaffia exareolata, commonly known as the barred leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a spiny exterior, with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soils and limited water availability.

Gutzlaffia exareolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and resilience.
