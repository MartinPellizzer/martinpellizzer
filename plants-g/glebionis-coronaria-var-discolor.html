Glebionis coronaria var. discolor

Glebionis coronaria var. discolor, commonly known as the pale crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a pale center.

This variety is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to other Glebionis species, making it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in its native habitat.
