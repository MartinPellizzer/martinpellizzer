Grevillea victoriae var. leptoneura

Grevillea victoriae var. leptoneura, commonly known as the slender-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, red or orange flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Proteaceae family and is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in coastal regions. Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, playing a vital role in local ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
