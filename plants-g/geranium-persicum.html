Geranium persicum

Geranium persicum, commonly known as the Persian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Geranium persicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
