Galium saxatile

Galium saxatile, commonly known as mountain bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems that form dense mats. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or rocky habitats.
