Guadua longifimbriata

Guadua longifimbriata, commonly known as the long-fringed bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges.

It is valued for its strength and is often used in construction and handicrafts.
