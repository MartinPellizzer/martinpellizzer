Gentiana affinis var. parvidentata

Gentiana affinis var. parvidentata, commonly known as the small-toothed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have small teeth along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high elevation conditions.
