Gerrardina foliosa

Gerrardina foliosa, commonly known as the leafy gerardina, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its large, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Gerrardina foliosa is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
