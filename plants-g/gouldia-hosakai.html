Gouldia hosakai

Gouldia hosakai, commonly known as the red-flowered gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 1 meter tall, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Gouldia hosakai is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
