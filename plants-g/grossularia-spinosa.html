Grossularia spinosa

Grossularia spinosa, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellow or orange berries. The plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
