Gaylussacia myrtifolia

Gaylussacia myrtifolia, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its woody stems and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands and streambanks.
