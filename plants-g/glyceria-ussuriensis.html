Glyceria ussuriensis

Glyceria ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib.

Glyceria ussuriensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
