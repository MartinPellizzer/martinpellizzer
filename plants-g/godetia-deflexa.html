Godetia deflexa

Godetia deflexa, commonly known as the bent-leaf godetia, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often slightly curled at the tips, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
