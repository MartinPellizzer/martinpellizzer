Gaultheria triangulanthera

Gaultheria triangulanthera, commonly known as the Chilean wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South America.

The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Gaultheria triangulanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
