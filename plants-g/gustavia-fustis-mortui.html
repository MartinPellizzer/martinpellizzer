Gustavia fustis-mortui

Gustavia fustis-mortui, commonly known as the ghost gum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
