Gossypium peruvianum var. braziliense

Gossypium peruvianum var. braziliense, commonly known as Brazilian cotton, is a wild cotton species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fibrous capsules that contain fluffy, white fibers used for textile production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pink to purple in color.

This variety is valued for its genetic diversity and potential in breeding programs for improved cotton varieties.
