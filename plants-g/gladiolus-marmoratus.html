Gladiolus marmoratus

Gladiolus marmoratus, commonly known as the marble gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, with intricate patterns resembling marble. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting its large, terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The marble gladiolus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
