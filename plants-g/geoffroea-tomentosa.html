Geoffroea tomentosa

Geoffroea tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
