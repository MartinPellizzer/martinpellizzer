Geranium crassicaule

Geranium crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems and palmate leaves with a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and violet, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
