Geranium holosericeum var. stuebelii

Geranium holosericeum var. stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, dry fruits known as capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
