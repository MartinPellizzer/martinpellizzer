Grewia brachypoda

Grewia brachypoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia brachypoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
