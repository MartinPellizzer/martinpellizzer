Grevillea parviflora subsp. supplicans

Grevillea parviflora subsp.

supplicans, commonly known as the small-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bright red or orange flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Grevillea parviflora subsp.

supplicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
