Gonostemon peglerae

Gonostemon peglerae, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Gonostemon peglerae is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.
