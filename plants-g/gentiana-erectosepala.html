Gentiana erectosepala

Gentiana erectosepala, commonly known as the upright-sepaled gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are tubular with five elongated sepals that stand upright, giving it its specific epithet.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blue blossoms.
