Geum macrosepalum

Geum macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with large, rounded sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
