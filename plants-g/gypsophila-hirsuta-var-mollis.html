Gypsophila hirsuta var. mollis

Gypsophila hirsuta var. mollis, commonly known as the soft hair grass or velvet gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements due to its airy appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name and distinctive texture.
