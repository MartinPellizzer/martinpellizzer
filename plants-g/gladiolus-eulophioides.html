Gladiolus eulophioides

Gladiolus eulophioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces tall, sturdy stems that bear clusters of brightly colored, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its inflorescence consists of multiple florets arranged in a spike, with each flower having three petals and three sepals.

Gladiolus eulophioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
