Galium triflorum f. rollandii

Galium triflorum f. rollandii, commonly known as the three-flowered bedstraw, is a rare and distinctive variant of the bedstraw genus.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its delicate appearance.

The inflorescence consists of clusters of three flowers, giving it the specific epithet "triflorum.".
