Grevillea guthrieana

Grevillea guthrieana, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea guthrieana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
