Gleditsia orientalis

Gleditsia orientalis, commonly known as the Persian honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including regions of China and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It is valued for its ornamental appeal, with its upright growth habit and showy, yellowish-white flowers in early summer.

Gleditsia orientalis is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and resistance to urban conditions.
