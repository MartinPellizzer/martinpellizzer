Glycicarpus edulis

Glycicarpus edulis, commonly known as the edible soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny fruit with a rough, green exterior. The fruit contains a white, fleshy pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a distinctive, sweet-and-sour flavor. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
