Grindelia adenodonta

Grindelia adenodonta, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Grindelia adenodonta is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
