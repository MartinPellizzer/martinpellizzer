Gnaphalium teydeum

Gnaphalium teydeum, commonly known as the Canary Island dwarf fleabane, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often found in coastal areas and is characteristic of the island's unique flora.

Gnaphalium teydeum is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
