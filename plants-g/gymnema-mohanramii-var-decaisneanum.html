Gymnema mohanramii var. decaisneanum

Gymnema mohanramii var. decaisneanum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive netted venation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by follicle fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and traditional medicine.
