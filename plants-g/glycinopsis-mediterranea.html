Glycinopsis mediterranea

Glycinopsis mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Glycinopsis mediterranea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
