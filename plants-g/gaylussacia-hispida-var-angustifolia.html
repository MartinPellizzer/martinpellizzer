Gaylussacia hispida var. angustifolia

Gaylussacia hispida var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and thickets. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters. The berries are red or black, maturing in the fall and attracting wildlife.

It plays a role in supporting local ecosystems and is often used in native plant landscaping.
