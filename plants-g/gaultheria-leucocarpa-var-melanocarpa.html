Gaultheria leucocarpa var. melanocarpa

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black mountain cranberry, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that produces small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a faint, pleasant aroma.

The berries are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor, making them valuable for both wildlife and human consumption.
