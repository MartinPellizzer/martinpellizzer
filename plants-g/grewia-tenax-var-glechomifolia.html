Grewia tenax var. glechomifolia

Grewia tenax var. glechomifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
