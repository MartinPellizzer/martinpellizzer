Geranium sibbaldioides subsp. elongatum

Geranium sibbaldioides subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with palmately divided, lobed leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and attractive foliage.
