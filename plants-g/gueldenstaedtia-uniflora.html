Gueldenstaedtia uniflora

Gueldenstaedtia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower vicia, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Gueldenstaedtia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in nitrogen fixation in soil.
