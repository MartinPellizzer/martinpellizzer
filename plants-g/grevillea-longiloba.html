Grevillea longiloba

Grevillea longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for honeyeaters and other native pollinators.
