Geranium aequatoriale

Geranium aequatoriale, commonly known as the Peruvian geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with a hairy surface and produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Geranium aequatoriale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
