Geodorum parviflorum

Geodorum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short, erect inflorescences. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both gardens and indoor cultivation.
