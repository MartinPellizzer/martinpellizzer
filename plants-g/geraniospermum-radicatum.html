Geraniospermum radicatum

Geraniospermum radicatum, commonly known as the desert geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with a compact structure.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
