Geranium eritreae

Geranium eritreae, commonly known as the Eritrean geranium, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Eritrea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium eritreae is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
