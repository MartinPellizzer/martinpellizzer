Grewia sessilifolia

Grewia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.
