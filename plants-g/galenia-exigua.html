Galenia exigua

Galenia exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf galenia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Galenia exigua is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
