Gymnopogon fastigiatus

Gymnopogon fastigiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
