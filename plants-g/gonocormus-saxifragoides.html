Gonocormus saxifragoides

Gonocormus saxifragoides, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Gonocormus saxifragoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
