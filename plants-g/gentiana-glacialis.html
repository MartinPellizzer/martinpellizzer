Gentiana glacialis

Gentiana glacialis, commonly known as the glacier gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana glacialis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in mountain meadows and alpine tundra.
