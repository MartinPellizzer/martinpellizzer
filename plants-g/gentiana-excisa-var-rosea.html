Gentiana excisa var. rosea

Gentiana excisa var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bright blue to violet flowers, which are often tinged with pink or rose-like hues, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
