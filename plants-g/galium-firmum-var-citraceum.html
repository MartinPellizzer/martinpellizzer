Galium firmum var. citraceum

Galium firmum var. citraceum, commonly known as the citron wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features slender, branched stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets that are typically hairy underneath.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
