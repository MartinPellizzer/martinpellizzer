Geranium core

Geranium core, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both garden and indoor settings.
