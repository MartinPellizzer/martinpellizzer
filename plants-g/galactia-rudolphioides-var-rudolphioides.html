Galactia rudolphioides var. rudolphioides

Galactia rudolphioides var. rudolphioides, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces legume pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
