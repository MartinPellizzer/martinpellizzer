Gentiana kingdonii

Gentiana kingdonii, commonly known as the Kingdon's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, aiding in water retention.

Gentiana kingdonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
