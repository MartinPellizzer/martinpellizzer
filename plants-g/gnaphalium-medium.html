Gnaphalium medium

Gnaphalium medium, commonly known as common cudweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gnaphalium medium is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
