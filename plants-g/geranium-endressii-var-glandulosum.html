Geranium endressii var. glandulosum

Geranium endressii var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
