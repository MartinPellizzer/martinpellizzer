Glandularia occulta

Glandularia occulta, commonly known as the hidden glandular bluemink, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, blue-violet flowers and glandular hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant belongs to the tribe Lophopetalae within the mallow family, Malvaceae. Its glandular structures are thought to play a role in defense against herbivores and water retention.

Glandularia occulta is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
