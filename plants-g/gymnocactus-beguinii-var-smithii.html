Gymnocactus beguinii var. smithii

Gymnocactus beguinii var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's naked cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in small, white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
