Gymnocalycium oenanthemum var. tillianum

Gymnocalycium oenanthemum var. tillianum, commonly known as the Tillianum moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
