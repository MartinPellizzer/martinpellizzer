Gelasia filifolia

Gelasia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf gelasia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
