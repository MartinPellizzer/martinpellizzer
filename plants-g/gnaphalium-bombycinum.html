Gnaphalium bombycinum

Gnaphalium bombycinum, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by silvery-gray, woolly leaves. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Gnaphalium bombycinum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
