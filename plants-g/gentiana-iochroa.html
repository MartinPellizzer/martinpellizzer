Gentiana iochroa

Gentiana iochroa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
