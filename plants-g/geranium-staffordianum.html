Geranium staffordianum

Geranium staffordianum, commonly known as the Stafford geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Geranium staffordianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
