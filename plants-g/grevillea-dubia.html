Grevillea dubia

Grevillea dubia, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and vibrant, red or orange inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with a spreading habit.

Grevillea dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
