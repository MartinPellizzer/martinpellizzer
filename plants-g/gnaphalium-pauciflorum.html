Gnaphalium pauciflorum

Gnaphalium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to white. The plant produces numerous tiny florets arranged in dense clusters, which contribute to its ability to retain its color and form for extended periods. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Gnaphalium pauciflorum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
