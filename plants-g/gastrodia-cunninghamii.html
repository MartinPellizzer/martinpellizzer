Gastrodia cunninghamii

Gastrodia cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain devil or Cunningham's gastrodia, is an orchid species native to Australia.

It is a tuberous plant that grows in shaded, moist environments, typically in forested areas. The plant lacks true leaves and instead relies on its underground tubers for photosynthesis. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it blooms in spring.

Gastrodia cunninghamii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often harvested for use in traditional remedies.
