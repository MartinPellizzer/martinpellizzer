Garcinia brieyi

Garcinia brieyi, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, acidic pulp, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Garcinia brieyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
