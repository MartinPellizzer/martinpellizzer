Glycosmis arborea var. linearifoliolata

Glycosmis arborea var. linearifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves with a distinctive linearly arranged leaflets. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
