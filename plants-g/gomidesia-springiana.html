Gomidesia springiana

Gomidesia springiana, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gomidesia springiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
