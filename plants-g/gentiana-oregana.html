Gentiana oregana

Gentiana oregana, commonly known as the Indian paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vivid red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges, often in areas with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The plant blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies with its nectar-rich flowers.
