Garhadiolus papposus

Garhadiolus papposus, commonly known as the hairy pappus grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a hairy pappus, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in dry, open habitats.

Garhadiolus papposus is often found in savannas and rocky areas, where it contributes to soil stability and provides habitat for small animals.
