Gomphrena perennis var. rosea

Gomphrena perennis var. rosea, commonly known as the rosea gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, adding a delicate texture to its overall appearance.
