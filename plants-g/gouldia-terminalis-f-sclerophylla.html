Gouldia terminalis f. sclerophylla

Gouldia terminalis f. sclerophylla, commonly known as the rock sheathveld, is a unique variant of the Gouldia species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
