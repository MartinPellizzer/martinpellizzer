Grewia aurantiaca

Grewia aurantiaca, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and bright red to orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Grewia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat and food for native fauna.
