Grewia burretii

Grewia burretii, commonly known as the sour fig or wild date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
