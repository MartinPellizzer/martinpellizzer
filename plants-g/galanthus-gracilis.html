Galanthus gracilis

Galanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender snowdrop, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces delicate, white flowers with a distinctive greenish tint, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in shady, moist woodland environments. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Galanthus gracilis is relatively hardy and can be cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
