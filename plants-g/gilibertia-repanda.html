Gilibertia repanda

Gilibertia repanda, commonly known as the red millet or red grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish tint that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Gilibertia repanda is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as a forage crop for livestock.
