Gossypium croceum

Gossypium croceum, commonly known as the yellow cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and produces fluffy, cotton-like fibers that are harvested for various industrial uses. Its botanical structure includes alternate leaves with a hairy surface and a woody stem that supports its growth.

Gossypium croceum is often cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textiles and other commercial applications.
