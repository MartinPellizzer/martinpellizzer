Gonolobus leucodermis

Gonolobus leucodermis, commonly known as the white-skinned morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a fibrous root system and can grow up to several meters in length. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open when mature.

It thrives in humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.
