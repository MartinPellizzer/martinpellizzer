Geraniospermum oxalidifolium

Geraniospermum oxalidifolium, commonly known as the oxalid-like geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves resembling those of the oxalis genus. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or red. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
