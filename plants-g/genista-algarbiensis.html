Genista algarbiensis

Genista algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarve broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in the Mediterranean climate. It produces yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its shrub-like structure and hardy nature make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
