Gongora nigropunctata

Gongora nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and striking, dark purple to black flowers with yellow markings. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
