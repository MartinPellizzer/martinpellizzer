Galium sparsiflorum

Galium sparsiflorum, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, often hairy stems that are typically less than 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium sparsiflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
