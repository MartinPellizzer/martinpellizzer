Georgina coccinea var. flava

Georgina coccinea var. flava, commonly known as the yellow waxflower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
