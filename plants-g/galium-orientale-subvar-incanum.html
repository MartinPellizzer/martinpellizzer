Galium orientale subvar. incanum

Galium orientale subvar. incanum, commonly known as the whiteweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
