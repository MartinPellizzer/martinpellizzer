Galium andrewsii subsp. gatense

Galium andrewsii subsp.

gatense, commonly known as the Gatens galium, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
