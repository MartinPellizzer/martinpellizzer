Gouldia terminalis f. kahili

Gouldia terminalis f. kahili, commonly known as the Kahili torch ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, red-orange inflorescences that resemble torches, which are borne on long, slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and contribute to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
