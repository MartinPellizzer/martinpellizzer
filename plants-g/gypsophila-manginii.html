Gypsophila manginii

Gypsophila manginii, commonly known as the Mongolian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its stems are often branched and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

Gypsophila manginii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
