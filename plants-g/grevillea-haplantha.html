Grevillea haplantha

Grevillea haplantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea haplantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
