Genista bourgaei

Genista bourgaei, commonly known as the Canary Islands broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
