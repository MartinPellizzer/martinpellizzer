Grindelia montana

Grindelia montana, commonly known as the mountain gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Grindelia montana is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
