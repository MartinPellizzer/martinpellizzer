Glochidion moluccanum

Glochidion moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
