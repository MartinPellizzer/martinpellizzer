Grewia palawanensis

Grewia palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan bush or Palawan gumamela, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Grewia palawanensis is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides, thriving in tropical climates.
