Geigeria rhodesiana

Geigeria rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodesian geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geigeria rhodesiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
