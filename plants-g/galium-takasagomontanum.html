Galium takasagomontanum

Galium takasagomontanum, commonly known as the Japanese bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with fine hairs and produces small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium takasagomontanum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
