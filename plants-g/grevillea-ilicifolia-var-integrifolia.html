Grevillea ilicifolia var. integrifolia

Grevillea ilicifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces striking red or orange cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
