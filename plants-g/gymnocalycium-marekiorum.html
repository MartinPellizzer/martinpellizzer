Gymnocalycium marekiorum

Gymnocalycium marekiorum, commonly known as the Marek's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a succulent plant, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry environments.

Gymnocalycium marekiorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
