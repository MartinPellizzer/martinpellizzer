Glossogyne orthochaeta

Glossogyne orthochaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
