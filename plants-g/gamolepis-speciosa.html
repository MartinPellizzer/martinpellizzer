Gamolepis speciosa

Gamolepis speciosa, commonly known as the showy gamolepis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Gamolepis speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
