Genea fasciculata

Genea fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered genea, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
