Gentiana stipitata subsp. stipitata

Gentiana stipitata subsp.

stipitata, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine habitats.
