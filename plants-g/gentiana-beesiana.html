Gentiana beesiana

Gentiana beesiana, commonly known as the bees' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and erect.

The flowers are tubular with prominent blue or purple petals, attracting various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
