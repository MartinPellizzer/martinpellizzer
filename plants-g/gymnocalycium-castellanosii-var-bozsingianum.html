Gymnocalycium castellanosii var. bozsingianum

Gymnocalycium castellanosii var. bozsingianum, commonly known as the Bozsing's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
