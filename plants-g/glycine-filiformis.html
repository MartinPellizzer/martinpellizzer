Glycine filiformis

Glycine filiformis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its thin, wiry stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Glycine filiformis is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often used as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment through nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
