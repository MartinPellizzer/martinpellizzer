Gomphrena aggregata

Gomphrena aggregata, commonly known as the clustered bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Gomphrena aggregata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flower clusters and drought tolerance.
