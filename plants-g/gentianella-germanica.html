Gentianella germanica

Gentianella germanica, commonly known as the German gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and dark green, with a dense cluster of blue to violet flowers at the top. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Gentianaceae family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
