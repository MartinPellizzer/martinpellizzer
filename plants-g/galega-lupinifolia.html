Galega lupinifolia

Galega lupinifolia, commonly known as the slender lupin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
