Gentiana phyllopoda

Gentiana phyllopoda, commonly known as the leafy gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gentiana phyllopoda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
