Grossularia × succirubra

Grossularia × succirubra, commonly known as the firecracker bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that resemble small firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and culinary uses.
