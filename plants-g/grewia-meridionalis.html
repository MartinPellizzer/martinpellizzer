Grewia meridionalis

Grewia meridionalis, commonly known as the sour fig or red bush willow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters and are often followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Grewia meridionalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
