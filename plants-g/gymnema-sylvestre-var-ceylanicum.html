Gymnema sylvestre var. ceylanicum

Gymnema sylvestre var. ceylanicum, commonly known as the Indian licorice or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive milky sap. The plant is known for its unique ability to inhibit the taste of sweetness, a property attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. Its leaves contain various bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Gymnema sylvestre var. ceylanicum is widely used in traditional medicine and is gaining attention for its possible role in managing blood sugar levels.
