Gentiana interrupta

Gentiana interrupta, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana interrupta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
