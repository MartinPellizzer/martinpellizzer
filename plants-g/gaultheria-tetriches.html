Gaultheria tetriches

Gaultheria tetriches, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are red when mature, are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating pain and inflammation.
