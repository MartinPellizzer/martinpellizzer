Glycine homblei

Glycine homblei, commonly known as the Hombli bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems, which help it climb support structures. Glycine homblei is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species within the legume family.
