Geranium fothergillianum

Geranium fothergillianum, commonly known as the Fothergill geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Geranium fothergillianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
