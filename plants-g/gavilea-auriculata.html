Gavilea auriculata

Gavilea auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped lily, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant's most distinctive feature is its large, ear-like bracts that surround the inflorescence, giving it its common name. Gavilea auriculata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and floral arrangements.
