Garcinia andersonii

Garcinia andersonii, commonly known as the Andes Garcinia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are typically harvested for their medicinal and culinary uses. Garcinia andersonii thrives in humid, cool climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptation to high-altitude environments and potential pharmacological properties.
