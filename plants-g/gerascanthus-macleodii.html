Gerascanthus macleodii

Gerascanthus macleodii, commonly known as the Macleod's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with upright stems and small, rounded leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Gerascanthus macleodii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
