Gentiana ligustica

Gentiana ligustica, commonly known as the Italian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
