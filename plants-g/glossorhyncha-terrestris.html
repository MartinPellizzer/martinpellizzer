Glossorhyncha terrestris

Glossorhyncha terrestris, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, showy flower that has three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being highly modified. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in soil while absorbing nutrients.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
