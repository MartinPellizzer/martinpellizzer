Gerardia pectinata

Gerardia pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Gerardia pectinata is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
