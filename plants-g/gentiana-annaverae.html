Gentiana annaverae

Gentiana annaverae, commonly known as the Annamite gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Gentiana annaverae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its limited distribution.
