Gagea aleppoana

Gagea aleppoana, commonly known as Aleppo squill, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, rounded berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Gagea aleppoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
