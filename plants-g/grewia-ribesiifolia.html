Grewia ribesiifolia

Grewia ribesiifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often ribbed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
