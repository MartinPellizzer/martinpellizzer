Galium penduliflorum

Galium penduliflorum, commonly known as the hangingflower bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium penduliflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
