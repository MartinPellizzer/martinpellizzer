Glossarion rhodanthum

Glossarion rhodanthum, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins contrasting against a pale background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Glossarion rhodanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
