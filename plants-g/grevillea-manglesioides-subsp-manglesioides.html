Grevillea manglesioides subsp. manglesioides

Grevillea manglesioides subsp.

manglesioides, commonly known as the Mangles' grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features bright orange to red flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, which are a characteristic feature of the Grevillea genus.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
