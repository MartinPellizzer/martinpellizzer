Grewia nitida

Grewia nitida, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are often mistaken for fruits. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
