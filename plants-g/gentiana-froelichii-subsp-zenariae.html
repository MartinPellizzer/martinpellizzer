Gentiana froelichii subsp. zenariae

Gentiana froelichii subsp.

zenariae, commonly known as the Altai gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
