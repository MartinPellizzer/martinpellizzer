Glycine apios

Glycine apios, commonly known as the climbing sweet pea, is a leguminous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Glycine apios is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
