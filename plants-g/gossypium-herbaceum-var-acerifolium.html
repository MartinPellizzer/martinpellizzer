Gossypium herbaceum var. acerifolium

Gossypium herbaceum var. acerifolium, commonly known as the maple-leaf cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves.

This plant is native to certain regions of South America and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with lobed edges, giving it a unique appearance compared to typical cotton species. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton bolls that are harvested for their fibers, which are used in textile manufacturing.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the genetic diversity of cotton species.
