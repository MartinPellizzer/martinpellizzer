Gnaphalium consanguineum

Gnaphalium consanguineum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, preferring temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by pale green bracts.

Its leaves are linear, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
