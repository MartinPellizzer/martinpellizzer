Grindelia stricta var. andersonii

Grindelia stricta var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's brittleblow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ecological role in stabilizing soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
