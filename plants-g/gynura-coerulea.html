Gynura coerulea

Gynura coerulea, commonly known as the blue gynura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Gynura coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its unique color and foliage.
