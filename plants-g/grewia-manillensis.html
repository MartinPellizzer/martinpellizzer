Grewia manillensis

Grewia manillensis, commonly known as the Manila globemallow, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Grewia manillensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
