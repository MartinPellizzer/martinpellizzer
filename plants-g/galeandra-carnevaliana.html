Galeandra carnevaliana

Galeandra carnevaliana, commonly known as the Carnaval orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces pendulous, elongated flower spikes that bear multiple blooms, which are often fragrant. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Galeandra carnevaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
