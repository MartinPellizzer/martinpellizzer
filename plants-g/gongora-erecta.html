Gongora erecta

Gongora erecta, commonly known as the erect gongora, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store moisture.

Gongora erecta is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
