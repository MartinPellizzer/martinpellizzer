Goodyera vieillardii

Goodyera vieillardii, commonly known as the copperleaf or fairy slipper, is a low-growing epiphytic orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It possesses waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive, pouch-like structure that resembles a slipper. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
