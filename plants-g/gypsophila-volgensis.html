Gypsophila volgensis

Gypsophila volgensis, commonly known as the Siberian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered blooms that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental value.

Gypsophila volgensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to its ecological significance.
