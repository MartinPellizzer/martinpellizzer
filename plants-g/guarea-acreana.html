Guarea acreana

Guarea acreana, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces woody capsules as fruits.

Guarea acreana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
