Geum coccineum

Geum coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are often hairy and have a reddish tint. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and wildflower landscapes.
