Gymnocalycium marsoneri subsp. megatae

Gymnocalycium marsoneri subsp.

megatae, commonly known as the giant pincushion cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Argentina. It is characterized by its large, spiny stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, forming a dense, globular shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically solitary and located at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
