Geranium fangii

Geranium fangii, commonly known as the Fang's geranium, is a species native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
