Gutierrezia paniculata var. floribunda

Gutierrezia paniculata var. floribunda, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
