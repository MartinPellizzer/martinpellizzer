Geraniospermum divaricatum

Geraniospermum divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Geraniospermum divaricatum is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and requires minimal water once established.
