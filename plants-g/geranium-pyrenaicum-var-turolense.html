Geranium pyrenaicum var. turolense

Geranium pyrenaicum var. turolense, commonly known as the Turolense geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
