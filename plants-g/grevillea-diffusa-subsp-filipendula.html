Grevillea diffusa subsp. filipendula

Grevillea diffusa subsp.

filipendula, commonly known as the pink-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are often pink or red in color, providing ornamental value in gardens.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and drier garden environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for native landscaping.
