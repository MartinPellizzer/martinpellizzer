Garcinia mungotia

Garcinia mungotia, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Garcinia mungotia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
