Gossypium barbadense var. hirsutum

Gossypium barbadense var. hirsutum, commonly known as upland cotton, is a species of cotton plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy gray-green foliage and produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the cotton fibers. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight. Its fibers are prized for their strength and are widely used in the textile industry.

The plant's botanical features make it a key crop in global agriculture.
