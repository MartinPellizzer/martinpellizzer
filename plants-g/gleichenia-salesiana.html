Gleichenia salesiana

Gleichenia salesiana, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, linear pinnae that give it a lacy appearance.

Gleichenia salesiana is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forest understories where it thrives in humid conditions.
