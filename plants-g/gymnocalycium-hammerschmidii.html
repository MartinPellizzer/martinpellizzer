Gymnocalycium hammerschmidii

Gymnocalycium hammerschmidii, commonly known as the Hammerschmid's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with thick, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

Gymnocalycium hammerschmidii is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in dry climates.
