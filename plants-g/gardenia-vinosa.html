Gardenia vinosa

Gardenia vinosa, commonly known as the purple gardenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia vinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique floral scent.
