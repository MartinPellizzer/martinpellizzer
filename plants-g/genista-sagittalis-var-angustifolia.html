Genista sagittalis var. angustifolia

Genista sagittalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are often spiny and covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive arrowhead shape, giving it its scientific name.

It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
