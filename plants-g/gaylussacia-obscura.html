Gaylussacia obscura

Gaylussacia obscura, commonly known as black huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
