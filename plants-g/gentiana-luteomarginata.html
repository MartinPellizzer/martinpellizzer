Gentiana luteomarginata

Gentiana luteomarginata, commonly known as the yellow-margin gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are tubular with prominent yellow margins, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
