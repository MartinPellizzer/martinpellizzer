Gardenia anisophylla

Gardenia anisophylla, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gardenia anisophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
