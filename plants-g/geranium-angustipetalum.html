Geranium angustipetalum

Geranium angustipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are hairy and often branched, supporting clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals that are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its common name. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
