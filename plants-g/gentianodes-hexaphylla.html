Gentianodes hexaphylla

Gentianodes hexaphylla, commonly known as the six-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
