Geissorhiza inaequalis

Geissorhiza inaequalis, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and has strap-like, grassy leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive purple or red throat.
