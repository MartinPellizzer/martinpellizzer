Galium erythrorrhizon var. debeauxii

Galium erythrorrhizon var. debeauxii, commonly known as the red-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its vibrant red stems and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
