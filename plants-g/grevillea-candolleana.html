Grevillea candolleana

Grevillea candolleana, commonly known as the pink-flowered grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-red inflorescences that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and inland regions.

Grevillea candolleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in native gardens.
