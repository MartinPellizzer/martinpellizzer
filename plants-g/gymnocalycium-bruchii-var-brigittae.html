Gymnocalycium bruchii var. brigittae

Gymnocalycium bruchii var. brigittae, commonly known as the Bridget's moon cactus, is a unique hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Gymnocalycium mihanthera and Gymnocalycium bruchii.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. The plant has a thick, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces a fleshy, segmented stem that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This cactus is well-suited for indoor growing and requires minimal water and bright, indirect sunlight.
