Gompholobium cyaninum

Gompholobium cyaninum, commonly known as the blue wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Gompholobium cyaninum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
