Gymnema affine

Gymnema affine, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its milky sap and the presence of gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. Gymnema affine thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
