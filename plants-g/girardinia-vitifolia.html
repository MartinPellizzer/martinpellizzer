Girardinia vitifolia

Girardinia vitifolia, commonly known as the wineleaf or wineleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. Girardinia vitifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
