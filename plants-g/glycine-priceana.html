Glycine priceana

Glycine priceana, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a perennial vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Glycine priceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
