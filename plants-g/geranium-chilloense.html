Geranium chilloense

Geranium chilloense, commonly known as the Chilean geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Geranium chilloense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
