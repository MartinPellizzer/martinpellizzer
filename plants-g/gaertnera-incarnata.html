Gaertnera incarnata

Gaertnera incarnata, commonly known as the pink-flowered shrub, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
