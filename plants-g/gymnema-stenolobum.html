Gymnema stenolobum

Gymnema stenolobum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap that contains various bioactive compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests and shrublands.

Gymnema stenolobum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
