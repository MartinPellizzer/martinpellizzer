Gomphrena decumbens subf. costaricensis

Gomphrena decumbens subf.

costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican gomphrena, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, spiky flower heads.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that range in color from pink to red, often resembling the shape of a crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
