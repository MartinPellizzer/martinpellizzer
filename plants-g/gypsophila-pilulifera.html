Gypsophila pilulifera

Gypsophila pilulifera, commonly known as the cushion gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth form, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in rockeries and alpine settings.
