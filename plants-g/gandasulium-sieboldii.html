Gandasulium sieboldii

Gandasulium sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
