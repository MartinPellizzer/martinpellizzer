Gnaphalium jaliscense

Gnaphalium jaliscense, commonly known as the Jalisco featherweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, fluffy white flowers that are often mistaken for cotton or down.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
