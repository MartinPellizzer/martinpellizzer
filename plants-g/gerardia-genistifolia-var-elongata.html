Gerardia genistifolia var. elongata

Gerardia genistifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
