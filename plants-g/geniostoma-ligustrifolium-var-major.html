Geniostoma ligustrifolium var. major

Geniostoma ligustrifolium var. major, commonly known as the large-leaved geniostoma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to a variety of garden conditions.
