Grewia cyclea

Grewia cyclea, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.
