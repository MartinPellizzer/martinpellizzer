Gilibertia membranifolia

Gilibertia membranifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf gilbertia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Gilibertia membranifolia is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
