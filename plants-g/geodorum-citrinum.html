Geodorum citrinum

Geodorum citrinum, commonly known as the golden bamboo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer.

Geodorum citrinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
