Glyceria vilfoidea

Glyceria vilfoidea, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This perennial species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Glyceria vilfoidea is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
