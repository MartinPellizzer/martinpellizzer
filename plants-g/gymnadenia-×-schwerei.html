Gymnadenia × schwerei

Gymnadenia × schwerei, commonly known as the hybrid marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, a common feature in orchids, and are often visited by various pollinators.

Gymnadenia × schwerei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
