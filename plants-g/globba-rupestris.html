Globba rupestris

Globba rupestris, commonly known as the rock globba, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Globba rupestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
