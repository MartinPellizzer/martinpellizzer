Gregoria vitaliana

Gregoria vitaliana, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.

Gregoria vitaliana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
