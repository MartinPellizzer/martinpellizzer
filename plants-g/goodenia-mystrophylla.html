Goodenia mystrophylla

Goodenia mystrophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.
