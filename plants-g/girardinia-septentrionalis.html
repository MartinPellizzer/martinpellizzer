Girardinia septentrionalis

Girardinia septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern girardinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Girardinia septentrionalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
