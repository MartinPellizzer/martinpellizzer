Gnaphalium gymnocephalum

Gnaphalium gymnocephalum, commonly known as the naked-headed cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy stems, often found in dry, open habitats. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
