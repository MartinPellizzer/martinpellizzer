Gymnocalycium hamatum

Gymnocalycium hamatum, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, often brightly colored flowers. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. Gymnocalycium hamatum typically grows in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
