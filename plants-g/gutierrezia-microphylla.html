Gutierrezia microphylla

Gutierrezia microphylla, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, silver-gray leaves and yellow flower heads make it visually distinctive.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
