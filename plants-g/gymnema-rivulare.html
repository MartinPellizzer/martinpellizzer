Gymnema rivulare

Gymnema rivulare, commonly known as the Indian nettle or small-leaved gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to suppress sweet taste, a property attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Gymnema rivulare is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
