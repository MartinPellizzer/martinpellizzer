Garcinia crassinervis

Garcinia crassinervis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible.

Garcinia crassinervis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
