Gentianella grantii

Gentianella grantii, commonly known as the Grant's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine pubescence.

Gentianella grantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
