Galium viridulum

Galium viridulum, commonly known as green bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium viridulum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
