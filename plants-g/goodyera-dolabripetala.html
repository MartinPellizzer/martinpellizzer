Goodyera dolabripetala

Goodyera dolabripetala, commonly known as the broad-lobed maidenhair fern, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate vein patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of many orchids. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
