Goodyera fusca

Goodyera fusca, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, strap-like leaves and its distinctive, pinkish-white, thread-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. It is a perennial herb that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Goodyera fusca is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
