Gymnosporia angustifolia

Gymnosporia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow and are followed by spiny capsules that protect the seeds.

Gymnosporia angustifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
