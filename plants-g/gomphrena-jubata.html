Gomphrena jubata

Gomphrena jubata, commonly known as the crimson globe flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spherical inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant is herbaceous, with branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Gomphrena jubata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
