Gerascanthus mairei

Gerascanthus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Gerascanthus mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
