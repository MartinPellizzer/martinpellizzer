Grastidium pulvinatum

Grastidium pulvinatum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its bark is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Grastidium pulvinatum thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
