Gladiolus longicollis subsp. longicollis

Gladiolus longicollis subsp.

longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of brightly colored, sword-shaped petals, typical of the Iridaceae family. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in grassland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
