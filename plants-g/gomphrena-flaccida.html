Gomphrena flaccida

Gomphrena flaccida, commonly known as the brittle gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, feathery bracts that form showy, flower-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful, papery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena flaccida is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
