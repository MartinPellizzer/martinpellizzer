Gaillardia pulchella var. albiflora

Gaillardia pulchella var. albiflora, commonly known as the white firewheel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The variety albiflora is distinguished by its predominantly white petals with orange or red centers, creating a striking visual contrast. It typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
