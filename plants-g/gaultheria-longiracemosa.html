Gaultheria longiracemosa

Gaultheria longiracemosa, commonly known as the long-stemmed wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are rich in aromatic oils and are used in various traditional and commercial applications. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Gaultheria longiracemosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
