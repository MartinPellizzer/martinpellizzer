Glebionis carinata

Glebionis carinata, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into long, slender seed heads, typical of the dandelion family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields.

Glebionis carinata is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
