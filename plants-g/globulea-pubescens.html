Globulea pubescens

Globulea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy globular plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and hairy, pubescent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Globulea pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
