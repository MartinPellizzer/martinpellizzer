Goniorrhachis marginata

Goniorrhachis marginata, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
