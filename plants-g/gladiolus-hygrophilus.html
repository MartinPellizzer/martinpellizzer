Gladiolus hygrophilus

Gladiolus hygrophilus, commonly known as the water gladiolus, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically blooms in late summer to early fall.

This species is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
