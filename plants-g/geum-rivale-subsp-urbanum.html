Geum rivale subsp. urbanum

Geum rivale subsp.

urbanum, commonly known as the urban garden sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically features trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in urban environments such as gardens and along sidewalks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary applications.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various garden settings.
