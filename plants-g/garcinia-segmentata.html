Garcinia segmentata

Garcinia segmentata, commonly known as the segmented garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, fleshy pulp. Garcinia segmentata is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Clusiaceae family.
