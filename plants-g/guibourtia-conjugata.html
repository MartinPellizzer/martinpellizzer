Guibourtia conjugata

Guibourtia conjugata, commonly known as African shea tree or butterwood, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that contain large, oily seeds used for making shea butter. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
