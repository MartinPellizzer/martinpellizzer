Gaultheria lanigera var. lanigera

Gaultheria lanigera var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.

Gaultheria lanigera var. lanigera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
