Gymnogramma cordata var. bipinnata

Gymnogramma cordata var. bipinnata, commonly known as the bipinnate staghorn fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clustered, leathery fronds that often resemble the antlers of a stag. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, with a unique, intricate branching pattern.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.
