Gasteria picta

Gasteria picta, commonly known as the painted gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers on tall, leafless stems during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Gasteria picta is popular as a low-maintenance houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and resilience.
