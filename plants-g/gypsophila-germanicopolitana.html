Gypsophila germanicopolitana

Gypsophila germanicopolitana, commonly known as the Germanicopolitan gypsophila, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to southern Europe. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila germanicopolitana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
