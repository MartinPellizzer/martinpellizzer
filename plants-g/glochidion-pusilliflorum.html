Glochidion pusilliflorum

Glochidion pusilliflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered Glochidion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Glochidion pusilliflorum is often found in lowland forests and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
