Grossularia × innominata

Grossularia × innominata, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety and maturity. These berries are typically harvested in late summer and are valued for their tart flavor and culinary uses.

Grossularia × innominata is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive leaves and seasonal fruit display.
