Galium asperum subsp. jordanii

Galium asperum subsp.

jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features slender, erect stems covered in stiff, hairy hairs, and its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Jordan's bedstraw is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
