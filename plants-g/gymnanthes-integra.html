Gymnanthes integra

Gymnanthes integra, commonly known as the golden star tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
