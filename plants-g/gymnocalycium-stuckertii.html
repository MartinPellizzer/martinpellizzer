Gymnocalycium stuckertii

Gymnocalycium stuckertii, commonly known as the Stuckert's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are often covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
