Glochidion heyneanum var. arunachalense

Glochidion heyneanum var. arunachalense, commonly known as the Arunachal glabrous leaf mallow, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions of Arunachal Pradesh.
