Geranium dissectum f. purpureum

Geranium dissectum f. purpureum, commonly known as the purpleleaf geranium, is a cultivar of the geranium plant characterized by its distinctive purple foliage.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are finely cut and often display a striking contrast of color against the stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
