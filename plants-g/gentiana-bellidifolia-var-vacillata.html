Gentiana bellidifolia var. vacillata

Gentiana bellidifolia var. vacillata, commonly known as the variable gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat succulent.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
