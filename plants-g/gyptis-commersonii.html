Gyptis commersonii

Gyptis commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. It produces small, edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Gyptis commersonii is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
