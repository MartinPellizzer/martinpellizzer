Gentiana papillosa

Gentiana papillosa, commonly known as the silky gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gentiana papillosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
