Gleditsia japonica f. inarmata

Gleditsia japonica f. inarmata, commonly known as the Japanese honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by flat, bean-like pods that contain a single, hard seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in urban environments.

The variety inarmata is distinguished by its lack of thorns, making it more suitable for landscaping in public spaces.
