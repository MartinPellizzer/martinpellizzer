Genista elatior

Genista elatior, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.
