Gordonia obtusifolia

Gordonia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gum tree, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.
