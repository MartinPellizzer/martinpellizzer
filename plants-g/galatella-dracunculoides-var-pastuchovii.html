Galatella dracunculoides var. pastuchovii

Galatella dracunculoides var. pastuchovii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine or subarctic environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the study of cold-climate flora.
