Geranium cinereum var. ponticum

Geranium cinereum var. ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically rounded with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular garden plant.
