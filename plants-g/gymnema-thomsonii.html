Gymnema thomsonii

Gymnema thomsonii, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of India and Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in research related to diabetes. Gymnema thomsonii contains compounds like gymnemic acids, which are believed to have beneficial effects on blood glucose levels.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
