Gypsophila davurica var. angustifolia

Gypsophila davurica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. It is also used in floral arrangements due to its feathery appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical name reflects its geographical origin and distinctive leaf morphology.
