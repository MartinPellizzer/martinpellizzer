Gardenia gardneri

Gardenia gardneri, commonly known as the Hawaiian gardenia, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Hawaiian culture.

The flowers are also used in lei-making, highlighting its cultural and botanical significance.
