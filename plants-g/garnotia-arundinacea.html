Garnotia arundinacea

Garnotia arundinacea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight waxy coating.

Garnotia arundinacea is often used in ornamental landscaping and has historical uses in basket-making and thatching.
