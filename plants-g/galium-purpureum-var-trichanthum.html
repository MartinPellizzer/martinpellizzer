Galium purpureum var. trichanthum

Galium purpureum var. trichanthum, commonly known as purple bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy stems and leaves are characteristic features of the species.
