Glycine viscida

Glycine viscida, commonly known as the climbing sensitive plant, is a leguminous vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and sensitive leaves that fold when touched. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Glycine viscida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
