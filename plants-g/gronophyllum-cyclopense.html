Gronophyllum cyclopense

Gronophyllum cyclopense, commonly known as the cyclopense ironwood, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark that provides a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its durability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
