Geigeria dinteri

Geigeria dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's geigeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, silver-gray leaves adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
