Grammitis serrulata

Grammitis serrulata, commonly known as the serrated grammitis, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth form. The fronds are usually slender and have a soft, grass-like appearance, with serrated margins that give the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis serrulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
