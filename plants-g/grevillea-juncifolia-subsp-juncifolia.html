Grevillea juncifolia subsp. juncifolia

Grevillea juncifolia subsp.

juncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
