Gnaphalium helleri

Gnaphalium helleri, commonly known as Heller's feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fluffy, white to pale yellow capitula composed of numerous small florets. It typically grows in alpine or arctic environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

Gnaphalium helleri is often found in open habitats such as meadows and scree slopes, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
