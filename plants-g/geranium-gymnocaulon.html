Geranium gymnocaulon

Geranium gymnocaulon, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil in partial to full sun.

Geranium gymnocaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
