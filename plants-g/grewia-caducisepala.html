Grewia caducisepala

Grewia caducisepala, commonly known as the delicate hairleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, often accompanied by fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
