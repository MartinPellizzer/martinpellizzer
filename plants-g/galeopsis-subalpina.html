Galeopsis subalpina

Galeopsis subalpina, commonly known as Alpine hedge-nettle, is a small annual herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs, with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are small, pale blue to purple, and clustered in whorls along the stems.

Galeopsis subalpina is often found in disturbed or open environments, contributing to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
