Geum aleppicum

Geum aleppicum, commonly known as Aleppo groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with orange centers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
