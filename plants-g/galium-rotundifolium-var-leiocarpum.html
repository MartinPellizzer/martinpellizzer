Galium rotundifolium var. leiocarpum

Galium rotundifolium var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth false agrimony, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has rounded, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
