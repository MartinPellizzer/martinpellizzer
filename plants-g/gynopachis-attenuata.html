Gynopachis attenuata

Gynopachis attenuata, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and specialized structures called pappi. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive bracts that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Gynopachis attenuata is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.
