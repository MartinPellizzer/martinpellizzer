Geranium pyrenaicum var. lusitanicum

Geranium pyrenaicum var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
