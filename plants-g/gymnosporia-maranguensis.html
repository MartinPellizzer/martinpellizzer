Gymnosporia maranguensis

Gymnosporia maranguensis, commonly known as the Maranguense thornveld, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The species is often found in grassland and thicket habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
