Gomphrena decumbens var. grandifolia

Gomphrena decumbens var. grandifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, feathery blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, adding a delicate, airy appearance to gardens.
