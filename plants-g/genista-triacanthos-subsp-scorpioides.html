Genista triacanthos subsp. scorpioides

Genista triacanthos subsp.

scorpioides, commonly known as the thorned broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, often thorned stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
