Genista postranensis

Genista postranensis, commonly known as the Transylvania broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in countries like Romania and Hungary.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense racemes.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.
