Gymnopetalum calyculatum

Gymnopetalum calyculatum, commonly known as the smooth petal orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with smooth, petal-like structures. The plant typically grows in high-altitude, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Gymnopetalum calyculatum is considered a rare and endangered species.
