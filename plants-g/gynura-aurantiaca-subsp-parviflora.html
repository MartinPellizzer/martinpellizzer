Gynura aurantiaca subsp. parviflora

Gynura aurantiaca subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the red-flowered spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia. It features prominently veined, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, bright orange to red flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
