Galium halleri

Galium halleri, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, thin, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Galium halleri is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions.
