Gentiana roseolilacina

Gentiana roseolilacina, commonly known as the pink gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or purple corolla, and they attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
