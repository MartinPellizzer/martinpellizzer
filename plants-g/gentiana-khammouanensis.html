Gentiana khammouanensis

Gentiana khammouanensis, commonly known as the Khammouan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Laos.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are characterized by their five-petaled structure and striking coloration.

Gentiana khammouanensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
