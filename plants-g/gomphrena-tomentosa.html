Gomphrena tomentosa

Gomphrena tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and spherical, showy flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its botanical features include small, lance-shaped leaves and bract-like petals that form tight, globular clusters.

Gomphrena tomentosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
