Guyonia ciliata

Guyonia ciliata, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Guyonia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
