Galax urceolata

Galax urceolata, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, reddish-purple leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
