Garayella

Garayella, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Garayella is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
