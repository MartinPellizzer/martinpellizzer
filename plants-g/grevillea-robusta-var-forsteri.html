Grevillea robusta var. forsteri

Grevillea robusta var. forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's waratah, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red, woody flower spikes that bloom in late summer to autumn. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and suburban gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
