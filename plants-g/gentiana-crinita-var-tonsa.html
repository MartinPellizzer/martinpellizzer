Gentiana crinita var. tonsa

Gentiana crinita var. tonsa, commonly known as the silky gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
