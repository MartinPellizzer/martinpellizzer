Galanthus elwesii subsp. tuebitaki

Galanthus elwesii subsp.

tuebitaki, commonly known as the Japanese snowdrop, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Japan. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, white, nodding flowers with green markings.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil. It is typically found in forest understories and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is noted for its distinct floral characteristics and is a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
