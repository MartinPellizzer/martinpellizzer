Geanthus vignaui

Geanthus vignaui, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geanthus vignaui is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
