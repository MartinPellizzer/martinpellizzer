Guiabara

Guiabara, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles. The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that resembles a pepper, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
