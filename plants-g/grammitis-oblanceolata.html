Grammitis oblanceolata

Grammitis oblanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of lance-shaped, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, forming compact, cushion-like clusters. Its fronds are usually dark green and glossy, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Grammitis oblanceolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
