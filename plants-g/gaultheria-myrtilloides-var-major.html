Gaultheria myrtilloides var. major

Gaultheria myrtilloides var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
