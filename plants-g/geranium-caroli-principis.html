Geranium caroli-principis

Geranium caroli-principis, commonly known as the royal geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
