Grossularia congdonii

Grossularia congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically covered in a fine white bloom. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.
