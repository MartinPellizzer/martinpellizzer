Grewia semlikiensis

Grewia semlikiensis, commonly known as the East African globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
