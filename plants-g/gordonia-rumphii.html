Gordonia rumphii

Gordonia rumphii, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a reddish tinge at the base. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are known to emit a sweet, fragrant scent, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and furniture making.
