Gaillardia hallii

Gaillardia hallii, commonly known as the Mexican firewheel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow ray flowers, which are surrounded by a central disk of yellow-orange petals. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across arid regions.

Gaillardia hallii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
