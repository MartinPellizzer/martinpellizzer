Grindelia sublanuginosa

Grindelia sublanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal regions. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic, resinous scent.

Grindelia sublanuginosa is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
