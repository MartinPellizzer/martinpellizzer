Gynura eximia

Gynura eximia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. Gynura eximia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.

It prefers warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
