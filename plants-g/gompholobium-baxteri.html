Gompholobium baxteri

Gompholobium baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, elliptical leaflets.

Gompholobium baxteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
