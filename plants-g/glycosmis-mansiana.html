Glycosmis mansiana

Glycosmis mansiana, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Glycosmis mansiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
