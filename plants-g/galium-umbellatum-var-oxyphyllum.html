Galium umbellatum var. oxyphyllum

Galium umbellatum var. oxyphyllum, commonly known as the white wood-sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features small, white, umbel-like clusters of flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
