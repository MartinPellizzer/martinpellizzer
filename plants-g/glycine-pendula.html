Glycine pendula

Glycine pendula, commonly known as the weeping bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, cascading stems that hang downward, producing small, bean-like pods. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces white or pale yellow flowers. Glycine pendula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
