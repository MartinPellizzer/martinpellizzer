Geoffroea jamaicensis var. inermis

Geoffroea jamaicensis var. inermis, commonly known as the Jamaican geoffroea, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne at the ends of the branches. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage and resilience to coastal conditions.
