Galium angustifolium var. diffusum

Galium angustifolium var. diffusum, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, covered in fine, white hairs. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
