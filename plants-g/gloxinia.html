Gloxinia

Gloxinia, commonly known as the silver velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. Gloxinias thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

They are typically propagated through tuberous roots or stem cuttings.
