Gunnera steyermarkii

Gunnera steyermarkii, commonly known as the giant water shield, is a striking member of the Polygonaceae family native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in diameter, which are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist, often aquatic environments, and its structure is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. It produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are typically greenish-white in color.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
