Genista salditana

Genista salditana, commonly known as the Sardinian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista salditana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
