Gilibertia pendula

Gilibertia pendula, commonly known as the hanging gilbertia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous inflorescences. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Gilibertia pendula is also known for its edible seeds, which are consumed by both humans and animals in some regions.
