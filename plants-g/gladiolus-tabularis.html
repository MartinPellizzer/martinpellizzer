Gladiolus tabularis

Gladiolus tabularis, commonly known as the flat-leaved gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, spike-like flowers. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of care.
