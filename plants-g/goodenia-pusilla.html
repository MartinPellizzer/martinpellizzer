Goodenia pusilla

Goodenia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf goodenia, is a small, flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or mauve flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
