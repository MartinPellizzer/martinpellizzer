Geranium madrense

Geranium madrense, commonly known as the Madrean geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Madrean region of North America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous areas, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

Geranium madrense is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
