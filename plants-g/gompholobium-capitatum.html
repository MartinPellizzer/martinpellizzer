Gompholobium capitatum

Gompholobium capitatum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

Gompholobium capitatum is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value.
