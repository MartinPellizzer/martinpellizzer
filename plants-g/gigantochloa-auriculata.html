Gigantochloa auriculata

Gigantochloa auriculata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive golden-green coloration on the underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is valued for construction and as a sustainable resource.
