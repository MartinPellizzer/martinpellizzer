Gymnanthemum secundifolium

Gymnanthemum secundifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, scale-like leaves arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
