Gymnogramma chaerophylla var. cryptogrammoides

Gymnogramma chaerophylla var. cryptogrammoides, commonly known as the cryptogrammoides variant of the common gymnogramma, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
