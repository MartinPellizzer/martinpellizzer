Gymnopogon floridanus

Gymnopogon floridanus, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a native grass species found primarily in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Gymnopogon floridanus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
