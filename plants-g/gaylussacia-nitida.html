Gaylussacia nitida

Gaylussacia nitida, commonly known as smooth waxberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its white flowers bloom in late spring, followed by small, dark red berries that are edible but tart.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands and swamps.
