Geranium potosinum

Geranium potosinum, commonly known as the Potosi geranium, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to arid landscapes.
