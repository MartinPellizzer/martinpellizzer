Guarea silvatica

Guarea silvatica, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
