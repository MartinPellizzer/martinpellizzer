Guilandina axillaris

Guilandina axillaris, commonly known as the climbing legume or velvet bean, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Guilandina axillaris is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
