Gigantochloa thoi

Gigantochloa thoi, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems and large, narrow leaves. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional construction and as a source of sustainable materials. Gigantochloa thoi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for ecological restoration and reforestation projects.
