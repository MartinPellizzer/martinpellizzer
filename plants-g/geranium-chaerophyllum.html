Geranium chaerophyllum

Geranium chaerophyllum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium chaerophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
