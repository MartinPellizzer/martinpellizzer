Goodenia atriplexifolia

Goodenia atriplexifolia, commonly known as the silver goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
