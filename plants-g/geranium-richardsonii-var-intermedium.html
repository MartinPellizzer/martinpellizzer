Geranium richardsonii var. intermedium

Geranium richardsonii var. intermedium, commonly known as the Richardson's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to drought conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its intermediate characteristics between the two main forms of Geranium richardsonii.
