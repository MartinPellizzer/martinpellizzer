Galium rubrum var. gracilentum

Galium rubrum var. gracilentum, commonly known as red-stemmed bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, reddish stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
