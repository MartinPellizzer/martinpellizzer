Gladiolus × mitchamiensis

Gladiolus × mitchamiensis, commonly known as the Mitchell's gladiolus, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing striking spikes of flowers that are popular in gardens and floral arrangements. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its vibrant and enduring blooms.
