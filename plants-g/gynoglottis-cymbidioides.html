Gynoglottis cymbidioides

Gynoglottis cymbidioides, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Gynoglottis cymbidioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
