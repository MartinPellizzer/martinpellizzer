Gentianella concinna

Gentianella concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Gentianella concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
