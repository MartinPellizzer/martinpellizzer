Gentiana amblyphylla

Gentiana amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

Gentiana amblyphylla is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
