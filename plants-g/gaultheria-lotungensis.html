Gaultheria lotungensis

Gaultheria lotungensis, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Gaultheria lotungensis is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
