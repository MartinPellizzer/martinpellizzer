Gomphrena decumbens subf. canescens

Gomphrena decumbens subf.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The leaves are typically hairy and gray-green in appearance, which helps to distinguish it from other species in the genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
