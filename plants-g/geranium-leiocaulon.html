Geranium leiocaulon

Geranium leiocaulon, commonly known as the silverleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its drought tolerance. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

The leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, compact shape that helps reduce water loss.
