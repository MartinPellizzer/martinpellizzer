Grewia araria

Grewia araria, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. Grewia araria thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and arid regions.
