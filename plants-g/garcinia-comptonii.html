Garcinia comptonii

Garcinia comptonii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are covered in a thin, leathery outer layer. Garcinia comptonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
