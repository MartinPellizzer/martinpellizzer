Gentianodes emodi

Gentianodes emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its strikingly blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are tubular and often have a prominent blue or purple coloration, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
