Gastrolobium melanocarpum

Gastrolobium melanocarpum, commonly known as the black seed wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's distinctive feature is its dark, hard seeds, which are enclosed in a woody capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Gastrolobium melanocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
