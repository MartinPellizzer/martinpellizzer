Godetia decumbens

Godetia decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features prostrate to ascending stems that often spread along the ground, covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in the morning. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often grown as a ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
