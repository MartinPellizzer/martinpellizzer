Gymnocalycium monvillei

Gymnocalycium monvillei, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. Gymnocalycium monvillei is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
