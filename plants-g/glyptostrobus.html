Glyptostrobus

Glyptostrobus, commonly known as the Chinese swamp cypress, is a rare and ancient coniferous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and its ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments. The plant has a distinctive bark that is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress. Glyptostrobus is classified as a gymnosperm, meaning it produces seeds but not flowers.
