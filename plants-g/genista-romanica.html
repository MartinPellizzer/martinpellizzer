Genista romanica

Genista romanica, commonly known as the Roman broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista romanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in landscaping.
