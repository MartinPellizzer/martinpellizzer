Gentiana prainii

Gentiana prainii, commonly known as the Prain's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

Gentiana prainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
