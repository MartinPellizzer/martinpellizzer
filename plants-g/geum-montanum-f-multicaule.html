Geum montanum f. multicaule

Geum montanum f. multicaule, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
