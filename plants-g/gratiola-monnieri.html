Gratiola monnieri

Gratiola monnieri, commonly known as lesser yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gratiola monnieri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
