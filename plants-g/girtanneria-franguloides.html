Girtanneria franguloides

Girtanneria franguloides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas, and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
