Gentiana scabrida var. punctulata

Gentiana scabrida var. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
