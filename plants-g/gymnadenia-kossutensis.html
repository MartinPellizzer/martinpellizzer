Gymnadenia kossutensis

Gymnadenia kossutensis, commonly known as the Kossut's grass-pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear whorled, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
