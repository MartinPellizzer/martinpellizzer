Gentiana castanetorum

Gentiana castanetorum, commonly known as the Castanet gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in open woodlands and mountainous regions. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, usually from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Gentiana castanetorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations.
