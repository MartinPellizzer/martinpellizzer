Gladiolus communis var. grandiflorus

Gladiolus communis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the garden gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that bear vibrant, showy flowers in a wide range of colors. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spike of brightly colored, spurred flowers that are typically arranged in a zigzag pattern. Its leaves are long, narrow, and sword-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as cut flowers.
