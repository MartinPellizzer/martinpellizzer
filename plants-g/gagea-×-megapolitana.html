Gagea × megapolitana

Gagea × megapolitana, commonly known as the hybrid gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and botanical collections.
