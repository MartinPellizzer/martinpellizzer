Gentiana quaterna subsp. sankarensis

Gentiana quaterna subsp.

sankarensis, commonly known as the Sankarensis gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gentianaceae. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is native to the highlands of East Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and bears the flowers at the apex.
