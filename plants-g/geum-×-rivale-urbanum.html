Geum × rivale-urbanum

Geum × rivale-urbanum, commonly known as the hybrid garden buttercup, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant color and long blooming period.
