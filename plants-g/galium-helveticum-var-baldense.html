Galium helveticum var. baldense

Galium helveticum var. baldense, commonly known as the alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Galium helveticum var. baldense thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories.
