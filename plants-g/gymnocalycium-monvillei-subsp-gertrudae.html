Gymnocalycium monvillei subsp. gertrudae

Gymnocalycium monvillei subsp.

gertrudae, commonly known as the Gertrudae moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its short, spiny stems and produces showy, yellow flowers during the growing season.

The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
