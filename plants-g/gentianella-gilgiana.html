Gentianella gilgiana

Gentianella gilgiana, commonly known as Gilgil's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella gilgiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
