Glyceria salina var. distans

Glyceria salina var. distans, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal and saline environments.

It thrives in brackish or saline soils, often found in marshes, estuaries, and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is well adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and periodic flooding.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
