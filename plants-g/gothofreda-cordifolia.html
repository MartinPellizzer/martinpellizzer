Gothofreda cordifolia

Gothofreda cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf fern, is a unique species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are deeply lobed and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures are small, inconspicuous spore-producing clusters located on the underside of the leaves.

Gothofreda cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive foliage.
