Gypsophila muralis subsp. repens

Gypsophila muralis subsp.

repens, commonly known as the creeping gypsophila, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a mat-forming habit, with slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
