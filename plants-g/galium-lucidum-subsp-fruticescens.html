Galium lucidum subsp. fruticescens

Galium lucidum subsp.

fruticescens, commonly known as shining bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
