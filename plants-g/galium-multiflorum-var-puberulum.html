Galium multiflorum var. puberulum

Galium multiflorum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the entire plant has a delicate, fern-like structure.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
