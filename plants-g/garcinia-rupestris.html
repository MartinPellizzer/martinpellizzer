Garcinia rupestris

Garcinia rupestris, commonly known as the rock garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia rupestris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
