Gaillardia aestivalis

Gaillardia aestivalis, commonly known as the annual blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals, which bloom from late spring to early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has linear, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Gaillardia aestivalis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
