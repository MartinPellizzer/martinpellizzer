Geranium molle subsp. brutium

Geranium molle subsp.

brutium, commonly known as the Balkan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
