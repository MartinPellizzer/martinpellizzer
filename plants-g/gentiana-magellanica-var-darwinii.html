Gentiana magellanica var. darwinii

Gentiana magellanica var. darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
