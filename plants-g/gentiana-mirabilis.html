Gentiana mirabilis

Gentiana mirabilis, commonly known as the splendid gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Gentiana mirabilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
