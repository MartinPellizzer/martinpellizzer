Gynerium purpureum

Gynerium purpureum, commonly known as the purple stem goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often display a striking purple hue, particularly in the lower parts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Gynerium purpureum is typically found in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands.
