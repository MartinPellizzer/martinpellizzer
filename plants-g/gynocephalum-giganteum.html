Gynocephalum giganteum

Gynocephalum giganteum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by the wind.

Gynocephalum giganteum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
