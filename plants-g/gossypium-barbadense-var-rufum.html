Gossypium barbadense var. rufum

Gossypium barbadense var. rufum, commonly known as the Crimson Cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and hairy, fluffy bolls that contain the cotton fibers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth. Its fibers are known for their strength and are often used in high-quality textiles.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for both fiber and aesthetic purposes.
