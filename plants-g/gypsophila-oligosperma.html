Gypsophila oligosperma

Gypsophila oligosperma, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It has a low-growing habit and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Gypsophila oligosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
