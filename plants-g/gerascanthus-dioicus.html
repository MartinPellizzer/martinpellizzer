Gerascanthus dioicus

Gerascanthus dioicus, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open grasslands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Gerascanthus dioicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
