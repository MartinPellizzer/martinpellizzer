Gleichenia papuana var. membranacea

Gleichenia papuana var. membranacea, commonly known as the Papuan Gleichenia, is a fern native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and surrounding islands.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds with a leathery texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of small, linear pinnae.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in humid, tropical gardens.
