Gentianella brandtiana

Gentianella brandtiana, commonly known as Brandt's gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Gentianella brandtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
