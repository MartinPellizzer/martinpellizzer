Gentianella fatrae

Gentianella fatrae, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

Gentianella fatrae plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
