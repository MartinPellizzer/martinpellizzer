Gentianella × aerimontana

Gentianella × aerimontana, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
