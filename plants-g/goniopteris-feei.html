Goniopteris feei

Goniopteris feei, commonly known as the Fee's moonwort, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed fronds that resemble the shape of a moon, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Goniopteris feei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
