Glandularia × hybrida

Glandularia × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, spike-like inflorescences that typically display vibrant shades of purple, blue, and pink. The plant features opposite, linear leaves with prominent glandular dots, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Glandularia × hybrida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
