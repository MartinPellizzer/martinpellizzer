Grevillea leptopoda

Grevillea leptopoda, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is well adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native region.

Grevillea leptopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
