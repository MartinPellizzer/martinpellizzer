Geum × rivalimontanum

Geum × rivalimontanum, commonly known as the hybrid garden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with palmate leaves that are deeply divided and covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wetland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
