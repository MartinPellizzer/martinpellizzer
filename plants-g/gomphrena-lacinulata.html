Gomphrena lacinulata

Gomphrena lacinulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky, pink or red flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Gomphrena lacinulata is also valued for its long-lasting flowers, which are often used in dried floral arrangements.
