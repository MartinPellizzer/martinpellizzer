Gardenia racemulosa

Gardenia racemulosa, commonly known as the clustered gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its white blossoms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia racemulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
