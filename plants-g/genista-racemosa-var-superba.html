Genista racemosa var. superba

Genista racemosa var. superba, commonly known as the showy broom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient plant for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
