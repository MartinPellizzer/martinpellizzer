Guzmania condorensis

Guzmania condorensis, commonly known as the Condor bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features a rosette of vibrant, colorful leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with long, spathaceous bracts that can be red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Guzmania condorensis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
