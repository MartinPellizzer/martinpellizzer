Garcinia spicata

Garcinia spicata, commonly known as the spike Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces orange-yellow berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
