Gomphrena caespitosa

Gomphrena caespitosa, commonly known as the cluster amaranth, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

Gomphrena caespitosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
