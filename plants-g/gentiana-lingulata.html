Gentiana lingulata

Gentiana lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana lingulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
