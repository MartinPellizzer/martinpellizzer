Grindelia hirsutula var. acutifolia

Grindelia hirsutula var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with sharp, pointed tips, giving it its common name.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
