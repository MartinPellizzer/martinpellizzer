Gentiana crassuloides

Gentiana crassuloides, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
