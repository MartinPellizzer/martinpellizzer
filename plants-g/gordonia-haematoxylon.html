Gordonia haematoxylon

Gordonia haematoxylon, commonly known as bloodwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, reddish-brown bark that contains a high concentration of tannins. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing elliptical seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses, with extracts from its bark used in traditional remedies.
