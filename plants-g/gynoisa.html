Gynoisa

Gynoisa, commonly known as the common gynoisa, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. The flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Gynoisa species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
