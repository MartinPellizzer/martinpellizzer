Goodenia montana

Goodenia montana, commonly known as the mountain goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to red, attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Goodenia montana is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environments.
