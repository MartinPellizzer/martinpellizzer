Gymnosporia masindei

Gymnosporia masindei, commonly known as the Masinde thornveld, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Gymnosporia masindei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
