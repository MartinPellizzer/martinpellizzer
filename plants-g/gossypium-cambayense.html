Gossypium cambayense

Gossypium cambayense, commonly known as the Cambay cotton, is a species of cotton native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white fibers that are used in textile production. The plant typically grows in warm, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces boll-like structures that encase the cotton fibers.

This species is valued for its high-quality fibers and is an important crop in certain regions of India.
