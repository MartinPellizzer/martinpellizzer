Geum urbanum subvar. orientale

Geum urbanum subvar. orientale, commonly known as the oriental avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense, flat-topped umbels. The flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in natural habitats.
