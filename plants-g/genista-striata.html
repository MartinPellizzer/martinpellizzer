Genista striata

Genista striata, commonly known as the striated broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Genista striata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
