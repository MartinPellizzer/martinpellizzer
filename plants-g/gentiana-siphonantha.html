Gentiana siphonantha

Gentiana siphonantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are tubular with five lobes, often adorned with dark spots.

Gentiana siphonantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
