Grevillea fasciculata var. divaricata

Grevillea fasciculata var. divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces striking, bright red or orange cylindrical flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is commonly found in coastal and tableland regions, often growing in open forests or heathlands.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
