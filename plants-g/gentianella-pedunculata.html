Gentianella pedunculata

Gentianella pedunculata, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
