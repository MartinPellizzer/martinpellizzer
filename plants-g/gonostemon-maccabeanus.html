Gonostemon maccabeanus

Gonostemon maccabeanus, commonly known as the Maccabean daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.

Gonostemon maccabeanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
