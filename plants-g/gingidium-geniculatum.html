Gingidium geniculatum

Gingidium geniculatum, commonly known as the zigzagging grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Gingidium geniculatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
