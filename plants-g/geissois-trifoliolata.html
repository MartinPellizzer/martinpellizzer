Geissois trifoliolata

Geissois trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved geissois, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Geissois trifoliolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
