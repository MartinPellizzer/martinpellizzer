Gentiana yokusai var. japonica

Gentiana yokusai var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
