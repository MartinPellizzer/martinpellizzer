Gardenia campanula

Gardenia campanula, commonly known as the bell gardenia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its bright blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
