Gaultheria acroleia

Gaultheria acroleia, commonly known as the dwarf snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Gaultheria acroleia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
