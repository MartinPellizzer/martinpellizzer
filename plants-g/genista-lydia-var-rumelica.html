Genista lydia var. rumelica

Genista lydia var. rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelica broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
