Gnaphalium purpureum var. chamissonis

Gnaphalium purpureum var. chamissonis, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy, often grayish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a low-growing habit and produces achenes as its fruit.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly different floral and vegetative features compared to the typical form of Gnaphalium purpureum.
