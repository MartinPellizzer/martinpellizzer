Grammitis grisebachii

Grammitis grisebachii, commonly known as the silver grammitis, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches, forming dense, compact colonies. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish coating, giving it its common name. Its fronds are usually dark green with a leathery texture and are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Grammitis grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
