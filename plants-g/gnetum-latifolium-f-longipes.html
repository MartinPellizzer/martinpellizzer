Gnetum latifolium f. longipes

Gnetum latifolium f. longipes, commonly known as the long-leaf gnetum, is a unique gymnosperm with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are often several times longer than they are wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among gymnosperms.

This variety is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and morphological diversity.
