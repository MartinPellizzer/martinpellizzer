Glastaria glastifolia

Glastaria glastifolia, commonly known as the glassleaf fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a glass-like appearance, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping, and it produces small, sporangia-bearing leaves known as sporophylls.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
