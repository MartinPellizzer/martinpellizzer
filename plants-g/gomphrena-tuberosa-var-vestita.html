Gomphrena tuberosa var. vestita

Gomphrena tuberosa var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, tuberous roots, which contribute to its name and help it store nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The foliage is typically gray-green, adding texture to its vibrant floral display.
