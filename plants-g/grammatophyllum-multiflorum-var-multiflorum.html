Grammatophyllum multiflorum var. multiflorum

Grammatophyllum multiflorum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces multiple flowers per inflorescence, often in clusters, and blooms during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
