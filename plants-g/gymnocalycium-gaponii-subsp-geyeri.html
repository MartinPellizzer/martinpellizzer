Gymnocalycium gaponii subsp. geyeri

Gymnocalycium gaponii subsp.

geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers during the warmer months, typically in shades of yellow or orange. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to rocky soils.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
