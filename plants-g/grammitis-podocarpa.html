Grammitis podocarpa

Grammitis podocarpa, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocks or trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, dark green fronds that are often divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis podocarpa is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
