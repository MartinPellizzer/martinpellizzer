Geissorhiza scillaris

Geissorhiza scillaris, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in riverine forests.

Geissorhiza scillaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
