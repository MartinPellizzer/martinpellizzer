Gentianella graebneriana

Gentianella graebneriana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gentianella graebneriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
