Grevillea striata

Grevillea striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a slight red or purple tinge on the underside. Grevillea striata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or dry forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
