Gaillonia puberula

Gaillonia puberula, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are also covered in a fine pubescence.

Gaillonia puberula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
