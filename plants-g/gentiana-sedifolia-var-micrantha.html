Gentiana sedifolia var. micrantha

Gentiana sedifolia var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its basal leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in alpine or woodland habitats. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a compact growth habit, making it a striking addition to rock gardens or shaded borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and ecological benefits.
