Gulubia costata var. pisiformis

Gulubia costata var. pisiformis, commonly known as the clustered leaf senna, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, each with a serrated margin. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release flat, bean-like seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
