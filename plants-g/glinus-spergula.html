Glinus spergula

Glinus spergula, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in some areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
