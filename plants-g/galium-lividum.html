Galium lividum

Galium lividum, commonly known as bluebed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium lividum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
