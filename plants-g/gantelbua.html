Gantelbua

Gantelbua, commonly known as the "hanging bell" or "carnation vine," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red, pink, or white flowers that resemble small bells, often hanging from its stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Gantelbua is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
