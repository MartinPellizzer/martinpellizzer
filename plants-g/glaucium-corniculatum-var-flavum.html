Glaucium corniculatum var. flavum

Glaucium corniculatum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in open habitats like grasslands and along coastal areas.
