Grewia montana

Grewia montana, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and attract various birds and mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Grewia montana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
