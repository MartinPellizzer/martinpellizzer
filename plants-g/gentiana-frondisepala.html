Gentiana frondisepala

Gentiana frondisepala, commonly known as the fronded gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are characterized by their five sepals that resemble petals, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.
