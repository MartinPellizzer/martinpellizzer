Glycine tabacina var. typica

Glycine tabacina var. typica, commonly known as the tobacco bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Glycine tabacina var. typica is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
