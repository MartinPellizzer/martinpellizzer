Gonolobus cundurango

Gonolobus cundurango, commonly known as the Cundurango bean, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit is typically perennial, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is also cultivated for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
