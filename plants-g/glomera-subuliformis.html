Glomera subuliformis

Glomera subuliformis, commonly known as the slender spike moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dendroceratops.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sporophytes, are elongated and often protrude from the leafy shoots.

Glomera subuliformis plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitat.
