Gnaphalium brevicilium

Gnaphalium brevicilium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feathergrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, clustered flowers with pale yellow to white petals. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its widespread distribution across various environments.
