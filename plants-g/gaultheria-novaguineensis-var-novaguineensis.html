Gaultheria novaguineensis var. novaguineensis

Gaultheria novaguineensis var. novaguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for its medicinal properties.
