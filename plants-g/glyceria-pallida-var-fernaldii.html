Glyceria pallida var. fernaldii

Glyceria pallida var. fernaldii, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a variant of the native grass species found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to wetland conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
