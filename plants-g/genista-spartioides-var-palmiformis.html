Genista spartioides var. palmiformis

Genista spartioides var. palmiformis, commonly known as the palmate genista, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are divided into several lobes resembling the shape of a hand. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
