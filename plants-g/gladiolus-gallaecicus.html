Gladiolus gallaecicus

Gladiolus gallaecicus, commonly known as the Galician gladiolus, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often arranged in dense spikes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
