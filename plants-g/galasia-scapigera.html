Galasia scapigera

Galasia scapigera, commonly known as the scapigerous grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the stem. Galasia scapigera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
