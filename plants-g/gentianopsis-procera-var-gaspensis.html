Gentianopsis procera var. gaspensis

Gentianopsis procera var. gaspensis, commonly known as Gaspensian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly larger size and more intense flower color compared to other gentian species.
