Galium verum var. praecox

Galium verum var. praecox, commonly known as early yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
