Geranium eriostemon f. hypoleucum

Geranium eriostemon f. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-veined geranium, is a variant of the common geranium species.

It is characterized by its pale green to white veins on the leaves, which distinguish it from the typical green-veined form. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
