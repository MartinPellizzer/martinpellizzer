Grewia heynei

Grewia heynei, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
