Gentiana baconi

Gentiana baconi, commonly known as the bacon gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs.

Gentiana baconi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
