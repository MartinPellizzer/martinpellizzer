Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. multiflorum

Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of linear, silver-haired leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white ray florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
