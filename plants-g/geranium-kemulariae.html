Geranium kemulariae

Geranium kemulariae, commonly known as the Kashmir geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice among horticulturists.
