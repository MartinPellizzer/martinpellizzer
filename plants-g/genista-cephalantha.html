Genista cephalantha

Genista cephalantha, commonly known as the Canary Islands broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in Mediterranean climates.
