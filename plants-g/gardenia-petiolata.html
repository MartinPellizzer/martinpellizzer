Gardenia petiolata

Gardenia petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, glossy evergreen leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia petiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
