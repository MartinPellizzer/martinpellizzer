Gymnocondylus galeopsifolius

Gymnocondylus galeopsifolius, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
