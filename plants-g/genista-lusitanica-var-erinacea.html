Genista lusitanica var. erinacea

Genista lusitanica var. erinacea, commonly known as the hairy burschtail broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bur-like seed pods that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
