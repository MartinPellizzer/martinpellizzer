Gingidium rosifolium

Gingidium rosifolium, commonly known as the roseleaf gingidium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, rose-like foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Gingidium rosifolium is typically found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
