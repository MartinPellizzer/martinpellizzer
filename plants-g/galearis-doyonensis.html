Galearis doyonensis

Galearis doyonensis, commonly known as the Doyon's larkspur, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galearis doyonensis is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
