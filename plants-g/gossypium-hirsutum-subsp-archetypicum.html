Gossypium hirsutum subsp. archetypicum

Gossypium hirsutum subsp.

archetypicum, commonly known as the archetypal cotton, is a subspecies of upland cotton native to the Americas. It is characterized by its fibrous seed coats and is primarily cultivated for its cotton fibers.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and has been studied for its potential in breeding programs.
