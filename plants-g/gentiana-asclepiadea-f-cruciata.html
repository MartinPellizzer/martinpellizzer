Gentiana asclepiadea f. cruciata

Gentiana asclepiadea f. cruciata, commonly known as the cruciate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
