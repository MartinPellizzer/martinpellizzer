Grewia forbesii

Grewia forbesii, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
