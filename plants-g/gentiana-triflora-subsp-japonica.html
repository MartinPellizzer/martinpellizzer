Gentiana triflora subsp. japonica

Gentiana triflora subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
