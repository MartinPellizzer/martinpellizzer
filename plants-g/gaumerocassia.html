Gaumerocassia

Gaumerocassia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-bearing structures. Gaumerocassia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
