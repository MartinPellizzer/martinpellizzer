Gleichenia brunei

Gleichenia brunei, commonly known as the Borneo fern, is a member of the Gleicheniaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a long, creeping rhizome and bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Gleichenia brunei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
