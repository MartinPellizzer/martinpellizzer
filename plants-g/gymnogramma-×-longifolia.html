Gymnogramma × longifolia

Gymnogramma × longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf staghorn fern, is a hybrid species within the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically develops a thick, woody rhizome that anchors it to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually bi-lobed and have a distinctive, rugged texture.

Gymnogramma × longifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
