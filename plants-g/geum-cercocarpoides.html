Geum cercocarpoides

Geum cercocarpoides, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in early to mid-summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
