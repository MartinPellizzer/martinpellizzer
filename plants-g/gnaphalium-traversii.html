Gnaphalium traversii

Gnaphalium traversii, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy stems, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

The plant produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.
