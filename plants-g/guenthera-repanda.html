Guenthera repanda

Guenthera repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Guenthera repanda is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
