Gentiana zollingeri

Gentiana zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana zollingeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
