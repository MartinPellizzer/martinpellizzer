Grastidium triste

Grastidium triste, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Grastidium triste is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
