Glandularia venturii

Glandularia venturii, commonly known as the Texas tansy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic properties.

Glandularia venturii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
