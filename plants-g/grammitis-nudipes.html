Grammitis nudipes

Grammitis nudipes, commonly known as the naked foot fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and small, delicate fronds. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth, glossy surface and no true midrib. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

The plant's unique appearance and growth habit make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
