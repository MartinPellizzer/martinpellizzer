Gentiana pseudolycopodium

Gentiana pseudolycopodium, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs.

Gentiana pseudolycopodium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
