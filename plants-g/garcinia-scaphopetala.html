Garcinia scaphopetala

Garcinia scaphopetala, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange or red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, aromatic rind and a juicy, acidic pulp, which contains several small, seed-like structures. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
