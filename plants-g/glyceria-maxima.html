Glyceria maxima

Glyceria maxima, commonly known as the tall flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense stands that help stabilize shoreline areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Glyceria maxima is also valued for its ability to filter water and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
