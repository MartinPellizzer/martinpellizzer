Gymnocalycium victorii

Gymnocalycium victorii, commonly known as the Victor's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.

Gymnocalycium victorii is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
