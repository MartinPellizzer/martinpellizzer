Gaultheria serrata var. organensis

Gaultheria serrata var. organensis, commonly known as the organensis wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a sharp, peppery flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The leaves contain aromatic compounds, making it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
