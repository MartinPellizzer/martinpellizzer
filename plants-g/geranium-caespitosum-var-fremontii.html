Geranium caespitosum var. fremontii

Geranium caespitosum var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features dense, compact growth with small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has basal leaves that are typically rounded or ovate, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
