Galium helveticum var. tendae

Galium helveticum var. tendae, commonly known as the Alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium helveticum var. tendae thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows.
