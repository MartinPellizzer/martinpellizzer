Gentiana rubricaulis

Gentiana rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The red-stemmed gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
