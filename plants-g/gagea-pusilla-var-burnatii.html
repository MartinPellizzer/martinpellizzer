Gagea pusilla var. burnatii

Gagea pusilla var. burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's dwarf spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller size and specific habitat preferences compared to other varieties of Gagea pusilla.
