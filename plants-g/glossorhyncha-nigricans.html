Glossorhyncha nigricans

Glossorhyncha nigricans, commonly known as the black-tongued orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy sepals and petals that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near the forest floor in rich, organic soil. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique coloration that ranges from deep purples to blacks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Glossorhyncha nigricans is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
