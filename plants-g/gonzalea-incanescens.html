Gonzalea incanescens

Gonzalea incanescens, commonly known as the white-leaved gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-white foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid climates.

Gonzalea incanescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
