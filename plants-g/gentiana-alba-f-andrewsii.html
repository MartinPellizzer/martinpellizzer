Gentiana alba f. andrewsii

Gentiana alba f. andrewsii, commonly known as the white gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
