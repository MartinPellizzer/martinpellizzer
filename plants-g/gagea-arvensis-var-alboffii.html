Gagea arvensis var. alboffii

Gagea arvensis var. alboffii, commonly known as the white-flowered gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
