Garcinia granulata

Garcinia granulata, commonly known as the granulated garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, granular texture. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that contain a single seed, surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp. Garcinia granulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dense foliage and attractive fruiting habit.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
