Gladiolus rubrocyanus

Gladiolus rubrocyanus, commonly known as the red and blue gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, blue, and purple. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense inflorescence. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, sturdy stems and are often used in floral arrangements.

The botanical name reflects its distinctive coloration, with "rubrocyanus" referring to its red and blue hues.
