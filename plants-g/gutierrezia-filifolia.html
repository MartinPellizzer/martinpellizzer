Gutierrezia filifolia

Gutierrezia filifolia, commonly known as silky goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia filifolia is known for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
