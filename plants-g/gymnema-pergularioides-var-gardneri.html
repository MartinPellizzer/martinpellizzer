Gymnema pergularioides var. gardneri

Gymnema pergularioides var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by follicles that release tiny seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
