Gilia tenuiflora subsp. hoffmannii

Gilia tenuiflora subsp.

hoffmannii, commonly known as Hoffmann's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
