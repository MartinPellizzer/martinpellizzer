Geranium paramicola

Geranium paramicola, commonly known as the narrowleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across the world.
