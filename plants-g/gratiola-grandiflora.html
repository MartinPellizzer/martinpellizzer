Gratiola grandiflora

Gratiola grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered gratiola, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its most notable characteristic is its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gratiola grandiflora is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
