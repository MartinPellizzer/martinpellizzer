Gomphrena elegans f. microcephala

Gomphrena elegans f. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf everlasting or small-headed gomphrena, is a compact, herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, rounded, pink or red flower heads that resemble tiny globes, giving it a unique and decorative appearance. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its extended blooming period from summer to fall adds long-lasting color to gardens.
