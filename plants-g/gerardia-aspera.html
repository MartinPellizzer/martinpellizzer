Gerardia aspera

Gerardia aspera, commonly known as the rough gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in rough, hairy textures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive rough surface.

Gerardia aspera is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
