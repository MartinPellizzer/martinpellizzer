Gypsophila obconica

Gypsophila obconica, commonly known as the baby's breath or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila obconica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
