Galarhoeus imbricatus

Galarhoeus imbricatus, commonly known as the mountain cedar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in cold, arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
