Grevillea pteridifolia

Grevillea pteridifolia, commonly known as the fern-leafed Grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, feather-like leaves that resemble ferns, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms contribute to its status as a valuable ornamental plant.
