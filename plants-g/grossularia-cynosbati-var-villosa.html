Grossularia cynosbati var. villosa

Grossularia cynosbati var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy pomegranate, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright red, edible berries that resemble those of the pomegranate. The plant thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and the fruit develops in clusters.

The variety is notable for its unique foliage and adaptability to arid conditions.
