Gaylussacia baccata

Gaylussacia baccata, commonly known as the waxmyrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, waxy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and grayish-white underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
