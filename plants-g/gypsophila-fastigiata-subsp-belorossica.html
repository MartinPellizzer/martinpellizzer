Gypsophila fastigiata subsp. belorossica

Gypsophila fastigiata subsp.

belorossica, commonly known as the Siberian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often fragrant blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its elegant appearance.
