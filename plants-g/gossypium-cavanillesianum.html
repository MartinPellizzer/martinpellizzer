Gossypium cavanillesianum

Gossypium cavanillesianum, commonly known as the Cavanilles cotton, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fluffy fibers that are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces capsule fruits that contain the cotton seeds.
