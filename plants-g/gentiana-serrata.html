Gentiana serrata

Gentiana serrata, commonly known as the serrated gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Gentiana serrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
