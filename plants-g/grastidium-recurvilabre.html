Grastidium recurvilabre

Grastidium recurvilabre, commonly known as the twisting leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and elongated, wavy leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high levels of atmospheric moisture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected natural reserves.
