Gymnema stenophyllum

Gymnema stenophyllum, commonly known as the Indian licorice or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its unique leaves that have a vein pattern resembling a net. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in natural medicine. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which are believed to have hypoglycemic properties.

Gymnema stenophyllum is often cultivated for its medicinal value and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
