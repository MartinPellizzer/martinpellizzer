Genipa gummifera

Genipa gummifera, commonly known as the rubber genipapo, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. Its latex, which is milky and sticky, is used in traditional medicine and as a natural rubber substitute.

The fruit is also edible and has a unique, sweet flavor when ripe.
