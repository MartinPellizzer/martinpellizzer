Grewia flavicans var. boinensis

Grewia flavicans var. boinensis, commonly known as the Bornean waxleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories or along riverbanks.

The fruit is a small, drupe that matures to a reddish color, attracting various bird species.
