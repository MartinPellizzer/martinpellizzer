Grewia multiflora

Grewia multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in gardens and natural landscapes.
