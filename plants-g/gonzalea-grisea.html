Gonzalea grisea

Gonzalea grisea, commonly known as the gray gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing structure and grayish-blue foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale blue to white, typically blooming in the spring. Gonzalea grisea is adapted to poor soil conditions and requires minimal water, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
