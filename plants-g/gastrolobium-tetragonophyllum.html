Gastrolobium tetragonophyllum

Gastrolobium tetragonophyllum, commonly known as the four-angled wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a distinctive four-angled cross-section. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in arid and semi-arid environments.
