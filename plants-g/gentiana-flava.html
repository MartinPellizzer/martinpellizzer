Gentiana flava

Gentiana flava, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

Gentiana flava is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
