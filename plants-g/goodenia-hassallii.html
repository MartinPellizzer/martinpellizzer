Goodenia hassallii

Goodenia hassallii, commonly known as the stinking goodenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to red, attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Goodenia hassallii is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
