Guarea carinata

Guarea carinata, commonly known as the ridged guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its distinctive ridged trunk and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.
