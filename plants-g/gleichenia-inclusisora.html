Gleichenia inclusisora

Gleichenia inclusisora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often closely spaced along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are found on the underside of the leaves.

Gleichenia inclusisora is notable for its primitive features and is often used in botanical studies to understand the evolution of ferns.
