Gentianella oranensis

Gentianella oranensis, commonly known as the Algerian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Gentianella oranensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
