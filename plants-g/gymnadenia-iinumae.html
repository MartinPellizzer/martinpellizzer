Gymnadenia iinumae

Gymnadenia iinumae, commonly known as the Iinuma's grass-pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its common name.

Gymnadenia iinumae is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in regions of East Asia, including Japan and parts of China.
