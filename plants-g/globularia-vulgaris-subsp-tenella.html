Globularia vulgaris subsp. tenella

Globularia vulgaris subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the woolly globe flower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and compact, spherical inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

The subspecies tenella is distinguished by its more compact growth and denser foliage compared to other subspecies of Globularia vulgaris.
