Galium gracilens var. lutchuense

Galium gracilens var. lutchuense, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical understory vegetation.
