Gaillardia pulchella

Gaillardia pulchella, commonly known as the firewheel or swamp firewheel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features bright orange to red flowers with yellow centers, arranged in a wheel-like pattern. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, often appearing fuzzy to the touch.

Gaillardia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
