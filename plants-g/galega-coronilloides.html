Galega coronilloides

Galega coronilloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved galega, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Galega coronilloides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
