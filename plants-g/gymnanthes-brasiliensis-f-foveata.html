Gymnanthes brasiliensis f. foveata

Gymnanthes brasiliensis f. foveata, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or foveate starfruit, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble star-shaped berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its fruit is edible when fully ripe, though it is often consumed in its immature, sour form.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
