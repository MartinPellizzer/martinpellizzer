Gothofreda squamulata

Gothofreda squamulata, commonly known as the silver-scaled orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves covered in fine, white scales that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pale pink and white, often blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited habitat and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
