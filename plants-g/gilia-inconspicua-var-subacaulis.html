Gilia inconspicua var. subacaulis

Gilia inconspicua var. subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
