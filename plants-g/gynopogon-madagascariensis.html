Gynopogon madagascariensis

Gynopogon madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of many grass species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in dry, open habitats.

Gynopogon madagascariensis is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
