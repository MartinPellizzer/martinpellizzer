Gymnocalycium chuquisacanum

Gymnocalycium chuquisacanum, commonly known as the Chuquisaca prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are often followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
