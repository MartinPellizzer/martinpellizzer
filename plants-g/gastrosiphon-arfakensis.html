Gastrosiphon arfakensis

Gastrosiphon arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfaken swamp bell, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Central Africa.

It features elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, with a dense network of air channels that help it remain buoyant. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the muddy substrate, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving water.

Due to habitat loss, Gastrosiphon arfakensis is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists studying aquatic flora.
