Gnaphalium affine var. pusillum

Gnaphalium affine var. pusillum, commonly known as the narrowleaf everlasting flower, is a herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, fluffy appearance.
