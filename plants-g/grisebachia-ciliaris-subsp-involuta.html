Grisebachia ciliaris subsp. involuta

Grisebachia ciliaris subsp.

involuta, commonly known as the curled leaf grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, grass-like leaves that are rolled or curled at the edges, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in grassland ecosystems.
