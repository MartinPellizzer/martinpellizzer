Gypsophila juzepczukii

Gypsophila juzepczukii, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered blooms that attract pollinators.

Gypsophila juzepczukii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
