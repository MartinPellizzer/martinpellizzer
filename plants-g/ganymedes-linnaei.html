Ganymedes linnaei

Ganymedes linnaei, commonly known as the giant whitebark, is a rare and endangered species of conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its distinctive, pale yellow to white bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Ganymedes linnaei is facing significant threats to its survival.
