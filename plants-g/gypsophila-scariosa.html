Gypsophila scariosa

Gypsophila scariosa, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Gypsophila scariosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
