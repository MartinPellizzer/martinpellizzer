Gentiana palcana

Gentiana palcana, commonly known as the pale gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
