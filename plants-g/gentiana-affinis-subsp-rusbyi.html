Gentiana affinis subsp. rusbyi

Gentiana affinis subsp.

rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
