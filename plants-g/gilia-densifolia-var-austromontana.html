Gilia densifolia var. austromontana

Gilia densifolia var. austromontana, commonly known as the coastal gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate mild drought conditions.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
