Gardenia maruba

Gardenia maruba, commonly known as the Japanese gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Gardenia maruba is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
