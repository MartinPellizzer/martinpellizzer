Gentianella ciliata f. albiflora

Gentianella ciliata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its distinct floral coloration, which sets it apart from the typical blue or purple flowers of the species.
