Glyceria airoides

Glyceria airoides, commonly known as the marsh grass or rivergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, such as marshes, ponds, and riverbanks.

Glyceria airoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
