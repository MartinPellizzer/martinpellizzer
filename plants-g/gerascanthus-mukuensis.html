Gerascanthus mukuensis

Gerascanthus mukuensis, commonly known as the Mukuensis geranium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched. Gerascanthus mukuensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
