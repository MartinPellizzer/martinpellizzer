Gladiolus telifer

Gladiolus telifer, commonly known as the carrier gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces striking, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a fan shape around the stem.

Gladiolus telifer is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
