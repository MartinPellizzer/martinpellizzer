Grindelia pectinata

Grindelia pectinata, commonly known as the silverleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Grindelia pectinata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
