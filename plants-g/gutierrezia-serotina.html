Gutierrezia serotina

Gutierrezia serotina, commonly known as silverleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its silver-gray leaves are covered in fine, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss.

Gutierrezia serotina is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and its drought tolerance.
