Galeopsis ladanum var. pyrenaica

Galeopsis ladanum var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats. The plant features slender stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have serrated edges. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are arranged in whorled clusters, typical of the mint family.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine areas, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
