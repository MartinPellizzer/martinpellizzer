Genista mollis

Genista mollis, commonly known as sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, thornless stems and produces clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its fragrant, honey-scented blooms. Genista mollis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
