Guatteria grandiflora

Guatteria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
