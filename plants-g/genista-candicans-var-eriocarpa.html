Genista candicans var. eriocarpa

Genista candicans var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems covered in fine, white, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and also bear the same silvery pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
