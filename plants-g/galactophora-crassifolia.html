Galactophora crassifolia

Galactophora crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is part of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Galactophora crassifolia is noted for its ability to attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
