Galactia michauxii

Galactia michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is notable for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Galactia.
