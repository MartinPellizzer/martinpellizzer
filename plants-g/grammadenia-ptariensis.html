Grammadenia ptariensis

Grammadenia ptariensis, commonly known as the Pteridium fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Grammadenia ptariensis plays a role in maintaining forest floor ecosystems by contributing to organic matter through leaf litter.
