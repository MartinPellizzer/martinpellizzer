Gynura maclurei

Gynura maclurei, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features lobed, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine yellowish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Gynura maclurei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
