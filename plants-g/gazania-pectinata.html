Gazania pectinata

Gazania pectinata, commonly known as the yellow gazania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. Gazania pectinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.
