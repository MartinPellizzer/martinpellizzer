Galanthus nivalis subsp. reginae-olgae

Galanthus nivalis subsp.

reginae-olgae, commonly known as the Queen of the Alps snowdrop, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers with a yellow-green tip, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky meadows and forested areas at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a symbol of resilience in harsh mountain environments.
