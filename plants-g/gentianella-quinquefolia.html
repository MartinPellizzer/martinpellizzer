Gentianella quinquefolia

Gentianella quinquefolia, commonly known as the five-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces several flowering stems.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
