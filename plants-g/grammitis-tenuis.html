Grammitis tenuis

Grammitis tenuis, commonly known as slender maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, linear fronds that are often arching or cascading. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are usually short and produce small, clustered leaves.

Grammitis tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
