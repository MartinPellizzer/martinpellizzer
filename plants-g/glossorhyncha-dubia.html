Glossorhyncha dubia

Glossorhyncha dubia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida and Cuba.

It is characterized by its unique, white, orchid-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant lacks traditional leaves and instead absorbs nutrients through its roots, making it a hemiparasitic species.
