Grevillea loboana

Grevillea loboana, commonly known as the pink-leafed grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, serrated leaves that range from bright pink to deep red, providing a dramatic visual display. The plant produces long, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in shades of yellow to orange, typically from late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
