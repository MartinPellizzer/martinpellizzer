Grindelia chiloensis

Grindelia chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean gum plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and fuzzy, glandular leaves. The plant is often found in dry, coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its hairy leaves and resinous secretions are notable botanical features.

Grindelia chiloensis is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
