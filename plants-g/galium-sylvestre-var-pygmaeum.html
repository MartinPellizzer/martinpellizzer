Galium sylvestre var. pygmaeum

Galium sylvestre var. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rubiaceae family.
