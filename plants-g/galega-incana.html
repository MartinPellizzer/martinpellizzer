Galega incana

Galega incana, commonly known as the hairy galega, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its trifoliate leaves are alternate and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Galega incana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
