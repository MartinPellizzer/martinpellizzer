Galium pumilum subsp. vulgatum

Galium pumilum subsp.

vulgatum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features slender stems that are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its creeping growth habit and its ability to form dense mats.
