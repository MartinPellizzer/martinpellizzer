Galactia macreei

Galactia macreei, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Galactia macreei is also known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
