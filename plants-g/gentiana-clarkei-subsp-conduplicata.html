Gentiana clarkei subsp. conduplicata

Gentiana clarkei subsp.

conduplicata, commonly known as the double-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
