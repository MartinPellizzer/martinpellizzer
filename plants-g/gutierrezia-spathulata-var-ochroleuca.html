Gutierrezia spathulata var. ochroleuca

Gutierrezia spathulata var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, globular flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has silver-gray foliage and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Gutierrezia spathulata var. ochroleuca is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is a component of desert ecosystems.
