Gladiolus malangensis

Gladiolus malangensis, commonly known as the Malang gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branched flower spikes. The flowers, which can be white, pink, or red, are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and bloom in late summer. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
