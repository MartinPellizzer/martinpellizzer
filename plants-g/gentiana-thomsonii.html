Gentiana thomsonii

Gentiana thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces.

Thomson's gentian is known for its short blooming period, usually in late summer, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
