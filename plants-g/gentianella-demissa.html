Gentianella demissa

Gentianella demissa, commonly known as the small gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue or violet flowers in late summer. Its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Gentianella demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
