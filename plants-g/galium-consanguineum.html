Galium consanguineum

Galium consanguineum, commonly known as redstem mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

Galium consanguineum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
