Grastidium greenianum

Grastidium greenianum, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has a compact, shrub-like appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive silvery sheen.

Grastidium greenianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
