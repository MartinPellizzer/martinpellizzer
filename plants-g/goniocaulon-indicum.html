Goniocaulon indicum

Goniocaulon indicum, commonly known as the Indian horned veldt, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
