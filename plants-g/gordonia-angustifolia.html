Gordonia angustifolia

Gordonia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Gordonia angustifolia is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
