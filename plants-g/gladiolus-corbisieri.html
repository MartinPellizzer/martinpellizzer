Gladiolus corbisieri

Gladiolus corbisieri, commonly known as the Corsican gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clustered inflorescences called spathes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant colors and elegant appearance.
