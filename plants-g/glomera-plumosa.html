Glomera plumosa

Glomera plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf glomera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
