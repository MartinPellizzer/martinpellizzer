Gymnocalycium riograndense

Gymnocalycium riograndense, commonly known as the Rio Grande moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Gymnocalycium riograndense is a succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
