Glycine wightii

Glycine wightii, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines. Glycine wightii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
