Gentiana indica

Gentiana indica, commonly known as the Indian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and branching.

Gentiana indica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
