Grevillea bipinnatifida var. longifolia

Grevillea bipinnatifida var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, bipinnate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
