Gongronema hemsleyanum

Gongronema hemsleyanum, commonly known as the African nettle, is a member of the Urticaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Gongronema hemsleyanum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
