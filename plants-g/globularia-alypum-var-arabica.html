Globularia alypum var. arabica

Globularia alypum var. arabica, commonly known as the Arabian globularia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh, dry environments.
