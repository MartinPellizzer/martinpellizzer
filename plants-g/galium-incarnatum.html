Galium incarnatum

Galium incarnatum, commonly known as redstem silverweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a silver or grayish underside.

Galium incarnatum is part of the Rubiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
