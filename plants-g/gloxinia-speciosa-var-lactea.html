Gloxinia speciosa var. lactea

Gloxinia speciosa var. lactea, commonly known as the White Gloxinia, is a striking plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers. The varietal name "lactea" refers to the milk-white color of its petals, which can range from pale cream to almost pure white. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically 5 to 7 cm in diameter, with a distinctive corolla that is often adorned with a yellow or orange throat.

It is a popular ornamental plant, valued for its elegance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil under indirect light.
