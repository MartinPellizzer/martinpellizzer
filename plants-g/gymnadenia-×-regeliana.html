Gymnadenia × regeliana

Gymnadenia × regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's grasspocket, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Gymnadenia × regeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique floral structure.
