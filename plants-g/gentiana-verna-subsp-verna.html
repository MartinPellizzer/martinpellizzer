Gentiana verna subsp. verna

Gentiana verna subsp.

verna, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
