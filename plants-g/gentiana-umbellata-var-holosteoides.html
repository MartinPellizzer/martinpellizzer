Gentiana umbellata var. holosteoides

Gentiana umbellata var. holosteoides, commonly known as the Holosteoides gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its blue to violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
