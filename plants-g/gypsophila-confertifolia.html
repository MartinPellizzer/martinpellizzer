Gypsophila confertifolia

Gypsophila confertifolia, commonly known as clustered gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed at the base.

Gypsophila confertifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.
