Galarhoeus pekinensis subsp. fauriei

Galarhoeus pekinensis subsp.

fauriei, commonly known as the Peking bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
