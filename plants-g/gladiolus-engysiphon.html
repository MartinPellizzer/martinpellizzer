Gladiolus engysiphon

Gladiolus engysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vividly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, yellow, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Gladiolus engysiphon is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
