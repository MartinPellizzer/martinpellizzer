Gypsophila spergulifolia

Gypsophila spergulifolia, commonly known as the mountain flax or silky gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, fluffy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Gypsophila spergulifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden designs for its ethereal appearance.
