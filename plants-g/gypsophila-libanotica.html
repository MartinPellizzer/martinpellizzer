Gypsophila libanotica

Gypsophila libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese baby's-breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy flower heads composed of numerous small, white or pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its slender stems and small leaves contribute to its overall appearance, while its blooms are often used in floral arrangements.

Gypsophila libanotica is valued for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
