Genyorchis apertiflora

Genyorchis apertiflora, commonly known as the open-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with brightly colored, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers typically open in the morning, hence the species name "apertiflora," meaning "opening flower.".

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
