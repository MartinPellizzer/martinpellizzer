Gastrochilus calceolaris var. biflora

Gastrochilus calceolaris var. biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered star orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, producing white to pale yellow petals with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
