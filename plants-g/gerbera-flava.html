Gerbera flava

Gerbera flava, commonly known as the Orange Gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features vibrant orange petals with a yellow center, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and hairy. Gerbera flava thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world for decorative purposes.
