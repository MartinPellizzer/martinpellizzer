Grammitis flagelliformis

Grammitis flagelliformis, commonly known as the whip fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, clustered fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinct, narrow shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is primarily used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
