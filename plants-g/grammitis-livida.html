Grammitis livida

Grammitis livida, commonly known as the silver moss, is a member of the Hypnaceae family and is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically pale green to silvery in color, giving it its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees and rocks without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis livida plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing microhabitats for various small organisms.
