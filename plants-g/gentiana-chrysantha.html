Gentiana chrysantha

Gentiana chrysantha, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The golden gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
