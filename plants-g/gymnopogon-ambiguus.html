Gymnopogon ambiguus

Gymnopogon ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. Gymnopogon ambiguus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
