Gomphrena scapigera var. lanigera

Gomphrena scapigera var. lanigera, commonly known as the wooly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched, upright stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.
