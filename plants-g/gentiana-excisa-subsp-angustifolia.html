Gentiana excisa subsp. angustifolia

Gentiana excisa subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathians.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
