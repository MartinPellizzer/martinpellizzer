Geraniospermum nervifolium

Geraniospermum nervifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geraniosperm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep roots and a waxy leaf coating to reduce water loss. It belongs to the family Geraniaceae, which includes many species with similar morphological traits.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
