Glycine biflora

Glycine biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered soybean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. Glycine biflora is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, which enrich the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
