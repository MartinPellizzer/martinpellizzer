Grisebachia velleriflora

Grisebachia velleriflora, commonly known as the silky-flowered grisebachia, is a striking member of the Cyclanthaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its unique, velvety, and often hairy inflorescences, which give it its scientific name. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in color from bright green to reddish hues depending on the environment. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
