Gentiana filistyla var. parviflora

Gentiana filistyla var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and hairy.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
