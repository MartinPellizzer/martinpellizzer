Gilia pungens subsp. eupungens

Gilia pungens subsp.

eupungens, commonly known as the sharpseed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
