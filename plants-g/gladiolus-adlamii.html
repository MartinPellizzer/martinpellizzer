Gladiolus adlamii

Gladiolus adlamii, commonly known as the Adlam's gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements. Its ornamental value and adaptability have contributed to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
