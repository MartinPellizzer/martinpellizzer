Glossochilopsis chamaeorchis

Glossochilopsis chamaeorchis, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, fleshy stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, violet-purple flowers with a prominent, lip-like structure that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
