Gentiana rhodocalyx

Gentiana rhodocalyx, commonly known as the pink gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are strikingly blue with a pinkish hue at the base.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hair.
