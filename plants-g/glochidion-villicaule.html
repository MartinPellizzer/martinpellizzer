Glochidion villicaule

Glochidion villicaule, commonly known as the hairy glochidion, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers clustered in small inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
