Grewia sulcata var. obovata

Grewia sulcata var. obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and obovate, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
