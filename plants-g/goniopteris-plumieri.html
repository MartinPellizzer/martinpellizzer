Goniopteris plumieri

Goniopteris plumieri, commonly known as the plumier fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features large, intricately divided leaves that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Goniopteris plumieri thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
