Geranium betonicum

Geranium betonicum, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Geranium betonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
