Gladiolus illyricus subsp. reuteri

Gladiolus illyricus subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the Balkan gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, trumpet-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
