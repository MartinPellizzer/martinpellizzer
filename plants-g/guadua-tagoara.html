Guadua tagoara

Guadua tagoara, commonly known as the Tagoara bamboo, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Guadua tagoara is also valued for its strong and flexible culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts.
