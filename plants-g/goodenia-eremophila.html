Goodenia eremophila

Goodenia eremophila, commonly known as the desert goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Goodenia eremophila plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying drought-resistant flora.
