Gnaphalium peruvianum

Gnaphalium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy florets that are often mistaken for seeds, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its delicate, airy look.
