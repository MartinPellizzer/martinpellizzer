Glycine claessensii

Glycine claessensii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds that are rich in protein. Glycine claessensii thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
