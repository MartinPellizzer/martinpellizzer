Gilia gracilis subvar. spathulifolia

Gilia gracilis subvar. spathulifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture. This subvariety belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It blooms during the summer months and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
