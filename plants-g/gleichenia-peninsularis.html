Gleichenia peninsularis

Gleichenia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Gleichenia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that are typically divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Gleichenia peninsularis is native to regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
