Geranium elegans

Geranium elegans, commonly known as the elegant geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with serrated edges and a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
