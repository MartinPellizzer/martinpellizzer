Galphimia brasiliensis var. typica

Galphimia brasiliensis var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It features vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
