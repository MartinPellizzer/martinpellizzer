Gymnocalycium castellanosii var. armillatum

Gymnocalycium castellanosii var. armillatum, commonly known as the "armored cactus," is a succulent native to the deserts of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom from the areoles along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
