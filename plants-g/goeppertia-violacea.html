Goeppertia violacea

Goeppertia violacea, commonly known as the violet arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or violet hues, especially along the veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Goeppertia violacea is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
