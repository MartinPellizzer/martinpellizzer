Grammitis nigrolimbata

Grammitis nigrolimbata, commonly known as the black-limbed moss-fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually dark green with a distinct dark margin, contributing to its common name.

Grammitis nigrolimbata plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
