Grevillea acuminata

Grevillea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, red or orange inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
