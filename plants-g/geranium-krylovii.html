Geranium krylovii

Geranium krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
