Gnetum loerzingii

Gnetum loerzingii, commonly known as the Loerzing's gymnosperm, is a member of the Gnetophyta class, which includes plants with unique evolutionary traits.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with opposite, simple leaves and unisexual flowers, typical of gymnosperms. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it produces small, woody cones.

Gnetum loerzingii is notable for its role in the study of plant evolution due to its intermediate characteristics between gymnosperms and angiosperms.
