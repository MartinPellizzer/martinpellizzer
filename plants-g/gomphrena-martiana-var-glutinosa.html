Gomphrena martiana var. glutinosa

Gomphrena martiana var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, spiky, pink to red bracts that resemble tiny flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to the plant's distinctive appearance. This variety is known for its sticky secretions on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
