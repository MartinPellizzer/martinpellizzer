Gynopachis

Gynopachis, commonly known as the sand mat or desert sandmat, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by their low-growing, mat-forming habit. The leaves are usually small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern to reduce water loss. They produce small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white, adapted to attract pollinators in harsh environments.

Gynopachis species are valued for their resilience and are often used in xeriscaping due to their ability to thrive with minimal water.
