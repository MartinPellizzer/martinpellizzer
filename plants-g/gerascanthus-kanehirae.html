Gerascanthus kanehirae

Gerascanthus kanehirae, commonly known as the Kihara's geranium, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, five-petaled flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
