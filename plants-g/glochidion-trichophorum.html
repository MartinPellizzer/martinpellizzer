Glochidion trichophorum

Glochidion trichophorum, commonly known as the hairy glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
