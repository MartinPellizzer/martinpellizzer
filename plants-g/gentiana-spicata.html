Gentiana spicata

Gentiana spicata, commonly known as the spiked gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana spicata is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
