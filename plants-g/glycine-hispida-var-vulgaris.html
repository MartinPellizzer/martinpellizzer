Glycine hispida var. vulgaris

Glycine hispida var. vulgaris, commonly known as the hairy peanut vine, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

It produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.
