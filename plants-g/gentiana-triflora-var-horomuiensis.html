Gentiana triflora var. horomuiensis

Gentiana triflora var. horomuiensis, commonly known as the Horomui gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous forests. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
