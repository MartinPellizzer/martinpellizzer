Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. hyptiacanthum

Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. hyptiacanthum, commonly known as the rock cactus or hyptiacanthum cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, globular cactus with a spiny exterior and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, appearing in late spring or early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
