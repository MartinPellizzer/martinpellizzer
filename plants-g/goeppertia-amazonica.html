Goeppertia amazonica

Goeppertia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant coloration. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, which includes many ornamental foliage plants. Goeppertia amazonica thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
