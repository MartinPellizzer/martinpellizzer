Griffinia albolineata

Griffinia albolineata, commonly known as the white-lined fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clusters of small, bright green, bi-lobed leaves. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The white veins on the leaves give it its distinctive appearance and contribute to its aesthetic appeal.
