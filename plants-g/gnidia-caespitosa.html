Gnidia caespitosa

Gnidia caespitosa, commonly known as the mountain thorn, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Gnidia caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
