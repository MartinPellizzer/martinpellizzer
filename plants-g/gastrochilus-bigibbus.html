Gastrochilus bigibbus

Gastrochilus bigibbus, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed lip, which is a key feature in its classification. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Gastrochilus bigibbus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
