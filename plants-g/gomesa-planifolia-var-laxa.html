Gomesa planifolia var. laxa

Gomesa planifolia var. laxa, commonly known as the slender flatleaf gomesa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
