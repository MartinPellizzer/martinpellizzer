Gentiana hirsuta

Gentiana hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it its common name, and the stems are often woody at the base.

Gentiana hirsuta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
