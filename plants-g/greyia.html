Greyia

Greyia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It consists of a single species, Greyia sinuata, native to South Africa. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

Greyia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
