Gentiana nivalis var. albiflora

Gentiana nivalis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
