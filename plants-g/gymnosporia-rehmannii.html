Gymnosporia rehmannii

Gymnosporia rehmannii, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and spine-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, hard, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Gymnosporia rehmannii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
