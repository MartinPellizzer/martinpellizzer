Gagea soleirolii subsp. nevadensis

Gagea soleirolii subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra gagea, is a bulbous plant native to the western United States. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in early spring.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its bulbs are often used for propagation and are an important part of the local flora in mountainous regions.
