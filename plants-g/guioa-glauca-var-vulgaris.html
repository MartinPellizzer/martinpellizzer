Guioa glauca var. vulgaris

Guioa glauca var. vulgaris, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and parks.
