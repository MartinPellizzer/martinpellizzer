Grammitis aurita

Grammitis aurita, commonly known as the golden maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slight golden hue on the underside. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which produce spores for reproduction.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
