Gentiana hsinganica

Gentiana hsinganica, commonly known as the Hsingan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Gentiana hsinganica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
