Galium hispidulum

Galium hispidulum, commonly known as rough bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, glandular hairs.

Galium hispidulum is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
