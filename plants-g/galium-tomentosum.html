Galium tomentosum

Galium tomentosum, commonly known as woolly bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium tomentosum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes.
