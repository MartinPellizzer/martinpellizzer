Grevillea monticola

Grevillea monticola, commonly known as the mountain grevillea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and its striking, red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and coastal environments. It has a compact growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Grevillea monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
