Gymnadenia × anacamptis

Gymnadenia × anacamptis, commonly known as the hybrid grass of the genus Gymnadenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is known for its ability to hybridize with other Gymnadenia species, leading to variations in its morphology.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
