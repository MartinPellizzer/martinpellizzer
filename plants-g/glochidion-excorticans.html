Glochidion excorticans

Glochidion excorticans, commonly known as the peeling glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, and its fruits are capsules that release seeds when mature.

Glochidion excorticans is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
