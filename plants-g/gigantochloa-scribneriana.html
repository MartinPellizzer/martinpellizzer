Gigantochloa scribneriana

Gigantochloa scribneriana, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, typical of many bamboo species. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas.

Its rapid growth and strong structural properties make it valuable for both ecological and practical applications.
