Gentiana stellulata

Gentiana stellulata, commonly known as the star gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are tubular with five spreading lobes, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
