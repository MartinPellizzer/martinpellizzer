Galactia speciosa var. acutisepala

Galactia speciosa var. acutisepala, commonly known as the acutely-sepalated milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with a pinnate structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with long, feathery appendages.

This species is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
