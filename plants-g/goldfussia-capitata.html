Goldfussia capitata

Goldfussia capitata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many other bellflowers and related species.
