Geranium rovirae

Geranium rovirae, commonly known as the Maltese geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Malta and the surrounding islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
