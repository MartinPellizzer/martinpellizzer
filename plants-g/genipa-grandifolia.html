Genipa grandifolia

Genipa grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or genipapo, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's most notable feature is its fruit, which turns from yellow to red as it ripens, containing a single seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp. Genipa grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
