Gaultheria kemulensis

Gaultheria kemulensis, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor.

Gaultheria kemulensis is often found in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.
