Gaylussacia setosa

Gaylussacia setosa, commonly known as the slender waxmyrtle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
