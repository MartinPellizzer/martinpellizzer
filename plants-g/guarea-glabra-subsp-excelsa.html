Guarea glabra subsp. excelsa

Guarea glabra subsp.

excelsa, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
