Gaillardia linearis

Gaillardia linearis, commonly known as the linear gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright red and yellow striped petals, which resemble flames, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open areas such as prairies and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and linear, contributing to its common name.

Gaillardia linearis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
