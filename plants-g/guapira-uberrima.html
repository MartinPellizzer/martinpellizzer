Guapira uberrima

Guapira uberrima, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian sandwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. The plant has simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
