Gymnocalycium intertextum

Gymnocalycium intertextum, commonly known as the Desert Rose Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stems. Gymnocalycium intertextum is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is often cultivated as a ornamental species in dry climates.
