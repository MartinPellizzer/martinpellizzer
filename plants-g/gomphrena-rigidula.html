Gomphrena rigidula

Gomphrena rigidula, commonly known as the rigid plume flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, feathery bracts that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Gomphrena rigidula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
