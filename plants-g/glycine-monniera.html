Glycine monniera

Glycine monniera, commonly known as the climbing laburnum, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that resemble those of the golden chain tree. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Glycine monniera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
