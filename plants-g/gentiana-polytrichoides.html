Gentiana polytrichoides

Gentiana polytrichoides, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Gentiana polytrichoides plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
