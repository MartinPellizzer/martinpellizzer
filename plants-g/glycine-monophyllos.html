Glycine monophyllos

Glycine monophyllos, commonly known as the narrow-leafed soybean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in food and animal feed. Glycine monophyllos has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.

Its growth habits make it an important crop in sustainable agricultural practices.
