Glaucium grandiflorum subsp. refractum

Glaucium grandiflorum subsp.

refractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
