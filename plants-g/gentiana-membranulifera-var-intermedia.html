Gentiana membranulifera var. intermedia

Gentiana membranulifera var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in forest understories or along rocky slopes.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
