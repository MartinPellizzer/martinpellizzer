Glechoma daenensis

Glechoma daenensis, commonly known as Persian ground-ivy, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its flowers are small and tubular, usually blue or purple in color.

Glechoma daenensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
