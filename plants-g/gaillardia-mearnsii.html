Gaillardia mearnsii

Gaillardia mearnsii, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its vibrant red and yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
