Guarea pubescens

Guarea pubescens, commonly known as the yellow wood or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a rich, reddish hue. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release elongated seeds.
