Garuga javanica

Garuga javanica, commonly known as the Java garuga, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as food for certain animals. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
