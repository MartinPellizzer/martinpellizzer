Gladiolus montanus

Gladiolus montanus, commonly known as the mountain gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous brightly colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Gladiolus montanus is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
