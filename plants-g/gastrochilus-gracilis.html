Gastrochilus gracilis

Gastrochilus gracilis, commonly known as the slender lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.
