Grevillea haplantha subsp. recedens

Grevillea haplantha subsp.

recedens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in summer.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. It is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display. Grevillea haplantha subsp.

recedens is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
