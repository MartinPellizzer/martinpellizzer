Grevillea eriostachya

Grevillea eriostachya, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea eriostachya is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract native pollinators.
