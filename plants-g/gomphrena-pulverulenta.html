Gomphrena pulverulenta

Gomphrena pulverulenta, commonly known as the dusty miller, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a unique visual interest to the landscape.

Gomphrena pulverulenta is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
