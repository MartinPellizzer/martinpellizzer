Gladiolus segetum var. guepinii

Gladiolus segetum var. guepinii, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves, and it produces seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
