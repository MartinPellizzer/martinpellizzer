Gymnocalycium zegarrae var. riograndense

Gymnocalycium zegarrae var. riograndense, commonly known as the Rio Grande prickly pear, is a cactus species native to northern Argentina and southern Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
