Graphistemma pictum

Graphistemma pictum, commonly known as the painted stem plant, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves that exhibit a range of colors, including green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
