Gladiolus gracillimus

Gladiolus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance.
