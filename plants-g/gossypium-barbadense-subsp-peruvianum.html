Gossypium barbadense subsp. peruvianum

Gossypium barbadense subsp.

peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian cotton plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru. It is characterized by its fine, fluffy fibers that are harvested from its seed pods.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

This subspecies is valued for its high-quality cotton fibers, which are used in textile production.
