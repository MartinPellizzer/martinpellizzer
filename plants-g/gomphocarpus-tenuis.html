Gomphocarpus tenuis

Gomphocarpus tenuis, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap.

Gomphocarpus tenuis thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
