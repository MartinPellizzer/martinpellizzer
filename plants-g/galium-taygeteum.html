Galium taygeteum

Galium taygeteum, commonly known as the Greek bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium taygeteum is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
