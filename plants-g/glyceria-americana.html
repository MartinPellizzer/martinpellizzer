Glyceria americana

Glyceria americana, commonly known as American glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in regions with high moisture levels and is adapted to tolerate flooding.

Glyceria americana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
