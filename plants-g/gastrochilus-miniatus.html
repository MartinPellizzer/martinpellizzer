Gastrochilus miniatus

Gastrochilus miniatus, commonly known as the miniature stomach orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, with its roots anchored in the bark. The plant features a short, compact stem with several small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique, intricate structure that resembles a stomach, hence its scientific name.

It is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and rarity in cultivation.
