Gilia gilioides subsp. glutinosa

Gilia gilioides subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as sticky gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the tribe Polemonieae within the family Polemoniaceae, characterized by its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

The plant is distinguished by its sticky, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
