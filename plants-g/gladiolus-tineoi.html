Gladiolus tineoi

Gladiolus tineoi, commonly known as the Tineo gladiolus, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, brightly colored flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive three-lobed structure. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
