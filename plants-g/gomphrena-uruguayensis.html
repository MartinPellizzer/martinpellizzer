Gomphrena uruguayensis

Gomphrena uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay carnation, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bract-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that resemble carnations, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.
