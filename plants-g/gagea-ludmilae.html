Gagea ludmilae

Gagea ludmilae, commonly known as the Ludmila's squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
