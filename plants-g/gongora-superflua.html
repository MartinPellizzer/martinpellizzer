Gongora superflua

Gongora superflua, commonly known as the fluted orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a combination of red, yellow, and purple hues. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its high-altitude environment. Gongora superflua is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its intricate floral structure.

It thrives in cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
