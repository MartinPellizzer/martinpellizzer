Gentianella quipuscoana

Gentianella quipuscoana, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and its stems are often short and sturdy.

Gentianella quipuscoana plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
