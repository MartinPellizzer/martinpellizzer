Gladiolus tristis var. punctatus

Gladiolus tristis var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically arranged in racemes at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its ecological benefits.
