Gazania mucronata

Gazania mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped gazania, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and composite structure. The plant features bright yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by a ring of tubular disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Gazania mucronata is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
