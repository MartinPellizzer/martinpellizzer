Galeopsis tetrahit var. parviflora

Galeopsis tetrahit var. parviflora, commonly known as lesser hedge-nettle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically pale blue or purple in color.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
