Gleasonia cururuensis

Gleasonia cururuensis, commonly known as the Cururu palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compound inflorescences, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits. Gleasonia cururuensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
