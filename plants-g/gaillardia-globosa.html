Gaillardia globosa

Gaillardia globosa, commonly known as the globular gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from orange to red, with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces fluffy, seed-filled bracts that aid in wind pollination.

Gaillardia globosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
