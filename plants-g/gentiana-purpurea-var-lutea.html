Gentiana purpurea var. lutea

Gentiana purpurea var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which contrast with its dark green, velvety leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
