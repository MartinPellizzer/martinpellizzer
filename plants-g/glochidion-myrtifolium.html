Glochidion myrtifolium

Glochidion myrtifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that range in color from dark green to reddish-brown, depending on the season. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Glochidion myrtifolium is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
