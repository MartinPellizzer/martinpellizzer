Grewia nossibeensis

Grewia nossibeensis, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in dune environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
