Gomphrena elegans var. mesopotamica

Gomphrena elegans var. mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces vibrant, spiky flower heads in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
