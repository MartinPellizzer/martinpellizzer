Garcinia preussii

Garcinia preussii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange-yellow berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia preussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
