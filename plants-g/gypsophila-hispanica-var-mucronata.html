Gypsophila hispanica var. mucronata

Gypsophila hispanica var. mucronata, commonly known as the SpanishBaby's Breath, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
