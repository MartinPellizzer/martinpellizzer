Geum sieversii

Geum sieversii, commonly known as Sievers' avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geum sieversii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
