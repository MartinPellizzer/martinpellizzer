Grisebachia eriocephala

Grisebachia eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and native plant gardens.
