Gagea taurica var. conjungens

Gagea taurica var. conjungens, commonly known as the Taurian spring beauty, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in late winter or early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its harsh, mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
