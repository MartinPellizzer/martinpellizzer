Geranium pilosum var. potentilloides

Geranium pilosum var. potentilloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a soft, fuzzy texture that gives it its common name.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
