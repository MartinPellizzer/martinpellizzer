Gmelina tonkinensis

Gmelina tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin ash or Vietnamese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland areas and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.

Gmelina tonkinensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
