Geranium circinatum

Geranium circinatum, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, heart-shaped to ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in cymes, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Geranium circinatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
