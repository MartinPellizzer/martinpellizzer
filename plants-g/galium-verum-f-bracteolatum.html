Galium verum f. bracteolatum

Galium verum f. bracteolatum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw plant.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
