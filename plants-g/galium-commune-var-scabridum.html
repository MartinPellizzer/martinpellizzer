Galium commune var. scabridum

Galium commune var. scabridum, commonly known as rough bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its distinctive texture and floral arrangement.
