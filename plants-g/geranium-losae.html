Geranium losae

Geranium losae, commonly known as the Sierra geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, often found in California and Nevada. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy flowers, which range from pink to purple, bloom in late summer and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Geranium losae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
