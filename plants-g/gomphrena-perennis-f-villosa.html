Gomphrena perennis f. villosa

Gomphrena perennis f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "villosa.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The hairy gomphrena is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
