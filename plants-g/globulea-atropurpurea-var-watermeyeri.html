Globulea atropurpurea var. watermeyeri

Globulea atropurpurea var. watermeyeri, commonly known as the purple globular hibiscus, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bold, showy appearance.
