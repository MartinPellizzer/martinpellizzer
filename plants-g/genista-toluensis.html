Genista toluensis

Genista toluensis, commonly known as Toluensis broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Genista toluensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
