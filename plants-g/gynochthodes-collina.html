Gynochthodes collina

Gynochthodes collina, commonly known as the mountain marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Gynochthodes collina is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
