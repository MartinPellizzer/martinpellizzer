Guerreroia tridentata

Guerreroia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically divided into three distinct tooth-like segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Guerreroia tridentata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
