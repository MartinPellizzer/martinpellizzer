Genoplesium tasmanicum

Genoplesium tasmanicum, commonly known as the Tasmanian Christmas Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid endemic to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant, greenish-yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred labellum, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
