Glyceria distans var. intermedia

Glyceria distans var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate glyceria, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variant is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
