Gossypium peruvianum var. racemosum

Gossypium peruvianum var. racemosum, commonly known as the Peruvian cotton plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that produces fluffy, white fibers used for textile purposes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish, and it has a fibrous, woody stem.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than other cotton species but holds significance in traditional textile practices.
