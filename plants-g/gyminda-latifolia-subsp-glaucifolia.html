Gyminda latifolia subsp. glaucifolia

Gyminda latifolia subsp.

glaucifolia, commonly known as the glaucous broadleaf gyminda, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often exhibit a distinctive blue-gray or whitish coating, known as a glaucous bloom.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in native gardens.
