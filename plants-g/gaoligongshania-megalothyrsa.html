Gaoligongshania megalothyrsa

Gaoligongshania megalothyrsa, commonly known as the giant-leafed rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its unique botanical features and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
