Garcinia arbuscula

Garcinia arbuscula, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a juicy, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
