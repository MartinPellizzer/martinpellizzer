Gonocarpus tetragynus

Gonocarpus tetragynus, commonly known as the four-ovary sea grape, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, round, and fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Gonocarpus tetragynus thrives in saline environments and is often found growing in shallow coastal waters and estuaries.
