Gaillardia henricksonii

Gaillardia henricksonii, commonly known as the Henry's blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its vibrant orange and yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gaillardia henricksonii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
