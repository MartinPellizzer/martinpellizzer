Grias grandifolia

Grias grandifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a robust, woody stem and can grow into a tall tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Grias grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
