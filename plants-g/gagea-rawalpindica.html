Gagea rawalpindica

Gagea rawalpindica, commonly known as the Rawalpindi spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are characterized by their narrow, grass-like appearance.

Gagea rawalpindica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
