Gentiana aspera var. calycinoidea

Gentiana aspera var. calycinoidea, commonly known as the Calycinoidea gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
