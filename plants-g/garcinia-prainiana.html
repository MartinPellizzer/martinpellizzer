Garcinia prainiana

Garcinia prainiana, commonly known as the Malay bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia prainiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
