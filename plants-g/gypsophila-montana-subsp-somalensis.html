Gypsophila montana subsp. somalensis

Gypsophila montana subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.
