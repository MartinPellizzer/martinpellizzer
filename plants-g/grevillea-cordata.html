Grevillea cordata

Grevillea cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features long, narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces striking, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Grevillea cordata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
