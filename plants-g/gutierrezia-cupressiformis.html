Gutierrezia cupressiformis

Gutierrezia cupressiformis, commonly known as brittlebrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Gutierrezia cupressiformis is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
