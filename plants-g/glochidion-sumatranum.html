Glochidion sumatranum

Glochidion sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
