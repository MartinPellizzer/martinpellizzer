Gardenia bignoniiflora

Gardenia bignoniiflora, commonly known as the silver gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
