Gutierrezia chubutensis

Gutierrezia chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the dry regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Gutierrezia chubutensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
