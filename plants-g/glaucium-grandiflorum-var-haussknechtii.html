Glaucium grandiflorum var. haussknechtii

Glaucium grandiflorum var. haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussknecht's gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
