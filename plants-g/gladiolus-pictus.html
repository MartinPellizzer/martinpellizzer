Gladiolus pictus

Gladiolus pictus, commonly known as the painted gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns and hues of red, orange, and yellow. The foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to the showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold, ornamental plants.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for floral arrangements.
