Glandularia lucanensis

Glandularia lucanensis, commonly known as the Lucanian glandular eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Glandularia lucanensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
