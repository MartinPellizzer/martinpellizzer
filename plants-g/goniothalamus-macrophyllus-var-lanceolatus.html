Goniothalamus macrophyllus var. lanceolatus

Goniothalamus macrophyllus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed tree of the family Annonaceae, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with a distinctive star-shaped corolla, typically arranged in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain multiple seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
