Glycine elliptica

Glycine elliptica, commonly known as the broad-leafed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often opening in the early morning. Glycine elliptica thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
