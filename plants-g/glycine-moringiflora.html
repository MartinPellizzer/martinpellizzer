Glycine moringiflora

Glycine moringiflora, commonly known as the African bean tree, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes. It can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often cultivated for its edible pods and seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
