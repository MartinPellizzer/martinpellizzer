Gelasine gigantea subsp. flexitepala

Gelasine gigantea subsp.

flexitepala, commonly known as the giant gelasine, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive red calyx. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling or climbing form depending on the environment.

Botanically, it is noted for its complex inflorescence and unique floral structure, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
