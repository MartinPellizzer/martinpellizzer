Grevillea banksii

Grevillea banksii, commonly known as the Banks' waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and its distinctive, showy red or pink flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and dry forest environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of birds, particularly honeyeaters.

Grevillea banksii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of garden conditions.
