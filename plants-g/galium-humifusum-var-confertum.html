Galium humifusum var. confertum

Galium humifusum var. confertum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a hairy surface that gives the plant its common name. Small white flowers are arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
