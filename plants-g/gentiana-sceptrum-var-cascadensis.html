Gentiana sceptrum var. cascadensis

Gentiana sceptrum var. cascadensis, commonly known as the Cascade gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and erect.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
