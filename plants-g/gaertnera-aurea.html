Gaertnera aurea

Gaertnera aurea, commonly known as the golden gaertnera, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gaertnera aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
