Gynura simplex

Gynura simplex, commonly known as the silver queen or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Gynura simplex is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
