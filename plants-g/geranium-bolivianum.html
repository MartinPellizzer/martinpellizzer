Geranium bolivianum

Geranium bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated edge and a rough texture.

Geranium bolivianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
