Grona retroflexa

Grona retroflexa, commonly known as the backward-bent grass, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that curve backward, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, Grona retroflexa is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and wetland ecosystems.
