Grewia cordata

Grewia cordata, commonly known as the African starfruit or wild starfruit, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that appear in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have a tart, acidic flavor.
