Gahnia psittacorum f. psilocaulon

Gahnia psittacorum f. psilocaulon, commonly known as the red-flowered gahnia, is a variant of the gahnia plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink flower spikes that stand tall above the grass-like foliage. The plant belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is typically found in wet or damp environments. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
