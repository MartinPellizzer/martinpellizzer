Gentiana volubilis

Gentiana volubilis, commonly known as the climbing gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers, typically arranged in clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana volubilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
