Grewia cuneifolia

Grewia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia cuneifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
