Gerascanthus troyanus

Gerascanthus troyanus, commonly known as the Troyan's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Gerascanthus troyanus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
