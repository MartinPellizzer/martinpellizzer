Guapebeira laurifolia

Guapebeira laurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded understories of humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
