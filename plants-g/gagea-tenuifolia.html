Gagea tenuifolia

Gagea tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its bulbs are underground and serve as storage organs, supporting the plant through dormant periods.

Gagea tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
