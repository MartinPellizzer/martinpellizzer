Guajava umbrosa

Guajava umbrosa, commonly known as the dark guajava, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, dark purple fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Guajava umbrosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional medicine.
