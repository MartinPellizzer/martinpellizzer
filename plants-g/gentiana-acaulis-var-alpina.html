Gentiana acaulis var. alpina

Gentiana acaulis var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often leafless.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
