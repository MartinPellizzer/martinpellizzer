Geranium ruthenicum

Geranium ruthenicum, commonly known as the Ruthenian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium ruthenicum is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
