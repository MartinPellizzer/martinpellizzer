Gomphrena colosacana

Gomphrena colosacana, commonly known as the Mexican featherplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or purple bracts that resemble feathery plumes, which are not true flowers but modified leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Gomphrena colosacana is also known for its drought tolerance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
