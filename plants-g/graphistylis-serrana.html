Graphistylis serrana

Graphistylis serrana, commonly known as the serrated spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often green or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
