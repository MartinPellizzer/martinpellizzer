Gynopogon ceylanicus

Gynopogon ceylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon sedge, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands and marshy areas of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil in its wetland habitat.

Gynopogon ceylanicus plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its environment by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
