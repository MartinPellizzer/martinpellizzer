Galega toxicaria

Galega toxicaria, commonly known as the toxic goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is known for its erect, hairy stems that can grow up to several feet tall. Galega toxicaria is toxic to both humans and animals, containing compounds that can cause severe health issues if ingested.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and toxicological studies.
