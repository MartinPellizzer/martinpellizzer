Gnaphalium alboluteum

Gnaphalium alboluteum, commonly known as the white-flowered everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences composed of numerous small white to pale yellow florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, often in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a woolly texture.

The species is known for its ability to retain its color and form when dried, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
