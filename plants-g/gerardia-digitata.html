Gerardia digitata

Gerardia digitata, commonly known as the clustered gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Gerardia digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
