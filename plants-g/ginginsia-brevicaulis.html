Ginginsia brevicaulis

Ginginsia brevicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Ginginsia brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
