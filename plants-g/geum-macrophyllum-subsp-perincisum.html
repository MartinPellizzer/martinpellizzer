Geum macrophyllum subsp. perincisum

Geum macrophyllum subsp.

perincisum, commonly known as the large-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
