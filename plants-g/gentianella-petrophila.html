Gentianella petrophila

Gentianella petrophila, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
