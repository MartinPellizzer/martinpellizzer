Gomphrena platandra

Gomphrena platandra, commonly known as the pink gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves and has a long blooming period, often extending from summer into autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena platandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
