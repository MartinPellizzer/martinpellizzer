Grevillea willisii subsp. pachylostyla

Grevillea willisii subsp.

pachylostyla, commonly known as the broad-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to the southwestern region of Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
