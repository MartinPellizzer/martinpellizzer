Geraniospermum asarifolium

Geraniospermum asarifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geraniospermum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae, which includes species known for their showy flowers and often succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
