Gompholobium wonganense

Gompholobium wonganense, commonly known as the Wongan wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters along the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
