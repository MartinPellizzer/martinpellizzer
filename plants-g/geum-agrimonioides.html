Geum agrimonioides

Geum agrimonioides, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, with stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
