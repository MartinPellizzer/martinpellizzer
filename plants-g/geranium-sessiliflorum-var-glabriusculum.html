Geranium sessiliflorum var. glabriusculum

Geranium sessiliflorum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long flowering period contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
