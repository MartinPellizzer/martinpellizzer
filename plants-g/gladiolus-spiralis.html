Gladiolus spiralis

Gladiolus spiralis, commonly known as the spiral gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral arrangement of petals that form a distinctive, almost helical shape. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and bloom in late summer.

The spiral gladiolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
