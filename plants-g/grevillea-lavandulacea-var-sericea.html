Grevillea lavandulacea var. sericea

Grevillea lavandulacea var. sericea, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
