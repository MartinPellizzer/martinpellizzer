Gymnocalycium ferrarii

Gymnocalycium ferrarii, commonly known as the Ferrari's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and a distinctive, showy flower that blooms in shades of yellow or orange. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its succulent stems. Gymnocalycium ferrarii is typically found in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among cactus enthusiasts.
