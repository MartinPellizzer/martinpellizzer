Garcinia cordata

Garcinia cordata, commonly known as the African mango or butter fruit, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its fruit contains a high amount of lipids and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.

Garcinia cordata thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
