Geranium onaei f. albiflorum

Geranium onaei f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular plant among gardeners.
