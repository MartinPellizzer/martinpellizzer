Gentiana stracheyi

Gentiana stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
