Grevillea rivularis

Grevillea rivularis, commonly known as the river grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in wetter habitats such as riverbanks and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is a member of the Proteaceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and ecological importance in native ecosystems.

Grevillea rivularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
