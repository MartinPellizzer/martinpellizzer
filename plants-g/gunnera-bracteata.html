Gunnera bracteata

Gunnera bracteata, commonly known as the giant water shield, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a unique visual appearance. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are typically floating or submerged, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Gunnera bracteata plays an important ecological role by supporting aquatic life and stabilizing shoreline vegetation.
