Gentiana helophila subsp. helophila

Gentiana helophila subsp.

helophila, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often near streams or in marshy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
