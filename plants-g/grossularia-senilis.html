Grossularia senilis

Grossularia senilis, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Grossularia senilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
