Grevillea neorigida subsp. distans

Grevillea neorigida subsp.

distans, commonly known as the slender grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright red or orange inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, persistent flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
