Grevillea linearis var. incarnata

Grevillea linearis var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink ribbon grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, linear leaves and striking clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

Grevillea linearis var. incarnata is well-suited for coastal gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
