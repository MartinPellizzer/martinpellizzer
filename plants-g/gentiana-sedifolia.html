Gentiana sedifolia

Gentiana sedifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps retain moisture.

Gentiana sedifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
