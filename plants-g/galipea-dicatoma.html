Galipea dicatoma

Galipea dicatoma, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Galipea dicatoma plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
