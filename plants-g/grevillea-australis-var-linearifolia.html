Grevillea australis var. linearifolia

Grevillea australis var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant produces striking, blood-red flowers in dense, globular clusters, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
