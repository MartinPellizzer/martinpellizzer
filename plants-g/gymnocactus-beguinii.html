Gymnocactus beguinii

Gymnocactus beguinii, commonly known as the beguin's gymnocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. Gymnocactus beguinii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
