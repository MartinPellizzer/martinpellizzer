Gentianodes ornata

Gentianodes ornata, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentianodes ornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
