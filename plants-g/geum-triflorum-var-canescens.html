Geum triflorum var. canescens

Geum triflorum var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, saucer-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure includes a branched stem and capsule fruits that release tiny seeds.
