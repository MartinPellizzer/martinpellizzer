Genista subcapitata f. viridis

Genista subcapitata f. viridis, commonly known as the green-headed broom, is a variant of the broom plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, giving the plant a distinct appearance. The foliage is notable for its bright green color, which sets it apart from the typical grey-green of the species.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
