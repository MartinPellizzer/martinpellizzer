Genista dorycnioides

Genista dorycnioides, commonly known as the golden brier, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, twisted seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
