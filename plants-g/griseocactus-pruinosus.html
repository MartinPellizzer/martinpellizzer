Griseocactus pruinosus

Griseocactus pruinosus, commonly known as the silver barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in a dense, waxy coating, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its grayish-green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
