Geranium caespitosum var. eremophilum

Geranium caespitosum var. eremophilum, commonly known as the desert geranium, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features dense, compact growth with small, showy flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
