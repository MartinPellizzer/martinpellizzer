Grewia nagensium

Grewia nagensium, commonly known as the red-flowered bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia nagensium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
