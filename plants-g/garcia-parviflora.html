Garcia parviflora

Garcia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Garcia, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that supports various forms of wildlife.

Garcia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
