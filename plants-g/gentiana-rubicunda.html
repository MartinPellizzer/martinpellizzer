Gentiana rubicunda

Gentiana rubicunda, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or moist mountainous habitats, often at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
