Galium mollugo var. guilhelmii

Galium mollugo var. guilhelmii, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
