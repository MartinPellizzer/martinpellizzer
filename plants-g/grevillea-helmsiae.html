Grevillea helmsiae

Grevillea helmsiae, commonly known as the Cape York grevillea, is a shrub native to the Cape York Peninsula in Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

Grevillea helmsiae is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
