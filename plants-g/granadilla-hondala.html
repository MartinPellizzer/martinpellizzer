Granadilla hondala

Granadilla hondala, commonly known as the Honduras granadilla, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It produces striking red, fleshy fruits that are often mistaken for passion fruit. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive purple corona, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

The fruit's pulp is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines.
