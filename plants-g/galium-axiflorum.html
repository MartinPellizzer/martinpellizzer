Galium axiflorum

Galium axiflorum, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive veining pattern.

Galium axiflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
