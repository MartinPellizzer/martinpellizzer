Gaillardia megapotamica f. biflava

Gaillardia megapotamica f. biflava, commonly known as the Mexican blanket flower, is a hybrid cultivar of the genus Gaillardia, native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its showy blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
