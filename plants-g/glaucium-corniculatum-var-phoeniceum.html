Glaucium corniculatum var. phoeniceum

Glaucium corniculatum var. phoeniceum, commonly known as the red poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
