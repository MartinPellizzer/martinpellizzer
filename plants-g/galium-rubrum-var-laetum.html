Galium rubrum var. laetum

Galium rubrum var. laetum, commonly known as the red-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium rubrum var. laetum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
