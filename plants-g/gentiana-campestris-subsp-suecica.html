Gentiana campestris subsp. suecica

Gentiana campestris subsp.

suecica, commonly known as the Swedish gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
