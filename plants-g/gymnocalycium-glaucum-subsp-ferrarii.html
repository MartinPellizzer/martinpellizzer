Gymnocalycium glaucum subsp. ferrarii

Gymnocalycium glaucum subsp.

ferrarii, commonly known as the Ferrari's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
