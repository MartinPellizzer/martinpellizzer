Geranium aculeatum

Geranium aculeatum, commonly known as the mouse-ear geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Geranium aculeatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
