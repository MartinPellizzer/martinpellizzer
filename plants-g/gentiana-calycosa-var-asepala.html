Gentiana calycosa var. asepala

Gentiana calycosa var. asepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
