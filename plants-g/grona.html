Grona

Grona, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Grona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion in dry environments.
