Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. parvifolia

Gaylussacia brasiliensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, often along stream banks or in wetlands. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its showy, fragrant white to pinkish flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, attracting various wildlife species.
