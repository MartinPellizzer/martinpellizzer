Gethyllis barbarae

Gethyllis barbarae, commonly known as the Cape Mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small florets that bloom in spring.

Gethyllis barbarae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive, daisy-like appearance.
