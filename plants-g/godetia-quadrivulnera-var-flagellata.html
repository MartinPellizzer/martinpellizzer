Godetia quadrivulnera var. flagellata

Godetia quadrivulnera var. flagellata, commonly known as the weeping godetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has slender, pendulous stems that give it a weeping appearance, which is a key feature of the varietal name "flagellata.". Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and rock gardens.
