Glycosmis pentaphylla var. linearifoliolis

Glycosmis pentaphylla var. linearifoliolis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hong kong hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
