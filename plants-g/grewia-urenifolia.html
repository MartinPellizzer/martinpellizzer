Grewia urenifolia

Grewia urenifolia, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Grewia urenifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
