Glyceria arctica var. dasyantha

Glyceria arctica var. dasyantha, commonly known as the Arctic glyceria, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically green or brown, adapted to the short growing season of its habitat.

Glyceria arctica var. dasyantha plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is an important part of the tundra ecosystem.
