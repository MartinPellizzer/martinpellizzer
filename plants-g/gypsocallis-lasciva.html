Gypsocallis lasciva

Gypsocallis lasciva, commonly known as the naked lady orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces a single, erect stem with a cluster of white or pink petals and a distinctive, recurved lip. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Gypsocallis lasciva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
