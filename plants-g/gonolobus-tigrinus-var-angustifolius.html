Gonolobus tigrinus var. angustifolius

Gonolobus tigrinus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tigrinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
