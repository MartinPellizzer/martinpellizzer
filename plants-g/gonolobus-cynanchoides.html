Gonolobus cynanchoides

Gonolobus cynanchoides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in the morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

Gonolobus cynanchoides is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
