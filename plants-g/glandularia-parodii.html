Glandularia parodii

Glandularia parodii, commonly known as the Parodii's verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
