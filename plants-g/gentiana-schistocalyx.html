Gentiana schistocalyx

Gentiana schistocalyx, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
