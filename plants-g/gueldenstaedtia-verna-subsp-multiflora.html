Gueldenstaedtia verna subsp. multiflora

Gueldenstaedtia verna subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of biodiversity-rich ecosystems.
