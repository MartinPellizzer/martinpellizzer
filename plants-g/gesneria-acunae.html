Gesneria acunae

Gesneria acunae, commonly known as the Acuna's gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Gesneria acunae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
