Gypsophila simonii

Gypsophila simonii, commonly known as the Simon's baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its small, fluffy flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appeal.

Gypsophila simonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
