Galium sylvaticum var. atrovirens

Galium sylvaticum var. atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems contain oxalic acid, which can be mildly toxic if ingested in large quantities.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
