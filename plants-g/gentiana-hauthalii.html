Gentiana hauthalii

Gentiana hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
