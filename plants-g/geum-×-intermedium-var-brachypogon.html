Geum × intermedium var. brachypogon

Geum × intermedium var. brachypogon, commonly known as the narrowleaf bronze begonia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and bronze. The foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a textured, slightly hairy surface. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
