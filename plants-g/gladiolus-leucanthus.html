Gladiolus leucanthus

Gladiolus leucanthus, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Gladiolus leucanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
