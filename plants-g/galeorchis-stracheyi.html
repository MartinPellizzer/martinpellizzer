Galeorchis stracheyi

Galeorchis stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricately shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
