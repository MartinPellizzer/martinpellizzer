Gymnosporia senegalensis var. spinosa

Gymnosporia senegalensis var. spinosa, commonly known as the thorned gymnosporia, is a shrub native to the savannas of West Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny branches, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or loamy soils.

Gymnosporia senegalensis var. spinosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.
