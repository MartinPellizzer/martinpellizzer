Gilia tenuiflora subsp. tenuiflora

Gilia tenuiflora subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as the slenderflower gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom during the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces seeds in slender, hairy pods.
