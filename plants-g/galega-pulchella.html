Galega pulchella

Galega pulchella, commonly known as the showy galega, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Galega pulchella is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
