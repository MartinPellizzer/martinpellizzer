Geranium luteum

Geranium luteum, commonly known as the yellow geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Geranium luteum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
