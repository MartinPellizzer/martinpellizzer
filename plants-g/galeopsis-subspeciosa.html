Galeopsis subspeciosa

Galeopsis subspeciosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

Galeopsis subspeciosa is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
