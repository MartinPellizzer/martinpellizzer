Gleichenia volubilis

Gleichenia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a species of liverwort that belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and its ability to form dense mats on moist, shaded ground. The plant has a distinctive morphology with elongated, strap-like leaves that often curl at the edges. It thrives in humid environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gleichenia volubilis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
