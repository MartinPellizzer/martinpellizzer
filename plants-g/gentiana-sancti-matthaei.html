Gentiana sancti-matthaei

Gentiana sancti-matthaei, commonly known as the Saint Matthew's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
