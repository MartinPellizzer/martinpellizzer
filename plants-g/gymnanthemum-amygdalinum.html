Gymnanthemum amygdalinum

Gymnanthemum amygdalinum, commonly known as the almondleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is known for containing amygdalin, a compound related to the bitter almond flavor.
