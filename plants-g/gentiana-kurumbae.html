Gentiana kurumbae

Gentiana kurumbae, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Gentiana kurumbae is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
