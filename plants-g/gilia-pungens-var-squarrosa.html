Gilia pungens var. squarrosa

Gilia pungens var. squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
