Geranium robertianum subsp. maritimum

Geranium robertianum subsp.

maritimum, commonly known as the sea geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt-laden coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
