Genista florida f. latifolia

Genista florida f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flowering broom, is a cultivar of the Genista genus native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and profuse displays of bright yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring. The leaves are broader and more leathery compared to the typical species, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
