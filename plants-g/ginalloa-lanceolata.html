Ginalloa lanceolata

Ginalloa lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ginalloa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a slender stem and hairy foliage, which aid in its adaptation to humid conditions.

Ginalloa lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
