Gravesia porphyrovalvis

Gravesia porphyrovalvis, commonly known as the red-veined gravesia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves with contrasting green veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
