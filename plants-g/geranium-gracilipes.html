Geranium gracilipes

Geranium gracilipes, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Geranium gracilipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
