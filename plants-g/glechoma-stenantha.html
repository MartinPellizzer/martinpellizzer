Glechoma stenantha

Glechoma stenantha, commonly known as narrowleaf ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a creeping growth habit with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Glechoma stenantha is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
