Guaiabara laurifolia

Guaiabara laurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
