Gymnosporia berberoides

Gymnosporia berberoides, commonly known as the African barberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits that mature in winter. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
