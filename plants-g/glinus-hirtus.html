Glinus hirtus

Glinus hirtus, commonly known as hairy glinus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Glinus hirtus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
