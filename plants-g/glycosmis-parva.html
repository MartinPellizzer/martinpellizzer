Glycosmis parva

Glycosmis parva, commonly known as the dwarf mooli or small bitter apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Glycosmis parva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive addition to subtropical gardens.
