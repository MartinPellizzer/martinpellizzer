Guettarda nitida

Guettarda nitida, commonly known as silverleaf guettarda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal panicles.

Guettarda nitida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
