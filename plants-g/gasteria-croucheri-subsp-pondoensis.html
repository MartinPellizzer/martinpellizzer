Gasteria croucheri subsp. pondoensis

Gasteria croucheri subsp.

pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with white or yellowish markings.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in arid environments.
