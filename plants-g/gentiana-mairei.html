Gentiana mairei

Gentiana mairei, commonly known as the Maire's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana mairei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological significance.
