Gymnema stramineum

Gymnema stramineum, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to elliptical, yellow fruits. The plant is well-known for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. It contains compounds such as gymnemic acids, which are believed to have hypoglycemic properties.

The leaves are often used in traditional Ayurvedic practices for their purported health benefits.
