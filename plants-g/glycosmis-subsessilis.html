Glycosmis subsessilis

Glycosmis subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mastic tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.
