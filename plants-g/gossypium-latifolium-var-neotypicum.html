Gossypium latifolium var. neotypicum

Gossypium latifolium var. neotypicum, commonly known as the broadleaf cotton, is a variant of the cotton genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that develop in its flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a potential source of natural fibers and its genetic diversity within the cotton species.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other cotton varieties due to its specific environmental requirements.
