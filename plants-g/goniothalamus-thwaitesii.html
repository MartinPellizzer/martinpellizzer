Goniothalamus thwaitesii

Goniothalamus thwaitesii, commonly known as the Malayan star-flower, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
