Guzmania squarrosa f. lutea

Guzmania squarrosa f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small aquatic environment for microorganisms. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

The foliage is often adorned with red or orange hues, adding to its ornamental appeal.
