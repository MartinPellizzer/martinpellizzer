Gentiana ericothamna

Gentiana ericothamna, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually low-growing and often form dense clusters.

Gentiana ericothamna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
