Gerontogea racemosa

Gerontogea racemosa, commonly known as the silverleaf gerontogea, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Gerontogea racemosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
