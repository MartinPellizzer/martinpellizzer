Gymnosporia leptopus

Gymnosporia leptopus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-angled fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Gymnosporia leptopus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
