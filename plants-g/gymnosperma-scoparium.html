Gymnosperma scoparium

Gymnosperma scoparium, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Gymnosperma scoparium is native to dry regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
