Geunsia subternata

Geunsia subternata, commonly known as the slender swamp gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a striking blue color that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
