Geum vernum

Geum vernum, commonly known as spring avens, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has basal leaves with toothed edges. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in umbels.

Geum vernum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
