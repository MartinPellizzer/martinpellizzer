Gymnocalycium damsii

Gymnocalycium damsii, commonly known as the desert rose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical stem that often develops a woody texture over time. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Gymnocalycium damsii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
