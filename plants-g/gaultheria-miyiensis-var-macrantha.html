Gaultheria miyiensis var. macrantha

Gaultheria miyiensis var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered wintergreen, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing a high concentration of aromatic compounds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
