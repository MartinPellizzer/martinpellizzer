Gouldochloa curvifolia

Gouldochloa curvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gouldochloa curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
