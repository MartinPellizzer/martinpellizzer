Garnotia patula

Garnotia patula, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Garnotia patula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
