Geoffroea superba

Geoffroea superba, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Geoffroea superba is a symbol of Australia and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
