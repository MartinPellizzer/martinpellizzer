Gomphocarpus glaberrimus

Gomphocarpus glaberrimus, commonly known as the yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its erect stem and alternate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
