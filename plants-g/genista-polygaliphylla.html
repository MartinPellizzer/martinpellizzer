Genista polygaliphylla

Genista polygaliphylla, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Genista polygaliphylla is also known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
