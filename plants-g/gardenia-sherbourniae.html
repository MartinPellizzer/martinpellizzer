Gardenia sherbourniae

Gardenia sherbourniae, commonly known as the Sherbourn's gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers warm, humid climates and well-draining soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
