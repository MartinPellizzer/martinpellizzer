Guatteria rigidipes

Guatteria rigidipes, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive rigid appearance.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
