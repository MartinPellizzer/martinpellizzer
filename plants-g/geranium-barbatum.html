Geranium barbatum

Geranium barbatum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a fuzzy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Geranium barbatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
