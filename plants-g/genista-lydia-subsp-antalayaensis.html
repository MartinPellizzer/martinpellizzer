Genista lydia subsp. antalayaensis

Genista lydia subsp.

antalayaensis, commonly known as the Antalya genista, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, evergreen leaves that are usually needle-like.

The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
