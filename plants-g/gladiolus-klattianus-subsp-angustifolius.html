Gladiolus klattianus subsp. angustifolius

Gladiolus klattianus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors.

The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, contributing to its elegant appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown for its vibrant blooms.
