Goodyera similis f. albonervosa

Goodyera similis f. albonervosa, commonly known as the white-veined pterostigma.

This orchid is a hybrid form native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its striking white veins on the leaves. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, with small, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a faint purple tint, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
