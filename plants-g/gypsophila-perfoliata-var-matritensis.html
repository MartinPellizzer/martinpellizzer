Gypsophila perfoliata var. matritensis

Gypsophila perfoliata var. matritensis, commonly known as the Matritensis sweet pea flower, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blooms that are often used in floral arrangements due to their ethereal appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.
