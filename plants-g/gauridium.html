Gauridium

Gauridium, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Gauridium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
