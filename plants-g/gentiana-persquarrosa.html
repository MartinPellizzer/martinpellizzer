Gentiana persquarrosa

Gentiana persquarrosa, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
