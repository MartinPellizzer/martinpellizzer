Grubbia rosmarinifolia subsp. rosmarinifolia

Grubbia rosmarinifolia subsp.

rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features compact, shrub-like growth with dense, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
