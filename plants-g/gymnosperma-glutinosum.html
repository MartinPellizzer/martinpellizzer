Gymnosperma glutinosum

Gymnosperma glutinosum, commonly known as sticky grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, sticky leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Africa and Asia. Its seeds are enclosed in small, membranous sacs, a feature typical of gymnosperms.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
