Genista versicolor

Genista versicolor, commonly known as the variable broom, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and woodland edges.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
