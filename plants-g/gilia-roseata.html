Gilia roseata

Gilia roseata, commonly known as the roseate gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Gilia roseata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
