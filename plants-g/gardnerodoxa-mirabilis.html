Gardnerodoxa mirabilis

Gardnerodoxa mirabilis, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that turn yellow in autumn before falling off. Its flowers are typically tubular and are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
