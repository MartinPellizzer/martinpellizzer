Garcinia magnifolia

Garcinia magnifolia, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Garcinia magnifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
