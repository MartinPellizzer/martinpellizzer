Glycine parviflora

Glycine parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications. Glycine parviflora is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as both an agricultural crop and an ornamental plant.
