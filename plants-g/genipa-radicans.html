Genipa radicans

Genipa radicans, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small tree or shrub that produces large, spiky fruits with a thick, greenish-yellow skin. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in humid, lowland areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it relies on insect pollination.

The fruit is edible, with a creamy, aromatic flesh that is popular in both fresh and processed forms.
