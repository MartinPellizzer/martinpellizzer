Gnidiopsis juniperifolia

Gnidiopsis juniperifolia, commonly known as the juniper-like gnidiopsis, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles the appearance of juniper. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries.

Gnidiopsis juniperifolia is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
