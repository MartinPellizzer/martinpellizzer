Glycine hirticaulis

Glycine hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy pinto bean, is a species of legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. Glycine hirticaulis is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
