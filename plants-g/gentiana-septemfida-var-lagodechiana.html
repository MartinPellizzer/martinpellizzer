Gentiana septemfida var. lagodechiana

Gentiana septemfida var. lagodechiana, commonly known as the seven-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in subalpine and alpine meadows.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
