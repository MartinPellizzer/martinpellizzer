Galeopsis tetrahit proles bifida

Galeopsis tetrahit proles bifida, commonly known as the bifid hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its common name refers to the distinctive bifid (split) shape of its leaves.
