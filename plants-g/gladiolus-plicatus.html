Gladiolus plicatus

Gladiolus plicatus, commonly known as the pleated gladiolus, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, pleated leaves that give it its common name, and its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of brightly colored flowers. The flowers are typically pink, red, or white, with a distinctive three-lobed shape and a prominent central vein. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
