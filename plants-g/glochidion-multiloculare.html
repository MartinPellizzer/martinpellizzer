Glochidion multiloculare

Glochidion multiloculare, commonly known as the multilocular glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cup-like structures, followed by capsule fruits that release tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
