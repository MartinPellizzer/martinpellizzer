Garcinia picrorhiza

Garcinia picrorhiza, commonly known as Indian bitter orange, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that contain high concentrations of active compounds such as hydroxycitric acid and punicalagin. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and yellow.

The roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine and are valued for their purported health benefits.
