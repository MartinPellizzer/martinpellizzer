Gigantochloa manggong

Gigantochloa manggong, commonly known as the giant bamboo of Borneo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and arranged in a whorled pattern around the nodes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Gigantochloa manggong is valued for its strength and flexibility, making it useful in traditional construction and crafts.
