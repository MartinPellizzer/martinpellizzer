Galium pumilum subsp. fleurotii

Galium pumilum subsp.

fleurotii, commonly known as the dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Galium pumilum subsp.

fleurotii thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
