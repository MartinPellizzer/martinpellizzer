Gymnocalycium taningaense var. lukasikii

Gymnocalycium taningaense var. lukasikii, commonly known as the Teninga Desert Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
