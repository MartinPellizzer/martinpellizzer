Gagea saxatilis var. sicula

Gagea saxatilis var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian rock garlic, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, sunny environments.
