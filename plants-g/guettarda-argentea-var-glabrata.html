Guettarda argentea var. glabrata

Guettarda argentea var. glabrata, commonly known as silverleaf guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in a waxy coating that gives them their distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
