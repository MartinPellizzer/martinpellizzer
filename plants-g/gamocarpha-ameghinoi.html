Gamocarpha ameghinoi

Gamocarpha ameghinoi, commonly known as the Argentine thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its structure includes a central stem with alternate leaves and a composite flower head composed of both ray and disk flowers.

Gamocarpha ameghinoi is adapted to withstand arid conditions and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
