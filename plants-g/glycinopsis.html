Glycinopsis

Glycinopsis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, lowland areas and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Glycinopsis has a fibrous root system and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its aggressive spread.
