Glyceria

Glyceria, commonly known as sweetflag, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It includes several species that are often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plants typically have slender, grass-like leaves and produce small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Some species of Glyceria are used in traditional medicine and have been historically valued for their aromatic properties.

They play an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
