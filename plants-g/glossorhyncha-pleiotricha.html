Glossorhyncha pleiotricha

Glossorhyncha pleiotricha, commonly known as the common red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Glossorhyncha pleiotricha is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
