Gypsophila reticulata

Gypsophila reticulata, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers on slender stems, which give it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila reticulata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
