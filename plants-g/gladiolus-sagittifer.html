Gladiolus sagittifer

Gladiolus sagittifer, commonly known as the arrowhead gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that bear showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant's leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its flowers are typically borne on one side of the stem, a characteristic known as scorpioid cleistogamy.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
