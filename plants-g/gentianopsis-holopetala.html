Gentianopsis holopetala

Gentianopsis holopetala, commonly known as the holopetal gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers. The plant features a single, large, radially symmetrical flower that is typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flower's structure is adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.
