Gaillardia serotina

Gaillardia serotina, commonly known as late blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange and yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, preferring full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, contributing to the landscape with its vibrant colors.

Gaillardia serotina is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
