Galium coronatum var. mesopotamicum

Galium coronatum var. mesopotamicum, commonly known as the Mesopotamic bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs and possesses small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rubiaceae family.
