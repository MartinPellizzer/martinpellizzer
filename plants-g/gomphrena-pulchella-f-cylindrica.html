Gomphrena pulchella f. cylindrica

Gomphrena pulchella f. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical plume flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
