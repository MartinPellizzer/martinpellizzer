Gerardia lancifolia

Gerardia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive leaf shape and its historical association with the botanist John Gerard.

Gerardia lancifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought-resistant nature.
