Gladiolus cordatus

Gladiolus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
