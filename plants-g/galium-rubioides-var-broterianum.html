Galium rubioides var. broterianum

Galium rubioides var. broterianum, commonly known as the Mexican bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
