Gossypium klotzschianum

Gossypium klotzschianum, commonly known as the Klotzsch cotton, is a species of wild cotton native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fluffy fibers that are used in textile production. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces capsules that contain the cotton seeds.

This species is valued for its fiber quality and is sometimes cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
