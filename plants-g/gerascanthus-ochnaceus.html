Gerascanthus ochnaceus

Gerascanthus ochnaceus, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a member of the Ochnaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries containing a single seed.

Its latex sap is milky and has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a remedy for wounds and skin conditions.
