Galium vernum var. ruthenicum

Galium vernum var. ruthenicum, commonly known as the Ruthenian bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
