Gladiolus debeerstii

Gladiolus debeerstii, commonly known as the Beersfontein gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces numerous spikes of flowers, each with three to five petals and three sepals, creating a dramatic visual display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
