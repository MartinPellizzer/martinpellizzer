Grevillea integrifolia subsp. incrassata

Grevillea integrifolia subsp.

incrassata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
