Gasteranthus crispus

Gasteranthus crispus, commonly known as the curled bellflower, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bell-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, red or orange fruits. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a wrinkled texture.

Gasteranthus crispus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
