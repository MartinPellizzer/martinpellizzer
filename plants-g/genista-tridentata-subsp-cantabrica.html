Genista tridentata subsp. cantabrica

Genista tridentata subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features slender, thorned stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
