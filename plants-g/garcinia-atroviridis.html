Garcinia atroviridis

Garcinia atroviridis, commonly known as the black garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn red or orange depending on the variety. Garcinia atroviridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
