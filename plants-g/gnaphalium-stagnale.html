Gnaphalium stagnale

Gnaphalium stagnale, commonly known as bog myrtle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flower heads. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Gnaphalium stagnale plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
