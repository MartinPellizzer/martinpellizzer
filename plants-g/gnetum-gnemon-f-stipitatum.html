Gnetum gnemon f. stipitatum

Gnetum gnemon f. stipitatum, commonly known as the stipulate gnetum, is a unique variant of the genus Gnetum, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical rainforest environments. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a broad ovate shape with prominent stipules. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are unisexual, with separate male and female structures.

It is also known for its ability to produce a resinous substance, which has been historically used in traditional practices.
