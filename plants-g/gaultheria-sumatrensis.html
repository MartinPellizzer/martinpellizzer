Gaultheria sumatrensis

Gaultheria sumatrensis, commonly known as the Sumatran wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Gaultheria sumatrensis is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
