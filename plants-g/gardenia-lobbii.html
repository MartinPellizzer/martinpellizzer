Gardenia lobbii

Gardenia lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are often used in perfumery due to their strong scent. Gardenia lobbii is characterized by its showy, waxy petals and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in tropical gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for landscapers and horticulturists.
