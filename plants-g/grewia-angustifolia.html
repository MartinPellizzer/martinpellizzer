Grewia angustifolia

Grewia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
