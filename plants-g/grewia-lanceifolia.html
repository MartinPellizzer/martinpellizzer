Grewia lanceifolia

Grewia lanceifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Grewia lanceifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
