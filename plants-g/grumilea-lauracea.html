Grumilea lauracea

Grumilea lauracea, commonly known as the lauraceous grumilea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. Grumilea lauracea produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
