Gynura flava

Gynura flava, commonly known as the golden shield plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Gynura flava is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.
