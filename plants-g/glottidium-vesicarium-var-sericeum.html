Glottidium vesicarium var. sericeum

Glottidium vesicarium var. sericeum, commonly known as the silky bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by inflated, bladder-like pods that give it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
