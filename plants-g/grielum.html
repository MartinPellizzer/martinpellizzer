Grielum

Grielum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of still water bodies. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Grielum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various traditions.
