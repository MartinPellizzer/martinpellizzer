Geranium lentum

Geranium lentum, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped or ovate, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

Geranium lentum is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in partial to full sun.
