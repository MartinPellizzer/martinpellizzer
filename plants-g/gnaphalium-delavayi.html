Gnaphalium delavayi

Gnaphalium delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's feather grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its delicate appearance and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological surveys.
