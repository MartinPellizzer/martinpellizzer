Galium angustifolium var. siccatum

Galium angustifolium var. siccatum, commonly known as the dryleaf woodryard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and lanceolate. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often dry and brittle, giving the variety its common name.

It thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland and swampy areas.
