Gaultheria purpurea

Gaultheria purpurea, commonly known as the purple wintergreen, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but have a sharp, acidic flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Gaultheria purpurea is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of methyl salicylate in its leaves.
