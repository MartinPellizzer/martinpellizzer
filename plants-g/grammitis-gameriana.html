Grammitis gameriana

Grammitis gameriana, commonly known as the Hawaiian fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Grammitis gameriana plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of Hawaiian ecosystems.
