Gouffeia crassiuscula

Gouffeia crassiuscula, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Gouffeia crassiuscula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
