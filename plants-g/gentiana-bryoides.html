Gentiana bryoides

Gentiana bryoides, commonly known as the moss gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette, which helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

Gentiana bryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
