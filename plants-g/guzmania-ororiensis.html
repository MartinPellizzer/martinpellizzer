Guzmania ororiensis

Guzmania ororiensis, commonly known as the red-tipped guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence that features bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater, creating a small ecosystem within its foliage. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and the plant thrives in bright, indirect light.

Guzmania ororiensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
