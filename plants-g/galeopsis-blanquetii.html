Galeopsis blanquetii

Galeopsis blanquetii, commonly known as Blanquet's horseweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Galeopsis blanquetii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a minor weed in some regions.
