Glochidion heyneanum var. mishmiense

Glochidion heyneanum var. mishmiense, commonly known as the Mishmi glabrous leaf.

It is a small shrub native to the eastern Himalayas, characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, globose berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within montane forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest for botanists and ecologists.
