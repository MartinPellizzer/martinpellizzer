Gelseminum fulvum

Gelseminum fulvum, commonly known as the yellow oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a lance-shaped appearance. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes other toxic plants, and its sap contains cardiac glycosides.

Gelseminum fulvum is often cultivated for its ornamental value but requires careful handling due to its poisonous nature.
