Gilia rubra var. capillacea

Gilia rubra var. capillacea, commonly known as the red gilia or redstem gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, bright red to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
