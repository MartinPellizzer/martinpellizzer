Gaertnera paniculata

Gaertnera paniculata, commonly known as the panicle hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, branched inflorescences that produce numerous small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Gaertnera paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
