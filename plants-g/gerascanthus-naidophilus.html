Gerascanthus naidophilus

Gerascanthus naidophilus, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in sandy and rocky soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Gerascanthus naidophilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
