Gigasiphon

Gigasiphon, commonly known as the giant siphon, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a broad, colorful spathe. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often reaching several feet in length.

Gigasiphon thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
