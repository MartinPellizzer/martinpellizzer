Graptopetalum saxifragoides var. saxifragoides

Graptopetalum saxifragoides var. saxifragoides, commonly known as the stonecrop jade plant, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and minimal care requirements make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
