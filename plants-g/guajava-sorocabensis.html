Guajava sorocabensis

Guajava sorocabensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Guajava sorocabensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
