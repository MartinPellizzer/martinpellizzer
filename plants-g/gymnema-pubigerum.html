Gymnema pubigerum

Gymnema pubigerum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by follicular fruits containing seeds. Gymnema pubigerum is notable for its unique chemical composition, including gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional Ayurvedic medicine and ecological systems.
