Gentiana affinis var. forwoodii

Gentiana affinis var. forwoodii, commonly known as the Forwood's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is upright and bears the showy flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
