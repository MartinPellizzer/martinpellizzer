Gyminda latifolia glaucifolia

Gyminda latifolia glaucifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf glaucous gyminda, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, known as glaucous. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
