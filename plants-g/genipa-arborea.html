Genipa arborea

Genipa arborea, commonly known as the genipapo tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and dark green, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red or orange berries. Its fruit, known as genipap, is notable for its distinctive flavor and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
