Gastrochilus hallieri

Gastrochilus hallieri, commonly known as the Halli's toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive toothed lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in forest understories.

Gastrochilus hallieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
