Geranium petroselinum

Geranium petroselinum, commonly known as parsley geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves that resemble those of parsley, hence its common name. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer with flowers in shades of pink, red, or white.

Geranium petroselinum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
