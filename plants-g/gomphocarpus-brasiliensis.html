Gomphocarpus brasiliensis

Gomphocarpus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats.

Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides that deter herbivores.
