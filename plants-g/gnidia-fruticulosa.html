Gnidia fruticulosa

Gnidia fruticulosa, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gnidia fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
