Glycine javanica var. claessensii

Glycine javanica var. claessensii, commonly known as the Java bean vine, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
