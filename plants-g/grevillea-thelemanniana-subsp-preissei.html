Grevillea thelemanniana subsp. preissei

Grevillea thelemanniana subsp.

preissei, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
