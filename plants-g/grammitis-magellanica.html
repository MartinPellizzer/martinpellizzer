Grammitis magellanica

Grammitis magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky surfaces. Grammitis magellanica is notable for its intricate, lace-like foliage and its ability to form dense mats.

It plays a minor but ecologically significant role in maintaining soil moisture and providing microhabitats for small invertebrates.
