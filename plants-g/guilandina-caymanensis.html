Guilandina caymanensis

Guilandina caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman Islands climbing legume, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
