Gentiana triflora subvar. horomuiensis

Gentiana triflora subvar. horomuiensis, commonly known as the Horomui gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are clustered in umbels.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
