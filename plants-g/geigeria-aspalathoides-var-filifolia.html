Geigeria aspalathoides var. filifolia

Geigeria aspalathoides var. filifolia, commonly known as the Filifoliated Geigeria, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
