Guzmania morreniana

Guzmania morreniana, commonly known as the Morren's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting bracts that come in a variety of colors, often resembling flowers. The plant has a rosette-shaped leaf structure that collects rainwater, providing a microhabitat for small organisms. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Guzmania morreniana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
