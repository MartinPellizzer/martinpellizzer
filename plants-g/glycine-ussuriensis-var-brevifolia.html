Glycine ussuriensis var. brevifolia

Glycine ussuriensis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the Ussuri climbing bean, is a perennial legume native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both garden use and food.
