Gastrodia dyeriana

Gastrodia dyeriana, commonly known as the Dyer's ginseng, is a rare and endangered orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a mycoheterotrophic plant, meaning it relies entirely on fungi for its nutrition and does not perform photosynthesis. The plant produces a single, fleshy, yellowish-brown underground tuber that is the primary part used in traditional medicine. It has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and a single, small, greenish-white flower.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
