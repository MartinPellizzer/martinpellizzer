Gossampinus insignis

Gossampinus insignis, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-yellow fruits that split open to release seeds.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in some regions.
