Gentiana verna

Gentiana verna, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and branched.

Gentiana verna is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
