Gentiana tornezyana

Gentiana tornezyana, commonly known as the Torneza gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana tornezyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
