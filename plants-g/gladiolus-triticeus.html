Gladiolus triticeus

Gladiolus triticeus, commonly known as the tufted gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces spikes of colorful, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, giving it a resemblance to wheat, hence its species name *triticeus*.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.
