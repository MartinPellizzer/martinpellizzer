Galedupa piscidia

Galedupa piscidia, commonly known as the Madagascar water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that float on the water surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water currents for spore dispersal.

Galedupa piscidia plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
