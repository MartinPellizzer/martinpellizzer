Gaillardia pulchella var. simplex

Gaillardia pulchella var. simplex, commonly known as the swamp redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of orange to brown tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
