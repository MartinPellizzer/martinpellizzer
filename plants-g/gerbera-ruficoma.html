Gerbera ruficoma

Gerbera ruficoma, commonly known as the red-stemmed gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gerbera ruficoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
