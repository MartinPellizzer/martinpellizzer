Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. sapindoides

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. sapindoides, commonly known as the blue-fruited gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn blue when mature, giving it its common name. These fruits are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries in tropical gardens.
