Gymnosporia rufa var. latifolia

Gymnosporia rufa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved African burr acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
