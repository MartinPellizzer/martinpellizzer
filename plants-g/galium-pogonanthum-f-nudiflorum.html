Galium pogonanthum f. nudiflorum

Galium pogonanthum f. nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower bedstraw, is a variant of the bedstraw genus characterized by its reduced floral structure.

It is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers, which are often fewer and less showy than in the typical species, are usually white or pale green and clustered in small umbels.

This form is notable for its adaptation to specific ecological niches and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
