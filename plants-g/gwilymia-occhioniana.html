Gwilymia occhioniana

Gwilymia occhioniana, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Gwilymia occhioniana make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
