Galega barba-jovis

Galega barba-jovis, commonly known as goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features long, slender, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a beard-like appearance. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

Galega barba-jovis is adapted to dry, sunny habitats and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
