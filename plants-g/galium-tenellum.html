Galium tenellum

Galium tenellum, commonly known as small whitlow grass, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
