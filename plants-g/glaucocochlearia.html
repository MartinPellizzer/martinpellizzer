Glaucocochlearia

Glaucocochlearia, commonly known as the blue ear cactus, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, cylindrical stem covered in dense, blue-gray spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Glaucocochlearia thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.
