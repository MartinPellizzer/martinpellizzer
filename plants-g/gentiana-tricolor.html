Gentiana tricolor

Gentiana tricolor, commonly known as the three-color gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue, white, and yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana tricolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
