Garcinia hennecartii

Garcinia hennecartii, commonly known as the African butter fruit, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and striking orange-red fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
