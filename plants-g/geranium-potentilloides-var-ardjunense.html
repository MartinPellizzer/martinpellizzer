Geranium potentilloides var. ardjunense

Geranium potentilloides var. ardjunense, commonly known as the Armenian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It features upright stems that are typically covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
