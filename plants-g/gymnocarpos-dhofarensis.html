Gymnocarpos dhofarensis

Gymnocarpos dhofarensis, commonly known as the Dhofar date palm, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, fleshy fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing high drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically narrow and dark green, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Gymnocarpos dhofarensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has historical significance in the region.
