Geissomeria longiflora var. paniculata

Geissomeria longiflora var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in moist, wooded areas. Its fruits, which are small berries, are typically red or orange when mature and are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
