Gentiana singgalangensis

Gentiana singgalangensis, commonly known as the Singgalang gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers are characterized by their five to eight petals, often with a striking blue or violet hue.

Gentiana singgalangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
