Genista tinctoria f. pubescens

Genista tinctoria f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, pubescent hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, with a leathery texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.
