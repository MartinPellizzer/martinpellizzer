Galium gracile var. parisiense

Galium gracile var. parisiense, commonly known as Parisian bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, with a delicate, herbaceous texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
