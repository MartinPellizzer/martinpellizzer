Galatella dahurica

Galatella dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to tolerate harsh winter conditions.
