Grangeria buxifolia

Grangeria buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage is aromatic and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

Grangeria buxifolia thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
