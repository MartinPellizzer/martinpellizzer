Grusonia arbuscula

Grusonia arbuscula, commonly known as the tree of life, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern that resembles a tree, hence its common name. The plant has a woody structure with a central trunk and multiple lateral branches. Its leaves are typically small and clustered at the ends of the branches.

Grusonia arbuscula is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
