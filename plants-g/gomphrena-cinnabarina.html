Gomphrena cinnabarina

Gomphrena cinnabarina, commonly known as the red gayfeather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to pink bract-like flowers that resemble plumes, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are often branched and sturdy.

Gomphrena cinnabarina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
