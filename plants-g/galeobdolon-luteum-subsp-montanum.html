Galeobdolon luteum subsp. montanum

Galeobdolon luteum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue-black seeds in its fruit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
