Gladiolus prismatosiphon

Gladiolus prismatosiphon, commonly known as the prismatocarpus gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with its foliage forming a dense rosette at the base. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, yellow, and white, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
