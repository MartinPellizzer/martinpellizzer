Gumira nauseosa

Gumira nauseosa, commonly known as the stinking gumira, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its strong odor, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
