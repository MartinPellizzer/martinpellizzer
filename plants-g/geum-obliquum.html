Geum obliquum

Geum obliquum, commonly known as zigzag goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Geum obliquum thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
