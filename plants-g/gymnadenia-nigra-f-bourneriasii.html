Gymnadenia nigra f. bourneriasii

Gymnadenia nigra f. bourneriasii, commonly known as the Bourneriasii form of the black orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often adorned with intricate markings. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a tall flowering stem that bears a cluster of fragrant blooms.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance.
