Gentiana detonsa var. nana

Gentiana detonsa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy flowers.
