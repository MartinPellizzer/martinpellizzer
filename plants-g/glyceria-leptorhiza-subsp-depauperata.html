Glyceria leptorhiza subsp. depauperata

Glyceria leptorhiza subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a grass species native to wetland and marsh environments in North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. It has a limited number of flowering structures, which is reflected in its subspecies name "depauperata," meaning "poor in flowers.".

This subspecies is often found in the eastern United States and is adapted to nutrient-poor conditions.
