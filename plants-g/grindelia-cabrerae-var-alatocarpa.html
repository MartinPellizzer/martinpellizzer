Grindelia cabrerae var. alatocarpa

Grindelia cabrerae var. alatocarpa, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
