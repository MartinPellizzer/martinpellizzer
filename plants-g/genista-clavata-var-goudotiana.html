Genista clavata var. goudotiana

Genista clavata var. goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly larger and more robust growth compared to the typical form of Genista clavata.
