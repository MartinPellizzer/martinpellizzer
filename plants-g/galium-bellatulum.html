Galium bellatulum

Galium bellatulum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate in shape. Small white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters at the leaf axils, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
