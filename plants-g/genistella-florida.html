Genistella florida

Genistella florida, commonly known as the Florida broom, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
