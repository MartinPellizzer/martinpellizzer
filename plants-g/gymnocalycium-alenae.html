Gymnocalycium alenae

Gymnocalycium alenae, commonly known as the Alena's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Gymnocalycium alenae is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
