Gnaphalium unionis

Gnaphalium unionis, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically has hairy, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Gnaphalium unionis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of soil types.
