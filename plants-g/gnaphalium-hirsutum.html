Gnaphalium hirsutum

Gnaphalium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers are typically visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination and ecological balance.
