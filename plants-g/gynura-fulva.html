Gynura fulva

Gynura fulva, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Chinese evergreen, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and produces small yellow flowers in clusters. Gynura fulva thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
