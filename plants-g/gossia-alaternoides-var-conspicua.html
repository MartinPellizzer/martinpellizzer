Gossia alaternoides var. conspicua

Gossia alaternoides var. conspicua, commonly known as the showy gossia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers a humid, temperate climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
