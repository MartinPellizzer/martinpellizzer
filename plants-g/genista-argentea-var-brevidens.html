Genista argentea var. brevidens

Genista argentea var. brevidens, commonly known as silverbush broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
