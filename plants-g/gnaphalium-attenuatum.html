Gnaphalium attenuatum

Gnaphalium attenuatum, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly woolly texture.

Gnaphalium attenuatum is commonly found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
