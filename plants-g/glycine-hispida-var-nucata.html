Glycine hispida var. nucata

Glycine hispida var. nucata, commonly known as the hairy poinsettia, is a variant of the poinsettia plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a hairy texture, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant during the holiday season.
