Gilia parviflora

Gilia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Gilia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
