Goodenia gypsicola

Goodenia gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on gypsum soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The flowers, which can be pink or red, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.
