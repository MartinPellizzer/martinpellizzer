Grewia serratula

Grewia serratula, commonly known as the roughleaf grewia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, serrated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia serratula is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
