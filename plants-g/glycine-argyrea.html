Glycine argyrea

Glycine argyrea, commonly known as silver wisteria, is a climbing legume native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of small, pale to silvery-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Glycine argyrea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
