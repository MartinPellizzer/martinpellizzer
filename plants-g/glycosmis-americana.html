Glycosmis americana

Glycosmis americana, commonly known as the American gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The fruits, which resemble small berries, are often used in local cuisines and have a tart, acidic flavor.
