Gagea serotina var. parva

Gagea serotina var. parva, commonly known as the small late-flowering gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the bulbs are small and clustered.

It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
