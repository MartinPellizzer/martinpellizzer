Gilia divaricata var. volcanica

Gilia divaricata var. volcanica, commonly known as the volcanic gilia, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in volcanic landscapes, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
