Gentiana × vaccarii

Gentiana × vaccarii, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
