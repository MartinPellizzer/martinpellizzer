Galactia debilis

Galactia debilis, commonly known as the weak milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has slender, hairy stems.

Galactia debilis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
