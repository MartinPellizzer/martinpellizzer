Griffonia icondere

Griffonia icondere, commonly known as the African devil tree, is a climbing shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing flat, bean-like seeds. The seeds contain a high concentration of 5-hydroxytryptophan, which has been studied for its potential health benefits.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is also valued in traditional medicine and as a source of natural supplements.
