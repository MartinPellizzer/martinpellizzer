Gymnema longipedunculatum

Gymnema longipedunculatum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which contains various chemical compounds. It is often found in dense forests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
