Gambeya excelsa

Gambeya excelsa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that are often a vibrant green, and its showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gambeya excelsa is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
