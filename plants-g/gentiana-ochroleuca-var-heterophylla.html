Gentiana ochroleuca var. heterophylla

Gentiana ochroleuca var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are often variable in shape, giving rise to its varietal name, "heterophylla," which means "different leaves.".

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
