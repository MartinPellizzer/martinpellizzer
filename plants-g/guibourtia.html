Guibourtia

Guibourtia, commonly known as African mahogany, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It consists of several species native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in the rainforests and savannas. The trees are valued for their hard, durable wood, which is often used in furniture and construction. They have bipinnate leaves and produce small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes.

The fruits are legumes, typically containing several seeds.
