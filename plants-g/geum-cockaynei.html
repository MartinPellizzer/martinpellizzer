Geum cockaynei

Geum cockaynei, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Geum cockaynei plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
