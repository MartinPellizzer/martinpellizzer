Galarhoeus ebracteolatus var. coreanus

Galarhoeus ebracteolatus var. coreanus, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces showy blue to purple flowers during the summer months. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
