Goeppertia variegata

Goeppertia variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, silver, and white, creating a visually appealing pattern. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its foliage is not only decorative but also contributes to air purification, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
