Grevillea integrifolia

Grevillea integrifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats.

Grevillea integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
