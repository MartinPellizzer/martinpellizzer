Geum chiloense

Geum chiloense, commonly known as Chilean avens, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately lobed and its showy, pink to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Geum chiloense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
