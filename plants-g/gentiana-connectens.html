Gentiana connectens

Gentiana connectens, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue throat, making it a visually distinctive species in its habitat.
