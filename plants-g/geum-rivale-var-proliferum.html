Geum rivale var. proliferum

Geum rivale var. proliferum, commonly known as the meadow avens, is a perennial herb native to moist meadows and stream banks in Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained, fertile soils and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
