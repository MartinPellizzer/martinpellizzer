Gongrothamnus corradianus

Gongrothamnus corradianus, commonly known as the Sierra goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Gongrothamnus corradianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
