Gladiolus testaceus

Gladiolus testaceus, commonly known as the golden gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces numerous blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting nature.
