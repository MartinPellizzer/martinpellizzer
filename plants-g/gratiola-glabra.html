Gratiola glabra

Gratiola glabra, commonly known as smooth gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or prairie habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple, tubular, and bloom in late summer.

Gratiola glabra is part of the Gentianaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
