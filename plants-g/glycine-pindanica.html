Glycine pindanica

Glycine pindanica, commonly known as the Pindan bean tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine.

Glycine pindanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
