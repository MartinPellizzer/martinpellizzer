Gentiana helophila

Gentiana helophila, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often near streams or in marshy areas. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana helophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
