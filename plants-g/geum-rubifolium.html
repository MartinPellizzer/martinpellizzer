Geum rubifolium

Geum rubifolium, commonly known as scarlet avens, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, crimson-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Geum rubifolium is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health properties.
