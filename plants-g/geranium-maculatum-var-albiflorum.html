Geranium maculatum var. albiflorum

Geranium maculatum var. albiflorum, commonly known as white geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often adorned with red or purple veins. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
