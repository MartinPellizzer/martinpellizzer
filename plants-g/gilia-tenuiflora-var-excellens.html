Gilia tenuiflora var. excellens

Gilia tenuiflora var. excellens, commonly known as the slender-flowered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
