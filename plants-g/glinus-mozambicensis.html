Glinus mozambicensis

Glinus mozambicensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the rainy season.

Glinus mozambicensis is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.
