Galeandra villosa

Galeandra villosa, commonly known as the hairy galeandra, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Galeandra villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
