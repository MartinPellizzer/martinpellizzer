Galaxia alata

Galaxia alata, commonly known as the winged galaxia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, winged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Galaxia alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
