Glomera retusimentum

Glomera retusimentum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited glomera, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Glomeraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, smooth, and often globular fruits that mature to a deep purple or reddish color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
