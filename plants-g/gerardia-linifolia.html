Gerardia linifolia

Gerardia linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Gerardia linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
