Geranium lebelii

Geranium lebelii, commonly known as the Lebel's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Geranium lebelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
