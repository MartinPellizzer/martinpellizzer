Gymnogramma pedata

Gymnogramma pedata, commonly known as the creeping staghorn fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, shield-like appearance. Gymnogramma pedata is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks, where it attaches itself using its rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant cultivation.
