Gentianella polysperes

Gentianella polysperes, commonly known as the many-seeded gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Gentianella polysperes is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
