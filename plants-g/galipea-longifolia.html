Galipea longifolia

Galipea longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf galipea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability to various soil types make it a notable species in its native habitat.
