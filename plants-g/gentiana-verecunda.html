Gentiana verecunda

Gentiana verecunda, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
