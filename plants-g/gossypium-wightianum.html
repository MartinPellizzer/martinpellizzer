Gossypium wightianum

Gossypium wightianum, commonly known as Wight's cotton, is a species of shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy burs that contain the cotton fibers, which are used for textile purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it is an important plant in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
