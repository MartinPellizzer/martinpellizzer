Glochidion billardierei

Glochidion billardierei, commonly known as the Australian holly.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. Glochidion billardierei is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
