Gomphia aquatica

Gomphia aquatica, commonly known as the water horsetail, is a perennial plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of branches and spore-producing structures. The plant has a hollow, jointed stem that is typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies in marshy areas.

Gomphia aquatica plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
