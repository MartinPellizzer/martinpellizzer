Gastrochilus laxiflorus

Gastrochilus laxiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered stomach-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of fleshy, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Gastrochilus laxiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
