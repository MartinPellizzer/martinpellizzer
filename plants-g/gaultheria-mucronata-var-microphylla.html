Gaultheria mucronata var. microphylla

Gaultheria mucronata var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf strawberry bush, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, edible berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
