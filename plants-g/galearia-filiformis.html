Galearia filiformis

Galearia filiformis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, thread-like stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Galearia filiformis thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in alpine meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
