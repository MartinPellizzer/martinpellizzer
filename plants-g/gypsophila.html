Gypsophila

Gypsophila, commonly known as laceflower or baby's breath, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy flowers that resemble clouds. The plants typically grow in dry, rocky soils and are often found in alpine or arid environments. Gypsophila species are herbaceous perennials, with stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

Their flowers are usually white, pink, or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.
