Guioa acuminata

Guioa acuminata, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries containing a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
