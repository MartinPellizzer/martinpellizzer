Gonolobus cordatus

Gonolobus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Gonolobus cordatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
