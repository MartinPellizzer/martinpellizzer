Guzmania amplectens

Guzmania amplectens, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a cluster of flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features a rosette of waxy, strap-like leaves that are dark green with a velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush appearance.

Guzmania amplectens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
