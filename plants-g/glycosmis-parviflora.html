Glycosmis parviflora

Glycosmis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered glycosmis, is a species of flowering plant in the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Glycosmis parviflora is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
