Garcinia longifolia

Garcinia longifolia, commonly known as the long-fruited garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single, hard, seed-like structure surrounded by a thin, acidic pulp. The plant is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
