Grastidium pogonantherum

Grastidium pogonantherum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered grastidium, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Grastidium pogonantherum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
