Gymnadenia ornithis

Gymnadenia ornithis, commonly known as the bird's nest orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique, bird's nest-like arrangement of leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late summer and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Gymnadenia ornithis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
