Gonzalagunia dicocca

Gonzalagunia dicocca, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in subtropical climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
