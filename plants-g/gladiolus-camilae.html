Gladiolus camilae

Gladiolus camilae, commonly known as the Camilae gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
