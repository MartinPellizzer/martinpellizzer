Gordonia szechuanensis

Gordonia szechuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan gordonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible for certain wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
