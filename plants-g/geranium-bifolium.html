Geranium bifolium

Geranium bifolium, commonly known as the fringed geranium, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem and is often found in clusters, with each stem producing a single flower.

Geranium bifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
