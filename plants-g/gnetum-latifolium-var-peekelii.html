Gnetum latifolium var. peekelii

Gnetum latifolium var. peekelii, commonly known as the broadleaf gnetum, is a unique gymnosperm that exhibits characteristics of both conifers and angiosperms.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often leathery leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed cone. It is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary position, bridging the gap between gymnosperms and flowering plants.
