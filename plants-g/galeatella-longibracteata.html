Galeatella longibracteata

Galeatella longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
