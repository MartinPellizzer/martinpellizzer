Geum × rhaeticum

Geum × rhaeticum, commonly known as the early bloomers' garden beauty, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring flowers that range from red to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features palmate leaves with a serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Geum × rhaeticum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
