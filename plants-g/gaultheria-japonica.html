Gaultheria japonica

Gaultheria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese wintergreen, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are glossy and oval, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gaultheria japonica is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
