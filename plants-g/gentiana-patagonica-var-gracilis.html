Gentiana patagonica var. gracilis

Gentiana patagonica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
