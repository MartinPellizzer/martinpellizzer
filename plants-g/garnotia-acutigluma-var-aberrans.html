Garnotia acutigluma var. aberrans

Garnotia acutigluma var. aberrans, commonly known as the aberrant spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctively pointed tip. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying grassland ecosystems and plant adaptation.
