Gilibertia macradenia

Gilibertia macradenia, commonly known as the giant grass-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Gilibertia macradenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
