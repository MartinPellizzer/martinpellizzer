Genista tournefortii var. transiens

Genista tournefortii var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its rapid growth and seasonal flowering make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
