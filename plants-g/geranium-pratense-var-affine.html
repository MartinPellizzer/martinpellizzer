Geranium pratense var. affine

Geranium pratense var. affine, commonly known as the meadow geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are arranged oppositely along the stems. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
