Griseocereus fimbriatus

Griseocereus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fimbriate cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a short, thick stem covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its adaptations include succulent stems that store water and a shallow root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Griseocereus fimbriatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
