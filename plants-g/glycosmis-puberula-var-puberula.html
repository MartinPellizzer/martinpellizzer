Glycosmis puberula var. puberula

Glycosmis puberula var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Its fruits are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, making it a valued plant in local cuisines.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and fruit.
