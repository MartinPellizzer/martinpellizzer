Gaura coccinea var. arizonica

Gaura coccinea var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces slender, branching stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
