Gladiolus sufflavus

Gladiolus sufflavus, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive orange throat, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, sword-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
