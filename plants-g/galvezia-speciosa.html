Galvezia speciosa

Galvezia speciosa, commonly known as the showy galvezia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Galvezia speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
