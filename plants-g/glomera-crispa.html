Glomera crispa

Glomera crispa, commonly known as the crinkled glomera, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly crinkled texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Glomera crispa is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
