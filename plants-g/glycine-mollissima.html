Glycine mollissima

Glycine mollissima, commonly known as the silky bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Glycine mollissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
