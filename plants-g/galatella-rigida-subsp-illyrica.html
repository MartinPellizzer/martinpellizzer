Galatella rigida subsp. illyrica

Galatella rigida subsp.

illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian rigid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and somewhat woody at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
