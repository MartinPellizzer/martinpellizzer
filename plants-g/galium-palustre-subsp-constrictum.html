Galium palustre subsp. constrictum

Galium palustre subsp.

constrictum, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America. It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
