Gerardia macrodonta var. latifolia

Gerardia macrodonta var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and in wetlands. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in wetland ecosystems.
