Geum rossii

Geum rossii, commonly known as the Rosy Globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Geum rossii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
