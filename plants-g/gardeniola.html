Gardeniola

Gardeniola, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Gardeniola thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
