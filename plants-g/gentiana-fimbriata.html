Gentiana fimbriata

Gentiana fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and erect.

The fringed gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
