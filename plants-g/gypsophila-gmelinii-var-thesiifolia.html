Gypsophila gmelinii var. thesiifolia

Gypsophila gmelinii var. thesiifolia, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
