Grastidium ochranthum

Grastidium ochranthum, commonly known as the crimson flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red and orange inflorescences.

Native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of rainfall. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that help retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
