Gladiolus hyalinus

Gladiolus hyalinus, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem, with three to five petals and a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Gladiolus hyalinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
