Gentiana saponaria var. alba

Gentiana saponaria var. alba, commonly known as the white gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy layer.

The genus Gentiana is known for its medicinal properties, and this variety is no exception, containing compounds with potential therapeutic uses.
