Gypsophila paniculata var. araratica

Gypsophila paniculata var. araratica, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, delicate white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that add texture to the garden landscape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
