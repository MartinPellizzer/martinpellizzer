Genista pubescens

Genista pubescens, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas. Its small yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The shrub is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
