Gironniera hirta

Gironniera hirta, commonly known as rough gironniera, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
