Gnaphalium purpureum subsp. pensylvanicum

Gnaphalium purpureum subsp.

pensylvanicum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania everlasting, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by pale purple bracts. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its fibrous roots and silvery-gray foliage make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
