Grusonia tunicata

Grusonia tunicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Grusonia tunicata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
