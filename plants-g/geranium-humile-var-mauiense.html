Geranium humile var. mauiense

Geranium humile var. mauiense, commonly known as the Maui geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal and dry garden conditions.
