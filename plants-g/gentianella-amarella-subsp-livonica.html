Gentianella amarella subsp. livonica

Gentianella amarella subsp.

livonica, commonly known as the Livonian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or heathland habitats. It is a perennial herb with a short stem and lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
