Galactia earlei subsp. earlei

Galactia earlei subsp.

earlei, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers.

The plant produces small, spherical pods that contain seeds coated in a sticky, milky substance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
