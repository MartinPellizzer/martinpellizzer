Gentiana sclerophylla

Gentiana sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters.

Gentiana sclerophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine flora displays.
