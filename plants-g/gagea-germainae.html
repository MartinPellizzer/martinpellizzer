Gagea germainae

Gagea germainae, commonly known as the Germain's spring beauty, is a small, perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
