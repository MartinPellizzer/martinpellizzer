Grammitis paucinervata

Grammitis paucinervata, commonly known as the slender grammitis, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a simple, unbranched rhizome and produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its intricate structure and specialized habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies focused on epiphytic flora.
