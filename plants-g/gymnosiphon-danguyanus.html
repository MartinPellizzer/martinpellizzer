Gymnosiphon danguyanus

Gymnosiphon danguyanus, commonly known as the Danguyanus ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a ghostly, translucent form. The plant lacks true leaves and instead relies on its specialized roots for nutrient absorption, making it a mycotrophic species. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with a delicate, almost ethereal appearance.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered and is rarely seen in the wild.
