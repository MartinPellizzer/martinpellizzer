Gagea lutea f. maxima

Gagea lutea f. maxima, commonly known as the giant white bulbous plant, is a variant of the Gagea lutea species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a single, sturdy stem that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its bulbs are fleshy and store nutrients, allowing the plant to survive in dry conditions.

It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and woodland settings.
