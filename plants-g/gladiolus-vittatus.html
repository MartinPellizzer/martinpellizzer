Gladiolus vittatus

Gladiolus vittatus, commonly known as the zigzag glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and vibrant, colorful flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces clusters of flowers at the top of its stems. Its botanical name, *vittatus*, refers to the striped or patterned appearance of its leaves.

The zigzag glory is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
