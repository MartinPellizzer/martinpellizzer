Gracilea

Gracilea, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. Native to tropical regions, Gracilea thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in dense undergrowth. The plant's roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Due to its ornamental value, Gracilea is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
