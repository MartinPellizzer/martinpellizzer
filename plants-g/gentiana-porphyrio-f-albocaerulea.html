Gentiana porphyrio f. albocaerulea

Gentiana porphyrio f. albocaerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered gentian, is a striking botanical variant of the gentian genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
