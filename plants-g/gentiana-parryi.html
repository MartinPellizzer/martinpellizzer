Gentiana parryi

Gentiana parryi, commonly known as Parry's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Gentiana parryi is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
