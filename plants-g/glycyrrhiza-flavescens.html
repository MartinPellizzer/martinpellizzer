Glycyrrhiza flavescens

Glycyrrhiza flavescens, commonly known as the yellow licorice, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and stout, woody root system. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its roots contain glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza flavescens is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
