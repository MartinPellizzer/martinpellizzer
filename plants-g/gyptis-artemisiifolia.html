Gyptis artemisiifolia

Gyptis artemisiifolia, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near waterways or in wet meadows.

Due to its aromatic compounds, it has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a pesticide.
