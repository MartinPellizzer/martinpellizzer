Glycine velutina

Glycine velutina, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Glycine velutina also has medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional remedies.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment.
