Gentianella amarella subsp. occidentalis

Gentianella amarella subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
