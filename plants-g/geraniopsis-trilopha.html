Geraniopsis trilopha

Geraniopsis trilopha, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
