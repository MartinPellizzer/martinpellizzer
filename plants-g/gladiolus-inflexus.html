Gladiolus inflexus

Gladiolus inflexus, commonly known as the bent-sword gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, sword-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with strap-like leaves that are dark green and narrow. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
