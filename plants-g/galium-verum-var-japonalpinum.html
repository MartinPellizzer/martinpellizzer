Galium verum var. japonalpinum

Galium verum var. japonalpinum, commonly known as the Japanese or Japanese alpine wood sorrel, is a variant of the common wood sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan and Korea. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Rubiaceae family.
