Gouldia axillaris var. hawaiiensis

Gouldia axillaris var. hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian gouldia, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
