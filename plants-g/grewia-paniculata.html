Grewia paniculata

Grewia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
