Gentiana campestris f. hartmaniana

Gentiana campestris f. hartmaniana, commonly known as the Hartman's gentian, is a botanical variant of the common gentian.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
