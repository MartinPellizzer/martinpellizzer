Galanthus plicatus var. byzantinus

Galanthus plicatus var. byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of delicate, white, drooping flowers with green markings, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulb and are adapted to thrive in woodland or shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.

The species is also noted for its resilience and ability to naturalize over time in suitable conditions.
