Grindelia inuloides var. hirtella

Grindelia inuloides var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
