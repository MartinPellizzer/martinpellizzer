Garcinia kingii

Garcinia kingii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are rich in xanthones and other bioactive compounds. Garcinia kingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
