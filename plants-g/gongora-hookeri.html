Gongora hookeri

Gongora hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's gongora, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flower spikes that can reach several inches in length. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Gongora hookeri is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
