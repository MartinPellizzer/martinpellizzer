Gymnema crenatum

Gymnema crenatum, commonly known as the "sugar bush," is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for containing gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and modern research.
