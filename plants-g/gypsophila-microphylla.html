Gypsophila microphylla

Gypsophila microphylla, commonly known as baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila microphylla is also known for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
