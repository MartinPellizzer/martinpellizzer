Guajava cuprea

Guajava cuprea, commonly known as the red guava, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, smooth skin and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit develops from a five-lobed flower and is often harvested when fully ripe for its best flavor.
