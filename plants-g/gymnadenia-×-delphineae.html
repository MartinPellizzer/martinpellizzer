Gymnadenia × delphineae

Gymnadenia × delphineae, commonly known as the hybrid marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as meadows, bogs, and marshes. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive two-lipped structure, typical of many orchids.

Gymnadenia × delphineae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
