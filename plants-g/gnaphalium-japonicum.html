Gnaphalium japonicum

Gnaphalium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese toothache plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and often grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including treating respiratory and digestive ailments.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to its native habitat.
