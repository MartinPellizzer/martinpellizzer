Gompholobium gompholobioides

Gompholobium gompholobioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, which contribute to soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
