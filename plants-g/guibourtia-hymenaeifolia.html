Guibourtia hymenaeifolia

Guibourtia hymenaeifolia, commonly known as the African teak or yellowheart, is a tropical tree native to West and Central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in panicles. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a fragrant resin, which contributes to its ecological and economic significance.
