Gastrolobium henfreyi

Gastrolobium henfreyi, commonly known as the Henfrey's wattle, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers, which appear in late summer, attract a variety of pollinators.

Gastrolobium henfreyi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
