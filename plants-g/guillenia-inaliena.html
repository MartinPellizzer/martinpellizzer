Guillenia inaliena

Guillenia inaliena, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in a distinctive, spiky structure. Guillenia inaliena thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
