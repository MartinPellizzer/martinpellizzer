Globimetula assiana

Globimetula assiana, commonly known as the African globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive globe-shaped flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of basal, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments.

Globimetula assiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
