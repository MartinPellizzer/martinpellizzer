Gentianella ericoides

Gentianella ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in boreal and alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually oval and slightly hairy, with a dense, compact growth habit.

Gentianella ericoides is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
