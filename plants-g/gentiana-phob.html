Gentiana phob

Gentiana phob, commonly known as the ghost gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, downy layer, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
