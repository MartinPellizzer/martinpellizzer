Garcinia papilla

Garcinia papilla, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a bright red rind and a white, segmented flesh. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife.

Garcinia papilla is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
