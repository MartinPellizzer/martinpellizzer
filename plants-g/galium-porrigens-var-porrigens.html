Galium porrigens var. porrigens

Galium porrigens var. porrigens, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, with a distinctive waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
