Grazielanthus

Grazielanthus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-like flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy leaves. Grazielanthus thrives in arid and rocky environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
