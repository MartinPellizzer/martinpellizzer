Genista leptoclada

Genista leptoclada, commonly known as the slender broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista leptoclada is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
