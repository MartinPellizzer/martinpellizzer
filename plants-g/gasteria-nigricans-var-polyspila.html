Gasteria nigricans var. polyspila

Gasteria nigricans var. polyspila, commonly known as the Black-leaf Gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of dark, glossy, waxy leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems, typically in shades of yellow or orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

This variety is prized for its compact size and striking foliage, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
