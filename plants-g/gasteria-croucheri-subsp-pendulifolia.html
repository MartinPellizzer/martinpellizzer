Gasteria croucheri subsp. pendulifolia

Gasteria croucheri subsp.

pendulifolia, commonly known as the hanging gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.

The plant produces long, pendant inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
