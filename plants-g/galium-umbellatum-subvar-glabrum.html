Galium umbellatum subvar. glabrum

Galium umbellatum subvar. glabrum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
