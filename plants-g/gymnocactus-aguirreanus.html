Gymnocactus aguirreanus

Gymnocactus aguirreanus, commonly known as the Aguirre's naked cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically covered with white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
