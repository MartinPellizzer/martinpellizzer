Gleichenia blepharolepis

Gleichenia blepharolepis, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and slightly curled at the tips. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

Gleichenia blepharolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
