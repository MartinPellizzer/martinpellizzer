Grevillea pieroniae

Grevillea pieroniae, commonly known as the pink mist grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that produce clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Grevillea pieroniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
