Gastrosiphon fenestratus

Gastrosiphon fenestratus, commonly known as the window siphon, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are often described as resembling a coiled spring. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread across wetland surfaces, forming dense mats.

The botanical features of Gastrosiphon fenestratus make it an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
