Gaillardia candelaria var. candelaria

Gaillardia candelaria var. candelaria, commonly known as the Mexican firewheel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright red and yellow ray florets that radiate from a central orange disk, giving it a striking, sun-like appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
