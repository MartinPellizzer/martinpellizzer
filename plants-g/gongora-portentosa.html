Gongora portentosa

Gongora portentosa, commonly known as the showy ginger orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble ginger blossoms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of a cluster of showy, fragrant flowers.

Gongora portentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
