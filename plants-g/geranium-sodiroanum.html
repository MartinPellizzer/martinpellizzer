Geranium sodiroanum

Geranium sodiroanum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium sodiroanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
