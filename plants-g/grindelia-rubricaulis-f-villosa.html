Grindelia rubricaulis f. villosa

Grindelia rubricaulis f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a variant of the species native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
