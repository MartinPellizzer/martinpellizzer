Gnaphalium xanthinum

Gnaphalium xanthinum, commonly known as the yellow cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
