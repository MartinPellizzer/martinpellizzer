Grevillea pungens

Grevillea pungens, commonly known as the spiked grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

Grevillea pungens is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
