Geissomeria fulgida

Geissomeria fulgida, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet.

Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
