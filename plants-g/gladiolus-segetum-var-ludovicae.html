Gladiolus segetum var. ludovicae

Gladiolus segetum var. ludovicae, commonly known as the Ludovician gladiolus, is a variety native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late summer and produces numerous spike-like inflorescences. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable for horticultural study.
