Gongora quinquenervis var. atropurpurea

Gongora quinquenervis var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the Purple-veined Gongora, is a striking orchid species native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black petals and sepals, which contrast sharply with its white or pale yellow lip. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and dramatic floral display.
