Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. cerebriformis

Gymnocalycium gibbosum var. cerebriformis, commonly known as the brain-shaped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive, brain-like appearance due to its segmented, rounded ribs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
