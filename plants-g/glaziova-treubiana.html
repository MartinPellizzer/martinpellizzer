Glaziova treubiana

Glaziova treubiana, commonly known as the White Vanda, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy texture, adapted to its humid tropical environment.

Glaziova treubiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
