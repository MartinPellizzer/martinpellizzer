Geocaryum cynapioides

Geocaryum cynapioides, commonly known as the Indian cress or wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is native to parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
