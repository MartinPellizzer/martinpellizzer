Galium minutiflorum

Galium minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flower bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

Galium minutiflorum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
