Gymnocalycium hybopleurum var. ferocior

Gymnocalycium hybopleurum var. ferocior, commonly known as the fierce-tipped moon cactus, is a cactus species native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a thick, spiny exterior that protects it from herbivores. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its greenish-gray body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
