Gastrolobium congestum

Gastrolobium congestum, commonly known as the clustered wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
