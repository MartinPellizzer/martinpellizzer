Grewia mollis var. morifolia

Grewia mollis var. morifolia, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
