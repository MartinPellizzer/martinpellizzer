Grewia oligoneura

Grewia oligoneura, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a thin skin and a sweet, juicy pulp.

Grewia oligoneura is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
