Gerbera rupicola

Gerbera rupicola, commonly known as the rock gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces showy blooms throughout the summer months.

Gerbera rupicola is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
