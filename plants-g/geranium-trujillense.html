Geranium trujillense

Geranium trujillense, commonly known as the Trujillo geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Dominican Republic and Haiti.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically features palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Geranium trujillense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
