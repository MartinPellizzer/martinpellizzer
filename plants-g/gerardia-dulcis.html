Gerardia dulcis

Gerardia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Gerardia dulcis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses reported in local herbal practices.
