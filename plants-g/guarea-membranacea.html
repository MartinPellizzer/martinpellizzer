Guarea membranacea

Guarea membranacea, commonly known as the "Balsa tree," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is one of the lightest woods in the world. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces fruit that is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds.

The species is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its unique physical properties.
