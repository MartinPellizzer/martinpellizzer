Gardenia merianae

Gardenia merianae, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white or yellowish flowers. The plant is evergreen and typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.
