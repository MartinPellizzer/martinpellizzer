Gilia inconspicua subsp. interior

Gilia inconspicua subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
