Gymnadenia austriaca

Gymnadenia austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian grasspinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale pink to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in a raceme.

Gymnadenia austriaca is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
