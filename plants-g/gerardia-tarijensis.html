Gerardia tarijensis

Gerardia tarijensis, commonly known as the Tarijensis gerardia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
