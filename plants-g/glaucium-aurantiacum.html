Glaucium aurantiacum

Glaucium aurantiacum, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
