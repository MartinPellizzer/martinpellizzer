Gentiana verna var. obtusifolia

Gentiana verna var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and have a rounded or obtuse tip, distinguishing it from other varieties of gentians.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
