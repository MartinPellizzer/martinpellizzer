Gynerium dioicum

Gynerium dioicum, commonly known as common arrowhead, is a herbaceous wetland plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

Gynerium dioicum thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
