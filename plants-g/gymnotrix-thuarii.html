Gymnotrix thuarii

Gymnotrix thuarii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces showy, lantern-shaped blooms that emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Gymnotrix thuarii is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Its unique flowering pattern and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
