Gymnosporia addat

Gymnosporia addat, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are a defense mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along roadsides.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
