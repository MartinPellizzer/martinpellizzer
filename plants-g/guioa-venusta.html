Guioa venusta

Guioa venusta, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are fragrant and appear in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pulp.

Guioa venusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
