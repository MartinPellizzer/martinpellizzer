Gomesa planifolia var. crocea

Gomesa planifolia var. crocea, commonly known as the golden gomesa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor settings.
