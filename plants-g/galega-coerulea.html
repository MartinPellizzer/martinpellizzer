Galega coerulea

Galega coerulea, commonly known as blue goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
