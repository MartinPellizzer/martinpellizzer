Grindelia volkensii var. angustifolia

Grindelia volkensii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gumweed, is a perennial plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, while the stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
