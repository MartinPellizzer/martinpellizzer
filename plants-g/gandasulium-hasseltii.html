Gandasulium hasseltii

Gandasulium hasseltii, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
