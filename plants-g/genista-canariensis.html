Genista canariensis

Genista canariensis, commonly known as the Canary broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with slender, often thorny stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Genista canariensis is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.
