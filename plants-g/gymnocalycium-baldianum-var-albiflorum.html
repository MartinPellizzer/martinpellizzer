Gymnocalycium baldianum var. albiflorum

Gymnocalycium baldianum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that store water. The plant produces showy, white flowers during the warm season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry environments.

Gymnocalycium baldianum var. albiflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
