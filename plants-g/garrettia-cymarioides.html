Garrettia cymarioides

Garrettia cymarioides, commonly known as the slender leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant thrives in shallow water and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and poor soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Garrettia cymarioides plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and supporting biodiversity.
