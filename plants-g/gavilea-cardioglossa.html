Gavilea cardioglossa

Gavilea cardioglossa, commonly known as the heart-leaved gavilea, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to purple. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Gavilea cardioglossa thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
