Genista jacquiniana

Genista jacquiniana, commonly known as the Jacquin's broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its slender, thorny stems and evergreen foliage make it a distinctive ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
