Glycosmis sapindoides var. pilosa

Glycosmis sapindoides var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy honeycomb tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble tiny honeycombs. Its fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, fleshy pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
