Gentianusa purpurea

Gentianusa purpurea, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of down.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
