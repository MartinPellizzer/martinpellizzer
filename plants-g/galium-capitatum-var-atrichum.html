Galium capitatum var. atrichum

Galium capitatum var. atrichum, commonly known as the blackstem bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often hairy stems that can become dark purple or black with age. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate, and often have a slightly toothed margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
