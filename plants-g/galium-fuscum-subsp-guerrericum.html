Galium fuscum subsp. guerrericum

Galium fuscum subsp.

guerrericum, commonly known as the dark wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in umbels. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue on the underside.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its preference for cool, humid climates.
