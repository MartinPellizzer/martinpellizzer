Genista leptophylla

Genista leptophylla, commonly known as the slender leaf broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
