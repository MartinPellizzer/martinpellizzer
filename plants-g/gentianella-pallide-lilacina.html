Gentianella pallide-lilacina

Gentianella pallide-lilacina, commonly known as the pale-lilac gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often growing in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianella pallide-lilacina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
