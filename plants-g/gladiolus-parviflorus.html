Gladiolus parviflorus

Gladiolus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is appreciated by gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
