Gaudinia biloba

Gaudinia biloba, commonly known as the pink gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Gaudinia biloba is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
