Gilia humilis subsp. glabella

Gilia humilis subsp.

glabella, commonly known as the hairy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and leaves, typically reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
