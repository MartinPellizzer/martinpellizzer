Gymnopteris nicotianifolia

Gymnopteris nicotianifolia, commonly known as the tobacco-leaf fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lobed fronds that resemble the leaves of the tobacco plant, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Gymnopteris nicotianifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
