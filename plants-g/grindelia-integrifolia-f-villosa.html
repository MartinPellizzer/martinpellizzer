Grindelia integrifolia f. villosa

Grindelia integrifolia f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy desert gumweed, is a variant of the desert gumweed species native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
