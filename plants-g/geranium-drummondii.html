Geranium drummondii

Geranium drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with a compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
