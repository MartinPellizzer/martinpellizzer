Grastidium virotii

Grastidium virotii, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique flowering species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery hair. The plant produces striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Grastidium virotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
