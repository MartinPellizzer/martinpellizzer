Gerardianella scopiformis

Gerardianella scopiformis, commonly known as the creeping gerardia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with slender, branching stems that root at the nodes. Its small, narrow leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color.

Gerardianella scopiformis is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
