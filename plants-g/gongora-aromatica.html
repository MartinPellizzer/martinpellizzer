Gongora aromatica

Gongora aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Gongora aromatica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its subtle, aromatic fragrance.
