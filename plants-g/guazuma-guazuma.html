Guazuma guazuma

Guazuma guazuma, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
