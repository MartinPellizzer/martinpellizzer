Guzmania blassii

Guzmania blassii, commonly known as the Blassii torch plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in tropical rainforests. The plant features a striking rosette of broad, colorful leaves that can range from green to red or orange. Its inflorescence is a tall, branched structure that bears small, showy flowers, typically in shades of red or yellow.

Guzmania blassii is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
