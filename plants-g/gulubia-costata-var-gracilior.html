Gulubia costata var. gracilior

Gulubia costata var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender leafed gulubia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
