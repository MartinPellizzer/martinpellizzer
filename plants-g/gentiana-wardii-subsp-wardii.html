Gentiana wardii subsp. wardii

Gentiana wardii subsp.

wardii, commonly known as the Ward's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
