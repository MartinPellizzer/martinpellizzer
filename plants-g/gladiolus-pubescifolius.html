Gladiolus pubescifolius

Gladiolus pubescifolius, commonly known as the hairy-leaf gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, often colorful flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the diversity of the Gladiolus genus.
