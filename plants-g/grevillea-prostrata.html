Grevillea prostrata

Grevillea prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate grevillea, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking, cylindrical red or orange flower spikes that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Grevillea prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
