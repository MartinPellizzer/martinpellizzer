Galium × raisiae

Galium × raisiae, commonly known as the hybrid wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Galium verum (common wood sorrel) and Galium aparine (sticklegrass), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. It features small, yellow flowers clustered in umbels, and its stems are often covered with stiff, hooked hairs.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
