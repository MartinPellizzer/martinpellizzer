Gentiana atkinsonii

Gentiana atkinsonii, commonly known as Atkinson's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Gentiana atkinsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
