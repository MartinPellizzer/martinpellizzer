Gastrochilus siamensis

Gastrochilus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Gastrochilus siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
