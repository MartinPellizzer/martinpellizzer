Glandulicereus thurberi subsp. littoralis

Glandulicereus thurberi subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family. It is native to coastal regions of Mexico and the southern United States.

The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure adapted to arid environments. It produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for coastal and xeric habitats.
