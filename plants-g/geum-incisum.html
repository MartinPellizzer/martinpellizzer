Geum incisum

Geum incisum, commonly known as the cutleaf avens, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed or cut, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Geum incisum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
