Gentianella crassulifolia

Gentianella crassulifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Gentianella crassulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
