Gisekia pharnaceoides var. pharnaceoides

Gisekia pharnaceoides var. pharnaceoides, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in tropical and subtropical gardens.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
