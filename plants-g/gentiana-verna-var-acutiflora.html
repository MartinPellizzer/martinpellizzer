Gentiana verna var. acutiflora

Gentiana verna var. acutiflora, commonly known as the acute-flower gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
