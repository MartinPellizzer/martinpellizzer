Gleditsia sinensis var. pendula

Gleditsia sinensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. The tree produces small, green pods that turn brown and dry as they mature. Its compound leaves are feathery and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
