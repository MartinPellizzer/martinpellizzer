Gifola canescens

Gifola canescens, commonly known as the hairy goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Gifola canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
