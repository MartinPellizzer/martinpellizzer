Goodenia racemosa

Goodenia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
