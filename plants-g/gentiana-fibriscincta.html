Gentiana fibriscincta

Gentiana fibriscincta, commonly known as the fibrous gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and its stems are often covered in fine, fibrous hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to its high-altitude habitat.
