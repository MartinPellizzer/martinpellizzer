Grevillea obliquistigma subsp. obliquistigma

Grevillea obliquistigma subsp.

obliquistigma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are usually green with a slight reddish tinge.

The plant produces striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments. Grevillea obliquistigma subsp.

obliquistigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
