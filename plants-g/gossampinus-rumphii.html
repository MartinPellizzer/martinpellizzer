Gossampinus rumphii

Gossampinus rumphii, commonly known as the Java tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow-orange pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
