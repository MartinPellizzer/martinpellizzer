Gymnosporia crataegiflora

Gymnosporia crataegiflora, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces woody, red-brown fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, adapted to arid conditions.

Gymnosporia crataegiflora is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
