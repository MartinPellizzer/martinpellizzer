Gymnostyles nasturtiifolia

Gymnostyles nasturtiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
