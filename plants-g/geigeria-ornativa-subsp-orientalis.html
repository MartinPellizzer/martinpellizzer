Geigeria ornativa subsp. orientalis

Geigeria ornativa subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern ornamental geigeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically features showy yellow or orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is often aromatic, adding both visual and sensory appeal to gardens.
