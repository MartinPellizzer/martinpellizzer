Grevillea synapheae

Grevillea synapheae, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea synapheae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
