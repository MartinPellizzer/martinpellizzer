Gentianella patula

Gentianella patula, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Gentianella patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
