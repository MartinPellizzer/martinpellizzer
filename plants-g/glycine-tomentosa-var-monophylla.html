Glycine tomentosa var. monophylla

Glycine tomentosa var. monophylla, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its high-protein seeds, which are used in animal feed and human food in some cultures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
