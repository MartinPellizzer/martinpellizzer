Gossypium barbadense var. microcarpum

Gossypium barbadense var. microcarpum, commonly known as the Egyptian cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to the Nile Valley.

It is characterized by its fine, long staple fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant produces small, round bolls that contain the fluffy cotton fibers. It thrives in warm, arid climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is historically significant for its role in the development of high-quality textile production.
