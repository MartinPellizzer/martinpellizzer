Gymnema barbatum

Gymnema barbatum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which contains various bioactive compounds. Gymnema barbatum is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.

Its leaves are used to treat diabetes due to their hypoglycemic effects.
