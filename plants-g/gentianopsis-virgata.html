Gentianopsis virgata

Gentianopsis virgata, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of gentians. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
