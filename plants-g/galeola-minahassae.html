Galeola minahassae

Galeola minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa galea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that often feature a blend of colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
