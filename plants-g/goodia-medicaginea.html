Goodia medicaginea

Goodia medicaginea, commonly known as the medicinal goodia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodiaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Goodia medicaginea is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional Australian herbal medicine.
