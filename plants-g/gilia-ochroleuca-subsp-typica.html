Gilia ochroleuca subsp. typica

Gilia ochroleuca subsp.

typica, commonly known as the yellow gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
