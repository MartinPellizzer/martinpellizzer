Gaura gracilis var. typica

Gaura gracilis var. typica, commonly known as the common gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems with showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its delicate appearance and extended blooming period make it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly landscapes.
