Gratiola violacea var. saginoides

Gratiola violacea var. saginoides, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
