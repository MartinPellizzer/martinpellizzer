Geum sordidum

Geum sordidum, commonly known as the stinking beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Geum sordidum is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
