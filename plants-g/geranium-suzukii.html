Geranium suzukii

Geranium suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium suzukii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
