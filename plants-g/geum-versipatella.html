Geum versipatella

Geum versipatella, commonly known as the purple avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Geum versipatella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
