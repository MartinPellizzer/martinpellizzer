Gigantochloa kurzii

Gigantochloa kurzii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a smooth texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its rapid growth and structural strength, it is also used in construction and as a source of sustainable materials.
