Geissois rubifolia

Geissois rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed geissois, is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange-red foliage, which contrasts with its smaller, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

Geissois rubifolia is relatively low maintenance but requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
