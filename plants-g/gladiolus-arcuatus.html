Gladiolus arcuatus

Gladiolus arcuatus, commonly known as the curving gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus arcuatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
