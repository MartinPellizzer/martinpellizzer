Gonostemon pulvinatus

Gonostemon pulvinatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, waxy leaves that are typically green or purple in color. The plant produces showy spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.
