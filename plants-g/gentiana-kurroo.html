Gentiana kurroo

Gentiana kurroo, commonly known as the Kashmiri gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and upright.

Gentiana kurroo is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
