Guatteria juruensis

Guatteria juruensis, commonly known as the Juruensis arrowleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
