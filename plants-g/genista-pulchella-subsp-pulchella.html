Genista pulchella subsp. pulchella

Genista pulchella subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a pale, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
