Gerascanthus tinifolius

Gerascanthus tinifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive five-petaled structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
