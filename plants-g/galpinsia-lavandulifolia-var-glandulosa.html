Galpinsia lavandulifolia var. glandulosa

Galpinsia lavandulifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular lavenderleaf.

This plant is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
