Gomphrena iresinoides var. riedeliana

Gomphrena iresinoides var. riedeliana, commonly known as the red gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often mistaken for dried flowers due to their long-lasting appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The botanical features of this plant include its bract-like leaves and its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
