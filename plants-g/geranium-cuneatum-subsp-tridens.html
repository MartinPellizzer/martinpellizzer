Geranium cuneatum subsp. tridens

Geranium cuneatum subsp.

tridens, commonly known as the narrowleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
