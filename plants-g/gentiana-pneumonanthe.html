Gentiana pneumonanthe

Gentiana pneumonanthe, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are sturdy and often branching.

Gentiana pneumonanthe is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
