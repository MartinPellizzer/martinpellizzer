Geranium caeruleum var. subushkanense

Geranium caeruleum var. subushkanense, commonly known as the subushkanense geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
