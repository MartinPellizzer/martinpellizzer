Grewia speciosa

Grewia speciosa, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Grewia speciosa is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms in gardens and landscapes.
