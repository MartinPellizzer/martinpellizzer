Gymnocalycium sanluisense

Gymnocalycium sanluisense, commonly known as the San Luis cactus, is a species native to northern Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical stem that is often ribbed. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Gymnocalycium sanluisense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in dry gardens and collections.
