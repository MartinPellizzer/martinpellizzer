Gentianella weigendii

Gentianella weigendii, commonly known as the Weigend's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Gentianella weigendii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
