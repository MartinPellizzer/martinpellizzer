Gnaphalium anomalum

Gnaphalium anomalum, commonly known as the silverleaf weed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia and New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming growth habit, often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in water conservation.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
