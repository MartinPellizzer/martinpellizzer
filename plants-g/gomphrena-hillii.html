Gomphrena hillii

Gomphrena hillii, commonly known as the purple gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, lance-shaped leaves.

Gomphrena hillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
