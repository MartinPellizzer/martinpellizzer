Gymnadenia conopsea var. angustifolia

Gymnadenia conopsea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its habitat specificity.
