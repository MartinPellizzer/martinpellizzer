Grevillea decipiens

Grevillea decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and it is well adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Grevillea decipiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
