Gentiana × media

Gentiana × media, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana × media is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
