Graphorkis meleagris

Graphorkis meleagris, commonly known as the spotted rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a central disc of darker florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
