Gentiana ornata var. veitchii

Gentiana ornata var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is known for its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
