Glycine formosana

Glycine formosana, commonly known as the Formosan soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil. Glycine formosana typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
