Gymnelaea vaccinioides

Gymnelaea vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like blueberries and heathers. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from white to pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are similar in flavor to blueberries.

Gymnelaea vaccinioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
