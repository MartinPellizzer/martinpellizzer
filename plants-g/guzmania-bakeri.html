Guzmania bakeri

Guzmania bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range in color from red to orange. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports various microorganisms and small invertebrates. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can exhibit a range of hues depending on environmental conditions.

Guzmania bakeri is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
