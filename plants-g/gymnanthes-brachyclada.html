Gymnanthes brachyclada

Gymnanthes brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gymnanthes, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
