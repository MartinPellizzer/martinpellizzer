Gossypium hirsutum var. ekmanianum

Gossypium hirsutum var. ekmanianum, commonly known as the Ekmanian cotton, is a variant of upland cotton native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft fibers and compact growth habit, making it suitable for certain textile applications. The plant typically produces small, fluffy bolls that contain the valuable cotton fibers. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with a serrated margin.
