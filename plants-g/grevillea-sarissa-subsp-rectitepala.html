Grevillea sarissa subsp. rectitepala

Grevillea sarissa subsp.

rectitepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Grevillea, is a shrub native to western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
