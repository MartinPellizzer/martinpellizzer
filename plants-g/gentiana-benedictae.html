Gentiana benedictae

Gentiana benedictae, commonly known as Benedict's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana benedictae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and ecological benefits.
