Gilbertiodendron taiense

Gilbertiodendron taiense, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
