Guajava firma

Guajava firma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered together.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, making it a popular ingredient in jams, desserts, and beverages.
