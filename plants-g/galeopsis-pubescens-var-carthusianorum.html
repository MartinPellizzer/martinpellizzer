Galeopsis pubescens var. carthusianorum

Galeopsis pubescens var. carthusianorum, commonly known as Carthusian monk's hood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall hairy appearance.
