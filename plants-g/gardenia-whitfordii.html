Gardenia whitfordii

Gardenia whitfordii, commonly known as the Kauai gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is evergreen and typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are highly prized for their ornamental value and are often used in lei-making.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
