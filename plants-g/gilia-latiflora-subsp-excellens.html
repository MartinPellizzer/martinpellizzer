Gilia latiflora subsp. excellens

Gilia latiflora subsp.

excellens, commonly known as the broadleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
