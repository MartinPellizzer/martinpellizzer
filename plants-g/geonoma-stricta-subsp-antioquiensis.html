Geonoma stricta subsp. antioquiensis

Geonoma stricta subsp.

antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian sword fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia. It is characterized by its slender, upright rhizome and the production of narrow, lance-shaped fronds.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as montane forests and cloud forests. Its fronds are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
