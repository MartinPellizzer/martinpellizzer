Gymnocalycium hossei subsp. ferrarii

Gymnocalycium hossei subsp.

ferrarii, commonly known as the Ferrari's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems and produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months.

The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic differences from the main species.
