Geranium lineatum

Geranium lineatum, commonly known as the zigzag geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium lineatum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both ornamental and ecological gardening.
