Glochisandra acuminata

Glochisandra acuminata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Glochisandra acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
