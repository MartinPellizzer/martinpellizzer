Geranium marginatum

Geranium marginatum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that often trail or spread along the ground, making it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a serrated edge and a distinctive margin that is often flushed with color.

Geranium marginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
