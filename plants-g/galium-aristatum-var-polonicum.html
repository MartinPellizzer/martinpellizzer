Galium aristatum var. polonicum

Galium aristatum var. polonicum, commonly known as the Polish bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
