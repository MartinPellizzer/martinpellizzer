Gleichenia rigida

Gleichenia rigida, commonly known as rigid spleenwort, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is a terrestrial, non-vascular plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive morphology, featuring a dense, moss-like thallus with a central axis and lateral branches. Its leaves are closely arranged and often have a slightly serrated or toothed margin.

Gleichenia rigida is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its role as an indicator species for forest health.
