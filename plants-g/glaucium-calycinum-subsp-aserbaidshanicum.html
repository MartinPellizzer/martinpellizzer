Glaucium calycinum subsp. aserbaidshanicum

Glaucium calycinum subsp.

aserbaidshanicum, commonly known as Armenian gloryflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
