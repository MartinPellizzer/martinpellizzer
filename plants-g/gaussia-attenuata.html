Gaussia attenuata

Gaussia attenuata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

Gaussia attenuata blooms in the spring and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
