Gelseminum garrocha

Gelseminum garrocha, commonly known as the yellow moon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and is often found growing along riverbanks and in humid forests. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes many plants with milky sap and cardiac glycosides.
