Gymnosporia divaricata

Gymnosporia divaricata, commonly known as the African thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Gymnosporia divaricata plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
