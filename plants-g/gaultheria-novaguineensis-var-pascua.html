Gaultheria novaguineensis var. pascua

Gaultheria novaguineensis var. pascua, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in aromatic compounds and have a sharp, minty flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and regions with similar climates.
