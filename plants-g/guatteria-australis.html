Guatteria australis

Guatteria australis, commonly known as the Brazilian huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange in color.
