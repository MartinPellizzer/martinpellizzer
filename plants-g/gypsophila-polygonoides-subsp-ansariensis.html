Gypsophila polygonoides subsp. ansariensis

Gypsophila polygonoides subsp.

ansariensis, commonly known as the Persian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
