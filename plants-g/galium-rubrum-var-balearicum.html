Galium rubrum var. balearicum

Galium rubrum var. balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

Galium rubrum var. balearicum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
