Glossoloma bicolor

Glossoloma bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, contrasting coloration on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but showy, often featuring a combination of white, yellow, and green hues.

Glossoloma bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
