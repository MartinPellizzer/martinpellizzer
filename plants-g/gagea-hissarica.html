Gagea hissarica

Gagea hissarica, commonly known as the Caucasian spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It produces delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters. Its bulbous structure allows it to store nutrients and water, aiding survival in its harsh environment.

Gagea hissarica is often found in alpine meadows and forested slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
