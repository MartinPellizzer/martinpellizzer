Geum viscosum

Geum viscosum, commonly known as sticky cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

Geum viscosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
