Genista minor

Genista minor, commonly known as lesser broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and produces yellow or orange flowers in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista minor is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
