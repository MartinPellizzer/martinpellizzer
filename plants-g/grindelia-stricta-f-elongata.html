Grindelia stricta f. elongata

Grindelia stricta f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bitterroot, is a variant of the bitterroot plant native to the western United States.

It is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae, characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical form of G. stricta.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
