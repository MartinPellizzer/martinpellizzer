Geraniospermum ribifolium

Geraniospermum ribifolium, commonly known as the redleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geraniospermum ribifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
