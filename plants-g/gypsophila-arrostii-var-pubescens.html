Gypsophila arrostii var. pubescens

Gypsophila arrostii var. pubescens, commonly known as the silvery flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
