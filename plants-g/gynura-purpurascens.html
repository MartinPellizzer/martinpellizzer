Gynura purpurascens

Gynura purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple-leafed Cudweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which contrasts with its green stems. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Gynura purpurascens thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
