Gentiana longicollis

Gentiana longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and sturdy.

Gentiana longicollis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
