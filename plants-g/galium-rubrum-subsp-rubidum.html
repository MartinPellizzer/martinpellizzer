Galium rubrum subsp. rubidum

Galium rubrum subsp.

rubidum, commonly known as red-stemmed bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants.

The plant features slender, reddish stems that are often hairy, and its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in umbels. The leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
