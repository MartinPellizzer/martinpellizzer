Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. tomentosum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in compact heads, and the leaves are linear with a hairy texture.

It is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
