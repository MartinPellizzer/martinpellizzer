Geigeria natalensis

Geigeria natalensis, commonly known as Natal fevervetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Geigeria natalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
