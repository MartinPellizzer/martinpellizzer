Gomphostemma strobilinum var. elatius

Gomphostemma strobilinum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall greenbark.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive greenish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
