Galium watsonii

Galium watsonii, commonly known as Watson's bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, ascending stems covered in fine hairs and produces tiny, bead-like fruits called drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants.
