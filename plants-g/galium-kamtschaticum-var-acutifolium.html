Galium kamtschaticum var. acutifolium

Galium kamtschaticum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the acute-leaved bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of eastern Russia and northern China.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a pointed tip, giving the plant its specific epithet "acutifolium.". Small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
