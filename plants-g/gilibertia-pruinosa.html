Gilibertia pruinosa

Gilibertia pruinosa, commonly known as the white-flowered grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Gilibertia pruinosa is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
