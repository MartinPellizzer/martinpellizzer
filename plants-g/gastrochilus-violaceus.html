Gastrochilus violaceus

Gastrochilus violaceus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Gastrochilus violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
