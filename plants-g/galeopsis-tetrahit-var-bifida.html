Galeopsis tetrahit var. bifida

Galeopsis tetrahit var. bifida, commonly known as the bifid hedge-nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are typically pale blue or purple in color.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
