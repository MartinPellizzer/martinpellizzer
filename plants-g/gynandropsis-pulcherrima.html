Gynandropsis pulcherrima

Gynandropsis pulcherrima, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is erect and branched.

Gynandropsis pulcherrima thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
