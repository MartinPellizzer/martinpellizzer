Gilia crebrifolia

Gilia crebrifolia, commonly known as the common gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gilia crebrifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
