Gossypium barbadense var. peruvianum

Gossypium barbadense var. peruvianum, commonly known as Peruvian cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the cotton fibers, which are harvested for textile production. It thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes due to its unique botanical features.
