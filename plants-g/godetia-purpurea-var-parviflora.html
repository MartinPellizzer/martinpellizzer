Godetia purpurea var. parviflora

Godetia purpurea var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf purple godetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
