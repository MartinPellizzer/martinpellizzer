Gagea vvedenskyi

Gagea vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian spring beauty, is a small, perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Gagea vvedenskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
