Gomphrena hirtula

Gomphrena hirtula, commonly known as the hairy gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, rounded heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena hirtula is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
