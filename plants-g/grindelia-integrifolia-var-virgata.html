Grindelia integrifolia var. virgata

Grindelia integrifolia var. virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant.
