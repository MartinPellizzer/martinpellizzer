Gentianodes cachemirica

Gentianodes cachemirica, commonly known as the Kashmir gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold mountain climates. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
