Gomphrena perennis f. ramosissima

Gomphrena perennis f. ramosissima, commonly known as the red globe amaranth, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces distinctive, spherical flower heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

Its botanical features include palmate leaves and a fibrous root system.
