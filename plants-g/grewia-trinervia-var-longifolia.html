Grewia trinervia var. longifolia

Grewia trinervia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often hairy and have three prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
