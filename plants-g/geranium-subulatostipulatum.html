Geranium subulatostipulatum

Geranium subulatostipulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
