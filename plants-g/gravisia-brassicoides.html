Gravisia brassicoides

Gravisia brassicoides, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Gravisia brassicoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
