Grevillea juniperina subsp. sulphurea

Grevillea juniperina subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to orange in color.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
