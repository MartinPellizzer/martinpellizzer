Gnaphalium oxyphyllum var. semilanatum

Gnaphalium oxyphyllum var. semilanatum, commonly known as the slender silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It produces a large number of tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution.

The species is noted for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
