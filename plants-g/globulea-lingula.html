Globulea lingula

Globulea lingula, commonly known as the slender globular vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thin, twining stems and small, clustered flowers that resemble tiny globes, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Globulea lingula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique floral structure and growth habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
