Grosourdya fasciculata

Grosourdya fasciculata, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Grosourdya fasciculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
