Glyceria acutiflora subsp. japonica

Glyceria acutiflora subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
