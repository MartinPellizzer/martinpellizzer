Garcinia calycina

Garcinia calycina, commonly known as the mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and segmented fruit, which contains a bright red or purple aril surrounding the seeds. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and fragrant flowers. Its fruit is often consumed for its sweet, tangy flavor and is also used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia calycina thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
