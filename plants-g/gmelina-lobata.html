Gmelina lobata

Gmelina lobata, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Gmelina lobata is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
