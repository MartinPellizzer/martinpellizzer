Galium schultesii

Galium schultesii, commonly known as Schultes' bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the United States, particularly found in the western regions.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry berries that contain several seeds, typical of the Rubiaceae family.

Galium schultesii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
