Gothofreda villosa

Gothofreda villosa, commonly known as the hairy gothofreda, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
