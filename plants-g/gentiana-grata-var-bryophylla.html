Gentiana grata var. bryophylla

Gentiana grata var. bryophylla, commonly known as the mossy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of moss, giving it its common name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
