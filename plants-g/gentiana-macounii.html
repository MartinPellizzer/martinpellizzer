Gentiana macounii

Gentiana macounii, commonly known as Macoun's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana macounii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
