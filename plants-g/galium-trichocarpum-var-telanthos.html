Galium trichocarpum var. telanthos

Galium trichocarpum var. telanthos, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various insects and birds.
