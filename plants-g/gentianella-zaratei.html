Gentianella zaratei

Gentianella zaratei, commonly known as the Zarate's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five petals that are often blue or purple in color.

Gentianella zaratei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral characteristics.
