Genista tenuispina

Genista tenuispina, commonly known as narrow-leaved broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, spiny stems and narrow, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a coastal plant.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
