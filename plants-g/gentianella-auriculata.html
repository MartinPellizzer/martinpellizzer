Gentianella auriculata

Gentianella auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base.

Gentianella auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
