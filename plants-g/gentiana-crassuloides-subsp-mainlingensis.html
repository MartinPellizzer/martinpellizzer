Gentiana crassuloides subsp. mainlingensis

Gentiana crassuloides subsp.

mainlingensis, commonly known as the Mainling gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
