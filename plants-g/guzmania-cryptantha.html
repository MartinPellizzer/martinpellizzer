Guzmania cryptantha

Guzmania cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha bromeliad, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color depending on light exposure.

Guzmania cryptantha is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
