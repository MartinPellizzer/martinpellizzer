Gymnosporia gracilis subsp. usambarensis

Gymnosporia gracilis subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara slender thornveld, is a shrub native to the Usambara Mountains in Tanzania. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by legume-like pods containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
