Glochidion arboreum var. pauciflorum

Glochidion arboreum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered glabrous wood, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are small, fleshy drupes that contain a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
