Gymnocarpos rotundifolius

Gymnocarpos rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved burr bush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and produces small, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Gymnocarpos rotundifolius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
