Galium ceratopodum

Galium ceratopodum, commonly known as the horned chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, opposite leaves that are often lobed or toothed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
