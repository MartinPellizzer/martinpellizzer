Gelseminum cuspidatum

Gelseminum cuspidatum, commonly known as the yellow moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open at night. The plant has compound leaves with ovate leaflets and produces small, round berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
