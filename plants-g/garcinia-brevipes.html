Garcinia brevipes

Garcinia brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. Garcinia brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
