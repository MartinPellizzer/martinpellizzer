Geranium andicola var. longipedicellatum

Geranium andicola var. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the Andean geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
