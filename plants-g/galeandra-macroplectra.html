Galeandra macroplectra

Galeandra macroplectra, commonly known as the large-leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
