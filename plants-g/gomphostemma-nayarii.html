Gomphostemma nayarii

Gomphostemma nayarii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
