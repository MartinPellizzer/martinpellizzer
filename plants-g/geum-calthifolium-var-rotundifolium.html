Geum calthifolium var. rotundifolium

Geum calthifolium var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved avens, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the top of its stems, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
