Gagea rhodiaca

Gagea rhodiaca, commonly known as the Rhodian spring beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often with a blue-green hue.

Gagea rhodiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
