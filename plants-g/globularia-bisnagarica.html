Globularia bisnagarica

Globularia bisnagarica, commonly known as the Spanish globularia, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to its arid environment. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean regions.

Globularia bisnagarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and floral display.
