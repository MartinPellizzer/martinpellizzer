Gnaphalium suffruticosum

Gnaphalium suffruticosum, commonly known as the silkyweed or sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry environments.
