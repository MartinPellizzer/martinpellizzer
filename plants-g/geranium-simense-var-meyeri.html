Geranium simense var. meyeri

Geranium simense var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
