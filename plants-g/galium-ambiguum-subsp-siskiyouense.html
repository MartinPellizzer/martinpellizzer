Galium ambiguum subsp. siskiyouense

Galium ambiguum subsp.

siskiyouense, commonly known as the Siskiyou bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's understory vegetation.
