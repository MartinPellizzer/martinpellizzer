Gentianella foliosa

Gentianella foliosa, commonly known as the leafy gentian, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianella foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
