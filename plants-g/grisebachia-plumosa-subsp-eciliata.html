Grisebachia plumosa subsp. eciliata

Grisebachia plumosa subsp.

eciliata, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a soft, plume-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery-green hue.

The subspecies eciliata is distinguished by its slightly different inflorescence structure compared to other subspecies of Grisebachia plumosa.
