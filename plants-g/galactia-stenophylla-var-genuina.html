Galactia stenophylla var. genuina

Galactia stenophylla var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf galactia, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers, which are followed by cylindrical, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
