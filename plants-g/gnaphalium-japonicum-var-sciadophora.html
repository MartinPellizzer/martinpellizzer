Gnaphalium japonicum var. sciadophora

Gnaphalium japonicum var. sciadophora, commonly known as the Japanese hair grass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a useful plant for erosion control and ground cover.
