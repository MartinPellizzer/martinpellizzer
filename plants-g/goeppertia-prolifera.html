Goeppertia prolifera

Goeppertia prolifera, commonly known as the "Brazillian Star" or "Goeppertia," is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage that often features intricate patterns and shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Goeppertia prolifera is popular among indoor gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
