Gymnocalycium amoenum

Gymnocalycium amoenum, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, and often striking flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a globular or cylindrical shape with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, typical of desert plants. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it produces small, spiny fruits.

Gymnocalycium amoenum is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements.
