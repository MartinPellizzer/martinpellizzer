Gleditsia horrida var. caspica

Gleditsia horrida var. caspica, commonly known as the Caspian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves that are typically 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
