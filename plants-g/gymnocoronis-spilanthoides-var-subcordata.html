Gymnocoronis spilanthoides var. subcordata

Gymnocoronis spilanthoides var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spilanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
