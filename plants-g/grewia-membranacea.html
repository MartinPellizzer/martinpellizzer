Grewia membranacea

Grewia membranacea, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Grewia membranacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
