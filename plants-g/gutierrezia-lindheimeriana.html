Gutierrezia lindheimeriana

Gutierrezia lindheimeriana, commonly known as Lindheimer's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gutierrezia lindheimeriana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
