Galphimia cauca

Galphimia cauca, commonly known as the Cauca flame flower, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Galphimia cauca is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and regular watering.
