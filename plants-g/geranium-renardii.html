Geranium renardii

Geranium renardii, commonly known as the Renard's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium renardii is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
