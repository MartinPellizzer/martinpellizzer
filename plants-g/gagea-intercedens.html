Gagea intercedens

Gagea intercedens, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full shade. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette at the base.

Gagea intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
