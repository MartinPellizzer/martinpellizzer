Gilibertia caloneura

Gilibertia caloneura, commonly known as the purple grass or purple brome, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear purple to violet-colored florets, giving it its common name. It is often found in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in temperate climates.

Gilibertia caloneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and color.
