Gossypium barbadense var. braziliense

Gossypium barbadense var. braziliense, commonly known as Brazilian cotton, is a species of cotton native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and quality. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample sunlight. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, and the cotton bolls mature into fluffy, white fibers.

This variety is often cultivated for both commercial cotton production and ornamental purposes.
