Gnaphalium filaginoides

Gnaphalium filaginoides, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Gnaphalium filaginoides is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
