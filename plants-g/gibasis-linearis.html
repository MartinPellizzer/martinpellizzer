Gibasis linearis

Gibasis linearis, commonly known as the slender gibasis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
