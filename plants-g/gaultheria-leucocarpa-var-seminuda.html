Gaultheria leucocarpa var. seminuda

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. seminuda, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible and have a sharp, peppery flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for traditional herbal use.
