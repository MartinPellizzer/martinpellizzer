Gaultheria auyantepuiensis

Gaultheria auyantepuiensis, commonly known as the Angel's Tears plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tepuis of southeastern Venezuela.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Gaultheria auyantepuiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
