Githago segetum

Githago segetum, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Githago segetum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
