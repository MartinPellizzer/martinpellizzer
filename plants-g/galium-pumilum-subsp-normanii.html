Galium pumilum subsp. normanii

Galium pumilum subsp.

normanii, commonly known as the dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It typically forms dense mats of slender, prostrate stems covered with fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
