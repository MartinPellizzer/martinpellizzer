Goodyera longicolumna

Goodyera longicolumna, commonly known as the long-stemmed lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and its striking, hooded flower that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Goodyera longicolumna is a perennial species that thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in association with other epiphytic and terrestrial orchids.
