Gymnocalycium paraguayense f. fleischerianum

Gymnocalycium paraguayense f. fleischerianum, commonly known as the Fleischer's prickly pear, is a rare and unique cactus species native to Paraguay.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often adorned with white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible and contain sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
