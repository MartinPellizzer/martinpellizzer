Gossypium klotzschianum var. davidsonii

Gossypium klotzschianum var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's cotton, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, fluffy bolls containing fibrous seeds, similar to other cotton species. It thrives in arid to semi-arid climates and requires well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
