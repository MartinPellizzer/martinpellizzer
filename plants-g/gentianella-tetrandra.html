Gentianella tetrandra

Gentianella tetrandra, commonly known as the four-angled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella tetrandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and hardy nature.
