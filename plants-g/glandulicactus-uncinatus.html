Glandulicactus uncinatus

Glandulicactus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked glandular cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, glandular tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert conditions.
