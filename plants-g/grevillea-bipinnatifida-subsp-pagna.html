Grevillea bipinnatifida subsp. pagna

Grevillea bipinnatifida subsp.

pagna, commonly known as the Punga grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and insects.
