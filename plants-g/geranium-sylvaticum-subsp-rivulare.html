Geranium sylvaticum subsp. rivulare

Geranium sylvaticum subsp.

rivulare, commonly known as the water geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a prominent white throat, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in damp conditions.
