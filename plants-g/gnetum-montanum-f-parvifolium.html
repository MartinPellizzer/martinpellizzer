Gnetum montanum f. parvifolium

Gnetum montanum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf-leaved tropical gnetum, is a variant of the genus Gnetum, which is part of the order Gnetales.

It is characterized by its small, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant is a gymnosperm, possessing needle-like leaves and a unique vascular system. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern-like plant exhibits a variety of ecological adaptations, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
