Gingidium petraeum

Gingidium petraeum, commonly known as the rock ginger, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.

Gingidium petraeum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in local herbal practices.
