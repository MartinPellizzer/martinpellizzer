Galactia mexicana

Galactia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid environments and has deep taproots to access water.

Galactia mexicana is also noted for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
