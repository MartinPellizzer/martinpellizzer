Gentiana myrsinites

Gentiana myrsinites, commonly known as the Myrsine gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana myrsinites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
