Grevillea sparsiflora

Grevillea sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Grevillea sparsiflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
