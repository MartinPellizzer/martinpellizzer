Goniophlebium giganteum

Goniophlebium giganteum, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Blechnaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its unique structure includes a thick, segmented rhizome that supports its robust growth.

The fronds are divided into numerous pinnae, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.
