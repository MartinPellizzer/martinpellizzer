Glycosmis perakensis

Glycosmis perakensis, commonly known as the Java gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Glycosmis perakensis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its potential medicinal properties.
