Garcinia acutifolia

Garcinia acutifolia, commonly known as the African mango or wild mango, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a yellow-orange berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Garcinia acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential health benefits.
