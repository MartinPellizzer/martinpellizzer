Gardenia amoena

Gardenia amoena, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and fragrance.

The plant's aromatic properties make it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in perfumery.
