Glyceria arundinacea subsp. grandis

Glyceria arundinacea subsp.

grandis, commonly known as tall glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
