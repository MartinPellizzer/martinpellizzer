Gymnocalycium gaponii

Gymnocalycium gaponii, commonly known as the Gapon's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems and produces yellow to orange flowers during the cooler months. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to survive in arid environments with minimal water. Its fleshy stems store water, and it has a shallow root system to absorb moisture from the soil.

Gymnocalycium gaponii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
