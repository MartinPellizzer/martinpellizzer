Gnaphalium australe

Gnaphalium australe, commonly known as the Australian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
