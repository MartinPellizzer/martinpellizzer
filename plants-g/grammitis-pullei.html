Grammitis pullei

Grammitis pullei, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis pullei is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance.
