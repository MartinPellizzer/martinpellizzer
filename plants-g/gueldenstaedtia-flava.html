Gueldenstaedtia flava

Gueldenstaedtia flava, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Gueldenstaedtia flava is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
