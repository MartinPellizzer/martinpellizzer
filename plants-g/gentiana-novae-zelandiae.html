Gentiana novae-zelandiae

Gentiana novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand gentian, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
