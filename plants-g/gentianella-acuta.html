Gentianella acuta

Gentianella acuta, commonly known as the acute gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Gentianella acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
