Guilandina rotunda

Guilandina rotunda, commonly known as the round legume or the sacred bean, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It produces large, inflated pods that contain numerous small, edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
