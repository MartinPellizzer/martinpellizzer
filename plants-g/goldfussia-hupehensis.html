Goldfussia hupehensis

Goldfussia hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Goldfussia hupehensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
