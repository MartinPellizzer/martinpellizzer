Gymnocalycium amerhauseri

Gymnocalycium amerhauseri, commonly known as the "Amerhauser's moon cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing plant with a short, thick stem and produces vibrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gymnocalycium amerhauseri is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems to survive in dry conditions.
