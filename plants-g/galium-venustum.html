Galium venustum

Galium venustum, commonly known as silverweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Galium venustum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
