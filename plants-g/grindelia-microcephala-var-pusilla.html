Grindelia microcephala var. pusilla

Grindelia microcephala var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf desert gumweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions. Its compact growth habit and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it a common sight in desert environments.

The species plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
