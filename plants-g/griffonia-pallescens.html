Griffonia pallescens

Griffonia pallescens, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Griffonia pallescens is valued for its seeds, which contain high levels of the neurochemical compound 5-HTP, making it useful in dietary supplements.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
