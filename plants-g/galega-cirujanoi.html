Galega cirujanoi

Galega cirujanoi, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its aggressive growth habits.
