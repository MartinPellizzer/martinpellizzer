Geraniospermum astragalifolium

Geraniospermum astragalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geraniosperm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae, which includes plants with showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
