Gundlachia corymbosa var. apiculata

Gundlachia corymbosa var. apiculata, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its drought tolerance and adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
