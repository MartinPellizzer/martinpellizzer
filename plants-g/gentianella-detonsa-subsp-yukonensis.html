Gentianella detonsa subsp. yukonensis

Gentianella detonsa subsp.

yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Yukon region of Canada. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as alpine meadows and riverbanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
