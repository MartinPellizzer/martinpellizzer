Galium insubricum

Galium insubricum, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. Galium insubricum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
