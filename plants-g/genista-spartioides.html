Genista spartioides

Genista spartioides, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and scrubland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
