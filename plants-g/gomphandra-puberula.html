Gomphandra puberula

Gomphandra puberula, commonly known as the hairy gomphandra, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
