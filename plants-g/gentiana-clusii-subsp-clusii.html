Gentiana clusii subsp. clusii

Gentiana clusii subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the Clusius gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and ecological benefits.
