Gypsophila linearifolia

Gypsophila linearifolia, commonly known as the linear leaf baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and floral arrangements. Its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms contribute to its use in both ornamental and horticultural settings.

Gypsophila linearifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
