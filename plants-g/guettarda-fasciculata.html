Guettarda fasciculata

Guettarda fasciculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its dense, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its flowers are fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The tree is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
