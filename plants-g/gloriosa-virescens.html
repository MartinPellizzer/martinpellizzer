Gloriosa virescens

Gloriosa virescens, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid orange to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a tuberous root system and produces long, slender stems with heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their intense color and fragrance.

Gloriosa virescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
