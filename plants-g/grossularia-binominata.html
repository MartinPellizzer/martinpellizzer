Grossularia binominata

Grossularia binominata, commonly known as the Himalayan currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
