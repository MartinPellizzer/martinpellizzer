Gilibertia oppositifolia

Gilibertia oppositifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals.

The plant is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
