Glandulicactus

Glandulicactus, commonly known as the hairy cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs that help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warm season, which are often followed by small, red fruits. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Glandulicactus is well adapted to desert environments, making it a resilient and fascinating example of succulent flora.
