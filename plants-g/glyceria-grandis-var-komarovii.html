Glyceria grandis var. komarovii

Glyceria grandis var. komarovii, commonly known as the Komarovii glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers, which are usually greenish to brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
