Gilbertiodendron limba

Gilbertiodendron limba, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad canopy. Its bark is often cracked and fissured, and it produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its shea nuts, which are used to make shea butter, an important commodity in local economies.
