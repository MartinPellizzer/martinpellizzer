Geranium grevilleanum

Geranium grevilleanum, commonly known as the Grevillea geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant features upright, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium grevilleanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
