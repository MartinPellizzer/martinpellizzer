Galinsoga aristulata

Galinsoga aristulata, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, and it produces numerous small, oval-shaped fruits. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Galinsoga aristulata is also recognized as a weed in agricultural settings due to its invasive tendencies.
