Gaultheria gracilescens

Gaultheria gracilescens, commonly known as the slender waxleaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. These fruits are edible and have a spicy, aromatic flavor due to the presence of methyl salicylate.

Gaultheria gracilescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist woodland environments.
