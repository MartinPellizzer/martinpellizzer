Geissomeria schottiana f. floribunda

Geissomeria schottiana f. floribunda, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, showy clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The subspecies floribunda is distinguished by its more abundant and showy floral display compared to the typical form.
