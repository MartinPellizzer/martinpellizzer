Gaillardia aestivalis var. winkleri

Gaillardia aestivalis var. winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its vibrant, orange and red composite flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
