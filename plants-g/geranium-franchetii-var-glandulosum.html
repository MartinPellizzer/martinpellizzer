Geranium franchetii var. glandulosum

Geranium franchetii var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular geranium, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pentagonal flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive glandular texture, which gives it its varietal name. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to red flowers.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in partial shade to full sun.
