Gesneria cuneifolia subsp. disjuncta

Gesneria cuneifolia subsp.

disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Gesneria, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces striking tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
