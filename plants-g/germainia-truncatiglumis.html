Germainia truncatiglumis

Germainia truncatiglumis, commonly known as the roughleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Germainia truncatiglumis is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained to slightly moist soils.
