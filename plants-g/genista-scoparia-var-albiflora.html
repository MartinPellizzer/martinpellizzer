Genista scoparia var. albiflora

Genista scoparia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered broom, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The variety albiflora distinguishes itself with its white rather than the typical yellow blooms. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
