Gladiolus communis var. parviflorus

Gladiolus communis var. parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

This variety is well-suited for container growing and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
