Gochnatia paniculata var. polycephala

Gochnatia paniculata var. polycephala, commonly known as the clustered gochnatia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite or alternate, and its inflorescences that form dense, branched clusters. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and numerous stamens, making them visually striking. This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in the family Asteraceae.
