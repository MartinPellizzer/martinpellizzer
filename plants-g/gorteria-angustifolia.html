Gorteria angustifolia

Gorteria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gorteria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters.

Gorteria angustifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
