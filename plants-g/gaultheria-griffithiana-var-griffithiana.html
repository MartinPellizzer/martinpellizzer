Gaultheria griffithiana var. griffithiana

Gaultheria griffithiana var. griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
