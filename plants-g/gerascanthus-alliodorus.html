Gerascanthus alliodorus

Gerascanthus alliodorus, commonly known as the sweet-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant, spicy scent. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its foliage is typically glossy and lobed, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
