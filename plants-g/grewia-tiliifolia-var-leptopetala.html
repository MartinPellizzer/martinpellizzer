Grewia tiliifolia var. leptopetala

Grewia tiliifolia var. leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-leaved gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, round berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.
