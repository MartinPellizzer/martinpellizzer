Gilia achilleifolia subsp. abrotanifolia

Gilia achilleifolia subsp.

abrotanifolia, commonly known as the blue gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
