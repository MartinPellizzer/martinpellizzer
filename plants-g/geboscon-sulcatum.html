Geboscon sulcatum

Geboscon sulcatum, commonly known as the wrinkled fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, deeply lobed leaves that resemble a series of overlapping scales.

Native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. The plant's rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread slowly across the forest floor. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which may serve as a natural defense against pests.

While not widely cultivated, Geboscon sulcatum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
