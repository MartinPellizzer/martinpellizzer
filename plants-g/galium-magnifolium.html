Galium magnifolium

Galium magnifolium, commonly known as the showy bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels, and the plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Galium magnifolium is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
