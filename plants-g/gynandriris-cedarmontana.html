Gynandriris cedarmontana

Gynandriris cedarmontana, commonly known as the cedarmontan orchid, is a rare and specialized plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow-green hue. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in the understory of dense forests. Its reproductive system is notable for its complex pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for fertilization.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
