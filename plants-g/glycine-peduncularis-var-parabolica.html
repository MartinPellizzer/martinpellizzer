Glycine peduncularis var. parabolica

Glycine peduncularis var. parabolica, commonly known as the parabolic cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique parabolic growth pattern, which gives it its varietal name. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Its botanical structure supports its role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility.
