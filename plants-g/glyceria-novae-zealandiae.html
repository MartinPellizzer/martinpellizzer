Glyceria novae-zealandiae

Glyceria novae-zealandiae, commonly known as New Zealand glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often grow in wet or waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, grass-like flowers. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in marshy areas or along streambanks.

Glyceria novae-zealandiae plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
