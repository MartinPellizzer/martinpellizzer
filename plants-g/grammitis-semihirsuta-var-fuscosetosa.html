Grammitis semihirsuta var. fuscosetosa

Grammitis semihirsuta var. fuscosetosa, commonly known as the hairy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered with fine, short hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
