Gentiana scabrida subsp. horaimontana

Gentiana scabrida subsp.

horaimontana, commonly known as the Horai Mountain gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on rocky or mountainous slopes. Its leaves are usually serrated and covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
