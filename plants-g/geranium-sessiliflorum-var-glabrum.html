Geranium sessiliflorum var. glabrum

Geranium sessiliflorum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features upright stems covered in velvety, glandular hairs, and its leaves are typically rounded with a smooth texture. The flowers are showy, usually pink or red, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
