Glochidion velutinum var. nubigenum

Glochidion velutinum var. nubigenum, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insect species.
