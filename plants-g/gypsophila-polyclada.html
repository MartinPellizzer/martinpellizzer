Gypsophila polyclada

Gypsophila polyclada, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, delicate pink or white flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Gypsophila polyclada is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
