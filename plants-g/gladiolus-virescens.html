Gladiolus virescens

Gladiolus virescens, commonly known as the green gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, yellow, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in floral arrangements and landscaping.
