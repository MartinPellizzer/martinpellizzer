Gerascanthus scabra

Gerascanthus scabra, commonly known as the rough gerascanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gerascanthus scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and bloom.
