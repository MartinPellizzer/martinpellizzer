Grewia mollis

Grewia mollis, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, oval leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Grewia mollis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
