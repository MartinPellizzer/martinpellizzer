Gentiana caulescens

Gentiana caulescens, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with five lobes and are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. The plant has a slender, upright stem that is often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Gentiana caulescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
