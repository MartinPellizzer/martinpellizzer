Guatteria hypoglauca

Guatteria hypoglauca, commonly known as the glossy guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
