Gymnartocarpus venenosa

Gymnartocarpus venenosa, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and has a dense, spreading canopy. Its bark and leaves contain various toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.

Despite its toxicity, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
