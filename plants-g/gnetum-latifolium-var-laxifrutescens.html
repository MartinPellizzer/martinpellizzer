Gnetum latifolium var. laxifrutescens

Gnetum latifolium var. laxifrutescens, commonly known as the broadleaf gnetum, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, characterized by its unique combination of gymnosperm and angiosperm traits.

It is a tropical evergreen tree with opposite, simple, and often broad leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, and its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed aril, which is a distinctive feature of the Gnetophyta group. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some use in traditional medicine.
