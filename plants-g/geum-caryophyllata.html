Geum caryophyllata

Geum caryophyllata, commonly known as Indian blanket or pot marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with bright orange to red petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are alternate and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
