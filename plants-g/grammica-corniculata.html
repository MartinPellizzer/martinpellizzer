Grammica corniculata

Grammica corniculata, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Grammica corniculata thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
