Gynura nitida

Gynura nitida, commonly known as the silver senna, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white underside on its leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Gynura nitida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
