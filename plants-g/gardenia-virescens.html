Gardenia virescens

Gardenia virescens, commonly known as the emerald gardenia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, are known for their strong, sweet scent and are often used in perfumery.

The fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds, which can be propagated for new plants.
