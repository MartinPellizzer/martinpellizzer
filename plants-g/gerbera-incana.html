Gerbera incana

Gerbera incana, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and prefers well-drained soil. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in summer and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
