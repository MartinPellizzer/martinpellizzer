Gentianella septentrionalis

Gentianella septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella septentrionalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
