Gagea foliosa subvar. glauca

Gagea foliosa subvar. glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.
