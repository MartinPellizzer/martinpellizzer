Genista monosperma

Genista monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
