Gomphocarpus aureus

Gomphocarpus aureus, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces spherical seed pods that turn red as they mature, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists interested in its unique morphology and ecological role.
