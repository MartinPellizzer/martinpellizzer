Glycyrrhiza hirsuta var. echinata

Glycyrrhiza hirsuta var. echinata, commonly known as the spiny licorice, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The roots of this species are used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
