Glechoma annua

Glechoma annua, commonly known as ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant spreads rapidly through stolons, forming dense mats that can cover large areas. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lawns, gardens, and along woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
