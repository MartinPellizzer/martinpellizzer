Gentiana amarella var. pyramidalis

Gentiana amarella var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
