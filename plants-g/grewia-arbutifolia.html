Grewia arbutifolia

Grewia arbutifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
