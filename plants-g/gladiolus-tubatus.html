Gladiolus tubatus

Gladiolus tubatus, commonly known as the tuberous gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences that display a variety of colors. The plant produces tubers as its primary storage organs, which are used for propagation. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant appearance.
