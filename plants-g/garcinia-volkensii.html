Garcinia volkensii

Garcinia volkensii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a white, edible pulp, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia volkensii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential applications in pharmaceutical research.
