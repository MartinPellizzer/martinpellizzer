Glossorhyncha pulchra

Glossorhyncha pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful horned orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive horn-like structure on the lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Glossorhyncha pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
