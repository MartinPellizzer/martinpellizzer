Gymnocalycium fischeri subsp. suyuquense

Gymnocalycium fischeri subsp.

suyuquense, commonly known as the Suyuquense prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent nature and ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils make it an important component of its ecosystem.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
