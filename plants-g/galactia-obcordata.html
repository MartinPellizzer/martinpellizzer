Galactia obcordata

Galactia obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling or climbing structure depending on environmental conditions.

Galactia obcordata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
