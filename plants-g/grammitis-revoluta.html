Grammitis revoluta

Grammitis revoluta, commonly known as the revolute grammitis, is a small, evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or revolute, giving it its common name. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a velvety texture.

Grammitis revoluta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
