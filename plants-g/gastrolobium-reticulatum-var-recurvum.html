Gastrolobium reticulatum var. recurvum

Gastrolobium reticulatum var. recurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
