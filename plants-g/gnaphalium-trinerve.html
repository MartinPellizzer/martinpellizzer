Gnaphalium trinerve

Gnaphalium trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant features narrow, often hairy leaves with three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

Gnaphalium trinerve is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
