Gymnocalycium horridispinum

Gymnocalycium horridispinum, commonly known as the spiny moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are covered with sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, which are typically short-lived. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Gymnocalycium horridispinum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
