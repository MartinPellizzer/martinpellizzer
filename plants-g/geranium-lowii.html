Geranium lowii

Geranium lowii, commonly known as the Sierra geranium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are typically ovate to lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Geranium lowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
