Gratiola pilosa

Gratiola pilosa, commonly known as the hairy yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a creeping rhizome and produces small, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, woodland soils. It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.

Gratiola pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
