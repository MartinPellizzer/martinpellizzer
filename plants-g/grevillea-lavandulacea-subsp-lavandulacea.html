Grevillea lavandulacea subsp. lavandulacea

Grevillea lavandulacea subsp.

lavandulacea, commonly known as the blue grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are predominantly red or pink, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in dry climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
