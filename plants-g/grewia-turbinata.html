Grewia turbinata

Grewia turbinata, commonly known as the turban bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Grewia turbinata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
