Gentiana luridoviolacea

Gentiana luridoviolacea, commonly known as the purple-lavender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies with their vibrant color and nectar.
