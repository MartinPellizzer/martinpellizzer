Gymnocalycium riojense var. guthianum

Gymnocalycium riojense var. guthianum, commonly known as the Rioja Moon Cactus, is a rare cactus species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is a succulent, adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
