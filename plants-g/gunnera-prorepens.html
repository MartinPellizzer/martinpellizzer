Gunnera prorepens

Gunnera prorepens, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Gunneraceae and is known for its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

While it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
