Grumilea cabrae

Grumilea cabrae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red, fleshy berries resembling small tomatoes. These berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in culinary applications.

Grumilea cabrae is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
