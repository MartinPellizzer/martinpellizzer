Gentiana porphyrio

Gentiana porphyrio, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana porphyrio is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
