Grindelia pulchella var. normalis

Grindelia pulchella var. normalis, commonly known as the common brittle bladder, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-green flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky resin. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Grindelia pulchella var. normalis is often used in traditional medicine and is also a source of natural resins.
