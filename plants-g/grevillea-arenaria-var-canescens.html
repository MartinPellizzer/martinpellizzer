Grevillea arenaria var. canescens

Grevillea arenaria var. canescens, commonly known as the silver-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Grevillea genus. It is well adapted to arid and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant for coastal and dry landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
