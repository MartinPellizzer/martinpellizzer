Geranium emirnense

Geranium emirnense, commonly known as the Emirnense geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among gardeners in temperate climates.
