Gynura colorata

Gynura colorata, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its striking red veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are often used in local cuisines for their tangy flavor, and the plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
