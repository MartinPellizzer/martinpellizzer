Gladiolus carneus var. hibbertii

Gladiolus carneus var. hibbertii, commonly known as the Hibbert's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and bloom during the summer months. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for garden beds or containers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and floral arrangements.
