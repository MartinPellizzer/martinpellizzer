Galoglychia martinicensis

Galoglychia martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. The plant produces showy, pale blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Galoglychia martinicensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some Caribbean communities.
