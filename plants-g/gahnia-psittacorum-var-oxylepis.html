Gahnia psittacorum var. oxylepis

Gahnia psittacorum var. oxylepis, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a variant of the Gahnia genus native to Australia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
