Gymnosporia championii

Gymnosporia championii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-colored aril. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Gymnosporia championii is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
