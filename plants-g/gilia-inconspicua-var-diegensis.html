Gilia inconspicua var. diegensis

Gilia inconspicua var. diegensis, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its flowers are often clustered in loose racemes, and the leaves are linear to lanceolate in shape.

This species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
