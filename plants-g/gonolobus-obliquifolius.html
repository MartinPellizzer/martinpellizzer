Gonolobus obliquifolius

Gonolobus obliquifolius, commonly known as the zigzag senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender stems that often exhibit a zigzag or twisted growth pattern. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are clustered in racemes and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.
