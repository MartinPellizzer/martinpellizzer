Glaziophyton

Glaziophyton, commonly known as the "frozen fern," is an ancient plant that lived during the Silurian period.

It is characterized by its complex, branching structure and leaf-like appendages, which suggest a transition from aquatic to terrestrial life. Its fossils are often found in coal deposits, indicating its role in early plant evolution. The plant's morphology shows adaptations for water retention and photosynthesis in a changing environment.

Glaziophyton is considered a key genus in understanding the early diversification of vascular plants.
