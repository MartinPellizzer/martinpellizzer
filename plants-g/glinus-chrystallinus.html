Glinus chrystallinus

Glinus chrystallinus, commonly known as the crystalline glinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, translucent, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
