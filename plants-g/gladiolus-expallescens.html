Gladiolus expallescens

Gladiolus expallescens, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
