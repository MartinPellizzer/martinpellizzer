Gymnocalycium alboareolatum

Gymnocalycium alboareolatum, commonly known as the white areolate cactus, is a species native to northern Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, areolate tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the stem tips. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to cactus collections.
