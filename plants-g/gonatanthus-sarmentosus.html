Gonatanthus sarmentosus

Gonatanthus sarmentosus, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red and green veined petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are highly ornamental, featuring intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
