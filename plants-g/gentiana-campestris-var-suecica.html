Gentiana campestris var. suecica

Gentiana campestris var. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish gentian, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white down.
