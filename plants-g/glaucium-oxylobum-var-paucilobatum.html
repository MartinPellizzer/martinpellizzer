Glaucium oxylobum var. paucilobatum

Glaucium oxylobum var. paucilobatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glory flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
