Gentianella cupiformis

Gentianella cupiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive cup-like shape, which gives it its common name.

Gentianella cupiformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
