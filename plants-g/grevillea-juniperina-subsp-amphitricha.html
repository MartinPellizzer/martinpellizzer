Grevillea juniperina subsp. amphitricha

Grevillea juniperina subsp.

amphitricha, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to the southern and western regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and its striking, red or orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to coastal and sandy environments. Its floral structure consists of a central column of stamens surrounded by a single pistil, typical of the Grevillea genus.

This subspecies is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand salt spray, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
