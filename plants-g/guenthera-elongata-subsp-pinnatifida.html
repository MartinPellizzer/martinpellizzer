Guenthera elongata subsp. pinnatifida

Guenthera elongata subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guenthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
