Geranium phaeum f. pallidum

Geranium phaeum f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale geranium, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the British Isles.

It features small, pale pink to almost white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are rounded, velvety, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden conditions.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often used in woodland gardens or as a ground cover.
