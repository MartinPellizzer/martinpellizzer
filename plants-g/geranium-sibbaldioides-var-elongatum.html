Geranium sibbaldioides var. elongatum

Geranium sibbaldioides var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed geranium, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.
