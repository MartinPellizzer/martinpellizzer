Garcinia loureiroi

Garcinia loureiroi, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, acidic pulp, which is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
