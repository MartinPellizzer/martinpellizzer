Gentiana verna subsp. nevadensis

Gentiana verna subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest clearings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic value in alpine landscapes.
