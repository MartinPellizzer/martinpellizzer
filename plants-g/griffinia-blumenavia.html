Griffinia blumenavia

Griffinia blumenavia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.

Griffinia blumenavia is also valued for its resilience to drought and mild frost conditions.
