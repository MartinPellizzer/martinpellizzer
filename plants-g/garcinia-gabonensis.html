Garcinia gabonensis

Garcinia gabonensis, commonly known as African yellow wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia gabonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its wood is prized for its durability and aesthetic qualities in furniture-making.
