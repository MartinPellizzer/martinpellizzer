Gaya mollendoensis

Gaya mollendoensis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Gaya mollendoensis is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
