Gerbera bonatiana

Gerbera bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati's gerbera, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features bright, colorful petals that range from red to yellow, with a prominent central disk. Gerbera bonatiana thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its use in horticulture, though it is less commonly cultivated than other gerbera species.
