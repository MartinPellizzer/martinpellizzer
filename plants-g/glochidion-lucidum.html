Glochidion lucidum

Glochidion lucidum, commonly known as the shining glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
