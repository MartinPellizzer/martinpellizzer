Gnetum gnemon

Gnetum gnemon, commonly known as the kempas or tropical gnetum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gnetophyta class, which includes a small group of gymnosperm-like plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed capsule, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Gnetum gnemon is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
