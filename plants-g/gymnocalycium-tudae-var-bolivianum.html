Gymnocalycium tudae var. bolivianum

Gymnocalycium tudae var. bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a thick, ribbed stem that stores water. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
