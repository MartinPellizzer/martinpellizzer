Gamochaeta affinis

Gamochaeta affinis, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The small yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
