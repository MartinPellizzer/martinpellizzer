Glycine reticulata

Glycine reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated soybean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Glycine reticulata is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread cultivation.
