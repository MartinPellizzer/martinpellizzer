Gilia aggregata var. typica

Gilia aggregata var. typica, commonly known as the clustered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
