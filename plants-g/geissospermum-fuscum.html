Geissospermum fuscum

Geissospermum fuscum, commonly known as the dark geissosperm, is a woody climbing vine native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in axillary inflorescences. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, woody fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
