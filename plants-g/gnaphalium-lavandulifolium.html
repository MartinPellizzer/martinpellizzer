Gnaphalium lavandulifolium

Gnaphalium lavandulifolium, commonly known as silverleaf ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its common name reflects the lavender-like appearance of its foliage.
