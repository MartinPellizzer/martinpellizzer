Grammitis leucolepis

Grammitis leucolepis, commonly known as the silver-stemmed huperbole, is a fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on moist, shaded rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name.
