Gymnocalycium bruchii var. papschii

Gymnocalycium bruchii var. papschii, commonly known as the Papschii moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny exterior. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
