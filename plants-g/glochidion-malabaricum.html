Glochidion malabaricum

Glochidion malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar glabrous leaf.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India, characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by globose, yellowish fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
