Gunnaria

Gunnaria, commonly known as the ferns of the genus Gunnaria, are a group of small, terrestrial ferns belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

They are characterized by their delicate, often intricately divided fronds and are typically found in moist, shaded environments. These plants are native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including South America, Africa, and Australia. Gunnaria species usually have a short, creeping rhizome and produce sporangia on the underside of their fronds.

Their botanical features make them valuable subjects for studying fern morphology and ecological adaptations.
