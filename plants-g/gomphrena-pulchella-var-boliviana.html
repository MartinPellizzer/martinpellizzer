Gomphrena pulchella var. boliviana

Gomphrena pulchella var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian plume flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
