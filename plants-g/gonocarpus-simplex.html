Gonocarpus simplex

Gonocarpus simplex, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in saline or brackish environments, making it well adapted to coastal and estuarine habitats. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by water.

Gonocarpus simplex plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
