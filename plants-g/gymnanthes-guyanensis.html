Gymnanthes guyanensis

Gymnanthes guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana sponge tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, soft, sponge-like fruit. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped capsules that mature into yellow or orange, fleshy structures. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
