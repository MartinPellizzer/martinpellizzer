Garrya gracilis

Garrya gracilis, commonly known as silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Garrya gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
