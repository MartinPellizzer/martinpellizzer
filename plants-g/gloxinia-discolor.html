Gloxinia discolor

Gloxinia discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from silver to dark green. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxinia discolor is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and vibrant blooms.
