Gibbaeum perviride

Gibbaeum perviride, commonly known as the green knobbed pincushion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny tubercles that give it a pincushion-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in tight, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Gibbaeum perviride is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique morphology and drought tolerance.
