Gilia linearis var. subulata

Gilia linearis var. subulata, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and spikes of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
