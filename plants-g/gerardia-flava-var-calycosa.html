Gerardia flava var. calycosa

Gerardia flava var. calycosa, commonly known as the yellow gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
