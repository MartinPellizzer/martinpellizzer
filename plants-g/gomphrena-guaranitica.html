Gomphrena guaranitica

Gomphrena guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian plume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gomphrena guaranitica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
