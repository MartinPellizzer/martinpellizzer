Gouldia lanai

Gouldia lanai, commonly known as the Lanai spike rush, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Gouldia lanai thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for native Hawaiian wildlife.
