Glaucium corniculatum var. aurantiacum

Glaucium corniculatum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden flax or yellow hawksbeard, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, hairy seed pods that mature into a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
