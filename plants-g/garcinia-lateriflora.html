Garcinia lateriflora

Garcinia lateriflora, commonly known as the Malayan tamarind or wild tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing acidic, orange-red seeds. Garcinia lateriflora is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
