Gomesa trichodes

Gomesa trichodes, commonly known as the hairy gomesa, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gomesa trichodes is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
