Gleichenia argentea

Gleichenia argentea, commonly known as silver fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are usually bright green, with a silvery underside that reflects light, contributing to its common name.

Gleichenia argentea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with moderate moisture.
