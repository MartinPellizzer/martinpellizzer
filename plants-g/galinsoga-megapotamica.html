Galinsoga megapotamica

Galinsoga megapotamica, commonly known as the common gallinsoga, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, characterized by its serrated leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous seeds in slender, hairy capsules, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
