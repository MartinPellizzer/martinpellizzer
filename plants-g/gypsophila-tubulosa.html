Gypsophila tubulosa

Gypsophila tubulosa, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Gypsophila tubulosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
