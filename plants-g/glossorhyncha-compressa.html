Glossorhyncha compressa

Glossorhyncha compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Glossorhyncha compressa plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
