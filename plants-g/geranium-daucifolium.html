Geranium daucifolium

Geranium daucifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive central spur. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Geranium daucifolium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
