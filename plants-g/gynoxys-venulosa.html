Gynoxys venulosa

Gynoxys venulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, featuring a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gynoxys venulosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its adaptability.

It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
