Galium punctatum

Galium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered with small, glandular dots, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a reddish or purplish tinge. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Galium punctatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
