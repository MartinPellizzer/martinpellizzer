Genista retamoides var. mauritiana

Genista retamoides var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius genista, is a shrub native to the Mascarene Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal and limestone habitats, showing adaptability to poor soils. It produces small yellow flowers in spring, followed by narrow, elongated pods.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
