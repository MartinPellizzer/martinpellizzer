Gaultheria leucocarpa var. papuana

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan white-flowered wintergreen, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the cooler months. The plant produces bright red, waxy berries that are edible and have a sharp, acidic flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
