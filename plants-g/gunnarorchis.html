Gunnarorchis

Gunnarorchis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of visible leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. The plant produces delicate, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the summer months. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Gunnarorchis is considered a critically endangered species.
