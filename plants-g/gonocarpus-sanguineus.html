Gonocarpus sanguineus

Gonocarpus sanguineus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in traditional crafts and construction.
