Grewia biloba var. biloba

Grewia biloba var. biloba, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
