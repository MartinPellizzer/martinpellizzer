Galium propinquum

Galium propinquum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Galium propinquum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
