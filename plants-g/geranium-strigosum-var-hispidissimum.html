Geranium strigosum var. hispidissimum

Geranium strigosum var. hispidissimum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
