Gymnema reticulatum

Gymnema reticulatum, commonly known as the mesh leaf or net-veined gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, reticulate (net-like) leaf veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and capsule fruits that contain seeds. Gymnema reticulatum is often found in moist, tropical forests and is known for its medicinal properties.

It has been used traditionally in Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
