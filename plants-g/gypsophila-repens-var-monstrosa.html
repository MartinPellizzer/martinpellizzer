Gypsophila repens var. monstrosa

Gypsophila repens var. monstrosa, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath" or "Snow-in-Summer," is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, creating a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and easy to cultivate in a variety of garden settings.
