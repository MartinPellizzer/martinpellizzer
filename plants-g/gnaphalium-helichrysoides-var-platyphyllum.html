Gnaphalium helichrysoides var. platyphyllum

Gnaphalium helichrysoides var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically broad and fleshy, arranged in a basal rosette, and are covered with a layer of fine, white pubescence. This plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
