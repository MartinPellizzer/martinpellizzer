Glochidion gardneri var. acuminatum

Glochidion gardneri var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glabrous hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
