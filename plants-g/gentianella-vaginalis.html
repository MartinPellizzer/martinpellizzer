Gentianella vaginalis

Gentianella vaginalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
