Gomphostemma strobilinum var. variegatum

Gomphostemma strobilinum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated starvine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinctive variegation patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage, making it a sought-after plant for gardeners.
