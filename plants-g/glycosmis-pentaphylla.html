Glycosmis pentaphylla

Glycosmis pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed sweet fruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that often have five lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to orange-red when mature. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Glycosmis pentaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
