Gymnogramma calomelanos

Gymnogramma calomelanos, commonly known as the silver-veined huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive silver veins that contrast with its dark green color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, a feature common among ferns in this family.

Gymnogramma calomelanos is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing its adaptability to humid climates.
