Grevillea scortechinii subsp. sarmentosa

Grevillea scortechinii subsp.

sarmentosa, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in spikes, which are typically borne along the stems. It is adapted to sandy soils and salty sea breezes, making it resilient in coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, coastal conditions.
