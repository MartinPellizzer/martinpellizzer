Glechoma mariae

Glechoma mariae, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, spreading rapidly along the ground through stolons. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Glechoma mariae is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks.
