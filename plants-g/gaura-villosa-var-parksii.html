Gaura villosa var. parksii

Gaura villosa var. parksii, commonly known as Indian Blanket or Blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of orange, yellow, and red. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.

Its long blooming period, from late spring to early fall, adds color to the landscape throughout the growing season.
