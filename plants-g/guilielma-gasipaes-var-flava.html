Guilielma gasipaes var. flava

Guilielma gasipaes var. flava, commonly known as the yellow tamarind, is a variant of the tamarind tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, yellowish fruits that contain a sweet, sticky pulp surrounded by a fibrous outer layer. The plant is a deciduous tree with compound leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its botanical features include a thick, ridged bark and a preference for tropical climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
