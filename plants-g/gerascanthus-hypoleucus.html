Gerascanthus hypoleucus

Gerascanthus hypoleucus, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
