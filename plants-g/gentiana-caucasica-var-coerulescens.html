Gentiana caucasica var. coerulescens

Gentiana caucasica var. coerulescens, commonly known as the Caucasian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat woody at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
