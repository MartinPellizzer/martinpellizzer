Gentianella maddenii

Gentianella maddenii, commonly known as the Madden's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of gentians known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in late summer to early fall.

Gentianella maddenii is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves.
