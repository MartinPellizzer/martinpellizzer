Garrya macrophylla

Garrya macrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
