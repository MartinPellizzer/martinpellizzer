Gentiana juniperina

Gentiana juniperina, commonly known as the juniper gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Gentiana juniperina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
