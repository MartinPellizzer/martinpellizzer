Grajalesia

Grajalesia, commonly known as the "Mexican flame tree," is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Grajalesia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to grow successfully.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
