Gymnanthemum andrangovalense

Gymnanthemum andrangovalense, commonly known as the Andes mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Gymnanthemum andrangovalense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
