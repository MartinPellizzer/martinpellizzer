Gaultheria rupestris var. subcorymbosa

Gaultheria rupestris var. subcorymbosa, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a shrub native to the high elevations of the Andes.

It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has evergreen leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
