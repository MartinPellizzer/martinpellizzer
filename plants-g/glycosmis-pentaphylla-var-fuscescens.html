Glycosmis pentaphylla var. fuscescens

Glycosmis pentaphylla var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-leaved sweet orange.

This variant is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, often used in traditional cuisines. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, sharing botanical traits with other citrus relatives.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native regions.
