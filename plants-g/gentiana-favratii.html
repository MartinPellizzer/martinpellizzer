Gentiana favratii

Gentiana favratii, commonly known as the Favrat's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana favratii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
