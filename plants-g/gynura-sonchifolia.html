Gynura sonchifolia

Gynura sonchifolia, commonly known as the silver spoon plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-white underside on its leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Gynura sonchifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
