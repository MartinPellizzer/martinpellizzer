Grevillea bracteosa subsp. howatharra

Grevillea bracteosa subsp.

howatharra, commonly known as the Howatharra grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and striking, red-orange cylindrical flowers that appear in spring.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. Its floral structure is typical of the Grevillea genus, with stamens fused to the petals.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued in native landscaping.
