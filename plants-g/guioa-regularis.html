Guioa regularis

Guioa regularis, commonly known as the white guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, slightly waxy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
