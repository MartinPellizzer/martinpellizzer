Galactia sericea

Galactia sericea, commonly known as silky galactia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, silvery appearance.

Galactia sericea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
