Gymnocalycium sanguiniflorum

Gymnocalycium sanguiniflorum, commonly known as the bloodflower cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Gymnocalycium sanguiniflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and seasonal flowering.
