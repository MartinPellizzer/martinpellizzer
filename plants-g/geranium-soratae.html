Geranium soratae

Geranium soratae, commonly known as the Sierra geranium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is a perennial species that prefers full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

Geranium soratae plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
