Guzmania farciminiformis

Guzmania farciminiformis, commonly known as the pink-veined guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with distinctive pink veins that contrast beautifully with its lush green leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a crown, featuring small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Guzmania farciminiformis is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
