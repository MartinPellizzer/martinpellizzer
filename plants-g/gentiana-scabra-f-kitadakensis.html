Gentiana scabra f. kitadakensis

Gentiana scabra f. kitadakensis, commonly known as the Kitadaken gentian, is a botanical variant of the gentian species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
