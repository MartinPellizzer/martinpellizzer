Guapira opposita

Guapira opposita, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Guapira opposita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
