Gastrolobium parvifolium

Gastrolobium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to the southern regions of Western Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean climate zones.

Gastrolobium parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
