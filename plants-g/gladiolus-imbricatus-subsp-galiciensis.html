Gladiolus imbricatus subsp. galiciensis

Gladiolus imbricatus subsp.

galiciensis, commonly known as the Galician gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and are known for their striking appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
