Gilia pinnatifida var. integrescens

Gilia pinnatifida var. integrescens, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a hairy texture. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, non-woody stems and the presence of fringed petals.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
