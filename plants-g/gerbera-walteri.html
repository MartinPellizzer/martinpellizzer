Gerbera walteri

Gerbera walteri, commonly known as the Walter's gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a central dark area.

Gerbera walteri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
