Geranium tovarii

Geranium tovarii, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium tovarii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
