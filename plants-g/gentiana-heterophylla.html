Gentiana heterophylla

Gentiana heterophylla, commonly known as the variable gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are often variable in shape, contributing to its common name, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and conservation efforts.
