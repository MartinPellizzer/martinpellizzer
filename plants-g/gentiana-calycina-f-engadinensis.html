Gentiana calycina f. engadinensis

Gentiana calycina f. engadinensis, commonly known as the Engadine gentian, is a rare and striking plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine botanical gardens.
