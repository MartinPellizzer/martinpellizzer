Gamocarpha breviscapa

Gamocarpha breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stemmed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite and usually have a rough texture, while its stems are short and sturdy.

Gamocarpha breviscapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
