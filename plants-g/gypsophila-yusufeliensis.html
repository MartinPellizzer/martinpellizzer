Gypsophila yusufeliensis

Gypsophila yusufeliensis, commonly known as the Turkish laceflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with hairy stems that help reduce water loss.

Gypsophila yusufeliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
