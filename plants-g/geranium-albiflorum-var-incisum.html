Geranium albiflorum var. incisum

Geranium albiflorum var. incisum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
