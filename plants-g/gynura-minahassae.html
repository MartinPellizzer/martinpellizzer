Gynura minahassae

Gynura minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa gynura, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive rosette growth habit, featuring large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
