Guyonia cinerascens

Guyonia cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Guyonia cinerascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
