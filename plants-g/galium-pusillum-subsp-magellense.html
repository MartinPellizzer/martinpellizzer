Galium pusillum subsp. magellense

Galium pusillum subsp.

magellense, commonly known as the Magellanic bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped form. Galium pusillum subsp.

magellense thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats.
