Gentianella detonsa subsp. holopetala

Gentianella detonsa subsp.

holopetala, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters.

The plant thrives in rocky and mountainous environments, often growing at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
