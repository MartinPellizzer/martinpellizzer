Gelseminum viminale

Gelseminum viminale, commonly known as the creeping yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender stems and trailing growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Gelseminum viminale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
