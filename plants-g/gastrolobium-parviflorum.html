Gastrolobium parviflorum

Gastrolobium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Gastrolobium parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
