Gibbaeum esterhuyseniae

Gibbaeum esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia's rock agave, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Gibbaeum esterhuyseniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
