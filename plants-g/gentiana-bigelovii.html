Gentiana bigelovii

Gentiana bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentiana bigelovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
