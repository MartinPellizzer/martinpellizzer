Gentiana montana f. novae-zelandiae

Gentiana montana f. novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the alpine regions of New Zealand.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or boggy habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
