Grevillea ramosissima

Grevillea ramosissima, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.

Grevillea ramosissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
