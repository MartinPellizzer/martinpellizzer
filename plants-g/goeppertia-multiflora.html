It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

Goeppertia multiflora is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in humid, warm environments.
