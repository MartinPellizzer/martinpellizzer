Gladiolus dalmaticus

Gladiolus dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The Dalmatian gladiolus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is valued for its striking floral display.
