Geranium rugosum

Geranium rugosum, commonly known as the rough geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geranium rugosum is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
