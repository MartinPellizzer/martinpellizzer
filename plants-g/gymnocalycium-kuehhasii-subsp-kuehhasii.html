Gymnocalycium kuehhasii subsp. kuehhasii

Gymnocalycium kuehhasii subsp.

kuehhasii, commonly known as the Kuehne's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
