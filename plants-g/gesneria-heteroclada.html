Gesneria heteroclada

Gesneria heteroclada, commonly known as the shield fern, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gesneria heteroclada is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
