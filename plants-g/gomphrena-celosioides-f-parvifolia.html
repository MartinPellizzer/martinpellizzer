Gomphrena celosioides f. parvifolia

Gomphrena celosioides f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered gomphrena, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to reseed itself makes it a hardy and long-lasting addition to the garden.
