Gentiana marginata subsp. nasirii

Gentiana marginata subsp.

nasirii, commonly known as the Nasiri's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other gentian species. It thrives in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, often in areas with cool, moist climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant blooms.
