Geum japonicum var. iyoanum

Geum japonicum var. iyoanum, commonly known as Japanese avens, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features palmate, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
