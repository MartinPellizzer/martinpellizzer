Genista oxycedrina

Genista oxycedrina, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista oxycedrina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
