Gnaphalium krasense

Gnaphalium krasense, commonly known as the Krasen's cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter scent.

Gnaphalium krasense is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
