Geranium harveyi

Geranium harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
