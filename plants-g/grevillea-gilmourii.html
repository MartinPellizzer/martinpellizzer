Grevillea gilmourii

Grevillea gilmourii, commonly known as the Gilmour's waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, crimson or red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea gilmourii is also valued for its ability to attract native birds and its resilience to drought conditions.
