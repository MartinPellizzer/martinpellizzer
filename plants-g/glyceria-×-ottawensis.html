Glyceria × ottawensis

Glyceria × ottawensis, commonly known as the Ottawa glyceria, is a hybrid grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and culms that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Glyceria × ottawensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
