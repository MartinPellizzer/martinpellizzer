Gleichenia bifida var. ferruginea

Gleichenia bifida var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusted Gleichenia, is a fern species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern and fronds that are typically divided into two parts. The leaves are usually dark green with a rusty or reddish hue on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
