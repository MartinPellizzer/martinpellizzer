Gastridium muticum

Gastridium muticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass of the genus Gastridium, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in woodland or heathland environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Gastridium muticum is primarily found in the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, including parts of Europe and Asia.
