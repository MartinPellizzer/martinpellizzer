Galium setaceum var. longipes

Galium setaceum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascend at a low angle. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
