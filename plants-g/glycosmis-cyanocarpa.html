Glycosmis cyanocarpa

Glycosmis cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright blue, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically edible and have a sour taste, often used in traditional medicine.

Glycosmis cyanocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
