Gypsophila stepposa

Gypsophila stepposa, commonly known as the steppe lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Gypsophila stepposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
