Grevillea glaucina

Grevillea glaucina, commonly known as the silver grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-blue leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water once established.

Grevillea glaucina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
