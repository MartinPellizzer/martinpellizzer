Gahnia aspera

Gahnia aspera, commonly known as the rough sedgeland sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Gahnia aspera produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
