Gentiana larecajensis

Gentiana larecajensis, commonly known as the Larecaja gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
