Goodenia berringbinensis

Goodenia berringbinensis, commonly known as the Berringbin Goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
