Gazania longifolia

Gazania longifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a white or pink center. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically 5 to 7 cm in diameter and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Gazania longifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
