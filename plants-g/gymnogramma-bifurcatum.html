Gymnogramma bifurcatum

Gymnogramma bifurcatum, commonly known as the bifurcated huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically divided into two parts, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. Gymnogramma bifurcatum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
