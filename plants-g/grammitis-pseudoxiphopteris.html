Grammitis pseudoxiphopteris

Grammitis pseudoxiphopteris, commonly known as the slender grammitis, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, scale-like leaves are adapted to retain moisture in its moist, low-light habitat.

Grammitis pseudoxiphopteris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
