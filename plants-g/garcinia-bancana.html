Garcinia bancana

Garcinia bancana, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that are highly acidic. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appearance and medicinal properties. Its fruit contains a high concentration of tannins and is used in traditional medicine for its astringent effects.

The tree thrives in humid climates and is often found in forested areas across its native range.
