Geum idahoense

Geum idahoense, commonly known as Idaho avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a hardy and resilient species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
