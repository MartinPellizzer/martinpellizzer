Genista purpurea

Genista purpurea, commonly known as the purple broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, shrub-like growth habit and its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

After flowering, it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in ecological succession and soil stabilization.
