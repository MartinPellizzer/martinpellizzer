Geranium aristatum

Geranium aristatum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Geranium aristatum is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in dry, sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
