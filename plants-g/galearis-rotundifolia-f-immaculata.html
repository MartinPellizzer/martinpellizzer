Galearis rotundifolia f. immaculata

Galearis rotundifolia f. immaculata, commonly known as the white-flowered roundleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded foliage and showy, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies 'immaculata' is distinguished by its lack of the typical purple spots found on the petals of the typical form.
