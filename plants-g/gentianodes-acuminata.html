Gentianodes acuminata

Gentianodes acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia, particularly in China and Nepal, where it grows in alpine or rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
