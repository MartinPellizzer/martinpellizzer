Gnemon brunoniana

Gnemon brunoniana, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a dark green color that becomes more pronounced in the shade.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.
