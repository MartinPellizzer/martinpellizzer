Gymnotrix chilensis

Gymnotrix chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the Andes of Chile.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence and striking, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, growing on tree trunks and branches in humid, high-altitude environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it has a limited geographic distribution.

Due to habitat destruction and its elusive nature, Gymnotrix chilensis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
