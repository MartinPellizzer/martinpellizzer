Garcinia succifolia

Garcinia succifolia, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit's pulp is juicy and has a mild, sweet flavor, while its seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

Garcinia succifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
