Gastrorchis humblotii

Gastrorchis humblotii, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Gastrorchis, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a compact, showy flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks, and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on orchid ecology and evolutionary adaptation.
