Gladiolus praecostatus

Gladiolus praecostatus, commonly known as the Cape goldclaw, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring moderate water and care for optimal growth.
