Gnetum cruzianum

Gnetum cruzianum, commonly known as the Cruzian ghost tree, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which includes gymnosperms.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is wind-pollinated, with seeds enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed capsule.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.
