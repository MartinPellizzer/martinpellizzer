Garcinia ramosii

Garcinia ramosii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The fruit is small, round, and has a vibrant red pericarp with a sour-sweet flavor. It contains bioactive compounds such as xanthones, which have been studied for their potential health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
