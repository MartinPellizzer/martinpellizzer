Gastrochilus albosanguineus

Gastrochilus albosanguineus, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, glossy surface.

It is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
