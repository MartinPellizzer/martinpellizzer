Gasparinia virescens

Gasparinia virescens, commonly known as the green gasparia, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense panicles. Gasparinia virescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and estuaries.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
