Glochidion bicolor

Glochidion bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and typically grows on the branches of other trees, drawing nutrients and water from its host. Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host plants if left unchecked.
