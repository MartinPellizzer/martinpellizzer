Grindelia oregana

Grindelia oregana, commonly known as Oregon gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and fuzzy, silver-green foliage. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Grindelia oregana plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
