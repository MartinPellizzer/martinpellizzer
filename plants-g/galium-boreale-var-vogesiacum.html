Galium boreale var. vogesiacum

Galium boreale var. vogesiacum, commonly known as the Alpine bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous environments where temperatures are cool and conditions are harsh. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium boreale var. vogesiacum is adapted to withstand cold climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
