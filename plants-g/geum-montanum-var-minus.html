Geum montanum var. minus

Geum montanum var. minus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
