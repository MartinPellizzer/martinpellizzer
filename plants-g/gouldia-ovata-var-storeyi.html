Gouldia ovata var. storeyi

Gouldia ovata var. storeyi, commonly known as the Storey's gouldia, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are particularly prominent during the flowering season. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is known for its compact, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
