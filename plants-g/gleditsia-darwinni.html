Gleditsia darwinni

Gleditsia darwinni, commonly known as the Darwin's honeylocust, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in warmer climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique branching pattern.
