Garnotia acutigluma var. longiseta

Garnotia acutigluma var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
