Grossularia rotundifolia

Grossularia rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is part of the Saxifragaceae family and is adapted to cold climates, often growing at high altitudes. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
