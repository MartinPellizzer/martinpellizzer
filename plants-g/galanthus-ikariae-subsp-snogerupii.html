Galanthus ikariae subsp. snogerupii

Galanthus ikariae subsp.

snogerupii, commonly known as the Ikaria snowdrop, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the snowdrop genus. It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers with two prominent white petals and a short, upright stem.

Native to the island of Ikaria in Greece, this plant thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging early in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
