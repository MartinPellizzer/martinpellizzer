Gongora philippica

Gongora philippica, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white floral displays, which are highly prized in horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The genus Gongora is known for its complex floral structures, and Gongora philippica is no exception, featuring intricate petal arrangements and specialized nectar guides.
