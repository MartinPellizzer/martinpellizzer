Gibbaria ilicifolia

Gibbaria ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Gibbaria ilicifolia is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
