Geum pictum

Geum pictum, commonly known as the painted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red-pink center, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Geum pictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
