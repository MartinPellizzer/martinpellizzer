Grusonia cereiformis

Grusonia cereiformis, commonly known as the candleplant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like appearance, with long, cylindrical stems that resemble a candle. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Grusonia cereiformis is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
