Glechoma multifida

Glechoma multifida, commonly known as creeping Jenny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma multifida is known for its rapid spreading habit and is sometimes considered a weed in garden settings.
