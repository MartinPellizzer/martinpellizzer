Galeopsis tetrahit var. idiotropa

Galeopsis tetrahit var. idiotropa, commonly known as the common galeopsis, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides or in waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
