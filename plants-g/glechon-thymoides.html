Glechon thymoides

Glechon thymoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Glechon thymoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
