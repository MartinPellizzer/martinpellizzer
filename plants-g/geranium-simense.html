Geranium simense

Geranium simense, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped or ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Geranium simense is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
