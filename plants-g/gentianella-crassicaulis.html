Gentianella crassicaulis

Gentianella crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella crassicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
