Gaultheria rubella

Gaultheria rubella, commonly known as the red alpine heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Gaultheria rubella is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
