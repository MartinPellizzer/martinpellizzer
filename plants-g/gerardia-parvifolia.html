Gerardia parvifolia

Gerardia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are small, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

Gerardia parvifolia has a fibrous root system and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
