Gymnocalycium stellatum var. flavispinum

Gymnocalycium stellatum var. flavispinum, commonly known as the yellow spiny cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Argentina.

It features a spiny, ribbed stem that can grow up to 30 cm in height, with a pale green to bluish-gray coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
