Gleichenia circinnata var. rupestris

Gleichenia circinnata var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock fern, is a variant of the Gleichenia circinnata species, characterized by its robust, creeping growth habit.

It is a member of the Gleicheniaceae family and is native to regions with temperate to subtropical climates. The plant produces distinctive, fern-like fronds with a serrated margin and a dark green, leathery texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
