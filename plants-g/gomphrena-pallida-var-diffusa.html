Gomphrena pallida var. diffusa

Gomphrena pallida var. diffusa, commonly known as the pale gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
