Geranium pallidifolium

Geranium pallidifolium, commonly known as the pale-leaved geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are oval to heart-shaped with a pale green coloration. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in the summer months, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and wetland restoration projects.
