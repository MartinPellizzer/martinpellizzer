Gronophyllum procerum

Gronophyllum procerum, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and spiny, woody stems. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in reforestation projects in degraded landscapes.
