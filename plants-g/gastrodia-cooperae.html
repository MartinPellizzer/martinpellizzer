Gastrodia cooperae

Gastrodia cooperae, commonly known as the Chinese thunder god mushroom, is a unique orchidaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous rhizomes and lack of true leaves, relying instead on its mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a distinctive, spicy aroma, which is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Gastrodia cooperae is typically found in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
