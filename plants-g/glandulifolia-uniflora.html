Glandulifolia uniflora

Glandulifolia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower glandular plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its glandular structures on its stems and leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a hairy texture and prominent glandular dots.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage, which releases a distinctive scent when crushed.
