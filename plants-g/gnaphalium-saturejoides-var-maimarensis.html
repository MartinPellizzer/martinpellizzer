Gnaphalium saturejoides var. maimarensis

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. maimarensis, commonly known as the Maimaran goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has a compact, low-growing habit with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
