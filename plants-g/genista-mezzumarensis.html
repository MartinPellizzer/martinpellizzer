Genista mezzumarensis

Genista mezzumarensis, commonly known as the Mezzumarensis broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense foliage structure.
