Gomphrena lactea

Gomphrena lactea, commonly known as the milkweed bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for coastal or sandy environments.

Gomphrena lactea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
