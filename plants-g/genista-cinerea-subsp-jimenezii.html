Genista cinerea subsp. jimenezii

Genista cinerea subsp.

jimenezii, commonly known as Jimenez's whitethorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
