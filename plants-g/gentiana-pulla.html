Gentiana pulla

Gentiana pulla, commonly known as the small gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana pulla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
