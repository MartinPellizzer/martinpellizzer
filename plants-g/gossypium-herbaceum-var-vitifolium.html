Gossypium herbaceum var. vitifolium

Gossypium herbaceum var. vitifolium, commonly known as the grape-leaved cotton, is a variant of the cotton plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton fibers that are harvested for textile use, similar to other cotton species. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, giving it a unique appearance that sets it apart from other cotton varieties.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native habitat.
