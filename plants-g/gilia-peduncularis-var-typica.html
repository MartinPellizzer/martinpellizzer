Gilia peduncularis var. typica

Gilia peduncularis var. typica, commonly known as the clustered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its slender, branched stems support clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
