Gentiana saxatilis

Gentiana saxatilis, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana saxatilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
