Glyceria notata subsp. declinata

Glyceria notata subsp.

declinata, commonly known as the bentgrass sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and riverbanks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-parted flowers in spikelets. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
