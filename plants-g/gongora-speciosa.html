Gongora speciosa

Gongora speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly prized for their beauty and are often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Gongora speciosa requires specific care, including high humidity and well-draining soil, to thrive.
