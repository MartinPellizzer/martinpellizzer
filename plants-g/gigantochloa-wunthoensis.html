Gigantochloa wunthoensis

Gigantochloa wunthoensis, commonly known as the Wuntho bamboo, is a tall, robust grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a fibrous texture.

This bamboo is valued for its strong and durable stalks, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.
