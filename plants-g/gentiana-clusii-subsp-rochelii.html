Gentiana clusii subsp. rochelii

Gentiana clusii subsp.

rochelii, commonly known as the Rochel's gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
