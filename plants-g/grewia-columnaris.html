Grewia columnaris

Grewia columnaris, commonly known as the columnar grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, which makes it suitable for hedging and ornamental use. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Grewia columnaris is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid or semi-arid climates.
