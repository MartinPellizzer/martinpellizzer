Gnaphalium ramosum

Gnaphalium ramosum, commonly known as the slender everlasting or slender cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, daisy-like flower heads composed of numerous tiny white to pale yellow florets. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gnaphalium ramosum is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and meadows.
