Gentiana trichotoma var. chingii

Gentiana trichotoma var. chingii, commonly known as the Ching's gentian, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and erect.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
