Gagea taurica

Gagea taurica, commonly known as the Tauric scilla, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers in early spring. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

Gagea taurica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
