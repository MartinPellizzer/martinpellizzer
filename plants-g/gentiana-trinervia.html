Gentiana trinervia

Gentiana trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have three distinct veins, giving it its common name.

Gentiana trinervia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
