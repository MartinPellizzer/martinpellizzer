Geum rivale var. strictum

Geum rivale var. strictum, commonly known as water avens, is a flowering plant native to moist, shaded areas in temperate regions.

It features small, showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in wet soils near streams or in marshy habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to other members of the Ranunculaceae family.
