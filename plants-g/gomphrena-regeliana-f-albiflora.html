Gomphrena regeliana f. albiflora

Gomphrena regeliana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered gomphrena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, spherical inflorescences. The flowers are predominantly white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or purple calyx that adds visual contrast. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which can persist throughout the growing season.
