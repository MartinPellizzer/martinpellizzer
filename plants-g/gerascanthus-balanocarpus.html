Gerascanthus balanocarpus

Gerascanthus balanocarpus, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
