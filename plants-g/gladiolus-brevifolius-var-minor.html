Gladiolus brevifolius var. minor

Gladiolus brevifolius var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the broader gladiolus genus.
