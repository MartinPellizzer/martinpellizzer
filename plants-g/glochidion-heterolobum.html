Glochidion heterolobum

Glochidion heterolobum, commonly known as the broadleaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by glochidiate (spiny) fruits, which are a distinctive feature of the genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
