Gardenia hageniana

Gardenia hageniana, commonly known as the Hagenia gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gardenia hageniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive aromatic blooms.
