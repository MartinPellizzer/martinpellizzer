Galium trifidum var. pusillum

Galium trifidum var. pusillum, commonly known as the small-fruited bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
