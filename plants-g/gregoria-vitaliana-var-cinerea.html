Gregoria vitaliana var. cinerea

Gregoria vitaliana var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray gregoria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Gregoria vitaliana var. cinerea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
