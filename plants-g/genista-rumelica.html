Genista rumelica

Genista rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Genista rumelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
