Gaultheria × wisleyensis

Gaultheria × wisleyensis, commonly known as the hybrid wintergreen, is a species resulting from the cross between Gaultheria shallon and Gaultheria procumbens.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of North America. The plant features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring. Its berries are typically red or black and contain aromatic, spicy seeds.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter interest and fragrance.
