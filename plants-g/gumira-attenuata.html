Gumira attenuata

Gumira attenuata, commonly known as the slender gumira, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
