Gentiana cochabambensis

Gentiana cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Gentiana cochabambensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
