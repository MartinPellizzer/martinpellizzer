Gamogyne lurida

Gamogyne lurida, commonly known as the rusty leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a rusty or reddish color, especially along the edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in spikelets and bloom in early summer.

Gamogyne lurida plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
