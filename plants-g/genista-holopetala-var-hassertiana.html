Genista holopetala var. hassertiana

Genista holopetala var. hassertiana, commonly known as the Hassert's whitethorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
