Glycine hispida var. similis

Glycine hispida var. similis, commonly known as the hairy bean or similar hairy bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and produces small, yellow to green flowers. The plant bears pods that contain several seeds, which are typically round and smooth. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Glycine hispida var. similis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vigorous growth and attractive foliage.
