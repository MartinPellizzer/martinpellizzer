Gordonia ceylanica var. elliptica

Gordonia ceylanica var. elliptica, commonly known as the Ceylon gordonia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the wet tropical forests of Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often glaucous underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in high humidity and well-drained soil conditions.
