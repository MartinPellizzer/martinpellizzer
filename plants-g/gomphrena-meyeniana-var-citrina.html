Gomphrena meyeniana var. citrina

Gomphrena meyeniana var. citrina, commonly known as the citron gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flower heads that resemble small spheres. The plant produces feathery, pinkish-white bracts that surround the central flower clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
