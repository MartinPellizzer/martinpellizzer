Gynopleura solanoides

Gynopleura solanoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its fruits are berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Gynopleura solanoides is often found in disturbed areas and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
