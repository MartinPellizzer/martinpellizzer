Gissonia riparia

Gissonia riparia, commonly known as the riverbank sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist to aquatic environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and flooded meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in waterlogged areas.

Gissonia riparia plays an important ecological role in maintaining the health of wetland ecosystems.
