Genista cinerea f. leptoclada-microphylla

Genista cinerea f. leptoclada-microphylla, commonly known as the silver broom or dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
