Geranium selinum

Geranium selinum, commonly known as lesser geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium selinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
