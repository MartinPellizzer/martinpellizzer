Grindelia coronopifolia

Grindelia coronopifolia, commonly known as brittleblow or desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

The genus Grindelia is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is known for its medicinal and ecological significance.
