Glycine schimperi

Glycine schimperi, commonly known as Schimper's soybean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several round, edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Glycine schimperi is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
