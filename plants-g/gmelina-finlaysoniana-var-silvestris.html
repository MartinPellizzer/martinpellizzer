Gmelina finlaysoniana var. silvestris

Gmelina finlaysoniana var. silvestris, commonly known as the wild gum tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
