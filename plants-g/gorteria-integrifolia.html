Gorteria integrifolia

Gorteria integrifolia, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Gorteria integrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
