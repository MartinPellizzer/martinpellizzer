Gagea persica var. kashmirensis

Gagea persica var. kashmirensis, commonly known as the Kashmirian gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale pink blooms that typically appear in spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold, rocky environments, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.
