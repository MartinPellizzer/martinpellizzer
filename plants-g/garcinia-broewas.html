Garcinia broewas

Garcinia broewas, commonly known as the Borneo Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties contribute to its significance in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
