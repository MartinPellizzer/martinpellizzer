Gentianella florida

Gentianella florida, commonly known as the Florida gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Gentianella florida is often found in wetlands and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
