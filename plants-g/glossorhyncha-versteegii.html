Glossorhyncha versteegii

Glossorhyncha versteegii, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration, often in shades of yellow and green. Its inflorescence is a single, erect spike bearing several small, showy blooms.

Glossorhyncha versteegii is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
