Gymnocalycium bruchii var. rubroalabastrum

Gymnocalycium bruchii var. rubroalabastrum, commonly known as the red-tipped moon cactus, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines and a distinctive red-tipped crown. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, and the plant stores water in its stem for survival in dry conditions.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
