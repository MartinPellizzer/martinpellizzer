Geranium robertianum subvar. mediterraneum

Geranium robertianum subvar. mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and wildflower arrangements.
