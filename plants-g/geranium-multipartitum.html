Geranium multipartitum

Geranium multipartitum, commonly known as the clustered geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium multipartitum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
