Geranium pyrenaicum

Geranium pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, producing showy pink or purple blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
