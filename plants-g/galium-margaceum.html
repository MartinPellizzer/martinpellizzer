Galium margaceum

Galium margaceum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascending. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium margaceum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
