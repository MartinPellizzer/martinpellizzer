Galeandra cristata

Galeandra cristata, commonly known as the crested galeandra, is a striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often crested inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
