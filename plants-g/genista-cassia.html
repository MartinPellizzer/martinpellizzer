Genista cassia

Genista cassia, commonly known as goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and drought tolerance also make it suitable for xeriscaping.
