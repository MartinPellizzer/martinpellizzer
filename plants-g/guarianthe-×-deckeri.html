Guarianthe × deckeri

Guarianthe × deckeri, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and elongated, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
