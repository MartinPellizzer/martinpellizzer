Gentiana lowndesii

Gentiana lowndesii, commonly known as the Lowndes gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana lowndesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
