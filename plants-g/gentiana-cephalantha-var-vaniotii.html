Gentiana cephalantha var. vaniotii

Gentiana cephalantha var. vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniot's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are upright and branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
