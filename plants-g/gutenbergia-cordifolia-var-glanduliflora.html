Gutenbergia cordifolia var. glanduliflora

Gutenbergia cordifolia var. glanduliflora, commonly known as the glandular-heartleaf gutenbergia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
