Goniopteris amazonica

Goniopteris amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian shield fern, is a tropical fern native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in maintaining forest biodiversity.
