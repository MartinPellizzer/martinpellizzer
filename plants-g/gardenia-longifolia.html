Gardenia longifolia

Gardenia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces yellow berries that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gardenia longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
