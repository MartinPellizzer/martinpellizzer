Geranium fuscicaule

Geranium fuscicaule, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Geranium fuscicaule is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
