Gentiana jamesonii

Gentiana jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Gentiana jamesonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
