Gentiana albanica

Gentiana albanica, commonly known as the Albanian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are erect and hairy.

Gentiana albanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
