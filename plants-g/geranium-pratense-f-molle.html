Geranium pratense f. molle

Geranium pratense f. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved cranesbill, is a horticultural form of the common cranesbill.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
