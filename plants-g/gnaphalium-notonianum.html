Gnaphalium notonianum

Gnaphalium notonianum, commonly known as the New Zealand everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom from spring to autumn. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gnaphalium notonianum is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.
