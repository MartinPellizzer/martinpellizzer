Gymnocalycium anisitsii var. tucavocense

Gymnocalycium anisitsii var. tucavocense, commonly known as the Tucavoco cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spiny areoles. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
