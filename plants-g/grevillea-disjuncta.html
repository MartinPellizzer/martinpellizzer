Grevillea disjuncta

Grevillea disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open woodlands. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Grevillea disjuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
