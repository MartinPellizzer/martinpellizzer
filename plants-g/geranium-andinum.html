Geranium andinum

Geranium andinum, commonly known as the Andean geranium, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough, serrated edge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
