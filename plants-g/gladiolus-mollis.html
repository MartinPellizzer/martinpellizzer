Gladiolus mollis

Gladiolus mollis, commonly known as the soft gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant's leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets.

Gladiolus mollis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.
