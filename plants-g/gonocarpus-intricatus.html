Gonocarpus intricatus

Gonocarpus intricatus, commonly known as the intricate sea sapphire, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers, which are adapted to thrive in saline environments. The plant's roots form a dense network that helps stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, allowing it to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Gonocarpus intricatus plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine and terrestrial species.
