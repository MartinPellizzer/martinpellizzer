Gladiolus longiflorus

Gladiolus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
