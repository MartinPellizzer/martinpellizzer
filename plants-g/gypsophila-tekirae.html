Gypsophila tekirae

Gypsophila tekirae, commonly known as the Turkish laceflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, round fruits called capsules, which contain numerous seeds.

Gypsophila tekirae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden designs.
