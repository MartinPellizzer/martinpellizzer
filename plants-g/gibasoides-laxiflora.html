Gibasoides laxiflora

Gibasoides laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered gibasoid, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth margin and a distinct, fragrant aroma.

The flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
