Gymnogramma feei

Gymnogramma feei, commonly known as the Fee's rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and small, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Gymnogramma feei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
