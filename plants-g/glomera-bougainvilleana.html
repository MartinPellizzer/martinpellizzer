Glomera bougainvilleana

Glomera bougainvilleana, commonly known as the velvetleaf bougainvillea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that come in shades of pink, purple, and red, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has woody stems and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
