Grevillea thelemanniana subsp. pinaster

Grevillea thelemanniana subsp.

pinaster, commonly known as the Sydney golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in winter and spring.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of pale hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
