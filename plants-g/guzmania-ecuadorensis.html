Guzmania ecuadorensis

Guzmania ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian flaming sword, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features a vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a sword, typically in shades of red or orange. The plant possesses a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small aquatic environment for microorganisms. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Guzmania ecuadorensis thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
