Gentiana suborbisepala

Gentiana suborbisepala, commonly known as the suborbital gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its flowers are characterized by their five deeply lobed petals and a prominent blue or violet corolla.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
