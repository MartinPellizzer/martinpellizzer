Grevillea batrachioides

Grevillea batrachioides, commonly known as the frog's tongue grevillea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces striking, red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Grevillea batrachioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
