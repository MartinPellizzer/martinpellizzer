Gonolobus albomarginatus

Gonolobus albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin velvetleaf, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it a common weed in agricultural fields.
