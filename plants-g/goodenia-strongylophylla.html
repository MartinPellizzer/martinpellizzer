Goodenia strongylophylla

Goodenia strongylophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, producing pink to purple flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native gardens.
