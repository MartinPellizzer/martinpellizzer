Geranium myrrhifolium

Geranium myrrhifolium, commonly known as the myrrh-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and often reddish or purple flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves with a strong, aromatic scent, which is reminiscent of myrrh. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
