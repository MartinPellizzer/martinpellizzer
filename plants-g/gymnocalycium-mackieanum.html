Gymnocalycium mackieanum

Gymnocalycium mackieanum, commonly known as the Mackie's moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to southern South America.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often featuring a vibrant, colorful bloom. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
