Gorteria mitis

Gorteria mitis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Gorteria mitis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
