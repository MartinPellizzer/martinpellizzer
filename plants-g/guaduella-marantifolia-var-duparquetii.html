Guaduella marantifolia var. duparquetii

Guaduella marantifolia var. duparquetii, commonly known as the red-stemmed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with a vibrant red or purple sheen on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by edible tubers resembling those of arrowroot. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary properties.
