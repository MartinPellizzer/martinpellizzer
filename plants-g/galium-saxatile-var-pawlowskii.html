Galium saxatile var. pawlowskii

Galium saxatile var. pawlowskii, commonly known as the alpine bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
