Gardenia grandis

Gardenia grandis, commonly known as the large gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Gardenia grandis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
