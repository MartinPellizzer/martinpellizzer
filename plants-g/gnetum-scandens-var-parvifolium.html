Gnetum scandens var. parvifolium

Gnetum scandens var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved climbing gymnosperm, is a member of the Gnetophyta division, which is a unique group of gymnosperms.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and simple, alternate leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces male and female cones separately, with the female cones bearing ovules that develop into seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studying evolutionary adaptations in gymnosperms.
