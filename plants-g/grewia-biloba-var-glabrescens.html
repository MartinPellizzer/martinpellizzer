Grewia biloba var. glabrescens

Grewia biloba var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-stalked globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia biloba var. glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
