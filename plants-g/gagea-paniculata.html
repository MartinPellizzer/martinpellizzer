Gagea paniculata

Gagea paniculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Gagea paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
