Genista carpetana subsp. carpetana

Genista carpetana subsp.

carpetana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various insect species.
