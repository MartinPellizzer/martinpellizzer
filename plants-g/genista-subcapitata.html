Genista subcapitata

Genista subcapitata, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista subcapitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
