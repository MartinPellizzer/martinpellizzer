Gaylussacia thymelaeoides var. acutifolia

Gaylussacia thymelaeoides var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to support local wildlife.
