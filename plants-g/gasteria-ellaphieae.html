Gasteria ellaphieae

Gasteria ellaphieae, commonly known as the Ellaphieae gasteria, is a rare and compact succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white markings and a textured surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
