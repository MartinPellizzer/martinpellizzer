Globifera rotundata

Globifera rotundata, commonly known as the globe-shaped fern, is a unique plant characterized by its spherical, clustered growth pattern.

It features a compact rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.

Globifera rotundata is known for its slow growth and ability to form dense, rounded clusters over time.
