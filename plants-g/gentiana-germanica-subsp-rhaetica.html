Gentiana germanica subsp. rhaetica

Gentiana germanica subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas of the Alps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of ecological importance in its native habitats.
