Glaucium grandiflorum var. iranicum

Glaucium grandiflorum var. iranicum, commonly known as Persian poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust taproot and hairy stems, with palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically large, with five petals and a distinctive central disk, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
