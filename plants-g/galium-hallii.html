Galium hallii

Galium hallii, commonly known as Hall's bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Galium hallii has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated margin.
