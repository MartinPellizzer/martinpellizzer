Geranium magellanicum

Geranium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Geranium magellanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
