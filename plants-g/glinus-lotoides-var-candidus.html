Glinus lotoides var. candidus

Glinus lotoides var. candidus, commonly known as the white-flowered glinus, is a variant of the glinus plant characterized by its distinctive white blooms.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to certain regions of Africa, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
