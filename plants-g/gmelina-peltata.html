Gmelina peltata

Gmelina peltata, commonly known as the broad-leafed gum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, peltate leaves that are often divided into lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
