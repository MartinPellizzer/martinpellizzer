Gomphrena maitlandii

Gomphrena maitlandii, commonly known as the red globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gomphrena maitlandii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
