Gagea dschungarica

Gagea dschungarica, commonly known as the Central Asian spring beauty, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to cold climates and rocky soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its bulb serves as a storage organ, allowing it to survive harsh winters and regenerate in the following season.

Gagea dschungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
