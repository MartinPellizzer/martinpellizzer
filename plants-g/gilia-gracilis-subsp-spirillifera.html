Gilia gracilis subsp. spirillifera

Gilia gracilis subsp.

spirillifera, commonly known as the spiralleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
