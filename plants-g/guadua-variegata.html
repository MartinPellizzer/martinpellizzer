Guadua variegata

Guadua variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and striking variegated leaves with alternating green and white or yellow markings. This plant is highly valued for its strong, lightweight culms, which are used in construction and handicrafts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Guadua variegata is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
