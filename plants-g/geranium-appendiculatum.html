Geranium appendiculatum

Geranium appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

Geranium appendiculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
