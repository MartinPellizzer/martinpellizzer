Githopsis diffusa subsp. filicaulis

Githopsis diffusa subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed githopsis, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit with multiple stems that often root at the base, forming a dense mat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
