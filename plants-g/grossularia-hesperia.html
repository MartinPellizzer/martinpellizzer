Grossularia hesperia

Grossularia hesperia, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The berries, which are usually red or black when ripe, are a significant food source for wildlife and humans alike.
