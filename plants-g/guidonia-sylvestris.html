Guidonia sylvestris

Guidonia sylvestris, commonly known as the wild guillemot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

The plant is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
