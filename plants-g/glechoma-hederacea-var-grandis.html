Glechoma hederacea var. grandis

Glechoma hederacea var. grandis, commonly known as greater ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly across lawns and garden beds. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
