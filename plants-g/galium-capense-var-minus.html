Galium capense var. minus

Galium capense var. minus, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
