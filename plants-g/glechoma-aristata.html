Glechoma aristata

Glechoma aristata, commonly known as the creeping Jenny, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue or violet flowers in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Glechoma aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly through stolons.
