Geranium fremontii

Geranium fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Geranium fremontii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.
