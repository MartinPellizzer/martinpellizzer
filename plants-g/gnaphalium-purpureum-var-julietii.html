Gnaphalium purpureum var. julietii

Gnaphalium purpureum var. julietii, commonly known as Juliet's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flower heads that are often mistaken for daisies. Its leaves are linear, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
