Genista numidica var. supravillosa

Genista numidica var. supravillosa, commonly known as the silky broom.

It is a shrub native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
