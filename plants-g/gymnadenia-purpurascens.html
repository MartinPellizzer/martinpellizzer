Gymnadenia purpurascens

Gymnadenia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple fringed orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-purple flowers with fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, usually from July to August, and it is often found in open habitats with partial shade.

Gymnadenia purpurascens is also known for its delicate, fragrant blooms and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
