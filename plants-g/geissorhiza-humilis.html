Geissorhiza humilis

Geissorhiza humilis, commonly known as the dwarf orchid or the yellow orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-orange blooms that are clustered in a loose raceme. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Geissorhiza humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
