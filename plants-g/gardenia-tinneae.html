Gardenia tinneae

Gardenia tinneae, commonly known as the Kauai gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight sheen. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia tinneae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
