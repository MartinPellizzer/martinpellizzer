Gesneria picta

Gesneria picta, commonly known as the painted gesneria, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thick, fleshy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually dark green with a glossy surface and can have intricate venation patterns.

Gesneria picta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its ornamental foliage and flowers.
