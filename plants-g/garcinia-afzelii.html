Garcinia afzelii

Garcinia afzelii, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Its botanical features make it valuable for both traditional medicine and potential pharmaceutical applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.
