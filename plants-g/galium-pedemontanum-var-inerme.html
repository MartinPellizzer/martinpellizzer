Galium pedemontanum var. inerme

Galium pedemontanum var. inerme, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with thin, wiry stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
