Glochidion xerocarpum

Glochidion xerocarpum, commonly known as the hairy glabrous leaf vine, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by clustered, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
