Grevillea latrobei var. leiostylis

Grevillea latrobei var. leiostylis, commonly known as the red-veined grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-veined leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
