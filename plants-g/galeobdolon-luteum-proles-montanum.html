Galeobdolon luteum proles montanum

Galeobdolon luteum proles montanum, commonly known as the mountain yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, playing a role in the local ecosystem.
