Gonzalea pulverulenta

Gonzalea pulverulenta, commonly known as the powderbug gorse, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or coastal environments.

Gonzalea pulverulenta is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
