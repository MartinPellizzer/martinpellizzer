Geranium ribifolium

Geranium ribifolium, commonly known as the redstem geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a pale underside. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
