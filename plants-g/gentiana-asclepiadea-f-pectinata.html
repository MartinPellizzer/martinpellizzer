Gentiana asclepiadea f. pectinata

Gentiana asclepiadea f. pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata gentian, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The pectinata form is distinguished by its more robust growth and larger flowers compared to the typical species.
