Galium trifidum var. modestum

Galium trifidum var. modestum, commonly known as slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
