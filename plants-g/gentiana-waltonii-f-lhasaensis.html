Gentiana waltonii f. lhasaensis

Gentiana waltonii f. lhasaensis, commonly known as the Lhasa gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are notable for their deep coloration and five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems.
