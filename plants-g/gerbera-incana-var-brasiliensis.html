Gerbera incana var. brasiliensis

Gerbera incana var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian gerbera, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
