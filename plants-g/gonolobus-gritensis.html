Gonolobus gritensis

Gonolobus gritensis, commonly known as the Grits vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in humid, tropical environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
