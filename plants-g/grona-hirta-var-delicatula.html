Grona hirta var. delicatula

Grona hirta var. delicatula, commonly known as the delicate hairy grona, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it may be considered invasive in certain areas.
