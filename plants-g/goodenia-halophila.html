Goodenia halophila

Goodenia halophila, commonly known as the saltbush goodenia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its halophytic nature. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in response to drought conditions.

Goodenia halophila plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
