Graffenrieda limbata

Graffenrieda limbata, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Graffenrieda limbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
