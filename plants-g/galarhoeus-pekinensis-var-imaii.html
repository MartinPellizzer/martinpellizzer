Galarhoeus pekinensis var. imaii

Galarhoeus pekinensis var. imaii, commonly known as the Peking honeysuckle, is a variant of the honeysuckle plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
