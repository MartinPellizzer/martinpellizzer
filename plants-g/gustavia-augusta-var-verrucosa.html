Gustavia augusta var. verrucosa

Gustavia augusta var. verrucosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems forming a dense, bushy structure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
