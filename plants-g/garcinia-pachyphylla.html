Garcinia pachyphylla

Garcinia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible. Garcinia pachyphylla is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
