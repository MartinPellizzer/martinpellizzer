Gentiana pedata subsp. pedata

Gentiana pedata subsp.

pedata, commonly known as blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
