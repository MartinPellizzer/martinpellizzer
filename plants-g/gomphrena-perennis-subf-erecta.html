Gomphrena perennis subf. erecta

Gomphrena perennis subf.

erecta, commonly known as the upright gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its production of small, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble tiny fireworks.

The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a resilient and low-maintenance plant.
