Greeniopsis megalantha

Greeniopsis megalantha, commonly known as the giant greeniopsis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
