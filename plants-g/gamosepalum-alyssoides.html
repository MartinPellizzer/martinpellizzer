Gamosepalum alyssoides

Gamosepalum alyssoides, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has five fused sepals that form a gamosepalous calyx, a key botanical feature. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Gamosepalum alyssoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
