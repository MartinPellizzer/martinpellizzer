Gerardia levigata

Gerardia levigata, commonly known as the smooth gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Gerardia levigata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
