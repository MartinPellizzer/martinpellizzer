Gymnanthemum

Gymnanthemum, commonly known as the common gymnanthemum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plants typically have alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves and are often found in moist, temperate regions. Gymnanthemum species are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and their resilience to mild environmental conditions.

They play a role in supporting local pollinators and are sometimes used in horticulture for their ornamental value.
