Gentiana pavonii

Gentiana pavonii, commonly known as the peacock gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or violet corolla, often with a white or yellow throat, and are pollinated primarily by flies and bees.
