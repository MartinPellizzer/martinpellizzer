Gladiolus menitskyi

Gladiolus menitskyi, commonly known as the Menitsky gladiolus, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of flowers at the top of the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens with adequate space.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
