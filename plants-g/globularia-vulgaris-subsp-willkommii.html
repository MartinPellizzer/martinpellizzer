Globularia vulgaris subsp. willkommii

Globularia vulgaris subsp.

willkommii, commonly known as the willkommii globularia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and compact growth habit.
