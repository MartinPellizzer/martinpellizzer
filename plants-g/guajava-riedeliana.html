Guajava riedeliana

Guajava riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's guajava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
