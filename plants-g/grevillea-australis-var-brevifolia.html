Grevillea australis var. brevifolia

Grevillea australis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and sandstone regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and evergreen foliage.
