Gentianella primulifolia

Gentianella primulifolia, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and usually unbranched.

Gentianella primulifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
