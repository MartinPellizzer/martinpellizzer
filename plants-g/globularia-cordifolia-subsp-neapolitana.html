Globularia cordifolia subsp. neapolitana

Globularia cordifolia subsp.

neapolitana, commonly known as the Neapolitan globularia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, rounded clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
