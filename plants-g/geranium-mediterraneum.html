Geranium mediterraneum

Geranium mediterraneum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for containers and hanging baskets.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a favored plant in both gardens and landscapes.
