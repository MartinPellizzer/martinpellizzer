Gentiana golowninia var. oblonga

Gentiana golowninia var. oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
