Gymnocalycium terweemeanum

Gymnocalycium terweemeanum, commonly known as the Terweem's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a low-growing, compact plant with a short, thick stem that often branches out. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

This species is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
