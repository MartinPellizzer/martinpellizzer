Glochidion seemannii

Glochidion seemannii, commonly known as the Singapore glabrous hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in lowland forest areas, often in humid and shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
