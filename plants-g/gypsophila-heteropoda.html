Gypsophila heteropoda

Gypsophila heteropoda, commonly known as the baby's breath or fairy flowers, is a species of flowering plant in the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and the Caucasus. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Gypsophila heteropoda is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
