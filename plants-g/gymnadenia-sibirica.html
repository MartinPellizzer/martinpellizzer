Gymnadenia sibirica

Gymnadenia sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian grasspinks, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and tundra, where it thrives in cool climates. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gymnadenia sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
