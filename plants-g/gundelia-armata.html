Gundelia armata

Gundelia armata, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive, fibrous root system. It is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture conditions.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest in both botanical research and potential agricultural applications.
