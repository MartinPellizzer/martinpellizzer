Guapira uleana

Guapira uleana, commonly known as the Brazilian apple or yellow apple tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow, apple-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Guapira uleana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
