Gnaphalium berlandieri

Gnaphalium berlandieri, commonly known as the desert everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across arid environments. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive component of desert flora.
