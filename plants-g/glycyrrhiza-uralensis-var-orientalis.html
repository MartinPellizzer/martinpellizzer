Glycyrrhiza uralensis var. orientalis

Glycyrrhiza uralensis var. orientalis, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including Russia and China.

It is characterized by its woody, cylindrical root system that is highly valued for its medicinal and commercial properties. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets. The root contains high concentrations of glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its sweet taste and various pharmacological effects.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic uses.
