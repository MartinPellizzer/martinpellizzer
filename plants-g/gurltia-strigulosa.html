Gurltia strigulosa

Gurltia strigulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf gurltia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions.

Gurltia strigulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
