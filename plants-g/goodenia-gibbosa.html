Goodenia gibbosa

Goodenia gibbosa, commonly known as the rough goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Goodenia gibbosa thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
