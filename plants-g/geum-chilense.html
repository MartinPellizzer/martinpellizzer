Geum chilense

Geum chilense, commonly known as Chilean avens, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Geum chilense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
