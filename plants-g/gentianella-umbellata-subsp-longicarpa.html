Gentianella umbellata subsp. longicarpa

Gentianella umbellata subsp.

longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, cylindrical fruits, which set it apart from other members of the genus. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
