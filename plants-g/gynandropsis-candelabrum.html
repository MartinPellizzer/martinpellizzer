Gynandropsis candelabrum

Gynandropsis candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick candelabra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its distinctive branching pattern that resembles a candlestick. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in arid or semi-arid regions.
