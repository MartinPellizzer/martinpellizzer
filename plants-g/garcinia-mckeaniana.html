Garcinia mckeaniana

Garcinia mckeaniana, commonly known as the Kauai Garcinia, is a rare and endangered species native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows in wet tropical forests, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its fruit contains a single seed and is adapted to disperse via animals, though its population has been severely reduced due to habitat loss.
