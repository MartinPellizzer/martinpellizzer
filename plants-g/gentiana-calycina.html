Gentiana calycina

Gentiana calycina, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and upright.

Gentiana calycina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
