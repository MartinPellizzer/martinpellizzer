Gentiana × thomasiana

Gentiana × thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomasian gentian, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
