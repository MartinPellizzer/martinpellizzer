Gerbera ovalifolia

Gerbera ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features bright yellow to orange centers surrounded by white or pink ray florets. Its leaves are oval-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gerbera ovalifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
