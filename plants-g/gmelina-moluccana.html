Gmelina moluccana

Gmelina moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and dense foliage, which contribute to its ecological importance. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical fruits containing seeds.

Gmelina moluccana is often found in lowland rainforests and plays a significant role in local ecosystems.
