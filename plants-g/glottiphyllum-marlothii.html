Glottiphyllum marlothii

Glottiphyllum marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's glottiphyllum, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners in dry climates.
