Gentianella viridiflora

Gentianella viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Gentianella viridiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
