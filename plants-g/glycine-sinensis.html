Glycine sinensis

Glycine sinensis, commonly known as Chinese cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and often used in traditional cuisines. Glycine sinensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its botanical features make it both a useful food source and a valuable plant for ecological restoration.
