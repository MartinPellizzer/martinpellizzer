Gentiana cernua

Gentiana cernua, commonly known as the drooping gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana cernua is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
