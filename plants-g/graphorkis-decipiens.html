Graphorkis decipiens

Graphorkis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a rare and fascinating member of the Orchidaceae family.

Native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its striking, often colorful flowers that mimic other species to attract pollinators. Its unique floral structure and deceptive appearance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
