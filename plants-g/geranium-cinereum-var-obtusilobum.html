Geranium cinereum var. obtusilobum

Geranium cinereum var. obtusilobum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with rounded to obtuse tips and are also hairy, contributing to its distinctive look. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
