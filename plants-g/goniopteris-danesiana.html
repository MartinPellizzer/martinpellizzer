Goniopteris danesiana

Goniopteris danesiana, commonly known as the Danes' fern, is a fossilized plant from the Carboniferous period.

It belongs to the genus Goniopteris, which is characterized by its large, intricate fronds with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant's leaves were likely semi-herbaceous, with a complex structure that suggests it was adapted to a moist, tropical environment. Its fossils are often found in coal-bearing strata, indicating its prevalence in ancient swamp ecosystems.

The study of Goniopteris danesiana provides valuable insights into the paleobotany and climate of the Carboniferous era.
