Gossypium hirsutum var. paniculatum

Gossypium hirsutum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the wild cotton plant, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fibrous seeds encased in a fluffy, cotton-like substance, which is a key botanical feature. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in dry, arid regions.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural use.
