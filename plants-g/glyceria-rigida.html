Glyceria rigida

Glyceria rigida, commonly known as rigid glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. This plant is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. Glyceria rigida has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce minute flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
