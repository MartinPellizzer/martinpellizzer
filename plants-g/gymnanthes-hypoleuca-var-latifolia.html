Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. latifolia

Gymnanthes hypoleuca var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gymnanthes, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are often surrounded by brightly colored bracts.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
