Gagea saxatilis subsp. bohemica

Gagea saxatilis subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian spring beauty, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
