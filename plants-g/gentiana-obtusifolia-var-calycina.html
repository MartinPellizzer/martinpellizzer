Gentiana obtusifolia var. calycina

Gentiana obtusifolia var. calycina, commonly known as the Calycine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it forms dense clumps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
