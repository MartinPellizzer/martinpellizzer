Gynura pinnatifida

Gynura pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the painted nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically bright green with a velvety texture, and the plant can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Gynura pinnatifida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
