Geranium cinereum var. elatius

Geranium cinereum var. elatius, commonly known as the tall purple geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.
