Gentiana nubigena var. parviflora

Gentiana nubigena var. parviflora, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps and other mountainous areas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to other gentian species, making it a unique and sought-after ornamental plant.
