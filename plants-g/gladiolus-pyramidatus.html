Gladiolus pyramidatus

Gladiolus pyramidatus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base of the petals. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gladiolus pyramidatus is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and is valued for its striking appearance and resilience in various climates.
