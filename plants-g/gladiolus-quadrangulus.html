Gladiolus quadrangulus

Gladiolus quadrangulus, commonly known as the four-cornered gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, upright stems that bear clusters of colorful, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to different environmental conditions, making it a resilient choice for gardeners.
