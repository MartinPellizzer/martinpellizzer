Grangea ceruanoides

Grangea ceruanoides, commonly known as the woolly antelope plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Grangea ceruanoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
