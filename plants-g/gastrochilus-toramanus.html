Gastrochilus toramanus

Gastrochilus toramanus, commonly known as the Toraman Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Gastrochilus toramanus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
