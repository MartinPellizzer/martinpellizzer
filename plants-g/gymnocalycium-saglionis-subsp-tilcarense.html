Gymnocalycium saglionis subsp. tilcarense

Gymnocalycium saglionis subsp.

tilcarense, commonly known as the Talcarense Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection from extreme temperatures.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
