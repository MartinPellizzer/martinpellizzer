Gladiolus woodii

Native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, it is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, though it is sensitive to frost and requires careful cultivation.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat loss, Gladiolus woodii is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
