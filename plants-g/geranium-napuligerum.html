Geranium napuligerum

Geranium napuligerum, commonly known as the Naples geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, palmate leaves with a slightly lobed appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
