Gastrolobium callistachys

Gastrolobium callistachys, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular plant in native gardens.
