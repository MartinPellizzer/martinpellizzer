Grewia corylifolia

Grewia corylifolia, commonly known as the Indian rose or wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
