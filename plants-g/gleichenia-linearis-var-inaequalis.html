Gleichenia linearis var. inaequalis

Gleichenia linearis var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal fern, is a variant of the linear fern that exhibits distinct morphological differences.

It is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae, characterized by its elongated, linear fronds. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae that are unequal in size, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its delicate, feathery appearance.
