Grewia bakeriana

Grewia bakeriana, commonly known as the Natal glory bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that appear in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems, and it produces small, drupaceous fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable addition to horticultural collections.
