Gardenia grandiflora

Gardenia grandiflora, commonly known as the Hawaiian gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, waxy, white flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Hawaiian culture.

Gardenia grandiflora is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and cosmetics.
