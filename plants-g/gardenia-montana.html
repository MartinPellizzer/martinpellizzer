Gardenia montana

Gardenia montana, commonly known as the mountain gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are highly valued for their beauty and are often used in ornamental horticulture.

Gardenia montana thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
