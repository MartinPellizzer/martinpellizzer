Galarhoeus pallasii f. pilosus

Galarhoeus pallasii f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy pallas's bellflower, is a botanical variant of the species Galarhoeus pallasii.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. This plant is native to specific regions in Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects, and it is often found in alpine or mountainous environments.

The f. pilosus form is distinguished by its increased pubescence compared to the typical form of the species.
