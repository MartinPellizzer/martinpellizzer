Glycyrrhiza aculeata

Glycyrrhiza aculeata, commonly known as the hairy licorice, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, followed by pods containing several seeds. Its root is the primary part used in traditional medicine and is known for its sweet, licorice-like flavor.

Glycyrrhiza aculeata is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
