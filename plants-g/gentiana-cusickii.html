Gentiana cusickii

Gentiana cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana cusickii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
