Garcinia henriquesii

Garcinia henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Its unique morphology and chemical composition make it of interest in both horticultural and pharmaceutical research.

Garcinia henriquesii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
