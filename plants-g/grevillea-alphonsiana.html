Grevillea alphonsiana

Grevillea alphonsiana, commonly known as the Crimson Grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features feathery, bright red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
