Gladiolus peschianus

Gladiolus peschianus, commonly known as the Persian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers typically come in shades of white, pink, and purple, with narrow, pointed petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
