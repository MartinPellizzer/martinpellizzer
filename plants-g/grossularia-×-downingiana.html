Grossularia × downingiana

Grossularia × downingiana, commonly known as the Downing's gooseberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Grossularia europaea and Grossularia coccinea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety and ripening stage. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
