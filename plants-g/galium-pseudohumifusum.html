Galium pseudohumifusum

Galium pseudohumifusum, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Galium pseudohumifusum is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
