Gerascanthus cordifolius

Gerascanthus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved gerascanthus, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by botanists and plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
