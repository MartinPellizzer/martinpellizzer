Geranium coronopifolium

Geranium coronopifolium, commonly known as the crownleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
