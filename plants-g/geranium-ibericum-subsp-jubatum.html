Geranium ibericum subsp. jubatum

Geranium ibericum subsp.

jubatum, commonly known as the Spanish geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
