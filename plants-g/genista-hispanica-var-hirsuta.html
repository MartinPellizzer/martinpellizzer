Genista hispanica var. hirsuta

Genista hispanica var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Spanish broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
