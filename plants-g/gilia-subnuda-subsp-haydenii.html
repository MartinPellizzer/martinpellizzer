Gilia subnuda subsp. haydenii

Gilia subnuda subsp.

haydenii, commonly known as the Hayden's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive, narrow corolla. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
