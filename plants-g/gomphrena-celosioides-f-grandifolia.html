Gomphrena celosioides f. grandifolia

Gomphrena celosioides f. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright red, pink, or purple bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The foliage is typically gray-green, adding visual interest even when the plant is not in bloom.
