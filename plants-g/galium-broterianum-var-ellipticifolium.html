Galium broterianum var. ellipticifolium

Galium broterianum var. ellipticifolium, commonly known as the elliptic-leaved bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface, and the stems are often prostrate or ascending.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
