Gypsophila filipes

Gypsophila filipes, commonly known as the filiped gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, fluffy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila filipes is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy, lace-like appearance.
