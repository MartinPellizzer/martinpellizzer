Gomphrena viridis

Gomphrena viridis, commonly known as green plume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, feathery bracts that range in color from green to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Gomphrena viridis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
