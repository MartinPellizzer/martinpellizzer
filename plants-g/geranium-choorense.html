Geranium choorense

Geranium choorense, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of five-petaled blooms in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium choorense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
