Gentianodes lowndesii

Gentianodes lowndesii, commonly known as the Lowndes' gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
