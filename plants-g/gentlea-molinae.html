Gentlea molinae

Gentlea molinae, commonly known as the Molina's gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are known for their attractive appearance and ecological importance to pollinators.
