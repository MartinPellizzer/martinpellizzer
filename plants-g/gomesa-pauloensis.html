Gomesa pauloensis

Gomesa pauloensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
