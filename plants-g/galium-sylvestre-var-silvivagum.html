Galium sylvestre var. silvivagum

Galium sylvestre var. silvivagum, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant bears clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in forest understories and garden settings.
