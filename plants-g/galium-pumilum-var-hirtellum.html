Galium pumilum var. hirtellum

Galium pumilum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and stems that often root at the nodes.
