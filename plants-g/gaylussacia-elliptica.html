Gaylussacia elliptica

Gaylussacia elliptica, commonly known as the oval bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the year. Its showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

The oval bearberry produces dark blue berries that are edible but generally not palatable to humans.
