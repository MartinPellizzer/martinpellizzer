Glycine hispida var. affine-lutescens

Glycine hispida var. affine-lutescens, commonly known as the yellow hairy bean, is a variant of the hairy bean plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
