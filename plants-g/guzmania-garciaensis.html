Guzmania garciaensis

Guzmania garciaensis, commonly known as the Garcia's torch plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a torch, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insect life. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Guzmania garciaensis is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
