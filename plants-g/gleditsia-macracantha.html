Gleditsia macracantha

Gleditsia macracantha, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to the central and eastern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed leaves and its distinctive, spiny bur-like fruits that mature in the fall. The tree has a robust, often sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
