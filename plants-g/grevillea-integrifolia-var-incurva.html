Grevillea integrifolia var. incurva

Grevillea integrifolia var. incurva, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and garden environments.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
