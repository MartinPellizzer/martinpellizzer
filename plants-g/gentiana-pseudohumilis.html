Gentiana pseudohumilis

Gentiana pseudohumilis, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. This species typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
