Grevillea barklyana

Grevillea barklyana, commonly known as the Western Australian grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight zigzag pattern, and its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Grevillea barklyana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
