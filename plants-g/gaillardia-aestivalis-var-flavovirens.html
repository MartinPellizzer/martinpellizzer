Gaillardia aestivalis var. flavovirens

Gaillardia aestivalis var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers with greenish centers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and native plant landscapes.
