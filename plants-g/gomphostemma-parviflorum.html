Gomphostemma parviflorum

Gomphostemma parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered gomphostemma, is a member of the Menispermaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its smooth, often reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Gomphostemma parviflorum is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
