Gaylussacia densa

Gaylussacia densa, commonly known as blackhaw viburnium, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many ericaceous plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and turn red in the fall.
