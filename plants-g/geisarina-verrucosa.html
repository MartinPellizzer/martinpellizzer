Geisarina verrucosa

Geisarina verrucosa, commonly known as the rough geisarina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
