Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. cymosa

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. cymosa, commonly known as the blue-fruited hawthorn, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small white flowers. Its fruit is a small, blue-black drupe that ripens in late summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
