Grindelia brachystephana

Grindelia brachystephana, commonly known as the narrowstep gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, sticky leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Grindelia brachystephana is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
