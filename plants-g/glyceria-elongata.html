Glyceria elongata

Glyceria elongata, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly bent or curved, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

Glyceria elongata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
