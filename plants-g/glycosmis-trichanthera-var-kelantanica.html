Glycosmis trichanthera var. kelantanica

Glycosmis trichanthera var. kelantanica, commonly known as the Kelantan gooseberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Thailand.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a slightly rough texture and emits a faint citrus scent. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
