Gnaphalium verbascifolium

Gnaphalium verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly willow or silverleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats, with hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.
