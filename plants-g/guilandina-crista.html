Guilandina crista

Guilandina crista, commonly known as the crown bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Guilandina crista is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
