Gentiana kochiana var. minor

Gentiana kochiana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
