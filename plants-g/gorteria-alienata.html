Gorteria alienata

Gorteria alienata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Gorteria alienata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
