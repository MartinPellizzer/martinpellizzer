Gaultheria trichophylla var. ovata

Gaultheria trichophylla var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its aromatic leaves, which contain compounds used in traditional medicine.
