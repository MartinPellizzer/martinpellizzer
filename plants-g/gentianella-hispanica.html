Gentianella hispanica

Gentianella hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually basal, opposite, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

Gentianella hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
