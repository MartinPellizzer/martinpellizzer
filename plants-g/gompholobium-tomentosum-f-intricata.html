Gompholobium tomentosum f. intricata

Gompholobium tomentosum f. intricata, commonly known as the intricate hairy wattle, is a variant of the hairy wattle species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage and bright yellow floral clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

The intricate form of this plant makes it a visually striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
