Gentianella calanchoides

Gentianella calanchoides, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentianella calanchoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
