Geissorhiza imbricata var. concolor

Geissorhiza imbricata var. concolor, commonly known as the purple-flowered geissorhiza, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.
