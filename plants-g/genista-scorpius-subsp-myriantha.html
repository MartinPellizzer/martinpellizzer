Genista scorpius subsp. myriantha

Genista scorpius subsp.

myriantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical subspecies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.
