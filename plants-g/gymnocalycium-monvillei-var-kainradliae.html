Gymnocalycium monvillei var. kainradliae

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. kainradliae, commonly known as the Kainradliae Moon Cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a single, fleshy, globular body that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its compact size and striking appearance.
