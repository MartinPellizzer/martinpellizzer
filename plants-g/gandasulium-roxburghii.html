Gandasulium roxburghii

Gandasulium roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian frankincense tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The tree is characterized by its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, elliptical leaflets.

The bark is thick and fissured, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters in height.
