Glycinopsis coccinea

Glycinopsis coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet wisteria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, pendulous clusters of flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

However, due to its invasive potential, it is often restricted in certain regions.
