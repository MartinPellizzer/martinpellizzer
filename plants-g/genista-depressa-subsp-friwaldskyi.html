Genista depressa subsp. friwaldskyi

Genista depressa subsp.

friwaldskyi, commonly known as the Friwaldsky's genista, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has compound leaves with fine, needle-like leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in short, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
