Gonzalea asperula

Gonzalea asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, often gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

Gonzalea asperula is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.
