Guarea subsetulosa

Guarea subsetulosa, commonly known as the "southern guarea," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has a smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
