Gilia pinnata var. orthotuba

Gilia pinnata var. orthotuba, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slight pubescence. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.
