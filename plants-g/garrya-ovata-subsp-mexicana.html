Garrya ovata subsp. mexicana

Garrya ovata subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as Mexican olive, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
