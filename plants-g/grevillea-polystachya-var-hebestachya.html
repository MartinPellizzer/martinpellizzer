Grevillea polystachya var. hebestachya

Grevillea polystachya var. hebestachya, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking red or orange flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are grey-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
