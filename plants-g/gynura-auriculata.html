Gynura auriculata

Gynura auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf spurge, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive ear-like auricle at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Gynura auriculata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
