Genista demnatensis

Genista demnatensis, commonly known as the Demnat's broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, serrated leaflets.

Genista demnatensis is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrubland habitats.
