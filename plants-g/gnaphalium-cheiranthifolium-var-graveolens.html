Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. graveolens

Gnaphalium cheiranthifolium var. graveolens, commonly known as the stinking willow-leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces compact, spherical clusters of flower heads that are often used in dried floral arrangements. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is native to parts of southern Africa.

The variety graveolens is distinguished by its stronger, more pungent aroma compared to other varieties.
