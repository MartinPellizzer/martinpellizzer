Gigantochloa vinhphuica

Gigantochloa vinhphuica, commonly known as the Vinh Phu bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of several meters. The plant is notable for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
