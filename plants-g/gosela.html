Gosela

Gosela, commonly known as the false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous and deep. Gosela thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
