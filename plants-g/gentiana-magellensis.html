Gentiana magellensis

Gentiana magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountain meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana magellensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
