Gymnocalycium brachyanthum

Gymnocalycium brachyanthum, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Gymnocalycium brachyanthum is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within the Cactaceae family.
