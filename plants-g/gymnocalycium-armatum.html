Gymnocalycium armatum

Gymnocalycium armatum, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces bright, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Gymnocalycium armatum is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
