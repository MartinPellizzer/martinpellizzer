Gelasia eriophora

Gelasia eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf gelasia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Gelasia eriophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
