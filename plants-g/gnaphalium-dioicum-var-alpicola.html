Gnaphalium dioicum var. alpicola

Gnaphalium dioicum var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
