Garcinia viridiflava

Garcinia viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia viridiflava is valued for its potential health benefits, including its high concentration of hydroxycitric acid.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it an important species in both horticulture and phytotherapy.
