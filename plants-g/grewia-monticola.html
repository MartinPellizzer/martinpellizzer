Grewia monticola

Grewia monticola, commonly known as the mountain grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Grewia monticola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
