Gnaphalium landbeckii

Gnaphalium landbeckii, commonly known as the silverleaf weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white florets that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.
