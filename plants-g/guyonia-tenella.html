Guyonia tenella

Guyonia tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guyonia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a compact cluster.

Guyonia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
