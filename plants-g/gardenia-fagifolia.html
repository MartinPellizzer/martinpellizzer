Gardenia fagifolia

Gardenia fagifolia, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and scent.

Gardenia fagifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
