Gilia gracilis subvar. bellidifolia

Gilia gracilis subvar. bellidifolia, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It blooms in the summer months, producing numerous flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in native plant gardens.
