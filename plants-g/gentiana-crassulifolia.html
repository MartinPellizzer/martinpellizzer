Gentiana crassulifolia

Gentiana crassulifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in its harsh environment.

Gentiana crassulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
