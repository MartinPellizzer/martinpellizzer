Garcinia myrtifolia

Garcinia myrtifolia, commonly known as the Java apple or mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, citrusy flavor. Garcinia myrtifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
