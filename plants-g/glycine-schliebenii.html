Glycine schliebenii

Glycine schliebenii, commonly known as the Schliebenii bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Glycine schliebenii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is also cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
