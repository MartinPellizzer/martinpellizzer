Gymnocalycium carminanthum

Gymnocalycium carminanthum, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green with red or purple spines. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Gymnocalycium carminanthum is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
