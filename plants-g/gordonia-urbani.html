Gordonia urbani

Gordonia urbani, commonly known as the urban gordonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and corky, which provides some resistance to environmental stress.

Gordonia urbani is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
