Gerascanthus morelosanus

Gerascanthus morelosanus, commonly known as the Morelos geranium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and palmate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
