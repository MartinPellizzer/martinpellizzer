Greenwoodia

Greenwoodia, commonly known as the "evergreen fern," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. Greenwoodia is native to regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
