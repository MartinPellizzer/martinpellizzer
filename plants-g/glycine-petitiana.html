Glycine petitiana

Glycine petitiana, commonly known as the smallleaf soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its indeterminate growth habit and ability to form dense foliage. It is often cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties and use in agricultural systems.

Glycine petitiana is also valued for its potential in sustainable farming practices.
