Gymnocalycium poeschlii subsp. dolezalii

Gymnocalycium poeschlii subsp.

dolezalii, commonly known as the Poeschli's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of spines.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.

The subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-draining soil.
