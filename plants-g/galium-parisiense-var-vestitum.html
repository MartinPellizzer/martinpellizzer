Galium parisiense var. vestitum

Galium parisiense var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
