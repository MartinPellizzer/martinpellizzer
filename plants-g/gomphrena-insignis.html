Gomphrena insignis

Gomphrena insignis, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking, spiky inflorescences that range in color from red to purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered into dense, globular heads. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

Gomphrena insignis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
