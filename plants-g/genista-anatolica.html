Genista anatolica

Genista anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian Broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
