Gentiana naitoana

Gentiana naitoana, commonly known as the Naito's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
