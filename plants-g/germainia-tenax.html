Germainia tenax

Germainia tenax, commonly known as the sticky germander, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
