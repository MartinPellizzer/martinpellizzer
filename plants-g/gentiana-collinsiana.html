Gentiana collinsiana

Gentiana collinsiana, commonly known as Collins' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana collinsiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
