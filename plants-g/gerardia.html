Gerardia

Gerardia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often blue or purple, ray florets and bract-like leaves that resemble petals. Gerardia species are typically found in coastal areas, where they thrive in saline and sandy soils. They have a compact growth habit and are often used in ornamental gardening for their visual appeal.

The genus includes several species, such as Gerardia glomerata and Gerardia thunbergii, which are valued for their hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
