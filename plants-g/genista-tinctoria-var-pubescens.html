Genista tinctoria var. pubescens

Genista tinctoria var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in dye production.
