Galega purpurea

Galega purpurea, commonly known as the purple hop-crop, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. Galega purpurea is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
