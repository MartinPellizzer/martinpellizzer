Galactia speciosa f. paraguariensis

Galactia speciosa f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to soil fertility.
