Gladiolus richardsiae

Gladiolus richardsiae, commonly known as the Richards' gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers typically come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, with a striking, sword-shaped appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements.
