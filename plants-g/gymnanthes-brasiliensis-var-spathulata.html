Gymnanthes brasiliensis var. spathulata

Gymnanthes brasiliensis var. spathulata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
