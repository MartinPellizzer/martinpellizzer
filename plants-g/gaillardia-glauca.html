Gaillardia glauca

Gaillardia glauca, commonly known as blue gaylordia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like blooms. The plant produces showy, semi-double flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a blueish or purplish center. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gaillardia glauca is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
