Genipa mussaendae

Genipa mussaendae, commonly known as the red-flowered genipa, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Mussaenaceae and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are usually ovate and have a glossy appearance, while its bark is smooth and often reddish in color.

Genipa mussaendae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
