Gastrolobium punctatum

Gastrolobium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
