Gaillardia aristata

Gaillardia aristata, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in a fluffy, composite flower head.

Gaillardia aristata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
