Geranium molle subsp. villosum

Geranium molle subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the downy geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically rounded with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
