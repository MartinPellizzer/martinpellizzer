Graffenrieda sessilifolia subsp. cardonae

Graffenrieda sessilifolia subsp.

cardonae, commonly known as the Cardonae graffenrieda, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant a unique, almost shrub-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
