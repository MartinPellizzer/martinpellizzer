Gilia aggregata var. candida

Gilia aggregata var. candida, commonly known as the white gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and the stems are slender and branched.

The white gilia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
