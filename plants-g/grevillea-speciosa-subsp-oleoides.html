Grevillea speciosa subsp. oleoides

Grevillea speciosa subsp.

oleoides, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It features striking, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
