Guidonia ulmifolia

Guidonia ulmifolia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruits are small, aggregate fruits that mature into a dark red or black color in autumn.

Guidonia ulmifolia is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
