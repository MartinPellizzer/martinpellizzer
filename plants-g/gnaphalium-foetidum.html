Gnaphalium foetidum

Gnaphalium foetidum, commonly known as stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, and the plant produces a strong, often unpleasant odor.

Gnaphalium foetidum is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows.
