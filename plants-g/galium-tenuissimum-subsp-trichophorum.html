Galium tenuissimum subsp. trichophorum

Galium tenuissimum subsp.

trichophorum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, with small, opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are usually arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the plant is known for its delicate appearance. Galium tenuissimum subsp.

trichophorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
