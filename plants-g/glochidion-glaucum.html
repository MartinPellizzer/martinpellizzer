Glochidion glaucum

Glochidion glaucum, commonly known as the silver glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Glochidion glaucum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
