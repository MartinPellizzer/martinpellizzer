Geranium hirsutum

Geranium hirsutum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Geranium hirsutum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
