Grammica macvaughii

Grammica macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's gramminoid, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
