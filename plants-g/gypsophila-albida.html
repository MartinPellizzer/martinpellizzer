Gypsophila albida

Gypsophila albida, commonly known as the snowball gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces elongated, branched flower heads that resemble small snowballs.

Gypsophila albida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
