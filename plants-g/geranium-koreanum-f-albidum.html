Geranium koreanum f. albidum

Geranium koreanum f. albidum, commonly known as the Korean geranium or white geranium, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red calyx that persists after the petals fall. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
