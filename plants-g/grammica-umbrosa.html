Grammica umbrosa

Grammica umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a delicate perennial plant native to shaded forest environments.

It features slender, arching fronds that are typically a deep green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Grammica umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add a lush, green texture to woodland gardens.
