Gymnadenia miniata f. hygrophila

Gymnadenia miniata f. hygrophila, commonly known as the wetland gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, boggy environments. The plant features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense clumps.

This subspecies is adapted to wet soils and is an indicator species for hydric habitats.
