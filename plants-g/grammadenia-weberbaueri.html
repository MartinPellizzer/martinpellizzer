Grammadenia weberbaueri

Grammadenia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's grammadenia, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru, where it thrives in humid, high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Grammadenia weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic orchids.
