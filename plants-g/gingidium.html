Gingidium

Gingidium, commonly known as the Indian ginger or wild ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features erect, branched stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically yellow or greenish in color.

Gingidium is valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
