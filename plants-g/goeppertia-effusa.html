Goeppertia effusa

Goeppertia effusa, commonly known as the Snowy Bucephalandra, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth pattern and its ability to produce long, thin, white to pale green shoots that resemble delicate tendrils. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value and ease of care. It has a unique ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it beneficial for maintaining water quality in planted tanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among aquarists seeking both aesthetic appeal and functional benefits.
