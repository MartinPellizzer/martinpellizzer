Grewia chungii

Grewia chungii, commonly known as the Chinese globemallow, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Grewia chungii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
