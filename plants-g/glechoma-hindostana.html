Glechoma hindostana

Glechoma hindostana, commonly known as Persian ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

The small, blue or violet flowers are borne in whorled clusters and bloom from spring to early summer.
