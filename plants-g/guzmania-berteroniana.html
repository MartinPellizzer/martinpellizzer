Guzmania berteroniana

Guzmania berteroniana, commonly known as the pink guzmania, is a striking epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that often displays shades of pink and red. The plant features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening, though it requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.

The plant's unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
