Galvezia leucantha subsp. pubescens

Galvezia leucantha subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf galvezia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas. Its botanical features include a woody stem and alternate, lance-shaped foliage.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
