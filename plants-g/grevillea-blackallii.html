Grevillea blackallii

Grevillea blackallii, commonly known as the Blackall's waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red or crimson flower spikes that emerge from a dense cluster of leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrublands and open woodlands. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Grevillea blackallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
