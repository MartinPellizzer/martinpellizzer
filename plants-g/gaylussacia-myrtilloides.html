Gaylussacia myrtilloides

Gaylussacia myrtilloides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many ericaceous plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
