Gentiana incisiflora

Gentiana incisiflora, commonly known as the sharp-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Gentiana incisiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
