Gnaphalium undulifolium

Gnaphalium undulifolium, commonly known as the undulating leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically narrow, undulating, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.
