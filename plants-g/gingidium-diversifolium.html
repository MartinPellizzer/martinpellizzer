Gingidium diversifolium

Gingidium diversifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf gingidium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Gingidium diversifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.
