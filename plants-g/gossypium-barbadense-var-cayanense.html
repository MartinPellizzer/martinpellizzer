Gossypium barbadense var. cayanense

Gossypium barbadense var. cayanense, commonly known as the Cayman Island cotton, is a variety of cotton native to the Cayman Islands.

It is characterized by its fine, long staple fibers, which are highly valued for their strength and softness. The plant typically grows in coastal areas with well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, and it produces fluffy bolls containing the cotton fibers.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological adaptations.
