Guibourtia leonensis

Guibourtia leonensis, commonly known as African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
