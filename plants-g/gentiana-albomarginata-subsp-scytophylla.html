Gentiana albomarginata subsp. scytophylla

Gentiana albomarginata subsp.

scytophylla, commonly known as the white-margin gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers, which are typically tubular with white margins, and its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
