Gmelina papuana

Gmelina papuana, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Gmelina papuana is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and ornamental qualities.
