Geissorhiza ixioides

Geissorhiza ixioides, commonly known as the yellow bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It produces a dense cluster of fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geissorhiza ixioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
