Gerardia heterophylla

Gerardia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable gerardia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in shape depending on the environmental conditions. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often growing in moist, calcareous soils.

Gerardia heterophylla is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
