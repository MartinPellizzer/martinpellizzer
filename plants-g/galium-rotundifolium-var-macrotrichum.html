Galium rotundifolium var. macrotrichum

Galium rotundifolium var. macrotrichum, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
