Glycosmis trifoliata

Glycosmis trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf mallow, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Glycosmis trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
