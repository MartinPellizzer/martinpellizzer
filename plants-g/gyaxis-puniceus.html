Gyaxis puniceus

Gyaxis puniceus, commonly known as the red gyaxis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs.

Gyaxis puniceus is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
