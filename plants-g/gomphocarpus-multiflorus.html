Gomphocarpus multiflorus

Gomphocarpus multiflorus, commonly known as the Bluebonnet or Crown of Thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it store water, making it drought-tolerant. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Gomphocarpus multiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
