Gastrodia uraiensis

Gastrodia uraiensis, commonly known as the Japanese thunder god vine, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of trees like the Japanese cedar. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous structure and its ability to produce a distinctive, spicy aroma. It blooms with small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in a spike-like formation.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Gastrodia uraiensis is considered a valuable and endangered species in conservation efforts.
