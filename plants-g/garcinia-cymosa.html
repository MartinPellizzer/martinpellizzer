Garcinia cymosa

Garcinia cymosa, commonly known as the tropical garcinia or white garcinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that contain a high concentration of bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia cymosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
