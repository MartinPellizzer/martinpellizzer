Guilandina dioica

Guilandina dioica, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Guilandina dioica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
