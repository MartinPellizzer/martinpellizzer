Gypsophila syriaca

Gypsophila syriaca, commonly known as Syrian snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Syria and Turkey.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It blooms in early summer, producing small, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila syriaca is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
