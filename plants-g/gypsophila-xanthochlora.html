Gypsophila xanthochlora

Gypsophila xanthochlora, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
