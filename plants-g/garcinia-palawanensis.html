Garcinia palawanensis

Garcinia palawanensis, commonly known as Palawan garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Garcinia palawanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its habitat is primarily restricted to the lowland rainforests of Palawan and nearby islands.
