Gnaphalium canariense

Gnaphalium canariense, commonly known as Canary Islands cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance.
