Gamblea

Gamblea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Gamblea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

It is also valued for its ability to provide shade and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
