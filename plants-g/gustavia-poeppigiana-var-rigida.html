Gustavia poeppigiana var. rigida

Gustavia poeppigiana var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid gustavia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
