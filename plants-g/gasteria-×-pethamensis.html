Gasteria × pethamensis

Gasteria × pethamensis, commonly known as the hybrid gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks that bear small, tubular flowers, usually in shades of yellow or orange. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Gasteria × pethamensis thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.
