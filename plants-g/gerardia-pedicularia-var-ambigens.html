Gerardia pedicularia var. ambigens

Gerardia pedicularia var. ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous gerardia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a species of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
