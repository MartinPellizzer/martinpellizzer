Grastidium salaccense

Grastidium salaccense, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to Singapore.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
