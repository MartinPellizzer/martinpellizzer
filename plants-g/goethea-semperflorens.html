Goethea semperflorens

Goethea semperflorens, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, colorful florets that resemble tiny daisies. The plant is known for its ability to retain its color and shape for an extended period, making it popular in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Goethea semperflorens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in various environmental conditions.
