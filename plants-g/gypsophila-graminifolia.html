Gypsophila graminifolia

Gypsophila graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its slender, grass-like leaves give it a unique appearance, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its ethereal beauty.

Gypsophila graminifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
