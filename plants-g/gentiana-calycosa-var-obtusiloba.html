Gentiana calycosa var. obtusiloba

Gentiana calycosa var. obtusiloba, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded apex, giving it its specific variety name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
