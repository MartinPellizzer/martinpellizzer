Galium aristatum subsp. papillosum

Galium aristatum subsp.

papillosum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often reddish or pinkish stems, which are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
