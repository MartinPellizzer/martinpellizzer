Gentiana lilacina

Gentiana lilacina, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its flowers are tubular with five lobes.

Gentiana lilacina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
