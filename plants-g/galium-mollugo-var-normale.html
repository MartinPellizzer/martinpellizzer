Galium mollugo var. normale

Galium mollugo var. normale, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
