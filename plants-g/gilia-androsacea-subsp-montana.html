Gilia androsacea subsp. montana

Gilia androsacea subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It blooms in the summer months, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
