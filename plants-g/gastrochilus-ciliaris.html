Gastrochilus ciliaris

Gastrochilus ciliaris, commonly known as the fringed lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive fringed lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help retain moisture.

The orchid's flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators.
