Gaura coccinea var. integerrima

Gaura coccinea var. integerrima, commonly known as the scarlet gaura, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
