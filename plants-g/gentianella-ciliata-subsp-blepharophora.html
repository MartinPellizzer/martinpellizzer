Gentianella ciliata subsp. blepharophora

Gentianella ciliata subsp.

blepharophora, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely ciliated leaves and the presence of fringed petals, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
