Githopsis specularioides f. hirsuta

Githopsis specularioides f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy githopsis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
