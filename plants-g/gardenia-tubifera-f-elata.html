Gardenia tubifera f. elata

Gardenia tubifera f. elata, commonly known as the giant gardenia, is a rare and striking variant of the gardenia genus.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and fragrant white flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
