Gentianella odontosepala

Gentianella odontosepala, commonly known as the toothed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in high-altitude grasslands or forests. Its flowers are characterized by their five deeply lobed petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns.

The leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
