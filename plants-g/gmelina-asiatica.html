Gmelina asiatica

Gmelina asiatica, commonly known as the Indian tulip or champa, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a fast-growing species with a straight trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.

Its wood is lightweight and is used in construction and furniture-making.
