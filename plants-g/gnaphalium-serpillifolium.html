Gnaphalium serpillifolium

Gnaphalium serpillifolium, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, often found in dry, disturbed soils. The plant features small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are typically woolly and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
