Galium aparine f. leiocarpum

Galium aparine f. leiocarpum, commonly known as smooth bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its stems are typically hairless or only slightly hairy. Its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive notched or lobed appearance, and the plant produces small, round fruits that are often covered in hooked bristles.

This subspecies is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
