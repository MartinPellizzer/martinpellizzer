Glyceria tenuifolia

Glyceria tenuifolia, commonly known as slender glyceria, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland health.
