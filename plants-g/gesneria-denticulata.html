Gesneria denticulata

Gesneria denticulata, commonly known as the toothed gesneria, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Gesneria denticulata produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom during the warmer months.
