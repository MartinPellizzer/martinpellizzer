Glochidion oblongifolium

Glochidion oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
