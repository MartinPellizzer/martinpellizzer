Gentlea parviflora

Gentlea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Gentlea parviflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
