Glycosmis oxyphylla

Glycosmis oxyphylla, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Glycosmis oxyphylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
