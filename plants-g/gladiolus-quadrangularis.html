Gladiolus quadrangularis

Gladiolus quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust stem that is often quadrangular in cross-section, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are borne in racemes and come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
