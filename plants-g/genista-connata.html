Genista connata

Genista connata, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Genista connata is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy plant in maritime environments.
