Geranium rubifolium

Geranium rubifolium, commonly known as the red-leaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
