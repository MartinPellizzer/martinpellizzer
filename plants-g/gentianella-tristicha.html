Gentianella tristicha

Gentianella tristicha, commonly known as the weedy gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has slender, branching stems with small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces.

Gentianella tristicha is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
