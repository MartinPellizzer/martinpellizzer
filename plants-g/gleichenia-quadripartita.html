Gleichenia quadripartita

Gleichenia quadripartita, commonly known as the four-parted fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically divided into four parts, giving it its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually light green and have a soft, leathery texture.

Gleichenia quadripartita is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and greenhouses.
