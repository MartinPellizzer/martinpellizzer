Gynopogon ruscifolius

Gynopogon ruscifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf gynopogon, is a flowering plant species native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Gynopogon ruscifolius is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants such as buckwheat and sorrel.
