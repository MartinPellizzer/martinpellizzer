Gentiana meyeri

Gentiana meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often unbranched and erect.

Gentiana meyeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
