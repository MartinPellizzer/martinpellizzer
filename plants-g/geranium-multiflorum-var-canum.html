Geranium multiflorum var. canum

Geranium multiflorum var. canum, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and adaptability to various garden conditions.
