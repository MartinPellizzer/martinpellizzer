Guettarda cordata

Guettarda cordata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its most distinctive feature is the long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The flowers are fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
