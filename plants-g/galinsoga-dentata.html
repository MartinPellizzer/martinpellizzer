Galinsoga dentata

Galinsoga dentata, commonly known as the common gallinsonga, is an annual herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often toothed or lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Galinsoga dentata is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
