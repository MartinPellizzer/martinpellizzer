Gongora boracayanensis

Gongora boracayanensis, commonly known as the Boracay ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and unique, ghost-like flower shape. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate patterns, adapting to its specific ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
