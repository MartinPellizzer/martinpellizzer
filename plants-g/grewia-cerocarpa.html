Grewia cerocarpa

Grewia cerocarpa, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, red, spherical drupe that splits open to release seeds.

Grewia cerocarpa is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
