Glyptopetalum poilanei

Glyptopetalum poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers with intricate petal arrangements. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives in the cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple, creating a visually captivating display.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Glyptopetalum poilanei is considered a conservation concern.
