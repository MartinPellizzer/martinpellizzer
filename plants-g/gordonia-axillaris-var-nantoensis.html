Gordonia axillaris var. nantoensis

Gordonia axillaris var. nantoensis, commonly known as the Nantoensis gordonia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
