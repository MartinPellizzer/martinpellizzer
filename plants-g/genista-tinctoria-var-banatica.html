Genista tinctoria var. banatica

Genista tinctoria var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat dwarf broom, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
