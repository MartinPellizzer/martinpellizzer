Garcinia cataractalis

Garcinia cataractalis, commonly known as the golden apple or cataract tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright yellow or orange berries that are edible. Garcinia cataractalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
