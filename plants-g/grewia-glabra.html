Grewia glabra

Grewia glabra, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a globular appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Grewia glabra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
