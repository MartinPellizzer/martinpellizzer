Gomphrena exaltata

Gomphrena exaltata, commonly known as the tall globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Gomphrena exaltata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
