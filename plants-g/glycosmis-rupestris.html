Glycosmis rupestris

Glycosmis rupestris, commonly known as the rock apple or wild mango, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but have a sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky soils.

Glycosmis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
