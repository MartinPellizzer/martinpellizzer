Glossorhyncha inconspicua

Glossorhyncha inconspicua, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and showy, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its delicate nature and habitat requirements, it is considered endangered in many areas.
