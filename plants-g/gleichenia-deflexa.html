Gleichenia deflexa

Gleichenia deflexa, commonly known as the flexible shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often curled when young. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia deflexa is widely distributed across temperate regions and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
