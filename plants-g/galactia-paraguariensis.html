Galactia paraguariensis

Galactia paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its pods contain seeds that are encased in a fibrous, hairy covering.

Galactia paraguariensis is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
