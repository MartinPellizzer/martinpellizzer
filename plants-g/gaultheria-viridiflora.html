Gaultheria viridiflora

Gaultheria viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered wintergreen, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. Gaultheria viridiflora is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
