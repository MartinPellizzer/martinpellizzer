Gongora arcuata

Gongora arcuata, commonly known as the curved lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals and a distinctive, recurved lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental value.

Gongora arcuata is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and unique morphology.
