Gentiana grumii

Gentiana grumii, commonly known as the Ethiopian gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, cobalt-blue to violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Gentiana grumii is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or violet flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in its native region.
