Geranium reniforme

Geranium reniforme, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
