Geranium campanulatum

Geranium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring through summer.
