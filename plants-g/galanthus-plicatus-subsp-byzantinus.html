Galanthus plicatus subsp. byzantinus

Galanthus plicatus subsp.

byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkans and parts of Turkey. It produces delicate white flowers with a distinctive greenish tinge, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has strap-shaped leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and its flowers have two outer petals that are reflexed or twisted. It thrives in woodland or shaded areas with moist, well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
