Gerardia linarioides var. ramosissima

Gerardia linarioides var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
