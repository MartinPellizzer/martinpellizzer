Gleditsia microcarpa

Gleditsia microcarpa, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and its distinctive, compound leaves that are typically 10 to 20 inches long. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in drooping clusters, which are followed by spiny, woody fruits known as burrs. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly adaptable to different environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and urban landscaping.
