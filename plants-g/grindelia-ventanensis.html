Grindelia ventanensis

Grindelia ventanensis, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
