Gleichenia cunninghamii

Gleichenia cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's Gleichenia, is a fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and fronds that are typically divided into lanceolate segments. The plant is native to Australia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Gleichenia cunninghamii plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
