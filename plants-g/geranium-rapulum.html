Geranium rapulum

Geranium rapulum, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range from pink to purple, bloom in late summer and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Geranium rapulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or alpine displays.
