Geum chilense var. grandiflorum

Geum chilense var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Chilean avens, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are basal, simple, and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
