Gentiana weschniakowii

Gentiana weschniakowii, commonly known as the Western gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with five lobes, and are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lanceolate, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Gentiana weschniakowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
