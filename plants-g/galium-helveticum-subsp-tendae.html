Galium helveticum subsp. tendae

Galium helveticum subsp.

tendae, commonly known as the Alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.
