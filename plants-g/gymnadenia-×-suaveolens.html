Gymnadenia × suaveolens

Gymnadenia × suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented grass-pink, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with slender stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Gymnadenia × suaveolens is often found in meadows and woodland edges, where it contributes to the floral diversity of these habitats.
