Galium aculeolatum

Galium aculeolatum, commonly known as prickly bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium aculeolatum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
