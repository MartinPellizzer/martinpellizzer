Gymnocalycium pflanzii

Gymnocalycium pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spherical or cylindrical stem that often appears segmented. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Gymnocalycium pflanzii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
