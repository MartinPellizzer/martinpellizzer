Gymnadenia conopsea var. praecox

Gymnadenia conopsea var. praecox, commonly known as the early sweet-flag, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and spikes of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. This variety is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in open woodlands and meadows. The plant blooms in early summer, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a relatively rare species, often requiring specific habitat conditions to thrive.
