Glochidion johnstonei

Glochidion johnstonei, commonly known as the Johnstone's glabrous hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
