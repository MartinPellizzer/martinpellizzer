Garcinia umbonata

Garcinia umbonata, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to round, green fruits with a white, fleshy pulp. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in subtropical regions.
