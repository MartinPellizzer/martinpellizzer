Gymnosporia oxycarpa

Gymnosporia oxycarpa, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and woody, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are yellow to orange in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gymnosporia oxycarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
