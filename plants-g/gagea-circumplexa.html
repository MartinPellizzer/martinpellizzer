Gagea circumplexa

Gagea circumplexa, commonly known as the clustered gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Gagea circumplexa is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
