Gaylussacia bracteata

Gaylussacia bracteata, commonly known as the bracted highbush blueberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue to black berry that ripens in the summer, providing food for various wildlife species.

The leaves are oval with a smooth texture and turn red in the fall, adding to the plant's ornamental value.
