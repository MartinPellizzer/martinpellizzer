Grangea anthemoides

Grangea anthemoides, commonly known as the rock jasmine, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Grangea anthemoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate dry conditions.
