Geranium proximum

Geranium proximum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy pink or red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
