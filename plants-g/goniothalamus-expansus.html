Goniothalamus expansus

Goniothalamus expansus, commonly known as the broad-leafed tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by berries containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.
