Geissomeria

Geissomeria, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Geissomeria decumbens, which is native to the western United States. The plants are shrubs or subshrubs, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats. Their flowers are small, white or pink, and often have a pleasant fragrance.

The leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
