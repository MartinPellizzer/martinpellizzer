Glycine wightii var. longicauda

Glycine wightii var. longicauda, commonly known as the long-tailed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with long, slender tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers followed by elongated, slender pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
