Gentiana meiantha

Gentiana meiantha, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are tubular with two lips, and it blooms during the summer months.

Gentiana meiantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
