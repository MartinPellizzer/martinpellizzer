Gossypium jamaicense f. janiphifolium

Gossypium jamaicense f. janiphifolium, commonly known as the Jamaican cotton or Janiphifolium cotton, is a cultivated variant of the cotton plant native to Jamaica.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and often lobed leaves, which set it apart from other cotton species. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers that are harvested for textile use, similar to other cotton varieties. It thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and historical significance in Caribbean agriculture.
