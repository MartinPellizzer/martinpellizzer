Grammitis calliope

Grammitis calliope, commonly known as the golden grammitis, is a member of the Pteridaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often bright green with a metallic sheen, especially under sunlight. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure.

Grammitis calliope is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
