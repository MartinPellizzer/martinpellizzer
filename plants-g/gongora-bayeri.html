Gongora bayeri

Gongora bayeri, commonly known as the Beyer's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

Gongora bayeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
