Glyceria maritima subsp. foucaudii

Glyceria maritima subsp.

foucaudii, commonly known as the coastal glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and estuarine environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
