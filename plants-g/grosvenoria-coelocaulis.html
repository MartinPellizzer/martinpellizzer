Grosvenoria coelocaulis

Grosvenoria coelocaulis, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, cork-like bark that becomes thick and flaky with age. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Grosvenoria coelocaulis is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun.
