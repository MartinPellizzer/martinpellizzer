Guaiacum verticale

Guaiacum verticale, commonly known as the Virgin's tears or the sacred wood, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody, aromatic trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, red berries. Its bark and wood have been traditionally used in medicine and for dyeing purposes.

Guaiacum verticale is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
