Galium mucroniferum var. dregeanum

Galium mucroniferum var. dregeanum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
