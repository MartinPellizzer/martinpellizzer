Garcinia monosperma

Garcinia monosperma, commonly known as the kokum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
