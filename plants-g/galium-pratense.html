Galium pratense

Galium pratense, commonly known as bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium pratense is commonly found in meadows, woodland edges, and moist soils, and it is known for its fragrant, slightly sweet scent.
