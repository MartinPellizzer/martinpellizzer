Goldfussia tristis

Goldfussia tristis, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a purple or violet tinge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base, and it produces long, drooping inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Goldfussia tristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
