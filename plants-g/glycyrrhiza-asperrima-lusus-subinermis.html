Glycyrrhiza asperrima lusus subinermis

Glycyrrhiza asperrima lusus subinermis, commonly known as the rough licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several seeds. Its root is the primary part used, known for its intense sweetness and medicinal properties.

This species is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
