Goodenia taylori

Goodenia taylori, commonly known as Taylor's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
