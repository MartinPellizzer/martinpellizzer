Galium boreale var. roseum

Galium boreale var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in loose racemes. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This species is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinators.
