Gentiana autumnalis

Gentiana autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
