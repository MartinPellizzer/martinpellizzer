Gleichenia lepidota

Gleichenia lepidota, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are often divided into small, overlapping segments that give it a lush, fern-like appearance.

Gleichenia lepidota is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture.
