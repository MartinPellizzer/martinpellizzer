Galium rubidiflorum

Galium rubidiflorum, commonly known as scarlet wood-sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a trailing or ascending growth habit, often forming dense mats.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
