Gossypium microcarpum var. rufum

Gossypium microcarpum var. rufum, commonly known as the red-seed cotton, is a botanical variant of the cotton genus Gossypium.

It is characterized by its small, hairy capsules and distinctive red-colored seeds. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soils and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Due to its unique seed color and morphology, it is of interest in both agricultural and horticultural research.
