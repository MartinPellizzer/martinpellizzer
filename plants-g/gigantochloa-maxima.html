Gigantochloa maxima

Gigantochloa maxima, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features hollow, jointed stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gigantochloa maxima is often used in landscaping and for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
