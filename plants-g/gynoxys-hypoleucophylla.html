Gynoxys hypoleucophylla

Gynoxys hypoleucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed gynoxys, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly white or pale green, with a contrasting dark green margin. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its foliage. Its unique leaf coloration and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Gynoxys hypoleucophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
