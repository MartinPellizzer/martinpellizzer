Gerascanthus vestitus

Gerascanthus vestitus, commonly known as the silver-stemmed geranium, is a striking plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-veined stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, fuzzy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
