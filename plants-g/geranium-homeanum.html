Geranium homeanum

Geranium homeanum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive tubular throat. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Geranium homeanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
