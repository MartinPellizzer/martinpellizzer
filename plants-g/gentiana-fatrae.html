Gentiana fatrae

Gentiana fatrae, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
