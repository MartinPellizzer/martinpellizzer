Geranium amoenum

Geranium amoenum, commonly known as the Armenian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branching stems covered with fine hairs and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are ovate to lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
