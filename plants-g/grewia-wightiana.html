Grewia wightiana

Grewia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
