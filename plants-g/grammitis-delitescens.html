Grammitis delitescens

Grammitis delitescens, commonly known as the fading fern, is a delicate epiphytic fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, linear leaves that often appear translucent or pale in color, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to its fragile nature, it requires careful handling and specific environmental conditions to thrive.
