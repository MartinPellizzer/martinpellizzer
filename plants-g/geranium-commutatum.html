Geranium commutatum

Geranium commutatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, pink to violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
