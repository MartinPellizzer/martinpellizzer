Gilbertiodendron quadrifolium

Gilbertiodendron quadrifolium, commonly known as the four-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with four leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
