Gomesa rupestris

Gomesa rupestris, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Gomesa rupestris is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
