Grastidium multistriatum

Grastidium multistriatum, commonly known as the multistratified grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, elongated leaves with distinct longitudinal striations, giving it a unique visual texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its root system is fibrous and extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Grastidium multistriatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
