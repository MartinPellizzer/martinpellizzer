Grevillea lavandulacea subsp. rogersii

Grevillea lavandulacea subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the pink grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, adding visual interest.

The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-like flowers in shades of pink to red, which appear in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
