Garcinia verrucosa subsp. verrucosa

Garcinia verrucosa subsp.

verrucosa, commonly known as the rough Garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange fruits that are covered in a rough, warty surface.

The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties. Its fruits contain high levels of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
