Gentiana aspera f. anisiaca

Gentiana aspera f. anisiaca, commonly known as the Anise Gentian, is a botanical variant of the Gentiana aspera species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathians. The plant features dark blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
