Gymnocalycium tillianum subsp. montanum

Gymnocalycium tillianum subsp.

montanum, commonly known as the mountain moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces spiny, globular stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Gymnocalycium tillianum subsp.

montanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
