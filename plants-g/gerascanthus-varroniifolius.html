Gerascanthus varroniifolius

Gerascanthus varroniifolius, commonly known as the Varro's leafy gerascan, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are deeply lobed and emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet and white, which bloom during the late summer months. Its unique leaf morphology and limited geographic distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires specialized care in cultivation.
