Gambeya perpulchra

Gambeya perpulchra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a broad, spreading canopy with large, palmate leaves that provide dense shade. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

Gambeya perpulchra is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other pollinators.
