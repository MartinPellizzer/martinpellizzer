Galium rubrum var. alpicola

Galium rubrum var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite and often hairy, with a slightly succulent texture.

Galium rubrum var. alpicola is an important part of alpine ecosystems, providing food for various insects and small mammals.
