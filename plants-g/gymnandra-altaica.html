Gymnandra altaica

Gymnandra altaica, commonly known as the Altai gymnandra, is a perennial shrub native to the Altai Mountains in Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by small, edible berries that are rich in nutrients. Gymnandra altaica thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
