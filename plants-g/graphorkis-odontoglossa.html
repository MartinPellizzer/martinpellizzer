Graphorkis odontoglossa

Graphorkis odontoglossa, commonly known as the toothed tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, elongated leaves that are often marked with distinctive patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white with a yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
