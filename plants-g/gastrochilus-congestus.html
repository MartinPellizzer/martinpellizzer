Gastrochilus congestus

Gastrochilus congestus, commonly known as the clustered lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy surface to retain moisture.

The orchid's unique floral structure and adaptability to humid climates make it a notable species in botanical studies.
