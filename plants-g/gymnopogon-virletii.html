Gymnopogon virletii

Gymnopogon virletii, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often feather-like in appearance, giving it its common name. Gymnopogon virletii is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
