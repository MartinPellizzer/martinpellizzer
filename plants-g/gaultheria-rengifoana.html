Gaultheria rengifoana

Gaultheria rengifoana, commonly known as the Andean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are edible, are red or black and contain several seeds.

Gaultheria rengifoana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
