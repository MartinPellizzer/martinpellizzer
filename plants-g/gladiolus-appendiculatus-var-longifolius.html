Gladiolus appendiculatus var. longifolius

Gladiolus appendiculatus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its use in horticultural displays.
