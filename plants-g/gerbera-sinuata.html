Gerbera sinuata

Gerbera sinuata, commonly known as the zigzag gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
