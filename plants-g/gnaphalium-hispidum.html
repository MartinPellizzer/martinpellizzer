Gnaphalium hispidum

Gnaphalium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, aromatic blooms.

Its hairy texture and compact growth make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
