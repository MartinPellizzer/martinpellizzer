Gnaphalium canescens subsp. beneolens

Gnaphalium canescens subsp.

beneolens, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant produces small, fluffy capitula composed of numerous white to pale yellow ray florets. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried flower arrangements.
