Grewia africana var. drummondiana

Grewia africana var. drummondiana, commonly known as the Drummond's globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
