Gentianella saposhnikovii

Gentianella saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist climates. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Gentianella saposhnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
