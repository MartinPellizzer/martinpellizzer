Gentiana rhaetica var. calcicola

Gentiana rhaetica var. calcicola, commonly known as the Rhaetian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its adaptation to harsh mountain environments.
