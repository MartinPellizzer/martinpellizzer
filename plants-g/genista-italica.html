Genista italica

Genista italica, commonly known as the Italian broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by slender, woody pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste ground.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
