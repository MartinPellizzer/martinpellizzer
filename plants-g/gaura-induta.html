Gaura induta

Gaura induta, commonly known as the showy gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, adding a delicate texture to the garden.

Gaura induta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
