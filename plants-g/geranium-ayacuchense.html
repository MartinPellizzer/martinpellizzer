Geranium ayacuchense

Geranium ayacuchense, commonly known as the Ayacuchense geranium, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Geranium ayacuchense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
