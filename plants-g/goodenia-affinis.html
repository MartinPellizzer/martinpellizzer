Goodenia affinis

Goodenia affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Goodenia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
