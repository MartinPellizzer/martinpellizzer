Grevillea shuttleworthiana subsp. canarina

Grevillea shuttleworthiana subsp.

canarina, commonly known as the Canarina Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
