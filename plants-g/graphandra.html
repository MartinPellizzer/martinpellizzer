Graphandra

Graphandra, commonly known as the goldenstar, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Graphandra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
