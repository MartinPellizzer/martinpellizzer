Gaudinia pluriflora

Gaudinia pluriflora, commonly known as the Hawaiian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of Hawaii. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Gaudinia pluriflora plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is a symbol of Hawaii's natural beauty.
