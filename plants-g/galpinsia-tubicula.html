Galpinsia tubicula

Galpinsia tubicula, commonly known as the narrowleaf galpinsia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in its dry environment. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
