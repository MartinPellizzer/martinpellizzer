Gnidia francisci

Gnidia francisci, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a small, deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, waxy flowers that bloom in winter, giving it a striking appearance against the winter landscape. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Gnidia francisci is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
