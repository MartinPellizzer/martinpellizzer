Geocharis rubra var. puberula

Geocharis rubra var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy red geocharis, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are typically borne in dense clusters atop slender stalks. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with a pale green or yellowish hue. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
