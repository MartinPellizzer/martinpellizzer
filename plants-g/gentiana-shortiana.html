Gentiana shortiana

Gentiana shortiana, commonly known as the short's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Gentiana shortiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
