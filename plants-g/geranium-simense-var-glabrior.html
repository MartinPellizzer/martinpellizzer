Geranium simense var. glabrior

Geranium simense var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth, glabrous surface that distinguishes it from other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
