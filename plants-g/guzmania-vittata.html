Guzmania vittata

Guzmania vittata, commonly known as the spotted flame plant, is a tropical bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that resembles a flame, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that can be green, silver, or variegated, with a central cup that collects water. Guzmania vittata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its vibrant appearance and relatively low maintenance.
