Gerascanthus calophyllus

Gerascanthus calophyllus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable feature in botanical gardens and landscaped areas.
