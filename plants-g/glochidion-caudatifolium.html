Glochidion caudatifolium

Glochidion caudatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved glabrous wood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The leaves are usually glossy and have a distinctive acuminate tip, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
