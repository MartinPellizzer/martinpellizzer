Grammitis semihirsuta

Grammitis semihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, thin, and often hairy fronds that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually small, elongated, and have a distinctive appearance with a waxy coating.

Grammitis semihirsuta is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade.
