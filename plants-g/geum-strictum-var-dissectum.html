Geum strictum var. dissectum

Geum strictum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features finely cut, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
