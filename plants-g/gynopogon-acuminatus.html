Gynopogon acuminatus

Gynopogon acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Gynopogon acuminatus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
