Gomphrena riedelii

Gomphrena riedelii, commonly known as the red-headed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, lance-shaped leaves.

Gomphrena riedelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
