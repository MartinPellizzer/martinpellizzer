Gillettia

Gillettia, commonly known as the common gillettia, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, often yellow or white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas. Gillettia species are known for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which make them valuable in soil enrichment.

They are also sometimes cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and flowers.
