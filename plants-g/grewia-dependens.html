Grewia dependens

Grewia dependens, commonly known as the hanging globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a dry, capsule-like structure that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Grewia dependens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
