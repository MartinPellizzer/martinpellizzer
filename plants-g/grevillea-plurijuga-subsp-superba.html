Grevillea plurijuga subsp. superba

Grevillea plurijuga subsp.

superba, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island grevillea, is a striking shrub native to South Australia. It features vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
