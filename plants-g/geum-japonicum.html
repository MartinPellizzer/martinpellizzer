Geum japonicum

Geum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to red. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Geum japonicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
