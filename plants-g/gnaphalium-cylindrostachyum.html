Gnaphalium cylindrostachyum

Gnaphalium cylindrostachyum, commonly known as the cylindrical feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, feathery leaves. The plant produces fluffy, spherical flower heads that are often mistaken for cotton balls. It is native to South America and thrives in moist, open habitats.

Gnaphalium cylindrostachyum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
