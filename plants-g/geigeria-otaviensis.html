Geigeria otaviensis

Geigeria otaviensis, commonly known as the Otavi geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Geigeria otaviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
