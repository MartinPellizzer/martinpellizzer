Gymnanthocereus chlorocarpus

Gymnanthocereus chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green fruit cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, followed by green, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and botanical collections.
