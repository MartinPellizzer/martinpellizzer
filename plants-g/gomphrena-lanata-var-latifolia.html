Gomphrena lanata var. latifolia

Gomphrena lanata var. latifolia, commonly known as the woolly gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, pink to red bracts that resemble cotton-like structures, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by the showy, leaf-like bracts. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is hairy and gray-green, adding texture to garden landscapes.
