Gastrorchis peyrotii

Gastrorchis peyrotii, commonly known as the Peyrot's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and unique growth habit, often growing epiphytically on trees or rocks. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, slender stem that bears a few flowers. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity.

Gastrorchis peyrotii is listed as endangered, highlighting the need for protection of its natural environment.
