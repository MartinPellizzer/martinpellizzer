Gynerium neesii

Gynerium neesii, commonly known as the giant water shield, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix-like structure. Gynerium neesii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats on the water surface.
