Genista Cinerea Subsp. Murcica

Murcian whitethorn, scientifically known as Genista cinerea subsp. murcica, is a rare and striking plant native to southern Spain. This subspecies of the common whitethorn thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. With its yellow flowers and thorny stems, it plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for local wildlife. Despite its resilience, the Murcian whitethorn faces threats from habitat loss and climate change. Protecting this unique plant is essential for preserving biodiversity in its native region.
