Gaura coccinea var. parvifolia

Gaura coccinea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf pink gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its compact size and ornamental flowers that attract pollinators.
