Gymnocalycium berchtii

Gymnocalycium berchtii, commonly known as the Potosi apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly spines that help protect it from intense sunlight and herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green body. Gymnocalycium berchtii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
