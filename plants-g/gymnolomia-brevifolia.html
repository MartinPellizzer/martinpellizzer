Gymnolomia brevifolia

Gymnolomia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved gymnolomia, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season. Gymnolomia brevifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
