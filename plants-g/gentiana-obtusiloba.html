Gentiana obtusiloba

Gentiana obtusiloba, commonly known as the obtuse gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
