Geranium cinereum var. pallidum

Geranium cinereum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and have a distinctive silver or grayish underside, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
