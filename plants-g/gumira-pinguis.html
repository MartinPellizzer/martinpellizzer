Gumira pinguis

Gumira pinguis, commonly known as the fatty gumira, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by nocturnal insects. Its sap contains a high concentration of lipids, which has led to its use in traditional medicine and as a natural moisturizer.

The botanical features of Gumira pinguis make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
