Gilia lutea subsp. micrantha

Gilia lutea subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
