Glyptopetalum zeylanicum

Glyptopetalum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its star-shaped, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
