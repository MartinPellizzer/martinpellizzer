Galium spurium var. leiospermon

Galium spurium var. leiospermon, commonly known as the smooth-stalked bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features slender, creeping stems that form dense mats, often covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
