Geranium saxatile

Geranium saxatile, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Geranium saxatile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
