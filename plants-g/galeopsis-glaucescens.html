Galeopsis glaucescens

Galeopsis glaucescens, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and reaches heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its small, blue to purple flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from June to August.

Galeopsis glaucescens is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
