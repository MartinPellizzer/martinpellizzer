Gonolobus cinctus

Gonolobus cinctus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.

Gonolobus cinctus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
