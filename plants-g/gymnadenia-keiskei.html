Gymnadenia keiskei

Gymnadenia keiskei, commonly known as the Keiske's grasspocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Gymnadenia keiskei is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
