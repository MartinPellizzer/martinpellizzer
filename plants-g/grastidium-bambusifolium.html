Grastidium bambusifolium

Grastidium bambusifolium, commonly known as bamboo-leaf grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that resemble bamboo leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in shaded areas. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets, typical of many grass species.

Grastidium bambusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
