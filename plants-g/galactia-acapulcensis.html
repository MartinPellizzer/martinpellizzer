Galactia acapulcensis

Galactia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Galactia acapulcensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
