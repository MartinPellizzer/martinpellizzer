Gentiana maeulchanensis subsp. maeulchanensis

Gentiana maeulchanensis subsp.

maeulchanensis, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
