Garcinia clarensis

Garcinia clarensis, commonly known as the Clarendon garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, red-orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia clarensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and role in local ecosystems.
