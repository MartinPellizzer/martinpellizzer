Galium × ochroleucum var. fallacinum

Galium × ochroleucum var. fallacinum, commonly known as the fall goldenweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems that often form a low, spreading mat. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a soft, hairy texture, while the flowers are small, white, and clustered in cymes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is notable for its seasonal color change, turning reddish-brown in the fall.
