Gerascanthus grandifolius

Gerascanthus grandifolius, commonly known as the grandifolius gerascantus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators.
