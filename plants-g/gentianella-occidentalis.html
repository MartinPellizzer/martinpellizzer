Gentianella occidentalis

Gentianella occidentalis, commonly known as the western gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy, tubular flowers.

Gentianella occidentalis is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
