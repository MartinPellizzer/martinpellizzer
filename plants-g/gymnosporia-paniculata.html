Gymnosporia paniculata

Gymnosporia paniculata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Gymnosporia paniculata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
