Gymnocarpos przewalskii

Gymnocarpos przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's gymnocarpos, is a shrub native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, spine-tipped leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Gymnocarpos przewalskii is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water.
