Galactia rugosa f. angustifolia

Galactia rugosa f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf galactia, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, angular stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
