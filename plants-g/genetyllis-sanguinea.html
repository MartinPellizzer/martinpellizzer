Genetyllis sanguinea

Genetyllis sanguinea, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
