Glandularia cabrerae

Glandularia cabrerae, commonly known as the Cape Verde ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and may play a role in its ecological interactions. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Glandularia cabrerae is known for its bright purple to pink flowers, which bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.
