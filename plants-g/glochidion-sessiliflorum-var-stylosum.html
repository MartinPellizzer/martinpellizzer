Glochidion sessiliflorum var. stylosum

Glochidion sessiliflorum var. stylosum, commonly known as the slender-flowered glochidion, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically with five petals and stamens, and they bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
