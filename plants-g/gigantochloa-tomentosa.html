Gigantochloa tomentosa

Gigantochloa tomentosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray sheaths and long, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Gigantochloa tomentosa is also valued for its potential in erosion control and sustainable land management.
