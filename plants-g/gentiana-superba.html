Gentiana superba

Gentiana superba, commonly known as the noble gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Gentiana superba is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
