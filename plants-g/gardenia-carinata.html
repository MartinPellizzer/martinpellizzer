Gardenia carinata

Gardenia carinata, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is a bright red, fleshy capsule that contains seeds, which are dispersed by animals.
