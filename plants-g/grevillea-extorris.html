Grevillea extorris

Grevillea extorris, commonly known as the Macarthur's grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, recurved red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.
