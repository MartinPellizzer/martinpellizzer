Garcinia cowa

Garcinia cowa, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are often glossy green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia cowa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its wood is durable and has been used in various crafts and construction.
