Grewia pallida

Grewia pallida, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Grewia pallida is also known for its ability to provide food and shelter for various wildlife species.
