Grammangis fallax

Grammangis fallax, commonly known as the Florida anise tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, berry-like fruits.

Grammangis fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
