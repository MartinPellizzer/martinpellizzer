Gladiolus infestus

Gladiolus infestus, commonly known as the woolly gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
