Gladiolus viridis

Gladiolus viridis, commonly known as the green gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, sword-shaped leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of colorful, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, showcasing shades of yellow, orange, and red. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
