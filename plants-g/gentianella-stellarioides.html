Gentianella stellarioides

Gentianella stellarioides, commonly known as star-like gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous small, star-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
