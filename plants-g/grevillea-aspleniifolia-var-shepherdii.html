Grevillea aspleniifolia var. shepherdii

Grevillea aspleniifolia var. shepherdii, commonly known as the Shepherd's Grevillea, is a unique and striking plant native to Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that resemble those of the asplenium fern, giving it its species name. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
