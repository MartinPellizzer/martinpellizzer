Gentianusa acaulis

Gentianusa acaulis, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and is often found growing on rocky slopes and cliffs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy layer.

Gentianusa acaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
