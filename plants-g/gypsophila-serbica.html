Gypsophila serbica

Gypsophila serbica, commonly known as the Serbian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its small, fluffy flowers give it a romantic and ethereal appearance, often used in floral arrangements.
