Gastrolobium bilobum

Gastrolobium bilobum, commonly known as the two-lobed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with two leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in spikes. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Gastrolobium bilobum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
