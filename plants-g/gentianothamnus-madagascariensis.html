Gentianothamnus madagascariensis

Gentianothamnus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar gentian, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
