Geranium sylvaticum var. batrachioides

Geranium sylvaticum var. batrachioides, commonly known as the frog's-bit geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright red or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
