Gymnandra

Gymnandra, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Southeast Asia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy spathaceous inflorescence that encloses the true flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. Gymnandra has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
