Gunnera microcarpa

Gunnera microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited gunnera, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a dramatic visual presence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
