Grumilea fimbricalyx

Grumilea fimbricalyx, commonly known as the silky-leaved grumilea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, and it produces small, round fruits.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
