Gomphostemma strobilinum subsp. wallichii

Gomphostemma strobilinum subsp.

wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan greenbark, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ethnobotanical studies.
