Geissorhiza imbricata var. obtusata

Geissorhiza imbricata var. obtusata, commonly known as the Cape goldbush, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
