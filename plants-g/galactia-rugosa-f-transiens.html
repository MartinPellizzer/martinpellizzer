Galactia rugosa f. transiens

Galactia rugosa f. transiens, commonly known as the transiens form of the hairy galactia, is a variant of the species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinguishing botanical feature.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
