Gardenia platixylon

Gardenia platixylon, commonly known as the thickwood gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody stem with a thick, dense texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are not only beautiful but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
