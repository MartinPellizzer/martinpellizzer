Gelseminum amoenum var. pubescens

Gelseminum amoenum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow lantern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant has hairy foliage, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.
