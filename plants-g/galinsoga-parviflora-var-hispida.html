Galinsoga parviflora var. hispida

Galinsoga parviflora var. hispida, commonly known as hispid galinsoga, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves. The plant grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a rapid growth cycle and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate climates.
