Galium vernum subsp. alpinum

Galium vernum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It features slender, often hairy stems that are typically less than 20 cm in height.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
