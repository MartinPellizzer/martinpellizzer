Gunnera atropurpurea

Gunnera atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple giant rhubarb, is a striking member of the Gunneraceae family native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, with a deep purple to reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in summer, which are clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic foliage.

Due to its size and growth habits, it requires careful management in cultivation to prevent it from becoming invasive.
