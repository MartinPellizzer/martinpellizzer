Gentiana canaliculata

Gentiana canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-cup gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
