Gerardia erecta

Gerardia erecta, commonly known as the upright gerardia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Gerardia erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
