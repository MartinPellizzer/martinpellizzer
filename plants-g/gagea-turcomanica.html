Gagea turcomanica

Gagea turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan spring beauty, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats.

Gagea turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
