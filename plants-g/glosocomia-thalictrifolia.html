Glosocomia thalictrifolia

Glosocomia thalictrifolia, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
