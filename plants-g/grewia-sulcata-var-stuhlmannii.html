Grewia sulcata var. stuhlmannii

Grewia sulcata var. stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African wild date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, fleshy, orange-red fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid and semi-arid environments.

The fruits are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor, often used in local cuisines.
