Guidonia tomentosa

Guidonia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly guidonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
