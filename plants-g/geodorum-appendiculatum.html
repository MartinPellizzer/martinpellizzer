Geodorum appendiculatum

Geodorum appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinct lip, and it blooms during the wet season.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible tubers.
