Gigantochloa maxima var. ridleyi

Gigantochloa maxima var. ridleyi, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clump-forming grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, grass-like texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts in its native regions.
