Gyrocheilos lasiocalyx

Gyrocheilos lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture due to their hairy surfaces.

Gyrocheilos lasiocalyx is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
