Gypsophila fruticulosa

Gypsophila fruticulosa, commonly known as sweet pea flower or baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favorite among horticulturists and florists alike.
