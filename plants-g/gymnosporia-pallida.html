Gymnosporia pallida

Gymnosporia pallida, commonly known as the white thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain seeds, typical of gymnosporangiate structures. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with a greyish-green appearance.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
