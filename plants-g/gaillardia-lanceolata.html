Gaillardia lanceolata

Gaillardia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange petals with a yellow center, and its leaves are lance-shaped, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia lanceolata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
