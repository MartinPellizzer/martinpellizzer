Gaertnera longivaginalis var. louisii

Gaertnera longivaginalis var. louisii, commonly known as the Louisiana holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
