Gorteria hispida

Gorteria hispida, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gorteria hispida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
