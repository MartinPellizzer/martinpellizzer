Gentiana campestris var. montana

Gentiana campestris var. montana, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
