Gossypium sandvicense

Gossypium sandvicense, commonly known as Hawaiian cotton, is a species of flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that produces fluffy, white fibers used for textile production. The plant has small, simple leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of Hawaii.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
