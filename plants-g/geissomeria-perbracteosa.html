Geissomeria perbracteosa

Geissomeria perbracteosa, commonly known as the slenderleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, edible berries. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
