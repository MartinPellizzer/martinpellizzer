Geranium sylvaticum var. albiflorum

Geranium sylvaticum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered wood geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky habitats, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, showy white or pale pink flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
