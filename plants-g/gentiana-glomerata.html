Gentiana glomerata

Gentiana glomerata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence.

Gentiana glomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
