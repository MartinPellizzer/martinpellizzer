Geranium guanacosense

Geranium guanacosense, commonly known as the Andean geranium, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
