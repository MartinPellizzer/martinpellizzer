Gomphima vaginalis

Gomphima vaginalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf gomphima, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a distinct violet or blue coloration.

Gomphima vaginalis is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
