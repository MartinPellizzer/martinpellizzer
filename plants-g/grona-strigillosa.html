Grona strigillosa

Grona strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy grona, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
