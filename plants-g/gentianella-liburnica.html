Gentianella liburnica

Gentianella liburnica, commonly known as the Balkan gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
