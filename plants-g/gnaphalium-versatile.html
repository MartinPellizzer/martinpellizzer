Gnaphalium versatile

Gnaphalium versatile, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
