Gerardia floridana

Gerardia floridana, commonly known as the Florida gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Gerardia floridana produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, aiding in wind pollination and seed dispersal.
