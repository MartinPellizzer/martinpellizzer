Gilia gilioides

Gilia gilioides, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep root systems and efficient water use.
