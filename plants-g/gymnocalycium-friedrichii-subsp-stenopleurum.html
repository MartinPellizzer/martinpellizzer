Gymnocalycium friedrichii subsp. stenopleurum

Gymnocalycium friedrichii subsp.

stenopleurum, commonly known as the narrow-pleated cactus, is a species native to Argentina. It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
