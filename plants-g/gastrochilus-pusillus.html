Gastrochilus pusillus

Gastrochilus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf stomach orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features compact, fleshy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is characterized by its unique root system, which helps it absorb moisture and nutrients from its surroundings.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
