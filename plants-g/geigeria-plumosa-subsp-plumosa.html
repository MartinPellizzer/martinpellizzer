Geigeria plumosa subsp. plumosa

Geigeria plumosa subsp.

plumosa, commonly known as the silky geigeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its soft, silky hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
