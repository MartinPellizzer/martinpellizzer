Gnaphalium saturejoides

Gnaphalium saturejoides, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its small, yellowish flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
