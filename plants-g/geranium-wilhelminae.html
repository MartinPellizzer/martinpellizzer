Geranium wilhelminae

Geranium wilhelminae, commonly known as the upright geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 3 feet tall, covered in small, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Geranium wilhelminae is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
