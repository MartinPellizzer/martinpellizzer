Galium densiflorum f. leiocarpum

Galium densiflorum f. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that are often covered with short, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
