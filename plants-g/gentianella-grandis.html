Gentianella grandis

Gentianella grandis, commonly known as the large gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and hairy.

Gentianella grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
