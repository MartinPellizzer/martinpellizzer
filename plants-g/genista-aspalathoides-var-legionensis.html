Genista aspalathoides var. legionensis

Genista aspalathoides var. legionensis, commonly known as the Cape broom, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
