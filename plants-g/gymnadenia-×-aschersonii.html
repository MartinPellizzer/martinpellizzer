Gymnadenia × aschersonii

Gymnadenia × aschersonii, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is a hybrid between Gymnadenia conopsea and Gymnadenia odoratissima, exhibiting traits from both parents.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected in natural areas.
