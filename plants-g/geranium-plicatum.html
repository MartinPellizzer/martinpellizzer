Geranium plicatum

Geranium plicatum, commonly known as the zigzag geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
