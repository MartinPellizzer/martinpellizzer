Glycine soja var. brevifolia

Glycine soja var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved soybean, is a variant of the soybean plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact leaves compared to standard soybean varieties. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
