Glomera tamiana

Glomera tamiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are known to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient uptake.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
