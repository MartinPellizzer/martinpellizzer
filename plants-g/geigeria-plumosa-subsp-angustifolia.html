Geigeria plumosa subsp. angustifolia

Geigeria plumosa subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed geigeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
