Gypsophila acantholimoides

Gypsophila acantholimoides, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
