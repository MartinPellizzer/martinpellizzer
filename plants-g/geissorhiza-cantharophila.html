Geissorhiza cantharophila

Geissorhiza cantharophila, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange or red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid or rocky environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish tinge.

Geissorhiza cantharophila is valued in gardens for its bold appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
