Gesneria quadriflora

Gesneria quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered gesneria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Gesneria quadriflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
