Gnaphalium pilulare

Gnaphalium pilulare, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dense, soft, white to grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, often shaded environments such as meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and medicinal properties historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
