Gleditsia microphylla

Gleditsia microphylla, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces distinctive, spiny fruit clusters called burrs, which contain seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for landscaping and reforestation projects.
