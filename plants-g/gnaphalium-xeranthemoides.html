Gnaphalium xeranthemoides

Gnaphalium xeranthemoides, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
