Gentianella clelandii

Gentianella clelandii, commonly known as Cleland's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Gentianella clelandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
