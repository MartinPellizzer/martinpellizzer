Godetia purpurea var. procera

Godetia purpurea var. procera, commonly known as the purple flax or tall godetia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and slender, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
