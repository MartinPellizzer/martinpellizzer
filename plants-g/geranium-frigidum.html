Geranium frigidum

Geranium frigidum, commonly known as the Arctic geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in cold, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Geranium frigidum is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
