Grastidium falcipetalum

Grastidium falcipetalum, commonly known as the curved-palm fern, is a rare tropical plant characterized by its unique frond structure.

It features feather-like leaves that are deeply divided, with a distinctive falcate (sickle-shaped) petiole that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures include small, spore-producing cones located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Grastidium falcipetalum is considered a delicate and ecologically significant species.
