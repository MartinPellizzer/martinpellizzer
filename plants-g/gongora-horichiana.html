Gongora horichiana

Gongora horichiana, commonly known as the Horichiana orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Gongora horichiana is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
