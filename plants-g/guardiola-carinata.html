Guardiola carinata

Guardiola carinata, commonly known as the spiked guardiola, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Guardiola carinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
