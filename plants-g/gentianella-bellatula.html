Gentianella bellatula

Gentianella bellatula, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are tubular with five petals that curve outward.

Gentianella bellatula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
