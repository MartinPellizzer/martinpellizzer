Geissorhiza secunda var. bicolor

Geissorhiza secunda var. bicolor, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Geissorhiza, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, typically from October to December. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
