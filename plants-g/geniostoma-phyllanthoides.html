Geniostoma phyllanthoides

Geniostoma phyllanthoides, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, reddish seeds.

Geniostoma phyllanthoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
