Gentianella calcis subsp. waipara

Gentianella calcis subsp.

waipara, commonly known as the Waipara gentian, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The subspecies is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
