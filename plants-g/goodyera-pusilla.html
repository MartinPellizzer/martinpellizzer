Goodyera pusilla

Goodyera pusilla, commonly known as the small pogostemon, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, epiphytic plant that thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found on tree trunks or branches. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a dark green color, and it produces small, white to pale green flowers. The plant has a short rhizome and lacks true roots, relying on its leaves for nutrient absorption.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings.
