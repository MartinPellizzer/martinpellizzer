Gladiolus namaquensis

Gladiolus namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua gladiolus, is a striking perennial herb native to the semi-arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
