Galium erectum var. boreanum

Galium erectum var. boreanum, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to cold climates in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, bladder-like berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of early successional plant communities in boreal and temperate regions.
