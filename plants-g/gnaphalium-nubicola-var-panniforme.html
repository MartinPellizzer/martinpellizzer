Gnaphalium nubicola var. panniforme

Gnaphalium nubicola var. panniforme, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, dry conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety panniforme is distinguished by its distinctive, pinnately divided leaves and compact growth habit.
