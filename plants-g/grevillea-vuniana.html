Grevillea vuniana

Grevillea vuniana, commonly known as the Vuniana grass tree, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of northern New South Wales and southern Queensland in Australia.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and an upright, branched trunk. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in winter, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and environmental purposes.
