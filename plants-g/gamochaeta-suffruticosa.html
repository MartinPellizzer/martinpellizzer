Gamochaeta suffruticosa

Gamochaeta suffruticosa, commonly known as the rough false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features wiry, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense, compact clusters.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
