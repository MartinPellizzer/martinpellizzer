Galium cornigerum

Galium cornigerum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground, and it produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium cornigerum is known for its delicate foliage and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
