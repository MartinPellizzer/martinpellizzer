Gelseminum garrocha var. rubrum

Gelseminum garrocha var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-flowered gelsemium, is a variant of the gelsemium plant native to parts of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers that are highly fragrant, especially in the evening. Its flowers give way to long, slender pods containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
