Gmelina moluccana f. glandulosa

Gmelina moluccana f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular gmelina, is a variant of the gmelina tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its leaves and stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Oleaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The glandular features of this plant contribute to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
