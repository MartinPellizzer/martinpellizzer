Gerbera glandulosa

Gerbera glandulosa, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it its common name and help it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Gerbera glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
