Gastrochilus ochraceus

Gastrochilus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, adding vibrant color to the forest canopy.

The botanical features of Gastrochilus ochraceus make it a notable species in the Orchidaceae family.
