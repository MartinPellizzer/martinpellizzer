Gastrolychnis angustiflora subsp. tenella

Gastrolychnis angustiflora subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gastrolychnis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.
