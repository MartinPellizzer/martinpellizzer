Gnaphalium chamissonis

Gnaphalium chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tiny tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
