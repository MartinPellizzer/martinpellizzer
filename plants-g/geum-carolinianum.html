Geum carolinianum

Geum carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina grass pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright, hairy stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geum carolinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
