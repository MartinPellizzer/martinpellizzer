Galium canum subsp. canum

Galium canum subsp.

canum, commonly known as bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
