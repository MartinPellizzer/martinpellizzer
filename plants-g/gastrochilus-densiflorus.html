Gastrochilus densiflorus

Gastrochilus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered stomach lip, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
