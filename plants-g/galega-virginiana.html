Galega virginiana

Galega virginiana, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields.

Goat's rue is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health purposes.
