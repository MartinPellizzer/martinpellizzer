Genista artwinensis

Genista artwinensis, commonly known as the Artwin broom, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
