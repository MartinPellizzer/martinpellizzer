Gongora vitellina

Gongora vitellina, commonly known as the Yellow Ladies' Tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, erect stem with a dense cluster of showy, yellowish-green to white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Gongora vitellina is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in wetland habitats across Florida and Georgia.
