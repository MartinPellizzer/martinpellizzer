Gavilea araucana

Gavilea araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown composed of scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Gavilea araucana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
