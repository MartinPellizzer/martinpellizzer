Gentiana sturmiana f. anisiaca

Gentiana sturmiana f. anisiaca, commonly known as the Anise Gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, radial flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are usually blue to violet, with five petals and prominent yellow veins.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its striking blooms.
