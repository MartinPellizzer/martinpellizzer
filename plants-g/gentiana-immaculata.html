Gentiana immaculata

Gentiana immaculata, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
