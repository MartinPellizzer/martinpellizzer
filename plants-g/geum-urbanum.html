Geum urbanum

Geum urbanum, commonly known as wild garden sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Geum urbanum is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
