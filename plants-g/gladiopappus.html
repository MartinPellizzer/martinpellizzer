Gladiopappus

Gladiopappus, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, slender stems. Its inflorescence consists of small, cup-like structures that give it its scientific name.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
