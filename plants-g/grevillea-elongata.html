Grevillea elongata

Grevillea elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed waratah, is an evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. Its flowers are composed of multiple petal-like structures and are highly attractive to birds and insects.

Grevillea elongata is well-suited to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
