Geum capense

Geum capense, commonly known as the Cape buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers with five petals that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
