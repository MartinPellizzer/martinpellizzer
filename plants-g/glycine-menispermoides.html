Glycine menispermoides

Glycine menispermoides, commonly known as the climbing sweet senna, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are yellow, pea-like, and arranged in racemes. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
