Gomidesia gaudichaudiana

Gomidesia gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Guadaloupean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Guadalupe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Gomidesia gaudichaudiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
