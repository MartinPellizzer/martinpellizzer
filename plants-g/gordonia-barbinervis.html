Gordonia barbinervis

Gordonia barbinervis, commonly known as the broad-leafed gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often deeply veined and have a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
