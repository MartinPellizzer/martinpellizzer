Gentiana germanica var. degeniana

Gentiana germanica var. degeniana, commonly known as the Degen's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
