Globularia nudicaulis proles gracilis

Globularia nudicaulis proles gracilis, commonly known as the slender globularia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, white flowers.

It typically thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry landscapes.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
