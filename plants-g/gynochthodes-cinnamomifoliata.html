Gynochthodes cinnamomifoliata

Gynochthodes cinnamomifoliata, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf gynochthodes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, broad, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of cinnamon trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
