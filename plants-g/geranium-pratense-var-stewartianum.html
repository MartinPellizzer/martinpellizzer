Geranium pratense var. stewartianum

Geranium pratense var. stewartianum, commonly known as Stewart's cranesbill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while the plant produces small, round fruits called achenes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
