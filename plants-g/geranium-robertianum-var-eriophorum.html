Geranium robertianum var. eriophorum

Geranium robertianum var. eriophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink or red, arranged in dense, rounded clusters at the ends of the stems.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its propagation.
