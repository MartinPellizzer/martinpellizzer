Goodyera repens var. ophioides

Goodyera repens var. ophioides, commonly known as the creeping lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, producing a dense carpet of dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of pale green to white flowers with three petals and three sepals, arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found in association with decaying organic matter.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
