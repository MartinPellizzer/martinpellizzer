Gerascanthus decipiens

Gerascanthus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of other geranium species. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gerascanthus decipiens is known for its unique ability to attract pollinators through mimicry, making it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
