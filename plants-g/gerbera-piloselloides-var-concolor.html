Gerbera piloselloides var. concolor

Gerbera piloselloides var. concolor, commonly known as the narrowleaf gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and has hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The varietal name 'concolor' refers to its uniform coloration across the petals.
