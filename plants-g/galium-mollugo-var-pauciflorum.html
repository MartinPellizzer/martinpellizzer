Galium mollugo var. pauciflorum

Galium mollugo var. pauciflorum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in damp, temperate regions.
