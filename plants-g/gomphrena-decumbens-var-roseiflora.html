Gomphrena decumbens var. roseiflora

Gomphrena decumbens var. roseiflora, commonly known as the roseflower bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and vibrant, rose-like bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful, papery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
