Gossypium indicum

Gossypium indicum, commonly known as Indian cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to India and is characterized by its fluffy, white fibers that are harvested from the seed pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

The botanical features of Gossypium indicum make it an important crop for textile production.
