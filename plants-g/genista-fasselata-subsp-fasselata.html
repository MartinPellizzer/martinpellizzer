Genista fasselata subsp. fasselata

Genista fasselata subsp.

fasselata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring, which are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to dry, rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
