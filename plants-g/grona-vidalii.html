Grona vidalii

Grona vidalii, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, silver-green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, Grona vidalii is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
