Gentiana sino-ornata f. saxatilis

Gentiana sino-ornata f. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
