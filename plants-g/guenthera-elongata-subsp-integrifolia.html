Guenthera elongata subsp. integrifolia

Guenthera elongata subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed guenthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, usually with a pale greenish-white color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its entire leaf margins, which set it apart from closely related species.
