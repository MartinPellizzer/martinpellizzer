Galium kwanzanense

Galium kwanzanense, commonly known as the Japanese bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shady gardens.
