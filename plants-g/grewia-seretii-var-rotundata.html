Grewia seretii var. rotundata

Grewia seretii var. rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, woolly inflorescences that resemble small globes, typically ranging from pink to reddish-purple in color. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
