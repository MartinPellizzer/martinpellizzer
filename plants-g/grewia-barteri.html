Grewia barteri

Grewia barteri, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Grewia barteri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
