Garcinia barteri

Garcinia barteri, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed.

Garcinia barteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
