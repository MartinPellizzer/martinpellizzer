Galactia elliottii

Galactia elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Galactia elliottii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant for soil enrichment.
