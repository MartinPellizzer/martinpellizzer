Galium munzii subsp. ambivalens

Galium munzii subsp.

ambivalens, commonly known as the desert bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
