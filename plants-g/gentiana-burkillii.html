Gentiana burkillii

Gentiana burkillii, commonly known as Burkill's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Gentiana burkillii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
