Galium papillosum var. hirsutum

Galium papillosum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy texture and unique leaf morphology make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
