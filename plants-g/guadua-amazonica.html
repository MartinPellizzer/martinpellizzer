Guadua amazonica

Guadua amazonica, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It possesses a robust rhizomatic system that allows it to spread and form dense clumps. The plant has hollow, segmented stems that are strong and flexible, making it suitable for construction and handicrafts. Its leaves are long and narrow, contributing to its ornamental value and ecological role.

Guadua amazonica plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
