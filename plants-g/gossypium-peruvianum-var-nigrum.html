Gossypium peruvianum var. nigrum

Gossypium peruvianum var. nigrum, commonly known as the Peruvian black cotton tree, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, fibrous seeds and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fibrous seeds are used for their lint, which has been historically used in textile production.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
