Gentiana minuta

Gentiana minuta, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are short and often sprawling.

Gentiana minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
