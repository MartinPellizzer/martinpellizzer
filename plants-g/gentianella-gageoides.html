Gentianella gageoides

Gentianella gageoides, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. It has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Gentianella gageoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
