Gentiana jirasekii

Gentiana jirasekii, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Gentiana jirasekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
