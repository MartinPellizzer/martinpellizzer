Gilia ochroleuca subsp. clokeyi

Gilia ochroleuca subsp.

clokeyi, commonly known as the Clokey's gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
