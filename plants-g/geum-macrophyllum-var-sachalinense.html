Geum macrophyllum var. sachalinense

Geum macrophyllum var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sachalin avens, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct central vein. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
