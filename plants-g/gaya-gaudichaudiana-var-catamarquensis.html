Gaya gaudichaudiana var. catamarquensis

Gaya gaudichaudiana var. catamarquensis, commonly known as the Catamarca gaya, is a shrub native to the high Andean regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting frost resistance and drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
