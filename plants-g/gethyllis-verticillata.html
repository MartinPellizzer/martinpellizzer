Gethyllis verticillata

Gethyllis verticillata, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a whorl around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Gethyllis verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
