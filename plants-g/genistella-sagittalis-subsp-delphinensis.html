Genistella sagittalis subsp. delphinensis

Genistella sagittalis subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinine Broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
