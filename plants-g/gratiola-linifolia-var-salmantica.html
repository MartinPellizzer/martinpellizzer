Gratiola linifolia var. salmantica

Gratiola linifolia var. salmantica, commonly known as the Salmantic hairvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie prostrate or are slightly ascending, covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and also hairy, contributing to its overall delicate appearance. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer.

This plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
