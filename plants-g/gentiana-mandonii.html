Gentiana mandonii

Gentiana mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in open meadows or on slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
