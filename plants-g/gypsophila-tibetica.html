Gypsophila tibetica

Gypsophila tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers. Gypsophila tibetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
