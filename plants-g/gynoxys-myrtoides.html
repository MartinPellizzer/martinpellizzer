Gynoxys myrtoides

Gynoxys myrtoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
