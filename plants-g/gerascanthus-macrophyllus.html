Gerascanthus macrophyllus

Gerascanthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant leaf geranium, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
