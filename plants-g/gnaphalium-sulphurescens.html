Gnaphalium sulphurescens

Gnaphalium sulphurescens, commonly known as sulfur tuft, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
