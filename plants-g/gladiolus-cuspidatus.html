Gladiolus cuspidatus

Gladiolus cuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a sword-like fashion, giving it a striking appearance.

Gladiolus cuspidatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various growing conditions.
