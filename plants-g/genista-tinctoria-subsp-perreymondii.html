Genista tinctoria subsp. perreymondii

Genista tinctoria subsp.

perreymondii, commonly known as the Perreymond's yellow broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant is distinguished by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its historical use in dyeing textiles has contributed to its botanical significance.
