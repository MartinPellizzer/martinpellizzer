Galium californicum subsp. primum

Galium californicum subsp.

primum, commonly known as the California bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
