Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. citrinum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. citrinum, commonly known as the golden marshweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, while the flowers are yellow and borne in clusters.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
