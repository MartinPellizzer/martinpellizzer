Geracium paludosum

Geracium paludosum, commonly known as the marsh geranium, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy or boggy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soil conditions. Geracium paludosum is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants, contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.

Its delicate appearance and ecological significance make it an important species for wetland conservation efforts.
