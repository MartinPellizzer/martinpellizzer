Gentiana pedicellata subsp. zeylanica

Gentiana pedicellata subsp.

zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
