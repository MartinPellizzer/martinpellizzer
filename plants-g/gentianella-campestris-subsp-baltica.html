Gentianella campestris subsp. baltica

Gentianella campestris subsp.

baltica, commonly known as the Baltic gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist meadows and wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
