Geniostoma quadrangulare

Geniostoma quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-cornered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Geniostoma quadrangulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
