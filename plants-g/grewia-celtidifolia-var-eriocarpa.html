Grewia celtidifolia var. eriocarpa

Grewia celtidifolia var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly globe-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, woolly inflorescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal.
