Garuga pinnata var. sikkimensis

Garuga pinnata var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim garuga, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
