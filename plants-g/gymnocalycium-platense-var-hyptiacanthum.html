Gymnocalycium platense var. hyptiacanthum

Gymnocalycium platense var. hyptiacanthum, commonly known as the Hyptiacanthum cactus, is a rare and striking species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
