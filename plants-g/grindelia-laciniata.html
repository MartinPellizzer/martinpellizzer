Grindelia laciniata

Grindelia laciniata, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Grindelia laciniata is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
