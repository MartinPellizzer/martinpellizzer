Gossypium herbaceum var. wightianum

Gossypium herbaceum var. wightianum, commonly known as Wight's cotton, is a wild species of cotton native to India.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into fluffy bolls containing fibrous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its fibers, though not commercially cultivated, are of interest for their unique properties and potential in ecological studies.
