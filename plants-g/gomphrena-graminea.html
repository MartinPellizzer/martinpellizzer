Gomphrena graminea

Gomphrena graminea, commonly known as the common gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, and the plant produces feathery, bract-like structures that give it a unique appearance. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
