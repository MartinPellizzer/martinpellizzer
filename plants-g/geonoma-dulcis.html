Geonoma dulcis

Geonoma dulcis, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowering spikes produce small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by bright red berries.

Geonoma dulcis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its unique foliage and compact growth habit.
