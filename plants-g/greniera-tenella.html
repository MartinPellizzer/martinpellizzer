Greniera tenella

Greniera tenella, commonly known as the dwarf rock cress, is a small, low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate cold and arid conditions.

Greniera tenella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
