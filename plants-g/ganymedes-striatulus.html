Ganymedes striatulus

Ganymedes striatulus, commonly known as the striped waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often striped with contrasting colors. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Ganymedes striatulus thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
