Guarea tuberculata

Guarea tuberculata, commonly known as the bullet tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is often used in construction and as a source of timber. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces fruit that contains a single large, hard seed.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
