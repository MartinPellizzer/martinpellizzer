Gentianella amarella subsp. sandiana

Gentianella amarella subsp.

sandiana, commonly known as the sand gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
