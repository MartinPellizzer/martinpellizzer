Gymnosporia angularis

Gymnosporia angularis, commonly known as the angular thornapple, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its angular, thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits containing seeds. Gymnosporia angularis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought resistance.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
