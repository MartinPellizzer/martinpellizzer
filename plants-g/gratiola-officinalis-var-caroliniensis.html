Gratiola officinalis var. caroliniensis

Gratiola officinalis var. caroliniensis, commonly known as Carolina goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
