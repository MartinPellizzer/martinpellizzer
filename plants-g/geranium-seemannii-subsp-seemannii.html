Geranium seemannii subsp. seemannii

Geranium seemannii subsp.

seemannii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features upright, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
