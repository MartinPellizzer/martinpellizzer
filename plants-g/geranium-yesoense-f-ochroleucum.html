Geranium yesoense f. ochroleucum

Geranium yesoense f. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often hairy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
