Gardenia pseudoternifolia

Gardenia pseudoternifolia, commonly known as the slender gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
