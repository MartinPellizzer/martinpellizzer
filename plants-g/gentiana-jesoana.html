Gentiana jesoana

Gentiana jesoana, commonly known as the Jeso gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
