Galium speciosum

Galium speciosum, commonly known as showy bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces tiny, bead-like fruits called drupes, which are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
