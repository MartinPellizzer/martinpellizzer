Genista spartioides subsp. pseudoretamoides

Genista spartioides subsp.

pseudoretamoides, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has slender, spiny stems and small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
