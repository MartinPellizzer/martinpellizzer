Geum rivale f. typicum

Geum rivale f. typicum, commonly known as the water avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in cymes and are attractive to pollinators like bees and flies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland environments.
