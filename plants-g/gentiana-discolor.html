Gentiana discolor

Gentiana discolor, commonly known as the pale gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
