Gilia leptantha subsp. leptantha

Gilia leptantha subsp.

leptantha, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are slender and branching.

It is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
