Glochidotheca foeniculacea

Glochidotheca foeniculacea, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in glandular dots. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Glochidotheca foeniculacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
