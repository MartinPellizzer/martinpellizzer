Grewia rupestris

Grewia rupestris, commonly known as the rock gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Grewia rupestris thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.
