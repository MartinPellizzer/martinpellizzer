Gomphocarpus spathulatus

Gomphocarpus spathulatus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Gomphocarpus spathulatus is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
