Georgia rosea

Georgia rosea, commonly known as the Georgia rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Georgia rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
