Glossorhyncha altomontana

Glossorhyncha altomontana, commonly known as the high mountain beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in high-altitude environments.

It is characterized by its intricate, leaf-like thallus with a distinct, often hairy appearance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and cyanobacteria, which contribute to its photosynthetic capabilities. It is typically found on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its slow growth and specialized habitat make it a rare and ecologically significant species.
