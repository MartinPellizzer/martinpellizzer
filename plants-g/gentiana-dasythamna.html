Gentiana dasythamna

Gentiana dasythamna, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana dasythamna is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
