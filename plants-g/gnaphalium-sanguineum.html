Gnaphalium sanguineum

Gnaphalium sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy, reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
