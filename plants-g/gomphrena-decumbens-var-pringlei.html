Gomphrena decumbens var. pringlei

Gomphrena decumbens var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
