Grevillea brachystylis

Grevillea brachystylis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern and western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Grevillea brachystylis is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
