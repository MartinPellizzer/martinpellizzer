Garcinia engleriana

Garcinia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia engleriana is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
