Gnaphalium saturejoides var. angustifolia

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
