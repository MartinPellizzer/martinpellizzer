Gomphrena viridifolia

Gomphrena viridifolia, commonly known as greenleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, often with a distinctive red or orange calyx. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Gomphrena viridifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
