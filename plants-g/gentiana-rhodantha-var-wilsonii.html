Gentiana rhodantha var. wilsonii

Gentiana rhodantha var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens due to its vibrant floral display.
