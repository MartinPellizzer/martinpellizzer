Gentiana clarkei var. lutescens

Gentiana clarkei var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features striking yellow flowers that contrast sharply with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
