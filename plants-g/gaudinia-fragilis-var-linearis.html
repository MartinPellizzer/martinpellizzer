Gaudinia fragilis var. linearis

Gaudinia fragilis var. linearis, commonly known as the slender gaudinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
