Galium lucidum var. viridulum

Galium lucidum var. viridulum, commonly known as the glossy wood-sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on forest floors. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and have a distinct veining pattern.

This variety is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.
