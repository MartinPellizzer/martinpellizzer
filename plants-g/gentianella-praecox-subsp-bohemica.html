Gentianella praecox subsp. bohemica

Gentianella praecox subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian early gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring temperate climates. It is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in late spring.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.
