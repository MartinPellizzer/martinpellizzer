Georgina coccinea

Georgina coccinea, commonly known as the red georgina, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, vibrant red blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, hardy plants.

The botanical name reflects its bright red color, with "coccinea" derived from the Latin word for "crimson.".
