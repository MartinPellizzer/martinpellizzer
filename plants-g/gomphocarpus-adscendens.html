Gomphocarpus adscendens

Gomphocarpus adscendens, commonly known as the climbing milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as fences or trees. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The plant also contains milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed genus.
