Galium cuspidatum

Galium cuspidatum, commonly known as spiked bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium cuspidatum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
