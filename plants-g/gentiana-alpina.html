Gentiana alpina

Gentiana alpina, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Gentiana alpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
