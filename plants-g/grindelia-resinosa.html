Grindelia resinosa

Grindelia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, resinous leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in arid and coastal environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in its ecosystem.

Grindelia resinosa is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and its role in supporting local pollinators.
