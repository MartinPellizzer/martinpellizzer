Grevillea elaeocarpifolia

Grevillea elaeocarpifolia, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, linear leaves that are typically pale green with a reddish or pinkish underside. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal and drought tolerance.
