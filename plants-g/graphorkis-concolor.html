Graphorkis concolor

Graphorkis concolor, commonly known as the smooth-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a faint fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
