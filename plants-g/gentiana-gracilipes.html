Gentiana gracilipes

Gentiana gracilipes, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

Gentiana gracilipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
