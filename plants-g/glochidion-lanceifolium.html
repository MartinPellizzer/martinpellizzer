Glochidion lanceifolium

Glochidion lanceifolium, commonly known as the lanceleaf glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing tiny seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
