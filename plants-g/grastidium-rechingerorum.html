Grastidium rechingerorum

Grastidium rechingerorum, commonly known as the Rechinger's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Grastidium rechingerorum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to alpine conditions.
