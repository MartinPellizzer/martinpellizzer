Gigantochloa glabrata

Gigantochloa glabrata, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, rapidly growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.

Due to its fast growth and robust nature, it is often used in reforestation and as a source of sustainable bamboo products.
