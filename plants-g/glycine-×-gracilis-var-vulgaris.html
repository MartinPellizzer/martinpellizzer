Glycine × gracilis var. vulgaris

Glycine × gracilis var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common climbing bean, is a leguminous plant characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a cover crop or in ornamental landscaping.
