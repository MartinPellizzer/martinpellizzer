Gymneia ampelophylla

Gymneia ampelophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and bright yellow dandelion-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy coating, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Gymneia ampelophylla is adapted to arid conditions and has a long flowering period during the warmer months.
