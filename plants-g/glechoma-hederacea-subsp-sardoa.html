Glechoma hederacea subsp. sardoa

Glechoma hederacea subsp.

sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian ground ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its creeping growth habit and ability to spread through runners make it a common ornamental and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
