Gongrothamnus conyzoides

Gongrothamnus conyzoides, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
