Genista defoliata

Genista defoliata, commonly known as the leafless broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in Mediterranean climates, characterized by its sparse foliage and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its lack of leaves, with most of its photosynthetic function carried out by its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
