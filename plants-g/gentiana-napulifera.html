Gentiana napulifera

Gentiana napulifera, commonly known as the Naples gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
