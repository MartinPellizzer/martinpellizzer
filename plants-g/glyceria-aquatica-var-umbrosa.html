Glyceria aquatica var. umbrosa

Glyceria aquatica var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that often appear in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in loose clusters along the stems. It thrives in shaded conditions, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life, it plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
