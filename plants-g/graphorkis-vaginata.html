Graphorkis vaginata

Graphorkis vaginata, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
