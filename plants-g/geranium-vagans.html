Geranium vagans

Geranium vagans, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and often prostrate. This species is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
