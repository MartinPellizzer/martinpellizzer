Geum latifolium

Geum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf avens, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Geum latifolium is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
