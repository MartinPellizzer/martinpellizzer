Guatteria magnifica

Guatteria magnifica, commonly known as the golden guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
