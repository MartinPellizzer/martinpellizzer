Geranium costaricense

Geranium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican geranium, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Geranium costaricense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
