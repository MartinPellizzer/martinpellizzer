Goodyera foliosa f. albiflora

Goodyera foliosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered maidenhair fern, is a rare and striking variant of the native fern species.

It is characterized by its elegant, leathery leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
