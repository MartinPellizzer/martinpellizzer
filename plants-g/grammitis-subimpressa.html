Grammitis subimpressa

Grammitis subimpressa, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact rosette. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually hidden among the foliage, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Grammitis subimpressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
