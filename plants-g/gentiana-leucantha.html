Gentiana leucantha

Gentiana leucantha, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentiana leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
