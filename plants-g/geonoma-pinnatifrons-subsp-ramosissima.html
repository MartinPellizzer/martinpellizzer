Geonoma pinnatifrons subsp. ramosissima

Geonoma pinnatifrons subsp.

ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy-veined bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador.

The plant is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and elongated, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
