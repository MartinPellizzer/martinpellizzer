Gomphrena hygrophila

Gomphrena hygrophila, commonly known as the water globe flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical, pink or red flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gomphrena hygrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with proper water conditions.
