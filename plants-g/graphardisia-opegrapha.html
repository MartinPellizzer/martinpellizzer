Graphardisia opegrapha

Graphardisia opegrapha, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and thick, woody stems that provide structural support. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough, leathery texture.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
