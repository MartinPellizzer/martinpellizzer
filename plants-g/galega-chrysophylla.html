Galega chrysophylla

Galega chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf galega, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with a striking yellow-green coloration, which is a distinguishing trait. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by slender, bean-like pods.

Galega chrysophylla is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
