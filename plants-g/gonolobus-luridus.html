Gonolobus luridus

Gonolobus luridus, commonly known as the yellow tephrosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.
