Gentianella antipoda

Gentianella antipoda, commonly known as the New Zealand gentian, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gentianella antipoda is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
