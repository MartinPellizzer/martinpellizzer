Gymnadenia × eggeriana

Gymnadenia × eggeriana, commonly known as the Egger's grass pink, is a hybrid orchid species native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pink or reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils among grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers at the top of the stem.

Gymnadenia × eggeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
