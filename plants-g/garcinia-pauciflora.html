Garcinia pauciflora

Garcinia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Garcinia pauciflora is also noted for its ability to grow in poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various ecological niches.
