Garcinia sampitana

Garcinia sampitana, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is typically reddish-purple when mature, contains a single large seed surrounded by a segmented, fleshy aril. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
