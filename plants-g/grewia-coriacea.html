Grewia coriacea

Grewia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when ripe.

Grewia coriacea is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
