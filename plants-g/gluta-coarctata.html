Gluta coarctata

Gluta coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gluta, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The bark and leaves of Gluta coarctata have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
