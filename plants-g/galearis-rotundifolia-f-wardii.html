Galearis rotundifolia f. wardii

Galearis rotundifolia f. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's roundleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has ovate to rounded leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
