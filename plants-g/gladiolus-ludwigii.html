Gladiolus ludwigii

Gladiolus ludwigii, commonly known as Ludwig's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing dense clusters of colorful, sword-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its vibrant hues, which range from deep reds to soft pinks and yellows. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Gladiolus ludwigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
