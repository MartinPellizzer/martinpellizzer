Graphorkis dahliana

Graphorkis dahliana, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in dense clusters. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among hobbyists and is often used in terrariums and as a houseplant.
