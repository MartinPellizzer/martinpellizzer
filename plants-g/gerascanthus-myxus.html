Gerascanthus myxus

Gerascanthus myxus, commonly known as the sticky root orchid, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fibrous, creeping rhizomes and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant's unique feature is its sticky, mucilaginous roots, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a cool, moist climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species.
