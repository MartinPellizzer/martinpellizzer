Guajava hians

Guajava hians, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is well-known for its edible red berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in warm climates.
