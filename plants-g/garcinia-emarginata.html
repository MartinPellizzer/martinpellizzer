Garcinia emarginata

Garcinia emarginata, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia emarginata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
