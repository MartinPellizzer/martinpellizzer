Glyceria nervata var. stricta

Glyceria nervata var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
