Genista obsoleta

Genista obsoleta, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.
