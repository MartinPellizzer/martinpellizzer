Gypsophila paniculata var. hirta

Gypsophila paniculata var. hirta, commonly known as the spiked milkweed or garden gothics, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
