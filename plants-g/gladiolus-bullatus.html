Gladiolus bullatus

Gladiolus bullatus, commonly known as the spotted gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, colorful flowers. The plant produces large, spurred blooms that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

The spotted gladiolus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
