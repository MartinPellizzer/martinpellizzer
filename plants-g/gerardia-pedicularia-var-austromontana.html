Gerardia pedicularia var. austromontana

Gerardia pedicularia var. austromontana, commonly known as the Australian gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
