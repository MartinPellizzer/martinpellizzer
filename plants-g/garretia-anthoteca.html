Garretia anthoteca

Garretia anthoteca, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
