Geranium strictipes var. grandiflorum

Geranium strictipes var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the tall geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
