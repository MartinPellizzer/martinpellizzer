Gentiana crawfurdioides

Gentiana crawfurdioides, commonly known as the Sierra foothill gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gentiana crawfurdioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
