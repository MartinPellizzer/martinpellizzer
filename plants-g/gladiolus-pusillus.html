Gladiolus pusillus

Gladiolus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters. The flowers are usually pale purple to white in color and bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
