Genista parviflora

Genista parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, spiny stems and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
