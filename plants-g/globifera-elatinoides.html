Globifera elatinoides

Globifera elatinoides, commonly known as the silky green alga, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its gelatinous, translucent thallus that forms dense, mat-like structures in freshwater environments.

It thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and is often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. The plant's structure is composed primarily of a delicate, branching network of filaments that contribute to its ability to absorb nutrients efficiently. Globifera elatinoides plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing microhabitats for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its fragile nature, it is sensitive to water pollution and serves as an indicator of water quality.
