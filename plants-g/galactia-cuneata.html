Galactia cuneata

Galactia cuneata, commonly known as the small cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers are typical of the family.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional cuisines.
