Gentianella ottonis

Gentianella ottonis, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella ottonis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine gardens and wildflower meadows.
