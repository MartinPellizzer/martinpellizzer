Glechoma biondiana

Glechoma biondiana, commonly known as the silverweed or white dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Glechoma biondiana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
