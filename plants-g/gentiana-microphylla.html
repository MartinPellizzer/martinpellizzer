Gentiana microphylla

Gentiana microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
