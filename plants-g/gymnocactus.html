Gymnocactus

Gymnocactus, commonly known as the naked cactus, is a genus of cacti native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

These plants are characterized by their lack of spines, which distinguishes them from other cactus genera. Gymnocactus species typically have smooth, green stems that store water, allowing them to thrive in arid environments. They produce vibrant flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red, during the spring and summer months.

Due to their unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, they are popular among succulent enthusiasts.
