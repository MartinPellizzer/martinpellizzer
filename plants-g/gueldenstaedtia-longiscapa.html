Gueldenstaedtia longiscapa

Gueldenstaedtia longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed vetch, is a perennial legume belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its root system forms nodules that host nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Native to temperate regions of Asia, it is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.
