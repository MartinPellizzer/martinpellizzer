Grimaldia opifera

Grimaldia opifera, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Grimaldia opifera is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in Native American herbal remedies.
