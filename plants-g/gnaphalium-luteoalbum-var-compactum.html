Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. compactum

Gnaphalium luteoalbum var. compactum, commonly known as the compact goldenweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense heads, typical of the Compositae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
