Globba pendula

Globba pendula, commonly known as the hanging globba, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Globba pendula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add visual interest to shaded garden spaces.
