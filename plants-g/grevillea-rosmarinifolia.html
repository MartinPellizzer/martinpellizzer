Grevillea rosmarinifolia

Grevillea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bottlebrush-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
