Gymnadenia scabrilinguis

Gymnadenia scabrilinguis, commonly known as the rough-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Gymnadenia scabrilinguis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
