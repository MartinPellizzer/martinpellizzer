Gypsophila malyeri

Gypsophila malyeri, commonly known as the Siberian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like inflorescences composed of numerous small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its ethereal appearance and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Gypsophila malyeri is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
