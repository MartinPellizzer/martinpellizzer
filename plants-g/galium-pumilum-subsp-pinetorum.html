Galium pumilum subsp. pinetorum

Galium pumilum subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically thrives in moist, acidic soils found in pine forest ecosystems.

The plant features slender, trailing stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

It plays a role in providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
