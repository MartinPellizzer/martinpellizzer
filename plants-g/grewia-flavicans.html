Grewia flavicans

Grewia flavicans, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stems. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Grewia flavicans is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid conditions.
