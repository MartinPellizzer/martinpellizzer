Gumira coriacea

Gumira coriacea, commonly known as the rough bark gumira, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark that provides protection against environmental stress. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Gumira coriacea is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
