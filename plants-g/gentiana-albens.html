Gentiana albens

Gentiana albens, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and the Carpathians. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. Gentiana albens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
