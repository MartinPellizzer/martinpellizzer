Gentianusa cruciata

Gentianusa cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Gentianusa cruciata is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
