Galium commune subvar. laeve

Galium commune subvar. laeve, commonly known as smooth bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy but become smooth with age. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
