Genista anglica subsp. ancistrocarpa

Genista anglica subsp.

ancistrocarpa, commonly known as the English broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and for erosion control.
