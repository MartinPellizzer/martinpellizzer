Grevillea miqueliana subsp. cincta

Grevillea miqueliana subsp.

cincta, commonly known as the Cincta Grevillea, is a species native to Western Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
