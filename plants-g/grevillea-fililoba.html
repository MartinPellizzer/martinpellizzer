Grevillea fililoba

Grevillea fililoba, commonly known as the silky grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea fililoba is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
