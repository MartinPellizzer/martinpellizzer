Geranium penniforme

Geranium penniforme, commonly known as the heartleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Geranium penniforme is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
