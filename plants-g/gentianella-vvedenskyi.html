Gentianella vvedenskyi

Gentianella vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern regions of Russia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are usually blue or purple in color.

Gentianella vvedenskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
