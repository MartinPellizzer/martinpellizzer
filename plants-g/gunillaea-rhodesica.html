Gunillaea rhodesica

Gunillaea rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
