Gentianella beesiana

Gentianella beesiana, commonly known as the bees' gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive two-lipped structure, typical of the gentian family.

Gentianella beesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
