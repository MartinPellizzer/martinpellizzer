Gastrorchis tuberculosa

Gastrorchis tuberculosa, commonly known as the tuberculate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Gastrorchis tuberculosa is a relatively rare species, often found in rocky or arid habitats.
