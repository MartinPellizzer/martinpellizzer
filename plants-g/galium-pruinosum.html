Galium pruinosum

Galium pruinosum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features opposite, slender, slightly hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Galium pruinosum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
