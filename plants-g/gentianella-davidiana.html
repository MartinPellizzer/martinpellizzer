Gentianella davidiana

Gentianella davidiana, commonly known as the David's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentianella davidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
