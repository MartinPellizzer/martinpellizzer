Gladiolus citrinus

Gladiolus citrinus, commonly known as the lemon gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and are typically arranged in dense spikes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are bright green and often have a waxy coating. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both gardeners and florists.
