Galium bigeminum

Galium bigeminum, commonly known as twinstem bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that produce small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in whorls. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically borne in pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium bigeminum is often found in moist soils and is part of the Rubiaceae family, known for its diverse and often fragrant flowers.
