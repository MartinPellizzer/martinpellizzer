Gymnadenia conopsea subsp. peloria

Gymnadenia conopsea subsp.

peloria, commonly known as the fairy wand or ghost orchid, is a striking orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale pink or white flowers that resemble tiny wands.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, slender flowers and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
