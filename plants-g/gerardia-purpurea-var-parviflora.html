Gerardia purpurea var. parviflora

Gerardia purpurea var. parviflora, commonly known as the purple gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of five-petaled flowers during the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
