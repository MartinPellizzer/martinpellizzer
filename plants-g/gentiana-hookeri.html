Gentiana hookeri

Gentiana hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and alpine meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana hookeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological value.
