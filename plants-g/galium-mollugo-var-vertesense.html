Galium mollugo var. vertesense

Galium mollugo var. vertesense, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium mollugo var. vertesense thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
