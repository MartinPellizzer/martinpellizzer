Gentiana centamalensis

Gentiana centamalensis, commonly known as the hundred-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its striking blue petals and is often found in alpine meadows.
