Gentiana pleurogynoides var. umbellata

Gentiana pleurogynoides var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
