It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces oval, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Galium × gyoerffyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
