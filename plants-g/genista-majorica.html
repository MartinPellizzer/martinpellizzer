Genista majorica

Genista majorica, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Genista majorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
