Garcinia pachyantha

Garcinia pachyantha, commonly known as the Indian mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, spiny bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. Garcinia pachyantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its fruit contains high levels of antioxidants and is often consumed fresh or processed into jams and juices.
