Gymnema lacei

Gymnema lacei, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which contains various bioactive compounds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Gymnema lacei is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to different soil types.
