Grewia eberhardtii

Grewia eberhardtii, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia eberhardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes.
