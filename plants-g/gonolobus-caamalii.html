Gonolobus caamalii

Gonolobus caamalii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
