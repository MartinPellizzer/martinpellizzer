Gongora quinquenervis var. leucochila

Gongora quinquenervis var. leucochila, commonly known as the white-lipped Gongora, is a fascinating orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive white lip and vibrant purple petals. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
