Gnaphalium heteroides

Gnaphalium heteroides, commonly known as the silverleaf mouseear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, silvery-gray leaves and fluffy, white flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

Gnaphalium heteroides is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
