Galium rotundifolium var. angustifolium

Galium rotundifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
