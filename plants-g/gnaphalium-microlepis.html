Gnaphalium microlepis

Gnaphalium microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaved feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, pale yellow to white composite flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Gnaphalium microlepis is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
