Gnaphalium indicum

Gnaphalium indicum, commonly known as Indian feather grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that are clustered in dense, fluffy inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic feathery appearance.

Gnaphalium indicum is widely distributed across Asia and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
