Garcinia wrayi

Garcinia wrayi, commonly known as the African yellow wood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, yellowish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate to flourish.
