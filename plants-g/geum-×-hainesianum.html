Geum × hainesianum

Geum × hainesianum, commonly known as the Himalayan fireweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its stems are hairy and often reddish in color, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
