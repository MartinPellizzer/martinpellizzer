Gloriosa grandiflora

Gloriosa grandiflora, commonly known as the flame lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers with a distinctive curling shape. The plant produces a climbing stem with heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Gloriosa grandiflora is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
