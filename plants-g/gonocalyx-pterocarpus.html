Gonocalyx pterocarpus

Gonocalyx pterocarpus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a fibrous, buttressed trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, flat pods that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
