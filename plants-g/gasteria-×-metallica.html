Gasteria × metallica

Gasteria × metallica, commonly known as the hybrid African violet plant, is a succulent belonging to the Asphodelaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pale yellow, emerging from the center of the rosette. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
