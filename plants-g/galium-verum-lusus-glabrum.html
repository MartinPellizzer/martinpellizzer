Galium verum lusus glabrum

Galium verum lusus glabrum, commonly known as golden groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant has slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
