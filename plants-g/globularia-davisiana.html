Globularia davisiana

Globularia davisiana, commonly known as the Davis's globularia, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit, with dense clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a low, mat-like structure.

Globularia davisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
