Guadua calderoniana

Guadua calderoniana, commonly known as the Colombian bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is highly valued for its sustainable use in construction and handicrafts due to its durability and rapid regeneration. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Guadua calderoniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
