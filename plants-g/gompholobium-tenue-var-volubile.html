Gompholobium tenue var. volubile

Gompholobium tenue var. volubile, commonly known as the wattle vine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, twining stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.
