Gypsophila spinosa

Gypsophila spinosa, commonly known as the desert candytuft, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers resembling tiny stars. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gypsophila spinosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
