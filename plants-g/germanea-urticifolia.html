Germanea urticifolia

Germanea urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small greenish-white flowers.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is known for its stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a food source after cooking.

Germanea urticifolia plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
