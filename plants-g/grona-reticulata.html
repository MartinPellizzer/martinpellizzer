Grona reticulata

Grona reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, net-like vein patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
