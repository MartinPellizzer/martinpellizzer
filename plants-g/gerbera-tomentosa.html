Gerbera tomentosa

Gerbera tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
