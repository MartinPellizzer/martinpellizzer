Gentianella inaequicalyx

Gentianella inaequicalyx, commonly known as the unequal-lobed gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
