Grindelia hirsutula var. maritima

Grindelia hirsutula var. maritima, commonly known as sea gumweed, is a coastal plant native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in dune environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
