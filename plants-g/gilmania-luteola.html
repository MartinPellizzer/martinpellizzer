Gilmania luteola

Gilmania luteola, commonly known as the yellow gilmania, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gilmania luteola is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
