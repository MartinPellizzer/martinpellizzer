Glinus lotoides var. typicus

Glinus lotoides var. typicus, commonly known as the hairy leaf glimmer, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its fibrous roots are known for their medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
