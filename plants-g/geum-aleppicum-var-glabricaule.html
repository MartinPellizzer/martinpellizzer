Geum aleppicum var. glabricaule

Geum aleppicum var. glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are smooth and often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
