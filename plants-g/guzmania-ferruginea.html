Guzmania ferruginea

Guzmania ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf bromeliad, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that resembles a flower, though it is actually a spike of small, clustered blooms. The plant features a rosette of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a rusty or reddish hue, giving it its common name. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for humid environments.

Guzmania ferruginea is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.
