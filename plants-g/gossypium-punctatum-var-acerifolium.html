Gossypium punctatum var. acerifolium

Gossypium punctatum var. acerifolium, commonly known as the maple-leaf cotton, is a rare and ornamental variant of the cotton genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves with lobed edges and a vibrant autumnal coloration. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers, though it is not typically cultivated for commercial cotton production. Native to specific regions in China, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage and historical significance, it is often grown in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
