Galium floribundum

Galium floribundum, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are often used in floral arrangements and as a flavoring agent in beverages.
