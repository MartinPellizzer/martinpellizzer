Gazania krebsiana subsp. krebsiana

Gazania krebsiana subsp.

krebsiana, commonly known as the Cape goldball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange ray florets and dark brown disk florets.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact growth habit and long blooming period from spring to early summer add ornamental value to gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the shape and coloration of its flowers.
