Guapira laxa

Guapira laxa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian redwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
