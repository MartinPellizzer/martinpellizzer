Grastidium crassiflorum

Grastidium crassiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower grastidium, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged periods of drought. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage organs, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Grastidium crassiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
