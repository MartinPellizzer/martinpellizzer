Gentianodes rigescens

Gentianodes rigescens, commonly known as the rigid gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vibrant blue and purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, bright blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
