Germanea parviflora

Germanea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Germanea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Germanea parviflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
