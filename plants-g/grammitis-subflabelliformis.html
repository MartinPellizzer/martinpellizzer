Grammitis subflabelliformis

Grammitis subflabelliformis, commonly known as the flabelliform maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis subflabelliformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
