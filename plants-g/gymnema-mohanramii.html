Gymnema mohanramii

Gymnema mohanramii, commonly known as the Indian bitter leaf, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive bitter taste, which is attributed to the presence of gymnemic acids. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
