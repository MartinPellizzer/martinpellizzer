Genista tricuspidata var. stipulacea

Genista tricuspidata var. stipulacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, often thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a stipulate base. Its yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
