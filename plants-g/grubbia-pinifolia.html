Grubbia pinifolia

Grubbia pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf liverwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with tiny, scale-like leaves arranged in two rows. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Grubbia pinifolia lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients through its leaf-like structures.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
