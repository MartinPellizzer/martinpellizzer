Gentianusa amarella

Gentianusa amarella, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The genus Gentianusa belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species known for their showy, blue or purple flowers.
