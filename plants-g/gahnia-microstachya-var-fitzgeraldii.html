Gahnia microstachya var. fitzgeraldii

Gahnia microstachya var. fitzgeraldii, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's spikegrass, is a variant of the common gahnia species native to Australia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.

This variant is often found in the eastern regions of Australia, particularly in Queensland and New South Wales.
