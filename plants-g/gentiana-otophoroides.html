Gentiana otophoroides

Gentiana otophoroides, commonly known as the hooded gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

The hooded gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
