Gaertneria linearis

Gaertneria linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Gaertneria linearis requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
