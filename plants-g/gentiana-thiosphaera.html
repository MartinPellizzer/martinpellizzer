Gentiana thiosphaera

Gentiana thiosphaera, commonly known as the sulfur globe gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of gentians known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its small, globe-shaped flower clusters that are typically a vivid blue or violet color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
