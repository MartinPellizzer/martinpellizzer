Geranium pyrenaicum var. malvaceum

Geranium pyrenaicum var. malvaceum, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family, which is distinct from the Malvaceae family, despite its varietal name. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the summer months.

It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
