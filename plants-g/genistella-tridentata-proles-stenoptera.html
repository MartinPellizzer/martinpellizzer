Genistella tridentata proles stenoptera

Genistella tridentata proles stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical pods.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.
