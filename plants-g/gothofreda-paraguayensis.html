Gothofreda paraguayensis

Gothofreda paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the wetlands of Paraguay.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often ghostly white or pale pink petals that resemble ethereal apparitions. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is adapted to the seasonal flooding of its habitat. Its flowers emit a faint, sweet fragrance that attracts specific pollinators, such as certain species of moths.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered critically endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
