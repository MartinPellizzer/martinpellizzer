Genipa oleosa

Genipa oleosa, commonly known as the genipapo tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit, known as the genipapo, is a small, round, and fleshy berry.

The botanical features of Genipa oleosa include its simple leaves, which are alternate and ovate in shape, and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
