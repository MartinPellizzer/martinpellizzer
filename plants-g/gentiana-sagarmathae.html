Gentiana sagarmathae

Gentiana sagarmathae, commonly known as the Sagarmatha gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing at elevations above 4,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
