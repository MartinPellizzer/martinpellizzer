Genista procera

Genista procera, commonly known as the tall broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring slender, branching stems covered in small, thorn-like structures. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae (legume) family. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Genista procera is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
