Grevillea spinosissima

Grevillea spinosissima, commonly known as the spinous grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea spinosissima is also valued for its ability to attract native wildlife, including birds and insects.
