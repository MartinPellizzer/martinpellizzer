Grevillea wickhamii subsp. macrodonta

Grevillea wickhamii subsp.

macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange cylindrical flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
