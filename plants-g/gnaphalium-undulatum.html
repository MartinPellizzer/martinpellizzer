Gnaphalium undulatum

Gnaphalium undulatum, commonly known as the wavyleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with tightly packed ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy margin, contributing to its common name.

Gnaphalium undulatum is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its long-lasting flowers that are used in floral arrangements.
