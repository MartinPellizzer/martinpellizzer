Glechoma orlichiana

Glechoma orlichiana, commonly known as the Polish ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features creeping stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Glechoma orlichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
