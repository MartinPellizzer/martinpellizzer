Gladiolus imbricatus f. robustus

Gladiolus imbricatus f. robustus, commonly known as the robust gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are often used in cut flower arrangements due to their longevity and vibrant colors.
