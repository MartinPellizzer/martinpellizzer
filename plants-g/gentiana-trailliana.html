Gentiana trailliana

Gentiana trailliana, commonly known as the Traill's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana trailliana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
