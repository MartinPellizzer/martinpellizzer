Glochidion calciphilum

Glochidion calciphilum, commonly known as the limestone goosefoot, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions with limestone soils.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, stellate hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in rocky or calcareous environments, showcasing its calciphilic nature.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
