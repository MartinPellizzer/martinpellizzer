Gnaphalium roseum var. stramineum

Gnaphalium roseum var. stramineum, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The variety stramineum is distinguished by its pale, almost white, floral coloration compared to the typical pink of the species.
