Guzmania monostachia var. variegata

Guzmania monostachia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated pineapple plant, is a striking epiphytic bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with distinct variegation patterns of green, white, and pink. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, featuring a central spadix surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
