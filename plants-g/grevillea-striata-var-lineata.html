Grevillea striata var. lineata

Grevillea striata var. lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting native birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pale hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
