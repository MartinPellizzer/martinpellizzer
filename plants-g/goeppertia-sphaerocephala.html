Goeppertia sphaerocephala

Goeppertia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the "Cushion Calathea," is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, waxy leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. The leaves are typically a deep green with intricate, variegated patterns that shift in color depending on light exposure. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
