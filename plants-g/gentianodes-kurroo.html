Gentianodes kurroo

Gentianodes kurroo, commonly known as the Kurroo gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
