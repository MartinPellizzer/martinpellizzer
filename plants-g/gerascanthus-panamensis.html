Gerascanthus panamensis

Gerascanthus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers range in color from pink to red, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
