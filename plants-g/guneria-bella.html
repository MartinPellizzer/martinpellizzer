Guneria bella

Guneria bella, commonly known as the beautiful gunnera, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
