Grewia flavescens var. olukondae

Grewia flavescens var. olukondae, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern coast of South Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in coastal and moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
