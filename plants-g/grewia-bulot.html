Grewia bulot

Grewia bulot, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various birds and animals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
