Glomera macrophylla

Glomera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant glomera, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its roots are known to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing its ability to absorb nutrients from the soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
