Gifola pyramidata

Gifola pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Gifola pyramidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
