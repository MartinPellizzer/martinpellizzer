Guarea bilocularis

Guarea bilocularis, commonly known as the two-lobed guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody capsules. Its bark and wood have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
