Glandulicactus crassihamatus

Glandulicactus crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-ribbed glochidion, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with glochids, which are small, barbed spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a popular choice for collectors and xerophytic gardens.
