Geranium papuanum var. alpestre

Geranium papuanum var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in alpine environments.
