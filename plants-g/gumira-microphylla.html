Gumira microphylla

Gumira microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved gumira, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow in color. Gumira microphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some local communities.
