Garcinia fagraeoides

Garcinia fagraeoides, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright yellow-orange berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmaceutical research. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Garcinia fagraeoides thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
