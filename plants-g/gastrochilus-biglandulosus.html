Gastrochilus biglandulosus

Gastrochilus biglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a cluster of fleshy, often hairy pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often hairy or glandular. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
