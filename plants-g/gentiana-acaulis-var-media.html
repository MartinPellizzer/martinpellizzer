Gentiana acaulis var. media

Gentiana acaulis var. media, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer and are arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with a fine layer of hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
