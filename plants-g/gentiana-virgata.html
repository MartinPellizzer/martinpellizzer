Gentiana virgata

Gentiana virgata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and often branched.

Gentiana virgata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
