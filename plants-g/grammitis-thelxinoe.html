Grammitis thelxinoe

Grammitis thelxinoe, commonly known as the silver hair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Grammitis thelxinoe is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks without being parasitic.

Its intricate structure and unique appearance make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among fern enthusiasts.
