Gaultheria myrtilloides var. minor

Gaultheria myrtilloides var. minor, commonly known as dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract wildlife, particularly birds, due to its nutritious fruit.
