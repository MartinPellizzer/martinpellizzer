Gomphrena strigosa

Gomphrena strigosa, commonly known as the rough gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena strigosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
