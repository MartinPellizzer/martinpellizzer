Gentiana rupicola var. grandifolia

Gentiana rupicola var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
