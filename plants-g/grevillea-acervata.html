Grevillea acervata

Grevillea acervata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is often found in woodland and heathland environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Grevillea acervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
