Geranium angelense

Geranium angelense, commonly known as the angel's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
