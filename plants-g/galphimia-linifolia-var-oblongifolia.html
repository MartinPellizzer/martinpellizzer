Galphimia linifolia var. oblongifolia

Galphimia linifolia var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
