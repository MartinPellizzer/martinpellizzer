Gongora claviodora

Gongora claviodora, commonly known as the Claviodora orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature intricate patterns and a unique lip structure. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that can range in color from white to pink and purple.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
