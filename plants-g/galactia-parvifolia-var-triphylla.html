Galactia parvifolia var. triphylla

Galactia parvifolia var. triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, trifoliate leaves composed of three small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its pods are slender and contain several seeds enclosed in a fibrous membrane.

It is known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus Galactia.
