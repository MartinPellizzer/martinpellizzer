Goodenia glandulosa

Goodenia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its structure includes a low-growing, herbaceous stem with opposite leaves that have prominent glands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
