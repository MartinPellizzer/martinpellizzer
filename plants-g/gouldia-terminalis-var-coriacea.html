Gouldia terminalis var. coriacea

Gouldia terminalis var. coriacea, commonly known as the hardy gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Australia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

The varietal name "coriacea" refers to its tough, leathery leaves, which help it withstand harsh environmental conditions.
