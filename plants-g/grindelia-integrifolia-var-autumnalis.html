Grindelia integrifolia var. autumnalis

Grindelia integrifolia var. autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-white composite flower heads and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
