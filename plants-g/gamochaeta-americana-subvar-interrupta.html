Gamochaeta americana subvar. interrupta

Gamochaeta americana subvar. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. It produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.
