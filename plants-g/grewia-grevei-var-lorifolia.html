Grewia grevei var. lorifolia

Grewia grevei var. lorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
