Gentiana andrewsii

Gentiana andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrews' gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana andrewsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
