Gentiana szeewaldiana

Gentiana szeewaldiana, commonly known as the Szeewald's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
