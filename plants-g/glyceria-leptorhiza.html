Glyceria leptorhiza

Glyceria leptorhiza, commonly known as slender glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity.

Glyceria leptorhiza plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
