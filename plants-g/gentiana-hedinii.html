Gentiana hedinii

Gentiana hedinii, commonly known as the Hedin's gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentiana hedinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
