Grevillea hakeoides subsp. hakeoides

Grevillea hakeoides subsp.

hakeoides, commonly known as the Hakea-like Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The plant produces striking, crimson or red-orange inflorescences that are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. These flowers are adapted for pollination by nectar-feeding birds, particularly honeyeaters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.
