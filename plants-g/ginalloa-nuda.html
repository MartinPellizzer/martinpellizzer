Ginalloa nuda

Ginalloa nuda, commonly known as the naked ginalloa, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems and small, pale green leaves that lack any visible pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom during the early morning hours. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in the field of plant morphology and ecology.
