Gentiana filiformis

Gentiana filiformis, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Gentiana filiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
