Gaultheria leucocarpa var. hirsuta

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy white wax myrtle, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, waxy, white berries that are typically eaten by wildlife. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It has aromatic leaves that release a pleasant scent when crushed, and its evergreen nature makes it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing food and habitat for various bird species.
