Grammica occidentalis

Grammica occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Grammica, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the western hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Grammica occidentalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low-maintenance once established.
