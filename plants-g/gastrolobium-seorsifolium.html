Gastrolobium seorsifolium

Gastrolobium seorsifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in winter. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Gastrolobium seorsifolium is also known for its role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
