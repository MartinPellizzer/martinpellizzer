Geranium pichinchense

Geranium pichinchense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium pichinchense is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and adaptability to various soil conditions.
