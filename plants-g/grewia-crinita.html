Grewia crinita

Grewia crinita, commonly known as the silky bush or white silk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but often have a bland taste. Grewia crinita thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
