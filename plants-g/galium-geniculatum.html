Galium geniculatum

Galium geniculatum, commonly known as the zigzag wood-sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Galium geniculatum is often found in the understory of forests and is part of the Rubiaceae family.
