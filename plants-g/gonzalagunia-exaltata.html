Gonzalagunia exaltata

Gonzalagunia exaltata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Gonzalagunia exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
