Gladiolus angustus

Gladiolus angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gladiolus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking, spike-like inflorescence composed of brightly colored, spurred flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
