Gomphrena hygrophila var. subecristata

Gomphrena hygrophila var. subecristata, commonly known as the narrow-crested gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink or red bracts that resemble flower heads. The plant has succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
