Grypocarpha liebmannii

Grypocarpha liebmannii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, alternate leaves and produces seeds in a distinctive crown-like structure.

Grypocarpha liebmannii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
