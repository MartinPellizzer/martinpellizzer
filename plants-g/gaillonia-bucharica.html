Gaillonia bucharica

Gaillonia bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Gaillonia bucharica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
