Geranium fulgidum

Geranium fulgidum, commonly known as the silverleaf geranium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems covered in soft, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are rounded and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Geranium fulgidum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
