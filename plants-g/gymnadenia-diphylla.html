Gymnadenia diphylla

Gymnadenia diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has two pairs of opposite leaves, which give it its common name.

Gymnadenia diphylla is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
