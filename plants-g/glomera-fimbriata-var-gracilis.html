Glomera fimbriata var. gracilis

Glomera fimbriata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fringed glomera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and intricate, fringed floral structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
