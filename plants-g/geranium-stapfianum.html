Geranium stapfianum

Geranium stapfianum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces vibrant blooms in shades of pink, red, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium stapfianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
