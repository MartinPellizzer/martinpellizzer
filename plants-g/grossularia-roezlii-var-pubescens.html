Grossularia roezlii var. pubescens

Grossularia roezlii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rosaceae family, valued for both its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
