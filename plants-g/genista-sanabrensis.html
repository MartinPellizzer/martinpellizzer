Genista sanabrensis

Genista sanabrensis, commonly known as the Sanabrensis Broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista sanabrensis is often found in scrublands and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
