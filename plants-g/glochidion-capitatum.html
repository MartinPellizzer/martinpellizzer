Glochidion capitatum

Glochidion capitatum, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Glochidion capitatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
