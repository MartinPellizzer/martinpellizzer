Genista angustifolia

Genista angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
