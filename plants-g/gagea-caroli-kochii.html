Gagea caroli-kochii

Gagea caroli-kochii, commonly known as the Caroli-Koch's spring beauty, is a small perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gagea caroli-kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
