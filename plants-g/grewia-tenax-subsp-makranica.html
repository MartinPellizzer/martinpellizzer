Grewia tenax subsp. makranica

Grewia tenax subsp.

makranica, commonly known as the Makranic bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and saline conditions.

The berries are edible and used in local cuisines, while the plant also serves as a habitat for various insects and birds.
