Garcinia nigrolineata

Garcinia nigrolineata, commonly known as the black-line garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent black veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. Garcinia nigrolineata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and rainforest ecosystems.
