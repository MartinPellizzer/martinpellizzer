Gyrostephium rhizocephalum

Gyrostephium rhizocephalum, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, allowing it to anchor deeply in the soil and access moisture from deeper layers.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses including the treatment of skin conditions and inflammation.
