Gentiana myriantha

Gentiana myriantha, commonly known as the many-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its roots are tuberous and used in traditional medicine.

Gentiana myriantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
