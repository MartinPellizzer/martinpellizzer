Gouldia

Gouldia, commonly known as the "sea holly," is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has silvery, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Gouldia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
