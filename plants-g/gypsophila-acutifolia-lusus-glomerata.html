Gypsophila acutifolia lusus glomerata

Gypsophila acutifolia lusus glomerata, commonly known as the baby's breath or lace flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favorite in both ornamental gardening and cut flower production.
