Grevillea hookeriana

Grevillea hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, recurved red or orange flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
