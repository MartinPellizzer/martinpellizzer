Gastrolobium wonganense

Gastrolobium wonganense, commonly known as Wongan wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Gastrolobium wonganense is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
