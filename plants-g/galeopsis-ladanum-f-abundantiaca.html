Galeopsis ladanum f. abundantiaca

Galeopsis ladanum f. abundantiaca, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
