Gymnocalycium hyptiacanthum subsp. uruguayense

Gymnocalycium hyptiacanthum subsp.

uruguayense, commonly known as the Uruguay prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Uruguay and parts of Argentina. It is characterized by its globular to cylindrical stems, which are covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
