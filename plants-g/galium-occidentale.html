Galium occidentale

Galium occidentale, commonly known as western bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

Galium occidentale is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.
