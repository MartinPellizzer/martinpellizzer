Geranium jahnii

Geranium jahnii, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Geranium jahnii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
