Gomphrena denudata

Gomphrena denudata, commonly known as the naked stem gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive, papery bract that gives the plant its common name. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.

Gomphrena denudata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
