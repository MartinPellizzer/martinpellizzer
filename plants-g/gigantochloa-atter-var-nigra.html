Gigantochloa atter var. nigra

Gigantochloa atter var. nigra, commonly known as black bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast sharply with its green leaves. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with tall, sturdy stalks that can reach heights of several meters.

The botanical features of Gigantochloa atter var. nigra make it a striking ornamental plant in both gardens and horticultural collections.
