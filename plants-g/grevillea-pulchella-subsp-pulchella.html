Grevillea pulchella subsp. pulchella

Grevillea pulchella subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the pink flowering waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of a dense cluster of small, pinkish-red flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces woody, cylindrical flower spikes that are often adorned with bracts resembling petals. Grevillea pulchella subsp.

pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
