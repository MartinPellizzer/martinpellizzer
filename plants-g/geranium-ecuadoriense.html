Geranium ecuadoriense

Geranium ecuadoriense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features rounded leaves with serrated edges and produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both horticulture and ecological settings.
