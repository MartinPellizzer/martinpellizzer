Griersonia oblongifolia

Griersonia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf griersonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Griersonia oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
