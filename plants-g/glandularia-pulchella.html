Glandularia pulchella

Glandularia pulchella, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with glandular hairs that secrete a sticky substance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
