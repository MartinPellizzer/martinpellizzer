Gentiana recurvata subsp. prainii

Gentiana recurvata subsp.

prainii, commonly known as the Prain's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
