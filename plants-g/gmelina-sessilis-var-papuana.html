Gmelina sessilis var. papuana

Gmelina sessilis var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dark, hard, and durable wood, which is highly valued for construction and furniture-making. The tree has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn red during the dry season. It produces small, white to greenish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in reforestation efforts.
