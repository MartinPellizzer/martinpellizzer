Gasteria lutzii

Gasteria lutzii, commonly known as the Zulu candle.

It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are fleshy and often adorned with a waxy coating. The leaves are typically dark green with a bluish tinge and have a prominent central vein. It produces clusters of small, white flowers on tall, erect stems during the growing season.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
