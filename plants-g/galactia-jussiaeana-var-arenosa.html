Galactia jussiaeana var. arenosa

Galactia jussiaeana var. arenosa, commonly known as sand milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
