Gymnaster angustifolius

Gymnaster angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gymnaster, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It typically grows in temperate regions with well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges or open fields. The plant features slender, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers cluster in umbrella-like inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gymnaster angustifolius is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
