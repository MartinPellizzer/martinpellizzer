Grevillea bipinnatifida subsp. bipinnatifida

Grevillea bipinnatifida subsp.

bipinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a species native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red flower spikes that bloom in spring.

The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves arranged in a feathery pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in heathlands and open forests. Grevillea bipinnatifida subsp.

bipinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
