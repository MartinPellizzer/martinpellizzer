Gentianella amarella subsp. takedae

Gentianella amarella subsp.

takedae, commonly known as the Japanese yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
