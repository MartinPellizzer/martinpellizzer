Genista aspalathoides

Genista aspalathoides, commonly known as the Cape broom, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
