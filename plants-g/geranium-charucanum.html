Geranium charucanum

Geranium charucanum, commonly known as the Chilean geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of bright red or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Geranium charucanum is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
