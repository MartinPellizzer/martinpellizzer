Galeola siamensis

Galeola siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing numerous small, colorful blossoms.

Galeola siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
