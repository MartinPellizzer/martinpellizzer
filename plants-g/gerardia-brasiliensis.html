Gerardia brasiliensis

Gerardia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian gerardia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Gerardia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
