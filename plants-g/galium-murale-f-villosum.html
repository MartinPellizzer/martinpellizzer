Galium murale f. villosum

Galium murale f. villosum, commonly known as hairy wallge.

It is a variant of the common bedstraw, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and slender, ascending stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this form.
