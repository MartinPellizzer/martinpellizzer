Genista catalaunica

Genista catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Genista catalaunica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
