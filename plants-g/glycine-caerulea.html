Glycine caerulea

Glycine caerulea, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking blue to violet flowers with a white throat, blooming in the early morning. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets and is often found in moist, shaded areas. Glycine caerulea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
