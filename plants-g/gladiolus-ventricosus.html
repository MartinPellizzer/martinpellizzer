Gladiolus ventricosus

Gladiolus ventricosus, commonly known as the swollen gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its robust growth habit. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it typically blooms in late spring to early summer.

The swollen gladiolus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
