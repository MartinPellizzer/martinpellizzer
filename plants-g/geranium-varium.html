Geranium varium

Geranium varium, commonly known as the variable geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in various habitats including grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with opposite, serrated leaves that are typically hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, making it visually striking.

Geranium varium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.
