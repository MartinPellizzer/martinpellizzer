Gentiana straminea

Gentiana straminea, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded locations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

The yellow gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
