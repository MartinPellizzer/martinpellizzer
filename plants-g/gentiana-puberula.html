Gentiana puberula

Gentiana puberula, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name.

Gentiana puberula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
