Gynamblosis maritima

Gynamblosis maritima, commonly known as the coastal gynamblosis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in areas with high saline content. It has adapted to coastal conditions through its drought-resistant properties and salt tolerance.

Gynamblosis maritima plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
