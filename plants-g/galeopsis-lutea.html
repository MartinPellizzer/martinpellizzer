Galeopsis lutea

Galeopsis lutea, commonly known as yellow hedge nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Galeopsis lutea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
