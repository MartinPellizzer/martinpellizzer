Galanthus nivalis f. pictus

Galanthus nivalis f. pictus, commonly known as the "Picotee snowdrop," is a variant of the snowdrop plant characterized by its distinctive white petals with a greenish or yellowish stripe.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces delicate, nodding flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in woodland or shady garden settings, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge alongside the flower stems.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
