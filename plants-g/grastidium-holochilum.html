Grastidium holochilum

Grastidium holochilum, commonly known as the red-flowered grassveld daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Grastidium holochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
