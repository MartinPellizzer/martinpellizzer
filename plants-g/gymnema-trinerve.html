Gymnema trinerve

Gymnema trinerve, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, trifoliate leaves and distinctive, milky sap. The plant is notable for its ability to inhibit sweet taste perception, a property that has been studied for its potential in managing diabetes. Gymnema trinerve produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
