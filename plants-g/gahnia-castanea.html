Gahnia castanea

Gahnia castanea, commonly known as the red tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown tussocks and is often found in wetland and swampy areas. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Gahnia castanea is adapted to moist environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
