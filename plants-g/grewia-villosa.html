Grewia villosa

Grewia villosa, commonly known as the silky bush or white globemallow, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, ovate leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Grewia villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
