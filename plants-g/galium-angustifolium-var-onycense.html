Galium angustifolium var. onycense

Galium angustifolium var. onycense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
