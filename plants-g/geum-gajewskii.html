Geum gajewskii

Geum gajewskii, commonly known as the Siberian avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided with serrated edges, and its stems are hairy and often branched. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Geum gajewskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to colder climates.
