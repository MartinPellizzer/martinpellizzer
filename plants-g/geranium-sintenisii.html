Geranium sintenisii

Geranium sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
