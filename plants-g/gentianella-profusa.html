Gentianella profusa

Gentianella profusa, commonly known as the common gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella profusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
