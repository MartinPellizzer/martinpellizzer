Gentianella eichleri

Gentianella eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and erect.

Gentianella eichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
