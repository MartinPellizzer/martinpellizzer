Gymnogramma calomelanos f. albo-farinosa

Gymnogramma calomelanos f. albo-farinosa, commonly known as the white-fuzzy gymnogramma, is a variant of the gymnogramma species native to tropical regions.

It is a fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped and exhibit a light green color with a distinctive waxy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

This form is prized for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
