Galium proliferum

Galium proliferum, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Galium proliferum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons, making it a common weed in various habitats.
