Gloxiniopsis racemosa

Gloxiniopsis racemosa, commonly known as the clustered gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gloxiniopsis racemosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
