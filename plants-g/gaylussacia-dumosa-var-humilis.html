Gaylussacia dumosa var. humilis

Gaylussacia dumosa var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf sweet pepperbush, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a serrated edge.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.
