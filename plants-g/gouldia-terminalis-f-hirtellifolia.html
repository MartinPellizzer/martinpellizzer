Gouldia terminalis f. hirtellifolia

Gouldia terminalis f. hirtellifolia, commonly known as the woolly-flowered gouldia, is a distinctive member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or swampy areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticultural displays.
