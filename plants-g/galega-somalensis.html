Galega somalensis

Galega somalensis, commonly known as the Somali goatherb, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Galega somalensis is often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock in its native region.
