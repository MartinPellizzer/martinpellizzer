Gypsophila silenoides

Gypsophila silenoides, commonly known as the sweet William or mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, delicate, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other ornamental plants.

Gypsophila silenoides is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and floral arrangements.
