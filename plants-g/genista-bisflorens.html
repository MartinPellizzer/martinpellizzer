Genista bisflorens

Genista bisflorens, commonly known as the two-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is shrub-like, with slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista bisflorens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments.
