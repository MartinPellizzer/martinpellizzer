Gravesia scripta

Gravesia scripta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gravesia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom during the wet season and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Gravesia scripta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
