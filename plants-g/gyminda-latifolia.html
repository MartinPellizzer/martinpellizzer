Gyminda latifolia

Gyminda latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gyminda, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of white and pink. Gyminda latifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
