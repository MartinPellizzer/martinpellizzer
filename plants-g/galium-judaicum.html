Galium judaicum

Galium judaicum, commonly known as the Jerusalem candle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium judaicum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
