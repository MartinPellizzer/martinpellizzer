Gentiana depressa

Gentiana depressa, commonly known as the drooping gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
