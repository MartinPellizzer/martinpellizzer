Gardenia cuneata

Gardenia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Gardenia cuneata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive scent.
