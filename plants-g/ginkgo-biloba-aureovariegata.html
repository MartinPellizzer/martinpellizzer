Ginkgo biloba aureovariegata

Ginkgo biloba aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden fan ginkgo, is a striking variant of the ginkgo tree characterized by its variegated, golden-yellow leaves.

This tree is a deciduous species native to China and belongs to the family Ginkgoaceae, making it one of the oldest living tree species. Its leaves are fan-shaped and exhibit a unique pattern of yellow and green pigmentation due to the presence of chlorophyll and carotenoids. The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It produces small, scaly fruits that are inedible for humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife species.
