Geum schofieldii

Geum schofieldii, commonly known as Schofield's avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with serrated edges and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers are borne on slender, upright stems and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Geum schofieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
