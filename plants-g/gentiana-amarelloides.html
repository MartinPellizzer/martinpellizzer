Gentiana amarelloides

Gentiana amarelloides, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

The genus Gentiana is known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers, and Gentiana amarelloides is no exception.
