Gentianella campanuliformis

Gentianella campanuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
