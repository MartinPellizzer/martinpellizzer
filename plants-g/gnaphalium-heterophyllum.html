Gnaphalium heterophyllum

Gnaphalium heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
