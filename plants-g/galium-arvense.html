Galium arvense

Galium arvense, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Galium arvense is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
