Glandonia

Glandonia, commonly known as the "scented orchid," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators. Glandonia species typically have elongated, slender stems and showy, colorful blooms with intricate patterns. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in South America and parts of Africa.

The genus is noted for its diverse morphology and ecological adaptations, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
