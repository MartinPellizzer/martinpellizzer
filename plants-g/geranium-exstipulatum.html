Geranium exstipulatum

Geranium exstipulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are arranged in dense inflorescences.

Geranium exstipulatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
