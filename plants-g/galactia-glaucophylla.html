Galactia glaucophylla

Galactia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf galactia, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Galactia glaucophylla thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
