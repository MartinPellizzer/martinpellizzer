Gnaphalium fuscum var. subacaule

Gnaphalium fuscum var. subacaule, commonly known as the slender woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience.

The variety subacaule is distinguished by its shorter stem compared to the typical form of Gnaphalium fuscum.
