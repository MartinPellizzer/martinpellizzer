Gymnocalycium kieslingii subsp. castaneum

Gymnocalycium kieslingii subsp.

castaneum, commonly known as the red-tipped moon cactus, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It features a short, thick stem with a distinctive red or purple coloration at the tips, which contrasts with its pale green body.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It has thick, fleshy stems adapted for water storage, typical of desert plants.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
