Genista tetragona subsp. tetragona

Genista tetragona subsp.

tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that are typically four-angled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
