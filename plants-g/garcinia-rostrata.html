Garcinia rostrata

Garcinia rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Garcinia rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal fruit.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to the flora of tropical and subtropical regions.
