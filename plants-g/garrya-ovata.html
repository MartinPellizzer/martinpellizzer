Garrya ovata

Garrya ovata, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, dark blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Garrya ovata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
