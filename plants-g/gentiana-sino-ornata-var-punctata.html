Gentiana sino-ornata var. punctata

Gentiana sino-ornata var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched. This variety is distinguished by the presence of small, dark spots on its petals, which give it its common name.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
