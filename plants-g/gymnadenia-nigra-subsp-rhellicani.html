Gymnadenia nigra subsp. rhellicani

Gymnadenia nigra subsp.

rhellicani, commonly known as the black orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking dark purple to black flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
