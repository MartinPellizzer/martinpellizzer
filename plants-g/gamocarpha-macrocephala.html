Gamocarpha macrocephala

Gamocarpha macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed gamocarpha, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like composite flowers that consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Gamocarpha macrocephala is native to regions of North America and is often found in the western United States.
