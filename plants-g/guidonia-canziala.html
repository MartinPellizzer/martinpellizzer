Guidonia canziala

Guidonia canziala, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
