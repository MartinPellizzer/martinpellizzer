Gasteria fasciata var. laxa

Gasteria fasciata var. laxa, commonly known as the Zulu bulb.

This compact, evergreen succulent is native to South Africa and features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white or yellowish markings. The leaves are arranged in a tight spiral, giving the plant a distinctive, almost geometric appearance. It produces small, pale greenish-white flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.
