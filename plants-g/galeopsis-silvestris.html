Galeopsis silvestris

Galeopsis silvestris, commonly known as field hemp or wild hemp, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated margins.

Galeopsis silvestris is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a source of nectar for pollinators.
