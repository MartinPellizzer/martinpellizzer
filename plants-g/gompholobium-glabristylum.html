Gompholobium glabristylum

Gompholobium glabristylum, commonly known as the silky gompholobium, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
