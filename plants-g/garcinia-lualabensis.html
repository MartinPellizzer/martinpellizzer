Garcinia lualabensis

Garcinia lualabensis, commonly known as the Lualabensis Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it has potential applications in traditional medicine and natural health products.
