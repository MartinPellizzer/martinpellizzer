Gomphrena affinis

Gomphrena affinis, commonly known as the common globe flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spherical flower heads that range in color from red to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gomphrena affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
