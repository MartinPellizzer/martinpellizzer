Gladiolus violaceus

Gladiolus violaceus, commonly known as the violet gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a corm as its primary storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and long-lasting cut flowers.
