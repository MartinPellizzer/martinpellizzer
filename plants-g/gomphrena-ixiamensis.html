Gomphrena ixiamensis

Gomphrena ixiamensis, commonly known as the ixia gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, pink or red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.

Gomphrena ixiamensis is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
