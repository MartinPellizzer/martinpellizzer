Gymnosporia amaniensis

Gymnosporia amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani burr bush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Tanzania.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that enclose its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Gymnosporia amaniensis is adapted to dry and rocky habitats, making it a resilient species in its native environment.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
