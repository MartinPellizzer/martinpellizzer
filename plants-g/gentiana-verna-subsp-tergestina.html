Gentiana verna subsp. tergestina

Gentiana verna subsp.

tergestina, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter petals compared to the typical G. verna.
