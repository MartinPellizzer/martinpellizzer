Glicophyllum chamaecerasifolium

Glicophyllum chamaecerasifolium, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and desert regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry climates.
