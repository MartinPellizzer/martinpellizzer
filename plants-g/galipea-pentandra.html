Galipea pentandra

Galipea pentandra, commonly known as the purpleheart tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Galipea pentandra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its valuable timber.
