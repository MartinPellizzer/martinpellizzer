Guioa dasyantha

Guioa dasyantha, commonly known as the hairy guioa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, smooth fruits that split open to release seeds. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
