Gentiana recurvata subsp. recurvata

Gentiana recurvata subsp.

recurvata, commonly known as the recurved gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
