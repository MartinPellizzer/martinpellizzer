Grammitis blepharodes

Grammitis blepharodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grammitis blepharodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
