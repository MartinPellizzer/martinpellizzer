Geranium sanguineum var. majus

Geranium sanguineum var. majus, commonly known as the Greater Garden Geranium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a textured surface that adds to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
