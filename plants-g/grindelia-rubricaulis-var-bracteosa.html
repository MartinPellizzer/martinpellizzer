Grindelia rubricaulis var. bracteosa

Grindelia rubricaulis var. bracteosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
