Gentianella silenoides var. striaticalyx

Gentianella silenoides var. striaticalyx, commonly known as the striped gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in early spring.

This variant is distinguished by its distinctive, narrow stripes on the petals, which give it its common name.
