Galium angustifolium var. pinetorum

Galium angustifolium var. pinetorum, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, particularly in coniferous or mixed woodlands. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. Small white flowers appear in clusters during the summer, followed by small, dry fruits.

It plays a role in providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
