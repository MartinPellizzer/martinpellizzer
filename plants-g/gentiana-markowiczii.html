Gentiana markowiczii

Gentiana markowiczii, commonly known as the Markowicz gentian, is a rare alpine plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are tubular with a prominent blue corolla, and it blooms in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
