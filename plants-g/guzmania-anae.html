Guzmania anae

Guzmania anae, commonly known as the red-tipped pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with the most distinguishing feature being its red-tipped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces an inflorescence that resembles a pineapple, with bright orange or red flowers emerging from the center. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Guzmania anae is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
