Goniopteris tetragona var. martinicensis

Goniopteris tetragona var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique shield fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed shield fern native to the West Indies.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive veining pattern.

This fern is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in dense forests or along stream banks.
