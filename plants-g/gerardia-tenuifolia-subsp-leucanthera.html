Gerardia tenuifolia subsp. leucanthera

Gerardia tenuifolia subsp.

leucanthera, commonly known as the white-flowered gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, erect stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to ecological and horticultural studies.
