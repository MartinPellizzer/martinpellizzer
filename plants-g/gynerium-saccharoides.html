Gynerium saccharoides

Gynerium saccharoides, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to wetlands and riparian zones in the Americas.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a fibrous, hollow stem that is often used in basket-making. The plant thrives in moist environments and is characterized by its ability to form dense stands, which can help prevent soil erosion. Its flowering spikes are slender and appear in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Gynerium saccharoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wet garden settings.
