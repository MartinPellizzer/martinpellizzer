Gentiana affinis var. bigelovii

Gentiana affinis var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
