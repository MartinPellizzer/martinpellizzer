Glyceria canadensis var. laxa

Glyceria canadensis var. laxa, commonly known as softleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to moist soil conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
