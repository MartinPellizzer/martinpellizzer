Guadua glomerata

Guadua glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Guadua glomerata is known for its strong, lightweight culms, which make it a valuable resource for construction and crafts.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
