Gleditsia bujotii

Gleditsia bujotii, commonly known as the Bujotii honeylocust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorned stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments and is often used for soil stabilization and ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
