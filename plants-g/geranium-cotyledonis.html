Geranium cotyledonis

Geranium cotyledonis, commonly known as the Cretan geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Geranium cotyledonis is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
