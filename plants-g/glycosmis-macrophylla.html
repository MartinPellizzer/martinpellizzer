Glycosmis macrophylla

Glycosmis macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed sweetgum, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically large and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are red when mature. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
