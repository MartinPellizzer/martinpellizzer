Gentiana clarkei subsp. clarkei

Gentiana clarkei subsp.

clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
