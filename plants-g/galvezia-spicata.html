Galvezia spicata

Galvezia spicata, commonly known as the spiny galvezia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually simple, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

Galvezia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
