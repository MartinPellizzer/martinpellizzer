Galeandra minax

Galeandra minax, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Galeandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

The flowers of Galeandra minax are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
