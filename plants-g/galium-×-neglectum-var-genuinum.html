Galium × neglectum var. genuinum

Galium × neglectum var. genuinum, commonly known as the common wood-sorrel, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside, and produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it spreads by rhizomes or stolons.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often found in disturbed or partially shaded soils.
