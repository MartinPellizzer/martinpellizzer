Gentiana farreri

Gentiana farreri, commonly known as the Tibetan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana farreri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some Asian cultures.
