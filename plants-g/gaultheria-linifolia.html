Gaultheria linifolia

Gaultheria linifolia, commonly known as the evergreen trailing cranberry bush, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a waxy coating. Its flowers are white and bell-shaped, appearing in clusters, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Gaultheria linifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
