Genista longipes subsp. gadorensis

Genista longipes subsp.

gadorensis, commonly known as the Gadorensis Broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in sunny, open habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a relatively small species, with slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves.
