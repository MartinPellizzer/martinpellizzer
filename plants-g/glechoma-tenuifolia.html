Glechoma tenuifolia

Glechoma tenuifolia, commonly known as creeping ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Glechoma tenuifolia is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
