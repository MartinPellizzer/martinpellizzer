Gossypium macrospermum

Gossypium macrospermum, commonly known as the large-seed cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and large, fluffy bolls that contain the cotton fibers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy surface, and it produces showy, pink to red flowers.

Gossypium macrospermum is valued for its high-quality cotton fibers, which are used in textile production.
