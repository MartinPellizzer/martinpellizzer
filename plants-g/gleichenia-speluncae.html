Gleichenia speluncae

Gleichenia speluncae, commonly known as the cave fern, is a rare and specialized plant species adapted to grow in dark, humid environments such as caves.

It belongs to the family Gleicheniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, strap-like fronds that are often pale green or brownish in color. This fern thrives in low-light conditions and relies on moisture from dripping water or high humidity levels. Its reproductive structures are typically found on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Gleichenia speluncae is often found in isolated cave systems and is considered a unique example of subterranean plant life.
