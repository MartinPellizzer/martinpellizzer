Gesneria libanensis subsp. lopezii

Gesneria libanensis subsp.

lopezii, commonly known as the Lebanese gesneria, is a rare and endemic species native to parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a silvery underside.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange, typically blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
