Geranium squarrosum

Geranium squarrosum, commonly known as the rough geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in regions with suitable climates.
