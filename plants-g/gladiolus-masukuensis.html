Gladiolus masukuensis

Gladiolus masukuensis, commonly known as the Masukuensis gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its use in cut flower markets.
