Gladiolus laxiflorus

Gladiolus laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are typically borne on one side of the stem, a characteristic known as scorpioid fasciation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
