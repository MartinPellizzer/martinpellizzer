Guapira pernambucensis

Guapira pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco guapira, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
