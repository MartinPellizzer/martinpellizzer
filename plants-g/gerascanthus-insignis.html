Gerascanthus insignis

Gerascanthus insignis, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions where it tolerates cool temperatures and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
