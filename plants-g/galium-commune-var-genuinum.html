Galium commune var. genuinum

Galium commune var. genuinum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and can reach up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
