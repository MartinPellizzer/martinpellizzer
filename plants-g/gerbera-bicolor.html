Gerbera bicolor

Gerbera bicolor, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with two distinct color zones. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow, orange, red, and white, often with contrasting central disks.

Gerbera bicolor is popular in horticulture for its vibrant colors and relatively easy care requirements.
