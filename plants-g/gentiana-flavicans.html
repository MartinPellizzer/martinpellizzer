Gentiana flavicans

Gentiana flavicans, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or yellow corolla, often with five lobes, and are pollinated primarily by insects.
