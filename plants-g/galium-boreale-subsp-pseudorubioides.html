Galium boreale subsp. pseudorubioides

Galium boreale subsp.

pseudorubioides, commonly known as the northern bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in whorled clusters along the stem, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.
