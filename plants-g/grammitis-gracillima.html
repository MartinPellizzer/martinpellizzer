Grammitis gracillima

Grammitis gracillima, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Grammitis gracillima has small, spore-bearing structures called sori that are located on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, indoor setting.
