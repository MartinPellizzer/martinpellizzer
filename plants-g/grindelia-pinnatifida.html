Grindelia pinnatifida

Grindelia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, while its stems are erect and branched.
