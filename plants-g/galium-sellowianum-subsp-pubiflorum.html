Galium sellowianum subsp. pubiflorum

Galium sellowianum subsp.

pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and have a serrated edge, with a generally herbaceous texture.

The species is characterized by its subshrub growth form and is often found in humid, temperate regions.
