Glossorhyncha fruticula

Glossorhyncha fruticula, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Glossorhyncha fruticula is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
