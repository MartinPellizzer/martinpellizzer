Galenia steingroeveri

Galenia steingroeveri, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy habit with narrow, linear leaves. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Galenia steingroeveri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
