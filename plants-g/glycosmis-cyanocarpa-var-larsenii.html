Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. larsenii

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. larsenii, commonly known as the blue-fruited haw, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a distinctive blue-black drupe, which is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
