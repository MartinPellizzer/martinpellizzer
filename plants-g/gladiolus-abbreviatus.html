Gladiolus abbreviatus

Gladiolus abbreviatus, commonly known as the dwarf gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, making it a compact addition to gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is relatively easy to cultivate and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.
