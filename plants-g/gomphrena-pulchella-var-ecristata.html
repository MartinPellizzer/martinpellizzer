Gomphrena pulchella var. ecristata

Gomphrena pulchella var. ecristata, commonly known as the pink plume or red plume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant pink or red bract-like flowers that resemble plumes or feathery structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by showy, papery bracts that give it its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
