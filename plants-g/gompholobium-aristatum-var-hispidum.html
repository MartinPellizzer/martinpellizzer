Gompholobium aristatum var. hispidum

Gompholobium aristatum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy spear wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, spiky pods. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy texture on the stems and leaves, giving it its common name.
