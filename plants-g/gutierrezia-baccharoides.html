Gutierrezia baccharoides

Gutierrezia baccharoides, commonly known as silverleaf greasewood, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and adapted to stabilize sandy soils, making it important in desert ecosystems.

Gutierrezia baccharoides is often found in saline and alkali soils, showcasing its tolerance to harsh environmental conditions.
