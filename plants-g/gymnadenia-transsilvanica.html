Gymnadenia transsilvanica

Gymnadenia transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian grasspocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive hooded lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Gymnadenia transsilvanica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
