Galium × elegans

Galium × elegans, commonly known as the silverweed or white bedstraw, is a hybrid species within the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in cymes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often reddish and covered with fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Galium × elegans is commonly found in moist, disturbed soils in meadows and along stream banks.
