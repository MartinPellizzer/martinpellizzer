Gentiana linearis var. rubricaulis

Gentiana linearis var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a reddish hue, especially when mature. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a sought-after species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
