Geranium pylzowianum var. alpinum

Geranium pylzowianum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and rockeries.
