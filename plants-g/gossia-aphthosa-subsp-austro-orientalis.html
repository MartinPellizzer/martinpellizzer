Gossia aphthosa subsp. austro-orientalis

Gossia aphthosa subsp.

austro-orientalis, commonly known as the eastern gossia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or near-coastal regions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
