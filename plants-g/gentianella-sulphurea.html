Gentianella sulphurea

Gentianella sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. The plant prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows, often growing in rocky or limestone soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
