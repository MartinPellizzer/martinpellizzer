Gleditsia pachycarpa

Gleditsia pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited honey locust, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and often have small, thorn-like spines. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, followed by woody, pod-like fruits that contain sweet, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
