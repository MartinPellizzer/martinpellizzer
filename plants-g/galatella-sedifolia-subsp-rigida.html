Galatella sedifolia subsp. rigida

Galatella sedifolia subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the rigid leaf daphne, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically thick, waxy, and arranged in a rosette, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
