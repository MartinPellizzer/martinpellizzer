Geoffroea spinulosa

Geoffroea spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny geoffroea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, spiny appearance. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geoffroea spinulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
