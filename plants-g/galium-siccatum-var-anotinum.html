Galium siccatum var. anotinum

Galium siccatum var. anotinum, commonly known as the Sicilian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.
