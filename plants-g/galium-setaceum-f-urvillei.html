Galium setaceum f. urvillei

Galium setaceum f. urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's bedstraw, is a rare and distinctive variant of the bedstraw genus.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is often found in specific regions of eastern Asia.
