Globba saltatoria

Globba saltatoria, commonly known as the jumping globba, is a unique member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant, often colorful inflorescences. The plant's name derives from its ability to "jump" or spread through rhizome growth, allowing it to colonize new areas. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Globba saltatoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
