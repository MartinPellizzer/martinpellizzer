Goodyera secundiflora

Goodyera secundiflora, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive inflorescence of pink to white flowers that bloom in a loose, trailing cluster. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating.

Goodyera secundiflora is a perennial herb that reproduces through rhizomes and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
