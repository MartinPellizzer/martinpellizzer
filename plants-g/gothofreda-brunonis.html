Gothofreda brunonis

Gothofreda brunonis, commonly known as the blackleaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity. The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires careful cultivation to ensure its survival.
