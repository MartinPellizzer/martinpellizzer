Genista hirsuta subvar. glabior

Genista hirsuta subvar. glabior, commonly known as the hairy bursch or glabrous bursch, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, thorn-like stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
