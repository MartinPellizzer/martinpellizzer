Germaria latifolia

Germaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf germaria, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Germaria latifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
