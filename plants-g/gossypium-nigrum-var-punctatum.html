Gossypium nigrum var. punctatum

Gossypium nigrum var. punctatum, commonly known as the black cotton plant, is a variant of the cotton genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, white fibers within its boll-like capsules, which are harvested for textile use. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
