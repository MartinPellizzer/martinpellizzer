Gymnocalycium mostii var. miradorense

Gymnocalycium mostii var. miradorense, commonly known as the Mirador cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water retention in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by small, spiny fruits. Its growth habit is typically solitary or clustered, and it is well-suited for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
