Gilibertia samydifolia

Gilibertia samydifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
