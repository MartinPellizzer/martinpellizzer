Gentiana campestris var. islandica

Gentiana campestris var. islandica, commonly known as the Island Gentian, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
