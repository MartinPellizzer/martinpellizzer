Gasteranthus maculatus

Gasteranthus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted gasteranthus, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a unique morphology, with a swollen, fleshy base that resembles a stomach, hence its genus name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Gasteranthus maculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
