Glochidion caloneurum

Glochidion caloneurum, commonly known as the broad-leaved glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
