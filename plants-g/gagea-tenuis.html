Gagea tenuis

Gagea tenuis, commonly known as the slender gagea, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in temperate climates, allowing it to go dormant during harsh weather.

Gagea tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
