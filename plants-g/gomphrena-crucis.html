Gomphrena crucis

Gomphrena crucis, commonly known as the cross flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble crosses, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Gomphrena crucis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
