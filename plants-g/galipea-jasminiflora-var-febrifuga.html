Galipea jasminiflora var. febrifuga

Galipea jasminiflora var. febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional medicine for treating fever and headaches. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticultural and pharmacological contexts.
