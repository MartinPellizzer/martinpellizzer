Galega filifolia

Galega filifolia, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often growing in dry, rocky soils. It produces cylindrical pods that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Galega filifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
