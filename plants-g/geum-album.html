Geum album

Geum album, commonly known as white avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Geum album is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
