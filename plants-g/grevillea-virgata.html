Grevillea virgata

Grevillea virgata, commonly known as the silver honeycomb grevillea, is an evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, honeycomb-like inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are adapted to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Grevillea virgata is well-suited to coastal and sandy soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
