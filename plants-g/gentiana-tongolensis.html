Gentiana tongolensis

Gentiana tongolensis, commonly known as the Tongol gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests and grasslands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
