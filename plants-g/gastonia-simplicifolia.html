Gastonia simplicifolia

Gastonia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf gastonia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Gastonia simplicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
