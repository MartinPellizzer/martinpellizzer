Gentiana graminea

Gentiana graminea, commonly known as the grass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Gentiana graminea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
