Geniostoma haemospermum var. angustifolium

Geniostoma haemospermum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bloodseed plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its most notable botanical feature is the production of blood-red seeds, which give it its common name.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
