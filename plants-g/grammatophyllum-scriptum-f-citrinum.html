Grammatophyllum scriptum f. citrinum

Grammatophyllum scriptum f. citrinum, commonly known as the golden torch ginger, is a variant of the torch ginger plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that resemble torches, which are predominantly yellow in this cultivar. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, sharing close relations with ginger and turmeric. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, featuring both male and female reproductive structures, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
