Geranium crenatifolium

Geranium crenatifolium, commonly known as the serrated-leaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Geranium crenatifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
