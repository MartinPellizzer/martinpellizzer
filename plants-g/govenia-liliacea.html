Govenia liliacea

Govenia liliacea, commonly known as the pink-flowered lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or lilac in color, with six petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Govenia liliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
