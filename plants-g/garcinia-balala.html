Garcinia balala

Garcinia balala, commonly known as the Balala fruit, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit is notable for its high content of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits.
