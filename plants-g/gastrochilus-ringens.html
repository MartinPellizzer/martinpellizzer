Gastrochilus ringens

Gastrochilus ringens, commonly known as the ringed slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pouch-like structure that resembles a slipper, which is a distinctive feature of many orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

The plant's morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
