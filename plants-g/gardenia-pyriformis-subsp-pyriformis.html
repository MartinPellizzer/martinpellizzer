Gardenia pyriformis subsp. pyriformis

Gardenia pyriformis subsp.

pyriformis, commonly known as the oval gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture.

The plant produces fragrant, waxy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellowish or greenish tint at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
