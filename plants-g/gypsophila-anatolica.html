Gypsophila anatolica

Gypsophila anatolica, commonly known as the Turkish laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble lace, composed of numerous small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Gypsophila anatolica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
