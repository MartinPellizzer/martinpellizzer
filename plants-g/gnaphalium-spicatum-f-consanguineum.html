Gnaphalium spicatum f. consanguineum

Gnaphalium spicatum f. consanguineum, commonly known as the pink sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, pink to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and its delicate appearance.
