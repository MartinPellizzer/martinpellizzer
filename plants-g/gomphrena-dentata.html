Gomphrena dentata

Gomphrena dentata, commonly known as the toothed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are usually narrow and have a serrated or toothed edge, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
