Gilia lutea var. luteola

Gilia lutea var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
