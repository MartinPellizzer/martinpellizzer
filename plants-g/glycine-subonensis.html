Glycine subonensis

Glycine subonensis, commonly known as the Subon bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Glycine subonensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
