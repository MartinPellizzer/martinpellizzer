Galium lucidum subsp. corrudifolium

Galium lucidum subsp.

corrudifolium, commonly known as shining bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a shiny, waxy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
