Gnaphalium hookerianum

Gnaphalium hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's feather grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy appearance due to its numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize open, disturbed areas. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
