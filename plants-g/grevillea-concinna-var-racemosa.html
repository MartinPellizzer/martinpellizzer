Grevillea concinna var. racemosa

Grevillea concinna var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange cylindrical flower spikes, which appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
