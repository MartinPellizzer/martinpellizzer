Galium procumbens

Galium procumbens, commonly known as bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
