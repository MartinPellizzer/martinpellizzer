Guajava arborea

Guajava arborea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. Its botanical features include a thick, aromatic bark and a dense, spreading canopy.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural fruit.
