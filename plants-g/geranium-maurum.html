Geranium maurum

Geranium maurum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Geranium maurum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and as a ground cover in various landscapes.
