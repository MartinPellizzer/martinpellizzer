Gentianella fruticulosa

Gentianella fruticulosa, commonly known as the shrubby gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers in late summer. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Gentianella fruticulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
