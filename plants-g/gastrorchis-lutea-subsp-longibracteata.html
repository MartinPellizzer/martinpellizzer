Gastrorchis lutea subsp. longibracteata

Gastrorchis lutea subsp.

longibracteata, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and slender, erect stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
