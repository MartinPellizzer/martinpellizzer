Genipa striiflora

Genipa striiflora, commonly known as the streaked genipapo, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to a striking red or yellow color. The fruit's pulp is used in traditional beverages and has a unique, slightly acidic flavor.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
