Geranium cicutarium

Geranium cicutarium, commonly known as the common cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or red flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Geranium cicutarium is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also a popular garden plant due to its ornamental value.
