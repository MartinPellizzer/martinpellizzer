Geoffroea decorticans

Geoffroea decorticans, commonly known as the goldenbark acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels off in strips, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of many small, oval leaflets.
