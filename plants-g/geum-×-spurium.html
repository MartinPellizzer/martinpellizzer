Geum × spurium

Geum × spurium, commonly known as New England alyssum, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on slender, upright stems. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Geum × spurium is valued for its early spring bloom and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
