Gasteria subverrucosa var. parvipunctata

Gasteria subverrucosa var. parvipunctata, commonly known as the dwarf dotted gasteria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

This variety is prized for its compact size and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
