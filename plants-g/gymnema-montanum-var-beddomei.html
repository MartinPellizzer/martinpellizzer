Gymnema montanum var. beddomei

Gymnema montanum var. beddomei, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Beddome's gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded forests and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential in managing diabetes.
