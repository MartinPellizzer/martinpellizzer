Grewia adolfi-frederici

Grewia adolfi-frederici, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments.
