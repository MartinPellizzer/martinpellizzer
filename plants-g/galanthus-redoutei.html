Galanthus redoutei

Galanthus redoutei, commonly known as the Redoute's snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, nodding white flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow spathe, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Galanthus redoutei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
