Gypsophila altissima var. elongata

Gypsophila altissima var. elongata, commonly known as the Chinese fairy flower, is a perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
