Gymnocalycium saglionis f. columnare

Gymnocalycium saglionis f. columnare, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem that is often covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
