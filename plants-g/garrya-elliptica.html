Garrya elliptica

Garrya elliptica, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, elliptical leaves that have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats.

Garrya elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
