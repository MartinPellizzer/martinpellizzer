Gentianella bulgarica subsp. bulgarica

Gentianella bulgarica subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and mountain meadows.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
