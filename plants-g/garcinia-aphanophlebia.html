Garcinia aphanophlebia

Garcinia aphanophlebia, commonly known as the ghost fruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are covered in a thin, waxy layer. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its unique, elongated fruit shape. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a reddish or purple hue when young.

Garcinia aphanophlebia is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine.
