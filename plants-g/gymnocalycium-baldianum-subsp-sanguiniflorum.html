Gymnocalycium baldianum subsp. sanguiniflorum

Gymnocalycium baldianum subsp.

sanguiniflorum, commonly known as the bloodflower cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is a succulent cactus characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense covering of spines.

The plant produces striking red or pink flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
