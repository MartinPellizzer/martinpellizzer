Gaillonia asperuliformis

Gaillonia asperuliformis, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Gaillonia asperuliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
