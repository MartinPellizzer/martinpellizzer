Gaura racemulosa

Gaura racemulosa, commonly known as the whorled gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are often arranged in racemes, which are elongated clusters of small blooms.

Gaura racemulosa thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
