Grevillea dunlopii

Grevillea dunlopii, commonly known as the Western Australian waratah, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to greyish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Grevillea dunlopii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the biodiversity of Western Australia.
