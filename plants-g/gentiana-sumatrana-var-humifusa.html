Gentiana sumatrana var. humifusa

Gentiana sumatrana var. humifusa, commonly known as the Sumatran gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
