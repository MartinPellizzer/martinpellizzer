Guazuma blumei

Guazuma blumei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guazuma, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy green leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
