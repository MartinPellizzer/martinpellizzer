Gerardia scabra

Gerardia scabra, commonly known as the rough gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gerardia scabra is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
