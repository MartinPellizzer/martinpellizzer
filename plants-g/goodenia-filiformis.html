Goodenia filiformis

Goodenia filiformis, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
