Gastrolobium spinosum var. angustum

Gastrolobium spinosum var. angustum, commonly known as narrowleaf burr-fig, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions. It has small, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for some native animals.

This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
