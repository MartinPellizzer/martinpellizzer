Grevellina scoparia

Grevellina scoparia, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system, allowing it to store water and survive drought conditions. Its flowers produce small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Grevellina scoparia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
