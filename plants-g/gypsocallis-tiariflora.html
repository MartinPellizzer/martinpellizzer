Gypsocallis tiariflora

Gypsocallis tiariflora, commonly known as the tiariflora orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a crown or tiara, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsocallis tiariflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
