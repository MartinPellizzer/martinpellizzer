Guizotia schimperi

Guizotia schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are rich in oils and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

Guizotia schimperi plays a significant role in local ecosystems and agricultural practices in its native habitat.
