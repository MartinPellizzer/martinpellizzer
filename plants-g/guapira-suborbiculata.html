Guapira suborbiculata

Guapira suborbiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian star fruit or the golden star tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
