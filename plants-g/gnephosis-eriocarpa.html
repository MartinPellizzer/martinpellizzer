Gnephosis eriocarpa

Gnephosis eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly fruit-bearing plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Gnephosis eriocarpa is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
