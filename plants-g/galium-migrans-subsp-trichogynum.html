Galium migrans subsp. trichogynum

Galium migrans subsp.

trichogynum, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, with the female flowers occurring on separate inflorescences.
