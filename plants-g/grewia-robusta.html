Grewia robusta

Grewia robusta, commonly known as the roughleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
