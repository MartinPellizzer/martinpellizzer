Gesneria douglasii

Gesneria douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it attaches itself to other plants without being parasitic. Its leaves are usually glossy and elongated, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Gesneria douglasii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
