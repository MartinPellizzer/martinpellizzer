Gentiana soratensis

Gentiana soratensis, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Gentiana soratensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
