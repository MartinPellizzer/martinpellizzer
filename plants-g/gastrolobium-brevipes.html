Gastrolobium brevipes

Gastrolobium brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often twisted stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and feathery, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Gastrolobium brevipes is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits the surrounding soil.
