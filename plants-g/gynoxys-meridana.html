Gynoxys meridana

Gynoxys meridana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. These flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

Gynoxys meridana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
