Geanthus minor

Geanthus minor, commonly known as the small geanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Geanthus minor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
