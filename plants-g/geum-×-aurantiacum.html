Geum × aurantiacum

Geum × aurantiacum, commonly known as the fiery poppy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat coarse texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Geum × aurantiacum is often cultivated in gardens for its showy flowers and ornamental value.
