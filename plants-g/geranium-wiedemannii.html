Geranium wiedemannii

Geranium wiedemannii, commonly known as the Mexican geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features upright stems that are typically hairy and bear opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its foliage is often aromatic, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Geranium wiedemannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
