Gladiolus floribundus subsp. milleri

Gladiolus floribundus subsp.

milleri, commonly known as the Milleri gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces numerous spikes of flowers, which bloom in summer, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
