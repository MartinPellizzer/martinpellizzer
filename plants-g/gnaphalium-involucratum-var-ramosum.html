Gnaphalium involucratum var. ramosum

Gnaphalium involucratum var. ramosum, commonly known as the hairy panicled everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are small and yellowish, forming dense panicles.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried flower arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
