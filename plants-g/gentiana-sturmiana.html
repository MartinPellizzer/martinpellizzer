Gentiana sturmiana

Gentiana sturmiana, commonly known as the Sturm's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana sturmiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological significance.
