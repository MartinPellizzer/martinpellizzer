Geoblasta penicillata

Geoblasta penicillata, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form, with numerous small, hairy leaves arranged in a rosette. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with four petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
