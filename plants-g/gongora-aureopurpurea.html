Gongora aureopurpurea

Gongora aureopurpurea, commonly known as the golden-purple Gongora, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that display a mix of golden and purple hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests. Its flowers are relatively small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

The orchid's unique coloration and delicate structure make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
