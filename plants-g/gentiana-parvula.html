Gentiana parvula

Gentiana parvula, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its vibrant blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gentiana parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.
