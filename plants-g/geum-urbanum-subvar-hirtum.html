Geum urbanum subvar. hirtum

Geum urbanum subvar. hirtum, commonly known as hairy wood avens, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and heart-shaped, with a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its hairy stems and leaves help it retain moisture and resist herbivory.
