Gastrochilus calophyllus

Gastrochilus calophyllus, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and several erect, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with dark veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
