Gentiana patula

Gentiana patula, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana patula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
