Gaylussacia rupestris

Gaylussacia rupestris, commonly known as the rocky alpenstrife, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in upland areas and open woodlands. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, turning red in the fall.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
