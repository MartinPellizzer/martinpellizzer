Gypsophila yorae

Gypsophila yorae, commonly known as the Japanese gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle, ethereal beauty to the landscape.

Gypsophila yorae is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
