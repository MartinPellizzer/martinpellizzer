Gilia cana subsp. cana

Gilia cana subsp.

cana, commonly known as the pale gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
