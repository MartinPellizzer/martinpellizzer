Gentiana subocculta

Gentiana subocculta, commonly known as the hidden gentian, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branched.

Gentiana subocculta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
