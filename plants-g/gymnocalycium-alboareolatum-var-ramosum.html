Gymnocalycium alboareolatum var. ramosum

Gymnocalycium alboareolatum var. ramosum, commonly known as the White-Freckled Barrel Cactus, is a cacti species native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, waxy tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
