Gerardia integrifolia

Gerardia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, and are covered in a fine, silvery hair.

Gerardia integrifolia is known for its resilience to arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects in its native habitat.
