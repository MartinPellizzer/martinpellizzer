Gentiana × curtisii

Gentiana × curtisii, commonly known as the hybrid gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentiana × curtisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
