Gladiolus grantii

Gladiolus grantii, commonly known as the Grant's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

The flowers are borne on tall spikes and are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance.
