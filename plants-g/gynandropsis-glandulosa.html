Gynandropsis glandulosa

Gynandropsis glandulosa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Gynandropsis glandulosa is also known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
