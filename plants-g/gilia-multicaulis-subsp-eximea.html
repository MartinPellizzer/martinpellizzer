Gilia multicaulis subsp. eximea

Gilia multicaulis subsp.

eximea, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow throat.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyons, and it thrives in full sun. Its stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
