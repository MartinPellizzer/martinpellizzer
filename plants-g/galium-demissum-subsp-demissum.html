Galium demissum subsp. demissum

Galium demissum subsp.

demissum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
