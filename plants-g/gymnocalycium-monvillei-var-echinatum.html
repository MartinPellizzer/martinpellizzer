Gymnocalycium monvillei var. echinatum

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. echinatum, commonly known as the Echinatium Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This cactus is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in low-maintenance environments.
