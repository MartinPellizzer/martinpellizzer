Genista cinerea var. obtusiramea

Genista cinerea var. obtusiramea, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, often branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are alternate, narrow, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
