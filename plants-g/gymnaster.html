Gymnaster

Gymnaster, commonly known as the "naked stem" plant, is a unique species characterized by its lack of leaves and its distinctive, often woody stem.

It belongs to the family Lepidopteraceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant's botanical features include small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered near the base of the stem. Gymnaster is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.

Its structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
