Gongora galeata

Gongora galeata, commonly known as the Galeata orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Gongora galeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
