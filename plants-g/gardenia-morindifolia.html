Gardenia morindifolia

Gardenia morindifolia, commonly known as the Indian gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and scent.
