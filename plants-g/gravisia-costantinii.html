Gravisia costantinii

Gravisia costantinii, commonly known as the Constantini's grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Gravisia costantinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
