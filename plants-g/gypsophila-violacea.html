Gypsophila violacea

Gypsophila violacea, commonly known as the "Baby's Breath" or "Gypsophila," is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their airy appearance and long-lasting nature.

Gypsophila violacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
