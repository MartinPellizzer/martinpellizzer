Gnidia carinata

Gnidia carinata, commonly known as the ridged gnidiaceae, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive ridge or vein pattern.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
