Goeppertia erecta

Goeppertia erecta, commonly known as the upright arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green hues. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its ability to store starch, which is harvested for culinary use.

Goeppertia erecta thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
