Gypsophila repens f. pienina

Gypsophila repens f. pienina, commonly known as the dwarf baby's breath, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its compact form and extended blooming period make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in floral arrangements.
