Gentiana × hybrida var. reynieri

Gentiana × hybrida var. reynieri, commonly known as the Reynier's gentian, is a hybrid species within the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with two lips, and are arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
