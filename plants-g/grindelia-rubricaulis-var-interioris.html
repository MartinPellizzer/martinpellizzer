Grindelia rubricaulis var. interioris

Grindelia rubricaulis var. interioris, commonly known as the interior prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its common name reflects the rough texture of its leaves and its habitat preferences.
