Gentiana clusii subsp. alpina

Gentiana clusii subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
