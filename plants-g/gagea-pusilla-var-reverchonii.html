Gagea pusilla var. reverchonii

Gagea pusilla var. reverchonii, commonly known as the dwarf gagea, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or alpine meadows. The plant produces delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its compact size and is often mistaken for other similar species due to its subtle appearance.

This variety is of interest to botanists and gardeners for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
