Gaultheria minima

Gaultheria minima, commonly known as the dwarf wintergreen, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and reaches a height of only a few inches. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a sharp, minty flavor. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Gaultheria minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
