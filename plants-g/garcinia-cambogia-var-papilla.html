Garcinia cambogia var. papilla

Garcinia cambogia var. papilla, commonly known as Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid (HCA). The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface. The fruit develops from a fleshy, five-lobed calyx and is typically harvested when fully ripe.

It is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.
