Gossypium barbadense var. apospermum

Gossypium barbadense var. apospermum, commonly known as the African cotton or Malawian cotton, is a variety of cotton native to Africa.

It is characterized by its long, fine fibers and high-quality cotton production. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the cotton fibers, which are harvested for textile use.

This variety is notable for its resilience to certain environmental conditions, making it an important crop in some African countries.
