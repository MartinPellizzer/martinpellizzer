Gentiana chazaroi

Gentiana chazaroi, commonly known as the Chazarov's gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana chazaroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
