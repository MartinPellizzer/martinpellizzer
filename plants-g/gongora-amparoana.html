Gongora amparoana

Gongora amparoana, commonly known as the Amparo orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in the humid, shaded environment.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
