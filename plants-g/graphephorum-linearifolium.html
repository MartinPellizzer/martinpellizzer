Graphephorum linearifolium

Graphephorum linearifolium, commonly known as the slender leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf morphology makes it a distinctive and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and greenhouses.
