Geryonia

Geryonia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid or rocky environments, where they have adapted to thrive in harsh conditions. Geryonia species are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. They possess specialized root systems that help them absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

The botanical study of Geryonia provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of plants in extreme climates.
