Gomphocarpus concinnus

Gomphocarpus concinnus, commonly known as the silverleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is typical of the milkweed genus.
