Grevillea evanescens

Grevillea evanescens, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green foliage and its striking, urn-shaped red or orange flowers. The plant is evergreen and typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Grevillea evanescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
