Gastridium ventricosum subsp. phleoides

Gastridium ventricosum subsp.

phleoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed gastridium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems that bear opposite, broad, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated fruit capsules that split open to release its seeds.
