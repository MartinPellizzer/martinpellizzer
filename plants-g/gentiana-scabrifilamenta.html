Gentiana scabrifilamenta

Gentiana scabrifilamenta, commonly known as the rough-hair gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Gentiana scabrifilamenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
