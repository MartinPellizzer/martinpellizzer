Galvezia juncea var. foliosa

Galvezia juncea var. foliosa, commonly known as the broadleaf galvezia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. The plant features lance-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in panicles, and are typically white or pale pink in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
