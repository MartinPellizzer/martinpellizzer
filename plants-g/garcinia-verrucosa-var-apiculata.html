Garcinia verrucosa var. apiculata

Garcinia verrucosa var. apiculata, commonly known as the horned mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pink hues. The fruit is typically oval or ellipsoid, with a thick, purple rind and a segmented, edible pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth is often associated with moist, shaded environments in tropical forests.
