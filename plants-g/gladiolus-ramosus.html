Gladiolus ramosus

Gladiolus ramosus, commonly known as the branched gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, sword-shaped flowers in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gladiolus ramosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
