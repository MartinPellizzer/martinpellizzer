Gentiana verna f. magellensis

Gentiana verna f. magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
