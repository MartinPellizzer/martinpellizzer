Galium falconeri

Galium falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri Bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

Galium falconeri is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.
