Gentianella dielsiana

Gentianella dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it blooms during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
