Gladiolus aequinoctialis var. aequinoctialis

Gladiolus aequinoctialis var. aequinoctialis, commonly known as the equinoctial gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It features tall, sword-like leaves and produces vibrant, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its inflorescence consists of multiple florets arranged in a spike. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is valued for its beauty and adaptability in various climatic conditions.
