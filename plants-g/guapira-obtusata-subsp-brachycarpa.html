Guapira obtusata subsp. brachycarpa

Guapira obtusata subsp.

brachycarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceratostigma brachycarpa*, is a shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its small, dark red or purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which turns vibrant red in the fall.
