Gentiana barbata var. richardsoniana

Gentiana barbata var. richardsoniana, commonly known as the Richardson's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
