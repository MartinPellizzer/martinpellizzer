Grossera quintasii

Grossera quintasii, commonly known as the Quintasii orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense, velvety coating, which helps in water retention.

Due to its limited habitat and specific environmental requirements, Grossera quintasii is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
