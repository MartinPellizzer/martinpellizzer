Gentiana × grandilacustris

Gentiana × grandilacustris, commonly known as the lake gentian, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana × grandilacustris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
