Gilia liniflora var. filipes

Gilia liniflora var. filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

The species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, scrublands, and along rocky slopes.
