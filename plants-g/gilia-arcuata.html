Gilia arcuata

Gilia arcuata, commonly known as the bentstem gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and dry slopes. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Gilia arcuata is an important plant for pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
