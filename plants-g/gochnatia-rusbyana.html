Gochnatia rusbyana

Gochnatia rusbyana, commonly known as the Cape mountain ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by insects. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Gochnatia rusbyana is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
