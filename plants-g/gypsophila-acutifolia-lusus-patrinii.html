Gypsophila acutifolia lusus patrinii

Gypsophila acutifolia lusus patrinii, commonly known as the Armenian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its ability to produce numerous small flowers that create a fluffy appearance.

This species is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental value and long-lasting blooms.
