Gymnema lactiferum

Gymnema lactiferum, commonly known as the Indian bitter melon or gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its milky sap, which contains compounds like gymnemic acids. Gymnema lactiferum is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
