Gentiana liniflora

Gentiana liniflora, commonly known as the threadleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, linear leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Gentiana liniflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
