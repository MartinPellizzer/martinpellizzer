Graphorkis baginsensis

Graphorkis baginsensis, commonly known as the Baginsko-Bogatyr orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rock faces or in forest understories. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
