Galium bourgaeanum

Galium bourgaeanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Galium bourgaeanum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
