Geum montanum elatius

Geum montanum elatius, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats. It has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Geum montanum elatius plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
