Gentiana latifolia

Gentiana latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Gentiana latifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
