Gundelia aragatsi subsp. steineri

Gundelia aragatsi subsp.

steineri, commonly known as Armenian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Armenia. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its milky sap, which is toxic to livestock, is a notable botanical feature.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a unique morphology suited to its harsh habitat.
