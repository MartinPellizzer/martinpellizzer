Galium commune var. oxyphyllum

Galium commune var. oxyphyllum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
