Grevillea apiciloba subsp. digitata

Grevillea apiciloba subsp.

digitata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive reddish or brownish midrib.

The plant produces striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
