Garcinia valetoniana

Garcinia valetoniana, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, thorny stems and elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright orange, fleshy berries. The fruit contains a single seed and is known for its sharp, acidic taste.

Garcinia valetoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
