Grevillea roycei

Grevillea roycei, commonly known as the red grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea roycei is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
