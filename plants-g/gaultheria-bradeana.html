Gaultheria bradeana

Gaultheria bradeana, commonly known as the Bradean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Gaultheria bradeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
