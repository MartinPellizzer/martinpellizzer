Garcinia leptophylla

Garcinia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf garcinia, is a species of evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary tropical forests.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest in both botanical research and natural medicine.
