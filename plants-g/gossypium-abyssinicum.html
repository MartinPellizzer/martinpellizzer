Gossypium abyssinicum

Gossypium abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its soft, fluffy fibers that are harvested from its seed pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale pink to white.

The fibers of Gossypium abyssinicum are valued for their strength and are used in the production of textiles and other industrial applications.
