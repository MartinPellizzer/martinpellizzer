Gentiana androtricha

Gentiana androtricha, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps and Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Gentiana androtricha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
