Gentiana namlaensis

Gentiana namlaensis, commonly known as the Namla gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana namlaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
