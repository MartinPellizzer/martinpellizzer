Gentianella chathamica subsp. chathamica

Gentianella chathamica subsp.

chathamica, commonly known as the Chatham Island gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
