Grewia philippinensis

Grewia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine globemallow, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits that split open when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Grewia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
