Gaillardia richardsonii

Gaillardia richardsonii, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals, often with a dark central disk. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its vibrant colors attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gaillardia richardsonii is valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
