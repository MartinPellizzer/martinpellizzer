Gomphia schoenleiniana

Gomphia schoenleiniana, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its fibrous root system helps it anchor in loose, sandy soils.

Gomphia schoenleiniana is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
