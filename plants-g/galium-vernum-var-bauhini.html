Galium vernum var. bauhini

Galium vernum var. bauhini, commonly known as the spring bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that often lie prostrate, covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
