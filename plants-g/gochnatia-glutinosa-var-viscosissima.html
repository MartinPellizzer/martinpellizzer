Gochnatia glutinosa var. viscosissima

Gochnatia glutinosa var. viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky gochnatia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, viscid, and often sticky bark, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a rough texture.

The species is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
