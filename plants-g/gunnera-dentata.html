Gunnera dentata

Gunnera dentata, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a serrated edge, giving the plant its scientific name "dentata," which means "toothed" in Latin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
