Glycine bituminosa

Glycine bituminosa, commonly known as the black bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Glycine bituminosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
