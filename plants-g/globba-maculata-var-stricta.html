Globba maculata var. stricta

Globba maculata var. stricta, commonly known as the spotted globba, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive red or purple stripe down the center. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with dark green spots, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Globba maculata var. stricta is relatively low maintenance once established, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical regions.
