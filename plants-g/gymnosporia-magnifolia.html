Gymnosporia magnifolia

Gymnosporia magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificent thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of gymnosperms. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a serrated edge.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.
