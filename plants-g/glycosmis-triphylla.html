Glycosmis triphylla

Glycosmis triphylla, commonly known as the three-leafed sweetleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Glycosmis triphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some Asian cultures.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting species within the Rutaceae family.
