Gypsophila muralis

Gypsophila muralis, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous leaves along its stems.

Gypsophila muralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
