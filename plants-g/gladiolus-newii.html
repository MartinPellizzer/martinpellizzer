Gladiolus newii

Gladiolus newii, commonly known as the Newii gladiolus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus newii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
