Genista palmiformis

Genista palmiformis, commonly known as the palmate broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has palmate leaves, which are divided into several lobes resembling a hand, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Genista palmiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
