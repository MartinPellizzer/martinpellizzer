Glycine hispida var. oodes

Glycine hispida var. oodes, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in animal feed and human consumption.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
