Gomphrena breviflora

Gomphrena breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescences that resemble small globes, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical formations, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Gomphrena breviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
