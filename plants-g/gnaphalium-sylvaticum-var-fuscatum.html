Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. fuscatum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. fuscatum, commonly known as the gray everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, white to grayish seed heads. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and often have a silvery or grayish hue.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
