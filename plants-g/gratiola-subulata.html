Gratiola subulata

Gratiola subulata, commonly known as the slender yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Gratiola subulata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
