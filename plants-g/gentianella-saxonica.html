Gentianella saxonica

Gentianella saxonica, commonly known as Saxon gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in early summer.

Gentianella saxonica is often found in the British Isles and parts of Central Europe.
