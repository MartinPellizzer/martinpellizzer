Goodyera rostellata

Goodyera rostellata, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and fascinating epiphytic orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically growing on the trunks or branches of trees in the understory of forests. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and highly fragrant, with a unique, ghost-like appearance that gives it its common name.

Goodyera rostellata is known for its delicate beauty and ecological significance, making it a prized species in botanical conservation efforts.
