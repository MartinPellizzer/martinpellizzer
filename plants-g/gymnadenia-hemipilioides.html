Gymnadenia hemipilioides

Gymnadenia hemipilioides, commonly known as the small-flowered grass-pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, greenish-brown capsules as fruit, containing numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and declining species in many parts of its range.
