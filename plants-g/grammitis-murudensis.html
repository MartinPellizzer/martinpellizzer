Grammitis murudensis

Grammitis murudensis, commonly known as the Murudensis Fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant's rhizomes are wiry and often form dense mats on tree trunks or branches, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Grammitis murudensis has small, clustered spore-producing structures called sori, which are located on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to forest biodiversity.
