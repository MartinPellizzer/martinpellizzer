Gasteria loedolffiae

Gasteria loedolffiae, commonly known as the Zulu Lily, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often marked with white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, tubular, white or pale yellow flowers. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Gasteria loedolffiae is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in succulent gardens.
