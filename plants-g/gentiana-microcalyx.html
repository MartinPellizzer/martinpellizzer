Gentiana microcalyx

Gentiana microcalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Gentiana microcalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
