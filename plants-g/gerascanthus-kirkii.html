Gerascanthus kirkii

Gerascanthus kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's ericoid, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
