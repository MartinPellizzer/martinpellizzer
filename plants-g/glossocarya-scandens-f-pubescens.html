Glossocarya scandens f. pubescens

Glossocarya scandens f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent honeysuckle, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
