Galanthus nivalis var. spitzneri

Galanthus nivalis var. spitzneri, commonly known as the Spitzner's snowdrop, is a rare and delicate variety of the snowdrop genus.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers with a distinctive greenish tinge on the inner petals. This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a touch of elegance to woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the study of alpine plant adaptations.
