Geum × navarroi

Geum × navarroi, commonly known as the Navarre garden buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rosaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
