Geranium maculatum f. albiflorum

Geranium maculatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered spotted geranium, is a cultivar of the spotted geranium, native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale white to pinkish flowers, which contrast with its dark green, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

This cultivar is a popular choice for native plant gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
