Geranium japonicum f. adpressipilosum

Geranium japonicum f. adpressipilosum, commonly known as the Japanese geranium, is a compact perennial herb native to Japan.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, rounded leaves that are often described as being densely covered with fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
