Gentiana brunneotincta

Gentiana brunneotincta, commonly known as the brown-stained gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Gentiana brunneotincta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
