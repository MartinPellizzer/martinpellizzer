Gentiana xanthonannos

Gentiana xanthonannos, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are tubular with a short, prominent corona, making it a distinctive and attractive species in its habitat.
