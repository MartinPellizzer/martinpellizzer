Galium cincinnatum

Galium cincinnatum, commonly known as small whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale green, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Galium cincinnatum thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
