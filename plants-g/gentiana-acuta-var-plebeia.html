Gentiana acuta var. plebeia

Gentiana acuta var. plebeia, commonly known as the lesser gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
