Geum montanum var. grandiflorum

Geum montanum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
