Gentiana wissmannii

Gentiana wissmannii, commonly known as the Wissmann's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
