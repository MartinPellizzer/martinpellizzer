Gentianodes stragulata

Gentianodes stragulata, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy and oval-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Gentianodes stragulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
