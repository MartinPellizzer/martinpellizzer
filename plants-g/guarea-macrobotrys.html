Guarea macrobotrys

Guarea macrobotrys, commonly known as the red cedar or bloodwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and distinctive, compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a reddish sap that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
