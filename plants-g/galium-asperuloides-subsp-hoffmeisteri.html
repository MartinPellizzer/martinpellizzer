Galium asperuloides subsp. hoffmeisteri

Galium asperuloides subsp.

hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine pubescence.

The species is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitats.
