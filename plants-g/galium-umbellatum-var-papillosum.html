Galium umbellatum var. papillosum

Galium umbellatum var. papillosum, commonly known as the showy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

This species thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
