Geigeria burkei var. elata

Geigeria burkei var. elata, commonly known as the slender geigeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
