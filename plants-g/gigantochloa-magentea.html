Gigantochloa magentea

Gigantochloa magentea, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a tall, slender grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-colored culms and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and elegant appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
