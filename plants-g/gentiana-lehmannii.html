Gentiana lehmannii

Gentiana lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
