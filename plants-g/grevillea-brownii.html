Grevillea brownii

Grevillea brownii, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island grevillea, is a striking shrub native to South Australia.

It features vibrant red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea brownii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
