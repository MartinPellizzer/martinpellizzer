Gaertnera furcellata

Gaertnera furcellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing as a shrub or small tree.

Gaertnera furcellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
