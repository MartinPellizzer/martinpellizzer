Gilbertiodendron ogoouense

Gilbertiodendron ogoouense, commonly known as the African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to termites, making it an important commercial species.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
