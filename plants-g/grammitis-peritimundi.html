Grammitis peritimundi

Grammitis peritimundi, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often form dense, carpet-like mats on trees. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest canopies where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its small, inconspicuous spore-producing structures are located on the undersides of its leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Grammitis peritimundi is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in controlled, humid conditions.
