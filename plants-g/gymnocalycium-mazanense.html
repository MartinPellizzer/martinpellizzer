Gymnocalycium mazanense

Gymnocalycium mazanense, commonly known as the Mendoza prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Gymnocalycium mazanense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.
