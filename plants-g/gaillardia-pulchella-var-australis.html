Gaillardia pulchella var. australis

Gaillardia pulchella var. australis, commonly known as the Australian flame flower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with bright orange and red hues, often with a dark central disk. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in both landscapes and floral arrangements.
