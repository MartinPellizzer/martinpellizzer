Geracium incarnatum

Geracium incarnatum, commonly known as the pink geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Geracium incarnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
