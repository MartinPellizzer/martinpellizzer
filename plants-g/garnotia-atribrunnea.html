Garnotia atribrunnea

Garnotia atribrunnea, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and its flowers are star-shaped with yellow stamens.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
