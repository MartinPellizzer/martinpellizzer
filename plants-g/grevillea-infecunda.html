Grevillea infecunda

Grevillea infecunda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often used in native gardens.

Grevillea infecunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
