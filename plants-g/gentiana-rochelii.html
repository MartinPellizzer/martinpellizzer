Gentiana rochelii

Gentiana rochelii, commonly known as Rochel's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or grassy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or violet corolla.

Gentiana rochelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
