Glycine javanica var. mearnsii

Glycine javanica var. mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' soybean vine, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential for agricultural and medicinal applications.
