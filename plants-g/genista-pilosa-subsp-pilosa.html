Genista pilosa subsp. pilosa

Genista pilosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, often branched stems covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are small, alternate, and pinnate, with each leaflet being narrowly elliptic.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
