Gynopogon labillardierei

Gynopogon labillardierei, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant produces distinctive, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flowering habit, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
