Geranium molle var. caucasicum

Geranium molle var. caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
