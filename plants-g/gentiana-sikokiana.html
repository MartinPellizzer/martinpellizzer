Gentiana sikokiana

Gentiana sikokiana, commonly known as the Korean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Gentiana sikokiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
