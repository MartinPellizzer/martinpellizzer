Goodenia helenae

Goodenia helenae, commonly known as the Helen's goodenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its delicate, grass-like foliage. Goodenia helenae is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
