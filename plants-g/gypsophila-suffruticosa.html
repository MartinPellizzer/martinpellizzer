Gypsophila suffruticosa

Gypsophila suffruticosa, commonly known as the sweet pea flower or baby's breath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fluffy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has wiry, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a favorite among florists and gardeners alike.
