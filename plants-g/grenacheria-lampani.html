Grenacheria lampani

Grenacheria lampani, commonly known as the Lampani grass, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in humid climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences.

Grenacheria lampani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in tropical regions.
