Gypsophila grandiflora

Gypsophila grandiflora, commonly known as the fringed poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, papery bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Gypsophila grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden arrangements and floral designs.
