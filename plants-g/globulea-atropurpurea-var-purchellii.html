Globulea atropurpurea var. purchellii

Globulea atropurpurea var. purchellii, commonly known as the purple globular hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant purple flowers.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features showy, globe-shaped blooms that are typically 5-7 cm in diameter, with five recurved petals. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
