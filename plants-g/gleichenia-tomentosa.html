Gleichenia tomentosa

Gleichenia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, creeping rhizome and its fronds that are covered in dense, soft, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant is native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including parts of Africa, Australia, and South America. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Gleichenia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
