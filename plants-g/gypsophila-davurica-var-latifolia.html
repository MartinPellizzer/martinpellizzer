Gypsophila davurica var. latifolia

Gypsophila davurica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms contribute to its ornamental value.
