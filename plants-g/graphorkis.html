Graphorkis

Graphorkis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the wet tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It has small, scale-like leaves and relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Graphorkis is considered endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
