Grindelia oxylepis var. eligulata

Grindelia oxylepis var. eligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gumweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite-like clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
