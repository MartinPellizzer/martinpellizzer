Guentheria

Guentheria, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, often blooming at night. Guentheria has a robust root system that anchors it in shallow water and absorbs nutrients from the substrate.

Its unique structure and ecological role make it an important component of tropical wetland ecosystems.
