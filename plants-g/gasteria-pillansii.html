Gasteria pillansii

Gasteria pillansii, commonly known as the Zulu dwarf lily, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white to yellowish margins. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers on tall, leafless stems during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Gasteria pillansii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
