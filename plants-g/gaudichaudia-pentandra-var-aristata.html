Gaudichaudia pentandra var. aristata

Gaudichaudia pentandra var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate gaudichaudia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
