Goeppertia pseudoveitchiana

Goeppertia pseudoveitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, mottled foliage that displays shades of green, silver, and red. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a textured surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
