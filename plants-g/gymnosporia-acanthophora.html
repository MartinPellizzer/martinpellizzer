Gymnosporia acanthophora

Gymnosporia acanthophora, commonly known as the spiked burr plant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny, globose fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Gymnosporia acanthophora is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
