Gerardia rigida

Gerardia rigida, commonly known as the rigid gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, rigid stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Gerardia rigida plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
