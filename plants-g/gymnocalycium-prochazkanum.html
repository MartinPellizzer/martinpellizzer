Gymnocalycium prochazkanum

Gymnocalycium prochazkanum, commonly known as the Prochazka's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red or orange fruits. Gymnocalycium prochazkanum is a succulent plant that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
