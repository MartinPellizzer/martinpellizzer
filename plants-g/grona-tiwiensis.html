Grona tiwiensis

Grona tiwiensis, commonly known as the Tiwi Island grape, is a flowering plant native to the Tiwi Islands in the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is a woody vine that produces small, fragrant flowers and dark purple berries. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in scrublands and near the shoreline. Its foliage is characterized by glossy, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

Grona tiwiensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for local wildlife.
