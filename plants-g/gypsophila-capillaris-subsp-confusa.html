Gypsophila capillaris subsp. confusa

Gypsophila capillaris subsp.

confusa, commonly known as the clustered baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are linear and hairy, adapting to arid and high-altitude conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental use in gardens and floral arrangements.
