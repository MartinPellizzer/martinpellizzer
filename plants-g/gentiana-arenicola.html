Gentiana arenicola

Gentiana arenicola, commonly known as the sand gentian, is a flowering plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, well-drained habitats such as coastal cliffs and limestone formations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Gentiana arenicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
