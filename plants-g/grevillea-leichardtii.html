Grevillea leichardtii

Grevillea leichardtii, commonly known as the Leichardt's grevillea, is a striking shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes and bloom during the cooler months. The plant has needle-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh, dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Grevillea leichardtii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
