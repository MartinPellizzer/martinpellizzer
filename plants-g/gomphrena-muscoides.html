Gomphrena muscoides

Gomphrena muscoides, commonly known as the muskweed or muskplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with bracts that resemble tiny, rounded leaves. Gomphrena muscoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its aromatic scent, which is more pronounced when the leaves are crushed.
