Galatella glandulosa

Galatella glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its glandular, hairy leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Galatella glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
