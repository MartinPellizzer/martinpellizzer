Gypsophila patrinii var. thesiifolia

Gypsophila patrinii var. thesiifolia, commonly known as the Persian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its legume-like pods.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
