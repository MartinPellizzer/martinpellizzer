Genista numidica subsp. dorycnifolia

Genista numidica subsp.

dorycnifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are characteristic of the Genista genus. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, demonstrating adaptability to arid environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
