Gentiana cruciata subsp. phlogifolia

Gentiana cruciata subsp.

phlogifolia, commonly known as the fire gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and deeper flower color compared to the typical Gentiana cruciata.
