Glochidion Rigens

Common glochidion, scientifically known as Glochidion rigens, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an important species in its native ecosystems. Despite its modest appearance, the common glochidion plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity.
