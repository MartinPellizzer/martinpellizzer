Gymnema melicida

Gymnema melicida, commonly known as the Indian bitter leaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is known for its bitter taste, which is attributed to the presence of compounds like gymnemic acids. Gymnema melicida is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits, particularly in managing diabetes.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
