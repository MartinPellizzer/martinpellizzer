Galeopsis tetrahit subsp. glaucoserata

Galeopsis tetrahit subsp.

glaucoserata, commonly known as the blue-hairy hedgenettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, blue-gray hairs, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
