Gentianopsis barbata subsp. barbata

Gentianopsis barbata subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the barbed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This species thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
