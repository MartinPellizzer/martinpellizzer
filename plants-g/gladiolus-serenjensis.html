Gladiolus serenjensis

Gladiolus serenjensis, commonly known as the Serenje gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to the highlands of Tanzania.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is known for its lance-shaped leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is also valued for its potential in horticultural and medicinal applications.
