Graphorkis cristata

Graphorkis cristata, commonly known as the crested graphorkis, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, crested leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
