Gunnera chilensis var. pilosa

Gunnera chilensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the Andean giant curlleaf, is a striking perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests or along riverbanks.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and small animals, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
