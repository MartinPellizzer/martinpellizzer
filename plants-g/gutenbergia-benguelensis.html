Gutenbergia benguelensis

Gutenbergia benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela gutenbergia, is a rare and unique plant native to the coastal regions of Angola.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to the arid and semi-arid environments it inhabits. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
