Galactia sagotii

Galactia sagotii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds coated in a sticky, milky sap. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Galactia sagotii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native environment.
