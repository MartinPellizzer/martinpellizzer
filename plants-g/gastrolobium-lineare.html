Gastrolobium lineare

Gastrolobium lineare, commonly known as the linear wattle, is a shrub native to western and southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has compound leaves with numerous thread-like leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in dense spikes. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Gastrolobium lineare is a hardy plant that plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
