Grevillea ilicifolia subsp. ilicifolia

Grevillea ilicifolia subsp.

ilicifolia, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are highly attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray.
