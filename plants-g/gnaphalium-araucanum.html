Gnaphalium araucanum

Gnaphalium araucanum, commonly known as the Andean feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy white or yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its name derives from the Greek "gnaphalos," referring to its feathery appearance.
