Galium aristatum

Galium aristatum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a waxy texture.

Galium aristatum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
