Geigeria schinzii subsp. sebungweensis

Geigeria schinzii subsp.

sebungweensis, commonly known as the Sebungwe geigeria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
