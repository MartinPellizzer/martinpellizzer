Gonocaryum lobosporum

Gonocaryum lobosporum, commonly known as the golden spore fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching fronds that bear small, golden spore capsules.

It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving streams and marshy areas, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. The plant's reproductive structures, known as sporangia, release spores into the water, facilitating its spread. Its leaves are typically slender and ribbon-like, with a distinct coloration that varies depending on light exposure.

Gonocaryum lobosporum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
