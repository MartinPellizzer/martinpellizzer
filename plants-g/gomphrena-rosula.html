Gomphrena rosula

Gomphrena rosula, commonly known as the rose-leaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, rose-like bracts that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the showy, leaf-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena rosula is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
