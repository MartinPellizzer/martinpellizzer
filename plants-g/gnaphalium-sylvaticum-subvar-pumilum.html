Gnaphalium sylvaticum subvar. pumilum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum subvar. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or pale purple. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces a characteristic scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common component of wetland and meadow ecosystems.
