Galium megalospermum var. hierosolymithanum

Galium megalospermum var. hierosolymithanum, commonly known as the Jerusalem nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as wastelands and along paths.
