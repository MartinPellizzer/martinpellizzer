Genista armeniaca

Genista armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian broom, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
