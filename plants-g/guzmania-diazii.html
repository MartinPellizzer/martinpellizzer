Guzmania diazii

Guzmania diazii, commonly known as the pink-flowered bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that often displays shades of pink and red. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small animals. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical displays.

Guzmania diazii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.
