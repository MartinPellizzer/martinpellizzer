Galactia mucronata

Galactia mucronata, commonly known as the small-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it has a distinctive, slender stem.

Galactia mucronata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitats.
