Gymnocactus gielsdorfianus

Gymnocactus gielsdorfianus, commonly known as the Giel's Gymnocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
