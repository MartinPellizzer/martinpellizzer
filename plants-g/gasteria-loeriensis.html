Gasteria loeriensis

Gasteria loeriensis, commonly known as the Leopard's Ear, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with white or yellow markings resembling a leopard's spots. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
