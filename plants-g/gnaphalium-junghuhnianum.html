Gnaphalium junghuhnianum

Gnaphalium junghuhnianum, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
