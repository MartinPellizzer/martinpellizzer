Goodenia sepalosa

Goodenia sepalosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in various regions across southeastern Australia. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Goodenia sepalosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
