Glycyrrhiza spinescens

Glycyrrhiza spinescens, commonly known as the spiny licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by legume pods containing elliptical seeds. Glycyrrhiza spinescens is valued for its roots, which contain glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to arid environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
