Genista sericea var. subcapitata

Genista sericea var. subcapitata, commonly known as the silky broom.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, needle-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
