Galium problematicum

Galium problematicum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant features small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Galium problematicum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
