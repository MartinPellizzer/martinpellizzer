Grammitis similis

Grammitis similis, commonly known as the common grammitis, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Grammitis similis is noted for its small size and intricate leaf structure, making it a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
