Gomphrena elegans

Gomphrena elegans, commonly known as the elegant bluemink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces feathery bracts that give it a distinctive, almost feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena elegans is also valued for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist throughout the growing season.
