Gastrolobium rubrum

Gastrolobium rubrum, commonly known as the red wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its distinctive red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are arranged in whorls, and its flowers are typically clustered in spikes. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Gastrolobium rubrum is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
