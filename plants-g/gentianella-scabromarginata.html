Gentianella scabromarginata

Gentianella scabromarginata, commonly known as the rough-margin gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Gentianella scabromarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and naturalized areas.
