Galactia oxyphylla

Galactia oxyphylla, commonly known as the redstem burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is known for its distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Galactia oxyphylla is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small animals.
