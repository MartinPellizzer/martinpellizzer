Graphardisia seibertii

Graphardisia seibertii, commonly known as the Seibert's ghost orchid, is a rare and endangered species of orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers and its unique growth habit, often growing epiphytically on trees. The plant has a complex root system that helps it absorb nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its humid environment.

Due to habitat destruction and limited natural pollinators, conservation efforts are critical for its survival.
