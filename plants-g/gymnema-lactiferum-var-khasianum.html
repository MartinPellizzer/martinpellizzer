Gymnema lactiferum var. khasianum

Gymnema lactiferum var. khasianum, commonly known as the Indian birch or Khasi gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its ability to secrete a milky latex, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its potential in herbal remedies and its role in local ecosystems.
