Gilia eryngioides

Gilia eryngioides, commonly known as the silverleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and foothill habitats. It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its showy, short-lived blooms.

Gilia eryngioides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
