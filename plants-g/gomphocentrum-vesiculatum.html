Gomphocentrum vesiculatum

Gomphocentrum vesiculatum, commonly known as the African bubble blushing, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons, which are a unique botanical feature. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The botanical interest of Gomphocentrum vesiculatum lies in its distinctive morphology and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
