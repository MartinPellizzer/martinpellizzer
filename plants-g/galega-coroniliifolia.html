Galega coroniliifolia

Galega coroniliifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf goatweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Galega coroniliifolia is a perennial herb that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
