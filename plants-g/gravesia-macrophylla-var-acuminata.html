Gravesia macrophylla var. acuminata

Gravesia macrophylla var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species in horticulture.
