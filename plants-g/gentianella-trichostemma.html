Gentianella trichostemma

Gentianella trichostemma, commonly known as the hairy gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are radially symmetrical and feature five petals, with the inner ones being larger and more showy.

The leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its common name.
