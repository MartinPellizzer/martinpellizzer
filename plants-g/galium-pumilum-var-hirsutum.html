Galium pumilum var. hirsutum

Galium pumilum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium pumilum var. hirsutum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
