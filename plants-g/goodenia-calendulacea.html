Goodenia calendulacea

Goodenia calendulacea, commonly known as the golden goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
