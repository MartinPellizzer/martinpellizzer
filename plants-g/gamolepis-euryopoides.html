Gamolepis euryopoides

Gamolepis euryopoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gamolepis, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
