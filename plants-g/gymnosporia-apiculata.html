Gymnosporia apiculata

Gymnosporia apiculata, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated, spine-tipped leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
