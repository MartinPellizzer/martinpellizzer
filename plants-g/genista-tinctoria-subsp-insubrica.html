Genista tinctoria subsp. insubrica

Genista tinctoria subsp.

insubrica, commonly known as the yellow broom subsp. insubrica, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
