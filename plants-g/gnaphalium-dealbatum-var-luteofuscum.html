Gnaphalium dealbatum var. luteofuscum

Gnaphalium dealbatum var. luteofuscum, commonly known as the yellow-fuscous cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fluffy yellowish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters, often resembling a cloud-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its pale coloration and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
