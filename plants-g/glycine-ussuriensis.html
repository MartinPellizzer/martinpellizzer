Glycine ussuriensis

Glycine ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri soybean, is a climbing legume native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Glycine ussuriensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
