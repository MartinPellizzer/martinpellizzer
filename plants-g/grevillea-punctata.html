Grevillea punctata

Grevillea punctata, commonly known as the spotted grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
