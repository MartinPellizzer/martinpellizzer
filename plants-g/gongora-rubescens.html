Gongora rubescens

Gongora rubescens, commonly known as the red orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Gongora rubescens is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
