Geophila hirsuta f. brevifolia

Geophila hirsuta f. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved hairy wood sorrel, is a variant of the geophila genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and conservation efforts.
