Gentiana rubicunda subsp. rubicunda

Gentiana rubicunda subsp.

rubicunda, commonly known as the red gentian, is a species of flowering plant in the gentian family, Gentianaceae. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Iberian Peninsula.

The plant is characterized by its striking deep blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and mountain meadows.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intensely colored flowers compared to other subspecies within the Gentiana rubicunda complex.
