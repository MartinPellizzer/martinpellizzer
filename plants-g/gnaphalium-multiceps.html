Gnaphalium multiceps

Gnaphalium multiceps, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble small dandelions. It is often found in moist meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Gnaphalium multiceps is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
