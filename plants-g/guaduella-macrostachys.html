Guaduella macrostachys

Guaduella macrostachys, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens and parks.
