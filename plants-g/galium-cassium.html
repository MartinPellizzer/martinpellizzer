Galium cassium

Galium cassium, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium cassium is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
