Gentiana macrauchena

Gentiana macrauchena, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually tubular with a distinctive blue or violet hue, and it blooms during the summer months.

Gentiana macrauchena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
