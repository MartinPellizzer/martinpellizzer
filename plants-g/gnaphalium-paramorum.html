Gnaphalium paramorum

Gnaphalium paramorum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white or pale yellow blooms that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh mountain conditions.
