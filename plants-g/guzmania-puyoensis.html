Guzmania puyoensis

Guzmania puyoensis, commonly known as the Puyo guzmania, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range from red to orange, often resembling a torch. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect rainwater, creating a small ecosystem within the rosette. Its foliage is typically dark green with a metallic sheen, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Guzmania puyoensis thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its lush appearance.
