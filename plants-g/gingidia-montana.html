Gingidia montana

Gingidia montana, commonly known as the mountain ginger, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Gingidia montana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
