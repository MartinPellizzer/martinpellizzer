Geranium asphodeloides subsp. nemorosum

Geranium asphodeloides subsp.

nemorosum, commonly known as the woodland geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in woodland and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
