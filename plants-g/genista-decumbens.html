Genista decumbens

Genista decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, often prostrate stems that are covered with fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista decumbens is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.
