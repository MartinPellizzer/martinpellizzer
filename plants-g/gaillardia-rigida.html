Gaillardia rigida

Gaillardia rigida, commonly known as rigid gaillardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woods and sandy soils. The plant features upright, branching stems and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with red and yellow petals. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures.

Gaillardia rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
