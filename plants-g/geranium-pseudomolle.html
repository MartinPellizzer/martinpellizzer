Geranium pseudomolle

Geranium pseudomolle, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and also bear a fine layer of hair, contributing to its tactile texture.

Geranium pseudomolle is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
