Gentiana gracillima

Gentiana gracillima, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing striking blue petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
