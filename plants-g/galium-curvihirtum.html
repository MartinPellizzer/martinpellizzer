Galium curvihirtum

Galium curvihirtum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually opposite, with a hairy texture and a slightly succulent appearance.

Galium curvihirtum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
