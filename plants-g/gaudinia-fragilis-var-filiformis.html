Gaudinia fragilis var. filiformis

Gaudinia fragilis var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf gaudinia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
