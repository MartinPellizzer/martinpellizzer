Gymnanthocereus macracanthus

Gymnanthocereus macracanthus, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stem and dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and full sun.

Gymnanthocereus macracanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering display.
