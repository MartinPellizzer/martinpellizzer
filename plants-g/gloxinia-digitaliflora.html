Gloxinia digitaliflora

Gloxinia digitaliflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf gloxinia, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with dark green foliage that has a soft, fuzzy texture. Its flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat.

Gloxinia digitaliflora thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
