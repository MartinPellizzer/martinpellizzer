Glechoma benthamii

Glechoma benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, blue to violet flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Glechoma benthamii is known for its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats.
