Galactia scarlatina f. sericicalyx

Galactia scarlatina f. sericicalyx, commonly known as the silkypod milkvetch, is a variant of the species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, silky-haired pods that enclose its seeds, a feature that aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its role in local ecosystems and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
