Glycine humifusa

Glycine humifusa, commonly known as the ground bean or creeping soybean, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It has a trailing growth habit with hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Glycine humifusa is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
