Geum rivale var. leonardii

Geum rivale var. leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's sandstone beauty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
