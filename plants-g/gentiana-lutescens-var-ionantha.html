Gentiana lutescens var. ionantha

Gentiana lutescens var. ionantha, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white to grey hairs.

The flowers are tubular with a prominent blue or violet corolla, and they attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
