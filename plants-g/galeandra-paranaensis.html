Galeandra paranaensis

Galeandra paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shady environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
