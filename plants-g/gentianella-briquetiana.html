Gentianella briquetiana

Gentianella briquetiana, commonly known as the Briquet's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Gentianella briquetiana is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.
