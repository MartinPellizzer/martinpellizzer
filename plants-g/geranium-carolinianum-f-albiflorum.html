Geranium carolinianum f. albiflorum

Geranium carolinianum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered wild geranium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The foliage consists of heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This cultivar is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the dark green leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
