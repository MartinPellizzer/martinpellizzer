Grastidium malacanthum

Grastidium malacanthum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that reflect light, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of white and pale purple, are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Grastidium malacanthum is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
