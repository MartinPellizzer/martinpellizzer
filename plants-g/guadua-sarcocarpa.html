Guadua sarcocarpa

Guadua sarcocarpa, commonly known as the pink bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its inflorescence is a panicle with pale pink to reddish-purple bracts, which give it its common name.

Guadua sarcocarpa is valued for its strong, lightweight structure and is used in construction and handicrafts in its native regions.
