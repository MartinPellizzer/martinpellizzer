Grindelia hirsutula f. pedunculoides

Grindelia hirsutula f. pedunculoides, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a variant of the hairy globemallow, characterized by its distinctive floral structure and growth habits.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features erect stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, which contribute to its aromatic and sometimes sticky appearance. Its flowers are yellow and occur in dense, globular clusters, making it visually striking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, and its morphology reflects adaptations to arid environments.
