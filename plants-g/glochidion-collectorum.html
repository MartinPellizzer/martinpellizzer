Glochidion collectorum

Glochidion collectorum, commonly known as the collector's glochidion, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are a distinctive feature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
