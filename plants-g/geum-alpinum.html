Geum alpinum

Geum alpinum, commonly known as Alpine avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in cold, harsh conditions.

Geum alpinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden settings.
