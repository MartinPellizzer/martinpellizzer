Geboscon anisanthum

Geboscon anisanthum, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
