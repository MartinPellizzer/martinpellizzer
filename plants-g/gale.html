Gale

Gale, commonly known as the common gorse, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Ulex in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Gale is often found in heathland and coastal areas, where it plays a role in preventing soil erosion.
