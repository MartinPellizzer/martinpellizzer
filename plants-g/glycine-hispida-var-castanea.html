Glycine hispida var. castanea

Glycine hispida var. castanea, commonly known as the Chinese wisteria, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets and produces fragrant, clustered flowers in shades of purple or pink. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and persistent, spiny stipules. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its vigorous growth habit, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
