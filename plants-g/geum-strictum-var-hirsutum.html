Geum strictum var. hirsutum

Geum strictum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
