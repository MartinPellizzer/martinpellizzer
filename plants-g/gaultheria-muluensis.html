Gaultheria muluensis

Gaultheria muluensis, commonly known as the Mulu wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries that are edible. Gaultheria muluensis is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
