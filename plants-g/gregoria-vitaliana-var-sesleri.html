Gregoria vitaliana var. sesleri

Gregoria vitaliana var. sesleri, commonly known as the Sesler's Gregoria, is a rare and ornamental plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to purplish tones. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
