Gladiolus candidus

Gladiolus candidus, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy white flowers that have a distinctive sword-like shape. The plant produces numerous narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a striking visual element to gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its elegant, pure white blooms.
