Gaultheria leucocarpa var. cumingiana

Gaultheria leucocarpa var. cumingiana, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The berries that follow the flowers are red and edible, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
