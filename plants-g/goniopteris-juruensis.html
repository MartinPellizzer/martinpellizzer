Goniopteris juruensis

Goniopteris juruensis, commonly known as the Juru fern, is a species of tree fern native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered in a thick layer of sori, which are the spore-producing structures. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the trunk, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This fern thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
