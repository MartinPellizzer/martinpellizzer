Glycine glabra

Glycine glabra, commonly known as the smooth soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Glycine glabra has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
