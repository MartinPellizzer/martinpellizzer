Grewia triflora

Grewia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered bush or sour fig, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters of three, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Grewia triflora thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
