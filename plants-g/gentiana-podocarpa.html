Gentiana podocarpa

Gentiana podocarpa, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy flowers.

Gentiana podocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine forests.
