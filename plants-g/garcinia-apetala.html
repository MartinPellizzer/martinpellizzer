Garcinia apetala

Garcinia apetala, commonly known as the white-fruited garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. The fruits contain a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

Garcinia apetala is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
