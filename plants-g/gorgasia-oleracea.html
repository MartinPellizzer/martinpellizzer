Gorgasia oleracea

Gorgasia oleracea, commonly known as the Mexican gourd or chayote, is a tropical plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and a distinctive, swollen stem that resembles a gourd. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when green. Its roots and stems are also edible and used in various culinary traditions.

Gorgasia oleracea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
