Gonocarpus teucrioides

Gonocarpus teucrioides, commonly known as the creeping senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its fruits are dry pods that release seeds upon maturity.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
