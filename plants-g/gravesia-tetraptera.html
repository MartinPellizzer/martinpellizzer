Gravesia tetraptera

Gravesia tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged gravesia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
