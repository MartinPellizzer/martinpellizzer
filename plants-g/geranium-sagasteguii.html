Geranium sagasteguii

Geranium sagasteguii, commonly known as the Sagastegui geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Andean region of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems with opposite, palmate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowers are clustered in cymes, producing showy, five-petaled blooms in shades of pink to red.

Geranium sagasteguii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
