Gladiolus vigilans

Gladiolus vigilans, commonly known as the night gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, often nocturnal blooms that open in the evening. The flowers are typically brightly colored, with hues of red, pink, and white, and they emit a subtle fragrance. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value make it a notable species in the Iridaceae family.
