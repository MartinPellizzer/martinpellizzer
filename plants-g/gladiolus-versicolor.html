Gladiolus versicolor

Gladiolus versicolor, commonly known as the variable gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that can range from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped leaves and a tall, branched stem. Its flowers are radially symmetrical and consist of three petals and three sepals, often with a distinctive central spur.

The botanical name "versicolor" refers to its variable coloration, which can change depending on environmental conditions.
