Gnetum latifolium var. macropodum

Gnetum latifolium var. macropodum, commonly known as the broadleaf gnetum, is a species of gymnosperm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical or ovate leaves that are typically broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
