Galianthe angulata

Galianthe angulata, commonly known as the angular galianthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features narrow, angular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
