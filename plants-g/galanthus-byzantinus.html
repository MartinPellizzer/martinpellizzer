Galanthus byzantinus

Galanthus byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine snowdrop, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces delicate, white flowers with prominent yellow markings, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulb and are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Galanthus byzantinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
