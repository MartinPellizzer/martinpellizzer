Grammitis trichomanoides

Grammitis trichomanoides, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Grammitis trichomanoides has small, clustered sporangia that are often found on the undersides of its leaves.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a subject of study in plant morphology.
