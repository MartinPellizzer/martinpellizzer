Grastidium stenophyllum

Grastidium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass-tongue, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish or pale in color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Grastidium stenophyllum is noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
