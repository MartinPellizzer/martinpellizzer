Gentilia chevalieri

Gentilia chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentilia chevalieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
