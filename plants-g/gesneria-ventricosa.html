Gesneria ventricosa

Gesneria ventricosa, commonly known as the swollen-leaf gesneria, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a striking addition to botanical collections.
