Gaya hermannioides

Gaya hermannioides, commonly known as the Hermann's gailardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with bright yellow centers and reddish-orange petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Gaya hermannioides thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
