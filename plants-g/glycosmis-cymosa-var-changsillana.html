Glycosmis cymosa var. changsillana

Glycosmis cymosa var. changsillana, commonly known as the Changsillan orange.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
