Geranium koraiense var. chejuense

Geranium koraiense var. chejuense, commonly known as the Cheju geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula, particularly the island of Jeju.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often reddish in color. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
