Glycine multijuga var. alba

Glycine multijuga var. alba, commonly known as the white sweet pea, is a climbing legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

The variety 'alba' is distinguished by its predominantly white flowers, setting it apart from other Glycine species.
