Gentiana venosa

Gentiana venosa, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is often reddish in color.

Gentiana venosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
