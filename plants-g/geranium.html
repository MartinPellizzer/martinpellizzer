Geranium

Geranium, commonly known as the cranesbill, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often pink or red flowers and serrated leaves. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. Geraniums thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them popular choices for rock gardens and borders.

Many species are also valued for their medicinal properties and ability to attract pollinators.
