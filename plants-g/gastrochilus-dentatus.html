Gastrochilus dentatus

Gastrochilus dentatus, commonly known as the toothed lip orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizome and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive toothed lip that gives it its common name. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
