Grossularia uva

Grossularia uva, commonly known as the gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a tart flavor, making it popular in jams and preserves.
