Grindelia nana var. turbinella

Grindelia nana var. turbinella, commonly known as the dwarf brittleblow, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
