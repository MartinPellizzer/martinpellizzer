Gutierrezia mendocina

Gutierrezia mendocina, commonly known as Mendocino gutierrezia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the aster family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branching and wiry.

Gutierrezia mendocina is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native range.
