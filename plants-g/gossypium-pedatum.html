Gossypium pedatum

Gossypium pedatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf cotton or Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are encased in a burs, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Gossypium pedatum is also known for its potential medicinal uses and its role in ecological systems.
