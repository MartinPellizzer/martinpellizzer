Glaucium pumilum

Glaucium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue-violet flowers in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, lobed leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Glaucium pumilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
