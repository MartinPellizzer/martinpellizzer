Gossypium rufum

Gossypium rufum, commonly known as the red cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy, red-tinged stems and fluffy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces fibrous capsules that contain fluffy seeds, which are often used in traditional textiles and crafts. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Gossypium rufum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
