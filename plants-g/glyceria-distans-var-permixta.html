Glyceria distans var. permixta

Glyceria distans var. permixta, commonly known as the mixed glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in moist to aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
