Galium murale f. hispidulum

Galium murale f. hispidulum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender, sprawling stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
