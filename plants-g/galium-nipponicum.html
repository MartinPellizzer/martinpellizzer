Galium nipponicum

Galium nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
