Gaultheria buxifolia var. buxifolia

Gaultheria buxifolia var. buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Gaultheria buxifolia var. buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
