Garcinia pluvialis

Garcinia pluvialis, commonly known as the rainforest Garcinia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research. Garcinia pluvialis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Its unique botanical features contribute to its ecological significance and potential medicinal value.
