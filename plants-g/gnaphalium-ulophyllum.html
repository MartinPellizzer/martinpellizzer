Gnaphalium ulophyllum

Gnaphalium ulophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny, white to pale yellow florets arranged in compact, rounded clusters. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its silver-gray foliage and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
