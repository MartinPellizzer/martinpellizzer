Gentiana scarlatina

Gentiana scarlatina, commonly known as the scarlet gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and adorned with a prominent yellow or orange spot at the base. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows, often growing at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a deep green color.

The genus Gentiana is part of the family Gentianaceae, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.
