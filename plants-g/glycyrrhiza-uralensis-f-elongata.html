Glycyrrhiza uralensis f. elongata

Glycyrrhiza uralensis f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated licorice, is a variant of the licorice plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and smaller leaves compared to the standard licorice plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and develops pods containing seeds. It is cultivated for its roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The botanical features of this form make it distinct within the Glycyrrhiza genus.
