Gypsophila knorringiana

Gypsophila knorringiana, commonly known as the Knorring's baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Gypsophila knorringiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
