Goodenia heterophylla subsp. teucriifolia

Goodenia heterophylla subsp.

teucriifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant has alternate, narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is part of the Goodeniaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.
