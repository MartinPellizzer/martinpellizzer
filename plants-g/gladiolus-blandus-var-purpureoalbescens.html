Gladiolus blandus var. purpureoalbescens

Gladiolus blandus var. purpureoalbescens, commonly known as the white and purple gladiolus, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of white and purple, often with a striking contrast between the two colors. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, which can range from 10 to 30 cm in length.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
