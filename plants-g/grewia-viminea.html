Grewia viminea

Grewia viminea, commonly known as the silky bush or silky grewia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a silky texture, giving it its common name.

Grewia viminea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
