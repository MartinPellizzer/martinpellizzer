Guarea ternifoliola

Guarea ternifoliola, commonly known as the "Cupuaçu tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a large, hairy capsule that contains a sweet, creamy pulp, which is the source of cupuaçu butter.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and economic importance in the region.
