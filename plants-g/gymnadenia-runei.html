Gymnadenia runei

Gymnadenia runei, commonly known as the Runnies or the Alpine grass pink, is a species of orchid native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers, which are often fragrant. Gymnadenia runei thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
