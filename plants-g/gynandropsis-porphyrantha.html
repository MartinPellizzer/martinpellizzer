Gynandropsis porphyrantha

Gynandropsis porphyrantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive, milky sap. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
