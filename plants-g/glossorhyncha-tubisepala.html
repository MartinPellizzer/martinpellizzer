Glossorhyncha tubisepala

Glossorhyncha tubisepala, commonly known as the tubisepal orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a tubular sepal that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on tree bark. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
