Glandora rosmarinifolia

Glandora rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf glandora, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Glandora rosmarinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinctive fragrance.
