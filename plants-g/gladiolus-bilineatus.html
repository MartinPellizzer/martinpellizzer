Gladiolus bilineatus

Gladiolus bilineatus, commonly known as the two-lined gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces tall, upright stems with showy, brightly colored flowers. The flowers typically have two distinct color zones, giving it its common name. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
