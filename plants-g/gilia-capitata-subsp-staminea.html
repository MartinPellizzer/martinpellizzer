Gilia capitata subsp. staminea

Gilia capitata subsp.

staminea, commonly known as the fringed gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the top of the plant.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its attractive, tubular petals that are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the summer months, adding vibrant color to its native landscape.
