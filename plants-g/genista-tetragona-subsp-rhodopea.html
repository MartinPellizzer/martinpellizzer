Genista tetragona subsp. rhodopea

Genista tetragona subsp.

rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope genista, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
