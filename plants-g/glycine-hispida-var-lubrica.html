Glycine hispida var. lubrica

Glycine hispida var. lubrica, commonly known as the smooth cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and trifoliate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that give way to cylindrical, dry pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Glycine hispida var. lubrica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
