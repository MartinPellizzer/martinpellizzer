Gerardia macrodonta

Gerardia macrodonta, commonly known as the large-fruited gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small seeds.

Gerardia macrodonta is typically found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
