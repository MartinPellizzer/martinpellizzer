Gossypium nobile

Gossypium nobile, commonly known as the Chinese cotton plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fluffy seeds encased in a fibrous husk. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fibers of Gossypium nobile are used in textile production, similar to other cotton species.
