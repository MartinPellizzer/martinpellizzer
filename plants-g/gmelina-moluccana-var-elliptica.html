Gmelina moluccana var. elliptica

Gmelina moluccana var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gum tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
