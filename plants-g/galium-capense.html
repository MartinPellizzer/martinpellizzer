Galium capense

Galium capense, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or yellow wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, followed by spherical, bright orange or red berries.

Galium capense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
