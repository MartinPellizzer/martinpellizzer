Gentianodes microdonta

Gentianodes microdonta, commonly known as the small gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemose inflorescences.

Gentianodes microdonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
