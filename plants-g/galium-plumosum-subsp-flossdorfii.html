Galium plumosum subsp. flossdorfii

Galium plumosum subsp.

flossdorfii, commonly known as the Flossdorf hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered with soft, silky hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its delicate, grass-like appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
