Grevillea diversifolia

Grevillea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique floral structure and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
