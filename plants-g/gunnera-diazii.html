Gunnera diazii

Gunnera diazii, commonly known as the Andes giant water shield, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gunneraceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, cool environments, often found near streams and wetlands in the Andes. Its foliage is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against harsh weather conditions.

Gunnera diazii is also notable for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.
