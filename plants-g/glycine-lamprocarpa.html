Glycine lamprocarpa

Glycine lamprocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf sweetpea, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Glycine lamprocarpa is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
