Geranium hybridum

Geranium hybridum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are often hairy. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
