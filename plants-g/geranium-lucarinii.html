Geranium lucarinii

Geranium lucarinii, commonly known as the Cape vermeil.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Geranium lucarinii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact size.
