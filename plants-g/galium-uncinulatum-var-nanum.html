Galium uncinulatum var. nanum

Galium uncinulatum var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
