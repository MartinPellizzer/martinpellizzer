Gomphrena elegans f. latifolia

Gomphrena elegans f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Gomphrena elegans f. latifolia is also valued for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist into the fall.
