Glossostigma cleistanthum

Glossostigma cleistanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that appear in clusters. It is valued in aquatic gardening for its ability to oxygenate water and provide habitat for small aquatic life.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
