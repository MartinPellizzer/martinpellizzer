Gentiana perretii

Gentiana perretii, commonly known as the Perret's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are usually unbranched and ascending.

Gentiana perretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
