Galium gaudichaudii var. typicum

Galium gaudichaudii var. typicum, commonly known as the Hawaiian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces tiny white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Galium gaudichaudii var. typicum is an important component of Hawaii's native flora and is adapted to the island's unique ecological conditions.
