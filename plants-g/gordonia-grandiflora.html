Gordonia grandiflora

Gordonia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 10 feet tall, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its natural distribution.
