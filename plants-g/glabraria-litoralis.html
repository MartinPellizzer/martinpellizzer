Glabraria litoralis

Glabraria litoralis, commonly known as the salt marsh fleabane, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of salt marshes and tidal flats. It has a low, spreading growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Glabraria litoralis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
