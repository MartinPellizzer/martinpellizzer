Geranium cataractarum var. occitanicum

Geranium cataractarum var. occitanicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France and Spain.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
