Geraniospermum alpinum

Geraniospermum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Geraniospermum alpinum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
