Grewia hypotephra

Grewia hypotephra, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in shrubland or along riverbanks.

Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
