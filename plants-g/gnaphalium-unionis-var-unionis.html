Gnaphalium unionis var. unionis

Gnaphalium unionis var. unionis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its basal leaves are narrow and linear, while the upper leaves are broader and more lobed.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in heads, with pale green to yellow ray florets.
