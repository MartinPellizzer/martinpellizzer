Genoplesium ruppii

Genoplesium ruppii, commonly known as the mountain leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its inflorescence is a raceme of elongated, slender flowers with prominent sepals and petals.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
