Gilia inconspicua var. sinuata

Gilia inconspicua var. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated or sinuate margin, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
