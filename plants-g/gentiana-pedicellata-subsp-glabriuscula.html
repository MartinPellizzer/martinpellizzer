Gentiana pedicellata subsp. glabriuscula

Gentiana pedicellata subsp.

glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, while the stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
