Gerascanthus acutifolius

Gerascanthus acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved gerascanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
