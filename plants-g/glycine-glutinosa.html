Glycine glutinosa

Glycine glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Glycine glutinosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
