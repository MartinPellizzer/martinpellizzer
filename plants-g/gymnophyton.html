Gymnophyton

Gymnophyton, commonly known as the "gymnosperm tree," is a unique plant that belongs to the group of seed plants.

It is characterized by its naked seeds, which are not enclosed in an ovary, a feature that distinguishes it from angiosperms. Gymnophyton typically has needle-like or scale-like leaves, and its reproductive structures are cones rather than flowers. The plant is often found in temperate and cold climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a primitive seed plant and its evolutionary adaptations to harsh environments.
