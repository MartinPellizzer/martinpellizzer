Geranium anemonifolium var. canariense

Geranium anemonifolium var. canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its palmate, anemone-like leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
