Gentiana douglasiana

Gentiana douglasiana, commonly known as the Douglas's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountain meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Gentiana douglasiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
