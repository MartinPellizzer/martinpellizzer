Gelsemium sempervirens

Gelsemium sempervirens, commonly known as yellow jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and dark green leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its stems are woody and covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

Gelsemium sempervirens contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous if ingested.
