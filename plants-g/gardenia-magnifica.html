Gardenia magnifica

Gardenia magnifica, commonly known as the giant gardenia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and fragrant white flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant has glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Gardenia magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique, intoxicating fragrance.
