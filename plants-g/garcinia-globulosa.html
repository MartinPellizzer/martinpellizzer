Garcinia globulosa

Garcinia globulosa, commonly known as the globe fruit or globular garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, orange-yellow fruits that resemble tiny globes, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers and has glossy, dark green leaves. The fruit contains a single, large seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pericarp.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
