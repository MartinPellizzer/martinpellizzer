Guilandina murifructa

Guilandina murifructa, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, trailing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not managed properly.
