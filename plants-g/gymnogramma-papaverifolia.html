Gymnogramma papaverifolia

Gymnogramma papaverifolia, commonly known as the "papaver flower fern," is a species of polypod fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the plant.

Gymnogramma papaverifolia is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled indoor environment.
