Geum nitidostylum

Geum nitidostylum, commonly known as the silverleaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Geum nitidostylum is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
