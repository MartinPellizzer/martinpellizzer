Gossia randiana

Gossia randiana, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming during the wet season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Gossia randiana is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.
