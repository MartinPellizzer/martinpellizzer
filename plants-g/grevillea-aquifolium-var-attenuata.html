Grevillea aquifolium var. attenuata

Grevillea aquifolium var. attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Grevillea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which are often visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
