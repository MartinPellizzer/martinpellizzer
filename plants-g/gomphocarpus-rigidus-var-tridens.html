Gomphocarpus rigidus var. tridens

Gomphocarpus rigidus var. tridens, commonly known as the rigid milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a slender, spiny capsule that contains several seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
