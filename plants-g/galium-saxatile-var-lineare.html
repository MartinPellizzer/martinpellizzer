Galium saxatile var. lineare

Galium saxatile var. lineare, commonly known as the linear wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to rocky and alpine environments.

It features slender, upright stems covered with small, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, rocky habitats.
