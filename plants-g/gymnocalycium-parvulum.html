Gymnocalycium parvulum

Gymnocalycium parvulum, commonly known as the small-leafed moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Gymnocalycium parvulum is well adapted to arid environments and is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
