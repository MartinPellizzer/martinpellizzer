Geranium igualatense var. laxius

Geranium igualatense var. laxius, commonly known as the Iguales Mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to tropical climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic plant, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
