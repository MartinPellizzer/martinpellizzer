Gentiana quadrifaria var. zeylanica

Gentiana quadrifaria var. zeylanica, commonly known as the Zeylanic gentian, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of southern Asia, including parts of India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
