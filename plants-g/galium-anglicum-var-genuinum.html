Galium anglicum var. genuinum

Galium anglicum var. genuinum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
