Gymnema dunnii

Gymnema dunnii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky sap that contains various chemical compounds. Gymnema dunnii is often found growing in dense forests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
