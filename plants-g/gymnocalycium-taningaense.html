Gymnocalycium taningaense

Gymnocalycium taningaense, commonly known as the Tanning's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globose or cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Gymnocalycium taningaense is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
