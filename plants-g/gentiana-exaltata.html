Gentiana exaltata

Gentiana exaltata, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana exaltata is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
