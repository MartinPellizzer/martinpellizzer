Gomphrena globosa var. albiflora

Gomphrena globosa var. albiflora, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, pink to white flower heads that resemble small globes, which are composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant produces bracts that are often more showy than the actual flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to reseed itself, contributing to its natural spread in its native habitat.
