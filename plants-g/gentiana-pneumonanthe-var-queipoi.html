Gentiana pneumonanthe var. queipoi

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. queipoi, commonly known as the Queipo's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
