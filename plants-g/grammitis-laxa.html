Grammitis laxa

Grammitis laxa, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Grammitis laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
