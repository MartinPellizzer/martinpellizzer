Glycyrrhiza flavescens subsp. antalyensis

Glycyrrhiza flavescens subsp.

antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya licorice, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume pods containing several seeds. It is known for its high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound responsible for its sweet taste and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
