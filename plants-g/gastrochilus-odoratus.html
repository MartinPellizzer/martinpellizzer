Gastrochilus odoratus

Gastrochilus odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow, emit a sweet, pleasant aroma, especially in the evening.
