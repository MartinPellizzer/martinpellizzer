Gentianella campestris f. hypericifolia

Gentianella campestris f. hypericifolia, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
