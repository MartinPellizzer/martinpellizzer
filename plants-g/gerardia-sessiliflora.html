Gerardia sessiliflora

Gerardia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, often with yellow or white petals. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Gerardia sessiliflora is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
