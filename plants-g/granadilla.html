Granadilla

Granadilla, commonly known as the passion fruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces vibrant orange or red fruits with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a juicy, aromatic pulp filled with small, seed-like structures. Botanically, it belongs to the genus Passiflora and is part of the Rubiaceae family.

Its unique appearance and flavorful pulp make it a popular fruit in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
