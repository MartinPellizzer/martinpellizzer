Gastrochilus griffithii

Gastrochilus griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Gastrochilus griffithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
