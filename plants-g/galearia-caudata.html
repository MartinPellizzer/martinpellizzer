Galearia caudata

Galearia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Galearia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
