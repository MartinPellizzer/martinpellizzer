Gossypium labillarderianum

Gossypium labillarderianum, commonly known as the Bolivian cotton or Andean cotton, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white cotton-like fibers that are harvested for textile use. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.
