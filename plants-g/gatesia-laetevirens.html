Gatesia laetevirens

Gatesia laetevirens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish or purple throat. These flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to rocky slopes and alpine meadows.

Gatesia laetevirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
