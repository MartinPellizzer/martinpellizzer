Grevillea nutans

Grevillea nutans, commonly known as the sickle-leaf grevillea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are adapted for nectar-feeding birds, particularly honeyeaters, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Grevillea nutans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
