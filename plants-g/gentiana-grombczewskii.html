Gentiana grombczewskii

Gentiana grombczewskii, commonly known as the Grombchevsky gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana grombczewskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
