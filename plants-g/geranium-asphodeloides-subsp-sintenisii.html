Geranium asphodeloides subsp. sintenisii

Geranium asphodeloides subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
