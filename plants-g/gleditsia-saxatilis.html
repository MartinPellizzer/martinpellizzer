Gleditsia saxatilis

Gleditsia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock locust, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 10 to 20 inches long and composed of 15 to 30 small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are often wind-pollinated. Its fruit is a small, flat pod that splits open to release seeds.

Gleditsia saxatilis is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species that thrives in harsh environmental conditions.
