Genista demnatensis var. gomarica

Genista demnatensis var. gomarica, commonly known as the Gomarica broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, typical of many Genista species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
