Gentiana decumbens

Gentiana decumbens, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a low-growing, sprawling habit with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its most striking feature is its blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

Gentiana decumbens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
