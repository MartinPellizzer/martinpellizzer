Gentianella devendrae

Gentianella devendrae, commonly known as the Devendra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and its flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
