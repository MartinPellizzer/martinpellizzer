Geranium cuneatum var. hypoleucum

Geranium cuneatum var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
