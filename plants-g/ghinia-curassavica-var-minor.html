Ghinia curassavica var. minor

Ghinia curassavica var. minor, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
