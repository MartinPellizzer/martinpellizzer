Gordonia gigantiflora

Gordonia gigantiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has large, leathery leaves that provide dense shade and contribute to its ecological role in the forest canopy. Its unique floral structure supports a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Gordonia gigantiflora is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant display.
