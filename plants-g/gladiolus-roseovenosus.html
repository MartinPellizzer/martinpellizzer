Gladiolus roseovenosus

Gladiolus roseovenosus, commonly known as the pink-veined gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences with vibrant pink flowers that have distinctive rose-colored veins. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, adding a dramatic touch to floral displays.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
