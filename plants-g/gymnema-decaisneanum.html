Gymnema decaisneanum

Gymnema decaisneanum, commonly known as the Indian bitter leaf, is a climbing plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Gymnema decaisneanum is known for its bitter taste and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
