Garcinia havilandii

Garcinia havilandii, commonly known as the Hawaiian Garcinia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring glossy dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia havilandii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
