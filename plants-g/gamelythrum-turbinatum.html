Gamelythrum turbinatum

Gamelythrum turbinatum, commonly known as the turbinated gamelythrum, is a rare and lesser-known plant species belonging to the family Gamelythraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive turbinate (turret-like) inflorescence, which consists of tightly packed, small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are often pale green to white in color.

Gamelythrum turbinatum is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
