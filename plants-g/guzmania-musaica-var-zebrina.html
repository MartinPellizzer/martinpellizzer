Guzmania musaica var. zebrina

Guzmania musaica var. zebrina, commonly known as the Zebra Bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with distinctive zebra-like markings. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect rainwater, creating a mini-ecosystem. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spathaceous flower spike that bears small, colorful blooms.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.
