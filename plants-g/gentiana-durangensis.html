Gentiana durangensis

Gentiana durangensis, commonly known as the Durango gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals and a prominent tubular corolla.

Gentiana durangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
