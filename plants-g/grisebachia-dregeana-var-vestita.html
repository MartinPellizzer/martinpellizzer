Grisebachia dregeana var. vestita

Grisebachia dregeana var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet kudu, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
