Geocardia cordata

Geocardia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf geocardia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Geocardia cordata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
