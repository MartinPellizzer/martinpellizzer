Guidonia gelonioides

Guidonia gelonioides, commonly known as the gelonioid sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant's botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and spikelets that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water edges, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
