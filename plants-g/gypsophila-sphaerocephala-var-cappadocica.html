Gypsophila sphaerocephala var. cappadocica

Gypsophila sphaerocephala var. cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are slender and often branched, supporting the small, showy blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
