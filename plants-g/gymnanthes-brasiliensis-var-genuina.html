Gymnanthes brasiliensis var. genuina

Gymnanthes brasiliensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark green bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, waxy, orange-red fruits. These fruits are typically clustered in panicles and are known for their unique appearance and fragrance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
