Gastrolobium semiteres

Gastrolobium semiteres, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr medic, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Gastrolobium semiteres is also known for its potential as a forage plant for livestock.
