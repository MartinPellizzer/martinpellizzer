Genista lydia

Genista lydia, commonly known as Lydia's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by slender, bean-like pods containing seeds. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
