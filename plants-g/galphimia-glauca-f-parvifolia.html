Galphimia glauca f. parvifolia

Galphimia glauca f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf blue bonnet, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery blue-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid climates.
