Garcinia bussei

Garcinia bussei, commonly known as the red mangostin, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits with a thick, yellowish rind. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in wet, swampy environments.

Garcinia bussei is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
