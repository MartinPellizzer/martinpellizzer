Geranium yesoense f. leucanthum

Geranium yesoense f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a botanical variant of the species Geranium yesoense.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the parent species. This form is native to specific regions in East Asia, particularly in the Korean Peninsula. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
