Gentianella empetroides

Gentianella empetroides, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and unbranched.

Gentianella empetroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
