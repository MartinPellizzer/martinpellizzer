Gilia gracilis subvar. congesta

Gilia gracilis subvar. congesta, commonly known as the clustered gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
