Guadua maclurei

Guadua maclurei, commonly known as the MacLure bamboo, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles. Its rhizomes are robust and contribute to its ability to form dense stands in tropical and subtropical environments.

Guadua maclurei is valued for its strong, lightweight structure, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.
