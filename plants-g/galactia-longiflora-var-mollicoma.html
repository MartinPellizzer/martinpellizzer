Galactia longiflora var. mollicoma

Galactia longiflora var. mollicoma, commonly known as the soft-hairy galactia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are densely covered in soft, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a fine layer of hair.

This variety is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
