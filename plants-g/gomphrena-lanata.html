Gomphrena lanata

Gomphrena lanata, commonly known as the hairy globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, pink or red flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Gomphrena lanata is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance.
