Gentiana pedicellata var. chingii

Gentiana pedicellata var. chingii, commonly known as the Ching's gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or meadows at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
