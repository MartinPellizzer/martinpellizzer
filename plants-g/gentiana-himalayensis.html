Gentiana himalayensis

Gentiana himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and supports the showy inflorescence.

Gentiana himalayensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological significance.
