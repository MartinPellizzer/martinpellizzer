Glomera ambricaulis

Glomera ambricaulis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendant clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as trellises or trees. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

The botanical features of Glomera ambricaulis make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
