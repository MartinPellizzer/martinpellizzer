Granatum moluccense

Granatum moluccense, commonly known as the Moluccan granadilla, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a member of the Lychee family (Sapindaceae) and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, reddish-purple rind and a sweet, juicy pulp.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential medicinal purposes.
