Gloriosa carsonii

Gloriosa carsonii, commonly known as the Carson's flame lily, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Colocasieae and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from orange to red. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces showy, pendulous blooms that attract pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
