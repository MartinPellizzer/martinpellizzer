Garcinia vidalii

Garcinia vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which has been studied for its potential health benefits. Garcinia vidalii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacology.
