Galium glaucum var. lugdunense

Galium glaucum var. lugdunense, commonly known as the Alpine bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. The plant has slender, wiry stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.
