Genista tripolitana

Genista tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripoli broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid soils, exhibiting drought tolerance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its hardiness.

Genista tripolitana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
