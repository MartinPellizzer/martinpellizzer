Guajava decussata

Guajava decussata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum decussatum*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
