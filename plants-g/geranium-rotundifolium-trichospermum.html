Geranium rotundifolium trichospermum

Geranium rotundifolium trichospermum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
