Gnaphalium dioicum var. plantaginifolium

Gnaphalium dioicum var. plantaginifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This plant is commonly found in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
