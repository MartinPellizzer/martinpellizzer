Gastrolobium brownii

Gastrolobium brownii, commonly known as the Brown's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Gastrolobium brownii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Western Australia.
