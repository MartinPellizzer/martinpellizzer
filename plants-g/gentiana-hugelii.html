Gentiana hugelii

Gentiana hugelii, commonly known as the Hugel's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

Gentiana hugelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
