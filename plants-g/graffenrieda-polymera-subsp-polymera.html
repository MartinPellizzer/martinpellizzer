Graffenrieda polymera subsp. polymera

Graffenrieda polymera subsp.

polymera, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters.

The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.
