Genista umbellata

Genista umbellata, commonly known as the common broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, umbrella-like clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
