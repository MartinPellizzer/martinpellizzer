Gerardia viridis

Gerardia viridis, commonly known as green gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Gerardia viridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
