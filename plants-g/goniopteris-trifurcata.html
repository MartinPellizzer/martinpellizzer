Goniopteris trifurcata

Goniopteris trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched water fern, is a prehistoric plant that thrived during the Carboniferous and Permian periods.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that often split into three main branches, giving it its scientific name. The plant is notable for its intricate venation patterns and its role in the formation of coal deposits. Its fossils are frequently found in sedimentary rocks, providing valuable insights into ancient ecosystems.

Despite its ancient origins, Goniopteris trifurcata remains a subject of study for understanding paleobotany and evolutionary plant development.
