Goeppertia paucifolia

Goeppertia paucifolia, commonly known as the pink star sedge, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a subtle pinkish hue along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in dense spikes during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for both horticultural collections and landscape design.
