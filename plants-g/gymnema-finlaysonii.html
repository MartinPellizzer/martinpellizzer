Gymnema finlaysonii

Gymnema finlaysonii, commonly known as the Indian sugar candy plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its unique ability to suppress the taste of sweetness, a property that has been studied for its potential in managing diabetes. Its leaves contain compounds such as gymnemic acids, which may contribute to its medicinal properties.

Gymnema finlaysonii is often cultivated for both ornamental and traditional medicinal purposes.
