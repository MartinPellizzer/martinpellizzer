Gentianella gibbsii

Gentianella gibbsii, commonly known as the Gibbs' gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five petals that are often blue or violet in color.

Gentianella gibbsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
