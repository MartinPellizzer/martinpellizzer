Galactia lamprophylla

Galactia lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf cowpea, is a species of legume native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Galactia lamprophylla is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
