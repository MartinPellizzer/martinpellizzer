Guadua trinii var. scabra

Guadua trinii var. scabra, commonly known as the rough bamboo, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust, woody stems that are highly valued for their strength and durability. The plant exhibits a distinct rough texture on its surface, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in humid, mountainous environments and is well adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.

This bamboo variety is also noted for its rapid growth rate and potential use in sustainable construction and crafts.
