Galium japonicum var. bracteatum

Galium japonicum var. bracteatum, commonly known as Japanese bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, with opposite, oval to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
