Gymnocalycium mostii subsp. prochazkanum

Gymnocalycium mostii subsp.

prochazkanum, commonly known as the Prochazkana's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina. It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green or blue-green stems. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
