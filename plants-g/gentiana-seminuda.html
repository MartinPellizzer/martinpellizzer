Gentiana seminuda

Gentiana seminuda, commonly known as the naked gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and erect.

The flowers are tubular with prominent blue or purple corollas, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
