Genista equisetiformis

Genista equisetiformis, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, needle-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring, and it produces small, leathery pods that contain seeds.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
