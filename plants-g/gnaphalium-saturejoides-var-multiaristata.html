Gnaphalium saturejoides var. multiaristata

Gnaphalium saturejoides var. multiaristata, commonly known as the hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
