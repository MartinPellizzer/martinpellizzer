Gymnema sylvestre

Gymnema sylvestre, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the tropical rainforests of India and Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce glucose absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in diabetes research. Gymnema sylvestre contains compounds called gymnemic acids, which are believed to inhibit the taste of sweetness.

It is often used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
