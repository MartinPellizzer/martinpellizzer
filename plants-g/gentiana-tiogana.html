Gentiana tiogana

Gentiana tiogana, commonly known as the Colorado gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana tiogana is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
