Gnaphalium javanum

Gnaphalium javanum, commonly known as Java feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, fluffy flowers that resemble miniature dandelions. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gnaphalium javanum is also noted for its ability to produce a fine, powdery substance from its flower heads, which can be easily dispersed by the wind.
