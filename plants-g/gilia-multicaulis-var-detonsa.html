Gilia multicaulis var. detonsa

Gilia multicaulis var. detonsa, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as slopes and meadows. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
