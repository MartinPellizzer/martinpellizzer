Gironniera costata

Gironniera costata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges.

Gironniera costata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
