Gentiana ottonis

Gentiana ottonis, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and slender.

Gentiana ottonis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
