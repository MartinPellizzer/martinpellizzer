Gnidia setosa

Gnidia setosa, commonly known as the silverleaf gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that split open to release its seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Gnidia setosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
