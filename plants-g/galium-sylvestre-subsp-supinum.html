Galium sylvestre subsp. supinum

Galium sylvestre subsp.

supinum, commonly known as woodland bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
