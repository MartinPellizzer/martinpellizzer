Geodorum laxiflorum

Geodorum laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.
