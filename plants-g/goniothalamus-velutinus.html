Goniothalamus velutinus

Goniothalamus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
