Geranion

Geranion, commonly known as the geranium, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often colorful flowers and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. Geranions are typically herbaceous perennials, though some species may be annuals or shrubs. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are widely cultivated for their ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features include a hairy stem, alternate leaves, and a wide variety of flower colors, including red, pink, and white.
