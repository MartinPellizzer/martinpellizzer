Geraniospermum ternatum

Geraniospermum ternatum, commonly known as the desert geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

Geraniospermum ternatum plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
