Gymnocalycium prochazkanum subsp. simile

Gymnocalycium prochazkanum subsp.

simile, commonly known as the "Fuzzy Pincushion Cactus," is a small, globular cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina. It features a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and water loss.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its stems are typically short and thick, adapted for storing water in its native desert environment.

This subspecies is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
