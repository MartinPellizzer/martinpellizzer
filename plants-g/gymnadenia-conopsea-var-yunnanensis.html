Gymnadenia conopsea var. yunnanensis

Gymnadenia conopsea var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan grass-pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in moist meadows and forest clearings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
