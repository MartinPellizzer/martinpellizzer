Gibsoniothamnus parvifolius

Gibsoniothamnus parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf thamnus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with small, leathery leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
