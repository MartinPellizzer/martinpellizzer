Galium campanulatum var. hirsutum

Galium campanulatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, sprawling stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
