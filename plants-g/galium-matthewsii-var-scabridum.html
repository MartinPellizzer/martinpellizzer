Galium matthewsii var. scabridum

Galium matthewsii var. scabridum, commonly known as the rough bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered with fine, rough hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface. Small white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.
