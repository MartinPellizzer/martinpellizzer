Grastidium dulce

Grastidium dulce, commonly known as the sweetgrass, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Grastidium dulce is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a ceremonial plant in various cultures.
