Grevillea riparia

Grevillea riparia, commonly known as the river grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows near water sources, such as riverbanks and wetlands, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Grevillea riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in supporting local wildlife.
