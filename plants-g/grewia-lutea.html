Grewia lutea

Grewia lutea, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and smooth, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Grewia lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
