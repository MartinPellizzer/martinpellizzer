Galium firmum subsp. aegeum

Galium firmum subsp.

aegeum, commonly known as the Aegean bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Galium firmum subsp.

aegeum is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to thrive in coastal and mountainous areas.
