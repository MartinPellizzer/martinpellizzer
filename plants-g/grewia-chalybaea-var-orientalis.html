Grewia chalybaea var. orientalis

Grewia chalybaea var. orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring or early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a rough texture. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that turns red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
