Gilia scopulorum var. deformis

Gilia scopulorum var. deformis, commonly known as the twisted gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
