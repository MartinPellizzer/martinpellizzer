Gentianella solstitialis

Gentianella solstitialis, commonly known as the summer solstice gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their vivid blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
