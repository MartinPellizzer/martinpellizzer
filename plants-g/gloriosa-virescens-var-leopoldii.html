Gloriosa virescens var. leopoldii

Gloriosa virescens var. leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's flame lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
