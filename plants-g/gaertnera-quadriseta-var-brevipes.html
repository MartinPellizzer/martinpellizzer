Gaertnera quadriseta var. brevipes

Gaertnera quadriseta var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed four-branched geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features upright, hairy stems that branch four times, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
