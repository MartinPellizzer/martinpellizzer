Galium multiflorum subsp. puberulum

Galium multiflorum subsp.

puberulum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in umbels.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft, hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
