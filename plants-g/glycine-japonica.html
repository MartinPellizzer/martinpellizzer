Glycine japonica

Glycine japonica, commonly known as Japanese soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various culinary applications. Glycine japonica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable crop.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules enhances soil fertility, benefiting agricultural systems.
