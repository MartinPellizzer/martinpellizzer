Galium abaujense subsp. abaujense

Galium abaujense subsp.

abaujense, commonly known as the Abojense bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender stems that are often sprawling or ascending, and its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its distinctive fruiting structures, which are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.
