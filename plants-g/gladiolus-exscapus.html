Gladiolus exscapus

Gladiolus exscapus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gladiolus exscapus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
