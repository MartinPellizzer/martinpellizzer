Glycyrrhiza bucharica

Glycyrrhiza bucharica, commonly known as the Central Asian licorice, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing several seeds. Its roots are widely used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhiza bucharica is also valued for its high glycyrrhizin content, which contributes to its sweet taste and therapeutic uses.
