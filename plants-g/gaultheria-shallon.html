Gaultheria shallon

Gaultheria shallon, commonly known as the salal, is a species of evergreen shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when ripe, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal rainforests.

Gaultheria shallon also has medicinal properties, with its leaves containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
