Gypsophila pallasii

Gypsophila pallasii, commonly known as the grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Gypsophila pallasii is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and its long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators.
