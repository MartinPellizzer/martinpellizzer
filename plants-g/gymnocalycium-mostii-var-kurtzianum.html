Gymnocalycium mostii var. kurtzianum

Gymnocalycium mostii var. kurtzianum, commonly known as the Kurtz's moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by bright red fruits. Its leaves are usually short-lived and fall off as the plant matures, giving way to the fleshy, photosynthetic stems.

This variety is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
