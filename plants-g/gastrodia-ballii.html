Gastrodia ballii

Gastrodia ballii, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a unique plant belonging to the family Gastrodiaceae.

It is characterized by its fleshy, club-shaped rhizome and lacks true leaves, instead having scale-like structures. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments, typically in forested areas. It is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Gastrodia ballii is primarily cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.
