Guadua dioica

Guadua dioica, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its strong, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is highly valued for its sustainable use in construction and furniture due to its durability and lightweight properties. Its growth pattern involves clumping and can form dense stands in moist, well-drained soils.

Guadua dioica also plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
