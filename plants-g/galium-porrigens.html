Galium porrigens

Galium porrigens, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its stems are slender and often prostrate, with a tendency to root at the nodes.

Galium porrigens is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
