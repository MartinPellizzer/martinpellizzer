Geanthus densiusculus

Geanthus densiusculus, commonly known as the dense hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
