Gamochaeta ustulata

Gamochaeta ustulata, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters called spikes. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in the Florida panhandle.

Gamochaeta ustulata is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
