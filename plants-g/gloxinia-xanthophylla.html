Gloxinia xanthophylla

Gloxinia xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow gloxinia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically tubular with a broad, spurred lower lip. The plant produces large, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. It is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Gloxinia xanthophylla is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and ornamental foliage.
