Gynandropsis puberula

Gynandropsis puberula, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and produces a milky sap that is toxic to some animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Gynandropsis puberula is also known for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system.
