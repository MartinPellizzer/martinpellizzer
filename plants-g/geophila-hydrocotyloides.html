Geophila hydrocotyloides

Geophila hydrocotyloides, commonly known as the water bladder grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its roots are often submerged, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Geophila hydrocotyloides plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
