Gomphocarpus brevicuspis

Gomphocarpus brevicuspis, commonly known as the small-horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Gomphocarpus brevicuspis is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
