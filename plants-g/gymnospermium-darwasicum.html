Gymnospermium darwasicum

Gymnospermium darwasicum, commonly known as the Darwin's gymnosperm, is a unique coniferous plant characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

It belongs to the gymnosperm group, which includes plants that produce seeds without fruits. This species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as thick cuticles and deep root systems. Its reproductive structures, known as cones, contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, facilitating its spread across rocky and mountainous terrains.

The plant's evolutionary significance lies in its ancient lineage, offering insights into early plant adaptations to terrestrial life.
