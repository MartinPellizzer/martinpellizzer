Gilia latiflora subsp. triceps

Gilia latiflora subsp.

triceps, commonly known as the broadleaf gilia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features slender, branching stems that often grow from the base, with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy.

The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal, which is a key botanical feature. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
