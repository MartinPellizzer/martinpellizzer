Galeopsis tetrahit f. albiflora

Galeopsis tetrahit f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hedge nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The white-flowered form is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to the typical variety.
