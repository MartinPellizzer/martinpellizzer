Gyrostachys pauciflora

Gyrostachys pauciflora, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, pappus-equipped seeds for wind dispersal.
