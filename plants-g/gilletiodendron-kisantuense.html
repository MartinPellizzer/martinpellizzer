Gilletiodendron kisantuense

Gilletiodendron kisantuense, commonly known as the Kisantu flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often marked with distinctive patterns. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
