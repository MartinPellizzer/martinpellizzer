Grevillea macmillanii

Grevillea macmillanii, commonly known as the Macmillan's waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features vibrant red flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, typically during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in both residential and public gardens.
