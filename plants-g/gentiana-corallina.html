Gentiana corallina

Gentiana corallina, commonly known as the coral gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Gentiana corallina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
