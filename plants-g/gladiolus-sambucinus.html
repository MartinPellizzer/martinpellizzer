Gladiolus sambucinus

Gladiolus sambucinus, commonly known as the black-leaf gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
