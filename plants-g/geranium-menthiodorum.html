Geranium menthiodorum

Geranium menthiodorum, commonly known as the Menthol Geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

Geranium menthiodorum is also valued for its use in essential oil production due to its strong, distinctive fragrance.
