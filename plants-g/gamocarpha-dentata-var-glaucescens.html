Gamocarpha dentata var. glaucescens

Gamocarpha dentata var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous toothed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are toothed and often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
