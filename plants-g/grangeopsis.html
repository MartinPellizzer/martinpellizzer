Grangeopsis

Grangeopsis, commonly known as the evergreen shrub, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Grangeopsis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
