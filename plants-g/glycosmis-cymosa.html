Glycosmis cymosa

Glycosmis cymosa, commonly known as the Chinese date tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, edible fruits that resemble dates. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces yellowish-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Glycosmis cymosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
