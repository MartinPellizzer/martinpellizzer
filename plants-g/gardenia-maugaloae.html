Gardenia maugaloae

Gardenia maugaloae, commonly known as the Maugaloa gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
