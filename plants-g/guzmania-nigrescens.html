Guzmania nigrescens

Guzmania nigrescens, commonly known as the blackened guzmania, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that transitions from bright red to deep purple and eventually to a dark, almost black hue as it matures. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small aquatic life. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it relatively easy to care for in home environments.
