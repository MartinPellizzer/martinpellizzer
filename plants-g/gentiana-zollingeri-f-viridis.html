Gentiana zollingeri f. viridis

Gentiana zollingeri f. viridis, commonly known as the green gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
