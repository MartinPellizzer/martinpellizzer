Geum riojense

Geum riojense, commonly known as the Rioja avens, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Geum riojense is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
