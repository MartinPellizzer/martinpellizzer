Gifola pyramidalis

Gifola pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, pyramid-shaped clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Gifola pyramidalis is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
