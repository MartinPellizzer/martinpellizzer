Geranium pratense var. album

Geranium pratense var. album, commonly known as the white garden geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive calyx that remains prominent even after the petals fall. This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
