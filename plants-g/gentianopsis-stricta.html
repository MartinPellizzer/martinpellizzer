Gentianopsis stricta

Gentianopsis stricta, commonly known as the stiff gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Gentianopsis stricta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
