Gibasis chihuahuensis

Gibasis chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Chihuahua Desert in northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh desert conditions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.
