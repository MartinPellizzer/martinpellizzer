Grevillea rigida subsp. distans

Grevillea rigida subsp.

distans, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Grevillea rigida subsp.

distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
