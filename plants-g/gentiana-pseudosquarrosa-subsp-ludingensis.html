Gentiana pseudosquarrosa subsp. ludingensis

Gentiana pseudosquarrosa subsp.

ludingensis, commonly known as the Ludingensis gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
