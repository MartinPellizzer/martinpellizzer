Genista tinctoria f. hungarica

Genista tinctoria f. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian broom, is a variant of the yellow broom plant native to southern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
