Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. minus

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. minus, commonly known as lesser wood sage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive aromatic scent.
