Gentiana kauffmanniana subsp. kauffmanniana

Gentiana kauffmanniana subsp.

kauffmanniana, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine conditions.
