Gladiolus segetum subsp. bornetii

Gladiolus segetum subsp.

bornetii, commonly known as the Armenian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
