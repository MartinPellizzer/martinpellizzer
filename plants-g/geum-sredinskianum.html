Geum sredinskianum

Geum sredinskianum, commonly known as the Sredinsky's avens, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves and showy, pinkish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for rock gardens and alpine collections.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
