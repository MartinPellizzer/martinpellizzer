Goniophlebium adnatum

Goniophlebium adnatum, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually broad and ovate in shape. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Goniophlebium adnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
