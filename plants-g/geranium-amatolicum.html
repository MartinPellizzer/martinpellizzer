Geranium amatolicum

Geranium amatolicum, commonly known as the Cape sugarloaf, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, lobed shape. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Geranium amatolicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
