Genista syriaca

Genista syriaca, commonly known as Syrian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include the presence of thorny stems and a preference for well-drained soils.

Genista syriaca is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid environments.
