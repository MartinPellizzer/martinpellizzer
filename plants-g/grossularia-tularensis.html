Grossularia tularensis

Grossularia tularensis, commonly known as the Tulare mountain ash, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
