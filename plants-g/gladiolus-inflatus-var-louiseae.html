Gladiolus inflatus var. louiseae

Gladiolus inflatus var. louiseae, commonly known as the Louiseae gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant features long, sword-shaped leaves and a robust, erect stem. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
