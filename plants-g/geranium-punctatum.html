Geranium punctatum

Geranium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage features a unique pattern of dark spots, which helps in identifying the species.

Geranium punctatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
