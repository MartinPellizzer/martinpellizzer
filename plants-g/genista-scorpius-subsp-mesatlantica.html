Genista scorpius subsp. mesatlantica

Genista scorpius subsp.

mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
