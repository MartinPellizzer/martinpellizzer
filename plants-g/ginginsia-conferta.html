Ginginsia conferta

Ginginsia conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gum tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and often peels to reveal a mottled pattern, and it is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Ginginsia conferta is an important component of the eucalyptus forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
