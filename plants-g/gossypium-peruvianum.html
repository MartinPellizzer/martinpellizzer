Gossypium peruvianum

Gossypium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian cotton plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a shrub that produces fluffy, white fibers used for textile production. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other cotton species and related plants. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and the plant thrives in warm, dry climates.
