Gentiana amarella var. germanica

Gentiana amarella var. germanica, commonly known as the German gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
