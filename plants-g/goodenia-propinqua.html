Goodenia propinqua

Goodenia propinqua, commonly known as the slender goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
