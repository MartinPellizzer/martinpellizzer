Galianthe aurelii

Galianthe aurelii, commonly known as the golden galianthe, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
