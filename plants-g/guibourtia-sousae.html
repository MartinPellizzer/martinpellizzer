Guibourtia sousae

Guibourtia sousae, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its straight grain and high density, making it valuable for timber. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
