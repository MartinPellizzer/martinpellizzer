Gastonia heptapleurum

Gastonia heptapleurum, commonly known as the Chinese holly or holly tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle with care.
