Gaillardia roezlii

Gaillardia roezlii, commonly known as the Mexican firewheel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright orange and red ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Gaillardia roezlii thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden ornamental use.
