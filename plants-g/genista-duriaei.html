Genista duriaei

Genista duriaei, commonly known as the Duriaei Broom, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista duriaei thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
