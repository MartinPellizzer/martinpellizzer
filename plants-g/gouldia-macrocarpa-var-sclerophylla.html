Gouldia macrocarpa var. sclerophylla

Gouldia macrocarpa var. sclerophylla, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
