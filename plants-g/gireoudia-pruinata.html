Gireoudia pruinata

Gireoudia pruinata, commonly known as the silverleaf gireoudia, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pale, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Gireoudia pruinata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
