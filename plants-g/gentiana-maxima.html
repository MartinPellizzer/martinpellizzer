Gentiana maxima

Gentiana maxima, commonly known as the giant gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers are rich in nectar, making them a valuable resource for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
