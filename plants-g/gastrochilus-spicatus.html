Gastrochilus spicatus

Gastrochilus spicatus, commonly known as the spicate gastrochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Gastrochilus spicatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
