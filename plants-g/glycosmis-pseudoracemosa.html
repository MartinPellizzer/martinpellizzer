Glycosmis pseudoracemosa

Glycosmis pseudoracemosa, commonly known as the false mango tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.
