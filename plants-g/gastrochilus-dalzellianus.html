Gastrochilus dalzellianus

Gastrochilus dalzellianus, commonly known as the Dalzell's arrowroot, is a rare and distinctive orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
