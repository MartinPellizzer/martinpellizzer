Gerascanthus rufescens

Gerascanthus rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked geranium, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Gerascanthus rufescens is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
