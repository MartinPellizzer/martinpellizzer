Gouldia elongata var. hirtellicostata

Gouldia elongata var. hirtellicostata, commonly known as the Hirtellicostate Gouldia, is a striking flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences that resemble small, lantern-like structures. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its vibrant, showy flowers, which range from pink to red, bloom throughout the warmer months, adding ornamental value to the garden.
