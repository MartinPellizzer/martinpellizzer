Gloxinia ichthyostoma

Gloxinia ichthyostoma, commonly known as the fish-scale gloxinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive fish-scale pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
