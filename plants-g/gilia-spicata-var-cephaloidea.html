Gilia spicata var. cephaloidea

Gilia spicata var. cephaloidea, commonly known as the mountain gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, such as slopes and meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid and high-altitude environments.
