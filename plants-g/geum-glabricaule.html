Geum glabricaule

Geum glabricaule, commonly known as smooth-stemmed avens, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early summer. Its stems are usually hairless and can become woody with age.

Geum glabricaule thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.
