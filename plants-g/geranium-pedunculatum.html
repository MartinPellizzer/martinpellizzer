Geranium pedunculatum

Geranium pedunculatum, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium pedunculatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
