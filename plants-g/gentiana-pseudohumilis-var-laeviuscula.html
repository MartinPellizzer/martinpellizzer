Gentiana pseudohumilis var. laeviuscula

Gentiana pseudohumilis var. laeviuscula, commonly known as the smooth gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, smooth texture, while its stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
