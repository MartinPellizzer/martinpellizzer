Gnaphalium asteroides

Gnaphalium asteroides, commonly known as the starless everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
