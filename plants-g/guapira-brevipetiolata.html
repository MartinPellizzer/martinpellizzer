Guapira brevipetiolata

Guapira brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled guapira, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves with short petioles, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces small, oval berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Guapira brevipetiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
