Gentiana tetrasticha

Gentiana tetrasticha, commonly known as the four-stamen gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The four-stamen gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
