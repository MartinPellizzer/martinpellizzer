Gypsophila arrostii var. glaberrima

Gypsophila arrostii var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy or glabrous leaves depending on the variety.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
