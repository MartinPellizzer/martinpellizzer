Gossypium bani

Gossypium bani, commonly known as Bani cotton, is a species of cotton plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, fluffy seeds that are used for textile production. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces capsules that contain the cotton fibers.

Gossypium bani is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its importance in traditional textile industries.
