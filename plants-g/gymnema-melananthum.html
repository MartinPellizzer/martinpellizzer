Gymnema melananthum

Gymnema melananthum, commonly known as black gymnema, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, opposite leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which contains various bioactive compounds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
