Grevillea maccutcheonii

Grevillea maccutcheonii, commonly known as the Maccutcheon's grevillea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color, and it produces vibrant red or orange flowers in late summer to autumn. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal gardens. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.

Grevillea maccutcheonii is a key species in the conservation of local ecosystems and is often used in habitat restoration projects.
