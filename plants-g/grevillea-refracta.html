Grevillea refracta

Grevillea refracta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family and is well adapted to arid and coastal environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Grevillea refracta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
