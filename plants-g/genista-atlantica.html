Genista atlantica

Genista atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by slender, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Genista atlantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
