Geum parviflorum

Geum parviflorum, commonly known as slender-stemmed avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and hairy, with slender, branching stems that rise from the ground. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Geum parviflorum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
