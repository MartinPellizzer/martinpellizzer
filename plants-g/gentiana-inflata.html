Gentiana inflata

Gentiana inflata, commonly known as the inflated gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and sometimes branched.

Gentiana inflata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
