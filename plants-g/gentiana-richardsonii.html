Gentiana richardsonii

Gentiana richardsonii, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forested areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentiana richardsonii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
