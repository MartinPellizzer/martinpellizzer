Gentiana membranulifera var. membranulifera

Gentiana membranulifera var. membranulifera, commonly known as the membranous-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a distinctive membranous texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
