Gnaphalium sartorii

Gnaphalium sartorii, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers. The plant typically has slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Gnaphalium sartorii is also known for producing a mild, aromatic scent, which may help deter herbivores.
