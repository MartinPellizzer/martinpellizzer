Gentiana langbianensis subsp. langbianensis

Gentiana langbianensis subsp.

langbianensis, commonly known as the Langbian gentian, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and prominent yellow veins.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
