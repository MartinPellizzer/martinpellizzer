Gymnocalycium mazanense var. rhodantherum

Gymnocalycium mazanense var. rhodantherum, commonly known as the red-flowered moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
