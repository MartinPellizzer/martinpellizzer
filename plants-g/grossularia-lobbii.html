Grossularia lobbii

Grossularia lobbii, commonly known as the Tibetan pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its small, orange-red berries that resemble pomegranates. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The fruit is edible, though it is less commonly cultivated compared to other members of the genus.
