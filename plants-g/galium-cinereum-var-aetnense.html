Galium cinereum var. aetnense

Galium cinereum var. aetnense, commonly known as the Etna bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant features opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Galium cinereum var. aetnense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
