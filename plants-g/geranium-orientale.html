Geranium orientale

Geranium orientale, commonly known as the Persian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
