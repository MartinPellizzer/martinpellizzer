Gouldia macrocarpa var. sambucina

Gouldia macrocarpa var. sambucina, commonly known as the purple gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique coloration and architectural form make it a popular choice for adding visual interest to garden landscapes.
