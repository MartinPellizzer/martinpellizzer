Geranium asiaticum var. alpestre

Geranium asiaticum var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
