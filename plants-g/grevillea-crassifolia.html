Grevillea crassifolia

Grevillea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Grevillea crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
