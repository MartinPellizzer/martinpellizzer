Gossypium versicolor

Gossypium versicolor, commonly known as the variable cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or green, and it produces fluffy cotton-like fibers.

Gossypium versicolor is primarily cultivated for its fibers, which are used in textile production.
