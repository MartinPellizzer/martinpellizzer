Galium megalospermum

Galium megalospermum, commonly known as the large-seeded bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.
