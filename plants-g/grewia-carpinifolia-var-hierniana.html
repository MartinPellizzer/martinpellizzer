Grewia carpinifolia var. hierniana

Grewia carpinifolia var. hierniana, commonly known as the African starfruit or white starfruit, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a yellow or orange skin and a sweet, acidic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
