Guyonia obamae

Guyonia obamae, commonly known as the Obama fern, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sorangia. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and private collections.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in several countries.
