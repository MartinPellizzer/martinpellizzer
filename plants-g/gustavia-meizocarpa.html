Gustavia meizocarpa

Gustavia meizocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruit hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy fruits that are orange to red in color, containing several seeds. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Gustavia meizocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
