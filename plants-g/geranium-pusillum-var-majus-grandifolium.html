Geranium pusillum var. majus-grandifolium

Geranium pusillum var. majus-grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved trailing geranium, is a compact, mat-forming perennial native to southern Africa.

It features velvety, dark green leaves that are ovate to cordate in shape, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to violet, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
