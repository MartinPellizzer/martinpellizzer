Gymnopsis divaricata

Gymnopsis divaricata, commonly known as the variable burr plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

Gymnopsis divaricata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
