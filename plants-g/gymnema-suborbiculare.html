Gymnema suborbiculare

Gymnema suborbiculare, commonly known as the Indian nettle or gymnema, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in umbels, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Gymnema suborbiculare is often cultivated for its potential health benefits and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
