Gentianella pleurogynoides

Gentianella pleurogynoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Gentianella pleurogynoides is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
