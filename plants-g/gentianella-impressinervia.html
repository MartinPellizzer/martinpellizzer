Gentianella impressinervia

Gentianella impressinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
