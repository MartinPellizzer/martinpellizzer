Gesneria brachysepala

Gesneria brachysepala, commonly known as the short-lobed gesneria, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically showy, with brightly colored petals and a distinctive tubular shape.

Gesneria brachysepala is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
