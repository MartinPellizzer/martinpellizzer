Grevillea nematophylla subsp. planicosta

Grevillea nematophylla subsp.

planicosta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces striking red or orange cylindrical flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained conditions to thrive.

This subspecies is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
