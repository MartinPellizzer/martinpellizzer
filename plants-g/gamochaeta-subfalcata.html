Gamochaeta subfalcata

Gamochaeta subfalcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false fleabane, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Gamochaeta subfalcata is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
