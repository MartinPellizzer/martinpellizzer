Guettarda echinodendron

Guettarda echinodendron, commonly known as the rough-leaved guettarda, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It features simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to coastal and disturbed habitats, showing a degree of salt tolerance.
