Glycosmis chlorosperma var. bidiensis

Glycosmis chlorosperma var. bidiensis, commonly known as the Bidi tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
