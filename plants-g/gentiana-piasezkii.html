Gentiana piasezkii

Gentiana piasezkii, commonly known as the Paisezkii gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals that are often marked with dark veins.

Gentiana piasezkii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
