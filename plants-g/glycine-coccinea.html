Glycine coccinea

Glycine coccinea, commonly known as the red bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces vibrant red, bean-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using tendrils. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Glycine coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible beans.
