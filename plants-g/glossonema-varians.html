Glossonema varians

Glossonema varians, commonly known as the variable tongue grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. Glossonema varians is often found in wetlands and along water edges, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Its adaptability to various wetland habitats makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
