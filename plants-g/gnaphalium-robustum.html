Gnaphalium robustum

Gnaphalium robustum, commonly known as the robust cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish, often forming dense clusters.

Gnaphalium robustum is also known for producing a distinctive, aromatic resin that can be used in traditional herbal medicine.
