Gerardia aphylla

Gerardia aphylla, commonly known as the leafless gerardia, is a unique flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its smooth, green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Gerardia aphylla is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological adaptations.
