Genista salesii

Genista salesii, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant features slender, often branched stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Genista salesii is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
