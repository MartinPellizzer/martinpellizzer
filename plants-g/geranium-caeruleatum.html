Geranium caeruleatum

Geranium caeruleatum, commonly known as the blue geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, five-petaled blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Geranium caeruleatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
