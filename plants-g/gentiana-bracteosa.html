Gentiana bracteosa

Gentiana bracteosa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Gentiana bracteosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
