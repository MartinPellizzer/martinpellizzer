Gladiolus leptosiphon

Gladiolus leptosiphon, commonly known as the slender-sheath gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem. Its flowers are typically borne in racemes and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Gladiolus leptosiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
