Gymnocalycium kuehhasii subsp. incurvatispinum

Gymnocalycium kuehhasii subsp.

incurvatispinum, commonly known as the Curved Spine Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically followed by red, spherical fruits. Its adaptations include a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and spines for protection against herbivores.

This subspecies is well-suited to harsh desert environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
