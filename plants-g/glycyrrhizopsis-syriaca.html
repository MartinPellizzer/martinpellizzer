Glycyrrhizopsis syriaca

Glycyrrhizopsis syriaca, commonly known as Syrian licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used, known for their sweet taste and medicinal properties.

Glycyrrhizopsis syriaca is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
