Grewia lasioclada

Grewia lasioclada, commonly known as the roughleaf grewia, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, often hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Grewia lasioclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
