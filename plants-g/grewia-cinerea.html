Grewia cinerea

Grewia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale grayish-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Grewia cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
