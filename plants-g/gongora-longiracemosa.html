Gongora longiracemosa

Gongora longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
