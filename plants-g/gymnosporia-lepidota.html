Gymnosporia lepidota

Gymnosporia lepidota, commonly known as the white-knuckle burr, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are covered in soft, silky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gymnosporia lepidota is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
