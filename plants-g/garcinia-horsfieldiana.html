Garcinia horsfieldiana

Garcinia horsfieldiana, commonly known as the Horsfield's garcinia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that are rich in bioactive compounds, particularly hydroxycitric acid. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and evergreen foliage, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Garcinia horsfieldiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
