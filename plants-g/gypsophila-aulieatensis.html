Gypsophila aulieatensis

Gypsophila aulieatensis, commonly known as the Mongolian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences composed of numerous small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila aulieatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ethereal, lace-like appearance.
