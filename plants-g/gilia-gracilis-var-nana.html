Gilia gracilis var. nana

Gilia gracilis var. nana, commonly known as dwarf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
