Gymnotrix cenchroides

Gymnotrix cenchroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Gymnotrix cenchroides is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its invasive potential in certain regions.
