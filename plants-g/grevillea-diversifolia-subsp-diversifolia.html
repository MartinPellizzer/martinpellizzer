Grevillea diversifolia subsp. diversifolia

Grevillea diversifolia subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the variable grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or greyish tinge.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom from spring to autumn. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
