Gonospermum revolutum

Gonospermum revolutum, commonly known as the rolling seed plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive seed morphology.

The plant produces small, spiral-shaped seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal, enhancing its ability to spread across open habitats. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments.

Its reproductive strategy involves the release of seeds in a coiled structure that unrolls upon exposure to air, facilitating seed dispersal.
