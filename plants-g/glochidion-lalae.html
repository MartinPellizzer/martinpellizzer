Glochidion lalae

Glochidion lalae, commonly known as the Lalae glabrous leaf vine, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and ecological interactions.
