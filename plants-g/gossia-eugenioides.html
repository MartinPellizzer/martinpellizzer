Gossia eugenioides

Gossia eugenioides, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Gossia eugenioides is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
