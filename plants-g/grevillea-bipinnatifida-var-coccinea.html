Grevillea bipinnatifida var. coccinea

Grevillea bipinnatifida var. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes, typically during the warmer months. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract native birds and insects.
