Geranium keniense

Geranium keniense, commonly known as Kenyan geranium, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Kenya.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically has serrated leaves and produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium keniense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
