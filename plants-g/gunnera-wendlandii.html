Gunnera wendlandii

Gunnera wendlandii, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, palmate leaves that can grow up to three meters in diameter, making it a dramatic focal point in gardens. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like the common buckthorn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest.
