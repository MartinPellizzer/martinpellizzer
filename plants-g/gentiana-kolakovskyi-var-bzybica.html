Gentiana kolakovskyi var. bzybica

Gentiana kolakovskyi var. bzybica, commonly known as the Bzyb gentian, is a rare and striking perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
