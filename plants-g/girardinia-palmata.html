Girardinia palmata

Girardinia palmata, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the red palm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Girardinia palmata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil types.
