Gaertnera brevipedicellata

Gaertnera brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed Gaertnera, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
