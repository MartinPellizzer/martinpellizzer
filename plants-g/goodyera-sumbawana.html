Goodyera sumbawana

Goodyera sumbawana, commonly known as the Sumbawa lady's tresses, is an epiphytic orchid native to the Indonesian island of Sumbawa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose raceme. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Goodyera sumbawana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
