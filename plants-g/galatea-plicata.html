Galatea plicata

Galatea plicata, commonly known as the pleated rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with mustard-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring.

Galatea plicata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
