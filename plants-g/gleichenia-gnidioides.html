Gleichenia gnidioides

Gleichenia gnidioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Gleichenia gnidioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
