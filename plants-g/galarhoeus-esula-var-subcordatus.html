Galarhoeus esula var. subcordatus

Galarhoeus esula var. subcordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
