Grewia rotunda

Grewia rotunda, commonly known as the roundleaf globemallow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has oval, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Grewia rotunda is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
