Garcinia lanceolata

Garcinia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. These berries contain a single seed and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
