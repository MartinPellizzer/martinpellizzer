Gentiana pneumonanthe var. latifolia

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture, and its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are tubular with a short throat and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
