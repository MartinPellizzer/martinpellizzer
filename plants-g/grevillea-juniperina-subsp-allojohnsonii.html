Grevillea juniperina subsp. allojohnsonii

Grevillea juniperina subsp.

allojohnsonii, commonly known as the Heath Grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers. It is well-suited for drought-tolerant gardens and contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in suitable climates.
