Genista tinctoria subsp. delarbrei

Genista tinctoria subsp.

delarbrei, commonly known as the Delarbre broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, yellowish-green stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
