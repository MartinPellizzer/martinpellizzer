Gilia ludens

Gilia ludens, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gilia ludens is known for its adaptability to harsh desert conditions and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
