Guettardella rhamnoides

Guettardella rhamnoides, commonly known as the creeping buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries.

This plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.
