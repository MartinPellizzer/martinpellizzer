Garcinia ochrocarpoides

Garcinia ochrocarpoides, commonly known as the yellow-fruited garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by bright orange, fleshy berries that are edible. Its fruit is notable for its high vitamin C content and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

Garcinia ochrocarpoides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
