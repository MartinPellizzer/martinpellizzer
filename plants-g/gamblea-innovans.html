Gamblea innovans

Gamblea innovans, commonly known as the blue bottle plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple tubular flowers that resemble tiny bottles. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a prominent central vein. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Gamblea innovans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
