Gypsophila imbricata

Gypsophila imbricata, commonly known as the baby's breath or ice plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy appearance and is also cultivated as a garden ornamental.

Gypsophila imbricata is drought-tolerant and can grow in rocky or sandy environments.
