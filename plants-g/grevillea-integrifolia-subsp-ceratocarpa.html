Grevillea integrifolia subsp. ceratocarpa

Grevillea integrifolia subsp.

ceratocarpa, commonly known as the coastal grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The plant produces striking, crimson-red flowers in clusters, which are borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
