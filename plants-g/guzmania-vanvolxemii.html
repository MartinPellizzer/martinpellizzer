Guzmania vanvolxemii

Guzmania vanvolxemii, commonly known as the Vanvolxemii bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can last for several months. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and small animals. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding environment.

Guzmania vanvolxemii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
