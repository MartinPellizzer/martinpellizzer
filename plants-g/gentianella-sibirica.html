Gentianella sibirica

Gentianella sibirica, commonly known as Siberian gentian, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in alpine or subarctic environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella sibirica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
