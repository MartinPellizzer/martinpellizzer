Grevillea singuliflora

Grevillea singuliflora, commonly known as the single-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, crimson-red flowers that are clustered in a single, elongated spike, making it visually distinctive. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Grevillea singuliflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds.
