Gentianella quinquefolia subsp. occidentalis

Gentianella quinquefolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
