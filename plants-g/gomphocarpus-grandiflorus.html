Gomphocarpus grandiflorus

Gomphocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
