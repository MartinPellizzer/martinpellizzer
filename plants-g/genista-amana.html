Genista amana

Genista amana, commonly known as the sweetbriar or Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The shrub is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various wildlife species.
