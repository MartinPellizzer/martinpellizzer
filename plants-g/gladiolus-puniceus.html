Gladiolus puniceus

Gladiolus puniceus, commonly known as the crimson gladiolus, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, colorful flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
