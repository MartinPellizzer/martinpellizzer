Gaillardia lanceolata var. winkleri

Gaillardia lanceolata var. winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flower heads with yellow centers, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces seeds in the form of small, fluffy pappi that aid in wind dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
