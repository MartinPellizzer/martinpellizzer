Gagea callosa

Gagea callosa, commonly known as the scaly gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of white to pale purple flowers. Its bulbs are small and ovoid, with a fibrous outer covering.

Gagea callosa thrives in woodland and open areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
