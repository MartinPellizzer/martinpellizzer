Gentianella strictiflora

Gentianella strictiflora, commonly known as the rigid gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has erect, leafy stems with small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
