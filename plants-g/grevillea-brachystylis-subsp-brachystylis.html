Grevillea brachystylis subsp. brachystylis

Grevillea brachystylis subsp.

brachystylis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers in spikes.

The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in heathland and coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its short, upright growth habit and distinctive floral structure.
