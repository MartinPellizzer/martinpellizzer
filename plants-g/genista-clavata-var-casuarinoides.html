Genista clavata var. casuarinoides

Genista clavata var. casuarinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are compound and arranged in a spiral pattern. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
