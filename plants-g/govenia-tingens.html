Govenia tingens

Govenia tingens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock heath.

It is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Govenia tingens is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
