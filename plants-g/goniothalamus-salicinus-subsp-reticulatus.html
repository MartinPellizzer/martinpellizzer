Goniothalamus salicinus subsp. reticulatus

Goniothalamus salicinus subsp.

reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulate goniothalamus, is a member of the Annonaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical rainforests of Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a reticulate venation pattern. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are surrounded by a fleshy, spathaceous bract.

The fruit is a fleshy drupe, which contains a single seed and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
