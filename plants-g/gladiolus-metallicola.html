Gladiolus metallicola

Gladiolus metallicola, commonly known as the silver gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The flowers are typically borne in spikes and display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow, with a unique metallic sheen that gives it its common name. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a bulbous corm, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
