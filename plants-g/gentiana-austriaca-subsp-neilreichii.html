Gentiana austriaca subsp. neilreichii

Gentiana austriaca subsp.

neilreichii, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
