Gentiana olgae

Gentiana olgae, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its vivid blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana olgae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
