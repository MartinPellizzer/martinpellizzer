Gentianella meyeniana

Gentianella meyeniana, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Gentianella meyeniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
