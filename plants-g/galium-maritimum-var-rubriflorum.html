Galium maritimum var. rubriflorum

Galium maritimum var. rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.
