Galeopsis tetrahit

Galeopsis tetrahit, commonly known as lesser hedge-nettle, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant has hairy, square stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in gardens and along roadsides.
