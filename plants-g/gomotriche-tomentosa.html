Gomotriche tomentosa

Gomotriche tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly gomotriche, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a dense covering of fine hairs that help reduce water loss.
