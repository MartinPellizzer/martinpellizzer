Grewia villifera

Grewia villifera, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Grewia villifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
