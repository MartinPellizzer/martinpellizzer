Galium trifidum subsp. pacificum

Galium trifidum subsp.

pacificum, commonly known as Pacific bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It typically has slender, creeping stems that produce small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters.

The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of riparian and woodland ecosystems.
