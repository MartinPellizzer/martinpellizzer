Gigantochloa ridleyi

Gigantochloa ridleyi, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Due to its rapid growth, it plays an important role in soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
