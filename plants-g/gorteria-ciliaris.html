Gorteria ciliaris

Gorteria ciliaris, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gorteria ciliaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
