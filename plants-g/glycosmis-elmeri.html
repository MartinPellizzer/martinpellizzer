Glycosmis elmeri

Glycosmis elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's bitter apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-yellow fruits that are typically sour and bitter in taste. Glycosmis elmeri is often found in moist, shaded habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
