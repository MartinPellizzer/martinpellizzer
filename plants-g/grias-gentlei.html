Grias gentlei

Grias gentlei, commonly known as the gentle giant, is a striking plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Grias gentlei thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
