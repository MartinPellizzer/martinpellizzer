Gentiana crassa subsp. rigescens

Gentiana crassa subsp.

rigescens, commonly known as the broad gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
