Geranium gardneri

Geranium gardneri, commonly known as the Sierra geranium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
