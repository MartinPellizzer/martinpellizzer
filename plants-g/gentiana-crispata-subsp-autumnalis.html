Gentiana crispata subsp. autumnalis

Gentiana crispata subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with two lips, and they attract various pollinators including bees and butterflies.
