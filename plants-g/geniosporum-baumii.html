Geniosporum baumii

Geniosporum baumii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
