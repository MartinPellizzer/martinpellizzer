Galium pringlei

Galium pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Galium pringlei thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is often found in areas with rich, organic soil and is an important component of the local flora.
