Grumilea aurantiaca var. parvifolia

Grumilea aurantiaca var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved golden grumilea, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are small, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
