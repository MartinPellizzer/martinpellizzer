Goebelia alopecuroides

Goebelia alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly goebelia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Goebelia alopecuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
