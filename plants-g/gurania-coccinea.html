Gurania coccinea

Gurania coccinea, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Gurania coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
