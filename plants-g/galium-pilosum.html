Galium pilosum

Galium pilosum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. Galium pilosum produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.
