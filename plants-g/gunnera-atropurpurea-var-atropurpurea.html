Gunnera atropurpurea var. atropurpurea

Gunnera atropurpurea var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple giant rhubarb, is a striking member of the Gunneraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces showy, red-purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. Native to the Andes Mountains, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique foliage and dramatic floral display make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
