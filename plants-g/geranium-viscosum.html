Geranium viscosum

Geranium viscosum, commonly known as sticky geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Geranium viscosum thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
