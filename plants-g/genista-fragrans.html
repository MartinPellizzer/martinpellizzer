Genista fragrans

Genista fragrans, commonly known as sweet goldenbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and dry habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Genista fragrans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
