Guarea grandifoliola

Guarea grandifoliola, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody fruits containing flat, seed-like structures.

The plant is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
