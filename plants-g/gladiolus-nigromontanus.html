Gladiolus nigromontanus

Gladiolus nigromontanus, commonly known as the black mountain gladiolus, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers. The flowers typically feature deep purple to black petals, which are often speckled with contrasting colors. This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
