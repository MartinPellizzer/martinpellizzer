Geraniospermum bicolor

Geraniospermum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored geraniosperm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored leaves, typically featuring a green upper surface and a red or purple underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access groundwater.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.
