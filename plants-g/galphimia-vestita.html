Galphimia vestita

Galphimia vestita, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Galphimia vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add color to gardens.
