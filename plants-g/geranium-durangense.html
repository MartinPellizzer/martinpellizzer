Geranium durangense

Geranium durangense, commonly known as the Durango geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink to purple blooms that appear in the summer months. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

Geranium durangense is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
