Galium elatum subsp. erectum

Galium elatum subsp.

erectum, commonly known as white bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive hairy texture. Galium elatum subsp.

erectum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
