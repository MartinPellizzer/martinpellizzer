Gymnadenia odoratissima var. macrocheilea

Gymnadenia odoratissima var. macrocheilea, commonly known as the sweet grass or the greater grass of the mountains, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows across Europe. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

The species is noted for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is released when the flowers are disturbed.
