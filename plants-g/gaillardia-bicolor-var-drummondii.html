Gaillardia bicolor var. drummondii

Gaillardia bicolor var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and red, orange, or brown petals. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
