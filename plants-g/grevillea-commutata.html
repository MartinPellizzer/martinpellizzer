Grevillea commutata

Grevillea commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Grevillea commutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
