Gratiola mauretanica

Gratiola mauretanica, commonly known as the Moroccan yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

Gratiola mauretanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
