Guilandina solomonensis

Guilandina solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, serpentine stems and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Guilandina solomonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some island cultures.
