Gentiana beamanii

Gentiana beamanii, commonly known as Beaman's gentian, is a striking perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada and Cascade mountain ranges in North America.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in meadows or along streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Gentiana beamanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
