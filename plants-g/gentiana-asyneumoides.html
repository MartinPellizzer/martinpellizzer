Gentiana asyneumoides

Gentiana asyneumoides, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
