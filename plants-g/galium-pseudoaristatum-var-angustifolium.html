Galium pseudoaristatum var. angustifolium

Galium pseudoaristatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of North America.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
