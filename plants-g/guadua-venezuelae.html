Guadua venezuelae

Guadua venezuelae, commonly known as the Venezuelan bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Venezuela, Colombia, and parts of Ecuador. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The leaves are long and narrow, with a prominent midrib, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Guadua venezuelae is valued for its strong, lightweight wood, which is used in construction and handicrafts.
