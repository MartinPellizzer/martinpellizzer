Gerardia glutinosa

Gerardia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky gerardia, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Gerardia glutinosa is known for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
