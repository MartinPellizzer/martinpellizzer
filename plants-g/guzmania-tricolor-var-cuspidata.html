Guzmania tricolor var. cuspidata

Guzmania tricolor var. cuspidata, commonly known as the rainbow bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a visually stunning display. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool that supports aquatic life. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spike-like structure bearing small, white flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
