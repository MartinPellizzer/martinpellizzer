Gentiana albicalyx

Gentiana albicalyx, commonly known as the white gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to sky-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Gentiana albicalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
