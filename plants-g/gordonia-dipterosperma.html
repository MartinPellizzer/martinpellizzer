Gordonia dipterosperma

Gordonia dipterosperma, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features include a rugged, fissured bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils in its natural habitat.
