Gentiana pedicellata var. wightii

Gentiana pedicellata var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
