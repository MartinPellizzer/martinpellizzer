Geissomeria pubescens var. velutina

Geissomeria pubescens var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf gooseberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to reddish berries. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
