Geonoma bondariana

Geonoma bondariana, commonly known as the Brazilian sword fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorangia, are located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in tropical ecosystems.
