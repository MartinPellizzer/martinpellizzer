Grewia rugulosa

Grewia rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Grewia rugulosa is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
