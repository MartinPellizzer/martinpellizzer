Gladiolus affinis

Gladiolus affinis, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, and are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
