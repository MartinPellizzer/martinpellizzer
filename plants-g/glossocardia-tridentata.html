Glossocardia tridentata

Glossocardia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warm season, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Glossocardia tridentata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
