Gentiana septemfida var. diversifolia

Gentiana septemfida var. diversifolia, commonly known as the seven-leaved gentian with variegated leaves, is a striking member of the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually divided into seven segments, giving it the species name "septemfida," while the varietal name "diversifolia" refers to its varied leaf patterns.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
