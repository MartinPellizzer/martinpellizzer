Gymnema glabrum

Gymnema glabrum, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and distinctive milky sap. The plant is notable for its ability to inhibit sweet taste perception, a property that has been historically used in Ayurvedic medicine. Gymnema glabrum contains compounds such as gymnemic acids, which are believed to have hypoglycemic effects.

Its botanical significance extends to its role in traditional medicine and modern research into diabetes management.
