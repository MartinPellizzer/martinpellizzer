Galium cilicicum var. suffrutescens

Galium cilicicum var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the Cilician bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically features slender, prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium cilicicum var. suffrutescens is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
