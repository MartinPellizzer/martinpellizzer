Goodenia marginata

Goodenia marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and showy inflorescences.

Goodenia marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
