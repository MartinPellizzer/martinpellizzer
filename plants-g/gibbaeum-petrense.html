Gibbaeum petrense

Gibbaeum petrense, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
