Gamblea ciliata

Gamblea ciliata, commonly known as the silky bristled hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive silky, hairy texture on the petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces yellow stamens that contrast with the pink or red petals.

Gamblea ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
