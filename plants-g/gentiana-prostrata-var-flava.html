Gentiana prostrata var. flava

Gentiana prostrata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
