Gastrolea beguinii

Gastrolea beguinii, commonly known as the Beguin's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a slender, creeping rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. Its flowers are typically clustered in a loose inflorescence and are pollinated by specific insects, highlighting its ecological specificity.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, Gastrolea beguinii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
