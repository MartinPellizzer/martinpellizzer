Gymnadenia secunda

Gymnadenia secunda, commonly known as the lesser grass pink, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and other open areas with well-drained soil. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Gymnadenia secunda is often found in association with other orchid species and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
