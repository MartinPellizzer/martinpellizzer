Goniopteris tetragona var. tetragonoides

Goniopteris tetragona var. tetragonoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Goniopteris.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically divided into multiple segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The botanical features of this variant include its distinctive leaf morphology and adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
