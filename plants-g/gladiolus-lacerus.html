Gladiolus lacerus

Gladiolus lacerus, commonly known as the ragged glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often marked with purple or red veins. The plant produces large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for both its beauty and its use in floral arrangements.
