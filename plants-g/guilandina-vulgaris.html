Guilandina vulgaris

Guilandina vulgaris, commonly known as the climbing hyacinth or wild hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and it has a vigorous climbing habit with tendrils.

Guilandina vulgaris is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
