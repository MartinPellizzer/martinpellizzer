Gardnerina

Gardnerina, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, velvety flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers come in a wide range of colors including purple, pink, white, and yellow. Gardnerina species are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
