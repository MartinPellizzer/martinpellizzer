Genlisea tuberosa

Genlisea tuberosa, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are modified into these bladders, which are used to capture prey. It thrives in nutrient-poor water environments, supplementing its diet with captured prey.

Its unique trapping mechanism makes it an interesting subject in the study of plant adaptation and evolution.
