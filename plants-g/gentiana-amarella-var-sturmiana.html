Gentiana amarella var. sturmiana

Gentiana amarella var. sturmiana, commonly known as the Sturm's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
