Goldfussia paniculata var. alata

Goldfussia paniculata var. alata, commonly known as the winged panicled goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated panicles that are adorned with bright golden-yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical appeal lies in its unique winged bracts and showy inflorescences, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
