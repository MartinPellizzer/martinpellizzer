Gagea lutea var. gracillima

Gagea lutea var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender white squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in open woodland and chaparral habitats. Its bulbs are small and rounded, storing nutrients for the plant's survival through dry periods.

The species is noted for its early spring bloom and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
