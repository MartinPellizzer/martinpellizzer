Galium boreale var. lanceolatum

Galium boreale var. lanceolatum, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often hairy and bear small, opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal practices, though its medicinal applications are limited.

Galium boreale var. lanceolatum thrives in moist, open habitats such as tundra, wet meadows, and rocky slopes.
