Glyceria ischyroneura

Glyceria ischyroneura, commonly known as the narrowleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and is typically found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively along water edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Glyceria ischyroneura plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
