Goniopteris tetragona var. megalodus

Goniopteris tetragona var. megalodus, commonly known as the giant fern, is a large, tree-like fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length, with a distinctive, fan-like shape. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, ovate pinnae arranged in a regular pattern.

This variety is notable for its size and ornamental value in horticulture.
