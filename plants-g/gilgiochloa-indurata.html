Gilgiochloa indurata

Gilgiochloa indurata, commonly known as the hard-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its rigid, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny grains that are dispersed by wind.

Gilgiochloa indurata is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant in certain regions.
