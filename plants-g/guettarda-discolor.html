Guettarda discolor

Guettarda discolor, commonly known as the white guettarda, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen branches and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
