Gaultheria mundula var. mundula

Gaultheria mundula var. mundula, commonly known as the small-leaved wintergreen, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a waxy coating. Its flowers are white and bell-shaped, appearing in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for both humans and wildlife.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
