Gentiana grata subsp. grata

Gentiana grata subsp.

grata, commonly known as the sweet gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps retain moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
