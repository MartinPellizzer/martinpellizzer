Gnaphalium nitidulum

Gnaphalium nitidulum, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are yellow in color, surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. Its leaves are narrow, silvery-green, and covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements.
