Gomphrena chrestoides

Gomphrena chrestoides, commonly known as the purple globe flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical flower heads that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena chrestoides is also known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist throughout the summer and into the fall.
