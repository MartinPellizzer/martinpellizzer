Galium rubrum subsp. balearicum

Galium rubrum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers are often visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.
