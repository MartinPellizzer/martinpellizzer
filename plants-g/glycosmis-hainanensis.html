Glycosmis hainanensis

Glycosmis hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Glycosmis hainanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
