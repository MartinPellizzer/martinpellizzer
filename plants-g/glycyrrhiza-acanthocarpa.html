Glycyrrhiza acanthocarpa

Glycyrrhiza acanthocarpa, commonly known as the prickly licorice, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and its root is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Glycyrrhiza acanthocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
