Gabunia macrocarpa

Gabunia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant seed tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Gabunia macrocarpa is valued for its durable timber and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various forest species and contributing to soil stability.
