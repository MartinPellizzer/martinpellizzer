Gentiana harrowiana

Gentiana harrowiana, commonly known as the Harrow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The Harrow gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
