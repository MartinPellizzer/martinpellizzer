Gilia densifolia var. mohavensis

Gilia densifolia var. mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils in desert regions. It produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
