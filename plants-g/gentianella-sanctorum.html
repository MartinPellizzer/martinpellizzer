Gentianella sanctorum

Gentianella sanctorum, commonly known as the sacred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Gentianella sanctorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
