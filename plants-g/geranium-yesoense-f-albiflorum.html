Geranium yesoense f. albiflorum

Geranium yesoense f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Siberian geranium, is a variant of the species Geranium yesoense, native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral coloration and adaptation to cold, high-altitude environments.
