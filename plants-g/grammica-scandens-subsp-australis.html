Grammica scandens subsp. australis

Grammica scandens subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian climbing verbena, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows as a climbing or trailing plant, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
