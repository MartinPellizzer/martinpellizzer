Glinus herniarioides

Glinus herniarioides, commonly known as the creeping iron vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and can often be found in grasslands or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many areas where it grows prolifically.
