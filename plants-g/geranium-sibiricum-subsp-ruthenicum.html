Geranium sibiricum subsp. ruthenicum

Geranium sibiricum subsp.

ruthenicum, commonly known as Siberian geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
