Gerardia purpurea var. grandiflora

Gerardia purpurea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Purple Germander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
