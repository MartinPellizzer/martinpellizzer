Glycine max subsp. soja

Glycine max subsp.

soja, commonly known as soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is cultivated for its high protein content, making it an important crop in global agriculture. Soybean plants have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Its versatility extends to various products, including tofu, soy milk, and oils.
