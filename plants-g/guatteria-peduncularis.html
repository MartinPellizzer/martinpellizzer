Guatteria peduncularis

Guatteria peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.

Guatteria peduncularis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
