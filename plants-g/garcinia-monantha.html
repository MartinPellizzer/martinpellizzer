Garcinia monantha

Garcinia monantha, commonly known as the white garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elliptical, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp. Garcinia monantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Clusiaceae family.
