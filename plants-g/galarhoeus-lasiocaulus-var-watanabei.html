Galarhoeus lasiocaulus var. watanabei

Galarhoeus lasiocaulus var. watanabei, commonly known as the Watanabe's haircap moss, is a variant of the haircap moss family (Montiniaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of long, stiff hairs on its stem and leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinct midrib and a gradually tapering apex. The plant thrives in moist, acidic environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

Its reproductive structures, including the capsule and calyptra, are well-developed and typical of the family.
