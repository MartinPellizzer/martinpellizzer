Garcinia cladostigma

Garcinia cladostigma, commonly known as the red mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, rough bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit's pericarp contains a white, aromatic pulp surrounded by a hard, segmented rind.

Garcinia cladostigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
