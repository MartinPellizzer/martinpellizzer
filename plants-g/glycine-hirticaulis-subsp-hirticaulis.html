Glycine hirticaulis subsp. hirticaulis

Glycine hirticaulis subsp.

hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy pinto bean, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant produces elongated, hairy pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
