Gentiana flexicaulis var. flexicaulis

Gentiana flexicaulis var. flexicaulis, commonly known as the flexible gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
