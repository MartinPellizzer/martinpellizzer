Grevillea amplexans subsp. adpressa

Grevillea amplexans subsp.

adpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grevillea, is a shrub native to western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces striking, red or orange inflorescences that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
