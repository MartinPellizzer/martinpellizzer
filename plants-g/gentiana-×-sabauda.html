Gentiana × sabauda

Gentiana × sabauda, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with a prominent blue or purple throat. Native to the alpine regions of Europe, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
