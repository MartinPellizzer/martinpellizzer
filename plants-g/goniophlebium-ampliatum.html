Goniophlebium ampliatum

Goniophlebium ampliatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its large, broad, and often deeply lobed leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually evergreen, and they can reach lengths of up to several meters.

The spore-producing structures, known as sori, are arranged in rows on the underside of the fronds.
