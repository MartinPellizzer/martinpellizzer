Gnaphalium sinense

Gnaphalium sinense, commonly known as Chinese toothbrush or white cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gnaphalium sinense is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance.
