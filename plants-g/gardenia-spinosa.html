Gardenia spinosa

Gardenia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and attractive blooms, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
