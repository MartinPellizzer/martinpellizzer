Gaultheria myrtillus

Gaultheria myrtillus, commonly known as the wintergreen shrub, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of the Americas and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are rich in methyl salicylate, giving them a distinctive wintergreen aroma. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Gaultheria myrtillus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
