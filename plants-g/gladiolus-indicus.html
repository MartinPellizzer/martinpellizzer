Gladiolus indicus

Gladiolus indicus, commonly known as the Indian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its beauty and is cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
