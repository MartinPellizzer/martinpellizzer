Gagea granatellii subsp. maroccana

Gagea granatellii subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan gagea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing a compact growth habit. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help it survive in arid conditions.

It is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
