Guardiola angustifolia

Guardiola angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf guardiola, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.

Guardiola angustifolia is often studied for its potential medicinal properties and adaptability to harsh environments.
