Gladiolus pageae

Gladiolus pageae, commonly known as the Page's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically blooms in late summer and early autumn, producing clusters of flowers at the top of the stem. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Gladiolus pageae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
