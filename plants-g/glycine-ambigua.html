Glycine ambigua

Glycine ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. Glycine ambigua is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
