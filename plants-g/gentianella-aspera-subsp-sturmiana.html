Gentianella aspera subsp. sturmiana

Gentianella aspera subsp.

sturmiana, commonly known as the Sturm's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that gives it its species name "aspera.".

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
