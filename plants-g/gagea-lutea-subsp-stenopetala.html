Gagea lutea subsp. stenopetala

Gagea lutea subsp.

stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled white squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces a single, upright stem bearing a cluster of narrow, white, bell-shaped flowers.

The leaves are basal, grass-like, and often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in open meadows and forest edges.
