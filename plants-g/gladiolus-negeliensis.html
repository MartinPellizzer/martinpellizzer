Gladiolus negeliensis

Gladiolus negeliensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, colorful flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing spike-like inflorescences with vibrant petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, although it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
