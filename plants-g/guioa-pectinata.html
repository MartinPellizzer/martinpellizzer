Guioa pectinata

Guioa pectinata, commonly known as the silky guioa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale gray bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland to mid-elevation forests.

Guioa pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its aesthetic foliage and adaptability.
