Galium mollugo var. pallidulum

Galium mollugo var. pallidulum, commonly known as pale bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and covered with fine, silky hairs. Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and damp meadows.
