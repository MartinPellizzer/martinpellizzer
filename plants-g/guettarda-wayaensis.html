Guettarda wayaensis

Guettarda wayaensis, commonly known as the West Indian guettarda, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a glossy appearance.

Guettarda wayaensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
