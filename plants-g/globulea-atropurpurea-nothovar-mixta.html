Globulea atropurpurea nothovar. mixta

Globulea atropurpurea nothovar. mixta, commonly known as the hybrid purple globulea, is a cultivated form resulting from crosses between different species of the Globulea genus.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a velvety texture and a deep, rich purple coloration. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, exhibiting good drought tolerance once established.

This nothovar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
