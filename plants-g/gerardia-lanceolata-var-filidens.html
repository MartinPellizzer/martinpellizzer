Gerardia lanceolata var. filidens

Gerardia lanceolata var. filidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gerardia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
