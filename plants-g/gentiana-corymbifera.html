Gentiana corymbifera

Gentiana corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Gentiana corymbifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
