Gnaphalium germanicum var. lanuginosum

Gnaphalium germanicum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stolons that root at the nodes. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and sandy areas.
