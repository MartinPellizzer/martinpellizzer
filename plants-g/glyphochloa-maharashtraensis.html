Glyphochloa maharashtraensis

Glyphochloa maharashtraensis, commonly known as the Maharashtra grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in the western and central regions of India. This perennial grass typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
