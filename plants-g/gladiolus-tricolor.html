Gladiolus tricolor

Gladiolus tricolor, commonly known as the three-color gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence composed of three different colored florets arranged in a symmetrical pattern. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers bloom in summer and are popular in gardens for their vibrant colors and ornamental value.

The plant is a member of the Iridaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental plants like irises and crocuses.
