Gagea chlorantha var. cyprica

Gagea chlorantha var. cyprica, commonly known as the Cypric white bulbous plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bulbous base and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine and woodland habitats.
