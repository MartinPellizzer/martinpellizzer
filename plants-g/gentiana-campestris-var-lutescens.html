Gentiana campestris var. lutescens

Gentiana campestris var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, contrasting with its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers are tubular and bilaterally symmetrical, with five lobes that curve inward.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
