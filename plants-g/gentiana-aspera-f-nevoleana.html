Gentiana aspera f. nevoleana

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its lance-shaped, hairy leaves that help it withstand cold and arid conditions. This subspecies is typically found in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils.
