Gonocaryum impressinervium

Gonocaryum impressinervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominently impressed veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
