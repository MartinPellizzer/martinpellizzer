Grammitis colombiensis

Grammitis colombiensis, commonly known as the Colombian climbing fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on trees or rocky surfaces in rainforests. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Grammitis colombiensis plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing microhabitats for various small organisms.
