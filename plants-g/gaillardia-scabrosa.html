Gaillardia scabrosa

Gaillardia scabrosa, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its foliage is covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Gaillardia scabrosa is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
