Gleichenia linearis f. crenulata

Gleichenia linearis f. crenulata, commonly known as the serrated cliff fern, is a fern species belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its linear, strap-like fronds that are often serrated or crenulate, giving it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops or in moist, shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
