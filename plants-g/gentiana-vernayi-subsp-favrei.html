Gentiana vernayi subsp. favrei

Gentiana vernayi subsp.

favrei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific moisture and light requirements.
