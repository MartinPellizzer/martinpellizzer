Gladiolus carneus

Gladiolus carneus, commonly known as the pink gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The flowers are typically arranged in a spike and feature three petals and three sepals, with a distinctive central structure known as the column. This species is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for summer floral displays.
