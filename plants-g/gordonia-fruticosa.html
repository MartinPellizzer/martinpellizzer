Gordonia fruticosa

Gordonia fruticosa, commonly known as the African blueberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Gordonia fruticosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
