Gnidia sisparensis

Gnidia sisparensis, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a South African plant belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits.

Gnidia sisparensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
