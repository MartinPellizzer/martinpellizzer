Guatteria villosissima

Guatteria villosissima, commonly known as the hairy guatteria, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its hairy texture, from which it gets its common name, is a distinctive botanical feature.
