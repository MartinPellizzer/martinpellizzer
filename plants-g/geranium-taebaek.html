Geranium taebaek

Geranium taebaek, commonly known as the Taebaek geranium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Geranium taebaek is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
