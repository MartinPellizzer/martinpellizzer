Grevillea acrobotrya

Grevillea acrobotrya, commonly known as the red-stemmed grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Grevillea acrobotrya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
