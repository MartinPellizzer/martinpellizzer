Gossypium peruvianum var. caravonicum

Gossypium peruvianum var. caravonicum, commonly known as the Peruvian cotton plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fibrous, fluffy seed hairs that are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than other cotton species but holds historical and botanical significance in the region.
