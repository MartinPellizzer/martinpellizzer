Gayophytum humile var. gracile

Gayophytum humile var. gracile, commonly known as the slender gayophytum, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rockeries and coastal landscapes.
