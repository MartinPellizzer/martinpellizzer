Gossypium bricchettii

Gossypium bricchettii, commonly known as the Bricchett's cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. This plant is characterized by its soft, fluffy seed pods that contain fibrous material, which is similar to cotton but less commercially significant. It typically grows in dry, arid environments and has adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant's botanical features include lobed leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring.
