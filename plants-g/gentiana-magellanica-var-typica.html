Gentiana magellanica var. typica

Gentiana magellanica var. typica, commonly known as the Magellan gentian, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
