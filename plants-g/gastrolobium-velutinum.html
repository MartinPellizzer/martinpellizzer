Gastrolobium velutinum

Gastrolobium velutinum, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes.

The silky wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
