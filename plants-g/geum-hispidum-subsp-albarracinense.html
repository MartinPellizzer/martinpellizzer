Geum hispidum subsp. albarracinense

Geum hispidum subsp.

albarracinense, commonly known as the white-haired avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are hairy and heart-shaped, with a distinctive white or pale green coloration.

The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
