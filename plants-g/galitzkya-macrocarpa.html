Galitzkya macrocarpa

Galitzkya macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant kohlrabi, is a striking member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, swollen, bulbous stems that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces white or pale purple flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Native to South America, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible, nutrient-rich stems.
