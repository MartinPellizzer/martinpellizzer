Gurania capitata var. pallida

Gurania capitata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale-headed gurania, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It thrives in high-altitude, rocky environments and is adapted to cool, arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
