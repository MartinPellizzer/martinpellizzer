Glossorhyncha latipetala

Glossorhyncha latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip petal. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The orchid's reproductive strategy involves specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its habitat.
