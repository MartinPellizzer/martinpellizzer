Grona barbata var. barbata

Grona barbata var. barbata, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
