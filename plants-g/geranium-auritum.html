Geranium auritum

Geranium auritum, commonly known as the earleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium auritum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
