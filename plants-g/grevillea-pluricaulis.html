Grevillea pluricaulis

Grevillea pluricaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed waratah, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea pluricaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native birds and insects.
