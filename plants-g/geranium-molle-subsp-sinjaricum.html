Geranium molle subsp. sinjaricum

Geranium molle subsp.

sinjaricum, commonly known as the Sinjar geranium, is a species native to the Middle East and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers.

The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
