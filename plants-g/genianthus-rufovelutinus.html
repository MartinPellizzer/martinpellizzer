Genianthus rufovelutinus

Genianthus rufovelutinus, commonly known as the red velvet bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
