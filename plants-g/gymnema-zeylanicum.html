Gymnema zeylanicum

Gymnema zeylanicum, commonly known as the "sugar plant," is a climbing shrub native to India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to reduce sugar absorption in the intestines, making it useful in managing diabetes. Its leaves contain gymnemic acids, which have been studied for their potential anti-diabetic and antioxidant properties.

Gymnema zeylanicum is also valued in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its various health benefits.
