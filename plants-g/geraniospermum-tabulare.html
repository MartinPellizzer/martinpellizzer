Geraniospermum tabulare

Geraniospermum tabulare, commonly known as the table geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Geraniospermum tabulare is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
