Graptopetalum paraguayense

Graptopetalum paraguayense, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is a compact, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
