Gomesa pectoralis

Gomesa pectoralis, commonly known as the chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree's fruit is a spiny capsule containing edible seeds, often referred to as chestnuts. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Gomesa pectoralis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in local ecosystems.
