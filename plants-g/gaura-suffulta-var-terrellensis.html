Gaura suffulta var. terrellensis

Gaura suffulta var. terrellensis, commonly known as the Texas gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in gardens for its graceful appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
