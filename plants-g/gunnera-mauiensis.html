Gunnera mauiensis

Gunnera mauiensis, commonly known as the Maui giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the volcanic soils of Maui, Hawaii.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the Gunneraceae family and is known for its unique morphology, with a short, thick stem and a rosette of leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its size and distinctive appearance, it is a notable feature of the local flora and an important component of Hawaii's ecological landscape.
