Geranium stramineum

Geranium stramineum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has opposite, serrated leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Geranium stramineum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
