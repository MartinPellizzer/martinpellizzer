Gentiana parviflora

Gentiana parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana parviflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
