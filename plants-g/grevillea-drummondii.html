Grevillea drummondii

Grevillea drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's waratah, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, crimson-red flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters, typically during spring and summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Grevillea drummondii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
