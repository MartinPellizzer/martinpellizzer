Gladiolus intonsus

Gladiolus intonsus, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Gladiolus intonsus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
