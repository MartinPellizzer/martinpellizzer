Grenacheria montana

Grenacheria montana, commonly known as the mountain grenache, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it well adapted to rocky and mountainous environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are leathery and thick, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Grenacheria montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging growing conditions.
