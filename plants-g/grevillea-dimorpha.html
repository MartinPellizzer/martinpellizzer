Grevillea dimorpha

Grevillea dimorpha, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Grevillea dimorpha is also an important nectar source for native pollinators.
