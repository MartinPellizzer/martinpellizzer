Galium maximum

Galium maximum, commonly known as great cleavers, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium maximum is typically found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.
