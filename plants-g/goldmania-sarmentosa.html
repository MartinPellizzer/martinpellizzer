Goldmania sarmentosa

Goldmania sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping goldcup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
