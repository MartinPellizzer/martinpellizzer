Grammosciadium coloratum

Grammosciadium coloratum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its slender, pink-tinged stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months.

Grammosciadium coloratum is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
