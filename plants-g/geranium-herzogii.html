Geranium herzogii

Geranium herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of vibrant pink or red blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in both garden and horticultural settings.
