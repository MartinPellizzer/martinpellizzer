Gladiolus floribundus subsp. rudis

Gladiolus floribundus subsp.

rudis, commonly known as the rough gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for flower beds and borders.
