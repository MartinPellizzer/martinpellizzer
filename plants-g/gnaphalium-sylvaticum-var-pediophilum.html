Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. pediophilum

Gnaphalium sylvaticum var. pediophilum, commonly known as the sticky willow or pediophilum ragwort, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, with a soft, woolly texture.

It produces a distinctive, aromatic scent, which may aid in attracting pollinators.
