Gentiana fratris

Gentiana fratris, commonly known as the brother's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Gentiana fratris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
