Galarhoeus pekinensis subsp. subulatifolius

Galarhoeus pekinensis subsp.

subulatifolius, commonly known as the Peking subshrub, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of pink or white, adding ornamental value.
