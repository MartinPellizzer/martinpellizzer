Gerascanthus fulvosus

Gerascanthus fulvosus, commonly known as the golden sun cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with a waxy, pale yellow to orange hue, which helps it reflect intense sunlight. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and aiding in survival in harsh environments.

Gerascanthus fulvosus is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
