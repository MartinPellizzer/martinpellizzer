Gentiana atkinsonii var. formosana

Gentiana atkinsonii var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan gentian, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
