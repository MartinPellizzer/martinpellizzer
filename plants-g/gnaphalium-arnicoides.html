Gnaphalium arnicoides

Gnaphalium arnicoides, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of tiny yellow flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. The plant produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Its structure and growth habits make it a common component of various ecological communities.
