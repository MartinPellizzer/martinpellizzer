Gunnera atropurpurea var. munchicana

Gunnera atropurpurea var. munchicana, commonly known as the purple giant shield fern, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to three feet in diameter. The foliage is typically a deep purple or bronze color, with a velvety texture that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This variety is prized for its dramatic appearance and is a popular choice in specialty gardens and water gardens.
