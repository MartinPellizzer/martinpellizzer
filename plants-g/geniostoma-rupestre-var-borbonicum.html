Geniostoma rupestre var. borbonicum

Geniostoma rupestre var. borbonicum, commonly known as the Bourbon rockrose, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and grayish-green, adapted to withstand arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
