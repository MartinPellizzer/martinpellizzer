Glycine fortunei

Glycine fortunei, commonly known as the Fortune's climbing bean, is a vigorous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Glycine fortunei is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
