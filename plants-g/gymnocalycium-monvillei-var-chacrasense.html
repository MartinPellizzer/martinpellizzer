Gymnocalycium monvillei var. chacrasense

Gymnocalycium monvillei var. chacrasense, commonly known as the Chacrasense moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a low-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive swollen base and a crown of spiny, fleshy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for storing water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
