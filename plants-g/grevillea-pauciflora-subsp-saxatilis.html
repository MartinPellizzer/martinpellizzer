Grevillea pauciflora subsp. saxatilis

Grevillea pauciflora subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock grevillea, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that appear in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

This subspecies is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
