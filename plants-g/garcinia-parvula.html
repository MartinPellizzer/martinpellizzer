Garcinia parvula

Garcinia parvula, commonly known as the dwarf garcinia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 5 meters and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds. Garcinia parvula is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and potential medicinal contexts.
