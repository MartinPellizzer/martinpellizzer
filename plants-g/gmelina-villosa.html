Gmelina villosa

Gmelina villosa, commonly known as the silky ash or silky gmelina, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Gmelina villosa is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of charcoal.
