Gleditsia horrida subsp. delavayi

Gleditsia horrida subsp.

delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China. It is characterized by its thorny branches and compound leaves that are typically 5 to 9 pairs of oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
