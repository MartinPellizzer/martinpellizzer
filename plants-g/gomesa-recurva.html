Gomesa recurva

Gomesa recurva, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems that form a distinctive zigzag pattern as it grows. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Gomesa recurva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
