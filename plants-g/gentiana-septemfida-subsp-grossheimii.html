Gentiana septemfida subsp. grossheimii

Gentiana septemfida subsp.

grossheimii, commonly known as the seven-leaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
