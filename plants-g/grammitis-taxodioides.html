Grammitis taxodioides

Grammitis taxodioides, commonly known as the dragon's blood fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically green or reddish in color. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Grammitis taxodioides is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
