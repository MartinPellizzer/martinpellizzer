Gomphostemma cinereum

Gomphostemma cinereum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Gomphostemma cinereum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
