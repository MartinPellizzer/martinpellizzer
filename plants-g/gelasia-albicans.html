Gelasia albicans

Gelasia albicans, commonly known as the white gelasia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the spring and early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Gelasia albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
