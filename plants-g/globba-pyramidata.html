Globba pyramidata

Globba pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
