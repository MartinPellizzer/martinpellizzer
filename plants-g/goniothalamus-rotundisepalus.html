Goniothalamus rotundisepalus

Goniothalamus rotundisepalus, commonly known as the round-sepaled goniothalamus, is a member of the Annonaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The flowers are typically clustered in umbels and are often fragrant, with a distinctive structure that includes rounded sepals. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest environments.
