Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. cumbrecitense

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

cumbrecitense, commonly known as the Cumbrecitense prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense clusters of spines.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles, and are often followed by edible, red to orange fruits. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing typical succulent features for water storage.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
