Gordonia alpestris

Gordonia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Gordonia alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
