Gypsophila trichotoma

Gypsophila trichotoma, commonly known as the desert sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, delicate pink or white flowers. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Gypsophila trichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
