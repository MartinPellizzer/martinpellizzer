Gypsophila perfoliata var. anatolica

Gypsophila perfoliata var. anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian baby's breath, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, branching stems that often appear to be covered in small, leaf-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
