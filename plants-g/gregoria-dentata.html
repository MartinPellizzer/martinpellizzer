Gregoria dentata

Gregoria dentata, commonly known as the toothed gregoria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.

Gregoria dentata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
