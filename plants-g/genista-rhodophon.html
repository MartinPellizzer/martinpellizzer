Genista rhodophon

Genista rhodophon, commonly known as the red-flowered broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange-yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Genista rhodophon is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
