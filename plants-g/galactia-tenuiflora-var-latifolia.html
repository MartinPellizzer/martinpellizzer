Galactia tenuiflora var. latifolia

Galactia tenuiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, elliptical leaflets and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Galactia tenuiflora var. latifolia is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.
