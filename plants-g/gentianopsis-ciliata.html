Gentianopsis ciliata

Gentianopsis ciliata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are characterized by their striking blue color and five lobed petals.

Gentianopsis ciliata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
