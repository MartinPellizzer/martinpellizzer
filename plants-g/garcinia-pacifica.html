Garcinia pacifica

Garcinia pacifica, commonly known as the Hawaiian Garcinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, orange-red fruits. The fruit has a thick, waxy skin and contains a single large seed.

Garcinia pacifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some Pacific cultures.
