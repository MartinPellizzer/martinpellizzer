Gaertnera pendula

Gaertnera pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from clusters of stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Gaertnera pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
