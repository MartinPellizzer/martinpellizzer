Gaultheria myrtilloides

Gaultheria myrtilloides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing ornamental value in gardens.

Gaultheria myrtilloides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
