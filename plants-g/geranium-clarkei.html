Geranium clarkei

Geranium clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Geranium clarkei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
