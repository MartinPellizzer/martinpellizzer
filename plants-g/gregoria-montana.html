Gregoria montana

Gregoria montana, commonly known as the mountain gregonia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Gregoria montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
