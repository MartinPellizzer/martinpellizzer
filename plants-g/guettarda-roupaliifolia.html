Guettarda roupaliifolia

Guettarda roupaliifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf guettarda, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or whitish wax, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its adaptability to coastal and sandy soils makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
