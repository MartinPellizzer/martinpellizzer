Gypsophila fastigiata subsp. fastigiata

Gypsophila fastigiata subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the upright gypsophila, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, upright stems and small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for both garden displays and floral arrangements.
