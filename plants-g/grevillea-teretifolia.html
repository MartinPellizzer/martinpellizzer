Grevillea teretifolia

Grevillea teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It features vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea teretifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
