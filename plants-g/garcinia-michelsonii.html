Garcinia michelsonii

Garcinia michelsonii, commonly known as the Malay bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that contain a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
