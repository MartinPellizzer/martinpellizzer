Gymnosperma eriocarpa

Gymnosperma eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly gymnosperma, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy or woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which provides protection against environmental stressors. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in a cone-like structure, a feature typical of gymnosperms, though it is actually an angiosperm.

This plant is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
