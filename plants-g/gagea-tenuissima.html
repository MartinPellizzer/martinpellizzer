Gagea tenuissima

Gagea tenuissima, commonly known as the slender gagea, is a small, bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a single, narrow, tubular flower that is often adorned with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or meadows.

Gagea tenuissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
