Gasteria disticha var. disticha

Gasteria disticha var. disticha, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings. The plant produces tall, flowering stems with small, tubular flowers that are often white or pale green. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
