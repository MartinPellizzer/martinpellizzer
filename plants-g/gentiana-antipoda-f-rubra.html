Gentiana antipoda f. rubra

Gentiana antipoda f. rubra, commonly known as the red gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking deep red to purple flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and arranged in dense clusters. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
