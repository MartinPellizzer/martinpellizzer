Gunnera rheifolia

Gunnera rheifolia, commonly known as the Andean giant waterwheel, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Gunnera, which is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach several feet in diameter. The plant thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against intense sunlight.

Gunnera rheifolia is also notable for its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months.
