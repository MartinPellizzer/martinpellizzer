Gossypium leoninum

Gossypium leoninum, commonly known as the lion's head cotton, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that resemble a lion's head, hence its common name. It produces fluffy, white fibers similar to cotton, though it is not typically cultivated for textile use.

Gossypium leoninum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
