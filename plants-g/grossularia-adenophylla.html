Grossularia adenophylla

Grossularia adenophylla, commonly known as the Himalayan snowberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, fleshy berries that are edible. The plant thrives in cold, mountainous environments and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
