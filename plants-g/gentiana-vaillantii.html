Gentiana vaillantii

Gentiana vaillantii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is upright and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with a distinctive blue corolla, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
