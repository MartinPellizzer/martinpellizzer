Glycyrrhiza vulgaris

Glycyrrhiza vulgaris, commonly known as licorice, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used medicinally and commercially, containing compounds like glycyrrhizin.

Glycyrrhiza vulgaris is widely cultivated for its sweet flavor and various pharmaceutical applications.
