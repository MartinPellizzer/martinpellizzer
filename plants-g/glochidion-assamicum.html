Glochidion assamicum

Glochidion assamicum, commonly known as the Assam glabrous hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy or glabrous depending on the variety. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Glochidion assamicum is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
