Genista pomeliana

Genista pomeliana, commonly known as the Pomelina broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its shrub-like growth habit includes slender, woody stems with small, leathery leaves.

Genista pomeliana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
