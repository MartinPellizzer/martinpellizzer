Geranium palmeri

Geranium palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive calyx, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Geranium palmeri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and drought tolerance.
