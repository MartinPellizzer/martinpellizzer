Gentiana laevigata

Gentiana laevigata, commonly known as the smooth gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The smooth gentian is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
