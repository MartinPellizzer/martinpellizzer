Grevillea cyranostigma

Grevillea cyranostigma, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a flowering plant native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Grevillea cyranostigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
