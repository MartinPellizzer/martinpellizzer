Gymnocalycium horridispinum var. achirasense

Gymnocalycium horridispinum var. achirasense, commonly known as the Achiras Desert Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles, which provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation programs.
