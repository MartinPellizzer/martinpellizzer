Gladiolus atroviolaceus

Gladiolus atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, dark violet to almost black flowers with a rich, velvety texture. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, with each bloom having three sepals that are often more prominent than the petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
