Gardneria oblonga

Gardneria oblonga, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow or orange throat. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces fibrous, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Gardneria oblonga is also valued for its cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions.
