Geum albiflorum

Geum albiflorum, commonly known as white avens, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, hairy texture, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Geum albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
