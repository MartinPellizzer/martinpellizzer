Galium triflorum var. viridiflorum

Galium triflorum var. viridiflorum, commonly known as greenflower bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
