Globba secunda

Globba secunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf globba, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Globba secunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
