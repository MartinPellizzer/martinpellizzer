Gomphocentrum sacculatum

Gomphocentrum sacculatum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its vibrant blooms.
