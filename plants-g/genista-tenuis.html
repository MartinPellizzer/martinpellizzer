Genista tenuis

Genista tenuis, commonly known as the slender broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring, followed by narrow, pod-like seed capsules.

Its foliage consists of small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
