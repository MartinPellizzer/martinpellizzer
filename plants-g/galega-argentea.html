Galega argentea

Galega argentea, commonly known as silver goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
