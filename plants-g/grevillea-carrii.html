Grevillea carrii

Grevillea carrii, commonly known as the Western Australian grevillea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It features striking, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea carrii is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.
