Gentiana yakumontana f. viridis

Gentiana yakumontana f. viridis, commonly known as the Green gentian, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its vivid blue and violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its foliage is characterized by dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are renowned for their deep blue color and tubular shape, making them a favorite among botanists and horticulturists.
