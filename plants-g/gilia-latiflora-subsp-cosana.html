Gilia latiflora subsp. cosana

Gilia latiflora subsp.

cosana, commonly known as the broadleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping and native gardens. Gilia latiflora subsp.

cosana plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
