Gagea ova

Gagea ova, commonly known as the ovate gagea, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces rounded, egg-shaped bulbs that are typically covered with a thin, papery sheath. Native to the western United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Gagea ova is often found in forest understories and is valued for its delicate floral display and ecological significance.
