Gmelina finlaysoniana

Gmelina finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Philippine ash or white ash, is a member of the Oleaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for reforestation and landscape purposes.
