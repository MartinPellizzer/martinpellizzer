Glyceria lithuanica subsp. orientalis

Glyceria lithuanica subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, fens, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, moist soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
