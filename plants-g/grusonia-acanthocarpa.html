Grusonia acanthocarpa

Grusonia acanthocarpa, commonly known as the spiny-fruited grusonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by distinctive, spiny, orange-red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Grusonia acanthocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
