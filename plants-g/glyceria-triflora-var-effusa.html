Glyceria triflora var. effusa

Glyceria triflora var. effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf watergrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its adaptability to varying water levels contributes to its ecological significance in floodplain and marsh habitats.
