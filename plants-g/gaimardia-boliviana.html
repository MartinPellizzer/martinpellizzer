Gaimardia boliviana

Gaimardia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, pointed segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Gaimardia boliviana plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
