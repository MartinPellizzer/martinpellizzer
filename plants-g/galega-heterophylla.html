Galega heterophylla

Galega heterophylla, commonly known as the variable goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, elongated legumes that contain the seeds.

Galega heterophylla is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
