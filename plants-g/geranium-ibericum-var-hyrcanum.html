Geranium ibericum var. hyrcanum

Geranium ibericum var. hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian geranium, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
