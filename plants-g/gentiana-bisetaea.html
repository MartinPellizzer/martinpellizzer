Gentiana bisetaea

Gentiana bisetaea, commonly known as the Sierra Madre gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The Sierra Madre gentian is an important species for pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
