Gynopogon flavescens

Gynopogon flavescens, commonly known as the yellow false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale yellow to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
