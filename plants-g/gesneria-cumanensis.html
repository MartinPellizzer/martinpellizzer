Gesneria cumanensis

Gesneria cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanensis Gesneria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, meaning it thrives in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, bloom during the warmer months and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Gesneria cumanensis is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
