Gentiana kauffmanniana subsp. affghanica

Gentiana kauffmanniana subsp.

affghanica, commonly known as the Afghan gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
