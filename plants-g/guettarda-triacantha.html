Guettarda triacantha

Guettarda triacantha, commonly known as the thorned guettarda, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by bright orange or red berries. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical environments.
