Gomesa chrysostoma

Gomesa chrysostoma, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
