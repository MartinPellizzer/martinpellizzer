Gentianodes longipetiolata

Gentianodes longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled gentian, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features elongated petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in its identification. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentianodes longipetiolata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
