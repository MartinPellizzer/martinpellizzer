Garcinia disepala

Garcinia disepala, commonly known as the two-lobed garcinia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries that contain a single seed. Garcinia disepala belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is valued for its ornamental potential and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
