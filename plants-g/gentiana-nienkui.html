Gentiana nienkui

Gentiana nienkui, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
