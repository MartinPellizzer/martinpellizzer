Genista purgans var. brachycarpa

Genista purgans var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
