Gouldia terminalis f. sambucina

Gouldia terminalis f. sambucina, commonly known as the purple fountain grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to South Africa.

It features tall, slender culms with dense, feathery inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple and pink, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal landscapes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silvery-blue hue that contrasts beautifully with its vibrant flower spikes.

This cultivar is prized for its architectural form and seasonal color display, often used in mixed borders and as a focal point in garden design.
