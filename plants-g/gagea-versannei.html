Gagea versannei

Gagea versannei, commonly known as the Versanne's gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces a single bulb that serves as its primary storage organ, allowing it to survive in harsh, alpine environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
