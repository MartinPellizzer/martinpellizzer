Garnotia spadicea

Garnotia spadicea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Garnotia spadicea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
