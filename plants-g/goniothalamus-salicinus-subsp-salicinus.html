Goniothalamus salicinus subsp. salicinus

Goniothalamus salicinus subsp.

salicinus, commonly known as the slender yew, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, orange-red drupes that contain a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
