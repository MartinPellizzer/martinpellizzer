Geranium pseudofarreri

Geranium pseudofarreri, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
