Glaucium calycinum subsp. calycinum

Glaucium calycinum subsp.

calycinum, commonly known as the Turk's Cap or Persian Buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features striking blue to purple flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in early summer.

The plant has distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique appearance and long blooming period.
