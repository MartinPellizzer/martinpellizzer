Grewia stuhlmannii

Grewia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the red gum tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Grewia stuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
