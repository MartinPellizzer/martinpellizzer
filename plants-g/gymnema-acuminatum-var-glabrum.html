Gymnema acuminatum var. glabrum

Gymnema acuminatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth gymnema, is a climbing plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing tiny seeds. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is known for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the digestive system.

This species is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
