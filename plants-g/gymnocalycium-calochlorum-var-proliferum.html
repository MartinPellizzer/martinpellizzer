Gymnocalycium calochlorum var. proliferum

Gymnocalycium calochlorum var. proliferum, commonly known as the clustered moon cactus, is a succulent native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, red fruits. Its stems often branch out to form dense clusters, giving it its common name.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
