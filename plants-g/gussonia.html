Gussonia

Gussonia, commonly known as the silverweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of silver-gray leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. They are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in arid and semi-arid environments. Gussonia species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive foliage and resilience to drought.

The plants have a short growing season and are typically perennial, with some species thriving in rocky or sandy soils.
