Glycyrrhiza purpureiflora

Glycyrrhiza purpureiflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered licorice, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a thick, fleshy root that is rich in glycyrrhizin, a compound known for its sweet taste and medicinal properties. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and hairy.

This plant is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
