Geranium subcaulescens var. obtusilobum

Geranium subcaulescens var. obtusilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with rounded lobes, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
