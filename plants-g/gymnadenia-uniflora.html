Gymnadenia uniflora

Gymnadenia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is a distinguishing feature.

Gymnadenia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
