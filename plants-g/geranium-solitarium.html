Geranium solitarium

Geranium solitarium, commonly known as the solitary geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Geranium solitarium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
