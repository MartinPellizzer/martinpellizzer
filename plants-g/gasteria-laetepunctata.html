Gasteria laetepunctata

Gasteria laetepunctata, commonly known as the "Beaded Gasteria," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for succulent gardens.

Gasteria laetepunctata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid conditions.
