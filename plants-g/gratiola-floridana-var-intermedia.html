Gratiola floridana var. intermedia

Gratiola floridana var. intermedia, commonly known as the Florida grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and dry fields. It has a low-growing habit with creeping stems and produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
