Galium breviramosum

Galium breviramosum, commonly known as short-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium breviramosum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
