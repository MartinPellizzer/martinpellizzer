Gentiana marcowiczii

Gentiana marcowiczii, commonly known as the Marcowicz's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana marcowiczii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
