Gnaphalium leucocephalum

Gnaphalium leucocephalum, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
