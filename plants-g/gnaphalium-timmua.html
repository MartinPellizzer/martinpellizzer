Gnaphalium timmua

Gnaphalium timmua, commonly known as the silverleaf false heather, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
