Gonystylus acuminatus

Gonystylus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
