Gentiana aprica

Gentiana aprica, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Gentiana aprica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid color and resilience in cooler climates.
