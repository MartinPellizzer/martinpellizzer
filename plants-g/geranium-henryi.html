Geranium henryi

Geranium henryi, commonly known as Henry's geranium, is a species native to China.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and produces vibrant pink to red blooms. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Geranium henryi is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
