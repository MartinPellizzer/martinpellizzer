Gentiana poculifera

Gentiana poculifera, commonly known as the spoonleaf gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Gentiana poculifera thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
