Gymnogramma tartarea var. aurata

Gymnogramma tartarea var. aurata, commonly known as the golden gymnogramma, is a varietal form of the fern Gymnogramma tartarea.

It is characterized by its striking golden to yellow-green fronds, which contrast with the typical green coloration of the species. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and the Philippines. Its fronds are pinnate, with small, lanceolate pinnae that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative fern in shaded gardens and indoor settings.
