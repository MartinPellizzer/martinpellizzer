Gladiolus ukambanensis var. alatus

Gladiolus ukambanensis var. alatus, commonly known as the winged gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are broad and sword-shaped, contributing to its dramatic appearance. This variety is notable for its distinctive wing-like structures on the petals, which set it apart from other gladiolus species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
