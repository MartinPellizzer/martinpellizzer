Gnidia tenella var. elatior

Gnidia tenella var. elatior, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
