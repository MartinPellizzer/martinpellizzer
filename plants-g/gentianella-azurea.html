Gentianella azurea

Gentianella azurea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
