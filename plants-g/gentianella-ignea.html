Gentianella ignea

Gentianella ignea, commonly known as the fire gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.
