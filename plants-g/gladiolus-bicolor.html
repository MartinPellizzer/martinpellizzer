Gladiolus bicolor

Gladiolus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It features tall, sword-like leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in a variety of colors, including red, white, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with its flowers displaying a unique bicolor pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.
