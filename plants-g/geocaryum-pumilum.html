Geocaryum pumilum

Geocaryum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf geocaryum, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or white, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Geocaryum pumilum is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
