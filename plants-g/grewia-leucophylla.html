Grewia leucophylla

Grewia leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed grewia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Grewia leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
