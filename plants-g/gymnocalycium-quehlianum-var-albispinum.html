Gymnocalycium quehlianum var. albispinum

Gymnocalycium quehlianum var. albispinum, commonly known as the white-spined moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale spines. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, red fruits. It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
