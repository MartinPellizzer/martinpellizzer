Geranium villarsianum

Geranium villarsianum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Geranium villarsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
