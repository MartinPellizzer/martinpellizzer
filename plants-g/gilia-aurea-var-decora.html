Gilia aurea var. decora

Gilia aurea var. decora, commonly known as the golden gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
