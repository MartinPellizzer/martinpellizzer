Gentiana lewisiae

Gentiana lewisiae, commonly known as the Sierra gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Gentiana lewisiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
