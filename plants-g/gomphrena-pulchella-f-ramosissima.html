Gomphrena pulchella f. ramosissima

Gomphrena pulchella f. ramosissima, commonly known as the pink plume or red plume, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and showy, feathery inflorescences that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
