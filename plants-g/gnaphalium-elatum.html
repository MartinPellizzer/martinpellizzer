Gnaphalium elatum

Gnaphalium elatum, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flower heads that produce yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and it is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
