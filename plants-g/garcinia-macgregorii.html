Garcinia macgregorii

Garcinia macgregorii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a yellowish, fleshy drupe that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
