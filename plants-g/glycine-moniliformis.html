Glycine moniliformis

Glycine moniliformis, commonly known as the silverleaf bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Glycine moniliformis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

It is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a source of protein.
