Genista scolopendria

Genista scolopendria, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in Mediterranean climates, characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves made up of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a common ornamental plant in gardens.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and birds.
