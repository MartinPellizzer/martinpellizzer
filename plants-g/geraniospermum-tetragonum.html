Geraniospermum tetragonum

Geraniospermum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
