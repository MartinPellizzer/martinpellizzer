Gomphrena perennis var. subalpina

Gomphrena perennis var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, spiky, pink to red bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in cooler climates, contributing to its popularity among gardeners in temperate zones.
