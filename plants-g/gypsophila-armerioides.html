Gypsophila armerioides

Gypsophila armerioides, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

Its name "sweet everlasting" refers to its long-lasting flowers and the faintly sweet scent they emit.
