Galium vernum var. wirtgenii

Galium vernum var. wirtgenii, commonly known as the early wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, often hairy stems that arise from a rhizomatous root system. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Galium vernum var. wirtgenii thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
