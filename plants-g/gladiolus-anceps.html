Gladiolus anceps

Gladiolus anceps, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, with the outermost blooms being the most prominent.

Gladiolus anceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
