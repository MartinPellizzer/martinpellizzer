Gypsophila szovitsii var. glabrata

Gypsophila szovitsii var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth baby's breath, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences composed of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its elegant appearance.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
