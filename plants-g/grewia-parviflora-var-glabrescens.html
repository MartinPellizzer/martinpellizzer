Grewia parviflora var. glabrescens

Grewia parviflora var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf wild pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and is well-suited to arid and semi-arid climates.
