Gentianella krauseana

Gentianella krauseana, commonly known as Krause's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
