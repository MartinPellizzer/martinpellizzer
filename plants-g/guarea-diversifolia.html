Guarea diversifolia

Guarea diversifolia, commonly known as the "Cocowood" or "Cocobolo," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
