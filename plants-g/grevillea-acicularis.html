Grevillea acicularis

Grevillea acicularis, commonly known as the needle-leaf grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Grevillea acicularis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
