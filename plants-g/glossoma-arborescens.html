Glossoma arborescens

Glossoma arborescens, commonly known as the tree-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy, glossy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Glossoma arborescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in the understory of rainforests.

Its unique botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
