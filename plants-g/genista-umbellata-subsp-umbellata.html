Genista umbellata subsp. umbellata

Genista umbellata subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the common broom, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. It is often found in sunny, well-drained soils, particularly in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
