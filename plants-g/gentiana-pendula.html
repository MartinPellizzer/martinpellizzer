Gentiana pendula

Gentiana pendula, commonly known as the hanging gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, branched stems that often hang down, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Gentiana pendula thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland areas.
