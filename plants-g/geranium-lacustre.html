Geranium lacustre

Geranium lacustre, commonly known as water geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Geranium lacustre thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as lakes and streams.
