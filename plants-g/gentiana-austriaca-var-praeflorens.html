Gentiana austriaca var. praeflorens

Gentiana austriaca var. praeflorens, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
