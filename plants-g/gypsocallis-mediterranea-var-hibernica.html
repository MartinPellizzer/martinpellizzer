Gypsocallis mediterranea var. hibernica

Gypsocallis mediterranea var. hibernica, commonly known as the Hibernian rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense, evergreen foliage. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
