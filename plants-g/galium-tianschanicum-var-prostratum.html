Galium tianschanicum var. prostratum

Galium tianschanicum var. prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.
