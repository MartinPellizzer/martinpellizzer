Gormania anomala

Gormania anomala, commonly known as the strange gromwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Gormania anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
