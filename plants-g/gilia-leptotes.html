Gilia leptotes

Gilia leptotes, commonly known as the showy gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves help it conserve water efficiently.

Gilia leptotes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
