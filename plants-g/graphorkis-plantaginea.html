Graphorkis plantaginea

Graphorkis plantaginea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a notable species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
