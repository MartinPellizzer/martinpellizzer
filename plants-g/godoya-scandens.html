Godoya scandens

Godoya scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems that allow it to climb and twine around supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a glossy appearance.

Godoya scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
