Grevillea aquifolium var. truncata

Grevillea aquifolium var. truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Grevillea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a distinctive reddish or brownish midrib. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering display.
