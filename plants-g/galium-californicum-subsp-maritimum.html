Galium californicum subsp. maritimum

Galium californicum subsp.

maritimum, commonly known as coastal bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to coastal areas of California. It features slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub and dune environments.
