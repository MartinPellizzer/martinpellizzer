Gomidesia palustris var. angustifolia

Gomidesia palustris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf swamp gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes and swampy areas. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity and its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
