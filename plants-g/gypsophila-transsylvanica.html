Gypsophila transsylvanica

Gypsophila transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Gypsophila transsylvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements and garden landscapes.
