Geissorhiza parva

Geissorhiza parva, commonly known as the small yellow rain lily, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tubular, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
