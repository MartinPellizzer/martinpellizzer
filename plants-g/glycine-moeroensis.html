Glycine moeroensis

Glycine moeroensis, commonly known as the Moeroensis soybean, is a leguminous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Glycine moeroensis is typically cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth habits make it a valuable crop in sustainable agricultural systems.
