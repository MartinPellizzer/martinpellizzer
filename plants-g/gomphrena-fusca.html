Gomphrena fusca

Gomphrena fusca, commonly known as the purple feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gomphrena fusca is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
