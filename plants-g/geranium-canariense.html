Geranium canariense

Geranium canariense, commonly known as the Canary geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Geranium canariense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
