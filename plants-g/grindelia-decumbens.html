Grindelia decumbens

Grindelia decumbens, commonly known as desert gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Grindelia decumbens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
