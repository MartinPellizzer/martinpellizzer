Galium kamtschaticum subsp. oreganum

Galium kamtschaticum subsp.

oreganum, commonly known as Oregon bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features slender, often hairy stems that lie close to the ground, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, oval to lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
