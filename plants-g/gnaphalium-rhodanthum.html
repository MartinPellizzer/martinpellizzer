Gnaphalium rhodanthum

Gnaphalium rhodanthum, commonly known as the pink everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
