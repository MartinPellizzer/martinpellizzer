Gutierrezia texana var. glutinosa

Gutierrezia texana var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
