Geranium macrorrhizum var. microrhizon

Geranium macrorrhizum var. microrhizon, commonly known as the small-rooted geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental gardening and ecological habitats.
