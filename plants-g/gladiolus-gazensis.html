Gladiolus gazensis

Gladiolus gazensis, commonly known as the Gazenga gladiolus, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, showy flowers in a variety of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant produces sword-shaped leaves and has a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warm seasons, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Gladiolus gazensis is also valued for its resilience and ability to adapt to different climatic conditions.
