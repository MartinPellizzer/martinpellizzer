Gynura lutea

Gynura lutea, commonly known as the yellow gynura, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, dry fruits. Gynura lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
