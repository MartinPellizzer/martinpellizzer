Gelidocalamus longiinternodus

Gelidocalamus longiinternodus, commonly known as the long internode bamboo, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms with long internodes, which distinguish it from other bamboo species. The plant exhibits a fibrous root system and possesses a robust vascular structure that allows it to thrive in cold, mountainous environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green coloration.

This bamboo is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptations and potential horticultural value.
