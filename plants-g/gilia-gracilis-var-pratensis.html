Gilia gracilis var. pratensis

Gilia gracilis var. pratensis, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Miridae family and typically grows in open prairies and grasslands. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves are adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
