Gentiana pernettyoides

Gentiana pernettyoides, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana pernettyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
