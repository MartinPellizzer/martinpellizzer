Genista versicolor subsp. versicolor

Genista versicolor subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has needle-like leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its variability in flower color and leaf structure.
