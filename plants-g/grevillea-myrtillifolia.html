Grevillea myrtillifolia

Grevillea myrtillifolia, commonly known as the blueberry grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and full sun exposure.

Grevillea myrtillifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
