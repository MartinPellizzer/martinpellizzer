Gymnocalycium bruchii var. albispinum

Gymnocalycium bruchii var. albispinum, commonly known as the White-spined Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny tubercles, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
