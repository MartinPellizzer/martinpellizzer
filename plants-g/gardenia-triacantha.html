Gardenia triacantha

Gardenia triacantha, commonly known as the thorned gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant produces elliptical, yellowish-orange berries that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture.

Gardenia triacantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique botanical features.
