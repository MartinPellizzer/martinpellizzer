Gerascanthus dwyeri

Gerascanthus dwyeri, commonly known as the Dwyer's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Gerascanthus dwyeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
