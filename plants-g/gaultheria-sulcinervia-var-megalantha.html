Gaultheria sulcinervia var. megalantha

Gaultheria sulcinervia var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant red snowbell, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high altitudes.
