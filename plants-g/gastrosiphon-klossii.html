Gastrosiphon klossii

Gastrosiphon klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves and specialized structures called bladders, which it uses to trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its reproductive system involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its adaptability.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
