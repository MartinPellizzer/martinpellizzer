Gomidesia anacardiifolia

Gomidesia anacardiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble those of the cashew tree, hence its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
