Guajava persicifolia

Guajava persicifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters before the fruit develops.
