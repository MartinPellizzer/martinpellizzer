Grindelia squarrosa var. serrulata

Grindelia squarrosa var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, resinous, glandular leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.

Grindelia squarrosa var. serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
