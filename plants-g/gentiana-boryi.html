Gentiana boryi

Gentiana boryi, commonly known as the Bory's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or meadow environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine layer of down, while its stems are usually unbranched and upright.

Gentiana boryi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
