Garcinia urceolata

Garcinia urceolata, commonly known as the water apple or *Bhramara*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which resembles a small, round apple, has a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, acidic pulp. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
