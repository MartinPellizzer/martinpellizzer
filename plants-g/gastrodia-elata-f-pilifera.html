Gastrodia elata f. pilifera

Gastrodia elata f. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed tianma, is a rare variant of the Chinese tianma plant.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and distinctive hairy stems. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its botanical features include small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variant is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
