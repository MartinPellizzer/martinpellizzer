Geum × tirolense var. sudeticum

Geum × tirolense var. sudeticum, commonly known as the Sudetic avens, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
