Genista spartioides var. cossoniana

Genista spartioides var. cossoniana, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. It has small, leathery leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
