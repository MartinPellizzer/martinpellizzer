Gnetum giganteum

Gnetum giganteum, commonly known as the giant gnetum, is a unique gymnosperm native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure and the presence of specialized structures called phloem transfer cells. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with broad, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often found in humid, lowland areas and plays a role in local ecosystems.

Despite its rarity, it is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and adaptive features.
