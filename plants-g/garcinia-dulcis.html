Garcinia dulcis

Garcinia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree bears bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and contain bioactive compounds. Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.

Garcinia dulcis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
