Gigachilon × zhukovskyi

Gigachilon × zhukovskyi, commonly known as the hybrid giant sedge, is a unique plant resulting from the crossbreeding of two species within the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in height. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an uncommon and ecologically significant species.
