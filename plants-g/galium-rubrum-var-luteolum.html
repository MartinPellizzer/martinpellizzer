Galium rubrum var. luteolum

Galium rubrum var. luteolum, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, sprawling stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
