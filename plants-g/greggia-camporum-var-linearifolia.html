Greggia camporum var. linearifolia

Greggia camporum var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greggia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
