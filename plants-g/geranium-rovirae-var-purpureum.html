Geranium rovirae var. purpureum

Geranium rovirae var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaf geranium, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features attractive, lobed leaves that range from dark green to deep purple, depending on the light conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens around the world.
