Geranium nakaoanum

Geranium nakaoanum, commonly known as the Nakao's geranium, is a flowering plant native to the island of Niue in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Geranium nakaoanum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
