Gentianella austriaca subsp. fatrae

Gentianella austriaca subsp.

fatrae, commonly known as the Austrian gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
