Gaura oenotheriflora

Gaura oenotheriflora, commonly known as the everlasting gaura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its delicate appearance and long blooming period contribute to its appeal in native plant landscapes.
