Gaillardia lobata

Gaillardia lobata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright orange to red flowers with yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Gaillardia lobata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
