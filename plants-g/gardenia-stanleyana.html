Gardenia stanleyana

Gardenia stanleyana, commonly known as the Stanley gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the western Pacific region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and strong scent.

Gardenia stanleyana is relatively slow-growing and can reach heights of up to 6 feet in optimal conditions.
