Geranium irbyanum

Geranium irbyanum, commonly known as the Irby's geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a hairy texture.

Geranium irbyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
