Gasparinia

Gasparinia, commonly known as the false grass or gasparina, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in the Americas, with a range extending from the United States to South America. These plants are typically perennial and characterized by their tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Gasparinia species often thrive in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in their native ranges.
