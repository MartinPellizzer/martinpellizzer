Glaucothea aculeata

Glaucothea aculeata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant features spiny stems and produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.

The plant's ability to adapt to harsh environments makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
