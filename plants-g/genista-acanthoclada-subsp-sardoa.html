Genista acanthoclada subsp. sardoa

Genista acanthoclada subsp.

sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia. It features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, showcasing its resilience in arid environments. Its branching structure is dense and thorny, which helps in preventing herbivory.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
