Grevillea fasciculata var. typica

Grevillea fasciculata var. typica, commonly known as the golden grevillea, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and ability to withstand salty air make it a popular choice in horticulture.
