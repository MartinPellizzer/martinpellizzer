Genista dorycnifolia f. grosii

Genista dorycnifolia f. grosii, commonly known as the giant thorned broom, is a variant of the genista plant characterized by its robust growth and distinctive features.

This shrub is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is notable for its long, stiff, thorned stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
