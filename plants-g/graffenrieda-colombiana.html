Graffenrieda colombiana

Graffenrieda colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Graffenrieda colombiana thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
