Gomphrena demissa var. incana

Gomphrena demissa var. incana, commonly known as the silverleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, feathery, pink to purple bracts that resemble small flowers. The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, giving the plant its common name, and they are covered in fine, soft hairs. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist through the summer months.
