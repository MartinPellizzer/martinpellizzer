Galium rotundifolium subsp. ellipticum

Galium rotundifolium subsp.

ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its rounded, often reddish stems and trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common component of understory vegetation in temperate forests.
