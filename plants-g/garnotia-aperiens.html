Garnotia aperiens

Garnotia aperiens, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
