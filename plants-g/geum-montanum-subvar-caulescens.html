Geum montanum subvar. caulescens

Geum montanum subvar. caulescens, commonly known as the mountain avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, arranged in dense, upright clusters. Its stems are usually erect and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
