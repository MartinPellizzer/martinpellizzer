Geranium libanoticum

Geranium libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant pink or red flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Geranium libanoticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
