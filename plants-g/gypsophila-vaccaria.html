Gypsophila vaccaria

Gypsophila vaccaria, commonly known as baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fluffy flowers that resemble clouds, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

Gypsophila vaccaria is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
