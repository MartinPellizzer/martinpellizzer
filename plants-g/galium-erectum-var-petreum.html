Galium erectum var. petreum

Galium erectum var. petreum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium erectum var. petreum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
