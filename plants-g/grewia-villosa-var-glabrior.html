Grewia villosa var. glabrior

Grewia villosa var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky wild pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, orange-red berries. The leaves are typically ovate with a hairy surface, though the variety glabrior has a smoother texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
