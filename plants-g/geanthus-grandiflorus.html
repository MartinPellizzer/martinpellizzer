Geanthus grandiflorus

Geanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous geanthus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Geanthus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
