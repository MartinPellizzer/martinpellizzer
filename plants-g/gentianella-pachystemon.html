Gentianella pachystemon

Gentianella pachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which is known for its showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, with five petals and prominent yellow spots.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
