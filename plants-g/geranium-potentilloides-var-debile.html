Geranium potentilloides var. debile

Geranium potentilloides var. debile, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
