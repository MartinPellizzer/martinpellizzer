Gaylussacia reticulata

Gaylussacia reticulata, commonly known as the roughleaf huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, acidic berries. The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in local foraging and traditional practices.
