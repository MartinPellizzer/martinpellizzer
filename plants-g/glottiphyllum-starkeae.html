Glottiphyllum starkeae

Glottiphyllum starkeae, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in the unique microclimate of the Cape Peninsula. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Glottiphyllum starkeae is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to coastal conditions.
