Gymnocalycium pugionacanthum

Gymnocalycium pugionacanthum, commonly known as the spiny moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually ephemeral, appearing only during periods of adequate rainfall.

Gymnocalycium pugionacanthum is a drought-tolerant plant adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
