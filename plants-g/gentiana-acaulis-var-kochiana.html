Gentiana acaulis var. kochiana

Gentiana acaulis var. kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's gentian, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is herbaceous, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
