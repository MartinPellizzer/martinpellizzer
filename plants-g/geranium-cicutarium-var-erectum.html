Geranium cicutarium var. erectum

Geranium cicutarium var. erectum, commonly known as the upright cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
