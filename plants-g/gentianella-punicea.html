Gentianella punicea

Gentianella punicea, commonly known as the pink gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Gentianella punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
