Gentiana burmensis

Gentiana burmensis, commonly known as the Burmese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branched.

Gentiana burmensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
