Garrya laurifolia subsp. laurifolia

Garrya laurifolia subsp.

laurifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, waxy hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in woodland gardens.
