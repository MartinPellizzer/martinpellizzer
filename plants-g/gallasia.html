Gallasia

Gallasia, commonly known as the Gallasia tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to certain regions of Africa and is characterized by its deciduous nature, shedding its leaves during the dry season. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters. Gallasia species are often found in savanna and woodland habitats, adapting to seasonal changes in rainfall.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential for use in traditional medicine.
