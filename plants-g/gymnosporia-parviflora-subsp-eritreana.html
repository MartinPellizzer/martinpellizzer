Gymnosporia parviflora subsp. eritreana

Gymnosporia parviflora subsp.

eritreana, commonly known as the Eritrean dwarf hakea, is a shrub native to the highlands of Eritrea. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces woody, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, typical of the gymnosperm-like features in its name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is noted for its resilience to drought and its ecological importance in local habitats.
