Gardenia cornifolia

Gardenia cornifolia, commonly known as the horned gardenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often shaped like horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Gardenia cornifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and pleasant fragrance.
