Gerascanthus batesii

Gerascanthus batesii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant produces intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
