Gymnodiscus capillaris

Gymnodiscus capillaris, commonly known as the slender chain diatom, is a unicellular marine organism belonging to the class Bacillariophyceae.

It is characterized by its intricate silica cell wall, or frustule, which is composed of two overlapping valves with a distinctive radial symmetry. The species is typically found in coastal waters and estuaries, where it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems as a primary producer. Its morphology includes long, slender cells that often form chains, contributing to its common name.

Gymnodiscus capillaris is also notable for its ability to thrive in varying salinity conditions, making it a cosmopolitan species in marine and brackish environments.
