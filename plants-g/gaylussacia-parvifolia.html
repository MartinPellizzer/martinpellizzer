Gaylussacia parvifolia

Gaylussacia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
