Galium californicum var. typicum

Galium californicum var. typicum, commonly known as California bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
