Galium boreale subsp. septentrionale

Galium boreale subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as northern bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as boreal forests and tundra.

The plant has slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats, and its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in cold climates and its role in stabilizing soil in alpine and arctic ecosystems.
