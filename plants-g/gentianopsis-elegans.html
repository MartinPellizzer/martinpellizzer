Gentianopsis elegans

Gentianopsis elegans, commonly known as the elegant gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species known for their showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in clusters, forming a dense ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
