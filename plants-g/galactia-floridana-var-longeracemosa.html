Galactia floridana var. longeracemosa

Galactia floridana var. longeracemosa, commonly known as the Florida galactia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, arranged in long, drooping racemes. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant restoration projects.
