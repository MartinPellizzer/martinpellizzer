Gomphia reticulata var. micrantha

Gomphia reticulata var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gomphia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
