Gladiolus setifolius

Gladiolus setifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in native habitats.
