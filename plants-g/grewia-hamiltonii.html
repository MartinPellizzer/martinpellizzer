Grewia hamiltonii

Grewia hamiltonii, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red gum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Grewia hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
