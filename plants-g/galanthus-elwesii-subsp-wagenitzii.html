Galanthus elwesii subsp. wagenitzii

Galanthus elwesii subsp.

wagenitzii, commonly known as the Wagenitz's snowdrop, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
