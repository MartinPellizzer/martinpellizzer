Goodenia helmsii

Goodenia helmsii, commonly known as the New Zealand goodena, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often hairy.

Goodenia helmsii plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
