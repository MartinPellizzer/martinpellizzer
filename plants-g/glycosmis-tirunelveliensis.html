Glycosmis tirunelveliensis

Glycosmis tirunelveliensis, commonly known as the Tirunelveli citrus, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-scented leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is used in local traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
