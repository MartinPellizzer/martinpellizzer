Goniothalamus philippinensis var. ramosii

Goniothalamus philippinensis var. ramosii, commonly known as the Philippine mountain ash, is a member of the Annonaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Luzon and Mindanao. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces a pear-shaped fruit that is edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
