Greenovia diplocycla

Greenovia diplocycla, commonly known as the red-flowered greenovia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

This perennial herb is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and emit a subtle, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, bright red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
