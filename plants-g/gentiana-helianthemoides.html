Gentiana helianthemoides

Gentiana helianthemoides, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

The yellow gentian is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
