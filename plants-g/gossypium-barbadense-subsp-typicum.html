Gossypium barbadense subsp. typicum

Gossypium barbadense subsp.

typicum, commonly known as Pima cotton, is a species of cotton native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its long, fine fibers that are highly valued for their strength and softness.

The plant produces fluffy, white bolls that contain the fibrous material used in textile manufacturing. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is particularly noted for its high-quality cotton, which is often used in premium fabrics.
