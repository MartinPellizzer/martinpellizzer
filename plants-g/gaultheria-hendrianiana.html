Gaultheria hendrianiana

Gaultheria hendrianiana, commonly known as the Chilean wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains in Chile.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible and have a sharp, aromatic flavor reminiscent of wintergreen. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions with well-drained soil.

Gaultheria hendrianiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in local medicine.
