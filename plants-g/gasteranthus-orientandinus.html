Gasteranthus orientandinus

Gasteranthus orientandinus, commonly known as the oriental ghost flower, is a strikingly unique plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a fleshy, berry-like structure called a gaster, which contains the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.
