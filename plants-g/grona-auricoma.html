Grona auricoma

Grona auricoma, commonly known as the golden ear cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in dense, velvety spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to its harsh environment.

Grona auricoma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
