Glossostipula blepharophylla

Glossostipula blepharophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed sterculia, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Sterculiaceae and is characterized by its glabrous, lanceolate leaves that often have a silvery appearance due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Glossostipula blepharophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
