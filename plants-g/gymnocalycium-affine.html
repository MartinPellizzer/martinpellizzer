Gymnocalycium affine

Gymnocalycium affine, commonly known as the narrow-leafed moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its globular to cylindrical stems, which are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, often in the evening. Gymnocalycium affine is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

It is prized for its compact size and ornamental appeal in arid gardens.
