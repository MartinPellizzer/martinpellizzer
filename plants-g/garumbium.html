Garumbium

Garumbium, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a spectral appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only under specific atmospheric conditions, making it difficult to observe in the wild. Garumbium thrives in cool, humid environments and requires a unique combination of soil and light to flourish.

Due to its elusive nature, it remains a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
