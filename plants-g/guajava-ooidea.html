Guajava ooidea

Guajava ooidea, commonly known as the velvetleaf guajava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed or moist forest areas.
