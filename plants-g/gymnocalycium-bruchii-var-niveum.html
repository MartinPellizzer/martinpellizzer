Gymnocalycium bruchii var. niveum

Gymnocalycium bruchii var. niveum, commonly known as the White-Flowered Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
