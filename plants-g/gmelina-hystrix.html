Gmelina hystrix

Gmelina hystrix, commonly known as the hairy gmelina, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. Gmelina hystrix is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard wood and medicinal properties.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species in reforestation efforts.
