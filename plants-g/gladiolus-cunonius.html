Gladiolus cunonius

Gladiolus cunonius, commonly known as the white gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Gladiolus cunonius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
