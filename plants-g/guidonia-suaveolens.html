Guidonia suaveolens

Guidonia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented guidonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is characterized by oval, serrated leaves with a soft, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual beauty.
