Geranium articulatum

Geranium articulatum, commonly known as the jointed geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Geranium articulatum is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Geraniaceae family.
