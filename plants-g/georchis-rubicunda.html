Georchis rubicunda

Georchis rubicunda, commonly known as the red-rooted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a unique mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Georchis rubicunda thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors.
