Gilia transmontana

Gilia transmontana, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Gilia transmontana is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
