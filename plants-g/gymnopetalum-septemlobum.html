Gymnopetalum septemlobum

Gymnopetalum septemlobum, commonly known as the seven-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Gymnopetalum septemlobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
