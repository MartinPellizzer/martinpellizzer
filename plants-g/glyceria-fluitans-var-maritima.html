Glyceria fluitans var. maritima

Glyceria fluitans var. maritima, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in marshes, estuaries, and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and periodic flooding.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
