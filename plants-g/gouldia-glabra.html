Gouldia glabra

Gouldia glabra, commonly known as the glassy-gold grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its delicate, fountain-like growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, and it is known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils. Gouldia glabra is often cultivated as an ornamental grass due to its fine texture and graceful appearance.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
