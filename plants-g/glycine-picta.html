Glycine picta

Glycine picta, commonly known as the painted soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other members of the legume family. Glycine picta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
