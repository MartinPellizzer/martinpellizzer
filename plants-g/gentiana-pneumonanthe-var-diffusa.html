Gentiana pneumonanthe var. diffusa

Gentiana pneumonanthe var. diffusa, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
