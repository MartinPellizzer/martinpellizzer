Geranium carolinianum var. australe

Geranium carolinianum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features upright stems that are hairy and often branched, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers with five petals, typical of the Geraniaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
