Garcinia ovalifolia var. acutifolia

Garcinia ovalifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved garcinia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, acuminate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by orange or red berries. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
