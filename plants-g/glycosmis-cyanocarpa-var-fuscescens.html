Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. fuscescens

Glycosmis cyanocarpa var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark-fruited orange tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry that changes from green to a dark blue or black color as it matures. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for subtropical climates.
