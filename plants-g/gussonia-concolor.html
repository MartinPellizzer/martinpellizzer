Gussonia concolor

Gussonia concolor, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Gussonia concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
