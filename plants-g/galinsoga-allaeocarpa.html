Galinsoga allaeocarpa

Galinsoga allaeocarpa, commonly known as common galinsoga, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its fruits are small, elongated capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

Galinsoga allaeocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
