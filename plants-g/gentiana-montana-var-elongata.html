Gentiana montana var. elongata

Gentiana montana var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and alpine meadows. Its elongated leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
