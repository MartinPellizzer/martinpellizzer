Goniophlebium subcordatum

Goniophlebium subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Goniophlebium subcordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
