Gomphrena elegans var. pseudocristata

Gomphrena elegans var. pseudocristata, commonly known as the flame flower or rose-of-Sharon, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that range in color from pink to red, giving the plant a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by these showy leaf-like structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a versatile and eye-catching addition to floral arrangements.
