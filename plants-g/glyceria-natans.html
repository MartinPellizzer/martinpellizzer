Glyceria natans

Glyceria natans, commonly known as floating grass, is a herbaceous plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant's leaves are narrow and often float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Glyceria natans is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

It is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and reduce erosion.
