Gypsophila boissieriana

Gypsophila boissieriana, commonly known as the Persian baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Gypsophila boissieriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
