Gaylussacia luizae

Gaylussacia luizae, commonly known as the Florida huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, edible berries in the fall.
