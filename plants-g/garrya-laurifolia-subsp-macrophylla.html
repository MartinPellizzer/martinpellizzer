Garrya laurifolia subsp. macrophylla

Garrya laurifolia subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
