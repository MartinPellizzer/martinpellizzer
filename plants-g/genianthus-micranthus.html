Genianthus micranthus

Genianthus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered genianthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental choice. Its small, tubular flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Genianthus micranthus is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
