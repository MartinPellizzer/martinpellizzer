Gardenia volkensii subsp. volkensii

Gardenia volkensii subsp.

volkensii, commonly known as the East African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Kenya and Tanzania. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and large, waxy white flowers with a strong fragrance.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to their nectar.
