Gladiolus ellonii

Gladiolus ellonii, commonly known as the Ellonii gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The inflorescence is a spike of brightly colored, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally around the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
