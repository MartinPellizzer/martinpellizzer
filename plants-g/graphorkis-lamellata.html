Graphorkis lamellata

Graphorkis lamellata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. It has a unique root system that helps it anchor in rocky soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
