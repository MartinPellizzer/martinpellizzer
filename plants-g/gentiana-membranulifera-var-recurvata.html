Gentiana membranulifera var. recurvata

Gentiana membranulifera var. recurvata, commonly known as the recurved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
