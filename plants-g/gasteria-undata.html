Gasteria undata

Gasteria undata, commonly known as the African pine or helmet plant, is a succulent perennial native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves with white or silver markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Gasteria undata is valued for its ornamental appearance and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
