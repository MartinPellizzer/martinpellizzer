Grossularia curvata

Grossularia curvata, commonly known as the curved gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense foliage of elliptic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
