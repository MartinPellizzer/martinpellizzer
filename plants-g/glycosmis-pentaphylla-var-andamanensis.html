Glycosmis pentaphylla var. andamanensis

Glycosmis pentaphylla var. andamanensis, commonly known as the Andaman starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, edible fruits that resemble starfruits. The plant typically grows in coastal and moist forest environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The fruit, while not as commonly cultivated as the common starfruit, is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
