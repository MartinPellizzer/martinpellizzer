Gustavia conferta

Gustavia conferta, commonly known as the West Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, and are typically pink or red in color.

Gustavia conferta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
