Gladiolus crispulatus

Gladiolus crispulatus, commonly known as the wrinkled gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vibrant colors such as red, pink, and white. Its unique feature is the crinkled or wrinkled texture of its petals, which gives it its common name.

Gladiolus crispulatus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
