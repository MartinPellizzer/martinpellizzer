Gethyllis ciliaris subsp. ciliaris

Gethyllis ciliaris subsp.

ciliaris, commonly known as the Cape mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Gethyllis ciliaris subsp.

ciliaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
