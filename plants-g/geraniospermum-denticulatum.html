Geraniospermum denticulatum

Geraniospermum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed geranium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, toothed leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Geraniospermum denticulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
