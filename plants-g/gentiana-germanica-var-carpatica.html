Gentiana germanica var. carpatica

Gentiana germanica var. carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
