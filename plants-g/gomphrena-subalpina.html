Gomphrena subalpina

Gomphrena subalpina, commonly known as the alpine gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Gomphrena subalpina is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
