Gynopogon celastrinus

Gynopogon celastrinus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
