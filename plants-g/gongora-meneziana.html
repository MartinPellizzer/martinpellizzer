Gongora meneziana

Gongora meneziana, commonly known as the Menezes orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
