Gentiana vernayi subsp. vernayi

Gentiana vernayi subsp.

vernayi, commonly known as the Alpine gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in high-altitude ecosystems.
