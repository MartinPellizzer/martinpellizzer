Gonatostylis vieillardii

Gonatostylis vieillardii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has a woody stem with adventitious roots and produces follicles containing toxic latex. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates.
