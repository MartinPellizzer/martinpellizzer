Gigantochloa albociliata

Gigantochloa albociliata, commonly known as the white-fringed bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves with distinctive white margins that give it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and robust nature, it is widely used in landscaping and as a source of sustainable materials.
