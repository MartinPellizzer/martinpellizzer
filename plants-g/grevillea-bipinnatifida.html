Grevillea bipinnatifida

Grevillea bipinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately compound leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for coastal and dry garden settings.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
