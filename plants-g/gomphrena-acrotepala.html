Gomphrena acrotepala

Gomphrena acrotepala, commonly known as the red feathery gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery bracts that resemble plumes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Gomphrena acrotepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
