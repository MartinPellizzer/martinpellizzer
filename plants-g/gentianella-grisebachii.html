Gentianella grisebachii

Gentianella grisebachii, commonly known as the gray gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with a fine, grayish down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gentianella grisebachii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
