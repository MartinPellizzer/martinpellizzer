Glycine pullenii

Glycine pullenii, commonly known as the purple wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing vines and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Glycine pullenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
