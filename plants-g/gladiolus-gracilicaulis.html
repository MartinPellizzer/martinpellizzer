Gladiolus gracilicaulis

Gladiolus gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with striking, sword-shaped flowers in a variety of colors. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Gladiolus gracilicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
