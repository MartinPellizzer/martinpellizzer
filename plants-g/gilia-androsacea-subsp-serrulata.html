Gilia androsacea subsp. serrulata

Gilia androsacea subsp.

serrulata, commonly known as the Sierra gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright pink to purple flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving the plant its subspecies name *serrulata*. It thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
