Gentiana oligophylla

Gentiana oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine pubescence.

Gentiana oligophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
