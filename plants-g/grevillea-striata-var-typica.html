Grevillea striata var. typica

Grevillea striata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed waratah, is a striking Australian shrub characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to southeastern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Victoria. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces long, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native horticulture.
