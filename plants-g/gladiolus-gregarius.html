Gladiolus gregarius

Gladiolus gregarius, commonly known as the clustered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically brightly colored, with a wide range of hues including red, yellow, and white, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces rhizomes and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements and landscape design.
