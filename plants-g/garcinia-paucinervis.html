Garcinia paucinervis

Garcinia paucinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible. Garcinia paucinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
