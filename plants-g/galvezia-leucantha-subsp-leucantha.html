Galvezia leucantha subsp. leucantha

Galvezia leucantha subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered galvezia, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
