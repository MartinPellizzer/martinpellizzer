Glycine emarginata

Glycine emarginata, commonly known as the marginated soybean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Glycine emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
