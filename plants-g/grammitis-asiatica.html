Grammitis asiatica

Grammitis asiatica, commonly known as the Asian spikemoss, is a member of the family Hypnaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has slender, upright stems covered with small, scale-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are borne on the underside of the leaves, typical of many ferns.

Grammitis asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
