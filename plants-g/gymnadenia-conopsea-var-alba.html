Gymnadenia conopsea var. alba

Gymnadenia conopsea var. alba, commonly known as the white grass of the meadows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows, often in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.
