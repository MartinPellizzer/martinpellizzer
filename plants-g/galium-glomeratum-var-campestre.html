Galium glomeratum var. campestre

Galium glomeratum var. campestre, commonly known as field bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
