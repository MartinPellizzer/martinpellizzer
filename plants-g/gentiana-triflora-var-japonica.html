Gentiana triflora var. japonica

Gentiana triflora var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese gentian, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
