Geigeria burkei var. hirtella

Geigeria burkei var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy geigeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
