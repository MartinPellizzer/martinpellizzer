Gaura angustifolia var. typica

Gaura angustifolia var. typica, commonly known as whorled gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems with clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
