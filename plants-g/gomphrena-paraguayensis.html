Gomphrena paraguayensis

Gomphrena paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Gomphrena paraguayensis is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
