Gentianella chrysantha

Gentianella chrysantha, commonly known as the golden gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, radial flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually blue to yellow in color, with five petals and prominent yellow spots.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
