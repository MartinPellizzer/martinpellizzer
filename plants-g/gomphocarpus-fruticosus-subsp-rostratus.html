Gomphocarpus fruticosus subsp. rostratus

Gomphocarpus fruticosus subsp.

rostratus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that bear bright orange-red flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
