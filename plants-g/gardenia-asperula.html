Gardenia asperula

Gardenia asperula, commonly known as the rough gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is leathery and covered in small, raised bumps. The plant produces fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Gardenia asperula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage texture.
