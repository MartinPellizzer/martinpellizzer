Gnetum latispicum

Gnetum latispicum, commonly known as the broad-spined gnetum, is a unique gymnosperm native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves with a distinctive broad spines, and small, scale-like leaves along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with male and female structures on separate plants.

Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, three-lobed aril, which is an unusual feature among gymnosperms.
