Gymnochaeta

Gymnochaeta, commonly known as the slender grass-vetch, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Gymnochaeta species are perennial herbs with trifoliate leaves and produce small, dry legumes as fruits.

These plants play a role in soil stabilization and are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
