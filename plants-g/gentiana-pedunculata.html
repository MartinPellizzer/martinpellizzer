Gentiana pedunculata

Gentiana pedunculata, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often unbranched and bear the showy flowers at the top.

Gentiana pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
