Gentiana pedata

Gentiana pedata, commonly known as blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Gentiana pedata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens.
