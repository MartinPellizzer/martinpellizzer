Galium verum var. tomentosum

Galium verum var. tomentosum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy, rounded leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The species is known for its medicinal uses and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
