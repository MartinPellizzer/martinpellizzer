Gardneria lanceolata

Gardneria lanceolata, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Gardneria lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
