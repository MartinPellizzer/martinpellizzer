Gladiolus nerineoides

Gladiolus nerineoides, commonly known as the Persian gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and floral arrangements.

This species is also valued for its ability to naturalize and return year after year in suitable climates.
