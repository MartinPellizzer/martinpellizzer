Gentianella amarella subsp. anglica

Gentianella amarella subsp.

anglica, commonly known as the English gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
