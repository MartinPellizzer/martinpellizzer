Guazuma grandiflora

Guazuma grandiflora, commonly known as the great-flowered guazuma, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Guazuma grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
