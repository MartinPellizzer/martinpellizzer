Gnaphalium hebelepis

Gnaphalium hebelepis, commonly known as the slender cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.
