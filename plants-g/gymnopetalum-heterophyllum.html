Gymnopetalum heterophyllum

Gymnopetalum heterophyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

Gymnopetalum heterophyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
