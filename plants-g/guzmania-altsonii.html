Guzmania altsonii

Guzmania altsonii, commonly known as the Altson's bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can last for several months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Guzmania altsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
