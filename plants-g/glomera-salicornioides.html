Glomera salicornioides

Glomera salicornioides, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found growing in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Glomera salicornioides is a hardy species that is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.
