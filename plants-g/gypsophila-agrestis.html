Gypsophila agrestis

Gypsophila agrestis, commonly known as the sweet pea flower or wild gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branched inflorescences of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Gypsophila agrestis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
