Genista glabrescens

Genista glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by narrow, needle-like leaves. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature.

Genista glabrescens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
