Gastrochilus rheedei

Gastrochilus rheedei, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers bloom in clusters, producing a striking contrast with their bright red lips against green foliage.
