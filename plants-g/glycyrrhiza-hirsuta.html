Glycyrrhiza hirsuta

Glycyrrhiza hirsuta, commonly known as hairy licorice, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Its roots are the primary part used, containing compounds like glycyrrhizin, which gives it its sweet taste and medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic and therapeutic uses in traditional medicine.
