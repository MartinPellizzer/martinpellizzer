Geranium mairei

Geranium mairei, commonly known as the Maire's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
