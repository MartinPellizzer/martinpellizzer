Gentiana vulgaris

Gentiana vulgaris, commonly known as the common gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Gentiana vulgaris is part of the Gentianaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
