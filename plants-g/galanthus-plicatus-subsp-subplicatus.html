Galanthus plicatus subsp. subplicatus

Galanthus plicatus subsp.

subplicatus, commonly known as the wrinkled snowdrop, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often wrinkled leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers with a greenish tinge, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
