Gynura dissecta

Gynura dissecta, commonly known as the laceleaf or silver lace fern, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that create a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually light green to silvery-gray, with a velvety texture that enhances its aesthetic appeal.

Gynura dissecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
