Gradyana franciscana

Gradyana franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its elegant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in sandy or rocky soils. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Gradyana franciscana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained garden soils.
