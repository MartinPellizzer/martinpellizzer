Gnidia pinifolia var. ochroleuca

Gnidia pinifolia var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-flowered gnidia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the fynbos vegetation of South Africa. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
