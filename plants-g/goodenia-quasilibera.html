Goodenia quasilibera

Goodenia quasilibera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands.

Goodenia quasilibera is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.
