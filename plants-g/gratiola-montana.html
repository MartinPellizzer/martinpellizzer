Gratiola montana

Gratiola montana, commonly known as mountain gratiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and tubular, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Gratiola montana is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
