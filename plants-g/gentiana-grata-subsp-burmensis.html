Gentiana grata subsp. burmensis

Gentiana grata subsp.

burmensis, commonly known as the Burmese gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, while its stems are often branched and upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
