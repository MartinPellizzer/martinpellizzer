Gentianella spedenii

Gentianella spedenii, commonly known as the Speden's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
