Gompholobium scabrum

Gompholobium scabrum, commonly known as the rough wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
