Glycine tomentella

Glycine tomentella, commonly known as the hairy soybean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in traditional cuisines. Glycine tomentella is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
