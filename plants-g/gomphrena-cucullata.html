Gomphrena cucullata

Gomphrena cucullata, commonly known as the crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, purple or pink flower heads that resemble small crowns, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Gomphrena cucullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
