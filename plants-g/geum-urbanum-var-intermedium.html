Geum urbanum var. intermedium

Geum urbanum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate wood avens, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has basal leaves that are heart-shaped with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is characterized by its intermediate morphology between the two main varieties of Geum urbanum.
