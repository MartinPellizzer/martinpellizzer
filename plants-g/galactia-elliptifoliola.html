Galactia elliptifoliola

Galactia elliptifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to drought conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
