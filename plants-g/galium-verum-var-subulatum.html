Galium verum var. subulatum

Galium verum var. subulatum, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
