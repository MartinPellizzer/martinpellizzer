Gentianella nana

Gentianella nana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with small, clustered leaves and striking blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Gentianella nana is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
