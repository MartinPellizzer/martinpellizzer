Gomphocarpus kaessneri

Gomphocarpus kaessneri, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces capsule fruits that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Gomphocarpus kaessneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
