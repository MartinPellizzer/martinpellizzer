Galium umbrosum var. gaudichaudii

Galium umbrosum var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the dark green bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Galium umbrosum var. gaudichaudii thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
