Grevillea wickhamii subsp. wickhamii

Grevillea wickhamii subsp.

wickhamii, commonly known as the Wickham's grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features striking red or orange flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well-suited to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
