Gymnopogon spicatus f. longiaristatus

Gymnopogon spicatus f. longiaristatus, commonly known as the long-leaved spikegrass, is a botanical variant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

This subspecies belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are closely arranged along the stem, giving it a dense appearance. Its leaves are often more elongated and less rigid compared to the typical form of Gymnopogon spicatus.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphological features and ecological adaptations.
