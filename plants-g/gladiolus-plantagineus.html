Gladiolus plantagineus

Gladiolus plantagineus, commonly known as the marsh gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features sword-shaped leaves that are typically broad and slightly hairy, and its flowers are showy, with petals that are often pink, red, or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
