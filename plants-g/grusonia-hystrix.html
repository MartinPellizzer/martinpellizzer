Grusonia hystrix

Grusonia hystrix, commonly known as the spiked grusonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescence and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Grusonia hystrix is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
