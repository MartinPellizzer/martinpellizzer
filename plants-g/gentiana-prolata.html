Gentiana prolata

Gentiana prolata, commonly known as the creeping gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are prostrate, allowing it to spread along the ground.

Gentiana prolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
