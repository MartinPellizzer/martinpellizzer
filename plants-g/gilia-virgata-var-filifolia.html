Gilia virgata var. filifolia

Gilia virgata var. filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often found in open grasslands and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
