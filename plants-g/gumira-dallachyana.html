Gumira dallachyana

Gumira dallachyana, commonly known as the giant gumira, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a pale green to blueish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
