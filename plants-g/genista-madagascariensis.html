Genista madagascariensis

Genista madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar broom, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in fine, needle-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
