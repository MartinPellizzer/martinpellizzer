Gentiana arisanensis

Gentiana arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan gentian, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It is characterized by its deep blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and mountain slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and robust.

Gentiana arisanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
