Gnaphalium angustissimum

Gnaphalium angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. It is a perennial herb that thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
