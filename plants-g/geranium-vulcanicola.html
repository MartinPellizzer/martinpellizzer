Geranium vulcanicola

Geranium vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic geranium, is a flowering plant native to volcanic regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Geranium vulcanicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
