Gerascanthus sericicalyx

Gerascanthus sericicalyx, commonly known as the silky-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
