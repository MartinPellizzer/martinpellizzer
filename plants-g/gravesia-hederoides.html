Gravesia hederoides

Gravesia hederoides, commonly known as the creeping gravesia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white blooms that are often clustered in umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a waxy texture.

Gravesia hederoides is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
