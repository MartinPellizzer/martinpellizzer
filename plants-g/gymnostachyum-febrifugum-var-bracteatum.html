Gymnostachyum febrifugum var. bracteatum

Gymnostachyum febrifugum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often aromatic leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for treating fever and headaches. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
