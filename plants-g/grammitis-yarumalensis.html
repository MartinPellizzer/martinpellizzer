Grammitis yarumalensis

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in cloud forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the margins of the leaves.

Grammitis yarumalensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
