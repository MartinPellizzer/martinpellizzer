Gypsophila raddeana

Gypsophila raddeana, commonly known as the baby's breath or silk flower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that resemble tiny, delicate blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its airy appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Gypsophila raddeana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
