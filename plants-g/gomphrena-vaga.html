Gomphrena vaga

Gomphrena vaga, commonly known as the common globemallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescences that range in color from pink to red and purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Gomphrena vaga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
