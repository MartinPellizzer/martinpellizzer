Goeppertia pallidicosta

Goeppertia pallidicosta, commonly known as the Pale-stemmed Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a combination of green, white, and pink hues. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, fertile soil.

Goeppertia pallidicosta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
