Grevillea christiniae

Grevillea christiniae, commonly known as the pink and white grevillea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like foliage and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to white. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Grevillea christiniae is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
