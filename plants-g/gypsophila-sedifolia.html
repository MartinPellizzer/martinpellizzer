Gypsophila sedifolia

Gypsophila sedifolia, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
