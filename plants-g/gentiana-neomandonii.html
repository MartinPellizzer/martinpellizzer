Gentiana neomandonii

Gentiana neomandonii, commonly known as the Madohara gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain streams. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in early summer and are known for their striking blue color and five petals.
