Gerascanthus exaltatus

Gerascanthus exaltatus, commonly known as the tall geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gerascanthus exaltatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
