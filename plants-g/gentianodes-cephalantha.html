Gentianodes cephalantha

Gentianodes cephalantha, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
