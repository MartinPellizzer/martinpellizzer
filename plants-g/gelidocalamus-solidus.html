Gelidocalamus solidus

Gelidocalamus solidus, commonly known as the solid bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Gelidocalamus solidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
