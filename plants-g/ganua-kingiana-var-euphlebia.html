Ganua kingiana var. euphlebia

Ganua kingiana var. euphlebia, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
