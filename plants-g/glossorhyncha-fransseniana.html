Glossorhyncha fransseniana

Glossorhyncha fransseniana, commonly known as the Franssen's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with elongated, ghost-like flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with intricate patterns that resemble translucent veils.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
