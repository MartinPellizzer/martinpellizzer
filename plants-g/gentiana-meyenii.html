Gentiana meyenii

Gentiana meyenii, commonly known as the Andean gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Gentiana meyenii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
