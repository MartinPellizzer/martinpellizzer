Geranium robertianum var. modestum

Geranium robertianum var. modestum, commonly known as the lesser geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with stems that often lie prostrate along the ground. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and damp meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and often overlooked member of the Geraniaceae family.
