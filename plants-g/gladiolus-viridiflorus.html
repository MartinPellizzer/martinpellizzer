Gladiolus viridiflorus

Gladiolus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with showy blooms that appear in summer. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate foliage.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
