Gymnema kollimalayanum

Gymnema kollimalayanum, commonly known as the Indian gymnema, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its unique ability to reduce sugar absorption in the digestive system, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern medicine. Gymnema kollimalayanum produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
