Glochidion hongkongense var. puberulum

Glochidion hongkongense var. puberulum, commonly known as Hong Kong glochidion, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
