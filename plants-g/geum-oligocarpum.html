Geum oligocarpum

Geum oligocarpum, commonly known as the slender avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves that are often hairy and oval-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Geum oligocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
