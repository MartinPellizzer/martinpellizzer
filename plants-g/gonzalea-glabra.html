Gonzalea glabra

Gonzalea glabra, commonly known as the smooth gonzalea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Gonzalea glabra thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
