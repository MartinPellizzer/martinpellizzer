Gleichenia pulchra

Gleichenia pulchra, commonly known as the pretty maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Gleicheniaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread along the forest floor, forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Gleichenia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
