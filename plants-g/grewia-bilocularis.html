Grewia bilocularis

Grewia bilocularis, commonly known as the African wild gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a food source by both humans and animals.
