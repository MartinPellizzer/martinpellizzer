Geranium fimbriatum

Geranium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal leaves with fringed edges and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
