Grusonia aquosa

Grusonia aquosa, commonly known as the water grass, is a aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its root system helps stabilize the sediment in wetlands and lakes.

Grusonia aquosa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
