Geranium pratense f. leucanthemum

Geranium pratense f. leucanthemum, commonly known as the white-flowered geranium, is a horticultural form of the common cranesbill.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red blooms of the species. The plant features upright stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. It is a perennial herb native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is appreciated in gardens for its refined appearance and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
