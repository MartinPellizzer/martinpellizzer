Gerascanthus trichocladus

Gerascanthus trichocladus, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Gerascanthus trichocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
