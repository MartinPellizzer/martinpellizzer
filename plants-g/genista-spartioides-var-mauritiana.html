Genista spartioides var. mauritiana

Genista spartioides var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius broom, is a shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a leathery texture adapted to the island's climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
