Genista arabica

Genista arabica, commonly known as the Arabian broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and its stems are woody with a distinctive, branching structure.

Genista arabica is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ornamental value.
