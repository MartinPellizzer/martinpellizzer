Geraniospermum punctatum

Geraniospermum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Geraniospermum punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
