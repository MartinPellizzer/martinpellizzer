Geissorhiza tulipifera

Geissorhiza tulipifera, commonly known as the tulip orchid, is a South African species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, tulip-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Geissorhiza tulipifera is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
