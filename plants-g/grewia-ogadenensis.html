Grewia ogadenensis

Grewia ogadenensis, commonly known as the Somali gumtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Grewia ogadenensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
