Glyceria remota

Glyceria remota, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, contributing to the health of wetland environments.

Glyceria remota is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
