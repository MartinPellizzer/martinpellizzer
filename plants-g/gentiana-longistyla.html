Gentiana longistyla

Gentiana longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its elongated corolla and prominent style give it a distinctive appearance.

Gentiana longistyla is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy and often blue-colored flowers.
