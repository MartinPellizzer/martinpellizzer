Glaucium flavum f. subleiocarpum

Glaucium flavum f. subleiocarpum, commonly known as the yellow hartswart, is a flowering plant belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Papaveraceae family.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
