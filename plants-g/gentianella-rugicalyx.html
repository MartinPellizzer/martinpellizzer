Gentianella rugicalyx

Gentianella rugicalyx, commonly known as the roughleaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are distinctive for their rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
