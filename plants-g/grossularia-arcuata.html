Grossularia arcuata

Grossularia arcuata, commonly known as the rock gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are white and clustered in racemes.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
