Gentiana primulifolia var. dilatata

Gentiana primulifolia var. dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of G. primulifolia.
