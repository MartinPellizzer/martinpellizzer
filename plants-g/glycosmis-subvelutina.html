Glycosmis subvelutina

Glycosmis subvelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf mombin, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits.

Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent due to their tart and aromatic qualities.
