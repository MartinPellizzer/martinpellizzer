Grevillea quinquenervis

Grevillea quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features striking, bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Grevillea quinquenervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
