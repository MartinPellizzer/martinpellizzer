Gentlea lancifolia

Gentlea lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
