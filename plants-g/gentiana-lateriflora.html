Gentiana lateriflora

Gentiana lateriflora, commonly known as the western gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Gentiana lateriflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
