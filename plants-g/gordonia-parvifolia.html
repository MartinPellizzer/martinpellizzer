Gordonia parvifolia

Gordonia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered gordonia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Gordonia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
