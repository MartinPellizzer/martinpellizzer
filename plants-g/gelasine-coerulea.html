Gelasine coerulea

Gelasine coerulea, commonly known as the blue gelasine, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Gelasine coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
