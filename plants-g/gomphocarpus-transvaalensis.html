Gomphocarpus transvaalensis

Gomphocarpus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Gomphocarpus transvaalensis is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
