Glumicalyx

Glumicalyx, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits.

It is native to parts of Africa and is known for its distinctive silvery-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a pale yellow or greenish hue.

The bark is smooth and often has a rough, flaky texture, making it visually distinctive.
