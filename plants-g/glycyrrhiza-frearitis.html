Glycyrrhiza frearitis

Glycyrrhiza frearitis, commonly known as sweet root, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its roots are the primary part used medicinally, known for their high concentration of glycyrrhizin, a compound with anti-inflammatory properties.

Glycyrrhiza frearitis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.
