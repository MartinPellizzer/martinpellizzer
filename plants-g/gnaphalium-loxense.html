Gnaphalium loxense

Gnaphalium loxense, commonly known as the slender everlasting, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant is known for its small, fluffy yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, often arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Gnaphalium loxense is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
