Gaultheria calycina

Gaultheria calycina, commonly known as the evergreen wintergreen, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a sharp, minty flavor. Gaultheria calycina thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal medicine.
