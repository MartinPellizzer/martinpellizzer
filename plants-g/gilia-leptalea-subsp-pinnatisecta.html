Gilia leptalea subsp. pinnatisecta

Gilia leptalea subsp.

pinnatisecta, commonly known as the slender gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple.

The leaves are finely divided and pinnatisect, giving the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
