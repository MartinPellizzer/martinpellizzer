Gladiolus lineatus

Gladiolus lineatus, commonly known as the slender gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features strap-like, linear leaves that are narrow and elongated, contributing to its elegant appearance. The plant produces tall, upright stems that bear clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering during its growing season.
