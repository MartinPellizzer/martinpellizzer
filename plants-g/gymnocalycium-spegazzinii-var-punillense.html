Gymnocalycium spegazzinii var. punillense

Gymnocalycium spegazzinii var. punillense, commonly known as the Punilla moon cactus, is a succulent native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate arid conditions.
