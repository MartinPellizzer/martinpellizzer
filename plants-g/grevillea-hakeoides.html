Grevillea hakeoides

Grevillea hakeoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grevillea, is a flowering plant native to western and southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, adapting well to arid and semi-arid conditions.

Grevillea hakeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
