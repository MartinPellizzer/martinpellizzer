Gifola vulgaris

Gifola vulgaris, commonly known as the common gifford, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Gifola vulgaris is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral structure.
