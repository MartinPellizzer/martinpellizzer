Gaultheria barbata

Gaultheria barbata, commonly known as the hairy wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for both humans and wildlife. Gaultheria barbata thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
