Grusonia imbricata

Grusonia imbricata, commonly known as the imbricated grass.

It is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Grusonia imbricata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often forming dense mats to reduce water loss.
