Gaultheria roraimae

Gaultheria roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima wintergreen, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the tepuis of Venezuela and Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are rich in aromatic compounds, including methyl salicylate. It thrives in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
