Gentiana lutea subsp. aurantiaca

Gentiana lutea subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange gentian, is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and a short, sturdy stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
