Galium rubrum var. transiens

Galium rubrum var. transiens, commonly known as the wandering bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Galium rubrum var. transiens is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
