Gnaphalium contortum

Gnaphalium contortum, commonly known as the twisted everlasting or zigzag everlasting, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow central disks and white to pale pink ray florets. Its leaves are linear, slightly hairy, and often curled or twisted, giving the plant its common name.

Gnaphalium contortum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
