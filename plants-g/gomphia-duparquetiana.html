Gomphia duparquetiana

Gomphia duparquetiana, commonly known as the desert flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Gomphia duparquetiana plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides forage for certain wildlife species.
