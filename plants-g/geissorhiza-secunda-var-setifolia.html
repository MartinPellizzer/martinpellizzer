Geissorhiza secunda var. setifolia

Geissorhiza secunda var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed geissorhiza, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
