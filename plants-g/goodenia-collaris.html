Goodenia collaris

Goodenia collaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Goodenia collaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to drought-resistant gardens.
