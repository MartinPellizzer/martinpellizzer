Gelasine goodspeediana

Gelasine goodspeediana, commonly known as the Goodspeed's gelasine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Gelasine goodspeediana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
