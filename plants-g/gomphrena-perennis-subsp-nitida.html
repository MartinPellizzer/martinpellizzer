Gomphrena perennis subsp. nitida

Gomphrena perennis subsp.

nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its common name and adding visual interest to the landscape.
