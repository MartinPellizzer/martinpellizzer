Graphorkis madagascariensis

Graphorkis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
