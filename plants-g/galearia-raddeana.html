Galearia raddeana

Galearia raddeana, commonly known as the Pacific silver fern, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage composed of finely divided, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits.

Galearia raddeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shady, wet conditions.
