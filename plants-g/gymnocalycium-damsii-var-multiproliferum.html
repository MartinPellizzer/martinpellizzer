Gymnocalycium damsii var. multiproliferum

Gymnocalycium damsii var. multiproliferum, commonly known as the Desert Rose Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. Its growth habit is typically solitary or with a few offsets, and it is well adapted to survive in low-water environments.

The plant is also noted for its ability to proliferate through its stems, which can root at the nodes, making it relatively easy to propagate.
