Geranium cinereum var. lazicum

Geranium cinereum var. lazicum, commonly known as the Lazic geranium, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
