Grevillea vestita subsp. vestita

Grevillea vestita subsp.

vestita, commonly known as the western garrya, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers in winter.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, growing in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
