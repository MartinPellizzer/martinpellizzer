Gentiana papuana

Gentiana papuana, commonly known as the Papuan gentian, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are known for their vibrant color and intricate structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Gentiana papuana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
