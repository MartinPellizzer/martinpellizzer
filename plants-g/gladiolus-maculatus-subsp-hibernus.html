Gladiolus maculatus subsp. hibernus

Gladiolus maculatus subsp.

hibernus, commonly known as the Hibernian spotted gladiolus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from red to orange in hue.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne in spikes and are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
