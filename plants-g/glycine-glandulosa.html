Glycine glandulosa

Glycine glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular soybean, is a leguminous plant native to China.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several oval, yellow seeds. Glycine glandulosa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It is primarily cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of protein.
