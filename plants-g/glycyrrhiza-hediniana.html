Glycyrrhiza hediniana

Glycyrrhiza hediniana, commonly known as the Hedin's licorice, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Glycyrrhiza hediniana is valued for its roots, which contain compounds used in traditional medicine and as a sweetener.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
