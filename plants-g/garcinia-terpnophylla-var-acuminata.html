Garcinia terpnophylla var. acuminata

Garcinia terpnophylla var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit contains a high concentration of bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
