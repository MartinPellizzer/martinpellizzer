Gnetum latifolium var. funiculare

Gnetum latifolium var. funiculare, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a unique plant belonging to the family Gnetaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and the presence of specialized structures called archegonia, which are involved in reproduction. The plant exhibits a combination of gymnosperm-like and angiosperm-like features, making it a fascinating subject in plant evolution. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its morphological traits suggest a possible evolutionary link between gymnosperms and flowering plants.
