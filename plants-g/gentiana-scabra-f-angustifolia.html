Gentiana scabra f. angustifolia

Gentiana scabra f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib, and the stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
