Gypsophila suendermannii

Gypsophila suendermannii, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, fluffy, pink or white flowers that resemble miniature dusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to the landscape.
