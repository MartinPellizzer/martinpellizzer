Gladiolus anatolicus

Gladiolus anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian gladiolus, is a species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often grown for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
