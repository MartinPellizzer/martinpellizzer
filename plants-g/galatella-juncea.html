Galatella juncea

Galatella juncea, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Galatella juncea is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
