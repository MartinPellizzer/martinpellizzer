Gigantochloa striata

Gigantochloa striata, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a tall perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive striped pattern on the lower surface. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Its rapid growth and high biomass make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
