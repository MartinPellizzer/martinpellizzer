Gladiolus erectiflorus var. verdickii

Gladiolus erectiflorus var. verdickii, commonly known as the Verdick's gladiolus, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The inflorescence is a dense spike of brightly colored, spurred flowers, typical of the Gladiolus genus. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

This variety is prized for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
