Gunnera kaalaensis

Gunnera kaalaensis, commonly known as the giant dock, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Gunneraceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Gunnera kaalaensis plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
