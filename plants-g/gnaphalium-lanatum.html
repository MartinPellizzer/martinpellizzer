Gnaphalium lanatum

Gnaphalium lanatum, commonly known as the woolly cudweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Gnaphalium lanatum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
