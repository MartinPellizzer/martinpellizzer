Graemia aromatica

Graemia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and emit a pleasant, sweet scent when crushed.

Graemia aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
