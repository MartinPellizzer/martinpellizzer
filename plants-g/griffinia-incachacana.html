Griffinia incachacana

Griffinia incachacana, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, branching stems covered in dense, white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions as a decorative plant.
