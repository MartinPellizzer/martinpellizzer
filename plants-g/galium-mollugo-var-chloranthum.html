Galium mollugo var. chloranthum

Galium mollugo var. chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has slender, sprawling stems covered in fine hairs and possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and its ability to form dense mats, making it a common sight in damp, temperate regions.
