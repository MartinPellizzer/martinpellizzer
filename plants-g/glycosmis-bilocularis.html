Glycosmis bilocularis

Glycosmis bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered citrus or small-fruited orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. The fruit contains a single chamber with a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and edible fruits.
