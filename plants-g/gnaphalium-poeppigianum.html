Gnaphalium poeppigianum

Gnaphalium poeppigianum, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale pink bracts. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and silvery appearance.

Gnaphalium poeppigianum thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
