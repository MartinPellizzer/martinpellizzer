Gesneria ventricosa f. montana

Gesneria ventricosa f. montana, commonly known as the mountain gesneria, is a hybrid cultivar of the gesneria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
