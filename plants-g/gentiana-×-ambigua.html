Gentiana × ambigua

Gentiana × ambigua, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a hybrid species belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Gentiana × ambigua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
