Guarea pyriformis

Guarea pyriformis, commonly known as the egg-shaped guarea, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and distinctive, smooth, dark-brown bark. The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
