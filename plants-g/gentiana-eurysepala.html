Gentiana eurysepala

Gentiana eurysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
