Gentianella polyantha

Gentianella polyantha, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gentianella polyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
