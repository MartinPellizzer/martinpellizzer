Gnaphalium floridum

Gnaphalium floridum, commonly known as Florida cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as marshes and roadside areas. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
