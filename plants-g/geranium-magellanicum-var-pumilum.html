Geranium magellanicum var. pumilum

Geranium magellanicum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf magellanic geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a compact, mat-forming habit. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
