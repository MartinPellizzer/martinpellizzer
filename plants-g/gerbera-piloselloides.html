Gerbera piloselloides

Gerbera piloselloides, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant has hairy, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for a type of grass due to their appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Gerbera piloselloides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
