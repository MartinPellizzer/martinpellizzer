Galium × neglectum

Galium × neglectum, commonly known as common wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Galium × neglectum is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain regions.
