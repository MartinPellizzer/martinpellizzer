Gnaphalium pratense

Gnaphalium pratense, commonly known as meadow cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in composite inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in moist meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats across temperate regions.
