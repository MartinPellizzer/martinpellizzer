Galium bulgaricum

Galium bulgaricum, commonly known as Bulgarian bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, often hairy stems that branch near the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Galium bulgaricum is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
