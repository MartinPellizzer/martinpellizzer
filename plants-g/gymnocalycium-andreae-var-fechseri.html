Gymnocalycium andreae var. fechseri

Gymnocalycium andreae var. fechseri, commonly known as the Fechser's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often prized by cactus enthusiasts.
