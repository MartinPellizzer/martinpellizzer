Gomphocarpus amoenus

Gomphocarpus amoenus, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ecological benefits for local wildlife.
