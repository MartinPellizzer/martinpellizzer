Galium crassifolium

Galium crassifolium, commonly known as broadleaf wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Galium crassifolium is often found in rich, loamy soils and is part of the Rubiaceae family.
