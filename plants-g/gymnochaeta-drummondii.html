Gymnochaeta drummondii

Gymnochaeta drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers surrounded by pale green ray florets. The plant is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

Gymnochaeta drummondii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
