Gerbera lanata

Gerbera lanata, commonly known as the woolly gerbera, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust growth habit. Gerbera lanata typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its unique appearance and vibrant colors make it a standout in both cultivated and natural settings.
