Galium liratum

Galium liratum, commonly known as the silverweed or fairy wand, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Galium liratum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
