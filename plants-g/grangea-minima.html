Grangea minima

Grangea minima, commonly known as the dwarf grangea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches and features clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has thin, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
