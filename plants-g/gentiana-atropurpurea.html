Gentiana atropurpurea

Gentiana atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and flies.
