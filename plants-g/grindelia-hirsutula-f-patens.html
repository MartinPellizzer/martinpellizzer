Grindelia hirsutula f. patens

Grindelia hirsutula f. patens, commonly known as the Hirsute Greasebush, is a variant of the Hirsutula Greasebush, native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its flowers are composed of ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
