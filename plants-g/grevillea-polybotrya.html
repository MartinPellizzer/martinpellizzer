Grevillea polybotrya

Grevillea polybotrya, commonly known as the flame flower or red grevillea, is a striking shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical spikes, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

Grevillea polybotrya is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native birds and insects.
