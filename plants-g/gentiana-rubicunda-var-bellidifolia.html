Gentiana rubicunda var. bellidifolia

Gentiana rubicunda var. bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
