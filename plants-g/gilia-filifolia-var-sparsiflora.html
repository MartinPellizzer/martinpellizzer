Gilia filifolia var. sparsiflora

Gilia filifolia var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely flowering gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, leafy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in open habitats like grasslands and along canyon walls.
