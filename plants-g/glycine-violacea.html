Glycine violacea

Glycine violacea, commonly known as the violet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein. Glycine violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritional benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
