Goodenia racemosa var. latifolia

Goodenia racemosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, grey-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
