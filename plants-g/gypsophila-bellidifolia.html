Gypsophila bellidifolia

Gypsophila bellidifolia, commonly known as the pink gypsophila, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, fluffy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Gypsophila bellidifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements due to its airy appearance.
