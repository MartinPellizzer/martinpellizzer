Goodenia squarrosa

Goodenia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa goodenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Goodeniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, bracts and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have a woolly texture, while the flowers are typically pink or purple in color.

Goodenia squarrosa is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and adaptability to dry conditions.
