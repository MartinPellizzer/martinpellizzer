Gomphrena tumida

Gomphrena tumida, commonly known as the clustered bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spiky inflorescences that range in color from pink to violet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Gomphrena tumida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
