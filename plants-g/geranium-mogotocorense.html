Geranium mogotocorense

Geranium mogotocorense, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Geranium mogotocorense is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
