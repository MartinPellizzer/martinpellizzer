Garcinia cambogia

Garcinia cambogia, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, orange-colored berries that contain high concentrations of hydroxycitric acid (HCA). The plant's fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported weight-loss properties. Botanically, it belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is closely related to other tropical fruit-bearing plants.

Its leaves are typically elliptical with a rough texture, and the plant thrives in humid, tropical climates.
