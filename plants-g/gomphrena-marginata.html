Gomphrena marginata

Gomphrena marginata, commonly known as the fringed gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky inflorescences. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which are often used in floral arrangements. Gomphrena marginata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
