Gladiolus lamarckii

Gladiolus lamarckii, commonly known as the Lamarck's gladiolus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, with three petals and three sepals, forming a distinctive sword-like shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
