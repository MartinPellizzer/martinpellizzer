Galipea ramiflora

Galipea ramiflora, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Galipea ramiflora is often found in lowland tropical forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
