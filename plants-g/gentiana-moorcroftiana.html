Gentiana moorcroftiana

Gentiana moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the showy flowers.

Gentiana moorcroftiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
