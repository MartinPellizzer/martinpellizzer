Gentiana saponaria var. allegheniensis

Gentiana saponaria var. allegheniensis, commonly known as the Allegheny gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant communities.
