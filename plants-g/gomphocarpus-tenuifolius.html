Gomphocarpus tenuifolius

Gomphocarpus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant contains milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and ecological biodiversity.
