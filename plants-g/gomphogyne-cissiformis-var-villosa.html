Gomphogyne cissiformis var. villosa

Gomphogyne cissiformis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gomphogyne, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, vine-like growth habit.

It features elongated, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental qualities make it a desirable species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
