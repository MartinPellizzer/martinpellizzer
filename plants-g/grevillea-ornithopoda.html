Grevillea ornithopoda

Grevillea ornithopoda, commonly known as the blackboy, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the south-west of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, dark green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in clusters, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Grevillea ornithopoda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
