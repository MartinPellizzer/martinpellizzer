Gagea provisa

Gagea provisa, commonly known as the common gagea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded environments, thriving in well-drained, loamy soils. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant's growth and reproduction.

Gagea provisa is often found in moist, temperate climates and is known for its delicate, early spring bloom.
