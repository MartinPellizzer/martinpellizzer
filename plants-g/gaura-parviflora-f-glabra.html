Gaura parviflora f. glabra

Gaura parviflora f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth gaura, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has smooth, hairless leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, often with a slightly wavy margin. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
