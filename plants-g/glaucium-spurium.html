Glaucium spurium

Glaucium spurium, commonly known as the yellow hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, fleshy roots and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, often arranged in a whorl at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
