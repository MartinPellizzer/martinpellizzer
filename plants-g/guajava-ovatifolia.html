Guajava ovatifolia

Guajava ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf guajava, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
