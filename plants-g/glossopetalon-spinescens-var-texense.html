Glossopetalon spinescens var. texense

Glossopetalon spinescens var. texense, commonly known as the Texas spineflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of Texas.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires protection to ensure its survival.
