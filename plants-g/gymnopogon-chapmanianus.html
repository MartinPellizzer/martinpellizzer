Gymnopogon chapmanianus

Gymnopogon chapmanianus, commonly known as Chapman's grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

It is often found in open, sandy or rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
