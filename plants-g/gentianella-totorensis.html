Gentianella totorensis

Gentianella totorensis, commonly known as the Totoral gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentianella totorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystems of the Andes.
