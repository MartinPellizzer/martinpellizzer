Glochidion acuminatum var. acuminatum

Glochidion acuminatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the pointed glochidion, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often glandular-stipulate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in umbellate inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that dehisce to release tiny seeds.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
