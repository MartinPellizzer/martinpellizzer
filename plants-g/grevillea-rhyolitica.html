Grevillea rhyolitica

Grevillea rhyolitica, commonly known as the red grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid and rocky environments. It is often found growing on granite outcrops and in sandy soils.

Grevillea rhyolitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a key species in the conservation of native flora.
