Galium tetragonum

Galium tetragonum, commonly known as common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are quadrangular, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and disturbed soils.

Galium tetragonum is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
