Gentianella germanica subsp. semleri

Gentianella germanica subsp.

semleri, commonly known as the slender gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
