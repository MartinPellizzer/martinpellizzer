Glomera umbrosa

Glomera umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow glomera, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the dense, shaded understories of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are adapted to low-light conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the early morning. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption from the poor soil.

Due to habitat destruction, Glomera umbrosa is considered endangered and is often difficult to locate in the wild.
