Glandularia tenera

Glandularia tenera, commonly known as the sticky verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves, which secrete a sticky substance, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Glandularia tenera is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
