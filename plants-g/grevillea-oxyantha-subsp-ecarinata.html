Grevillea oxyantha subsp. ecarinata

Grevillea oxyantha subsp.

ecarinata, commonly known as the long-horned grevillea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features striking, elongated, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
