Geunsia farinosa var. callicarpoides

Geunsia farinosa var. callicarpoides, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky habitats in the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in extreme conditions, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
