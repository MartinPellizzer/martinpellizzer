Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp. implexum

Gymnocalycium bruchii subsp.

implexum, commonly known as the tangled moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular stem that is often covered in a dense layer of white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
