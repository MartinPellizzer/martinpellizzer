Galium verum var. leiophyllum

Galium verum var. leiophyllum, commonly known as yellow bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
