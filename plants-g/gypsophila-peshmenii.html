Gypsophila peshmenii

Gypsophila peshmenii, commonly known as the Peshmenian laceflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms during the summer months.

Gypsophila peshmenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its ethereal appearance.
