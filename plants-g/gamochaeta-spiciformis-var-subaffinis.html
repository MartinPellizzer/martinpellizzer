Gamochaeta spiciformis var. subaffinis

Gamochaeta spiciformis var. subaffinis, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, oval seeds dispersed by wind.
