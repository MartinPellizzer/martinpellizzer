Garcinia pushpangadaniana

Garcinia pushpangadaniana, commonly known as the Nilgiri Garcinia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its elliptical, orange-red fruits, which are often used in traditional medicine. Garcinia pushpangadaniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various insects and birds.
