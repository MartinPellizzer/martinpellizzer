Gentiana ovatiloba subsp. chazaroi

Gentiana ovatiloba subsp.

chazaroi, commonly known as the Chazaroi gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are slender and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
