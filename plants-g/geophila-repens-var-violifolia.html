Geophila repens var. violifolia

Geophila repens var. violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf geophila, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with rhizomatous stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and blue, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
