Gentiana curvianthera

Gentiana curvianthera, commonly known as the curved-thorn gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are characterized by their deep blue color and five petals, with the unique curved shape of its stamens.

Gentiana curvianthera is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
