Gomphocarpus fruticosus subsp. flavidus

Gomphocarpus fruticosus subsp.

flavidus, commonly known as the yellow bushveld milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance.

The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is also known for its ability to attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.
