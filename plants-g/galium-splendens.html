Galium splendens

Galium splendens, commonly known as shining bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Galium splendens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
