Gnaphalium lagopodioides

Gnaphalium lagopodioides, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble small, fluffy balls. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its prolonged flowering period.
