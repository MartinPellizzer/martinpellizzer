Glycosmis parviflora var. obtusa

Glycosmis parviflora var. obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candy apple tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellow or orange fruits that resemble small apples. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
