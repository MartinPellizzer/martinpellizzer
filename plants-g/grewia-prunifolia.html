Grewia prunifolia

Grewia prunifolia, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Grewia prunifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
