Glosocomia lurida

Glosocomia lurida, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces striking white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
