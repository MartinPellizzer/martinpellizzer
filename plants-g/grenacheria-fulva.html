Grenacheria fulva

Grenacheria fulva, commonly known as the golden grenacher, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Grenacheria fulva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
