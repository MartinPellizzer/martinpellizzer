Gerardia tenuifolia var. leucanthera

Gerardia tenuifolia var. leucanthera, commonly known as the white-flowered gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It is often found in maritime climates and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in similar climates.
