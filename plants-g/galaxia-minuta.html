Galaxia minuta

Galaxia minuta, commonly known as the small galaxia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from April to May. The plant has delicate, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland understories.

Galaxia minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
