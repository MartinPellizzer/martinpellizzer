Gagea polyphylla

Gagea polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved gagea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, grass-like leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. Gagea polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
