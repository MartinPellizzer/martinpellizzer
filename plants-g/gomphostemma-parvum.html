Gomphostemma parvum

Gomphostemma parvum, commonly known as smallleaf gomphostemma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a follicle containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
