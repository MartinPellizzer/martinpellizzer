Gentiana newberryi subsp. newberryi

Gentiana newberryi subsp.

newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows, often in areas with well-drained soil.

The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
