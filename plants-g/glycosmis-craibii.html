Glycosmis craibii

Glycosmis craibii, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Glycosmis craibii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.
