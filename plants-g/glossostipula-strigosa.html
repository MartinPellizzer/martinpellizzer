Glossostipula strigosa

Glossostipula strigosa, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed sterculia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stem covered in fine, hairy pubescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is found in parts of Southeast Asia. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Glossostipula strigosa produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
