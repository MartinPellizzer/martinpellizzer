Garcinia borneensis

Garcinia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean garcinia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant bears orange-red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, including hydroxycitric acid. Garcinia borneensis is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
