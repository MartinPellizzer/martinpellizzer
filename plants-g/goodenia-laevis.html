Goodenia laevis

Goodenia laevis, commonly known as the smooth goodenia, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Goodeniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ascending stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically linear and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
