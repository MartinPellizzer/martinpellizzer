Gireoudia sarcophylla

Gireoudia sarcophylla, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features hairy, grey-green leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Gireoudia sarcophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
