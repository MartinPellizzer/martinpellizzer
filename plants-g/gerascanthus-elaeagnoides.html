Gerascanthus elaeagnoides

Gerascanthus elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf geranium, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
