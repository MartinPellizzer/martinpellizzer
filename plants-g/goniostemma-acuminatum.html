Goniostemma acuminatum

Goniostemma acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed goniostemma, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in humid, shaded environments.
