Garcinia neglecta

Garcinia neglecta, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically orange to red in color, with a thick, leathery rind. Garcinia neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
