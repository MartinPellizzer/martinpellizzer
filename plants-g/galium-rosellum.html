Galium rosellum

Galium rosellum, commonly known as Roselle bedstraw, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Galium rosellum is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
