Gentiana waltonii f. nana

Gentiana waltonii f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf gentian, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and vibrant flowers make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
