Goodyera reticulata

Goodyera reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate saxifrage, is a small, evergreen orchid native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate network of veins on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or among leaf litter.

Goodyera reticulata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
