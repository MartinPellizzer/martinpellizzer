Gouldia hillebrandii var. typica

Gouldia hillebrandii var. typica, commonly known as the red lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal in gardens and landscapes.
