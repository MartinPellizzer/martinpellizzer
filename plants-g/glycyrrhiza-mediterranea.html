Glycyrrhiza mediterranea

Glycyrrhiza mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean licorice, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stem and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing small, rounded seeds. Glycyrrhiza mediterranea is valued for its roots, which contain glycyrrhizin, a compound used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
