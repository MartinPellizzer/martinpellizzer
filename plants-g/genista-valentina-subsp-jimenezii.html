Genista valentina subsp. jimenezii

Genista valentina subsp.

jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces yellow, clustered flowers in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
