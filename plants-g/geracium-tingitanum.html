Geracium tingitanum

Geracium tingitanum, commonly known as the Cape Verde geranium, is a flowering plant native to the island of Santiago in the Cape Verde archipelago.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its bright red or pink flowers bloom during the dry season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Geracium tingitanum is considered a rare and endangered species.
