Glochidion beehleri

Glochidion beehleri, commonly known as the Beelarum glabrous leaf.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by glochidiate fruits that are covered in tiny, hooked spines. Glochidion beehleri is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
