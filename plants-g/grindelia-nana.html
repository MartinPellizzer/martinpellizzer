Grindelia nana

Grindelia nana, commonly known as dwarf gumweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color.

Grindelia nana is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.
