Gussonia echinata

Gussonia echinata, commonly known as the prickly gussonia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Gussonia echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
