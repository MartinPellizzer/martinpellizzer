Gleditsia sinensis var. inermis

Gleditsia sinensis var. inermis, commonly known as the Chinese honey locust, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and can reach up to 30 inches in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by slender, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
