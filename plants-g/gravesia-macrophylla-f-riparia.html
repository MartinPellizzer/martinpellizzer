Gravesia macrophylla f. riparia

Gravesia macrophylla f. riparia, commonly known as the riparian gravesia, is a variant of the Gravesia macrophylla species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and yellowish-green, appearing in clusters.

This form is notable for its adaptability to riparian habitats and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
