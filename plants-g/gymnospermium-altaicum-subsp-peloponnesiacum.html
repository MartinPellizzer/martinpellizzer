Gymnospermium altaicum subsp. peloponnesiacum

Gymnospermium altaicum subsp.

peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian pine, is a rare gymnosperm native to specific regions of Greece. It belongs to the conifer family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant exhibits a unique morphological adaptation to its Mediterranean climate, featuring thick, waxy foliage to reduce water loss. Its reproductive structures are typically found in clusters, and it plays a minor but ecologically significant role in local forest ecosystems.
