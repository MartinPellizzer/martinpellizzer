Gunnia picta

Gunnia picta, commonly known as the painted gunnia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical with a waxy texture and often display a range of hues including green, red, and purple. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
