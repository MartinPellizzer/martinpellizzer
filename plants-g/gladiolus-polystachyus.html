Gladiolus polystachyus

Gladiolus polystachyus, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant flowers in a range of colors, including red, yellow, and orange, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

Gladiolus polystachyus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
