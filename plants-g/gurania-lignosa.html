Gurania lignosa

Gurania lignosa, commonly known as the hardy shrub or tree, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, which contribute to its structural resilience. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small and leathery, providing efficient water retention.

Gurania lignosa also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by small, woody fruits.
