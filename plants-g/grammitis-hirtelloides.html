Grammitis hirtelloides

Grammitis hirtelloides, commonly known as the hairy grammitis, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The fronds are typically dark green and may have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
