Garcinia pachyclada

Garcinia pachyclada, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody pods containing acidic, orange-red fruits. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Garcinia pachyclada thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
