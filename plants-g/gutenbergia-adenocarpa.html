Gutenbergia adenocarpa

Gutenbergia adenocarpa, commonly known as the woolly apple tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Gutenbergia adenocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
