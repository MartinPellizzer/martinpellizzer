Glyceria spectabilis var. flaccida

Glyceria spectabilis var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are generally bluish-green in color and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, contributing to its role in wetland ecosystems.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions, making it an important component of aquatic plant communities.
