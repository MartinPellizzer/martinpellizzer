Gladiolus blandus

Gladiolus blandus, commonly known as the pale gladiolus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes and are composed of three outer petals and three inner sepals that resemble petals. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
