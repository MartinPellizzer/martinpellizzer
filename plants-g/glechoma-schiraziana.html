Glechoma schiraziana

Glechoma schiraziana, commonly known as Persian ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, which bloom in the spring and early summer. Glechoma schiraziana is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
