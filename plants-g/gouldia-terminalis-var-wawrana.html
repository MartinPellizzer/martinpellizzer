Gouldia terminalis var. wawrana

Gouldia terminalis var. wawrana, commonly known as the Wawrana gouldia, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic landscapes.
