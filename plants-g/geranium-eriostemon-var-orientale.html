Geranium eriostemon var. orientale

Geranium eriostemon var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental geranium, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded and have a soft, hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in arid environments. This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
