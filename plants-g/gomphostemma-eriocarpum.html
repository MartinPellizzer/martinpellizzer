Gomphostemma eriocarpum

Gomphostemma eriocarpum, commonly known as the hairy fruit gomphostemma, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, hairy, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
