Glicophyllum stylopterum

Glicophyllum stylopterum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. The plant features a compact, upright growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a single, erect stem. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a dense spike, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Glicophyllum stylopterum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
