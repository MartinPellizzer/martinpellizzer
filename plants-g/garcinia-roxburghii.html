Garcinia roxburghii

Garcinia roxburghii, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into sour, orange-red fruits. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a thick, acidic pulp. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is commonly found in forested areas.
