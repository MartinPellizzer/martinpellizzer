Gymnopteris bernoullii, commonly known as the Bernoulli's barren fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a smooth, glossy texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. Gymnopteris bernoullii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
