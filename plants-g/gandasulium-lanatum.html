Gandasulium lanatum

Gandasulium lanatum, commonly known as the silvery flax plant, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Gandasulium lanatum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
