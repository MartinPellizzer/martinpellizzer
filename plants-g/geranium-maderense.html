Geranium maderense

Geranium maderense, commonly known as the Madeira geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Azores and Madeira islands.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces vibrant red or pink blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Geranium maderense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
