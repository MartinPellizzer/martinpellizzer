Galactia excisa

Galactia excisa, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems and leaves contain milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Galactia.

Galactia excisa is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
