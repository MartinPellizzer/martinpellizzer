Gilbertiodendron klainei

Gilbertiodendron klainei, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
