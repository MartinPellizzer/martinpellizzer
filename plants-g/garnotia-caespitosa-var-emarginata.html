Garnotia caespitosa var. emarginata

Garnotia caespitosa var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate garnotia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright, clump-forming stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
