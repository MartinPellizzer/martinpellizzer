Gynura acuminatissima

Gynura acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
