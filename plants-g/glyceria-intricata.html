Glyceria intricata

Glyceria intricata, commonly known as the slender glyceria, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. This species is often found in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Glyceria intricata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its growth patterns and ecological significance make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
