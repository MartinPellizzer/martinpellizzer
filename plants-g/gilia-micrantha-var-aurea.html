Gilia micrantha var. aurea

Gilia micrantha var. aurea, commonly known as golden gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are arranged in clusters and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
