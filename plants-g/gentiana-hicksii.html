Gentiana hicksii

Gentiana hicksii, commonly known as the Hick's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Gentiana hicksii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
