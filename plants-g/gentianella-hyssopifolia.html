Gentianella hyssopifolia

Gentianella hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species with showy, spurred flowers.

Gentianella hyssopifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
