Grevillea hislopii

Grevillea hislopii, commonly known as the pink spike heath, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in heathland and sandplain environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Grevillea hislopii is well adapted to the harsh, dry conditions of its native habitat.
