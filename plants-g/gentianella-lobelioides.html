Gentianella lobelioides

Gentianella lobelioides, commonly known as the small gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
