Gardenia pubescens

Gardenia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy gardenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Gardenia pubescens is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
