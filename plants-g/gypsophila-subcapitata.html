Gypsophila subcapitata

Gypsophila subcapitata, commonly known as the baby's breath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often overlooked due to the showy flower heads.

Gypsophila subcapitata is widely used in floral arrangements and as a garden ornamental due to its delicate beauty.
