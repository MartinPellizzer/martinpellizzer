Gladiolus fulvescens

Gladiolus fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive spathe and spadix structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
