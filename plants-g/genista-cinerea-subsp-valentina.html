Genista cinerea subsp. valentina

Genista cinerea subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
