Guaiabara

Guaiabara, commonly known as *Pouteria campechiana*, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a fleshy, sweet-smelling fruit that is highly valued for its culinary and medicinal properties. Its bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
