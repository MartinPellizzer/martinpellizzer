Gentiana amarella f. michauxiana

Gentiana amarella f. michauxiana, commonly known as the Michaux's gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
