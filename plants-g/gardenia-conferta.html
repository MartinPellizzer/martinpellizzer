Gardenia conferta

Gardenia conferta, commonly known as the African gardenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with yellow stamens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and scent.

Gardenia conferta prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.
