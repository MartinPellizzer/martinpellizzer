Gyrocheilos

Gyrocheilos, commonly known as the spiral lip orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped lip, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plants are native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions like China, India, and Indonesia. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

Gyrocheilos species often have showy flowers with intricate patterns, making them popular among orchid enthusiasts.
