Gnaphalium minimum var. erectum

Gnaphalium minimum var. erectum, commonly known as the upright feather grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery foliage. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with fine, soft hairs, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ability to stabilize soil, it is valued in ecological restoration projects.
