Galium pogonanthum var. setuliflorum

Galium pogonanthum var. setuliflorum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
