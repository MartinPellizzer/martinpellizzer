Geranium diversifolium

Geranium diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lobed leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The flowers are typically pink to purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
