Gentiana altaica

Gentiana altaica, commonly known as the Siberian gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Gentiana altaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
