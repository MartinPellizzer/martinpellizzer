Glyceria latifolia

Glyceria latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf glyceria, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist soil and is often found along the edges of ponds, lakes, and wet meadows.

Glyceria latifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
