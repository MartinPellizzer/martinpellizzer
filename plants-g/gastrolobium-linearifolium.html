Gastrolobium linearifolium

Gastrolobium linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its linear, often narrow and slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in raceme-like clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
