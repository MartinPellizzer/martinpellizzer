Guadua angustifolia var. bicolor

Guadua angustifolia var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor bamboo, is a variety of the Andean bamboo species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking two-toned coloration, with green stems contrasting against a lighter, often white or pale yellow hue. This plant is a tall, fast-growing grass that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a valuable resource for construction and landscaping. Its hollow, jointed stalks are strong and durable, used in traditional and modern applications.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and ample sunlight.
