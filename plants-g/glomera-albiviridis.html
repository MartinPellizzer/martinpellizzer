Glomera albiviridis

Glomera albiviridis, commonly known as the white-veined glomera, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white and green variegated leaves, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
