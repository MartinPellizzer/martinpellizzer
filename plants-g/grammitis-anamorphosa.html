Grammitis anamorphosa

Grammitis anamorphosa, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and typically attaches itself to trees or rocks. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing capsules, are small and clustered near the base of the fronds.

Grammitis anamorphosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
