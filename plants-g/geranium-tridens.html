Geranium tridens

Geranium tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, featuring upright stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom from late spring to early fall. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in woodland or open areas.

Geranium tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
