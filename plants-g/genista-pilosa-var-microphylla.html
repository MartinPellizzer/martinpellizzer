Genista pilosa var. microphylla

Genista pilosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. The flowers are typically yellow, occurring in clusters and blooming in early spring. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid environments.
