Genista tricuspidata var. villosa

Genista tricuspidata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy thorned broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several pairs of oval, hairy leaflets. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
