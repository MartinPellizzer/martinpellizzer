Gentiana amarella f. acutifolia

Gentiana amarella f. acutifolia, commonly known as the acuteleaf gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it the specific epithet "acutifolia.".

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
