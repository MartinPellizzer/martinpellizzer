Gentiana quadrifaria var. wightii

Gentiana quadrifaria var. wightii, commonly known as the Wight's gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
