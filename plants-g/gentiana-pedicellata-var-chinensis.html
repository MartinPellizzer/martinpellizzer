Gentiana pedicellata var. chinensis

Gentiana pedicellata var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese gentian, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects like bees and butterflies.
