Genista biflora

Genista biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Genista biflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
