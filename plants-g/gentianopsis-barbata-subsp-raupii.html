Gentianopsis barbata subsp. raupii

Gentianopsis barbata subsp.

raupii, commonly known as the barbed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
