Galipea congestiflora

Galipea congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered galipea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution in its native habitat.
