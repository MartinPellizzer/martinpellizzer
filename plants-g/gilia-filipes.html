Gilia filipes

Gilia filipes, commonly known as the featherleaf gilia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and feathery, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Gilia filipes is an annual plant that blooms from spring to early summer, contributing to the floral diversity of its native ecosystems.
