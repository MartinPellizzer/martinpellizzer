Geranium asiaticum f. molle

Geranium asiaticum f. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved geranium, is a compact perennial plant native to regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and adaptability to various growing conditions.
