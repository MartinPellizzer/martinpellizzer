Gossia scottiana

Gossia scottiana, commonly known as the Scott's gossia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the southeastern region of Australia, specifically found in New South Wales and Queensland. The plant is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
