Genista laburnum

Genista laburnum, commonly known as the goldenrod.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Genista laburnum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
