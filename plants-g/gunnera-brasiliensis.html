Gunnera brasiliensis

Gunnera brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian giant water shield, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, which are often floating on the water surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and wetlands, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix, typically appearing in late summer or early autumn.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
