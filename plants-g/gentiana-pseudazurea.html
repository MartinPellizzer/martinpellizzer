Gentiana pseudazurea

Gentiana pseudazurea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
