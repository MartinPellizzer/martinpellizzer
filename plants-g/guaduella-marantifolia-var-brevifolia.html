Guaduella marantifolia var. brevifolia

Guaduella marantifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
