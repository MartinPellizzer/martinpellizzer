Galactia jussiaeana var. peruviana

Galactia jussiaeana var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, often white or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
