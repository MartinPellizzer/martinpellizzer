Geranium nanum

Geranium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring compact foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has opposite leaves that are rounded to ovate in shape, often with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Geranium nanum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
