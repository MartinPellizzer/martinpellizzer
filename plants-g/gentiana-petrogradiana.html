Gentiana petrogradiana

Gentiana petrogradiana, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high mountain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in rocky, alpine environments where it forms dense clusters on steep slopes and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly toothed, adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Gentiana petrogradiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
