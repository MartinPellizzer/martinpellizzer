Gnaphalium squamosum

Gnaphalium squamosum, commonly known as the silverleaf mouseear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces tiny yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Gnaphalium squamosum is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
