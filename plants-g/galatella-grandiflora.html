Galatella grandiflora

Galatella grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered galatella, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Galatella grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
