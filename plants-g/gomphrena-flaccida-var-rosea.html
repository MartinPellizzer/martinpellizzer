Gomphrena flaccida var. rosea

Gomphrena flaccida var. rosea, commonly known as the rosea blushing gomphrena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its clusters of pink to rose-colored bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically hidden within the showy bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its long-lasting, vibrant blooms that add color to gardens throughout the growing season.
