Gladiopappus vernonioides

Gladiopappus vernonioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its striking blooms and adaptability to various climates.
