Goekyighitia yildirimlii

Goekyighitia yildirimlii, commonly known as the lightning vine, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length, often cascading over rocky outcrops. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid environment.

Goekyighitia yildirimlii is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
