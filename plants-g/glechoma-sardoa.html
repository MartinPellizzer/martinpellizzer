Glechoma sardoa

Glechoma sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian ground-ivy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Glechoma sardoa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
