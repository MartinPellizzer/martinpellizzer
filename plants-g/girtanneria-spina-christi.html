Girtanneria spina-christi

Girtanneria spina-christi, commonly known as the Christ's thorn, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant's leaves are typically small, oval, and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
