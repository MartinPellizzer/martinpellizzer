Grammitis blechnoides

Grammitis blechnoides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Blechnaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Grammitis blechnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
