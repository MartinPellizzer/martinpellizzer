Gossypium album

Gossypium album, commonly known as the white cotton plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its fluffy, white fibers that are harvested for textile use. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

Gossypium album is an important crop in many parts of the world for its economic value in the textile industry.
