Globba parva

Globba parva, commonly known as the dwarf ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant features erect, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a subtle, spicy aroma.
