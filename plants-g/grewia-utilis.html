Grewia utilis

Grewia utilis, commonly known as the sour fig or red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and small, red or orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

The fruit is edible, though its taste is often described as sour or tart.
