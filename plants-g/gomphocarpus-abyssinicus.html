Gomphocarpus abyssinicus

Gomphocarpus abyssinicus, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are alternate and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
