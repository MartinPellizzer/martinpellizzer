Gentiana bellidifolia var. australis

Gentiana bellidifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern regions of Australia.

It is a perennial herb with striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
