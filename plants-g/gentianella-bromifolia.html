Gentianella bromifolia

Gentianella bromifolia, commonly known as the bromegrass gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, blue to violet flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Gentianella bromifolia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
